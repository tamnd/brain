## Problem-Type Check

This is a **Type B - "Prove that"** problem. The statement asks to compute the ratio of volumes of two solids obtained by a plane intersecting a tetrahedron in a specified manner. The proof must show:

1. The ratio of the distances from the plane to the skew edges determines the parameter $t$ of the section uniquely.
2. The volumes below and above the plane are computed exactly and their ratio is expressed in terms of $k$.

The proof must cover **all tetrahedra with arbitrary skew lines and distances**, not just a special coordinate choice, and must correctly translate the ratio condition $x:y=k$ to the plane's position.

## Step-by-Step Verification

Step 1: Place coordinates such that $AB$ is in $z=0$ and $CD$ in $z=d$ - **VALID**

The argument that any plane parallel to both lines must have normal in the $z$-direction is correct. The coordinates are general enough to represent any tetrahedron via an affine transformation. No gap.

Step 2: Express points in tetrahedron as

P=(1-\lambda)((1-\mu)A+\mu B)+\lambda((1-\nu)C+\nu D),\quad 0\le\lambda,\mu,\nu\le1 \] - **VALID** This is a standard linear interpolation along skew edges and fully parameterizes the tetrahedron. Step 3: Observe that \(z=\lambda d\) - **VALID** Follows directly from the previous step. Step 4: Claim that distance from plane \(z=t\) to \(AB\) is \(t\) and to \(CD\) is \(d-t\) - **VALID** Because the lines lie entirely in planes \(z=0\) and \(z=d\), the perpendicular distance is exactly the vertical distance. No geometric assumption skipped. Step 5: Solve \(\frac{t}{d-t}=k \Rightarrow t = \frac{kd}{k+1}\) - **VALID** Algebra is straightforward and correct. Step 6: Compute cross-sectional area at height \(z\): \[ A(z)=\left|\bigl((1-\frac{z}{d})a,0,0\bigr)\times (\frac{z}{d}b\cos\omega, \frac{z}{d}b\sin\omega,0)\right|=ab\sin\omega(1-\frac{z}{d})\frac{z}{d} \] - **VALID** Vector cross product calculation is correct; quadratic scaling in \(z\) is justified. Step 7: Integrate to find volume below plane: \[ V(t)=ab\sin\omega \int_0^t \left(\frac{z}{d}-\frac{z^2}{d^2}\right)dz = ab\sin\omega \left(\frac{t^2}{2d}-\frac{t^3}{3d^2}\right) \] - **VALID** Integration is standard; formula matches previous analysis. Step 8: Total volume \(V_\text{tot}=V(d)=\frac{abd\sin\omega}{6}\) - **VALID** Step 9: Fraction below plane: \[ \frac{V(t)}{V_\text{tot}} = 3(t/d)^2 - 2(t/d)^3 \] - **VALID** Algebra verified.
