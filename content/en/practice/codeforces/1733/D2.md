## Corrected Solution for Exercise 3.3.4.1 [_M10_]

In one dimension, the spectral test reduces to a question about the spacing of points generated by a one-dimensional linear congruential generator modulo the modulus. A linear congruential generator produces a sequence

$$X_{n+1} = (a X_n + c) \bmod m$$

and the spectral test in higher dimensions analyzes the lattice structure formed by the tuples $(X_n, X_{n+1}, \dots, X_{n+t-1})$. When $t = 1$, each tuple is just a single number modulo $m$.

In this case, the lattice reduces to points on the one-dimensional unit interval, scaled by $1/m$. The quality of the generator is measured by the maximal distance between successive points in the sorted sequence of all generated numbers. Equivalently, the spectral test in one dimension checks the largest spacing between successive values of $X_n/m$ in $[0,1)$.

Since the generator produces a full period of integers modulo $m$ (assuming maximal period), these points are evenly spaced with spacing $1/m$. If the generator does not achieve full period, the maximal spacing will be larger, and this corresponds to a poor lattice quality in the spectral sense.

Formally, if the generator produces $m$ distinct values $X_0, X_1, \dots, X_{m-1}$ modulo $m$, then after scaling by $1/m$ the points are

$$u_i = \frac{X_i}{m}, \quad i = 0, \dots, m-1$$

and the one-dimensional spectral measure is

$$\max_{0 \le i < m} (u_{i+1} - u_i)$$

where $u_m = u_0 + 1$ for the circular wrap-around. This is exactly the length of the largest empty interval in $[0,1)$ between consecutive points.

Thus in one dimension, the spectral test reduces entirely to computing the largest gap between successive points generated by the sequence modulo the modulus. No multi-dimensional lattice calculations are required, and the quality is completely determined by this maximal spacing.
