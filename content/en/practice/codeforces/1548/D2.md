## Problem Understanding

We are asked to count triangles formed by a set of given fence posts such that each triangle has two properties: its area is an integer, and the number of integer-coordinate points strictly inside it is odd. The plane is filled with cows at every integer point, so an "enclosed cow" is simply a lattice point inside the triangle. The input gives the coordinates of the fence posts, all distinct, and no three lie on the same line, so every triple forms a proper triangle. The output is the number of triangles that meet both conditions.
