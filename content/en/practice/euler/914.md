[Project Euler Problem 914](https://projecteuler.net/problem=914)

## Solution

**Answer:** `414213562371805310`

Let

$$(a,b,c)=(m^2-n^2,\,2mn,\,m^2+n^2)$$

be a primitive Pythagorean triple, where

- $m>n>0$,
- $\gcd(m,n)=1$,
- $m-n$ is odd.

For a right triangle:

- circumradius $=\dfrac c2$,
- inradius $= \dfrac{a+b-c}{2}=n(m-n)$.

The triangle fits strictly inside a circle of radius $R$ iff

$$\frac c2<R
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
m^2+n^2<2R.$$

Therefore

$$F(R)=\max n(m-n)$$

subject to

$$m^2+n^2<2R,$$

with the primitive conditions above.

Write

$$p=m-n,\qquad q=n.$$

Then

$$m=p+q,$$

and the constraint becomes

$$(p+q)^2+q^2<2R$$

i.e.

$$p^2+2pq+2q^2<2R.$$

The inradius is simply

$$r=pq.$$

For fixed $q$, the largest admissible $p$ is

$$p_{\max}=\left\lfloor \sqrt{2R-q^2}-q \right\rfloor .$$

The continuous optimum occurs near

$$\frac pq=\sqrt2,$$

so it is enough to search in a narrow interval around

$$q_0=\sqrt{\frac{R}{2+\sqrt2}}.$$

Using an exact integer search around this optimum for

$$R=10^{18},$$

the best admissible pair is

$$(p,q)=(765366155,\ 541196602),$$

giving

$$r=pq
 =765366155\cdot 541196602
 =414213562371805310.$$

This satisfies

$$p^2+2pq+2q^2
=1999999999995787453
<2\cdot 10^{18},$$

so the triangle indeed fits strictly inside the circle.

Also,

$$F(100)=36,$$

matching the statement.

Therefore,

**Answer: 414213562371805310**
