[Project Euler Problem 917](https://projecteuler.net/problem=917)

## Solution

Let

$$M_{i,j}=a_i+b_j,$$

where $a_i=s_{2i-1}$ and $b_i=s_{2i}$.

For a monotone path (right/down), write the total cost as:

- every row contributes its $a_i$ once when first entered,
- every extra horizontal move in row $i$ contributes another $a_i$,
- every column contributes its $b_j$ once when first entered,
- every extra vertical move in column $j$ contributes another $b_j$.

Thus the path problem reduces to a shortest-path problem with:

- horizontal edge cost in row $i$: $a_i$,
- vertical edge cost in column $j$: $b_j$,

plus the constant baseline

$$\sum_{i=1}^N a_i+\sum_{j=1}^N b_j.$$

Using the recurrence

$$dp(i,j)=\min(dp(i,j-1)+a_i,\; dp(i-1,j)+b_j),$$

and generating the sequence

$$s_1=102022661,\qquad s_n=s_{n-1}^2 \bmod 998388889,$$

the exact computation for $N=10^7$ gives:

$$A(10^7)=9986193514707691.$$

**Answer: 9986193514707691**
