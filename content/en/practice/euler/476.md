[Project Euler Problem 476](https://projecteuler.net/problem=476)

## Solution

Let the triangle have inradius $r$, and let the three vertex half-angle sines be

$$s_A=\sin\frac A2,\qquad s_B=\sin\frac B2,\qquad s_C=\sin\frac C2.$$

A circle tangent to the two sides meeting at a vertex and tangent to a previous circle of radius $R$ in that corner has radius

$$R\cdot \frac{1-s}{1+s}.$$

Thus, in each corner we get an independent geometric chain of tangent circles:

$$r q,\quad r q^2,\quad r q^3,\ldots$$

where

$$q=\frac{1-\sin(\theta/2)}{1+\sin(\theta/2)}.$$

The key observation is that once the incircle is placed (always optimal as the largest possible circle), the remaining circles in different corners never interact. Therefore:

- place the incircle $r$,
- then choose the **two largest radii** among the three corner sequences.

Since every sequence is decreasing geometrically, only the first two terms from each corner can possibly matter:

$$r q_i,\qquad r q_i^2.$$

Using

$$\sin\frac A2
=
\sqrt{\frac{(s-b)(s-c)}{bc}}$$

(and cyclic permutations), with semiperimeter $s=\frac{a+b+c}{2}$, we can compute

$$R(a,b,c)
=
\pi r^2
\left(
1 + \lambda_1 + \lambda_2
\right),$$

where $\lambda_1,\lambda_2$ are the two largest values among

$$q_A^2,\ q_A^4,\ q_B^2,\ q_B^4,\ q_C^2,\ q_C^4.$$

This reproduces the checks:

- $S(2)=R(1,1,1)\approx 0.31998$,
- $S(5)\approx 1.25899$,

matching the problem statement.

Evaluating over all integer triples

$$1\le a\le b\le c<a+b\le 1803$$

gives

$$S(1803)=110242.877944313\ldots$$

Rounded to five decimal places:

**Answer: 110242.87794**
