[Project Euler Problem 904](https://projecteuler.net/problem=904)

## Solution

**Answer:** `880652522278760`

Let the primitive Pythagorean triple be parameterized by

$$a=m^2-n^2,\qquad b=2mn,\qquad c=m^2+n^2,$$

with $m>n$, $\gcd(m,n)=1$, and $m+n$ odd.

For the angle between the two medians on the legs,

$$\tan\theta=\frac{3ab}{2c^2}.$$

Substituting the Euclid parametrization gives

$$\tan\theta
=\frac{3mn(m^2-n^2)}{(m^2+n^2)^2}.$$

Writing $u=m/n$,

$$\tan\theta=\frac{3u(u^2-1)}{(u^2+1)^2}.$$

For each target angle $\alpha=\sqrt[3]{n}$, the optimal rational approximation $u=m/n$ under the constraint

$$m^2+n^2\le 10^{10}$$

can be found efficiently using continued fractions / semiconvergents, checking both monotone branches of the function above.

Scaling by

$$k=\left\lfloor \frac{10^{10}}{m^2+n^2}\right\rfloor$$

maximizes the area among tied angular errors, and the perimeter contributed is

$$2k\,m(m+n).$$

The implementation reproduces the checkpoints:

$$f(30,10^2)=198,$$

$$f(10,10^6)=1600158,$$

$$F(10,10^6)=16684370.$$

Carrying out the full computation for

$$F(45000,10^{10})$$

gives:

**Answer: 880652522278760**
