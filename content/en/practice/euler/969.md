[Project Euler Problem 969](https://projecteuler.net/problem=969)

## Solution

**Answer:** `412543690`

Let $H(x)$ be the expected number of hops needed to pass $x$.

For $x>0$,

$$H(x)=1+\int_0^1 H(x-t)\,dt,$$

because after the first hop of size $t\in[0,1]$, the remaining expected number is $H(x-t)$.

Differentiating gives the delay differential equation

$$H'(x)=H(x)-H(x-1),$$

with $H(x)=0$ for $x<0$.

Solving recursively on intervals $[n,n+1]$ yields the classical formula

$$H(x)=\sum_{k=0}^{\lfloor x\rfloor}
\frac{(-1)^k}{k!}(x-k)^k e^{\,x-k}.$$

Since $\alpha=H(1)=e$, for integer $n$,

$$H(n)=\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}
\frac{(-1)^k}{k!}(n-k)^k \alpha^{\,n-k}.$$

Hence the coefficient of $\alpha^{n-k}$ is

$$\frac{(-1)^k (n-k)^k}{k!}.$$

We need the integer coefficients only.

---

A coefficient is integral iff

$$k! \mid (n-k)^k.$$

Using $v_p(k!)\le k$ for every prime $p\le k$, this is equivalent to requiring that every prime $p\le k$ divides $n-k$. Therefore,

$$\prod_{p\le k} p \mid (n-k).$$

Let

$$P(k)=\prod_{p\le k} p$$

(the primorial up to $k$).

Then

$$S(n)=\sum_{\substack{k\ge0\\ P(k)\mid (n-k)}}
\frac{(-1)^k (n-k)^k}{k!}.$$

Therefore

$$\sum_{n\le N} S(n)
=
\sum_{k\ge0}\frac{(-1)^k}{k!}
\sum_{\substack{m\le N-k\\ P(k)\mid m}} m^k.$$

Write $m=P(k)t$:

$$=
\sum_{k\ge0}
\frac{(-1)^k P(k)^k}{k!}
\sum_{t\le (N-k)/P(k)} t^k.$$

For $N=10^{18}$, only $k\le 52$ contribute because $P(53)>10^{18}$.

Evaluating these 53 finite terms modulo $10^9+7$ gives

$$412543690.$$

(Checked against the sample:

$\sum_{n=1}^{10}S(n)=43$.)

**Answer: 412543690**
