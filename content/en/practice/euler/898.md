[Project Euler Problem 898](https://projecteuler.net/problem=898)

## Solution

**Answer:** `0.9861343531`

By Bayesian optimal play, Claire should choose the outcome with the larger posterior probability given the students’ answers. Since the coin is fair and lies are independent, this reduces to comparing the likelihood ratio

$$\prod_i \frac{P(\text{report}_i \mid H)}{P(\text{report}_i \mid T)}.$$

For lie probabilities $25\%,26\%,\dots,75\%$, the $50\%$ student contributes no information, and the remaining students pair symmetrically as $p$ and $1-p$. Using a meet-in-the-middle dynamic enumeration of the resulting weighted evidence states gives Claire’s optimal success probability.
