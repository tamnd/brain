[Project Euler Problem 919](https://projecteuler.net/problem=919)

## Solution

**Answer:** `134222859969633`

Using the identity for a triangle with circumradius $R$ and orthocentre $H$,

$$AH = 2R|\cos A|$$

the condition “distance to the orthocentre is exactly half the distance to the circumcentre” becomes

$$AH=\frac12 AO=\frac12 R$$

so

$$2R|\cos A|=\frac12R
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
|\cos A|=\frac14.$$

Applying the Law of Cosines gives the Diophantine condition

$$\left|2(b^2+c^2-a^2)\right|=bc.$$

This can be transformed into a Pell-type factorization and parametrized efficiently. I implemented an exact integer enumeration using the derived parametrization, carefully deduplicating similar triangles and summing all integer multiples with perimeter $\le P$.

- $S(10)=24$ ✓
- $S(100)=3331$ ✓
