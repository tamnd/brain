[Project Euler Problem 162](https://projecteuler.net/problem=162)

## Solution

Let $f(n)$ be the number of valid $n$-digit hexadecimal numbers (no leading zero) that contain each of the digits $0$, $1$, and $\mathrm A$ at least once.

We want

$$\sum_{n=1}^{16} f(n)$$

and the final answer must be written in hexadecimal.

---

# Step 1: Count all $n$-digit hexadecimal numbers

For an $n$-digit hexadecimal number:

- first digit: $15$ choices ($1$–$F$)
- remaining $n-1$ digits: $16$ choices each

So:

$$T(n)=15\cdot 16^{n-1}$$

---

# Step 2: Inclusion–Exclusion

We require the digits $0,1,\mathrm A$ all to appear.

Define:

- $A_0$: numbers missing digit $0$
- $A_1$: numbers missing digit $1$
- $A_A$: numbers missing digit $\mathrm A$

Then

$$f(n)=T(n)-|A_0\cup A_1\cup A_A|$$

Apply inclusion–exclusion.

---

## Single exclusions

### Missing $0$

If $0$ never appears, every position may use only the other $15$ digits.

Leading digit restriction disappears automatically because $0$ is unavailable anyway.

$$|A_0| = 15^n$$

---

### Missing $1$

Available digits: all except $1$.

- leading digit: cannot be $0$ or $1$ $\Rightarrow 14$ choices
- remaining digits: $15$ choices

$$|A_1| = 14\cdot 15^{n-1}$$

Similarly,

$$|A_A| = 14\cdot 15^{n-1}$$

---

## Double exclusions

### Missing $0$ and $1$

Allowed digits: $14$ digits.

Leading digit automatically nonzero because $0$ excluded.

$$|A_0\cap A_1| = 14^n$$

Similarly,

$$|A_0\cap A_A| = 14^n$$

---

### Missing $1$ and $\mathrm A$

Allowed digits: $14$ digits including $0$.

Leading digit cannot be $0$, $1$, or $\mathrm A$:

$$13 \text{ choices}$$

Remaining positions:

$$14^{n-1}$$

Hence

$$|A_1\cap A_A| = 13\cdot 14^{n-1}$$

---

## Triple exclusion

Missing all $0,1,\mathrm A$.

Allowed digits: $13$ digits.

Leading digit automatically nonzero because $0$ excluded.

$$|A_0\cap A_1\cap A_A| = 13^n$$

---

# Step 3: Final formula

Therefore,

$$\boxed{
f(n)=15\cdot16^{n-1}
-15^n
-2\cdot14\cdot15^{n-1}
+2\cdot14^n
+13\cdot14^{n-1}
-13^n
}$$

A slightly cleaner form is:

$$\boxed{
f(n)=15\cdot16^{n-1}
-43\cdot15^{n-1}
+41\cdot14^{n-1}
-13^n
}$$

---

# Step 4: Verify small cases

For $n=3$:

The problem statement lists:

$$10A,\ 1A0,\ A10,\ A01$$

Let's compute:

$$f(3)
=
15\cdot16^2
-43\cdot15^2
+41\cdot14^2
-13^3$$

$$=3840-9675+8036-2197
=4$$

Correct.

---

# Step 5: Python implementation

```
def count_n_digit(n):
    return (
        15 * 16**(n - 1)
        - 15**n
        - 2 * 14 * 15**(n - 1)
        + 2 * 14**n
        + 13 * 14**(n - 1)
        - 13**n
    )

total = sum(count_n_digit(n) for n in range(1, 17))

print(total)
print(hex(total)[2:].upper())
```

- $n<3$ gives $0$, which is correct.

**Answer: 3D58725572C62302**
