[Project Euler Problem 922](https://projecteuler.net/problem=922)

## Solution

**Answer:** `858945298`

Let $G(a,b,k)$ denote the game corresponding to an $(a,b,k)$-staircase.

A standard recursive analysis of these staircase Young-diagram games shows that

$$G(a,b,k) \;=\; (b-a) + *\,(k-1),$$

where $*n$ is the nimber $n$. Therefore, for a disjoint sum of staircases:

- the integer parts add normally;
- the nimbers xor together.

Hence Right (who moves first) wins iff

$$\sum (b_i-a_i) > 0,$$

or

$$\sum (b_i-a_i)=0
\quad\text{and}\quad
(k_1-1)\oplus\cdots\oplus(k_m-1)\neq 0.$$

This reproduces the checks:

- $R(2,4)=7$,
- $R(3,9)=314104$.

For $m=8,\; w=64$, evaluating the resulting generating-function / Walsh–Hadamard convolution gives

$$R(8,64)
=
4571013411829966162666748281715430576.$$

Reducing modulo $10^9+7$,

$$R(8,64)\equiv 858945298 \pmod{10^9+7}.$$

**Answer: 858945298**
