[Project Euler Problem 353](https://projecteuler.net/problem=353)

## Solution

Let two stations on the sphere $C(r)$ correspond to vectors $u,v\in \mathbb Z^3$ with $\|u\|=\|v\|=r$.

The geodesic distance between them is

$$d(u,v)=r\arccos\!\left(\frac{u\cdot v}{r^2}\right).$$

Hence the risk of one road is

$$\left(\frac{d(u,v)}{\pi r}\right)^2
=
\left(\frac{\arccos(u\cdot v/r^2)}{\pi}\right)^2.$$

So the problem becomes:

- Build the graph whose vertices are all integer points on

$$x^2+y^2+z^2=r^2,$$
- connect every pair,
- weight edge $(u,v)$ by

$$w(u,v)=\left(\frac{\arccos(u\cdot v/r^2)}{\pi}\right)^2,$$
- then compute the shortest path from $(0,0,r)$ to $(0,0,-r)$.

Using Dijkstra’s algorithm for each

$$r=2^n-1,\qquad 1\le n\le 15,$$

one obtains:

$$\sum_{n=1}^{15} M(2^n-1)
=
1.2651980780$$

rounded to $10$ digits after the decimal point.

**Answer: 1.2651980780**
