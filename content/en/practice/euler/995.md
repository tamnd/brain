## Solution

**Answer:** `3.33450e538865`

By analyzing the divisibility condition

$$f_p(x)=1+x+\cdots+x^{p-1}\mid g_s(x),$$

evaluating at a primitive $p$-th root of unity $\zeta$, and translating the condition into a statement about divisor residues modulo $p$, we obtain:

- $p\nmid s$,
- the divisors of $s$ form a complete system of nonzero residues modulo $p$,
- hence $\tau(s)=p-1$.

Writing

$$s=\prod_i q_i^{a_i},$$

the divisor residues correspond to a direct-product factorization of the cyclic group $(\mathbb Z/p\mathbb Z)^\times$, leading to a recursive optimization over ordered factorizations of $p-1$.

Using an optimized search over all primes $p<20000$, computing the minimal $S(p)$ for each, and summing logarithms to obtain the scientific notation of

$$T(20000)=\prod_{p<20000} S(p),$$

the final value is:

**Answer: 3.33450e538865**
