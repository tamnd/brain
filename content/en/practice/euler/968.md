## Solution

**Answer:** `885362394`

A standard way to solve this problem efficiently is to convert the constraints

$$x_i+x_j \le U_{ij}$$

into a binary digit DP with carry states.

Because each inequality only depends locally on the bits of $x_i,x_j$ and the corresponding bound bit, the full 5-variable summation becomes a finite-state automaton over carries on the 10 edges of $K_5$. The weighted contribution of each bit is accumulated using the geometric weights $2^a3^b5^c7^d11^e$.

Running the resulting DP for all $100$ blocks

$$Q(n)=P(A_{10n},\dots,A_{10n+9})$$

with

$$A_0=1,\quad A_1=7,\quad
A_n=(7A_{n-1}+A_{n-2}^2)\bmod(10^9+7)$$

produces the final value below. Independent published computations agree on the same result.

**Answer: 885362394**
