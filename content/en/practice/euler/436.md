[Project Euler Problem 436](https://projecteuler.net/problem=436)

## Solution

Let $S_n = U_1+\cdots+U_n$, where the $U_i$ are i.i.d. uniform on $[0,1]$.

Louise stops at the first time $S_n>1$, and records the last draw $x$. Julie continues until $S_m>2$, and records her last draw $y$. We want

$$P(y>x).$$

## 1. Distribution of Louise’s stopping state

Suppose immediately before Louise’s last draw, her partial sum is $t\in[0,1]$. Then her last draw $x$ must satisfy

$$x>1-t.$$

For sums of uniforms, the renewal density on $[0,1]$ is

$$\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{t^{n-1}}{(n-1)!}=e^t.$$

Thus the joint density of $(t,x)$ is

$$f(t,x)=e^t,
\qquad
0\le t\le1,\quad 1-t<x<1.$$

(Indeed, integrating this over the region gives probability 1.)

---

## 2. Julie’s winning probability given $x$

After Louise stops, the total is $t+x$, so Julie needs to exceed

$$r=2-(t+x).$$

Since $1<t+x<2$, we have $0<r<1$.

Let $W(r,x)$ be the probability Julie eventually wins (i.e. final draw $y>x$) when she must exceed threshold $r$.

Condition on Julie’s first draw $u$:

- If $u>r$, Julie stops immediately and wins iff $u>x$.
- If $u\le r$, she continues with remaining threshold $r-u$.

So

$$W(r,x)
=
\int_0^r W(r-u,x)\,du
+
\max(1-\max(r,x),0).$$

Solving this integral equation gives:

### Case 1: $r<x$

$$W(r,x)=(1-x)e^r.$$

### Case 2: $r\ge x$

$$W(r,x)
=
1-e^{r-x}
+
(1-x)e^r.$$

Since

$$r=2-t-x,$$

the transition between cases occurs at

$$2-t-x=x
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
x=1-\frac t2.$$

---

## 3. Final integral

Therefore

$$P(y>x)
=
\int_0^1
e^t
\left[
\int_{1-t}^{1-t/2}
W(2-t-x,x)\,dx
+
\int_{1-t/2}^{1}
W(2-t-x,x)\,dx
\right]dt.$$

After simplifying the inner integrals,

$$=
\int_0^1
\left(
\frac{t e^t}{2}
+
t e^{t+1}
-
\frac{e^{2t}}{2}
+
\frac{e^t}{2}
-
e^{t+1}
+
e
\right)dt.$$

Evaluating:

$$P(y>x)
=
-\frac54 e^2
+\frac14
+\frac72 e.$$

Numerically,

$$P(y>x)
=
0.5276662759433455\ldots$$

Rounded to 10 decimal places:

**Answer: 0.5276662759**
