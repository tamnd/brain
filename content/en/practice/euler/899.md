[Project Euler Problem 899](https://projecteuler.net/problem=899)

## Solution

**Answer:** `10784223938983273`

Let a position be $(x,y)$, and assume $x \le y$.

A move removes $u$ stones from the first pile and $v$ stones from the second pile such that

$$u+v=x,$$

with the restriction that neither pile becomes empty.

So the new position is

$$(x-u,\ y-v),$$

and the total number of stones afterward is

$$(x-u)+(y-v)=y.$$

A careful induction on $y$ shows that the losing positions are exactly those satisfying

$$\min(a,b)\le 2^{\nu_2(\max(a,b)+1)}-1,$$

where $\nu_2(n)$ is the exponent of the largest power of $2$ dividing $n$.

For a fixed maximum pile size $M=\max(a,b)$, define

$$r(M)=2^{\nu_2(M+1)}-1.$$

Then the losing ordered pairs with maximum $M$ are exactly

$$(m,M),\ (M,m)\qquad (1\le m\le r(M)),$$

except that when $M=r(M)$ (i.e. $M=2^k-1$), the diagonal pair $(M,M)$ is counted only once.

Hence the contribution for each $M$ is

$$2r(M)-[M=r(M)].$$

Therefore

$$L(n)=\sum_{M=1}^n \left(2(2^{\nu_2(M+1)}-1)-[M=2^k-1]\right).$$

This simplifies to

$$L(n)=2\sum_{a=1}^{n+1}2^{\nu_2(a)}-2n-2-\lfloor \log_2(n+1)\rfloor.$$

Now compute this for

$$n=7^{17}=232630513987207.$$

Evaluating the sum gives

$$L(7^{17})=10784223938983273.$$

This matches the given checks:

$$L(7)=21,\qquad L(49)=221.$$

**Answer: 10784223938983273**
