[Project Euler Problem 239](https://projecteuler.net/problem=239)

## Solution

There are $25$ prime numbers between $1$ and $100$.

We want the probability that **exactly 22 prime-numbered discs are not in their natural positions**.

Equivalently:

- exactly $3$ prime discs are fixed,
- the other $22$ prime discs are displaced,
- non-prime discs may be anywhere.

Let us derive the count carefully.

---

# Step 1: Choose the 3 fixed prime discs

There are

$$\binom{25}{3}$$

ways to choose which prime discs remain in their correct positions.

After fixing these $3$ primes, we are left with:

- $97$ movable discs,
- among them, $22$ primes that are forbidden from occupying their own positions.

The non-prime discs have no restrictions.

---

# Step 2: Count permutations where the remaining 22 primes are displaced

We now count permutations of the remaining $97$ discs such that none of the $22$ distinguished primes are fixed.

This is a standard inclusion–exclusion problem.

Total permutations of the $97$ remaining discs:

$$97!$$

If we force a particular set of $k$ of the $22$ primes to be fixed, then the remaining objects can be permuted in

$$(97-k)!$$

ways.

There are

$$\binom{22}{k}$$

ways to choose those $k$ primes.

Thus, by inclusion–exclusion, the number of valid permutations is

$$\sum_{k=0}^{22}
(-1)^k
\binom{22}{k}
(97-k)!$$

Therefore the total number of favorable permutations is

$$\binom{25}{3}
\sum_{k=0}^{22}
(-1)^k
\binom{22}{k}
(97-k)!$$

Since the total number of permutations of all $100$ discs is

$$100!$$

the desired probability is

$$P=
\frac{
\binom{25}{3}
\displaystyle\sum_{k=0}^{22}
(-1)^k
\binom{22}{k}
(97-k)!
}{
100!
}$$

# Python implementation

```
from math import comb, factorial
from decimal import Decimal, getcontext

# high precision arithmetic
getcontext().prec = 50

# Inclusion-exclusion sum
s = 0
for k in range(23):
    s += ((-1) ** k) * comb(22, k) * factorial(97 - k)

# Number of favorable permutations
numerator = comb(25, 3) * s

# Total permutations
denominator = factorial(100)

# Probability
p = Decimal(numerator) / Decimal(denominator)

print(p)
print(round(p, 12))
```
