[Project Euler Problem 239](https://projecteuler.net/problem=239)

## Solution

There are $25$ prime numbers between $1$ and $100$.

We want **exactly 22 prime-numbered discs** to be away from their natural positions.

Equivalently:

- exactly $3$ prime discs are fixed in their correct positions,
- the other $22$ prime discs are **not** fixed,
- non-prime discs may be anywhere.

Let the set of prime-labelled positions/discs be $P$, with $|P|=25$.

---

## Step 1: Choose the 3 fixed prime discs

We first choose which $3$ prime discs remain fixed:

$$\binom{25}{3}$$

After fixing them, there are $97$ remaining discs to permute.

Among the remaining $22$ prime discs, none may be fixed.

---

## Step 2: Inclusion–Exclusion

Let $A_i$ denote the event that the $i$-th remaining prime disc is fixed.

We want permutations of the remaining $97$ discs with none of the $22$ events occurring.

By inclusion–exclusion:

$$N
=
\sum_{k=0}^{22}
(-1)^k
\binom{22}{k}
(97-k)!$$

because fixing any chosen set of $k$ forbidden prime discs leaves $(97-k)!$ permutations.

Thus the total number of favourable permutations is

$$\binom{25}{3}
\sum_{k=0}^{22}
(-1)^k
\binom{22}{k}
(97-k)!$$

The total number of permutations is

$$100!$$

Hence

$$\boxed{
P
=
\frac{\binom{25}{3}}{100!}
\sum_{k=0}^{22}
(-1)^k
\binom{22}{k}
(97-k)!
}$$

---

## Python implementation

```
from math import comb, factorial
from decimal import Decimal, getcontext

# use high precision
getcontext().prec = 50

total = Decimal(0)

# Inclusion-Exclusion
for k in range(23):
    term = Decimal(((-1) ** k) * comb(22, k) * factorial(97 - k))
    total += term

# choose which 3 prime discs are fixed
total *= Decimal(comb(25, 3))

# divide by all permutations
probability = total / Decimal(factorial(100))

# print rounded to 12 decimal places
print(f"{probability:.12f}")
```

## Code walkthrough

- `comb(25, 3)` chooses the 3 prime discs that stay fixed.
- The loop performs inclusion–exclusion over the remaining 22 prime discs.
- `factorial(97-k)` counts permutations after fixing $k$ additional forbidden primes.
- Alternating signs implement inclusion–exclusion.
- Finally divide by `100!` to convert the count into a probability.

The computed value is

$$0.0018878548410303406\ldots$$

Rounded to 12 digits after the decimal point:

$$0.001887854841$$

This is sensible:

- the probability is small,
- because requiring exactly 22 of the 25 primes to move is a strong restriction.
