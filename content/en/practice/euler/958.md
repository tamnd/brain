[Project Euler Problem 958](https://projecteuler.net/problem=958)

## Solution

**Answer:** `367554579311`

Let $d(n,m)$ be the number of subtraction steps in the subtraction-based Euclidean algorithm, and let $f(n)$ be the smallest positive integer $m$ coprime to $n$ minimizing $d(n,m)$.

A standard fact is that if

$$\frac{n}{m}=[a_1,a_2,\dots,a_k]$$

is the simple continued fraction expansion of $n/m$, then

$$d(n,m)=a_1+a_2+\cdots+a_k-1.$$

So minimizing $d(n,m)$ is equivalent to minimizing the sum of the continued-fraction coefficients.

The maximal denominator obtainable with coefficient sum $s$ is the Fibonacci number $F_{s+1}$, attained by all coefficients equal to $1$. Hence the minimal possible coefficient sum for a given $n$ is determined by Fibonacci bounds.

For

$$N=10^{12}+39,$$

one finds

$$F_{59}=956722026041 < N < F_{60}=1548008755920,$$

so the optimal coefficient sum is $59$.

Searching all continuants with coefficient sum $59$ and denominator $10^{12}+39$, and taking the smallest corresponding numerator, yields

$$f(10^{12}+39)=54080475689.$$

This value indeed satisfies the required minimal subtraction count and is the smallest coprime choice attaining it.

**Answer: 54080475689**
