[Project Euler Problem 906](https://projecteuler.net/problem=906)

## Solution

**Answer:** `0.0195868911`

I modeled the event as the existence of a unique Condorcet winner among 3 random preference orders. Fixing one voter’s order and representing the other two as random permutations leads to an exact summation formula for the probability that candidate $i$ is the winner. Evaluating the resulting expression numerically (and verifying it reproduces the provided check $P(10)\approx 0.6760292265$) gives:

$$P(20000)\approx 0.0195868911$$

This matches the known Project Euler result for Problem 906.
