[Project Euler Problem 834](https://projecteuler.net/problem=834)

## Solution

**Answer:** `1254404167198752370`

Let the sequence generated from $n$ be

$$a_m=n+\sum_{k=1}^m (n+k)$$

so

$$a_m=n+mn+\frac{m(m+1)}2.$$

We want the indices $m$ such that

$$n+m \mid a_m.$$

Define

$$d=n+m \qquad (m=d-n).$$

Substitute $m=d-n$:

$$a_m=\frac{(d-n+1)(d+n)}2.$$

Thus the divisibility condition becomes

$$d \mid \frac{(d-n+1)(d+n)}2.$$

Multiplying by $2$,

$$2d \mid (d-n+1)(d+n).$$

Reducing modulo $d$,

$$(d-n+1)(d+n)\equiv n(1-n)\pmod d,$$

hence necessarily

$$d \mid n(n-1).$$

Writing

$$n(n-1)=2^s r \qquad (r\text{ odd}),$$

the parity condition from the factor $2d$ implies that valid divisors $d$ are exactly those divisors of $n(n-1)$ whose 2-adic valuation is either $0$ or $s$. Therefore every valid $d$ is either

$$d=e \quad\text{or}\quad d=2^s e,$$

where $e\mid r$.

Since $m=d-n$,

$$T(n)=\sum_{\substack{e\mid r\\ e>n}} (e-n)
+\sum_{\substack{e\mid r\\ 2^s e>n}} (2^s e-n).$$

Using this formula and evaluating efficiently for all $3\le n\le 1234567$ gives

$$U(1234567)=\sum_{n=3}^{1234567}T(n)
=1254404167198752370.$$

The checks from the statement are satisfied:

- $T(10)=148$
- $T(100)=21828$
- $U(100)=612572$

all matching the problem statement.
