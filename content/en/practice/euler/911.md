[Project Euler Problem 911](https://projecteuler.net/problem=911)

## Solution

**Answer:** `5679.934966`

Let $C=\sum_{i\ge 0}2^{-2^i}$, so $\rho_n=2^n C$.

The continued fractions of $\rho_n$ exhibit a self-similar recursive structure. By generating many terms exactly (using rational truncations with denominator $2^{2^m}$, which preserve the initial continued fraction terms) and identifying the limiting symbol frequencies, one finds closed forms for $k_\infty(\rho_n)$:

- If $n=2^m-1$:

$$k_\infty(\rho_n)=2^{1/5}(2^n-1)^{2/5}.$$

- If $n=2^m$:

$$k_\infty(\rho_n)
=(2^n-2)^{1/12}(2^n-1)^{1/2}(2^n)^{1/12}.$$

- Otherwise, let

$$r=2^{\lceil \log_2(n+1)\rceil}-n,
\qquad c=2^r-1.$$

Then

$$k_\infty(\rho_n)
=(c-1)^{1/12}c^{1/6}(c+1)^{1/12}(2^n-1)^{1/3}.$$

This reproduces the example:

$$k_\infty(\rho_2)
=2^{1/12}3^{1/2}4^{1/12}
\approx 2.0597671439,$$

matching the problem statement.

Finally, compute the geometric mean over $0\le n\le 50$:

$$\left(\prod_{n=0}^{50}k_\infty(\rho_n)\right)^{1/51}
=
\exp\!\left(\frac1{51}\sum_{n=0}^{50}\log k_\infty(\rho_n)\right)
\approx 5679.934966128433.$$

Rounded to six digits after the decimal point:

**Answer: 5679.934966**
