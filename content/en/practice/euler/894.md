[Project Euler Problem 894](https://projecteuler.net/problem=894)

## Solution

**Answer:** `0.7718678168`

I modeled the circle system as a logarithmic spiral of similar circles.

Let:

- $s$ be the constant scaling factor ($0<s<1$),
- $\theta$ the constant rotation angle,
- $d$ the distance from the origin to the center of $C_0$.

Because every circle is obtained from the previous one by the same scaling and rotation about the origin,

$$r_k = s^k,\qquad
z_k = d\, s^k e^{ik\theta}.$$

The tangency condition between $C_0$ and $C_k$ gives

$$|z_0-z_k| = 1+s^k.$$

Using the given tangencies with $C_1,C_7,C_8$, we solve:

$$d\,|1-se^{i\theta}|=1+s,$$

$$d\,|1-s^7e^{i7\theta}|=1+s^7,$$

$$d\,|1-s^8e^{i8\theta}|=1+s^8.$$

Numerically this yields:

$$s \approx 0.906331406148595,
\qquad
\theta \approx 0.826729539414059.$$

The tangency graph repeats self-similarly: the only circular triangles are of two similarity types:

1. $(C_k,C_{k+1},C_{k+8})$,
2. $(C_k,C_{k+7},C_{k+8})$,

each scaled by $s^{2k}$ in area.

For three mutually tangent circles of radii $r_1,r_2,r_3$, the curvilinear triangle area is

$$A
=
[\triangle]
-\frac12
\left(
r_1^2\alpha_1+r_2^2\alpha_2+r_3^2\alpha_3
\right),$$

where $[\triangle]$ is the area of the triangle of centers and $\alpha_i$ are its angles.

Summing the two prototype regions as a geometric series gives

$$A_{\text{total}}
=
\frac{A(1,s,s^8)+A(1,s^7,s^8)}{1-s^2}$$

which evaluates to

$$0.7718678168436464\ldots$$

Rounded to 10 decimal places:

**Answer: 0.7718678168**
