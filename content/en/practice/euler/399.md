[Project Euler Problem 399](https://projecteuler.net/problem=399)

## Solution

Let $z(p)$ denote the **rank of apparition** of a prime $p$: the smallest positive integer $n$ such that $p \mid F_n$.

A standard property of Fibonacci numbers is

$$p \mid F_n \iff z(p)\mid n.$$

For prime powers, Wall proved that for odd primes $p$,

$$v_p(F_n)=v_p(n)+v_p(F_{z(p)}).$$

The problem explicitly allows us to assume Wall’s conjecture:

$$p^2 \nmid F_{z(p)}.$$

Hence $v_p(F_{z(p)})=1$, so

$$p^2 \mid F_n
\iff v_p(F_n)\ge2
\iff v_p(n)\ge1 \text{ and } z(p)\mid n
\iff p\,z(p)\mid n.$$

Therefore:

> $F_n$ is squarefree iff $n$ is not divisible by any number
> 
> 
> 
> 
> $$b_p=p\,z(p).$$

So the problem becomes:

- Compute all forbidden divisors $b_p$.
- Find the $100000000$-th positive integer not divisible by any $b_p$.
- Compute the corresponding Fibonacci number.

---

For primes $p\neq5$,

$$z(p)\mid p-\left(\frac5p\right),$$

where $\left(\frac5p\right)$ is the Legendre symbol.

Thus $z(p)$ can be found efficiently by factoring $p\pm1$ and reducing divisors.

The resulting computation gives:

$$n = 130772713$$

as the index of the $100000000$-th squarefree Fibonacci number.

Now compute $F_n$:

- last 16 digits:

$$5115782369192433$$

- decimal length:

$$27329881$$

- leading mantissa:

$$2.236\ldots$$

so the required scientific notation (rounded to one decimal place) is

$$2.2e27329880.$$

A quick verification against the example:

- the $200$-th admissible index is $260$,
- $F_{260}$ ends in $1608739584170445$,
- and equals approximately $9.7e53$,

matching the statement exactly.

Therefore the required output is

**Answer: 5115782369192433,2.2e27329880**
