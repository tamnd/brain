[Project Euler Problem 931](https://projecteuler.net/problem=931)

## Solution

**Answer:** `128856311`

Let

$$n=\prod_{i=1}^k p_i^{e_i}.$$

An edge in the divisor graph corresponds to a divisor $b\mid n$ and a prime $p$ such that $bp\mid n$.

The edge weight is

$$\phi(bp)-\phi(b).$$

Write $b=p^a m$ with $p\nmid m$.

Then:

- If $a=0$,

$$\phi(bp)-\phi(b)=(p-1)\phi(m)-\phi(m)=(p-2)\phi(m).$$

- If $a\ge1$,

$$\phi(bp)-\phi(b)
= p^{a}(p-1)\phi(m)-p^{a-1}(p-1)\phi(m)
= p^{a-1}(p-1)^2\phi(m).$$

Summing over all admissible $b$ for a fixed prime power $p^e\|n$, one gets

$$\sum_{a=0}^{e-1}\bigl(\phi(p^{a+1})-\phi(p^a)\bigr)
= \phi(p^e)-1.$$

Since

$$\sum_{d\mid m}\phi(d)=m,$$

the contribution from $p^e\|n$ is

$$\frac{n}{p^e}\bigl(\phi(p^e)-1\bigr).$$

Hence

$$t(n)=\sum_{p^e\parallel n}\frac{n}{p^e}\bigl(\phi(p^e)-1\bigr).$$

From this one derives

$$T(N)=\sum_{p\le N}(p-2)\,S\!\left(\left\lfloor\frac Np\right\rfloor\right)
+\sum_{e\ge2}\sum_{p^e\le N}(p-1)\,
S\!\left(\left\lfloor\frac N{p^e}\right\rfloor\right),$$

where

$$S(x)=\frac{x(x+1)}2.$$

Using a Min_25-style prime summatory sieve to evaluate the prime sums efficiently for $N=10^{12}$, the computation reproduces:

- $T(10)=26$
- $T(10^2)=5282$

and finally gives

$$T(10^{12}) \equiv 128856311 \pmod{715827883}.$$
