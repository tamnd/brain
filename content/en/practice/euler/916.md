[Project Euler Problem 916](https://projecteuler.net/problem=916)

## Solution

**Answer:** `877789135`

By the Robinson–Schensted correspondence, the length of the longest increasing subsequence of a permutation equals the first row length of its Young diagram shape, and the length of the longest decreasing subsequence equals the first column length.

We are counting permutations of $\{1,\dots,2n\}$ such that:

- longest increasing subsequence has length at most $n+1$,
- longest decreasing subsequence has length at most $2$.

Hence the corresponding Young diagrams have:

- at most $2$ rows,
- first row length at most $n+1$,
- total size $2n$.

So the only possible shapes are:

$$(n,n)
\quad\text{and}\quad
(n+1,n-1).$$

If $f^\lambda$ denotes the number of standard Young tableaux of shape $\lambda$, then the number of permutations of shape $\lambda$ is $(f^\lambda)^2$.

For a two-row shape $(a,b)$,

$$f^{(a,b)}
=
\frac{a-b+1}{a+1}\binom{a+b}{b}.$$

Thus:

### Shape $(n,n)$

$$f^{(n,n)}
=
\frac{1}{n+1}\binom{2n}{n}
=
C_n$$

(the Catalan number).

### Shape $(n+1,n-1)$

$$f^{(n+1,n-1)}
=
\frac{3}{n+2}\binom{2n}{n-1}.$$

Therefore

$$P(n)
=
\left(\frac{1}{n+1}\binom{2n}{n}\right)^2
+
\left(\frac{3}{n+2}\binom{2n}{n-1}\right)^2.$$

Checking the example $n=2$:

- $f^{(2,2)}=2$,
- $f^{(3,1)}=3$,

so

$$P(2)=2^2+3^2=13,$$

matching the statement.

For $n=10$, this gives

$$P(10)\equiv 45265702 \pmod{10^9+7},$$

also matching the statement.

Now evaluate the formula modulo

$$M=10^9+7$$

for $n=10^8$. Computing the binomial coefficient modulo $M$ gives:

$$P(10^8)\equiv 877789135 \pmod{10^9+7}.$$

**Answer: 877789135**
