[Project Euler Problem 545](https://projecteuler.net/problem=545)

## Solution

Using Faulhaber’s formula and Bernoulli numbers,

$$\sum_{i=1}^n i^k
=
\frac1{k+1}\sum_{j=0}^k (-1)^j \binom{k+1}{j} B_j n^{k+1-j}.$$

The coefficient of $n$ is exactly $B_k$. Hence

$$D(k)=\text{denominator}(B_k).$$

By the von Staudt–Clausen theorem,

$$\operatorname{den}(B_k)=\prod_{p-1\mid k} p
\quad (k\ge2,\ k \text{ even}),$$

where the product runs over all primes $p$ such that $p-1\mid k$.

We need

$$D(k)=20010.$$

Factor:

$$20010=2\cdot3\cdot5\cdot23\cdot29.$$

Therefore the only primes $p$ with $p-1\mid k$ must be

$$p\in\{2,3,5,23,29\},$$

whose corresponding values of $p-1$ are

$$1,2,4,22,28.$$

Thus:

- $k$ must be divisible by

$$\operatorname{lcm}(1,2,4,22,28)=308,$$

- and for every other prime $q$,

$$q-1\nmid k.$$

So every valid $k$ has the form

$$k=308n,$$

where no divisor of $308n$ is one less than a forbidden prime.

A complete search using deterministic primality testing and divisor generation gives:

- $F(1)=308$,
- $F(10)=96404$ (matching the problem statement),
- and finally

$$F(10^5)=921107572.$$

**Answer: 921107572**
