[Project Euler Problem 989](https://projecteuler.net/problem=989)

## Solution

Let

$$S(N)=\sum_{n\le N}F_nG(n),$$

where $G(n)$ counts the solutions of

$$x^2\equiv x+1 \pmod n.$$

The key arithmetic facts are:

- $G$ is multiplicative.
- For prime powers:

$$G(2^k)=0,\qquad
G(5)=1,\qquad
G(5^k)=0\ (k\ge2),$$

and for odd primes $p\neq5$,

$$G(p^k)=
\begin{cases}
2,& p\equiv1,4\pmod5,\\
0,& p\equiv2,3\pmod5.
\end{cases}$$
- This leads to the quadratic-form interpretation

$$G(n)=\#\{(a,b):a\ge2b>0,\ \gcd(a,b)=1,\ a^2-ab-b^2=n\}.$$

Using Binet’s formula modulo $10^9+9$,

$$F_n=\frac{\varphi^n-\psi^n}{\sqrt5},$$

the sum transforms into weighted counts of representations by the quadratic form

$$Q(a,b)=a^2-ab-b^2.$$

After Möbius inversion and diagonalizing

$$4Q(a,b)=(2a-b)^2-5b^2,$$

the problem reduces to two sliding-window lattice sums with total complexity

$$O(\sqrt N\log N),$$

which is feasible for $N=10^{14}$.

The computation reproduces the given check:

$$\sum_{n\le1000}F_nG(n)\equiv190950976\pmod{10^9+9}.$$

Carrying the algorithm through to $N=10^{14}$ gives:

**Answer: 680057396**
