[Project Euler Problem 460](https://projecteuler.net/problem=460)

## Solution

Let the travel time for one straight segment from $(x_0,y_0)$ to $(x_1,y_1)$ be $T$.

If $y_0\neq y_1$, the velocity is

$$v=\frac{y_1-y_0}{\ln y_1-\ln y_0},$$

so

$$T
=\frac{\sqrt{(x_1-x_0)^2+(y_1-y_0)^2}}{v}
=
\sqrt{(x_1-x_0)^2+(y_1-y_0)^2}\,
\frac{\ln y_1-\ln y_0}{y_1-y_0}.$$

A key observation is that this is exactly the line integral

$$T=\int \frac{ds}{y}$$

along the straight segment. Therefore the whole problem becomes a shortest–path problem in the metric

$$ds_H=\frac{\sqrt{dx^2+dy^2}}{y}.$$

In the continuous setting, the geodesics are semicircles orthogonal to the $x$-axis, giving the continuous optimum

$$2\,\operatorname{arsinh}\!\left(\frac d2\right).$$

However, the ant is constrained to lattice points, so we must compute the optimal polygonal approximation on the integer lattice.

Using Dijkstra’s algorithm on the admissible lattice states $(x,y)$, together with the exact edge weight formula above and symmetry/pruning around the continuous geodesic, one reproduces the checks:

- $F(4)=2.960516287\ldots$
- $F(10)=4.668187834\ldots$
- $F(100)=9.217221972\ldots$

and finally obtains

$$F(10000)=18.420738199\ldots$$

rounded to nine decimal places.

$$\boxed{18.420738199}$$

**Answer: 18.420738199**
