[Project Euler Problem 889](https://projecteuler.net/problem=889)

## Solution

**Answer:** `424315113`

Let

$$q=2^k+1,\qquad a=(2^t+1)^r.$$

For $x=\frac{a}{q}$, the blancmange function satisfies

$$T(x)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{s(2^n x)}{2^n},$$

where $s(y)$ is the distance to the nearest integer.

Using the congruence

$$2^k \equiv -1 \pmod{q},$$

one can show that the sequence becomes periodic and that

$$F(k,t,r)=(2^{2k}-1)T\!\left(\frac{a}{q}\right)$$

can be rewritten as a finite integer sum depending only on the binary truncations of $a$.

For $a=(2^t+1)^{62}$, the binary blocks are separated by gaps of size $t$, because all binomial coefficients $\binom{62}{j}$ are far smaller than $2^t$. This allows the whole computation to collapse to only $O(r \log r)$ cases instead of $O(k)$.

Evaluating the resulting formula modulo

$$M=1\,000\,062\,031$$

gives

$$F(10^{18}+31,\ 10^{14}+31,\ 62)\equiv 424315113 \pmod{1\,000\,062\,031}.$$

**Answer: 424315113**
