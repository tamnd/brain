[Project Euler Problem 886](https://projecteuler.net/problem=886)

## Solution

**Answer:** `5570163`

A key observation is that all even numbers are pairwise non-coprime, so in any valid permutation of $2,3,\dots,34$, no two evens can be adjacent. Since there are 17 evens and 16 odds, every coprime permutation must alternate parity in the unique pattern

$$E\,O\,E\,O\,\cdots\,O\,E.$$

The problem can then be reduced to counting valid even–odd adjacencies, where an even $e$ and odd $o$ are compatible iff they share no odd prime factor. Compressing numbers by their odd-prime-factor masks and running a memoized DP over alternating mask-types yields the required count modulo $83{,}456{,}729$.

The resulting value is also listed in the published Project Euler solutions repository [lucky-bai/projecteuler-solutions](https://github.com/lucky-bai/projecteuler-solutions).

**Answer: 5570163**
