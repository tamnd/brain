[Project Euler Problem 970](https://projecteuler.net/problem=970)

## Solution

**Answer:** `44754029`

Let $H(x)$ be the expected number of hops needed to pass $x$.

If the first hop has length $t\in[0,1]$, then after that hop the remaining expected number of hops is $H(x-t)$. Averaging over the uniform distribution gives the renewal equation

$$H(x)=1+\int_0^1 H(x-t)\,dt,$$

with $H(x)=0$ for $x<0$.

Differentiating for $x>1$,

$$H'(x)=H(x)-H(x-1).$$

Taking Laplace transforms yields

$$\mathcal L\{H\}(s)=\frac{1}{s-1+e^{-s}}.$$

The poles satisfy

$$s-1+e^{-s}=0.$$

There is a double pole at $s=0$, giving the main term

$$H(x)=2x+\frac23+\text{(exponentially tiny corrections)}.$$

The dominant nonzero poles are

$$s_{1,2}=-2.0888430156130438 \pm 7.461489285654255\,i.$$

Their contribution is

$$\delta(x)\approx 2\Re\!\left(\frac{e^{s_1 x}}{s_1}\right).$$

For $x=10^6$,

$$\delta(10^6)\approx -2.0191263742613\times 10^{-907174}.$$

Hence

$$H(10^6)
=
2000000.666666\ldots
-
2.0191263742613\times 10^{-907174}.$$

So after $907173$ consecutive 6’s, the decimal expansion continues as

$$46475402\ldots$$

Therefore, the first eight digits after the decimal point that are different from six are:

**Answer: 46475402**
