[Project Euler Problem 252](https://projecteuler.net/problem=252)

## Solution

Let the first $500$ points be generated by the recurrence

$$S_{n+1}=S_n^2 \bmod 50515093,\qquad
T_n=(S_n \bmod 2000)-1000$$

and the points be

$$P_k=(T_{2k-1},T_{2k}).$$

## Key insight

A convex hole can be triangulated from any one of its vertices $r$ (choose the leftmost vertex to avoid duplicates). If the polygon is

$$(r,v_1,v_2,\dots,v_m),$$

then its area is the sum of triangle areas

$$[r,v_1,v_2] + [r,v_2,v_3] + \cdots + [r,v_{m-1},v_m].$$

The crucial observation is:

> The polygon is a valid convex hole iff every added triangle $(r,v_i,v_{i+1})$ is **empty** (contains no point strictly inside) and convexity is maintained.

We precompute, for every directed edge $a\to b$, the set of points strictly to the left of that edge as a bitset.

Then triangle $(a,b,c)$ is empty iff

$$L(a,b)\cap L(b,c)\cap L(c,a)=\varnothing,$$

because any point inside the triangle must lie to the left of all three directed edges.

This enables a dynamic program over angular order around a fixed root $r$:

$$dp[b][c]$$

= maximum doubled area of a convex chain ending with vertices $b,c$.

Transition:

$$dp[b][c]
=
\max\bigl(
dp[a][b] + 2\cdot \text{Area}(r,b,c)
\bigr)$$

for all $a$ such that:

1. $a,b,c$ make a left turn (convexity),
2. triangle $(r,b,c)$ is empty.

I verified the implementation on the problem’s $20$-point example, reproducing the stated maximum area:

$$1049694.5$$

Running the exact same algorithm on the first $500$ points gives:

**Answer: 104924.0**
