[Project Euler Problem 959](https://projecteuler.net/problem=959)

## Solution

**Answer:** `0.857162085`

Let $S_n$ be the frog’s position after $n$ steps, with increments

$$X_i=\begin{cases}
-b & \text{with probability } \tfrac12,\\
+a & \text{with probability } \tfrac12.
\end{cases}$$

(Equivalent to the problem statement up to sign convention.)

We are asked for

$$f(a,b)=\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{c_n}{n},$$

where $c_n$ is the expected number of distinct sites visited in the first $n$ steps.

For a transient random walk on $\mathbb Z$, a standard result gives:

$$f(a,b)=\frac1{G(0)},$$

where

$$G(0)=\sum_{n\ge0}\Pr(S_n=0)$$

is the Green function at the origin.

---

A return to the origin occurs only when the total displacement is zero.

If the walk uses $ak$ jumps of size $+b$ and $bk$ jumps of size $-a$, then

$$bk\cdot(+a) + ak\cdot(-b)=0,$$

so the walk returns after exactly

$$(a+b)k$$

steps.

Thus

$$\Pr(S_{(a+b)k}=0)
=
\frac{\binom{(a+b)k}{ak}}{2^{(a+b)k}}.$$

Therefore

$$G(0)=
\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}
\frac{\binom{(a+b)k}{ak}}{2^{(a+b)k}},$$

and hence

$$f(a,b)=
\left(
\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}
\frac{\binom{(a+b)k}{ak}}{2^{(a+b)k}}
\right)^{-1}.$$

---

For the check case $(a,b)=(1,2)$:

$$G(0)=\sum_{k\ge0}\frac{\binom{3k}{k}}{8^k}
=2.341640786\ldots$$

so

$$f(1,2)=\frac1{2.341640786\ldots}
=0.427050983\ldots$$

which matches the statement.

---

Now compute for $(a,b)=(89,97)$:

$$f(89,97)=
\left(
\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}
\frac{\binom{186k}{89k}}{2^{186k}}
\right)^{-1}.$$

Evaluating the series numerically to high precision gives

$$f(89,97)=0.8571620850938173327\ldots$$

Rounded to nine digits after the decimal point:

**Answer: 0.857162085**
