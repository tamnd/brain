[Project Euler Problem 8](https://projecteuler.net/problem=8)

## Solution

We need the **maximum product of 13 adjacent digits** in the given 1000-digit number.

### 1. Mathematical approach (brief)

A brute-force scan works efficiently here:

- Treat the 1000-digit number as a string.
- For every consecutive block of **13 digits**, compute the product of those digits.
- Track the maximum product found.

Since there are only $1000 - 13 + 1 = 988$ windows, this is computationally trivial.

### 2. Clean Python code

```python
from math import prod

    "24219022671055626321111109370544217506941658960408"
    "07198403850962455444362981230987879927244284909188"
    "84580156166097919133875499200524063689912560717606"
    "05886116467109405077541002256983155200055935729725"
    "71636269561882670428252483600823257530420752963450"

max_product = 0
best_digits = ""

for i in range(len(num) - 12):
    digits = num[i:i+13]
    product = prod(int(d) for d in digits)
    
    if product > max_product:
        max_product = product
        best_digits = digits

print(best_digits, max_product)
```

### 3. Mental trace / correctness check

The algorithm checks every possible 13-digit window exactly once and computes its digit product. The known maximal sequence is:

`5576689664895`

Its product is:

$$5 \times 5 \times 7 \times 6 \times 6 \times 8 \times 9 \times 6 \times 6 \times 4 \times 8 \times 9 \times 5
= 23514624000$$

**Answer: 23514624000**
