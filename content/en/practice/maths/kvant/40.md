## Problem Understanding

The problem asks first for the sum $1\cdot n + 2\cdot (n-1) + \ldots + n\cdot 1$, then for a more general sum $S_{n,k}$ involving products of $k$ consecutive integers multiplied in a mirrored fashion. This is a Type C problem: we are asked to find the explicit value of a sum. The core difficulty is the generalization to $S_{n,k}$, which requires expressing products of consecutive integers and detecting a telescoping pattern or combinatorial identity that reduces the sum to a single factorial expression. Intuitively, the symmetry between the indices $i$ and $n-i+1$ suggests a binomial coefficient structure, as small cases show rapid growth similar to factorials.
