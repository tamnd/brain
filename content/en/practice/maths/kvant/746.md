A paper square is folded in half along some line $l$ passing through its center, forming a (non-convex) nonagon.

1. How should the line $l$ be drawn so that the resulting nonagon has the greatest area?
2. How should the line $l$ be drawn so that the largest possible circle can be inscribed in it (Fig. 1)?

K. Vulfson
