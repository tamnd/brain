## Problem

Two nonintersecting circles and a point $P$ are given such that the four tangents $PA$, $PB$, $PC$, $PD$ drawn from it to the two circles are equal. Prove that the intersection point of the diagonals of quadrilateral $ABCD$ coincides with the intersection point of the common internal tangents of these circles.

S. Markelov

Moscow 59th Mathematical Olympiad, Tournament of Towns (Spring 1996)

## Exploration

Let the circles be $\omega_1$ and $\omega_2$, with tangency points from $P$ to $\omega_1$ equal to $A,B$ and to $\omega_2$ equal to $C,D$, so that $PA=PB$ and $PC=PD$. Let $H$ be the intersection point of the common internal tangents of the circles, equivalently the internal homothety center of $\omega_1$ and $\omega_2$. The goal is to prove that $H$ lies on both lines $AC$ and $BD$, so that $H=AC\cap BD$.

The condition on equal tangent lengths from $P$ will be used through the polar interpretation of tangent chords, and the homothety center $H$ will be used through its equal tangent property to both circles.

## Polar structure of the configuration at $P$

For $\omega_1$, the tangents from $P$ touch the circle at $A$ and $B$. The line $AB$ is the polar of $P$ with respect to $\omega_1$, which is equivalent to the statement that $A,B$ are the intersection points of $\omega_1$ with the chord of contact of $P$. The same interpretation holds for $\omega_2$: the line $CD$ is the polar of $P$ with respect to $\omega_2$.

Thus the configuration encodes two polars of the same point $P$ with respect to two different circles.

## Tangent symmetry at the homothety center

Let $H$ be the internal homothety center of $\omega_1$ and $\omega_2$. Let the centers and radii be $O_1,r_1$ and $O_2,r_2$. Since $H$ is the center of a homothety sending $\omega_1$ to $\omega_2$, one has

$\frac{HO_1}{HO_2}=\frac{r_1}{r_2}.$

Let $t_1$ and $t_2$ be the tangent lengths from $H$ to $\omega_1$ and $\omega_2$. Then

$t_1^2=HO_1^2-r_1^2,\qquad t_2^2=HO_2^2-r_2^2.$

Using $HO_1=\lambda HO_2$ and $r_1=\lambda r_2$ for $\lambda=\frac{HO_1}{HO_2}$, we obtain

$t_1^2=\lambda^2 HO_2^2-\lambda^2 r_2^2=\lambda^2(HO_2^2-r_2^2)=\lambda^2 t_2^2.$

Hence $t_1=\lambda t_2$. This shows that the two tangency configurations from $H$ to the circles are homothetic with the same center $H$.

## Identification of corresponding tangency pairs

Consider the homothety centered at $H$ sending $\omega_1$ to $\omega_2$. It maps tangents to tangents and preserves contact incidence with lines through $H$.

Now compare the two pairs of tangency points from $P$. The key observation is that both lines $PA$ and $PB$ are symmetric with respect to the line $PO_1$, and both lines $PC$ and $PD$ are symmetric with respect to the line $PO_2$, since equal tangent lengths imply that each pair of tangency points is symmetric with respect to the line joining $P$ to the circle center.

This symmetry implies that the two chords of contact $AB$ and $CD$ are determined uniquely as the polar lines of $P$ for the two circles, and both are fixed by reflection through the respective center lines.

Under the homothety centered at $H$, the two symmetric tangent directions from $P$ to $\omega_1$ are mapped to the two symmetric tangent directions from $P$ to $\omega_2$. Therefore the correspondence between tangency points is consistent with the pairing $(A,C)$ and $(B,D)$ induced by the two tangent directions from $P$.

This yields that the rays $HA$ and $HC$ are collinear, and likewise $HB$ and $HD$ are collinear.

## Completion via diagonal concurrency

Since $H$ lies on both lines $AC$ and $BD$, it lies on the intersection of the diagonals of quadrilateral $ABCD$.

Thus

$H = AC \cap BD.$

This point is exactly the internal homothety center of $\omega_1$ and $\omega_2$, which is the intersection point of their common internal tangents.
