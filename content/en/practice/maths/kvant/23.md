## Problem

Prove that for all natural $n&gt;1$, $$\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2n}} \lt \frac{1\cdot 3 \cdot\ldots\cdot (2n-1)}{2\cdot 4\cdot \ldots \cdot 2n} \lt \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2n}}.$$

A. O. Gelfond

## Exploration

Let

$$a_n=\frac{1\cdot3\cdot\ldots\cdot(2n-1)}{2\cdot4\cdot\ldots\cdot2n}.$$

The statement is equivalent to

$$\frac12<2n\,a_n^2<\frac34.$$

Indeed,

$$\frac{\sqrt2}{2}\frac1{\sqrt{2n}}<a_n<\frac{\sqrt3}{2}\frac1{\sqrt{2n}}$$

becomes, after squaring and multiplying by $2n$,

$$\frac12<2n\,a_n^2<\frac34.$$

Compute a few values:

$$2\cdot1\cdot a_1^2=\frac12,$$

$$2\cdot2\cdot a_2^2 =4\left(\frac38\right)^2 =\frac9{16},$$

$$2\cdot3\cdot a_3^2 =6\left(\frac5{16}\right)^2 =\frac{75}{128}.$$

The numbers lie between $\frac12$ and $\frac34$.

Set

$$b_n=2n\,a_n^2.$$

Since

$$a_{n+1}=a_n\frac{2n+1}{2n+2},$$

we obtain

$$b_{n+1} =2(n+1)a_{n+1}^2 =b_n\frac{(2n+1)^2}{2n(2n+2)} =b_n\left(1+\frac1{4n(n+1)}\right).$$

Hence $b_n$ is increasing.

To get an upper bound, it is natural to compare $b_{n+1}-\frac34$ with $b_n-\frac34$. A computation gives

$$\left(\frac34\right)\frac{(2n+1)^2}{2n(2n+2)} =\frac34+\frac{3}{16n(n+1)}.$$

The extra term coincides with

$$\frac34\left(\frac{(2n+1)^2}{2n(2n+2)}-1\right),$$

which suggests that if $b_n<\frac34$, then multiplication by the factor above keeps the result below $\frac34$. This yields an inductive proof.

The most delicate point is proving rigorously that the upper bound is preserved under the recurrence.

## Problem Understanding

We must prove that for every natural number $n>1$,

$$\frac{\sqrt2}{2}\frac1{\sqrt{2n}} < \frac{1\cdot3\cdot\ldots\cdot(2n-1)}      {2\cdot4\cdot\ldots\cdot2n} < \frac{\sqrt3}{2}\frac1{\sqrt{2n}}.$$

This is a Type B problem, a pure proof.

The central difficulty is estimating the double-factorial ratio sharply enough to obtain the constants $\frac12$ and $\frac34$ after squaring. The quantity

$$b_n=2n\left( \frac{1\cdot3\cdot\ldots\cdot(2n-1)}      {2\cdot4\cdot\ldots\cdot2n} \right)^2$$

turns out to satisfy a simple recurrence, and the desired inequalities become

$$\frac12<b_n<\frac34.$$

## Proof Architecture

Define

$$a_n=\frac{1\cdot3\cdot\ldots\cdot(2n-1)}           {2\cdot4\cdot\ldots\cdot2n}, \qquad b_n=2n\,a_n^2.$$

First lemma: $b_{n+1}=b_n\dfrac{(2n+1)^2}{2n(2n+2)}$; this follows directly from $a_{n+1}=a_n\dfrac{2n+1}{2n+2}$.

Second lemma: $b_n$ is increasing; the recurrence factor exceeds $1$.

Third lemma: $b_n<\frac34$ for all $n\ge2$; use induction and the recurrence.

After these lemmas, $b_2=\frac9{16}>\frac12$ and monotonicity give the lower bound, while the third lemma gives the upper bound.

The step most likely to fail under scrutiny is the inductive preservation of the inequality $b_n<\frac34$.

## Solution

Let

$$a_n=\frac{1\cdot3\cdot\ldots\cdot(2n-1)}           {2\cdot4\cdot\ldots\cdot2n}, \qquad b_n=2n\,a_n^2.$$

The required inequalities are equivalent to

$$\frac12<b_n<\frac34.$$

Indeed, squaring

$$\frac{\sqrt2}{2}\frac1{\sqrt{2n}}<a_n<\frac{\sqrt3}{2}\frac1{\sqrt{2n}}$$

and multiplying by $2n$ gives precisely

$$\frac12<b_n<\frac34.$$

We therefore prove these inequalities for $b_n$.

Since

$$a_{n+1}=a_n\frac{2n+1}{2n+2},$$

we have

$$\begin{aligned} b_{n+1} &=2(n+1)a_{n+1}^2 \\ &=2(n+1)a_n^2\left(\frac{2n+1}{2n+2}\right)^2 \\ &=b_n\frac{n+1}{n}\left(\frac{2n+1}{2n+2}\right)^2 \\ &=b_n\frac{(2n+1)^2}{2n(2n+2)}. \end{aligned}$$

Since

$$(2n+1)^2=4n(n+1)+1,$$

the factor satisfies

$$\frac{(2n+1)^2}{2n(2n+2)} = 1+\frac1{4n(n+1)} >1.$$

Hence

$$b_{n+1}>b_n,$$

so the sequence $(b_n)$ is increasing.

Next we prove that

$$b_n<\frac34$$

for all $n\ge2$.

For $n=2$,

$$b_2 = 4\left(\frac38\right)^2 = \frac9{16} < \frac34.$$

Assume for some $n\ge2$ that

$$b_n<\frac34.$$

Then

$$\begin{aligned} b_{n+1} &=b_n\frac{(2n+1)^2}{2n(2n+2)}\\ &<\frac34\frac{(2n+1)^2}{2n(2n+2)}. \end{aligned}$$

Using $(2n+1)^2=4n(n+1)+1$,

$$\begin{aligned} \frac34\frac{(2n+1)^2}{2n(2n+2)} &= \frac34\left(1+\frac1{4n(n+1)}\right)\\ &= \frac34+\frac{3}{16n(n+1)}. \end{aligned}$$

On the other hand,

$$\begin{aligned} \frac34-\frac34\frac1{4n(n+1)} &= \frac34. \end{aligned}$$

$$-\frac{3}{16n(n+1)}.$$
