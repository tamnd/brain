# IMO 1978 LL VIE49

**Origin:** VIE

## Problem

Let A, B, C, D be four arbitrary distinct points in space.
(a) Prove that using the segments AB + CD, AC + BD and AD + BC it
is always possible to construct a triangle T that is nondegenerate and
has no obtuse angle.
(b) What should these four points satisfy in order for the triangle T to be
right-angled?
