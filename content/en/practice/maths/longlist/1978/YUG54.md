# IMO 1978 LL YUG54

**Origin:** YUG

## Problem

Let p, q and r be three lines in space such that there is no plane
that is parallel to all three of them. Prove that there exist three planes
\alpha, \beta, and \gamma, containing p, q, and r respectively, that are perpendicular to
each other (\alpha \perp\beta, \beta \perp\gamma, \gamma \perp\alpha).
