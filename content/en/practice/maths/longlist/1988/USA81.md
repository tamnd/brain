# IMO 1988 LL USA81

**Origin:** USA

## Problem

There are n \geq3 job openings at a factory, ranked 1 to n in
order of increasing pay. There are n job applicants, ranked 1 to n in order
of increasing ability. Applicant i is qualiﬁed for job j if and only if i \geqj.
The applicants arrive one at a time in random order. Each in turn is
hired to the highest-ranking job for which he or she is qualiﬁed and that
is lower in rank than any job already ﬁlled. (Under these rules, job 1 is
always ﬁlled and hiring terminates thereafter.)
Show that applicants n and n−1 have the same probability of being hired.
