# IMO 1988 LL SIN73

**Origin:** SIN

## Problem

In a group of n people each one knows exactly three others. They
are seated around a table. We say that the seating is perfect if everyone
knows the two sitting by their sides. Show that if there is a perfect seating
S for the group, then there is always another perfect seating that cannot
be obtained from S by rotation or reﬂection.
