# IMO 1971 LL NET33

**Origin:** NET

## Problem

A square 2n \times 2n grid is given. Let us consider all possible
paths along grid lines, going from the center of the grid to the border,
such that (1) no point of the grid is reached more than once, and (2) each
of the squares homothetic to the grid having its center at the grid center
is passed through only once.
(a) Prove that the number of all such paths is equal to 4 $n
i=2(16i −9).
(b) Find the number of pairs of such paths that divide the grid into two
congruent ﬁgures.
(c) How many quadruples of such paths are there that divide the grid into
four congruent parts?
