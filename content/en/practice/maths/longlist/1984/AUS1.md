# IMO 1984 LL AUS1

**Origin:** AUS

## Problem

The fraction
10 can be written as the sum of two positive
fractions with numerator 1 as follows:
10 = 1
5 + 1
10 and also
10 = 1
4 + 1
20.
There are the only two ways in which this can be done.
In how many ways can
1984 be written as the sum of two positive fractions
with numerator 1?
Is there a positive integer n, not divisible by 3, such that 3
n can be written
as the sum of two positive fractions with numerator 1 in exactly 1984
ways?
