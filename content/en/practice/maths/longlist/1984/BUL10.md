# IMO 1984 LL BUL10

**Origin:** BUL

## Problem

Assume that the bisecting plane of the dihedral angle at edge
AB of the tetrahedron ABCD meets the edge CD at point E. Denote by
S1, S2, S3, respectively the areas of the triangles ABC, ABE, and ABD.
Prove that no tetrahedron exists for which S1, S2, S3 (in this order) form
an arithmetic or geometric progression.
