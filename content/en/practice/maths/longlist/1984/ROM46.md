# IMO 1984 LL ROM46

**Origin:** ROM

## Problem

Let (an)n\geq1 and (bn)n\geq1 be two sequences of natural numbers
such that an+1 = nan + 1, bn+1 = nbn −1 for every n \geq1. Show that
these two sequences can have only a ﬁnite number of terms in common.
