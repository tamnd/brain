# IMO 1984 LL USA62

**Origin:** USA

## Problem

From a point P exterior to a circle K, two rays are drawn
intersecting K in the respective pairs of points A, A′ and B, B′. For any
other pair of points C, C′ on K, let D be the point of intersection of the
circumcircles of triangles PAC and PB′C′ other than point P. Similarly,
let D′ be the point of intersection of the circumcircles of triangles PA′C′
and PBC other than point P. Prove that the points P, D, and D′ are
collinear.
