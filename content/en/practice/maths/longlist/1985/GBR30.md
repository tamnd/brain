# IMO 1985 LL GBR30

**Origin:** GBR

## Problem

A plane rectangular grid is given and a “rational point” is
deﬁned as a point (x, y) where x and y are both rational numbers. Let
A, B, A′, B′ be four distinct rational points. Let P be a point such that
A′B′
AB = B′P
BC = PA′
PA . In other words, the triangles ABP, A′B′P are directly
or oppositely similar. Prove that P is in general a rational point and ﬁnd
the exceptional positions of A′ and B′ relative to A and B such that there
exists a P that is not a rational point.
