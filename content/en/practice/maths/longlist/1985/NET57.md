# IMO 1985 LL NET57

**Origin:** NET

## Problem

The solid S is deﬁned as the intersection of the six spheres with
the six edges of a regular tetrahedron T , with edge length 1, as diameters.
Prove that S contains two points at a distance
\sqrt
6.
(NET 1a) Using the same assumptions, prove that no pair of points in
S has a distance larger than
\sqrt
6.
