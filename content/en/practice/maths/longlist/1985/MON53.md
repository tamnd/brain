# IMO 1985 LL MON53

**Origin:** MON

## Problem

For each P inside the triangle ABC, let A(P), B(P), and
C(P) be the points of intersection of the lines AP, BP, and CP with the
sides opposite to A, B, and C, respectively. Determine P in such a way
that the area of the triangle A(P)B(P)C(P) is as large as possible.
