# IMO 1985 LL CZS20

**Origin:** CZS

## Problem

Let T be the set of all lattice points (i.e., all points with
integer coordinates) in three-dimensional space. Two such points (x, y, z)
and (u, v, w) are called neighbors if |x −u| + |y −v| + |z −w| = 1. Show
that there exists a subset S of T such that for each p \inT , there is exactly
one point of S among p and its neighbors.
