# IMO 1985 LL ROM69

**Origin:** ROM

## Problem

Let A and B be two ﬁnite disjoint sets of points in the plane
such that any three distinct points in A\cupB are not collinear. Assume that
at least one of the sets A, B contains at least ﬁve points. Show that there
exists a triangle all of whose vertices are contained in A or in B that does
not contain in its interior any point from the other set.
