# IMO 1983 LL NET47

**Origin:** NET

## Problem

In a plane, three pairwise intersecting circles C1, C2, C3 with
centers M1, M2, M3 are given. For i = 1, 2, 3, let Ai be one of the points of
intersection of Cj and Ck ({i, j, k} = {1, 2, 3}). Prove that if \angleM3A1M2 =
\angleM1A2M3 = \angleM2A3M1 = \pi/3 (directed angles), then M1A1, M2A2, and
M3A3 are concurrent.
