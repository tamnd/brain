# IMO 1979 LL SWE62

**Origin:** SWE

## Problem

T is a given triangle with vertices P1, P2, P3. Consider an arbi-
trary subdivision of T into ﬁnitely many subtriangles such that no vertex
of a subtriangle lies strictly between two vertices of another subtriangle.
To each vertex V of the subtriangles there is assigned a number n(V )
according to the following rules:
(i) If V = Pi, then n(V ) = i.
(ii) If V lies on the side PiPj of T , then n(V ) = i or j.
(iii) If V lies inside the triangle T , then n(V ) is any of the numbers 1,2,3.
Prove that there exists at least one subtriangle whose vertices are num-
bered 1, 2, and 3.
