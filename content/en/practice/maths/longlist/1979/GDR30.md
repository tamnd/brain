# IMO 1979 LL GDR30

**Origin:** GDR

## Problem

Let M be a set of points in a plane with at least two elements.
Prove that if M has two axes of symmetry g1 and g2 intersecting at an
angle \alpha = q\pi, where q is irrational, then M must be inﬁnite.
