# IMO 1979 LL BUL13

**Origin:** BUL

## Problem

The plane is divided into equal squares by parallel lines; i.e.,
a square net is given. Let M be an arbitrary set of n squares of this net.
Prove that it is possible to choose no fewer than n/4 squares of M in such
a way that no two of them have a common point.
