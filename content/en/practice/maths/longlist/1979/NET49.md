# IMO 1979 LL NET49

**Origin:** NET

## Problem

Let there be given two sequences of integers fi(1), fi(2), . . .
(i = 1, 2) satisfying:
(i) fi(nm) = fi(n)fi(m) if gcd(n, m) = 1;
(ii) for every prime P and all k = 2, 3, 4, . . ., fi(P k) = fi(P)fi(P k−1) −
P 2f(P k−2).
Moreover, for every prime P:
(iii) f1(P) = 2P,
(iv) f2(P) < 2P.
Prove that |f2(n)| < f1(n) for all n.
