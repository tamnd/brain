# IMO 1979 LL HUN38

**Origin:** HUN

## Problem

Prove the following statement: If a polynomial f(x) with
real coeﬃcients takes only nonnegative values, then there exists a positive
integer n and polynomials g1(x), g2(x), . . . , gn(x) such that
f(x) = g1(x)2 + g2(x)2 + \cdot \cdot \cdot + gn(x)2.
