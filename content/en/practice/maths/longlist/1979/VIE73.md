# IMO 1979 LL VIE73

**Origin:** VIE

## Problem

In a plane a ﬁnite number of equal circles are given. These circles
are mutually nonintersecting (they may be externally tangent). Prove that
one can use at most four colors for coloring these circles so that two circles
tangent to each other are of diﬀerent colors. What is the smallest number
of circles that requires four colors?
