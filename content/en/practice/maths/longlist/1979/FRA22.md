# IMO 1979 LL FRA22

**Origin:** FRA

## Problem

Euclidian plane such that AB = A′B′, BC = B′C′, CD = C′D′, and
DA = D′A′. Prove that the following two statements are true:
(a) If the diagonals BD and AC are mutually perpendicular, then the
diagonals B′D′ and A′C′ are also mutually perpendicular.
(b) If the perpendicular bisector of BD intersects AC at M, and that
of B′D′ intersects A′C′ at M ′, then MA
MC = M′A′
M′C′ (if MC = 0 then
M ′C′ = 0).
