# IMO 1992 LL MON50

**Origin:** MON

## Problem

Let N be a point inside the triangle ABC. Through the mid-
points of the segments AN, BN, and CN the lines parallel to the opposite
sides of \triangleABC are constructed. Let AN, BN, and CN be the intersection
points of these lines. If N is the orthocenter of the triangle ABC, prove
that the nine-point circles of \triangleABC and \triangleANBNCN coincide.
Remark. The statement of the original problem was that the nine-point
circles of the triangles ANBNCN and AMBMCM coincide, where N and
M are the orthocenter and the centroid of \triangleABC. This statement is false.
