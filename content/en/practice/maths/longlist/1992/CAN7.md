# IMO 1992 LL CAN7

**Origin:** CAN

## Problem

Let X be a bounded, nonempty set of points in the Cartesian
plane. Let f(X) be the set of all points that are at a distance of at most 1
from some point in X. Let f n(X) = f(f(. . . (f(X)) . . . )) (n times). Show
that f n(X) becomes “more circular” as n gets larger. In other words, if
rn = sup{radii of circles contained in f n(X)} and Rn = inf{radii of circles
containing f n(X)}, then show that Rn/rn gets arbitrarily close to 1 as n
becomes arbitrarily large.
