# IMO 1977 LL ROM37

**Origin:** ROM

## Problem

Let A1, A2, . . . , An+1 be positive integers such that (Ai, An+1)
= 1 for every i = 1, 2, . . ., n. Show that the equation
xA1
+ xA2
+ \cdot \cdot \cdot + xAn
n
= xAn+1
n+1
has an inﬁnite set of solutions (x1, x2, . . . , xn+1) in positive integers.

## Solution

We look for a solution with xA1
= \cdot \cdot \cdot = xAn
n
= nA1A2\cdot\cdot\cdotAnx and xn+1 =
ny. In order for this to be a solution we must have A1A2 \cdot \cdot \cdot Anx + 1 =

An+1y. This equation has inﬁnitely many solutions (x, y) in N, since
A1A2 \cdot \cdot \cdot An and An+1 are coprime.
