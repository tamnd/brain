# IMO 1977 LL USA55

**Origin:** USA

## Problem

Through a point O on the diagonal BD of a parallelogram
ABCD, segments MN parallel to AB, and PQ parallel to AD, are drawn,
with M on AD, and Q on AB. Prove that diagonals AO, BP, DN (ex-
tended if necessary) will be concurrent.

## Solution

The statement is true without the assumption that O \inBD. Let BP \cap
DN = {K}. If we denote −−\to
AB = a, −−\to
AD = b and −\to
AO = \alphaa + \betab for some
\alpha, \beta \inR, 1/\alpha + 1/\beta ̸= 1, by straightforward calculation we obtain that

−−\to
AK =
\alpha
\alpha + \beta −\alpha\beta a +
\beta
\alpha + \beta −\alpha\beta b =
\alpha + \beta −\alpha\beta

AO.
Hence A, K, O are collinear.
