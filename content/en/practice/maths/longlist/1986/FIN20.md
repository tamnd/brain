# IMO 1986 LL FIN20

**Origin:** FIN

## Problem

For any angle \alpha with 0 < \alpha < 180◦, we call a closed convex
planar set an \alpha-set if it is bounded by two circular arcs (or an arc and a
line segment) whose angle of intersection is \alpha. Given a (closed) triangle
T , ﬁnd the greatest \alpha such that any two points in T are contained in an
\alpha-set S \subsetT .
