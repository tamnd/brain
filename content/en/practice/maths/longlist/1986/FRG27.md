# IMO 1986 LL FRG27

**Origin:** FRG

## Problem

In an urn there are n balls numbered 1, 2, . . . , n. They are
drawn at random one by one one without replacement and the numbers are
recorded. What is the probability that the resulting random permutation
has only one local maximum?
A term in a sequence is a local maximum if it is greater than all its
neighbors.
