# IMO 1986 LL ISR48

**Origin:** ISR

## Problem

Let P be a convex 1986-gon in the plane. Let A, D be interior
points of two distinct sides of P and let B, C be two distinct interior
points of the line segment AD. Starting with an arbitrary point Q1 on
the boundary of P, deﬁne recursively a sequence of points Qn as follows:
given Qn extend the directed line segment QnB to meet the boundary of
P in a point Rn and then extend RnC to meet the boundary of P again
in a point, which is deﬁned to be Qn+1. Prove that for all n large enough
the points Qn are on one of the sides of P containing A or D.
