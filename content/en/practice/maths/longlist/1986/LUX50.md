# IMO 1986 LL LUX50

**Origin:** LUX

## Problem

Let D be the point on the side BC of the triangle ABC such
that AD is the bisector of \angleCAB. Let I be the incenter of \triangleABC.
(a) Construct the points P and Q on the sides AB and AC, respectively,
such that PQ is parallel to BC and the perimeter of the triangle APQ
is equal to k \cdot BC, where k is a given rational number.
(b) Let R be the intersection point of PQ and AD. For what value of k
does the equality AR = RI hold?
(c) In which case do the equalities AR = RI = ID hold?
