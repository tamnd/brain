# IMO 1986 LL CZS17

**Origin:** CZS

## Problem

We call a tetrahedron right-faced if each of its faces is a right-
angled triangle.
(a) Prove that every orthogonal parallelepiped can be partitioned into six
right-faced tetrahedra.
(b) Prove that a tetrahedron with vertices A1, A2, A3, A4 is ﬁght-faced
if and only if there exist four distinct real numbers c1, c2, c3, and
c4 such that the edges AjAk have lengths AjAk =
|cj −ck| for
1 \leqj < k \leq4.
