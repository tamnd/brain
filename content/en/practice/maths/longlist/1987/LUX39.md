# IMO 1987 LL LUX39

**Origin:** LUX

## Problem

Let A be a set of polynomials with real coeﬃcients and let
them satisfy the following conditions:
(i) if f \inA and deg f \leq1, then f(x) = x −1;
(ii) if f \inA and deg f \geq2, then either there exists g \inA such that
f(x) = x2+deg g + xg(x) −1 or there exist g, h \inA such that f(x) =
x1+deg gg(x) + h(x);
(iii) for every f, g \inA, both x2+deg f + xf(x) −1 and x1+deg ff(x) + g(x)
belong to A.
Let Rn(f) be the remainder of the Euclidean division of the polynomial
f(x) by xn. Prove that for all f \inA and for all natural numbers n \geq1
we have
Rn(f)(1) \leq0
and
Rn(f)(1) = 0 ⇒Rn(f) \inA.
