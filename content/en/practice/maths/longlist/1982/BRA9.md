# IMO 1982 LL BRA9

**Origin:** BRA

## Problem

Let n be a natural number, n \geq2, and let \varphi be Euler’s function;
i.e., \varphi(n) is the number of positive integers not exceeding n and coprime
to n. Given any two real numbers \alpha and \beta, 0 \leq\alpha < \beta \leq1, prove that
there exists a natural number m such that
\alpha < \varphi(m)
m
< \beta.
