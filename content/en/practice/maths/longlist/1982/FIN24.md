# IMO 1982 LL FIN24

**Origin:** FIN

## Problem

Prove that if a person a has inﬁnitely many descendants (chil-
dren, their children, etc.), then a has an inﬁnite sequence a0, a1, . . . of
descendants (i.e., a = a0 and for all n \geq1, an+1 is always a child of an).
It is assumed that no-one can have inﬁnitely many children.
Variant 1. Prove that if a has inﬁnitely many ancestors, then a has an
inﬁnite descending sequence of ancestors (i.e., a0, a1, . . . where a = a0 and
an is always a child of an+1).
Variant 2. Prove that if someone has inﬁnitely many ancestors, then all
people cannot descend from A(dam) and E(ve).
