# IMO 1982 LL BRA12

**Origin:** BRA

## Problem

Let there be 3399 numbers arbitrarily chosen among the ﬁrst
6798 integers 1, 2, . . ., 6798 in such a way that none of them divides an-
other. Prove that there are exactly 1982 numbers in {1, 2, . . ., 6798} that
must end up being chosen.
