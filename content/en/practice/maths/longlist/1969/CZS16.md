# IMO 1969 LL CZS16

**Origin:** CZS

## Problem

A convex quadrilateral ABCD with sides AB = a, BC = b,
CD = c, DA = d and angles \alpha = \angleDAB, \beta = \angleABC, \gamma = \angleBCD, and
\delta = \angleCDA is given. Let s = (a + b + c + d)/2 and P be the area of the
quadrilateral. Prove that
P 2 = (s −a)(s −b)(s −c)(s −d) −abcd cos2 \alpha + \gamma
.
