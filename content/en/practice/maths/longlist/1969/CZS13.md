# IMO 1969 LL CZS13

**Origin:** CZS

## Problem

Let p be a prime odd number. Is it possible to ﬁnd p−1 natural
numbers n + 1, n + 2, . . . , n + p −1 such that the sum of the squares of
these numbers is divisible by the sum of these numbers?
