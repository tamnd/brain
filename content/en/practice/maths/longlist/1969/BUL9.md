# IMO 1969 LL BUL9

**Origin:** BUL

## Problem

One hundred convex polygons are placed on a square with edge
of length 38 cm. The area of each of the polygons is smaller than \pi cm2,
and the perimeter of each of the polygons is smaller than 2\pi cm. Prove
that there exists a disk with radius 1 in the square that does not intersect
any of the polygons.
