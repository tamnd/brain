# IMO 1969 LL BUL11

**Origin:** BUL

## Problem

Let Z be a set of points in the plane. Suppose that there exists
a pair of points that cannot be joined by a polygonal line not passing
through any point of Z. Let us call such a pair of points unjoinable. Prove
that for each real r > 0 there exists an unjoinable pair of points separated
by distance r.
