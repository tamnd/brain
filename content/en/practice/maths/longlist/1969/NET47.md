# IMO 1969 LL NET47

**Origin:** NET

## Problem

Let A and B be points on the circle \gamma. A point C, diﬀerent
from A and B, is on the circle \gamma. Let D be the projection of the point
C onto the line AB. Consider three other circles \gamma1, \gamma2, and \gamma3 with the
common tangent AB: \gamma1 inscribed in the triangle ABC, and \gamma2 and \gamma3
tangent to both (the segment) CD and \gamma. Prove that \gamma1, \gamma2, and \gamma3 have
two common tangents.
