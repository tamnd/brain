# IMO 1969 LL NET49

**Origin:** NET

## Problem

A boy has a set of trains and pieces of railroad track. Each
piece is a quarter of circle, and by concatenating these pieces, the boy
obtained a closed railway. The railway does not intersect itself. In passing
through this railway, the train sometimes goes in the clockwise direction,
and sometimes in the opposite direction. Prove that the train passes an
even number of times through the pieces in the clockwise direction and an
even number of times in the counterclockwise direction. Also, prove that
the number of pieces is divisible by 4.
