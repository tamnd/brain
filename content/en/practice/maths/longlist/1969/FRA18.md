# IMO 1969 LL FRA18

**Origin:** FRA

## Problem

Let a and b be two nonnegative integers. Denote by H(a, b)
the set of numbers n of the form n = pa + qb, where p and q are positive
integers. Determine H(a) = H(a, a). Prove that if a ̸= b, it is enough to
know all the sets H(a, b) for coprime numbers a, b in order to know all the
sets H(a, b). Prove that in the case of coprime numbers a and b, H(a, b)
contains all numbers greater than or equal to \omega = (a −1)(b −1) and also
\omega/2 numbers smaller than \omega.
