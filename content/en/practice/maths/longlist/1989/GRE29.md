# IMO 1989 LL GRE29

**Origin:** GRE

## Problem

Let L denote the set of all lattice points of the plane (points
with integral coordinates). Show that for any three points A, B, C of L
there is a fourth point D, diﬀerent from A, B, C, such that the interiors of
the segments AD, BD, CD contain no points of L. Is the statement true
if one considers four points of L instead of three?
