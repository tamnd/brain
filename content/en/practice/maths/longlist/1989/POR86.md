# IMO 1989 LL POR86

**Origin:** POR

## Problem

Given two natural numbers w and n, the tower of n w’s is the
natural number Tn(w) deﬁned by
Tn(w) = ww...w
,
with n w’s on the right side. More precisely, T1(w) = w and Tn+1(w) =
wTn(w). For example, T3(2) = 222 = 16, T4(2) = 216 = 65536, and T2(3) =
33 = 27.
Find the smallest tower of 3’s that exceeds the tower of 1989 2’s. In other
words, ﬁnd the smallest value of n such that Tn(3) > T1989(2). Justify
your answer.
