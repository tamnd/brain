# IMO 1989 LL POR83

**Origin:** POR

## Problem

Poldavia is a strange kingdom. Its currency unit is the bourbaki
and there exist only two types of coins: gold ones and silver ones. Each
gold coin is worth n bourbakis and each silver coin is worth m bourbakis
(n and m are positive integers). Using gold and solver coins, it is possible
to obtain sums such as 10000 bourbakis, 1875 bourbakis, 3072 bourbakis,
and so on. But Poldavia’s monetary system is not as strange as it seems:
(a) Prove that it is possible to buy anything that costs an integral number
of bourbakis, as long as one can receive change.
(b) Prove that any payment above mn−2 bourbakis can be made without
the need to receive change.
