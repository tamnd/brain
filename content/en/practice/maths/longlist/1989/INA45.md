# IMO 1989 LL INA45

**Origin:** INA

## Problem

The expressions a + b + c, ab + ac + bc, and abc are called the
elementary symmetric expressions on the three letters a, b, c; symmetric
because if we interchange any two letters, say a and c, the expressions
remain algebraically the same. The common degree of its terms is called
the order of the expression.
Let Sk(n) denote the elementary expression on k diﬀerent letters of order
n; for example S4(3) = abc + abd + acd + bcd. There are four terms in
S4(3). How many terms are there in S9891(1989)? (Assume that we have
9891 diﬀerent letters.)
