# IMO 1989 LL INA47

**Origin:** INA

## Problem

Let log2
2 x −4 log2 x −m2 −2m −13 = 0 be an equation in x.
Prove:
(a) For any real value of m the equation has has two distinct solutions.
(b) The product of the solutions of the equation does not depend on m.
(c) One of the solutions of the equation is less than 1, while the other
solution is greater than 1.
Find the minimum value of the larger solution and the maximum value of
the smaller solution.
