# IMO 1970 LL FRA23

**Origin:** FRA

## Problem

Let E be a ﬁnite set, PE the family of its subsets, and f a
mapping from PE to the set of nonnegative real numbers such that for
any two disjoint subsets A, B of E,
f(A \cupB) = f(A) + f(B).
Prove that there exists a subset F of E such that if with each A \subsetE we
associate a subset A′ consisting of elements of A that are not in F, then
f(A) = f(A′), and f(A) is zero if and only if A is a subset of F.
