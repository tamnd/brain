# IMO 1970 LL FRA26

**Origin:** FRA

## Problem

Consider a ﬁnite set of vectors in space {a1, a2, . . . , an} and
the set E of all vectors of the form x = \lambda1a1 +\lambda2a2 +\cdot \cdot \cdot+\lambdanan, where \lambdai
are nonnegative numbers. Let F be the set consisting of all the vectors in
E and vectors parallel to a given plane P. Prove that there exists a set of
vectors {b1, b2, . . . , bp} such that F is the set of all vectors y of the form
y = µ1b1 + µ2b2 + \cdot \cdot \cdot + µpbp,
where the µj are nonnegative.
