# IMO 1976 LL USS43

**Origin:** USS

## Problem

Prove that if for a polynomial P(x, y) we have
P(x −1, y −2x + 1) = P(x, y),
then there exists a polynomial \Phi(x) with P(x, y) = \Phi(y −x2).
