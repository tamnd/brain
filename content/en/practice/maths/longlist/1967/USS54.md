# IMO 1967 LL USS54

**Origin:** USS

## Problem

Is it possible to put 100 (or 200) points on a wooden cube such
that by all rotations of the cube the points map into themselves? Justify
your answer.

## Solution

Let S be the given set of points on the cube. Let x, y, z denote the numbers
of points from S lying at a vertex, at the midpoint of an edge, at the
midpoint of a face of the cube, respectively, and let u be the number of
all other points from S.
Either there are no points from S at the vertices of the cube, or there
is a point from S at each vertex. Hence x is either 0 or 8. Similarly, y
is either 0 or 12, and z is either 0 or 6. Any other point of S has 24
possible images under rotations of the cube. Hence u is divisible by 24.
Since n = x + y + z + u and 6 | y, z, u, it follows that either 6 | n or
6 | n −8, i.e., n \equiv0 or n \equiv2 (mod 6). Thus n = 200 is possible, while
n = 100 is not, because n \equiv4 (mod 6).
