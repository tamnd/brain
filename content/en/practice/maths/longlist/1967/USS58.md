# IMO 1967 LL USS58

**Origin:** USS

## Problem

A linear binomial l(z) = Az + B with complex coeﬃcients A
and B is given. It is known that the maximal value of |l(z)| on the segment
−1 \leqx \leq1 (y = 0) of the real line in the complex plane (z = x + iy) is
equal to M. Prove that for every z
|l(z)| \leqM\rho,
where \rho is the sum of distances from the point P = z to the points Q1:
z = 1 and Q3: z = −1.

## Solution

The following sequence of equalities and inequalities gives an even stronger
estimate than needed.
|l(z)| = |Az + B| = 1
2|(z + 1)(A + B) + (z −1)(A −B)|
= 1
2|(z + 1)f(1) + (z −1)f(−1)|
\leq1
2(|z + 1| \cdot |f(1)| + |z −1| \cdot |f(−1)|)
\leq1
2(|z + 1| + |z −1|)M = 1
2\rhoM.
