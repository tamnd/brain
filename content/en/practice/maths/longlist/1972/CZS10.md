# IMO 1972 LL CZS10

**Origin:** CZS

## Problem

Given ﬁve points in the plane, no three of which are collinear,
prove that there can be found at least two obtuse-angled triangles with
vertices at the given points. Construct an example in which there are
exactly two such triangles.
