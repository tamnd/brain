# IMO 1972 LL GBR16

**Origin:** GBR

## Problem

Consider the set S of all the diﬀerent odd positive integers
that are not multiples of 5 and that are less than 30m, m being a positive
integer. What is the smallest integer k such that in any subset of k integers
from S there must be two integers one of which divides the other? Prove
your result.
