# IMO 1972 LL CZS12

**Origin:** CZS

## Problem

A circle k = (S, r) is given and a hexagon AA′BB′CC′ inscribed
in it. The lengths of sides of the hexagon satisfy AA′ = A′B, BB′ = B′C,
CC′ = C′A. Prove that the area P of triangle ABC is not greater than
the area P ′ of triangle A′B′C′. When does P = P ′ hold?
