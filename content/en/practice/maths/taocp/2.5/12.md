[Section 2.5: Dynamic Storage Allocation](../)

**Exercise 12.** [**] [20] Modify Algorithm A so that it follows the boundary-tag
conventions of (7)-(9), uses the modified step A4' described in the text,
and also incorporates the improvement of exercise 6.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 5m01s  

---

Under conventions (7) to (9), every block contains boundary tags at its two ends. The tags record the block length and the free/occupied status. If a block is free, its first words also contain the doubly linked list fields $L$ and $R$. Thus a free block of length $m$ has the form

$$
\boxed{\text{tag }m}\;\;L\;\;R\;\;\cdots\;\;\boxed{\text{tag }m},
$$

while an occupied block has the same boundary-tag information but does not participate in the free list.

Exercise 6 is incorporated by maintaining a predecessor pointer $P$ during the search. Thus, whenever $Q$ is the block currently being examined,

$$
P=\Lambda
$$

if $Q=AVAIL$; otherwise $P$ points to the predecessor of $Q$ in the free list.

The modified Algorithm A is as follows.

### A1. [Initialize.]

Set

$$
P\leftarrow \Lambda,\qquad Q\leftarrow AVAIL.
$$

### A2. [Search.]

If

$$
Q=\Lambda,
$$

the search fails.

Let

$$
m=\text{length}(Q),
$$

obtained from the left boundary tag of $Q$.

If

$$
m\ge N,
$$

go to A4.

Otherwise set

$$
P\leftarrow Q,\qquad Q\leftarrow R(Q),
$$

and return to A2.

### A4. [Found a suitable block.]

Let $m=\text{length}(Q)$.

#### A4a. Exact fit.

If

$$
m=N,
$$

remove $Q$ from the free list.

Let

$$
S=R(Q).
$$

If $P=\Lambda$, set

$$
AVAIL\leftarrow S;
$$

otherwise set

$$
R(P)\leftarrow S.
$$

If $S\neq\Lambda$, set

$$
L(S)\leftarrow P.
$$

Now change the free/occupied indication in both boundary tags of $Q$ from free to occupied. The length field remains $N$.

Return $Q$.

#### A4b. Split a larger block, using A4′.

Assume

$$
m>N.
$$

Let the first address of $Q$ be $q$. The free block occupies addresses

$$
q,\; q+1,\; \ldots,\; q+m-1.
$$

The modified step A4′ allocates from the high-address end of the block, leaving the original free-block header and free-list links undisturbed.

Let

$$
m'=m-N.
$$

The residual free block therefore occupies

$$
q,\; q+1,\; \ldots,\; q+m'-1,
$$

and the allocated block occupies

$$
q+m',\; q+m'+1,\; \ldots,\; q+m-1.
$$

The old boundary tags of $Q$ are replaced as follows.

1. The left boundary tag at address $q$ is changed from length $m$ to length $m'$, marked free.
2. The right boundary tag of the residual free block is written at address

$$
q+m'-1,
$$

with length $m'$, marked free.

1. The left boundary tag of the allocated block is written at address

$$
q+m',
$$

with length $N$, marked occupied.

1. The right boundary tag of the allocated block is written at address

$$
q+m-1,
$$

with length $N$, marked occupied.

The fields $L(Q)$ and $R(Q)$ remain unchanged, because the residual free block is still the same free-list node, merely shortened. Hence neither $AVAIL$ nor any neighboring links need be altered.

Return the newly allocated block whose initial address is

$$
q+m'.
$$

### A5. [Terminate.]

Return the address produced in A4.

### Correctness

For an exact fit, the block is removed from the doubly linked free list using the predecessor pointer $P$, exactly as required by Exercise 6. Both boundary tags are changed from free to occupied, so the boundary-tag representation remains valid.

For a split block, A4′ leaves the residual free block at the same location $q$. Therefore its $L$ and $R$ fields remain valid, and the free-list structure is unchanged. The residual block receives matching free boundary tags of length $m'$, while the allocated portion receives matching occupied boundary tags of length $N$. Thus conventions (7) to (9) hold for both resulting blocks.

Consequently the algorithm combines all three requested modifications:

1. boundary tags are used throughout;
2. the modified step A4′ allocates from the upper end of a free block and leaves the remainder on the free list;
3. the Exercise 6 improvement maintains the predecessor pointer $P$, eliminating any secondary search when a free block must be removed.
