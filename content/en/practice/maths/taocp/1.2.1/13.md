**Exercise 13.** &#9654; [*M23*] Extend Algorithm E by adding a new variable $T$ and adding the operation “$T \leftarrow T + 1$” at the beginning of each step. (Thus, $T$ is like a clock, counting the number of steps executed.) Assume that $T$ is initially zero, so that assertion *A1* in Fig. 4 becomes “$m > 0$, $n > 0$, $T = 0$.” The additional condition “$T = 1$” should similarly be appended to *A2*. Show how to append additional conditions to the assertions in such a way that any one of *A1*, *A2*, $\ldots$, *A6* implies $T \le 3n$, and such that the inductive proof can still be carried out. (Hence the computation must terminate in at most $3n$ steps.)

## Solution

We extend Algorithm E by introducing a new variable $T$ to count the number of steps executed. Initially, we set $T \leftarrow 0$. At the beginning of each step, we increment $T$ by 1: $T \leftarrow T + 1$. This operation makes $T$ a clock, measuring progress through the algorithm.

To carry out an inductive proof analogous to that in Section 1.2.1, we append $T$ to the assertions _A1_, _A2_, $\ldots$, _A6_ as follows:

- _A1_: $m > 0$, $n > 0$, $T = 0$.
- _A2_: $m > 0$, $n \ge 0$, $T = 1$.
- _A3_: $m = d \cdot q + r$, $0 \le r < d$, $T \le k$ (where $k$ is the step count at this point).
- _A4_: $r = 0 \implies am + bn = d$, $T \le k$.
- _A5_: $r \neq 0$, and after the recycling assignments $c \leftarrow d$, $d \leftarrow r$, $t \leftarrow a'$, $a' \leftarrow a$, $a \leftarrow t - qa$, $t \leftarrow b'$, $b' \leftarrow b$, $b \leftarrow t - qb$, we have $T \le k + 1$.
- _A6_: Algorithm invariant: $am + bn = c$, $a'm + b'n = d$, $c \ge d \ge 0$, $T \le k$.

We now prove by induction on the number of recursive calls that $T \le 3n$ at all times. Let $P(i)$ denote the statement: “At the $i$-th step executed, $T \le 3n$.”

**Base case** ($i = 0$): Initially $T = 0 \le 3n$. This establishes $P(0)$.

**Inductive step**: Assume $P(k)$ holds for some $k \ge 0$, that is, $T \le 3n$ at the $k$-th step. We analyze the next increment $T \leftarrow T + 1$:

- Each execution of steps E2–E4 involves at most three updates of the form $c \leftarrow d$, $d \leftarrow r$, and two pairs of assignments for $a$, $a'$, $b$, $b'$; counting $T \leftarrow T + 1$ at the beginning of the step ensures that each iteration increases $T$ by exactly 1.
- The Euclidean algorithm reduces the second argument $d$ strictly at each non-terminating iteration. Since $d$ starts at $n$ and decreases by at least 1 at each step until termination, the total number of iterations cannot exceed $n$.
- Each iteration involves at most three increments of $T$ corresponding to steps E2, E3, and E4. Therefore, after all iterations, $T \le 3n$.

By induction, every assertion _A1_, _A2_, $\ldots$, _A6_, when extended to include the condition on $T$, satisfies $T \le 3n$. Consequently, the algorithm must terminate after at most $3n$ steps, because $T$ strictly increases and cannot exceed $3n$.

This completes the proof.
