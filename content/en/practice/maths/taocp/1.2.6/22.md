**Exercise 22.** [*M20*] Prove Eq. (26) for the special case $s=(n-1)r+nt$.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 30s

## Solution

We are asked to prove Eq. (26) for the special case

$$
s = (n-1)r + nt.
$$

Equation (26) in the text states

$$
\sum_{i=0}^{s} \binom{r+i}{r} \binom{t+s-i}{t} = \binom{r+t+s+1}{r+t+1}, \qquad \text{integer } r,t \ge 0. \tag{26}
$$

Substituting $s=(n-1)r+nt$ gives

$$
\sum_{i=0}^{(n-1)r+nt} \binom{r+i}{r} \binom{t+(n-1)r+nt - i}{t}. \tag{A}
$$

Let us denote $s'=(n-1)r+nt$. The summation can be rewritten by setting $i = kr + j$, where $k$ ranges from $0$ to $n$ and $0 \le j < r$, but a more systematic approach is to apply repeated use of the summation formula Eq. (11):

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{m} \binom{k}{p} = \binom{m+1}{p+1}, \qquad \text{integer } m,p \ge 0.
$$

First, observe that $\binom{r+i}{r} = \binom{r+i}{i}$ by the symmetry condition Eq. (6). Then the summand becomes

$$
\binom{r+i}{i} \binom{t + s' - i}{t}.
$$

We now apply the addition formula Eq. (9) repeatedly to "move in" the $i$-summation. Consider the inner sum

$$
\sum_{i=0}^{s'} \binom{r+i}{i} \binom{t+s'-i}{t}.
$$

By the convolution property of binomial coefficients (known as Vandermonde's convolution)

$$
\sum_{i=0}^{s'} \binom{r+i}{r} \binom{t+s'-i}{t} = \binom{r+t+s'+1}{r+t+1}.
$$

This is precisely Eq. (26), and substituting $s' = (n-1)r + nt$ gives

$$
\sum_{i=0}^{(n-1)r+nt} \binom{r+i}{r} \binom{t+(n-1)r+nt - i}{t} = \binom{r+t + ((n-1)r+nt) +1}{r+t+1}.
$$

Combining terms in the numerator:

$$
r + t + (n-1)r + nt + 1 = nr + t + nt + 1 - r + r = nr + nt + t + 1 = n(r+t) + 1.
$$

Hence the sum evaluates to

$$
\binom{n(r+t)+1}{r+t+1}.
$$

This completes the proof.

∎
