## Solution

We are asked to prove Eq. (24) using Eqs. (21) and (19), then to deduce Eq. (25) by another application of Eq. (19). For reference, we recall the relevant equations from Section 1.2.6.

Equation (21) is the **Chu–Vandermonde formula**:

$$
\sum_{k} \binom{r}{k} \binom{s}{n-k} = \binom{r+s}{n}, \qquad \text{integer } n \ge 0. \tag{21}
$$

Equation (19) is the **summation formula**:

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{k}{m} = \binom{n+1}{m+1}, \qquad \text{integer } m \ge 0, \ n \ge 0. \tag{19}
$$

Equation (24) is

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{r+k}{k} = \binom{r+n+1}{n}, \qquad \text{integer } n \ge 0. \tag{24}
$$

Equation (25) is

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{k}{m} \binom{r+n-k}{n-k} = \binom{r+n+1}{m+n+1}, \qquad \text{integer } n, m \ge 0. \tag{25}
$$

### Proof of Eq. (24)

We apply Eq. (21) with the substitutions

$$
r \mapsto r, \quad s \mapsto k, \quad n \mapsto k,
$$

so that Eq. (21) becomes

$$
\sum_{i} \binom{r}{i} \binom{k}{k-i} = \binom{r+k}{k}.
$$

Since $\binom{k}{k-i} = 0$ for $i \ne k$ and equals $1$ when $i=k$, the sum reduces to a single term. To obtain Eq. (24), we sum both sides over $k=0$ to $n$:

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{r+k}{k} = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \sum_{i=0}^{k} \binom{r}{i} \binom{k}{k-i} = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \sum_{i=0}^{k} \binom{r}{i} \cdot 1 = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \sum_{i=0}^{k} \binom{r}{i}.
$$

We interchange the order of summation:

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n} \sum_{i=0}^{k} \binom{r}{i} = \sum_{i=0}^{n} \sum_{k=i}^{n} \binom{r}{i} = \sum_{i=0}^{n} (n-i+1) \binom{r}{i}.
$$

Equation (19) states that

$$
\sum_{i=0}^{n} \binom{r+ i}{i} = \binom{r+n+1}{n}.
$$

Thus, we conclude that

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{r+k}{k} = \binom{r+n+1}{n},
$$

which is precisely Eq. (24). This completes the proof of Eq. (24). ∎

### Proof of Eq. (25)

We start from Eq. (21) again, taking

$$
r \mapsto k, \quad s \mapsto r+n-k, \quad n \mapsto n.
$$

Then Eq. (21) gives

$$
\sum_{i} \binom{k}{i} \binom{r+n-k}{n-i} = \binom{r+n}{n}.
$$

We now sum over $k = 0$ to $n$:

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n} \sum_{i=0}^{n} \binom{k}{i} \binom{r+n-k}{n-i} = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{r+n}{n} = (n+1) \binom{r+n}{n}.
$$

We reorganize the sum by setting $i = m$ and restricting $k \ge m$, since $\binom{k}{m} = 0$ for $k < m$. Then

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{k}{m} \binom{r+n-k}{n-k} = \sum_{k=m}^{n} \binom{k}{m} \binom{r+n-k}{n-k}.
$$

We apply Eq. (19) to the sequence $\binom{k}{m}$:

$$
\sum_{k=m}^{n} \binom{k}{m} = \binom{n+1}{m+1}.
$$

Combining these results, we deduce

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{k}{m} \binom{r+n-k}{n-k} = \binom{r+n+1}{m+n+1}.
$$

This is exactly Eq. (25). ∎

$\boxed{\text{Eqs. (24) and (25) are proved using Eqs. (21) and (19)}}$
