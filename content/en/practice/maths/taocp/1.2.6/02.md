**Exercise 2.** [*00*] What is $0^0$?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 26s  

---

By definition, for any integer $k \ge 0$ we have

$$
x^0 = 1 \quad \text{for any } x \ne 0,
$$

and the binomial theorem (13) gives

$$
(x+y)^0 = \sum_{k} \binom{0}{k} x^k y^{0-k} = \binom{0}{0} x^0 y^0 = 1.
$$

Therefore, to maintain consistency with the binomial theorem and combinatorial formulas, it is convenient to define

$$
0^0 = 1.
$$

This completes the proof.

∎

$\boxed{0^0 = 1}$
