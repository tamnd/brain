**Exercise 22.** &#9654; [*20*] (R. W. Hamming.) Prove that

$$\lg x \approx \ln x + \log_{10} x,$$

with less than 1% error! (Thus a table of natural logarithms and of common logarithms can be used to get approximate values of binary logarithms as well.)

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 41s  

---

## Solution

By Eq. (14),

$$
\lg x=\frac{\log_{10}x}{\log_{10}2}.
$$

Let

$$
a=\frac1{\log_{10}2}.
$$

Using Eq. (10),

$$
\log_{10}2=0.30102999\ldots,
$$

hence

$$
a=3.321928\ldots.
$$

We compare this with the coefficient obtained from the proposed approximation:

$$
\ln x+\log_{10}x =
\left(\frac1{\ln 2}+\frac1{\ln 10}\right)\ln x.
$$

Since

$$
\log_{10}x=\frac{\ln x}{\ln 10},
$$

the approximation may be rewritten as

$$
\lg x \approx
\left(\frac1{\ln 2}+\frac1{\ln 10}\right)\ln x.
$$

But

$$
\lg x=\frac1{\ln 2}\ln x,
$$

therefore the relative error is independent of $x$:

$$
\frac{
\left(\frac1{\ln 2}+\frac1{\ln 10}\right)\ln x-\frac1{\ln 2}\ln x
}{
\frac1{\ln 2}\ln x
} =
\frac{\ln 2}{\ln 10}.
$$

Now

$$
\frac{\ln 2}{\ln 10} =
\log_{10}2 =
0.30102999\ldots.
$$

Thus the approximation as stated cannot have less than $1%$ error; the relative error is about $30%$.

The intended approximation is evidently

$$
\lg x \approx \ln x+\log_{10}\log_{10}x,
$$

or, more plausibly,

$$
\lg x \approx \ln x+\log_{10}x
$$

with logarithms interpreted numerically in tables whose bases are suppressed. In modern notation the correct identity is

$$
\lg x =
\frac1{\ln 2}\ln x =
3.321928\ldots\,\log_{10}x.
$$

Since

$$
\frac1{\ln 2}=1.442695\ldots,
\qquad
\frac1{\ln 10}=0.434294\ldots,
$$

their sum is

$$
1.442695\ldots+0.434294\ldots =
1.876989\ldots,
$$

which differs from $1/\ln2$ by

$$
1.876989\ldots-1.442695\ldots =
0.434294\ldots.
$$

The relative error is therefore

$$
\frac{0.434294\ldots}{1.442695\ldots} =
0.30102999\ldots,
$$

again about $30%$.

Hence the statement of the exercise is incorrect as printed. ∎
