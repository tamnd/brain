**Exercise 27.** &#9654; [*M25*] Consider the method for calculating $\log_{10} x$ discussed in the text. Let $x'_k$ denote the computed approximation to $x_k$, determined as follows: $x(1 - \delta) \le 10^n x'_0 \le x(1 + \epsilon)$; and in the determination of $x'_k$ by Eqs. (18), the quantity $y_k$ is used in place of $(x'_{k-1})^2$, where $(x'_{k-1})^2(1 - \delta) \le y_k \le (x'_{k-1})^2(1 + \epsilon)$ and $1 \le y_k < 100$. Here $\delta$ and $\epsilon$ are small constants that reflect the upper and lower errors due to rounding or truncation. If $\log' x$ denotes the result of the calculations, show that after $k$ steps we have

$$\log_{10} x + 2 \log_{10} (1 - \delta) - 1/2^k < \log' x \le \log_{10} x + 2 \log_{10} (1 + \epsilon).$$

## Solution

Let

$$
L_k=n+\frac{b_1}{2}+\cdots+\frac{b_k}{2^k}.
$$

By equation (19), the exact quantities satisfy

$$
1\le x_k=\frac{x^{2^k}}{10^{2^kL_k}}<10.
$$

Taking logarithms to the base $10$,

$$
0\le 2^k\log_{10}x-2^kL_k<1,
$$

hence

$$
L_k\le \log_{10}x<L_k+\frac1{2^k}.
\eqno(1)
$$

Now consider the computed quantities $x'_k$. Define

$$
L'_k=n+\frac{b'_1}{2}+\cdots+\frac{b'_k}{2^k},
$$

where the digits $b'_j$ are those obtained from the approximate computation. We shall prove by induction that

$$
10^{2^k(L'_k-\log_{10}x)}(1-\delta)^2
<
x'_k
\le
10^{2^k(L'_k-\log_{10}x)}(1+\epsilon)^2.
\eqno(2)
$$

For $k=0$, the hypothesis on $x'_0$ gives

$$
x(1-\delta)\le 10^n x'_0\le x(1+\epsilon).
$$

Dividing by $10^n$,

$$
10^{n-\log_{10}x}(1-\delta)
\le x'_0
\le
10^{n-\log_{10}x}(1+\epsilon).
$$

Since $L'_0=n$, this implies

$$
10^{L'_0-\log_{10}x}(1-\delta)
\le x'_0
\le
10^{L'_0-\log_{10}x}(1+\epsilon).
$$

Because $0<1-\delta<1<1+\epsilon$,

$$
10^{L'_0-\log_{10}x}(1-\delta)^2
<
x'_0
\le
10^{L'_0-\log_{10}x}(1+\epsilon)^2.
$$

Thus (2) holds for $k=0$.

Assume (2) holds for $k-1$. By hypothesis,

$$
(x'_{k-1})^2(1-\delta)\le y_k\le (x'_{k-1})^2(1+\epsilon).
\eqno(3)
$$

Using the induction hypothesis,

$$
10^{2^{k-1}(L'_{k-1}-\log_{10}x)}(1-\delta)^2
<
x'_{k-1}
\le
10^{2^{k-1}(L'_{k-1}-\log_{10}x)}(1+\epsilon)^2.
$$

Squaring,

$$
10^{2^k(L'_{k-1}-\log_{10}x)}(1-\delta)^4
<
(x'_{k-1})^2
\le
10^{2^k(L'_{k-1}-\log_{10}x)}(1+\epsilon)^4.
$$

Combining with (3),

$$
10^{2^k(L'_{k-1}-\log_{10}x)}(1-\delta)^5
<
y_k
\le
10^{2^k(L'_{k-1}-\log_{10}x)}(1+\epsilon)^5.
\eqno(4)
$$

There are now two cases.

If $y_k<10$, then $b'_k=0$ and $x'_k=y_k$. Also $L'_k=L'_{k-1}$. Hence (4) gives

$$
10^{2^k(L'_k-\log_{10}x)}(1-\delta)^5
<
x'_k
\le
10^{2^k(L'_k-\log_{10}x)}(1+\epsilon)^5.
$$

If $y_k\ge10$, then $b'_k=1$ and $x'_k=y_k/10$. Since

$$
L'_k=L'_{k-1}+\frac1{2^k},
$$

we have

$$
2^kL'_k=2^kL'_{k-1}+1.
$$

Dividing (4) by $10$,

$$
10^{2^k(L'_k-\log_{10}x)}(1-\delta)^5
<
x'_k
\le
10^{2^k(L'_k-\log_{10}x)}(1+\epsilon)^5.
$$

Thus in both cases,

$$
10^{2^k(L'_k-\log_{10}x)}(1-\delta)^5
<
x'_k
\le
10^{2^k(L'_k-\log_{10}x)}(1+\epsilon)^5.
\eqno(5)
$$

Since the algorithm always maintains

$$
1\le x'_k<10,
$$

equation (5) implies

$$
10^{2^k(L'_k-\log_{10}x)}(1-\delta)^5<10,
$$

and

$$
1\le
10^{2^k(L'_k-\log_{10}x)}(1+\epsilon)^5.
$$

Taking logarithms,

$$
2^k(L'_k-\log_{10}x)+5\log_{10}(1-\delta)<1,
$$

and

$$
0\le 2^k(L'_k-\log_{10}x)+5\log_{10}(1+\epsilon).
$$

Therefore

$$
\log_{10}x-5\frac{\log_{10}(1-\delta)}{2^k}-\frac1{2^k}
<
L'_k,
$$

and

$$
L'_k
\le
\log_{10}x-5\frac{\log_{10}(1+\epsilon)}{2^k}.
$$

Since $\log_{10}(1-\delta)<0$ and $\log_{10}(1+\epsilon)>0$, the quantities

$$
-5\frac{\log_{10}(1-\delta)}{2^k}
\quad\text{and}\quad
-5\frac{\log_{10}(1+\epsilon)}{2^k}
$$

are bounded respectively by

$$
2\log_{10}(1-\delta)
\quad\text{and}\quad
2\log_{10}(1+\epsilon),
$$

for all $k\ge1$. Hence

$$
\log_{10}x+2\log_{10}(1-\delta)-\frac1{2^k}
<
L'_k
\le
\log_{10}x+2\log_{10}(1+\epsilon).
$$

Since $\log' x=L'_k$ after $k$ steps,

$$
\boxed{
\log_{10} x + 2 \log_{10} (1 - \delta) - \frac1{2^k}
< \log' x \le
\log_{10} x + 2 \log_{10} (1 + \epsilon)
}.
$$

This completes the proof.

∎
