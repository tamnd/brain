**Exercise 11.** &#9654; [*10*] If $b = 10$ and $x \approx \log_{10} 2$, to how many decimal places of accuracy will we need to know the value of $x$ in order to determine the first three decimal places of the decimal expansion of $b^x$? [*Note:* You may use the result of exercise 10 in your discussion.]

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 35s  

---

We are asked to determine the precision required for $x \approx \log_{10} 2$ in order to compute the first three decimal places of $10^x$. Let $x_0 = \log_{10} 2$; then $10^{x_0} = 2$. Denote an approximation $x = x_0 + \varepsilon$, where $|\varepsilon|$ is the error in $x$. Then

$10^x = 10^{x_0 + \varepsilon} = 10^{x_0} \cdot 10^{\varepsilon} = 2 \cdot 10^{\varepsilon}.$

To determine the first three decimal places of $10^x$, we must have

$|10^x - 2| < 0.0005,$

so that rounding to three decimal places is correct. Since $10^\varepsilon \approx 1 + \varepsilon \ln 10$ for small $\varepsilon$, we have

$|10^x - 2| = 2 |10^{\varepsilon} - 1| \approx 2 |\varepsilon| \ln 10.$

Thus, we require

$2 |\varepsilon| \ln 10 < 0.0005 \implies |\varepsilon| < \frac{0.0005}{2 \ln 10} = \frac{0.00025}{\ln 10}.$

Using $\ln 10 \approx 2.302585$, we find

$|\varepsilon| < \frac{0.00025}{2.302585} \approx 0.0001085.$

Therefore, $x$ must be known to about five decimal places in order to determine the first three decimal places of $10^x$. Since $\log_{10} 2$ is not rational, exact determination is impossible, but an approximation to five decimal places suffices.

$\boxed{5 \text{ decimal places}}$
