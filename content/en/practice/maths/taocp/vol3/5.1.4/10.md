**Exercise 10.** [*M20*] [M20] How many permutations of the multiset $\{1^a, 2^b, 3^c\}$ have the property that, as we read the permutation from left to right, the number of 3‚Äôs never exceeds the number of 2‚Äôs, and the number of 2‚Äôs never exceeds the number of 1‚Äôs? (For example, $aabcabbcac$ is such a permutation.)

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m05s  

---

Let $w$ be a permutation of the multiset $\{1^a,2^b,3^c\}$ satisfying

$$
x_3(i)\le x_2(i)\le x_1(i)\quad \text{for all } i.
$$

Such words are exactly **Yamanouchi (lattice) words of type $(a,b,c)$**. We now give a correct derivation of their number.

## 1. Reduction to standard Young tableaux

Interpret the word $w$ as a sequence of insertions into three rows:

- place each $1$ in row 1,
- each $2$ in row 2,
- each $3$ in row 3,

recording after each prefix the row lengths

$$
(x_1(i),x_2(i),x_3(i)).
$$

The condition $x_3(i)\le x_2(i)\le x_1(i)$ ensures that at every prefix these row lengths form a Young diagram (a partition shape). Thus the word encodes a standard growth process of a Young diagram of final shape $(a,b,c)$.

Conversely, any **standard Young tableau (SYT)** of shape $(a,b,c)$ determines such a word by reading entries in increasing order of labels $1,2,3$. This is a standard bijection between Yamanouchi words and SYT of the same shape.

Hence the required number is

$$
f^{(a,b,c)} = \#\{\text{SYT of shape }(a,b,c)\}.
$$

We now compute this using the hook-length formula.

## 2. Hook-length computation

For a cell $(i,j)$, the hook length is

$$
h(i,j)=\text{(cells to the right)}+\text{(cells below)}+1.
$$

We compute row by row for the Ferrers diagram $(a,b,c)$, assuming $a\ge b\ge c$.

### Row 1

- For $1\le j\le c$:

$$
h_{1j}=a-j+3.
$$

- For $c<j\le b$:

$$
h_{1j}=a-j+2.
$$

- For $b<j\le a$:

$$
h_{1j}=a-j+1.
$$

Thus

$$
\prod_{j=1}^c (a-j+3)=\frac{(a+2)!}{(a-c+2)!},
$$

$$
\prod_{j=c+1}^b (a-j+2)=\frac{(a-c+1)!}{(a-b+1)!},
$$

$$
\prod_{j=b+1}^a (a-j+1)=(a-b)!.
$$

### Row 2

- For $1\le j\le c$:

$$
h_{2j}=b-j+2,
\quad \Rightarrow \quad \prod_{j=1}^c (b-j+2)=\frac{(b+1)!}{(b-c+1)!}.
$$

- For $c<j\le b$:

$$
h_{2j}=b-j+1,
\quad \Rightarrow \quad \prod_{j=c+1}^b (b-j+1)=(b-c)!.
$$

### Row 3

$$
\prod_{j=1}^c (c-j+1)=c!.
$$

## 3. Assemble the hook product

Multiply all contributions:

$$
H=
\frac{(a+2)!}{(a-c+2)!}
\cdot
\frac{(a-c+1)!}{(a-b+1)!}
\cdot (a-b)!
\cdot
\frac{(b+1)!}{(b-c+1)!}
\cdot (b-c)!
\cdot c!.
$$

Now simplify carefully.

### Step 1: simplify the $a$-row terms

$$
\frac{(a-c+1)!}{(a-c+2)!}=\frac{1}{a-c+2},
\quad
\frac{(a-b)!}{(a-b+1)!}=\frac{1}{a-b+1}.
$$

So the row 1 contribution becomes

$$
\frac{(a+2)!}{(a-c+2)(a-b+1)}.
$$

### Step 2: simplify the $b$-row terms

$$
\frac{(b-c)!}{(b-c+1)!}=\frac{1}{b-c+1}.
$$

Thus the row 2 and row 3 contributions give

$$
\frac{(b+1)!c!}{b-c+1}.
$$

### Final hook product

$$
H=\frac{(a+2)!(b+1)!c!}{(a-c+2)(a-b+1)(b-c+1)}.
$$

## 4. Apply hook-length formula

The hook-length formula gives

$$
f^{(a,b,c)}=\frac{(a+b+c)!}{H}.
$$

Substitute $H$:

$$
f^{(a,b,c)}
=
\frac{(a+b+c)!(a-c+2)(a-b+1)(b-c+1)}{(a+2)!(b+1)!c!}.
$$

## 5. Final answer

$$
\boxed{
\frac{(a+b+c)!\,(a-b+1)(b-c+1)(a-c+2)}{(a+2)!(b+1)!c!}
}
$$

This is the number of permutations of $\{1^a,2^b,3^c\}$ whose prefix counts satisfy $x_3(i)\le x_2(i)\le x_1(i)$ for all $i$.
