**Exercise 4.** [**] [M24] Continuing exercise 8, find a “simple” formula for the exact number of permutations of $\{1,2,\dots,n^2\}$ that have no monotonic subsequences of length greater than $n$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m17s  

---

We restart from the correct form of the Robinson–Schensted–Knuth (RSK) correspondence.

Let $a_1 a_2 \dots a_{n^2}$ be a permutation of $\{1,2,\dots,n^2\}$, and let $(P,Q)$ be the pair of standard Young tableaux obtained from RSK. Let $\lambda$ be the shape of $P$ (equivalently $Q$).

### 1. Correct RSK facts

For a permutation:

- The length of the longest increasing subsequence equals $\lambda_1$, the length of the first row of $P$.
- The length of the longest decreasing subsequence equals $\lambda'_1$, the length of the first column of $P$.

These are the correct structural invariants of RSK.

### 2. Translating the condition

The problem requires that no monotonic subsequence has length greater than $n$. Thus:

- Longest increasing subsequence $\le n$ implies $\lambda_1 \le n$.
- Longest decreasing subsequence $\le n$ implies $\lambda'_1 \le n$.

So the Young diagram $\lambda$ fits inside an $n \times n$ rectangle: it has at most $n$ rows and at most $n$ columns.

### 3. Using the size constraint

Because we are dealing with permutations of $\{1,2,\dots,n^2\}$, the total number of boxes in the Young diagram is fixed:

$$
|\lambda| = n^2.
$$

But any Ferrers diagram contained in an $n \times n$ box has at most $n^2$ cells, with equality only when every row has length $n$ and there are $n$ rows.

Hence the only possible shape is

$$
\lambda = (n^n),
$$

the full $n \times n$ rectangle.

### 4. Counting permutations via RSK

RSK gives a bijection between permutations and pairs $(P,Q)$ of standard Young tableaux of the same shape. Therefore the number of permutations satisfying the condition is

$$
(f^{(n^n)})^2,
$$

where $f^{(n^n)}$ is the number of standard Young tableaux of rectangular shape $(n^n)$.

### 5. Hook-length formula

For the square partition $(n^n)$, each cell $(i,j)$ has hook length

$$
h_{i,j} = (n-i) + (n-j) + 1 = 2n - i - j + 1.
$$

Thus

$$
f^{(n^n)} = \frac{(n^2)!}{\prod_{i=1}^{n}\prod_{j=1}^{n} (2n - i - j + 1)}.
$$

### 6. Final answer

Therefore, the required number of permutations is

$$
\boxed{
\left(\frac{(n^2)!}{\prod_{i=1}^{n}\prod_{j=1}^{n} (2n - i - j + 1)}\right)^2
}.
$$

This completes the corrected solution.
