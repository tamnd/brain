\end{array}$$

```
5 | 9 8) 9
```

For the first part, the previous solution is correct. We compute the Robinson-Schensted correspondence for the two-line array

$$
\begin{pmatrix}
1&2&3&4&5&6&7&8&9\\
6&4&9&5&7&1&2&8&3
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Since the top row is $1,2,\dots,9$, we insert the sequence

$$
6,4,9,5,7,1,2,8,3
$$

into $P$, and record in $Q$ the labels $1,\dots,9$ in the cells created by successive insertions.

Carrying out the row insertions gives

$$
P=
\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|}
\hline
1&2&3&8\\
\hline
4&5&7\\
\cline{1-3}
6&9\\
\cline{1-2}
\end{array},
$$

and the corresponding recording tableau is

$$
Q=
\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|}
\hline
1&3&5&8\\
\hline
2&4&9\\
\cline{1-3}
6&7\\
\cline{1-2}
\end{array}.
$$

Hence

$$
\boxed{
(P,Q)=
\left(
\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|}
\hline
1&2&3&8\\
\hline
4&5&7\\
\cline{1-3}
6&9\\
\cline{1-2}
\end{array},
\;
\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|}
\hline
1&3&5&8\\
\hline
2&4&9\\
\cline{1-3}
6&7\\
\cline{1-2}
\end{array}
\right).
}
$$

For the second part, we are given

$$
P=
\begin{array}{ccc}
1&4&7\\
2&8\\
5&9
\end{array},
\qquad
Q=
\begin{array}{ccc}
1&3&7\\
4&5\\
8&9
\end{array}.
$$

We must reconstruct the two-line array by the inverse Robinson-Schensted algorithm.

The entries of $Q$ are

$$
1,3,4,5,7,8,9,
$$

so these will form the first row of the array. We remove them from largest to smallest, carrying out inverse row insertion on $P$.

### Step 1: label $9$

The entry $9$ in $Q$ occupies cell $(3,2)$. The corresponding corner cell of $P$ contains $9$.

Remove this corner and reverse-bump.

Start with $x=9$.

In row $2$, the rightmost entry $<9$ is $8$. Replace $8$ by $9$ and set $x=8$.

In row $1$, the rightmost entry $<8$ is $7$. Replace $7$ by $8$ and set $x=7$.

The value ejected from the first row is therefore $7$.

Thus

$$
a_9=7.
$$

The tableau becomes

$$
P=
\begin{array}{ccc}
1&4&8\\
2&9\\
5
\end{array}.
$$

### Step 2: label $8$

The entry $8$ in $Q$ is at $(3,1)$. Remove the corner cell containing $5$.

Start with $x=5$.

In row $2$, the rightmost entry $<5$ is $2$. Replace $2$ by $5$ and set $x=2$.

In row $1$, the rightmost entry $<2$ is $1$. Replace $1$ by $2$ and set $x=1$.

Hence

$$
a_8=1.
$$

The tableau becomes

$$
P=
\begin{array}{ccc}
2&4&8\\
5&9
\end{array}.
$$

### Step 3: label $7$

The entry $7$ in $Q$ is at $(1,3)$. Remove the corner cell containing $8$.

Since this corner lies in the first row, no reverse bumping is needed.

Thus

$$
a_7=8.
$$

The tableau becomes

$$
P=
\begin{array}{cc}
2&4\\
5&9
\end{array}.
$$

### Step 4: label $5$

The entry $5$ in $Q$ is at $(2,2)$. Remove the corner cell containing $9$.

Start with $x=9$.

In row $1$, the rightmost entry $<9$ is $4$. Replace $4$ by $9$ and set $x=4$.

Therefore

$$
a_5=4.
$$

The tableau becomes

$$
P=
\begin{array}{cc}
2&9\\
5
\end{array}.
$$

### Step 5: label $4$

The entry $4$ in $Q$ is at $(2,1)$. Remove the corner cell containing $5$.

Start with $x=5$.

In row $1$, the rightmost entry $<5$ is $2$. Replace $2$ by $5$ and set $x=2$.

Hence

$$
a_4=2.
$$

The tableau becomes

$$
P=
\begin{array}{cc}
5&9
\end{array}.
$$

### Step 6: label $3$

The entry $3$ in $Q$ is at $(1,2)$. Remove the corner cell containing $9$.

Thus

$$
a_3=9.
$$

The tableau becomes

$$
P=
\begin{array}{c}
5
\end{array}.
$$

### Step 7: label $1$

The entry $1$ in $Q$ is at $(1,1)$. Remove the remaining cell.

Thus

$$
a_1=5.
$$

Collecting the recovered values:

$$
a_1=5,\qquad
a_3=9,\qquad
a_4=2,\qquad
a_5=4,\qquad
a_7=8,\qquad
a_8=1,\qquad
a_9=7.
$$

Therefore the corresponding two-line array is

$$
\boxed{
\begin{pmatrix}
1&3&4&5&7&8&9\\
5&9&2&4&8&1&7
\end{pmatrix}.
}
$$

This is the unique two-line array corresponding to the given pair $(P,Q)$ under Theorem A.
