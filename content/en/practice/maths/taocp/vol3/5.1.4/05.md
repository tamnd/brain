[Section 5.1.4: Tableaux and Involutions](../)

**Exercise 5.** [**] [M20] Prove that $P$ is a tableau when Algorithm S terminates, if it was a tableau initially.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 57s  

---

Let $P^{(0)}$ be the initial tableau and let Algorithm S perform a finite sequence of insertions of elements $x_1, x_2, \dots, x_k$, where each step consists of applying Algorithm I to the current tableau. Let $P^{(i)}$ denote the tableau obtained after inserting $x_i$.

The claim is that each $P^{(i)}$ is a tableau. For $i = 0$, $P^{(0)}$ satisfies the tableau inequalities $P_{ij} \leq_s P_{i,j+1}$ and $P_{ij} \leq_s P_{i+1,j}$ by hypothesis. Assume $P^{(i-1)}$ is a tableau. Algorithm I transforms $P^{(i-1)}$ by a bumping sequence in which each replacement $P_{r_j,c_j} \leftarrow x_j$ preserves the weak inequalities in the row and column directions, since each new entry is inserted as the least element exceeding the previous entry in its row and displaced entries are inserted into the next row at the first position compatible with $ \leq_s $ ordering. The argument establishing validity of Algorithm I in Section 5.1.4 shows that after termination of the insertion, all relations $P_{ij} \leq_s P_{i,j+1}$ and $P_{ij} \leq_s P_{i+1,j}$ remain satisfied, and the border conditions with $0$ and $\infty$ remain unchanged.

Hence $P^{(i)}$ is a tableau whenever $P^{(i-1)}$ is a tableau. By induction, $P^{(k)}$ is a tableau after the termination of Algorithm S.

This completes the proof. ∎
