**Exercise 2.** [**] [M20] (C. Schensted.) Let $P$ be the tableau corresponding to the permutation $a_1 a_2 \dots a_n$. Prove that the number of columns in $P$ is the longest length $c$ of an increasing subsequence $a_{i_1} < a_{i_2} < \dots < a_{i_c}$, where $i_1 < i_2 < \dots < i_c$; the number of rows in $P$ is the longest length $r$ of a decreasing subsequence $a_{j_1} > a_{j_2} > \dots > a_{j_r}$, where $j_1 < j_2 < \dots < j_r$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 56s  

---

Let $c$ be the number of columns of $P$. During the construction of Theorem A, an element $a_i$ is placed in column $t$ of row $1$ precisely when $(i,a_i)$ is in class $t$. By the discussion preceding Theorem B, class $1$ consists of the left-to-right minima; after removing these, class $2$ consists of the left-to-right minima of the remaining array; and so on. Hence the permutation is partitioned into $c$ classes, each class forming a decreasing sequence when read from left to right, by (18). Therefore every increasing subsequence can contain at most one element from each class, so its length is at most $c$.

Conversely, if $a_i$ lies in class $t$, then there exist elements of classes $1,2,\dots,t-1$ occurring earlier than $a_i$ and smaller than $a_i$; otherwise $a_i$ would have belonged to a smaller class. Choosing such predecessors successively yields an increasing subsequence of length $t$ ending at $a_i$. Taking $t=c$, there is an increasing subsequence of length $c$. Hence the longest increasing subsequence has length exactly $c$, which is the number of columns of $P$.

Apply the same result to the inverse permutation. By Theorem B, the tableaux corresponding to the inverse permutation are $(Q,P)$. The number of columns of $Q$ equals the number of rows of $P$. An increasing subsequence of the inverse permutation corresponds exactly to a decreasing subsequence of the original permutation. Therefore the maximum length $r$ of a decreasing subsequence of $a_1a_2\dots a_n$ is equal to the number of columns of $Q$, hence to the number of rows of $P$. This completes the proof.
