**Exercise 2.** [**] [M20] In the classical problem of Josephus (exercise 1.3.2, 22), $n$ men are initially arranged in a circle; the $m$th man is executed, the circle closes, and every $m$th man is repeatedly eliminated until all are dead. The resulting execution order is a permutation

of {1,2,...,n}. For example, when $n = 8$ and $m = 4$ the order is 54613872 (man 1 is 5th out, etc.); the inversion table corresponding to this permutation is 36310010.

Give a simple recurrence relation for the elements $b_1, b_2, \dots, b_n$ of the inversion table in the general Josephus problem for $n$ men, when every $m$th man is executed.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m28s  

---

Let $a_1,a_2,\dots,a_n$ be the Josephus execution permutation. Let $x_1,\dots,x_n$ be defined by $x_k=a_k$. Let $s_k$ be the size of the remaining circle after $k-1$ eliminations, so $s_1=n$ and $s_{k+1}=s_k-1$. Let $r_k$ be the position eliminated at step $k$. Then

$r_1 \equiv m \pmod{n}, \quad 1 \le r_1 \le n,$

and for $k \ge 2$,

$r_k \equiv r_{k-1}+m-1 \pmod{s_k}, \quad 1 \le r_k \le s_k.$

The label removed at step $k$ is $a_k$, obtained by reading the current circle in cyclic order and taking the $r_k$th remaining element.

For the inversion table $b_1,b_2,\dots,b_n$, initialization is $b_j=0$ for $1 \le j \le n$. After the $k$th removal, the value $b_{a_k}$ is increased by the number of earlier removed elements that are larger than $a_k$, that is,

$b_{a_k} \leftarrow b_{a_k} + \left|\{\,i \mid 1 \le i < k,\ a_i > a_k\,\}\right|.$

This recurrence determines $b_j$ for all $j$, since each $j$ appears exactly once as some $a_k$, and the increment at step $k$ depends only on previously determined $a_i$ with $i<k$. This completes the construction. ∎
