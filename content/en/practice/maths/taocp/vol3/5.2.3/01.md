[Section 5.2.3: Sorting by Selection](../)

**Exercise 1.** [**] [M25] Step S3 of Algorithm S accomplishes nothing when i = j; is it a good idea to test whether or not i = j before doing step S3? What is the average number of times the condition i = j will occur in step S3 for random input?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m01s  

---

The condition $i=j$ holds in step S3 precisely when the search in step S2 never finds a key larger than the initial value $K_j$. Therefore $K_j$ must be the maximum of the first $j$ keys. For random input, each of the first $j$ positions is equally likely to contain this maximum, so

$$
\Pr(i=j)=\frac1j.
$$

Since step S3 is executed for $j=N,N-1,\ldots,2$, the average number of occurrences of $i=j$ is

$$
\sum_{j=2}^{N}\frac1j=H_N-1.
$$

Testing whether $i=j$ before step S3 would avoid the three assignments of the exchange only on these $H_N-1$ occasions, but it would require an additional test during every one of the $N-1$ iterations. Since $H_N-1=O(\log N)$ while the extra test is performed $N-1$ times, the saving is far too small to compensate for the added overhead. Hence such a test is not a good idea. The average number of times that $i=j$ occurs is

$$
\boxed{H_N-1.}
$$
