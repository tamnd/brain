**Exercise 9.** [**] [M20] How many times will −∞ be compared with −∞ when the bottom-up method of Fig. 23 is used to sort a file of 2ⁿ elements into order?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 1m20s  

---

Consider the complete binary tree with $2^n$ terminal nodes used in Fig. 23. Each internal node performs exactly one comparison whenever its two children have both become $-\infty$ for the first time during the execution of the bottom-up selection process.

For a fixed internal node $v$, let $T_v$ denote the set of terminal nodes in its subtree. Initially, $T_v$ contains at least one finite key. The operation that eventually makes all elements of $T_v$ equal to $-\infty$ occurs precisely when the last finite key in $T_v$ is selected and replaced by $-\infty$. At that moment, the update path from the corresponding leaf to the root passes through $v$. At $v$, both children subtrees already contain only $-\infty$, since otherwise a finite key in one child subtree would still be eligible to move upward. The comparison performed at $v$ during this update is therefore between $-\infty$ and $-\infty$, and it occurs exactly once for this node. After this moment, no further update path enters $v$, since no finite keys remain in $T_v$.

Every comparison of $-\infty$ with $-\infty$ arises in this way from a unique internal node, since such a comparison can occur only at the moment when a subtree becomes entirely $-\infty$, and this event is uniquely associated with its root internal node. Conversely, every internal node contributes exactly one such comparison.

A complete binary tree with $2^n$ terminal nodes has $2^n - 1$ internal nodes. Therefore the total number of comparisons of $-\infty$ with $-\infty$ equals the number of internal nodes.

Hence the required number is

$\boxed{2^n - 1}.$
