**Exercise 4.** [**] [M41] Find the variance of the quantity B in Program S, as a function of N, assuming random input.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 3m18s

### 1. Where the previous solution fails

The entire argument based on $C_k$ is unnecessary and introduces false probabilistic structure. The key structural error is that it replaces a simple record process with a dependent “waiting time” model, leading to invented constants and incorrect scaling.

The correct approach uses the standard fact about record indicators, which was never used.

### 2. Correct structural starting point

Let $K_1,\dots,K_N$ be a random permutation of $\{1,\dots,N\}$.

Define the indicator of a record at position $j$:

$$
I_j = \mathbf{1}\{K_j = \max(K_1,\dots,K_j)\}.
$$

Then

$$
R_j = \sum_{i=1}^j I_i.
$$

So the quantity

$$
S := \sum_{j=1}^N R_j
$$

becomes

$$
S = \sum_{j=1}^N \sum_{i=1}^j I_i.
$$

Swap summations:

$$
S = \sum_{i=1}^N (N-i+1)\, I_i.
$$

Since $B = S - N$, and constants do not affect variance,

$$
\mathrm{Var}(B) = \mathrm{Var}(S).
$$

### 3. Crucial correction: independence structure

A standard theorem for random permutations:

- The variables $I_1, \dots, I_N$ are independent.
- $\mathbb{P}(I_j = 1) = \frac{1}{j}$.

This invalidates all covariance and $C_k$ analysis in the previous solution.

### 4. Exact variance computation

Using independence:

$$
\mathrm{Var}(S)
= \sum_{j=1}^N (N-j+1)^2 \, \mathrm{Var}(I_j).
$$

Since $I_j$ is Bernoulli$(1/j)$,

$$
\mathrm{Var}(I_j) = \frac{1}{j}\left(1 - \frac{1}{j}\right).
$$

Hence

$$
\mathrm{Var}(B)
=
\sum_{j=1}^N (N-j+1)^2 \left(\frac{1}{j} - \frac{1}{j^2}\right).
$$

This is an exact expression.

### 5. Asymptotic evaluation

Split into two sums:

$$
\mathrm{Var}(B) = A - B
$$

where

$$
A = \sum_{j=1}^N \frac{(N-j+1)^2}{j}, 
\qquad
B = \sum_{j=1}^N \frac{(N-j+1)^2}{j^2}.
$$

#### 5.1 Leading term from $A$

Expand:

$$
(N-j+1)^2 = N^2 - 2N(j-1) + (j-1)^2.
$$

Then

$$
A = N^2 \sum_{j=1}^N \frac{1}{j}
- 2N \sum_{j=1}^N \frac{j-1}{j}
+ \sum_{j=1}^N \frac{(j-1)^2}{j}.
$$

Compute each part:

- $\sum_{j=1}^N \frac{1}{j} = H_N$
- $\sum_{j=1}^N \frac{j-1}{j} = N - H_N$
- $\sum_{j=1}^N \frac{(j-1)^2}{j} = \sum (j - 2 + 1/j) = \frac{N(N+1)}{2} - 2N + H_N$

Thus:

$$
A = N^2 H_N - 2N(N - H_N) + \frac{N(N+1)}{2} - 2N + H_N.
$$

So the leading term is:

$$
A = N^2 H_N + O(N^2).
$$

#### 5.2 Lower-order term $B$

$$
B = \sum_{j=1}^N \frac{(N-j+1)^2}{j^2}
\le N^2 \sum_{j=1}^N \frac{1}{j^2}
= O(N^2).
$$

### 6. Final variance

Combine:

$$
\mathrm{Var}(B)
= N^2 H_N + O(N^2).
$$

Using

$$
H_N = \log N + \gamma + O\!\left(\frac{1}{N}\right),
$$

we obtain:

$$
\boxed{
\mathrm{Var}(B)
= N^2 \log N + \gamma N^2 + O(N^2)
}.
$$

More compactly:

$$
\boxed{
\mathrm{Var}(B) = \Theta(N^2 \log N)
}.
$$

### 7. Resolution of the reviewer’s points

- The incorrect $N^3$ scaling: resolved by correct linear representation of $S$, eliminating spurious $C_k$ aggregation.
- The unjustified $\pi^2/18$: disappears entirely since no zeta-2 term arises.
- Expectation and rank conditioning: unnecessary and removed.
- Independence issues: corrected via the standard independence of record indicators.
- Covariances: exactly zero due to independence, not heuristic $O(N^2 \log N)$.

### Final result

$$
\boxed{\mathrm{Var}(B)
= \sum_{j=1}^N (N-j+1)^2\left(\frac{1}{j} - \frac{1}{j^2}\right)
= N^2 \log N + O(N^2)}
$$
