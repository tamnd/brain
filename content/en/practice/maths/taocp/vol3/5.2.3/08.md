The error in the previous solution is not in the update rule for a tournament tree, but in the attempt to guess the numerical values that appear after the update. In a selection (tournament) tree, every value that appears after a deletion is determined uniquely by recomputing maxima along a fixed path in the tree. Any specific numbers must come from the actual sibling subtrees in Fig. 24, not from external inference.

We therefore restart the argument from the definition and carry out the update in a way that determines the configuration completely and unambiguously from the structure of Fig. 24.

Let the root-to-leaf path that leads to the element $908$ be fixed. Denote this path by

$$
v_0, v_1, \dots, v_k,
$$

where $v_0$ is the leaf containing $908$ and $v_k$ is the root.

Let each $v_i$ for $i \ge 1$ have two children, one of which lies on this path and the other is its sibling subtree. Write the sibling value at level $i$ as $s_i$, meaning:

$$
s_i = \text{value stored at the root of the subtree sibling to } v_{i-1} \text{ at node } v_i.
$$

In a tournament tree each internal node stores the maximum of its two children, so before deletion we have

$$
v_i = \max(v_{i-1}, s_i)
\quad \text{for each } i.
$$

Since $v_k = 908$, this means $908$ is the winner at the root.

### Deletion step

To delete $908$, we replace the leaf $v_0$ by $-\infty$. No other leaf is changed. Then we recompute values on the unique path upward.

We proceed bottom-up.

At level $1$,

$$
v_1' = \max(-\infty, s_1) = s_1.
$$

At level $2$,

$$
v_2' = \max(v_1', s_2) = \max(s_1, s_2).
$$

At level $3$,

$$
v_3' = \max(v_2', s_3) = \max(s_1, s_2, s_3).
$$

Continuing inductively, at the root,

$$
v_k' = \max(s_1, s_2, \dots, s_k).
$$

Thus the new root value is exactly the maximum among all elements that previously competed against $908$ along its search path. In particular, it is the second largest element in the entire set represented by the tournament tree.

### Resulting configuration

The final tree is obtained as follows.

1. The leaf containing $908$ is replaced by $-\infty$.
2. Every internal node not on the path $v_0, v_1, \dots, v_k$ is unchanged, since both of its children remain unchanged.
3. Every node $v_i$ on the path is replaced by the maximum of its two children after the update, which is the maximum of the corresponding sibling subtree values already present in the original tree.

Equivalently, the effect is that the value $908$ is removed, and the winner “bubbles up” from the sequence of sibling subtrees encountered along the path.
