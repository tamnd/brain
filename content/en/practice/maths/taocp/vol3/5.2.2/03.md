[Section 5.2.2: Sorting by Exchanging](../)

**Exercise 3.** [**] [10] Is the bubble sort Algorithm B a stable sorting algorithm?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m34s  

---

Yes. Algorithm B is stable.

The only operation that changes the relative order of records is the interchange in step B3, and that interchange is performed only when $K_j > K_{j+1}$. If two adjacent records have equal keys, the condition fails, so they are never exchanged. Since every change in the order of records is a sequence of adjacent interchanges, and no adjacent interchange ever reverses the order of equal keys, the relative order of any two records having equal keys is preserved throughout the algorithm. Hence the output order of equal keys is the same as their input order.

$$
\boxed{\text{Algorithm B is stable.}}
$$
