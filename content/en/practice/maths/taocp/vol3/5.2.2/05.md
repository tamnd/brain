[Section 5.2.2: Sorting by Exchanging](../)

**Exercise 5.** [**] [M25] Let $b_1 b_2 \dots b_n$ be the inversion table for the permutation $a_1 a_2 \dots a_n$. Show that the value of `BOUND` after $r$ passes of the bubble sort is

$$\max \{b_i + r \mid b_i > r\} - r, \quad \text{for } 0 < r < \max(b_1, \dots, b_n).$$

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 2m16s  

---

The exercise, as printed, cannot be proved because the stated formula is false.

Indeed,

$$
\max\{b_i+r\mid b_i>r\}-r
=\max\{b_i\mid b_i>r\},
$$

which depends only on the inversion-table values and is independent of the indices $i$. However, the value of $\mathrm{BOUND}$ depends on the positions of the remaining inversions, not merely on their sizes. This contradicts the previously established characterization of $\mathrm{BOUND}$ in terms of the inversion table.

The correct statement is

$$
\boxed{\;
\mathrm{BOUND}
=
\max\{\,b_i+i\mid b_i>r\,\}-r,
\qquad
0<r<\max(b_1,\ldots,b_n).
\;}
$$

This is the formula proved below.

**Proof.**

Let $b_1,\ldots,b_n$ be the inversion table of the initial permutation.

A previously established property of bubble sort states that one pass decreases every positive inversion-table entry by $1$, while zero entries remain zero. Therefore, after $r$ passes,

$$
b_i^{(r)}=\max(b_i-r,0),
$$

where $b_i^{(r)}$ denotes the inversion table after the first $r$ passes.

Another previously established result, Equation (5), states that if $c_1,\ldots,c_n$ is the inversion table at the beginning of a pass, then

$$
\mathrm{BOUND}
=
\max\{\,c_i+i\mid c_i>0\,\}.
$$

Applying this to the inversion table after $r$ passes gives

$$
\mathrm{BOUND}
=
\max\{\,b_i^{(r)}+i\mid b_i^{(r)}>0\,\}.
$$

Now substitute the expression for $b_i^{(r)}$. Since

$$
b_i^{(r)}>0
\iff
\max(b_i-r,0)>0
\iff
b_i>r,
$$

we obtain

$$
\mathrm{BOUND}
=
\max\{(b_i-r)+i\mid b_i>r\}.
$$

Because the quantity $-r$ is independent of the index $i$, it may be factored outside the maximum:

$$
\mathrm{BOUND}
=
\max\{\,b_i+i\mid b_i>r\,\}-r.
$$

This is exactly the desired identity for the corrected statement. ∎

**Remark.** The printed formula

$$
\max\{b_i+r\mid b_i>r\}-r
$$

simplifies to

$$
\max\{b_i\mid b_i>r\},
$$

which cannot equal $\mathrm{BOUND}$ in general because it omits the index $i$. Thus the appearance of $r$ in place of $i$ is a typographical error in the exercise statement.
