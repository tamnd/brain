[Section 5.1.2: Permutations of a Multiset](../)

**Exercise 1.** [**] [M05] True or false: Let $M_1$ and $M_2$ be multisets. If $\alpha$ is a permutation of $M_1$ and $\beta$ is a permutation of $M_2$, then $\alpha \mathbin{\text{?}} \beta$ is a permutation of $M_1 \cup M_2$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m24s  

---

True.

Let $\alpha$ be a permutation of $M_1$ and $\beta$ be a permutation of $M_2$. Each element of $M_1$ occurs in $\alpha$ with exactly its multiplicity in $M_1$, and each element of $M_2$ occurs in $\beta$ with exactly its multiplicity in $M_2$. The operation $\alpha \mathbin{\text{?}} \beta$ forms a permutation whose multiset of entries is the disjoint union of the entries of $\alpha$ and $\beta$, hence each element occurs with multiplicity equal to the sum of its multiplicities in $M_1$ and $M_2$, which is precisely its multiplicity in $M_1 \cup M_2$. Therefore $\alpha \mathbin{\text{?}} \beta$ is a permutation of $M_1 \cup M_2$. ∎
