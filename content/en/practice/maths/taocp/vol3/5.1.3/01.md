We derive Euler’s formula for connected planar graphs:

$$
V - E + F = 2,
$$

where $V$ is the number of vertices, $E$ the number of edges, and $F$ the number of faces in a fixed planar embedding (including the unbounded outer face).

## Definitions and setup

Let $G$ be a connected planar graph together with a fixed embedding in the plane.

- A **face** is a connected component of the complement of the drawing of $G$ in the plane.
- $F$ counts all faces, including the unbounded exterior face.
- We assume $G$ is finite and connected.

We prove that $V - E + F = 2$ for every such $G$.

## Step 1: Base case (trees)

First consider the case where $G$ is a tree.

A tree with $V$ vertices has

$$
E = V - 1.
$$

In any planar embedding of a tree, the graph contains no cycles, so the complement of the drawing is connected. Hence there is exactly one face:

$$
F = 1.
$$

Therefore,

$$
V - E + F = V - (V - 1) + 1 = 2.
$$

So Euler’s formula holds for all connected planar trees.

## Step 2: Edge addition argument

Now take a connected planar graph $G$. We show how it can be reduced to a tree while preserving the quantity $V - E + F$.

If $G$ is not a tree, it contains an edge that lies on a cycle. Such an edge is not a bridge.

Remove such an edge $e$.

- The number of vertices $V$ does not change.
- The number of edges decreases by 1:

$$
E \mapsto E - 1.
$$
- In a planar embedding, removing an edge on a cycle merges two adjacent faces into one, so:

$$
F \mapsto F - 1.
$$

Thus,

$$
V - (E - 1) + (F - 1) = V - E + F.
$$

So the quantity $V - E + F$ is invariant under deletion of cycle edges.

## Step 3: Reduction to a tree

Repeatedly delete edges lying on cycles. Since $G$ is finite and connected, this process eventually yields a spanning tree $T$.

Throughout the process, the value $V - E + F$ remains unchanged. Therefore,

$$
V - E + F = V_T - E_T + F_T.
$$

## Step 4: Evaluate at the tree

From Step 1, for the spanning tree $T$,

$$
V_T - E_T + F_T = 2.
$$

Hence the same value holds for the original graph $G$.

## Conclusion

For every connected planar graph,

$$
V - E + F = 2,
$$

which is Euler’s formula.
