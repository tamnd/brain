**Exercise 11.** [*M40*] [M40] Let $H_k(z) = \sum_m p_{km} z^m$, where $p_{km}$ is the probability that $m$ is the length of the $k$th run in a random (infinite) sequence. Express $H_1(z)$, $H_2(z)$, and the super generating function

$$
H(z,x) = \sum_k H_k(z)x^k
$$

in terms of familiar functions.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 4m53s  

---

Restart from the underlying probabilistic model of the exercise.

## 1. Model of the random infinite sequence

Consider an infinite sequence of independent fair coin flips

$$
X_1, X_2, X_3, \dots,
\qquad \Pr(X_i=0)=\Pr(X_i=1)=\tfrac12.
$$

A **run** is a maximal block of consecutive equal symbols. Let $L_k$ be the length of the $k$-th run, and define

$$
H_k(z)=\mathbb{E}[z^{L_k}]=\sum_{m\ge 1} p_{km} z^m.
$$

We derive the distribution of $L_k$ directly from the sequence model.

## 2. Distribution of the first run $L_1$

Condition on the first symbol. By symmetry it is $0$ or $1$ with probability $1/2$, and the run structure is identical in either case.

Assume $X_1=0$. Then $L_1=m$ occurs exactly when:

- $X_1=X_2=\cdots=X_m=0$,
- $X_{m+1}=1$.

Thus,

$$
\Pr(L_1=m \mid X_1=0)=\left(\tfrac12\right)^m \cdot \tfrac12 = 2^{-(m+1)}.
$$

The same probability holds conditioned on $X_1=1$. Hence

$$
\Pr(L_1=m)=2 \cdot 2^{-(m+1)}=2^{-m}, \qquad m\ge 1.
$$

Therefore the first-run distribution is geometric:

$$
p_{1m}=2^{-m}.
$$

## 3. Distribution of $L_k$ for general $k$

After each run ends, a symbol change occurs. At the start of the next run:

- the next symbol is the opposite of the previous one,
- but the future evolution from that point is independent of the past (independence of the coin flips).

Thus, conditional on the start of any run, the process “restarts” with the same distributional rules as at time $1$. Therefore:

$$
L_k \overset{d}{=} L_1 \quad \text{for all } k\ge 1.
$$

Moreover, run lengths are independent because they are determined by disjoint blocks of independent coin flips.

Hence:

$$
p_{km}=2^{-m} \quad \text{for all } k\ge 1.
$$

## 4. Computation of $H_1(z)$

$$
H_1(z)=\sum_{m\ge 1} 2^{-m} z^m
= \sum_{m\ge 1} \left(\frac{z}{2}\right)^m.
$$

This is a geometric series, so for $|z|<2$,

$$
H_1(z)=\frac{\frac{z}{2}}{1-\frac{z}{2}}=\frac{z}{2-z}.
$$

## 5. Computation of $H_2(z)$

Since $L_2 \overset{d}{=} L_1$,

$$
H_2(z)=H_1(z)=\frac{z}{2-z}.
$$

## 6. Super generating function $H(z,x)$

By definition,

$$
H(z,x)=\sum_{k\ge 1} H_k(z)x^k.
$$

Since $H_k(z)$ is independent of $k$,

$$
H(z,x)=H_1(z)\sum_{k\ge 1} x^k.
$$

For $|x|<1$,

$$
\sum_{k\ge 1} x^k=\frac{x}{1-x}.
$$

Thus,

$$
H(z,x)=\frac{z}{2-z}\cdot \frac{x}{1-x}.
$$

## Final answers

$$
H_1(z)=\frac{z}{2-z}, \qquad H_2(z)=\frac{z}{2-z},
$$

$$
H(z,x)=\frac{x}{1-x}\cdot \frac{z}{2-z}.
$$

## 7. Justification summary

- The run-length distribution is derived directly from independence of coin flips, not assumed.
- The geometric law $2^{-m}$ comes from “no change for $m-1$ steps, then a change”.
- Independence and identical distribution of runs follow from the renewal property of i.i.d. sequences after each change point.
- All generating functions are then straightforward consequences of geometric series identities.
