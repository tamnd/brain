**Exercise 5.** [*M20*] [M20] Deduce the value of $(p!) \bmod p$ when $p$ is prime.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m08s  

---

## Solution

Let $p$ be a prime with $p \ge 2$. Write

$$
p! = 1 \cdot 2 \cdots (p-1)\cdot p.
$$

The factor $p$ divides this product, so there exists an integer $Q$ such that

$$
p! = pQ,
$$

where $Q = (p-1)!$.

By definition of congruence modulo $p$, this implies

$$
p! \equiv 0 \pmod{p}.
$$

Thus,

$$
(p!) \bmod p = 0.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
