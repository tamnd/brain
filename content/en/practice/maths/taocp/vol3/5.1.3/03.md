**Exercise 3.** [**] [HM25] Evaluate the sum $\sum_{k} \binom{n}{k} (-1)^k$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m12s  

---

The binomial theorem gives

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k}(-1)^k = (1-1)^n.
$$

For $n \ge 1$, this equals $0$, since $(1-1)^n = 0$. For $n = 0$, the sum reduces to $\binom{0}{0} = 1$, hence the value is $1$.

Thus the sum is $0$ for $n \ge 1$, and $1$ for $n = 0$. ∎
