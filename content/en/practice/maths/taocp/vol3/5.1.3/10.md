**Exercise 10.** [*HM30*] [HM30] Find the asymptotic behavior of the mean and variance of the distributions $h_k(z)$ in the preceding exercise, for large $k$.

We work in the standard TAOCP model of an infinite random permutation obtained by taking a uniform random permutation of $\{1,2,\dots,n\}$ and letting $n\to\infty$, or equivalently by assigning i.i.d. continuous labels and ordering them. Runs are maximal increasing consecutive segments.

Let $L_1, L_2, \dots$ be successive run lengths and $T_k=L_1+\cdots+L_k$.

## 1. Structure of successive runs (correct justification)

The key point is to justify that the run lengths are i.i.d. with

$$
\mathbb{P}(L_1=m)=\frac{1}{m!}-\frac{1}{(m+1)!}.
$$

### First run distribution

As in the preceding exercise, the first run has length $m$ exactly when the first $m$ entries are increasing and the $(m+1)$st entry breaks the increase. This gives

$$
\mathbb{P}(L_1=m)=\frac{1}{m!}-\frac{1}{(m+1)!}, \quad m\ge 1.
$$

### Independence and identical distribution

Fix $m\ge 1$. Condition on the event $L_1=m$. Then:

- The first $m$ entries are in increasing order.
- The $(m+1)$st entry is smaller than the $m$th entry, so a descent occurs at position $m$.

Now use the symmetry of random permutations:

Given any fixed set of values occupying the first $m$ positions, their relative order is uniform over all $m!$ possibilities. Conditioning on them being increasing selects exactly one of these possibilities, but does not affect the distribution of the remaining entries except for relabeling.

More importantly, after revealing the first $m$ values and their order, the remaining sequence consists of the unused values in a uniformly random order. This is a standard closure property of random permutations: removing a subset of positions and conditioning on their relative order leaves a uniform random permutation on the remaining symbols.

Hence the suffix after position $m$ is independent of $L_1$ and has the same distributional structure as the original permutation.

Therefore:

- $L_2$ has the same distribution as $L_1$,
- the process regenerates at each run boundary,
- $(L_i)_{i\ge 1}$ are independent and identically distributed.

Thus $T_k$ is a sum of i.i.d. variables with the stated law.

## 2. Mean and second moment of one run

Let

$$
p_m=\frac{1}{m!}-\frac{1}{(m+1)!}.
$$

### Mean

$$
\mu=\sum_{m\ge 1} m p_m.
$$

Split:

$$
\mu=\sum_{m\ge 1}\frac{m}{m!}-\sum_{m\ge 1}\frac{m}{(m+1)!}.
$$

First term:

$$
\sum_{m\ge 1}\frac{m}{m!}=\sum_{m\ge 1}\frac{1}{(m-1)!}=e.
$$

Second term (shift $j=m+1$):

$$
\sum_{m\ge 1}\frac{m}{(m+1)!}
=\sum_{j\ge 2}\frac{j-1}{j!}
=\sum_{j\ge 2}\frac{j}{j!}-\sum_{j\ge 2}\frac{1}{j!}.
$$

Compute:

$$
\sum_{j\ge 2}\frac{j}{j!}=e-1,\qquad \sum_{j\ge 2}\frac{1}{j!}=e-2,
$$

so the second term equals $1$. Hence

$$
\mu=e-1.
$$

### Second moment

$$
\mathbb{E}[L_1^2]=\sum_{m\ge 1} m^2 p_m
=\sum_{m\ge 1}\frac{m^2}{m!}-\sum_{m\ge 1}\frac{m^2}{(m+1)!}.
$$

First sum:

$$
\sum_{m\ge 1}\frac{m^2}{m!}
=\sum_{m\ge 1}\frac{m(m-1)}{m!}+\sum_{m\ge 1}\frac{m}{m!}
= e + e = 2e.
$$

Second sum (shift $j=m+1$):

$$
\sum_{m\ge 1}\frac{m^2}{(m+1)!}
=\sum_{j\ge 2}\frac{(j-1)^2}{j!}.
$$

Expand:

$$
(j-1)^2=j^2-2j+1.
$$

Thus

$$
\sum_{j\ge 2}\frac{(j-1)^2}{j!}
=\sum_{j\ge 2}\frac{j^2}{j!}
-2\sum_{j\ge 2}\frac{j}{j!}
+\sum_{j\ge 2}\frac{1}{j!}.
$$

Use standard exponential sums:

$$
\sum_{j\ge 2}\frac{j^2}{j!}=2e-1,\quad
\sum_{j\ge 2}\frac{j}{j!}=e-1,\quad
\sum_{j\ge 2}\frac{1}{j!}=e-2.
$$

Hence the second sum equals $e-1$. Therefore

$$
\mathbb{E}[L_1^2]=2e-(e-1)=e+1.
$$

### Variance

$$
\sigma^2=\mathrm{Var}(L_1)=\mathbb{E}[L_1^2]-\mu^2
=(e+1)-(e-1)^2.
$$

So

$$
\sigma^2=e+1-(e^2-2e+1)=e(3-e).
$$

## 3. Mean and variance of $T_k$

Since $L_1,L_2,\dots$ are i.i.d.,

$$
\mathbb{E}[T_k]=\sum_{i=1}^k \mathbb{E}[L_i]=k\mu,
\qquad
\mathrm{Var}(T_k)=\sum_{i=1}^k \mathrm{Var}(L_i)=k\sigma^2.
$$

No covariance terms appear because of independence.

Substituting constants,

$$
\mathbb{E}[T_k]=k(e-1),\qquad
\mathrm{Var}(T_k)=k\,e(3-e).
$$

## 4. Asymptotic form

These are exact linear expressions, hence as $k\to\infty$,

$$
\mathbb{E}[T_k]\sim k(e-1),\qquad
\mathrm{Var}(T_k)\sim k\,e(3-e).
$$

## Final result

$$
\boxed{\mathbb{E}[T_k]=k(e-1), \qquad \mathrm{Var}(T_k)=k\,e(3-e),}
$$

and these give the required asymptotic behavior.
