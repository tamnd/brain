**Exercise 4.** [**] [M20] Find the minimum and maximum running times for Program S, as a function of N.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 5m47s

We correct the argument from the ground up by working directly with the objective

$$
T = 9B + 10N - 3A - 9.
$$

Since $10N-9$ is constant in $N$, we optimize

$$
9B - 3A.
$$

We use two basic facts valid for every permutation of $\{1,\dots,N\}$:

- $0 \le B \le \frac{N(N-1)}{2}$, with equality at the reverse permutation.
- $0 \le A \le N-1$, with equality at the identity permutation.
- $A = N-1$ occurs only for the identity permutation.
- $B=0$ occurs only for the identity permutation.

## 1. Minimum of $T$

We want to minimize $9B - 3A$, so we simultaneously:

- minimize $B$,
- maximize $A$,

since the coefficients have opposite signs.

The identity permutation achieves:

$$
A = N-1,\quad B = 0.
$$

We now prove this is optimal.

For any permutation,

$$
B \ge 0,\quad A \le N-1,
$$

so

$$
9B - 3A \ge 9\cdot 0 - 3(N-1) = -3(N-1).
$$

Hence

$$
T \ge 10N - 9 - 3(N-1).
$$

Simplifying:

$$
T_{\min} = 10N - 9 - 3N + 3 = 7N - 6.
$$

This bound is attained by the identity permutation, so it is sharp.

$$
\boxed{T_{\min} = 7N - 6.}
$$

## 2. Maximum of $T$

We now maximize $9B - 3A$. Since $B$ has coefficient $9$ and $A$ has coefficient $-3$, we first maximize $B$.

The maximum inversion count is achieved by the reverse permutation:

$$
B = \frac{N(N-1)}{2}.
$$

In this permutation every element is a new left-to-right minimum, so:

$$
A = 0.
$$

Thus:

$$
T = 9\cdot \frac{N(N-1)}{2} + 10N - 9.
$$

So:

$$
T_{\max} = \frac{9}{2}N(N-1) + 10N - 9.
$$

To justify optimality, note that for every permutation:

$$
B \le \frac{N(N-1)}{2},\quad A \ge 0,
$$

so

$$
9B - 3A \le 9\cdot \frac{N(N-1)}{2}.
$$

Equality forces $B$ maximal and $A=0$, which is exactly the reverse permutation.

Hence the bound is tight.

$$
\boxed{T_{\max} = \frac{9}{2}N(N-1) + 10N - 9.}
$$

## 3. Final result

$$
\boxed{T_{\min} = 7N - 6}
$$

$$
\boxed{T_{\max} = \frac{9}{2}N(N-1) + 10N - 9}
$$
