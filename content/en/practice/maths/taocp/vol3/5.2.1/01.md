[Section 5.2.1: Sorting by Insertion](../)

**Exercise 1.** [**] [10] Is Algorithm S a stable sorting algorithm?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m42s  

---

Algorithm S moves $R_j$ leftward through positions $i,j-1,\dots$ while $K < K_i$, replacing each $R_i$ by $R_{i+1}$. The loop therefore shifts only those records whose keys are strictly greater than $K$. When the scan stops at some position $i$, we have $K \ge K_i$, and $R_j$ is inserted into position $i+1$.

Let $R_p$ and $R_q$ be two records with equal keys, $K_p = K_q$, and suppose $p < q$. When $R_q$ is processed, every record $R_i$ with $i \ge p$ and $i < q$ that has key strictly greater than $K_q$ is shifted right, while any record with key equal to $K_q$ is not shifted. Hence the block of equal keys remains contiguous during the algorithm, and the scan for $R_q$ stops at the rightmost position of the existing equal block. The insertion position for $R_q$ is immediately to the right of that block, so $R_q$ is placed after $R_p$.

Thus no operation places a later record with key $K$ before an earlier record with key $K$, and stability is preserved.

This completes the proof. ∎
