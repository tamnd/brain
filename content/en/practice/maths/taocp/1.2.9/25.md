[Section 1.2.9 — Generating Functions](../)

**Exercise 25.** [*M23*] Evaluate the sum

$$
\sum_k \binom{n}{k} 2^{n-2k}(-2)^k
$$

by simplifying the equivalent formula

$$
\sum_k [w^k](1-2w)^n [z^{n-k}](1+z)^{2n-2k}.
$$

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m29s  

---

## Solution

Let

$$
S=\sum_k [w^k](1-2w)^n\,[z^{\,n-k}](1+z)^{2n-2k}.
$$

We simplify this coefficient-extraction formula directly.

First observe that

$$
[z^{\,n-k}](1+z)^{2n-2k}
=[z^{\,n-k}](1+z)^{2(n-k)}
=\binom{2(n-k)}{\,n-k\,}.
$$

Hence

$$
S=\sum_k \binom{n}{k}(-2)^k
      \binom{2(n-k)}{\,n-k\,},
$$

since

$$
[w^k](1-2w)^n=\binom{n}{k}(-2)^k.
$$

To evaluate this sum, convert it back into a single coefficient.

Using

$$
[z^{\,n-k}](1+z)^{2n-2k}
=[z^n]\,z^k(1+z)^{2n-2k},
$$

we obtain

$$
S
=\sum_k [w^k](1-2w)^n\,
      [z^n]\,z^k(1+z)^{2n-2k}.
$$

Since coefficient extraction is linear,

$$
S
=[z^n]\sum_k [w^k](1-2w)^n
      z^k(1+z)^{2n-2k}.
$$

Factor out $(1+z)^{2n}$:

$$
S
=[z^n](1+z)^{2n}
   \sum_k [w^k](1-2w)^n
   \left(\frac{z}{(1+z)^2}\right)^k .
$$

Now use the generating-function identity

$$
\sum_k [w^k]F(w)\,x^k = F(x).
$$

With $F(w)=(1-2w)^n$ and $x=\dfrac{z}{(1+z)^2}$, this gives

$$
S
=[z^n](1+z)^{2n}
\left(1-\frac{2z}{(1+z)^2}\right)^n.
$$

Simplifying,

$$
1-\frac{2z}{(1+z)^2}
=\frac{(1+z)^2-2z}{(1+z)^2}
=\frac{1+z^2}{(1+z)^2}.
$$

Therefore

$$
S
=[z^n](1+z)^{2n}
\left(\frac{1+z^2}{(1+z)^2}\right)^n
=[z^n](1+z^2)^n.
$$

But

$$
(1+z^2)^n=\sum_{j=0}^{n}\binom{n}{j}z^{2j},
$$

which contains only even powers of $z$. Hence

$$
[z^n](1+z^2)^n=
\begin{cases}
\binom{n}{n/2}, & n \text{ even},\\[6pt]
0, & n \text{ odd}.
\end{cases}
$$

Consequently,

$$
\boxed{
\sum_k \binom{n}{k}(-2)^k
\binom{2(n-k)}{\,n-k\,} =
[z^n](1+z^2)^n =
\begin{cases}
\binom{n}{n/2}, & n \text{ even},\\[6pt]
0, & n \text{ odd}.
\end{cases}
}
$$

Thus the given coefficient-extraction formula simplifies to $[z^n](1+z^2)^n$, and its value is the central binomial coefficient $\binom{n}{n/2}$ when $n$ is even and $0$ when $n$ is odd. ∎
