[Section 1.2.7 — Harmonic Numbers](../)

**Exercise 10.** [*M20*] (Summation by parts.) Prove the general formula

$$
\sum_{1 \le k < n}(a_{k+1}-a_k)b_k
= a_nb_n - a_1b_1 - \sum_{1 \le k < n}a_{k+1}(b_{k+1}-b_k).
$$

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 31s  

---

## Solution

Let

$$S=\sum_{1 \le k < n}(a_{k+1}-a_k)b_k.$$

Expand the sum:

$$S
= (a_2-a_1)b_1+(a_3-a_2)b_2+\cdots+(a_n-a_{n-1})b_{n-1}.$$

Distribute each factor:

$$S
= a_2b_1-a_1b_1+a_3b_2-a_2b_2+\cdots+a_nb_{n-1}-a_{n-1}b_{n-1}.$$

Group terms according to the coefficients of the $a_k$'s:

$$S
= a_nb_{n-1}-a_1b_1
+\sum_{k=2}^{n-1}a_k(b_{k-1}-b_k).$$

Reindex the sum by replacing $k$ with $k+1$:

$$S
= a_nb_{n-1}-a_1b_1
+\sum_{1 \le k < n-1}a_{k+1}(b_k-b_{k+1}).$$

Since $b_k-b_{k+1}=-(b_{k+1}-b_k)$,

$$S
= a_nb_{n-1}-a_1b_1
-\sum_{1 \le k < n-1}a_{k+1}(b_{k+1}-b_k).$$

Now add and subtract $a_nb_n$:

$$a_nb_{n-1}
=
a_nb_n-a_n(b_n-b_{n-1}).$$

Substituting this into the previous equation gives

$$S
=
a_nb_n-a_1b_1
-\sum_{1 \le k < n-1}a_{k+1}(b_{k+1}-b_k)
-a_n(b_n-b_{n-1}).$$

Combine the final term with the summation:

$$S
=
a_nb_n-a_1b_1
-\sum_{1 \le k < n}a_{k+1}(b_{k+1}-b_k).$$

Therefore

$$\sum_{1 \le k < n}(a_{k+1}-a_k)b_k
=
a_nb_n-a_1b_1
-\sum_{1 \le k < n}a_{k+1}(b_{k+1}-b_k).$$

This completes the proof.

∎
