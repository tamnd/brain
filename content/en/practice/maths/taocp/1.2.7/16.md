[Section 1.2.7 — Harmonic Numbers](../)

**Exercise 16.** [*18*] Express the sum $1 + \frac{1}{3} + \cdots + \frac{1}{2n-1}$ in terms of harmonic numbers.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 34s  

---

## Solution

Let

$$
S_n = 1 + \frac{1}{3} + \cdots + \frac{1}{2n-1}
= \sum_{k=1}^{n}\frac{1}{2k-1}.
$$

The harmonic number $H_{2n}$ contains all reciprocals from $1$ to $2n$:

$$
H_{2n}
= \sum_{k=1}^{2n}\frac{1}{k}
= \sum_{k=1}^{n}\frac{1}{2k-1}
+ \sum_{k=1}^{n}\frac{1}{2k}.
$$

The second sum is

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{n}\frac{1}{2k}
= \frac12\sum_{k=1}^{n}\frac{1}{k}
= \frac12 H_n.
$$

Hence

$$
H_{2n}
= S_n + \frac12 H_n.
$$

Solving for $S_n$ gives

$$
S_n
= H_{2n} - \frac12 H_n.
$$

Therefore,

$$
1 + \frac{1}{3} + \cdots + \frac{1}{2n-1}
= H_{2n} - \frac12 H_n.
$$

$$
\boxed{H_{2n}-\frac12 H_n}
$$
