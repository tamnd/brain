**Exercise 4.3.3.4** [*28*] (K. Baker)  

We are asked to show that evaluating a polynomial \(W(x)\) at symmetric points \(x=-r,\ldots,0,\ldots,r\) is advantageous over evaluating at \(x=0,1,\ldots,2r\) as in Algorithm T, by exploiting the decomposition

\[
U(x) = U_e(x^2) + x U_o(x^2),
\]

and similarly for \(V(x)\) and \(W(x)\), and to indicate how this accelerates steps T7 and T8.

**Solution**

Let \(U(x) = U_e(x^2) + x U_o(x^2)\) denote the decomposition of \(U\) into its even and odd parts, where \(U_e(x^2)\) is a polynomial in \(x^2\) containing only even powers of \(x\) and \(x U_o(x^2)\) contains only odd powers. Analogously, we write

\[
V(x) = V_e(x^2) + x V_o(x^2), \quad W(x) = W_e(x^2) + x W_o(x^2).
\]

The product polynomial \(W(x) = U(x) V(x)\) then expands as:

\[
\begin{aligned}
W(x) &= \big(U_e(x^2) + x U_o(x^2)\big)\big(V_e(x^2) + x V_o(x^2)\big) \\
&= U_e(x^2) V_e(x^2) + x^2 U_o(x^2) V_o(x^2) + x\big(U_e(x^2) V_o(x^2) + U_o(x^2) V_e(x^2)\big) \\
&= \underbrace{U_e(x^2) V_e(x^2) + x^2 U_o(x^2) V_o(x^2)}_{W_e(x^2)} + x \underbrace{\big(U_e(x^2) V_o(x^2) + U_o(x^2) V_e(x^2)\big)}_{W_o(x^2)}.
\end{aligned}
\]

This yields the decomposition

\[
W(x) = W_e(x^2) + x W_o(x^2),
\]

where \(W_e(x^2)\) is even and \(x W_o(x^2)\) is odd.

**Evaluation at symmetric points**

Consider evaluating \(W(x)\) at the points \(x=-r,\ldots,r\). Observe:

\[
W(-x) = W_e(x^2) - x W_o(x^2).
\]

Thus, for every positive \(x\),

\[
W(-x) + W(x) = 2 W_e(x^2), \quad W(x) - W(-x) = 2 x W_o(x^2).
\]

This allows us to compute \(W(x)\) and \(W(-x)\) simultaneously by first evaluating \(W_e(x^2)\) and \(W_o(x^2)\) at \(x^2\) and then combining them with simple additions and subtractions. In particular:

1. Compute \(W_e(y)\) and \(W_o(y)\) for \(y = 0, 1, \ldots, r^2\).
2. For each positive \(x = 1, \ldots, r\):
   \[
   W(x) = W_e(x^2) + x W_o(x^2), \quad W(-x) = W_e(x^2) - x W_o(x^2).
   \]
3. For \(x=0\), we simply have \(W(0) = W_e(0)\).

Consequently, we only need **half as many evaluations of \(W_e\) and \(W_o\)** compared to evaluating at \(0,1,\ldots,2r\). The symmetry reduces the number of independent computations.

**Acceleration in steps T7 and T8 of Algorithm T**

Recall that in Algorithm T (step T7/T8), we evaluate \(W(x) = U(x) V(x)\) at consecutive points for the purpose of computing the convolution. Using the decomposition above:

1. Let \(y = x^2\) and compute \(U_e(y), U_o(y), V_e(y), V_o(y)\) once for each \(y\).  
2. Construct \(W_e(y) = U_e(y)V_e(y) + y U_o(y)V_o(y)\) and \(W_o(y) = U_e(y)V_o(y) + U_o(y)V_e(y)\).  
3. Use the formulas
   \[
   W(x) = W_e(y) + x W_o(y), \quad W(-x) = W_e(y) - x W_o(y)
   \]
   to obtain **both positive and negative evaluations simultaneously**.

This modification reduces the number of polynomial multiplications in T7/T8 roughly by a factor of two and eliminates redundant computations.

**Summary of Advantages**

1. **Reduction in evaluations:** Only evaluate for \(x^2 = 0,1,\ldots,r^2\) instead of \(x = 0,1,\ldots,2r\).  
2. **Symmetric reuse:** \(W(-x)\) is obtained from \(W(x)\) without extra multiplications.  
3. **Algorithmic simplification:** Steps T7 and T8 require fewer multiplications and can reuse precomputed \(U_e, U_o, V_e, V_o\).

**Conclusion**

By decomposing \(U, V, W\) into even and odd parts in \(x\), we exploit the symmetry of the points \(x=-r,\ldots,r\) to halve the number of evaluations and simplify the computation of \(W(x)\). Steps T7 and T8 of Algorithm T are accelerated because the products \(U(x) V(x)\) are computed using only the even and odd components, avoiding redundant calculations at symmetric points. This fully demonstrates the advantage of evaluating at symmetric points.  

\[
\boxed{\text{Evaluation at } x=-r,\dots,r \text{ using } W(x)=W_e(x^2)+x W_o(x^2) \text{ is faster than } x=0,\dots,2r.}
\]
