**Exercise 11.** &#9654; [*M26*] If $n$ is fixed, how many of the automata in the linear iterative array defined by (37) and (38) are needed to compute the product of $n$-bit numbers? (Notice that the automaton $M_j$ is influenced only by the component $z_j^i$ of the machine on its right, so we may remove all automata whose $z_0$ component is always zero whenever the inputs are $n$-bit numbers.)

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 4m23s

Let the two $n$-bit numbers be

$$
A = \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} a_i 2^i, \quad B = \sum_{j=0}^{n-1} b_j 2^j.
$$

Their product is

$$
AB = \sum_{i=0}^{n-1}\sum_{j=0}^{n-1} a_i b_j 2^{i+j}.
$$

The key structural fact behind the linear iterative array defined by (37) and (38) is that information propagates along “diagonals” indexed by the sum $i+j$. The component $z_j^i$ of automaton $M_j$ receives influence only from the corresponding component of its right neighbor, so the index $j$ behaves like a conserved position in the propagation: data starting at position $j$ can only move leftward through automata with the same index structure and cannot generate activity at indices outside the range determined by initial nonzero inputs.

## Step 1: Identify possible nonzero indices

Initially, nonzero data exists only in positions $0,1,\dots,n-1$ for each input number. Hence any nonzero partial product $a_i b_j$ is associated with index $i+j$, where

$$
0 \le i \le n-1, \quad 0 \le j \le n-1.
$$

Therefore,

$$
0 \le i+j \le 2n-2.
$$

Thus, every potentially nonzero contribution to the computation lies in indices

$$
0,1,\dots,2n-2.
$$

## Step 2: Consequence for the automata

The automaton $M_k$ is responsible for carrying information associated with index $k$. Since no computation ever generates a nonzero contribution at any index $k \ge 2n-1$, all components $z_0$ in automata beyond this range remain zero for the entire computation.

More precisely, if $k \ge 2n-1$, then there are no pairs $(i,j)$ with $i+j=k$, so no signal can ever be created or propagated into $M_k$. By the locality condition (each automaton is influenced only by the corresponding $z_k$-component of its right neighbor), such zero state cannot be activated later.

Hence, for all $k \ge 2n-1$,

$$
z_0 \text{ in } M_k \equiv 0 \quad \text{throughout the computation}.
$$

These automata can therefore be removed.

## Step 3: Minimal required array size

The only automata that may ever become active are

$$
M_0, M_1, \dots, M_{2n-2}.
$$

This is a total of

$$
(2n-2) - 0 + 1 = 2n-1
$$

automata.

## Conclusion

The linear iterative array requires exactly

$$
\boxed{2n-1}
$$

automata to compute the product of two $n$-bit numbers, since all automata $M_k$ with $k \ge 2n-1$ remain permanently inactive (their $z_0$ component is always zero) and can be removed without affecting the computation.
