[Section 4.2.4: Distribution of Floating Point Numbers](../)

**Exercise 16.** [*HM28*] (P. Diaconis.) Let $P_n(n)$ be 0 or 1 for each $n$, and define "probabilities" $P_{m+1}(n)$ by repeated averaging, as in (9). Show that if $\lim_{n\to\infty} P_1(n)$ does not exist, neither does $\lim_{n\to\infty} P_m(n)$ for any $m$. [*Hint:* Prove that $a_n \to 0$ whenever we have $(a_1 + \cdots + a_n)/n = 0$ and $a_{n+1} \le a_n + M/n$, for some fixed constant $M > 0$.]

Let $P_{m+1}(n)=\dfrac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1}^n P_m(k)$. Write

$$
A_n = P_m(n), \qquad B_n = P_{m+1}(n), \qquad 0 \le A_n \le 1.
$$

Then

$$
nB_n = \sum_{k=1}^n A_k, \qquad (n+1)B_{n+1} = \sum_{k=1}^{n+1} A_k.
$$

Hence the exact increment identity is

$$
(n+1)B_{n+1} - nB_n = A_{n+1},
$$

so

$$
B_{n+1} - B_n = \frac{A_{n+1} - B_n}{n+1}.
$$

Since $0 \le A_{n+1}, B_n \le 1$, we have

$$
-1 \le A_{n+1} - B_n \le 1,
$$

and therefore

$$
|B_{n+1} - B_n| \le \frac{1}{n+1}, \qquad \text{in particular } B_{n+1} \le B_n + \frac{1}{n+1}.
$$

## Step 1. Reduction

Assume $B_n \to L$. Define

$$
a_n = B_n - L.
$$

Then $a_n \to 0$ is what we must prove.

From $B_n \to L$,

$$
\frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1}^n a_k = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1}^n (B_k - L) = B_{n+1} - L - \frac{A_{n+1}-L}{n+1} \to 0,
$$

so in particular

$$
\frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1}^n a_k \to 0.
$$

Thus $a_n$ has Cesàro mean $0$.

We now verify the Tauberian condition from the hint:

$$
a_{n+1} \le a_n + \frac{M}{n}
$$

for some fixed $M$.

## Step 2. One-step growth bound

From the identity for $B_n$,

$$
B_{n+1} - B_n = \frac{A_{n+1} - B_n}{n+1}.
$$

Hence

$$
a_{n+1} - a_n = B_{n+1} - B_n = \frac{A_{n+1} - B_n}{n+1}.
$$

Using $A_{n+1} \le 1$ and $B_n \ge 0$,

$$
A_{n+1} - B_n \le 1,
$$

so

$$
a_{n+1} - a_n \le \frac{1}{n+1}.
$$

Thus, for all $n$,

$$
a_{n+1} \le a_n + \frac{1}{n}.
$$

So the Tauberian condition holds with $M=1$.

## Step 3. Apply the hint

We now apply the stated lemma:

If a sequence $a_n$ satisfies

$$
\frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1}^n a_k \to 0,
\qquad
a_{n+1} \le a_n + \frac{M}{n},
$$

then $a_n \to 0$.

All hypotheses are satisfied for $a_n = B_n - L$. Therefore,

$$
B_n - L \to 0,
$$

so

$$
B_n \to L.
$$

## Step 4. Transfer one level down

We have shown:

> If $P_{m+1}(n)$ converges, then $P_m(n)$ also converges (to the same limit).

Iterating this implication,

$$
P_m \text{ converges } \Rightarrow P_{m-1} \text{ converges } \Rightarrow \cdots \Rightarrow P_1 \text{ converges}.
$$

Taking the contrapositive,

$$
\text{If } \lim_{n\to\infty} P_1(n) \text{ does not exist, then } \lim_{n\to\infty} P_m(n) \text{ does not exist for any } m \ge 1.
$$

## Conclusion

Repeated averaging cannot create convergence at any finite level if the original $0$-$1$ sequence fails to converge.
