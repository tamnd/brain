**Exercise 9.** [*HM25*] (P. Diaconis.) Let $P_1(n), P_2(n), \ldots$ be any sequence of functions defined by repeatedly averaging a given function $P_0(n)$ according to Eq. (9). Prove that $\lim_{n \to \infty} P_m(n) = P_0(1)$ for all fixed $n$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m26s  

---

## Solution

Define the averaging operator $A$ on sequences by

$$
(AP)(n)=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{j=1}^n P(j),
$$

and let $P_{m+1}=A P_m$ with initial function $P_0$. Then for each $n\ge 1$,

$$
P_{m+1}(n)=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{j=1}^n P_m(j).
$$

For $n=1$,

$$
P_{m+1}(1)=P_m(1),
$$

so $P_m(1)=P_0(1)$ for all $m\ge 0$.

Define

$$
d_m(n)=P_m(n)-P_m(1)=P_m(n)-P_0(1).
$$

Then $d_m(1)=0$ for all $m$ and for $n\ge 2$,

$$
d_{m+1}(n)=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{j=1}^n P_m(j)-P_m(1)
=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{j=1}^n d_m(j).
$$

For $n=2$,

$$
d_{m+1}(2)=\frac{1}{2}(d_m(1)+d_m(2))=\frac{1}{2}d_m(2),
$$

hence

$$
d_m(2)=2^{-m}d_0(2),
$$

so $\lim_{m\to\infty} d_m(2)=0$.

Assume for some $n\ge 2$ that $\lim_{m\to\infty} d_m(j)=0$ for all $1\le j<n$. Then

$$
d_{m+1}(n)=\frac{1}{n}\left(\sum_{j=1}^{n-1} d_m(j)+d_m(n)\right).
$$

Let

$$
s_m(n-1)=\sum_{j=1}^{n-1} d_m(j).
$$

By the induction hypothesis, $s_m(n-1)\to 0$ as $m\to\infty$. Hence

$$
d_{m+1}(n)=\frac{1}{n}s_m(n-1)+\frac{1}{n}d_m(n).
$$

Iterating this relation $k$ times yields

$$
d_{m+k}(n)=\frac{1}{n^k}d_m(n)+\sum_{t=0}^{k-1}\frac{1}{n^{t+1}}\, s_{m+k-1-t}(n-1).
$$

Fix $\varepsilon>0$. Choose $M$ such that for all $r\ge M$,

$$
|s_r(n-1)|<\varepsilon.
$$

Then for $m\ge M$ and $k\ge 1$,

$$
|d_{m+k}(n)|
\le \frac{|d_m(n)|}{n^k}+\sum_{t=0}^{k-1}\frac{\varepsilon}{n^{t+1}}
<\frac{|d_m(n)|}{n^k}+\frac{\varepsilon}{n-1}.
$$

Letting $k\to\infty$ gives

$$
\limsup_{k\to\infty} |d_{m+k}(n)| \le \frac{\varepsilon}{n-1}.
$$

Since $\varepsilon$ is arbitrary,

$$
\lim_{m\to\infty} d_m(n)=0.
$$

Therefore, for each fixed $n$,

$$
\lim_{m\to\infty} P_m(n)=P_0(1).
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
