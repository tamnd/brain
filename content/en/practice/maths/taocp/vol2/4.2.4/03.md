[Section 4.2.4: Distribution of Floating Point Numbers](../)

**Exercise 3.** [*15*] What is the probability that the two leading digits of a floating decimal number are "23", according to the logarithmic law?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m02s  

---

Let $U$ be a positive normalized floating decimal number written as

$$
U = 10^e f, \qquad 1 \le f < 10.
$$

In the logarithmic law (Section 4.2.4), the random variable with distribution is the normalized significand $f$, not $10f$. It satisfies

$$
\Pr(f \le r) = \log_{10} r, \qquad 1 \le r < 10.
$$

The leading digits of $U$ depend only on $f$. Specifically, the number $U$ has leading digits “23” if and only if its significand lies in the interval corresponding to those digits:

$$
23 \le 10f < 24.
$$

Dividing by $10$ (which preserves inequalities since $f>0$) gives the equivalent condition

$$
2.3 \le f < 2.4.
$$

We now apply the logarithmic law directly to $f$. The probability that $f$ lies in an interval $[a,b)\subset [1,10)$ is

$$
\Pr(a \le f < b) = \log_{10} b - \log_{10} a.
$$

Therefore,

$$
\Pr(2.3 \le f < 2.4)
= \log_{10}(2.4) - \log_{10}(2.3)
= \log_{10}\!\left(\frac{2.4}{2.3}\right).
$$

Simplifying the fraction,

$$
\frac{2.4}{2.3} = \frac{24}{23}.
$$

Hence the required probability is

$$
\boxed{\log_{10}\!\left(\frac{24}{23}\right)}.
$$
