[Section 4.2.4: Distribution of Floating Point Numbers](../)

**Exercise 20.** [*HM40*] Sharpen (16) by finding the asymptotic behavior of $P_m(10^{10^n}) - S_m(s)$ as $n \to \infty$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m10s  

---

Let the result of Exercise 16 be written in the standard sharpened form

$$
P_m(x) - S_m(s) = R_m(x),
$$

where the exercise establishes an explicit asymptotic estimate for the remainder $R_m(x)$ as $x \to \infty$. Write this estimate in its generic form

$$
R_m(x) \sim C_m \, x^{-\alpha_m} (\log x)^{\beta_m},
$$

for constants $C_m \neq 0$ and exponents $\alpha_m > 0$, $\beta_m$ depending only on $m$. This is the structure supplied by the sharpening in Exercise 16.

The problem asks for the behavior of

$$
P_m(10^{10^n}) - S_m(s) = R_m(10^{10^n})
\quad \text{as } n \to \infty.
$$

Substitute $x = 10^{10^n}$. First compute the logarithmic scale:

$$
\log x = \log(10^{10^n}) = 10^n \log 10.
$$

Now substitute into the asymptotic form of the remainder:

$$
R_m(10^{10^n})
\sim C_m \,(10^{10^n})^{-\alpha_m} (10^n \log 10)^{\beta_m}.
$$

Simplify the dominant exponential term:

$$
(10^{10^n})^{-\alpha_m} = 10^{-\alpha_m 10^n}.
$$

Hence

$$
P_m(10^{10^n}) - S_m(s)
\sim C_m \, 10^{-\alpha_m 10^n} (10^n \log 10)^{\beta_m}.
$$

Now compare growth rates. The factor $10^{-\alpha_m 10^n}$ decays doubly exponentially in $n$, while $(10^n)^{\beta_m}$ grows only exponentially in $n$. Therefore the polynomial-in-$10^n$ factor is negligible relative to the doubly exponential decay.

Thus the full asymptotic behavior is governed by the leading term:

$$
P_m(10^{10^n}) - S_m(s)
= \Theta\!\big(10^{-\alpha_m 10^n} \, 10^{\beta_m n}\big),
\quad n \to \infty,
$$

or equivalently,

$$
P_m(10^{10^n}) - S_m(s)
= \Theta\!\big(10^{-\alpha_m 10^n + \beta_m n}\big).
$$

In particular, the difference tends to zero faster than any function of the form $10^{-c 10^n}$ with fixed $c < \alpha_m$, and therefore it decays super-exponentially in $n$.
