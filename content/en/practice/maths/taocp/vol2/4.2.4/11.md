[Section 4.2.4: Distribution of Floating Point Numbers](../)

**Exercise 11.** [*M15*] Given that $U$ is a random variable distributed according to the logarithmic law, prove that $1/U$ is also.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 59s  

---

## Solution

Let $U$ be a positive random variable satisfying the logarithmic law in base $10$, meaning that for every integer $k$ and every real $r$ with $1 \le r < 10$,

$\Pr(10^k \le U < r \cdot 10^k) = \log_{10} r.$

Let $V = 1/U$. Then

$\log_{10} V = -\log_{10} U.$

Write

$X = (\log_{10} U) \bmod 1,$

so that $X$ is the fractional part of $\log_{10} U$ in $[0,1)$. The logarithmic law implies that $X$ is uniformly distributed on $[0,1)$.

Let

$Y = (\log_{10} V) \bmod 1 = (-\log_{10} U) \bmod 1.$

For $x \in [0,1)$, the identity

0, & x = 0,\\ 1 - x, & 0 < x < 1 \end{cases}$$
holds, and changing a value on a null set does not affect distributional properties. Hence $Y = 1 - X$ in distributional terms. For $0 \le t \le 1$,
$$\Pr(Y \le t) = \Pr(1 - X \le t) = \Pr(X \ge 1 - t).$$
Since $X$ is uniform on $[0,1)$,
$$\Pr(X \ge 1 - t) = t.$$
Therefore $Y$ is also uniformly distributed on $[0,1)$. For $1 \le r < 10$, the leading-digit probability for $V$ is
$$\Pr(\text{leading digit of } V \le r) = \Pr(Y \le \log_{10} r).$$
Using the distribution of $Y$,
$$\Pr(Y \le \log_{10} r) = \log_{10} r.$$ Thus $V$ satisfies the same logarithmic law as $U$. This completes the proof. ∎
