[Section 4.2.4: Distribution of Floating Point Numbers](../)

**Exercise 11.** [*M15*] Given that $U$ is a random variable distributed according to the logarithmic law, prove that $1/U$ is also.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 1m01s  

---

## Solution

Let $U>0$ be a random variable whose distribution satisfies the logarithmic law in base $10$. Write

$X = \log_{10} U.$

The assumption means that the fractional part ${X} = X \bmod 1$ is uniformly distributed on $[0,1)$, since for $1 \le r < 10$,

$\Pr(\{X\} \le \log_{10} r) = \log_{10} r.$

Define $V = 1/U$. Then

$\log_{10} V = -\log_{10} U = -X.$

Let $Y = {-X}$ denote the fractional part of $-X$. The goal is to show that $Y$ is uniformly distributed on $[0,1)$.

Write $X = k + t$ where $k \in \mathbb{Z}$ and $t = {X} \in [0,1)$. Then

$-X = -k - t.$

If $t = 0$, then ${-X} = 0$. If $0 < t < 1$, then

$-k - t = (-k-1) + (1-t),$

so

$\{-X\} = 1 - t.$

Hence, except on the event ${X} = 0$, which has probability $0$ under a continuous uniform distribution, the relation

$\{-X\} = 1 - \{X\}$

holds.

Let $y \in [0,1]$. Then

$\Pr(\{-X\} \le y) = \Pr(1 - \{X\} \le y) = \Pr(\{X\} \ge 1 - y).$

Since ${X}$ is uniform on $[0,1)$,

$\Pr(\{X\} \ge 1 - y) = y.$

Therefore ${-X}$ is uniformly distributed on $[0,1)$.

Since $\log_{10} V = -X$ has fractional part uniformly distributed on $[0,1)$, the variable $V$ satisfies the same logarithmic law as $U$.

This completes the proof. ∎
