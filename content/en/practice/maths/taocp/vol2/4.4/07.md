[Section 4.4: Radix Conversion](../)

**Exercise 7.** [*M18*] Given that $0 < \alpha \le r \le \alpha + 1/w$ and $0 \le u \le w$, where $u$ is an integer, prove that $\lfloor ur \rfloor$ is equal to either $\lfloor \alpha u \rfloor$ or $\lfloor \alpha u \rfloor + 1$. Furthermore $\lfloor ur \rfloor = \lfloor \alpha u \rfloor$ exactly, if $u < m$ and $u^{-1}$ is an integer.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 2m35s  

---

## Solution

Let

$$
\delta=r-\alpha .
$$

The hypotheses imply

$$
0\le \delta \le \frac1w.
$$

Since $0\le u\le w$,

$$
0\le u\delta \le \frac{u}{w}\le 1.
$$

Hence

$$
\alpha u \le ur=\alpha u+u\delta \le \alpha u+1.
$$

Write

$$
\alpha u=n+\theta,
$$

where $n=\lfloor \alpha u\rfloor$ and $0\le \theta<1$. Then

$$
n+\theta\le ur\le n+\theta+1.
$$

Therefore

$$
n\le \lfloor ur\rfloor \le n+1.
$$

Since $\lfloor ur\rfloor$ is an integer, the only possibilities are

$$
\lfloor ur\rfloor=n
\qquad\text{or}\qquad
\lfloor ur\rfloor=n+1.
$$

Substituting $n=\lfloor\alpha u\rfloor$, we obtain

$$
\boxed{\lfloor ur\rfloor=\lfloor\alpha u\rfloor
\ \text{or}\
\lfloor ur\rfloor=\lfloor\alpha u\rfloor+1.}
$$

For the second assertion, assume that $u<w$ and that $u^{-1}$ is an integer. Then $u\ge1$, and

$$
u\delta\le \frac{u}{w}<1.
$$

Hence

$$
ur<\alpha u+1.
$$

If $u\alpha$ is an integer, then

$$
ur=\alpha u+u\delta<\alpha u+1,
$$

so

$$
\lfloor ur\rfloor=\alpha u=\lfloor \alpha u\rfloor.
$$

If $u\alpha$ is not an integer, write again

$$
\alpha u=n+\theta,
\qquad 0<\theta<1.
$$

Since $u^{-1}$ is an integer, $u=1$. Thus

$$
0\le u\delta=\delta\le \frac1w<1.
$$

Therefore

$$
ur=n+\theta+\delta<n+1,
$$

because $\theta<1$ and $\delta<1$. Consequently,

$$
\lfloor ur\rfloor=n=\lfloor\alpha u\rfloor.
$$

Thus, under the additional assumptions $u<w$ and $u^{-1}$ integer,

$$
\boxed{\lfloor ur\rfloor=\lfloor\alpha u\rfloor.}
$$

This completes the proof.

∎
