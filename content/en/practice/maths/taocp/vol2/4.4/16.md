[Section 4.4: Radix Conversion](../)

**Exercise 16.** [*41*] Construct a fast linear iterative array for radix conversion from decimal to binary (see Section 4.3.3E).

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m12s  

---

## Setup

Let

$$
u=(u_m u_{m-1}\ldots u_1u_0)_{10}
$$

be the decimal number to be converted into binary notation. Exercise 4.3.3E asks for a linear iterative array, therefore the computation must be organized as a sequence of identical cells through which information propagates in one direction.

Method 1b of Section 4.4 evaluates

$$
u=u_m10^m+\cdots+u_1 10+u_0
$$

in the nested form

$$
\bigl(\cdots((u_m\cdot10+u_{m-1})\cdot10+u_{m-2})\cdots\bigr)\cdot10+u_0.
$$

Since the target radix is $2$, multiplication by $10$ must be performed in binary arithmetic. The identity

$$
10=2^3+2
$$

gives

$$
10x=(x\ll3)+(x\ll1),
$$

where $\ll k$ denotes a shift left by $k$ binary places.

The problem is to construct a fast linear iterative array realizing this recurrence.

## Solution

Let the binary representation of the partial value after the $j$th decimal digit has been processed be

$$
V^{(j)}=\sum_{r\ge0} v_r^{(j)}2^r,
\qquad
v_r^{(j)}\in\{0,1\}.
$$

The recurrence corresponding to Method 1b is

$$
V^{(0)}=u_m,
$$

and

$$
V^{(j+1)}=10V^{(j)}+u_{m-j-1}.
\eqno(1)
$$

Substituting $10=2^3+2$ into (1),

$$
V^{(j+1)}
=(V^{(j)}\ll3)+(V^{(j)}\ll1)+u_{m-j-1}.
\eqno(2)
$$

Equation (2) is suitable for a linear iterative array. Arrange cells in binary-position order. Cell $r$ stores the bit $v_r$ together with a carry variable $c_r$. During one iteration corresponding to a decimal digit, cell $r$ receives:

$$
v_{r-1},\qquad v_{r-3},\qquad c_{r-1},
$$

where nonexistent indices contribute $0$.

The quantity to be formed at position $r$ is

$$
s_r=v_{r-1}+v_{r-3}+c_{r-1}+\delta_{r0}d,
$$

where $d=u_{m-j-1}$ and $\delta_{r0}$ is $1$ for $r=0$ and $0$ otherwise.

Since

$$
0\le v_{r-1}\le1,\qquad
0\le v_{r-3}\le1,
$$

and the incoming carry is at most $1$, while the digit injection occurs only for $r=0$ and satisfies $0\le d\le9$, every cell performs the local transformation

$$
v_r' = s_r \bmod 2,
$$

$$
c_r=\bigl\lfloor s_r/2\bigr\rfloor.
\tag{3}
$$

The same operation is executed by every cell. Information travels only from lower-numbered positions toward higher-numbered positions; hence the array is linear and iterative.

To obtain high speed, the decimal digit $d$ is introduced only into cell $0$. All other cells execute the fixed rule (3). Multiplication by $10$ is realized simultaneously throughout the array by the two shifted copies required by (2). Therefore each new decimal digit is absorbed in one pass through the array, and no global multiplication is required.

The resulting iterative array consists of identical cells implementing

$$
(v_{r-1},v_{r-3},c_{r-1})
\longmapsto
(v_r',c_r),
$$

with digit injection at the left boundary. This is the direct linear-array realization of Method 1b for decimal-to-binary conversion.

## Verification

Assume that before a stage the stored bits represent

$$
V=\sum_{r\ge0} v_r2^r.
$$

The contribution of the shifted-by-one input is

$$
\sum_{r\ge0} v_{r-1}2^r
=2V.
$$

The contribution of the shifted-by-three input is

$$
\sum_{r\ge0} v_{r-3}2^r
=8V.
$$

The local carry propagation defined by (3) is exactly the ordinary binary addition of these two quantities together with the injected decimal digit $d$. Consequently the output bits represent

$$
2V+8V+d
=
10V+d.
$$

This is precisely recurrence (2), hence after processing digits

$$
u_m,u_{m-1},\ldots,u_0
$$

the final binary word equals

$$
(\cdots((u_m\cdot10+u_{m-1})\cdot10+u_{m-2})\cdots)\cdot10+u_0
=u.
$$

Therefore the array performs radix conversion from decimal to binary.

This completes the proof.

∎

## Notes

The construction is the array analogue of program (6) in Section 4.4. Its speed derives from replacing multiplication by $10$ with the parallel computation of

$$
10V=(V\ll3)+(V\ll1),
$$

so that every cell performs only fixed local binary-addition operations. This yields a fast linear iterative array in the sense of Section 4.3.3E.

$$
\boxed{\text{A linear iterative array implementing }V\leftarrow(V\ll3)+(V\ll1)+d}
$$
