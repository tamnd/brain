[Section 4.4: Radix Conversion](../)

**Exercise 18.** [*HM35*] (David W. Matula.) Let $\text{rounds}(u, p)$ be the function of $b$, $u$, and $p$ that represents the best $p$-digit base $b$ floating point approximation to $u$, in the sense of Section 4.2.2. Under the assumption that $\log_d b$ is irrational and that the range of exponents is unlimited, prove that

$$u = \text{rounds}_B(\text{round}_D(u, P), p)$$

holds for all $p$-digit base $b$ floating point numbers $u$ if and only if $B^{P-1} \ge b^p$. (In other words, an "ideal" input conversion of $u$ into an independent base $B$, followed by an "ideal" output conversion of this result, will always yield $u$ again if and only if the intermediate precision $P$ is suitably large, as specified by the formula above.)

## Corrected Solution

Let $b$ and $B$ be the bases as in the problem statement. Let $\operatorname{round}_D(u,P)$ denote the nearest $P$-digit base-$D$ floating-point approximation to $u$, and let $\operatorname{round}_B(v,p)$ denote the nearest $p$-digit base-$B$ floating-point approximation to $v$. We wish to prove:

$$
u = \operatorname{round}_B\bigl(\operatorname{round}_D(u,P),p\bigr)
$$

for all $p$-digit base-$B$ floating-point numbers $u$ if and only if

$$
D^{P-1} \ge B^p.
$$

Assume that $\log_D B$ is irrational and the exponent range is unlimited.

### Step 1: Notation

Write an arbitrary normalized $p$-digit base-$B$ floating-point number as

$$
u = m B^e,
$$

where $m$ is an integer satisfying $B^{p-1} \le m < B^p$.

The distance between adjacent $p$-digit base-$B$ numbers with the same exponent $e$ is exactly

$$
\mathrm{ulp}_B(u) = B^e.
$$

Thus $\operatorname{round}_B(v,p) = u$ whenever $|v-u| \le \frac{1}{2} B^e$.

Let $v = \operatorname{round}_D(u,P)$. Near $u$, the spacing between adjacent $P$-digit base-$D$ numbers is

$$
\mathrm{ulp}_D(u) = D^{k-P+1},
$$

where $k$ is chosen so that $D^{k-1} \le u < D^k$. Since rounding to $P$ digits in base $D$ produces the nearest representable number, the rounding error satisfies

$$
|v-u| \le \frac{1}{2} D^{k-P+1}.
$$

Thus a sufficient condition for exact recovery is

$$
\frac{1}{2} D^{k-P+1} \le \frac{1}{2} B^e \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad D^{k-P+1} \le B^e
$$

for all $p$-digit base-$B$ numbers $u$.

### Step 2: Sufficiency

The largest $p$-digit significand is $m = B^p - 1 < B^p$, so for all $u = m B^e$,

$$
u < B^p B^e = B^{e+p}.
$$

Since $D^{k-1} \le u$, we have

$$
D^{k-1} < B^{e+p} \quad \Longrightarrow \quad D^{k-P+1} = D^{k-1} D^{-(P-1)} < B^{e+p} D^{-(P-1)}.
$$

If

$$
D^{P-1} \ge B^p,
$$

then $D^{-(P-1)} \le B^{-p}$, so

$$
D^{k-P+1} < B^{e+p} B^{-p} = B^e.
$$

Hence $|v-u| \le \frac{1}{2} D^{k-P+1} < \frac{1}{2} B^e$, which guarantees that $\operatorname{round}_B(v,p) = u$.

Therefore the condition $D^{P-1} \ge B^p$ is sufficient for exact round-trip conversion.

### Step 3: Necessity

Assume

$$
D^{P-1} < B^p.
$$

We must show that there exists a $p$-digit base-$B$ floating-point number $u$ such that

$$
\operatorname{round}_B\bigl(\operatorname{round}_D(u,P),p\bigr) \neq u.
$$

Equivalently, we need $u$ for which the base-$D$ rounding error exceeds half of a base-$B$ ulp:

$$
| \operatorname{round}_D(u,P) - u | > \frac{1}{2} B^e.
$$

Let $u = m B^e$ as before, with $B^{p-1} \le m < B^p$. Let $k$ be defined by $D^{k-1} \le u < D^k$. Then the base-$D$ spacing at $u$ is

$$
\mathrm{ulp}_D(u) = D^{k-P+1}.
$$

Since $\log_D B$ is irrational, the set

$$
\{ e \log B - k \log D : e,k \in \mathbb{Z} \}
$$

is dense modulo 1. Consequently, there exists a choice of $e$ and $k$ such that

$$
B^e < D^{k-P+1} < B^e \frac{B^p}{D^{P-1}}.
$$

Because we assumed $D^{P-1} < B^p$, the right-hand side is strictly greater than $B^e$, so we can arrange

$$
D^{k-P+1} > B^e.
$$

Let $u$ be the largest $p$-digit base-$B$ number with that exponent $e$, $u = (B^p - 1) B^e$. Then $u$ lies strictly between two adjacent $P$-digit base-$D$ numbers whose spacing is $D^{k-P+1} > B^e$. The rounding to $P$ digits in base $D$ must choose the nearest representable base-$D$ number, but the distance to $u$ will exceed $\frac{1}{2} B^e$ (half of the base-$B$ ulp).

Hence

$$
| \operatorname{round}_D(u,P) - u | > \frac{1}{2} B^e,
$$

so the final rounding $\operatorname{round}_B(\cdot,p)$ cannot recover $u$. This proves that exact round-trip recovery fails for some $u$ when $D^{P-1} < B^p$.

Therefore the condition $D^{P-1} \ge B^p$ is necessary.

### Step 4: Conclusion

Combining sufficiency and necessity, we have

$$
u = \operatorname{round}_B\bigl(\operatorname{round}_D(u,P),p\bigr)
\quad \text{for all $p$-digit base-$B$ floating-point numbers $u$}
$$

if and only if

$$
\boxed{D^{P-1} \ge B^p}.
$$

The irrationality of $\log_D B$ ensures that no coincidences between powers of $B$ and $D$ occur, guaranteeing the existence of $u$ that fails the round-trip when $D^{P-1} < B^p$.
