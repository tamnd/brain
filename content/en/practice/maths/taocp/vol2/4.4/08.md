**Exercise 8.** [*24*] Write a MIX program analogous to (1) that uses (5) and includes no division instructions.

## Solution

Equation (5) shows how to replace division by $10$ with multiplication by an approximation to $\frac1{10}$. Let

$$
x
$$

be a fixed radix-2 fraction satisfying

$$
\frac1{10}<x<\frac1{10}+\frac1w,
$$

where $w$ is the word size. Given an integer $u$ with $0\le u<w$, define

$$
q'=\lfloor ux\rfloor .
$$

By Exercise 7, $q'$ is either

$$
\left\lfloor \frac u{10}\right\rfloor
\qquad\text{or}\qquad
\left\lfloor \frac u{10}\right\rfloor+1.
$$

Equation (5) determines the correct quotient:

$$
\left\lfloor \frac u{10}\right\rfloor
=
q'-\lfloor u<10q'\rfloor .
$$

The remainder is then

$$
u-10\left\lfloor \frac u{10}\right\rfloor .
$$

To imitate program (1), we repeatedly replace $u$ by $\lfloor u/10\rfloor$, storing the remainder digit at each stage. The test $u<10q'$ is implemented by forming $10q'$ and comparing it with $u$.

A MIX program is therefore:

```
        ENT1 0          Set j ← 0.
        LDX  U          rX ← u.
1H      STX  TEMP       Save u.

        MUL  XRECIP     rA ← q' = ⌊ux⌋.
        STA  Q

        MUL  =10=       rA ← 10q'.
        CMPX TEMP       Compare 10q' with u.
        JL   2F         Jump if 10q' < u.

        LDA  Q          q ← q' − 1.
        DEC1A 1
        STA  Q
2H
        LDA  Q          Form 10q.
        MUL  =10=
        STA  T

        LDX  TEMP       rX ← u.
        SUB  T          rA ← u − 10q.
        STA  ANSWER,1   Uj ← u mod 10.

        INC1 1          j ← j + 1.

        LDX  Q          u ← q.
        JXNZ 1B         Repeat until q = 0.
```

The correctness follows directly from (5). At the beginning of each pass, register $X$ contains the current value $u$. The multiplication by $x$ yields $q'$. If $10q'>u$, then $q'= \lfloor u/10\rfloor+1$, so the program decrements $q'$. If $10q'<u$, then $q'=\lfloor u/10\rfloor$ and no correction is made. Equality cannot occur when $q'=\lfloor u/10\rfloor+1$, since then

$$
10q' > u.
$$

Hence after the correction step,

$$
q=\left\lfloor \frac u{10}\right\rfloor .
$$

The stored digit is

$$
u-10q
=
u-10\left\lfloor \frac u{10}\right\rfloor
=
u\bmod 10.
$$

Finally, $u$ is replaced by $q$, exactly as in Method 1a. Therefore the successive digits deposited in `ANSWER` are

$$
U_0,U_1,U_2,\ldots,
$$

and the process terminates when the quotient becomes zero. Thus the program is completely analogous to (1) and contains no division instructions.

$$

$$
