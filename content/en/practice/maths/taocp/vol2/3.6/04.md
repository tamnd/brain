### 1. Random Number Generator

We use the **linear congruential generator (LCG)** suggested in Section 3.6:

\[
X_{n+1} = (a X_n + c) \bmod m,
\]

with standard parameters recommended by Knuth:

\[
a = 6364136223846793005, \quad c = 1, \quad m = 2^{64}.
\]

The generator produces a sequence of integers \(X_0, X_1, X_2, \dots\). To generate a random integer in the range \(1,\dots,N\), we scale:

\[
r = 1 + \left\lfloor \frac{X_n}{m} \cdot N \right\rfloor.
\]

This ensures uniformity over the range \(1,\dots,N\) as required.

### 2. Deck Representation and Shuffle

Represent a standard deck of 52 cards as an array:

\[
D[1], D[2], \dots, D[52],
\]

where each card is encoded as a rank-suit pair (e.g., \(2H\) for two of hearts, \(QS\) for queen of spades).

**Initialization:**

\[
D[i] = \text{card corresponding to } i, \quad 1 \le i \le 52.
\]

**Shuffling (Fisher-Yates algorithm):**

For \(j = 52, 51, \dots, 2\):

1. Generate \(r \in \{1,2,\dots,j\}\) using the LCG.
2. Swap \(D[r]\) and \(D[j]\).

**Justification:** Each swap exchanges the current card with a uniformly random earlier card. Standard induction shows that every permutation occurs with probability \(1/52!\), provided the random numbers are uniform.
