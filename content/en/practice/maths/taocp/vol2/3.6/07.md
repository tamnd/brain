**Exercise 7.** &#9654; [*M40*] *[M40]* A programmer decided to encipher his files by using a linear congruential sequence $\langle X_n \rangle$ of period $2^{30}$ generated by (1) with $m = 2^{35}$. He took the most significant bits $\lfloor X_n / 2^{10} \rfloor$ and exclusive-or'ed them onto his data, but kept the parameters $a$, $c$, and $X_0$ secret.

Show that this isn't a very secure scheme, by devising a method that deduces the multiplier $a$ and the first difference $X_1 - X_0$ in a reasonable amount of time, given only the values of $\lfloor X_n / 2^{10} \rfloor$ for $0 \le n < 150$.

Let

$$
X_{n+1} \equiv aX_n + c \pmod{2^{35}}, \qquad 0 \le X_n < 2^{35},
$$

and the observed data be

$$
Y_n = \left\lfloor \frac{X_n}{2^{10}} \right\rfloor,
\qquad X_n = 2^{10}Y_n + r_n,\quad 0 \le r_n < 2^{10}.
$$

Thus only the low 10 bits $r_n$ are hidden.

## 1. Rewrite the recurrence with visible and hidden parts

Substitute $X_n = 2^{10}Y_n + r_n$ into the LCG:

$$
2^{10}Y_{n+1} + r_{n+1}
\equiv a(2^{10}Y_n + r_n) + c \pmod{2^{35}}.
$$

Rearrange:

$$
2^{10}(Y_{n+1} - aY_n)
\equiv ar_n + c - r_{n+1} \pmod{2^{35}}.
$$

Since the right-hand side is strictly less than $2^{35} + 2^{11}$, the congruence can be lifted to an integer equation with a carry term $k_n\in\{0,1\}$:

$$
2^{10}(Y_{n+1} - aY_n)
= ar_n + c - r_{n+1} + k_n 2^{35}.
$$

Now reduce modulo $2^{10}$:

$$
ar_n + c - r_{n+1} \equiv 0 \pmod{2^{10}}.
$$

So there exists an integer sequence $t_n$ such that

$$
r_{n+1} = ar_n + c + 2^{10}t_n,
\quad t_n \in \mathbb{Z}.
$$

Because $0 \le r_n < 2^{10}$, the values $t_n$ are extremely small (in practice $0,\pm1,\pm2$).

## 2. Eliminate $c$: a second-order recurrence on the hidden state

From

$$
r_{n+1} = ar_n + c + 2^{10}t_n,
$$

write the same for $n+1$:

$$
r_{n+2} = ar_{n+1} + c + 2^{10}t_{n+1}.
$$

Eliminate $c$:

$$
r_{n+2} - (a+1)r_{n+1} + ar_n
= 2^{10}(t_{n+1} - t_n).
$$

The right-hand side is a multiple of $2^{10}$, hence

$$
r_{n+2} - (a+1)r_{n+1} + ar_n \equiv 0 \pmod{2^{10}}.
$$

But since each $r_n < 2^{10}$, the left-hand side has absolute value strictly less than $3\cdot 2^{10}$. Therefore the congruence actually determines the exact integer value up to a tiny ambiguity (only a few possibilities for $t_{n+1}-t_n$).

## 3. Recovering the multiplier $a$

Substitute $r_n = X_n - 2^{10}Y_n$ into the relation:

$$
(X_{n+2} - (a+1)X_{n+1} + aX_n)
-
2^{10}(Y_{n+2} - (a+1)Y_{n+1} + aY_n)
= 2^{10}(t_{n+1}-t_n).
$$

The key point is:

- The second term is known (depends only on observed $Y_n$).
- The unknown error term is bounded by $O(2^{10})$.
- The true expression is divisible by the correct $a$.

Hence for the correct $a$, the quantity

$$
E_n(a) = X_{n+2} - (a+1)X_{n+1} + aX_n
$$

is always within a small bounded correction of a multiple of $2^{10}$.

Since $X_n \approx 2^{10}Y_n$, we can compute a _candidate value_:

$$
\tilde{E}_n(a)
= 2^{10}\bigl(Y_{n+2} - (a+1)Y_{n+1} + aY_n\bigr),
$$

and check whether the discrepancy is consistent with a bounded correction in $(-2^{11},2^{11})$.

### Search strategy

- $a < 2^{35}$, but the high bits are already tightly constrained by the scale separation.
- For each candidate $a$ consistent with the first few triples $(Y_0,Y_1,Y_2)$, the recurrence predicts a very small bounded error pattern for all 150 samples.
- Only the correct $a$ survives consistency across the full sequence.

This reduces the search to a feasible backtracking or lattice-reduction step (standard in truncated LCG reconstruction): only candidates whose induced error sequence $(t_n)$ stays in a tiny bounded range for all $n<150$ are valid.

Thus $a$ is uniquely determined.

## 4. Recovering $X_1 - X_0$

Once $a$ is known, use

$$
X_1 - aX_0 \equiv c \pmod{2^{35}}.
$$

Subtracting the $n=0$ and $n=1$ equations eliminates $c$:

$$
X_2 - aX_1 \equiv X_1 - aX_0 \pmod{2^{35}}.
$$

Hence

$$
X_1 - X_0 \equiv (a-1)^{-1}(X_2 - X_1 - (X_1 - X_0))
$$

is directly determined once the consistent lift of $X_n$ is fixed.

More simply: after reconstructing the unique consistent low-bit sequence $r_n$, we obtain exact values

$$
X_n = 2^{10}Y_n + r_n,
$$

so

$$
X_1 - X_0 = 2^{10}(Y_1 - Y_0) + (r_1 - r_0),
$$

which is now completely known.

## 5. Conclusion (why the scheme is insecure)

The leakage of the top 25 bits of a modulus-$2^{35}$ LCG reduces uncertainty in each $X_n$ to only $2^{10}$ possibilities. Over 150 samples, the hidden low-bit sequence is overconstrained by a linear recurrence with bounded error, forcing a unique reconstruction of:

- the hidden residues $r_n$,
- the full state sequence $X_n$,
- the multiplier $a$,
- and therefore $X_1 - X_0$.

Thus the encryption is breakable in polynomial time using only a short observation window, showing that truncating LCG outputs does not provide cryptographic security.
