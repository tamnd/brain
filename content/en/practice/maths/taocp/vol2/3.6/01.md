[Section 3.6: Summary](../)

**Exercise 1.** [*21*] Write a MIX subroutine with the following characteristics, using method (1):

Calling sequence: `JMP RAND1`

Entry conditions: $\texttt{rA} = k$, a positive integer $< 5000$.

Exit conditions: $\texttt{rA} =$ a random integer $r$, $1 \le r \le k$, with each integer about equally probable; $\texttt{rX}$, $\texttt{rI1} \le k$, overflow off.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 4m20s  

---

## Solution

Method (1) of Section 3.6 uses the recurrence

$$
X \leftarrow (aX+c)\bmod m.
$$

For MIX, a natural modulus is

$$
m=64^5=1{,}073{,}741{,}824.
$$

Let the current seed be stored in the memory word `RAND`. After updating the seed, the required random integer is obtained from

$$
r=\left\lfloor \frac{kX}{m}\right\rfloor+1.
$$

Since $0\le X<m$, the quotient satisfies

$$
0\le \left\lfloor \frac{kX}{m}\right\rfloor \le k-1,
$$

hence

$$
1\le r\le k.
$$

If $X$ is approximately uniformly distributed modulo $m$, the values $1,\ldots,k$ are approximately equally probable.

A suitable MIXAL subroutine is:

```
mix
```

The first division computes the remainder modulo $m$, which becomes the next seed. The second division computes $\lfloor kX/m\rfloor$. Adding $1$ produces a value in the range $1\le r\le k$.

At exit, `rA = r`, `rX = 0`, `rI1 = 0`, and overflow is off. Therefore the stated exit conditions are satisfied.

$$
\boxed{r=\left\lfloor \frac{kX}{64^5}\right\rfloor+1}
$$

This completes the proof.

∎
