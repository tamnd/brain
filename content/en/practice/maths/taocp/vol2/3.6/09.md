## Part (a)

The routine _run_array_ produces a sequence $\{X_n\}$ defined by a lagged recurrence with lags $100$ and $27$. The essential property used in this exercise is that the update rule is linear over $\mathbb{F}_2$. Reducing all arithmetic modulo $2$, subtraction and addition coincide, so the update has the form

$$
X_{n+100} \equiv X_{n+27} + X_n \pmod 2.
$$

This is not an assumption but a direct consequence of the lagged-Fibonacci structure of _run_array_, where the only operations are addition and subtraction modulo $2^{30}$, hence linearity is preserved mod $2$.

Now consider the formal power series

$$
F_s(z) = \sum_{k \ge 0} X_{s+k} z^k.
$$

Multiplying the recurrence by $z^{n}$ and summing over $n \ge s$ yields

$$
F_s(z)\,(1 + z^{27} + z^{100}) \equiv Q_s(z),
$$

where $Q_s(z)$ is a polynomial determined entirely by the initial 100 values $X_s,\dots,X_{s+99}$.

Thus $F_s(z)$ represents an element of the quotient module

$$
\mathbb{F}_2[z] / (1 + z^{27} + z^{100}).
$$

In this quotient, multiplication by $z$ corresponds to shifting the sequence.

### Structure of $P_s(z)$

The polynomial $P_s(z)$ is a linear projection of a 100-step window of the sequence, written in a reversed but fixed basis of this same 100-dimensional $\mathbb{F}_2$-vector space. The irregular indexing does not change the key fact: every term in $P_s(z)$ is one of the basis coordinates of the state vector at time $s$, hence

$$
P_s(z) \equiv \sum_{i=0}^{99} X_{s+i} z^{\sigma(i)}

for a fixed permutation $\sigma$ induced by the definition.

Since the recurrence identifies the quotient with a 100-dimensional module generated by $1,z,\dots,z^{99}$, every state corresponds to a unique residue class of a polynomial modulo $1+z^{27}+z^{100}$. Therefore there exists a unique exponent $h(s)$ such that the state at time $s$ is the shift of a reference state by $h(s)$, meaning

$$

$$

This follows because multiplication by $z$ corresponds to a one-step shift of the state, so every state lies on a single orbit generated by $z$ in the quotient module.

## Part (b)

Let $V \cong \mathbb{F}_2^{100}$ be the state space of the generator modulo $2$. The transition rule defines a linear map

$$
T : V \to V,

which is invertible because the recurrence has full rank in the lagged-Fibonacci construction.

Thus the state space decomposes into orbits under $T$. The polynomial representation identifies these orbits with multiplication by $z$ in the quotient ring

$$
R = \mathbb{F}_2[z]/(1+z^{27}+z^{100}).
$$

Hence there exists a distinguished reference state $v_0$ such that every reachable state has the form

$$
v(s) = T^{h(s)} v_0.
$$

The map $s \mapsto v(s)$ produced by _run_start_ is linear in the binary digits of $s$, because initialization uses only XOR-type operations. Therefore $v(s)$ is obtained by applying a fixed linear transformation $A$ to the binary vector of $s$.

Since the action of $T$ is cyclic on the reachable subspace, there is an induced identification between states and exponents modulo the period. Therefore $h(s)$ is characterized by

$$
v(s) = T^{h(s)} v_0,
$$

so $h(s)$ is the discrete logarithm of the state $v(s)$ in the cyclic group generated by $T$. Equivalently, writing $s = \sum s_i 2^i$, each bit contributes additively in the group action, so

$$
h(s) \equiv \sum_i s_i \, h(2^i) \pmod{2^{100}-1}.
$$

This gives the required expression: $h(s)$ is the linear combination of seed bits under the group homomorphism induced by initialization.
