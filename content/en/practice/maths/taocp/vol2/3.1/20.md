**Exercise 20.** [**] *[30]* Find all nonnegative $X < 10^{10}$ that lead ultimately via Algorithm K to the self-reproducing number in Table 1.

**Verified:** no

K(X)
$$

denote one application of Algorithm K. Table 1 exhibits the unique self-reproducing number

$$
N=6065038420,
$$

that is,

$$
K(N)=N.
$$

The problem is to determine all $X<10^{10}$ for which some iterate $K^{m}(X)$ equals $N$.

The essential point is that this is a backward-search problem. One must determine every predecessor of $N$, then every predecessor of those predecessors, and continue until no new predecessors occur.

For Algorithm K, a predecessor $Y$ of a value $Z$ must satisfy all digit constraints imposed by the reconstruction steps corresponding to K1, K2, K10, and K11.
