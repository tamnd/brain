Let the trajectory be

$$
X_0,\;X_1=f(X_0),\;X_2=f(X_1),\ldots
$$

and let the eventual cycle have length $\lambda$. For each element $x$ of the set $\{0,1,\ldots,m-1\}$, the value $f(x)$ is chosen independently and uniformly from the same set.

Average cycle length

For a fixed element $x$, the probability that $x$ lies on a cycle of length exactly $k$ is

$$
\frac{(m-1)(m-2)\cdots(m-k+1)}{m^k}
$$

for $1\le k\le m$.

Indeed, after starting at $x$, the first $k-1$ images must be distinct and different from $x$, and then the next image must return to $x$.

The expected cycle length containing a uniformly chosen element is therefore

$$
E(\lambda)=\sum_{k=1}^{m}\frac{(m-1)(m-2)\cdots(m-k+1)}{m^k}.
$$

Writing the product as $\frac{(m-1)!}{(m-k)!}$ and using the standard asymptotic expansion for this sum gives

$$
E(\lambda)=\sqrt{\frac{\pi m}{2}}-\frac13+O(m^{-1/2}).
$$
