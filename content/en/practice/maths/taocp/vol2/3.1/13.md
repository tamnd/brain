**Exercise 13.** [**] [Mg2] If $f(x)$ is chosen at random in the sense of exercise 11, what is the average length of the *longest* cycle obtainable by varying the starting value $X_0$? [*Note:* We have already considered the analogous problem in the case that $f(x)$ is a random permutation; see exercise 1.3.2–23.]

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 32m54s  

---

Let $L_m$ denote the length of the longest cycle in the functional digraph of a random mapping $f$ on an $m$-element set. This is the classical random mapping model of exercise 11. The asymptotic theory of random mappings shows that the largest cycle has order $\sqrt m$; more precisely,

$$
E(L_m)\sim c\sqrt m,
$$

where

$$
c=\sqrt{\frac{\pi}{2}}\int_0^\infty
\left(1-\exp\!\left(-\int_x^\infty \frac{e^{-u}}{u}\,du\right)\right)dx
\approx 0.78248.
$$

Hence the average length of the longest cycle obtainable by varying the starting value $X_0$ is asymptotically

$$
E(L_m)\sim 0.78248\,\sqrt m.
$$

Therefore the longest cycle in a random random-number generator is typically much larger than the average cycle reached from a single starting value, but it is still only of order $\sqrt m$, not of order $m$.

$$
\boxed{E(L_m)\sim 0.78248\,\sqrt m}
$$
