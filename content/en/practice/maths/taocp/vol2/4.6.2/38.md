Let $u(x) = x^n + u_{n-1}x^{n-1} + \cdots + u_1x + u_0$ be a polynomial with integer coefficients, $u_0 \ne 0$, and suppose either

$$
|u_{n-1}| > 1 + |u_{n-2}| + \cdots + |u_0|
$$

or the variant case in the exercise holds. We want to show that $u(x)$ is irreducible over $\mathbb{Z}$.

Let $\alpha$ be any root of $u(x)$. Then

$$
0 = u(\alpha) = \alpha^n + u_{n-1} \alpha^{n-1} + \cdots + u_1 \alpha + u_0.
$$

Take absolute values:

$$
|\alpha|^n = |u_{n-1} \alpha^{n-1} + \cdots + u_1 \alpha + u_0| \le |u_{n-1}| |\alpha|^{n-1} + |u_{n-2}| |\alpha|^{n-2} + \cdots + |u_1| |\alpha| + |u_0|.
$$

Denote $R = |\alpha|$. Then

$$
R^n \le |u_{n-1}| R^{n-1} + |u_{n-2}| R^{n-2} + \cdots + |u_1| R + |u_0|.
$$

Assume $R \ge 1$. Then $R^k \le R^n$ for all $0 \le k \le n$, so

$$
R^n \le (|u_{n-1}| + |u_{n-2}| + \cdots + |u_0|) R^{n-1} = \left( \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} |u_i| \right) R^{n-1}.
$$

Divide both sides by $R^{n-1} > 0$:

$$
R \le \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} |u_i|.
$$

By hypothesis, $|u_{n-1}| > 1 + |u_{n-2}| + \cdots + |u_0|$. Observe that

$$
|u_{n-1}| > 1 + \sum_{i=0}^{n-2} |u_i| \ge 1 + |u_0| + \cdots + |u_{n-2}|,
$$

so

$$
|u_{n-1}| > \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} |u_i| - |u_{n-1}| + 1,
$$

which implies

$$
|u_{n-1}| > \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} |u_i| - |u_{n-1}|.
$$

Hence $|u_{n-1}| > \frac{1}{2} \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} |u_i|$. Therefore $R < 1 + \varepsilon$ for some $\varepsilon < 1$. In particular, all roots satisfy $|\alpha| < 1$ except possibly one root of large absolute value to balance the dominant $x^n$ term.
