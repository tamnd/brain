[Section 4.6.2: Factorization of Polynomials](../)

**Exercise 26.** [*20*] [20] Let $d_1, \ldots, d_r$ be the degrees of the irreducible factors of $u(x)$ modulo $p$, with proper multiplicity, so that $d_1 + \cdots + d_r = \deg(u)$. Explain how to compute the set $\{\deg(v) : v(x) \equiv v(x)^p \pmod{p}$ for some $c(x), v(x)\}$ by performing $O(r)$ operations on binary bit strings of length $n$.

## Solution

Let $u(x) = p_1(x)\cdots p_r(x)$ be squarefree, and let $\deg p_i(x) = d_i$ with $\sum_{i=1}^r d_i = n = \deg(u)$.

Every solution of

$v(x)^p \equiv v(x) \pmod{u(x)}$

corresponds, by the Chinese remainder theorem in Section 4.6.2, to a unique tuple $(s_1,\ldots,s_r)$ with $s_i \in {0,1,\ldots,p-1}$ such that

$v(x) \equiv s_i \pmod{p_i(x)}.$

For each $i$, define the polynomial $e_i(x)$ by the congruences

$e_i(x) \equiv 1 \pmod{p_i(x)}, \qquad e_i(x) \equiv 0 \pmod{p_j(x)} \ (j \ne i).$

Then every solution has the form

$v(x) = \sum_{i=1}^r s_i e_i(x).$

Each $e_i(x)$ can be written explicitly as

$e_i(x) = \frac{u(x)}{p_i(x)} \cdot t_i(x),$

where $t_i(x)$ is the inverse of $\frac{u(x)}{p_i(x)}$ modulo $p_i(x)$. Hence

$\deg(e_i) = \deg(u) - \deg(p_i) = n - d_i.$

Fix a nonzero solution $v(x)$ and let

$S = \{ i : s_i \ne 0 \}.$

Then

$v(x) = \sum_{i \in S} s_i e_i(x).$

Each term $e_i(x)$ has degree $n - d_i$, and these degrees differ whenever the $d_i$ differ. The leading term of $e_i(x)$ cannot cancel with the leading term of $e_j(x)$ for $i \ne j$, since the representations are supported on disjoint residue conditions modulo the $p_i(x)$ and the construction fixes each $e_i(x)$ uniquely as a degree $<n$ representative of its CRT class. Therefore the degree of the sum is determined by the largest degree among the summands:

$\deg(v) = \max_{i \in S} (n - d_i) = n - \min_{i \in S} d_i.$

If $S = \varnothing$, then $v(x) = 0$, giving the degree contribution corresponding to the zero polynomial.

Thus the degree of any solution is either $0$ (for $v(x)=0$) or of the form

$\deg(v) = n - d_i$

for some $i$, since for any fixed $i$ we may choose $S = {i}$.

Conversely, for each $i$, choosing $s_i = 1$ and $s_j = 0$ for $j \ne i$ yields $v(x) = e_i(x)$, which has degree $n - d_i$. Hence all values $n - d_i$ occur.

Therefore the full set of attainable degrees is

$\{ \deg(v) \} = \{0\} \cup \{n - d_i : 1 \le i \le r\}.$

This set can be computed by reading the list $d_1,\ldots,d_r$ once, forming the $r$ values $n - d_i$, and appending $0$, which requires $O(r)$ operations on bit strings of length $n$. ∎
