**Exercise 5.** [*M20*] Equation (6) holds for $k \ge 0$. If possible, give a formula that expresses $X_{n+k}$ in terms of $X_n$ for *negative* values of $k$.

## Solution

Equation (6) asserts that, for $k \ge 0$,

$X_{n+k} = \bigl(a^k X_n + (a^k - 1)c/b\bigr) \bmod m, \qquad b = a-1. \eqno(6)$

We seek an expression for $X_{n+k}$ when $k < 0$. Let $k = -\ell$ with $\ell > 0$. Then we aim to express $X_{n-\ell}$ in terms of $X_n$.

Start from the recurrence

$X_{n} = (a X_{n-1} + c) \bmod m.$

Repeatedly applying this in reverse gives

$X_{n-1} \equiv a^{-1} (X_n - c) \pmod{m},$

where $a^{-1}$ is the multiplicative inverse of $a$ modulo $m$. The existence of $a^{-1} \pmod{m}$ requires $\gcd(a,m) = 1$, which is the standard assumption in the context of maximal-period linear congruential sequences.

Inductively, for $\ell \ge 1$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
X_{n-\ell} &\equiv a^{-1} (X_{n-\ell+1} - c) \pmod{m} \\
&\equiv a^{-2} (X_{n-\ell+2} - c - a c) \pmod{m} \\
&\ \ \vdots \\
&\equiv a^{-\ell} X_n - c \bigl(a^{-\ell} + a^{-\ell+1} + \cdots + a^{-1}\bigr) \pmod{m}.
\end{aligned}
$$

The geometric sum simplifies using $b = a-1$:

$$
\begin{aligned}
a^{-\ell} + a^{-\ell+1} + \cdots + a^{-1} &= a^{-1} (1 - a^{-\ell}) / (1 - a^{-1}) \\
&= (1 - a^{-\ell}) / (a-1) \\
&= (1 - a^{-\ell}) / b.
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence

$$
X_{n-\ell} \equiv a^{-\ell} X_n - c \frac{1 - a^{-\ell}}{b} \pmod{m}.
$$

Replacing $\ell$ by $-k$ gives the formula for negative $k$:

$$
\boxed{X_{n+k} \equiv a^k X_n + \frac{a^k - 1}{b} c \pmod{m}, \qquad k < 0, \ \gcd(a,m) = 1.}
$$

This expression matches the form of Eq. (6) but now allows $k$ to be negative. The factor $a^k$ is interpreted as the modular inverse power $a^{- \lvert k \rvert} \bmod m$, which exists precisely because $\gcd(a,m) = 1$. This completes the proof.
