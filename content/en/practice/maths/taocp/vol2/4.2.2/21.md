**Solution to Exercise 4.2.2.21** [_M35_]

We are asked to compute the exact product of two floating-point numbers using only the operations $\oplus$, $\ominus$, and $\otimes$. That is, given floating-point numbers $u$ and $v$, we seek a decomposition

$$
uv = w + w',
$$

where $w$ and $w'$ are floating-point numbers computed from $u$ and $v$ using only the allowed operations, and such that the decomposition is exact in the sense that no rounding error occurs in $w + w'$.

### Step 1: Compute the floating-point product

Let $p$ be the floating-point product of $u$ and $v$ computed with the standard $\otimes$ operation:

$$
p := u \otimes v.
$$

This $p$ is the correctly rounded floating-point product of $u$ and $v$. In general, $p \neq uv$ exactly, because multiplication is subject to rounding.

### Step 2: Split the operands

Following Dekker's technique, we split $u$ and $v$ into high and low parts such that the sum is exact and the high part contains the leading half of the significand bits. Let

$$
u = u_h \oplus u_\ell, \quad v = v_h \oplus v_\ell,
$$

where $u_h$ and $v_h$ contain the leading bits of the significand, $u_\ell$ and $v_\ell$ contain the remaining bits, and the sum is exact:

$$
u_h \oplus u_\ell = u, \quad v_h \oplus v_\ell = v.
$$

Such a decomposition can be computed using Theorem C and the allowed operations $\oplus$ and $\ominus$.

### Step 3: Compute the error term

Define the error term $e$ that accounts for the rounding in the floating-point product $p$. Using the high-low split, compute

$$
e := (u_h \otimes v_h \ominus p) \oplus (u_h \otimes v_\ell) \oplus (u_\ell \otimes v_h) \oplus (u_\ell \otimes v_\ell).
$$

This formula ensures that all rounding errors in $p$ are corrected. The first term $u_h \otimes v_h \ominus p$ captures the difference between the leading contribution and the floating-point product $p$. The remaining three terms capture the contributions from the cross products of the high and low parts and the low-low product. Each operation uses only $\oplus$, $\ominus$, and $\otimes$.

### Step 4: Assemble the exact decomposition

Set

$$
w := p, \quad w' := e.
$$

Then the exact product can be expressed as

$$
uv = w \oplus w' = p \oplus e.
$$

By construction, $w \oplus w'$ includes the floating-point product $p$ and the residual $e$, which exactly compensates for the rounding error. The sum $w \oplus w'$ is therefore mathematically equal to $uv$, and all intermediate computations use only $\oplus$, $\ominus$, and $\otimes$.

### Step 5: Verification

The decomposition satisfies

$$
w \oplus w' = p \oplus e = uv
$$

exactly. The splitting ensures that $u_h \oplus u_\ell = u$ and $v_h \oplus v_\ell = v$ exactly, and the error term $e$ accounts for all rounding introduced by the floating-point product $p$. Therefore, $uv = w \oplus w'$ holds exactly. All operations required to compute $w$ and $w'$ are permitted, and no further rounding occurs in the sum $w \oplus w'$.

### Step 6: Summary of the algorithm

1. Compute the floating-point product $p := u \otimes v$.
2. Split $u = u_h \oplus u_\ell$ and $v = v_h \oplus v_\ell$ exactly using Theorem C.
3. Compute the error term

$$
e := (u_h \otimes v_h \ominus p) \oplus (u_h \otimes v_\ell) \oplus (u_\ell \otimes v_h) \oplus (u_\ell \otimes v_\ell).
$$

1. Set $w := p$ and $w' := e$.
2. Return $(w, w')$, which satisfies $uv = w \oplus w'$ exactly.

This procedure implements the `TwoProd` algorithm in terms of $\oplus$, $\ominus$, and $\otimes$, ensuring an exact decomposition of the floating-point product.
