[Section 4.2.2: Accuracy of Floating Point Arithmetic](../)

**Exercise 2.** [*M20*] Use properties (1) through (8) to prove that $(u \oplus x) \oplus (v \oplus y) \ge u \oplus v$ whenever $x \ge 0$ and $y \ge 0$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m35s  

---

## Solution

Assume that $x\ge0$ and $y\ge0$. By (6),

$u=u\oplus0,\qquad v=v\oplus0.$

Since $0\le x$, property (8) with $w=u$ gives

$0\oplus u\le x\oplus u.$

By commutativity (2) and (6),

$u=0\oplus u,\qquad x\oplus u=u\oplus x,$

hence

$u\le u\oplus x. \eqno(1)$

Similarly, from $0\le y$ and property (8) with $w=v$,

$0\oplus v\le y\oplus v.$

Using (2) and (6) again,

$v\le v\oplus y. \eqno(2)$

Apply property (8) to inequality (1) with $w=v$:

$u\oplus v\le (u\oplus x)\oplus v. \eqno(3)$

Apply property (8) to inequality (2) with $w=u\oplus x$:

$v\oplus(u\oplus x)\le (v\oplus y)\oplus(u\oplus x). \eqno(4)$

By commutativity (2),

$v\oplus(u\oplus x)=(u\oplus x)\oplus v,$

and

$(v\oplus y)\oplus(u\oplus x)=(u\oplus x)\oplus(v\oplus y).$

Therefore (4) becomes

$(u\oplus x)\oplus v\le (u\oplus x)\oplus(v\oplus y). \eqno(5)$

Combining (3) and (5) yields

$u\oplus v\le (u\oplus x)\oplus(v\oplus y).$

Hence

$\boxed{(u\oplus x)\oplus(v\oplus y)\ge u\oplus v}.$

This completes the proof.
