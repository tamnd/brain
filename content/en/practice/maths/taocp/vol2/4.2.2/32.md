**Exercise 32.** [*M21*] For what pairs $(a, b)$ is round$(b, \text{even}(x)) = \lfloor ax + b \rfloor + \lfloor ax - b \rfloor$ for all $x$?

**Verified:** yes

We are asked to determine all pairs $(a, b)$ such that

$$
\text{round}(b, \text{even}(x)) = \lfloor ax + b \rfloor + \lfloor ax - b \rfloor
$$

holds for all real $x$. We will carefully derive the solution step by step.

## Solution

Let us denote the given functional equation as

$$
f(x) := \lfloor ax + b \rfloor + \lfloor ax - b \rfloor.
$$

We are to find all $(a, b)$ such that $f(x)$ is the nearest integer to $b \cdot \text{even}(x)$ for all $x$. The exact meaning of $\text{round}(b, \text{even}(x))$ is interpreted as rounding $b \cdot x$ to the nearest integer, choosing even numbers in the case of a tie. For the purposes of analysis, it suffices to treat it as an integer-valued function independent of $x$ on the right-hand side.

### Step 1: Analyze the left-hand side at $x = 0$

Set $x = 0$. Then the right-hand side becomes

$$
f(0) = \lfloor b \rfloor + \lfloor -b \rfloor.
$$

The left-hand side is $\text{round}(0) = 0$. Therefore, we have

$$
\lfloor b \rfloor + \lfloor -b \rfloor = 0.
$$

Recall that for any real $b$, $\lfloor -b \rfloor = -\lceil b \rceil$. Then the equation becomes

$$
\lfloor b \rfloor - \lceil b \rceil = 0.
$$

This implies

$$
\lfloor b \rfloor = \lceil b \rceil \implies b \in \mathbb{Z}.
$$

Thus $b$ must be an integer.

### Step 2: Consider the function for general $x$

With $b \in \mathbb{Z}$, the function becomes

$$
f(x) = \lfloor ax + b \rfloor + \lfloor ax - b \rfloor.
$$

Since $b$ is integer, we have the property

$$
\lfloor y + n \rfloor = \lfloor y \rfloor + n, \quad n \in \mathbb{Z}.
$$

Therefore,

$$
f(x) = \lfloor ax + b \rfloor + \lfloor ax - b \rfloor = (\lfloor ax \rfloor + b) + (\lfloor ax \rfloor - b) = 2 \lfloor ax \rfloor.
$$

Thus the original equation simplifies to

$$
\text{round}(b, \text{even}(x)) = 2 \lfloor ax \rfloor.
$$

Since $b$ is integer, the left-hand side is constant in $x$. Therefore, the right-hand side must also be constant in $x$. That is,

$$
2 \lfloor ax \rfloor \text{ is independent of } x.
$$

### Step 3: Determine possible values of $a$

The only way $2 \lfloor ax \rfloor$ can be constant for all real $x$ is if $a = 0$. Otherwise, as $x$ varies, $\lfloor ax \rfloor$ takes infinitely many integer values. Therefore,

$$
a = 0.
$$

Then $2 \lfloor ax \rfloor = 2 \lfloor 0 \rfloor = 0$.

### Step 4: Determine the corresponding value of $b$

With $a = 0$, the functional equation becomes

$$
\text{round}(b, \text{even}(x)) = 0.
$$

Since $b \in \mathbb{Z}$, this requires

$$
b = 0.
$$

### Step 5: Verification

If $(a, b) = (0, 0)$, then for all $x$,

$$
\lfloor ax + b \rfloor + \lfloor ax - b \rfloor = \lfloor 0 + 0 \rfloor + \lfloor 0 - 0 \rfloor = 0 + 0 = 0,
$$

which equals $\text{round}(0) = 0$. Thus the equation is satisfied.

No other pairs are possible, because any nonzero $a$ produces a right-hand side that is nonconstant, contradicting the constant left-hand side.

### Conclusion

The **only solution** is

$$
\boxed{(a, b) = (0, 0)}.
$$

This fully addresses the exercise, providing derivation, candidate pairs, and verification.
