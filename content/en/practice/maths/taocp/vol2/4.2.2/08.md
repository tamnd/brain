[Section 4.2.2: Accuracy of Floating Point Arithmetic](../)

**Exercise 8.** &#9654; [*20*] Let $\epsilon = 0.0001$; which of the relations

$$u < v \quad \text{(c)}, \qquad u \sim v \quad \text{(c)}, \qquad u > v \quad \text{(c)}, \qquad u \approx v \quad \text{(c)}$$

hold for the following pairs of one's complement, eight-digit floating point numbers?

a) $u = (1, +.34415927)$, $v = (1, +.31416000)$;

b) $u = (0, +.99997000)$, $v = (1, +.10000039)$;

c) $u = (24, +.60221400)$, $v = (27, +.00006022)$;

d) $u = (24, +.60221400)$, $v = (31, +.10000000)$;

e) $u = (24, +.60221400)$, $v = (28, +.10000000)$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m30s  

---

## Solution

In one's complement notation, the value represented by $(e,+.f)$ is $+.f\times 2^e$. Since all fractions listed are positive, the ordering relation is determined by their numerical values.

The relations $\sim$ and $\approx$ are those introduced in Section 4.2.2 with $\epsilon=0.0001$. Thus

$u\sim v\quad\text{(c)}$

means that

$\left|\frac{u-v}{u}\right|\le\epsilon,$

while

$u\approx v\quad\text{(c)}$

means that

$\left|\frac{u-v}{v}\right|\le\epsilon.$

The numerical values are now examined case by case.

For part (a),

$u=.34415927\times2=.68831854,$

$v=.31416000\times2=.62832000.$

Hence

$u-v=.05999854>0,$

so

$u>v\quad\text{(c)}.$

Furthermore,

=\frac{.05999854}{.68831854} \approx .08717>10^{-4},$$
and
$$\left|\frac{u-v}{v}\right| =\frac{.05999854}{.62832000} \approx .09549>10^{-4}.$$
Therefore neither $u\sim v$ (c) nor $u\approx v$ (c) holds. Thus
$$\boxed{u>v\text{ (c)}}.$$
For part (b),
$$u=.99997000,$$ $$v=.10000039\times2=.20000078.$$
Hence
$$u-v=.79996922>0,$$
so
$$u>v\quad\text{(c)}.$$
Also,
$$\left|\frac{u-v}{u}\right| =\frac{.79996922}{.99997000} \approx .79999>10^{-4},$$
and
$$\left|\frac{u-v}{v}\right| =\frac{.79996922}{.20000078} \approx3.99983>10^{-4}.$$
Therefore neither approximate-equality relation holds. Hence
$$\boxed{u>v\text{ (c)}}.$$
For part (c),
$$u=.60221400\times2^{24},$$ $$v=.00006022\times2^{27} =(.00006022\times8)\times2^{24} =.00048176\times2^{24}.$$
Since
$$.60221400>.00048176,$$
we have
$$u>v\quad\text{(c)}.$$
Moreover,
$$u-v=(.60221400-.00048176)\times2^{24} =.60173224\times2^{24},$$
and therefore
$$\left|\frac{u-v}{u}\right| =\frac{.60173224}{.60221400} \approx .99920>10^{-4},$$
while
$$\left|\frac{u-v}{v}\right| =\frac{.60173224}{.00048176} \approx1249.0>10^{-4}.$$
Thus neither $u\sim v$ (c) nor $u\approx v$ (c) is valid. Hence
$$\boxed{u>v\text{ (c)}}.$$
For part (d),
$$u=.60221400\times2^{24},$$ $$v=.10000000\times2^{31} =(.10000000\times128)\times2^{24} =12.8000000\times2^{24}.$$
Since
$$12.8000000>.60221400,$$
it follows that
$$u<v\quad\text{(c)}.$$
Also,
$$v-u=(12.8000000-.60221400)\times2^{24} =12.1977860\times2^{24},$$
whence
$$\left|\frac{u-v}{u}\right| =\frac{12.1977860}{.60221400} \approx20.256>10^{-4},$$
and
$$\left|\frac{u-v}{v}\right| =\frac{12.1977860}{12.8000000} \approx .9530>10^{-4}.$$
Neither approximate-equality relation holds. Therefore
$$\boxed{u<v\text{ (c)}}.$$
For part (e),
$$u=.60221400\times2^{24},$$ $$v=.10000000\times2^{28} =(.10000000\times16)\times2^{24} =1.60000000\times2^{24}.$$
Since
$$1.60000000>.60221400,$$
we obtain
$$u<v\quad\text{(c)}.$$
Furthermore,
$$v-u=(1.60000000-.60221400)\times2^{24} =.99778600\times2^{24},$$
so
$$\left|\frac{u-v}{u}\right| =\frac{.99778600}{.60221400} \approx1.6569>10^{-4},$$
and
$$\left|\frac{u-v}{v}\right| =\frac{.99778600}{1.60000000} \approx.6236>10^{-4}.$$
Hence neither $u\sim v$ (c) nor $u\approx v$ (c) is satisfied. The answers are
\boxed{
\begin{array}{c|c}
\text{Part}&\text{Valid relation}\
\hline
(a)&u>v\text{ (c)}\
(b)&u>v\text{ (c)}\
(c)&u>v\text{ (c)}\
(d)&u<v\text{ (c)}\
(e)&u<v\text{ (c)}
\end{array}}
$$$$
