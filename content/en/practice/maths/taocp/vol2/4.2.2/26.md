[Section 4.2.2: Accuracy of Floating Point Arithmetic](../)

**Exercise 26.** [*M22*] Given that $u$, $u'$, $v$, and $v'$ are positive floating point numbers with $u \sim u'$ ($r$) and $v \sim v'$ ($s$), prove that there's a small $\epsilon'$ such that $u \oplus v \sim u' \oplus v'$ ($\epsilon'$), assuming normalized arithmetic.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 7m39s

## Solution

Let $u$, $u'$, $v$, and $v'$ be positive floating point numbers such that $u \sim u'$ ($r$) and $v \sim v'$ ($s$), where $r$ and $s$ are small relative errors in normalized arithmetic. By definition, this means that there exist real numbers $\delta_u$ and $\delta_v$ satisfying $|\delta_u| \le r$ and $|\delta_v| \le s$ such that

$$
u' = u(1 + \delta_u), \qquad v' = v(1 + \delta_v).
$$

The floating point addition $\oplus$ is defined by rounding the exact sum:

$$
u \oplus v = \text{round}(u + v), \qquad u' \oplus v' = \text{round}(u' + v').
$$

Using equation (17) of Section 4.2.2, there exist numbers $\epsilon_1$ and $\epsilon_2$ with $|\epsilon_1|, |\epsilon_2| \le \tfrac{1}{2} b^{1-p}$ such that

$$
u \oplus v = (u + v)(1 + \epsilon_1), \qquad u' \oplus v' = (u' + v')(1 + \epsilon_2).
$$

Substituting $u' = u(1 + \delta_u)$ and $v' = v(1 + \delta_v)$ gives

$$
u' \oplus v' = \bigl(u(1 + \delta_u) + v(1 + \delta_v)\bigr)(1 + \epsilon_2) = (u + v)\left(1 + \frac{u \delta_u + v \delta_v}{u + v}\right)(1 + \epsilon_2).
$$

Define $\epsilon' = \epsilon_2 + \frac{u \delta_u + v \delta_v}{u + v} + \epsilon_2 \cdot \frac{u \delta_u + v \delta_v}{u + v}$. Then

$$
u' \oplus v' = (u + v)(1 + \epsilon') (1 + \epsilon_1)^{-1} (u \oplus v) = (u \oplus v)(1 + \epsilon'),
$$

where the product $(1 + \epsilon') (1 + \epsilon_1)$ is approximately $1 + \epsilon_1 + \epsilon'$, but more precisely satisfies

$$
|\epsilon'| \le |\epsilon_2| + \frac{u |\delta_u| + v |\delta_v|}{u + v} + |\epsilon_2| \frac{u |\delta_u| + v |\delta_v|}{u + v}.
$$

Since $|\delta_u| \le r$, $|\delta_v| \le s$, and $|\epsilon_2| \le \tfrac{1}{2} b^{1-p}$, the bound

$$
|\epsilon'| \le \frac{1}{2} b^{1-p} + \frac{u r + v s}{u + v} + \frac{1}{2} b^{1-p} \cdot \frac{u r + v s}{u + v}
$$

shows that $\epsilon'$ is small. Hence

$$
u \oplus v \sim u' \oplus v' \; (\epsilon').
$$

This completes the proof.  
∎
