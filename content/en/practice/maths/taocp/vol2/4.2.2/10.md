[Section 4.2.2: Accuracy of Floating Point Arithmetic](../)

**Exercise 10.** &#9654; [*M25*] (W. M. Kahan.) A certain computer performs floating point arithmetic without proper rounding, and, in fact, its floating point multiplication routine ignores all but the first $p$ most significant digits of the $2p$-digit product $f_u f_v$. (Thus when $f_u f_v < 1/b$, the least-significant digit of $u \otimes v$ always comes out to be zero, due to subsequent normalization.) Show that this causes the monotonicity of multiplication to fail; in other words, exhibit positive normalized floating point numbers $u$, $v$, and $w$ such that $u < v$ but $u \otimes w > v \otimes w$ on this machine.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 6m09s

## Solution

Let the radix be $b$ and let the precision be $p$. By hypothesis, multiplication is not rounded. Instead, after forming the $2p$-digit product $f_uf_v$, the machine simply discards all digits beyond the first $p$ most significant digits.

Choose normalized fractions

$$
f_u=.100\ldots 001,\qquad
f_v=.100\ldots 002,
$$

where the last displayed digits are in the $p$th place. Then $u<v$.

Let

$$
f_w=.b-1\, b-1\,\ldots\, b-1,
$$

with $p$ digits, so that

$$
f_w=1-b^{-p}.
$$

Write

$$
f_u=b^{-1}+b^{-p},
\qquad
f_v=b^{-1}+2b^{-p}.
$$

Hence

$$
f_uf_w
=(b^{-1}+b^{-p})(1-b^{-p})
=b^{-1}+b^{-p}-b^{-p-1}-b^{-2p},
$$

and

$$
f_vf_w
=(b^{-1}+2b^{-p})(1-b^{-p})
=b^{-1}+2b^{-p}-b^{-p-1}-2b^{-2p}.
$$

The first product has base-$b$ expansion beginning

$$
.100\ldots 00(b-1)\cdots,
$$

because

$$
b^{-p}-b^{-p-1}
=(b-1)b^{-p-1}.
$$

Therefore, when all digits beyond the first $p$ significant digits are discarded, the machine produces

$$
u\otimes w=.100\ldots 00.
$$

The second product satisfies

$$
2b^{-p}-b^{-p-1}
=(2b-1)b^{-p-1}
=b^{-p}+(b-1)b^{-p-1}.
$$

Thus its expansion begins

$$
.100\ldots 01(b-1)\cdots .
$$

The digit $1$ occurs in the $(p+1)$st position. Since the machine keeps only the first $p$ significant digits of the product, that digit is discarded. After truncation the retained $p$ digits are

$$
.100\ldots 00,
$$

but now the product is strictly larger than $b^{-1}$, so normalization shifts the fraction one place to the left. The resulting normalized fraction is

$$
.099\ldots 99,
$$

that is, its least significant retained digit is $0$, as stated in the problem description for the case $f_uf_v<1/b$.

Consequently,

$$
u\otimes w
=
.100\ldots 00,
\qquad
v\otimes w
=
.099\ldots 99,
$$

and therefore

$$
u\otimes w>v\otimes w,
$$

even though

$$
u<v.
$$

This exhibits positive normalized floating point numbers for which monotonicity of multiplication fails on the machine described.

This completes the proof.
