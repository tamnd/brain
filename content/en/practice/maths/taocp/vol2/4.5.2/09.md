## Solution

We are asked to compute $\gcd(31408, 2718)$ using Algorithm B and then to find integers $m$ and $n$ such that

$31408 \, m + 2718 \, n = \gcd(31408, 2718)$

using Algorithm X. We proceed step by step, following the notation and procedures from Section 4.5.2.

### Step 1: Compute $\gcd(31408, 2718)$ using Algorithm B

Algorithm B is the standard Euclidean algorithm: given positive integers $u$ and $v$ with $u > v$, we repeatedly replace $u \leftarrow v$ and $v \leftarrow u \bmod v$ until $v = 0$, at which point $\gcd(u, v) = u$.

Set $A = 31408$, $C = 2718$.

Compute the remainders:

1. $31408 \bmod 2718$.

Divide: $31408 \div 2718 = 11$ remainder $31408 - 11 \cdot 2718 = 31408 - 29898 = 1510$.

Update: $A \leftarrow 2718$, $C \leftarrow 1510$.
2. $2718 \bmod 1510 = 2718 - 1 \cdot 1510 = 1208$.

Update: $A \leftarrow 1510$, $C \leftarrow 1208$.
3. $1510 \bmod 1208 = 1510 - 1 \cdot 1208 = 302$.

Update: $A \leftarrow 1208$, $C \leftarrow 302$.
4. $1208 \bmod 302 = 1208 - 4 \cdot 302 = 1208 - 1208 = 0$.

Update: $A \leftarrow 302$, $C \leftarrow 0$.

The algorithm terminates with $C = 0$. Therefore,

$\gcd(31408, 2718) = 302.$

### Step 2: Find integers $m$ and $n$ such that $31408 , m + 2718 , n = 302$ using Algorithm X

Algorithm X is the extended Euclidean algorithm. We record the sequence of divisions and back-substitute to express the gcd as a linear combination.

From Step 1, the division sequence is:

$$
\begin{aligned}
31408 &= 11 \cdot 2718 + 1510 \\
2718 &= 1 \cdot 1510 + 1208 \\
1510 &= 1 \cdot 1208 + 302 \\
1208 &= 4 \cdot 302 + 0
\end{aligned}
$$

We now express each remainder in terms of the previous numbers:

1. $302 = 1510 - 1 \cdot 1208$.
2. Substitute $1208 = 2718 - 1 \cdot 1510$:

$$
302 = 1510 - (2718 - 1510) = 1510 - 2718 + 1510 = 2 \cdot 1510 - 2718
$$

1. Substitute $1510 = 31408 - 11 \cdot 2718$:

$$
302 = 2 \cdot (31408 - 11 \cdot 2718) - 2718 = 2 \cdot 31408 - 22 \cdot 2718 - 2718 = 2 \cdot 31408 - 23 \cdot 2718
$$

Hence, integers satisfying the linear combination are:

$$
m = 2, \quad n = -23
$$

We can check:

$$
31408 \cdot 2 + 2718 \cdot (-23) = 62816 - 62514 = 302
$$

which agrees with the gcd.

### Answer

$$
\gcd(31408, 2718) = 302, \quad m = 2, \quad n = -23
$$

$$
\boxed{302, \ m = 2, \ n = -23}
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
