## Setup

Exercise 27 established equation (58), expressing $\psi_n$ in terms of Bernoulli numbers. In the notation of that equation,

$$
\psi_n=\frac{2(2n)!}{(2\pi)^{2n}}\zeta(2n),
$$

where

$$
\zeta(2n)=\sum_{k\ge1}\frac1{k^{2n}}.
$$

The problem asks for the asymptotic behavior of $\psi_n$ as $n\to\infty$. Equation (58) is the classical Euler formula relating Bernoulli numbers and $\zeta(2n)$.

## Solution

Since

$$
\zeta(2n)=1+\sum_{k\ge2}\frac1{k^{2n}},
$$

we have

$$
0<\zeta(2n)-1
<\sum_{k\ge2}\frac1{2^{2n}}\frac1{k^0}
\le
\sum_{m\ge1}\frac1{2^{2nm}}
=
\frac{2^{-2n}}{1-2^{-2n}}.
$$

Hence

$$
\zeta(2n)\to1.
$$

Therefore equation (58) yields

$$
\psi_n
\sim
\frac{2(2n)!}{(2\pi)^{2n}}.
$$

To estimate the factorial, apply Stirling's formula, as suggested by the hint:

$$
(2n)!
\sim
\sqrt{4\pi n}\left(\frac{2n}{e}\right)^{2n}.
$$

Substituting into the preceding expression gives

$$
\begin{aligned}
\psi_n
&\sim
\frac{2\sqrt{4\pi n}}{(2\pi)^{2n}}
\left(\frac{2n}{e}\right)^{2n} \\
&=
4\sqrt{\pi n}
\left(\frac{n}{\pi e}\right)^{2n}.
\end{aligned}
$$

Thus

$$
\boxed{\psi_n\sim4\sqrt{\pi n}\left(\frac{n}{\pi e}\right)^{2n}}.
$$

Equivalently,

$$
\log \psi_n
=
2n\log n
-2n(1+\log\pi)
+\frac12\log(\pi n)
+\log4
+o(1).
$$

## Verification

Starting from

$$
\psi_n=\frac{2(2n)!}{(2\pi)^{2n}}\zeta(2n),
$$

the factor $\zeta(2n)$ tends to $1$, because every term except the first in the defining series is exponentially small:

$$
\zeta(2n)-1
=
O(2^{-2n}).
$$

Hence the asymptotics of $\psi_n$ are determined entirely by the factorial term.

Applying Stirling's formula,

$$
(2n)!
=
\sqrt{4\pi n}
\left(\frac{2n}{e}\right)^{2n}
(1+o(1)),
$$

and simplifying,

$$
\frac{2(2n)!}{(2\pi)^{2n}}
=
4\sqrt{\pi n}
\left(\frac{n}{\pi e}\right)^{2n}
(1+o(1)).
$$

Multiplication by $\zeta(2n)=1+o(1)$ leaves the same leading term. Therefore the asymptotic formula above is correct.

## Notes

The same argument shows the sharper estimate

$$
\psi_n
=
4\sqrt{\pi n}
\left(\frac{n}{\pi e}\right)^{2n}
\bigl(1+O(1/n)\bigr),
$$

when the first correction term in Stirling's expansion is included.

This completes the proof.

∎
