**Exercise 6.** [*M22*] Given that $u$ and $v$ are random positive integers, find the mean and the standard deviation of the quantity $A$ that enters into the timing of Program B. (This is the number of right shifts applied to both $u$ and $v$ during the preparatory phase.)

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m42s  

---

## Solution

Let $u$ and $v$ be independent random positive integers chosen with uniform density over the positive integers. Let $v_2(n)$ denote the exponent of $2$ in the canonical factorization of $n$, so that $n = 2^{v_2(n)} n'$ with $n'$ odd.

In Program B, the preparatory phase repeatedly replaces $(u,v)$ by $(u/2, v/2)$ while both are even. The number of such simultaneous right shifts is therefore

$A = \min\bigl(v_2(u), v_2(v)\bigr).$

Let $X = v_2(u)$ and $Y = v_2(v)$. For a random positive integer $n$, the probability that $2^k \mid n$ is $2^{-k}$, since exactly half of the integers are divisible by $2$, one quarter by $4$, and so on. Hence

$\Pr(X \ge k) = 2^{-k}, \quad \Pr(Y \ge k) = 2^{-k},$

and $X$ and $Y$ are independent.

For $k \ge 0$,

$\Pr(A \ge k) = \Pr(X \ge k \ \text{and}\ Y \ge k) = 2^{-k} \cdot 2^{-k} = 4^{-k}.$

Thus,

$\Pr(A = k) = \Pr(A \ge k) - \Pr(A \ge k+1) = 4^{-k} - 4^{-(k+1)} = \frac{3}{4^{k+1}}.$

### Mean

The expected value is

$\mathbb{E}[A] = \sum_{k \ge 0} k \cdot \frac{3}{4^{k+1}} = \frac{3}{4} \sum_{k \ge 0} k \left(\frac{1}{4}\right)^k.$

Using the identity

$\sum_{k \ge 0} k r^k = \frac{r}{(1-r)^2} \quad (|r|<1),$

with $r = \frac{1}{4}$ gives

$\sum_{k \ge 0} k \left(\frac{1}{4}\right)^k = \frac{\frac{1}{4}}{\left(1-\frac{1}{4}\right)^2} = \frac{\frac{1}{4}}{\left(\frac{3}{4}\right)^2} = \frac{4}{9}.$

Hence

$\mathbb{E}[A] = \frac{3}{4} \cdot \frac{4}{9} = \frac{1}{3}.$

### Second moment and variance

Compute

$\mathbb{E}[A^2] = \sum_{k \ge 0} k^2 \cdot \frac{3}{4^{k+1}} = \frac{3}{4} \sum_{k \ge 0} k^2 \left(\frac{1}{4}\right)^k.$

Using the identity

$\sum_{k \ge 0} k^2 r^k = \frac{r(1+r)}{(1-r)^3},$

with $r=\frac{1}{4}$ yields

= \frac{\frac{1}{4}\cdot \frac{5}{4}}{\left(\frac{3}{4}\right)^3} = \frac{5}{16} \cdot \frac{64}{27} = \frac{20}{27}.$$
Thus
$$\mathbb{E}[A^2] = \frac{3}{4} \cdot \frac{20}{27} = \frac{5}{9}.$$
The variance is
$$\mathrm{Var}(A) = \mathbb{E}[A^2] - \mathbb{E}[A]^2 = \frac{5}{9} - \frac{1}{9} = \frac{4}{9}.$$
Hence the standard deviation is
$$\sigma_A = \sqrt{\frac{4}{9}} = \frac{2}{3}.$$ $$\boxed{\mathbb{E}[A] = \frac{1}{3}, \quad \sigma_A = \frac{2}{3}}$$ This completes the solution. ∎
