[Section 4.5.2: The Greatest Common Divisor](../)

**Exercise 20.** [*M37*] [M37] Let $u$ and $v$ be odd integers, independently and uniformly distributed in the ranges $2^{n'} \le u < 2^{n'+1}$, $2^{n'} \le v < 2^{n'+1}$. What is the exact probability that a single subtract-and-shift cycle in Algorithm B reduces $u$ and $v$ to the ranges $2^{n'} \le u < 2^{n'+1}$, $2^{n'} \le v < 2^{n'+1}$, and $n$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 16m42s  

---

## Setup

Let

$$
M=2^{n'},
\qquad
\Omega=\{x:\; M\le x<2M,\; x\ \text{odd}\}.
$$

The set $\Omega$ contains

$$
N=2^{n'-1}
$$

elements. The integers $u$ and $v$ are chosen independently and uniformly from $\Omega$, hence there are $N^2$ ordered pairs $(u,v)$.

In Algorithm B, when $u$ and $v$ are odd and unequal, a subtract-and-shift cycle replaces the larger argument by

$$
w=\frac{|u-v|}{2^{\nu_2(|u-v|)}},
$$

where $\nu_2(m)$ is the exponent of the highest power of $2$ dividing $m$. The other argument remains odd and still lies in $\Omega$.

Fix an integer $n<n'$. We must determine the probability that after one subtract-and-shift cycle the new odd value $w$ lies in the range

$$
2^n\le w<2^{n+1}.
$$

Since the unchanged argument remains in $\Omega$, this is exactly the probability requested.

## Solution

Let $w$ be a fixed odd integer satisfying

$$
2^n\le w<2^{n+1}.
$$

Write

$$
|u-v|=2^t w,
\qquad t\ge1.
$$

Since $u,v\in[M,2M)$, we have $|u-v|<M$, therefore

$$
2^t w<M.
$$

Define

$$
T(w)=\left\lfloor
\log_2\frac{M-1}{w}
\right\rfloor .
$$

Then $t$ may take precisely the values

$$
1\le t\le T(w).
$$

For a fixed difference

$$
d=2^t w,
$$

the number of ordered pairs $(u,v)\in\Omega^2$ satisfying $|u-v|=d$ is

$$
2\Bigl(N-\frac d2\Bigr).
$$

Indeed, among the $N$ odd numbers in $\Omega$, exactly $N-d/2$ pairs satisfy $u-v=d$, and the same number satisfy $v-u=d$.

Hence the number of ordered pairs producing the reduced odd value $w$ is

$$
\sum_{t=1}^{T(w)}
2\Bigl(N-2^{t-1}w\Bigr).
$$

Using $N=M/2$, this becomes

$$
\sum_{t=1}^{T(w)}
\left(M-2^t w\right)
=
T(w)M-2w\left(2^{T(w)}-1\right).
$$

Therefore the total number of ordered pairs for which the reduced odd value lies in the interval $[2^n,2^{n+1})$ equals

$$
\sum_{\substack{
w\ \text{odd}\\
2^n\le w<2^{n+1}
}}
\Bigl(
T(w)M
-
2w\bigl(2^{T(w)}-1\bigr)
\Bigr).
$$

Dividing by the total number $N^2=2^{2n'-2}$ of ordered pairs yields the exact probability:

$$
P_{n',n}
=
\frac{
\displaystyle
\sum_{\substack{
w\ \text{odd}\\
2^n\le w<2^{n+1}
}}
\Bigl(
T(w)2^{n'}
-
2w\bigl(2^{T(w)}-1\bigr)
\Bigr)
}
{2^{\,2n'-2}},
$$

where

$$
T(w)=
\left\lfloor
\log_2\frac{2^{n'}-1}{w}
\right\rfloor .
$$

Thus

$$
\boxed{
P_{n',n}
=
\frac{
\displaystyle
\sum_{\substack{
w\ \text{odd}\\
2^n\le w<2^{n+1}
}}
\left[
2^{n'}\!\left\lfloor
\log_2\frac{2^{n'}-1}{w}
\right\rfloor
-
2w
\left(
2^{\left\lfloor
\log_2\frac{2^{n'}-1}{w}
\right\rfloor}
-1
\right)
\right]
}
{2^{\,2n'-2}}
}.
$$

## Verification

For a fixed odd $w$, every pair counted in the formula satisfies

$$
|u-v|=2^t w
$$

for some admissible $t$, hence the subtract-and-shift cycle produces exactly the odd number $w$.

Conversely, if a subtract-and-shift cycle produces $w$, then

$$
|u-v|=2^t w
$$

with $t\ge1$ and $2^t w<M$, so the pair appears in exactly one term of the count above.

Therefore the numerator counts precisely the ordered pairs whose reduced odd value lies in the interval $[2^n,2^{n+1})$, and division by $N^2$ gives the required probability.

## Notes

The counting argument depends only on the fact that the odd numbers in $\Omega$ form an arithmetic progression of common difference $2$. The same method yields the complete distribution of the reduced odd value after one subtract-and-shift cycle of Algorithm B.
