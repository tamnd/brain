**Exercise 29.** &#9654; [*HM46*] *[HM46]* [R. P. Brent.] Find $G_1(x)$, the distribution of $\min(u, v)/\max(u, v)$ after the first subtract-and-shift cycle of Algorithm B as defined in (35). *Hint:* Let $S_{n+1}(x) = \sum_{k=2}^{\infty} 2^{-k} G_1(1/(1+2^k x))$, and use the method of Mellin transforms for harmonic sums [see P. Flajolet, X. Gourdon, and P. Dumas, *Theor. Comp. Sci.* **144** (1995), 3–58].

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 5m36s

Let

$$
R=\frac{\min(u,v)}{\max(u,v)}\in[0,1].
$$

In Brent's probabilistic model for the binary gcd algorithm, let

$$
g_n(x)=\Pr(R_n\ge x),
\qquad
G_n(x)=\Pr(R_n\le x)=1-g_n(x).
$$

The initial ratio is uniformly distributed on $[0,1]$, hence

$$
g_0(x)=1-x,
\qquad
G_0(x)=x.
$$

The operator governing one subtract-and-shift cycle is Brent's operator

$$
(Fh)(x)
=
\sum_{k\ge1}2^{-k}
\left(
h\!\left(\frac{x}{x+2^k}\right)
-
h\!\left(\frac1{1+2^k x}\right)
\right).
$$

Therefore

$$
g_1=F(g_0).
$$

The exercise asks for $G_1$, the distribution after the first cycle.

## 1. Compute $g_1$

Substituting $g_0(x)=1-x$,

$$
g_1(x)
=
\sum_{k\ge1}2^{-k}
\left[
\left(1-\frac{x}{x+2^k}\right)
-
\left(1-\frac1{1+2^k x}\right)
\right].
$$

Hence

$$
g_1(x)
=
\sum_{k\ge1}2^{-k}
\left(
\frac1{1+2^k x}
-
\frac{x}{x+2^k}
\right).
$$

A direct simplification gives

$$
\frac1{1+2^k x}
-
\frac{x}{x+2^k}
=
\frac{2^k(1-x^2)}
     {(1+2^k x)(x+2^k)},
$$

so that

$$
g_1(x)
=
(1-x^2)
\sum_{k\ge1}
\frac1{(1+2^k x)(x+2^k)}.
$$

Using

$$
\frac1{(x+2^k)(1+2^k x)}
=
\frac1{1-x^2}
\left(
\frac1{x+2^k}
-
\frac{x}{1+2^k x}
\right),
$$

we obtain the simpler form

$$
\boxed{
g_1(x)
=
\sum_{k\ge1}
\left(
\frac1{x+2^k}
-
\frac{x}{1+2^k x}
\right).
}
$$

This already satisfies

$$
g_1(0)=1,\qquad g_1(1)=0,
$$

as required for a survival function.

## 2. Obtain the distribution function $G_1$

Since $G_1=1-g_1$,

$$
G_1(x)
=
1-
\sum_{k\ge1}
\left(
\frac1{x+2^k}
-
\frac{x}{1+2^k x}
\right).
$$

Equivalently,

$$
\boxed{
G_1(x)
=
1-(1-x^2)
\sum_{k\ge1}
\frac1{(x+2^k)(1+2^k x)},
\qquad 0\le x\le1.
}
$$

This is the distribution after one subtract-and-shift cycle.

## 3. Connection with the hint

The hint introduces harmonic sums of the form

$$
S(x)
=
\sum_{k\ge2}
2^{-k}
G_1\!\left(\frac1{1+2^k x}\right),
$$

which are exactly of the type

$$
\sum_k \lambda_k\, g(\mu_k x)
$$

treated by Mellin-transform methods. The Mellin transform separates the dyadic frequencies $2^k$ from the base function and yields a Dirichlet factor

$$
\sum_{k\ge2}2^{-k(1+s)}
=
\frac{2^{-2(1+s)}}{1-2^{-(1+s)}}.
$$

That is the harmonic-sum structure referred to in the exercise. For the first iterate, however, no further inversion is needed: substituting $g_0(x)=1-x$ into Brent's operator gives the explicit formula above.

Therefore

$$
\boxed{
G_1(x)
=
1-
\sum_{k\ge1}
\left(
\frac1{x+2^k}
-
\frac{x}{1+2^k x}
\right)
=
1-(1-x^2)
\sum_{k\ge1}
\frac1{(x+2^k)(1+2^k x)}.
}
$$

This is the correct distribution of $\min(u,v)/\max(u,v)$ after the first subtract-and-shift cycle.
