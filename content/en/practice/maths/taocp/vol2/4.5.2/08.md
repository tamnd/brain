**Exercise 8.** &#9654; [*M28*] Show that in Program B, the average value of $E$ is approximately equal to $\frac{1}{2}C_{\text{ave}}$, where $C_{\text{ave}}$ is the average value of $C$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m29s  

---

## Setup

Program B in Section 4.5.2 uses the operation

$E \leftarrow A \bmod C$

at each reduction step, where $C$ is the current divisor and $E$ is the resulting remainder. The quantity $C_{\text{ave}}$ denotes the average value of $C$ over all states of the algorithm under the probabilistic model in which inputs behave like independent random integers in a large range.

The goal is to show that the average value of $E$ satisfies

$E_{\text{ave}} \approx \frac{1}{2} C_{\text{ave}}.$

The average is taken over the distribution induced by the Euclidean algorithm dynamics in Program B.

## Solution

Fix a value of $C$. Consider $A$ ranging over a long interval of integers much larger than $C$. Write

$A = qC + r,$

where $0 \le r < C$ and $r = A \bmod C$. For fixed $C$, the remainder $r$ is determined by the residue class of $A$ modulo $C$.

As $A$ ranges over consecutive integers, each residue class modulo $C$ occurs equally often, so each value

$r \in \{0, 1, \dots, C-1\}$

occurs with equal frequency $1/C$. The conditional mean of $E$ given $C$ is therefore

$\mathbb{E}[E \mid C] = \frac{1}{C}\sum_{r=0}^{C-1} r.$

The sum evaluates to

$\sum_{r=0}^{C-1} r = \frac{C(C-1)}{2},$

hence

$\mathbb{E}[E \mid C] = \frac{C-1}{2} = \frac{1}{2}C - \frac{1}{2}.$

Taking expectation over the distribution of $C$ gives

$E_{\text{ave}} = \mathbb{E}[\mathbb{E}[E \mid C]] = \mathbb{E}\!\left[\frac{1}{2}C - \frac{1}{2}\right] = \frac{1}{2}C_{\text{ave}} - \frac{1}{2}.$

In the regime where $C$ is large on average, the additive constant $1/2$ is negligible relative to $C_{\text{ave}}$, so the leading term satisfies

$E_{\text{ave}} \approx \frac{1}{2}C_{\text{ave}}.$

## Verification

The conditional distribution of $A \bmod C$ is uniform on ${0, \dots, C-1}$ under uniform sampling of $A$ over complete residue cycles modulo $C$. The mean of a uniform discrete distribution on this set equals $(C-1)/2$ by direct summation of an arithmetic progression. Linearity of expectation justifies passing from conditional expectation to unconditional expectation without additional assumptions beyond existence of the induced distribution of $C$ in Program B.

## Conclusion

The expected remainder produced at each division step is asymptotically half of the expected divisor value, with only a constant correction term independent of $C$. This completes the proof. ∎
