[Section 4.5.2: The Greatest Common Divisor](../)

**Exercise 15.** [*M21*] [M21] What are the values of $v_1$ and $v_2$ when Algorithm X terminates?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 7m54s  

---

The key correction is that Algorithm X never introduces a “reset” or “nullification” of its working variables at termination. Therefore the final values of $v_1$ and $v_2$ must be read directly from the last executed assignment that occurred along the successful search path.

In Knuth’s formulation of Algorithm X, the computation proceeds by recursively choosing a column $c$, then selecting a row $r$ that covers $c$, and performing the covering operations that remove conflicting rows and columns. In implementations written in MMIX-style pseudocode, the variables $v_1$ and $v_2$ record the most recent choice of column and row at each recursive level, namely

$$
v_1 := c, \qquad v_2 := r,
$$

at the moment the algorithm commits to extending the partial solution.

Termination in the successful case occurs when the set of columns becomes empty after a sequence of such choices. Importantly, this event does not correspond to a separate “cleanup step” that reassigns or clears $v_1$ and $v_2$. Instead, it occurs immediately after the last covering operation induced by the final chosen pair $(c,r)$. The recursion then returns because the termination condition is satisfied.

At the base case, Algorithm X simply detects that no columns remain and returns success. There are no assignments in this base case that modify $v_1$ or $v_2$. Hence the variables retain their most recent values from the last level of recursion.

Consequently, when the algorithm terminates, $v_1$ and $v_2$ contain the last column–row choice made in the successful branch of the search. Equivalently, they are the final selected pair that caused the remaining set of columns to become empty.

Thus,

$$
v_1 = c_{\text{final}}, \qquad v_2 = r_{\text{final}},
$$

where $c_{\text{final}}$ is the last column chosen and $r_{\text{final}}$ is the row selected to cover it in the successful exact cover solution.
