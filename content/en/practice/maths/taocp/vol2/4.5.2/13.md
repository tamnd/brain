**Exercise 13.** [*HM23*] [HM23] Given that $u$ and $v$ are random odd positive integers, show that they are relatively prime with probability $8/\pi^2$.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 11m47s  

---

## Solution

Let

$$
P=\Pr(\gcd(u,v)=1),
$$

where $u$ and $v$ are chosen uniformly from the odd positive integers.

Exercise 10 established that for unrestricted positive integers,

$$
\Pr(\gcd(u,v)=1)=\frac6{\pi^2}.
$$

The restriction to odd integers changes only the contribution of the prime $2$.

For each odd prime $p$, the probability that $p$ divides a randomly chosen odd integer is still $1/p$. Hence the probability that $p$ divides both $u$ and $v$ is

$$
\frac1{p^2}.
$$

Therefore the probability that $p$ does not divide both numbers is

$$
1-\frac1{p^2}.
$$

Since divisibility by distinct primes is asymptotically independent, the probability that no odd prime divides both $u$ and $v$ is

$$
P=\prod_{p\text{ odd prime}}
\left(1-\frac1{p^2}\right).
$$

From Euler's product for the result of Exercise 10,

$$
\prod_{p\text{ prime}}
\left(1-\frac1{p^2}\right)
=
\frac6{\pi^2}.
$$

Separating the factor corresponding to $p=2$ gives

$$
\frac6{\pi^2}
=
\left(1-\frac1{2^2}\right)
\prod_{p\text{ odd prime}}
\left(1-\frac1{p^2}\right).
$$

Hence

$$
P
=
\frac{\frac6{\pi^2}}{1-\frac14}
=
\frac{\frac6{\pi^2}}{\frac34}
=
\frac8{\pi^2}.
$$

Thus the probability that two random odd positive integers are relatively prime is

$$
\boxed{\frac8{\pi^2}}.
$$

This completes the proof.

∎
