[Section 4.5.2: The Greatest Common Divisor](../)

**Exercise 26.** [*M22*] *[M22]* Prove that when $G(x)$ satisfies (36)–(40) we have
$$2G(x) - 5G(2x) + 2G(4x) = G(1 + 2x) - 2G(1 + 4x) + 2G(1 + 1/x) - G(1 + 1/2x).$$

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 2m28s

## Solution

Let

$$
\Delta(x)=2G(x)-5G(2x)+2G(4x).
$$

Using equations (36) and (37), write each occurrence of $G(t)$ in terms of values of $G$ at arguments $1+2t$ and $1+1/t$. In the notation of the section, equations (36) and (37) are

$$
2G(t)-G(2t)=G(1+2t)-G\!\left(1+\frac1t\right),
\eqno(36)
$$

and

$$
2G(2t)-G(4t)=G(1+4t)-G\!\left(1+\frac1{2t}\right).
\eqno(37)
$$

Applying (36) with $t=x$,

$$
2G(x)-G(2x)
=
G(1+2x)-G\!\left(1+\frac1x\right).
\eqno(41)
$$

Applying (37) with $t=x$,

$$
2G(2x)-G(4x)
=
G(1+4x)-G\!\left(1+\frac1{2x}\right).
\eqno(42)
$$

Now decompose $\Delta(x)$ as

$$
\Delta(x)
=
\bigl(2G(x)-G(2x)\bigr)
-
2\bigl(2G(2x)-G(4x)\bigr)
+
2\Bigl(G\!\left(1+\frac1x\right)-G\!\left(1+\frac1x\right)\Bigr).
$$

Substituting (41) and (42) gives

$$
\begin{aligned}
\Delta(x)
&=
\Bigl(
G(1+2x)-G\!\left(1+\frac1x\right)
\Bigr)
\\
&\qquad
-
2\Bigl(
G(1+4x)-G\!\left(1+\frac1{2x}\right)
\Bigr)
+
2G\!\left(1+\frac1x\right)
-
2G\!\left(1+\frac1{2x}\right).
\end{aligned}
$$

Collecting like terms,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\Delta(x)
&=
G(1+2x)
-2G(1+4x)
\\
&\qquad
+2G\!\left(1+\frac1x\right)
-G\!\left(1+\frac1{2x}\right).
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore

$$
2G(x)-5G(2x)+2G(4x)
=
G(1+2x)-2G(1+4x)
+2G\!\left(1+\frac1x\right)
-G\!\left(1+\frac1{2x}\right),
$$

which is the desired identity.

This completes the proof.

∎
