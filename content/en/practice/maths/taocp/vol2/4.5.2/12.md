**Exercise 12.** [*M24*] [M24] (E. Cesàro.) If $u$ and $v$ are random positive integers, what is the average number of (positive) divisors they have in common? [*Hint:* See the identity in exercise 10(d), with $a_k = b_m = 1$.]

## Solution

Let $u$ and $v$ range uniformly over the integers $1 \le u, v \le n$. Let $c(u,v)$ denote the number of positive integers $d$ that divide both $u$ and $v$. Then

$$
c(u,v) = \sum_{d \mid u,\, d \mid v} 1.
$$

The average value over the square $1 \le u, v \le n$ is

$$
A_n = \frac{1}{n^2} \sum_{u=1}^n \sum_{v=1}^n c(u,v)
     = \frac{1}{n^2} \sum_{u=1}^n \sum_{v=1}^n \sum_{d \mid u,\, d \mid v} 1.
$$

Reordering summation over $d$ gives

$$
A_n = \frac{1}{n^2} \sum_{d=1}^n \sum_{\substack{1 \le u \le n \\ d \mid u}} \sum_{\substack{1 \le v \le n \\ d \mid v}} 1.
$$

For fixed $d$, the number of integers $u$ in $1 \le u \le n$ divisible by $d$ is $\lfloor n/d \rfloor$, and the same holds for $v$. Hence the inner sum equals $\lfloor n/d \rfloor^2$, so

$$
A_n = \frac{1}{n^2} \sum_{d=1}^n \left\lfloor \frac{n}{d} \right\rfloor^2.
$$

For each fixed $d$,

$$
\frac{1}{n^2} \left\lfloor \frac{n}{d} \right\rfloor^2 \to \frac{1}{d^2}
\quad \text{as } n \to \infty.
$$

Also,

$$
0 \le \frac{1}{n^2} \left\lfloor \frac{n}{d} \right\rfloor^2 \le \frac{1}{d^2},
$$

and $\sum_{d \ge 1} 1/d^2$ converges. Therefore the limit may be passed through the sum, giving

$$
\lim_{n \to \infty} A_n = \sum_{d=1}^\infty \frac{1}{d^2}.
$$

The series is the value of the Riemann zeta function at $2$,

$$
\sum_{d=1}^\infty \frac{1}{d^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{6}.
$$

Thus the average number of positive common divisors of two random positive integers is

$$
\boxed{\frac{\pi^2}{6}}.
$$
