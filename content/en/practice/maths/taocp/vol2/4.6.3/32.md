**Exercise 32.** [*M30*] *[M30]* (A. C. Yao, F. F. Yao, R. L. Graham.) Associate the "cost" $a_j a_k$ with each step $a_i = a_j + a_k$ of an addition chain (1). Show that the left-to-right binary method yields a chain of minimum total cost, for all positive integers $n$.

Let the addition chain be

$$
1=a_1<a_2<\cdots<a_m=n,\qquad a_i=a_j+a_k\ (j,k<i),
$$

and assign cost $a_j a_k$ to step $a_i=a_j+a_k$. The total cost is

$$
C=\sum_i a_j a_k.
$$

## 1. Rewriting the cost

For each step $a_i=a_j+a_k$,

$$
a_j a_k=\frac{a_i^2-a_j^2-a_k^2}{2}.
$$

Summing over all steps gives

$$
C=\frac12\left(n^2-1-\sum_{t=2}^{m-1}\deg(t)\,a_t^2\right),
$$

where $\deg(t)$ is the number of times $a_t$ is used as an operand.

Since $n$ is fixed, minimizing $C$ is equivalent to maximizing

$$
S=\sum_{t=2}^{m-1}\deg(t)\,a_t^2.
$$

We now work entirely with $S$.

## 2. Key exchange principle

### Lemma (monotone replacement)

Suppose at some stage a chain contains values $x>y$. If a later step uses $y$ as an operand, replacing that use by $x$ (whenever valid) does not decrease $S$, because it increases the contribution by at least

$$
x^2-y^2>0.
$$

Moreover, such a replacement can be propagated forward consistently: every future use of $y$ can be replaced by $x$ without breaking validity, since $x\ge y$ and all future sums only require previously constructed numbers, not their identities.

Hence, in an optimal chain, whenever a larger value is available, it is never worse to use it instead of a smaller one, and such exchanges can be made globally.

This principle allows us to “push upward” usage toward larger values.

## 3. Powers of two must be formed by doubling

We prove by induction that an optimal chain contains

$$
1,2,4,\dots,2^b
$$

and each is obtained by doubling.

Assume $x=2^t$ is already present. Consider the first time a value $y>x$ is created. Write $y=a_i+a_j$ with $a_i\ge a_j$.

If both $a_i,a_j<x$, then $y<2x$, so $x<y<2x$.

Now compare two possibilities:

- current step: $y=a_i+a_j$
- modified step: $x' = x+x$

The modified construction replaces the use of smaller operands by the largest available one $x$. By the exchange principle, replacing any use of $a_i,a_j<x$ by $x$ can only increase contributions to $S$, because every occurrence contributes a square term and

$$
x^2 \ge a_i^2,\ a_j^2,
$$

with strict inequality unless equal.

Thus any optimal chain can be transformed so that the first value exceeding $x$ is $2x$, not an intermediate value.

Hence all powers of two must appear, and necessarily via doubling.
