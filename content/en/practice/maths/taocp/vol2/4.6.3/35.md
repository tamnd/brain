[Section 4.6.3: Evaluation of Powers](../)

**Exercise 35.** [*M27*] *[M27]* How many addition chains without useless steps are equivalent to each of the addition chains discussed in exercise 34, when $e_0 > e_1 + 1$?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 8m45s  

---

Let the chains in Exercise 34 be written in the standard form determined by the exponents

\[
e_0>e_1>\cdots,
\]

and recall that two addition chains are regarded as equivalent when they have the same pattern of non-doubling steps and differ only by the order in which independent doubling blocks are carried out.

The hypothesis

\[
e_0>e_1+1
\]

is exactly the condition that separates the highest two nonzero binary exponents by at least one level. Consequently, the doubling steps between levels \(e_0\) and \(e_1\) are completely independent of the lower part of the chain. No useless step can occur, because every intermediate value is needed in order to reach the next non-doubling step.

Hence the number of equivalent chains is obtained by counting the possible permutations of the independent non-doubling blocks.

For the chains of Exercise 34:

1. **The first chain contains one non-doubling block.**

   There is no nontrivial permutation, so the number of equivalent chains is

   \[
   1!=1.
   \]

2. **The second chain contains two independent non-doubling blocks.**

   They may occur in either order, giving

   \[
   2!=2
   \]

   equivalent chains.

3. **The third chain contains three independent non-doubling blocks.**

   Any permutation of the three blocks yields an equivalent chain without useless steps, so the number of equivalent chains is

   \[
   3!=6.
   \]

Therefore, when \(e_0>e_1+1\), the numbers of equivalent addition chains without useless steps corresponding to the three chains of Exercise 34 are

\[
\boxed{1,\;2,\;6}.
\]

The counting follows because the gap condition \(e_0>e_1+1\) makes the relevant blocks independent, and every permutation of those blocks produces a distinct equivalent chain while preserving the absence of useless steps.
