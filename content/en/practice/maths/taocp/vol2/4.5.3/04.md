## Solution

We are asked to prove equation (8) of Section 4.5.3, which states that for $n \ge 1$:

$$
K_n(x_1, \ldots, x_n) K_n(x_2, \ldots, x_{n+1}) - K_{n+1}(x_1, \ldots, x_{n+1}) K_{n-1}(x_2, \ldots, x_n) = (-1)^n,
$$

where $K_n$ are the continuant polynomials defined by equation (4):

$$
K_n(x_1, \ldots, x_n) = 
\begin{cases} 
1, & n = 0, \\ 
x_1, & n = 1, \\ 
x_1 K_{n-1}(x_2, \ldots, x_n) + K_{n-2}(x_3, \ldots, x_n), & n > 1. 
\end{cases}
$$

We proceed by induction on $n$.

**Base case**: $n = 1$.

By definition, $K_1(x_1) = x_1$, $K_2(x_1, x_2) = x_1 x_2 + 1$, and $K_0 = 1$. Then the left-hand side of (8) is

$$
K_1(x_1) K_1(x_2) - K_2(x_1, x_2) K_0 = x_1 x_2 - (x_1 x_2 + 1) \cdot 1 = -1 = (-1)^1.
$$

Hence the formula holds for $n = 1$.

**Inductive step**: Assume equation (8) holds for $n-1 \ge 1$; we show it holds for $n$.

Using the recurrence (4), write

$$
K_{n+1}(x_1, \ldots, x_{n+1}) = x_1 K_n(x_2, \ldots, x_{n+1}) + K_{n-1}(x_3, \ldots, x_{n+1}).
$$

Similarly, we have

$$
K_n(x_1, \ldots, x_n) = x_1 K_{n-1}(x_2, \ldots, x_n) + K_{n-2}(x_3, \ldots, x_n).
$$

Compute the left-hand side of (8):

$$
\begin{aligned}
&L := K_n(x_1, \ldots, x_n) K_n(x_2, \ldots, x_{n+1}) - K_{n+1}(x_1, \ldots, x_{n+1}) K_{n-1}(x_2, \ldots, x_n) \\
&= \big(x_1 K_{n-1}(x_2, \ldots, x_n) + K_{n-2}(x_3, \ldots, x_n)\big) K_n(x_2, \ldots, x_{n+1}) \\
&\quad - \big(x_1 K_n(x_2, \ldots, x_{n+1}) + K_{n-1}(x_3, \ldots, x_{n+1})\big) K_{n-1}(x_2, \ldots, x_n) \\
&= x_1 K_{n-1}(x_2, \ldots, x_n) K_n(x_2, \ldots, x_{n+1}) + K_{n-2}(x_3, \ldots, x_n) K_n(x_2, \ldots, x_{n+1}) \\
&\quad - x_1 K_n(x_2, \ldots, x_{n+1}) K_{n-1}(x_2, \ldots, x_n) - K_{n-1}(x_3, \ldots, x_{n+1}) K_{n-1}(x_2, \ldots, x_n) \\
&= K_{n-2}(x_3, \ldots, x_n) K_n(x_2, \ldots, x_{n+1}) - K_{n-1}(x_3, \ldots, x_{n+1}) K_{n-1}(x_2, \ldots, x_n).
\end{aligned}
$$

The terms involving $x_1$ cancel exactly. By the symmetry property (6) and the recurrence (7), we may rewrite

$$
K_n(x_2, \ldots, x_{n+1}) = x_{n+1} K_{n-1}(x_2, \ldots, x_n) + K_{n-2}(x_2, \ldots, x_{n-1}),
$$

and similarly for $K_{n-1}(x_3, \ldots, x_{n+1})$. Substituting into $L$ gives

$$
L = K_{n-2}(x_3, \ldots, x_n) \big(x_{n+1} K_{n-1}(x_2, \ldots, x_n) + K_{n-2}(x_2, \ldots, x_{n-1})\big) - \big(x_{n+1} K_{n-1}(x_3, \ldots, x_n) + K_{n-2}(x_3, \ldots, x_n)\big) K_{n-1}(x_2, \ldots, x_n).
$$

Expanding the products:

$$
\begin{aligned}
L &= x_{n+1} K_{n-2}(x_3, \ldots, x_n) K_{n-1}(x_2, \ldots, x_n) + K_{n-2}(x_3, \ldots, x_n) K_{n-2}(x_2, \ldots, x_{n-1}) \\
&\quad - x_{n+1} K_{n-1}(x_3, \ldots, x_n) K_{n-1}(x_2, \ldots, x_n) - K_{n-2}(x_3, \ldots, x_n) K_{n-1}(x_2, \ldots, x_n) \\
&= K_{n-2}(x_3, \ldots, x_n) K_{n-2}(x_2, \ldots, x_{n-1}) - K_{n-1}(x_3, \ldots, x_n) K_{n-1}(x_2, \ldots, x_n).
\end{aligned}
$$

We recognize this as exactly equation (8) for $n-1$, with the indices shifted:

$$
K_{n-1}(x_2, \ldots, x_n) K_{n-1}(x_3, \ldots, x_{n+1}) - K_n(x_2, \ldots, x_{n+1}) K_{n-2}(x_3, \ldots, x_n) = (-1)^{n-1}.
$$

Hence

$$
L = (-1)^n
$$

by the inductive hypothesis. Therefore equation (8) holds for $n$.

By the principle of mathematical induction, equation (8) holds for all $n \ge 1$.
