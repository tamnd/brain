[Section 4.5.3: Analysis of Euclid's Algorithm](../)

**Exercise 19.** [*M20*] Prove $F(x) = \log_2(1 + x)$ satisfies Eq. (24).

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m11s  

---

## Solution

Equation (24) is

$$
F(x)=\sum_{m\ge1}\left(F\!\left(\frac1m\right)-F\!\left(\frac1{m+x}\right)\right),
\qquad 0\le x\le1.
$$

Let

$$
F(x)=\log_2(1+x).
$$

Then

$$
F\!\left(\frac1m\right)-F\!\left(\frac1{m+x}\right)
=
\log_2\!\left(1+\frac1m\right)
-
\log_2\!\left(1+\frac1{m+x}\right),
$$

hence

$$
F\!\left(\frac1m\right)-F\!\left(\frac1{m+x}\right)
=
\log_2
\frac{(m+1)(m+x)}
     {m(m+x+1)}.
$$

Therefore the partial sum through $m=N$ is

$$
S_N
=
\sum_{m=1}^{N}
\log_2
\frac{(m+1)(m+x)}
     {m(m+x+1)}
=
\log_2
\prod_{m=1}^{N}
\frac{(m+1)(m+x)}
     {m(m+x+1)}.
$$

The product factors into two telescoping products:

$$
\prod_{m=1}^{N}\frac{m+1}{m}=N+1,
$$

and

$$
\prod_{m=1}^{N}\frac{m+x}{m+x+1}
=
\frac{1+x}{N+x+1}.
$$

Hence

$$
S_N
=
\log_2\!\left(
(N+1)\frac{1+x}{N+x+1}
\right).
$$

Letting $N\to\infty$ gives

$$
\lim_{N\to\infty}S_N
=
\log_2(1+x),
$$

since

$$
\lim_{N\to\infty}\frac{N+1}{N+x+1}=1.
$$

Thus

$$
\sum_{m\ge1}
\left(
F\!\left(\frac1m\right)
-
F\!\left(\frac1{m+x}\right)
\right)
=
\log_2(1+x)
=
F(x).
$$

Therefore $F(x)=\log_2(1+x)$ satisfies Eq. (24).

This completes the proof.

∎
