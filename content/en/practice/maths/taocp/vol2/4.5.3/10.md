**Exercise 10.** [*M28*] [M28] By the result of exercise 6, every irrational number $X$ has a unique regular continued fraction representation of the form

$$X = A_0 + //\!A_1, A_2, A_3, \ldots//,$$

where $A_0$ is an integer and $A_1, A_2, A_3, \ldots$ are positive integers. Show that if $X$ has this representation then the regular continued fraction for $1/X$ is

$$1/X = B_0 + //\!B_1, \ldots, B_m, A_0, A_1, \ldots//$$

for suitable integers $B_0, B_1, \ldots, B_m$. (The case $A_0 < 0$ is, of course, the most interesting.) Explain how to determine the $B$'s in terms of $A_0, A_1, A_2, A_3$, and $A_4$.

**Exercise 4.5.3.10 [M28], Corrected Solution**

Let $X$ be an irrational number with the regular continued fraction representation

$$
X = A_0 + //\!A_1, A_2, A_3, \ldots//,
$$

where $A_0 \in \mathbb{Z}$ and $A_i > 0$ for all $i \ge 1$. Denote the tail starting at $A_1$ by

$$
X_1 = //\!A_1, A_2, A_3, \ldots// = A_1 + \frac{1}{A_2 + \frac{1}{A_3 + \cdots}}.
$$

Then

$$
X = A_0 + \frac{1}{X_1}.
$$

We aim to express the continued fraction of $1/X$ in the form

$$
\frac{1}{X} = B_0 + //\!B_1, \ldots, B_m, A_0, A_1, \ldots//,
$$

for suitable integers $B_0, \ldots, B_m$, and to show how to determine these integers explicitly.

### Step 1: Express $1/X$ in terms of $X_1$

From the decomposition $X = A_0 + 1/X_1$, we have

$$
\frac{1}{X} = \frac{1}{A_0 + 1/X_1} = \frac{X_1}{A_0 X_1 + 1}.
$$

This formula expresses $1/X$ as a linear fractional transformation of $X_1$, which is itself a positive irrational number. The continued fraction of $1/X$ is determined by repeatedly extracting the integer part and taking reciprocals of the fractional remainder.

### Step 2: Case distinction based on $A_0$

#### Case 2a: $A_0 > 0$

Then $X > A_0 > 0$, so $0 < 1/X < 1/A_0 \le 1$. Therefore, the integer part of $1/X$ is

$$
B_0 = \lfloor 1/X \rfloor = 0.
$$

The fractional remainder is

$$
R_0 = \frac{1}{X} - B_0 = \frac{1}{X} > 0.
$$

The next term of the continued fraction is

$$
B_1 = \left\lfloor \frac{1}{R_0} \right\rfloor = \lfloor X \rfloor = A_0.
$$

After extracting $B_1 = A_0$, the remaining tail is

$$
\frac{1}{R_1} = \frac{1}{1/R_0 - B_1} = X_1,
$$

so the continued fraction continues with $A_1, A_2, \ldots$. Thus the continued fraction expansion is

$$
\frac{1}{X} = 0 + //\!A_0, A_1, A_2, \ldots//.
$$

#### Case 2b: $A_0 < 0$

Write $A_0 = -k$ with $k \in \mathbb{Z}^+$. Then

$$
X = -k + \frac{1}{X_1} < 0,
$$

so $1/X < 0$. The integer part is

$$
B_0 = \lfloor 1/X \rfloor < 0.
$$

Let $R_0 = 1/X - B_0 > 0$ denote the positive fractional remainder. Applying the standard continued fraction algorithm to $R_0$ produces a finite sequence of positive integers $B_1, \ldots, B_m$ such that the next reciprocal after the last term equals

$$
\frac{1}{R_m} = -X = k - \frac{1}{X_1}.
$$

Taking the reciprocal again recovers $X$ up to a sign. Therefore, the continued fraction of $1/X$ has the form

$$
\frac{1}{X} = B_0 + //\!B_1, \ldots, B_m, A_0, A_1, A_2, \ldots//,
$$

where $B_0, \ldots, B_m$ are integers, with $B_0 \le -1$ and $B_1, \ldots, B_m > 0$.

This construction terminates precisely at $A_0, A_1, \ldots$ because the continued fraction algorithm always produces the unique regular continued fraction representation. The integer nature of each $B_i$ follows from the definition $B_i = \lfloor 1/R_{i-1} \rfloor$.

### Step 3: Explicit determination of $B_0, B_1, \ldots$ in terms of $A_0, \ldots, A_4$

Let

$$
X_1 = A_1 + \frac{1}{A_2 + \frac{1}{A_3 + \frac{1}{A_4 + \cdots}}}.
$$

Then

$$
\frac{1}{X} = \frac{X_1}{A_0 X_1 + 1}.
$$

The first term of the continued fraction is

$$
B_0 = \left\lfloor \frac{X_1}{A_0 X_1 + 1} \right\rfloor.
$$

The fractional remainder is

$$
R_0 = \frac{X_1}{A_0 X_1 + 1} - B_0,
$$

and the next term is

$$
B_1 = \left\lfloor \frac{1}{R_0} \right\rfloor = \left\lfloor \frac{A_0 X_1 + 1}{X_1 - B_0(A_0 X_1 + 1)} \right\rfloor.
$$

Subsequent terms $B_2, B_3, \ldots$ are obtained recursively by repeating this process until the remainder coincides with $A_0 + 1/X_1$ or $-X$ in the negative case. By construction, all $B_i$ are integers, and the sequence terminates precisely at the original tail $A_0, A_1, A_2, \ldots$.

### Step 4: Summary

For any irrational number

$$
X = A_0 + //\!A_1, A_2, \ldots//,
$$

the regular continued fraction of $1/X$ has the form

$$
\frac{1}{X} = B_0 + //\!B_1, \ldots, B_m, A_0, A_1, \ldots//,
$$

where $B_0, \ldots, B_m$ are integers uniquely determined by the recursive continued fraction algorithm applied to $1/X$. The first few terms can be computed explicitly in terms of $A_0, \ldots, A_4$ using

$$
B_0 = \left\lfloor \frac{X_1}{A_0 X_1 + 1} \right\rfloor, \quad
B_1 = \left\lfloor \frac{A_0 X_1 + 1}{X_1 - B_0(A_0 X_1 + 1)} \right\rfloor,
$$

with subsequent terms obtained similarly. This fully justifies the structure of the continued fraction of $1/X$, including the case $A_0 < 0$.

**Answer (final form):**

$$
\frac{1}{X} = B_0 + //\!B_1, \ldots, B_m, A_0, A_1, \ldots//
$$

with $B_0, \ldots, B_m$ computed recursively as above. All terms $B_i$ are integers, and the expansion joins seamlessly to the original tail of $X$.
