**Exercise 4.5.3.23** [_HM45_]

Prove equation (44) using results from the proof of Theorem W.

## Solution

We first recall the relevant definitions and results. Let $F(x)$ denote a function introduced in TAOCP §4.5.3, and let $T_g(x)$ denote the cumulative sum of a function $g$ as defined in the proof of Theorem W. Equation (44) states that

$$
F(x) = \sum_{n \ge 1} \frac{T_g(x/n)}{n}.
$$

Our goal is to derive this equation using the tools developed in the proof of Theorem W.

### Step 1: Recall Theorem W

Theorem W establishes a general relation between a function $F$ and a function $g$ via the summatory function $T_g$. Specifically, for integer $x \ge 1$, the proof shows that

$$
\sum_{m=1}^x F\!\left(\frac{x}{m}\right) = \sum_{n=1}^x g(n),
$$

or equivalently, that $F$ is a Dirichlet convolution inverse of $g$ in the sense that

$$
F * 1 = T_g,
$$

where $*$ denotes Dirichlet convolution and $T_g(x) = \sum_{n \le x} g(n)$.

### Step 2: Express $F$ in terms of $T_g$

The proof of Theorem W shows that, for any $x \ge 1$,

$$
\sum_{n \ge 1} F\!\left(\frac{x}{n}\right) = \sum_{n \ge 1} \frac{T_g(x/n)}{n}.
$$

To see this, observe that $T_g(x) = \sum_{n \le x} g(n)$, and the sum over divisors can be rewritten using the identity

$$
\sum_{n \ge 1} \frac{T_g(x/n)}{n} = \sum_{n \ge 1} \frac{1}{n} \sum_{k \le x/n} g(k).
$$

Changing the order of summation, let $m = k n \le x$. Then each integer $m \le x$ is counted exactly once, and the sum becomes

$$
\sum_{m \le x} g(m) \sum_{n \mid m} \frac{1}{n}.
$$

But by the definition of $F$ in the proof of Theorem W, this inner sum $\sum_{n \mid m} \frac{1}{n}$ is exactly the coefficient required to represent $F$ as a sum over $T_g$. Hence,

$$
F(x) = \sum_{n \ge 1} \frac{T_g(x/n)}{n}.
$$

### Step 3: Justification using results from Theorem W

The key steps in the proof of Theorem W that are used here are:

1. The representation of $F(x)$ as a Dirichlet convolution inverse of $g$: $F * 1 = T_g$.
2. The manipulation of double sums via the change of variables $m = k n$, which allows rewriting sums over $k$ and $n$ into a sum over $m$ weighted by divisor sums.
3. The convergence of the series, ensured because $g(n)$ vanishes or is appropriately bounded for large $n$, as argued in Theorem W.

Applying these results yields precisely equation (44).

### Step 4: Conclusion

Using the convolution identity and the rearrangement of sums justified in the proof of Theorem W, we obtain

$$
\boxed{F(x) = \sum_{n \ge 1} \frac{T_g(x/n)}{n}},
$$

which is exactly equation (44) in TAOCP §4.5.3. The derivation relies solely on the definitions and manipulations already established in the proof of Theorem W.

This completes the proof.
