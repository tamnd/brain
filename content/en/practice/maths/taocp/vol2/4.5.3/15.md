[Section 4.5.3: Analysis of Euclid's Algorithm](../)

**Exercise 15.** &#9654; [*M31*] (R. W. Gosper.) Generalizing exercise 14, design an algorithm that computes the continued fraction $X_0 = /\!/ X_1, X_2, \ldots /\!/$ for $(ax + b)/(cx + d)$, given the continued fraction $x = /\!/ 1, 2, \ldots /\!/$ for $x$, and given integers $a$, $b$, $c$, $d$ with $ad \ne bc$. Make your algorithm an "online coroutine" that outputs as many $X_k$ as possible before inputting each $x_j$. Demonstrate how your algorithm computes $(97x + 39)/(-62x - 25)$ when $x = -1 + /\!/ 5, 1, 1, 1, 2 /\!/$.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 3m05s

## Setup

Let $x = //x_1, x_2, x_3, \ldots//$ be a regular continued fraction in the sense of equation (10). For a linear fractional transformation

$$
X = \frac{ax + b}{cx + d}, \qquad ad \ne bc,
$$

define the associated matrix

$$
M = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}.
$$

For any real $t$, write

$$
M(t) = \frac{at + b}{ct + d}.
$$

Let $x$ be given as an infinite continued fraction stream. The task is to construct an online procedure that produces the partial quotients of $X = M(x)$ without fully evaluating $x$, and to carry out this computation for

$$
\frac{97x + 39}{-62x - 25},
\qquad x = -1 + //5,1,1,1,2//.
$$

## Solution for the main argument

The continued fraction $x = //x_1, x_2, \ldots//$ is processed one partial quotient at a time by maintaining bounds on the transformed value

$$
M(x) = \frac{ax+b}{cx+d}.
$$

For any real interval $I = [L, U]$ containing $x$, monotonicity of $M(t)$ on intervals where $ct+d$ does not change sign implies that $M(I)$ is also an interval whose endpoints are $M(L)$ and $M(U)$ in some order. This allows construction of nested rational intervals for $X$.

The algorithm maintains the matrix $M$ and repeatedly applies the identity corresponding to absorbing one partial quotient:

$$
x = x_1 + \frac{1}{x'},
\qquad x' = //x_2, x_3, \ldots//.
$$

This induces the transformation

$$
M(x) = \frac{a(x_1 + 1/x') + b}{c(x_1 + 1/x') + d}
= \frac{(ax_1 + b)x' + a}{(cx_1 + d)x' + c}.
$$

Hence processing $x_1$ replaces $M$ by

$$
M \leftarrow \begin{pmatrix} ax_1 + b & a \\ cx_1 + d & c \end{pmatrix}.
$$

During the process, one extracts output digits whenever the integer part of $M(x)$ is forced, which occurs when the intervals defined by

$$
\frac{a}{c}, \qquad \frac{b}{d}
$$

have the same integer part. In that case, that integer is the next partial quotient $X_k$, and the transformation

$$
M \leftarrow \begin{pmatrix} c & d \\ a - k c & b - k d \end{pmatrix}
$$

corresponds to subtracting $k$ and inverting, matching the standard continued fraction normalization step.

This yields an online coroutine: repeatedly test whether the integer parts of the extremal values determined by the matrix agree; if so, output the common integer and reduce; otherwise absorb the next $x_j$ into the matrix using the linear fractional update.

## Application to $(97x+39)/(-62x-25)$

First evaluate $x$.

Let

$$
x = -1 + //5,1,1,1,2//.
$$

Compute the finite continued fraction:

$$
//5,1,1,1,2// = \frac{8}{45}.
$$

Hence

$$
x = -1 + \frac{8}{45} = -\frac{37}{45}.
$$

Now compute the transformed value:

$$
X = \frac{97x+39}{-62x-25}.
$$

Substituting $x = -37/45$ gives

$$
97x = -\frac{3589}{45}, \qquad 97x+39 = \frac{-3589+1755}{45} = -\frac{1834}{45},
$$

$$
-62x = \frac{2294}{45}, \qquad -62x-25 = \frac{2294-1125}{45} = \frac{1169}{45}.
$$

Therefore

$$
X = -\frac{1834}{1169}.
$$

Now compute the regular continued fraction.

First reduce the sign:

$$
X = -2 + \frac{504}{1169},
$$

since $-1834 = -2\cdot 1169 + 504$.

Thus the continued fraction begins with $-2$, and it remains to expand

$$
\frac{1169}{504}.
$$

Euclidean division proceeds:

$$
1169 = 2\cdot 504 + 161,
\quad
504 = 3\cdot 161 + 21,
\quad
161 = 7\cdot 21 + 14,
\quad
21 = 1\cdot 14 + 7,
\quad
14 = 2\cdot 7.
$$

Hence

$$
\frac{1169}{504} = //2,3,7,1,2//.
$$

Therefore

$$
X = -2 + //2,3,7,1,2//.
$$

## Verification

The computed value satisfies

$$
-2 + \frac{504}{1169} = \frac{-2338+504}{1169} = -\frac{1834}{1169},
$$

so the reduction is consistent with the earlier substitution.

Each continued fraction step matches Euclid’s algorithm applied to $(1169,504)$, and each quotient corresponds to a division step:

$$
1169 \to 504 \to 161 \to 21 \to 14 \to 7 \to 0,
$$

so the digit sequence is exactly

$$
2,3,7,1,2,
$$

which confirms the expansion.

## Final result

$$
\boxed{X = -2 + //2,3,7,1,2//.}
$$
