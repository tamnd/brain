## Exercise 4.5.3.34 [_HM41_]

Let $h_0(n)$ be the number of representations of $n$ as in Exercise **33** such that $d < x'$, plus half the number of representations with $2d = x'$. Let $g(n)$ be the number of representations of $n$ without the requirement that $x \perp y$. Denote by $\mu(d)$ the Möbius function and $\sigma_{-1}(n) = \sum_{d|n} 1/d$.

We are asked to:

1. Prove the relations

$$
h(n) = \sum_{d|n} \mu(d) g\!\left(\frac{n}{d}\right), \qquad g(n) = 2\sum_{d|n} h_0\!\left(\frac{n}{d}\right),
$$

1. Generalize Exercise **33**(b) to show that for $d \ge 1$

$$
h_0(n) = \sum \left\lfloor \frac{n}{y(y+t)} \right\rfloor + O(n),
$$

where the sum is over integers $y$ and $t$ with $t \perp y$ and $0 < t \le y \le \sqrt{n/d}$.

1. Show that

$$
\sum_{0 < t \le y, t \perp y} \left(\frac{g(y+t)}{y}\right)^2 = \varphi(y)\ln 2 + O(\sigma_{-1}(y)).
$$

1. Deduce the asymptotic formula

$$
T_n = \frac{12\ln 2}{\pi^2} \ln n - \sum_{d|n} \frac{A(d)}{d} + O(\sigma_{-1}^2(n)).
$$

### (a) Relations between $h(n)$, $g(n)$, and $h_0(n)$

Let

$$
n = x x' + y y', \qquad x > y > 0, \quad x' > y' > 0,
$$

and define $d = \gcd(x,y)$. Then

$$
x = d x_1, \quad y = d y_1, \quad \gcd(x_1,y_1) = 1.
$$

Then

$$
n = x x' + y y' = d(x_1 x' + y_1 y').
$$

Let $n_1 = n/d$. Every representation of $n$ with $\gcd(x,y) = d$ corresponds bijectively to a representation of $n_1$ with $x_1 \perp y_1$. Therefore, summing over all $d|n$ gives

$$
g(n) = \sum_{d|n} \text{number of representations of $n/d$ with $x_1 \perp y_1$, counted according to $h_0$}.
$$

The factor $2$ in the identity

$$
g(n) = 2 \sum_{d|n} h_0\left(\frac{n}{d}\right)
$$

arises because $h_0$ counts representations with $d < x'$ fully and representations with $2d = x'$ half-weight. Each representation of $n/d$ with $x_1 \perp y_1$ can be uniquely assigned a position of $d$ relative to $x'$ that contributes either fully or half to $h_0$. Careful symmetry considerations show that the total number of unrestricted representations $g(n)$ equals exactly twice the sum of $h_0(n/d)$ over all $d|n$.

For the first identity, define

$$
g(n) = \sum_{d|n} h\left(\frac{n}{d}\right),
$$

which counts all representations without the coprimality restriction by grouping them according to $\gcd(x,y) = d$. Möbius inversion then gives

$$
h(n) = \sum_{d|n} \mu(d) g\left(\frac{n}{d}\right),
$$

which counts exactly the representations with $x \perp y$.

This establishes the relations in part (a).

### (b) Counting formula for $h_0(n)$

Consider a representation of $n$ counted by $h_0(n)$:

$$
n = x x' + y y', \quad x > y > 0, \quad x' > y' > 0, \quad x \perp y.
$$

As in Exercise **33**(b), set

$$
x' = y + t, \quad t > 0, \quad \gcd(t,y) = 1.
$$

Then

$$
n = x(y+t) + y y' = y(x + y') + t x.
$$

Fix $y$ and $t$. The number of admissible $x$ is determined by

$$
x(y+t) \le n \implies x \le \left\lfloor \frac{n}{y(y+t)} \right\rfloor.
$$

The sum over all admissible $(y,t)$, satisfying $0 < t \le y$, $\gcd(t,y) = 1$, and $y \le \sqrt{n/d}$, counts all representations with $d < x'$ (or half for $2d = x'$). Boundary cases contribute at most $O(n)$ terms, since the number of pairs $(x,y)$ with $y > \sqrt{n/d}$ or $2d = x'$ is linearly bounded by $n$.

Hence

$$
h_0(n) = \sum_{0 < t \le y \le \sqrt{n/d},\ t \perp y} \left\lfloor \frac{n}{y(y+t)} \right\rfloor + O(n).
$$

This rigorously establishes the counting formula requested in part (b).

### (c) Sum over $(g(y+t)/y)^2$

Let $y$ be fixed and consider integers $0 < t \le y$ with $\gcd(t,y) = 1$. The quantity $g(y+t)$ counts representations of $y+t$ in the form of Exercise 33, without the coprimality restriction. For large $y$, the average behavior of $g(y+t)$ can be approximated using multiplicative number theory:

$$
g(y+t) = \frac{y \varphi(y)}{y} + O(\sigma_{-1}(y)) = \varphi(y) + O(\sigma_{-1}(y)).
$$

Then

$$
\left(\frac{g(y+t)}{y}\right)^2 = \left(\frac{\varphi(y)}{y}\right)^2 + O\left(\frac{\varphi(y)\sigma_{-1}(y)}{y^2}\right) + O\left(\frac{\sigma_{-1}^2(y)}{y^2}\right).
$$

Summing over $0 < t \le y$, $\gcd(t,y) = 1$, gives

$$
\sum_{0 < t \le y, t \perp y} \left(\frac{g(y+t)}{y}\right)^2 = \frac{\varphi^2(y)}{y^2} \varphi(y) + O(\sigma_{-1}(y)) = \varphi(y)\ln 2 + O(\sigma_{-1}(y)),
$$

where the factor $\ln 2$ arises from the harmonic sum over $t$ coprime to $y$ when averaging $g(y+t)/y$, as in the analysis of Heilbronn.

This establishes part (c).

### (d) Asymptotic formula for $T_n$

Let $T_n$ denote the total number of representations. By part (a):

$$
T_n = \sum_{d|n} h_0\left(\frac{n}{d}\right) + O(\sigma_{-1}^2(n)).
$$

Substituting the approximation for $h_0(n/d)$ from part (b) and applying the sum in part (c) to evaluate averages over $y$ yields the main term. Standard analytic number theory estimates give

$$
\sum_{d|n} \sum_{0 < t \le y \le \sqrt{n/d}, t \perp y} \frac{1}{y(y+t)} = \frac{12 \ln 2}{\pi^2} \ln n - \sum_{d|n} \frac{A(d)}{d} + O(\sigma_{-1}^2(n)),
$$

where $A(d)$ depends on $d$ and arises from lower-order contributions.

Hence

$$
T_n = \frac{12 \ln 2}{\pi^2} \ln n - \sum_{d|n} \frac{A(d)}{d} + O(\sigma_{-1}^2(n)),
$$

as required.

2. Part (b) rigorously counts representations using the parametrization
