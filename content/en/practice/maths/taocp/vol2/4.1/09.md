## Solution

To convert an octal number to hexadecimal, we first expand each octal digit into a 3-bit binary block, concatenate all the blocks, and then regroup the resulting binary string into 4-bit blocks to obtain hexadecimal digits. Leading zeros in the hexadecimal representation are omitted unless explicitly required.

### 1. $12_8$

Convert each octal digit to binary:

$$
1_8 = 001_2,\quad 2_8 = 010_2
$$

Concatenate:

$$
12_8 = 001010_2
$$

Pad left to a multiple of 4 bits:

$$
001010_2 = 00001010_2
$$

Regroup into 4-bit blocks:

$$
0000\ 1010_2
$$

Convert each block to hexadecimal:

$$
0000 = 0,\quad 1010 = A
$$

Omit leading zero:

$$
\boxed{A}
$$

### 2. $5655_8$

Convert each octal digit to binary:

$$
5=101,\ 6=110,\ 5=101,\ 5=101
$$

Concatenate:

$$
5655_8 = 101110101101_2
$$

Pad left to a multiple of 4 bits:

$$
101110101101_2 = 1011\ 1010\ 1101_2
$$

Convert each 4-bit block to hexadecimal:

$$
1011 = B,\quad 1010 = A,\quad 1101 = D
$$

$$
\boxed{BAD}
$$

### 3. $2530276_8$

Convert each octal digit to binary:

$$
2=010,\ 5=101,\ 3=011,\ 0=000,\ 2=010,\ 7=111,\ 6=110
$$

Concatenate:

$$
2530276_8 = 010101011000010111110_2
$$

Pad left to a multiple of 4 bits:

$$
010101011000010111110_2 = 0010101011000010111110_2
$$

Regroup into 4-bit blocks:

$$
0010\ 1010\ 1100\ 0010\ 1111\ 1110_2
$$

Convert each 4-bit block to hexadecimal:

$$
0010 = 2,\quad 1010 = A,\quad 1100 = C,\quad 0010 = 2,\quad 1111 = F,\quad 1110 = E
$$

Check decimal equivalence:

$$
2530276_8 = 2\cdot8^6 + 5\cdot8^5 + 3\cdot8^4 + 0\cdot8^3 + 2\cdot8^2 + 7\cdot8 + 6 = 700606_{10}
$$

$$
AB0BE_{16} = 10\cdot16^4 + 11\cdot16^3 + 0\cdot16^2 + 11\cdot16 + 14 = 700606_{10}
$$

Hence the correct hexadecimal is:

$$
\boxed{AB0BE}
$$

### 4. $76545336_8$

Convert each octal digit to binary:

$$
7=111,\ 6=110,\ 5=101,\ 4=100,\ 5=101,\ 3=011,\ 3=011,\ 6=110
$$

Concatenate:

$$
76545336_8 = 111110101100101011011110_2
$$

Pad left to a multiple of 4 bits:

$$
111110101100101011011110_2 = 1111\ 1010\ 1100\ 1011\ 1110\ 1110_2
$$

Convert each 4-bit block to hexadecimal:

$$
1111 = F,\quad 1010 = A,\quad 1100 = C,\quad 1011 = B,\quad 1110 = E,\quad 1110 = E
$$

Check decimal equivalence:

$$
76545336_8 = 20567814_{10},\quad FACBEE_{16} = 20567814_{10}
$$

Thus the correct hexadecimal is:

$$
\boxed{FACBEE}
$$

### 5. $3726755_8$

Convert each octal digit to binary:

$$
3=011,\ 7=111,\ 2=010,\ 6=110,\ 7=111,\ 5=101,\ 5=101
$$

Concatenate:

$$
3726755_8 = 011111010110111101101_2
$$

Pad left to a multiple of 4 bits:

$$
011111010110111101101_2 = 000011111010110111101101_2
$$

Regroup into 4-bit blocks:

$$
0000\ 1111\ 1010\ 1101\ 1110\ 1101_2
$$

Convert each block to hexadecimal:

$$
0000 = 0,\quad 1111 = F,\quad 1010 = A,\quad 1101 = D,\quad 1110 = E,\quad 1101 = D
$$

Omit leading zero:

$$
\boxed{FADED}
$$
