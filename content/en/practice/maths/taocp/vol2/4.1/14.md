## Solution

Let the base be $2i$, as in the quater-imaginary system. We first evaluate the given number

$$
(1132)_{2i}.
$$

Expanding in powers of $2i$ gives

$$
(1132)_{2i}
= 1(2i)^3 + 1(2i)^2 + 3(2i)^1 + 2(2i)^0.
$$

Compute the powers:

$$
(2i)^2 = -4,\quad (2i)^3 = -8i.
$$

Substituting,

$$
(1132)_{2i} = (-8i) + (-4) + 6i + 2.
$$

Combine real and imaginary parts:

$$
(1132)_{2i} = (-4 + 2) + (-8i + 6i) = -2 - 2i.
$$

Now square the value:

$$
(-2 - 2i)^2 = 4(1 + i)^2.
$$

Since

$$
(1 + i)^2 = 1 + 2i + i^2 = 2i,
$$

we obtain

$$
(-2 - 2i)^2 = 4 \cdot 2i = 8i.
$$

It remains to express $8i$ in base $2i$.

We use powers of $2i$:

$$
(2i)^3 = -8i,\quad (2i)^5 = (2i)^2(2i)^3 = (-4)(-8i) = 32i.
$$

To represent $8i$, write it as a combination of $(2i)^5$ and $(2i)^3$:

$$
3(2i)^3 + (2i)^5 = 3(-8i) + 32i = -24i + 32i = 8i.
$$

Thus the digit representation has a $1$ in the $5$th position, a $3$ in the $3$rd position, and zeros elsewhere:

$$
8i = (1030000)_{2i}.
$$

Therefore,

$$
(1132)_{2i}^2 = (1030000)_{2i}.
$$
