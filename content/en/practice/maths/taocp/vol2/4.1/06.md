**Exercise 6.** [*16*] [16] What are the largest and smallest $p$-bit integers that can be represented in (a) signed magnitude binary notation (including one bit for the sign), (b) two's complement notation, (c) ones' complement notation?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m28s

## Solution

Let $p$ denote the total number of bits.

### (a) Signed magnitude binary notation

In signed magnitude representation, one bit stores the sign and the remaining $p-1$ bits store a nonnegative magnitude $M$, where

$0 \le M \le 2^{p-1} - 1.$

The represented value is

$X = (-1)^s \cdot M,$

where $s \in {0,1}$ is the sign bit.

The largest positive value occurs when $s = 0$ and $M = 2^{p-1} - 1$, giving

$X_{\max} = 2^{p-1} - 1.$

The smallest value occurs when $s = 1$ and $M = 2^{p-1} - 1$, giving

$X_{\min} = -(2^{p-1} - 1).$

Thus the representable range is symmetric except for the existence of both $+0$ and $-0$.

Hence

$\boxed{X_{\min} = -(2^{p-1} - 1), \quad X_{\max} = 2^{p-1} - 1.}$

### (b) Two's complement notation

In two's complement, a $p$-bit word $b_{p-1} b_{p-2} \cdots b_0$ represents the value

$X = -b_{p-1} 2^{p-1} + \sum_{i=0}^{p-2} b_i 2^i.$

To obtain the maximum value, set $b_{p-1} = 0$ and all remaining bits equal to $1$, giving

$X_{\max} = \sum_{i=0}^{p-2} 2^i = 2^{p-1} - 1.$

To obtain the minimum value, set $b_{p-1} = 1$ and all remaining bits equal to $0$, giving

$X_{\min} = -2^{p-1}.$

No other choice of bits can yield a smaller value, since any additional $1$ in a lower position increases the sum by a positive amount.

Thus

$\boxed{X_{\min} = -2^{p-1}, \quad X_{\max} = 2^{p-1} - 1.}$

### (c) Ones' complement notation

In ones' complement representation, a positive number is represented as in ordinary binary with $p-1$ magnitude bits, and a negative number is obtained by bitwise complementation of the positive representation. This yields two representations of zero and symmetry about zero.

The largest positive value occurs when all magnitude bits are $1$, giving

$X_{\max} = 2^{p-1} - 1.$

The most negative value is obtained by complementing this representation, giving

$X_{\min} = -(2^{p-1} - 1).$

No representation can exceed these magnitudes, since the magnitude field cannot exceed $2^{p-1} - 1$.

Thus

$\boxed{X_{\min} = -(2^{p-1} - 1), \quad X_{\max} = 2^{p-1} - 1.}$

This completes the solution. ∎
