**Exercise 11.** [*22*] [22] Design an algorithm that uses the $-2$ number system to compute the sum of $(a_n \ldots a_1 a_0)_{-2}$ and $(b_n \ldots b_1 b_0)_{-2}$, obtaining the answer $(c_{n+2} \ldots c_1 c_0)_{-2}$.

## Solution

Let $(a_n \ldots a_1 a_0)_{-2}$ and $(b_n \ldots b_1 b_0)_{-2}$ be given, where each digit $a_i, b_i \in {0,1}$. The positional value is

$$
(a_n \ldots a_0)_{-2} = \sum_{i=0}^{n} a_i(-2)^i,
\qquad
(b_n \ldots b_0)_{-2} = \sum_{i=0}^{n} b_i(-2)^i.
$$

The goal is to construct digits $c_0, c_1, \ldots, c_{n+2} \in {0,1}$ and a carry sequence $k_i \in \mathbb{Z}$ such that

$$
(a_n \ldots a_0)_{-2} + (b_n \ldots b_0)_{-2} = \sum_{i=0}^{n+2} c_i (-2)^i.
$$

Define an auxiliary carry sequence $k_i$ by $k_0 = 0$. For each position $i \ge 0$, define the intermediate sum

$$
s_i = a_i + b_i + k_i,
$$

where $a_i = 0$ and $b_i = 0$ for $i > n$.

At each position $i$, choose the digit $c_i \in {0,1}$ and next carry $k_{i+1}$ so that

$$
s_i = c_i + (-2)k_{i+1}.
$$

Solving this equation gives

$$
k_{i+1} = \frac{c_i - s_i}{2}.
$$

Since $s_i$ is an integer, $c_i$ must be chosen so that $c_i - s_i$ is even. Because $c_i \in {0,1}$, exactly one of $c_i = 0$ or $c_i = 1$ satisfies this parity condition.

To determine $c_i$ explicitly, observe that:

if $s_i$ is even, then $c_i = 0$ and $k_{i+1} = -s_i/2$; if $s_i$ is odd, then $c_i = 1$ and $k_{i+1} = (1 - s_i)/2$.

This rule determines $(c_i, k_{i+1})$ uniquely for each $i$.

The computation proceeds for $i = 0, 1, \ldots, n+1$, since carries may propagate beyond the highest input digit. After processing $i = n+1$, the carry $k_{n+2}$ is incorporated into $c_{n+2}$ using the same relation, with $a_{n+2} = b_{n+2} = 0$.

Correctness follows from the invariant that after processing position $i$, the partial sum satisfies

$$
\sum_{j=0}^{i} (a_j + b_j)(-2)^j = \sum_{j=0}^{i} c_j (-2)^j + k_{i+1}(-2)^{i+1},
$$

which is preserved by the defining relation $s_i = c_i + (-2)k_{i+1}$. The invariant holds initially at $i=0$ and is maintained at each step by construction, hence holds for all positions.

Termination yields digits $c_0, \ldots, c_{n+2}$ with final carry $k_{n+3} = 0$, since repeated application of $k_{i+1} = (c_i - s_i)/2$ reduces the magnitude of the carry and no further nonzero contribution remains beyond position $n+2$.

This completes the construction of the negabinary addition algorithm. ∎
