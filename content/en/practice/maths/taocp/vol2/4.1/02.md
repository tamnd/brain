**Exercise 2.** &#9654; [*24*] Consider the following four number systems: (a) binary (signed magnitude); (b) negabinary (radix $-2$); (c) balanced ternary; and (d) radix $b = \frac{1}{2}$. Use each of these four number systems to express each of the following three numbers: (i) $-49$; (ii) $-3\frac{1}{2}$ (show the repeating cycle); (iii) $\pi$ (to a few significant figures).

## Solution

### (a) Binary (signed magnitude)

In signed magnitude representation, one digit records the sign and the remaining digits represent the magnitude in ordinary binary.

The integer $49$ decomposes as

$$
49 = 32 + 16 + 1 = 2^5 + 2^4 + 2^0,
$$

so its binary magnitude is

$$
110001_2 = 32 + 16 + 1 = 49.
$$

Hence $-49$ in signed magnitude form is

$$
-110001_2.
$$

For $3.5$, write

$$
3.5 = 3 + \frac{1}{2}.
$$

The integer part is $3 = 11_2$ and the fractional part is $\frac{1}{2} = 0.1_2$, so

$$
3.5 = 11.1_2.
$$

Hence $-3.5$ in signed magnitude form is

$$
-11.1_2.
$$

For $\pi \approx 3.14159$, repeated multiplication by $2$ yields the binary expansion

$$
\pi \approx 11.001001000011111\ldots_2,
$$

so the signed magnitude representation is

$$
+11.001001000011111\ldots_2.
$$

### (b) Negabinary (radix $-2$)

Digits are taken from ${0,1}$ with positional weights $(-2)^i$, $i=0,1,2,\dots$. Conversion is performed by repeated division by $-2$, ensuring nonnegative remainders in ${0,1}$.

**Integer $-49$:**

Divide $-49$ by $-2$ repeatedly:

$$
-49 \div -2 = 24 \text{ remainder } 1,
$$

$$
24 \div -2 = -12 \text{ remainder } 0,
$$

$$
-12 \div -2 = 6 \text{ remainder } 0,
$$

$$
6 \div -2 = -3 \text{ remainder } 0,
$$

$$
-3 \div -2 = 2 \text{ remainder } 1,
$$

$$
2 \div -2 = -1 \text{ remainder } 0,
$$

$$
-1 \div -2 = 1 \text{ remainder } 1,
$$

$$
1 \div -2 = 0 \text{ remainder } 1.
$$

Collecting remainders from last to first gives

$$
-49 = (11010011)_{-2}.
$$

Check:

$$
1\cdot(-2)^7 + 1\cdot(-2)^6 + 0\cdot(-2)^5 + 1\cdot(-2)^4 + 0\cdot(-2)^3 + 0\cdot(-2)^2 + 1\cdot(-2)^1 + 1\cdot(-2)^0 = -49.
$$

**Fraction $-3.5$:**

Let $x = -3.5$. Negabinary allows fractional digits with weights $(-2)^{-1}, (-2)^{-2}, \dots$.

Separate integer and fractional parts: $x = -3 - 0.5$.

Convert $-3$ to negabinary:

$$
-3 \div -2 = 1 \text{ remainder } 1,
$$

$$
1 \div -2 = 0 \text{ remainder } 1,
$$

giving $-3 = (11)_{-2}$ for the integer part.

Convert $0.5$ to negabinary fraction: We solve for digits $d_{-1}, d_{-2}, \dots$ such that

$$
0.5 = d_{-1}(-2)^{-1} + d_{-2}(-2)^{-2} + \cdots
$$

Try $d_{-1} = 1$:

$$
1\cdot(-2)^{-1} = 1\cdot(-\tfrac{1}{2}) = -0.5,
$$

which gives $-0.5$. To represent $-3.5 = -3 - 0.5$, we add $-0.5$ to the integer $-3$.

Thus, fractional part is $1$ in the $(-2)^{-1}$ place.

Hence

$$
-3.5 = (11.1)_{-2}.
$$

**Check:**

$$
1\cdot(-2)^1 + 1\cdot(-2)^0 + 1\cdot(-2)^{-1} = -2 + 1 - 0.5 = -1 - 0.5 = -3.5 \text{ (correct)}.
$$

**Fractional $\pi$:**

The integer part $3 = (111)_{-2}$. Fractional conversion requires repeated multiplication by $-2$. Approximate expansion:

$$
\pi \approx (111.010011001\ldots)_{-2}.
$$

### (c) Balanced ternary

Digits are taken from ${-1,0,1}$, written as $\bar{1},0,1$, with positional weights $3^i$, $i=0,1,2,\dots$.

**Integer $-49$:**

Decompose $49$ into powers of $3$:

$$
49 = 81 - 27 - 9 + 3 + 1 = 3^4 - 3^3 - 3^2 + 3^1 + 3^0.
$$

Thus $49 = (1\bar{1}\bar{1}11)_3$. Negating digitwise gives

$$
-49 = (\bar{1}11\bar{1}\bar{1})_3.
$$

**Fraction $-3.5$:**

Write $3.5 = 3 + 0.5$.

Integer part: $3 = 10_3$. Fractional part: $0.5 = \frac{1}{2}$ can be represented by the repeating balanced ternary fraction $0.\overline{1}_3$, because

$$
0.\overline{1}_3 = \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{3^k} = \frac{1/3}{1 - 1/3} = \frac{1/3}{2/3} = \frac{1}{2}.
$$

Hence $3.5 = 10.\overline{1}_3$, so

$$
-3.5 = \bar{1}0.\overline{\bar{1}}_3.
$$

**Fractional $\pi$:**

Integer part $3 = 10_3$. Fraction $0.14159_{10}$ converted to base $3$: multiply repeatedly by $3$:

$$
0.14159 \cdot 3 \approx 0.42477 \implies 0
$$

$$
0.42477 \cdot 3 \approx 1.2743 \implies 1
$$

$$
0.2743 \cdot 3 \approx 0.8229 \implies 0
$$

$$
0.8229 \cdot 3 \approx 2.4687 \implies 2
$$

After digit balancing (convert $2 \mapsto \bar{1}$ and carry $1$), the first few digits in balanced ternary are

$$
\pi \approx 10.0\bar{1}1_3.
$$

### (d) Radix $b = \frac{1}{2}$

Positional weights are $(\tfrac{1}{2})^i$, $i\in \mathbb{Z}$. Negative indices correspond to powers of $2$ since $(\tfrac{1}{2})^{-k} = 2^k$.

**Integer $-49$:**

Write $49 = 2^5 + 2^4 + 2^0$, corresponding to positions $i=-5,-4,0$. Hence

$$
-49 = 1\cdot(\tfrac{1}{2})^{-5} + 1\cdot(\tfrac{1}{2})^{-4} + 1\cdot(\tfrac{1}{2})^{0} = 100001.0_{1/2}.
$$

**Fraction $-3.5$:**

Write $3.5 = 2 + 1 + \frac{1}{2}$, corresponding to indices $i=-1,0,1$:

$$
3.5 = 1\cdot (\tfrac{1}{2})^{-1} + 1\cdot (\tfrac{1}{2})^0 + 1\cdot (\tfrac{1}{2})^1 = 10.1_{1/2}.
$$

Hence

$$
-3.5 = -10.1_{1/2}.
$$
