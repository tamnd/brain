[Section 4.1: Positional Number Systems](../)

**Exercise 4.** [*15*] [15] Assume that we have a MIX program in which location A contains a number for which the radix point lies between bytes 2 and 3, while location B contains a number whose radix point lies between bytes 2 and 3. (The leftmost byte is number 1.) Where will the radix point be, in registers A and X, after the following instructions?

(a) LDA A; MUL B $\qquad$ (b) LDA A; SRAX 5; DIV B

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 1m31s

## Solution

We are asked to determine the position of the radix point in registers A and X after executing two sequences of MIX instructions, given that the operands in memory locations A and B have the radix point between bytes 2 and 3. Let the values in memory locations A and B be represented as

$A = \pm (a_1:a_2.a_3:a_5) \quad \text{and} \quad B = \pm (b_1:b_2.b_3:b_5),$

where the notation $a_1:a_2.a_3:a_5$ indicates that the radix point lies between bytes 2 and 3; similarly for $B$.

### Part (a) LDA A; MUL B

1. **Load A into register A**: `LDA A` copies the contents of memory location A into register A. After this instruction, register A contains

$A \leftarrow \pm(a_1:a_2.a_3:a_5),$

with the radix point still between bytes 2 and 3. Register X is unchanged.

1. **Multiply A by B**: `MUL B` computes the product of the contents of registers A and B and stores the result as a 10-byte number, with the high-order 5 bytes in A and the low-order 5 bytes in X. By definition of the MIX `MUL` instruction (Section 1.3.2), the radix point of the product is computed as follows:

- If the operands have radix points at positions $r_A$ and $r_B$ counted from the leftmost byte (with $r = 0$ before byte 1, $r = 1$ between bytes 1 and 2, $r = 2$ between bytes 2 and 3, ..., $r = 5$ after byte 5), then the product is a 10-byte number in A and X with the radix point at

$r_{AX} = r_A + r_B.$

Here, $r_A = 2$ and $r_B = 2$, so

$r_{AX} = 2 + 2 = 4.$

This means the radix point is located between the 4th and 5th bytes of the combined 10-byte register pair A and X. The first five bytes (register A) contain the most significant portion of the product, and the second five bytes (register X) contain the least significant portion.

**Conclusion for (a):** After `LDA A; MUL B`, the radix point is between bytes 4 and 5 of the combined A-X pair.

### Part (b) LDA A; SRAX 5; DIV B

1. **Load A into register A**: `LDA A` places

$A \leftarrow \pm(a_1:a_2.a_3:a_5),$

with the radix point between bytes 2 and 3.

1. **Shift right A into A and X**: `SRAX 5` shifts the contents of register A five bytes to the right. By definition (Section 1.3.3.2), the most significant 5 bytes remain in A and the least significant 5 bytes move into X. The radix point shifts along with the value. Initially, the radix point is after byte 2 in A. Shifting right by 5 bytes moves the radix point 5 bytes to the right in the combined A-X pair, so it lies between bytes $2 + 5 = 7$. Counting bytes 1 through 5 as register A and 6 through 10 as register X, this corresponds to byte 2 of X (since bytes 6–10 are X).
2. **Divide by B**: `DIV B` computes $(AX)/B$, placing the quotient in register A and the remainder in register X. By definition (Section 1.3.2), the quotient has the same number of bytes as A (5 bytes), and the radix point in the quotient aligns with the radix point of the dividend (the 10-byte AX pair) minus the radix point of the divisor (register B). Denote the initial radix points:

- Dividend: radix point in AX between byte 7 and 8 (after step 2).
- Divisor: radix point in B between bytes 2 and 3.

The quotient radix point in A is therefore at

$r_Q = (7) - (2) = 5,$

i.e., after byte 5 in A. Since register A has only 5 bytes, the radix point is effectively at the rightmost edge of A, corresponding to the fixed-point interpretation for MIX division. The remainder in X is not affected in position by the division; it represents the lower-order part of the result.

**Conclusion for (b):** After `LDA A; SRAX 5; DIV B`, the radix point in A lies at the right end of register A (after byte 5). Register X contains the remainder, with its radix point effectively not relevant to the quotient interpretation.

### Final Answer

(a) `LDA A; MUL B`: radix point between bytes 4 and 5 of the combined A-X register pair.

(b) `LDA A; SRAX 5; DIV B`: radix point at the right end of register A (after byte 5); X contains the remainder.

$\boxed{\text{(a) between bytes 4 and 5 of A-X; (b) at the right end of A, X = remainder}}$

This completes the proof.
