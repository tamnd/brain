**Exercise 24.** [*M20*] $[M20]$ Let $0 < l < k$. Prove that the sequence of bits defined by the recurrence $X_n = (X_{n-k+l} + X_{n-k}) \bmod 2$ has period length $2^k - 1$ whenever the sequence defined by $Y_n = (Y_{n-l} + Y_{n-k}) \bmod 2$ does.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 23m16s  

---

Let

$$
Y_n=(Y_{n-l}+Y_{n-k}) \pmod 2,
\qquad 0<l<k,
$$

and suppose that every nonzero sequence satisfying this recurrence has period

$$
2^k-1.
$$

For a linear recurrence over $\mathbf F_2$, this is equivalent to saying that its characteristic polynomial

$$
f(x)=x^k+x^{k-l}+1
$$

is primitive.

Now consider

$$
X_n=(X_{n-k+l}+X_{n-k}) \pmod 2.
$$

Replacing $n$ by $m+k$, we can write this as

$$
X_{m+k}=X_{m+l}+X_m .
$$

Hence the characteristic polynomial of the $X$-recurrence is

$$
g(x)=x^k+x^l+1.
$$

We compare $g$ with $f$. A direct calculation gives

$$
x^k f(x^{-1})
 =x^k\!\left(x^{-k}+x^{-(k-l)}+1\right)
 =1+x^l+x^k
 =g(x).
$$

Thus $g$ is the reciprocal polynomial of $f$.

Since $f$ is primitive, let $\alpha$ be a root of $f$ in

$\mathbf F_{2^k}$. Then $\alpha$ has multiplicative order

$$
2^k-1.
$$

Because $g(x)=x^k f(x^{-1})$, we have

$$
g(\alpha^{-1})
=(\alpha^{-1})^k f(\alpha)
=0,
$$

so $\alpha^{-1}$ is a root of $g$.

But

$$
\operatorname{ord}(\alpha^{-1})
=\operatorname{ord}(\alpha)
=2^k-1.
$$

Therefore a root of $g$ has order $2^k-1$. Since $\deg g=k$, it follows that $g$ is also primitive.
