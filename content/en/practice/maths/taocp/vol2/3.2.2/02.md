**Exercise 2.** [*M20*] A good source of random numbers will have $X_{n-1} < X_{n+1} < X_n$ about one-sixth of the time, since each of the six possible relative orders of $X_{n-1}$, $X_n$, and $X_{n+1}$ should be equally probable. However, show that the ordering above *never* occurs if the Fibonacci sequence (5) is used.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 15m52s  

---

## Solution

From the Fibonacci generator,

$$
X_{n+1} = (X_n + X_{n-1}) \bmod m,
$$

there exists an integer $t \in {0,1}$ such that

$$
X_{n+1} = X_n + X_{n-1} - tm,
$$

since $0 \le X_n, X_{n-1} \le m-1$ implies $0 \le X_n + X_{n-1} < 2m$.

Assume there exists an index $n$ such that

$$
X_{n-1} < X_{n+1} < X_n.
$$

First consider $t = 0$. Then

$$
X_{n+1} = X_n + X_{n-1}.
$$

This implies $X_{n+1} \ge X_n$, since $X_{n-1} \ge 0$, contradicting $X_{n+1} < X_n$. Hence $t \ne 0$.

Now consider $t = 1$. Then

$$
X_{n+1} = X_n + X_{n-1} - m.
$$

The inequality $X_{n+1} < X_n$ becomes

$$
X_n + X_{n-1} - m < X_n,
$$

which simplifies to $X_{n-1} < m$, which holds for all admissible values of $X_{n-1}$ and yields no restriction.

The inequality $X_{n-1} < X_{n+1}$ becomes

$$
X_{n-1} < X_n + X_{n-1} - m,
$$

which simplifies to

$$
0 < X_n - m,
$$

hence $X_n > m$, contradicting $0 \le X_n \le m-1$.

Both cases lead to contradictions, so no index $n$ satisfies $X_{n-1} < X_{n+1} < X_n$.

This completes the proof. ∎
