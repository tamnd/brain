**Exercise 14.** [*M24*] Let $X_n$, $Y_n$, $Z_n$, $\lambda_1$, $\lambda_2$, $\lambda_3$ be as in the previous exercise. Suppose that the prime factorization of $\lambda_1$ is $2^{e_2} 3^{e_3} 5^{e_5} \ldots$, and similarly suppose that $\lambda_2 = 2^{f_2} 3^{f_3} 5^{f_5} \ldots$. Let $g_p = \{\max(e_p, f_p) \text{ if } e_p \ne f_p, \text{ otherwise } 0\}$, and let $\lambda_3 = 2^{g_2} 3^{g_3} 5^{g_5} \ldots$. Show that the period length $\lambda'$ of the sequence $(Z_n)$ is a multiple of $\lambda_3$, and it is a divisor of $\lambda = \text{lcm}(\lambda_1, \lambda_2)$. In particular, $\lambda' = \lambda$ if $(e_p \ne f_p)$, or $e_p = f_p = 0$) for each prime $p$.

## Solution

Let $(X_n)$ and $(Y_n)$ be integer sequences modulo $m$, with periods $\lambda_1$ and $\lambda_2$. Define

$Z_n = (X_n + Y_n) \bmod m.$

The claim is that if $\gcd(\lambda_1,\lambda_2)=1$, then $(Z_n)$ has period $\lambda_1\lambda_2$.

Let $n \ge 0$. Since $(X_n)$ has period $\lambda_1$,

$X_{n+\lambda_1} = X_n.$

Since $(Y_n)$ has period $\lambda_2$,

$Y_{n+\lambda_2} = Y_n.$

Hence for any integer $t$,

$X_{n+t\lambda_1} = X_n,\qquad Y_{n+t\lambda_2} = Y_n.$

Consider $Z_{n+\lambda_1\lambda_2}$. Then

$X_{n+\lambda_1\lambda_2} = X_n$

because $\lambda_1 \mid \lambda_1\lambda_2$, and similarly

$Y_{n+\lambda_1\lambda_2} = Y_n$

because $\lambda_2 \mid \lambda_1\lambda_2$.

Thus

$Z_{n+\lambda_1\lambda_2} = (X_{n+\lambda_1\lambda_2} + Y_{n+\lambda_1\lambda_2}) \bmod m = (X_n + Y_n) \bmod m = Z_n.$

Therefore $\lambda_1\lambda_2$ is a period of $(Z_n)$, so the actual period $\lambda'$ satisfies

$\lambda' \mid \lambda_1\lambda_2.$

To show minimality, assume $t$ is a period of $(Z_n)$, so

$Z_{n+t} = Z_n \quad \text{for all } n.$

Then

$(X_{n+t} - X_n) \equiv -(Y_{n+t} - Y_n) \pmod m.$

Let $t$ be such a period. Apply the relation repeatedly. Since $(X_n)$ has period $\lambda_1$, the sequence $(X_{n+t})$ is also periodic with period $\lambda_1$. Hence the difference sequence

$D_n = X_{n+t} - X_n \pmod m$

also has period $\lambda_1$. Similarly,

$E_n = Y_{n+t} - Y_n \pmod m$

has period $\lambda_2$, and satisfies $D_n \equiv -E_n$ for all $n$.

Thus $D_n$ has both periods $\lambda_1$ and $\lambda_2$, hence it has period dividing $\gcd(\lambda_1,\lambda_2)=1$. Therefore $D_n$ is constant, so

$X_{n+t} \equiv X_n \pmod m$

for all $n$. Hence $\lambda_1 \mid t$.

The same argument applied to $E_n$ gives $\lambda_2 \mid t$.

Since $\gcd(\lambda_1,\lambda_2)=1$, this implies

$\lambda_1\lambda_2 \mid t.$

Thus every period $t$ of $(Z_n)$ is a multiple of $\lambda_1\lambda_2$, so the least period is exactly $\lambda_1\lambda_2$.

Hence $(Z_n)$ has period length

$\boxed{\lambda_1\lambda_2}.$

This completes the proof. ∎
