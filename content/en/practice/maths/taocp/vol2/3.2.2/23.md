**Exercise 23.** [*20*] $[20]$ Discuss the sequence defined by $X_n = (X_{n-31} - X_{n-24}) \bmod m$ as an alternative to (7).

## Solution

The recurrence is

$$
X_n=(X_{n-31}-X_{n-24})\pmod m.
$$

Equivalently,

$$
X_n-X_{n-31}+X_{n-24}\equiv 0 \pmod m.
$$

This is a linear recurrence of order $31$. Writing it in the standard form

$$
X_n=a_{24}X_{n-24}+a_{31}X_{n-31},
$$

with $a_{24}=-1$ and $a_{31}=1$, the associated characteristic polynomial is

$$
f(x)=x^{31}+x^7-1.
$$

Indeed,

$$
x^{31}-a_{24}x^{31-24}-a_{31}
=
x^{31}+x^7-1.
$$

Thus the generator is a lagged linear recurrence analogous to the additive lagged Fibonacci generator

$$
X_n=(X_{n-55}+X_{n-24})\pmod m,
$$

except that the lags are $31$ and $24$, and one coefficient is $-1$.

To understand its behavior, it is natural to examine the recurrence modulo $2$. Since

$$
-1\equiv 1 \pmod 2,
$$

the recurrence becomes

$$
X_n\equiv X_{n-31}+X_{n-24}\pmod 2.
$$

Hence the least significant bits satisfy a linear feedback shift-register recurrence with characteristic polynomial

$$
\bar f(x)=x^{31}+x^7+1
\in \mathbb F_2[x].
$$

If $\bar f(x)$ is primitive over $\mathbb F_2$, then every nonzero state of the $31$-stage register lies on a single cycle, and the binary sequence has the maximal possible period

$$
2^{31}-1.
$$

Thus the first question is whether $x^{31}+x^7+1$ is primitive. If it is not primitive, the period is a proper divisor of $2^{31}-1$.

This observation is the only period statement that follows immediately from the recurrence. One cannot conclude, merely from primitivity modulo $2$, that the sequence modulo $2^e$ has period

$$
2^{e-1}(2^{31}-1).
$$

Such a lifting result requires additional analysis of the recurrence over $\mathbb Z_{2^e}$. The recurrence modulo $2^e$ is indeed linear,

$$
X_n=X_{n-31}-X_{n-24}\pmod{2^e},
$$

but the cycle structure over $\mathbb Z_{2^e}$ is not determined solely by the binary characteristic polynomial. Therefore no general formula for the period over $2^e$ can be asserted here without further proof.

The main qualitative conclusions are nevertheless clear.

1. The generator is a linear lagged recurrence whose behavior is governed by the polynomial

$$
x^{31}+x^7-1.
$$
2. Modulo $2$, subtraction and addition coincide, so the least significant bit sequence is exactly the same as that produced by the trinomial

$$
x^{31}+x^7+1.
$$
3. If this trinomial is primitive, the binary sequence has period $2^{31}-1$.
4. The state space has dimension $31$, substantially smaller than the $55$-dimensional state space of Knuth's generator (7). Consequently, even in the best case, the attainable periods are of the scale of $2^{31}$ rather than $2^{55}$.
5. Replacing addition by subtraction does not introduce any nonlinearity. The generator remains a linear recurrence, so it belongs to the same general class as additive lagged Fibonacci generators and inherits the usual strengths and weaknesses of linear lagged generators.

Therefore the sequence

$$
X_n=(X_{n-31}-X_{n-24})\pmod m
$$

is best viewed as a shorter lagged Fibonacci type generator with characteristic polynomial $x^{31}+x^7-1$. Its binary behavior is determined by $x^{31}+x^7+1$, giving period $2^{31}-1$ when that polynomial is primitive, but no further period formula for modulus $2^e$ follows without additional analysis. ∎
