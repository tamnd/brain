**Exercise 4.** [*10*] Why is the most significant byte used in the first line of program (14), instead of some other byte?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 1m37s

In MIX arithmetic each word consists of several bytes, and operations such as multiplication and addition propagate carries from less significant positions toward more significant positions. The least significant byte of a product or sum depends only on the corresponding low-order digits of the operands, since all higher-order contributions are annihilated modulo the base of that byte. This creates strong structural bias: that byte evolves under a small modulus and therefore has a much shorter and more regular cycle than the full word.

The most significant byte is influenced by carries generated from all lower bytes during arithmetic operations, so it incorporates information from every part of the word. In the construction used in program (14), the goal is to extract a component of the computation that reflects the full mixing of the underlying arithmetic. Selecting the most significant byte achieves this, since it is the only byte whose value depends on the entire word rather than on a restricted residue class of low-order bits. This yields a more uniform and better mixed source of random variation than any lower byte. ∎
