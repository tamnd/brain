**Exercise 32.** [*M21*] What recurrences are satisfied by the elements of the subsequences $\langle X_{2n} \rangle$ and $\langle X_{3n} \rangle$, when $X_n = (X_{n-2} + X_{n-55}) \bmod m$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 6m57s  

---

## Solution

Let

$$
X_n=(X_{n-2}+X_{n-55})\pmod m .
$$

Since all calculations are modulo $m$, subtraction is also valid modulo $m$.

Define

$$
E_n=X_{2n}, \qquad T_n=X_{3n}.
$$

We seek recurrences involving only the terms of each subsequence.

For the even-indexed subsequence, begin with

$$
X_n=X_{n-2}+X_{n-55}.
$$

Replacing $n$ by $n-53$ gives

$$
X_{n-53}=X_{n-55}+X_{n-108}.
$$

Hence

$$
X_{n-55}=X_{n-53}-X_{n-108}.
$$

Substituting into the original recurrence yields

$$
X_n=X_{n-2}+X_{n-53}-X_{n-108}.
$$

Now set $n=2r$. Then

$$
X_{2r}=X_{2r-2}+X_{2r-53}-X_{2r-108}.
$$

The odd-indexed term $X_{2r-53}$ can be eliminated by applying the same relation with $n$ replaced by $2r-53$:

$$
X_{2r-53}=X_{2r-55}+X_{2r-106}-X_{2r-161}.
$$

Since

$$
X_{2r-55}=X_{2r-2}-X_{2r-108},
$$

obtained from the original recurrence at index $2r$, we get

$$
X_{2r-53}
=
X_{2r-2}+X_{2r-106}-X_{2r-108}-X_{2r-161}.
$$

Substituting into the formula for $X_{2r}$,

$$
X_{2r}
=
2X_{2r-2}+X_{2r-106}-2X_{2r-108}-X_{2r-161}.
$$

Using

$$
X_{2r-161}=X_{2r-108}-X_{2r-214},
$$

which follows from the original recurrence at index $2r-106$, we obtain

$$
X_{2r}
=
2X_{2r-2}+X_{2r-106}-3X_{2r-108}+X_{2r-214}.
$$

Therefore

$$
E_n
=
2E_{n-1}+E_{n-53}-3E_{n-54}+E_{n-107}
\pmod m.
$$

Next consider the subsequence $T_n=X_{3n}$.

The recurrence may be written with the shift operator $L$ as

$$
(1-L^2-L^{55})X_n=0.
$$

Let

$$
f(z)=1-z^2-z^{55}.
$$

The subsequence $T_n=X_{3n}$ satisfies the recurrence whose characteristic polynomial is obtained by replacing each root $\alpha$ of $f$ by $\alpha^3$. Algebraically this polynomial is

$$
g(z)=\prod_{\omega^3=1} f(\omega z).
$$

Since

$$
f(z)=1-z^2-z^{55},
$$

and since $55\equiv1\pmod3$,

$$
f(\omega z)=1-\omega^2 z^2-\omega z^{55}.
$$

Therefore

$$
\begin{aligned}
g(z)
&=(1-z^2-z^{55})
   (1-\omega^2 z^2-\omega z^{55})
   (1-\omega z^2-\omega^2 z^{55})\\
&=(1-z^2)^3-z^{165}.
\end{aligned}
$$

Using

$$
(1-z^2)^3
=
1-3z^2+3z^4-z^6,
$$

we obtain

$$
g(z)=1-3z^2+3z^4-z^6-z^{165}.
$$

Hence

$$
T_n-3T_{n-2}+3T_{n-4}-T_{n-6}-T_{n-165}=0
\pmod m,
$$

or

$$
T_n
=
3T_{n-2}-3T_{n-4}+T_{n-6}+T_{n-165}
\pmod m.
$$

Thus the subsequences satisfy

$$
\boxed{
X_{2n}
=
2X_{2n-2}+X_{2n-106}-3X_{2n-108}+X_{2n-214}
\pmod m
}
$$

and

$$
\boxed{
X_{3n}
=
3X_{3n-6}-3X_{3n-12}+X_{3n-18}+X_{3n-495}
\pmod m
}.
$$
