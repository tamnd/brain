**Exercise 26.** [*M48*] $[M48]$ (J. F. Reiser.) Let $p$ be prime and let $k$ be a positive integer. Given integers $a_1, \ldots, a_k$ and $x_1, \ldots, x_k$, let $\lambda_n$ be the period of the sequence $(X_n)$ generated by the recurrence

$$X_n = x_n \bmod p^n, \quad 0 \le n < k; \qquad X_n = (a_1 X_{n-1} + \cdots + a_k X_{n-k}) \bmod p^n, \quad n \ge k;$$

and let $N_\lambda$ be the number of $n$s that occur in the period (the number of indices $j$ such that $\mu_0 \le j < \mu_0 + \lambda_0$ and $X_j = 0$). Prove or disprove the following conjecture: There exists a constant $c$ (depending possibly on $p$ and $k$ and $a_1, \ldots, a_k$) such that $N_\lambda \le cp^{(k-A-1/2)\lambda}$ provided that $\lambda \ge \lambda_0$.

[*Notes:* Reiser has proved that if the recurrence has maximum period length $m_0$ (that is, if $\lambda_0 = p^k - 1$), and if the conjecture holds, then the $k$-dimensional discrepancy of $(X_n)$ will be $O(p^{p-m/(k+1/k-1)} \cdot n)$ as $\alpha \to \infty$; thus an additive generator like (7) would be well distributed in 55 dimensions, when $m = 2^t$ and the large period is confirmed. (See Section 3.3.4 for the definition of discrepancy in $k$ dimensions.) The conjecture is a very weak condition, for if $(X_n)$ takes on each value about equally often and if $\lambda_n = p^{k-1}(p^k - 1)$, the quantity $N_\lambda \approx (p^k - 1)/p$ does not grow at all as $\alpha$ increases. Reiser has verified the conjecture for $k = 1$. On the other hand he has shown that it is possible to find unusually bad starting values $x_1, \ldots, x_k$ (depending on $\alpha$) so that $N_{2\lambda} \ge p^t$, provided that $\lambda_n = p^{t-1}(p^k - 1)$ and $k \ge 3$ and $\alpha$ is sufficiently large.]

## Setup

Let $(X_n)$ be the sequence defined modulo $p^\lambda$ by

$$
X_n=x_n \pmod{p^\lambda}, \qquad 0\le n<k,
$$

and

$$
X_n=(a_1X_{n-1}+\cdots+a_kX_{n-k})\pmod{p^\lambda},
\qquad n\ge k.
$$

Let $\lambda_\lambda$ denote the period length of this sequence modulo $p^\lambda$, and let $N_\lambda$ denote the number of indices in one period for which $X_n=0$.

The conjecture asks whether there exists a constant $c$, independent of $\lambda$, such that

$$
N_\lambda\le c\,p^{(k-\lambda-\frac12)\lambda}
$$

for all sufficiently large $\lambda$.

The exercise asks for a proof or a disproof.

## Solution

This problem is not a routine theorem but a research conjecture due to Reiser. The information supplied in the statement already records the strongest results known in the context of Section 3.2.2.

For $k=1$, Reiser proved the conjecture. In that case the recurrence reduces to a multiplicative congruential sequence modulo powers of $p$, and the asserted bound on $N_\lambda$ is known to hold.

For $k\ge3$, the statement also records a family of constructions showing that exceptionally bad initial values can occur. More precisely, Reiser proved that, when

$$
\lambda_\lambda=p^{\lambda-1}(p^k-1),
$$

and $\lambda$ is sufficiently large, there exist starting values depending on $\lambda$ such that

$$
N_\lambda\ge p^\lambda .
$$

This demonstrates that $N_\lambda$ can grow exponentially with $\lambda$. However, this lower bound does not contradict the conjecture, because

$$
p^\lambda
=
p^{1\cdot \lambda}
\ll
p^{(k-\frac32)\lambda}
$$

whenever $k\ge3$.

Thus the known counterexamples do not violate the proposed upper bound.

No proof is known in the general case, and no counterexample is known that contradicts the stated estimate. Consequently the exercise cannot be completed by establishing either direction from the material of Section 3.2.2.

The correct conclusion is therefore that the conjecture remains unresolved in general. The only definitive statement available from the given context is:

$$
\boxed{\text{The conjecture is proved for }k=1,\text{ and is open in general.}}
$$

## Verification

The lower bound quoted in the notes is

$$
N_\lambda\ge p^\lambda .
$$

The conjectured upper bound is

$$
N_\lambda\le c\,p^{(k-\frac32)\lambda}.
$$

For every $k\ge3$,

$$
k-\frac32 \ge \frac32 >1,
$$

hence

$$
p^\lambda
=
o\!\left(p^{(k-\frac32)\lambda}\right).
$$

Therefore Reiser's construction of large values of $N_\lambda$ does not disprove the conjecture.

The notes explicitly state that Reiser verified the conjecture for $k=1$, so the conjecture is known in that special case.

No argument in Section 3.2.2 establishes the conjecture for arbitrary $k$, and no contradiction to it is provided. Hence neither a proof nor a disproof is presently available from the theory presented here.

## Notes

The importance of the conjecture is explained in the statement itself. Reiser showed that, when the recurrence has maximal period length and the conjectured estimate holds, strong bounds for the $k$-dimensional discrepancy follow. Consequently the conjecture serves as a bridge between the algebraic structure of linear recurrences modulo powers of $p$ and the distribution properties needed for random-number generation.
