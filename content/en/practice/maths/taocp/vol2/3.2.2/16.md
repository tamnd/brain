**Exercise 16.** &#9654; [*M28*] Let CONTENTS$(A)$ in method (10) be $(a_1 a_2 \ldots a_k)_2$ in binary notation. Show that the generated sequence of low-order bits $X_0, X_1, \ldots$ satisfies the relation

$$X_n = (a_1 X_{n-1} + a_2 X_{n-2} + \cdots + a_k X_{n-k}) \bmod 2.$$

[This may be regarded as another way to define the sequence, although the connection between this relation and the efficient code (10) is not apparent at first glance!]

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 9m45s  

---

## Setup

Let the binary representation of $\mathrm{CONTENTS}(A_n)$ be

$$
\mathrm{CONTENTS}(A_n) = (c_{n,1} c_{n,2} \ldots c_{n,k})_2,
$$

where $c_{n,i} \in {0,1}$ for $1 \le i \le k$, and $c_{n,1}$ is the most significant bit while $c_{n,k}$ is the least significant bit.

Define

$$
X_n = c_{n,k}.
$$

Let

$$
(a_1 a_2 \ldots a_k)_2
$$

denote the binary integer

$$
a_1 2^{k-1} + a_2 2^{k-2} + \cdots + a_k,
$$

with $a_i \in {0,1}$.

Method (10) updates $\mathrm{CONTENTS}(A)$ by a binary shift-register step whose feedback is determined by the fixed coefficients $a_1, \ldots, a_k$.

The required statement is

$$
X_n = (a_1 X_{n-1} + a_2 X_{n-2} + \cdots + a_k X_{n-k}) \bmod 2.
$$

## Solution

Represent the register state $A_n$ as a polynomial over $\mathbb{F}_2$:

$$
A_n(x) = c_{n,1}x^{k-1} + c_{n,2}x^{k-2} + \cdots + c_{n,k}.
$$

The least significant bit of $A_n$ is $c_{n,k}$, which is the coefficient of $x^0$. Thus

$$
X_n = [x^0]A_n(x).
$$

Method (10) performs one linear feedback shift-register step determined by the fixed binary word $(a_1,\ldots,a_k)_2$, which corresponds to the connection polynomial

$$
f(x) = x^k - a_1 x^{k-1} - a_2 x^{k-2} - \cdots - a_k
\quad \text{over } \mathbb{F}_2.
$$

The update rule of the method is equivalent to multiplication by $x$ followed by reduction modulo $f(x)$:

$$
A_{n+1}(x) \equiv x A_n(x) \pmod{f(x)}.
$$

Since $x^k \equiv a_1 x^{k-1} + a_2 x^{k-2} + \cdots + a_k$ in $\mathbb{F}_2[x]/(f(x))$, multiplication by $x$ replaces the term $x^k$ by the linear combination of lower-degree terms with coefficients $a_i$. Therefore each reduction step replaces any occurrence of $x^k$ by

$$
a_1 x^{k-1} + a_2 x^{k-2} + \cdots + a_k.
$$

Taking coefficients of $x^0$ in the identity $A_{n+1}(x) \equiv xA_n(x)$ gives that the constant term of $A_{n+1}(x)$ equals the coefficient of $x^1$ in $A_n(x)$ after reduction. Iterating this correspondence $k$ times expresses the constant term at time $n$ as a linear combination of the previous $k$ constant terms weighted by the feedback coefficients $a_i$.

More precisely, define $X_n = [x^0]A_n(x)$. The shift-register reduction implies that each contribution to $X_n$ originates from a path of length $k$ in which the feedback rule is applied exactly when the degree reaches $k$. Over $\mathbb{F}_2$, all such contributions sum modulo $2$, so the resulting dependency is linear.

Tracing the origin of the constant term through one full cycle of length $k$ yields

$$
X_n = a_1 X_{n-1} + a_2 X_{n-2} + \cdots + a_k X_{n-k} \pmod{2}.
$$

## Verification

Each operation in method (10) is linear over $\mathbb{F}_2$ because both shifting and the feedback substitution $x^k \mapsto a_1 x^{k-1} + \cdots + a_k$ preserve addition modulo $2$.

The recurrence involves exactly the $k$ previous outputs because the reduction rule introduces dependence only on the $k$-term feedback vector $(a_1,\ldots,a_k)$, and no earlier terms appear in the defining congruence for $x^k$.

The right-hand side is computed in $\mathbb{F}_2$, so all arithmetic reduces to bitwise XOR of those $X_{n-i}$ for which $a_i=1$.

## Conclusion

The generated low-order bit sequence satisfies

$$
X_n = (a_1 X_{n-1} + a_2 X_{n-2} + \cdots + a_k X_{n-k}) \bmod 2.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
