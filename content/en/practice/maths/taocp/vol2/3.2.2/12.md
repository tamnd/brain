**Exercise 12.** &#9654; [*M28*] Find integers $X_0$, $X_1$, $a$, $b$, and $c$ such that the sequence

$$X_{n+1} = (aX_n + bX_{n-1} + c) \bmod 2^e, \qquad n \ge 1,$$

has the longest period length of all sequences of this type. [*Hint:* It follows that $X_{n+2} = ((a+1)X_{n+1} + (b-a)X_n - bX_{n-1}) \bmod 2^e$; see exercise 11(c).]

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 18m59s  

---

## Setup

The sequence is defined modulo $2^e$ by

$X_{n+1} = aX_n + bX_{n-1} + c \pmod{2^e}, \qquad n \ge 1.$

The goal is to choose integers $a,b,c,X_0,X_1$ so that the resulting sequence has maximal possible period among all sequences of this form.

Introduce the second-order linear part

$L_n = aL_{n-1} + bL_{n-2} \pmod{2^e}.$

The affine recurrence can be reduced to this homogeneous form by removing a fixed point.

A fixed point $x^\ast$ satisfies

$x^\ast \equiv ax^\ast + bx^\ast + c \pmod{2^e},$

hence

$(1-a-b)x^\ast \equiv c \pmod{2^e}.$

Assume $1-a-b$ is odd, so it is invertible modulo $2^e$, and $x^\ast$ exists.

Define

$Y_n = X_n - x^\ast \pmod{2^e}.$

Then

$Y_{n+1} \equiv aY_n + bY_{n-1} \pmod{2^e},$

so the period of $\langle X_n\rangle$ equals the period of the homogeneous sequence $\langle Y_n\rangle$.

Thus the problem reduces to maximizing the period of a second-order linear recurrence modulo $2^e$.

## Solution

Consider the linear recurrence

$Y_{n+1} = aY_n + bY_{n-1} \pmod{2^e}.$

Let its state vector be $(Y_n, Y_{n-1})$. The transition matrix is

$M = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}.$

The period of the sequence equals the order of $M$ in the semigroup of invertible matrices modulo $2^e$, provided $(Y_0,Y_1)$ is chosen so the orbit is maximal.

Over $\mathbf{F}_2$, the reduction of $M$ has characteristic polynomial

$\chi(x) = x^2 - ax - b \pmod{2}.$

To achieve maximal period, $\chi(x)$ must be primitive over $\mathbf{F}_2$, which yields maximal order $2^2-1=3$ for the nonzero part of the state space.

The choice

$a=1,\qquad b=1$

gives

$\chi(x)=x^2+x+1,$

which is irreducible over $\mathbf{F}_2$ and generates all nonzero states in the associated linear recurrence modulo $2$.

For modulus $2^e$, the lifting property from Exercise 3.2.2.11(c) applied to this order-$2$ recurrence yields that the period increases by a factor of $2$ at each lift from $2^r$ to $2^{r+1}$ until stabilization occurs. Combined with the fact that the mod-$2$ period is $3$, the maximal period modulo $2^e$ becomes

$3 \cdot 2^{e-1}.$

To incorporate the affine term, choose $c=1$ and define the fixed point $x^\ast$ by

$(1-a-b)x^\ast \equiv c \pmod{2^e}.$

With $a=b=1$, this becomes

$-x^\ast \equiv 1 \pmod{2^e}, \qquad x^\ast \equiv -1 \pmod{2^e}.$

Then $Y_n = X_n - x^\ast$ satisfies the homogeneous recurrence with the same $a,b$, so the period of $\langle X_n\rangle$ equals the period of $\langle Y_n\rangle$.

It remains to ensure maximality of the orbit. This requires $Y_0$ and $Y_1$ not both even, equivalently $X_0 - x^\ast$ and $X_1 - x^\ast$ not both even. A convenient choice is

$X_0 = 0,\qquad X_1 = 1.$

## Verification

With $a=b=1$, the reduced characteristic polynomial over $\mathbf{F}_2$ is $x^2+x+1$, which is irreducible, so the induced map on nonzero states modulo $2$ has order $3$.

The lifting argument of Exercise 3.2.2.11(c) applies because the recurrence has the required linear form and modulus is a power of $2$, hence each successful lift from $2^r$ to $2^{r+1}$ multiplies the period by $2$ until modulus $2^e$ is reached.

Starting from period $3$ modulo $2$, repeated lifting through $e-1$ steps yields period

$3 \cdot 2^{e-1}.$

The affine shift by $x^\ast$ defines a bijection between sequences $Y_n$ and $X_n$, so it preserves the period length. Thus the affine term $c$ does not change the maximal attainable period.

The condition that $X_0$ and $X_1$ are not both even ensures that the initial state does not lie in the degenerate subcycle of even residues, so the full period is realized.

## Result

A valid choice is

$a=1,\qquad b=1,\qquad c=1,\qquad X_0=0,\qquad X_1=1,$

which yields maximal period

$\boxed{3 \cdot 2^{e-1}}.$

This completes the solution. ∎

## Notes

The affine term contributes only a translation in state space after fixing a particular equilibrium, so the periodic structure is governed entirely by the linear part. The maximal period is therefore identical to that of the Fibonacci-type generator modulo $2^e$ with optimal parameters.
