**Exercise 6.** [*M20*] Consider $F_n(x)$, as given in Eq. (10), for fixed $x$. What is the probability that $F_n(x) = s/n$, given an integer $s$? What is the mean value of $F_n(x)$? What is the standard deviation?

**Solve time:** 5m18s

## Solution

Let the underlying distribution function be $F(x)$. Equation (10) defines the empirical distribution function

$$
F_n(x)=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{j=1}^{n} I(X_j\le x),
$$

where $I(X_j\le x)$ is $1$ if $X_j\le x$ and $0$ otherwise.

For fixed $x$, define

$$
Y=\sum_{j=1}^{n} I(X_j\le x).
$$

Then

$$
F_n(x)=\frac{Y}{n}.
$$

Since each observation is independent and

$$
\Pr(X_j\le x)=F(x),
$$

the random variables $I(X_j\le x)$ are independent Bernoulli variables with parameter $F(x)$. Hence $Y$ has the binomial distribution with parameters $n$ and $F(x)$.

Therefore

$$
\Pr\!\left(F_n(x)=\frac{s}{n}\right)
=
\Pr(Y=s)
=
\binom{n}{s}F(x)^s\bigl(1-F(x)\bigr)^{\,n-s},
$$

for $s=0,1,\ldots,n$.

The mean value of a binomial random variable $Y$ is

$$
E(Y)=nF(x).
$$

Consequently,

$$
E\!\left(F_n(x)\right)
=
E\!\left(\frac{Y}{n}\right)
=
\frac{1}{n}E(Y)
=
F(x).
$$

The variance of a binomial random variable $Y$ is

$$
\operatorname{Var}(Y)
=
nF(x)\bigl(1-F(x)\bigr).
$$

Hence

$$
\operatorname{Var}\!\left(F_n(x)\right)
=
\operatorname{Var}\!\left(\frac{Y}{n}\right)
=
\frac{1}{n^2}\operatorname{Var}(Y)
=
\frac{F(x)\bigl(1-F(x)\bigr)}{n}.
$$

Therefore the standard deviation is

$$
\sqrt{\operatorname{Var}\!\left(F_n(x)\right)}
=
\sqrt{\frac{F(x)\bigl(1-F(x)\bigr)}{n}}.
$$

Thus

$$
\Pr\!\left(F_n(x)=\frac{s}{n}\right)
=
\binom{n}{s}F(x)^s\bigl(1-F(x)\bigr)^{\,n-s},
$$

$$
E\!\left(F_n(x)\right)=F(x),
$$

and

$$
\sigma\!\left(F_n(x)\right)
=
\sqrt{\frac{F(x)\bigl(1-F(x)\bigr)}{n}}.
$$

Therefore

$$
\boxed{
\Pr\!\left(F_n(x)=\frac{s}{n}\right)
=
\binom{n}{s}F(x)^s(1-F(x))^{n-s},
\quad
E(F_n(x))=F(x),
\quad
\sigma(F_n(x))
=
\sqrt{\frac{F(x)(1-F(x))}{n}}
}
$$

This completes the proof.
