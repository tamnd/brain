**Exercise 2.** [*20*] If two dice are "loaded" so that, on one die, the value 1 will turn up exactly twice as often as any of the other values, and the other die is similarly biased towards 6, compute the probability $p_s$ that a total of exactly $s$ will appear on the two dice, for $2 \le s \le 12$.

## Solution

Let the first die be biased toward the value $1$, and let the second die be biased toward the value $6$.

Since the value $1$ on the first die occurs exactly twice as often as any other value, if the probability of each of the values $2,3,4,5,6$ is $x$, then

$2x+5x=1,$

hence

$x=\frac17.$

Therefore

P(\text{first die}=i)=\frac17\quad(2\le i\le 6).$$
Similarly, for the second die,
$$P(\text{second die}=6)=\frac27,\qquad P(\text{second die}=j)=\frac17\quad(1\le j\le 5).$$
The two dice are independent, so the probability of any ordered pair $(i,j)$ is the product of the corresponding probabilities. It is convenient to multiply all probabilities by $49$. Then the weight of a pair $(i,j)$ is
$$49\,P(i,j)= \begin{cases} 4,&(i,j)=(1,6),\\ 2,&i=1,\ j\ne6,\\ 2,&i\ne1,\ j=6,\\ 1,&i\ne1,\ j\ne6. \end{cases}

For each total $s$, we sum the weights of all ordered pairs satisfying $i+j=s$.

For $s=2$, only $(1,1)$ contributes:

$49p_2=2.$

For $s=3$, the pairs are $(1,2)$ and $(2,1)$:

$49p_3=2+1=3.$

For $s=4$, the pairs are $(1,3),(2,2),(3,1)$:

$49p_4=2+1+1=4.$

For $s=5$, the pairs are $(1,4),(2,3),(3,2),(4,1)$:

$49p_5=2+1+1+1=5.$

For $s=6$, the pairs are $(1,5),(2,4),(3,3),(4,2),(5,1)$:

$49p_6=2+1+1+1+1=6.$

For $s=7$, the pairs are

$(1,6),(2,5),(3,4),(4,3),(5,2),(6,1),$

hence

$49p_7=4+1+1+1+1+1=9.$

For $s=8$, the pairs are

$(2,6),(3,5),(4,4),(5,3),(6,2),$

hence

$49p_8=2+1+1+1+1=6.$

For $s=9$, the pairs are

$(3,6),(4,5),(5,4),(6,3),$

hence

$49p_9=2+1+1+1=5.$

For $s=10$, the pairs are

$(4,6),(5,5),(6,4),$

hence

$49p_{10}=2+1+1=4.$

For $s=11$, the pairs are

$(5,6),(6,5),$

hence

$49p_{11}=2+1=3.$

For $s=12$, only $(6,6)$ contributes:

$49p_{12}=2.$

Therefore

$$
\begin{aligned}
p_2&=\frac{2}{49},&
p_3&=\frac{3}{49},&
p_4&=\frac{4}{49},&
p_5&=\frac{5}{49},&
p_6&=\frac{6}{49},\\
p_7&=\frac{9}{49},&
p_8&=\frac{6}{49},&
p_9&=\frac{5}{49},&
p_{10}&=\frac{4}{49},&
p_{11}&=\frac{3}{49},&
p_{12}&=\frac{2}{49}.
\end{aligned}
$$

The probabilities sum to

=\frac{49}{49} =1,$$
as required. Thus
$$\boxed{ (p_2,p_3,p_4,p_5,p_6,p_7,p_8,p_9,p_{10},p_{11},p_{12}) = \left( \frac{2}{49}, \frac{3}{49}, \frac{4}{49}, \frac{5}{49}, \frac{6}{49}, \frac{9}{49}, \frac{6}{49}, \frac{5}{49}, \frac{4}{49}, \frac{3}{49}, \frac{2}{49} \right) }.$$
