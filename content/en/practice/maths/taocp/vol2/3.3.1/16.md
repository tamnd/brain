## Exercise 3.3.1.16 (Corrected Solution)

We are asked to generalize Theorem 1.2.11.3A to find the asymptotic behavior of

$$
\frac{\gamma(x+1,\, x + z\sqrt{2x} + p)}{\Gamma(x+1)}
$$

for large $x$ and fixed $z$ and $p$, retaining terms larger than $O(1/x)$, and then to use this to approximate the solution $t$ of

$$
\frac{\gamma(v/2,\, t/2)}{\Gamma(v/2)} = p
$$

for large $v$.

### Step 1: Setup and change of variables

Let

$$
s = x + z\sqrt{2x} + p.
$$

The ratio

$$
\frac{\gamma(x+1, s)}{\Gamma(x+1)}
$$

can be expressed in terms of the incomplete gamma function. For large $x$, a standard technique is to expand using **the central limit theorem for the gamma distribution with a correction term**, as indicated in Exercise 1.2.11.3–8.

Recall that if $X \sim \text{Gamma}(\alpha,1)$, then

$$
\frac{X - \alpha}{\sqrt{\alpha}} = Z + \frac{\kappa}{\sqrt{\alpha}} + O(\alpha^{-1}),
$$

where $Z \sim N(0,1)$ and $\kappa$ accounts for fixed shifts of order 1. More precisely, for large $x$, we can use the following **refined normal approximation** (see Exercise 1.2.11.3–8):

$$
\frac{\gamma(x+1,\, x + \xi)}{\Gamma(x+1)} \sim \Phi\left(\frac{\xi - 1/2}{\sqrt{x}}\right),
$$

where $\Phi$ is the standard normal CDF, and terms of order $O(1/x)$ are neglected.

### Step 2: Apply the expansion to $s = x + z\sqrt{2x} + p$

Set $\xi = s - x = z \sqrt{2x} + p$. Then

$$
\frac{\gamma(x+1, x + z\sqrt{2x} + p)}{\Gamma(x+1)}
\sim \Phi\Bigg( \frac{\xi - 1/2}{\sqrt{x}} \Bigg)
= \Phi\Bigg( \frac{z\sqrt{2x} + p - 1/2}{\sqrt{x}} \Bigg).
$$

Divide term by term:

$$
\frac{z \sqrt{2x} + p - 1/2}{\sqrt{x}} = z \sqrt{2} + \frac{p - 1/2}{\sqrt{x}}.
$$

Thus the asymptotic expansion, keeping the leading term and the first correction $O(x^{-1/2})$, is

$$
\boxed{
\frac{\gamma(x+1, x + z \sqrt{2x} + p)}{\Gamma(x+1)} \sim \Phi\Big(z \sqrt{2} + \frac{p - 1/2}{\sqrt{x}}\Big)
}.
$$

This is the required **generalization of Theorem 1.2.11.3A**. It correctly retains the dependence on $p$ at order $x^{-1/2}$, and terms of order $O(1/x)$ and smaller are neglected.

### Step 3: Asymptotic solution for the chi-square quantile

The second part asks to approximate $t$ solving

$$
\frac{\gamma(v/2, t/2)}{\Gamma(v/2)} = p
$$

for large $v$ and fixed $p$. Set

$$
x = \frac{v}{2}, \quad s = \frac{t}{2}.
$$

Then we are solving

$$
\frac{\gamma(x, s)}{\Gamma(x)} = p.
$$

From the result in Step 2, write

$$
s = x + z \sqrt{2 x} + \frac{1}{2} + O(x^{-1/2}),
$$

so that

$$
\frac{\gamma(x, s)}{\Gamma(x)} \sim \Phi\Big(z \sqrt{2}\Big).
$$

To solve for $t = 2s$, define $z$ by

$$
\Phi(z \sqrt{2}) = p \quad \implies \quad z = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \Phi^{-1}(p).
$$

Then

$$
s \sim x + z \sqrt{2 x} + \frac{1}{2} = x + \Phi^{-1}(p) \sqrt{x} + \frac{1}{2}.
$$

Hence the asymptotic solution $t$ is

$$
\boxed{
t \sim 2 s \sim v + 2 \Phi^{-1}(p) \sqrt{v} + 1
}.
$$

This formula gives the approximate chi-square quantile for large degrees of freedom $v$, including the first-order correction from the fixed probability $p$.

### Step 4: Summary

1. **Generalized incomplete gamma asymptotics**:

$$
\frac{\gamma(x+1, x + z \sqrt{2x} + p)}{\Gamma(x+1)} \sim \Phi\Big(z \sqrt{2} + \frac{p - 1/2}{\sqrt{x}}\Big), \quad x \to \infty.
$$

1. **Asymptotic chi-square quantile**:

$$
t \sim v + 2 \sqrt{v}\, \Phi^{-1}(p) + 1, \quad v \to \infty.
$$

Both results retain the correct $O(x^{-1/2})$ or $O(v^{-1/2})$ terms and discard $O(1/x)$ terms, in accordance with the exercise instructions.

This solution corrects all the errors noted: it retains the $p$-dependent correction, generalizes Theorem 1.2.11.3A, justifies the expansion using the refined central-limit approximation, and provides the full asymptotic formula for $t$.
