The statistic $K_{10}^{+}$ is computed from blocks of length $10$, but the Kolmogorov-Smirnov test in this exercise is not being applied to the original observations within those blocks.

Instead, the experiment produced **20 values** of $K_{10}^{+}$. These 20 values were then treated as a sample and plotted to form the empirical distribution shown in Fig. 4. The Kolmogorov-Smirnov statistic was computed from that empirical distribution.

For a Kolmogorov-Smirnov test, the parameter $n$ is the number of observations in the sample whose empirical distribution is being compared with the theoretical distribution. In this case, the sample consists of the 20 observed values

$$
K_{10,1}^{+}, K_{10,2}^{+}, \ldots, K_{10,20}^{+}.
$$

Therefore the empirical distribution is based on $20$ observations, so the appropriate KS tables are those for

$$
n = 20.
$$

The value $10$ refers only to the length of the sequence used to generate each individual $K_{10}^{+}$. Once those statistics have been computed, the KS test is performed on the collection of 20 resulting values. Hence the table entries for $n = 20$ are the correct ones to use, not the entries for $n = 10$.
