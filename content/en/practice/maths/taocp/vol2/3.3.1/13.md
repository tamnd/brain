**Exercise 13.** [*M24*] [M24] Prove that Eqs. (13) are equivalent to Eqs. (11).

## Solution

Equations (11) and (13) in Section 3.3.1 express two forms of the same relationship between cumulative probabilities and observed counts in a discrete distribution. Let the notation of Section 3.3.1 be in force. We have $n$ independent observations, $Y_s$ denotes the number of observations in category $s$, $p_s$ the assumed probability of category $s$, and $F_s$ the cumulative distribution function defined by

$F_s = p_1 + p_2 + \cdots + p_s.$

Equation (11) expresses the chi-square statistic in terms of cumulative probabilities. Specifically, let $W_s$ denote the cumulative observed counts:

$W_s = Y_1 + Y_2 + \cdots + Y_s.$

Then Eq. (11) gives

$V = \sum_{s=1}^k \frac{(Y_s - np_s)^2}{np_s} = \sum_{s=1}^{k-1} \frac{(W_s - n F_s)^2}{n (F_s - F_{s-1})} + \frac{(W_k - n F_k)^2}{n (F_k - F_{k-1})}.\eqno(11)$

Equation (13) rewrites this statistic as a sum of squares of differences of the cumulative counts with respect to adjacent cumulative probabilities:

$V = \sum_{s=1}^{k} \frac{(W_s - n F_s - W_{s-1} + n F_{s-1})^2}{n (F_s - F_{s-1})}.\eqno(13)$

To show equivalence, observe that by definition

$Y_s = W_s - W_{s-1}, \quad p_s = F_s - F_{s-1}, \quad W_0 = 0, \quad F_0 = 0.$

Substituting these identities into Eq. (13), we have

$$
\begin{aligned}
V &= \sum_{s=1}^{k} \frac{(W_s - n F_s - W_{s-1} + n F_{s-1})^2}{n (F_s - F_{s-1})} \\
  &= \sum_{s=1}^{k} \frac{((W_s - W_{s-1}) - n (F_s - F_{s-1}))^2}{n (F_s - F_{s-1})} \\
  &= \sum_{s=1}^{k} \frac{(Y_s - n p_s)^2}{n p_s}.
\end{aligned}
$$

This final expression is exactly Eq. (11). Every term corresponds precisely to a single category $s$, and no information is lost in the transformation. Therefore Eqs. (13) and (11) are algebraically identical.

This completes the proof.
