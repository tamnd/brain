**Exercise 19.** [**] *[HM$\infty$]* Develop tests, analogous to the Kolmogorov–Smirnov test, for use with multivariate distributions $F(x_1, \ldots, x_s) = \Pr(X_1 \le x_1, \ldots, X_s \le x_s)$. (Such procedures could be used, for example, in place of the "serial test" in the next section.)

A natural multivariate analogue of the Kolmogorov-Smirnov test is obtained by comparing the empirical distribution function

$$
F_n(x_1,\ldots,x_s)
=
\frac1n
\#\{\,j: X_{j1}\le x_1,\ldots,X_{js}\le x_s\,\}
$$

with the theoretical distribution function $F(x_1,\ldots,x_s)$. Define

$$
K_n^+
=
\sqrt n\,
\sup_{x_1,\ldots,x_s}
\bigl(F_n(x_1,\ldots,x_s)-F(x_1,\ldots,x_s)\bigr),
$$

$$
K_n^-
=
\sqrt n\,
\sup_{x_1,\ldots,x_s}
\bigl(F(x_1,\ldots,x_s)-F_n(x_1,\ldots,x_s)\bigr),
$$

and

$$
K_n=\max(K_n^+,K_n^-).
$$

Large values of these statistics indicate disagreement between the observed sample and the hypothesized multivariate distribution.

More generally, since there are $2^s$ orthants determined by a point $(x_1,\ldots,x_s)$, one may compare empirical and theoretical probabilities for every orthant and take the maximum absolute discrepancy. This yields tests sensitive to departures in any direction and serves as a multivariate counterpart of the one-dimensional Kolmogorov-Smirnov procedure. Such tests can be applied to successive $s$-tuples of random numbers in place of the serial test.
