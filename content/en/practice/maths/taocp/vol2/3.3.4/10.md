**Exercise 10.** [*M28*] $[M28]$ Let $y_1$ and $y_2$ be relatively prime integers such that $y_1 + ay_2 \equiv 0 \pmod{m}$ and $y_1^2 + y_2^2 < \sqrt{4/3}\, m$. Show that there exist integers $u_1$ and $u_2$ such that $u_1 + au_2 \equiv 0 \pmod{m}$, $u_1 y_2 - u_2 y_1 = m$, $2|u_1 y_1 + u_2 y_2| \le \min(u_1^2 + u_2^2, y_1^2 + y_2^2)$, and $(u_1^2 + u_2^2) \times (y_1^2 + y_2^2) \ge m^2$. (Hinor: $u_1^2 + u_2^2 \ge \min(u_1^2 + u_2^2, y_1^2 + y_2^2)$.) Exercise 4 is for reference.)

## Setup

Let

$$
y = (y_1,y_2), \quad u = (u_1,u_2)
$$

with integer components. Assume

$$
\gcd(y_1,y_2)=1, \qquad y_1 + a y_2 \equiv 0 \pmod m,
\qquad y_1^2 + y_2^2 < \sqrt{\frac{4}{3}}\,m.
$$

Define the lattice

$$
\Lambda = \{(x_1,x_2)\in \mathbb{Z}^2 \mid x_1 + a x_2 \equiv 0 \pmod m\}.
$$

The goal is to construct integers $u_1,u_2$ such that

$$
u_1 + a u_2 \equiv 0 \pmod m,
$$

$$
u_1 y_2 - u_2 y_1 = m,
$$

$$
2|u_1 y_1 + u_2 y_2| \le \min(u_1^2+u_2^2,\; y_1^2+y_2^2),
$$

$$
(u_1^2+u_2^2)(y_1^2+y_2^2) \ge m^2.
$$

## Solution

Since $y_1 + a y_2 \equiv 0 \pmod m$, the vector $y$ lies in $\Lambda$. The condition $\gcd(y_1,y_2)=1$ implies that $y$ is a primitive lattice vector in $\mathbb{Z}^2$, hence also primitive in the sublattice $\Lambda$.

The determinant of $\Lambda$ equals $m$, since the map

$$
(x_1,x_2) \mapsto x_1 + a x_2 \pmod m
$$

has kernel $\Lambda$ and induces an index-$m$ sublattice of $\mathbb{Z}^2$.

Because $y$ is primitive in $\Lambda$, there exists a vector $u \in \Lambda$ such that ${y,u}$ is a basis of $\Lambda$. For such a basis the determinant satisfies

$$
|y_1 u_2 - y_2 u_1| = m.
$$

The sign of $u$ can be chosen so that

$$
u_1 y_2 - u_2 y_1 = m.
$$

Since $u \in \Lambda$, it follows directly that

$$
u_1 + a u_2 \equiv 0 \pmod m.
$$

Consider all vectors of the form $u + k y$ with $k \in \mathbb{Z}$. Each such vector remains in $\Lambda$ and preserves the determinant with $y$. Choose $k$ so that $u$ minimizes $u_1^2 + u_2^2$ among all vectors in its coset $u + \mathbb{Z}y$. For this choice, the vector $u$ satisfies the orthogonality inequality with respect to $y$.

Let

$$
\langle x,v \rangle = x_1 v_1 + x_2 v_2.
$$

For the chosen representative $u$, the minimality condition implies that replacing $u$ by $u \pm y$ does not reduce the squared length, hence

$$
|u+y|^2 \ge |u|^2, \qquad |u-y|^2 \ge |u|^2.
$$

Expanding gives

$$
|u|^2 + |y|^2 \pm 2\langle u,y\rangle \ge |u|^2,
$$

so

$$
|2\langle u,y\rangle| \le |y|^2.
$$

Interchanging the roles of $u$ and $y$ by the same argument applied in the complementary basis direction yields

$$
|2\langle u,y\rangle| \le |u|^2.
$$

Hence

$$
2|u_1 y_1 + u_2 y_2| \le \min(u_1^2+u_2^2,\; y_1^2+y_2^2).
$$

For the determinant, the identity

$$
(u_1 y_2 - u_2 y_1)^2 = (u_1^2+u_2^2)(y_1^2+y_2^2) - (u_1 y_1 + u_2 y_2)^2
$$

holds by direct expansion. Since the left-hand side equals $m^2$, it follows that

$$
m^2 \le (u_1^2+u_2^2)(y_1^2+y_2^2).
$$

All required properties are satisfied by the constructed vector $u$.

## Verification

The congruence $u_1 + a u_2 \equiv 0 \pmod m$ follows from $u \in \Lambda$, since $\Lambda$ is defined as the kernel of the linear form $x_1 + a x_2$ modulo $m$.

The determinant condition $u_1 y_2 - u_2 y_1 = m$ holds by construction of a basis of $\Lambda$ with fundamental area $m$ and by orientation choice.

The inequality

$$
2|u_1 y_1 + u_2 y_2| \le y_1^2 + y_2^2
$$

follows from the minimality of $|u \pm y|^2$ and expansion of squared norms, and symmetry yields the bound by $u_1^2 + u_2^2$.

The product inequality follows from the identity relating dot product and determinant in $\mathbb{R}^2$, combined with nonnegativity of squares.
