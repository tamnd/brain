**Exercise 3.3.4.16** [_M30_] asks us to modify Algorithm S to compute the minimum number $N_t$ of parallel hyperplanes intersecting the unit hypercube, over all matrices $U$ satisfying the conditions of Exercise 3.3.4.15, and hints that we should consider analogs of positive definite quadratic forms and Lemma A.

### Solution

Let us first recall the context from Exercise 3.3.4.15. There we considered a real $n \times n$ matrix $U$ with determinant 1, and we were asked to bound the number $N_t$ of parallel hyperplanes of the form

$$
H_k = \{x \in \mathbb{R}^n : u^\top x = k\}, \quad k \in \mathbb{Z},
$$

that intersect the unit hypercube $[0,1]^n$. The goal was to find a configuration of $U$ minimizing $N_t$.

### Step 1: Analogy to Positive Definite Quadratic Forms

The hint suggests using an analogy to positive definite quadratic forms. In the classical lattice-theoretic approach:

1. The unit hypercube $[0,1]^n$ defines the fundamental domain in $\mathbb{R}^n$.
2. The number of parallel hyperplanes intersecting the hypercube is analogous to the number of lattice points intersecting a domain under a linear transformation.

Define the linear form corresponding to the first row of $U$ as

$$
\ell(x) = u_1^\top x,
$$

where $u_1$ is the first row of $U$. Then the hyperplanes of interest are

$$
H_k = \{x : \ell(x) = k\}, \quad k \in \mathbb{Z}.
$$

The **number of hyperplanes intersecting the hypercube** is

$$
N_t = \max_{x \in [0,1]^n} \ell(x) - \min_{x \in [0,1]^n} \ell(x) + 1.
$$

Because $\ell$ is linear, its maximum and minimum are attained at vertices of the hypercube. Hence

$$
\max_{x \in [0,1]^n} \ell(x) = \sum_{j=1}^n \max(0, u_{1j}), \quad
\min_{x \in [0,1]^n} \ell(x) = \sum_{j=1}^n \min(0, u_{1j}).
$$

Therefore,

$$
N_t = 1 + \sum_{j=1}^n |u_{1j}|.
$$

Minimizing $N_t$ over all $U$ with $|\det U| = 1$ is analogous to minimizing a "norm" of the first row under the determinant constraint, which is exactly the type of problem encountered in lattice reduction and is analogous to **minimizing a positive definite quadratic form over integer vectors**, as in Minkowski’s theorem.

### Step 2: Modification of Algorithm S

Algorithm S in TAOCP Section 3.3.4 is designed to enumerate matrices $U$ with determinant 1 and to minimize a quadratic form $Q(U)$. In Exercise 15, $Q(U)$ corresponded to the sum of absolute values of entries in a row, squared, or to some other measure associated with the lattice basis.

To compute $N_t$, we proceed as follows:

1. **Choose a row** of $U$ corresponding to the direction orthogonal to the hyperplanes. Let this be the first row $u_1$.
2. **Enumerate candidate matrices $U$** using Algorithm S exactly as in the original, but at each candidate $U$:

- Compute

$$
N_t(U) = 1 + \sum_{j=1}^n |u_{1j}|.
$$
3. **Keep track of the minimum $N_t$** over all $U$ examined.

The steps in Algorithm S that ensure positivity of the quadratic form can be replaced by the criterion that $|\det U| = 1$ and that the candidate row $u_1$ is chosen to minimize $\sum |u_{1j}|$. The analog of **Lemma A** is that any candidate $u_1$ that is componentwise larger than the current minimum cannot yield a smaller $N_t$, so it can be pruned from enumeration.

### Step 3: Summary of the Algorithm

The modified Algorithm S for computing the minimum $N_t$ proceeds as:

1. Initialize $N_{\min} \gets \infty$.
2. For each matrix $U$ satisfying $|\det U| = 1$ (enumerated using Algorithm S):

1. Let $u_1$ be the first row of $U$.
2. Compute $N_t(U) = 1 + \sum_{j=1}^n |u_{1j}|$.
3. If $N_t(U) < N_{\min}$, set $N_{\min} \gets N_t(U)$.
3. Output $N_{\min}$ as the minimum number of hyperplanes intersecting the unit hypercube.

**Justification:**

- By the linearity of $\ell(x)$ and convexity of the hypercube, the maximum and minimum of $\ell$ occur at vertices, so the formula for $N_t$ is exact.
- The enumeration over all $U$ with $|\det U| = 1$ ensures that we consider all admissible bases, hence the global minimum is attained.
- Pruning via the analog of Lemma A reduces unnecessary computation without losing the minimum.

### Step 4: Analogy to Quadratic Forms

- The sum $\sum |u_{1j}|$ plays the role of a "norm" similar to a positive definite quadratic form in lattice reduction.
- Lemma A in Section 3.3.4 allows pruning based on partial sums of squares; here we prune based on partial sums of absolute values.
- This ensures efficiency while preserving correctness.

The minimum number $N_t$ of parallel hyperplanes intersecting the unit hypercube can be computed by modifying Algorithm S to track

$$
N_t(U) = 1 + \sum_{j=1}^n |u_{1j}|
$$

over all determinant-1 matrices $U$, pruning candidates using the analog of Lemma A. This modification is a natural generalization of the approach in Exercise 15 and correctly implements the hint.
