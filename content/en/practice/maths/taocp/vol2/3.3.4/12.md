**Exercise 12.** [*HM23*] $[HM23]$ Prove, without geometrical handwaving, that any solution to problem (b) following Eq. (23) must also satisfy the set of equations (26).

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 5m55s  

---

The earlier argument fails because it replaces a simple finite-generation observation with an incorrect lattice construction. The result does not require any lattice theory or integer bases. Everything follows from a direct reduction to differences with respect to a fixed base point.

## Corrected Solution

Let $u \in \mathbb{R}^t$ be a solution in the sense of problem (b) following Eq. (23). By definition, there exists a real number $\delta$ and integers $k(x)$ such that for every $x \in \{0,1,\dots,m-1\}$,

$$
u \cdot p(x) = k(x) + \delta.
$$

### Step 1: Eliminate $\delta$ via a fixed base point

Fix a reference index, say $0$. Subtract the equation for $x=0$ from the equation for arbitrary $x$:

$$
u \cdot p(x) - u \cdot p(0) = (k(x)+\delta) - (k(0)+\delta).
$$

Hence,

$$
u \cdot (p(x) - p(0)) = k(x) - k(0) \in \mathbb{Z}.
$$

Define the vectors

$$
d_x := p(x) - p(0), \quad x=1,\dots,m-1.
$$

Then the condition becomes

$$
u \cdot d_x \in \mathbb{Z}, \quad x=1,\dots,m-1. \tag{*}
$$

This is a finite system of $m-1$ linear congruence conditions.

### Step 2: Recover all pairwise differences

Now show that condition $(*)$ is equivalent to integrality for all differences $p(x)-p(y)$.

For any $x,y$,

$$
p(x)-p(y) = (p(x)-p(0)) - (p(y)-p(0)) = d_x - d_y.
$$

Therefore,

$$
u \cdot (p(x)-p(y)) = u \cdot d_x - u \cdot d_y.
$$

If $u \cdot d_x \in \mathbb{Z}$ for all $x$, then both terms on the right are integers, hence

$$
u \cdot (p(x)-p(y)) \in \mathbb{Z} \quad \forall x,y.
$$

Conversely, if all pairwise differences are integral, then in particular taking $y=0$ gives $u \cdot d_x \in \mathbb{Z}$. Thus the two formulations are equivalent.

This step uses only linearity and does not require any structural assumptions about lattices, rank, or spanning.

### Step 3: Matrix formulation (system (26))

Let $D$ be the $(m-1)\times t$ matrix whose rows are the vectors $d_x = p(x)-p(0)$, i.e.,

$$
D =
\begin{pmatrix}
(p(1)-p(0)) \\
(p(2)-p(0)) \\
\vdots \\
(p(m-1)-p(0))
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Then the condition $u \cdot d_x \in \mathbb{Z}$ for all $x$ is exactly

$$
Du \in \mathbb{Z}^{m-1}.
$$

This is precisely the finite system of linear congruences described in Eq. (26): each row enforces that the inner product of $u$ with a difference vector is an integer.

### Step 4: Conclusion

Starting from the covering condition in Eq. (23), we derived

$$
u \cdot (p(x)-p(y)) \in \mathbb{Z} \quad \forall x,y,
$$

and showed this is equivalent to the finite system

$$
u \cdot (p(x)-p(0)) \in \mathbb{Z}, \quad x=1,\dots,m-1,
$$

or in matrix form,

$$
Du \in \mathbb{Z}^{m-1},
$$

which is system (26).

No lattice structure, integer basis representation, or dimensional assumptions are required. The reduction is purely an application of subtracting a fixed reference equation and linearity of the inner product.

This completes the proof. ∎
