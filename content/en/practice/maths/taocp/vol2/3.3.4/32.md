**Exercise 32.** &#9654; [*M21*] Let $m_1 = 2^{31} - 1$ and $m_2 = 2^{31} - 249$ be the moduli of generator (38).

a) Show that if $U_n = (X_n/m_1 - Y_n/m_2) \bmod 1$, we have $U_n \approx Z_n/m_1$.

b) Let $W_0 = (X_0 m_2 - Y_0 m_1) \bmod m$ and $W_{n+1} = aW_n \bmod m$, where $a$ and $m$ have the values stated in the text following (38). Prove that there is a simple relation between $W_n$ and $U_n$.

## Solution

Let $m_1 = 2^{31} - 1$ and $m_2 = 2^{31} - 249$. In generator (38), $X_n$ and $Y_n$ are linear congruential sequences modulo $m_1$ and $m_2$ with the same multiplier $a$, so

$X_{n+1} \equiv aX_n \pmod{m_1}, \qquad Y_{n+1} \equiv aY_n \pmod{m_2}.$

### (a)

The definition

$U_n = \left(\frac{X_n}{m_1} - \frac{Y_n}{m_2}\right) \bmod 1$

can be rewritten by replacing $m_2$ with $m_1 - 249$.

Since $m_2 = m_1 - 249$, we expand

$\frac{1}{m_2} = \frac{1}{m_1 - 249} = \frac{1}{m_1}\cdot \frac{1}{1 - 249/m_1} = \frac{1}{m_1}\left(1 + \frac{249}{m_1} + O(m_1^{-2})\right).$

Hence

$\frac{Y_n}{m_2} = \frac{Y_n}{m_1} + O\!\left(\frac{Y_n}{m_1^2}\right).$

Since $0 \le Y_n < m_2 < m_1$, this implies

$\frac{Y_n}{m_2} = \frac{Y_n}{m_1} + O\!\left(\frac{1}{m_1}\right).$

Substituting into the definition of $U_n$ gives

$U_n = \left(\frac{X_n - Y_n}{m_1}\right) \bmod 1 + O\!\left(\frac{1}{m_1}\right).$

Let

$Z_n = (X_n - Y_n) \bmod m_1.$

Then

$\frac{X_n - Y_n}{m_1} \bmod 1 = \frac{Z_n}{m_1},$

so

$U_n = \frac{Z_n}{m_1} + O\!\left(\frac{1}{m_1}\right).$

This yields

$\boxed{U_n \approx \frac{Z_n}{m_1}}.$

### (b)

Let $m = m_1 m_2$ and define

$W_0 = (X_0 m_2 - Y_0 m_1) \bmod m,$

$W_{n+1} \equiv aW_n \pmod m.$

Since $X_{n+1} \equiv aX_n \pmod{m_1}$ and $Y_{n+1} \equiv aY_n \pmod{m_2}$, multiplication by $a$ preserves both congruences modulo $m_1$ and $m_2$. Therefore,

$aW_n \equiv aX_n m_2 - aY_n m_1 \pmod m,$

so

$W_{n+1} \equiv X_{n+1} m_2 - Y_{n+1} m_1 \pmod m.$

This determines

$W_n \equiv X_n m_2 - Y_n m_1 \pmod m$

for all $n$.

Reducing this identity modulo $m_1$ gives

$W_n \equiv -Y_n m_1 \pmod{m_1},$

so

$W_n \equiv X_n m_2 \pmod{m_1},$

since $m_2 \equiv -249 \pmod{m_1}$ and $m_1$ is invertible modulo $m_1$ trivially.

Similarly, reducing modulo $m_2$ gives

$W_n \equiv X_n m_2 \pmod{m_2}, \qquad W_n \equiv -Y_n m_1 \pmod{m_2}.$

Because $m_1$ and $m_2$ are coprime, these congruences determine $X_n$ and $Y_n$ uniquely from $W_n$.

Hence $W_n$ encodes the pair $(X_n, Y_n)$, and

$X_n \equiv W_n m_2^{-1} \pmod{m_1}, \qquad Y_n \equiv -W_n m_1^{-1} \pmod{m_2}.$

Substituting into the definition of $U_n$ yields a direct dependence on $W_n$:

$U_n = \left(\frac{W_n m_2^{-1} \bmod m_1}{m_1} - \frac{-W_n m_1^{-1} \bmod m_2}{m_2}\right) \bmod 1.$

Thus $U_n$ is obtained by applying fixed modular inverses and scaling to $W_n$, and the evolution of $U_n$ is entirely determined by the single LCG

$W_{n+1} \equiv aW_n \pmod m.$

This completes the proof. ∎
