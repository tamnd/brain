**Corrected Solution to Exercise 3.3.4.6**

Let

$$
\frac{a}{m} = [0; a_1, a_2, \dots, a_t]
$$

be the continued fraction expansion of $a/m$, and let

$$
A = \max_{1 \le j \le t} a_j.
$$

Let $p_j/q_j$ denote the $j$-th convergent of $a/m$. Define

$$
r_j = |p_j m - a q_j| \quad (0 \le j \le t-1),
$$

so that each vector $(r_j, q_j)$ is a lattice vector satisfying

$$
x + a y \equiv 0 \pmod m.
$$

The quantity $\nu_2$ is the length of the shortest nonzero vector in this lattice:

$$
\nu_2 = \min_{(x,y) \neq (0,0),\ x+ay\equiv 0\!\!\!\pmod m} \sqrt{x^2+y^2}.
$$

By the standard theory of continued fractions (see Theorem 3.3.3 in TAOCP), the shortest nonzero lattice vector occurs among $(r_j, q_j)$, that is,

$$
\nu_2^2 = \min_{0\le j \le t-1} (r_j^2 + q_j^2).
$$

This establishes the previously missing justification that the convergents yield all candidate shortest vectors.

We now aim to bound $\nu_2$ from below in terms of $A$.

$$

$$

$$
m = q_j r_{j+1} + q_{j+1} r_j
$$

for each $0 \le j \le t-1$.
