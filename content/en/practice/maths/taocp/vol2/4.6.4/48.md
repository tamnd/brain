[Section 4.6.4: Evaluation of Polynomials](../)

**Exercise 48.** [*M21*] If $\{t_{ijk}\}$ and $\{t'_{ijk}\}$ are tensors of sizes $m \times n \times s$ and $m' \times n' \times s'$, respectively, their *direct sum* $\{t_{ijk}\} \oplus \{t'_{ijk}\} = \{t''_{ijk}\}$ is the $(m + m') \times (n + n') \times (s + s')$ tensor defined by $t''_{ijk} = t_{ijk}$ if $i \le m$, $j \le n$, $k \le s$; $t''_{ijk} = t'_{i-m,j-n,k-s}$ if $i > m$, $j > n$, $k > s$; and $t''_{ijk} = 0$ otherwise. Their *direct product* $\{t_{ijk}\} \otimes \{t'_{ijk}\} = \{t''_{ijk}\}$ is the $mm' \times nn' \times ss'$ tensor defined by $t''_{(i,i')(j,j')(k,k')} = t_{ijk} t'_{i'j'k'}$. Prove the upper bounds $\text{rank}(t''_{ijk}) \le \text{rank}(t_{ijk}) + \text{rank}(t'_{ijk})$ and $\text{rank}(t''_{ijk}) \le \text{rank}(t_{ijk}) \cdot \text{rank}(t'_{ijk})$.

## Solution

Let ${t_{ijk}}$ be an $m \times n \times s$ tensor of rank $r = \text{rank}(t_{ijk})$, and let ${t'_{ijk}}$ be an $m' \times n' \times s'$ tensor of rank $r' = \text{rank}(t'_{ijk})$. By definition, there exist decompositions

t_{ijk} = \sum_{\alpha=1}^{r} a_i^{(\alpha)} b_j^{(\alpha)} c_k^{(\alpha)}, \qquad t'_{i'j'k'} = \sum_{\beta=1}^{r'} a'_{i'}^{(\beta)} b'_{j'}^{(\beta)} c'_{k'}^{(\beta)},

where $a_i^{(\alpha)}, b_j^{(\alpha)}, c_k^{(\alpha)}$ and $a'_{i'}^{(\beta)}, b'_{j'}^{(\beta)}, c'_{k'}^{(\beta)}$ are scalars.

### Direct Sum

The direct sum ${t''_{ijk}} = {t_{ijk}} \oplus {t'_{ijk}}$ is defined as an $(m + m') \times (n + n') \times (s + s')$ tensor with entries

$$
t''_{ijk} =
\begin{cases}
t_{ijk}, & 1 \le i \le m,\, 1 \le j \le n,\, 1 \le k \le s,\\[2mm]
t'_{i-m,j-n,k-s}, & m < i \le m+m',\, n < j \le n+n',\, s < k \le s+s',\\[1mm]
0, & \text{otherwise}.
\end{cases}
$$

Define extended vectors $\tilde a_i^{(\alpha)}$, $\tilde b_j^{(\alpha)}$, $\tilde c_k^{(\alpha)}$ for $1 \le i \le m+m'$, $1 \le j \le n+n'$, $1 \le k \le s+s'$ by

$$
\tilde a_i^{(\alpha)} =
\begin{cases}
a_i^{(\alpha)}, & i \le m,\\
0, & i > m,
\end{cases} \qquad
\tilde b_j^{(\alpha)} =
\begin{cases}
b_j^{(\alpha)}, & j \le n,\\
0, & j > n,
\end{cases} \qquad
\tilde c_k^{(\alpha)} =
\begin{cases}
c_k^{(\alpha)}, & k \le s,\\
0, & k > s,
\end{cases}
$$

and similarly

\tilde a_{i}^{\prime (\beta)} = \begin{cases} 0, & i \le m,\\ a'_{i-m}^{(\beta)}, & i > m, \end{cases} \quad \tilde b_{j}^{\prime (\beta)} = \begin{cases} 0, & j \le n,\\ b'_{j-n}^{(\beta)}, & j > n, \end{cases} \quad \tilde c_{k}^{\prime (\beta)} = \begin{cases} 0, & k \le s,\\ c'_{k-s}^{(\beta)}, & k > s. \end{cases}

Then the direct sum admits the decomposition

$$
t''_{ijk} = \sum_{\alpha=1}^{r} \tilde a_i^{(\alpha)} \tilde b_j^{(\alpha)} \tilde c_k^{(\alpha)}
+ \sum_{\beta=1}^{r'} \tilde a_i^{\prime (\beta)} \tilde b_j^{\prime (\beta)} \tilde c_k^{\prime (\beta)}.
$$

Each term in the sums is of rank one, so $\text{rank}(t''_{ijk}) \le r + r'$. This establishes the upper bound for the direct sum.

### Direct Product

The direct product ${t''_{(i,i')(j,j')(k,k')}} = {t_{ijk}} \otimes {t'_{ijk}}$ is defined as an $mm' \times nn' \times ss'$ tensor with entries

$$
t''_{(i,i')(j,j')(k,k')} = t_{ijk} \, t'_{i'j'k'}.
$$

Substitute the decompositions of $t_{ijk}$ and $t'_{i'j'k'}$:

\begin{aligned} t''_{(i,i')(j,j')(k,k')} &= \Bigg( \sum_{\alpha=1}^{r} a_i^{(\alpha)} b_j^{(\alpha)} c_k^{(\alpha)} \Bigg) \Bigg( \sum_{\beta=1}^{r'} a'_{i'}^{(\beta)} b'_{j'}^{(\beta)} c'_{k'}^{(\beta)} \Bigg)\\ &= \sum_{\alpha=1}^{r} \sum_{\beta=1}^{r'} \big( a_i^{(\alpha)} a'_{i'}^{(\beta)} \big) \big( b_j^{(\alpha)} b'_{j'}^{(\beta)} \big) \big( c_k^{(\alpha)} c'_{k'}^{(\beta)} \big). \end{aligned}

Each term in the double sum is of rank one as a product of vectors in the three indices. Since there are $r r'$ terms, it follows that $\text{rank}(t''_{(i,i')(j,j')(k,k')}) \le r \cdot r'$.
