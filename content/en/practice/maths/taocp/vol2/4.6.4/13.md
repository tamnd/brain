**Solution to Exercise 4.6.4.13**

We are asked to find the inverse of the general discrete Fourier transform (DFT) defined in equation (37). Let us restate the problem clearly. The general DFT of a function $F(t_1, \ldots, t_n)$ is defined by

$$
f(s_1, \ldots, s_n) = \sum_{t_1=0}^{m_1-1} \cdots \sum_{t_n=0}^{m_n-1} F(t_1, \ldots, t_n) \, \omega_1^{s_1 t_1} \cdots \omega_n^{s_n t_n},
$$

where $\omega_k = e^{2 \pi i / m_k}$ is a primitive $m_k$-th root of unity, for $1 \le k \le n$, and each $s_k$ and $t_k$ runs over integers $0, 1, \ldots, m_k-1$.

The goal is to express $F(t_1, \ldots, t_n)$ in terms of the values $f(s_1, \ldots, s_n)$.

### Step 1: Recall the orthogonality property of roots of unity

For a primitive $m$-th root of unity $\omega$, the sums satisfy

$$
\sum_{s=0}^{m-1} \omega^{s t} =
\begin{cases}
m, & \text{if } t \equiv 0 \pmod{m}, \\
0, & \text{otherwise}.
\end{cases}
$$

Equivalently, for $0 \le t, t' < m$,

$$
\frac{1}{m} \sum_{s=0}^{m-1} \omega^{s (t-t')} = \delta_{t t'},
$$

where $\delta_{t t'}$ is the Kronecker delta. This property underlies the inversion of the discrete Fourier transform.

### Step 2: Apply the orthogonality to invert each variable

We have

$$
f(s_1, \ldots, s_n) = \sum_{t_1=0}^{m_1-1} \cdots \sum_{t_n=0}^{m_n-1} F(t_1, \ldots, t_n) \prod_{k=1}^{n} \omega_k^{s_k t_k}.
$$

Multiply both sides by $\prod_{k=1}^{n} \omega_k^{-s_k t_k'}$ for a fixed multi-index $(t_1', \ldots, t_n')$ and sum over all $s_1, \ldots, s_n$:

$$
\sum_{s_1=0}^{m_1-1} \cdots \sum_{s_n=0}^{m_n-1} f(s_1, \ldots, s_n) \prod_{k=1}^{n} \omega_k^{-s_k t_k'} = \sum_{t_1=0}^{m_1-1} \cdots \sum_{t_n=0}^{m_n-1} F(t_1, \ldots, t_n) \sum_{s_1=0}^{m_1-1} \omega_1^{s_1 (t_1 - t_1')} \cdots \sum_{s_n=0}^{m_n-1} \omega_n^{s_n (t_n - t_n')}.
$$

By the orthogonality property, each inner sum satisfies

$$
\sum_{s_k=0}^{m_k-1} \omega_k^{s_k (t_k - t_k')} = m_k \, \delta_{t_k t_k'}.
$$

Hence the right-hand side collapses to

$$
\prod_{k=1}^{n} m_k \, F(t_1', \ldots, t_n').
$$

### Step 3: Solve for $F(t_1', \ldots, t_n')$

Dividing both sides by $\prod_{k=1}^{n} m_k$ gives the inverse DFT formula:

$$
F(t_1, \ldots, t_n) = \frac{1}{m_1 \cdots m_n} \sum_{s_1=0}^{m_1-1} \cdots \sum_{s_n=0}^{m_n-1} f(s_1, \ldots, s_n) \prod_{k=1}^{n} \omega_k^{-s_k t_k}.
$$

### Step 4: Summary

The inverse of the general discrete Fourier transform is

$$
\boxed{
F(t_1, \ldots, t_n) = \frac{1}{\prod_{k=1}^{n} m_k} \sum_{s_1=0}^{m_1-1} \cdots \sum_{s_n=0}^{m_n-1} f(s_1, \ldots, s_n) \prod_{k=1}^{n} \omega_k^{-s_k t_k}, \quad \omega_k = e^{2 \pi i / m_k}.
}
$$

This formula expresses each $F(t_1, \ldots, t_n)$ explicitly in terms of the DFT values $f(s_1, \ldots, s_n)$, as required. ∎
