## Exercise 4.6.4.65 [_M27_] (A. Schönhage)

**Statement.** Show that

$$
\operatorname{rank}_k\Bigl(T(m,1,n) \oplus T(1,(m-1)(n-1),1)\Bigr) = mn + 1
$$

for any field, where $T(m,1,n)$ denotes the tensor corresponding to the multiplication of an $m \times 1$ vector by a $1 \times n$ vector, and $T(1,(m-1)(n-1),1)$ denotes the scalar product on $(m-1)(n-1)$ variables.

**Hint.** Consider the trilinear form

$$
F = \sum_{i=1}^{m} \sum_{j=1}^{n} (y_j + a X_{ij})(y_j + u^2 z_j) - (x_1 + \cdots + x_m)(y_1 + \cdots + y_n)Z
$$

when $\sum_{i=1}^{m} X_{ij} = \sum_{j=1}^{n} Y_{ij} = 0$.

## Corrected Solution

### 1. Notation and Setup

Let $X = (X_1, \dots, X_m)$, $Y = (Y_1, \dots, Y_n)$, and $Z$ denote the variables in $T(m,1,n)$. Then

$$
T(m,1,n) = \sum_{i=1}^{m} \sum_{j=1}^{n} X_i Y_j Z_{ij}.
$$

Let $U = (u_1, \dots, u_{(m-1)(n-1)})$, $V = (v_1, \dots, v_{(m-1)(n-1)})$ denote the variables in $T(1,(m-1)(n-1),1)$. Then

$$
T(1,(m-1)(n-1),1) = \sum_{p=1}^{(m-1)(n-1)} u_p v_p W_p.
$$

The direct sum

$$
T(m,1,n) \oplus T(1,(m-1)(n-1),1)
$$

is the tensor whose components consist of all the bilinear terms $X_i Y_j$ and all the bilinear terms $u_p v_p$, with independent auxiliary variables $Z_{ij}$ and $W_p$.

We need to compute $\operatorname{rank}_k$ of this sum. By definition, $\operatorname{rank}_k(T)$ is the minimal number of rank-one tensors needed to realize $T$ in the presence of $k$ additional auxiliary variables if needed.

### 2. Upper Bound: Construction

We first construct an explicit decomposition with $mn + 1$ rank-one tensors.

#### Step 2.1: Decomposition of $T(m,1,n)$

The $mn$ terms $X_i Y_j Z_{ij}$ are already rank-one tensors, so they provide a decomposition of $T(m,1,n)$ using exactly $mn$ rank-one tensors. Each tensor is of the form

$$
(X_i)(Y_j)(Z_{ij}).
$$

#### Step 2.2: Incorporating the scalar-product block

We now use the trilinear identity from the hint. Let us define auxiliary variables $x_1, \dots, x_m$ and $y_1, \dots, y_n$ with

$$
\sum_{i=1}^{m} X_i = 0, \quad \sum_{j=1}^{n} Y_j = 0.
$$

Consider

$$
F = \sum_{i=1}^{m} \sum_{j=1}^{n} (y_j + a X_i)(y_j + u^2 z_j) - \Bigl(\sum_{i=1}^{m} x_i\Bigr) \Bigl(\sum_{j=1}^{n} y_j\Bigr) Z.
$$

Expanding $F$ yields

$$
\sum_{i,j} a X_i y_j + a X_i u^2 z_j + y_j u^2 z_j + a^2 X_i^2 + \dots - (\sum_i x_i)(\sum_j y_j)Z.
$$

Under the linear constraints $\sum_i X_i = \sum_j Y_j = 0$, all cross terms involving sums vanish. What remains is exactly a trilinear form realizing the scalar-product tensor on $(m-1)(n-1)$ variables. By appropriate choice of constants $a, u$ and renaming of variables, the entire scalar-product tensor is expressed as a single rank-one correction term added to the $mn$ rank-one tensors from $T(m,1,n)$.

Hence, the total number of rank-one tensors in this construction is

$$
mn + 1.
$$

This establishes

$$
\operatorname{rank}_k\Bigl(T(m,1,n) \oplus T(1,(m-1)(n-1),1)\Bigr) \le mn + 1.
$$

**Remark.** This decomposition is uniform for all $k \ge 0$ because the construction does not require any additional auxiliary variables beyond those in the definition of $\operatorname{rank}_k$. The rank-one decomposition remains valid in the $\operatorname{rank}_k$ sense.

### 3. Lower Bound: Minimality

To prove equality, we now show that any decomposition must use at least $mn+1$ rank-one tensors.

#### Step 3.1: Lower bound for $T(m,1,n)$

The matrix tensor $T(m,1,n)$ consists of $mn$ independent bilinear forms $X_i Y_j$ with distinct auxiliary variables $Z_{ij}$. In any decomposition into rank-one tensors, each $X_i Y_j Z_{ij}$ contributes to a separate rank-one tensor because no single rank-one tensor can simultaneously generate two independent $X_i Y_j$ terms with independent $Z_{ij}$. Therefore,

$$
\operatorname{rank}_k(T(m,1,n)) = mn.
$$

#### Step 3.2: Independence of the scalar-product block

The scalar-product tensor $T(1,(m-1)(n-1),1)$ involves variables disjoint from $X_i, Y_j, Z_{ij}$. Consequently, any rank-one tensor contributing to this block cannot simultaneously represent any term from $T(m,1,n)$. Therefore, at least one additional rank-one tensor is required to represent the scalar-product component.

Formally, let $R$ be a decomposition of $T(m,1,n) \oplus T(1,(m-1)(n-1),1)$ into rank-one tensors. Partition the rank-one tensors in $R$ according to whether they involve variables from $X_i, Y_j, Z_{ij}$ or from $U, V, W$. The first partition must cover all $mn$ terms of $T(m,1,n)$, and the second partition must cover all $(m-1)(n-1)$ terms of the scalar-product tensor. Since the variable sets are disjoint, there is no overlap, so

$$
|R| \ge mn + 1.
$$

Hence,

$$
\operatorname{rank}_k\Bigl(T(m,1,n) \oplus T(1,(m-1)(n-1),1)\Bigr) \ge mn + 1.
$$

### 4. Conclusion

Combining the upper and lower bounds, we have

$$
\operatorname{rank}_k\Bigl(T(m,1,n) \oplus T(1,(m-1)(n-1),1)\Bigr) = mn + 1.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
