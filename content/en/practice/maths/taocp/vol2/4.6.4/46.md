**Exercise 46.** [*M28*] Prove that all pairs $(z_1, z_2)$ of bilinear forms in $(x_1, x_2)$ and $(y_1, y_2)$ can be evaluated with at most three chain multiplications. In other words, show that every $2 \times 2 \times 2$ tensor has rank $\le 3$.

Let the two bilinear forms be

$$
z_1(x,y)=x^\top A y,\qquad z_2(x,y)=x^\top B y,
$$

where $A,B\in \mathbb{F}^{2\times 2}$. The associated $2\times 2\times 2$ tensor has slices $A$ and $B$. A chain multiplication corresponds to one rank-1 tensor $u(x)\,v(y)\,w$, i.e., one outer product $(a^\top x)(b^\top y)\,c$, so the goal is to express the pair $(A,B)$ as a sum of at most three such rank-1 terms.

We proceed by reducing the structure of the pair $(A,B)$ using allowable transformations. Replacing $(z_1,z_2)$ by any invertible linear combination does not change the number of required chain multiplications, since it corresponds to a change of basis in the output space. Likewise, invertible changes of variables in $x$ or $y$ preserve tensor rank. Thus we may freely apply transformations of the form

$$
A \mapsto P^\top A Q,\quad B \mapsto P^\top B Q,\quad
(A,B)\mapsto (A+tB,\,B),
$$

whenever these are invertible operations on the respective spaces.

If either $A$ or $B$ has rank $1$, suppose without loss of generality that $A=uv^\top$. Then the first bilinear form is already one chain multiplication:

$$
z_1(x,y)=(u^\top x)(v^\top y).
$$

The second matrix $B$ is a $2\times 2$ matrix, hence has rank at most $2$, so it can be decomposed as a sum of two rank-1 matrices:

$$
B = u_1 v_1^\top + u_2 v_2^\top.
$$

Therefore the tensor decomposes as

$$
(A,B)= (u,v,e_1) + (u_1,v_1,e_2) + (u_2,v_2,e_2),
$$

after grouping coefficients into the output vectors $e_1,e_2$. This gives a representation using at most three chain multiplications.

Now assume both $A$ and $B$ have rank $2$. Consider the determinant polynomial

$$
p(t)=\det(A+tB).
$$

This is a quadratic polynomial in $t$. Over a field in which quadratic polynomials have a root (for example over $\mathbb{R}$ or $\mathbb{C}$), there exists $t$ such that $p(t)=0$, so $A+tB$ has rank $1$. Perform an invertible change of basis in the output space:

$$
z_1' = z_1 + t z_2,\qquad z_2' = z_2.
$$

This replaces the pair $(A,B)$ by $(A+tB,\,B)$ without changing tensor rank. Now we are in the previous case, since the first slice has rank $1$. Hence the tensor can again be written as a sum of one rank-1 term for $A+tB$ and at most two rank-1 terms for $B$, giving a total of at most three chain multiplications.

In all cases, the tensor decomposes into at most three rank-1 tensors. Therefore every $2\times 2\times 2$ tensor has rank at most $3$, which is equivalent to the statement that every pair of bilinear forms in two variables each can be evaluated using at most three chain multiplications.
