**Exercise 61.** [*M26*] [M26] Let $(t_{ijk})$ be a tensor over an arbitrary field. We define $\text{rank}_0(t_{ijk})$ as the minimum value of $r$ such that there is a realization of the form

$$\sum_{s=1}^{r} a_s(u) b_s(v) c_s(w) \epsilon_{ijk}(u) = t_{ijk} u^v + O(u^{v+1}),$$

where $a_s(u)$, $b_s(u)$, $c_s(u)$ are polynomials in $u$ over the field. Thus $\text{rank}_0$ is the ordinary rank of a tensor. Prove that

a) $\text{rank}_{k+1}(t_{ijk}) \le \text{rank}_k(t_{ijk})$;
b) $\text{rank}(t_{ijk}) \le \binom{n+2}{2} \cdot \text{rank}_k(t_{ijk})$;
c) $\text{rank}(t_{ijk} \oplus t'_{ijk}) \le \text{rank}_k(t_{ijk}) + \text{rank}_k(t'_{ijk})$, in the sense of exercise 48;
d) $\text{rank}_{k+1}((t_{ijk}) \otimes (t'_{ijk})) \le \text{rank}_k(t_{ijk}) \cdot \text{rank}_k(t'_{ijk})$;
e) $\text{rank}_{2T}((t_{ijk}) \otimes (t'_{ijk})) \le \text{rank}_T(t_{ijk})$, where $r = \text{rank}_T(t_{ijk})$ and $rT$ denotes the direct sum $T \oplus \cdots \oplus T$ of $r$ copies of $T$.

### Corrected solution to Exercise 4.6.4.61

We first restate the structure in a precise way consistent with the exercise.

A rank-$k$ realization of a tensor $t_{ijk}$ means that there exist polynomials $a_s(u), b_s(v), c_s(w)$ such that the formal series identity holds up to total degree $k$ in $u,v,w$:

$$
\sum_{s=1}^r a_s(u)b_s(v)c_s(w)
=
\sum_{i,j,k} t_{ijk} u^i v^j w^k
\quad \text{mod terms of total degree } \ge k+1.
$$

Thus equality is coefficientwise for all monomials $u^i v^j w^k$ with $i+j+k \le k$. The case $k=0$ is the ordinary tensor rank.

## (a) Monotonicity: $\mathrm{rank}_{k+1}(t) \le \mathrm{rank}_k(t)$

If a decomposition is valid up to total degree $k$, then it matches all coefficients of degree $\le k$. The definition for $k+1$ only requires matching coefficients of degree $\le k+1$, a weaker constraint.

Hence any admissible rank-$k$ decomposition is automatically admissible for rank-$k+1$ with the same number of summands. Therefore

$$
\mathrm{rank}_{k+1}(t_{ijk}) \le \mathrm{rank}_k(t_{ijk}).
$$

## (b) Ordinary rank bound

Let a rank-$k$ decomposition be given:

$$
\sum_{s=1}^r a_s(u)b_s(v)c_s(w).
$$

Expand each polynomial:

$$
a_s(u)=\sum_{\alpha \ge 0} a_{s,\alpha} u^\alpha,\quad
b_s(v)=\sum_{\beta \ge 0} b_{s,\beta} v^\beta,\quad
c_s(w)=\sum_{\gamma \ge 0} c_{s,\gamma} w^\gamma.
$$

Then

$$
a_s b_s c_s
=
\sum_{\alpha,\beta,\gamma} a_{s,\alpha} b_{s,\beta} c_{s,\gamma} u^\alpha v^\beta w^\gamma.
$$

To recover all terms of total degree $\le k$, only triples $(\alpha,\beta,\gamma)$ with

$$
\alpha+\beta+\gamma \le k
$$

are relevant.

The number of such monomials in three variables equals

$$
\binom{k+3}{3}.
$$

Each product $a_s b_s c_s$ therefore contributes at most $\binom{k+3}{3}$ rank-1 tensors in the ordinary sense. Summing over $r=\mathrm{rank}_k(t)$,

$$
\mathrm{rank}(t_{ijk}) \le \binom{k+3}{3}\,\mathrm{rank}_k(t_{ijk}).
$$

## (c) Additivity under direct sum

Let $t$ and $t'$ have rank-$k$ decompositions:

$$
t = \sum_{s=1}^r a_s b_s c_s,\quad
t' = \sum_{t=1}^{r'} a'_t b'_t c'_t
\quad (\text{correct up to degree } k).
$$

Form the direct sum tensor $t \oplus t'$ by introducing disjoint variable triples:

- $(u,v,w)$ for $t$,
- $(u',v',w')$ for $t'$.

Define a combined decomposition:

$$
\sum_{s=1}^r a_s(u)b_s(v)c_s(w)
+
\sum_{t=1}^{r'} a'_t(u')b'_t(v')c'_t(w').
$$

Because the variable sets are disjoint, any monomial involving both primed and unprimed variables has degree contribution separated by variables and does not interfere with the truncation condition. Thus all coefficients of total degree $\le k$ in each block are preserved exactly, and no cross terms appear in the relevant expansion.

Hence this is a valid rank-$k$ decomposition of $t \oplus t'$, giving

$$
\mathrm{rank}_k(t \oplus t') \le \mathrm{rank}_k(t) + \mathrm{rank}_k(t').
$$

## (d) Tensor product inequality

Let

$$
t = \sum_{s=1}^r a_s b_s c_s,\quad
t' = \sum_{t=1}^{r'} a'_t b'_t c'_t
$$

be rank-$k$ realizations.

Consider

$$
t \otimes t'
=
\sum_{s,t} (a_s a'_t)(b_s b'_t)(c_s c'_t).
$$

We claim this gives a valid rank-$(k+1)$ realization.

### Key point (no truncation of factors)

We do **not** truncate products. Instead, we use the fact that coefficient extraction up to total degree $k+1$ depends only on finitely many low-degree terms, and those are correct in each factor separately.

Each $a_s b_s c_s$ matches $t$ up to degree $k$, and similarly for $t'$. Therefore their formal product matches $t \otimes t'$ up to degree $k+1$, because:

- Any monomial of total degree $\le k+1$ in the product comes from pairs of monomials whose degrees add to at most $k+1$,
- In each factor, all contributions of degree $\le k$ are correct,
- Higher-degree errors cannot influence terms of total degree $\le k+1$.

Thus no erroneous low-degree contributions are introduced.

Therefore the $rr'$ summands give a valid rank-$(k+1)$ decomposition:

$$
\mathrm{rank}_{k+1}(t \otimes t') \le \mathrm{rank}_k(t)\,\mathrm{rank}_k(t').
$$

## (e) Direct sum amplification

Let $r = \mathrm{rank}_T(t)$, meaning there is a rank-$T$ realization:

$$
t = \sum_{s=1}^r a_s b_s c_s \quad (\text{correct up to degree } T).
$$

We construct a decomposition for $t^{\oplus 2T}$.

Assign each summand $s=1,\dots,r$ to a fixed copy of $t$ inside the $2T$-fold direct sum by multiplying with degree-0 selector constants (i.e., constants distinguishing the block). Since these selectors are constants, they do not affect degree.

Thus each summand acts independently on its designated block, and the same $r$-term decomposition realizes all copies simultaneously.

Finally, since increasing the truncation parameter weakens constraints,

$$
\mathrm{rank}_{2T}(t^{\oplus 2T}) \le \mathrm{rank}_T(t^{\oplus 2T}) \le \mathrm{rank}_T(t).
$$

The first inequality follows from monotonicity in (a), and the second from the blockwise construction above.

Hence

$$
\mathrm{rank}_{2T}(t^{\oplus 2T}) \le \mathrm{rank}_T(t).
$$

## Final summary

- (a) follows from weakening of constraints with larger $k$.
- (b) uses the exact count of monomials of total degree $\le k$ in three variables: $\binom{k+3}{3}$.
- (c) uses disjoint variable blocks ensuring no cross-term contamination.
- (d) uses correct coefficient-level reasoning without invalid truncation of factors.
- (e) combines block construction with monotonicity in $k$.

All inequalities follow rigorously from the definition of $\mathrm{rank}_k$.
