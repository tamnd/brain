**Exercise 64.** [*M30*] [M30] (A. Schönhage.) Show that $\text{rank}(T(3, 3, 3)) \le 21$, so $M(N) = O(N^{2.79})$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 6m13s  

---

## Setup

Let

$$
A=(a_{ij})_{1\le i,j\le 3}, \qquad
B=(b_{ij})_{1\le i,j\le 3},
$$

and let

$$
C=AB=(c_{ij})_{1\le i,j\le 3}.
$$

The tensor $T(3,3,3)$ is the bilinear map $(A,B)\mapsto AB$. To prove

$$
\operatorname{rank}(T(3,3,3))\le 21,
$$

it suffices to exhibit a bilinear algorithm that computes all nine entries $c_{ij}$ using only $21$ products of linear forms in the entries of $A$ and $B$.

A 21-product decomposition due to Schönhage is known.

## Solution

Define the following $21$ bilinear products:

$$
\begin{aligned}
p_1&=(a_{12}+b_{12})(a_{11}+b_{21}),\\
p_2&=(a_{13}+b_{13})(a_{11}+b_{31}),\\
p_3&=(a_{13}+b_{23})(a_{12}+b_{32}),\\
p_4&=a_{11}(b_{11}-b_{12}-b_{13}-a_{12}-a_{13}),\\
p_5&=a_{12}(b_{22}-b_{21}-b_{23}-a_{11}-a_{13}),\\
p_6&=a_{13}(b_{33}-b_{31}-b_{32}-a_{11}-a_{12}),\\
p_7&=(a_{22}+b_{12})(a_{21}+b_{21}),\\
p_8&=(a_{23}+b_{13})(a_{21}+b_{31}),\\
p_9&=(a_{23}+b_{23})(a_{22}+b_{32}),\\
p_{10}&=a_{21}(b_{11}-b_{12}-b_{13}-a_{22}-a_{23}),\\
p_{11}&=a_{22}(b_{22}-b_{21}-b_{23}-a_{21}-a_{23}),\\
p_{12}&=a_{23}(b_{33}-b_{31}-b_{32}-a_{21}-a_{22}),\\
p_{13}&=(a_{32}+b_{12})(a_{31}+b_{21}),\\
p_{14}&=(a_{33}+b_{13})(a_{31}+b_{31}),\\
p_{15}&=(a_{33}+b_{23})(a_{32}+b_{32}),\\
p_{16}&=a_{31}(b_{11}-b_{12}-b_{13}-a_{32}-a_{33}),\\
p_{17}&=a_{32}(b_{22}-b_{21}-b_{23}-a_{31}-a_{33}),\\
p_{18}&=a_{33}(b_{33}-b_{31}-b_{32}-a_{31}-a_{32}),\\
p_{19}&=b_{12}b_{21},\\
p_{20}&=b_{13}b_{31},\\
p_{21}&=b_{23}b_{32}.
\end{aligned}
$$

Now set

$$
\begin{aligned}
c_{11}&=p_4+p_1+p_2-p_{19}-p_{20},\\
c_{12}&=p_5+p_1+p_3-p_{19}-p_{21},\\
c_{13}&=p_6+p_2+p_3-p_{20}-p_{21},\\
c_{21}&=p_{10}+p_7+p_8-p_{19}-p_{20},\\
c_{22}&=p_{11}+p_7+p_9-p_{19}-p_{21},\\
c_{23}&=p_{12}+p_8+p_9-p_{20}-p_{21},\\
c_{31}&=p_{16}+p_{13}+p_{14}-p_{19}-p_{20},\\
c_{32}&=p_{17}+p_{13}+p_{15}-p_{19}-p_{21},\\
c_{33}&=p_{18}+p_{14}+p_{15}-p_{20}-p_{21}.
\end{aligned}
\tag{*}
$$

We verify one representative identity. Expanding,

$$
\begin{aligned}
p_4+p_1+p_2-p_{19}-p_{20}
&=a_{11}(b_{11}-b_{12}-b_{13}-a_{12}-a_{13}) \\
&\quad +(a_{12}+b_{12})(a_{11}+b_{21}) \\
&\quad +(a_{13}+b_{13})(a_{11}+b_{31})
   -b_{12}b_{21}-b_{13}b_{31}.
\end{aligned}
$$

After expansion, the terms

$$
-a_{11}a_{12}+a_{12}a_{11},\qquad
-a_{11}a_{13}+a_{13}a_{11},
$$

cancel, as do

$$
-a_{11}b_{12}+b_{12}a_{11},\qquad
-a_{11}b_{13}+b_{13}a_{11},
$$

and the terms $b_{12}b_{21}$ and $b_{13}b_{31}$ cancel with the final subtraction. The remaining terms are

$$
a_{11}b_{11}+a_{12}b_{21}+a_{13}b_{31}=c_{11}.
$$

The formulas for the other eight entries are obtained by the same calculation. Hence the expressions $(*)$ indeed yield the matrix product $C=AB$.

Therefore $AB$ is computed from exactly $21$ bilinear products $p_1,\ldots,p_{21}$. Consequently

$$
\operatorname{rank}(T(3,3,3))\le 21.
$$

Exercise 63(c) states that if $T(m,n,s)$ has rank at most $R$, then

$$
M(N)=O\!\left(N^{\,3\log R/\log(mns)}\right).
$$

Substituting $m=n=s=3$ and $R=21$,

$$
M(N)
 =O\!\left(
 N^{\,3\log 21/\log 27}
 \right)
 =O\!\left(N^{\log_3 21}\right).
$$

Since

$$
\log_3 21
=\frac{\log 21}{\log 3}
\approx 2.77124,
$$

we obtain

$$
M(N)=O(N^{2.77124\ldots})
     =O(N^{2.79}).
$$

Thus

$$
\boxed{\operatorname{rank}(T(3,3,3))\le 21}
$$

and therefore

$$
\boxed{M(N)=O(N^{2.79}).}
$$

## Verification

Each $p_i$ is the product of two linear forms, hence contributes rank $1$.

There are exactly $21$ such products.

The reconstruction formulas $(*)$ are linear combinations of the $p_i$.

Direct expansion of any entry, for example $c_{11}$, leaves precisely the three monomials

$$
a_{11}b_{11},\qquad
a_{12}b_{21},\qquad
a_{13}b_{31},
$$

with all other terms cancelling. The same pattern holds for the remaining eight entries. Hence $(*)$ computes $AB$.

Therefore the displayed decomposition is a rank-$21$ realization of $T(3,3,3)$, and the complexity bound follows from Exercise 63(c).

## Notes

This construction is Schönhage's 21-multiplication algorithm for $3\times3$ matrix multiplication. It improves on the classical $27$-multiplication method and yields the exponent $\log_3 21\approx2.77124$. Later work produced stronger asymptotic bounds, but the exact rank of the $3\times3$ matrix multiplication tensor remains unknown. ∎
