**Exercise 14.** &#9654; [*HM28*] *(Fast Fourier transforms.)* Show that the scheme (40) can be used to evaluate the one-dimensional discrete Fourier transform

$$f(s) = \sum_{0 \le t < 2^n} F(t)\omega^{st}, \qquad \omega = e^{2\pi i/2^n}, \quad 0 \le s < 2^n,$$

using arithmetic on complex numbers. Estimate the number of arithmetic operations performed.

Let $N = 2^n$ and write $\omega = e^{2\pi i/N}$. The discrete Fourier transform is

$$
f(s) = \sum_{t=0}^{N-1} F(t)\,\omega^{st}, \qquad 0 \le s < N.
$$

The idea of scheme (40) is to exploit the binary structure of $N$ by separating the sum into even and odd indices.

Write $t = 2u$ and $t = 2u+1$, where $0 \le u < N/2$. Then

$$
f(s)
= \sum_{u=0}^{N/2-1} F(2u)\,\omega^{s(2u)}
+ \sum_{u=0}^{N/2-1} F(2u+1)\,\omega^{s(2u+1)}.
$$

Factor each term. Since $\omega^{2} = e^{2\pi i/(N/2)}$, we obtain

$$
\omega^{s(2u)} = (\omega^2)^{su},
\qquad
\omega^{s(2u+1)} = \omega^s (\omega^2)^{su}.
$$

Hence

$$
f(s)
=
\sum_{u=0}^{N/2-1} F(2u)(\omega^2)^{su}
+
\omega^s \sum_{u=0}^{N/2-1} F(2u+1)(\omega^2)^{su}.
$$

Define two length-$N/2$ sequences

$$
F_0(u) = F(2u), \qquad F_1(u) = F(2u+1),
$$

and two half-size Fourier transforms (with respect to $\omega^2$):

$$
f_0(s) = \sum_{u=0}^{N/2-1} F_0(u)(\omega^2)^{su}, \qquad
f_1(s) = \sum_{u=0}^{N/2-1} F_1(u)(\omega^2)^{su}.
$$

Then the original transform is expressed as

$$
f(s) = f_0(s) + \omega^s f_1(s), \qquad 0 \le s < N/2,
$$

and using periodicity of the DFT,

$$
f(s+N/2) = f_0(s) - \omega^s f_1(s), \qquad 0 \le s < N/2,
$$

since $\omega^{s+N/2} = -\omega^s$.

This is exactly the recursive structure of scheme (40): two DFTs of size $N/2$ are computed independently (for even and odd inputs), and then combined using the “butterfly” operations

$$
(a,b) \mapsto (a + \omega^s b,\; a - \omega^s b),
$$

where $a = f_0(s)$, $b = f_1(s)$.

Thus the scheme evaluates the $N$-point DFT using only arithmetic on complex numbers, recursively reducing the problem size by a factor of $2$ at each stage until size $1$, where no arithmetic is needed.

### Operation count

Let $T(N)$ denote the number of complex arithmetic operations required (each complex addition or multiplication counts as one operation).

At the top level:

- Two DFTs of size $N/2$, contributing $2T(N/2)$.
- For each $s = 0, \dots, N/2 - 1$, two complex additions and one complex multiplication by $\omega^s$, giving $N/2$ multiplications and $N$ additions.

Hence

$$
T(N) = 2T(N/2) + \Theta(N).
$$

Solving this recurrence yields

$$
T(N) = \Theta(N \log N).
$$

More precisely, at each of the $n = \log_2 N$ recursion levels, the combining step performs $N$ complex additions and $N/2$ complex multiplications. Therefore:

- Total complex additions: $N \log_2 N$,
- Total complex multiplications: $\frac{N}{2} \log_2 N$,

up to lower-order terms depending on the base case convention.

Thus scheme (40) evaluates the one-dimensional DFT in $O(N \log N)$ complex arithmetic operations.
