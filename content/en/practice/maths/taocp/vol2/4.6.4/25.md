[Section 4.6.4: Evaluation of Polynomials](../)

**Exercise 25.** [*M20*] [M20] When the construction in the proof of Theorem M is applied to the (inefficient) polynomial chain

$$\lambda_1 = \alpha_1 + \lambda_0, \qquad \lambda_2 = -\lambda_0 - \lambda_0, \qquad \lambda_3 = \lambda_1 + \lambda_1, \qquad \lambda_4 = \alpha_2 \times \lambda_3,$$
$$\lambda_5 = \lambda_0 - \lambda_0, \qquad \lambda_6 = \alpha_0 - \lambda_0, \qquad \lambda_7 = \lambda_5 \times \lambda_6, \qquad \lambda_8 = \lambda_5 \times \lambda_7,$$
$$\lambda_9 = \lambda_1 \times \lambda_1, \qquad \lambda_{10} = \alpha \times \lambda_9,$$

how can $\beta_1, \beta_2, \ldots, \beta_7$ be expressed in terms of $\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_s$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m50s  

---

## Solution

The construction in Theorem M is a straight-line program for a polynomial, and the associated coefficients $\beta_i$ are obtained by the reverse propagation rule for the final value $\lambda_{10}$.

We initialize

$$
\beta_{10} = 1,\qquad \beta_i = 0 \ (i < 10).
$$

We propagate backward through the chain using the rules:

If $\lambda_k = \lambda_i + \lambda_j$, then $\beta_i \mathrel{+}= \beta_k$ and $\beta_j \mathrel{+}= \beta_k$.

If $\lambda_k = \lambda_i - \lambda_j$, then $\beta_i \mathrel{+}= \beta_k$ and $\beta_j \mathrel{-}= \beta_k$.

If $\lambda_k = c \times \lambda_i$, then $\beta_i \mathrel{+}= c \beta_k$.

From $\lambda_{10} = \alpha \times \lambda_9$, we obtain

$$
\beta_9 = \alpha \beta_{10} = \alpha.
$$

From $\lambda_9 = \lambda_1 \times \lambda_1$, the product rule gives a doubled contribution to the single variable:

$$
\beta_1 \mathrel{+}= \lambda_1 \beta_9 + \lambda_1 \beta_9 = 2\lambda_1 \alpha.
$$

Hence

$$
\beta_1 = 2\alpha \lambda_1.
$$

All other backward paths from $\lambda_{10}$ pass through $\beta_8$, but $\lambda_8 = \lambda_5 \times \lambda_7$ does not affect $\lambda_{10}$ since $\beta_8 = 0$. Consequently,

$$
\beta_7 = \beta_6 = \beta_5 = \beta_4 = \beta_3 = \beta_2 = 0.
$$

It remains to express $\beta_1$ in terms of the parameters. From $\lambda_1 = \alpha_1 + \lambda_0$,

$$
\beta_1 = 2\alpha(\alpha_1 + \lambda_0).
$$

The coefficients $\beta_1, \beta_2, \ldots, \beta_7$ are therefore

$$
\beta_1 = 2\alpha(\alpha_1 + \lambda_0),\qquad
\beta_2 = 0,\qquad
\beta_3 = 0,\qquad
\beta_4 = 0,\qquad
\beta_5 = 0,\qquad
\beta_6 = 0,\qquad
\beta_7 = 0.
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
