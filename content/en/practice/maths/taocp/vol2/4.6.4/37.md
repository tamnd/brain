[Section 4.6.4: Evaluation of Polynomials](../)

**Exercise 37.** [*M21*] (T. S. Motzkin.) Show that "almost all" rational functions of the form

$$( a_n x^n + a_{n-1} x^{n-1} + \cdots + u_1 x + a_0 ) / (x^n + b_{n-1} x^{n-1} + \cdots + c_1 x + c_0),$$

with coefficients in a field $S$, can be evaluated using the expansion

$$\alpha_1 + \beta_1/(x + \alpha_2 + \beta_2/(x + \cdots + \beta_n/(x + \alpha_{n+1})\cdots)),$$

for suitable $\alpha_j$, $\beta_j$ in $S$. (This continued fraction scheme has $n$ divisions and $2n$ additions; by "almost all" rational functions we mean all except those whose coefficients satisfy some nontrivial polynomial equation.) Determine the $\alpha$'s and $\beta$'s for the rational function $(x^2 + 10x + 29)/(x^2 + 8x + 19)$.

## Solution

Let

$R(x)=\frac{x^2+10x+29}{x^2+8x+19}.$

### Step 1: first decomposition

Since numerator and denominator have the same degree, divide polynomials:

$$
(x^2+10x+29)-(x^2+8x+19)=2x+10.
$$

Hence

$$
R(x)=1+\frac{2x+10}{x^2+8x+19}.
$$

This gives

$$
\alpha_1=1.
$$

Write the remainder term as

$$
\frac{2x+10}{x^2+8x+19}=\frac{\beta_1}{x+\alpha_2+\frac{\beta_2}{x+\alpha_3}}.
$$

Taking reciprocals,

$$
\frac{x^2+8x+19}{2x+10}=\frac{1}{\beta_1}\left(x+\alpha_2+\frac{\beta_2}{x+\alpha_3}\right).
$$

### Step 2: Euclidean division

Divide $x^2+8x+19$ by $2x+10$:

$$
x^2+8x+19 = \left(\frac{1}{2}x\right)(2x+10) + (3x+19),
$$

since

$$
\left(\frac{1}{2}x\right)(2x+10)=x^2+5x,
\quad (x^2+8x+19)-(x^2+5x)=3x+19.
$$

Thus

$$
\frac{x^2+8x+19}{2x+10}=\frac{1}{2}x+\frac{3x+19}{2x+10}.
$$

### Step 3: second division

Divide $2x+10$ by $3x+19$:

$$
2x+10=\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)(3x+19)-\frac{8}{3},
$$

since

$$
\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)(3x+19)=2x+\frac{38}{3},
\quad (2x+10)-(2x+\frac{38}{3})=-\frac{8}{3}.
$$

Hence

$$
\frac{2x+10}{3x+19}=\frac{2}{3}-\frac{8/3}{3x+19}.
$$

Invert:

$$
\frac{3x+19}{2x+10}=\frac{1}{\frac{2}{3}-\frac{8/3}{3x+19}}.
$$

Combine the denominator:

$$
\frac{2}{3}-\frac{8/3}{3x+19}
=\frac{2(3x+19)-8}{3(3x+19)}
=\frac{6x+30}{3(3x+19)}
=\frac{2(x+5)}{3x+19}.
$$

Therefore

$$
\frac{3x+19}{2x+10}=\frac{3x+19}{2(x+5)}.
$$

Substitute:

$$
\frac{x^2+8x+19}{2x+10}=\frac{1}{2}x+\frac{3x+19}{2(x+5)}.
$$

### Step 4: final decomposition

Rewrite

$$
\frac{3x+19}{2(x+5)}=\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{3x+19}{x+5}
=\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{3(x+5)+4}{x+5}.
$$

Hence

$$
\frac{3x+19}{2(x+5)}=\frac{1}{2}\left(3+\frac{4}{x+5}\right)
=\frac{3}{2}+\frac{2}{x+5}.
$$

Thus

$$
\frac{x^2+8x+19}{2x+10}
=\frac{1}{2}x+\frac{3}{2}+\frac{2}{x+5}.
$$

Factor:

$$
\frac{x^2+8x+19}{2x+10}
=\frac{1}{2}\left(x+3+\frac{4}{x+5}\right).
$$

Taking reciprocals,

$$
\frac{2x+10}{x^2+8x+19}
=\frac{2}{x+3+\frac{4}{x+5}}.
$$

### Step 5: identification of parameters

Comparing with

$$
\frac{\beta_1}{x+\alpha_2+\frac{\beta_2}{x+\alpha_3}},
$$

gives

$$
\beta_1=2,\quad \alpha_2=3,\quad \beta_2=4,\quad \alpha_3=5.
$$

Together with $\alpha_1=1$, the continued fraction is determined.

$$
\boxed{\alpha_1=1,\ \beta_1=2,\ \alpha_2=3,\ \beta_2=4,\ \alpha_3=5}
$$
