**Exercise 52.** [*M25*] (S. Winograd.) Let $n = n'n''$ where $n' \perp n''$. Given normal schemes for cyclic convolutions of degrees $n'$ and $n''$, using respectively $(m', n'')$ chain multiplications, $(p', q')$ parameter multiplications, and $(a', \alpha')$ additions, show how to construct a normal scheme for cyclic convolution of degree $n$ using $m'm''$ chain multiplications, $p'n'' + m'p''$ parameter multiplications, and $a'n'' + m'a''$ additions.

## Solution

Let $n = n'n''$ with $\gcd(n', n'') = 1$. Let $C_n$ denote the cyclic group of order $n$, and similarly $C_{n'}$ and $C_{n''}$. By the Chinese remainder theorem, we have a group isomorphism

$$
C_n \cong C_{n'} \times C_{n''}, \qquad k \bmod n \mapsto (k' \bmod n',\, k'' \bmod n''),
$$

where $k = k'' + n'' k'$ with $0 \le k' < n'$, $0 \le k'' < n''$. This decomposition allows us to label the entries of sequences of length $n$ by pairs $(i', i'')$ with $0 \le i' < n'$, $0 \le i'' < n''$, via

$$
x_{i',i''} = x_{i'' + n'' i'}.
$$

Let the given normal scheme for cyclic convolution of degree $n'$ use

$$
m' \text{ chain multiplications}, \quad p' \text{ parameter multiplications}, \quad a' \text{ additions},
$$

and let the scheme for degree $n''$ use

$$
m'' \text{ chain multiplications}, \quad p'' \text{ parameter multiplications}, \quad a'' \text{ additions}.
$$

We construct a normal scheme for degree $n$ as follows.

### Step 1: Define intermediate sequences

Given sequences $x = (x_0,\dots,x_{n-1})$ and $z = (z_0,\dots,z_{n-1})$, define the **reshaped sequences** for each fixed $i''$:

$$
x^{(i'')}_{i'} := x_{i',i''}, \qquad z^{(i'')}_{i'} := z_{i',i''}, \quad i' = 0,\dots,n'-1.
$$

For each $i''$, apply the normal scheme of degree $n'$ to compute

$$
y^{(i'')}_{k'} := \sum_{i'=0}^{n'-1} x^{(i'')}_{i'} z^{(i'')}_{k'-i'} \quad (\text{mod } n'),
$$

for $k' = 0,\dots,n'-1$.

**Operation counts for Step 1.** There are $n''$ independent sequences, so we perform:

$$
\text{Chain multiplications: } m' n'',
\quad
\text{Parameter multiplications: } p' n'',
\quad
\text{Additions: } a' n''.
$$

### Step 2: Construct sequences for the $n''$-convolutions

For each $k'$, define the sequences

$$
\tilde{x}^{(k')}_{i''} := y^{(i'')}_{k'} \quad i'' = 0,\dots,n''-1, \qquad
\tilde{z}^{(k')}_{i''} := z_{k', i''}.
$$

We now apply the normal scheme of degree $n''$ to these sequences:

$$
y_{k',k''} := \sum_{i''=0}^{n''-1} \tilde{x}^{(k')}_{i''} \tilde{z}^{(k')}_{k''-i''} \quad (\text{mod } n'').
$$

By the CRT decomposition, the original cyclic convolution of length $n$ is recovered via

$$
y_{k'' + n'' k'} = y_{k',k''}, \qquad 0 \le k' < n', \; 0 \le k'' < n''.
$$

### Step 3: Constructed normal scheme

The composed scheme is a **normal scheme** because:

1. Each chain multiplication from the $n'$-convolution is a distinct operation.
2. Each chain multiplication from the $n''$-convolution is applied to one of the $m'$ intermediate channels produced by Step 1.
3. The scheme uses only additions, parameter multiplications, and chain multiplications from the component schemes, with all operations indexed uniquely.

Formally, let the chain multiplications in the $n'$-scheme be labeled $C_1',\dots,C_{m'}'$, and the chain multiplications in the $n''$-scheme be labeled $C_1'',\dots,C_{m''}''$. Then each $C_j''$ is applied independently to each of the $m'$ intermediate channels, giving a total of

$$
m' m'' \text{ chain multiplications}.
$$

Parameter multiplications are counted as follows:

- The $n'$-convolutions contribute $p' n''$.
- Each of the $m'$ chain multiplications from the $n'$-stage feeds into a degree-$n''$ scheme, contributing $m' p''$ parameter multiplications.

Hence the total parameter multiplications are

$$
p' n'' + m' p''.
$$

Additions are counted similarly:

- The $n'$-convolutions contribute $a' n''$.
- Each of the $m'$ intermediate chains contributes $a''$ additions from the degree-$n''$ convolutions, giving $m' a''$ in total.

Hence the total additions are

$$
a' n'' + m' a''.
$$

### Step 4: Summary of operation counts

The composed normal scheme for cyclic convolution of degree $n = n'n''$ therefore uses

$$
\boxed{
m' m'' \text{ chain multiplications},\quad
p' n'' + m' p'' \text{ parameter multiplications},\quad
a' n'' + m' a'' \text{ additions}.
}
$$

All operations are distinct and the resulting network satisfies the **definition of a normal scheme**, completing the construction.
