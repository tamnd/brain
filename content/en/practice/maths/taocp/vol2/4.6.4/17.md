## Solution

We are asked to show that the interpolation formula (45) reduces to a simple expression involving binomial coefficients when the nodes are in an arithmetic progression, namely

$x_k = x_0 + kh, \qquad 0 \le k \le n.$

The formula (45) in Section 4.6.4 expresses the Newton interpolation polynomial in terms of divided differences:

$P_n(x) = f[x_0] + f[x_0, x_1](x - x_0) + f[x_0, x_1, x_2](x - x_0)(x - x_1) + \cdots + f[x_0, \ldots, x_n](x - x_0)\cdots(x - x_{n-1}).$

Let us examine the divided differences when the nodes are equidistant. By definition, the $n$th divided difference is

$f[x_0, x_1, \ldots, x_n] = \sum_{k=0}^n \frac{f(x_k)}{\prod_{j\ne k} (x_k - x_j)}.$

Since $x_k = x_0 + kh$, the differences $x_k - x_j$ become $(k - j)h$. Therefore, the denominator factorizes as

$\prod_{j\ne k} (x_k - x_j) = \prod_{j\ne k} (k - j) h^{\,n} = h^n \prod_{j\ne k} (k - j).$

The product $\prod_{j\ne k} (k - j)$ is a standard combinatorial expression. Specifically, for $0 \le k \le n$, we have

$\prod_{j=0}^{k-1} (k - j) \cdot \prod_{j=k+1}^n (k - j) = k! \cdot (-1)^{n-k} (n-k)!.$

Hence

$f[x_0, x_1, \ldots, x_n] = \sum_{k=0}^n \frac{f(x_k)}{h^n \, k! \, (n-k)!} \, (-1)^{n-k} = \frac{1}{h^n} \sum_{k=0}^n (-1)^{n-k} \binom{n}{k} f(x_k).$

This is exactly the forward-difference form of the $n$th divided difference, often denoted $\Delta^n f(x_0)/h^n$ (see exercise 1.2.6–48). Substituting these divided differences into the Newton interpolation polynomial, we obtain

$$
\begin{aligned}
P_n(x) &= f(x_0) + \frac{\Delta f(x_0)}{h}(x - x_0) + \frac{\Delta^2 f(x_0)}{2! h^2} (x - x_0)(x - x_1) + \cdots + \frac{\Delta^n f(x_0)}{n! h^n} \prod_{j=0}^{n-1} (x - x_j) \\
&= \sum_{k=0}^n \binom{x-x_0}{k} \Delta^k f(x_0),
\end{aligned}
$$

where the generalized binomial coefficient is defined by

$\binom{x-x_0}{k} = \frac{(x - x_0)(x - x_1)\cdots(x - x_{k-1})}{k!} = \frac{\prod_{j=0}^{k-1} (x - x_0 - jh)}{k! \, h^k}.$

This completes the derivation, showing that the Newton interpolation formula simplifies to a sum of forward differences multiplied by binomial coefficients when the nodes are in an arithmetic progression.

∎
