[Section 4.6.4: Evaluation of Polynomials](../)

**Exercise 5.** [*M15*] Count the number of multiplications and additions required by the second-order rule (4).

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 4m01s  

---

## Solution

Let $n$ be given and write

$u(x)=u_n x^n+u_{n-1}x^{n-1}+\cdots+u_1x+u_0.$

Define the even and odd parts with respect to $x^2$:

$E(x)=\sum_{k\ge 0} u_{2k} x^{2k}, \qquad O(x)=\sum_{k\ge 0} u_{2k+1} x^{2k}.$

Then the second-order representation (4) is

$u(x)=E(x)+x\,O(x).$

Let $t=x^2$. Both $E(x)$ and $O(x)$ are evaluated as polynomials in $t$ by Horner’s rule.

Let $n=2m$ or $n=2m+1$. Set

$e=m+1, \qquad o=\left\lceil \frac{n}{2}\right\rceil.$

Then $e$ is the number of even-indexed coefficients and $o$ is the number of odd-indexed coefficients, and

$e+o=n+1.$

Evaluation of $E$ by Horner’s rule in the variable $t=x^2$ requires $e-1$ multiplications and $e-1$ additions. Evaluation of $O$ requires $o-1$ multiplications and $o-1$ additions. The value $t=x^2$ requires one multiplication.

After obtaining $E(t)$ and $O(t)$, the expression $x,O(t)$ requires one additional multiplication, and forming $E(t)+x,O(t)$ requires one addition.

The total number of multiplications is therefore

$1+(e-1)+(o-1)+1=e+o=n+1.$

The total number of additions is

$(e-1)+(o-1)+1=e+o-1=n.$

Thus the second-order rule uses exactly $n+1$ multiplications and $n$ additions.

$\boxed{(n+1,\;n)}$

This completes the solution. ∎
