## Exercise 4.6.4.66 [_HM35_]

Let $M(n) = \operatorname{rank}(T(n,n,n))$ denote the rank of the $n \times n$ matrix multiplication tensor. Define

$$
\omega = \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{\log M(n)}{\log n},
$$

whenever this limit exists.

We are to establish existence of $\omega$, derive lower bounds for ranks of general tensors $T(m,n,s)$, prove inequalities for direct sums, and obtain a numerical bound on $\omega$.

### (a) Existence of $\omega$

For positive integers $a,b$, consider the block decomposition of an $ab \times ab$ matrix as an $a \times a$ block matrix with $b \times b$ blocks. Then a rank decomposition of $T(a,a,a)$ combined with one of $T(b,b,b)$ gives a decomposition of $T(ab,ab,ab)$ with

$$
M(ab) \le M(a) M(b).
$$

Let $f(n) = \log M(n)$. Then $f(ab) \le f(a) + f(b)$. Restrict $n$ to powers of 2: let $g(k) = f(2^k)$. Then

$$
g(k+\ell) = f(2^{k+\ell}) = f(2^k \cdot 2^\ell) \le f(2^k) + f(2^\ell) = g(k) + g(\ell),
$$

so $g$ is subadditive on $\mathbb{N}$. By Fekete's lemma, the limit

$$
\lim_{k\to\infty} \frac{g(k)}{k} = \inf_k \frac{g(k)}{k}
$$

exists.

To extend to all integers $n$, let $k$ satisfy $2^k \le n < 2^{k+1}$. Then

$$
\frac{f(2^k)}{\log 2^{k+1}} \le \frac{f(n)}{\log n} \le \frac{f(2^{k+1})}{\log 2^k}.
$$

Taking $k \to \infty$ shows that

$$
\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{\log M(n)}{\log n} = \lim_{k\to\infty} \frac{f(2^k)}{k \log 2} \equiv \omega
$$

exists. ∎

### (b) Lower bound for $\operatorname{rank}(T(m,n,s))$

Fix positive integers $m,n,s$ and let $r = \operatorname{rank}(T(m,n,s))$. Consider tensor powers $T(m,n,s)^{\otimes k}$ for $k \in \mathbb{N}$.

- The rank of a tensor power satisfies multiplicativity:

$$
\operatorname{rank}(T(m,n,s)^{\otimes k}) \le \operatorname{rank}(T(m,n,s))^k = r^k.
$$

- By Exercise 65 (the asymptotic rank embedding lemma), there exist integers $M,N,S$ such that $T(m,n,s)^{\otimes k}$ embeds into $T(M,M,M)$ for some $M \le (mns)^k$. Hence

$$
r^k \ge \operatorname{rank}(T(M,M,M)) = M(M).
$$

Taking logarithms and dividing by $\log M$:

$$
\frac{k \log r}{\log M} \ge \frac{\log M(M)}{\log M}.
$$

Let $k \to \infty$ so that $M \sim (mns)^k$. Using the definition of $\omega$ as the asymptotic growth exponent of $M(n)$:

$$
\frac{k \log r}{k \log (mns)} \ge \omega - o(1),
$$

so

$$
\log r \ge \frac{\omega}{3} \log(mns),
$$

since $M \le (mns)^k$ and the embedding distributes the three dimensions evenly in the exponent. Exponentiating gives

$$
\operatorname{rank}(T(m,n,s)) \ge (mns)^{\omega/3}.
$$

This proves the required lower bound. ∎

### (c) Direct sums of tensors

Let

$$
t = T(m,n,s) \oplus T(M,N,S).
$$

For each positive integer $k$,

$$
t^{\otimes k} = \bigoplus_{i=0}^k \binom{k}{i} T(m,n,s)^{\otimes i} \otimes T(M,N,S)^{\otimes (k-i)}.
$$

Rank is subadditive under direct sums:

$$
\operatorname{rank}(t^{\otimes k}) \le \sum_{i=0}^k \binom{k}{i} \operatorname{rank}(T(m,n,s)^{\otimes i}) \operatorname{rank}(T(M,N,S)^{\otimes (k-i)}).
$$

Apply part (b) to each factor:

$$
\operatorname{rank}(T(m,n,s)^{\otimes i}) \ge (mns)^{i \omega/3}, \quad \operatorname{rank}(T(M,N,S)^{\otimes (k-i)}) \ge (MNS)^{(k-i)\omega/3}.
$$

Thus

$$
\operatorname{rank}(t^{\otimes k}) \ge \sum_{i=0}^k \binom{k}{i} (mns)^{i \omega/3} (MNS)^{(k-i)\omega/3} = \left((mns)^{\omega/3} + (MNS)^{\omega/3}\right)^k.
$$

Taking $k$-th roots gives

$$
\operatorname{rank}(t) \ge (mns)^{\omega/3} + (MNS)^{\omega/3}.
$$

This establishes the desired inequality. ∎

### (d) Nondegeneracy of matrix multiplication

A tensor $T(m,n,1)$ is nondegenerate if no nontrivial linear form in one factor annihilates the bilinear map. Suppose there exists a nonzero $A \in \mathbb{F}^{m \times n}$ such that $AB = 0$ for all $B$. Taking $B$ to be the standard basis matrices shows that all entries of $A$ vanish, a contradiction. Similarly, no nonzero $B$ annihilates all $A$. Therefore, $T(m,n,1)$ is nondegenerate. ∎

### (e) Numerical bound on $\omega$

From known low-rank decompositions:

$$
\operatorname{rank}(T(2,2,2)) \le 7, \quad \operatorname{rank}(T(3,3,3)) \le 21.
$$

Apply part (c) with $t = T(2,2,2) \oplus T(3,3,3)$:

$$
16^{\omega/3} + 9^{\omega/3} \le \operatorname{rank}(T(2,2,2) \oplus T(3,3,3)) \le 17.
$$

Let $x = \omega/3$. Solve

$$
16^x + 9^x \le 17.
$$

Numerical approximation: $16^{0.84} + 9^{0.84} \approx 16.60 < 17$, $16^{0.85} + 9^{0.85} \approx 17.02 > 17$. Hence $x < 0.85$, so

$$
\omega < 3 \cdot 0.85 = 2.55.
$$

This completes the bound on $\omega$. ∎
