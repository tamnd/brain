[Section 4.6.4: Evaluation of Polynomials](../)

**Exercise 8.** [*M20*] The factorial power $x^{\underline{k}}$ is defined to be $k!\binom{x}{k} = x(x-1)\ldots(x-k+1)$. Explain how to evaluate $u_n x^{\underline{n}} + \cdots + u_1 x^{\underline{1}} + u_0$ with at most $n$ multiplications and $2n - 1$ additions, computing first the sequence $u_n, u_n \cdot n + u_{n-1}, \ldots, u_0, 1, n - 1$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m50s

The clean way to remove the confusion is to derive the evaluation recurrence directly from the structure of the falling factorial basis, and then count operations in a single unified loop.

We start from the identity

$$
x^{\underline{k}} = (x-k+1)x^{\underline{k-1}}, \qquad x^{\underline{0}}=1.
$$

Let

$$
P(x)=\sum_{k=0}^n u_k x^{\underline{k}}.
$$

We reorganize this sum so that at each stage we peel off one basis level.

Fix an index $k$ and separate the $k$-th term:

$$
P(x)=u_k x^{\underline{k}} + \sum_{j=k+1}^n u_j x^{\underline{j}}.
$$

For the tail, factor out one falling factorial step from each term with $j\ge k+1$:

$$
x^{\underline{j}} = (x-k)\, x^{\underline{j-1}} \quad \text{for all } j\ge k+1.
$$

This follows directly from the definition since the factor $x-k$ appears exactly once in $x^{\underline{j}}$, and removing it leaves $x^{\underline{j-1}}$.

Hence,

$$
\sum_{j=k+1}^n u_j x^{\underline{j}}
= (x-k)\sum_{j=k+1}^n u_j x^{\underline{j-1}}.
$$

Now define the auxiliary quantity

$$
t_k = \sum_{j=k}^n u_j x^{\underline{j-k}}.
$$

Then $t_n=u_n$, and the previous identity becomes the recurrence

$$
t_k = u_k + (x-k)t_{k+1}, \qquad k=n-1,n-2,\ldots,0.
$$

In particular, $t_0=P(x)$.

This gives a direct evaluation algorithm.

We compute from $k=n$ down to $0$:

$$
t_n=u_n,
\qquad
t_k = u_k + (x-k)t_{k+1}.
$$

Now we analyze operations carefully in the standard TAOCP model, where addition includes subtraction.

At step $k$, we form $x-k$, multiply it by $t_{k+1}$, and add $u_k$. This is one subtraction, one multiplication, and one addition per iteration.

There are $n$ multiplications, one for each $k=n-1,\ldots,0$.

For additions, we account as follows. The explicit addition $u_k + (x-k)t_{k+1}$ occurs $n$ times. The value $x-k$ is obtained by decrementing a running quantity starting from $x$. Over the full run, we perform $n-1$ subtractions because at the final step $k=0$ we already have $x-0=x$ available without performing a subtraction if we initialize the decrement chain as $x-n, x-(n-1), \ldots, x-1$. Thus the subtraction cost contributes $n-1$ additions in TAOCP counting.

Therefore the total number of additions is $n + (n-1)=2n-1$.

To match the hinted “sequence computation” viewpoint, one can equivalently view the process as maintaining a running value $t_{k+1}$ while updating the multiplier $x-k$ by a single increment per step. The key point is that both the coefficient update and the change in the falling-factorial multiplier are performed in the same loop, so there is no double counting of operations.

Hence the polynomial $u_n x^{\underline{n}}+\cdots+u_0$ is evaluated using exactly $n$ multiplications and $2n-1$ additions.

This achieves the required bounds.
