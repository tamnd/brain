## Exercise 4.7.2 [20]

**2.** If the coefficients of $U(z)$ and $V(z)$ are integers and $V_0 \ne 0$, find a recurrence relation for the integers $V_0^{\,n+1} W_n$, where $W_n$ is defined by (3). How could you use this for power series division?

## Solution

Let

$$
U(z) = \sum_{n\ge 0} U_n z^n, \quad V(z) = \sum_{n\ge 0} V_n z^n, \quad V_0 \ne 0,
$$

and define the power series $W(z) = \sum_{n\ge 0} W_n z^n$ by

$$
U(z) = V(z) W(z), \quad\text{i.e., } W_n = [z^n]\frac{U(z)}{V(z)}.
$$

From the Cauchy product formula, we have for each $n \ge 0$:

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n} V_k W_{n-k} = U_n.
$$

Multiply both sides by $V_0^{\,n+1}$. Then

$$
V_0^{\,n+1} \sum_{k=0}^{n} V_k W_{n-k} = V_0^{\,n+1} U_n.
$$

Split off the term $k=0$:

$$
V_0^{\,n+1} V_0 W_n + V_0^{\,n+1} \sum_{k=1}^{n} V_k W_{n-k} = V_0^{\,n+1} U_n.
$$

Simplifying the first term gives

$$
V_0^{\,n+2} W_n + \sum_{k=1}^{n} V_0^{\,n+1} V_k W_{n-k} = V_0^{\,n+1} U_n.
$$

Now, note that for $k \ge 1$,

$$
V_0^{\,n+1} V_k W_{n-k} = V_0^{\,k} V_k \cdot V_0^{\,n+1 - k} W_{n-k}.
$$

Therefore, the recurrence for the integers $X_n := V_0^{\,n+1} W_n$ is

$$
X_n = V_0 U_n - \sum_{k=1}^{n} V_k V_0^{\,k} X_{n-k}, \quad n \ge 0,
$$

with the initial condition

$$
X_0 = V_0 W_0 = V_0 \frac{U_0}{V_0} = U_0.
$$

All coefficients $X_n$ are integers if the $U_n$ and $V_n$ are integers.

### Application to power series division

This recurrence allows one to compute $W_0, W_1, W_2, \dots$ **using only integer arithmetic**:

1. Compute $X_0 = U_0$.
2. For each $n \ge 1$, compute

$$
X_n = V_0 U_n - \sum_{k=1}^{n} V_k V_0^{\,k} X_{n-k}.
$$

1. Recover the power series coefficients by

$$
W_n = \frac{X_n}{V_0^{\,n+1}}.
$$

This method avoids fractions at intermediate steps, which is convenient for computer implementations and exact arithmetic, especially when $U_n, V_n \in \mathbb{Z}$.

### Summary

The recurrence for $X_n = V_0^{\,n+1} W_n$ is

$$
\boxed{
X_0 = U_0, \quad
X_n = V_0 U_n - \sum_{k=1}^{n} V_k V_0^{\,k} X_{n-k} \text{ for } n \ge 1.
}
$$

It produces the coefficients of $W(z) = U(z)/V(z)$ exactly using integer arithmetic.
