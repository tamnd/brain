[Section 4.7: Manipulation of Power Series](../)

**Exercise 1.** [*M10*] The text explains how to divide $U(z)$ by $V(z)$ when $V_0 \ne 0$; how should the division be done when $V_0 = 0$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m54s  

---

Let $V_m$ be the first nonzero coefficient of $V(z)$; thus

$V(z)=z^m\widehat V(z),\qquad \widehat V_0=V_m\ne0.$

If

$U(z)=z^r\widehat U(z),\qquad \widehat U_0=U_r\ne0,$

then

$\frac{U(z)}{V(z)}=z^{\,r-m}\frac{\widehat U(z)}{\widehat V(z)}.$

The quotient is a formal power series if and only if $r\ge m$. In that case $\widehat V_0\ne0$, so the division reduces to the ordinary case treated in the text: Compute

$\widehat W(z)=\frac{\widehat U(z)}{\widehat V(z)}$

by recurrence (2), then multiply the result by $z^{,r-m}$.

If $r<m$, the quotient contains negative powers of $z$ and therefore is not a formal power series. Thus division by a series with $V_0=0$ is handled by removing the common factor $z^m$ from the denominator (and the corresponding factor from the numerator when present), after which recurrence (2) applies to the reduced series.
