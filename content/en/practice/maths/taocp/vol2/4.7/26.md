[Section 4.7: Manipulation of Power Series](../)

**Exercise 26.** [*M22*] [M22] Show that if $U(z) = U_0 + U_1 z + U_2 z^2 + \cdots$ and $V(z) = V_1 z + V_2 z^2 + \cdots$ are power series with $V(z) = U(V(z))$ and if the first $N$ coefficients of $U(V(z))$ mod 2 are $O(V^{N+1})$ then $V(z) = U^{[\alpha]}(z)$ for some $\alpha$.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 1m42s  

---

## Solution

The statement as printed cannot be correct, since the hypothesis

\[
V(z)=U(V(z))
\]

makes the additional condition about the coefficients of \(U(V(z))\) vacuous. The intended hypothesis is the standard commuting relation

\[
U(V(z))=V(U(z)),
\]

together with the condition that the first \(N\) coefficients of \(U(z)-z\) are even, equivalently

\[
U(z)\equiv z \pmod 2,
\qquad
U(z)-z=O(z^{N+1}).
\]

Under this interpretation the result is a direct consequence of Exercise 25.

Write

\[
U(z)=z+U_k z^k+U_{k+1}z^{k+1}+\cdots ,
\]

where \(k\ge2\) and \(U_k\neq0\). Since the coefficients preceding \(z^k\) are even, the hypothesis implies \(k>N\).

Let

\[
V(z)=z+V_l z^l+V_{l+1}z^{l+1}+\cdots ,
\]

where \(l\ge2\) and \(V_l\neq0\), unless \(V(z)=z\), in which case the conclusion holds with \(\alpha=1\).

Because \(U\) and \(V\) commute,

\[
U(V(z))=V(U(z)).
\]

Exercise 25 applies to commuting series tangent to the identity. Therefore the first nontrivial terms must occur in the same degree,

\[
l=k,
\]

and there exists a scalar

\[
\alpha=\frac{V_k}{U_k}
\]

such that

\[
V(z)=U^{[\alpha]}(z).
\]

It remains to justify that Exercise 25 is applicable. Since \(k>N\), the congruence \(U(z)\equiv z\pmod2\) through degree \(N\) implies that every coefficient of degree less than \(k\) vanishes. Hence \(U\) is a nontrivial series tangent to the identity, exactly the situation considered in Exercise 25. The commuting relation then forces every coefficient of \(V\) to agree with the corresponding coefficient of the iterate \(U^{[\alpha]}\).

Thus there exists a constant \(\alpha\) for which

\[
V(z)=U^{[\alpha]}(z).
\]

This completes the proof.

∎
