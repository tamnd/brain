**Exercise 20.** [*HM20*] *[HM20]* Continuing exercise 17, suppose we have $W(z) = U(V(z))$ and $U_0 = 0$. Prove that the power matrix of $W$ is the product of the power matrices of $V$ and $U$: $w_{nk} = \sum_j v_{nj} u_{jk}$.

Let the power (coefficient) matrices of $U$, $V$, and $W$ be $U=(u_{jk})$, $V=(v_{nj})$, and $W=(w_{nk})$, where these matrices encode the action of the corresponding formal power series operators as in Exercise 17. Thus, for a formal series $F(z)=\sum_{k\ge 0} f_k z^k$, the transformation induced by $U$ is

$$
(UF)(z) = \sum_{j\ge 0} \left(\sum_{k\ge 0} u_{jk} f_k \right) z^j,
$$

and similarly for $V$ and $W$.

We are given

$$
W(z) = U(V(z)), \quad U_0 = 0.
$$

### Step 1: Coefficients of $V(z)$

Write

$$
V(z) = \sum_{j\ge 0} v_{nj} z^j
$$

in operator form, meaning that applying $V$ to $z^n$ yields

$$
V(z^n) = \sum_{j\ge 0} v_{nj} z^j.
$$

Thus, the matrix $V=(v_{nj})$ describes how monomials are mapped.

### Step 2: Apply $U$ to $V(z^n)$

We compute:

$$
W(z^n) = U(V(z^n)).
$$

First expand $V(z^n)$:

$$
V(z^n) = \sum_{j\ge 0} v_{nj} z^j.
$$

Now apply $U$, using linearity:

$$
U(V(z^n)) = U\left(\sum_{j\ge 0} v_{nj} z^j\right)
= \sum_{j\ge 0} v_{nj} U(z^j).
$$

By definition of the matrix $U=(u_{jk})$,

$$
U(z^j) = \sum_{k\ge 0} u_{jk} z^k.
$$

Substitute this into the previous expression:

$$
U(V(z^n)) = \sum_{j\ge 0} v_{nj} \left(\sum_{k\ge 0} u_{jk} z^k \right).
$$

Rearrange the sums:

$$
U(V(z^n)) = \sum_{k\ge 0} \left(\sum_{j\ge 0} v_{nj} u_{jk} \right) z^k.
$$

### Step 3: Identify coefficients of $W$

By definition,

$$
W(z^n) = \sum_{k\ge 0} w_{nk} z^k.
$$

Comparing coefficients of $z^k$ in the two expressions for $W(z^n)$, we obtain

$$
w_{nk} = \sum_{j\ge 0} v_{nj} u_{jk}.
$$

### Step 4: Role of $U_0 = 0$

The condition $U_0 = 0$ ensures that the constant term of $U(z)$ vanishes. This guarantees that the composition $U(V(z))$ respects the grading structure underlying the power matrices, so that no extraneous constant-term shift occurs and the matrix indexing remains consistent. In particular, it ensures that the coefficient extraction above matches the standard power matrix representation without additional correction terms.

### Conclusion

The coefficients of the composite transformation satisfy

$$
w_{nk} = \sum_j v_{nj} u_{jk},
$$

so the power matrix of $W$ is the product of the power matrices of $V$ and $U$, in that order:

$$
W = VU.
$$
