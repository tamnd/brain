## Exercise 4.7.14 [HM30]

**Problem.** Fill in the details of Brent and Traub's method for calculating $U^{[n]}(z)$ when

$$
U(z) = z + U_k z^k + U_{k+1} z^{k+1} + \cdots,
$$

using equations (27) and (28).

## Solution

We are asked to describe explicitly how to compute the coefficients of $U^{[n]}(z)$ using Brent and Traub's method. The key is to start from the **iterative logarithm** $L(z)$ of $U(z)$ and its formal inverse, the **iterative exponential** $\exp_*(z)$. Equations (27) and (28) provide recurrences for computing these coefficients. We proceed step by step.

### Step 1. Recall equations (27) and (28)

Let $U(z) = z + \sum_{m \ge k} U_m z^m$. Define the iterative logarithm $L(z) = \sum_{m \ge k} a_m z^m$. Then equation (27) expresses the coefficients $a_m$ recursively in terms of $U_k, U_{k+1}, \dots$:

$$
\sum_{j=k}^{m} j\, a_j \, c_{m,j} = U_m, \qquad m \ge k,
$$

where $c_{m,j}$ is the coefficient of $z^m$ in $(z + \sum_{i=k}^{m-1} a_i z^i)^j$.

Equation (28) gives the inverse procedure: if $A(z) = \sum_{m \ge k} a_m z^m$, then the iterative exponential

$\exp_*(A)(z) = z + \sum_{m \ge k} b_m z^m$

satisfies the recurrence

$$
b_m = \sum_{j=k}^{m} f_{m,j} a_j, \qquad m \ge k,
$$

where $f_{m,j}$ depends on the previously computed $b_i$ for $i< m$. These recurrences are explicitly obtained by expanding $(\exp_*(A)(z))^j$ and matching coefficients with the definition of $A$.

Equations (27) and (28) are thus **computational formulas** for obtaining $a_m$ from $U_m$ and $b_m$ from $a_m$.

### Step 2. Compute the iterative logarithm $L(z)$

Let $U(z) = z + U_k z^k + U_{k+1} z^{k+1} + \cdots$. We define

$$
L(z) = \sum_{m \ge k} a_m z^m.
$$

Substituting $U(z) = \exp_*(L)(z)$ into equation (28) gives

$$
U_m = \sum_{j=k}^{m} f_{m,j} a_j, \qquad m \ge k.
$$

This is a **triangular system**: each $a_m$ depends only on $U_k, \dots, U_m$ and $a_k, \dots, a_{m-1}$. We solve recursively:

1. Start with $a_k = U_k$.
2. For $m = k+1, k+2, \dots$, solve

$$
a_m = \frac{1}{f_{m,m}}\Big(U_m - \sum_{j=k}^{m-1} f_{m,j} a_j\Big).
$$

This determines all coefficients $a_m$ of the iterative logarithm $L(z)$ up to the desired order.

### Step 3. Scale the iterative logarithm

Once $L(z) = \sum_{m \ge k} a_m z^m$ is computed, Brent and Traub's method uses the identity

$$
U^{[n]}(z) = \exp_*(n L(z)).
$$

Define

$$
\tilde{A}(z) = n L(z) = \sum_{m \ge k} (n a_m) z^m.
$$

### Step 4. Compute the iterative exponential $\exp_*(n L(z))$

We now compute $U^{[n]}(z)$ as

$$
U^{[n]}(z) = z + \sum_{m \ge k} b_m z^m,
$$

where the coefficients $b_m$ are determined recursively from $\tilde{A}(z) = \sum_{m \ge k} \tilde{a}_m z^m$ via equation (28):

$$
b_m = \sum_{j=k}^{m} f_{m,j} \tilde{a}_j, \qquad m \ge k,
$$

with $f_{m,j}$ depending only on previously computed $b_i$ for $i< m$. This triangular recurrence allows sequential computation of each coefficient $b_m$.

### Step 5. Algorithm summary

The full coefficient-level procedure for computing $U^{[n]}(z)$ up to order $M$ is:

1. **Input:** Coefficients $U_k, \dots, U_M$ of $U(z)$ and integer $n$.
2. **Compute iterative logarithm coefficients $a_m$ via (27)/(28):**

$$
a_k = U_k, \quad
a_m = \frac{1}{f_{m,m}} \Big( U_m - \sum_{j=k}^{m-1} f_{m,j} a_j \Big), \quad m=k+1, \dots, M.
$$
3. **Scale coefficients:** $\tilde{a}_m = n a_m$.
4. **Compute iterative exponential coefficients $b_m$ via (28):**

$$
b_m = \sum_{j=k}^{m} f_{m,j} \tilde{a}_j, \quad m=k, \dots, M.
$$
5. **Output:** $U^{[n]}(z) = z + \sum_{m=k}^{M} b_m z^m$.

This procedure explicitly uses the recurrences implied by equations (27) and (28) and computes each coefficient in order. No structural theorems or group laws are assumed; the calculation proceeds by triangular coefficient recurrences.

### Step 6. Verification

Each step is justified by the definitions:

1. $L(z)$ satisfies $\exp_*(L(z)) = U(z)$.
2. Scaling $L(z)$ by $n$ corresponds to $n$-fold composition in the formal sense.
3. Expanding $\exp_*(n L(z))$ via the recurrence gives coefficients $b_m$ of $U^{[n]}(z)$.

Thus the method produces the coefficients of $U^{[n]}(z)$ correctly and efficiently.

### Step 7. Remarks

- The method is **triangular**: computing each coefficient depends only on previous coefficients.
- The key computational content comes from equations (27) and (28), which give explicit recurrences.
- The identity $U^{[n]} = \exp_*(n L)$ is used at the **coefficient level** via the triangular recurrences.

**Answer:** The coefficients of $U^{[n]}(z)$ are obtained by first computing the iterative logarithm $L(z)$ from $U(z)$ via the recurrence (27), scaling its coefficients by $n$, and then computing the iterative exponential $\exp_*(n L(z))$ via the recurrence (28). This fills in the detailed computational procedure of Brent and Traub's method.
