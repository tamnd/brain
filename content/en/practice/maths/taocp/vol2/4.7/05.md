[Section 4.7: Manipulation of Power Series](../)

**Exercise 5.** [*M00*] What happens when a power series is reverted twice, that is, if the output of Algorithm L or T is reverted again?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 8m45s  

---

Let the original relation be

$z = t + V_2 t^2 + V_3 t^3 + \cdots,$

and let its reversion be

$t = z + W_2 z^2 + W_3 z^3 + \cdots.$

Reversion constructs the compositional inverse in the sense that substituting the second series into the first yields $z$ again. If the reverted series is applied as an input to the same reversion process, the roles of the two variables are exchanged once more, and the defining relation becomes again a series of the form

$z = t + \widetilde{V}_2 t^2 + \widetilde{V}_3 t^3 + \cdots,$

where the coefficients $\widetilde{V}_n$ are exactly those obtained from substituting $t = z + W_2 z^2 + \cdots$ back into the original identity.

The reversion procedure therefore returns the original power series coefficients, since the construction produces the unique formal power series inverse under composition. Applying reversion twice restores the initial series term by term, so the operation is an involution on the class of power series with vanishing constant term and nonzero linear coefficient. This completes the determination of the effect of double reversion. ∎
