**Exercise 6.** [*M21*] (H. T. Kung.) Apply Newton's method to the computation of $W(z) = 1/V(z)$, when $V(0) \ne 0$, by finding the power series root of the equation $f(x) = 0$, where $f(x) = x^{-1} - V(z)$.

**Solve time:** 1m19s

## Solution

Let

$f(x) = x^{-1} - V(z).$

We seek a power series $x = W(z)$ such that $f(x)=0$, hence $W(z)^{-1} = V(z)$.

Newton’s method applied to $f(x)$ gives

$x_{\text{new}} = x - \frac{f(x)}{f'(x)}.$

Differentiate $f(x)$ with respect to $x$:

$f'(x) = -x^{-2}.$

Substituting into Newton’s iteration,

$x_{\text{new}} = x - \frac{x^{-1} - V(z)}{-x^{-2}}.$

Multiply numerator and denominator inside the fraction:

$\frac{x^{-1} - V(z)}{-x^{-2}} = -(x^{-1} - V(z))x^2.$

Hence

$x_{\text{new}} = x + (x^{-1} - V(z))x^2.$

Distribute $x^2$:

$x_{\text{new}} = x + x - V(z)x^2.$

Thus the iteration simplifies to

$x_{\text{new}} = 2x - V(z)x^2.$

Let $W^{(n)}(z)$ denote the $n$th Newton approximation. The iteration becomes

$W^{(n+1)}(z) = 2W^{(n)}(z) - V(z)\bigl(W^{(n)}(z)\bigr)^2.$

Since $V(0) \ne 0$, the constant term of $W(z)$ is $W_0 = 1/V_0$, which provides a valid initial approximation for the iteration in the ring of formal power series.

The product $V(z)(W^{(n)}(z))^2$ is computed by formal convolution of coefficients using equation (1), and the iteration determines successively higher-order coefficients of $W(z)$.

This completes the derivation of Newton’s method for computing the reciprocal of a power series. ∎
