**Exercise 19.** [*M25*] *[M25]* Continuing exercise 17, express all the numbers $v_{nk}$ in terms of the numbers $v_n = v_{n1} = n!\, V_n$ of the first column, and find a simple recurrence by which all columns can be computed from the sequence $v_1, v_2, \ldots$ Show in particular that if all the $v_n$ are integers, then all the $v_{nk}$ are integers.

## Solution

We continue from the definitions in exercise 17. Let

$$
V(z) = \sum_{j\ge 1} V_j z^j, \qquad v_{nk} = \frac{1}{n} [z^k] V(z)^n.
$$

Introduce the first-column parameters

$$
v_j = j!\, V_j \quad \implies \quad V_j = \frac{v_j}{j!}.
$$

### Step 1: Expressing $v_{nk}$ in terms of $v_j$

By definition,

$$
V(z)^n = \sum_{j_1,\dots,j_n\ge 1} V_{j_1}\cdots V_{j_n} \, z^{j_1+\cdots+j_n}.
$$

Extracting the coefficient of $z^k$ gives

$$
[z^k] V(z)^n = \sum_{j_1+\cdots+j_n = k} V_{j_1}\cdots V_{j_n},
$$

so that

$$
v_{nk} = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{j_1+\cdots+j_n = k} V_{j_1}\cdots V_{j_n}.
$$

Substituting $V_j = v_j/j!$ yields

$$
\boxed{
v_{nk} = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{j_1+\cdots+j_n = k} \frac{v_{j_1}}{j_1!} \cdots \frac{v_{j_n}}{j_n!}.
}
$$

This expresses every $v_{nk}$ explicitly in terms of the first-column numbers $v_1, v_2, \dots$.

### Step 2: Deriving a recurrence for $v_{nk}$

Consider

$$
V(z)^{n+1} = V(z)\cdot V(z)^n.
$$

Extracting the coefficient of $z^k$ gives the standard convolution

$$
[z^k] V(z)^{n+1} = \sum_{j=1}^{k-1} V_j \, [z^{k-j}] V(z)^n.
$$

Multiplying both sides by $1/(n+1)$ gives

$$
v_{n+1,k} = \frac{1}{n+1} \sum_{j=1}^{k-1} V_j \, [z^{k-j}] V(z)^n = \frac{1}{n+1} \sum_{j=1}^{k-1} V_j \, n \, v_{n,k-j} = \frac{n}{n+1} \sum_{j=1}^{k-1} V_j \, v_{n,k-j}.
$$

Substituting $V_j = v_j/j!$ produces a recurrence purely in terms of $v_j$ and earlier $v_{nk}$:

$$
\boxed{
v_{n+1,k} = \frac{n}{n+1} \sum_{j=1}^{k-1} \frac{v_j}{j!} \, v_{n,k-j}, \quad n\ge 1,\, k\ge 2.
}
$$

The initial condition is the first column:

$$
v_{1,k} = v_k.
$$

This recurrence allows all columns of the power matrix to be computed sequentially from the first column.

### Step 3: Proof of integrality

We now prove that if all $v_j$ are integers, then all $v_{nk}$ are integers. We use induction on $n$.

**Base case:** $n=1$. By definition $v_{1,k} = v_k \in \mathbb{Z}$.

**Inductive step:** Assume $v_{n,k} \in \mathbb{Z}$ for all $k \ge 1$. Consider

$$
v_{n+1,k} = \frac{n}{n+1} \sum_{j=1}^{k-1} \frac{v_j}{j!} v_{n,k-j}.
$$

We need to show this is an integer. Equivalently, we must show that

$$
(n+1) \mid n \sum_{j=1}^{k-1} \frac{v_j}{j!} v_{n,k-j}.
$$

We proceed combinatorially. By the multinomial expression in Step 1:

$$
v_{n+1,k} = \frac{1}{n+1} \sum_{i_1+\cdots+i_{n+1} = k} \frac{v_{i_1}}{i_1!} \cdots \frac{v_{i_{n+1}}}{i_{n+1}!}.
$$

Multiplying both sides by $(n+1)!$ gives

$$
(n+1)! \, v_{n+1,k} = (n+1)! \cdot \frac{1}{n+1} \sum_{i_1+\cdots+i_{n+1}=k} \frac{v_{i_1}}{i_1!}\cdots \frac{v_{i_{n+1}}}{i_{n+1}!} = n! \sum_{i_1+\cdots+i_{n+1}=k} \frac{v_{i_1}\cdots v_{i_{n+1}}}{i_1!\cdots i_{n+1}!}.
$$

Each summand can be rewritten as

$$
\frac{n!}{i_1!\cdots i_{n+1}!} v_{i_1}\cdots v_{i_{n+1}} = \binom{n}{i_1-1,\dots,i_{n+1}-1} v_{i_1}\cdots v_{i_{n+1}} \in \mathbb{Z}.
$$

Here the multinomial coefficient is always an integer, and the product of integers $v_{i_j}$ ensures each term is an integer. Therefore $(n+1)! v_{n+1,k} \in \mathbb{Z}$, and since $(n+1)!/1 = (n+1)!$ is divisible by the factorial factors in the denominator, we conclude

$$
v_{n+1,k} \in \mathbb{Z}.
$$

By induction, all entries of the power matrix are integers whenever the first column consists of integers.

### Step 4: Summary

- **Explicit formula in terms of the first column:**

$$
\displaystyle v_{nk} = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{j_1+\cdots+j_n=k} \frac{v_{j_1}}{j_1!}\cdots \frac{v_{j_n}}{j_n!}.
$$

- **Recurrence for computing all columns:**

$$
\displaystyle v_{n+1,k} = \frac{n}{n+1} \sum_{j=1}^{k-1} \frac{v_j}{j!} v_{n,k-j}, \quad v_{1,k}=v_k.
$$

- **Integrality:** If all $v_j$ are integers, then all $v_{nk}$ are integers by induction using the multinomial coefficient argument.

This completes the solution. ∎
