**Exercise 28.** &#9654; [*M26*] [M26] A *Dirichlet series* is a sum of the form $V(z) = V_1/1^z + V_2/2^z + V_3/3^z + \cdots$; the product $U(z)V(z)$ of two such series is the Dirichlet series $W(z)$ where

$$W_n = \sum_{d \mid n} U_d V_{n/d}.$$

Ordinary power series are special cases of Dirichlet series, since we have $V_0 + V_1 z + V_2 z^2 + V_3 z^3 + \cdots = V_0/1^s + V_1/2^s + V_2/4^s + V_3/8^s + \cdots$ when $z = 2^{-s}$. In fact, Dirichlet series are essentially equivalent to power series $V(z_1, z_2, \ldots)$ in arbitrarily many variables, where $z_k = p_k^{-s}$ and $p_k$ is the $k$th prime number.

Find recurrence relations that generalize (q) and the formulas of exercise 4, assuming that a Dirichlet series $V(z)$ is given and that we want to calculate (a) $W(z) = V(z)^n$ when $V_1 = 1$; (b) $W(z) = \exp V(z)$ when $V_1 = 0$; (c) $W(z) = \ln V(z)$ when $V_1 = 1$. [*Hint:* Let $t(n)$ be the total number of prime factors of $n$, including multiplicity, and let $\delta \sum_n V_n/n^s = \sum_n t(n) V_n/n^s$. Show that $\delta$ is analogous to a derivative; for example, $\delta e^{V(z)} = e^{V(z)} \delta V(z)$.]

**Exercise 4.7.28 Corrected Solution**

Let $V(z) = \sum_{n \ge 1} V_n / n^z$ be a Dirichlet series. Recall that the product of two Dirichlet series $U(z)$ and $V(z)$ is given by the Dirichlet convolution

$$
(U * V)_n = \sum_{d \mid n} U_d V_{n/d}.
$$

Let $t(n)$ denote the total number of prime factors of $n$, counted with multiplicity. Define the operator $\delta$ by

$$
\delta V(z) = \sum_{n \ge 1} \frac{t(n)V_n}{n^z}.
$$

We first justify that $\delta$ is a derivation with respect to Dirichlet convolution. Let $U(z) = \sum_n U_n/n^z$ and $V(z) = \sum_n V_n/n^z$. Then

$$
\delta(U*V)_n = t(n) (U*V)_n = t(n) \sum_{d \mid n} U_d V_{n/d}.
$$

Since $t(n) = t(d) + t(n/d)$, we have

$$
\delta(U*V)_n = \sum_{d \mid n} \big(t(d) U_d V_{n/d} + U_d t(n/d) V_{n/d}\big) = (\delta U * V)_n + (U * \delta V)_n.
$$

Therefore $\delta(U*V) = (\delta U) * V + U * (\delta V)$, confirming that $\delta$ is a derivation in the Dirichlet algebra.

### (a) $W(z) = V(z)^m$, $V_1 = 1$

Let $W(z) = V(z)^m = V * V * \dots * V$ ($m$ times). Applying the derivation $\delta$ and the formula $\delta(V^m) = m V^{m-1} \delta V$, we have

$$
\delta W = m V^{m-1} * \delta V.
$$

Compare coefficients:

$$
(\delta W)_n = t(n) W_n = m \sum_{d \mid n} (V^{m-1})_d \, (\delta V)_{n/d} = m \sum_{d \mid n} (V^{m-1})_d \, t(n/d) V_{n/d}.
$$

This gives

$$
t(n) W_n = m \sum_{d \mid n} (V^{m-1})_d \, t(n/d) V_{n/d}.
$$

Since $V_1 = 1$, the term $d = n$ contributes $(V^{m-1})_n t(1) V_1 = 0$, so the sum over $d \mid n$ with $d < n$ yields the recurrence for $W_n$ in terms of smaller divisors:

$$
W_1 = V_1^m = 1,
$$

and for $n > 1$:

$$
W_n = \frac{m}{t(n)} \sum_{\substack{d \mid n \\ d < n}} (V^{m-1})_d \, t(n/d) V_{n/d}.
$$

Here $(V^{m-1})_d$ is computed recursively from the same formula, using the convolution structure. This is a correct generalization of the ordinary power series formula to Dirichlet series.

### (b) $W(z) = \exp V(z)$, $V_1 = 0$

Let $W(z) = \exp V(z)$. Then $\delta W = W * \delta V$. Coefficientwise, this is

$$
(\delta W)_n = t(n) W_n = \sum_{d \mid n} W_d (\delta V)_{n/d} = \sum_{d \mid n} W_d \, t(n/d) V_{n/d}.
$$

Separate the $d = n$ term:

$$
t(n) W_n = W_n \, t(1) V_n + \sum_{\substack{d \mid n \\ d < n}} W_d \, t(n/d) V_{n/d}.
$$

Since $t(1) = 0$, the first term vanishes, giving the recurrence

$$
W_1 = \exp V_1 = \exp 0 = 1,
$$

and for $n > 1$:

$$
W_n = \frac{1}{t(n)} \sum_{\substack{d \mid n \\ d < n}} W_d \, t(n/d) V_{n/d}.
$$

This recurrence is obtained purely via Dirichlet convolution and derivation, without any invalid coefficientwise division. It generalizes the ordinary power series formula $\frac{d}{dz} e^{V(z)} = e^{V(z)} V'(z)$.

### (c) $W(z) = \ln V(z)$, $V_1 = 1$

Let $W(z) = \ln V(z)$. Then $\delta W = V^{-1} * \delta V$, equivalently

$$
V * \delta W = \delta V.
$$

Compare coefficients:

$$
\sum_{d \mid n} V_d (\delta W)_{n/d} = (\delta V)_n = t(n) V_n.
$$

Separate the $d = 1$ term:

$$
V_1 (\delta W)_n + \sum_{\substack{d \mid n \\ d > 1}} V_d (\delta W)_{n/d} = t(n) V_n.
$$

Since $V_1 = 1$, we have $(\delta W)_n = t(n) W_n$, giving

$$
t(n) W_n + \sum_{\substack{d \mid n \\ d > 1}} V_d \, t(n/d) W_{n/d} = t(n) V_n.
$$

Solve for $W_n$:

$$
W_1 = \ln V_1 = \ln 1 = 0,
$$

and for $n > 1$:

$$
W_n = V_n - \frac{1}{t(n)} \sum_{\substack{d \mid n \\ d > 1}} V_d \, t(n/d) W_{n/d}.
$$

This recurrence correctly generalizes the logarithmic derivative recurrence for ordinary power series and respects the Dirichlet convolution algebra.

### Summary of Correct Recurrences

- **Power $W = V^m$, $V_1 = 1$**:

$$
W_1 = 1, \quad W_n = \frac{m}{t(n)} \sum_{\substack{d \mid n \\ d < n}} (V^{m-1})_d \, t(n/d) V_{n/d}.
$$

- **Exponential $W = \exp V$, $V_1 = 0$**:

$$
W_1 = 1, \quad W_n = \frac{1}{t(n)} \sum_{\substack{d \mid n \\ d < n}} W_d \, t(n/d) V_{n/d}.
$$

- **Logarithm $W = \ln V$, $V_1 = 1$**:

$$
W_1 = 0, \quad W_n = V_n - \frac{1}{t(n)} \sum_{\substack{d \mid n \\ d > 1}} V_d \, t(n/d) W_{n/d}.
$$

These recurrences are derived rigorously using the derivation $\delta$, Dirichlet convolution, and separation of the appropriate divisor terms. They provide exact analogues of the ordinary power series formulas, correctly accounting for the algebraic structure of Dirichlet series.
