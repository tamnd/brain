**Exercise 4.** [*29*] Continue the process shown in (13); what would $m_5, m_6, m_9, \ldots$ be?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 6m27s

## Setup

Equation (13) is obtained by the rule stated immediately before it. Starting from the largest odd number below the word size, each subsequent modulus is chosen to be the largest odd integer smaller than the preceding modulus and relatively prime to every modulus already chosen.

For a decimal machine with word size $100$, the text gives

$$
m_1=99,\qquad
m_2=97,\qquad
m_3=95,\qquad
m_4=91,\qquad
m_5=89,\qquad
m_6=83.
$$

The exercise asks for the continuation of this sequence.

## Solution

The choice of $m_j$ is determined recursively. After $m_6=83$, we examine odd integers below $83$ in decreasing order and reject any number having a common factor with one of

$$
99=3^2\cdot11,\quad
97,\quad
95=5\cdot19,\quad
91=7\cdot13,\quad
89,\quad
83.
$$

The number $81$ is divisible by $3$, so $\gcd(81,99)\ne1$.

The number $79$ is prime and distinct from all previous prime factors, hence

$$
m_7=79.
$$

For $m_8$, test odd integers below $79$.
