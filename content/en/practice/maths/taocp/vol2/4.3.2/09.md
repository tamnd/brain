**Exercise 9.** [*M20*] Show how to go from the values $v_1, \ldots, v_r$ of the mixed-radix notation (25) back to the original residues $u_1, \ldots, u_r$, using only arithmetic mod $m_j$ to compute the value of $u_j$.

## Solution

From the construction of mixed-radix digits in (25), the integer $u$ is represented in the form

$$
u \equiv v_1 + m_1 v_2 + m_1 m_2 v_3 + \cdots + m_1 m_2 \cdots m_{r-1} v_r \pmod{m},
$$

and for each $j$ the residue $u_j$ is defined by

$$
u_j \equiv u \pmod{m_j}.
$$

Substituting the mixed-radix expansion of $u$ into this congruence yields

$$
u_j \equiv v_1 + m_1 v_2 + \cdots + m_1 m_2 \cdots m_{j-1} v_j \pmod{m_j},
$$

since every term containing a factor $m_k$ with $k \ge j$ vanishes modulo $m_j$.

Define, for each fixed $j$, a sequence of partial values in $\mathbb{Z}/m_j\mathbb{Z}$ by

$$
s_{j,1} \equiv v_1 \pmod{m_j},
$$

and for $2 \le k \le j$,

$$
s_{j,k} \equiv s_{j,k-1} + m_{k-1} v_k \pmod{m_j}.
$$

Each step uses only arithmetic modulo $m_j$, since $m_{k-1} v_k$ is first formed and then reduced modulo $m_j$.

The final value satisfies

$$
s_{j,j} \equiv v_1 + m_1 v_2 + \cdots + m_{j-1} v_j \pmod{m_j},
$$

hence $s_{j,j} \equiv u_j \pmod{m_j}$. Therefore the required residue is

$$
u_j \equiv v_1 + m_1 v_2 + \cdots + m_{j-1} v_j \pmod{m_j}.
$$

This expression determines each $u_j$ solely from $v_1, \ldots, v_j$ using arithmetic modulo $m_j$, and no other moduli are required. This completes the construction. ∎
