[Section 4.5.1: Fractions](../)

**Exercise 5.** [**] [$10$] Compute $(17/120) \div (-27/70)$ by the method recommended in the text.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 7m05s  

---

The recommended division method is the direct fraction-division algorithm. Let

$$
x=\frac{u}{u'}, \qquad y=\frac{v}{v'}.
$$

To compute $x\div y$, first determine the sign from $v$, then form

$$
d_1=\gcd(u,v), \qquad d_2=\gcd(u',v').
$$

The quotient is then

$$
\pm\,\frac{(u/d_1)(v'/d_2)}{(u'/d_2)(v/d_1)},
$$

where the sign is negative if $y$ is negative.

For

$$
\frac{17}{120}\div\frac{-27}{70},
$$

we have

$$
u=17,\qquad u'=120,\qquad v=-27,\qquad v'=70.
$$

Since $v<0$, the result will be negative.

Now compute the required common divisors:

$$
d_1=\gcd(17,27)=1,
\qquad
d_2=\gcd(120,70)=10.
$$

Applying the division algorithm gives

$$
\frac{17}{120}\div\frac{-27}{70}
=
-\frac{(17/d_1)(70/d_2)}
       {(120/d_2)(27/d_1)}
=
-\frac{17\cdot 7}{12\cdot 27}.
$$

Multiplying the reduced factors,

$$
17\cdot 7=119,
\qquad
12\cdot 27=324,
$$

so

$$
\frac{17}{120}\div\frac{-27}{70}
=
-\frac{119}{324}.
$$

Since

$$
119=7\cdot 17,
\qquad
324=2^2\cdot 3^4,
$$

the numerator and denominator are relatively prime, hence the fraction is already in lowest terms.

Therefore

$$
\boxed{\frac{17}{120}\div\frac{-27}{70}=-\frac{119}{324}}.
$$
