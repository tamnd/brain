[Section 4.5.1: Fractions](../)

**Exercise 3.** [**] [$M20$] Prove that $u \perp u'$ and $v \perp v'$ implies $\gcd(uv', u'v) = \gcd(u, v') \gcd(u', v)$.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 2m25s  

---

Let

$$
a=\gcd(u,v'), \qquad b=\gcd(u',v).
$$

Since $u\perp u'$ and $v\perp v'$, every prime factor of $a$ divides $u$ and $v'$ but cannot divide $u'$ or $v$; similarly, every prime factor of $b$ divides $u'$ and $v$ but cannot divide $u$ or $v'$. Hence $a\perp b$.

Write

$$
u=a\alpha,\qquad v'=a\beta,\qquad u'=b\gamma,\qquad v=b\delta,
$$

where $\alpha\perp\beta$ and $\gamma\perp\delta$. Then

$$
uv'=a^2\alpha\beta,\qquad u'v=b^2\gamma\delta .
$$

Because $a$ is relatively prime to $b$, $\gamma$, and $\delta$, and because $b$ is relatively prime to $\alpha$ and $\beta$, any common divisor of $uv'$ and $u'v$ must divide both $\alpha\beta$ and $\gamma\delta$. But $\alpha\perp\gamma$, $\alpha\perp\delta$, $\beta\perp\gamma$, and $\beta\perp\delta$; otherwise a common prime factor would contradict $u\perp u'$ or $v\perp v'$. Therefore

$$
\gcd(\alpha\beta,\gamma\delta)=1.
$$

Consequently,

$$
\gcd(uv',u'v)
=\gcd(a^2\alpha\beta,\; b^2\gamma\delta)
=ab.
$$

Since $ab=\gcd(u,v'),\gcd(u',v)$, we obtain

$$
\gcd(uv',u'v)=\gcd(u,v')\,\gcd(u',v).
$$

This completes the proof.

∎
