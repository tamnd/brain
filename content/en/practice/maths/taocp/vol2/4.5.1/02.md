[Section 4.5.1: Fractions](../)

**Exercise 2.** [**] [$M15$] Prove that if $d = \gcd(u, v)$ then $u/d$ and $v/d$ are relatively prime.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m48s  

---

Let

$$
d=\gcd(u,v),
$$

and define

$$
a=\frac{u}{d}, \qquad b=\frac{v}{d}.
$$

Suppose that $a$ and $b$ are not relatively prime. Then there exists an integer $c>1$ such that

$$
c\mid a,\qquad c\mid b.
$$

Hence

$$
u=acd,\qquad v=bcd,
$$

so $cd$ is a common divisor of $u$ and $v$. Since $c>1$, we have $cd>d$, contradicting the fact that $d=\gcd(u,v)$ is the greatest common divisor of $u$ and $v$.

Therefore no integer greater than $1$ divides both $a$ and $b$. Hence

$$
\gcd\!\left(\frac{u}{d},\frac{v}{d}\right)=1,
$$

so $u/d$ and $v/d$ are relatively prime.

This completes the proof.

∎
