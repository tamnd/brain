**Exercise 9.** [**] [$M23$] If $1 \le u'$, $u' < 2^k$, show that $\lfloor 2^{2k} u/u' \rfloor = \lfloor 2^{2k} u/u' \rfloor$ implies $u/u' = v/v'$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m32s  

---

Suppose $1 \le u' < 2^k$ and $1 \le v' < 2^k$, and assume that

$\lfloor 2^{2k} u/u' \rfloor = \lfloor 2^{2k} v/v' \rfloor.$

Let $x = 2^{2k} u/u'$ and $y = 2^{2k} v/v'$. Then $\lfloor x \rfloor = \lfloor y \rfloor$, so $|x - y| < 1$. That is,

$\left| \frac{2^{2k} u}{u'} - \frac{2^{2k} v}{v'} \right| < 1.$

Dividing both sides by $2^{2k}$ gives

$\left| \frac{u}{u'} - \frac{v}{v'} \right| < 2^{-2k}.$

Since $u/u'$ and $v/v'$ are fractions with denominators less than $2^k$, their difference can be written with denominator $u'v' < 2^{2k}$. Thus

$\frac{|uv' - u'v|}{u'v'} < 2^{-2k} \implies |uv' - u'v| < \frac{u'v'}{2^{2k}} < 1.$

Because $|uv' - u'v|$ is an integer, it follows that $uv' - u'v = 0$, hence $u/v = u'/v'$. Therefore

$\frac{u}{u'} = \frac{v}{v'}.$

This completes the proof.
