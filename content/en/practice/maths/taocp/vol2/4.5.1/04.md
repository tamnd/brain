[Section 4.5.1: Fractions](../)

**Exercise 4.** [**] [$1t$] Design a division algorithm for fractions, analogous to the second multiplication method of the text. (Note that the sign of $v$ must be considered.)

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m06s  

---

Let

$$
\frac{u}{u'} \div \frac{v}{v'},
$$

where $u \perp u'$ and $v \perp v'$. Since division by $(v/v')$ is multiplication by $(v'/v)$, and since the denominator of the result must be positive, the sign of $v$ must be taken into account.

Set

$$
a=\gcd(u,v), \qquad b=\gcd(u',v').
$$

Then compute

$$
w \leftarrow (u/a)(v'/b),
$$

$$
w' \leftarrow (u'/b)(v/a).
$$

If $w'<0$, replace $w$ by $-w$ and $w'$ by $-w'$. The answer is then $(w/w')$.

This is analogous to the second multiplication method of the text, because the common factors $\gcd(u,v)$ and $\gcd(u',v')$ are removed before the products are formed. Since

$$
u=a\alpha,\qquad v=a\beta,\qquad u'=b\gamma,\qquad v'=b\delta,
$$

with $\alpha\perp\beta$ and $\gamma\perp\delta$, the result becomes

$$
\frac{\alpha\delta}{\gamma\beta},
$$

and

$$
\gcd(\alpha\delta,\gamma\beta)=1
$$

by Exercise 3. Hence the fraction is already in lowest terms. ∎
