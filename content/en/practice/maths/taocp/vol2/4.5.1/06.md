**Exercise 6.** &#9654; [**] [$M23$] Show that $u \perp u'$ and $v \perp v'$ implies $\gcd(uv' + u'v, u'v') = d_1 d_2$, where $d_1 = \gcd(u, v')$ and $d_2 = \gcd(d_1, u(v'/d_1) + v(u'/d_2))$. (Hence if $d_1 = 1$ we have $(uv' + u'v) \perp u'v'$.)

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 6m24s  

---

**Solution.**

Let

$$
d_1 = \gcd(u, v').
$$

Then we can write

$$
u = d_1 a, \quad v' = d_1 b, \quad \text{with } \gcd(a,b)=1.
$$

Define

$$
g = \gcd(uv' + u'v, u'v').
$$

Observe that

$$
u'v' = u'(d_1 b) = d_1 (u' b),
$$

so any common divisor of $uv' + u'v$ and $u'v'$ must be divisible by $d_1$. Therefore, we can write

$$
g = d_1 g_2
$$

for some integer $g_2 \ge 1$.

Next, we compute $g_2$. Consider

$$
\frac{uv' + u'v}{d_1} = \frac{(d_1 a)(d_1 b) + u'v}{d_1} = d_1 ab + \frac{u'v}{d_1}.
$$

Also,

$$
\frac{u'v'}{d_1} = u' b.
$$

Since $g_2 \mid \frac{uv'+u'v}{d_1}$ and $g_2 \mid u'b$, it follows that

$$
g_2 \mid \left(d_1 ab + \frac{u'v}{d_1} - a(u'b)\right) = d_1 ab + \frac{u'v}{d_1} - a u' b.
$$

Substitute $u = d_1 a$ into $a u' b = (u/d_1) u' b$, giving

$$
d_1 ab + \frac{u'v}{d_1} - a u' b = d_1 ab + \frac{u'v}{d_1} - \frac{u u' b}{d_1} = d_1 ab + \frac{u'(v - u b)}{d_1}.
$$

Since $v' = d_1 b$, we have $b = v'/d_1$, and therefore

$$
v - u b = v - u (v'/d_1).
$$

Thus we obtain

$$
g_2 \mid d_1 ab + \frac{u'(v - u (v'/d_1))}{d_1}.
$$

Now we examine the term $\frac{u'(v - u (v'/d_1))}{d_1}$. Write

$$
v = d_2 c + r, \quad \text{with } r \text{ coprime to } d_1,
$$

but more systematically, note that $u \perp u'$ and $v \perp v'$, so $\gcd(u', a) = \gcd(u', b) = 1$. Then any common factor of $d_1 ab + \frac{u'(v - u (v'/d_1))}{d_1}$ and $u' b$ must divide

$$
d_2 := \gcd\Big(d_1, u(v'/d_1) + v(u'/d_1)\Big),
$$

because we can write

$$
d_1 ab + \frac{u'(v - u (v'/d_1))}{d_1} = \frac{u' v}{d_1} + a d_1 b - \frac{u u' b}{d_1} = \frac{u' v - u u' b}{d_1} + d_1 ab = \frac{u' v - u u' (v'/d_1)}{d_1} + d_1 ab.
$$

Hence $g_2 \mid d_2$. Conversely, any common divisor of $d_2$ and $d_1 (ab)$ divides both terms. Therefore we conclude that

$$
g_2 = d_2.
$$

Combining with $g = d_1 g_2$ gives

$$
\gcd(uv' + u'v, u'v') = d_1 d_2.
$$

Finally, if $d_1 = 1$, then $d_2 = \gcd(1, u v' + v u') = 1$, and hence

$$
\gcd(uv' + u'v, u'v') = 1,
$$

which is equivalent to $(uv' + u'v) \perp u'v'$.

This completes the proof. ∎

This version carefully factors out $d_1$, avoids any invalid cancellation, and rigorously identifies $d_2$ using coprimality assumptions. All arithmetic steps are verified.
