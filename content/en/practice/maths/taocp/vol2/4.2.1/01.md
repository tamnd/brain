**Exercise 1.** [*10*] How would Avogadro's number and Planck's constant (§3) be represented in base 100, excess 50, four-digit floating point notation? (This would be the representation used by MIX, as in §4, when the byte size is 100.)

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 8m05s  

---

In MIX floating point with byte size $100$, a number is represented as

$$
(\text{sign})\,(e,b_1,b_2,b_3,b_4),
$$

where

$$
x=\pm (0.b_1b_2b_3b_4)_{100}\,100^{\,e-50},
$$

with $1\le b_1\le 99$. The exponent is stored in excess-$50$ notation.

We use the values from §3:

$$
N_A = 6.02\times 10^{23},
$$

$$
h = 6.625\times 10^{-27}.
$$

The task is to normalize each quantity in base $100$ and retain four base-$100$ digits.

### Avogadro's number

Since

$$
100^{11}=10^{22},\qquad 100^{12}=10^{24},
$$

we write

$$
N_A=(6.02\times10^{23})=(0.602)\,100^{12}.
$$

Now convert the fraction $0.602$ to base $100$.

First digit:

$$
100(0.602)=60.2,
$$

hence $b_1=60$, remainder $0.2$.

Second digit:

$$
100(0.2)=20,
$$

hence $b_2=20$, remainder $0$.

Therefore

$$
0.602=(0.60\,20\,00\,00)_{100}.
$$

Thus

$$
N_A=(0.60\,20\,00\,00)_{100}\,100^{12}.
$$

Since the true exponent is $12$, the stored exponent is

$$
12+50=62.
$$

Hence Avogadro's number is represented by

$$
(+,\,62,\,60,\,20,\,00,\,00).
$$

### Planck's constant

Since

$$
100^{-13}=10^{-26},
$$

we have

$$
h=6.625\times10^{-27}
   =0.6625\times10^{-26}
   =(0.6625)\,100^{-13}.
$$

Convert $0.6625$ to base $100$.

First digit:

$$
100(0.6625)=66.25,
$$

so $b_1=66$, remainder $0.25$.

Second digit:

$$
100(0.25)=25,
$$

so $b_2=25$, remainder $0$.

Hence

$$
0.6625=(0.66\,25\,00\,00)_{100}.
$$

Therefore

$$
h=(0.66\,25\,00\,00)_{100}\,100^{-13}.
$$

The stored exponent is

$$
-13+50=37.
$$

Thus Planck's constant is represented by

$$
(+,\,37,\,66,\,25,\,00,\,00).
$$

Therefore the required four-digit base-$100$ floating-point representations are

$$
\boxed{N_A=(+,\,62,\,60,\,20,\,00,\,00)}
$$

and

$$
\boxed{h=(+,\,37,\,66,\,25,\,00,\,00)}.
$$
