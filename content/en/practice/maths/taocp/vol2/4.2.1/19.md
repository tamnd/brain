## Solution

The running time of the FADD subroutine in Program A depends on several characteristics of the input floating point numbers $u = (e_u, f_u)$ and $v = (e_v, f_v)$, and on the parameters $b$ (byte size) and $p$ (number of fraction digits). We analyze the steps of Algorithm A and the embedded normalization routine NORM systematically.

Step A1 (Unpack) consists of separating the exponent and fraction parts of $u$ and $v$. Each unpacking is a fixed, constant-time operation in MIX, requiring exactly one instruction per field. Thus the running time of A1 is independent of $u$ and $v$, a constant $T_1$.

Step A2 compares $e_u$ and $e_v$ and possibly interchanges the operands. The comparison requires one instruction, and the conditional interchange requires moving both the exponent and fraction fields. Let $T_2$ denote the constant cost of this operation.

Step A3 sets $e_w \leftarrow e_u$, which is a single assignment, taking constant time $T_3$.

Step A4 tests $e_u - e_v \ge p + 2$. This subtraction and comparison take constant time $T_4$. If the condition is satisfied, $f_w \leftarrow f_u$ is executed, and the algorithm jumps to normalization. This occurs when the exponents differ by at least $p+2$, which is the extremal case where the smaller number has no effect. In such a case, the total cost of A5 and A6 is skipped.

Step A5 scales $f_v$ to the right by $e_u - e_v$ places. Each right shift corresponds to a division by $b$ and consumes one instruction per shift. Let $d = e_u - e_v$. Step A5 thus requires exactly $d$ shifts. Since by A4 we have $d < p+2$, the maximum number of shifts in A5 is $p+1$.

Step A6 adds $f_u + f_v$. This addition uses one instruction for each of the $p$ fraction digits plus possible carry propagation. Since MIX uses a 5-byte accumulator but the effective accumulator for Algorithm A must handle $2p + 1$ digits, we assume that the addition of $f_u$ and $f_v$ is performed in a loop over the $p$ digits. Therefore the cost is proportional to $p$, denoted $T_6 = c_6 p$ for some constant $c_6$.

Step A7 invokes the normalization routine NORM. The running time of NORM depends on the magnitude of $f_w$ relative to the normalization threshold $1/b$ and the rounding required to $p$ digits.

In NORM, step N1 tests whether $|f_w| \ge 1$ or $f_w = 0$. Each test is constant time. If $|f_w| \ge 1$, step N4 scales $f_w$ right until $|f_w| < 1$, incrementing $e_w$ at each shift. The maximum number of right shifts occurs when $f_w$ has just overflowed one digit beyond $p$ places, that is, at most one right shift is needed in the worst case for normalized input.

If $|f_w| < 1$ but $|f_w| < 1/b$, step N3 scales left, multiplying by $b$ and decrementing $e$ at each shift. The maximum number of left shifts occurs when $f_w$ is of minimal normalized magnitude, $f_w = b^{-(p-1)}$, requiring $p-1$ left shifts to reach $|f_w| \ge 1/b$. Therefore the number of iterations of N3 is bounded above by $p-1$.

Step N5 rounds $f_w$ to $p$ digits. Rounding requires examination of the $(p+1)$-th digit and possibly incrementing the $p$-th digit. In the case of rounding overflow, step N4 is invoked once more, requiring at most one additional shift right. Therefore rounding adds at most one extra iteration of step N4.

Steps N6 and N7 check exponent range and pack the result. Each is constant time, independent of $p$ or the values of $f_u$ and $f_v$.

Summing the contributions, the total number of shifts (left or right) in NORM is at most $(p-1) + 1 = p$ in the worst case. The addition of $f_u$ and $f_v$ contributes $p$ steps. Scaling in A5 contributes at most $p+1$ shifts. All other steps (unpacking, comparisons, assignments, packing) contribute a constant number $C$ of instructions independent of $p$.

Therefore, the total running time $T$ of FADD is bounded by

$$
T \le C + (p+1) + p + p = C + 3p + 1,
$$

where each $p$ term counts digit-wise operations or shifts. More precisely, each digit shift or addition counts as a single instruction, while C accounts for the fixed overhead of unpacking, exponent comparisons, assignments, and control instructions.

The maximum running time occurs when the exponents are equal ($d = 0$, so A5 executes zero shifts) and $f_u + f_v$ has minimal normalized magnitude $b^{-(p-1)}$, requiring $p-1$ left shifts in NORM and no rounding overflow. In this extremal case, the number of shifts in A5 is zero, the number of additions is $p$, the number of left shifts in NORM is $p-1$, and there is one rounding operation. Thus the maximal running time is

T_\max = C + p + (p-1) + 1 = C + 2p.

This completes the analysis of the running time in terms of the fraction length $p$, the exponent difference $d = e_u - e_v$, and the normalized magnitude of $f_w$. The constants depend on the instruction set of MIX but are independent of the actual values of $f_u$ and $f_v$ beyond the bounds described.

The maximum running time over all inputs that do not cause exponent overflow or underflow is therefore linear in $p$, with

\boxed{T_\max = C + 2p}$$ instructions, where $C$ is the fixed overhead of the subroutine. ∎
