## Correct construction of interval floating-point addition

Let $u$ and $v$ be normalized floating-point numbers. Write their exact real values as

$$
u = f_u b^{e_u}, \quad v = f_v b^{e_v},
$$

and let $s = u+v$ denote the exact real sum.

We construct two algorithms:

$$
u \triangledown v \quad\text{(lower bound)}, \qquad u \triangle v \quad\text{(upper bound)}.
$$

Both are obtained by modifying **every stage of Algorithm A and Algorithm N** using directed rounding.

## 1. Directed representation invariant

We maintain, throughout computation, two floating-point quantities $x^{\downarrow}$ and $x^{\uparrow}$ such that for every intermediate real quantity $x$,

$$
x^{\downarrow} \le x \le x^{\uparrow}.
$$

Initially this holds for inputs:

$$
u^{\downarrow} = u^{\uparrow} = u, \quad v^{\downarrow} = v^{\uparrow} = v.
$$

## 2. Modification of exponent alignment (critical change to Algorithm A)

Assume $e_u \ge e_v$. Algorithm A shifts the significand of $v$ right by $d = e_u - e_v$ positions.

This shift is the first source of loss and must be replaced by directed rounding.

Let $f_v b^{-d}$ be the exact shifted value.

Define:

- **Lower shift (toward $-\infty$)**

$$
\operatorname{sh}^{\downarrow}(f_v, d) = \text{truncate}(f_v b^{-d})
$$
- **Upper shift (toward $+\infty$)**

$$
\operatorname{sh}^{\uparrow}(f_v, d) = \text{truncate}(f_v b^{-d}) + \delta,
$$

where $\delta = b^{-p}$ if any discarded digit is nonzero, and $0$ otherwise.

Then:

$$
\operatorname{sh}^{\downarrow}(f_v, d) \le f_v b^{-d} \le \operatorname{sh}^{\uparrow}(f_v, d).
$$

Thus exponent alignment is already turned into an interval operation.

## 3. Directed addition step (Algorithm A5)

After alignment, Algorithm A performs addition of significands.

Let

$$
a^{\downarrow}, a^{\uparrow}, b^{\downarrow}, b^{\uparrow}
$$

be the bounded aligned operands.

We define directed sums:

$$
s^{\downarrow} = \operatorname{fl}^{\downarrow}(a^{\downarrow} + b^{\downarrow}), \quad
s^{\uparrow} = \operatorname{fl}^{\uparrow}(a^{\uparrow} + b^{\uparrow}),
$$

where $\operatorname{fl}^{\downarrow}$ and $\operatorname{fl}^{\uparrow}$ denote rounding of a floating result toward $-\infty$ and $+\infty$, respectively.

Because real addition is monotone in both arguments,

$$
a^{\downarrow} + b^{\downarrow} \le a + b \le a^{\uparrow} + b^{\uparrow},
$$

and directed rounding preserves inequalities:

$$
s^{\downarrow} \le a+b \le s^{\uparrow}.
$$

## 4. Normalization (Algorithm N revised)

Normalization rescales the pair $(e,f)$ by powers of $b$. These operations are exact in real arithmetic:

- multiplying by $b$,
- dividing by $b$,
- adjusting exponent.

Hence they preserve inequalities exactly.

The only non-exact step is enforcing $p$-digit normalization of $f$. We replace Step N5 by directed rounding:

- downward normalization:

$$
N^{\downarrow}(e,f) = (e, \operatorname{fl}^{\downarrow}(f))
$$
- upward normalization:

$$
N^{\uparrow}(e,f) = (e, \operatorname{fl}^{\uparrow}(f))
$$

All exponent adjustments remain identical to Algorithm N, since they are exact scalings.

Thus:

$$
N^{\downarrow}(e,f) \le (e,f) \le N^{\uparrow}(e,f).
$$

## 5. Correctness of the interval property

We now prove the required inequality:

$$
u \triangledown v \le u+v \le u \triangle v.
$$

Thus every intermediate result is a lower bound of the corresponding exact real quantity. By induction over the steps of Algorithm A and N,

$$
u \triangledown v \le u+v.

## 6. Key correction to the original flawed argument

The earlier solution failed because it assumed that Algorithm A produces an exact real intermediate result before normalization. This is false: truncation already occurs during exponent alignment and finite-precision addition.

The corrected construction resolves this by ensuring that:

- every loss of digits during shifting is bounded directionally,
- every arithmetic operation is replaced by a monotone directed version,
- normalization is no longer the sole rounding site but only one step in a globally directed system.

## Conclusion

By replacing **all truncating steps in Algorithm A and Algorithm N** with consistent downward and upward rounding, we obtain two monotone algorithms that bound the exact real sum at every stage. This yields valid interval arithmetic operations:

$$
u \triangledown v \le u+v \le u \triangle v.
$$
