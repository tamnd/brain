## Solution

We are asked to construct **normalized eight-digit floating decimal numbers** $u$ and $v$ whose **sum produces rounding overflow** in the sense of TAOCP, Section 4.2.1.

We adopt the standard conventions for base-$10$ floating-point numbers with precision $p=8$:

- A normalized number has the form

$$
x = f_x \cdot 10^e, \quad 0.1 \le f_x < 1, \quad f_x \text{ represented with 8 decimal digits.}
$$

- **Rounding overflow** occurs in Algorithm N, step N5, when the rounded fraction becomes exactly $1.0000000$, producing a carry that requires incrementing the exponent.

### Step 1: Condition for rounding overflow

Let $u = f_u \cdot 10^e$ and $v = f_v \cdot 10^e$ be normalized numbers with equal exponents. Their exact sum in the extra-precision accumulator is

$$
f_w = f_u + f_v.
$$

Rounding overflow occurs when the exact sum satisfies

$$
f_w \ge 0.99999995
$$

so that rounding to $p=8$ digits produces

$$
\text{round}(f_w) = 1.0000000,
$$

which triggers a carry and requires normalization with exponent increment.

### Step 2: Construct 8-digit fractions

We must choose $f_u$ and $f_v$ as **8-digit numbers** in $[0.1,1)$. Let

$$
f_u = 0.50000000, \quad f_v = 0.49999999.
$$

- Both numbers have exactly 8 digits and are normalized.
- Their sum is

$$
f_w = f_u + f_v = 0.50000000 + 0.49999999 = 0.99999999.
$$

- The ninth digit of the exact sum (in extra precision) is greater than 5, so rounding to 8 digits gives

$$
f_w \longmapsto 1.0000000,
$$

producing **rounding overflow**.

### Step 3: Apply Algorithm N (addition)

Let the exponents of $u$ and $v$ be equal, $e_u = e_v = e$. Then the raw sum in the accumulator is

$$
w_{\text{raw}} = f_w \cdot 10^e = 0.99999999 \cdot 10^e.
$$

Rounding to $p=8$ digits produces

$$
f_w = 1.0000000.
$$

This is outside the normalized fraction range $[0.1,1)$, triggering the **rounding overflow step** (Algorithm N, step N4).

### Step 4: Normalize the result

Algorithm N handles rounding overflow by **shifting the fraction right** and incrementing the exponent:

$$
f_w = 1.0000000 \longmapsto 0.10000000 \cdot 10^{e+1}.
$$

Thus the sum is properly normalized and the rounding overflow is realized in accordance with TAOCP.

### Step 5: Final example

The requested numbers are

$$
\boxed{u = 0.50000000 \cdot 10^e, \quad v = 0.49999999 \cdot 10^e}.
$$

Both are **normalized 8-digit floating decimal numbers**, and their sum produces **rounding overflow** in the sense of Section 4.2.1.

∎

This example corrects the critical error in the previous solution by using **only 8-digit fractions**, fully satisfying the exercise requirements.
