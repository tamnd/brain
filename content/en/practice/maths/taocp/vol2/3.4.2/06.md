**Exercise 6.** [*M24*] What is the standard deviation of the value computed in exercise 5?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 4m24s

## Solution

Let $T$ be the value of $t$ when Algorithm S terminates. As shown in Exercise 5,

$$
\Pr(T=t)=\frac{\binom{t-1}{n-1}}{\binom{N}{n}},
\qquad n\le t\le N,
$$

and

$$
E(T)=\frac{n(N+1)}{n+1}.
$$

To obtain the standard deviation, we compute $E(T^2)$.

First write

$$
E(T^2)
=
\frac1{\binom{N}{n}}
\sum_{t=n}^{N}
t^2\binom{t-1}{n-1}.
$$

Using

$$
t\binom{t-1}{n-1}
=
n\binom{t}{n},
$$

we have

$$
t^2\binom{t-1}{n-1}
=
n\,t\binom{t}{n}.
$$

Now

$$
t\binom{t}{n}
=
n\binom{t}{n}
+
(n+1)\binom{t}{n+1},
$$

because

$$
(n+1)\binom{t}{n+1}
=
(t-n)\binom{t}{n}.
$$

Hence

$$
t^2\binom{t-1}{n-1}
=
n^2\binom{t}{n}
+
n(n+1)\binom{t}{n+1}.
$$

Therefore

$$
E(T^2)
=
\frac1{\binom{N}{n}}
\left(
n^2\sum_{t=n}^{N}\binom{t}{n}
+
n(n+1)\sum_{t=n}^{N}\binom{t}{n+1}
\right).
$$

Applying the hockey-stick identities,

$$
\sum_{t=n}^{N}\binom{t}{n}
=
\binom{N+1}{n+1},
$$

and

$$
\sum_{t=n}^{N}\binom{t}{n+1}
=
\binom{N+1}{n+2},
$$

gives

$$
E(T^2)
=
\frac{
n^2\binom{N+1}{n+1}
+
n(n+1)\binom{N+1}{n+2}
}
{\binom{N}{n}}.
$$

Using

$$
\binom{N+1}{n+1}
=
\frac{N+1}{n+1}\binom{N}{n},
$$

and

$$
\binom{N+1}{n+2}
=
\frac{(N+1)(N-n)}
{(n+1)(n+2)}
\binom{N}{n},
$$

we obtain

$$
E(T^2)
=
\frac{n^2(N+1)}{n+1}
+
\frac{n(N+1)(N-n)}{n+2}.
$$

Factoring,

$$
E(T^2)
=
n(N+1)
\left(
\frac{n}{n+1}
+
\frac{N-n}{n+2}
\right).
$$

With denominator $(n+1)(n+2)$,

$$
E(T^2)
=
\frac{
n(N+1)\bigl(nN+N+n\bigr)
}
{(n+1)(n+2)}.
$$

Now

$$
\operatorname{Var}(T)
=
E(T^2)-E(T)^2.
$$

Substituting the formulas for $E(T)$ and $E(T^2)$,

$$
\operatorname{Var}(T)
=
\frac{
n(N+1)(nN+N+n)
}
{(n+1)(n+2)}
-
\frac{n^2(N+1)^2}{(n+1)^2}.
$$

Taking a common denominator $(n+1)^2(n+2)$ and simplifying,

$$
\operatorname{Var}(T)
=
\frac{
n(N+1)(N-n)
}
{(n+1)^2(n+2)}.
$$

Therefore the standard deviation is

$$
\boxed{
\sigma_T
=
\sqrt{
\frac{
n(N+1)(N-n)
}
{(n+1)^2(n+2)}
}
}.
$$

This is the standard deviation of the termination point computed in Exercise 5. ∎
