**Exercise 7.** [*M25*] Prove that any given choice of $n$ records from the set of $N$ is obtained by Algorithm S with probability $1/\binom{N}{n}$. Therefore the sample is completely unbiased.

## Solution

Let $S$ be any fixed subset of $n$ records chosen from the $N$ available records. We shall prove that Algorithm S produces exactly the subset $S$ with probability

$$
\frac{1}{\binom{N}{n}}.
$$

Exercise 4 established that

$$
p(m,t)
=
\binom{t}{m}\binom{N-t}{n-m}
\Big/
\binom{N}{n},
\qquad
0\le t\le N,
$$

where $p(m,t)$ is the probability that exactly $m$ of the first $t$ records have been selected.

Let the elements of $S$ occur in positions

$$
1\le s_1<s_2<\cdots<s_n\le N.
$$

For $0\le t\le N$, define

$$
m(t)=|\{\,s_i\le t\,\}|,
$$

the number of members of $S$ among the first $t$ records.

Consider the event

$$
E_t:
\text{ among the first }t\text{ records, the selected records are precisely }
S\cap\{1,\ldots,t\}.
$$

We shall show by induction on $t$ that

$$
\Pr(E_t)
=
\frac{\binom{N-t}{\,n-m(t)\,}}
     {\binom{N}{n}}.
\tag{1}
$$

For $t=0$, no records have been examined and $m(0)=0$. Since $E_0$ is certain,

$$
\Pr(E_0)=1,
$$

while

$$
\frac{\binom{N}{n}}{\binom{N}{n}}=1,
$$

so (1) holds.

Assume (1) holds for $t$. We consider record $t+1$.

First suppose that $t+1\in S$. Then

$$
m(t+1)=m(t)+1.
$$

Given $E_t$, exactly $m(t)$ selections have already been made among the first $t$ records. Algorithm S is in state

$$
(T,N)=(N-t,\;n-m(t)).
$$

By equation (1) of Section 3.4.2, record $t+1$ is selected with probability

$$
\frac{n-m(t)}{N-t}.
$$

Hence

$$
\begin{aligned}
\Pr(E_{t+1})
&=
\Pr(E_t)\frac{n-m(t)}{N-t}
\\[2mm]
&=
\frac{\binom{N-t}{\,n-m(t)\,}}
     {\binom{N}{n}}
\cdot
\frac{n-m(t)}{N-t}.
\end{aligned}
$$

Using

$$
\binom{a}{b}\frac{b}{a}
=
\binom{a-1}{b-1},
$$

with

$$
a=N-t,\qquad b=n-m(t),
$$

we obtain

$$
\Pr(E_{t+1})
=
\frac{\binom{N-t-1}{\,n-m(t)-1\,}}
     {\binom{N}{n}}.
$$

Since

$$
n-m(t)-1=n-m(t+1),
$$

this is exactly

$$
\frac{\binom{N-(t+1)}{\,n-m(t+1)\,}}
     {\binom{N}{n}}.
$$

Thus (1) holds for $t+1$.

Next suppose that $t+1\notin S$. Then

$$
m(t+1)=m(t).
$$

Given $E_t$, Algorithm S must reject record $t+1$. The rejection probability is

$$
1-\frac{n-m(t)}{N-t}
=
\frac{N-t-n+m(t)}{N-t}.
$$

Therefore

$$
\begin{aligned}
\Pr(E_{t+1})
&=
\Pr(E_t)
\frac{N-t-n+m(t)}{N-t}
\\[2mm]
&=
\frac{\binom{N-t}{\,n-m(t)\,}}
     {\binom{N}{n}}
\cdot
\frac{N-t-n+m(t)}{N-t}.
\end{aligned}
$$

Using

$$
\binom{a}{b}\frac{a-b}{a}
=
\binom{a-1}{b},
$$

with

$$
a=N-t,\qquad b=n-m(t),
$$

gives

$$
\Pr(E_{t+1})
=
\frac{\binom{N-t-1}{\,n-m(t)\,}}
     {\binom{N}{n}}.
$$

Since

$$
m(t+1)=m(t),
$$

this again equals

$$
\frac{\binom{N-(t+1)}{\,n-m(t+1)\,}}
     {\binom{N}{n}}.
$$

Thus (1) holds for all $t$.

Setting $t=N$, we have $m(N)=n$ and therefore

$$
\Pr(E_N)
=
\frac{\binom{0}{0}}
     {\binom{N}{n}}
=
\frac{1}{\binom{N}{n}}.
$$

The event $E_N$ is precisely the event that Algorithm S selects the particular subset $S$. Since $S$ was arbitrary, every subset of $n$ records is produced with probability

$$
\boxed{\frac{1}{\binom{N}{n}}}.
$$

Hence all $\binom{N}{n}$ possible samples occur with equal probability, and the sample is completely unbiased.

This completes the proof.

∎
