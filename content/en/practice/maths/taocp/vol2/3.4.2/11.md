**Exercise 11.** &#9654; [*M25*] *[M25]* Let $p_m$ be the probability that exactly $m$ elements are put into the reservoir during the first pass of Algorithm R. Determine the generating function $G(z) = \sum_m p_m z^m$, and find the mean and standard deviation. (Use the ideas of Section 1.2.10.)

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m04s  

---

## Solution

Let $N$ be the total number of records in the input file. The reservoir contains the first $n$ records initially, so the final reservoir size is

$$
m=n+X,
$$

where $X$ is the number of times step R4 is executed while records $n+1,n+2,\ldots,N$ are processed.

When the $(t+1)$st record is considered, with $n\le t\le N-1$, step R4 is executed precisely when the random integer $M$ generated in step R3 satisfies $M\le n$. Since $M$ is uniformly distributed on ${1,\ldots,t+1}$,

$$
\Pr(\text{R4 at time }t+1)=\frac{n}{t+1}.
$$

Define indicator variables

$$
X_{t+1}=
\begin{cases}
1,&\text{if step R4 is executed for record }t+1,\\
0,&\text{otherwise}.
\end{cases}
$$

Then

$$
X=\sum_{t=n}^{N-1}X_{t+1}.
$$

The random choices made at different executions of step R3 are independent; hence the variables $X_{t+1}$ are independent Bernoulli variables with

$$
\Pr(X_{t+1}=1)=\frac{n}{t+1}.
$$

Therefore

$$
m=n+\sum_{t=n}^{N-1}X_{t+1}.
$$

The probability generating function of $X_{t+1}$ is

$$
E(z^{X_{t+1}})
=\left(1-\frac{n}{t+1}\right)
+\frac{n}{t+1}z.
$$

By independence,

$$
G(z)
=E(z^m)
=z^n\prod_{t=n}^{N-1}
\left(1-\frac{n}{t+1}+\frac{n}{t+1}z\right).
$$

Writing $k=t+1$ gives

$$
\boxed{
G(z)
=
z^n
\prod_{k=n+1}^{N}
\left(1+\frac{n(z-1)}{k}\right)
}.
$$

This is the required generating function.

To obtain the mean, differentiate $\log G(z)$:

$$
\log G(z)
=
n\log z
+
\sum_{k=n+1}^{N}
\log\!\left(1+\frac{n(z-1)}{k}\right).
$$

Hence

$$
\frac{G'(1)}{G(1)}
=
n+\sum_{k=n+1}^{N}\frac{n}{k}.
$$

Since $G(1)=1$,

$$
E(m)
=
G'(1)
=
n+n\sum_{k=n+1}^{N}\frac1k.
$$

Using harmonic numbers,

$$
E(m)
=
n\bigl(1+H_N-H_n\bigr).
$$

Next,

$$
\operatorname{Var}(m)
=
\sum_{k=n+1}^{N}
\frac{n}{k}
\left(1-\frac{n}{k}\right),
$$

because $m$ differs from $\sum X_k$ only by the constant $n$.

Therefore

$$
\operatorname{Var}(m)
=
n\sum_{k=n+1}^{N}\frac1k
-
n^2\sum_{k=n+1}^{N}\frac1{k^2}.
$$

Equivalently,

$$
\boxed{
\operatorname{Var}(m)
=
n(H_N-H_n)
-
n^2\sum_{k=n+1}^{N}\frac1{k^2}
}.
$$

The standard deviation is

$$
\boxed{
\sigma
=
\sqrt{
\,n(H_N-H_n)
-
n^2\sum_{k=n+1}^{N}\frac1{k^2}
}
}.
$$

Thus

$$
\boxed{
G(z)
=
z^n
\prod_{k=n+1}^{N}
\left(1+\frac{n(z-1)}{k}\right),
}
$$

$$
\boxed{
E(m)=n(1+H_N-H_n),
}
$$

and

$$
\boxed{
\sigma
=
\sqrt{
\,n(H_N-H_n)
-
n^2\sum_{k=n+1}^{N}\frac1{k^2}
}.
}
$$
