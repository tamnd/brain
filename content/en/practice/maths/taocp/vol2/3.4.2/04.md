**Exercise 4.** [*M23*] Let $p(m, t)$ be the probability that exactly $m$ items are selected from among the first $t$ in the selection sampling technique. Show directly from Algorithm S that

$$p(m,t) = \binom{t}{m}\binom{N-t}{n-m} \Big/ \binom{N}{n}, \qquad \text{for } 0 \le t \le N.$$

## Solution

Let $p(m,t)$ denote the probability that exactly $m$ items are selected from among the first $t$ in Algorithm S, where $0 \le t \le N$. We proceed by induction on $t$ and verify directly that the claimed formula satisfies the recurrence dictated by Algorithm S.

### Step 1: Base case

For $t = 0$, no items have been considered yet. The only possibility is that zero items have been selected:

$$
p(0,0) = 1, \qquad p(m,0) = 0 \text{ for } m > 0.
$$

The proposed formula

$$
p(m,0) = \frac{\binom{0}{m}\binom{N-0}{n-m}}{\binom{N}{n}}
$$

agrees with this because $\binom{0}{0} = 1$ and $\binom{0}{m} = 0$ for $m > 0$. Hence the boundary condition at $t=0$ is satisfied.

### Step 2: Recurrence from Algorithm S

Suppose the first $t$ items have been processed. Let $p(m,t)$ be the probability that exactly $m$ items are selected among them. When the $(t+1)$-st item is considered, it is chosen with probability

$$
\frac{n-m}{N-t}, \quad \text{if exactly } m \text{ items have been selected so far,}
$$

and skipped with probability

$$
1 - \frac{n-m}{N-t} = \frac{N-n-t+m}{N-t}.
$$

Hence, the probability that exactly $m$ items are selected from the first $t+1$ items satisfies

\begin{equation}

p(m,t+1) = \frac{n-(m-1)}{N-t},p(m-1,t) + \frac{N-n-t+m}{N-t},p(m,t),

\label{eq:recurrence}

\end{equation}

with the convention $p(-1,t) = 0$ for all $t$.

### Step 3: Proposed closed form

We claim that

\begin{equation}

p(m,t) = \frac{\binom{t}{m}\binom{N-t}{n-m}}{\binom{N}{n}}, \qquad 0 \le m \le n, \ 0 \le t \le N.

\label{eq:formula}

\end{equation}

We now verify that this formula satisfies the recurrence \eqref{eq:recurrence}.

### Step 4: Verification of the recurrence

Substitute \eqref{eq:formula} into the right-hand side of \eqref{eq:recurrence}:

$$
\frac{n-(m-1)}{N-t}\,p(m-1,t) = \frac{n-m+1}{N-t} \cdot \frac{\binom{t}{m-1}\binom{N-t}{n-m+1}}{\binom{N}{n}},
$$

$$
\frac{N-n-t+m}{N-t}\,p(m,t) = \frac{N-n-t+m}{N-t} \cdot \frac{\binom{t}{m}\binom{N-t}{n-m}}{\binom{N}{n}}.
$$

We need to show that

$$
\frac{n-m+1}{N-t}\binom{t}{m-1}\binom{N-t}{n-m+1} + \frac{N-n-t+m}{N-t}\binom{t}{m}\binom{N-t}{n-m} = \binom{t+1}{m}\binom{N-t-1}{n-m}.
$$

#### Step 4a: Factor denominators

Factor $1/(N-t)$ and work with the binomial coefficients:

$$
\text{LHS} = \frac{1}{N-t} \Big[ (n-m+1)\binom{t}{m-1}\binom{N-t}{n-m+1} + (N-n-t+m)\binom{t}{m}\binom{N-t}{n-m} \Big].
$$

#### Step 4b: Express in factorials

Use $\binom{r}{s} = \frac{r!}{s!(r-s)!}$:

$$
\binom{t}{m-1} = \frac{t!}{(m-1)!(t-m+1)!}, \quad
\binom{N-t}{n-m+1} = \frac{(N-t)!}{(n-m+1)!(N-t-n+m-1)!}.
$$

Then

$$
(n-m+1)\binom{t}{m-1}\binom{N-t}{n-m+1} = (n-m+1)\frac{t!}{(m-1)!(t-m+1)!} \cdot \frac{(N-t)!}{(n-m+1)!(N-t-n+m-1)!}.
$$

Cancel $n-m+1$ with the factorial:

$$
\frac{(n-m+1)}{(n-m+1)!} = \frac{1}{(n-m)!}.
$$

Thus the first term becomes

$$
\frac{t! (N-t)!}{(m-1)! (t-m+1)! (n-m)! (N-t-n+m-1)!}.
$$

Similarly, the second term:

$$
(N-n-t+m)\binom{t}{m}\binom{N-t}{n-m} = (N-n-t+m) \frac{t!}{m!(t-m)!} \cdot \frac{(N-t)!}{(n-m)! (N-t-n+m)!}.
$$

#### Step 4c: Combine fractions

Notice

$$
(t-m+1)(N-t-n+m) = ?
$$

Instead, observe the standard Pascal identity for "hypergeometric" coefficients:

$$
\binom{t+1}{m}\binom{N-t-1}{n-m} = \binom{t}{m-1}\binom{N-t}{n-m+1} + \binom{t}{m}\binom{N-t}{n-m}.
$$

To match coefficients, multiply the first term by $\frac{n-m+1}{N-t}$ and the second by $\frac{N-n-t+m}{N-t}$. These factors arise precisely from the identity

$$
\frac{n-m+1}{N-t}\binom{N-t}{n-m+1} = \binom{N-t-1}{n-m}, \quad
\frac{N-n-t+m}{N-t}\binom{N-t}{n-m} = \binom{N-t-1}{n-m}.
$$

Indeed, check the first:

$$
\frac{n-m+1}{N-t} \binom{N-t}{n-m+1} = \frac{n-m+1}{N-t} \cdot \frac{(N-t)!}{(n-m+1)! (N-t-n+m-1)!} = \frac{(N-t-1)!}{(n-m)! (N-t-n+m-1)!} = \binom{N-t-1}{n-m}.
$$

Similarly, the second term:

$$
\frac{N-n-t+m}{N-t}\binom{N-t}{n-m} = \frac{N-n-t+m}{N-t} \cdot \frac{(N-t)!}{(n-m)! (N-t-n+m)!} = \frac{(N-t-1)!}{(n-m)! (N-t-n+m-1)!} = \binom{N-t-1}{n-m}.
$$

Then factor $\binom{t}{m-1}$ and $\binom{t}{m}$ using Pascal's identity:

$$
\binom{t}{m-1} + \binom{t}{m} = \binom{t+1}{m}.
$$

Hence, the sum equals

$$
\binom{t+1}{m}\binom{N-t-1}{n-m} = p(m,t+1),
$$

as required.

### Step 5: Boundary conditions

The formula \eqref{eq:formula} satisfies the natural boundary conditions:

1. $p(m,t) = 0$ if $m < 0$ or $m > t$ because $\binom{t}{m} = 0$ in these cases.
2. $p(m,t) = 0$ if $m > n$ because $\binom{N-t}{n-m} = 0$ for $n-m < 0$.

The base case $t=0$ was already verified. Therefore, the formula is valid for all $0 \le t \le N$.

### Step 6: Conclusion

By induction, $p(m,t)$ defined by \eqref{eq:formula} satisfies the recurrence derived from Algorithm S and all boundary conditions
