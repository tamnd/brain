**Exercise 5.** [*M24*] What is the average value of $t$ when Algorithm S terminates? (In other words, how many of the $N$ records have been passed, on the average, before the sample is complete?)

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 2m20s  

---

## Solution

Let $T$ denote the value of $t$ when Algorithm S terminates. The algorithm stops precisely when the $n$th selected record is encountered. Therefore $T$ is the position, among the $N$ records, of the last element in the random sample.

Since Algorithm S produces an unbiased sample, every $n$-subset of ${1,\ldots,N}$ is equally likely. Hence $T$ is the maximum element of a uniformly chosen $n$-subset.

For $n \le t \le N$, the event $T=t$ occurs exactly when

1. Record $t$ belongs to the sample.
2. The other $n-1$ sampled records are chosen from ${1,\ldots,t-1}$.

The number of such samples is $\binom{t-1}{n-1}$. Since the total number of samples is $\binom{N}{n}$,

$$
\Pr\{T=t\}
=
\frac{\binom{t-1}{n-1}}{\binom{N}{n}}.
$$

Therefore

$$
E(T)
=
\sum_{t=n}^{N}
t\,\frac{\binom{t-1}{n-1}}{\binom{N}{n}}.
$$

Using

$$
t\binom{t-1}{n-1}
=
n\binom{t}{n},
$$

we obtain

$$
E(T)
=
\frac{n}{\binom{N}{n}}
\sum_{t=n}^{N}
\binom{t}{n}.
$$

The hockey-stick identity gives

$$
\sum_{t=n}^{N}\binom{t}{n}
=
\binom{N+1}{n+1}.
$$

Hence

$$
E(T)
=
\frac{n\binom{N+1}{n+1}}{\binom{N}{n}}.
$$

Since

$$
\binom{N+1}{n+1}
=
\frac{N+1}{n+1}\binom{N}{n},
$$

it follows that

$$
E(T)
=
\frac{n(N+1)}{n+1}.
$$

Thus the average number of records that have been passed before the sample is complete is

$$
\boxed{\frac{n(N+1)}{n+1}}.
$$

∎
