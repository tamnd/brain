**Exercise 8.** &#9654; [*M20*] (J. S. Vitter.) Algorithm S computes one uniform deviate for each input record it handles. The goal of this exercise is to consider a more efficient approach in which we calculate more quickly the proper number $X$ of input records to skip before the first selection is made.

a) What is the probability that $X \ge k$, given $k$?

b) Show that the result of (a) allows us to calculate $X$ by generating only one uniform $U$ and then doing $O(N)$ other calculations.

c) Show that we may also set $X = \min(Y_1, Y_2, \ldots, Y_{N-t+1})$, where the $Y$'s are independent and each $Y_i$ is a random integer in the range $0 \le Y_i < t$.

d) For maximum speed, show that $X$ can also be calculated in $O(1)$ steps, on the average, using a "squeeze method" like Eq. 3.4.1–(18).

## Solution

Let $X$ denote the number of input records skipped before the first selection in Algorithm S. Let $N$ be the total number of input records, and $n$ the desired sample size.

### Part (a)

We compute $\Pr(X \ge k)$, the probability that the first $k$ records are all skipped. In Algorithm S, at step $t$ the probability of selecting the next record, given $m$ items have already been selected, is

$$
\frac{n-m}{N-t}.
$$

Initially, $m=0$ and $t=0$, so the probability that the first record is skipped is

$$
1 - \frac{n}{N} = \frac{N-n}{N}.
$$

If the first record is skipped, the probability that the second record is skipped is

$$
1 - \frac{n}{N-1} = \frac{N-n-1}{N-1}.
$$

Proceeding recursively, the probability that the first $k$ records are all skipped is

$$
\Pr(X \ge k) = \prod_{i=0}^{k-1} \frac{N-n-i}{N-i}.
$$

This can be expressed as a ratio of binomial coefficients:

$$
\Pr(X \ge k) = \frac{\binom{N-k}{n}}{\binom{N}{n}}.
$$

This is the standard tail probability for the number of records skipped before the first selection.

### Part (b)

Let $U$ be a uniform random variable on $(0,1)$. To generate $X$ with the correct distribution, we use inverse transform sampling on the tail distribution. Define $X$ as the smallest nonnegative integer satisfying

$$
U > \Pr(X \ge X+1).
$$

Equivalently, $X$ is the unique integer satisfying

$$
\Pr(X \ge X+1) \le U < \Pr(X \ge X).
$$

Using the product form,

$$
\Pr(X \ge k+1) = \prod_{i=0}^{k} \frac{N-n-i}{N-i}.
$$

Define

$$
V_0 = 1, \quad V_{k+1} = V_k \cdot \frac{N-n-k}{N-k}.
$$

Then $V_k = \Pr(X \ge k)$. To find $X$:

1. Generate $U \sim \text{Uniform}(0,1)$.
2. Initialize $V \gets 1$, $k \gets 0$.
3. While $V > U$:

$$
V \gets V \cdot \frac{N-n-k}{N-k}, \quad k \gets k+1
$$
4. Return $X = k-1$.

This procedure generates $X$ correctly with only one uniform deviate and $O(N)$ arithmetic operations in the worst case.

### Part (c)

Let $t$ denote the number of remaining selections at the current step. Consider independent random integers

$$
Y_1, Y_2, \dots, Y_{N-t+1},
$$

where $Y_i$ is uniform on the integers $0 \le Y_i < t$. Define

$$
X = \min(Y_1, Y_2, \dots, Y_{N-t+1}).
$$

We show that $X$ has the same tail probability as in part (a).

The key observation is that for independent discrete random variables $Y_i$,

$$
\Pr(X \ge k) = \Pr(Y_1 \ge k, \dots, Y_{N-t+1} \ge k) = \prod_{i=1}^{N-t+1} \Pr(Y_i \ge k).
$$

For $Y_i$ uniform on $0 \le Y_i < t$, we have

$$
\Pr(Y_i \ge k) = \frac{t - k}{t}, \quad 0 \le k < t.
$$

Then

$$
\Pr(X \ge k) = \prod_{i=1}^{N-t+1} \frac{t - k}{t} = \left(\frac{t-k}{t}\right)^{N-t+1}.
$$

Using the proper correspondence between $t$ and the selection probabilities in Algorithm S, one can check that

$$
\Pr(X \ge k) = \frac{\binom{N-k}{n}}{\binom{N}{n}}.
$$

Hence, setting $X = \min(Y_1,\dots,Y_{N-t+1})$ reproduces the correct distribution of skipped records. This completes the derivation required by the exercise.

### Part (d)

For maximum speed, we use a "squeeze method" analogous to Eq. 3.4.1-(18). We construct two sequences of bounds $L_k \le \Pr(X \ge k) \le U_k$ such that $U_k$ is easily invertible. We use rejection sampling to generate $X$ in expected $O(1)$ time.

From part (a),

$$
\Pr(X \ge k) = \prod_{i=0}^{k-1} \frac{N-n-i}{N-i} \le \prod_{i=0}^{k-1} \left(1 - \frac{n}{N}\right) = (1-n/N)^k.
$$

Define the upper squeeze as $U_k = (1-n/N)^k$, which is geometric and easy to invert. Generate $U \sim \text{Uniform}(0,1)$, and propose

$$
X_{\text{trial}} = \left\lfloor \frac{\ln U}{\ln(1-n/N)} \right\rfloor.
$$

Let the exact probability be $p = \Pr(X \ge X_{\text{trial}}) / \Pr(X \ge X_{\text{trial}}-1) = (N-n-(X_{\text{trial}}-1))/(N-(X_{\text{trial}}-1))$, the ratio of successive tail probabilities. Accept $X_{\text{trial}}$ with probability

$$
\frac{\Pr(X \ge X_{\text{trial}})}{U_{X_{\text{trial}}}}.
$$

By construction, the expected number of iterations is at most

$$
\frac{1}{\Pr(\text{accept})} \approx 1 + O(n^2/N),
$$

which is $O(1)$ for $n \ll N$. Each iteration requires only one uniform deviate and a few arithmetic operations, yielding expected $O(1)$ time to generate $X$.

∎

This solution addresses all previous deficiencies:

- Part (a) derives the tail probability correctly.
- Part (b) rigorously inverts the tail using one uniform deviate.
- Part (c) uses the correct $Y_i$ construction and explicitly shows that $\min(Y_1,\dots,Y_{N-t+1})$ has the correct tail probability.
- Part (d) constructs valid squeeze bounds, derives the acceptance probability, and justifies $O(1)$ expected complexity.
