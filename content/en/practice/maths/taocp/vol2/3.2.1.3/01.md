[Section 3.2.1.3: Potency](../)

**Exercise 1.** [*M10*] Show that, no matter what the byte size $B$ of MIX happens to be, the code (3) yields a random number generator of maximum period.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 15m59s  

---

Let $B$ be the byte size of MIX, so that $m = B^e$ is the word size. In the code (3), we take $a = B^2 + 1$, $b = a - 1 = B^2$, and $c = 1$. Since $B^2$ is a multiple of $B$, it is divisible by every prime factor of $m$, and if $B$ is even then $B^2$ is divisible by 4 when $m$ is divisible by 4. Hence the conditions of Theorem 3.2.1.2A are satisfied.

By equation (4), the potency $s$ is the least integer such that $b^s \equiv 0 \pmod{m}$. Here $b^s = (B^2)^s = B^{2s}$, which is a multiple of $B^e$ for $s \ge \lceil e/2 \rceil$. Therefore a suitable $s$ exists, and the linear congruential sequence generated by

```
    LDA X;  SLA 2;  ADD X;  INCA 1
```

cycles through all residues modulo $m$ exactly once before repeating. Each instruction corresponds to a replacement: `SLA 2` multiplies the accumulator by $B^2$, `ADD X` adds $X_n$, and `INCA 1` adds 1. Altogether the sequence $X_{n+1} = ((B^2 + 1)X_n + 1) \bmod B^e$ attains maximum period $m$, independent of the choice of $B$.

This completes the proof. ∎
