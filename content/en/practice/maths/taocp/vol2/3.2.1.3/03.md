**Exercise 3.** [*11*] When $m = 2^{35}$, what is the potency of the linear congruential sequence with $a = 3141592621$? What is the potency if the multiplier is $a = 2^{23} + 2^{13} + 2^7 + 1$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 11m13s  

---

We consider the multiplicative linear congruential sequence modulo $m = 2^{35}$, so we study the multiplicative order of $a$ in the unit group $(\mathbb{Z}/2^{35}\mathbb{Z})^\times$. The “potency” is this order.

For $2^e$ with $e \ge 3$, every odd residue $a$ has an order that is a power of $2$. Write

$$
a = 1 + 2^k b, \quad b \text{ odd},
$$

so $k = v_2(a-1)$. Then:

- If $k = 1$ (i.e. $a \equiv 3 \pmod{4}$), the order is $2^{e-2}$.
- If $k \ge 2$ (i.e. $a \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$), the order is $2^{e-k}$.

Here $e = 35$.

### 1. $a = 3141592621$

Compute $v_2(a-1)$:

$$
a - 1 = 3141592620.
$$

Divide by 2:

$$
3141592620 = 2 \cdot 1570796310,
$$

$$
1570796310 = 2 \cdot 785398155,
$$

and $785398155$ is odd. Hence

$$
v_2(a-1) = 2.
$$

So $k = 2$, and we use the case $k \ge 2$:

$$
\text{order} = 2^{35 - 2} = 2^{33}.
$$

### 2. $a = 2^{23} + 2^{13} + 2^7 + 1$

First compute $a - 1$:

$$
a - 1 = 2^{23} + 2^{13} + 2^7.
$$

Factor out $2^7$:

$$
a - 1 = 2^7(2^{16} + 2^6 + 1).
$$

Now compute the parity of the remaining factor:

$$
2^{16} + 2^6 + 1 = 65536 + 64 + 1 = 65601,
$$

which is odd. Therefore,

$$
v_2(a-1) = 7.
$$

So $k = 7 \ge 2$, and again

$$
\text{order} = 2^{35 - 7} = 2^{28}.
$$

### Final answers

$$
\boxed{2^{33}} \quad \text{for } a = 3141592621,
\qquad
\boxed{2^{28}} \quad \text{for } a = 2^{23} + 2^{13} + 2^7 + 1.
$$
