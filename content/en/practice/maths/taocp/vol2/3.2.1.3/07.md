**Exercise 7.** [*M20*] [M20] When $a$ satisfies the conditions of Theorem 3.2.1.2A, it is relatively prime to $m$; hence there is a number $a'$ such that $aa' \equiv 1 \pmod{m}$. Show that $a'$ can be expressed simply in terms of $b$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 17m26s  

---

Since \(a\) satisfies the conditions of Theorem 3.2.1.2A, we have \(\gcd(a,m)=1\). Hence the multiplicative inverse \(a'\) exists and satisfies
\[
aa' \equiv 1 \pmod m.
\]

From Theorem 3.2.1.2A, there is also a number \(b\) such that
\[
ab \equiv 1 \pmod m.
\]

We now compare the two congruences:
\[
aa' \equiv 1 \pmod m, \qquad ab \equiv 1 \pmod m.
\]

Subtracting gives
\[
a(a' - b) \equiv 0 \pmod m.
\]

This means \(m \mid a(a' - b)\). Since \(\gcd(a,m)=1\), it follows that \(m \mid (a' - b)\). Therefore,
\[
a' \equiv b \pmod m.
\]

So \(a'\) can be expressed in terms of \(b\) as
\[
a' = b + km \quad \text{for some integer } k,
\]
or equivalently,
\[
a' \equiv b \pmod m.
\]
