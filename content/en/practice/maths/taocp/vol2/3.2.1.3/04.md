[Section 3.2.1.3: Potency](../)

**Exercise 4.** [*15*] Show that if $m = 2^e \ge 8$, maximum potency is achieved when $a \bmod 8 = 5$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 12m07s  

---

## Solution

Let $m = 2^e$ with $e \ge 3$. Write $a = 1 + b$, so $b = a - 1$.

For maximum period in the mixed linear congruential case with modulus $2^e$, Theorem 3.2.1.2A requires that $b$ be a multiple of $4$, hence

$$
4 \mid b.
$$

Write $v_2(b)$ for the exponent of $2$ in $b$, so $b = 2^{v_2(b)} u$ with $u$ odd and $v_2(b) \ge 2$.

The potency $s$ is defined as the least integer such that

$$
b^s \equiv 0 \pmod{2^e},
$$

which is equivalent to

$$
2^e \mid b^s.
$$

Since $b^s = 2^{s v_2(b)} u^s$ and $u^s$ is odd, this condition becomes

$$
s v_2(b) \ge e.
$$

Thus the potency is

$$
s = \left\lceil \frac{e}{v_2(b)} \right\rceil.
$$

For fixed $e$, the value of $s$ is maximized when $v_2(b)$ is minimized subject to the maximum-period constraint $v_2(b) \ge 2$. Hence the smallest possible value is

$$
v_2(b) = 2.
$$

This gives

$$
b \equiv 4 \pmod{8},
$$

and therefore

$$
a = 1 + b \equiv 5 \pmod{8}.
$$

With $v_2(b) = 2$, the potency is

$$
s = \left\lceil \frac{e}{2} \right\rceil.
$$

If $v_2(b) \ge 3$, then $v_2(b) \ge 3$ implies

$$
s = \left\lceil \frac{e}{v_2(b)} \right\rceil \le \left\lceil \frac{e}{3} \right\rceil \le \left\lceil \frac{e}{2} \right\rceil,
$$

with strict inequality for all sufficiently large $e$, so the potency cannot exceed the value obtained when $v_2(b) = 2$.

Therefore the maximum possible potency occurs exactly when $v_2(b) = 2$, that is when $a \equiv 5 \pmod{8}$.

This completes the proof. ∎
