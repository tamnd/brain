**Exercise 2.** [*10*] What is the potency of the generator represented by the MIX code (3)?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 7m27s  

---

In (3) the multiplier is $a=B^2+1$, hence $b=a-1=B^2$. For MIX, the modulus is $m=B^5$, since a word contains five bytes.

By definition, the potency is the least integer $s$ such that

$b^s\equiv0\pmod m.$

Since $b=B^2$ and $m=B^5$,

$b^s=(B^2)^s=B^{2s}.$

Therefore $b^s$ is a multiple of $m=B^5$ exactly when $2s\ge5$. The least such integer is $s=3$. Indeed,

$b^2=B^4\not\equiv0\pmod{B^5},\qquad b^3=B^6\equiv0\pmod{B^5}.$

Thus the generator represented by (3) has potency

$\boxed{3}.$
