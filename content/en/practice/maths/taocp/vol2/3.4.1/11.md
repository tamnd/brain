**Exercise 11.** &#9654; [*HM29*] Prove that steps M7–M8 of Algorithm M generate a random variable with the appropriate tail of the normal distribution; in other words, the probability that $X \le x$ should be exactly

$$\int_x^{\infty} e^{-t^2/2} \, dt \bigg/ \int_x^{\infty} e^{-t^2/2} \, dt, \qquad x \ge 3.$$

[*Hint:* Show that it is a special case of the rejection method, with $g(t) = Cte^{-t^2/2}$ for some $C$.]

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 5m13s  

---

The statement of the exercise contains an obvious typographical error, since

$$
\int_x^\infty e^{-t^2/2}\,dt
\bigg/
\int_x^\infty e^{-t^2/2}\,dt = 1.
$$

What is meant is that the variable generated by steps M7–M8 should have the normal-tail distribution on $[3,\infty)$, namely

$$
\Pr\{X\le x\}
=
\frac{\displaystyle\int_3^x e^{-t^2/2}\,dt}
{\displaystyle\int_3^\infty e^{-t^2/2}\,dt},
\qquad x\ge3.
$$

We shall prove this directly from the operations performed in M7–M8.

### The proposal distribution generated in M7

Step M7 generates a uniform random variable $U\in(0,1)$ and sets

$$
T=\sqrt{9-2\ln U}.
$$

Therefore

$$
U=e^{(9-T^2)/2}.
$$

Since $U$ is uniform on $(0,1)$,

$$
\Pr\{T\le t\}
=
\Pr\!\left(U\ge e^{(9-t^2)/2}\right),
\qquad t\ge3.
$$

Hence

$$
\Pr\{T\le t\}
=
1-e^{(9-t^2)/2}
=
1-e^{9/2}e^{-t^2/2},
\qquad t\ge3.
$$

Differentiating, we obtain the density of $T$:

$$
g(t)
=
e^{9/2}t\,e^{-t^2/2},
\qquad t\ge3.
$$

This is indeed a probability density, because

$$
\int_3^\infty e^{9/2}t e^{-t^2/2}\,dt
=
e^{9/2}
\left[-e^{-t^2/2}\right]_{3}^{\infty}
=
1.
$$

Thus step M7 generates a proposal variable having density

$$
g(t)=e^{9/2}t e^{-t^2/2}.
$$

### The acceptance probability in M8

In step M8 another independent uniform random variable $V\in(0,1)$ is generated, and the candidate $T$ is accepted precisely when

$$
V\le \frac3T.
$$

Therefore the conditional acceptance probability is

$$
A(t)=\Pr\!\left(V\le\frac3t\right)=\frac3t,
\qquad t\ge3.
$$

Since $t\ge3$, this is a valid probability.

Thus M7–M8 constitute a rejection procedure with proposal density $g$ and acceptance function $A(t)=3/t$.

### Identification with the rejection method

Let

$$
f(t)=e^{-t^2/2},
\qquad t\ge3.
$$

Then

$$
g(t)=e^{9/2}t\,f(t),
$$

so

$$
\frac{f(t)}{g(t)}
=
\frac1{e^{9/2}t}.
$$

Because $t\ge3$,

$$
\frac{f(t)}{g(t)}
\le
\frac1{3e^{9/2}},
$$

with equality at $t=3$. Hence

$$
M=\frac1{3e^{9/2}}
$$

is the rejection constant.

The corresponding acceptance probability is

$$
\frac{f(t)}{M g(t)}
=
\frac{1/(e^{9/2}t)}
     {1/(3e^{9/2})}
=
\frac3t,
$$

which is exactly the acceptance rule of M8.

Therefore M7–M8 are precisely a rejection sampler whose target density is proportional to $f(t)=e^{-t^2/2}$ on $[3,\infty)$.

### Distribution of the accepted value

By the standard rejection-method theorem, the density of accepted values is proportional to

$$
g(t)A(t)
=
e^{9/2}t e^{-t^2/2}\cdot\frac3t
=
3e^{9/2}e^{-t^2/2}.
$$

The factor $3e^{9/2}$ is constant, so after normalization the accepted density is

$$
p(t)
=
\frac{e^{-t^2/2}}
{\displaystyle\int_3^\infty e^{-u^2/2}\,du},
\qquad t\ge3.
$$

Consequently,

$$
\Pr\{X\le x\}
=
\int_3^x p(t)\,dt
=
\frac{\displaystyle\int_3^x e^{-t^2/2}\,dt}
{\displaystyle\int_3^\infty e^{-t^2/2}\,dt},
\qquad x\ge3.
$$

This is exactly the normal distribution conditioned on $X\ge3$. Hence steps M7–M8 generate the correct tail of the normal distribution. ∎
