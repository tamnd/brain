**Exercise 14.** [*M21*] If $X$ is a random variable with the continuous distribution $F(x)$, and if $c$ is a (possibly negative) constant, what is the distribution of $cX$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m36s  

---

## Solution

Let $X$ have continuous distribution function $F(x)=\Pr{X\le x}$ and define $Y=cX$, where $c$ is a constant.

The distribution function of $Y$ is

$G(y)=\Pr\{Y\le y\}=\Pr\{cX\le y\}.$

Three cases determine how the inequality transforms.

If $c>0$, division by $c$ preserves order, so $cX\le y$ is equivalent to $X\le y/c$. Hence

$G(y)=\Pr\{X\le y/c\}=F(y/c).$

If $c<0$, division by $c$ reverses order, so $cX\le y$ is equivalent to $X\ge y/c$. Since $X$ has a continuous distribution, $\Pr{X=y/c}=0$, so

$G(y)=\Pr\{X\ge y/c\}=1-\Pr\{X< y/c\}=1-F(y/c).$

If $c=0$, then $Y=0$ with probability $1$, so the distribution function satisfies

0, & y<0,\\ 1, & y\ge 0. \end{cases}$$
Combining the cases gives the distribution of $cX$.
$$\boxed{ G(y)= \begin{cases} F(y/c), & c>0,\\[4pt] 1-F(y/c), & c<0,\\[4pt] \mathbf{1}_{\{y\ge 0\}}, & c=0. \end{cases} }$$ This completes the solution. ∎
