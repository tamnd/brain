[Section 3.4.1: Numerical Distributions](../)

**Exercise 4.** [*M20*] Prove the two relations in (8).

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m  

---

## Solution

Let

$$
Y=\max(X_1,X_2),
$$

where $X_1$ and $X_2$ are independent random variables having distribution functions

$$
F_1(x)=\Pr\{X_1\le x\},\qquad
F_2(x)=\Pr\{X_2\le x\}.
$$

The event $\{Y\le x\}$ occurs precisely when both inequalities $X_1\le x$ and $X_2\le x$ hold simultaneously. Hence

$$
\Pr\{Y\le x\}
=
\Pr\{X_1\le x,\;X_2\le x\}.
$$

Since $X_1$ and $X_2$ are independent, the probability of the intersection equals the product of the probabilities:

$$
\Pr\{X_1\le x,\;X_2\le x\}
=
\Pr\{X_1\le x\}\Pr\{X_2\le x\}.
$$

Therefore

$$
\Pr\{\max(X_1,X_2)\le x\}
=
F_1(x)F_2(x),
$$

which establishes the first relation in (8).

Next let

$$
Z=\min(X_1,X_2).
$$

The event $\{Z\le x\}$ is the union of the events $\{X_1\le x\}$ and $\{X_2\le x\}$, because the smaller of the two variables does not exceed $x$ whenever at least one of them does. Thus

$$
\Pr\{Z\le x\}
=
\Pr\{X_1\le x\ \text{or}\ X_2\le x\}.
$$

By the addition law for probabilities,

$$
\Pr(A\cup B)
=
\Pr(A)+\Pr(B)-\Pr(A\cap B),
$$

with

$$
A=\{X_1\le x\},\qquad
B=\{X_2\le x\},
$$

we obtain

$$
\Pr\{Z\le x\}
=
\Pr\{X_1\le x\}
+
\Pr\{X_2\le x\}
-
\Pr\{X_1\le x,\;X_2\le x\}.
$$

Independence gives

$$
\Pr\{X_1\le x,\;X_2\le x\}
=
F_1(x)F_2(x),
$$

hence

$$
\Pr\{\min(X_1,X_2)\le x\}
=
F_1(x)+F_2(x)-F_1(x)F_2(x).
$$

Consequently,

$$
\boxed{
\Pr\{\max(X_1,X_2)\le x\}=F_1(x)F_2(x),
\qquad
\Pr\{\min(X_1,X_2)\le x\}
=
F_1(x)+F_2(x)-F_1(x)F_2(x)
}.
$$

This completes the proof.

∎
