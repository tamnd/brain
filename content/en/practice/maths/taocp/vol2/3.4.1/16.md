## Solution

Let $a$ be a given constant with $0 < a \le 1$. We are asked to generate a random variable $T$ with the gamma distribution of order $a$, whose density is

$f(t) = \frac{1}{\Gamma(a)} t^{a-1} e^{-t}, \quad t > 0.$

We employ the rejection method, splitting the domain into two regions: $0 < t < 1$ and $t \ge 1$. Define the comparison functions

\begin{cases} t^{a-1}, & 0 < t < 1,\\[2mm] e^{-t}, & t \ge 1, \end{cases}$$
with scaling constant $c = 1/\Gamma(a)$, so that $cg(t)$ bounds $f(t)$ from above in each region. --- **Step 1: Compute acceptance probabilities in each region.** Let
$$p_1 = \int_0^1 t^{a-1}\, dt = \frac{1}{a}, \qquad p_2 = \int_1^\infty e^{-t}\, dt = e^{-1}.$$
Normalize to obtain the probabilities of choosing a candidate from each region:
$$\pi_1 = \frac{p_1}{p_1 + p_2} = \frac{1/a}{1/a + e^{-1}} = \frac{1}{1 + a e^{-1}}, \qquad \pi_2 = 1 - \pi_1 = \frac{a e^{-1}}{1 + a e^{-1}}.$$
Thus we select region 1 with probability $\pi_1$ and region 2 with probability $\pi_2$. --- **Step 2: Generate candidate.** 1. Generate a uniform deviate $U \in [0,1]$. 2. If $U < \pi_1$, choose region 1: generate $V \in [0,1]$ uniformly and set $T \leftarrow V^{1/a}$. The density of $T$ in region 1 is
$$g_1(T) = a T^{a-1},$$
since $V$ is uniform and $T = V^{1/a} \implies dT/dV = 1/(a T^{a-1})$. 3. If $U \ge \pi_1$, choose region 2: generate $V \in [0,1]$ uniformly and set $T \leftarrow 1 - \ln V$. Then $T$ has density
$$g_2(T) = e^{-(T-1)} = e e^{-T}, \quad T \ge 1,$$
which is proportional to $e^{-T}$ as required. --- **Step 3: Apply rejection criterion.** For $T$ generated in region 1, generate independent uniform $W \in [0,1]$ and accept $T$ if
$$W \le \frac{f(T)}{c g_1(T)} = \frac{T^{a-1} e^{-T}}{\Gamma(a) \cdot T^{a-1}/\Gamma(a)} = e^{-T}.$$
