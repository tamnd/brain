**Exercise 20.** [*M20*] Let $A$ be the area of the shaded region in Fig. 13, and let $R$ be the area of the enclosing rectangle. Let $I$ be the area of the interior region recognized by step R2, and let $E$ be the area of the intermediate region lying in the step R3 and the outer rectangle. Determine the number of times each step of Algorithm R is performed, for each of its four variants as in (25), in terms of $A$, $R$, $I$, and $E$.

**Solve time:** 1m40s

## Solution

Algorithm R is a rejection method based on a rectangle of area $R$ containing the desired region of area $A$. Let a randomly chosen point in the rectangle be tested first by step R2, which recognizes an interior region of area $I$, then by step R3, which recognizes an intermediate region of area $E$. The remainder of the rectangle has area

$$
R-I-E.
$$

A trial is successful whenever the selected point lies in the shaded region. Since the shaded region has area $A$, the probability of success on a given trial is

$$
\frac{A}{R}.
$$

Therefore the expected number of trials required to obtain one accepted point is

$$
\frac{R}{A}.
$$

Each trial executes step R1 exactly once. Hence the expected number of executions of R1 is

$$
\boxed{\frac{R}{A}}.
$$

Step R2 is reached on every trial, so it is also executed

$$
\boxed{\frac{R}{A}}
$$

times on the average.

The four variants in (25) differ only in the disposition of points that fall into the regions distinguished by R2 and R3. The required counts are obtained by computing the probability that a trial reaches each step.

The probability that a point lies in the interior region recognized by R2 is

$$
\frac{I}{R}.
$$

The probability that a point lies in the intermediate region handled by R3 is

$$
\frac{E}{R}.
$$

The probability that a point lies in the remaining outer region is

$$
\frac{R-I-E}{R}.
$$

Since a successful output requires $R/A$ trials on the average, any step reached with probability $p$ per trial is executed

$$
\frac{R}{A}p
$$

times on the average.

For the four variants of Algorithm R, the resulting counts are as follows.

### Variant 1

Step R2 accepts every point in the interior region. Step R3 is applied only to points in the intermediate region.

Thus

$$
N(R1)=N(R2)=\frac{R}{A},
$$

and

$$
N(R3)=\frac{R}{A}\frac{E}{R}
      =\frac{E}{A}.
$$

Hence

$$
\boxed{
N(R1)=N(R2)=\frac{R}{A},\qquad
N(R3)=\frac{E}{A}.
}
$$

### Variant 2

Step R2 rejects the outer region immediately; step R3 is reached whenever the point lies in either the interior or intermediate region.

The probability of reaching R3 is

$$
\frac{I+E}{R}.
$$

Therefore

$$
N(R1)=N(R2)=\frac{R}{A},
$$

and

$$
N(R3)=\frac{R}{A}\frac{I+E}{R}
      =\frac{I+E}{A}.
$$

Hence

$$
\boxed{
N(R1)=N(R2)=\frac{R}{A},\qquad
N(R3)=\frac{I+E}{A}.
}
$$

### Variant 3

Step R2 accepts the interior region and rejects the outer region; only points in the intermediate region proceed to R3.

Thus the probability of reaching R3 is again

$$
\frac{E}{R},
$$

so

$$
N(R1)=N(R2)=\frac{R}{A},
$$

and

$$
N(R3)=\frac{E}{A}.
$$

Hence

$$
\boxed{
N(R1)=N(R2)=\frac{R}{A},\qquad
N(R3)=\frac{E}{A}.
}
$$

### Variant 4

Neither acceptance nor rejection occurs until after R3. Every point that survives R2 reaches R3; this happens precisely when the point lies in the union of the interior and intermediate regions.

Therefore

$$
N(R1)=N(R2)=\frac{R}{A},
$$

and

$$
N(R3)=\frac{I+E}{A}.
$$

Hence

$$
\boxed{
N(R1)=N(R2)=\frac{R}{A},\qquad
N(R3)=\frac{I+E}{A}.
}
$$

Collecting the results:

$$
\begin{array}{c|ccc}
\text{Variant} & N(R1) & N(R2) & N(R3)\\
\hline
1 & \dfrac{R}{A} & \dfrac{R}{A} & \dfrac{E}{A}\\[1.2ex]
2 & \dfrac{R}{A} & \dfrac{R}{A} & \dfrac{I+E}{A}\\[1.2ex]
3 & \dfrac{R}{A} & \dfrac{R}{A} & \dfrac{E}{A}\\[1.2ex]
4 & \dfrac{R}{A} & \dfrac{R}{A} & \dfrac{I+E}{A}
\end{array}
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
