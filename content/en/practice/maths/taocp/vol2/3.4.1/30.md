**Exercise 30.** [*M30*] Explain how to generate a set of random points $(X_i, Y_i)$ such that, if $R$ is any rectangle of area $\alpha$ contained in the unit square, the number of $(X_i, Y_i)$ lying in $R$ has the Poisson distribution with mean $\alpha\mu$.

## Solution

Let $\mu > 0$ be a given constant. We wish to generate a random set of points $(X_i, Y_i)$ in the unit square $[0,1]^2$ such that for any rectangle $R \subseteq [0,1]^2$ of area $\alpha$, the number of points lying in $R$ has a Poisson distribution with mean $\alpha \mu$.

We proceed in two steps.

### Step 1: Generate a Poisson number of points

Let $N$ be a Poisson random variable with mean $\mu$:

$$
\Pr(N = n) = \frac{\mu^n e^{-\mu}}{n!}, \qquad n = 0,1,2,\dots
$$

The total number of points in the square will be $N$. Conditional on $N$, we will distribute the points uniformly.

### Step 2: Distribute points uniformly in the unit square

Given $N = n$, let $(X_1, Y_1), \dots, (X_n, Y_n)$ be independent random points uniformly distributed in $[0,1]^2$. That is, each $X_i$ and $Y_i$ is independently uniform on $[0,1]$.

### Step 3: Verify the Poisson property for rectangles

Let $R \subseteq [0,1]^2$ be any rectangle of area $\alpha$. Conditional on $N = n$, the number of points in $R$ follows a binomial distribution:

\Pr\big(\text{# points in } R = k \,\big|\, N = n\big) = \binom{n}{k} \alpha^k (1-\alpha)^{\,n-k}, \qquad k = 0,1,\dots,n,

since each point independently falls in $R$ with probability $\alpha$.

Unconditionally, the number of points in $R$ is a mixture of binomial distributions with $n \sim \mathrm{Poisson}(\mu)$. By the standard Poisson thinning lemma, this mixture is itself Poisson with mean $\alpha \mu$:

\Pr(\text{# points in } R = k) = \sum_{n=k}^{\infty} \Pr(N = n) \binom{n}{k} \alpha^k (1-\alpha)^{n-k} = \frac{(\alpha \mu)^k e^{-\alpha \mu}}{k!}.

This verifies that for any rectangle $R$ of area $\alpha$, the number of points in $R$ is Poisson distributed with mean $\alpha \mu$.

### Step 4: Summary of the construction

1. Generate $N \sim \mathrm{Poisson}(\mu)$.
2. Conditional on $N$, sample $N$ independent points $(X_i, Y_i)$ uniformly in $[0,1]^2$.
3. The resulting set ${(X_i, Y_i)}_{i=1}^N$ satisfies the required property: for any rectangle $R \subseteq [0,1]^2$ of area $\alpha$, the number of points in $R$ is $\mathrm{Poisson}(\alpha \mu)$.

This construction defines a homogeneous Poisson point process of intensity $\mu$ on the unit square. ∎
