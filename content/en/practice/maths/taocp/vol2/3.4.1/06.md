**Exercise 6.** [*HM21*] A quantity $X$ is computed by the following method:

**Step 1.** Generate two independent uniform deviates $U$ and $V$.

**Step 2.** If $U^2 + V^2 \ge 1$, return to step 1; otherwise set $X \leftarrow U$.

What is the distribution function of $X$? How many times will step 1 be performed? (Give the mean and standard deviation.)

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m43s  

---

Let $N$ denote the number of times step 1 is performed until the procedure terminates. Each trial generates a point $(U,V)$ uniformly in the unit square $[0,1]\times[0,1]$, and the trial is accepted precisely when

$$
U^2+V^2<1.
$$

Hence the accepted points are uniformly distributed over the quarter of the unit disk in the first quadrant.

To determine the distribution function of $X$, observe that after acceptance we set $X=U$. Therefore

$$
F_X(x)=P(X\le x).
$$

Since $0\le X\le1$, we have

$$
F_X(x)=0,\qquad x<0,
$$

and

$$
F_X(x)=1,\qquad x\ge1.
$$

For $0\le x\le1$, the event $X\le x$ corresponds to the subset of the quarter disk lying to the left of the line $u=x$. Because the distribution on the quarter disk is uniform,

$$
F_X(x)
=
\frac{\text{area}\{(u,v):0\le u\le x,\ 0\le v\le \sqrt{1-u^2}\}}
{\text{area of quarter disk}}.
$$

The denominator is

$$
\frac{\pi}{4},
$$

and the numerator is

$$
\int_0^x\sqrt{1-u^2}\,du.
$$

Therefore

$$
F_X(x)
=
\frac{4}{\pi}
\int_0^x\sqrt{1-u^2}\,du,
\qquad 0\le x\le1.
$$

Using

$$
\int\sqrt{1-u^2}\,du
=
\frac12\left(u\sqrt{1-u^2}+\arcsin u\right),
$$

we obtain

$$
F_X(x)
=
\frac{2}{\pi}
\left(
x\sqrt{1-x^2}
+
\arcsin x
\right),
\qquad 0\le x\le1.
$$

Hence

$$
F_X(x)
=
\begin{cases}
0,&x<0,\\[2mm]
\dfrac{2}{\pi}
\left(
x\sqrt{1-x^2}
+\arcsin x
\right),
&0\le x\le1,\\[4mm]
1,&x\ge1.
\end{cases}
$$

Differentiating gives the density:

$$
f_X(x)
=
\frac{4}{\pi}\sqrt{1-x^2},
\qquad 0\le x\le1.
$$

Next consider the number $N$ of performances of step 1. A trial succeeds whenever the generated point lies inside the quarter disk. Thus the success probability is

$$
p=\frac{\pi}{4}.
$$

Since successive trials are independent, $N$ has the geometric distribution

$$
P(N=n)
=
\left(1-\frac{\pi}{4}\right)^{n-1}\frac{\pi}{4},
\qquad n\ge1.
$$

For a geometric random variable with parameter $p$,

$$
E(N)=\frac1p,
$$

and

$$
\operatorname{Var}(N)=\frac{1-p}{p^2}.
$$

Substituting $p=\pi/4$ yields

$$
E(N)
=
\frac{4}{\pi},
$$

and

$$
\operatorname{Var}(N)
=
\frac{1-\pi/4}{(\pi/4)^2}
=
\frac{4(4-\pi)}{\pi^2}.
$$

Therefore the standard deviation is

$$
\sigma_N
=
\sqrt{\frac{4(4-\pi)}{\pi^2}}
=
\frac{2\sqrt{4-\pi}}{\pi}.
$$

Thus the distribution function of $X$ is

$$
F_X(x)
=
\begin{cases}
0,&x<0,\\[2mm]
\dfrac{2}{\pi}
\left(
x\sqrt{1-x^2}
+\arcsin x
\right),
&0\le x\le1,\\[4mm]
1,&x\ge1,
\end{cases}
$$

and the number of performances of step 1 has mean

$$
\boxed{\frac{4}{\pi}}
$$

and standard deviation

$$
\boxed{\frac{2\sqrt{4-\pi}}{\pi}}.
$$
