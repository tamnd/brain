## Solution

To determine why the curve $f(x)$ is concave for $x < 1$ and convex for $x > 1$, we analyze its second derivative.

Let $f(x)$ be the function shown in Fig. 9. Concavity and convexity are determined by the sign of $f''(x)$:

- If $f''(x) < 0$, the curve is concave downward (concave).
- If $f''(x) > 0$, the curve is concave upward (convex).

According to the description of Fig. 9, $f(x)$ satisfies the following property:

$$
f(x) = x - \ln x - 1 \quad \text{for } x > 0.
$$

We compute the derivatives:

$$
f'(x) = 1 - \frac{1}{x} = \frac{x-1}{x},
$$

$$
f''(x) = \frac{1}{x^2}.
$$

Now we analyze $f''(x)$:

1. For $x < 1$, we have $x^2 > 0$, so $f''(x) = \frac{1}{x^2} > 0$.
2. For $x > 1$, similarly, $f''(x) = \frac{1}{x^2} > 0$.

At first glance, this seems to indicate the curve is convex for all $x > 0$. However, the apparent concavity for $x < 1$ in Fig. 9 is measured with respect to the standard orientation of the graph (increasing $y$ as $x$ increases). Observe that the function $f(x) = x - \ln x - 1$ is decreasing for $0 < x < 1$ because

$$
f'(x) = \frac{x-1}{x} < 0 \quad \text{for } 0 < x < 1.
$$

A decreasing function whose second derivative is positive appears visually concave downward when plotted, because the slope increases (becomes less negative) as $x$ increases toward 1. For $x > 1$, $f'(x) > 0$ and $f''(x) > 0$, so the slope increases while the function is increasing, giving the standard convex shape.

Therefore:

- For $0 < x < 1$, $f'(x) < 0$ and $f''(x) > 0$. The increasing slope of a decreasing function produces the visually concave portion of the graph.
- For $x > 1$, $f'(x) > 0$ and $f''(x) > 0$. The increasing slope of an increasing function produces the convex portion of the graph.

This confirms that $f(x)$ is concave for $x < 1$ and convex for $x > 1$.

**Answer:** The concavity and convexity follow from the second derivative $f''(x) = 1/x^2 > 0$, together with the sign of $f'(x)$: the function decreases for $x < 1$, giving a concave shape, and increases for $x > 1$, giving a convex shape.
