[Section 4.6: Polynomial Arithmetic](../)

**Exercise 4.** [*21*] Can the multiplication of polynomials modulo 2 be facilitated by using the ordinary arithmetic operations on a binary computer, if coefficients are packed into computer words?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m16s  

---

Let

$$
u(x)=\sum_{i\ge 0} u_i x^i,\qquad v(x)=\sum_{j\ge 0} v_j x^j,\qquad u_i,v_j\in\{0,1\}.
$$

Encode each polynomial as a binary word:

$$
U=\sum_{i\ge 0} u_i 2^i,\qquad V=\sum_{j\ge 0} v_j 2^j.
$$

### Addition

Polynomial addition over $\mathbb{F}_2$ is coefficientwise addition modulo $2$. Hence

$$
(u+v)_i = u_i \oplus v_i,
$$

so packed addition is exactly bitwise XOR:

$$
U+V \equiv U \oplus V.
$$

### Multiplication (correct construction)

The product is

$$
u(x)v(x)=\sum_{k\ge 0} \left(\sum_{i+j=k} u_i v_j \right)x^k,
$$

where all arithmetic is in $\mathbb{F}_2$, so inner sums are XORs.

Fix $j$. The term $v_j x^j$ contributes

$$
v_j \cdot u(x)\, x^j.
$$

Since $v_j \in \{0,1\}$, scalar multiplication is trivial:

$$
v_j \cdot u(x)=
\begin{cases}
u(x), & v_j=1,\\
0, & v_j=0.
\end{cases}
$$

Therefore the full product is

$$
u(x)v(x)=\bigoplus_{j:\,v_j=1} u(x)x^j.
$$

In packed form, multiplication by $x^j$ corresponds to a left shift by $j$ bits:

$$
u(x)x^j \;\longleftrightarrow\; U \ll j.
$$

Hence the correct word-level identity is

$$
U \star V = \bigoplus_{j:\,v_j=1} (U \ll j).
$$

This gives a direct implementation:

- initialize $W=0$
- for each bit position $j$:

- if the $j$-th bit of $V$ is $1$, set $W \leftarrow W \oplus (U \ll j)$

### Addressing the error in the previous solution

The incorrect step was the expression

$$
(U \ \&\ (V \text{ shifted by } j)) \ll j.
$$

This is invalid because:

1. $U$ and $V$ encode different coefficient sequences. A bitwise AND between shifted versions of $U$ and $V$ does not isolate products $u_i v_j$; it instead mixes unrelated indices $i$ and $j$ within word alignment.
2. The scalar product $u_i v_j$ is not a word-level interaction between aligned bit positions. It is a product of individual bits, which must be realized by selecting whether a shifted copy of $U$ is included, not by masking $U$ with $V$.

Thus the correct operation is conditional inclusion of shifted copies of $U$, not AND-based filtering between packed words.

### Conclusion

Polynomial arithmetic over $\mathbb{F}_2$ maps directly to word operations:

- Addition: bitwise XOR
- Multiplication: shift-and-XOR accumulation of $U$, controlled by the bits of $V$

Therefore, packing coefficients into machine words allows polynomial multiplication modulo $2$ to be implemented efficiently using ordinary shift, XOR, and bit testing operations on a binary computer.
