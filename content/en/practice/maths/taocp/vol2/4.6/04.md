[Section 4.6: Polynomial Arithmetic](../)

**Exercise 4.** [*21*] Can the multiplication of polynomials modulo 2 be facilitated by using the ordinary arithmetic operations on a binary computer, if coefficients are packed into computer words?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 1m32s  

---

## Solution

Represent a polynomial over $\mathbb{F}_2$ by a binary sequence of coefficients

$u(x) = u_n x^n + \cdots + u_1 x + u_0,\qquad u_i \in \{0,1\}.$

Pack these coefficients into a binary word

$U = \sum_{i \ge 0} u_i 2^i,$

and similarly for $V$.

The arithmetic in polynomial multiplication modulo $2$ is governed by

$w_k \equiv \sum_{i+j=k} u_i v_j \pmod 2.$

Each coefficient $w_k$ depends only on parity of the number of contributing products.

The binary computer’s ordinary integer multiplication computes

$UV = \sum_{i,j} u_i v_j 2^{i+j}$

together with carries propagated between bit positions. These carries correspond to additions in $\mathbb{Z}$, not in $\mathbb{F}_2$. Whenever two contributions land in the same bit position, the integer representation adds them and propagates overflow to higher bits, so the resulting bit pattern does not equal $(w_k)$ in general.

A concrete obstruction appears already when two ones contribute to the same position: in integer arithmetic $1+1=2$, producing a carry, while in $\mathbb{F}_2$ arithmetic $1+1=0$. The carry propagation alters higher coefficients, so direct integer multiplication of packed words does not produce polynomial multiplication modulo $2$.

Correct computation is obtained if operations are interpreted componentwise on bits. Addition must be bitwise exclusive-or, matching addition in $\mathbb{F}_2$. Multiplication of coefficients must be bitwise conjunction. Under these operations,

$w_k = \bigoplus_{i+j=k} (u_i \wedge v_j),$

and no carries occur between bit positions.

Packing coefficients into machine words remains useful because a single word operation acts on all bits in parallel. Shifting the word representing $U$ by $j$ positions produces the alignment of $x^j U(x)$, and bitwise conjunction with $V$ forms all products $u_i v_j$ in parallel. Accumulating shifted partial products with bitwise exclusive-or yields the final polynomial product.

Thus polynomial multiplication modulo $2$ can be carried out efficiently on a binary computer only when the machine provides word operations that act bitwise independently, such as shifts and logical conjunction and exclusive-or. Ordinary integer multiplication, which includes carry propagation, does not preserve the required coefficient-wise arithmetic.

This completes the solution. ∎
