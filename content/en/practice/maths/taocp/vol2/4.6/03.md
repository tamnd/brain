[Section 4.6: Polynomial Arithmetic](../)

**Exercise 3.** [*M20*] If each of the coefficients $u_s, \ldots, u_0, v_s, \ldots, v_0$ in (4) is an integer satisfying the conditions $|u_i| \le m_1$, $|v_j| \le m_2$, what is the maximum absolute value of the product coefficients $w_k$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m  

---

## Solution

From (4),

$$
w_k = u_0 v_k + u_1 v_{k-1} + \cdots + u_k v_0,
$$

where terms with indices outside $0 \le i \le s$ are taken as $0$. Since $u_i = 0$ and $v_j = 0$ for indices outside $0 \le i,j \le s$, the sum reduces to all pairs $(i,j)$ such that

$$
i + j = k,\quad 0 \le i \le s,\quad 0 \le j \le s.
$$

Hence

$$
w_k = \sum_{i=\max(0,k-s)}^{\min(k,s)} u_i v_{k-i}.
$$

Taking absolute values and applying the triangle inequality,

$$
|w_k| \le \sum_{i=\max(0,k-s)}^{\min(k,s)} |u_i|\,|v_{k-i}|.
$$

Using the hypotheses $|u_i| \le m_1$ and $|v_j| \le m_2$, each term satisfies

$$
|u_i|\,|v_{k-i}| \le m_1 m_2.
$$

Therefore,

$$
|w_k| \le N_k \, m_1 m_2,
$$

where $N_k$ is the number of integer indices $i$ satisfying $\max(0,k-s) \le i \le \min(k,s)$.

For $0 \le k \le s$, this range is $0 \le i \le k$, hence $N_k = k+1$. For $s \le k \le 2s$, the range is $k-s \le i \le s$, hence $N_k = 2s-k+1$. The function $N_k$ attains its maximum at $k=s$, where

$$
N_s = s+1.
$$

Thus, for every $k$,

$$
|w_k| \le (s+1)m_1 m_2.
$$

To show this bound is attained, take $u_i = m_1$ and $v_j = m_2$ for all $0 \le i,j \le s$. Then for $k=s$,

$$
w_s = \sum_{i=0}^{s} u_i v_{s-i} = \sum_{i=0}^{s} m_1 m_2 = (s+1)m_1 m_2,
$$

so

$$
|w_s| = (s+1)m_1 m_2.
$$

This shows no smaller uniform bound is possible.

Hence the maximum possible absolute value of any coefficient $w_k$ is

$$
\boxed{(s+1)m_1 m_2}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
