[Section 4.6: Polynomial Arithmetic](../)

**Exercise 1.** [*10*] If we are doing polynomial arithmetic modulo 10, what is $7x + 2$ minus $x^2 + 5$? What is $6x^2 + x + 3$ times $5x^2 + 2$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 55s  

---

Arithmetic is performed coefficientwise modulo $10$.

For subtraction,

$$
(7x+2) - (x^2+5) = (0-1)x^2 + (7-0)x + (2-5).
$$

Reducing each coefficient modulo $10$ gives $-1 \equiv 9$, $7 \equiv 7$, and $-3 \equiv 7$, hence

$$
\boxed{9x^2 + 7x + 7}.
$$

For multiplication,

$$
(6x^2+x+3)(5x^2+2)
$$

is expanded term by term and combined using coefficient arithmetic modulo $10$:

$$
6x^2 \cdot 5x^2 = 30x^4 \equiv 0x^4,
\quad
6x^2 \cdot 2 = 12x^2 \equiv 2x^2,
$$

$$
x \cdot 5x^2 = 5x^3,
\quad
x \cdot 2 = 2x,
$$

$$
3 \cdot 5x^2 = 15x^2 \equiv 5x^2,
\quad
3 \cdot 2 = 6.
$$

Collecting coefficients,

$$
x^3: 5,\quad x^2: 2+5=7,\quad x: 2,\quad x^0: 6,
$$

yields

$$
\boxed{5x^3 + 7x^2 + 2x + 6}.
$$
