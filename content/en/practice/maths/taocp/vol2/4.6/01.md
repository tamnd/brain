[Section 4.6: Polynomial Arithmetic](../)

**Exercise 1.** [*10*] If we are doing polynomial arithmetic modulo 10, what is $7x + 2$ minus $x^2 + 5$? What is $6x^2 + x + 3$ times $5x^2 + 2$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m22s  

---

Polynomial arithmetic modulo $10$ means all coefficients are reduced modulo $10$ after addition or multiplication.

For the first expression,

$$
(7x + 2) - (x^2 + 5) = 7x + 2 - x^2 - 5.
$$

Collecting like terms gives

$$
-x^2 + 7x - 3.
$$

Reducing coefficients modulo $10$ yields $-1 \equiv 9$, $7 \equiv 7$, and $-3 \equiv 7$, hence the result is

$$
9x^2 + 7x + 7.
$$

For the product,

$$
(6x^2 + x + 3)(5x^2 + 2)
$$

is expanded term by term:

$$
6x^2 \cdot 5x^2 = 30x^4,\quad 6x^2 \cdot 2 = 12x^2,
$$

$$
x \cdot 5x^2 = 5x^3,\quad x \cdot 2 = 2x,
$$

$$
3 \cdot 5x^2 = 15x^2,\quad 3 \cdot 2 = 6.
$$

Summing coefficients gives

$$
30x^4 + 5x^3 + (12x^2 + 15x^2) + 2x + 6
= 30x^4 + 5x^3 + 27x^2 + 2x + 6.
$$

Reducing modulo $10$ coefficient-wise produces

$$
0x^4 + 5x^3 + 7x^2 + 2x + 6,
$$

so the final result is

$$
5x^3 + 7x^2 + 2x + 6.
$$

Thus,

$$
\boxed{9x^2 + 7x + 7}
\quad\text{and}\quad
\boxed{5x^3 + 7x^2 + 2x + 6}.
$$
