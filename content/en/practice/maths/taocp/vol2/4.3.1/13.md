**Exercise 13.** [*21*] Write a MIX program that multiplies $(u_{n-1} \ldots u_1 u_0)_b$ by $v$, where $v$ is a single-precision number (that is, $0 \le v < b$), producing the answer $(w_n \ldots w_1 w_0)_b$. How much running time is required?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 7m36s  

---

Let the multiplicand be stored in memory as

$$
U=(u_{n-1}\ldots u_1u_0)_b,
$$

with one digit per word, and let $v$ be a single precision number,

$$
0\le v<b.
$$

We wish to compute

$$
W=Uv=(w_n\ldots w_1w_0)_b.
$$

The standard algorithm proceeds from least significant digit to most significant digit, propagating a carry. If $k$ denotes the carry entering position $j$, then

$$
t=u_jv+k,
$$

$$
w_j=t\bmod b,
$$

$$
k=\lfloor t/b\rfloor .
$$

After the most significant digit has been processed, the final carry becomes $w_n$.

Since $u_j<b$, $v<b$, and $k<b$, we have

$$
t\le (b-1)^2+(b-1)=b^2-b<b^2,
$$

so $t$ always fits into the double precision product register $AX$. Thus MIX multiplication may be used directly.

Assume that $u_0,\ldots,u_{n-1}$ occupy locations $U,U+1,\ldots,U+n-1$. The product digits $w_0,\ldots,w_n$ are written into locations $W,W+1,\ldots,W+n$. Let location $V$ contain $v$, location $C$ contain the current carry, and location $ONE$ contain $1$.

A MIX program is:

```
        ENT1    0           index j := 0
        ENTA    0
        STA     C           carry := 0

LOOP    LDA     U,1         A := uj
        MUL     V           AX := uj*v

        ADD     C           AX := uj*v + carry

        DIV     =B=         quotient in A, remainder in X

        STA     C           new carry
        STX     W,1         wj := remainder

        INC1    1
        CMP1    N
        JL      LOOP

        LDA     C
        STA     W+N         wn := final carry
        HLT
```

Here `=B=` denotes the radix $b$, stored as a MIX constant. After the `DIV`, the quotient $\lfloor t/b\rfloor$ is left in register $A$ and the remainder $t\bmod b$ is left in register $X$, exactly as required by the digit recurrence above.

To verify correctness, let $k_j$ be the carry entering the computation of digit $j$, with $k_0=0$. At the start of the $j$-th iteration, location $C$ contains $k_j$. The instructions

$$
\texttt{MUL V},\qquad \texttt{ADD C}
$$

form

$$
t=u_jv+k_j.
$$

The subsequent division by $b$ yields

$$
k_{j+1}=\left\lfloor \frac{t}{b}\right\rfloor,
\qquad
w_j=t\bmod b.
$$

The remainder is stored as $w_j$, and the quotient is stored back into $C$. Therefore, by induction on $j$, each digit $w_j$ and carry $k_{j+1}$ is computed correctly. After the last iteration, $C$ contains $k_n$, which is stored as $w_n$. Hence the output digits satisfy

$$
(w_n\ldots w_1w_0)_b
=
v(u_{n-1}\ldots u_1u_0)_b.
$$

For running time, each loop iteration executes one load, one multiply, one add, one divide, two stores, one index increment, one comparison, and one conditional jump. Using the timing conventions of Section 4.3.1, the dominant cost is the multiplication and division, both performed once per digit. Thus the running time is linear in the number of digits:

$$
T(n)=\Theta(n).
$$

More precisely, if $t_{\mathrm{mul}}$ and $t_{\mathrm{div}}$ denote the times for `MUL` and `DIV`, and $c$ is the total time of the remaining loop instructions, then

$$
T(n)
=
n\bigl(t_{\mathrm{mul}}+t_{\mathrm{div}}+c\bigr)+O(1).
$$

Therefore multiplication of an $n$-digit number by a single digit requires $\Theta(n)$ time.
