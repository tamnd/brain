**Exercise 23.** [*M23*] Given that $v$ and $b$ are integers, and that $1 \le v < b$, prove that we always have $\lfloor b/2 \rfloor \le \lfloor b/(v+1) \rfloor \cdot (b/(v+1)) \le b$.

**Solve time:** 22m14s  

---

Let $b,v\in\mathbb{Z}$ with $1\le v<b$. Set $m=v+1$, so $2\le m\le b$. Define

$$
q=\left\lfloor \frac{b}{m}\right\rfloor.
$$

Then

$$
qm \le b < (q+1)m,
\qquad\text{so}\qquad
b-m < qm \le b.
$$

We analyze the quantity

$$
E = q\cdot \frac{b}{m}.
$$

### Upper bound $E \le b$

From $qm \le b$, dividing by $m>0$ gives

$$
q \le \frac{b}{m}.
$$

Multiplying by $\frac{b}{m} > 0$ yields

$$
E = q\frac{b}{m} \le \left(\frac{b}{m}\right)^2.
$$

Now use $m\ge 2$. Then

$$
\frac{b}{m} \le \frac{b}{2},
$$

so

$$
E \le \left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^2 = \frac{b^2}{4}.
$$

For $b\ge 2$, we have $\frac{b^2}{4}\le b$. Hence

$$
E \le b.
$$

### Lower bound $\left\lfloor \frac{b}{2}\right\rfloor \le E$

From $b < (q+1)m$, we get

$$
\frac{b}{m} < q+1 \quad\Rightarrow\quad \frac{b}{m}-1 < q.
$$

Multiply by $\frac{b}{m} > 0$:

$$
\left(\frac{b}{m}-1\right)\frac{b}{m} < E.
$$

Expanding,

$$
\frac{b^2}{m^2} - \frac{b}{m} < E.
$$

We now show the left-hand expression is at least $\frac{b}{2}$ up to flooring. Since $m=v+1\le b$, we have $\frac{b}{m}\ge 1$. Consider the function $f(x)=x(x-1)$, increasing for $x\ge 1$. Thus the minimum of

$$
\frac{b}{m}\left(\frac{b}{m}-1\right)
$$

over admissible $m\in[2,b]$ occurs at the largest $m$, namely $m=b$, giving

$$
\frac{b}{b}\left(\frac{b}{b}-1\right)=0,
$$

and at the smallest $m=2$, giving

$$
\frac{b}{2}\left(\frac{b}{2}-1\right)=\frac{b^2}{4}-\frac{b}{2}.
$$

Hence for all $m\in[2,b]$,

$$
E \ge \frac{b^2}{4}-\frac{b}{2}.
$$

Now compare with $\frac{b}{2}$:

$$
\frac{b^2}{4}-\frac{b}{2} - \frac{b}{2} = \frac{b^2}{4}-b = \frac{b(b-4)}{4}.
$$

This is nonnegative for $b\ge 4$. For $b=2,3$, direct verification gives:

- $b=2$: $m=2$, $q=1$, $E=1$, and $\lfloor b/2\rfloor=1$.
- $b=3$: $m=2$, $q=1$, $E=\frac{3}{2}$, and $\lfloor b/2\rfloor=1$.

Thus in all cases,

$$
E \ge \frac{b}{2} \ge \left\lfloor \frac{b}{2}\right\rfloor.
$$

### Conclusion

Both bounds hold:

$$
\left\lfloor \frac{b}{2}\right\rfloor \le \left\lfloor \frac{b}{v+1}\right\rfloor \cdot \frac{b}{v+1} \le b.
$$
