**Exercise 43.** [*22*] [22] Shades of gray or components of color values in digitized images are usually represented as 8-bit numbers $u$ in the range $[0 \mathinner{..} 255]$, denoting the fraction $u/255$. Given two such fractions $u/255$ and $v/255$, graphical algorithms often need to compute their approximate product $w/255$, where $w$ is the nearest integer to $uv/255$. Prove that $w$ can be obtained from the efficient formula

$$t = uv + 128, \qquad w = \lfloor (t/256 + t)/256 \rfloor.$$

## Solution

The desired value of $w$ is the nearest integer to $uv/255$. Since $u$ and $v$ are integers in the range $[0\mathinner{..}255]$, the product $uv$ is an integer, hence

$$
w=\left\lfloor \frac{uv}{255}+\frac12\right\rfloor
=\left\lfloor \frac{2uv+255}{510}\right\rfloor .
$$

Put

$$
t=uv+128.
$$

Then

$$
2uv+255=2t-1,
$$

so

$$
w=\left\lfloor \frac{2t-1}{510}\right\rfloor .
$$

It remains to prove that

$$
\left\lfloor \frac{2t-1}{510}\right\rfloor
=
\left\lfloor \frac{t+\lfloor t/256\rfloor}{256}\right\rfloor .
$$

Write

$$
t=256q+r,
\qquad 0\le r<256.
$$

Since

$$
0\le uv\le255^2=65025,
$$

we have

$$
128\le t\le65153,
$$

hence

$$
0\le q\le254.
$$

Also,

$$
\left\lfloor \frac t{256}\right\rfloor=q,
$$

therefore

$$
\left\lfloor \frac{t+\lfloor t/256\rfloor}{256}\right\rfloor
=
\left\lfloor \frac{256q+r+q}{256}\right\rfloor
=
q+\left\lfloor \frac{q+r}{256}\right\rfloor .
$$

On the other hand,

$$
\frac{2t-1}{510}
=
\frac{512q+2r-1}{510}
=
q+\frac{2(q+r)-1}{510},
$$

so

$$
\left\lfloor \frac{2t-1}{510}\right\rfloor
=
q+\left\lfloor \frac{2(q+r)-1}{510}\right\rfloor .
$$

Hence it suffices to show that

$$
\left\lfloor \frac{2(q+r)-1}{510}\right\rfloor
=
\left\lfloor \frac{q+r}{256}\right\rfloor .
$$

Let

$$
s=q+r.
$$

Since $0\le q\le254$ and $0\le r\le255$,

$$
0\le s\le509.
$$

Therefore $\lfloor s/256\rfloor$ is either $0$ or $1$.

If $0\le s\le255$, then

$$
-1\le 2s-1\le509,
$$

hence

$$
-\frac1{510}\le\frac{2s-1}{510}<1,
$$

and therefore

$$
\left\lfloor \frac{2s-1}{510}\right\rfloor=0.
$$

Since $s<256$,

$$
\left\lfloor \frac s{256}\right\rfloor=0.
$$

If $256\le s\le509$, then

$$
511\le2s-1\le1017,
$$

so

$$
1<\frac{2s-1}{510}<2,
$$

and consequently

$$
\left\lfloor \frac{2s-1}{510}\right\rfloor=1.
$$

Since $256\le s<512$,

$$
\left\lfloor \frac s{256}\right\rfloor=1.
$$

Thus

$$
\left\lfloor \frac{2s-1}{510}\right\rfloor
=
\left\lfloor \frac s{256}\right\rfloor
$$

for all possible values of $s$, and therefore

$$
\left\lfloor \frac{2t-1}{510}\right\rfloor
=
\left\lfloor \frac{t+\lfloor t/256\rfloor}{256}\right\rfloor .
$$

Hence the nearest integer to $uv/255$ is obtained by

$$
t=uv+128,
\qquad
w=\left\lfloor \frac{t/256+t}{256}\right\rfloor .
$$

Therefore

$$
\boxed{
w=
\left\lfloor
\frac{t+\lfloor t/256\rfloor}{256}
\right\rfloor,
\qquad
t=uv+128
}.
$$

This completes the proof.
