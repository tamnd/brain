A correct design must first remove the ambiguity about what “word-level scaling” means and then express every operation in terms of explicit operations on fixed-size word arrays.

## Representation

A floating-point number is represented by a pair

$$
X = (M, E),
$$

where $E$ is a single full machine word storing a signed integer exponent (excess 0 means no bias is applied, so the stored exponent equals the true exponent), and $M$ is a mantissa stored as an array of 9 base-$b$ digits:

$$
M = (m_1, m_2, \dots, m_9),
\quad 0 \le m_i < b.
$$

The numeric value is

$$
X = \left(\sum_{i=1}^{9} m_i b^{9-i}\right) b^E.
$$

Normalization means:

either $M = (0,\dots,0)$ for zero, or

$$
m_1 \ne 0.
$$

All scaling is performed by shifting whole words in the mantissa array. A “word shift” means moving entries of the 9-word array; no intra-word bit shifting is used.

## Auxiliary subroutines on mantissas

Let arrays be 1-indexed.

### CLEAR(M)

Sets all 9 digits to zero.

### COPY(M, N)

Copies mantissa $N$ into $M$.

### SHIFT_RIGHT(M, k)

Shifts mantissa right by $k$ words:

$$
M[i+k] := M[i], \quad M[1..k] := 0,
$$

discarding overflow beyond position 9.

### SHIFT_LEFT(M, k)

Shifts mantissa left by $k$ words:

$$
M[i-k] := M[i], \quad M[10-k..9] := 0.
$$

### ADD_MANTISSAS(M, N, R)

Computes $R = M + N$ in base $b$ with carry, producing a 9-digit result plus possible overflow carry out of position 1.

### SUB_MANTISSAS(M, N, R)

Assumes $M \ge N$ in mantissa ordering and computes $R = M - N$ with borrows.

### LEADING_INDEX(M)

Returns the smallest index $j$ such that $m_j \ne 0$, or 0 if all zero.

## NORMALIZE(M, E)

This is the key correction: normalization is not repeated shifting but a bounded index computation.

If $M = 0$, then set $E := 0$ and return.

Let $j = \text{LEADING\_INDEX}(M)$. If $j > 1$, shift left by $j-1$:

$$
SHIFT\_LEFT(M, j-1),
\quad E := E - (j-1).
$$

Now $m_1 \ne 0$ holds.

If a carry out of position 1 exists (i.e. a 10th digit appears), shift right by 1 and increment exponent:

$$
SHIFT\_RIGHT(M, 1), \quad E := E + 1.
$$

This guarantees:

$$
b^8 \le M < b^9 \quad \text{or } M=0.
$$

## ADDITION

Let $X = (M, E)$, $Y = (N, F)$.

### Step 1: align exponents

If $E \ge F$, set $k = E - F$, $E_{\min} = F$.

Shift $N$ right by $k$ words:

$$
N' := SHIFT\_RIGHT(N, k).
$$

If $k \ge 9$, then $Y$ is too small to affect the sum, so return $X$.

If $F > E$, symmetrically shift $M$.

After alignment both numbers share exponent $E_{\min}$.

### Step 2: add mantissas

$$
R := M + N'.
$$

If there is carry out of the 9th position, treat it as a 10th digit and shift right once, increasing exponent.

### Step 3: normalize

$$
NORMALIZE(R, E_{\min}).
$$

Return $(R, E_{\min})$.

## SUBTRACTION

To compute $X - Y$, align exponents exactly as in addition.

If mantissas are unequal in magnitude, swap operands and compute subtraction, tracking sign separately.

The result is stored as a signed mantissa pair $(\pm M, E)$, or equivalently a sign flag plus absolute mantissa.

After subtraction:

$$
NORMALIZE(M, E).
$$

If all digits become zero, return zero representation.

## MULTIPLICATION

Let $X = (M, E)$, $Y = (N, F)$.

### Step 1: multiply mantissas

Form a 18-word product array $P$:

$$
P[i+j-1] += M[i] \cdot N[j], \quad 1 \le i,j \le 9,
$$

with carries propagated in base $b$.

This yields a 18-digit base-$b$ number.

### Step 2: determine leading position

Let $t = \text{LEADING\_INDEX}(P)$. The true exponent shift is:

$$
s = t - 1.
$$

### Step 3: extract 9-digit mantissa

Set:

$$
M' = \text{first 9 digits of } P \text{ starting at } t.
$$

If fewer than 9 digits remain, pad with zeros.

### Step 4: exponent adjustment

$$
E' = E + F + s.
$$

### Step 5: rounding

Inspect the discarded digits after the 9th retained digit. If they are nonzero and rounding mode is nearest, increment $M'$ with carry propagation.

### Step 6: normalize

$$
NORMALIZE(M', E').
$$

Return $(M', E')$.

## DIVISION

Let $X = (M, E)$, $Y = (N, F)$, assume $N \ne 0$.

### Step 1: initialize

Set quotient mantissa $Q := 0$, remainder $R := M$.

### Step 2: digit-by-digit long division

For $i = 1$ to $9$:

1. Shift remainder left by 1 word:

$$
R := SHIFT\_LEFT(R, 1).
$$
2. Determine digit $q_i$ as the largest integer in $[0,b-1]$ such that:

$$
q_i N \le R.
$$
3. Set $Q[i] := q_i$.
4. Update:

$$
R := R - q_i N.
$$

### Step 3: exponent

If the first quotient digit appears at position $j$, then exponent correction is:

$$
s = j - 1.
$$

Thus:

$$
E' = E - F - s.
$$

### Step 4: normalize and round

$$
NORMALIZE(Q, E').
$$

Return $(Q, E')$.

## SPECIAL CASES

Zero is represented by all-zero mantissa and exponent 0.

Overflow occurs if $E$ exceeds the machine word range; underflow if normalization requires exponent below minimum representable value. Both are flagged.

## SUBROUTINE SET

A complete implementation consists of:

- ADD(X, Y)
- SUB(X, Y)
- MUL(X, Y)
- DIV(X, Y)
- NORMALIZE(M, E)
- LEADING_INDEX(M)
- SHIFT_LEFT(M, k)
- SHIFT_RIGHT(M, k)
- ADD_MANTISSAS, SUB_MANTISSAS
- COMPARE(M, N)

## Correction summary embedded in design

The key fixes are:

1. Scaling is defined strictly as word-array shifting, not abstract digit shifting tied to exponents.
2. Normalization is done via a single leading-index computation, not repeated uncontrolled shifting.
3. Multiplication exponent shift is derived from the exact position of the leading nonzero digit in the 18-word product.
4. Division uses explicit digit-by-digit long division, not informal mantissa division.
5. The exponent is consistently a signed integer word with no conflicting interpretation.

This yields a fully implementable TAOCP-style decuple-precision arithmetic system consistent with fixed word-level scaling.
