**Solution to Exercise 4.3.1.20**

We adopt the notation of Exercise 4.3.1.19. Let the divisor be

$$
v = (v_{n-1} v_{n-2} \ldots)_b, \quad v_{n-1} \neq 0,
$$

and let the dividend be

$$
u = (u_n u_{n-1} u_{n-2} \ldots)_b.
$$

Define the trial quotient

$$
\hat{q} = \left\lfloor \frac{u_n b + u_{n-1}}{v_{n-1}} \right\rfloor
$$

and the corresponding remainder approximation

$$
\hat{r} = u_n b + u_{n-1} - \hat{q} v_{n-1}.
$$

Let the true quotient digit be $q$ and the true remainder for the first three digits be $r$, so that

$$
u_n b^2 + u_{n-1} b + u_{n-2} = q (v_{n-1} b + v_{n-2}) + r, \quad 0 \le r < v_{n-1} b + v_{n-2}.
$$

We are given

$$
\hat{q} v_{n-2} \le b \hat{r} + u_{n-2}, \quad \hat{q} < b,
$$

and must show that $\hat{q} = q$ or $\hat{q} = q - 1$.

**Step 1: Express the exact remainder in terms of $q$.**

Expand the right-hand side:

$$
q (v_{n-1} b + v_{n-2}) = q v_{n-1} b + q v_{n-2}.
$$

Thus

$$
r = u_n b^2 + u_{n-1} b + u_{n-2} - q v_{n-1} b - q v_{n-2} = (u_n b + u_{n-1} - q v_{n-1}) b + (u_{n-2} - q v_{n-2}).
$$

Define

$$
r_0 = u_n b + u_{n-1} - q v_{n-1}.
$$

Then

$$
r = r_0 b + (u_{n-2} - q v_{n-2}).
$$

**Step 2: Reformulate the given inequality.**

The inequality

$$
\hat{q} v_{n-2} \le b \hat{r} + u_{n-2}
$$

becomes, after substituting $\hat{r} = u_n b + u_{n-1} - \hat{q} v_{n-1}$:

$$
\hat{q} v_{n-2} \le b (u_n b + u_{n-1} - \hat{q} v_{n-1}) + u_{n-2} = (u_n b + u_{n-1} - \hat{q} v_{n-1}) b + u_{n-2}.
$$

Subtract $\hat{q} v_{n-2}$ from both sides:

$$
0 \le (u_n b + u_{n-1} - \hat{q} v_{n-1}) b + u_{n-2} - \hat{q} v_{n-2}.
$$

$$
(u_n b + u_{n-1} - \hat{q} v_{n-1}) b + u_{n-2} - \hat{q} v_{n-2} = r_0 b + (u_{n-2} - \hat{q} v_{n-2}) + (\hat{q} - q) v_{n-1} b.
$$
