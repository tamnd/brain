**Exercise 22.** &#9654; [*24*] Find an example of a four-digit number divided by a three-digit number for which step D6 is necessary in Algorithm D, when the radix $b$ is 10.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 3m20s

## Solution

Let the dividend be $(u_3 u_2 u_1 u_0)_{10} = (8500)_{10}$ and the divisor be $(v_2 v_1 v_0)_{10} = (101)_{10}$.

In Algorithm D, the first quotient digit is computed from the leading three digits of the dividend and the leading two digits of the divisor. Thus for the first division step we use
\[
(u_3 u_2 u_1)_{10} = 850,\quad (v_2 v_1)_{10} = 10.
\]

The initial quotient digit estimate is
\[
\hat{q} = \left\lfloor \frac{850}{10} \right\rfloor = 85.
\]
Since a single digit in radix $b=10$ is required, this is replaced by
\[
\hat{q} = 9.
\]

The correction test in Algorithm D compares $\hat{q} v$ with the current partial dividend segment. Compute
\[
9 \cdot 101 = 909.
\]
Since
\[
909 > 850,
\]
the estimated quotient digit is too large, so step D6 is entered and $\hat{q}$ is decreased by 1.

After decrement,
\[
\hat{q} = 8,
\]
and
\[
8 \cdot 101 = 808 \le 850,
\]
so the corrected digit is accepted and the algorithm proceeds without further repetition of D6.

Thus step D6 is necessary in this example because the initial estimate $\hat{q} = 9$ must be corrected once before the correct quotient digit $\hat{q} = 8$ is obtained.

This completes the solution. ∎
