**Exercise 11.** [*10*] Design an algorithm that compares two nonnegative $n$-place integers $u = (u_{n-1} \ldots u_1 u_0)_b$ and $v = (v_{n-1} \ldots v_1 v_0)_b$, to determine whether $u < v$, $u = v$, or $u > v$.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 3m32s  

---

Compare the digits beginning with the most significant position. Let $j \leftarrow n-1$. If $u_j=v_j$, decrease $j$ by one and continue. If a position is found for which $u_j\ne v_j$, terminate immediately and declare $u>v$ when $u_j>v_j$, or $u<v$ when $u_j<v_j$. If all positions satisfy $u_j=v_j$, terminate and declare $u=v$.

In algorithmic form:

C1. $j \leftarrow n-1$.

C2. If $u_j>v_j$, conclude $u>v$ and terminate. If $u_j<v_j$, conclude $u<v$ and terminate.

C3. If $j=0$, conclude $u=v$ and terminate.

C4. $j \leftarrow j-1$; return to C2.

The correctness follows from the radix-$b$ representation. Let $j$ be the largest index for which $u_j\ne v_j$. Then

$$
u-v=(u_j-v_j)b^j+\sum_{i=0}^{j-1}(u_i-v_i)b^i .
$$

Since

$$
\left|\sum_{i=0}^{j-1}(u_i-v_i)b^i\right|
\le (b-1)\sum_{i=0}^{j-1} b^i
= b^j-1,
$$

the sign of $u-v$ is the sign of $u_j-v_j$. Hence the first unequal pair of digits encountered when scanning from $u_{n-1},v_{n-1}$ downward determines whether $u>v$ or $u<v$; if no unequal pair exists, all digits are equal and therefore $u=v$. The algorithm uses exactly two replacement operations, namely $j\leftarrow n-1$ and the repeated assignment $j\leftarrow j-1$.
