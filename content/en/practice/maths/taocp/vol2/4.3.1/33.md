**Exercise 33.** [**] [$M40$] Design an algorithm for taking square roots, analogous to Algorithm D and to the traditional pencil-and-paper method for extracting square roots.

Let $u = (u_{n-1}\ldots u_1u_0)_b$. The goal is to construct digits of $\sqrt{u}$ in radix $b$ by imitating the classical pencil-and-paper square-root extraction, but phrased in the multiple-precision framework of Section 4.3.1.

We maintain a partial root $x = (x_{k-1}\ldots x_0)_b$ and a remainder $w$, where at each stage the invariant is that $w$ represents the portion of $u$ still not accounted for after subtracting the square of the already constructed prefix of the root, aligned in radix-$b$ digit blocks. The extraction proceeds two digits of $u$ at a time, since squaring interacts with base-$b$ positional shifts in pairs.

At each step, we bring down the next two digits of $u$ into $w$ by setting $w \leftarrow wb^2 + (u_{j}b + u_{j-1})$. Then we determine the next digit $x_k$ as the largest digit $d \in {0,\ldots,b-1}$ such that

$(20x)b + d \;\text{combined with}\; d \;\le\; w,$

which is the digit-level form of ensuring

$(2xb + d)d \le w,$

consistent with expanding $(xb + d)^2 = x^2b^2 + (2xb)d + d^2$.

Once $d$ is chosen, we update

$x \leftarrow xb + d,$

$w \leftarrow w - (2xb + d)d.$

Each comparison and subtraction is carried out using the primitive operations $\alpha_1$, $\alpha_2$, $\alpha_3$ exactly as in Algorithm S, since $(2xb + d)d$ is a two-stage product followed by accumulation into a multiple-precision accumulator.

Initialization sets $x \leftarrow 0$, $w \leftarrow 0$. Repeating the digit-pair process for all $n$ digits of $u$ yields a final $x$ such that

$x^2 \le u < (x+1)^2,$

because at each stage we choose the maximal admissible digit consistent with not exceeding the remaining value, exactly mirroring the correctness argument for Algorithm D but specialized to squaring. Hence $x = \lfloor \sqrt{u} \rfloor$. This completes the construction. ∎
