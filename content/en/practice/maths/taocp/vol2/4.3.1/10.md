**Exercise 10.** [*18*] Would Program S work properly if the instructions on lines 06 and 07 were interchanged? If the instructions on lines 05 and 06 were interchanged?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m34s  

---

## Solution

Program S represents the quantity $1+k$ in register A. Since $k\in{0,-1}$, the accumulator contains either $1$ or $0$ at the beginning of step S2.

Lines 05 and 06 compute

$$
(1+k)+u_j-v_j.
$$

Because location WN1 contains $b-1$, the subsequent division by WN1 extracts the digit and borrow information corresponding to

$$
u_j-v_j+k+b.
$$

The overflow condition after the division determines whether a borrow occurred.

Consider first interchanging lines 06 and 07.

The modified code would execute

```
05 ADD U+N,1
06 DIV WN1
07 SUB V+N,1
```

before storing the result.

After line 05 the accumulator contains

$$
u_j+(1+k).
$$

Since

$$
0\le u_j+(1+k)\le (b-1)+1=b,
$$

division by $b-1$ at line 06 produces a quotient and remainder depending only on $u_j$ and $k$. The subtraction of $v_j$ is then performed after the division, so the quotient and remainder no longer encode the quantity

$$
u_j-v_j+k+b.
$$

A specific counterexample suffices. Let $b=10$, $u_j=3$, $v_j=7$, $k=0$. The correct digit is

$$
(3-7)\bmod 10=6,
$$

with borrow $-1$.

With the modified code:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{line 04:}&\quad A=1,\\
\text{line 05:}&\quad A=4,\\
\text{line 06:}&\quad 4\div 9,
\end{aligned}
$$

giving quotient $0$, remainder $4$. Then line 07 subtracts $7$, leaving a negative value unrelated to the required digit $6$. Hence the program fails.

Now consider interchanging lines 05 and 06.

The modified code would execute

```
05 SUB V+N,1
06 ADD U+N,1
07 DIV WN1
```

The accumulator value before line 05 is $1+k$. Therefore after lines 05 and 06 it contains

$$
(1+k)-v_j+u_j
=
u_j-v_j+(1+k),
$$

which is exactly the same quantity obtained by the original order of lines 05 and 06. Addition and subtraction of fixed operands are associative in this context, and no overflow can occur because

$$
-(b-1)\le u_j-v_j+(1+k)\le b.
$$

Thus the value presented to the division instruction on line 07 is unchanged. The quotient, remainder, stored digit, and borrow determination are therefore identical to those of the original program.

Consequently:

$$
\boxed{\text{Interchanging lines 06 and 07 makes Program S incorrect.}}
$$

and

$$
\boxed{\text{Interchanging lines 05 and 06 leaves Program S correct.}}
$$
