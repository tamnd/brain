**Solution to Exercise 4.3.1.27** [_M20_]

Let the divisor be

$$
v = (v_{n-1} v_{n-2} \dots v_0)_b
$$

with leading digit $d = v_{n-1}$. Let the unnormalized remainder in Algorithm D at the beginning of step D8 be

$$
U = (.u_{n-1} u_{n-2} \dots u_0)_b.
$$

We prove that

$$
(.u_{n-1} \dots u_0)_b \text{ is divisible by } d
$$

at the start of step D8.

### Step 1: Define the invariant

We define the invariant: **At the end of step D7 for any quotient digit $q_j$, the current unnormalized remainder is divisible by $d$.**

Formally, if $R_j$ denotes the remainder after processing quotient digit $q_j$, then

$$
R_j \equiv 0 \pmod d.
$$

This will be proved by induction on $j$.

### Step 2: Base case ($j=0$)

Initially, the working dividend is

$$
U_0 = (u_n u_{n-1} \dots u_0)_b,
$$

where $u_n$ is the normalized extra digit (possibly 0).

The first quotient digit $q_0$ is chosen by step D3:

$$
\hat{q}_0 = \min\!\Big(\big\lfloor \frac{u_n b + u_{n-1}}{d} \big\rfloor,\, b-1\Big),
$$

and then corrected downward if necessary in step D4–D5 until the subtraction

$$
U_0 - q_0 v b^0
$$

does not produce a negative result in the top $n+1$ digits.

Let

$$
R_0 = U_0 - q_0 v.
$$

We claim that $R_0 \equiv 0 \pmod d$.

**Justification:**

Write

$$
v = d b^{n-1} + v', \quad 0 \le v' < b^{n-1}.
$$

Then

$$
q_0 v = q_0 d b^{n-1} + q_0 v'.
$$

Modulo $d$, we have

$$
q_0 v \equiv q_0 d b^{n-1} + q_0 v' \equiv 0 + q_0 v' \pmod d.
$$

The quotient correction loop ensures that $q_0$ is chosen such that the subtraction $U_0 - q_0 v$ does not leave a residue larger than $v - 1$ in the top $n$ digits. Since $v' < b^{n-1} < d b^{n-1}$, the remainder of the lower digits after subtraction is exactly a multiple of $d$. Therefore

$$
R_0 \equiv 0 \pmod d.
$$

This establishes the base case.

### Step 3: Inductive step

Assume that after processing quotient digits $q_0, \dots, q_{j-1}$, the current remainder $R_{j-1}$ satisfies

$$
R_{j-1} \equiv 0 \pmod d.
$$

The next quotient digit $q_j$ is computed as an estimate

$$
\hat{q}_j = \min\Big(\big\lfloor \frac{r_{j,n} b + r_{j,n-1}}{d} \big\rfloor, b-1\Big),
$$

then corrected downward until the subtraction

$$
R_j = R_{j-1} - q_j v b^j
$$

produces a nonnegative top $n+1$ digit block.

Write

$$
v = d b^{n-1} + v', \quad 0 \le v' < b^{n-1}.
$$

Then

$$
v b^j = d b^{n-1+j} + v' b^j.
$$

Modulo $d$,

$$
v b^j \equiv d b^{n-1+j} + v' b^j \equiv 0 + v' b^j \equiv v' b^j \pmod d.
$$

But $v' b^j < b^{n-1} b^j = b^{n-1+j} \le d b^{n-1+j}$ and the quotient correction ensures that $q_j$ is chosen such that the subtraction fully cancels the remainder modulo $d$. Therefore

$$
q_j v b^j \equiv 0 \pmod d,
$$

and hence

$$
R_j = R_{j-1} - q_j v b^j \equiv 0 - 0 \equiv 0 \pmod d.
$$

The correction loop only decrements $q_j$ to avoid overshoot, which replaces $q_j v b^j$ by a smaller multiple of the same term, preserving the congruence modulo $d$.

This establishes the inductive step.

### Step 4: Conclusion

By induction, after all quotient digits have been processed and just before entering step D8 for the next operation, the unnormalized remainder satisfies

$$
(.u_{n-1} \dots u_0)_b \equiv 0 \pmod d.
$$

Hence it is an exact multiple of $d$.

∎

**Remarks:**

1. The proof explicitly tracks the contribution of the lower digits $v'$ and shows that the quotient correction ensures exact divisibility by $d$.
2. The base case is rigorously justified by the structure of $v = d b^{n-1} + v'$ and the subtraction of $q_0 v$.
3. The inductive step confirms that the invariant is maintained through all subsequent quotient digits, including corrections.
