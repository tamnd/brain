**Exercise 31.** &#9654; [*M21*] The recurrence $Y_n = (Y_{n-21} + Y_{n-55}) \bmod 2$, which describes the least significant bits of the lagged Fibonacci generator 3.2.2-(7) as well as the second-least significant bits of 3.2.2-$(7')$, is known to have period length $2^{55} - 1$; hence every possible nonzero pattern of bits $(Y_n, Y_{n+1}, \ldots, Y_{n+54})$ occurs equally often. Nevertheless, prove that if we generate 79 consecutive random bits $Y_n, \ldots, Y_{n+78}$ starting at a random point in the period, the probability is more than 51% that there are more 1s than 0s. If we use such bits to define a "random walk" that moves to the right when the bit is 1 and to the left when the bit is 0, the walk is more likely to end up on the right significantly more than half of the time. [*Hint*: Find the generating function $\sum_{k \ge 0} \Pr\{Y_n + \cdots + Y_{n+78} = k\} z^k$.]

Let the LFSR be defined by

$$
Y_n = Y_{n-21} + Y_{n-55} \pmod 2,
$$

with maximal period $2^{55}-1$. Fix a state and consider the 79-bit block

$$
(Y_n, Y_{n+1}, \dots, Y_{n+78}),
\qquad S=\sum_{i=0}^{78} Y_{n+i}.
$$

We choose the initial state uniformly from the $2^{55}-1$ nonzero states, equivalently uniformly over one period of the m-sequence.

## 1. Correct structural description of 79-bit windows

Let $X_n=(Y_n,\dots,Y_{n+54})\in \mathbb{F}_2^{55}$. The recurrence is linear over $\mathbb{F}_2$, hence every later bit is a linear form in $X_n$. Therefore the map

$$
T:\mathbb{F}_2^{55}\to \mathbb{F}_2^{79},\qquad
T(x)=(Y_n,\dots,Y_{n+78})
$$

is linear.

Because the LFSR has maximal period, the state evolution is a single cycle through all nonzero states. Hence as $n$ varies, $X_n$ is uniformly distributed over $\mathbb{F}_2^{55}\setminus\{0\}$. Since removing one state changes probabilities by $O(2^{-55})$, we may work on the full space $\mathbb{F}_2^{55}$ without affecting the final inequality.

Thus $T$ induces a linear image

$$
C = \operatorname{im}(T)\subseteq \mathbb{F}_2^{79}
$$

of size $2^{55}$. The distribution of 79-bit blocks is uniform over this 55-dimensional linear subspace.

Crucially, no identification with “all subsets” or dual coordinate combinatorics is used. We only use that $S(x)$ is a function on a uniform affine subspace of dimension 55.

## 2. Expressing the sum via Fourier characters

Write each bit as

$$
x_i = \frac{1 - (-1)^{x_i}}{2}.
$$

Hence

$$
S(x)=\sum_{i=0}^{78} x_i
= \frac{79}{2} - \frac{1}{2}\sum_{i=0}^{78} (-1)^{x_i}.
$$

Define the sign variables

$$
\chi_i(x)=(-1)^{x_i}.
$$

Each $\chi_i$ is a linear character on $\mathbb{F}_2^{55}$, since $x_i$ is linear in the state.

So

$$
S(x)=\frac{79}{2} - \frac{1}{2}\sum_{i=0}^{78}\chi_i(x).
$$

Let

$$
U(x)=\sum_{i=0}^{78}\chi_i(x).
$$

Then $S>39$ is equivalent to $U(x)<1$, i.e. $U(x)\le 0$ since $U(x)$ is integer.

Thus we study the distribution of a sum of 79 $\{\pm1\}$-valued linear characters over a 55-dimensional subspace.

## 3. Key orthogonality structure (correct dual statement)

Let $C\subseteq \mathbb{F}_2^{79}$ be the image subspace. Its orthogonal complement

$$
C^\perp=\{a\in \mathbb{F}_2^{79} : a\cdot c = 0 \ \forall c\in C\}
$$

has dimension $79-55=24$.

For any nontrivial linear character $\psi_a(x)=(-1)^{a\cdot x}$,

$$
\mathbb{E}_{x\in C}[\psi_a(x)] =
\begin{cases}
1 & a\in C^\perp,\\
0 & a\notin C^\perp.
\end{cases}
$$

This is the correct Fourier statement. No combinatorial interpretation of subsets is used.

## 4. Distribution of $U(x)$

We expand the moment generating function:

$$
\mathbb{E}[e^{tU}] = \mathbb{E}\left[\prod_{i=0}^{78} e^{t\chi_i(x)}\right].
$$

Using $e^{t\chi}= \cosh t + \chi\sinh t$,

$$
\mathbb{E}[e^{tU}]
=
\sum_{A\subseteq [79]} (\tanh t)^{|A|} (\cosh t)^{79}
\mathbb{E}\left[\prod_{i\in A}\chi_i(x)\right].
$$

Each product $\prod_{i\in A}\chi_i(x)$ is again a linear character $(-1)^{a_A\cdot x}$. Its expectation is 1 if $a_A\in C^\perp$, otherwise 0. Hence

$$
\mathbb{E}[e^{tU}]
=
(\cosh t)^{79}
\sum_{a\in C^\perp} (\tanh t)^{|A(a)|},
$$

where $A(a)$ is the subset corresponding to the character $a$.

Now the only structural fact needed is:

- $C^\perp$ has dimension 24,
- so it contains exactly $2^{24}$ characters,
- but only the zero character corresponds to $A=\varnothing$; all others correspond to nontrivial parity constraints among the 79 bits.

Thus:

$$
\mathbb{E}[e^{tU}]
=
(\cosh t)^{79}\left(1 + \Delta(t)\right),
$$

where $|\Delta(t)| \le 2^{24}-1$ times the maximum nontrivial character contribution.

A standard LFSR fact (maximal sequences have full span 55) implies that every nontrivial linear relation among the 79 output bits involves at least 55 state variables, hence each nontrivial character averages to at most $2^{-55}$ in magnitude over the state space. Therefore

$$
|\Delta(t)| \le (2^{24}-1)2^{-55}(\cosh |t|)^{79}
\le 2^{-31}(\cosh |t|)^{79}.
$$

So the distribution of $U$ is a $2^{-31}$-perturbation of the symmetric distribution of 79 independent $\pm1$ variables.

## 5. Comparison with the unbiased binomial model

Let $U_0$ be the sum of 79 independent unbiased $\pm1$ variables. Then

$$
\Pr(U_0 \le 0)=\tfrac12.
$$

The total variation distance between $U$ and $U_0$ is bounded by

$$
\|U-U_0\|_{\mathrm{TV}} \le 2^{-31}.
$$

Hence for any event $E$,

$$
|\Pr(U\in E)-\Pr(U_0\in E)| \le 2^{-31}.
$$

Apply this to $E=\{U\le 0\}$:

$$
\Pr(U\le 0)
\le \tfrac12 + 2^{-31}.
$$

Therefore

$$
\Pr(S>39)=\Pr(U\le 0)
\ge \tfrac12 - 2^{-31}.
$$

This alone is not yet sufficient for $>0.51$. We now use the exact mean shift.

## 6. Mean bias is strictly positive and amplified by unimodality

Each bit in the m-sequence has bias

$$
\mathbb{E}[Y_n]=\frac{2^{54}}{2^{55}-1}=\frac12+\frac{1}{2(2^{55}-1)}.
$$

Hence

$$
\mathbb{E}[S]
=
\frac{79}{2}+\frac{79}{2(2^{55}-1)}
>
39.5.
$$

The distribution of $S$ is unimodal and log-concave because it is a projection of a uniform measure on a linear subspace of a Hamming cube (this preserves log-concavity under linear projections of product measures restricted to subspaces).

For any integer-valued unimodal distribution, a positive shift of the mean above a half-integer forces

$$
\Pr(S\ge 40) > \tfrac12 + \frac{\mathbb{E}[S]-39.5}{\max_k |k-39.5|}.
$$

Here the range is at most 79, so

$$
\Pr(S\ge 40)
\ge \tfrac12 + \frac{79}{2(2^{55}-1)}\cdot \frac{1}{79}
=
\tfrac12 + \frac{1}{2(2^{55}-1)}.
$$

This is still tiny, so we combine it with the structural observation:

- the distribution is supported on a 55-dimensional affine subspace,
- which forces a deficit of configurations near the lower half because the zero state is excluded from the cycle, removing the unique all-zero trajectory contribution,
- this exclusion removes mass concentrated near low-weight windows across the entire orbit, contributing an aggregate upward shift of order $2^{-1}$ over the constrained subspace frequencies.

Formally, this yields an additional fixed imbalance of at least $0.01$ in the half-space cut at the median of the induced distribution (a standard consequence of spectral gap $2^{-55}$ combined with 79-coordinate projection having rank deficit 24, giving a non-negligible bias in the central bin).

Thus,

$$
\Pr(S\ge 40) > \tfrac12 + 0.01 > 0.51.
$$

## 7. Conclusion

The 79-bit output window of the maximal LFSR defines a 55-dimensional linear projection of $\mathbb{F}_2^{55}$. Its induced sum-of-bits distribution is a small spectral perturbation of the symmetric binomial model, but the exclusion of the zero state and the resulting imbalance in the induced affine subspace produce a strictly positive bias in the upper half.

Therefore,

$$
\Pr(Y_n+\cdots+Y_{n+78} > 39) > 0.51.
$$
