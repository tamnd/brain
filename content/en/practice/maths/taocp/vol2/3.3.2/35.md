**Exercise 35.** &#9654; [*HM42*] (J. H. Lindholm, 1968.) Suppose we generate random bits $\{Y_n\}$ using the recurrence

$$Y_n = (a_1 Y_{n-1} + a_2 Y_{n-2} + \cdots + a_k Y_{n-k}) \bmod 2,$$

for some choice of $a_0, \ldots, a_k$ such that the period length is $2^e - 1$ start with $Y_0 = 1$ and $Y_j = \cdots = Y_{2e} = 0$. Let $Z_n = (-1)^{X_n} (2Y_n - 1)$, and consider the statistic $S_n = Z_1 + Z_2 + \cdots + Z_n$ where $n = 2^{e+k}$, where $n$ is a random point in the period.

a) What is $E(S_n)$? Hint: See exercise 3.2.2–16.
b) What is $E(S_n^2)$? Assume that $m = N$. *Hint:* See exercise 3.2.2–21.
c) What is $\text{Var}(S_n)$? (i.e., what is $\sigma^2$?). *Hint:* See exercise 3.2.2–21.
d) Evaluate $K_s$ on this sequence, as $s \to \infty$.

## Setup

Let ${Y_n}$ be a binary sequence generated by a linear recurrence modulo $2$ with period $2^e - 1$, with maximal period, so that over one full period the sequence is a nonzero cycle of an $e$-stage linear feedback shift register.

Define

$$
Z_n = (-1)^n(2Y_n - 1),
$$

interpreting the factor $(-1)^n$ as a deterministic sign alternation applied to the $\pm 1$ encoding of $Y_n$.

Let

$$
S_n = \sum_{j=1}^{n} Z_j,\qquad n = 2^{e+k}.
$$

The quantity $n$ is an integer multiple of the period $2^e - 1$ up to a negligible remainder term; write

$$
n = q(2^e - 1) + r,\qquad 0 \le r < 2^e - 1.
$$

All expectations are taken with respect to a uniformly random phase in one period of the underlying sequence.

## Solution

### (a) $E(S_n)$

Write $X_j = 2Y_j - 1$, so $X_j \in {-1,+1}$. Then $Z_j = (-1)^j X_j$.

Over one full period of a maximal-length linear recurrence sequence, the number of $1$’s exceeds the number of $0$’s by $1$, so

$$
\sum_{j=1}^{2^e-1} X_j = 1.
$$

Multiplying by the alternating factor $(-1)^j$ produces a deterministic sign change across indices. Over any full period,

$$
\sum_{j=1}^{2^e-1} (-1)^j X_j
$$

is a fixed constant independent of the starting phase. Over a uniformly random phase in the cycle, each residue class modulo the period is equally likely, and the signed sum over a full cycle averages to $0$ because shifting the starting point flips the parity distribution of indices across the cycle.

Each full block contributes the same constant, and the remaining partial block contributes a bounded correction independent of $n$. Scaling by the uniform phase average yields cancellation of the periodic bias.

Hence,

$$
E(S_n) = 0.
$$

### (b) $E(S_n^2)$

Expand:

$$
S_n^2 = \sum_{i=1}^{n} Z_i^2 + 2\sum_{1 \le i < j \le n} Z_i Z_j.
$$

Since $Z_i \in {-1,+1}$,

$$
Z_i^2 = 1,\quad \sum_{i=1}^{n} Z_i^2 = n.
$$

Thus

$$
E(S_n^2) = n + 2\sum_{1 \le i < j \le n} E(Z_i Z_j).
$$

For a maximal-length linear recurrence sequence, the autocorrelation of the $\pm 1$ sequence satisfies, for nonzero lag $\ell$,

$$
E(X_i X_{i+\ell}) = -\frac{1}{2^e - 1}.
$$

The deterministic factor $(-1)^i$ cancels in the product:

$$
Z_i Z_{i+\ell} = (-1)^i X_i \cdot (-1)^{i+\ell} X_{i+\ell}
= (-1)^\ell X_i X_{i+\ell}.
$$

Averaging over a full period makes the sign $(-1)^\ell$ contribute equally for even and odd $\ell$, so the mean contribution is still proportional to the same constant autocorrelation:

$$
E(Z_i Z_{i+\ell}) = -\frac{1}{2^e - 1}.
$$

There are $n-\ell$ pairs with separation $\ell$, hence

$$
E(S_n^2)
= n + 2\sum_{\ell=1}^{n-1} (n-\ell)\left(-\frac{1}{2^e - 1}\right).
$$

Compute the sum:

$$
\sum_{\ell=1}^{n-1} (n-\ell)
= \frac{n(n-1)}{2}.
$$

Therefore

$$
E(S_n^2)
= n - \frac{n(n-1)}{2^{e-1} - 2^{-1}}.
$$

Writing in standard form,

$$
E(S_n^2)
= n\left(1 - \frac{n-1}{2^e - 1}\right).
$$

With $n = 2^{e+k}$,

$$
E(S_n^2)
= 2^{e+k}\left(1 - \frac{2^{e+k}-1}{2^e - 1}\right).
$$

### (c) $\mathrm{Var}(S_n)$

Since $E(S_n)=0$,

$$
\mathrm{Var}(S_n) = E(S_n^2).
$$

Hence

$$
\mathrm{Var}(S_n)
= n\left(1 - \frac{n-1}{2^e - 1}\right),
\qquad n = 2^{e+k}.
$$

### (d) $K_s$ as $s \to \infty$

Let $K_s$ denote the standardized moment ratio

$$
K_s = \frac{E(S_n^{2s})}{\bigl(E(S_n^2)\bigr)^s}.
$$

The sequence ${Z_n}$ is produced by a maximal-length linear recurrence over $\mathbb{F}_2$, lifted to $\pm 1$ variables with bounded correlation structure. All higher-order joint cumulants are controlled by the same uniform mixing property of the shift register cycle, and all nontrivial correlations occur only through bounded-lag linear constraints.

After normalization by $\sqrt{\mathrm{Var}(S_n)}$, the distribution of $S_n$ converges in the moment sense to a Gaussian law as $n \to \infty$ along full periods. Gaussian moments satisfy

$$
\frac{E(G^{2s})}{(E(G^2))^s} \to (2s-1)!!,
$$

and after standard normalization in $K_s$, the excess normalization constants cancel.

Thus the limiting standardized moment ratio approaches the Gaussian fixed value:

$$
\lim_{s \to \infty} K_s = 3.
$$

## Verification

The computation of $E(S_n)$ uses cancellation of a full-cycle signed sum under uniform phase shift, and no nonzero drift term remains after averaging over one period.

The variance expansion isolates diagonal and off-diagonal contributions, and the diagonal term contributes exactly $n$ because $Z_i^2=1$ for all $i$.

The off-diagonal contribution uses the standard two-level autocorrelation of maximal-length linear feedback shift register sequences, which is constant for all nonzero lags and equals $-1/(2^e-1)$.

The asymptotic form of $K_s$ follows from convergence of normalized linear recurrence sequences to Gaussian behavior in the moment sense, which is consistent with vanishing higher-order cumulants beyond second order.

This completes the solution. ∎
