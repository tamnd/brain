[Section 3.3.2: Empirical Tests](../)

**Exercise 2.** [*10*] State an appropriate way to generalize the tests for triples, quadruples, etc., instead of pairs.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m13s  

---

For triples, quadruples, or generally $k$ successive values, the serial test is formed by grouping the sequence $\langle Y_n \rangle$ into disjoint blocks of length $k$. Thus one uses the $n$ observations

$(Y_{kj}, Y_{kj+1}, \ldots, Y_{kj+k-1}), \quad 0 \le j < n,$

so that $kn$ terms of the sequence are consumed to produce $n$ $k$-tuples. For each ordered $k$-tuple $(q_1, \ldots, q_k)$ with $0 \le q_i < d$, the number of occurrences among these blocks is counted.

A chi-square test is then applied to the $d^k$ categories, each having probability $1/d^k$ under the hypothesis of independence and uniformity. The choice of $d$ must be reduced as $k$ increases so that $n$ remains large compared with $d^k$, ensuring that the expected frequencies in the chi-square test are sufficiently large.
