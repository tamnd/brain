**Corrected Solution for Exercise 3.3.2.15 [_M10_]**

In the maximum-of-$t$ test, we begin with a sequence of independent random variables $U_0, U_1, \ldots, U_{nt-1}$ that are uniformly distributed on $[0,1]$. We form blocks of length $t$ and define

$$
V_j = \max(U_{jt}, U_{jt+1}, \ldots, U_{jt+t-1}), \quad j = 0, 1, \ldots, n-1.
$$

To perform the uniformity test, we transform $V_j$ to

$$
V_j' = V_j^{t}.
$$

We now justify why $V_0', V_1', \ldots, V_{n-1}'$ are uniformly distributed on $[0,1]$.

### Step 1: Distribution of the maximum of $t$ independent uniform variables

Let $X_1, X_2, \ldots, X_t$ be independent random variables uniformly distributed on $[0,1]$. The cumulative distribution function (CDF) of the maximum $M = \max(X_1, \ldots, X_t)$ is

$$
\Pr(M \le x) = \Pr(X_1 \le x, \ldots, X_t \le x) = \Pr(X_1 \le x)\cdots \Pr(X_t \le x) = x^t, \quad 0 \le x \le 1.
$$

Thus, the probability density function (PDF) of $M$ is

$$
f_M(x) = \frac{d}{dx} x^t = t x^{t-1}, \quad 0 \le x \le 1.
$$

### Step 2: Transformation to a uniform variable

Define $V_j' = (V_j)^t$. Then for any $y \in [0,1]$,

$$
\Pr(V_j' \le y) = \Pr(V_j^t \le y) = \Pr(V_j \le y^{1/t}) = (y^{1/t})^t = y.
$$

Hence, $V_j'$ has the CDF of a standard uniform random variable on $[0,1]$. Its PDF is constant: $f_{V_j'}(y) = 1$ for $0 \le y \le 1$.
