Let $X_0,X_1,\dots$ be a full-period linear congruential sequence modulo $m$, and let the serial test of order $s$ partition the state space into $d^s$ equal cells. Write

$$
\mathbf{Y}_n=(X_n,\dots,X_{n+s-1}),
$$

so exactly $m$ overlapping $s$-tuples occur in one period.

Let $O_i$ be the number of tuples falling in cell $i$, and let

$$
E=\frac{m}{d^s}.
$$

Then the serial statistic is

$$
K_s=\sum_{i=1}^{d^s}\frac{(O_i-E)^2}{E},
\qquad \sum_{i=1}^{d^s} O_i = m,\quad O_i\in\mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}.
$$

### 1. Reduction to an occupancy problem

Let $k$ be the number of nonempty cells. Then $k\le m$. On the $k$ occupied cells, write the counts $O_1,\dots,O_k$, each $\ge 1$, with

$$
\sum_{i=1}^k O_i = m.
$$

The contribution from empty cells is fixed:

$$
(d^s-k)\frac{(0-E)^2}{E} = (d^s-k)E.
$$

Hence

$$
K_s = \sum_{i=1}^k \frac{(O_i-E)^2}{E} + (d^s-k)E.
$$

### 2. Minimizing the occupied-cell contribution

For fixed $k$, the function

$$
f(x)=\frac{(x-E)^2}{E}
$$

is convex in $x$. Therefore, subject to $\sum_{i=1}^k O_i=m$, the sum $\sum f(O_i)$ is minimized when the $O_i$ are as equal as possible. Ignoring integer rounding (which only increases the value), the minimum occurs at

$$
O_i \approx \frac{m}{k}.
$$

Thus a valid lower bound is obtained by substituting $O_i=m/k$:

$$
\sum_{i=1}^k \frac{(O_i-E)^2}{E}
\;\ge\;
k \cdot \frac{\left(\frac{m}{k}-\frac{m}{d^s}\right)^2}{m/d^s}.
$$

### 3. Simplification

Compute:

$$
\frac{\left(\frac{m}{k}-\frac{m}{d^s}\right)^2}{m/d^s}
=
\frac{m^2\left(\frac{1}{k}-\frac{1}{d^s}\right)^2}{m/d^s}
=
m d^s \left(\frac{1}{k}-\frac{1}{d^s}\right)^2.
$$

Multiplying by $k$,

$$
\sum_{i=1}^k \frac{(O_i-E)^2}{E}
\ge
m d^s \, k \left(\frac{1}{k}-\frac{1}{d^s}\right)^2.
$$

Expand:

$$
k\left(\frac{1}{k}-\frac{1}{d^s}\right)^2
=
\frac{1}{k} - \frac{2}{d^s} + \frac{k}{d^{2s}}.
$$

Thus

$$
\sum_{i=1}^k \frac{(O_i-E)^2}{E}
\ge
m d^s\left(\frac{1}{k} - \frac{2}{d^s} + \frac{k}{d^{2s}}\right)
=
\frac{m d^s}{k} - 2m + \frac{mk}{d^s}.
$$

Adding empty cells:

$$
(d^s-k)E = m - \frac{mk}{d^s}.
$$

Therefore

$$
K_s \ge \left(\frac{m d^s}{k} - 2m + \frac{mk}{d^s}\right) + \left(m - \frac{mk}{d^s}\right),
$$

so cancellation yields

$$
K_s \ge \frac{m d^s}{k} - m.
$$

### 4. Final bound

Since $k \le m$,

$$
\frac{m d^s}{k} \ge d^s.
$$

Hence

$$
K_s \ge d^s - m.
$$

### 5. Asymptotic behavior

Because $d \ge 2$ in any nontrivial serial test partition,

$$
d^s - m \to \infty \quad \text{as } s \to \infty.
$$

Thus

$$
K_s \to \infty \quad \text{as } s \to \infty.
$$

### Conclusion

$$
\boxed{K_s \ge d^s - m \quad \text{and hence} \quad K_s \to \infty \text{ as } s \to \infty.}
$$

The divergence follows solely from the fact that at most $m$ overlapping $s$-tuples can occupy $d^s$ cells, so the effective sparsity forces the chi-square statistic to grow exponentially with $s$, independent of any independence assumptions.
