**Exercise 3.** &#9654; [*M20*] How many $U$'s need to be examined in the gap test (Algorithm G) before $n$ gaps have been found, on the average, assuming that the sequence is random? What is the standard deviation of this quantity?

## Solution

Let ${U_j}$ be a sequence of independent and uniformly distributed random variables on $[0,1)$, and let $p = \beta - \alpha$ denote the probability that $U_j$ lies in the interval $[\alpha, \beta)$. Consider Algorithm G, which counts the number of gaps of length $r$, where a gap of length $r$ is a sequence of $r$ consecutive $U_j$ outside $[\alpha, \beta)$ followed by a $U_j$ inside $[\alpha, \beta)$.

Let $X$ be the number of $U$'s examined before a single gap is found. The probability that a gap has length $r$ is $(1-p)^r p$ for $r = 0,1,2,\dots$, as in equation (4). Therefore, $X = r + 1$, since we must examine the $r$ $U$'s outside the interval plus the one $U$ inside the interval. Hence, $X$ is a geometric random variable with success probability $p$, shifted by 1. More precisely, the distribution of $X$ is

$$
\Pr(X = r+1) = (1-p)^r p, \quad r = 0,1,2,\dots
$$

The expectation of $X$ is given by the standard formula for the geometric distribution:

$$
\mathbb{E}[X] = \sum_{r=0}^{\infty} (r+1) (1-p)^r p = \frac{1}{p}.
$$

Similarly, the variance of $X$ is

$$
\operatorname{Var}(X) = \sum_{r=0}^{\infty} (r+1)^2 (1-p)^r p - \frac{1}{p^2} = \frac{1-p}{p^2}.
$$

Let $X_1, X_2, \dots, X_n$ denote the number of $U$'s examined to find the first, second, …, $n$-th gaps. These are independent and identically distributed geometric random variables, since each gap occurs independently of the previous gaps in a truly random sequence. Then the total number of $U$'s examined to find $n$ gaps is

$$
S_n = X_1 + X_2 + \cdots + X_n.
$$

By linearity of expectation,

$$
\mathbb{E}[S_n] = n \mathbb{E}[X] = \frac{n}{p}.
$$

Since the $X_i$ are independent, the variance of $S_n$ is

$$
\operatorname{Var}(S_n) = n \operatorname{Var}(X) = n \frac{1-p}{p^2}.
$$

Hence the standard deviation of $S_n$ is

$$
\sigma(S_n) = \sqrt{\operatorname{Var}(S_n)} = \frac{\sqrt{n(1-p)}}{p}.
$$

In summary, the number of $U$'s that must be examined to find $n$ gaps has expectation and standard deviation

$$
\boxed{\mathbb{E}[S_n] = \frac{n}{p}, \quad \sigma(S_n) = \frac{\sqrt{n(1-p)}}{p}}.
$$

This completes the proof.
