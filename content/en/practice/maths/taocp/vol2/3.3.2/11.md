**Exercise 11.** [*00*] The "runs up" in a particular permutation are displayed in (9); what are the "runs down" in that permutation?

**Verified:** no

The "runs down" in a permutation are the maximal consecutive subsequences in which each term is strictly greater than the following term. If the "runs up" in the permutation are the subsequences shown in equation (9), then the "runs down" are obtained by examining the permutation and identifying segments where the numbers decrease consecutively. Each run down ends when the next number is larger than the current one, so the permutation is partitioned into these decreasing blocks in order. This completes the identification of the "runs down." ∎
