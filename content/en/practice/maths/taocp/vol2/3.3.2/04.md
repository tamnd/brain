**Exercise 4.** [*M12*] Prove that the probabilities in (4) are correct for the gap test.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m47s

## Solution

Let

$p=\beta-\alpha.$

For a random sequence $\langle U_n\rangle$, each value $U_j$ is independently distributed uniformly on $[0,1)$. Hence

$$
\qquad
\Pr(U_j\notin[\alpha,\beta))=1-p.
$$

Algorithm G defines a gap of length $r$ to be a subsequence

$$$$

such that

$$
\qquad
U_{j+r}\in[\alpha,\beta).
$$

Therefore a gap has length $r$ exactly when the first $r$ observations fail to lie in the interval $[\alpha,\beta)$ and the next observation succeeds.

Since the $U_j$ are independent,

$$
\Pr(\text{gap length}=r)
=
(1-p)^r\,p.
$$

Thus, for every $r$ with $0\le r\le t-1$,

$$
p_r=p(1-p)^r.
$$

The final category of the chi-square test combines all gap lengths at least $t$. Hence

$$
p_t
=
\Pr(\text{gap length}\ge t).
$$

A gap length is at least $t$ precisely when the first $t$ observations all fail to lie in $[\alpha,\beta)$. Independence gives

$$
p_t=(1-p)^t.
$$

Equivalently,

$$
p_t
=
\sum_{r=t}^{\infty} p(1-p)^r
=
p(1-p)^t\sum_{m=0}^{\infty}(1-p)^m
=
(1-p)^t.
$$

Therefore the probabilities used in the gap test are

$$
p_r=p(1-p)^r,\qquad 0\le r\le t-1,
$$

and

$$
p_t=(1-p)^t,
$$

which are exactly the probabilities stated in equation (4). This completes the proof.

∎
