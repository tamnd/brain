**Exercise 28.** [*M26*] Let $b_{n,r,s}(m)$ be the number of $n$-tuples $(y_1, \ldots, y_n)$ with $0 \le y_j < m$ that have exactly $r$ equal spacings and $s$ zero spacings. [Check: The probability that $R = r$ in the birthday spacings test is $\sum_{s \ge 0} b_{n,r,s}(m)/m^n$.] Also let $p_n(m)$ be the number of partitions of $m$ into at most $n$ parts (exercise 5.1.1–15). (a) Express $b_{n,0,0}(m)$ in terms of partitions. [*Hint*: Consider cases with small $m$ and $n$.] (b) Show that there is a simple relation between $b_{n,r,s}(m)$ and $b_{n,r+s,0}(m)$ when $s > 0$. (c) Deduce an explicit formula for the probability that no spacings are equal.

The original argument fails because it misidentifies the structure of spacings and incorrectly introduces a partition model that does not correspond to the tuple process. We rebuild the counting from the actual combinatorial structure of the problem.

# 1. Correct structural model

Let $(y_1,\dots,y_n)\in\{0,\dots,m-1\}^n$. Let $a_x$ be the occupancy numbers

$$
a_x = |\{j : y_j = x\}|,\qquad \sum_{x=0}^{m-1} a_x = n.
$$

Let

$$
k = |\{x : a_x > 0\}|
$$

be the number of occupied values.

Then:

- Each occupied value contributes one “cluster”.
- Within each cluster of size $a_x$, there are $a_x-1$ **zero spacings** (adjacent equal values after sorting).
- Hence

$$
s = \sum_x (a_x - 1) = n - k,
\quad\Rightarrow\quad k = n-s.
$$

Now arrange the $k$ occupied positions on the cyclic group $\mathbb{Z}_m$. Let their cyclic gaps (empty runs between consecutive occupied points) be

$$
g_1,\dots,g_k \ge 1,\qquad g_1+\cdots+g_k = m-k.
$$

Crucially:

- The statistic $r$ depends only on equalities among the positive gaps $g_i$.
- The multiplicities $a_x$ affect only $s$, not the gap pattern.

For a fixed set of $k$ occupied positions, the number of ways to assign multiplicities $(a_x)$ with $a_x\ge1$ is the number of compositions of $n$ into $k$ positive parts:

$$
\binom{n-1}{k-1}.
$$

This is independent of the spacing structure.

# 2. Reduction to the collision-free case

Let $b_{k,r,0}(m)$ denote configurations with:

- $k$ distinct occupied values,
- no zero spacings,
- exactly $r$ equal spacings among the cyclic gaps.

Then for general $n,r,s$ with $k=n-s$,

$$
\boxed{
b_{n,r,s}(m)
=
\binom{n-1}{n-s-1}\, b_{n-s,r,0}(m)
}
$$

This is the correct replacement for the flawed relation in part (b).

# 3. Part (a): evaluation of $b_{n,0,0}(m)$

Now assume $r=0, s=0$. Then:

- $k=n$: all values are distinct.
- There are $n$ occupied points on the circle.
- Their cyclic gaps $g_1,\dots,g_n$ satisfy

$$
g_i \ge 1,\qquad \sum g_i = m-n.
$$
- The condition $r=0$ means all $g_i$ are distinct.

Thus we count cyclic sequences of $n$ distinct positive integers summing to $m-n$.

### Step 1: linearization

Number of linear ordered $n$-tuples of distinct positive integers summing to $m-n$ is:

$$
n!\, p_n^*(m-n),
$$

where $p_n^*(\cdot)$ counts partitions into exactly $n$ distinct parts.

### Step 2: cyclic identification

Each cyclic gap configuration has exactly $n$ rotations corresponding to choice of starting point on the circle. Hence:

$$
\#(\text{cyclic gap patterns}) = \frac{n!}{n} p_n^*(m-n) = (n-1)! \, p_n^*(m-n).
$$

Each such cyclic gap pattern corresponds to exactly one subset of size $n$ in $\mathbb{Z}_m$, hence one configuration.

Therefore:

$$
\boxed{
b_{n,0,0}(m)
=
(n-1)!\, p_n^*(m-n)
}
$$

# 4. Part (b): relation between $b_{n,r,s}(m)$ and $b_{n-s,r,0}(m)$

From the structure above:

- Removing zero spacings removes $s$ duplicated elements.
- Leaves $k=n-s$ distinct occupied values.
- Does not change the gap configuration on those $k$ points.
- Adds a multiplicity factor $\binom{n-1}{k-1}$.

Thus:

$$
\boxed{
b_{n,r,s}(m)
=
\binom{n-1}{n-s-1}\, b_{n-s,r,0}(m)
\qquad (s>0)
}
$$

This replaces the incorrect binomial shift in $r$ used in the flawed solution.

# 5. Part (c): probability that no spacings are equal

We want $R=0$. Then:

$$
\mathbb{P}(R=0)
=
\frac{1}{m^n}
\sum_{s\ge 0} b_{n,0,s}(m).
$$

Use part (b) with $r=0$:

$$
b_{n,0,s}(m)
=
\binom{n-1}{n-s-1} b_{n-s,0,0}(m).
$$

Let $k=n-s$. Then $k=1,\dots,n$, and:

$$
\mathbb{P}(R=0)
=
\frac{1}{m^n}
\sum_{k=1}^n
\binom{n-1}{k-1}
b_{k,0,0}(m).
$$

Insert part (a):

$$
b_{k,0,0}(m) = (k-1)!\, p_k^*(m-k).
$$

Hence the final explicit formula:

$$
\boxed{
\mathbb{P}(R=0)
=
\frac{1}{m^n}
\sum_{k=1}^n
\binom{n-1}{k-1}(k-1)!\, p_k^*(m-k)
}
$$

# Final remarks on corrections

- The incorrect solution failed because it replaced occupancy structure with a false bijection to partitions of $m$, ignoring the dependence on $m-n$.
- The correct decomposition separates:

- occupancy (zero spacings),
- cyclic gaps (equal-spacing statistic),
- compositions of $n$ into occupied clusters.
- The key identity is the reduction

$$
b_{n,r,s}(m)\to b_{n-s,r,0}(m)
$$

with factor $\binom{n-1}{n-s-1}$, which restores the correct combinatorial structure.
