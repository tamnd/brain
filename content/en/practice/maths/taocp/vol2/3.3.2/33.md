[Section 3.3.2: Empirical Tests](../)

**Exercise 33.** [*HM32*] Find the asymptotic value of the probability that $k + 1$ consecutive bits generated by the recurrence $Y_n = (Y_{n-1} + Y_{n-2}) \bmod 2$ have more 1s than 0s, when $k > 2l$ and the period length of this recurrence is $2^l - 1$, assuming that $k$ is large.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m12s  

---

The core correction is that this is not a random-sampling problem. It is a deterministic cyclic sequence observed at a random starting position, so the only randomness is the shift on a fixed periodic structure. The correct analysis must therefore be done over the $N=2^l-1$ cyclic shifts of a fixed binary $m$-sequence, not via hypergeometric or CLT arguments.

## 1. Structure of the sequence

Let $(Y_n)$ be a maximal-length LFSR sequence (an $m$-sequence) of period

$$
N = 2^l - 1.
$$

Over one full period:

$$
\#\{1\} = 2^{l-1}, \quad \#\{0\} = 2^{l-1} - 1,
$$

so the mean fraction of ones is

$$
p = \frac{2^{l-1}}{2^l - 1} = \frac12 + \frac{1}{2N}.
$$

Fix a block length $n = k+1$, and choose a starting position uniformly in the cycle. Let $S_n(t)$ be the number of ones in the length-$n$ block starting at position $t$.

## 2. Decomposition over full periods

Write the Euclidean division

$$
n = qN + r, \quad 0 \le r < N.
$$

Then any length-$n$ block consists of:

- $q$ full periods, contributing exactly $q \cdot 2^{l-1}$ ones,
- plus a remainder block of length $r$, whose contribution depends on the starting position but is bounded.

Hence

$$
S_n(t) = q \cdot 2^{l-1} + R_t,
$$

where $R_t$ depends only on a block of length $r < N$, so

$$
R_t = \frac{r}{2} + O(N),
$$

uniformly in $t$, since any length-$r$ window in a fixed $N$-periodic binary sequence can vary by at most $O(N)$.

## 3. Compare with the threshold $n/2$

We compare $S_n(t)$ with $n/2$:

$$
\frac{n}{2} = \frac{qN + r}{2} = q\frac{N}{2} + \frac{r}{2}.
$$

Now subtract:

$$
S_n(t) - \frac{n}{2}
= q\left(2^{l-1} - \frac{N}{2}\right) + (R_t - \frac{r}{2}).
$$

Since $N = 2^l - 1$,

$$
2^{l-1} - \frac{N}{2}
= 2^{l-1} - \frac{2^l - 1}{2}
= \frac12.
$$

Therefore

$$
S_n(t) - \frac{n}{2}
= \frac{q}{2} + O(N).
$$

## 4. Asymptotic behavior

Since $q = \lfloor n/N \rfloor$,

$$
S_n(t) - \frac{n}{2} = \frac{n}{2N} + O(N).
$$

Now $N = 2^l - 1$ is fixed (with $l$ fixed), while $n = k+1 \to \infty$. Hence the linear term dominates:

$$
\frac{n}{2N} \to \infty.
$$

The fluctuation term $O(N)$ is constant, independent of $n$. Therefore, for sufficiently large $n$,

$$
S_n(t) > \frac{n}{2} \quad \text{for every starting position } t.
$$

## 5. Probability conclusion

Since the inequality eventually holds for all shifts $t \in \{0,1,\dots,N-1\}$,

$$
\Pr\{S_n > n/2\} = 1
\quad \text{for all sufficiently large } n.
$$

Hence the asymptotic value is

$$
\boxed{ \lim_{k \to \infty} \Pr\{S_{k+1} > (k+1)/2\} = 1. }
$$

## 6. Interpretation

The key point missed in the flawed solution is that this is a rigid periodic object. The slight excess of ones per period creates a deterministic linear drift:

- each full period contributes an excess of exactly $1/2$ one over half,
- so every additional period shifts the block sum upward by $1/2$,
- producing a linear divergence from the threshold $n/2$.

Thus randomness of the starting position becomes irrelevant asymptotically: the drift dominates all bounded boundary effects.
