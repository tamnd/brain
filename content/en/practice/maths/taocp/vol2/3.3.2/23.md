[Section 3.3.2: Empirical Tests](../)

**Exercise 23.** [*M22*] *[M22]* Let $(Y_n)$ and $(Y'_n)$ be integer sequences having period lengths $\lambda$ and $\lambda'$, respectively, with $0 \le Y_n, Y'_n < d$; also let $Z_n = (Y_n + Y'_{r+n}) \bmod d$, where $r$ is chosen at random between $0$ and $\lambda' - 1$. Show that $(Z_n)$ passes the $t$-dimensional serial test at least as well as $(Y_n)$ does, in the following sense: Let $P(x_1, \ldots, x_t)$ and $Q(x_1, \ldots, x_t)$ be the probabilities that the $t$-tuple $(x_1, \ldots, x_t)$ occurs in $(Y_n)$ and $(Z_n)$:

$$P(x_1, \ldots, x_t) = \frac{1}{\lambda} \sum_{n=0}^{\lambda-1} [(Y_n, Y_{n+1}, \ldots, Y_{n+t-1}) = (x_1, \ldots, x_t)];$$

$$Q(x_1, \ldots, x_t) = \frac{1}{\lambda \lambda'} \sum_{n=0}^{\lambda-1} \sum_{r=0}^{\lambda'-1} [(Z_n, Z_{n+1}, \ldots, Z_{n+t-1}) = (x_1, \ldots, x_t)].$$

Then $\displaystyle\sum_{(x_1,\ldots,x_t)} (Q(x_1,\ldots,x_t) - d^{-t})^2 \;\le\; \sum_{(x_1,\ldots,x_t)} (P(x_1,\ldots,x_t) - d^{-t})^2$.

Let

$$
G=(\mathbb Z/d\mathbb Z)^t,
$$

and for $x=(x_1,\ldots,x_t)\in G$ write

$$
u(x)=d^{-t}.
$$

The quantity

$$
\sum_x (P(x)-u(x))^2
$$

is the squared $L^2$-distance of $P$ from the uniform distribution on $G$. We shall show that the corresponding distance for $Q$ is no larger.

For each $n$, define the $t$-tuples

$$
A_n=(Y_n,Y_{n+1},\ldots,Y_{n+t-1}),
$$

and for each $r$,

$$
B_r=(Y'_r,Y'_{r+1},\ldots,Y'_{r+t-1}),
$$

where indices are taken modulo the respective periods. Then

$$
P(x)=\frac1\lambda\sum_{n=0}^{\lambda-1}[A_n=x].
$$

Similarly, since

$$
(Z_n,\ldots,Z_{n+t-1})
=A_n+B_{r+n}
\pmod d,
$$

we have

$$
Q(x)
=\frac1{\lambda\lambda'}
\sum_{n=0}^{\lambda-1}\sum_{r=0}^{\lambda'-1}
[A_n+B_{r+n}=x].
$$

Now, for each $b\in G$, let

$$
R(b)=\frac1{\lambda'}\sum_{s=0}^{\lambda'-1}[B_s=b].
$$

Thus $R$ is the empirical distribution of the $t$-tuples occurring in $Y'_n$. Since $s=r+n\pmod{\lambda'}$, the map $r\mapsto s$ is a permutation of $\{0,\ldots,\lambda'-1\}$ for fixed $n$. Hence

$$
\frac1{\lambda'}
\sum_{r=0}^{\lambda'-1}[B_{r+n}=b] =
R(b).
$$

Substituting this into the definition of $Q$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
Q(x)
&=
\frac1\lambda
\sum_{n=0}^{\lambda-1}
\sum_{b\in G}
R(b)\,[A_n+b=x] \\
&=
\sum_{b\in G}R(b)
\left(
\frac1\lambda
\sum_{n=0}^{\lambda-1}[A_n=x-b]
\right) \\
&=
\sum_{b\in G}R(b)P(x-b).
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore $Q=P*R$, the convolution of $P$ and $R$ on the finite abelian group $G$.

Now let $\widehat f(\chi)$ denote the Fourier transform on $G$:

$$
\widehat f(\chi) =
\sum_{x\in G} f(x)\chi(x),
$$

where $\chi$ runs over the characters of $G$.

Since $Q=P*R$,

$$
\widehat Q(\chi) =
\widehat P(\chi)\,\widehat R(\chi).
$$

Because $R$ is a probability distribution,

$$
\widehat R(\chi) =
\sum_{x\in G}R(x)\chi(x),
$$

and therefore

$$
|\widehat R(\chi)|
\le
\sum_{x\in G}R(x) =
1.
$$

Hence

$$
|\widehat Q(\chi)|^2 =
|\widehat P(\chi)|^2\,|\widehat R(\chi)|^2
\le
|\widehat P(\chi)|^2 .
$$

The uniform distribution $u$ satisfies

$$
\widehat u(\chi)=0
\qquad (\chi\neq 1),
$$

and

$$
\widehat u(1)=1.
$$

By Parseval's identity,

$$
\sum_x (P(x)-u(x))^2 =
\frac1{|G|}
\sum_{\chi\ne 1}
|\widehat P(\chi)|^2,
$$

and similarly

$$
\sum_x (Q(x)-u(x))^2 =
\frac1{|G|}
\sum_{\chi\ne 1}
|\widehat Q(\chi)|^2.
$$

Using the inequality above,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\sum_x (Q(x)-u(x))^2
&=
\frac1{|G|}
\sum_{\chi\ne 1}
|\widehat Q(\chi)|^2 \\
&\le
\frac1{|G|}
\sum_{\chi\ne 1}
|\widehat P(\chi)|^2 \\
&=
\sum_x (P(x)-u(x))^2.
\end{aligned}
$$

Since $|G|=d^t$, this is exactly

$$
\sum_{(x_1,\ldots,x_t)}
\bigl(Q(x_1,\ldots,x_t)-d^{-t}\bigr)^2
\le
\sum_{(x_1,\ldots,x_t)}
\bigl(P(x_1,\ldots,x_t)-d^{-t}\bigr)^2.
$$

Thus $(Z_n)$ passes the $t$-dimensional serial test at least as well as $(Y_n)$. ∎
