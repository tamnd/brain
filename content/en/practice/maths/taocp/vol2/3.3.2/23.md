[Section 3.3.2: Empirical Tests](../)

**Exercise 23.** [*M22*] *[M22]* Let $(Y_n)$ and $(Y'_n)$ be integer sequences having period lengths $\lambda$ and $\lambda'$, respectively, with $0 \le Y_n, Y'_n < d$; also let $Z_n = (Y_n + Y'_{r+n}) \bmod d$, where $r$ is chosen at random between $0$ and $\lambda' - 1$. Show that $(Z_n)$ passes the $t$-dimensional serial test at least as well as $(Y_n)$ does, in the following sense: Let $P(x_1, \ldots, x_t)$ and $Q(x_1, \ldots, x_t)$ be the probabilities that the $t$-tuple $(x_1, \ldots, x_t)$ occurs in $(Y_n)$ and $(Z_n)$:

$$P(x_1, \ldots, x_t) = \frac{1}{\lambda} \sum_{n=0}^{\lambda-1} [(Y_n, Y_{n+1}, \ldots, Y_{n+t-1}) = (x_1, \ldots, x_t)];$$

$$Q(x_1, \ldots, x_t) = \frac{1}{\lambda \lambda'} \sum_{n=0}^{\lambda-1} \sum_{r=0}^{\lambda'-1} [(Z_n, Z_{n+1}, \ldots, Z_{n+t-1}) = (x_1, \ldots, x_t)].$$

Then $\displaystyle\sum_{(x_1,\ldots,x_t)} (Q(x_1,\ldots,x_t) - d^{-t})^2 \;\le\; \sum_{(x_1,\ldots,x_t)} (P(x_1,\ldots,x_t) - d^{-t})^2$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m20s

## Solution

Let $G = (\mathbb{Z}/d\mathbb{Z})^t$. Each $t$-tuple $(x_1,\ldots,x_t)$ is identified with an element $x \in G$. Let $u(x) = d^{-t}$ denote the uniform distribution on $G$.

Define

$P(x) = P(x_1,\ldots,x_t), \qquad Q(x) = Q(x_1,\ldots,x_t).$

Let $P_Y$ be the distribution of $(Y_n,\ldots,Y_{n+t-1})$ over one full period, and let $P_{Y'}$ be the corresponding distribution for $(Y'_n,\ldots,Y'_{n+t-1})$. Periodicity implies these distributions are independent of $n$.

For fixed $r$, define

$Z^{(r)}_n = (Y_n + Y'_{r+n},\ldots,Y_{n+t-1} + Y'_{r+n+t-1}) \pmod d.$

For a fixed $r$, the map

$(a_1,\ldots,a_t),(b_1,\ldots,b_t) \mapsto (a_1+b_1,\ldots,a_t+b_t)$

is coordinatewise addition in $G$, so the distribution of $(Z^{(r)}_n,\ldots,Z^{(r)}_{n+t-1})$ is the convolution

$P * P_{Y'}.$

The shift $r$ only permutes indices in the periodic sequence $(Y'_n)$, hence it does not change the distribution of $t$-tuples, so the convolution is independent of $r$. Therefore

$Q = P * P_{Y'}.$

Let $\widehat{f}$ denote the Fourier transform on the finite abelian group $G$. For any function $f : G \to \mathbb{C}$,

$\widehat{f}(\chi) = \sum_{x \in G} f(x)\chi(x),$

where $\chi$ ranges over characters of $G$.

For probability distributions $f,g$ on $G$, convolution satisfies

$\widehat{f*g}(\chi) = \widehat{f}(\chi)\widehat{g}(\chi).$

The uniform distribution satisfies

\begin{cases} 1, & \chi = \chi_0,\\ 0, & \chi \ne \chi_0. \end{cases}$$
Hence for the nontrivial characters $\chi \ne \chi_0$,
$$\widehat{Q}(\chi) = \widehat{P}(\chi)\widehat{P_{Y'}}(\chi).$$
Parseval's identity gives
$$\sum_{x \in G} (f(x) - u(x))^2 = \frac{1}{|G|}\sum_{\chi \ne \chi_0} |\widehat{f}(\chi)|^2.$$
Applying this to $P$ and $Q$,
$$\sum_x (P(x)-u(x))^2 = \frac{1}{|G|}\sum_{\chi \ne \chi_0} |\widehat{P}(\chi)|^2,$$ $$\sum_x (Q(x)-u(x))^2 = \frac{1}{|G|}\sum_{\chi \ne \chi_0} |\widehat{P}(\chi)|^2\,|\widehat{P_{Y'}}(\chi)|^2.$$
For every character $\chi$, since $P_{Y'}$ is a probability distribution,
$$|\widehat{P_{Y'}}(\chi)| \le \sum_{x \in G} P_{Y'}(x) |\chi(x)| = 1.$$
Therefore each term satisfies
$$|\widehat{P}(\chi)|^2\,|\widehat{P_{Y'}}(\chi)|^2 \le |\widehat{P}(\chi)|^2,$$
and summing over all nontrivial $\chi$ yields
$$\sum_x (Q(x)-u(x))^2 \le \sum_x (P(x)-u(x))^2.$$ This completes the proof. ∎
