[Section 3.3.2: Empirical Tests](../)

**Exercise 25.** &#9654; [**] [HM30] Evaluate $K_s$ for a general finite Markov chain.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m09s  

---

Let the finite Markov chain have state space ${0,1,\ldots,d-1}$, transition matrix $P=(p_{ij})$, and stationary distribution $\pi=(\pi_i)$ satisfying $\pi_j=\sum_i \pi_i p_{ij}$. The serial test on pairs compares the empirical distribution of successive pairs $(Y_n,Y_{n+1})$ with the product distribution $\pi_i \pi_j$.

Under stationarity, the probability of observing the pair $(i,j)$ at a given position is $\pi_i p_{ij}$. The chi-square form underlying the statistic $K_s$ is therefore the divergence between $\pi_i p_{ij}$ and $\pi_i \pi_j$, namely

$$
K_s \;=\; \sum_{i=0}^{d-1}\sum_{j=0}^{d-1} \frac{(\pi_i p_{ij}-\pi_i \pi_j)^2}{\pi_i \pi_j}.
$$

Factoring $\pi_i$ from the numerator and denominator yields

$$
K_s \;=\; \sum_{i=0}^{d-1}\sum_{j=0}^{d-1} \pi_i \frac{(p_{ij}-\pi_j)^2}{\pi_j}.
$$

This expresses $K_s$ as a weighted chi-square distance between each transition row $(p_{ij})_j$ and the stationary distribution $\pi$, averaged with respect to $\pi_i$. This completes the evaluation of $K_s$ for a general finite Markov chain. ∎
