**Exercise 8.** &#9654; [*M22*] How many $U$'s need to be found on the coupon collector's test, on the average, before $n$ complete sets have been found by Algorithm C, assuming that the sequence is random? What is the standard deviation? [*Hint:* See Eq. 1.2.9–(28).]

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m56s  

---

Let $T$ be the number of observations needed to obtain one complete set of $d$ coupons under Algorithm C. Each time a complete set is obtained, the process restarts from a fresh independent random sequence, so the total number of observations needed to obtain $n$ complete sets is

$$
S_n = T_1 + T_2 + \cdots + T_n,
$$

where $T_1, \dots, T_n$ are i.i.d. copies of $T$. Therefore,

$$
\mathbb{E}[S_n] = n\,\mathbb{E}[T], \qquad \mathrm{Var}(S_n) = n\,\mathrm{Var}(T).
$$

It remains to compute $\mathbb{E}[T]$ and $\mathrm{Var}(T)$.

We analyze one complete set. Let $X_i$ be the number of observations needed to increase the number of distinct coupons from $i-1$ to $i$, for $i = 1, \dots, d$. Then

$$
T = X_1 + X_2 + \cdots + X_d.
$$

When $i-1$ distinct coupons have already been seen, there are $d-(i-1) = d-i+1$ unseen coupons. Each observation is uniformly distributed over the $d$ possible types, so the probability that the next observation yields a new coupon is

$$
p_i = \frac{d-i+1}{d}.
$$

The waiting time $X_i$ is therefore geometric with success probability $p_i$, meaning

$$
\mathbb{E}[X_i] = \frac{1}{p_i} = \frac{d}{d-i+1}, \qquad \mathrm{Var}(X_i) = \frac{1-p_i}{p_i^2}.
$$

### Expectation

Using linearity of expectation,

$$
\mathbb{E}[T] = \sum_{i=1}^d \mathbb{E}[X_i]
= \sum_{i=1}^d \frac{d}{d-i+1}.
$$

Reindex with $k = d-i+1$, giving $k = 1, \dots, d$:

$$
\mathbb{E}[T] = d \sum_{k=1}^d \frac{1}{k} = d H_d,
$$

where $H_d$ is the $d$-th harmonic number.

Thus,

$$
\mathbb{E}[S_n] = n d H_d.
$$

### Variance

Since the waiting times $X_i$ depend on disjoint sets of future observations in the renewal structure of Algorithm C, they are independent, so

$$
\mathrm{Var}(T) = \sum_{i=1}^d \mathrm{Var}(X_i).
$$

For a geometric random variable,

$$
\mathrm{Var}(X_i) = \frac{1-p_i}{p_i^2}.
$$

Substituting $p_i = \frac{d-i+1}{d}$,

$$
\mathrm{Var}(X_i)
= \frac{1 - \frac{d-i+1}{d}}{\left(\frac{d-i+1}{d}\right)^2}
= \frac{\frac{i-1}{d}}{\frac{(d-i+1)^2}{d^2}}
= \frac{d(i-1)}{(d-i+1)^2}.
$$

Hence

$$
\mathrm{Var}(T)
= \sum_{i=1}^d \frac{d(i-1)}{(d-i+1)^2}.
$$

Reindex with $k = d-i+1$, so $i-1 = d-k$, giving

$$
\mathrm{Var}(T)
= d \sum_{k=1}^d \frac{d-k}{k^2}
= d \left( d \sum_{k=1}^d \frac{1}{k^2} - \sum_{k=1}^d \frac{1}{k} \right).
$$

Thus,

$$
\mathrm{Var}(T)
= d^2 H_d^{(2)} - d H_d,
$$

where $H_d^{(2)} = \sum_{k=1}^d \frac{1}{k^2}$.

Therefore,

$$
\mathrm{Var}(S_n) = n \left(d^2 H_d^{(2)} - d H_d\right).
$$

### Standard deviation

The standard deviation is

$$
\mathrm{SD}(S_n)
= \sqrt{n \left(d^2 H_d^{(2)} - d H_d\right)}.
$$

### Final results

$$
\mathbb{E}[S_n] = n d H_d,
\qquad
\mathrm{SD}(S_n) = \sqrt{n \left(d^2 H_d^{(2)} - d H_d\right)}.
$$
