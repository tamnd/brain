[Section 4.6.1: Division of Polynomials](../)

**Exercise 7.** [*M10*] What are the units in the domain of all polynomials over a unique factorization domain?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m28s  

---

Let $f(x)$ be a unit in the polynomial ring over a unique factorization domain $S$. Then there exists a polynomial $g(x)$ such that $f(x)g(x)=1$. Since $\deg(fg)=\deg(f)+\deg(g)$ whenever both factors are nonzero, property (i) of a unique factorization domain implies that a nonconstant polynomial cannot have a polynomial inverse. Hence $\deg(f)=\deg(g)=0$, so $f(x)$ and $g(x)$ are constant polynomials. Therefore every unit of the polynomial ring is a unit of $S$.

Conversely, if $a$ is a unit of $S$, say $ab=1$ for some $b\in S$, then the constant polynomials $a$ and $b$ satisfy $a\cdot b=1$. Hence $a$ is a unit in the polynomial ring.

Therefore the units in the domain of all polynomials over a unique factorization domain $S$ are precisely the constant polynomials whose coefficients are units of $S$.

$$
\boxed{\text{Units of }S[x]\text{ are exactly the units of }S\text{, viewed as constant polynomials.}}
$$
