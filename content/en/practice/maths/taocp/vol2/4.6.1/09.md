**Solution to Exercise 4.6.1.9 [_M25_]:**

Let $S$ be a unique factorization domain (UFD), and let $u(z),v(z)\in S[z]$ be primitive polynomials. We are to show that $u(z)$ and $v(z)$ are relatively prime if and only if there exist polynomials $U(z),V(z)\in S[z]$ such that

$$
u(z)V(z) + U(z)v(z)
$$

is a nonzero constant polynomial.

**Forward direction:** Assume that $u(z)$ and $v(z)$ are relatively prime. We want to construct $U(z)$ and $V(z)$ such that $u(z)V(z) + U(z)v(z)$ is a constant.

1. Since $S$ is a UFD and $u(z)$ and $v(z)$ are primitive, Gauss's lemma (Proposition 4.6.1 in TAOCP) implies that any common factor of $u(z)$ and $v(z)$ in $S[z]$ must divide a constant polynomial. Being relatively prime means that the only such common factor is a unit in $S$.
2. Apply the Euclidean Algorithm (Algorithm E) in $S[z]$ to $u(z)$ and $v(z]$. Let $r_0 = u(z)$ and $r_1 = v(z)$, and recursively compute

$$
r_{i-1} = q_i(z) r_i + r_{i+1}, \quad \deg r_{i+1} < \deg r_i
$$

until some $r_k$ is a nonzero constant $c\in S$ and $r_{k+1} = 0$.

1. Since $u(z)$ and $v(z)$ are relatively prime, the Euclidean Algorithm terminates with a remainder $r_k$ that is a unit multiple of a constant polynomial (degree zero). That is, there exist polynomials $U(z),V(z)\in S[z]$ obtained by back-substitution in the extended Euclidean Algorithm such that

$$
U(z)v(z) + V(z)u(z) = r_k,
$$

and $r_k$ is a constant.

1. This construction explicitly provides the required $U(z)$ and $V(z)$. Therefore, the forward implication holds.

**Converse direction:** Assume there exist $U(z),V(z)\in S[z]$ such that

$$
u(z)V(z) + U(z)v(z) = c
$$

for some nonzero constant $c\in S$. We want to show that $u(z)$ and $v(z)$ are relatively prime.

1. Suppose, for contradiction, that $u(z)$ and $v(z)$ have a nontrivial common factor $d(z)\in S[z]$ of positive degree. Then $d(z)$ divides both $u(z)$ and $v(z)$.
2. Since $d(z)$ divides $u(z)$ and $v(z)$, it must also divide any linear combination:

$$
u(z)V(z) + U(z)v(z) = c.
$$

1. But $c$ is a nonzero constant. The only divisors of a nonzero constant in $S[z]$ are units of $S$. Therefore, $d(z)$ must be a unit. This contradicts the assumption that $d(z)$ is a nontrivial common factor.
2. Hence, $u(z)$ and $v(z)$ cannot have any nontrivial common factor, so they are relatively prime.

**Conclusion:** We have shown both directions:

- If $u(z)$ and $v(z)$ are relatively prime, the extended Euclidean Algorithm produces polynomials $U(z),V(z)\in S[z]$ such that $u(z)V(z) + U(z)v(z)$ is a constant.
- If such $U(z)$ and $V(z)$ exist with a constant sum, then $u(z)$ and $v(z)$ must be relatively prime.

Thus, $u(z)$ and $v(z)$ are relatively prime if and only if there exist polynomials $U(z),V(z)$ over $S$ such that $u(z)V(z) + U(z)v(z)$ has degree zero.
