[Section 4.6.1: Division of Polynomials](../)

**Exercise 11.** [*M22*] What row names would have appeared in Table 1 if the sequence of degrees had been 9, 6, 5, 2, $-\infty$ instead of 8, 6, 4, 2, 1, 0?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 4m51s  

---

## Solution

The question refers to the row-naming convention of Table 1 in §4.6.1. In that table, the rows are labeled by consecutive letters corresponding to successive degree levels, beginning with the highest degree.

For the original degree sequence

$$
8,6,4,2,1,0,
$$

the table contains degree levels

$$
8,7,6,5,4,3,2,1,0,
$$

and these are named

$$
A,B,C,D,E,F,G,H,I,
$$

respectively.

Hence the correspondence is

$$
\begin{array}{c|ccccccccc}
\text{degree} & 8&7&6&5&4&3&2&1&0\\
\hline
\text{row name} & A&B&C&D&E&F&G&H&I
\end{array}
$$

If the degree sequence is changed to

$$
9,6,5,2,-\infty,
$$

then the degree levels present in the table are

$$
9,8,7,6,5,4,3,2,
$$

before the zero polynomial is reached. These levels receive the consecutive row names

$$
A,B,C,D,E,F,G,H.
$$

The actual remainders occur at degrees $9,6,5,$ and $2$. Using the correspondence above,

$$
9 \leftrightarrow A,\qquad
6 \leftrightarrow D,\qquad
5 \leftrightarrow E,\qquad
2 \leftrightarrow H.
$$

Therefore the row names that would appear in Table 1 are

$$
\boxed{A,\ D,\ E,\ H}.
$$

These are the rows corresponding to the degrees $9,6,5,$ and $2$; the process then terminates with the zero polynomial $(-\infty)$.
