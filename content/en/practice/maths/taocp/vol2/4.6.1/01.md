**Exercise 4.6.1.1** [_10_]

We are asked to compute the pseudo-quotient $q(x)$ and pseudo-remainder $r(x)$ for the polynomials

$$
u(x) = x^6 + x^5 - x^4 + 2x^3 + 3x^2 - x + 2, \quad 
v(x) = 2x^2 + 2x^2 - x + 3,
$$

over the integers.

## Step 1: Simplify the divisor

First, we simplify $v(x)$:

$$
v(x) = 2x^2 + 2x^2 - x + 3 = 4x^2 - x + 3.
$$

So the divisor is

$$
v(x) = 4x^2 - x + 3.
$$

The degree of $u(x)$ is $6$ and the degree of $v(x)$ is $2$.

## Step 2: Recall the pseudo-division formula

For polynomials over the integers, the pseudo-division identity states that for polynomials $u(x)$ and $v(x)$ with $\deg u = m \ge n = \deg v$ and leading coefficient of $v(x)$ equal to $b$, there exist polynomials $q(x)$ and $r(x)$ with integer coefficients such that

$$
b^{m-n+1} u(x) = q(x) v(x) + r(x), \quad \deg r < \deg v.
$$

Here the leading coefficient of $v(x)$ is $b = 4$, $m = 6$, $n = 2$, so $m-n+1 = 5$. Therefore we will compute $q(x)$ and $r(x)$ satisfying

$$
4^5 u(x) = q(x) v(x) + r(x), \quad \deg r < 2.
$$

Note that $4^5 = 1024$.

## Step 3: Initialize pseudo-division

We perform pseudo-division analogously to standard long division, but we multiply the dividend at each step by the leading coefficient of the divisor as needed to maintain integer coefficients.

Let us denote

$$
U_0 = 4^5 u(x) = 1024 u(x) = 1024 x^6 + 1024 x^5 - 1024 x^4 + 2048 x^3 + 3072 x^2 - 1024 x + 2048.
$$

We divide by $v(x) = 4x^2 - x + 3$.

### Step 3a: Divide leading term

The leading term of $U_0$ is $1024 x^6$. The leading term of $v(x)$ is $4x^2$. Compute the first term of the pseudo-quotient:

$$
\frac{1024 x^6}{4 x^2} = 256 x^4.
$$

Multiply $v(x)$ by $256 x^4$:

$$
256 x^4 \cdot (4x^2 - x + 3) = 1024 x^6 - 256 x^5 + 768 x^4.
$$

Subtract this from $U_0$:

$$
\begin{aligned}
U_1 &= U_0 - 256 x^4 v(x) \\
&= (1024 x^6 + 1024 x^5 - 1024 x^4 + \cdots) - (1024 x^6 - 256 x^5 + 768 x^4 + \cdots) \\
&= 1024 x^5 - 1792 x^4 + 2048 x^3 + 3072 x^2 - 1024 x + 2048.
\end{aligned}
$$

### Step 3b: Next term of quotient

Divide leading term $1024 x^5$ by $4 x^2$:

$$
1024 x^5 / 4 x^2 = 256 x^3.
$$

Multiply $v(x)$ by $256 x^3$:

$$
256 x^3 \cdot (4x^2 - x + 3) = 1024 x^5 - 256 x^4 + 768 x^3.
$$

Subtract:

$$
U_2 = U_1 - 256 x^3 v(x) = (-1792 + 256) x^4 + (2048 - 768) x^3 + \cdots = -1536 x^4 + 1280 x^3 + 3072 x^2 - 1024 x + 2048.
$$

### Step 3c: Next term

Divide leading term $-1536 x^4$ by $4x^2$:

$$
-1536 x^4 / 4 x^2 = -384 x^2.
$$

Multiply $v(x)$ by $-384 x^2$:

$$
-384 x^2 \cdot (4x^2 - x + 3) = -1536 x^4 + 384 x^3 - 1152 x^2.
$$

Subtract:

$$
U_3 = U_2 - (-384 x^2 v(x)) = (1280 - 384) x^3 + (3072 + 1152) x^2 - 1024 x + 2048 = 896 x^3 + 4224 x^2 - 1024 x + 2048.
$$

### Step 3d: Next term

Divide leading term $896 x^3$ by $4 x^2$:

$$
896 x^3 / 4 x^2 = 224 x.
$$

Multiply $v(x)$ by $224 x$:

$$
224 x \cdot (4x^2 - x + 3) = 896 x^3 - 224 x^2 + 672 x.
$$

Subtract:

$$
U_4 = (896 x^3 + 4224 x^2 - 1024 x + 2048) - (896 x^3 - 224 x^2 + 672 x) = 4448 x^2 - 1696 x + 2048.
$$

### Step 3e: Next term

Divide leading term $4448 x^2$ by $4x^2$:

$$
4448 x^2 / 4 x^2 = 1112.
$$

Multiply $v(x)$ by $1112$:

$$
1112 \cdot (4x^2 - x + 3) = 4448 x^2 - 1112 x + 3336.
$$

Subtract:

$$
U_5 = (4448 x^2 - 1696 x + 2048) - (4448 x^2 - 1112 x + 3336) = -584 x - 1288.
$$

The degree of $U_5$ is 1, which is less than $\deg v = 2$, so we have the pseudo-remainder:

$$
r(x) = -584 x - 1288.
$$

## Step 4: Collect the pseudo-quotient

The pseudo-quotient is

$$
q(x) = 256 x^4 + 256 x^3 - 384 x^2 + 224 x + 1112.
$$

## Step 5: Verify degrees

$\deg r = 1 < 2 = \deg v$, consistent with the pseudo-division definition.

## Step 6: State the pseudo-division identity

The defining pseudo-division identity is

$$
4^5 u(x) = q(x) v(x) + r(x),
$$

with

$$
q(x) = 256 x^4 + 256 x^3 - 384 x^2 + 224 x + 1112, \quad r(x) = -584 x - 1288.
$$

This satisfies the requirement of integer coefficients and $\deg r < \deg v$.

### Answer

$$
\boxed{
q(x) = 256 x^4 + 256 x^3 - 384 x^2 + 224 x + 1112, \quad
r(x) = -584 x - 1288
}
$$
