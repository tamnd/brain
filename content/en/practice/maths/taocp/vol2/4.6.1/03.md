**Exercise 3.** &#9654; [*M25*] Show that Euclid's algorithm for polynomials over a field $S$ can be extended to find polynomials $U(x)$ and $V(x)$ over $S$ such that

$$u(x)V(x) + U(x)v(x) = \gcd(u(x), v(x)).$$

(See Algorithm 4.5.2X.) What are the degrees of the polynomials $U(x)$ and $V(x)$ that are computed by this extended algorithm? Prove that if $S$ is the field of rational numbers, and if $u(x) = x^n - 1$ and $v(x) = x^n - 1$, then the extended algorithm yields polynomials $U(x)$ and $V(x)$ having *integer* coefficients. Find $U(x)$ and $V(x)$ when $u(x) = x^{11} - 1$ and $v(x) = x^9 - 1$.

## Solution

We are asked to compute the pseudo-quotient $q(x)$ and pseudo-remainder $r(x)$ over the integers for

$$
u(x) = x^6 + x^5 - x^4 + 2x^3 + 3x^2 - x + 2, \qquad 
v(x) = 2x^2 + 2x^2 - x + 3.
$$

First, we simplify $v(x)$ by combining like terms:

$$
v(x) = (2x^2 + 2x^2) - x + 3 = 4x^2 - x + 3.
$$

The leading coefficient of $v(x)$ is $v_2 = 4$. Since we are working over the integers, we must perform pseudo-division: we multiply $u(x)$ by $v_2^{m-n+1} = 4^{6-2+1} = 4^5 = 1024$ to ensure integer coefficients in the quotient and remainder. Let us denote this scaled polynomial by $u^*(x) = 1024 \cdot u(x)$. Then the pseudo-division relation is

$$
v_2^{m-n+1} \, u(x) = q(x) v(x) + r(x),
$$

where $\deg(r) < \deg(v) = 2$.

We perform Algorithm D over the integers with scaling incorporated:

1. Initialize $u^*(x) = 1024 x^6 + 1024 x^5 - 1024 x^4 + 2048 x^3 + 3072 x^2 - 1024 x + 2048$.

Set $m = 6$, $n = 2$.
2. Iterate $k = m - n, \dots, 0$; here $k = 4, 3, 2, 1, 0$.

**Step k = 4:**

Compute $q_4 = u^*_{6} / v_2 = 1024 / 4 = 256$.

Subtract $q_4 x^4 v(x) = 256 (4x^6 - x^5 + 3x^4) = 1024 x^6 - 256 x^5 + 768 x^4$ from $u^*(x)$:

$$
\begin{aligned}
u^*(x) &\gets u^*(x) - q_4 x^4 v(x) \\
&= (1024 x^6 + 1024 x^5 - 1024 x^4 + 2048 x^3 + 3072 x^2 - 1024 x + 2048) \\
&\quad - (1024 x^6 - 256 x^5 + 768 x^4) \\
&= 0 x^6 + (1024 + 256) x^5 + (-1024 - 768) x^4 + 2048 x^3 + 3072 x^2 - 1024 x + 2048 \\
&= 1280 x^5 - 1792 x^4 + 2048 x^3 + 3072 x^2 - 1024 x + 2048.
\end{aligned}
$$

**Step k = 3:**

$q_3 = u^*_5 / v_2 = 1280 / 4 = 320$.

Subtract $q_3 x^3 v(x) = 320 (4x^5 - x^4 + 3x^3) = 1280 x^5 - 320 x^4 + 960 x^3$:

$$
u^*(x) \gets (-1792 - (-320)) x^4 + (2048 - 960) x^3 + 3072 x^2 - 1024 x + 2048 = -1472 x^4 + 1088 x^3 + 3072 x^2 - 1024 x + 2048.
$$

**Step k = 2:**

$q_2 = -1472 / 4 = -368$.

Subtract $-368 x^2 v(x) = -368 (4x^4 - x^3 + 3x^2) = -1472 x^4 + 368 x^3 - 1104 x^2$:

$$
u^*(x) \gets (1088 - 368) x^3 + (3072 - (-1104)) x^2 - 1024 x + 2048 = 720 x^3 + 4176 x^2 - 1024 x + 2048.
$$

**Step k = 1:**

$q_1 = 720 / 4 = 180$.

Subtract $180 x v(x) = 180 (4x^3 - x^2 + 3x) = 720 x^3 - 180 x^2 + 540 x$:

$$
u^*(x) \gets (4176 + 180) x^2 + (-1024 - 540) x + 2048 = 4356 x^2 - 1564 x + 2048.
$$

**Step k = 0:**

$q_0 = 4356 / 4 = 1089$.

Subtract $1089 v(x) = 1089 (4 x^2 - x + 3) = 4356 x^2 - (-1089)(-? Wait careful)$

Compute carefully: $1089 v(x) = 1089 * 4 x^2 - 1089 * (-1?)?? + 1089 *3$

Check $v(x) = 4x^2 - x + 3$

Multiply: $1089 *4 x^2 = 4356 x^2$, $1089 * (-1) x = -1089 x$, $1089*3 = 3267$.

Subtract from $u^*(x) = 4356 x^2 -1564 x + 2048$:

$$
r(x) = (4356 - 4356) x^2 + (-1564 - (-1089)) x + (2048 - 3267) = -475 x - 1219.
$$

Hence the pseudo-remainder is $r(x) = -475 x - 1219$, and the pseudo-quotient is

$$
q(x) = 256 x^4 + 320 x^3 - 368 x^2 + 180 x + 1089.
$$

All coefficients are integers, as expected for pseudo-division over $\mathbb{Z}$.

$$
\boxed{q(x) = 256 x^4 + 320 x^3 - 368 x^2 + 180 x + 1089, \quad r(x) = -475 x - 1219}
$$

This completes the computation. ∎
