**Exercise 8.** &#9654; [*M22*] Show that if a polynomial with integer coefficients is irreducible over the domain of integers, it is irreducible when considered as a polynomial over the field of rational numbers.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m45s  

---

## Solution

Let $f(x)$ be a polynomial with integer coefficients, and suppose that $f(x)$ is irreducible over the domain of integers. We must prove that $f(x)$ is irreducible over the field of rational numbers.

Assume the contrary. Then $f(x)$ is reducible over $\mathbf Q$, so there exist nonconstant polynomials $g(x),h(x)\in\mathbf Q[x]$ such that

$$
f(x)=g(x)h(x).
$$

Write

$$
g(x)=\text{cont}(g)\,\text{pp}(g(x)),\qquad
h(x)=\text{cont}(h)\,\text{pp}(h(x)),
$$

where content and primitive part are defined by (3). Since we are working over a field, we may choose the primitive parts to be monic. Hence $\text{pp}(g(x))$ and $\text{pp}(h(x))$ are primitive polynomials with rational coefficients.

Choose nonzero integers $a$ and $b$ so that

$$
G(x)=a\,\text{pp}(g(x)),\qquad
H(x)=b\,\text{pp}(h(x))
$$

are primitive polynomials with integer coefficients. Then

$$
\text{pp}(g(x))=\frac{G(x)}{a},\qquad
\text{pp}(h(x))=\frac{H(x)}{b},
$$

and therefore

$$
f(x)
=\text{cont}(g)\,\text{cont}(h)\,
\frac{G(x)H(x)}{ab}.
$$

Thus

$$
ab\,f(x)
=c\,G(x)H(x),
$$

where

$$
c=ab\,\text{cont}(g)\,\text{cont}(h)\in\mathbf Q.
$$

The left-hand side has integer coefficients. Since $G(x)$ and $H(x)$ are primitive, Gauss's Lemma implies that $G(x)H(x)$ is primitive. By Lemma H, if a rational number multiplies a primitive polynomial and yields a polynomial with integer coefficients, that rational number must itself be an integer. Hence $c\in\mathbf Z$.

Therefore

$$
ab\,f(x)=c\,G(x)H(x)
$$

is a factorization in $\mathbf Z[x]$. Since $G(x)$ and $H(x)$ are nonconstant, this gives a factorization of $f(x)$ into two nonconstant polynomials over the integers after dividing out the common integer factor. More explicitly, because $G(x)H(x)$ is primitive, equation (4) shows that the content of the right-hand side is $c$; the content of the left-hand side is $ab,\text{cont}(f)$. Hence $c=\pm ab,\text{cont}(f)$, and

$$
\text{pp}(f(x))
=\pm G(x)H(x).
$$

Since $G(x)$ and $H(x)$ are nonconstant, $\text{pp}(f(x))$ is reducible in $\mathbf Z[x]$. Multiplying by the integer $\text{cont}(f)$ yields a nontrivial factorization of $f(x)$ in $\mathbf Z[x]$, contradicting the assumption that $f(x)$ is irreducible over the integers.

The contradiction arose from assuming that $f(x)$ is reducible over $\mathbf Q$. Therefore $f(x)$ is irreducible over $\mathbf Q$.

This completes the proof.

∎
