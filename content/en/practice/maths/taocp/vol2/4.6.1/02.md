## Exercise 4.6.1.2 [_15_]

**Problem 2.** Compute the greatest common divisor of

$$
f(x) = 3x^6 + x^5 + 4x^4 + 3x^3 + 4x + 3
$$

and its reverse

$$
g(x) = 2x^6 + 4x^5 + 3x^4 + 4x^3 + 4x^2 + x + 3
$$

over the field $\mathbb{F}_7$.

## Solution

All arithmetic is performed modulo 7. We will compute $\gcd(f, g)$ using the Euclidean algorithm for polynomials over $\mathbb{F}_7$.

### Step 1: Set up

Let

$$
u_0(x) = f(x), \qquad u_1(x) = g(x).
$$

We perform repeated polynomial division with remainder:

$$
u_{k-1}(x) = q_k(x) u_k(x) + u_{k+1}(x), \quad \deg(u_{k+1}) < \deg(u_k),
$$

until we reach a remainder of degree 0. The last nonzero remainder is the gcd.

### Step 2: First division $u_0 \div u_1$

The leading terms are $3x^6$ for $u_0$ and $2x^6$ for $u_1$. The quotient of leading coefficients is

$$
3 \cdot 2^{-1} \equiv 3 \cdot 4 \equiv 12 \equiv 5 \pmod{7}.
$$

Compute $5 u_1(x)$:

$$
5 u_1(x) \equiv 5 \cdot (2x^6 + 4x^5 + 3x^4 + 4x^3 + 4x^2 + x + 3) \pmod{7}.
$$

Compute term by term modulo 7:

$$
\begin{aligned}
5 \cdot 2x^6 &\equiv 10 x^6 \equiv 3 x^6,\\
5 \cdot 4x^5 &\equiv 20 x^5 \equiv 6 x^5,\\
5 \cdot 3x^4 &\equiv 15 x^4 \equiv 1 x^4,\\
5 \cdot 4x^3 &\equiv 20 x^3 \equiv 6 x^3,\\
5 \cdot 4x^2 &\equiv 20 x^2 \equiv 6 x^2,\\
5 \cdot x &\equiv 5 x,\\
5 \cdot 3 &\equiv 15 \equiv 1.
\end{aligned}
$$

Subtract term by term:

$$
\begin{aligned}
u_0 - 5 u_1 &\equiv (3 - 3)x^6 + (1 - 6)x^5 + (4 - 1)x^4 + (3 - 6)x^3 + (0 - 6)x^2 + (4 - 5)x + (3 - 1) \\
&\equiv 0x^6 + 2x^5 + 3x^4 + 4x^3 + x^2 + 6x + 2.
\end{aligned}
$$

Set

$$
u_2(x) = 2x^5 + 3x^4 + 4x^3 + x^2 + 6x + 2.
$$

### Step 3: Second division $u_1 \div u_2$

The leading terms are $2x^6$ for $u_1$ and $2x^5$ for $u_2$. The quotient of leading terms is

$$
\frac{2x^6}{2x^5} = x.
$$

Compute $x \cdot u_2(x)$:

$$
x \cdot u_2(x) = 2x^6 + 3x^5 + 4x^4 + x^3 + 6x^2 + 2x.
$$

Subtract from $u_1(x)$ term by term:

$$
\begin{aligned}
u_1 - x u_2 &= (2x^6 - 2x^6) + (4x^5 - 3x^5) + (3x^4 - 4x^4) + (4x^3 - x^3) \\
&\quad + (4x^2 - 6x^2) + (x - 2x) + (3 - 0) \\
&\equiv 0 x^6 + 1 x^5 + 6 x^4 + 3 x^3 + 5 x^2 + 6 x + 3 \pmod{7}.
\end{aligned}
$$

Set

$$
u_3(x) = x^5 + 6x^4 + 3x^3 + 5x^2 + 6x + 3.
$$

### Step 4: Third division $u_2 \div u_3$

Leading terms: $2x^5$ and $x^5$. Quotient of leading coefficients: $2 \cdot 1^{-1} = 2$.

Multiply $u_3(x)$ by 2:

$$
2 u_3(x) \equiv 2 x^5 + 5 x^4 + 6 x^3 + 3 x^2 + 5 x + 6.
$$

Subtract from $u_2(x)$ term by term:

$$
\begin{aligned}
u_2 - 2 u_3 &\equiv (2 - 2)x^5 + (3 - 5)x^4 + (4 - 6)x^3 + (1 - 3)x^2 + (6 - 5)x + (2 - 6) \\
&\equiv 0 x^5 + 5 x^4 + 5 x^3 + 5 x^2 + 1 x + 3.
\end{aligned}
$$

Set

$$
u_4(x) = 5 x^4 + 5 x^3 + 5 x^2 + x + 3.
$$

### Step 5: Fourth division $u_3 \div u_4$

Leading terms: $x^5$ and $5 x^4$. Quotient of leading terms: $\frac{1}{5} x = 3x$ because $5^{-1} \equiv 3 \pmod{7}$.

Compute $3x \cdot u_4(x)$:

$$
3x \cdot (5x^4 + 5x^3 + 5x^2 + x + 3) \equiv 1x^5 + 1x^4 + 1x^3 + 3x^2 + 2x \pmod{7}.
$$

Subtract from $u_3(x)$:

$$
\begin{aligned}
u_3 - 3x u_4 &\equiv (1 - 1)x^5 + (6 - 1)x^4 + (3 - 1)x^3 + (5 - 3)x^2 + (6 - 2)x + (3 - 0) \\
&\equiv 0 x^5 + 5 x^4 + 2 x^3 + 2 x^2 + 4 x + 3.
\end{aligned}
$$

Set

$$
u_5(x) = 5 x^4 + 2 x^3 + 2 x^2 + 4 x + 3.
$$

### Step 6: Fifth division $u_4 \div u_5$

Leading terms: $5 x^4$ and $5 x^4$. Quotient of leading terms: 1.

Subtract $1 \cdot u_5(x)$ from $u_4(x)$:

$$
(5-5)x^4 + (5-2)x^3 + (5-2)x^2 + (1-4)x + (3-3) \equiv 0 x^4 + 3 x^3 + 3 x^2 + 4 x + 0.
$$

Set

$$
u_6(x) = 3 x^3 + 3 x^2 + 4 x.
$$

### Step 7: Sixth division $u_5 \div u_6$

Leading terms: $5 x^4$ and $3 x^3$. Quotient: $5 \cdot 3^{-1} x = 5 \cdot 5 x = 4 x$ because $3^{-1} \equiv 5 \pmod{7}$.
