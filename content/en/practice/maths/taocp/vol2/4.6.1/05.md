**Exercise 5.** [*M22*] What is the probability that $u(x)$ and $v(x)$ are relatively prime modulo $p$, if $u(x)$ and $v(x)$ are independently and uniformly distributed monic polynomials of degree $n$?

## Solution

Let $S = \mathbb{F}_p$. Let $u(x)$ and $v(x)$ be independently and uniformly distributed monic polynomials of degree $n$. Let $\mathcal{M}_n$ denote the set of all such polynomials, so $|\mathcal{M}_n| = p^n$.

A monic polynomial $f(x)$ is a greatest common divisor of $u(x)$ and $v(x)$ if and only if $f(x)$ is monic and divides both $u(x)$ and $v(x)$.

Let $\mathcal{I}$ denote the set of monic irreducible polynomials over $\mathbb{F}_p$. Every nonzero polynomial admits a unique factorization into elements of $\mathcal{I}$, by the unique factorization property of $\mathbb{F}_p[x]$.

For each $P \in \mathcal{I}$ with $\deg P = d$, the condition that $P \mid u(x)$ is equivalent to the condition that $u(x) = P(x),u_1(x)$ for some monic polynomial $u_1(x)$ of degree $n-d$. The number of such polynomials is $p^{n-d}$, hence

$\Pr(P \mid u) = \frac{p^{n-d}}{p^n} = p^{-d}.$

The same computation gives $\Pr(P \mid v) = p^{-d}$. Independence of $u$ and $v$ yields

$\Pr(P \mid u \ \text{and}\ P \mid v) = p^{-2d}.$

Define the event $A$ that $\gcd(u,v)=1$. Its complement is the event that there exists at least one irreducible $P$ dividing both $u$ and $v$. Using unique factorization, the event $A$ is equivalent to the condition that no irreducible polynomial divides both.

Define the formal product over monic irreducibles

$\prod_{P \in \mathcal{I}} (1 - p^{-2\deg P}).$

Expanding this product corresponds to inclusion–exclusion over all finite sets of distinct irreducible divisors, since a monic polynomial is uniquely determined by its multiset of irreducible factors. The coefficient of a given squarefree product $\prod P_i$ contributes $(-1)^k p^{-2\sum \deg P_i}$, which matches the probability that all $P_i$ divide both $u$ and $v$. Therefore,

$\Pr(\gcd(u,v)=1) = \prod_{P \in \mathcal{I}} (1 - p^{-2\deg P}).$

The Euler product identity for $\mathbb{F}_p[x]$ gives the zeta function

$\zeta_{\mathbb{F}_p[x]}(s) = \prod_{P \in \mathcal{I}} (1 - p^{-s\deg P})^{-1} = \frac{1}{1 - p^{1-s}}.$

Setting $s = 2$ yields

$\prod_{P \in \mathcal{I}} (1 - p^{-2\deg P}) = 1 - p^{-1}.$

Hence,

$\Pr(\gcd(u(x),v(x)) = 1) = 1 - \frac{1}{p}.$

The value is independent of $n$.

$\boxed{1 - \frac{1}{p}}$
