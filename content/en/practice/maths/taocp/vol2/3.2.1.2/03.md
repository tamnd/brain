**Exercise 3.** [*13*] Suppose that $m = 10^e$, where $e \ge 2$, and suppose further that $c$ is odd and not a multiple of 5. Show that the linear congruential sequence will have the maximum length period if and only if $a \bmod 20 = 1$.

**Solve time:** 7m20s

## Solution

Let $m = 10^e$ with $e \ge 2$, and let $c$ be odd and not a multiple of 5. We wish to determine the necessary and sufficient condition on $a$ such that the linear congruential sequence

$X_{n+1} \equiv aX_n + c \pmod{10^e}$

has period length $m = 10^e$. By Theorem A of Section 3.2.1.2, the sequence achieves period $m$ if and only if the following three conditions hold:

i) $c$ is relatively prime to $m$;

ii) $b = a - 1$ is a multiple of $p$, for every prime $p$ dividing $m$;

iii) $b$ is a multiple of 4 if $m$ is a multiple of 4.

We verify each condition in turn. Condition (i) requires that $\gcd(c, m) = 1$. Since $m = 2 \cdot 5^e$ (up to powers), and $c$ is odd and not divisible by 5, we have $\gcd(c, 2) = 1$ and $\gcd(c, 5) = 1$. Therefore $c$ is relatively prime to $m$, and condition (i) is satisfied.

Next, condition (ii) requires that $b = a - 1$ be divisible by all primes dividing $m$. The prime divisors of $10^e$ are $2$ and $5$. Therefore, $a - 1$ must be divisible by $2$ and by $5$, which is equivalent to

$a - 1 \equiv 0 \pmod{2} \quad \text{and} \quad a - 1 \equiv 0 \pmod{5}.$

By the Chinese Remainder Theorem, these congruences together are equivalent to

$a - 1 \equiv 0 \pmod{10}.$

Condition (iii) requires that $b = a - 1$ be divisible by 4 if $m$ is divisible by 4. Since $m = 10^e$ and $e \ge 2$, we have $m \equiv 0 \pmod{4}$, because $10^2 = 100 \equiv 0 \pmod{4}$. Therefore $a - 1$ must also be divisible by 4:

$a - 1 \equiv 0 \pmod{4}.$

Combining conditions (ii) and (iii), we need $a - 1$ divisible by both 4 and 5. Since $\gcd(4,5) = 1$, this is equivalent to

$a - 1 \equiv 0 \pmod{20}.$

Hence the linear congruential sequence modulo $10^e$ has maximum period $10^e$ if and only if

$\boxed{a \bmod 20 = 1}.$

This completes the proof.
