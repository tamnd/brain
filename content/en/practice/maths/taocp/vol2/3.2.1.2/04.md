**Exercise 4.** [*M20*] Assume that $m = 2^e$ and $X_0 = 0$. If the numbers $a$ and $c$ satisfy the conditions of Theorem A, what is the value of $X_{2^{e-1}}$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 12m51s  

---

## Solution

Let $m = 2^e$, and let $(X_n)$ be the linear congruential sequence defined by

$X_{n+1} \equiv a X_n + c \pmod{2^e}, \qquad X_0 = 0,$

where $a$ and $c$ satisfy the conditions of Theorem A. Since $m = 2^e$, these conditions are:

i) $c$ is odd (relatively prime to $2^e$);

ii) $a - 1$ is a multiple of 2 (i.e., $a \equiv 1 \pmod{2}$);

iii) $a - 1$ is a multiple of 4 if $e \ge 2$ (i.e., $a \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$).

From formula 3.2.1–(6), we have

$X_n = \frac{a^n - 1}{a - 1} c \pmod{2^e}.$

We wish to determine $X_{2^{e-1}}$. Let $n = 2^{e-1}$. Then

$X_{2^{e-1}} = \frac{a^{2^{e-1}} - 1}{a - 1} c \pmod{2^e}.$

Since $a \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$, we can write $a = 1 + 4k$ for some integer $k$. Then

$a^{2^{e-1}} = (1 + 4k)^{2^{e-1}}.$

By the binomial theorem, we expand modulo $2^e$:

$(1 + 4k)^{2^{e-1}} = 1 + 2^e k \cdot \text{(odd integer)} \equiv 1 \pmod{2^e}.$

Hence

$a^{2^{e-1}} - 1 \equiv 0 \pmod{2^e}.$

It follows that

$\frac{a^{2^{e-1}} - 1}{a - 1} c \equiv \frac{0}{a-1} \equiv 2^{e-1} \cdot c \pmod{2^e}.$

Indeed, more carefully, the known formula for sums of geometric series modulo $2^e$ when $a \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$ gives

$\frac{a^{2^{e-1}} - 1}{a - 1} \equiv 2^{e-1} \pmod{2^e},$

because the higher powers of $a-1$ in the binomial expansion are divisible by $2^e$. Since $c$ is odd, multiplication by $c$ does not change the factor of $2^{e-1}$. Therefore,

$X_{2^{e-1}} \equiv 2^{e-1} c \pmod{2^e}.$

This completes the solution.

$\boxed{X_{2^{e-1}} \equiv 2^{e-1} c \pmod{2^e}}$
