**Exercise 1.** [*10*] What is the length of the period of the linear congruential sequence with $X_0 = 5772156648$, $a = 3141592621$, $c = 2718281829$, and $m = 10000000000$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 15m48s  

---

We first verify the conditions of Theorem A for the given parameters. The modulus is

$m = 10^{10} = 2^{10} \cdot 5^{10}.$

Condition (i) requires that $c$ be relatively prime to $m$. We have $c = 2718281829$, which is odd and not divisible by 5; hence $\gcd(c,m) = 1$, so condition (i) is satisfied.

Condition (ii) requires that $b = a - 1$ be divisible by each prime factor of $m$. We have $a = 3141592621$, so

$b = a - 1 = 3141592620.$

Dividing by 2 gives $3141592620 / 2 = 1570796310$, an integer, and dividing by 5 gives $3141592620 / 5 = 628318524$, also an integer. Therefore condition (ii) is satisfied.

Condition (iii) requires $b$ to be divisible by 4 because $m$ is divisible by 4. Dividing $b$ by 4 gives $3141592620 / 4 = 785398155$, an integer, so condition (iii) is satisfied.

Since all three conditions of Theorem A hold, the linear congruential sequence attains the maximum period length

$\lambda = m = 10^{10}.$

Hence the period of the sequence $(X_n)$ is

$\boxed{10^{10}}.$

This completes the proof. ∎
