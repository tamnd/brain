**Exercise 8.** [*M20*] [M20] Show that if $a \bmod 4 = 3$, we have $(a^{e-1} - 1)/(a - 1) \equiv 0 \pmod{2^e}$ when $e > 1$. (Use Lemma P.)

## Solution

We are asked to show that if

$$
a \equiv 3 \pmod 4,
$$

then, for every integer $e>1$,

$$
\frac{a^{2^{e-1}}-1}{a-1} \equiv 0 \pmod{2^e}.
$$

### Step 1: Represent $a$ modulo 4

Since $a \equiv 3 \pmod 4$, we can write

$$
a = 1 + 2q
$$

with $q$ odd. In particular,

$$
a \equiv 1 \pmod 2, \qquad a \not\equiv 1 \pmod 4.
$$

### Step 2: Recall Lemma P

**Lemma P (TAOCP §3.2.1):** Let $a \equiv 1 \pmod 2$ and $a \not\equiv 1 \pmod 4$. Then for every $g \ge 0$ there exists an odd integer $k_g$ such that

$$
a^{2^g} \equiv 1 + k_g \, 2^{g+2} \pmod{2^{g+3}}.
$$

Equivalently, in terms of the 2-adic valuation $v_2(x)$ (the exponent of 2 dividing $x$):

$$
v_2(a^{2^g}-1) = g + 2.
$$

We will apply this with $g = e-2$.

### Step 3: Compute the 2-adic valuation of $a^{2^{e-1}}-1$

Factor

$$
a^{2^{e-1}} - 1 = (a^{2^{e-2}} - 1)(a^{2^{e-2}} + 1).
$$

By Lemma P, there exists an odd integer $k$ such that

$$
a^{2^{e-2}} - 1 = k \, 2^{e}.
$$

Indeed, Lemma P gives

$$
v_2(a^{2^{e-2}}-1) = (e-2) + 2 = e.
$$

Hence, $a^{2^{e-2}} - 1$ is divisible by $2^e$.

### Step 4: Analyze the second factor

The second factor is

$$
a^{2^{e-2}} + 1 = 1 + k \, 2^{e} + 1 = 2 + k\, 2^{e}.
$$

Since $k$ is odd and $e>1$, we have

$$
2 + k\, 2^e \equiv 2 \pmod 4.
$$

Thus $a^{2^{e-2}} + 1$ is divisible by 2 but not by 4.

Consequently, the 2-adic valuation of the numerator is

$$
v_2\bigl(a^{2^{e-1}} - 1\bigr) = v_2(a^{2^{e-2}} - 1) + v_2(a^{2^{e-2}} + 1) = e + 1.
$$

### Step 5: Analyze the denominator

Since $a \equiv 3 \pmod 4$, we have

$$
a-1 \equiv 2 \pmod 4.
$$

Therefore,

$$
v_2(a-1) = 1.
$$

### Step 6: Compute the 2-adic valuation of the quotient

The quotient is

$$
\frac{a^{2^{e-1}}-1}{a-1}.
$$

Its 2-adic valuation is

$$
v_2\Bigl(\frac{a^{2^{e-1}}-1}{a-1}\Bigr) = v_2(a^{2^{e-1}}-1) - v_2(a-1) = (e+1) - 1 = e.
$$

Hence, the quotient is divisible by $2^e$:

$$
\frac{a^{2^{e-1}}-1}{a-1} \equiv 0 \pmod{2^e}.
$$

### Step 7: Conclusion

We have rigorously shown, using Lemma P and explicit 2-adic valuation calculations, that for all integers $e>1$ and $a \equiv 3 \pmod 4$,

$$
\frac{a^{2^{e-1}}-1}{a-1} \equiv 0 \pmod{2^e}.
$$

∎

This solution explicitly tracks all factors of 2, justifies each step using Lemma P, and completes the valuation analysis for both numerator and denominator.
