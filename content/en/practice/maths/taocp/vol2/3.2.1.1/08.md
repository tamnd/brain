[Section 3.2.1.1: Choice of Modulus](../)

**Exercise 8.** &#9654; [*20*] Write a MIX program analogous to (2) that computes $\lfloor aX \rfloor \bmod (w-1)$. The values $0$ and $w - 1$ are to be treated as equivalent in the input and output of your program.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 20m27s  

---

## Solution

Let

$$
aX=qw+r,\qquad 0\le r<w.
$$

For reduction modulo $w+1$, program (2) uses the identity

$$
aX=q(w+1)+(r-q).
$$

For reduction modulo $w-1$, the corresponding identity is

$$
aX=q(w-1)+(r+q).
$$

Hence

$$
aX\bmod (w-1)\equiv r+q \pmod{w-1}.
$$

Since $0\le q\le w-1$ and $0\le r\le w-1$, we have

$$
0\le r+q\le 2w-2=2(w-1).
$$

Therefore the residue modulo $w-1$ is obtained from $r+q$ by subtracting $w-1$ whenever $r+q\ge w-1$. Because the values $0$ and $w-1$ are to be treated as equivalent, the exceptional case $r+q=w-1$ needs no special treatment.

In MIX multiplication, the upper half of the product is $q$ and the lower half is $r$. Thus we should add the two halves of the product. The following program is analogous to (2):

```asm
01  LDA  A      rA ← a.
02  MUL  X      rAX ← (rA) · X.
03  STX  TEMP
04  ADD  TEMP   rA ← q + r.
05  JAN  **3    Exit if rA < w.
06  ENTA 1
07  SUB  ONE    rA ← rA − (w − 1).
```

Here `ONE` is a memory location containing the value $1$.

To verify correctness, observe that after line 04 register A contains $q+r$.

If $q+r<w$, then

$$
0\le q+r\le w-1,
$$

so $q+r$ already represents the residue class of $aX$ modulo $w-1$. The value $w-1$ is acceptable because $w-1$ and $0$ are identified.

If $q+r\ge w$, then

$$
w\le q+r\le 2w-2.
$$

Line 05 fails, because the addition in line 04 has overflowed. After an overflowing addition, register A contains

$$
q+r-w.
$$

Line 06 places $1$ in register A, and line 07 subtracts $1$, producing

$$
q+r-w+1=q+r-(w-1).
$$

Since

$$
aX=q(w-1)+(q+r),
$$

the quantity

$$
q+r-(w-1)
$$

is congruent to $aX$ modulo $w-1$, and it lies in the range

$$
1\le q+r-(w-1)\le w-1.
$$

Thus the result again represents the desired residue class.

The program therefore computes

$$
aX \bmod (w-1),
$$

with $0$ and $w-1$ treated as equivalent in both input and output.
