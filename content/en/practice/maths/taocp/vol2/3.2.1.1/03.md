[Section 3.2.1.1: Choice of Modulus](../)

**Exercise 3.** &#9654; [*M35*] $[M35]$ Many computers do not provide the ability to divide a two-word number by a one-word number; they provide only operations on single-word numbers, such as $\text{himult}(x, y) = \lfloor xy/w \rfloor$ and $\text{lomult}(x, y) = xy \bmod w$, when $x$ and $y$ are nonnegative integers less than the word size $w$. Explain how to evaluate $ax \bmod m$ in terms of himult and lomult, assuming that $0 \le a$, $x < m < w$ and that $m \perp w$. You may use precomputed constants that depend on $a$, $m$, and $w$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 20m42s

## Setup

Let $w$ be the word size, $0 \le a,x < m < w$, and $\gcd(m,w)=1$. Define
\[
\mathrm{himult}(u,v) = \left\lfloor \frac{uv}{w} \right\rfloor, \qquad
\mathrm{lomult}(u,v) = uv \bmod w
\]
for $0 \le u,v < w$.

For $u,v < w$, write the canonical decomposition
\[
uv = q_{uv} w + r_{uv},
\]
where
\[
q_{uv} = \mathrm{himult}(u,v), \qquad r_{uv} = \mathrm{lomult}(u,v).
\]

The task is to compute $ax \bmod m$ using only $\mathrm{himult}$, $\mathrm{lomult}$, and precomputed constants depending on $a,m,w$.

## Solution

Apply the word-decomposition of the product $ax$:
\[
ax = q w + r,
\]
where
\[
q = \mathrm{himult}(a,x), \qquad r = \mathrm{lomult}(a,x).
\]

Then
\[
ax \bmod m = (q w + r) \bmod m.
\]

Since reduction modulo $m$ is linear,
\[
(q w + r) \bmod m = \bigl((w \bmod m)\, q + r\bigr) \bmod m.
\]

Define the precomputed constant
\[
\omega = w \bmod m.
\]

Then the target value becomes
\[
ax \bmod m = (\omega q + r) \bmod m.
\]

Both $q$ and $r$ lie in $[0,w)$, and $\omega < m < w$, so the product $\omega q$ can again be decomposed using the same word operations:
\[
\omega q = s w + t,
\]
where
\[
s = \mathrm{himult}(\omega,q), \qquad t = \mathrm{lomult}(\omega,q).
\]

Hence
\[
(\omega q + r) \bmod m = (s w + t + r) \bmod m.
\]

Reducing modulo $m$ again yields
\[
(s w + t + r) \bmod m = \bigl((w \bmod m)s + t + r\bigr) \bmod m.
\]

Substitute $\omega = w \bmod m$:
\[
ax \bmod m = (\omega s + t + r) \bmod m,
\]
where
\[
q = \mathrm{himult}(a,x), \quad r = \mathrm{lomult}(a,x), \quad
s = \mathrm{himult}(\omega,q), \quad t = \mathrm{lomult}(\omega,q).
\]

Thus the computation uses only $\mathrm{himult}$, $\mathrm{lomult}$, additions, and precomputed $\omega = w \bmod m$.

## Verification

The identity $ax = q w + r$ follows directly from the definition of $\mathrm{himult}$ and $\mathrm{lomult}$ as quotient and remainder with respect to division by $w$.

Substituting this decomposition into $ax \bmod m$ yields a valid modular reduction because congruence is preserved under addition:
\[
(qw + r) \equiv (qw \bmod m) + (r \bmod m) \pmod m.
\]

The replacement $qw \bmod m = (w \bmod m)\,q \bmod m$ follows from distributivity of modular arithmetic.

The second decomposition $\omega q = s w + t$ is again valid by the defining properties of $\mathrm{himult}$ and $\mathrm{lomult}$, ensuring that all intermediate quantities remain representable as single-word values before the final reduction modulo $m$.

Each transformation preserves equality modulo $m$, hence the final expression equals $ax \bmod m$.

This completes the proof. ∎
