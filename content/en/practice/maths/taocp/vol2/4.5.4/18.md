**Exercise 18.** [*HM23*] Give a heuristic proof of (7), analogous to the text's derivation of (6). What is the approximate probability that $p_{n-1} \le \sqrt{p_n}$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m06s  

---

## Solution

Let $p_n$ and $p_{n-1}$ denote the two largest prime factors in a typical factorization, ordered so that $p_{n-1} \le p_n$. The condition

$p_{n-1} \le \sqrt{p_n}$

is equivalent to

$\log p_{n-1} \le \tfrac{1}{2}\log p_n.$

Heuristically model the logarithms of prime factors occurring in large integers as points distributed with density proportional to $1/x$ on $(0,1]$ after normalization by $\log N$, so that a random integer corresponds to a random partition of unit mass into decreasing components whose joint distribution is invariant under permutations. In this model, conditioning on the largest component having value $L$, the remaining components behave like unordered points uniformly distributed on $(0,L)$ when viewed on the logarithmic scale after normalization.

Let $X_1 = \log p_n$ and $X_2 = \log p_{n-1}$. Under the heuristic symmetry assumption, conditional on $X_1 = L$, the second largest logarithmic factor $X_2$ is uniformly distributed on $(0,L)$. The event $p_{n-1} \le \sqrt{p_n}$ becomes $X_2 \le L/2$.

Hence

$\mathbb{P}(p_{n-1} \le \sqrt{p_n} \mid p_n) = \mathbb{P}(X_2 \le L/2 \mid X_1 = L) = \frac{L/2}{L} = \frac{1}{2}.$

Removing the conditioning does not change the value, since the ratio is independent of $L$ in this heuristic model.

Therefore the approximate probability is

$\boxed{\tfrac{1}{2}}.$

This completes the argument. ∎
