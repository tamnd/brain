**Exercise 28.** [*M27*] *[M27]* Given a prime $p$ and a positive integer $d$, what is the value of $f(p, d)$, the average number of times that $p$ divides $A^2 - dB^2$ (counting multiplicity), when $A$ and $B$ are random integers that are independent except for the condition $A \perp B$?

## Setup

Let

$Q(A,B)=A^2-dB^2,$

and let $v_p(n)$ denote the exponent of the prime $p$ in $n$, with the convention that

$v_p(n)=k \iff p^k\mid n,\quad p^{k+1}\nmid n.$

The quantity to be determined is

$f(p,d)=\mathbb E\!\bigl[v_p(A^2-dB^2)\bigr],$

where $(A,B)$ is chosen at random subject to the condition $A\perp B$.

Since

$v_p(Q)=\sum_{k\ge1}[p^k\mid Q],$

the expected value is

$f(p,d)=\sum_{k\ge1}P_k,$

where $P_k$ is the probability that

$p^k\mid A^2-dB^2,$

for a random coprime pair $(A,B)$.

The problem therefore reduces to counting solutions of

$A^2-dB^2\equiv0\pmod{p^k}$

among residue classes $(A,B)\pmod{p^k}$ satisfying the coprimality condition.
