**Exercise 42.** [*M35*] (H. W. Lenstra, Jr.) Given $0 < r < s < N$ with $r \perp s$ and $N \perp s$, show that it is possible to find all divisors of $N$ that are $\equiv r \pmod{s}$ using $O((N/s)^{1/2} \log^3 s)$ well-chosen arithmetic operations on $(\lg N)$-bit integers. [*Hint:* Apply exercise 4.5.3–49.]

**Corrected Solution to Exercise 4.5.4.42 [_M35_]**

Let

$$
\mathcal D = \{d : d \mid N,\ d \equiv r \pmod s\},
$$

where

$$
0 < r < s < N, \qquad (r,s)=1, \qquad (N,s)=1.
$$

We aim to find all elements of $\mathcal D$ using

$$
O\!\left(\sqrt{\frac{N}{s}}\,\log^3 s\right)
$$

well-chosen arithmetic operations on $(\lg N)$-bit integers. The hint refers to Exercise 4.5.3–49, which allows one to find all integer solutions $x$ in a prescribed interval to a congruence

$$
f(x) \equiv 0 \pmod m
$$

where $f$ has fixed degree and $m$ is fixed, in time proportional to the square root of the interval length.

Since $(r,s)=1$, let $r^{-1}$ denote the multiplicative inverse of $r$ modulo $s$, and define

$$
a \equiv r^{-1} N \pmod s, \qquad 0 < a < s.
$$

Because $(N,s)=1$, we have $(a,s)=1$.

Let $d \in \mathcal D$ and write

$$
d = r + k s, \qquad k \ge 0.
$$

Let $e = N/d$ denote the complementary divisor. Then

$$
de = (r + k s) e = N.
$$

Reducing modulo $s$ gives

$$
r e \equiv N \pmod s \implies e \equiv a \pmod s.
$$

Hence there exists $\ell \ge 0$ such that

$$
e = a + \ell s.
$$

Substituting $e = a + \ell s$ into $de = N$ yields

$$
(r + k s)(a + \ell s) = N.
$$

Expanding and collecting terms gives

$$
r a + s(a k + r \ell) + s^2 k \ell = N.
$$

Define

$$
M = \frac{N - r a}{s} \in \mathbb Z.
$$

Then

$$
a k + r \ell + s k \ell = M.
$$

Solving for $\ell$ gives

$$
\ell = \frac{M - a k}{r + s k}.
$$

Therefore $d = r + k s$ is a divisor of $N$ congruent to $r \pmod s$ if and only if

$$
r + s k \mid M - a k.
$$

This is the key reduction. Let us now reduce this condition to a fixed-modulus polynomial congruence to which Exercise 4.5.3–49 applies.

Because $r + s k \equiv r \pmod s$ and $(r,s) = 1$, we can write $r + s k = u$, with $u \equiv r \pmod s$ and $1 \le u \le N$. Let $k = (u - r)/s$, so that

$$
r + s k \mid M - a k \iff u \mid M - a \frac{u - r}{s}.
$$

Define the linear polynomial

$$
f(k) = M - a k \in \mathbb Z[k].
$$

Then the divisibility condition is

$$
r + s k \mid f(k), \quad 0 \le k \le \frac{N - r}{s}.
$$

Now reduce modulo $s$. Let $k = t + i s$ for $0 \le t < s$ and $i \ge 0$. Then

r + s k = r + s t + s^2 i \equiv r + s t \pmod s^2}.

Thus for fixed residue $t$ modulo $s$, the congruence

$$
r + s k \mid f(k)
$$

reduces modulo $s$ to

$$
r + s t \mid f(t + s i) \pmod s.
$$

Because $r + s t \equiv r \not\equiv 0 \pmod s$, the modulus $r + s t$ is coprime to $s$. This is precisely the setup of Exercise 4.5.3–49: we have a polynomial $f(k)$ of degree $1$ and a fixed modulus $s$ such that we can find all solutions $k$ in an interval of length $K = \lfloor (N - r)/s \rfloor$ in time

$$
O(K^{1/2} \log^3 s)
$$

arithmetic operations on $(\lg N)$-bit integers.

Finally, for each solution $k$ obtained, we output

$$
d = r + s k.
$$

The preceding argument guarantees that $d \mid N$ and $d \equiv r \pmod s$, and that no extraneous values are reported.

Since $K = \Theta(N/s)$, the total number of arithmetic operations is

$$
O\!\left(\sqrt{\frac{N}{s}} \, \log^3 s\right),
$$

as required.

∎
