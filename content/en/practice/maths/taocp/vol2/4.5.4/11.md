## Solution

We are asked to determine the outputs of Algorithm E when

$$
N = 197209, \quad k = 5, \quad m = 1.
$$

Algorithm E (§4.5.4) constructs successive **best rational approximations** to $\sqrt{kN}$ using mediant-based refinement, subject to the bounds $k$ and $m$. The algorithm outputs a sequence of fractions $p/q$ that approximate $\sqrt{K}$, where $K = kN$.

### Step 1: Compute $K$ and its integer part

Let

$$
K = kN = 5 \cdot 197209 = 985045.
$$

The integer part of $\sqrt{K}$ is

$$
\lfloor \sqrt{985045} \rfloor = 992,
$$

since

$$
992^2 = 984064 < 985045 < 993^2 = 986049.
$$

This gives the first approximation output by Algorithm E:

$$
\frac{p_0}{q_0} = \frac{992}{1}.
$$

### Step 2: Set up Algorithm E parameters

Algorithm E uses a representation of approximations as

$$
x = a_0 + \frac{1}{a_1 + \frac{1}{a_2 + \cdots}}.
$$

Here $a_0 = 992$ and the fractional part is

$$
\delta_1 = \sqrt{K} - 992 \approx 0.498976.
$$

The algorithm generates successive convergents $p_n/q_n$ using **mediant updates**:

$$
\frac{p_n}{q_n} = \frac{p_L + p_R}{q_L + q_R},
$$

where $p_L/q_L$ and $p_R/q_R$ are the current lower and upper bounds of approximations.

### Step 3: Interpret the hint

The exercise gives the hint:

$$
\sqrt{K} \approx 992 + \frac{1}{1.495},\ \frac{1}{2.495},\ \frac{1}{2.495},\ \frac{1}{1.984}, \ldots
$$

These are the successive **partial quotients** in the continued fraction expansion of $\sqrt{K}$:

1. First fractional approximation: $992 + 1/1.495 \approx 992 + 0.669 \approx 992.669$
2. Second: $992 + 1/2.495 \approx 992 + 0.401 \approx 992.401$
3. Third: $992 + 1/2.495 \approx 992.401$
4. Fourth: $992 + 1/1.984 \approx 992 + 0.504 \approx 992.504$

Algorithm E only outputs convergents for which the **denominator does not exceed $m = 1$**. Since $m = 1$, the algorithm allows **only fractions with denominator 1**. Any convergent with denominator $>1$ is rejected.

### Step 4: Determine which convergents are accepted

- The first output is $992/1$, which has denominator 1. Accepted.
- The next convergents, according to the hint, have denominators greater than 1. To see this explicitly:

1. $992 + 1/1.495 \approx 992 + 0.669$ corresponds to the fraction $992 + 2/3 \approx 992.667$ → denominator 3 > 1 → **rejected**.
2. $992 + 1/2.495 \approx 992 + 0.401$ corresponds to fraction $992 + 2/5 \approx 992.4$ → denominator 5 > 1 → **rejected**.
3. All subsequent convergents have even larger denominators.

Therefore, **no further approximations are output** because they exceed the $m = 1$ bound.

### Step 5: Conclude the output sequence

The only valid output is the initial integer part:

$$
\frac{992}{1}.
$$

Hence, Algorithm E produces the sequence

$$
\boxed{992}.
$$

### Verification

1. $992^2 = 984064 < K = 985045 < 993^2 = 986049$, integer part correct.
2. $m = 1$ allows only denominators 1 → all fractional convergents rejected.
3. Matches the intended behavior of Algorithm E under the given bounds.

This fully explains why the sequence stops at the first term.

### Answer

$$
\displaystyle 992
$$
