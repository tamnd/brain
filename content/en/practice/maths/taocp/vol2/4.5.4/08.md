Show how to adapt the procedure just described into an algorithm that is directly suited to efficient computer calculation, using no multiplication.

**Exercise 4.5.4.8** [_23_] (_The sieve of Eratosthenes_, 3rd century b.c.)

We wish to adapt the sieve of Eratosthenes for direct, efficient computer implementation **without multiplication**. The goal is to generate all primes less than a given integer $N$, using addition-only arithmetic and maintaining constant-time updates of the state.

## Data Representation

Let $A[1..N]$ be a Boolean array representing candidate primes:

$$
A[x] = 
\begin{cases}
\text{true}, & \text{if } x \text{ is currently unmarked (potentially prime)},\\
\text{false}, & \text{if } x \text{ has been struck out as composite.}
\end{cases}
$$

Initialize the array:

$$
A[2] \leftarrow \text{true}, \quad A[x] \leftarrow \text{true for odd } x \ge 3, \quad A[x] \leftarrow \text{false for even } x>2.
$$

Maintain the following for each prime candidate $p$:

1. $p$, the current prime being processed.
2. $q = p^2$, the next multiple to strike out.
3. $d = 2p + 2$, the **increment for updating $q$ to $(p+2)^2$** in constant time.

## Initialization

$$
p \leftarrow 3, \quad q \leftarrow 9, \quad d \leftarrow 2p + 2 = 8.
$$

The initial prime 2 is handled separately by including $2$ in the output list; all further processing only considers odd numbers.

## Main Sieve Loop

While $q \le N$, perform the following steps.

### 1. Identify next prime

If $A[p] = \text{true}$, then $p$ is prime. Otherwise, increment $p \leftarrow p + 2$ repeatedly until $A[p] = \text{true}$.

### 2. Strike out multiples of $p$

Set

$$
j \leftarrow q.
$$

While $j \le N$:

$$
A[j] \leftarrow \text{false}, \quad j \leftarrow j + p + p.
$$

All steps use addition only.

### 3. Increment $p$ and update the square efficiently

Let $p' = p + 2$. Observe the identity:

$$
(p+2)^2 = p^2 + 2(p+1) + 2p = p^2 + 2p + 2p + 4 = p^2 + 4p + 4.
$$

Instead of recomputing $4p$ via a loop, maintain an auxiliary variable:

$$
d \leftarrow 2p + 2
$$

and then update $q$ and $d$ **in constant time**:

$$
q \leftarrow q + 2p + 2 + d
$$

More systematically, in TAOCP style:

1. Let $q \leftarrow p^2$ and $d \leftarrow 2p + 2$.
2. After processing $p$, set:

$$
q \leftarrow q + d, \quad d \leftarrow d + 4
$$

$$
p \leftarrow p + 2
$$

This ensures:

$$
q = p^2, \quad d = 2p + 2
$$

at the start of the next iteration **without any loops**.

## Termination

Stop when $q > N$. At this point, all remaining unmarked numbers in $A[x]$ are prime, since any composite number $n \le N$ has a least prime factor $p$ with $p^2 \le n$; such multiples have already been marked.

## Correctness

1. **Strike-out correctness:** Every composite number $n \le N$ has a least prime factor $p$, and is of the form $n = pk \ge p^2$. Since the sieve marks multiples starting at $p^2$ and increments by $p$, all composites are eventually struck out.
2. **Primes preserved:** Numbers not divisible by any prime $p \le \sqrt{N}$ remain unmarked. By definition, these numbers are prime.
3. **Multiplication-free:** All arithmetic operations are additions, comparisons, and assignments. The squares $p^2$ and multiples of $p$ are updated incrementally using a constant-time recurrence. There are no loops proportional to $p$.

## Efficiency

- Each prime $p$ has its multiples struck out via repeated addition in the array.
- The square $p^2$ and increment $d$ are maintained in **constant time** using the recurrence:

$$
q_{\text{new}} = q + d, \quad d_{\text{new}} = d + 4
$$

- This avoids the $O(p)$ linear addition loop in the previous flawed solution.
- Memory usage is $O(N)$; time complexity remains $O(N \log \log N)$ for the sieve process.

## Result

After termination, the set

$$
\{ x \le N : A[x] = \text{true} \}
$$

is exactly the set of prime numbers less than or equal to $N$, computed **without multiplication** and in a form directly suited to efficient computer calculation.
