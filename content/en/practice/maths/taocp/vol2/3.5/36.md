[Section 3.5: What Is a Random Sequence?](../)

**Exercise 36.** [*HM30*] Let $(X_n)$ be a binary sequence that is "random" according to Definition R6. Show that the $\{0\ldots 1\}$ sequence $(U_n)$ defined in binary notation by the scheme

$$U_0 = (0.X_0)_2, \quad U_1 = (0.X_1X_2)_2, \quad U_2 = (0.X_3X_4X_5)_2, \quad U_3 = (0.X_6X_7X_8X_9)_2, \quad \ldots$$

is random in the sense of Definition R6.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 18m

## Solution

Let $(X_n)$ be a binary sequence that is random according to Definition R6. By R6, there exists a constant $c$ such that for every $N$, the prefix $X_0 X_1 \dots X_{N-1}$ satisfies

$$
K(X_0 X_1 \dots X_{N-1}) \ge N - c,
$$

where $K(\cdot)$ denotes the prefix-free Kolmogorov complexity. This expresses the incompressibility of every finite initial segment of $(X_n)$.

Define the sequence $(U_n)$ as follows:

$$
U_0 = (0.X_0)_2, \quad 
U_1 = (0.X_1 X_2)_2, \quad 
U_2 = (0.X_3 X_4 X_5)_2, \quad 
U_3 = (0.X_6 X_7 X_8 X_9)_2, \quad \ldots
$$

so that $U_n$ is the dyadic rational in $[0,1)$ determined by the next $n+1$ bits of $(X_n)$. Let $b(n) = n+1$ denote the number of bits used to form $U_n$, and define

$$
N_n = \sum_{j=0}^{n} b(j) = 1 + 2 + \dots + (n+1) = \frac{(n+1)(n+2)}{2}.
$$

Thus the first $n+1$ terms of $(U_n)$ are completely determined by the first $N_n$ bits of $(X_n)$:

$$
X_0 X_1 \dots X_{N_n - 1} \longleftrightarrow (U_0, U_1, \dots, U_n).
$$

This mapping is computable and invertible: given $(U_0, \dots, U_n)$, one can recover the $N_n$ bits of $(X_n)$ used to form them, and given the $N_n$ bits, one can compute $(U_0, \dots, U_n)$. By the invariance theorem for prefix-free Kolmogorov complexity, if two finite strings $s$ and $t$ are mutually computable from one another, there exists a constant $c'$ such that

$$
|K(s) - K(t)| \le c'.
$$

Applying this to $s = X_0 X_1 \dots X_{N_n - 1}$ and $t$ the concatenation of the binary expansions of $U_0, \dots, U_n$, we obtain

$$
K(U_0, \dots, U_n) \ge K(X_0 \dots X_{N_n - 1}) - c' \ge N_n - c - c' = N_n - C,
$$

where $C = c + c'$ is an absolute constant independent of $n$.

The length of the binary encoding of $(U_0, \dots, U_n)$ used here is exactly $N_n$ bits, since each $U_j$ is represented by $b(j)$ bits. Therefore, the prefix $(U_0, \dots, U_n)$ cannot be significantly compressed relative to its bit length. This is precisely the condition required by Definition R6 for a sequence over dyadic rationals: for each $n$, the Kolmogorov complexity of the first $n+1$ terms, measured in bits according to a fixed encoding, is at least the total number of bits minus a constant.

Consequently, the sequence $(U_n)$ inherits the R6 randomness of $(X_n)$. The construction preserves incompressibility because it is a computable, invertible regrouping of bits, and the Kolmogorov complexity bound carries through up to an additive constant.

This completes the proof.
