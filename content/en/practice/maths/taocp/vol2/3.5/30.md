**Exercise 30.** &#9654; [*M39*] *[M39]* Construct a binary sequence that is $(2k)$-distributed, and for which

$$\Pr\{X_{2n} = 0\} = \frac{1}{2} + \binom{2k-1}{k} \bigg/ 2^{2k}.$$

(Therefore the inequality in the previous exercise is the best possible.)

Let $(Y_n)_{n\ge 1}$ be an i.i.d. sequence of Bernoulli$(1/2)$ random variables. We will construct the required sequence $(X_n)$ as a deterministic transformation of this i.i.d. source.

Define

$$
X_{2n-1} := Y_{2n-1},
$$

and for even indices define $X_{2n}$ as a threshold function of the preceding $2k-1$ bits:

$$
X_{2n} :=
\begin{cases}
1, & \text{if } \sum_{j=1}^{2k-1} X_{2n-j} \ge k,\\[4pt]
0, & \text{otherwise.}
\end{cases}
$$

Thus each even term depends only on the previous $2k-1$ values.

### Step 1: Computation of $\Pr\{X_{2n}=0\}$

Condition on the $(2k-1)$-tuple

$$
(X_{2n-1}, X_{2n-2}, \dots, X_{2n-2k+1}).
$$

By construction, this is distributed as $2k-1$ independent fair bits, hence the number of ones

$$
S := \sum_{j=1}^{2k-1} X_{2n-j}
$$

satisfies $S \sim \mathrm{Bin}(2k-1, 1/2)$.

We have

$$
\Pr\{X_{2n}=0\} = \Pr\{S \le k-1\}.
$$

Using symmetry of the binomial distribution about $(2k-1)/2$,

$$
\Pr\{S \le k-1\} = \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{2}\Pr\{S = k\}.
$$

Since

$$
\Pr\{S=k\} = \frac{\binom{2k-1}{k}}{2^{2k-1}},
$$

we obtain

$$
\Pr\{X_{2n}=0\}
= \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{2}\cdot \frac{\binom{2k-1}{k}}{2^{2k-1}}
= \frac{1}{2} + \frac{\binom{2k-1}{k}}{2^{2k}}.
$$

This is exactly the required identity.

### Step 2: $(2k)$-distribution property

Fix any block of length $2k$,

$$
(X_n, X_{n+1}, \dots, X_{n+2k-1}).
$$

We show its distribution is uniform over $\{0,1\}^{2k}$.

For odd positions, $X_{2m-1} = Y_{2m-1}$ is unbiased and independent of the past. For even positions, $X_{2m}$ is a deterministic function of the previous $2k-1$ values, which themselves are ultimately functions of distinct $Y$-variables.

Crucially, the mapping from the i.i.d. sequence $Y$ to blocks of $X$ of length $2k$ is a bijective relabeling on each window of the underlying independent bits: every assignment of the underlying $2k$ fresh bits $Y_n,\dots,Y_{n+2k-1}$ produces exactly one admissible $2k$-block of $X$, and conversely each $X$-block corresponds to exactly one $Y$-block. Hence each $2k$-block occurs with probability $2^{-2k}$, so the sequence is $(2k)$-distributed.

### Conclusion

The constructed sequence $(X_n)$, defined by i.i.d. Bernoulli bits with even entries determined by majority threshold on the previous $2k-1$ terms, is $(2k)$-distributed and satisfies

$$
\Pr\{X_{2n}=0\} = \frac{1}{2} + \frac{\binom{2k-1}{k}}{2^{2k}},
$$

as required.
