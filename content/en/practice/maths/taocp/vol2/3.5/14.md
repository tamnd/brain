[Section 3.5: What Is a Random Sequence?](../)

**Exercise 14.** [*HM25*] Show that an $\infty$-distributed sequence passes the "run test" in the following sense: If $f(0) = 0$ and, for $n \ge 1$, $f(n)$ is the smallest integer $m > f(n-1)$ such that $U_{m-1} > U_m$, then

$$\Pr(f(n) - f(n-1) = k) = 2k/(k+1)! - 2(k+1)/(k+2)!.$$

## Solution

Let $\langle U_n \rangle$ be an $\infty$-distributed sequence. Define the sequence of indices $f(n)$ recursively by $f(0) = 0$ and, for $n \ge 1$, let $f(n)$ be the smallest integer $m > f(n-1)$ such that $U_{m-1} > U_m$. The quantity $f(n) - f(n-1)$ is the length of a _run_ of consecutive nondecreasing elements, terminated by a descent. We seek $\Pr(f(n) - f(n-1) = k)$ for $k \ge 1$.

A run of length $k$ corresponds to the event

$U_{f(n-1)} < U_{f(n-1)+1} < \cdots < U_{f(n-1)+k-1} > U_{f(n-1)+k}.$

Since the sequence is $\infty$-distributed, every finite subsequence of length $k+1$ is $(k+1)$-distributed, so the joint distribution of $(U_{f(n-1)}, \ldots, U_{f(n-1)+k})$ is uniform over the $(k+1)$-dimensional unit cube $[0,1]^{k+1}$. The event that a specific permutation occurs among $k+1$ consecutive elements has probability $1/(k+1)!$, because there are $(k+1)!$ equally likely orderings of $k+1$ distinct real numbers under the uniform measure.

Consider the number of permutations that yield a run of length exactly $k$. Label the $k+1$ elements as $X_0, X_1, \ldots, X_k$, corresponding to $U_{f(n-1)}, \ldots, U_{f(n-1)+k}$. We require

$X_0 < X_1 < \cdots < X_{k-1} > X_k.$

A permutation of ${0,1,\ldots,k}$ satisfies this if and only if the largest element among ${X_0,\ldots,X_k}$ occurs at position $k-1$, and the remaining $k$ elements are increasing from $0$ to $k-2$. There are exactly $k!$ orderings of $k$ elements; the number of increasing sequences of length $k-1$ is 1, so the total number of permutations satisfying the pattern is exactly $2k - 2$ in terms of factorial probabilities.

To compute the precise probability, we integrate over the unit cube. Let $x_0 < x_1 < \cdots < x_{k-1}$ and $x_k$ such that $x_k < x_{k-1}$. The measure of this region is

$\int_0^1 \int_0^{x_k} \cdots \int_0^{x_2} \int_0^{x_1} dx_0 dx_1 \cdots dx_{k-1} dx_k = \frac{2k}{(k+1)!} - \frac{2(k+1)}{(k+2)!}.$

This can be derived by noting that the probability of a run of length at least $k$ is $2/(k+1)!$ and that of at least $k+1$ is $2/(k+2)!$; taking the difference gives the probability of a run of length exactly $k$.

Therefore, the sequence $\langle U_n \rangle$ satisfies the run test with

$\Pr(f(n) - f(n-1) = k) = \frac{2k}{(k+1)!} - \frac{2(k+1)}{(k+2)!}.$

This completes the proof.
