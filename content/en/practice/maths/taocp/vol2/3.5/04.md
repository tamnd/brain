[Section 3.5: What Is a Random Sequence?](../)

**Exercise 4.** &#9654; [*HM14*] Prove that $\Pr(S(n)$ and $T(n)) + \Pr(S(n)$ or $T(n)) = \Pr(S(n)) + \Pr(T(n))$, when $S$ and $T$ are any two statements and at least one of the limits exist. For example, if a sequence is 2-distributed, we would find that

$$\Pr(u_n \le U_n < v_1 \text{ or } u_2 \le U_{n+1} < v_2) = v_1 - u_1 + v_2 - u_2 - (v_1 - u_1)(v_2 - u_2).$$

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m11s  

---

## Solution

Let $\nu_S(n)$ be the number of $j$, $0 \le j < n$, for which $S(j)$ is true, and define $\nu_T(n)$ and $\nu_{S \lor T}(n)$ analogously. Define also $\nu_{S \land T}(n)$ for the number of $j$ with both $S(j)$ and $T(j)$ true.

For every fixed $n$, each index $j < n$ contributes to exactly one of the disjoint cases: $S(j)$ only, $T(j)$ only, both, or neither. This gives the exact counting identity

$$
\nu_{S \lor T}(n) = \nu_S(n) + \nu_T(n) - \nu_{S \land T}(n).
$$

Divide by $n$ to obtain

$$
\frac{\nu_{S \lor T}(n)}{n}
=
\frac{\nu_S(n)}{n}
+
\frac{\nu_T(n)}{n}
-
\frac{\nu_{S \land T}(n)}{n}.
$$

Assume the limits defining $\Pr(S(n))$, $\Pr(T(n))$, and $\Pr(S(n)\ \text{or}\ T(n))$ exist. Taking limits as $n \to \infty$ yields

$$
\Pr(S \lor T)
=
\Pr(S)
+
\Pr(T)
-
\lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{\nu_{S \land T}(n)}{n}.
$$

Rearranging gives

$$
\lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{\nu_{S \land T}(n)}{n}
=
\Pr(S) + \Pr(T) - \Pr(S \lor T).
$$

The right-hand side is a real number determined by existing limits, so the limit on the left exists and defines $\Pr(S \land T)$. Substituting this expression back into the previous identity yields

$$
\Pr(S \land T) + \Pr(S \lor T) = \Pr(S) + \Pr(T).
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
