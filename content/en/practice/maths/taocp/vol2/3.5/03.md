[Section 3.5: What Is a Random Sequence?](../)

**Exercise 3.** [*M25*] Construct a periodic ternary sequence that is 3-distributed.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m25s  

---

## Solution

Let the ternary sequence be periodic with period $P$. By Definition D, a 3-distributed ternary sequence must satisfy

$$
\Pr(X_nX_{n+1}X_{n+2}=x_1x_2x_3)=\frac1{3^3}=\frac1{27}
$$

for every ternary number $x_1x_2x_3$.

Since a periodic sequence has frequencies determined by one period, each of the $27$ ternary words of length $3$ must occur equally often among the $P$ cyclic triples

$$
X_0X_1X_2,\ X_1X_2X_3,\ \ldots,\ X_{P-1}X_0X_1.
$$

The sum of the frequencies of all $27$ words is $P$. Because each frequency must be $P/27$, the integer $P$ must be divisible by $27$. The smallest possible period is therefore $P=27$, and in that case every ternary word of length $3$ must occur exactly once as a cyclic block.

Such a sequence is obtained from a de Bruijn cycle of order $3$ on the alphabet ${0,1,2}$. One example is

$$
000100201101202102211121222.
$$

Regarded cyclically, its $27$ consecutive triples are

$$
\begin{aligned}
&000,\ 001,\ 010,\ 100,\ 002,\ 020,\ 201,\ 011,\ 110,\\
&101,\ 012,\ 120,\ 202,\ 021,\ 210,\ 102,\ 022,\ 221,\\
&211,\ 111,\ 112,\ 121,\ 212,\ 122,\ 222,\ 220,\ 200,
\end{aligned}
$$

which are exactly the $27$ ternary numbers of length $3$, each appearing once.

Therefore, in one period each ternary word $x_1x_2x_3$ occurs with frequency

$$
\frac{1}{27}.
$$

Hence

$$
\Pr(X_nX_{n+1}X_{n+2}=x_1x_2x_3)=\frac1{27}
$$

for every ternary word $x_1x_2x_3$. By Definition D, the sequence is 3-distributed.

Thus a periodic ternary 3-distributed sequence is obtained by repeating

$$
000100201101202102211121222.
$$

The required construction is

$$
\boxed{000100201101202102211121222000100201101202102211121222\cdots }.
$$

∎

## Notes

The construction is a ternary de Bruijn cycle of order $3$. In general, a de Bruijn cycle of order $k$ on $b$ symbols yields a periodic $b$-ary sequence that is $k$-distributed, because every $k$-digit $b$-ary number appears exactly once per period.
