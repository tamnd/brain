[Section 3.5: What Is a Random Sequence?](../)

**Exercise 3.** [*M25*] Construct a periodic ternary sequence that is 3-distributed.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m16s  

---

Let the alphabet be $\{0,1,2\}$. We construct a periodic ternary sequence in which every ternary word of length $3$ appears exactly once per period, which implies 3-distribution.

### Construction

Consider the directed de Bruijn graph $G$ of order $2$ over $\{0,1,2\}$. Its vertices are all words $ab$ with $a,b \in \{0,1,2\}$, so there are $3^2 = 9$ vertices. For each vertex $ab$ and each symbol $c \in \{0,1,2\}$, draw a directed edge

$$
ab \to bc
$$

and label this edge by the length-3 word $abc$.

Thus each vertex has outdegree $3$ and indegree $3$. Moreover, the graph is strongly connected because any word $ab$ can be shifted into any other word $cd$ by appropriate transitions.

Hence $G$ is Eulerian, so it contains an Eulerian circuit $C$.

### Formation of the sequence

Traverse an Eulerian circuit in $G$. Each edge traversal produces a length-3 word $abc$. Successive edges overlap in their last two symbols, so reading the labels in order produces a cyclic ternary sequence $S$ of period $3^3 = 27$, since the circuit uses each of the $27$ edges exactly once.

Equivalently, $S$ is a cyclic sequence whose length-3 consecutive blocks correspond exactly to the edges of $G$, each appearing once per period.

### Verification of 3-distribution

By construction, every possible ternary word of length $3$ corresponds to exactly one edge in $G$, hence appears exactly once in the Eulerian circuit. Therefore, in one period of $S$, each length-3 block over $\{0,1,2\}$ occurs exactly once.

Thus every length-3 word appears with equal frequency $1$ per period, so the sequence is 3-distributed.

### Periodic ternary sequence

The required periodic ternary sequence is obtained by repeating the cyclic sequence $S$ infinitely. This yields a periodic ternary sequence that is 3-distributed, as required.
