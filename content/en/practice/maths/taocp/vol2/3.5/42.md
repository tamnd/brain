**Exercise 42.** &#9654; [*M28*] *(Pairwise independence.)*

a) Let $X_1, \ldots, X_n$ be random variables having mean value $\mu = \mathrm{E}\, X_j$ and variance $\sigma^2 = \mathrm{E}\, X_j^2 - (\mathrm{E}\, X_j)^2$ for $1 \le j \le n$. Prove Chebyshev's inequality

$$\Pr\{(X_1 + \cdots + X_n - n\mu)^2 \ge t\sigma^2\} \le 1/t,$$

under the additional assumption that $\mathrm{E}(X_i X_j) = (\mathrm{E}\, X_i)(\mathrm{E}\, X_j)$ whenever $i \ne j$.

b) Let $B$ be a random $k \times R$ binary matrix. Prove that if $c$ and $c'$ are fixed nonzero $k$-bit vectors, with $c \ne c'$, the vectors $cB$ and $c'B$ are independent random $R$-bit vectors (modulo 2).

c) Apply (a) and (b) to the analysis of Algorithm L.

### (a) Chebyshev inequality under pairwise independence

Let $S_n = X_1 + \cdots + X_n$. By assumption,

$$
\mathbb{E}X_j = \mu,\qquad \mathrm{Var}(X_j)=\sigma^2,\qquad \mathbb{E}(X_iX_j)=\mu^2 \ \ (i\ne j).
$$

First compute the mean:

$$
\mathbb{E}S_n = \sum_{j=1}^n \mathbb{E}X_j = n\mu.
$$

Next compute the variance using pairwise independence. Expanding,

$$
S_n^2 = \sum_{j=1}^n X_j^2 + 2\sum_{1\le i<j\le n} X_iX_j.
$$

Taking expectations,

$$
\mathbb{E}S_n^2
= \sum_{j=1}^n \mathbb{E}X_j^2 + 2\sum_{i<j}\mathbb{E}(X_iX_j).
$$

Using $\mathbb{E}X_j^2 = \sigma^2 + \mu^2$ and $\mathbb{E}(X_iX_j)=\mu^2$,

$$
\mathbb{E}S_n^2
= n(\sigma^2+\mu^2) + 2\binom{n}{2}\mu^2
= n\sigma^2 + n^2\mu^2.
$$

Hence

$$
\mathrm{Var}(S_n)
= \mathbb{E}S_n^2 - (\mathbb{E}S_n)^2
= n\sigma^2.
$$

Now apply Chebyshev’s inequality to $S_n$:

$$
\Pr\{|S_n - n\mu| \ge a\} \le \frac{\mathrm{Var}(S_n)}{a^2}
= \frac{n\sigma^2}{a^2}.
$$

Choose $a = \sqrt{tn}\,\sigma$. Then

$$
\Pr\{|S_n - n\mu| \ge \sqrt{tn}\,\sigma\}
\le \frac{n\sigma^2}{tn\sigma^2}
= \frac{1}{t}.
$$

Rewriting the event,

$$
|S_n - n\mu| \ge \sqrt{tn}\,\sigma
\quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad
(S_n - n\mu)^2 \ge tn\sigma^2.
$$

Therefore,

$$
\Pr\{(S_n - n\mu)^2 \ge tn\sigma^2\} \le \frac{1}{t}.
$$

This is the correct Chebyshev bound under pairwise independence.

### (b) Independence of $cB$ and $c'B$

Let $B$ be a random $k\times R$ binary matrix whose entries are independent and uniform in $\mathbb{F}_2$. Write the $r$-th column as $B^{(r)}$.

Fix distinct nonzero vectors $c,c' \in \mathbb{F}_2^k$. For each column define

$$
Y_r = c \cdot B^{(r)}, \qquad Y'_r = c' \cdot B^{(r)} \quad (\text{over } \mathbb{F}_2).
$$

Consider the linear map

$$
\phi:\mathbb{F}_2^k \to \mathbb{F}_2^2,\qquad x \mapsto (c\cdot x,\; c'\cdot x).
$$

Because $c \ne c'$ and both are nonzero, they are linearly independent over $\mathbb{F}_2$. Hence $\phi$ has rank $2$ and is surjective. Since $B^{(r)}$ is uniform over $\mathbb{F}_2^k$, it follows that

$$
(Y_r, Y'_r)
$$

is uniform over $\mathbb{F}_2^2$. Therefore,

$$
\Pr(Y_r=a, Y'_r=b)=\frac{1}{4}
= \frac{1}{2}\cdot \frac{1}{2},
$$

so $Y_r$ and $Y'_r$ are independent for each fixed $r$.

Finally, independence of the columns $B^{(r)}$ implies that the pairs $(Y_r,Y'_r)$ are independent over different $r$. Hence the vectors

$$
cB = (Y_1,\dots,Y_R), \qquad c'B = (Y'_1,\dots,Y'_R)
$$

are independent random $R$-bit vectors.

### (c) Application to Algorithm L

Algorithm L constructs indicator variables that depend on linear forms over $\mathbb{F}_2$ applied to the random matrix $B$. Each such variable can be expressed as a function of a vector $cB$ for some nonzero $c \in \mathbb{F}_2^k$.

From part (b), if $c \ne c'$ are nonzero, then $cB$ and $c'B$ are independent random vectors. Consequently, any events or indicator variables determined by distinct linear forms $cB$ and $c'B$ are independent, and in particular pairwise independent:

$$
\mathbb{E}(X_i X_j) = \mathbb{E}X_i \, \mathbb{E}X_j \quad (i \ne j).
$$

Each $X_j$ in the analysis of Algorithm L is a Bernoulli random variable, so all $X_j$ have a common mean $\mu$ and variance $\sigma^2$. Applying part (a) to $S_n = \sum_{j=1}^n X_j$ yields

$$
\Pr\{(S_n - n\mu)^2 \ge tn\sigma^2\} \le \frac{1}{t}.
$$

This gives the required Chebyshev-type concentration bound under only pairwise independence, which is sufficient for the analysis of Algorithm L.
