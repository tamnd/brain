[Section 3.5: What Is a Random Sequence?](../)

**Exercise 12.** [*HM20*] Show that a $k$-distributed sequence passes the "maximum-of-$k$ test," in the following sense: $\Pr(v \le \max(U_n, U_{n+1}, \ldots, U_{n+k-1}) < v) = v^k - u^k$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m41s  

---

## Solution

There is a typographical error in the statement. The intended assertion is

$$
\Pr\bigl(u \le \max(U_n,U_{n+1},\ldots,U_{n+k-1}) < v\bigr)
   = v^k-u^k .
$$

Let

$$
M_n=\max(U_n,U_{n+1},\ldots,U_{n+k-1}).
$$

The event $M_n<v$ is equivalent to

$$
U_n<v,\ U_{n+1}<v,\ \ldots,\ U_{n+k-1}<v.
$$

Since the sequence is $k$-distributed, equation (5) gives

$$
\Pr(M_n<v)
 =\Pr(0\le U_n<v,\ldots,0\le U_{n+k-1}<v)
 =(v-0)^k
 =v^k.
$$

Similarly, the event $M_n<u$ is equivalent to

$$
U_n<u,\ U_{n+1}<u,\ \ldots,\ U_{n+k-1}<u,
$$

hence

$$
\Pr(M_n<u)
 =\Pr(0\le U_n<u,\ldots,0\le U_{n+k-1}<u)
 =(u-0)^k
 =u^k.
$$

Now

$$
\{u\le M_n<v\}
=
\{M_n<v\}\setminus\{M_n<u\},
$$

and ${M_n<u}\subseteq{M_n<v}$. Therefore, by additivity of probabilities for disjoint events,

$$
\Pr(u\le M_n<v)
=
\Pr(M_n<v)-\Pr(M_n<u)
=
v^k-u^k.
$$

Hence a $k$-distributed sequence passes the maximum-of-$k$ test:

$$
\boxed{\Pr\bigl(u \le \max(U_n,U_{n+1},\ldots,U_{n+k-1}) < v\bigr)=v^k-u^k.}
$$

This completes the proof.

∎
