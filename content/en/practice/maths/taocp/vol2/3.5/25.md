**Exercise 25.** [*HM20*] *[HM20]* A sequence is called a "white sequence" if all serial correlations are zero; that is, if the equation in Corollary S is true for all $k \ge 1$. (By Corollary S, an $\infty$-distributed sequence is white.) Show that if a $\{0\..1\}$ sequence is equidistributed, it is white if and only if

$$\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{n} \sum_{0 \le j < n} (U_j - \tfrac{1}{2})(U_{j+k} - \tfrac{1}{2}) = 0, \qquad \text{for all } k \ge 1.$$

Let $(U_n)$ be a $\{0,1\}$-valued sequence that is equidistributed in $[0,1)$. Then, in particular,

$$
\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{1}{n}\sum_{j<n} U_j = \frac{1}{2}.
$$

Hence the mean of the sequence is $1/2$, and we may work with centered variables $X_j := U_j - \tfrac{1}{2}$.

### Step 1. Serial correlations (Corollary S)

By Corollary S (TAOCP), the serial correlation at lag $k$ is

$$
\rho_k = \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{1}{n}\sum_{j<n} \left(U_j - \mu\right)\left(U_{j+k} - \mu\right),
$$

where $\mu = \mathbb{E}(U_j)$. For an equidistributed $\{0,1\}$-sequence, $\mu = 1/2$, so

$$
\rho_k = \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{1}{n}\sum_{j<n} \left(U_j - \tfrac{1}{2}\right)\left(U_{j+k} - \tfrac{1}{2}\right).
$$

A sequence is **white** iff $\rho_k = 0$ for all $k \ge 1$.

### Step 2. “Only if” direction

Assume the sequence is white. Then by definition, for every $k \ge 1$,

$$
\rho_k = 0.
$$

Using the expression from Corollary S,

$$
0 = \rho_k = \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{1}{n}\sum_{j<n} \left(U_j - \tfrac{1}{2}\right)\left(U_{j+k} - \tfrac{1}{2}\right).
$$

This gives the required limit condition.

### Step 3. “If” direction

Assume that for every $k \ge 1$,

$$
\lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{1}{n}\sum_{j<n} \left(U_j - \tfrac{1}{2}\right)\left(U_{j+k} - \tfrac{1}{2}\right) = 0.
$$

Then, again by Corollary S, the left-hand side is exactly the serial correlation $\rho_k$. Hence $\rho_k = 0$ for all $k \ge 1$, so the sequence is white by definition.

### Step 4. Role of equidistribution

Equidistribution ensures that the marginal frequency satisfies

$$
\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{1}{n}\sum_{j<n} U_j = \frac{1}{2},
$$

so the centering constant in Corollary S is $\mu = 1/2$. This allows the serial correlation to be written exactly in the symmetric form

$$
\left(U_j - \tfrac{1}{2}\right)\left(U_{j+k} - \tfrac{1}{2}\right),
$$

which is the expression appearing in the statement.

### Conclusion

For an equidistributed $\{0,1\}$-sequence, the definition of white sequence (vanishing serial correlations for all $k \ge 1$) is equivalent to

$$
\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{n} \sum_{0 \le j < n} (U_j - \tfrac{1}{2})(U_{j+k} - \tfrac{1}{2}) = 0
\quad \text{for all } k \ge 1.
$$
