**Exercise 15.** &#9654; [*HM30*] Show that an $\infty$-distributed sequence passes the "coupon-collector's test" when there are two kinds (kinds) of coupons, in the following sense: Let $X_1, X_2, \ldots$ be an $\infty$-distributed binary sequence. Let $f(0) = 0$ and for $n \ge 1$ let $f(n)$ be the smallest integer $m > f(n-1)$ such that $\{X_{f(n-1)+1}, \ldots, X_m\}$ is the set $\{0, 1\}$. Prove that $\Pr(f(n) - f(n-1) = k) = 2^{2-k}$, for $k \ge 2$. (See exercise 7.)

## Solution

Let

$$
Y_i=X_{f(n-1)+i}\qquad (i\ge 1).
$$

Since $X_1,X_2,\ldots$ is $\infty$-distributed, every finite binary block occurs with the same probability as in a sequence of independent fair coin tosses. In particular, for every $r\ge 1$ and every binary string $b_1\cdots b_r$,

$$
\Pr(Y_1=b_1,\ldots,Y_r=b_r)=2^{-r}.
$$

Hence the variables $Y_1,Y_2,\ldots$ are independent and each takes the values $0$ and $1$ with probability $1/2$.

By definition, $f(n)-f(n-1)$ is the length of the shortest block beginning at $f(n-1)+1$ that contains both symbols $0$ and $1$.

Fix $k\ge2$. The event

$$
f(n)-f(n-1)=k
$$

occurs exactly when the first $k-1$ symbols after time $f(n-1)$ are all equal, and the $k$th symbol is the opposite value. Indeed, if the first change of bit occurs earlier than position $k$, then both symbols would already have appeared before time $k$; and if no change occurs by position $k$, then the interval length exceeds $k$.

Therefore

$$
\{f(n)-f(n-1)=k\}
=
E_0\cup E_1,
$$

where

$$
E_0=\{Y_1=\cdots=Y_{k-1}=0,\;Y_k=1\},
$$

and

$$
E_1=\{Y_1=\cdots=Y_{k-1}=1,\;Y_k=0\}.
$$

These two events are disjoint.

For a fixed choice of the initial bit, all $k$ bits are prescribed. Hence

$$
\Pr(E_0)=2^{-k},
\qquad
\Pr(E_1)=2^{-k}.
$$

Since $E_0$ and $E_1$ are disjoint,

$$
\Pr(f(n)-f(n-1)=k)
=
\Pr(E_0)+\Pr(E_1)
=
2^{-k}+2^{-k}
=
2^{1-k}.
$$

Thus, for every $k\ge2$,

$$
\boxed{\Pr(f(n)-f(n-1)=k)=2^{\,1-k}.}
$$

As a check,

$$
\sum_{k=2}^{\infty}2^{1-k}
=
\sum_{j=1}^{\infty}2^{-j}
=
1,
$$

so this is a valid probability distribution.
