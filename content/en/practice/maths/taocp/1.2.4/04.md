[Section 1.2.4 — Integer Functions and Elementary Number Theory](../)

**Exercise 4.** &#9654; [*M10*] Using the previous exercise, prove that $\lfloor -x \rfloor = -\lceil x \rceil$.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 39s  

---

Let $n = \lceil x \rceil$. By definition, $n$ is the least integer such that $x \le n$, which implies $x - 1 < n - 1 \le x - 0$. Consider $-x$. We have

$-(x) - 1 < -n \le -x,$

because multiplying the inequalities $x \le n$ and $x - 1 < n - 1$ by $-1$ reverses the inequalities. By the characterization of the floor function, this inequality shows that $\lfloor -x \rfloor = -n = -\lceil x \rceil$.

This completes the proof. ∎
