[Section 1.2.4 — Integer Functions and Elementary Number Theory](../)

**Exercise 11.** [*00*] What does “$x \equiv y \pmod 0$” mean by our conventions?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 31s  

---

By definition (5), the statement

$x \equiv y \pmod 0$

means that

$x \bmod 0 = y \bmod 0.$

By the convention in equation (1),

$x \bmod 0 = x,$

hence

$x \equiv y \pmod 0$

means precisely that

$x=y.$

Therefore the congruence modulo $0$ reduces to ordinary equality:

$\boxed{x \equiv y \pmod 0 \iff x=y.}$
