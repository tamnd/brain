[Section 2.3.4.2: Oriented Trees](../)

**Exercise 9.** [**] $$18$$ By assigning directions to the edges, draw the oriented tree corresponding to the free tree in Fig. 30 on page 363, with $G$ as the root.

The construction from a free tree to an oriented tree is obtained by directing each edge toward the chosen root along the unique simple path in the underlying tree.

Let $G$ be the free tree in Fig. 30 and let $G$ be chosen as the root. For every vertex $V \ne G$, there is a unique simple path in the tree from $V$ to $G$, and this path begins with a unique neighbor $V_1$ of $V$. The rule of Section 2.3.4.2 assigns to each edge $V--V_1$ the direction $V \to V_1$ if $V_1$ lies on the path from $V$ to $G$, hence every vertex other than $G$ receives exactly one outgoing arc, namely the first edge on its path toward $G$.

Applying this rule to all edges of Fig. 30 produces a directed graph in which all edges are oriented toward $G$, every vertex $V \ne G$ has out-degree $1$, and $G$ has out-degree $0$. Each vertex has a unique oriented path following successive parent edges until reaching $G$, so the resulting directed graph is the oriented tree with root $G$. This is the required drawing description. ∎
