[Section 2.3.4.2: Oriented Trees](../)

**Exercise 15.** [**] $$M20$$ True or false: A directed graph that is connected and balanced is strongly connected.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m28s  

---

False.

Consider the directed graph with vertices $A$ and $B$, and with two arcs: one from $A$ to $B$ and one from $B$ to $A$. Add a third vertex $C$ together with a loop from $C$ to itself.

This graph is connected, because the corresponding undirected graph has the edge $A-B$, and the loop at $C$ is discarded when forming the graph $G_0$; hence the example must be modified so that $C$ is joined to the others. Instead, take vertices $A,B,C$ with arcs

$$
A\to B,\qquad B\to A,\qquad A\to C,\qquad C\to A.
$$

Then the graph is connected. Each vertex has equal in-degree and out-degree:

$$
\deg^+(A)=2=\deg^-(A),\qquad
\deg^+(B)=1=\deg^-(B),\qquad
\deg^+(C)=1=\deg^-(C).
$$

Hence the graph is balanced.

However, this example is actually strongly connected, so it does not serve as a counterexample. A correct counterexample is obtained by taking two disjoint directed cycles and identifying one vertex from the first cycle with one vertex from the second. The resulting graph is connected, and every vertex has equal in-degree and out-degree, so it is balanced. Yet there is no oriented path from a vertex on one cycle to a nonshared vertex on the other cycle, because every arc of each cycle remains directed around that cycle. Therefore the graph is not strongly connected.

Hence a connected and balanced directed graph need not be strongly connected. The statement is **false**.

$$
\boxed{\text{False}}
$$
