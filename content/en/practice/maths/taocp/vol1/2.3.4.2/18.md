[Section 2.3.4.2: Oriented Trees](../)

**Exercise 18.** [**] $$M30$$ Let $G$ be a graph with $n+1$ vertices $V_0,V_1,\ldots,V_n$ and $m$ edges $e_1,\ldots,e_m$. Make $G$ into a directed graph by assigning an arbitrary orientation to each edge; then construct the $m\times(n+1)$ matrix $A$ with

$$
a_{ij}=
\begin{cases}
+1, & \text{if } \operatorname{init}(e_i)=V_j;\\
-1, & \text{if } \operatorname{fin}(e_i)=V_j;\\
0, & \text{otherwise.}
\end{cases}
$$

Let $A_0$ be the $m\times n$ matrix $A$ with column 0 deleted.

a) If $m=n$, show that the determinant of $A_0$ is equal to 0 if $G$ is not a free tree, and equal to $\pm1$ if $G$ is a free tree.

b) Show that for general $m$ the determinant of $A_0^T A_0$ is the number of free subtrees of $G$ (namely the number of ways to choose $n$ of the $m$ edges so that the resulting graph is a free tree). [*Hint:* Use (a) and the result of exercise 1.2.3-46.]

Let the free graph $G$ with vertices $V_0,V_1,\ldots,V_n$ be given an arbitrary orientation of its $m$ edges, and form the incidence matrix $A$ and the reduced matrix $A_0$ by deleting the column corresponding to $V_0$.

For part (a), first assume that $G$ is not a free tree. Then $G$ contains a cycle in the underlying undirected sense, so there exists a nonempty set of edges whose incidences cancel in every vertex, producing a nontrivial linear dependence among the rows of $A_0$. Indeed, along any undirected cycle, each vertex is incident with exactly two chosen edges in the cycle, one contributing $+1$ and the other contributing $-1$ in the corresponding column of $A_0$, so the sum of the corresponding rows is the zero vector. Hence the rows of $A_0$ are linearly dependent, so $\det(A_0)=0$.

Now assume that $G$ is a free tree. Proceed by induction on $n$. For $n=1$, the matrix $A_0$ is $[1]$ or $[-1]$, so its determinant is $\pm1$. Assume the statement holds for all trees with $n-1$ edges. In a tree there exists a leaf vertex $V_j \ne V_0$ incident with exactly one edge $e_i$. In the column corresponding to $V_j$, exactly one entry of $A_0$ is nonzero, equal to $\pm1$. Expanding $\det(A_0)$ along this column reduces the determinant to $\pm \det(A_0')$, where $A_0'$ corresponds to the tree obtained by deleting $V_j$ and $e_i$. This reduced graph is again a free tree, so by the induction hypothesis $\det(A_0')=\pm1$, hence $\det(A_0)=\pm1$.

For part (b), let $m \ge n$ and consider all choices of $n$ edges among $e_1,\ldots,e_m$. Each choice determines an $n\times n$ submatrix of $A_0$, and by part (a) its determinant is $0$ unless the chosen edges form a free tree, in which case the determinant is $\pm1$. Therefore the determinant of $A_0^T A_0$ expands, via the Cauchy–Binet formula, as the sum of squares of these $n\times n$ minors. Each free subtree contributes $1$, and every non-tree selection contributes $0$. Hence $\det(A_0^T A_0)$ equals the number of free subtrees of $G$. ∎
