**Exercise 8.** [**] $$HM40$$ Study the properties of *automorphism groups* of oriented trees, namely the groups consisting of all permutations $\pi$ of the vertices and arcs for which we have $\operatorname{init}(e\pi)=\operatorname{init}(e)\pi$, $\operatorname{fin}(e\pi)=\operatorname{fin}(e)\pi$.

Let $T$ be an oriented tree with root $R$. An automorphism of $T$ is a permutation $\pi$ of the vertices and arcs that preserves the initial and final vertices of each arc, that is, $\operatorname{init}(e\pi)=\operatorname{init}(e)\pi$ and $\operatorname{fin}(e\pi)=\operatorname{fin}(e)\pi$. Since each vertex $V\ne R$ has a unique outgoing arc $e[V]$, the image of $V$ under $\pi$ must have its unique outgoing arc mapped to $e[V]\pi$, and the root $R$ must be fixed because it has no outgoing arcs. Hence any automorphism is completely determined by its action on the subtrees rooted at the children of $R$.

More generally, if a vertex $V$ has children $V_1,\ldots,V_k$, then $\pi$ can permute the subtrees rooted at these children independently, but the internal structure of each subtree must be preserved. Therefore the automorphism group of an oriented tree is the iterated wreath product of symmetric groups corresponding to the sets of children at each vertex. In particular, if all vertices have distinct numbers of children or asymmetrical subtrees, the automorphism group may be trivial; if multiple children have identical subtrees, the automorphism group contains nontrivial permutations exchanging these subtrees.
