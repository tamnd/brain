[Section 2.3.4.2: Oriented Trees](../)

**Exercise 21.** [**] $$HM38$$ (T. van Aardenne-Ehrenfest and N. G. de Bruijn.) Figure 36 is an example of a directed graph that is not only balanced, it is *regular*, which means that every vertex has the same in-degree and out-degree as every other vertex. Let $G$ be a regular digraph with $n$ vertices $V_0,V_1,\ldots,V_{n-1}$, in which every vertex has in-degree and out-degree equal to $m$. (Hence there are $mn$ arcs in all.) Let $G^*$ be the digraph with $mn$ vertices corresponding to the arcs of $G$; let a vertex of $G^*$ corresponding to an arc from $V_j$ to $V_k$ in $G$ be denoted by $V_{jk}$. An arc goes from $V_{jk}$ to $V_{j'k'}$ in $G^*$ if and only if $k=j'$. For example, if $G$ is the directed graph of Fig. 36, $G^*$ is shown in Fig. 37. An Eulerian trail in $G$ is a Hamiltonian cycle in $G^*$ and conversely.

Prove that the number of oriented subtrees of $G^*$ is $m^{(m-1)n}$ times the number of oriented subtrees of $G$. [*Hint:* Use exercise 19.]

Let $A$ be the matrix of exercise 19 for $G$, and let $A^_$ be the corresponding matrix for $G^_$. By exercise 19(b), the required numbers of oriented subtrees are $\det A_0$ and $\det A_0^_$, respectively. Index the rows and columns of $A^_$ by the vertices $V_{jk}$, where $V_j\to V_k$ is an arc of $G$. Since every vertex of $G^_$ has out-degree $m$, each diagonal entry of $A^_$ is $m$. Furthermore, the row indexed by $V_{jk}$ has entry $-1$ in every column indexed by a vertex $V_{k\ell}$, and $0$ elsewhere. Thus rows having the same terminal vertex $V_k$ are identical. If the $m$ columns corresponding to arcs entering a fixed vertex $V_k$ are added together, every row block becomes exactly the row of $A$ indexed by $V_k$. Hence, after this column operation is carried out for every $k$, the matrix $A_0^*$ becomes block triangular, with one copy of $A_0$ and, for each of the $n$ vertex blocks, an additional diagonal block of size $(m-1)\times(m-1)$ equal to $mI$.

Column additions do not change the determinant, and the determinant of a block triangular matrix is the product of the determinants of its diagonal blocks. Therefore

$$
\det A_0^*
=
\det A_0\,(m^{m-1})^n
=
m^{(m-1)n}\det A_0.
$$

Applying exercise 19(b) to both graphs, the number of oriented subtrees of $G^*$ is therefore

$$
\boxed{m^{(m-1)n}\times\text{(number of oriented subtrees of }G\text{)}.}
$$
