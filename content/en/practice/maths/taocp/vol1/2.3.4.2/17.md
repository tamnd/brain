**Exercise 17.** [**] $$M32$$ What is the probability of winning the solitaire game of clock (described in exercise 16), assuming the deck is randomly shuffled? What is the probability that exactly $k$ cards are still face down when the game is over?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m11s  

---

By Exercise 16, the game is won if and only if the digraph on $V_1,\ldots,V_{13}$ determined by the bottom cards is an oriented tree. Since the deck is randomly shuffled, the bottom card of each of the $12$ outer piles is equally likely to be any of the $13$ ranks, independently of the others. Hence the digraph is obtained by choosing, for each $j=1,\ldots,12$, an arc from $V_j$ to one of the $13$ vertices, all $13^{12}$ possibilities being equally likely.

An oriented tree on these vertices has root $V_{13}$, because $V_{13}$ is the unique vertex with no outgoing arc. By Cayley's formula, the number of free trees on $13$ labeled vertices is $13^{11}$. For each such tree there is exactly one orientation toward the chosen root $V_{13}$, and every oriented tree arises in this way. Therefore the number of winning configurations is $13^{11}$, so the probability of winning is

$$
\frac{13^{11}}{13^{12}}=\frac1{13}.
$$

More generally, if exactly $k$ cards remain face down at the end, the play has traversed exactly $52-k$ cards. The corresponding functional digraph contains a unique component rooted at $V_{13}$, and this component has exactly $(52-k)/4=13-k/4$ vertices. Choosing the vertices of this rooted component, then choosing an oriented tree on them directed toward $V_{13}$, and finally choosing arbitrary functional digraphs on the remaining vertices that do not contain $V_{13}$, gives

$$
\binom{12}{m-1}m^{\,m-2}(13-m)^{\,13-m}
$$

configurations, where $m=13-k/4$. Dividing by $13^{12}$ yields

$$
\Pr\{k\text{ cards remain face down}\}
=
\frac{\binom{12}{m-1}m^{\,m-2}(13-m)^{\,13-m}}{13^{12}},
\qquad m=13-\frac{k}{4}.
$$

In particular,

$$
\Pr\{\text{win}\}=\Pr\{k=0\}=\frac1{13}.
$$

$$
\boxed{\Pr\{\text{win}\}=\frac1{13},\qquad
\Pr\{k\text{ cards face down}\}
=
\frac{\binom{12}{\,12-k/4\,}(13-k/4)^{\,11-k/4}(k/4)^{\,k/4}}
{13^{12}}}
$$

for $k=0,4,8,\ldots,48$.
