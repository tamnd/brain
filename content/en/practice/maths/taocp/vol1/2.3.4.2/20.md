[Section 2.3.4.2: Oriented Trees](../)

**Exercise 20.** [**] $$M21$$ If $G$ is an undirected graph on $n+1$ vertices $V_0,\ldots,V_n$, let $B$ be the $n\times n$ matrix defined as follows for $1\le i,j\le n$:

$$
b_{ij}=
\begin{cases}
t, & \text{if } i=j \text{ and there are } t \text{ edges touching } V_j;\\
-1, & \text{if } i\ne j \text{ and } V_i \text{ is adjacent to } V_j;\\
0, & \text{otherwise.}
\end{cases}
$$

For example, if $G$ is the graph of Fig. 29 on page 363, with $(V_0,V_1,V_2,V_3,V_4)=(A,B,C,D,E)$, we find that

$$
B=
\begin{pmatrix}
3 & 0 & -1 & -1\\
0 & 2 & -1 & 0\\
-1 & -1 & 3 & -1\\
-1 & 0 & -1 & 2
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Show that the number of free subtrees of $G$ is $\det B$. [*Hint:* Use exercise 18 or 19.]

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m55s  

---

Assign an arbitrary orientation to each edge of $G$ and form the incidence matrix $A$ as in Exercise 18, with rows indexed by edges and columns indexed by vertices $V_0,\ldots,V_n$. The entry in row $e$ and column $V_j$ is $+1$ if $\operatorname{init}(e)=V_j$, $-1$ if $\operatorname{fin}(e)=V_j$, and $0$ otherwise. Let $A_0$ be obtained from $A$ by deleting column $0$.

By Exercise 18(b), the number of free subtrees of $G$ equals $\det(A_0^T A_0)$, so it suffices to show $A_0^T A_0 = B$.

Fix $1\le i,j\le n$. The $(i,j)$ entry of $A_0^T A_0$ is

$$
(A_0^T A_0)_{ij}=\sum_{e} a_{e i}a_{e j},
$$

where $a_{e i}$ denotes the entry of $A_0$ corresponding to edge $e$ and vertex $V_i$.

First consider $i=j$. For a fixed edge $e$, the value $a_{e i}^2$ equals $1$ if $e$ is incident with $V_i$ and equals $0$ otherwise, since $a_{e i}\in{-1,0,1}$. Therefore

$$
(A_0^T A_0)_{ii}=\sum_e a_{e i}^2
$$

counts exactly the number of edges incident with $V_i$. This is precisely the definition of $b_{ii}$.

Now consider $i\ne j$. For a fixed edge $e$, the product $a_{e i}a_{e j}$ is nonzero only if $e$ is incident with both $V_i$ and $V_j$, which occurs exactly when $e$ joins $V_i$ and $V_j$. In that case, the orientation of $e$ must be either $V_i\to V_j$ or $V_j\to V_i$. In the first case $a_{e i}=1$ and $a_{e j}=-1$, giving $a_{e i}a_{e j}=-1$. In the second case $a_{e i}=-1$ and $a_{e j}=1$, again giving $a_{e i}a_{e j}=-1$. Hence every edge between $V_i$ and $V_j$ contributes $-1$ to the sum, and all other edges contribute $0$. Therefore

$$
(A_0^T A_0)_{ij}=-\{\text{number of edges between }V_i\text{ and }V_j\},
$$

which matches $b_{ij}$ for $i\ne j$.

Thus every entry of $A_0^T A_0$ agrees with the corresponding entry of $B$, so $A_0^T A_0=B$. Taking determinants gives

$$
\det B=\det(A_0^T A_0),
$$

and by Exercise 18(b) this equals the number of free subtrees of $G$. This completes the proof. ∎
