**Exercise 24.** [**] $$M20$$ Let $G$ be a connected digraph with arcs $e_0,e_1,\ldots,e_m$. Let $E_0,E_1,\ldots,E_m$ be a set of positive integers that satisfy Kirchhoff's law for $G$; that is, for each vertex $V$,

$$
\sum_{\operatorname{init}(e_j)=V} E_j
=
\sum_{\operatorname{fin}(e_j)=V} E_j.
$$

Assume further that $E_0=1$. Prove that there is an oriented walk in $G$ from $\operatorname{fin}(e_0)$ to $\operatorname{init}(e_0)$ such that edge $e_j$ appears exactly $E_j$ times, for $1\le j\le m$, while edge $e_0$ does not appear. [*Hint:* Apply Theorem G to a suitable directed graph.]

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m52s  

---

Construct a directed multigraph $G'$ from $G$ by replacing each arc $e_j$ with $E_j$ parallel arcs, for $1\le j\le m$, and omitting $e_0$. Let $u=\operatorname{init}(e_0)$ and $v=\operatorname{fin}(e_0)$.

For each vertex $V$, the Kirchhoff law for $(E_0,\dots,E_m)$ gives

$$
\sum_{\operatorname{init}(e_j)=V} E_j=\sum_{\operatorname{fin}(e_j)=V} E_j.
$$

After deleting $e_0$, this balance is preserved at every vertex except $u$ and $v$. At $u$, the outgoing sum decreases by $1$, so in $G'$ we have $\operatorname{outdeg}(u)=\operatorname{indeg}(u)-1$, hence $\operatorname{indeg}(u)=\operatorname{outdeg}(u)+1$. At $v$, the incoming sum decreases by $1$, hence $\operatorname{outdeg}(v)=\operatorname{indeg}(v)+1$. All other vertices remain balanced.

Now adjoin one extra arc $f$ from $u$ to $v$. In the resulting directed multigraph $H$, every vertex has equal in-degree and out-degree. Since $G$ is connected and all edges of $G'$ lie in the underlying connected graph of $G$, the graph $H$ is connected.

By Theorem G, $H$ has an Eulerian circuit $(e_1,\ldots,e_M)$ using each arc exactly once. Removing the single occurrence of $f$ from this circuit produces an oriented walk in $G'$ that starts at $v$ and ends at $u$ and uses each copy of each $e_j$ ($1\le j\le m$) exactly once.

Translating back from parallel copies, this yields an oriented walk in $G$ from $\operatorname{fin}(e_0)$ to $\operatorname{init}(e_0)$ in which each edge $e_j$ appears exactly $E_j$ times for $1\le j\le m$, while $e_0$ does not appear.
