[Section 2.3.4.2: Oriented Trees](../)

**Exercise 6.** [**] $$M21$$ True or false: A directed graph that is rooted and contains no cycles and no oriented cycles is an oriented tree.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 3m31s  

---

False.

Let $G$ be a directed graph with vertices ${R, V, U_1, U_2}$ and arcs $V \to U_1$, $V \to U_2$, $U_1 \to R$, $U_2 \to R$. Every vertex has an oriented path to $R$, so $G$ is rooted with root $R$. There is no oriented cycle, since every arc strictly decreases distance to $R$, and there is no (undirected) cycle in the corresponding graph $G_0$.

However, $V$ is the initial vertex of two distinct arcs $V \to U_1$ and $V \to U_2$, so property (a) in the definition of an oriented tree is violated, which requires each vertex $V \ne R$ to be the initial vertex of exactly one arc. Hence $G$ is not an oriented tree.

This completes the proof. ∎
