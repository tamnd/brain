[Section 2.4: Multilinked Structures](../)

**Exercise 8.** [**] [10] Under what circumstances is "`MOVE CORRESPONDING \alpha TO \beta`"
exactly the same as "`MOVE \alpha TO \beta`", according to the definition in
the text?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m44s  

---

`MOVE CORRESPONDING \alpha TO \beta` transfers every subordinate item of $\alpha$ whose name also appears as a subordinate item of $\beta$, pairing items solely by identical names. Therefore it is exactly equivalent to `MOVE \alpha TO \beta` when every elementary item subordinate to $\alpha$ has a unique matching elementary item subordinate to $\beta$, every elementary item subordinate to $\beta$ has a unique matching elementary item subordinate to $\alpha$, and the corresponding items occupy the same hierarchical positions within the two structures.

Under these circumstances the two group items have identical structures and identical subordinate names throughout. A group move from $\alpha$ to $\beta$ then transfers precisely the same elementary data items that `MOVE CORRESPONDING \alpha TO \beta` would transfer, with no item omitted and no additional item included.
