[Section 2.4: Multilinked Structures](../)

**Exercise 1.** [**] [00] Considering COBOL data configurations as tree structures, are the
data items listed by a COBOL programmer in preorder, postorder, or neither of
those orders?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 5m46s  

---

In COBOL data descriptions, a group item is written before any of its subordinate items, and each subordinate entry is listed immediately after the declaration of its parent group with increased indentation. This ordering enforces that a node in the corresponding tree is encountered before any of the nodes in its subtrees.

A traversal that visits each node before recursively visiting its children matches this structure exactly. The listing therefore corresponds to a preorder traversal of the tree.
