**Exercise 6.** [**] [23] The PL/I language accepts data structures much like those in COBOL,
but does not make the restriction of rule (c); instead, we have the rule that a
qualified reference (3) is unambiguous if it shows "complete" qualification,
that is, if `A_{j+1}` is

the parent of `A_j` for `0 <= j < n`, and if `A_n` has no parent. Rule (c) is
now weakened to the simple condition that no two items of a group may have the
same name. The second "`CC`" in (2) would be referred to as "`CC OF AA`"
without ambiguity; the three data items

```text
1 A
  2 A
    3 A
```

would be referred to as "`A`", "`A OF A`", "`A OF A OF A`" with respect to the
PL/I convention just stated. [*Note:* Actually the word "`OF`" is replaced by a
period in PL/I, and the order is reversed; "`CC OF AA`" is really written
"`AA.CC`" in PL/I, but this is not important for the purposes of the present
exercise.] Show how to modify Algorithm B so that it follows the PL/I
convention.

It correctly recognizes that rule (c) is removed and that repeated local names are allowed, so uniqueness must come from structure rather than local naming constraints.

However, the exercise is not asking for a conceptual explanation of PL/I qualification, but for a precise modification of Algorithm B in Knuth’s sense. In Knuth-style exercises, this is essential: the solution must explicitly indicate what lines of the algorithm change or how its procedures are rewritten.

The second gap is that the construction of qualified names is described informally as “follow parent pointers upward,” but no explicit procedure is given for how this interacts with Algorithm B’s traversal order or storage structure. In particular, it does not specify whether the qualification is computed during a depth-first traversal, breadth-first traversal, or during insertion into the symbol table. This is a Justification gap because correctness depends on consistent traversal and construction order.
