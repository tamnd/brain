[Section 2.3: Trees](../)

**Exercise 4.** [**] [01] True or false: In a conventional tree diagram (root at the top), if node `X` has a higher level number than node `Y` then node `X` appears *lower* in the diagram than node `Y`.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 41s  

---

In a conventional tree diagram with the root drawn at the top, each node at level $k+1$ is placed below its parent at level $k$. Thus, the level number increases exactly in the downward direction of the drawing.

If node $X$ has a higher level number than node $Y$, then $X$ lies at a greater distance from the root than $Y$. Since greater distance from the root corresponds to being drawn further downward, $X$ appears lower in the diagram than $Y$. Hence the statement is true. ∎
