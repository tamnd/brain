[Section 1.2.7: Harmonic Numbers](../)

**Exercise 22.** [*M28*] Evaluate $\sum_{k=0}^{n} H_k H_{n-k}$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m58s  

---

The error identified in the review is genuine: the admissible region for $(i,j)$ was shifted from $i+j\le n$ to $i+j\le n+1$. The correct derivation must therefore be restarted from the triple-sum representation with the correct bounds.

## 1. Triple-sum expansion and correct region

Let $H_k=\sum_{i=1}^k \frac{1}{i}$, with $H_0=0$. Then

$$
S=\sum_{k=0}^n H_k H_{n-k}
= \sum_{k=0}^n \left(\sum_{i=1}^k \frac{1}{i}\right)\left(\sum_{j=1}^{n-k} \frac{1}{j}\right).
$$

Thus

$$
S=\sum_{k=0}^n \sum_{i=1}^k \sum_{j=1}^{n-k} \frac{1}{ij}.
$$

For fixed $i,j$, the term $\frac{1}{ij}$ appears exactly for those $k$ satisfying

$$
i \le k \le n-j.
$$

Such $k$ exist iff

$$
i \le n-j \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad i+j \le n.
$$

When $i+j\le n$, the number of admissible $k$ is

$$
(n-j) - i + 1 = n+1-i-j.
$$

Hence

$$
S=\sum_{\substack{i\ge 1,\; j\ge 1\\ i+j\le n}} \frac{n+1-i-j}{ij}.
$$

Equivalently,

$$
S=\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}\sum_{j=1}^{n-i} \frac{n+1-i-j}{ij}.
$$

## 2. Split into two sums

$$
S=(n+1)\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}\sum_{j=1}^{n-i}\frac{1}{ij}
-\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}\sum_{j=1}^{n-i}\left(\frac{1}{i}+\frac{1}{j}\right).
$$

Define

$$
A=\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}\sum_{j=1}^{n-i}\frac{1}{ij}, 
\qquad
B=\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}\sum_{j=1}^{n-i}\left(\frac{1}{i}+\frac{1}{j}\right).
$$

## 3. Evaluation of $A$

$$
A=\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}\frac{1}{i}\sum_{j=1}^{n-i}\frac{1}{j}
=\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}\frac{H_{n-i}}{i}.
$$

Let $k=n-i$. Then $k=0,\dots,n-1$, and

$$
A=\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\frac{H_k}{n-k}.
$$

Using the standard harmonic convolution identity (valid for this triangular form),

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{n-1}\frac{H_k}{n-k}=H_n^2-H_n^{(2)},
$$

and noting the $k=0$ term is zero, we obtain

$$
A=H_n^2-H_n^{(2)}.
$$

## 4. Evaluation of $B$

By symmetry of the region $i+j\le n$,

$$
B=2\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}\sum_{j=1}^{n-i}\frac{1}{i}
=2\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}\frac{n-i}{i}.
$$

Thus

$$
B=2\left(n\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}\frac{1}{i}-\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}1\right)
=2\bigl(nH_{n-1}-(n-1)\bigr).
$$

Using $H_{n-1}=H_n-\frac{1}{n}$,

$$
nH_{n-1}-(n-1)=nH_n-1-(n-1)=nH_n-n.
$$

Hence

$$
B=2(nH_n-n).
$$

## 5. Assemble $S$

$$
S=(n+1)(H_n^2-H_n^{(2)})-2(nH_n-n).
$$

## 6. Final form

$$
\boxed{
\sum_{k=0}^n H_k H_{n-k}
=(n+1)\bigl(H_n^2-H_n^{(2)}\bigr)-2nH_n+2n
}
$$

## 7. Consistency check

For $n=2$:

$$
S=H_1H_1=1,
$$

and the formula gives

$$
3\left(\left(\tfrac{3}{2}\right)^2-\left(1+\tfrac14\right)\right)-4\cdot\tfrac{3}{2}+4
=3(2)-6+4=1.
$$

Thus the corrected expression matches direct evaluation.

The correction consists entirely in restoring the correct constraint $i+j\le n$, which shifts all subsequent harmonic-sum identities to the correct index $n$.
