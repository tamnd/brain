[Section 1.2.7: Harmonic Numbers](../)

**Exercise 11.** [*M21*] Using summation by parts, evaluate

$$
\sum_{1<k \le n}\frac{1}{k(k-1)}H_k.
$$

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m37s  

---

## Solution

Let

$$
S=\sum_{1<k\le n}\frac{1}{k(k-1)}H_k
=\sum_{k=2}^{n}\frac{1}{k(k-1)}H_k.
$$

Set $j=k-1$. Then $k=j+1$ and $j$ runs from $1$ to $n-1$. Hence

$$
S=\sum_{j=1}^{n-1}\frac{1}{j(j+1)}H_{j+1}.
$$

Write

$$
\frac{1}{j(j+1)}=\frac{1}{j}-\frac{1}{j+1}.
$$

Define $a_j=\frac{1}{j}$ and $b_j=H_{j+1}$. Then

$$
S=\sum_{1\le j<n}(a_j-a_{j+1})b_j.
$$

Apply the summation by parts identity from Exercise 1.2.7.10:

$$
\sum_{1 \le k < n}(a_{k+1}-a_k)b_k
= a_nb_n - a_1b_1 - \sum_{1 \le k < n}a_{k+1}(b_{k+1}-b_k).
$$

Multiply both sides by $-1$ to obtain

$$
\sum_{1 \le k < n}(a_k-a_{k+1})b_k
= -a_nb_n + a_1b_1 + \sum_{1 \le k < n}a_{k+1}(b_{k+1}-b_k).
$$

With $a_k=\frac{1}{k}$ and $b_k=H_{k+1}$, compute each term.

First,

$$
a_n b_n=\frac{1}{n}H_{n+1}, \quad a_1 b_1 = H_2.
$$

Next,

$$
b_{k+1}-b_k = H_{k+2}-H_{k+1}=\frac{1}{k+2}.
$$

Thus

$$
\sum_{1 \le k < n} a_{k+1}(b_{k+1}-b_k)
= \sum_{k=1}^{n-1}\frac{1}{k+1}\cdot\frac{1}{k+2}
= \sum_{k=1}^{n-1}\frac{1}{(k+1)(k+2)}.
$$

Using

$$
\frac{1}{(k+1)(k+2)}=\frac{1}{k+1}-\frac{1}{k+2},
$$

the sum telescopes:

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{n-1}\frac{1}{(k+1)(k+2)}
= \frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{n+1}.
$$

Substitution yields

$$
S=-\frac{H_{n+1}}{n}+H_2+\frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{n+1}.
$$

Since $H_2=1+\frac{1}{2}=\frac{3}{2}$,

$$
H_2+\frac{1}{2}=2,
$$

so

$$
S=2-\frac{H_{n+1}}{n}-\frac{1}{n+1}.
$$

Using $H_{n+1}=H_n+\frac{1}{n+1}$,

$$
\frac{H_{n+1}}{n}=\frac{H_n}{n}+\frac{1}{n(n+1)}.
$$

Hence

$$
S=2-\frac{H_n}{n}-\frac{1}{n(n+1)}-\frac{1}{n+1}.
$$

Since

$$
\frac{1}{n(n+1)}+\frac{1}{n+1}=\frac{1}{n},
$$

it follows that

$$
S=2-\frac{H_n}{n}-\frac{1}{n}
=2-\frac{H_n+1}{n}.
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\sum_{1<k \le n}\frac{1}{k(k-1)}H_k = 2-\frac{H_n+1}{n}}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
