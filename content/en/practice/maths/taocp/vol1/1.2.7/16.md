[Section 1.2.7: Harmonic Numbers](../)

**Exercise 16.** [*18*] Express the sum $1 + \frac{1}{3} + \cdots + \frac{1}{2n-1}$ in terms of harmonic numbers.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m14s  

---

## Solution

Let

$$
S=\sum_{k=1}^{n}\frac{1}{2k-1}.
$$

The harmonic number $H_{2n}$ is defined by

$$
H_{2n}=\sum_{k=1}^{2n}\frac{1}{k}.
$$

Split this sum into contributions from odd and even indices:

$$
H_{2n}=\sum_{k=1}^{n}\frac{1}{2k-1}+\sum_{k=1}^{n}\frac{1}{2k}.
$$

The first term equals $S$. The second term simplifies using factorization of the denominator:

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{n}\frac{1}{2k}=\frac{1}{2}\sum_{k=1}^{n}\frac{1}{k}=\frac{1}{2}H_n.
$$

Substituting both expressions into the decomposition of $H_{2n}$ yields

$$
H_{2n}=S+\frac{1}{2}H_n.
$$

Solving for $S$ gives

$$
S=H_{2n}-\frac{1}{2}H_n.
$$

Hence,

$$
\boxed{1+\frac{1}{3}+\cdots+\frac{1}{2n-1}=H_{2n}-\frac{1}{2}H_n.}
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
