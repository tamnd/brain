[Section 1.2.7: Harmonic Numbers](../)

**Exercise 2.** [*13*] Show that the simple argument used in the text to prove that $H_{2^m} \ge 1 + m/2$ can be slightly modified to prove that $H_{2^m} \le 1 + m$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m02s  

---

## Solution

For $m \ge 0$, write

$$
H_{2^m} = \sum_{k=1}^{2^m} \frac{1}{k}.
$$

Partition the index set ${1,2,\dots,2^m}$ into dyadic blocks

$$
B_0 = \{1\}, \qquad B_i = \{2^{i-1}+1,\dots,2^i\} \quad (1 \le i \le m).
$$

Thus

$$
H_{2^m} = 1 + \sum_{i=1}^{m} \sum_{k \in B_i} \frac{1}{k}.
$$

For each $i \ge 1$ and each $k \in B_i$, one has $k \ge 2^{i-1}$, hence

$$
\frac{1}{k} \le \frac{1}{2^{i-1}}.
$$

Since $|B_i| = 2^{i-1}$, it follows that

$$
\sum_{k \in B_i} \frac{1}{k}
\le 2^{i-1} \cdot \frac{1}{2^{i-1}} = 1.
$$

Substituting into the decomposition of $H_{2^m}$ gives

$$
H_{2^m} \le 1 + \sum_{i=1}^{m} 1 = 1 + m.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
