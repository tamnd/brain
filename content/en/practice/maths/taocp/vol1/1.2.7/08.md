[Section 1.2.7: Harmonic Numbers](../)

**Exercise 8.** [*HM18*] Compare Eq. (8) with $\sum_{k=1}^{n}\ln k$; estimate the difference as a function of $n$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m22s  

---

## Solution

Define

$$
A_n=\sum_{k=1}^n H_k,\qquad B_n=\sum_{k=1}^n \ln k=\ln(n!).
$$

From Eq. (8),

$$
A_n=(n+1)H_n-n,
$$

so the difference is

$$
D_n=A_n-B_n=(n+1)H_n-n-\ln(n!).
$$

Use the standard asymptotic expansions

$$
H_n=\ln n+\gamma+\frac{1}{2n}+O\!\left(\frac{1}{n^2}\right),
$$

and

$$
\ln(n!)=n\ln n-n+\frac{1}{2}\ln(2\pi n)+O\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right).
$$

Substitute the expansion for $H_n$ into $(n+1)H_n-n$:

$$
(n+1)H_n-n
=(n+1)\left(\ln n+\gamma+\frac{1}{2n}+O\!\left(\frac{1}{n^2}\right)\right)-n.
$$

Expanding termwise gives

$$
(n+1)\ln n=n\ln n+\ln n,
$$

$$
(n+1)\gamma=\gamma n+\gamma,
$$

$$
(n+1)\frac{1}{2n}=\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{2n}.
$$

Hence

$$
(n+1)H_n-n
=n\ln n+\ln n+\gamma n+\gamma+\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{2n}-n+O\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right).
$$

Subtract $\ln(n!)$:

$$
D_n=
\bigl(n\ln n+\ln n+\gamma n+\gamma+\tfrac{1}{2}+\tfrac{1}{2n}-n\bigr)
-\bigl(n\ln n-n+\tfrac{1}{2}\ln(2\pi n)\bigr)
+O\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right).
$$

Cancellation of $n\ln n$ and $-n$ yields

$$
D_n=\gamma n+\ln n+\gamma+\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{2n}-\frac{1}{2}\ln(2\pi n)+O\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right).
$$

Rewrite the logarithmic terms:

$$
\ln n-\frac{1}{2}\ln(2\pi n)=\frac{1}{2}\ln n-\frac{1}{2}\ln(2\pi).
$$

Thus

$$
D_n=\gamma n+\frac{1}{2}\ln n+\left(\gamma+\frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{2}\ln(2\pi)\right)+O\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right).
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{
\sum_{k=1}^n H_k-\sum_{k=1}^n \ln k
=\gamma n+\frac{1}{2}\ln n+C+O\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)
}
$$

where

$$
C=\gamma+\frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{2}\ln(2\pi).
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
