[Section 1.2.7: Harmonic Numbers](../)

**Exercise 10.** [*M20*] (Summation by parts.) Prove the general formula

$$
\sum_{1 \le k < n}(a_{k+1}-a_k)b_k
= a_nb_n - a_1b_1 - \sum_{1 \le k < n}a_{k+1}(b_{k+1}-b_k).
$$

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m28s  

---

## Solution

Let

$$
S=\sum_{1\le k<n}(a_{k+1}-a_k)b_k.
$$

Expanding the product yields

$$
S=\sum_{1\le k<n}a_{k+1}b_k-\sum_{1\le k<n}a_k b_k.
$$

In the first sum, substitute $j=k+1$, giving

$$
\sum_{1\le k<n}a_{k+1}b_k=\sum_{2\le j\le n}a_j b_{j-1}.
$$

Hence

$$
S=\sum_{2\le j\le n}a_j b_{j-1}-\sum_{1\le k<n}a_k b_k.
$$

Rewrite the second sum with the same index symbol:

$$
S=\sum_{2\le j\le n}a_j b_{j-1}-\sum_{1\le j\le n-1}a_j b_j.
$$

Split the first sum at $j=n$:

$$
S=\left(\sum_{2\le j\le n-1}a_j b_{j-1}+a_n b_{n-1}\right)-\sum_{1\le j\le n-1}a_j b_j.
$$

Separating the last term in the second sum gives

$$
S=\sum_{2\le j\le n-1}a_j b_{j-1}+a_n b_{n-1}-a_1 b_1-\sum_{2\le j\le n-1}a_j b_j.
$$

Combining the sums over $2\le j\le n-1$ yields

$$
S=a_n b_{n-1}-a_1 b_1+\sum_{2\le j\le n-1}a_j(b_{j-1}-b_j).
$$

For the boundary term in the desired identity, rewrite

$$
a_n b_n=a_n b_{n-1}+a_n(b_n-b_{n-1}),
$$

and add and subtract $a_n(b_n-b_{n-1})$ inside the expression for $S$. This gives

$$
S=a_n b_n-a_1 b_1-\sum_{2\le j\le n-1}a_j(b_j-b_{j-1})-a_n(b_n-b_{n-1}).
$$

Extend the summation to include the endpoint $j=n$ since the added term matches the last difference:

$$
\sum_{2\le j\le n}a_j(b_j-b_{j-1})=\sum_{1\le k<n}a_{k+1}(b_{k+1}-b_k).
$$

Substituting this identity yields

$$
S=a_n b_n-a_1 b_1-\sum_{1\le k<n}a_{k+1}(b_{k+1}-b_k).
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
