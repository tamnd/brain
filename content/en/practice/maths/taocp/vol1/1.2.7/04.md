[Section 1.2.7: Harmonic Numbers](../)

**Exercise 4.** [*10*] Decide which of the following statements are true for all positive integers $n$:

1. $H_n < \ln n$.
2. $H_n > \ln n$.
3. $H_n > \ln n + \gamma$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m28s  

---

The integral comparison gives

$$
\int_1^n \frac{dx}{x} < \sum_{k=1}^n \frac{1}{k} < 1 + \int_1^n \frac{dx}{x},
$$

hence

$$
\ln n < H_n < 1 + \ln n.
$$

This shows $H_n > \ln n$ for every positive integer $n$, so statement 2 holds for all $n$, while statement 1 is false since it contradicts the established lower bound.

For the third statement, consider $H_n - \ln n$. The sequence $H_n - \ln n$ is strictly decreasing and converges to Euler’s constant $\gamma$, so for every $n$,

$$
H_n - \ln n > \gamma.
$$

Therefore $H_n > \ln n + \gamma$ holds for all positive integers $n$, and statement 3 is true.
