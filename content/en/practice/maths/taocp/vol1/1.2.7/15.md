[Section 1.2.7: Harmonic Numbers](../)

**Exercise 15.** [*M23*] Express $\sum_{k=1}^{n} H_k^2$ in terms of $n$ and $H_n$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m21s  

---

## Solution

Let

$$
S_n = \sum_{k=1}^{n} H_k^2.
$$

Expand $H_k$ as a partial sum,

$$
H_k = \sum_{i=1}^{k}\frac{1}{i}, \quad H_k^2 = \sum_{i=1}^{k}\sum_{j=1}^{k}\frac{1}{ij}.
$$

Hence

$$
S_n = \sum_{k=1}^{n}\sum_{i=1}^{k}\sum_{j=1}^{k}\frac{1}{ij}.
$$

Interchanging the order of summation over $k$ yields

$$
S_n = \sum_{i=1}^{n}\sum_{j=1}^{n}\sum_{k=\max(i,j)}^{n}\frac{1}{ij}
= \sum_{i=1}^{n}\sum_{j=1}^{n}\frac{n-\max(i,j)+1}{ij}.
$$

Split the double sum into diagonal and off-diagonal parts.

For $i=j$,

$$
\sum_{i=1}^{n}\frac{n-i+1}{i^2}
= (n+1)\sum_{i=1}^{n}\frac{1}{i^2} - \sum_{i=1}^{n}\frac{1}{i}
= (n+1)H_n^{(2)} - H_n.
$$

For $i \ne j$, symmetry gives

$$
\sum_{i\ne j}\frac{n-\max(i,j)+1}{ij}
= 2\sum_{1 \le i < j \le n}\frac{n-j+1}{ij}.
$$

For fixed $j$, the inner sum over $i<j$ satisfies

$$
\sum_{i=1}^{j-1}\frac{1}{ij} = \frac{H_{j-1}}{j}.
$$

Hence the off-diagonal contribution equals

$$
2\sum_{j=1}^{n}(n-j+1)\frac{H_{j-1}}{j}.
$$

Replace $H_{j-1}$ by $H_j - \frac{1}{j}$,

$$
\frac{H_{j-1}}{j} = \frac{H_j}{j} - \frac{1}{j^2}.
$$

Thus the off-diagonal sum becomes

$$
2\sum_{j=1}^{n}(n-j+1)\frac{H_j}{j}
- 2\sum_{j=1}^{n}\frac{n-j+1}{j^2}.
$$

The second term is twice the diagonal second component, since

$$
\sum_{j=1}^{n}\frac{n-j+1}{j^2} = (n+1)H_n^{(2)} - H_n.
$$

Therefore

$$
S_n
= 2\sum_{j=1}^{n}(n-j+1)\frac{H_j}{j}
- \bigl((n+1)H_n^{(2)} - H_n\bigr).
$$

Write

$$
\sum_{j=1}^{n}(n-j+1)\frac{H_j}{j}
= (n+1)\sum_{j=1}^{n}\frac{H_j}{j} - \sum_{j=1}^{n}H_j.
$$

From Exercise 1.2.7.14,

$$
\sum_{j=1}^{n}\frac{H_j}{j} = \frac{1}{2}\left(H_n^2 + H_n^{(2)}\right),
$$

and from equation (8),

$$
\sum_{j=1}^{n}H_j = (n+1)H_n - n.
$$

Substitution gives

$$
\sum_{j=1}^{n}(n-j+1)\frac{H_j}{j}
= \frac{n+1}{2}\left(H_n^2 + H_n^{(2)}\right) - (n+1)H_n + n.
$$

Multiply by $2$,

$$
2\sum_{j=1}^{n}(n-j+1)\frac{H_j}{j}
= (n+1)\left(H_n^2 + H_n^{(2)}\right) - 2(n+1)H_n + 2n.
$$

Hence

$$
S_n
= (n+1)\left(H_n^2 + H_n^{(2)}\right) - 2(n+1)H_n + 2n - (n+1)H_n^{(2)} + H_n.
$$

Cancellation of $(n+1)H_n^{(2)}$ yields

$$
S_n = (n+1)H_n^2 - (2n+1)H_n + 2n.
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\sum_{k=1}^{n} H_k^2 = (n+1)H_n^2 - (2n+1)H_n + 2n.}
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
