**Exercise 5.** [*15*] Give the value of $H_{10000}$ to 15 decimal places, using the tables in Appendix A.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m25s

We restart the computation from the Euler–Maclaurin expansion and carry all arithmetic consistently to the required precision.

## 1. Euler–Maclaurin expansion

For large $n$,

$$
H_n = \ln n + \gamma + \frac{1}{2n} - \frac{1}{12n^2} + \frac{1}{120n^4} + O(n^{-6}).
$$

For $n=10000$,

$$
\frac{1}{120n^4} = \frac{1}{120 \cdot 10^{16}} < 10^{-18},
$$

so it does not affect 15 decimal places. Hence we use

$$
H_{10000} = \ln(10000) + \gamma + \frac{1}{20000} - \frac{1}{120000000} + O(10^{-18}).
$$

## 2. High-precision constants

From standard table values:

$$
\ln(10) = 2.30258509299404568401799145468436420760110148862877\ldots
$$

so

$$
\ln(10000) = 4\ln(10)
= 9.21034037197618273607196581873745683040440595451508\ldots
$$

Euler’s constant:

$$
\gamma = 0.57721566490153286060651209008240243104215933593992\ldots
$$

## 3. First sum

$$
\ln(10000) + \gamma
= 9.78755603687771559667847790881985926144656529045500\ldots
$$

## 4. First correction

$$
\frac{1}{20000} = 0.00005
$$

$$
9.78755603687771559667847790881985926144656529045500
\+ 0.00005
$$

$$
= 9.78760603687771559667847790881985926144656529045500
$$

## 5. Second correction

$$
\frac{1}{120000000} = 0.000000008333333333333333\ldots
$$

Subtracting:

$$
9.78760603687771559667847790881985926144656529045500
- 0.000000008333333333333333
$$

$$
= 9.78760602854438226334514457548652592811323195712167\ldots
$$

Thus,

$$
H_{10000} = 9.787606028544382263\ldots
$$

## 6. Rounding to 15 decimal places

We take

$$
9.787606028544382\underline{263}\ldots
$$

The 16th decimal digit is $2$, so no rounding up occurs.

Therefore,

$$
H_{10000} = 9.787606028544382 \quad \text{(to 15 decimal places)}.
$$

## Final answer

$$
\boxed{9.787606028544382}
$$
