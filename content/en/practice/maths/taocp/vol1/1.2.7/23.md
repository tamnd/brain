[Section 1.2.7: Harmonic Numbers](../)

**Exercise 23.** [*HM20*] By considering the function $\Gamma'(x)/\Gamma(x)$, generalize $H_n$ to noninteger values of $n$. You may use the fact that $\Gamma'(1)=-\gamma$, anticipating the next exercise.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m16s  

---

## Solution

Let the digamma function be defined by

$$
\psi(x) = \frac{\Gamma'(x)}{\Gamma(x)}.
$$

From the functional equation $\Gamma(x+1)=x\Gamma(x)$, differentiation of $\ln \Gamma(x+1)=\ln x+\ln \Gamma(x)$ yields

$$
\psi(x+1)=\psi(x)+\frac{1}{x}, \tag{1}
$$

valid for all $x$ where $\Gamma$ is defined and nonzero.

Define a function $H(x)$ by

$$
H(x)=\psi(x+1)+\gamma.
$$

Using (1),

$$
H(x)-H(x-1)
= \psi(x+1)-\psi(x)+\gamma-\gamma
= \frac{1}{x}. \tag{2}
$$

For integers $n\ge 1$, repeated application of (2) gives

$$
H(n)=H(0)+\sum_{k=1}^{n}\frac{1}{k}.
$$

Using $\psi(1)=-\gamma$, we obtain

$$
H(0)=\psi(1)+\gamma=0,
$$

hence

$$
H(n)=\sum_{k=1}^{n}\frac{1}{k}=H_n.
$$

Therefore $H(x)=\psi(x+1)+\gamma$ extends harmonic numbers from integers to all $x$ where $\psi(x+1)$ is defined. Since $\psi(x)$ has simple poles at nonpositive integers, $H(x)$ is defined for all complex $x \notin {-1,-2,-3,\dots}$.

This completes the construction of the extension of $H_n$ to noninteger values of $n$ via the logarithmic derivative of $\Gamma$. ∎
