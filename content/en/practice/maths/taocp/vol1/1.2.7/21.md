[Section 1.2.7: Harmonic Numbers](../)

**Exercise 21.** [*M24*] Evaluate $\sum_{k=1}^{n} H_k/(n+1-k)$.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 1m10s  

---

## Solution

Let

$$
S=\sum_{k=1}^{n}\frac{H_k}{n+1-k}.
$$

Set $j=n+1-k$, so $k=n+1-j$ and $j$ runs from $1$ to $n$. Then

$$
S=\sum_{j=1}^{n}\frac{H_{n+1-j}}{j}.
$$

For each $j$,

$$
H_n = H_{n+1-j} + \sum_{t=n+2-j}^{n}\frac{1}{t},
$$

hence

$$
H_{n+1-j}=H_n-\sum_{t=n+2-j}^{n}\frac{1}{t}.
$$

Substitution yields

$$
S=\sum_{j=1}^{n}\frac{H_n}{j}-\sum_{j=1}^{n}\frac{1}{j}\sum_{t=n+2-j}^{n}\frac{1}{t}.
$$

The first term equals $H_n^2$. For the double sum, interchange order:

for fixed $t$, the condition $n+2-j\le t\le n$ is equivalent to $1\le j\le n+2-t$. Thus

$$
\sum_{j=1}^{n}\frac{1}{j}\sum_{t=n+2-j}^{n}\frac{1}{t}
=\sum_{t=2}^{n}\frac{1}{t}\sum_{j=1}^{n+2-t}\frac{1}{j}.
$$

The term $t=1$ is excluded since it yields an empty inner sum. Therefore

$$
\sum_{t=2}^{n}\frac{H_{n+2-t}}{t}.
$$

Insert this into $S$:

$$
S=H_n^2-\sum_{t=2}^{n}\frac{H_{n+2-t}}{t}.
$$

Rewrite the sum over $t=2,\dots,n$ by adding and subtracting the missing term $t=1$:

$$
\sum_{t=2}^{n}\frac{H_{n+2-t}}{t}
=\sum_{t=1}^{n}\frac{H_{n+2-t}}{t}-H_{n+1}.
$$

Hence

$$
S=H_n^2-\sum_{t=1}^{n}\frac{H_{n+2-t}}{t}+H_{n+1}.
$$

For each $t$,

$$
H_{n+2-t}=H_{n+1-t}+\frac{1}{n+2-t},
$$

so

$$
\sum_{t=1}^{n}\frac{H_{n+2-t}}{t}
=\sum_{t=1}^{n}\frac{H_{n+1-t}}{t}+\sum_{t=1}^{n}\frac{1}{t(n+2-t)}.
$$

The first sum is $S$, hence

$$
\sum_{t=1}^{n}\frac{H_{n+2-t}}{t}=S+T,
$$

where

$$
T=\sum_{t=1}^{n}\frac{1}{t(n+2-t)}.
$$

Substitution gives

$$
S=H_n^2-(S+T)+H_{n+1},
$$

hence

$$
2S=H_n^2+H_{n+1}-T.
$$

To evaluate $T$, use

$$
\frac{1}{t(n+2-t)}=\frac{1}{n+2}\left(\frac{1}{t}+\frac{1}{n+2-t}\right).
$$

Therefore

$$
T=\frac{1}{n+2}\left(\sum_{t=1}^{n}\frac{1}{t}+\sum_{t=1}^{n}\frac{1}{n+2-t}\right).
$$

The second sum equals $\sum_{j=2}^{n+1}\frac{1}{j}$, so

$$
T=\frac{1}{n+2}\left(H_n+(H_{n+1}-1)\right).
$$

Using $H_{n+1}=H_n+\frac{1}{n+1}$,

$$
T=\frac{1}{n+2}\left(2H_n+\frac{1}{n+1}-1\right).
$$

Substitution into $2S$ yields

$$
S=\frac{1}{2}\left(H_n^2+H_{n+1}-\frac{1}{n+2}\left(2H_n+\frac{1}{n+1}-1\right)\right).
$$

Thus,

$$
\boxed{
\sum_{k=1}^{n}\frac{H_k}{n+1-k}
=\frac{1}{2}\left(H_n^2+H_{n+1}-\frac{2H_n+\frac{1}{n+1}-1}{n+2}\right)
}.
$$
