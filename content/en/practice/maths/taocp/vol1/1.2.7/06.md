[Section 1.2.7: Harmonic Numbers](../)

**Exercise 6.** [*M15*] Prove that the harmonic numbers are directly related to Stirling's numbers, which were introduced in the previous section; in fact,

$$
H_n = \left[{n+1 \atop 2}\right] / n!.
$$

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m20s  

---

## Solution

Let $\left[{n \atop k}\right]$ denote the Stirling numbers of the first kind, defined by the recurrence

$$
\left[{n \atop k}\right] = (n-1)\left[{n-1 \atop k}\right] + \left[{n-1 \atop k-1}\right],
$$

with initial conditions $\left[{1 \atop 1}\right]=1$ and $\left[{n \atop 0}\right]=0$ for $n>0$.

We prove the identity

$$
H_n = \frac{\left[{n+1 \atop 2}\right]}{n!}
$$

by first determining a closed form for $\left[{n \atop 2}\right]$.

Consider the claim

$$
\left[{n \atop 2}\right] = (n-1)! H_{n-1}, \quad n \ge 2.
$$

For $n=2$, the left-hand side is $\left[{2 \atop 2}\right]=1$, while the right-hand side is $(1)! H_1 = 1 \cdot 1 = 1$, so the identity holds for $n=2$.

Assume the identity holds for some $n \ge 2$. Using the Stirling recurrence,

$$
\left[{n+1 \atop 2}\right] = n \left[{n \atop 2}\right] + \left[{n \atop 1}\right].
$$

The known value $\left[{n \atop 1}\right] = (n-1)!$ gives

$$
\left[{n+1 \atop 2}\right]
= n (n-1)! H_{n-1} + (n-1)!.
$$

Factoring $(n-1)!$ yields

$$
\left[{n+1 \atop 2}\right]
= (n-1)! \left(n H_{n-1} + 1\right)
= n! \left(H_{n-1} + \frac{1}{n}\right).
$$

Using $H_n = H_{n-1} + \frac{1}{n}$,

$$
\left[{n+1 \atop 2}\right] = n! H_n.
$$

Thus,

$$
H_n = \frac{\left[{n+1 \atop 2}\right]}{n!}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
