[Section 2.2.6: Arrays and Orthogonal Lists](../)

**Exercise 2.** &#9654; [**] [21] Formulas (5) and (6) have been derived from the assumption that $0 \le I_r \le d_r$ for $1 \le r \le k$. Give a general formula that applies to the case $l_r \le I_r \le u_r$, where $l_r$ and $u_r$ are any lower and upper bounds on the dimensionality.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 49s  

---

Define new indices $I'_r = I_r - l_r$ for $1 \le r \le k$. Then $0 \le I'_r \le u_r - l_r$, and the array can be regarded as a rectangular array in the variables $I'_1,\ldots,I'_k$ to which formulas (5) and (6) apply.

Let $d'_r = u_r - l_r$. By (6),

$$
\operatorname{LOC}(A[I'_1,\ldots,I'_k])
= \operatorname{LOC}(A[0,\ldots,0]) + \sum_{1 \le r \le k} a_r I'_r,
$$

where

$$
a_r = c \prod_{r < s \le k} (d'_s + 1) = c \prod_{r < s \le k} (u_s - l_s + 1).
$$

Substituting $I'_r = I_r - l_r$ yields

$$
\operatorname{LOC}(A[I_1,\ldots,I_k])
= \operatorname{LOC}(A[l_1,\ldots,l_k]) + \sum_{1 \le r \le k} a_r (I_r - l_r),
$$

with

$$
a_r = c \prod_{r < s \le k} (u_s - l_s + 1).
$$

This expresses the address as a linear function of the shifted indices with origin at $(l_1,\ldots,l_k)$ rather than $(0,\ldots,0)$. ∎
