**Exercise 2.** [*10*] The instructions `OUT 1000(6); JBUS *(6)` may be used to output a tape block in an unbuffered fashion, just as the instructions (1) did this for input. Give a method analogous to (2) and (3) that buffers this output, by using MOVE instructions and an auxiliary buffer in locations 2000-2099.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 10m48s  

---

The unbuffered method is

$$
\texttt{OUT 1000(6); JBUS *(6).}
$$

To introduce buffering, two areas 1000–1099 and 2000–2099 are used alternately in the same manner as the input buffering of sequence (3). Assume the first block to be output is already placed in locations 2000–2099. The program begins by initiating its transmission.

```
OUT 2000(6)    Begin output of first block.

JBUS *(6)      Wait until unit 6 becomes free.
MOVE 1000(50)  (1000–1049) → (2000–2049)
MOVE 1050(50)  (1050–1099) → (2050–2099)
OUT 1000(6)    Begin output of next block.
```

The same cycle then repeats with the roles of the two buffers interchanged, so that while one block is being transmitted, the next block is being prepared in the other buffer. This removes the idle waiting time on the I/O unit except for the unavoidable time required by the `MOVE` instructions.
