**Exercise 8.** [*11*] The text describes a hypothetical input scenario that leads from Fig. 23 through parts (a), (b), and (c) of Fig. 24. Interpret the same scenario under the assumption that output to the line printer is being done, instead of input from cards. (For example, what things are happening at the time shown in Fig. 23?)

## Solution

Section 1.4.4 models a circular array of $N$ buffers in which two activities advance independently around the cycle: one activity performs I/O with a peripheral device and the other performs computation with buffers that have already been transferred. Figure 23 depicts a single instant of this cyclic system; each buffer lies in one of the states determined by its position relative to the two advancing indices.

For line-printer output, the roles of producer and consumer are reversed while the circular structure and synchronization mechanism remain unchanged. The program becomes the producer, writing output words into buffers, and the line printer becomes the consumer, removing completed buffers in cyclic order.

Let the buffer cycle be indexed in the same order as in the text. At the instant corresponding to Fig. 23, one buffer is being transmitted to the line printer; this buffer is locked for output and cannot be modified while the printer advances through its words. The printer index in Fig. 23 is therefore positioned at this buffer exactly as the input device index was positioned at a reading buffer in the original interpretation.

A second buffer is being filled by the program. The program index is positioned at this buffer, and successive output words are stored into it until it becomes complete. This buffer corresponds to the “currently active” buffer on the producing side, dual to the input buffer being filled by the tape unit in the original scenario.

All remaining buffers lie between these two positions around the cycle. Each such buffer contains data that has already been completely generated by the program and is not yet consumed by the printer. These buffers are identical in status within the model: they are complete and waiting for the printer pointer to reach them, but they are not distinguished further into subcategories. In particular, no additional structural class beyond “being printed”, “being filled”, and “not currently active” is required to describe the configuration.

As the two indices advance, the printer continuously removes completed buffers in cyclic order, while the program continuously refills buffers behind it. The invariant of the system is that the printer never reaches an unfilled buffer and the program never overwrites a buffer that has not yet been passed by the printer.

Thus, at the instant shown in Fig. 23 under line-printer interpretation, the system consists of one buffer currently being printed, one buffer currently being filled by the program, and the remaining buffers already filled and awaiting consumption, arranged cyclically between these two active positions. This is exactly the dual of the input situation in the text, obtained by reversing the direction of data flow while preserving the same circular scheduling structure. ∎
