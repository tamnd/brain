**Exercise 1.** [*05*] (a) Would sequence (3) still be correct if the MOVE instructions were placed before the JBUS instruction instead of after it? (b) What if the MOVE instructions were placed after the IN command?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 6m22s  

---

(a) No. If the two `MOVE` instructions were executed before `JBUS *(5)`, they would copy locations $2000$-$2099$ into locations $1000$-$1099$ while the input operation initiated by the preceding execution of `IN 2000(5)` might still be in progress. By the definition of `IN`, the contents of the buffer cannot be assumed valid until the unit is no longer busy. The `MOVE` operations could therefore transfer incomplete or partially updated data. Hence the sequence would not be correct.

(b) No. After `JBUS *(5)` has established that the current input into locations $2000$-$2099$ is complete, the instruction `IN 2000(5)` immediately begins reading the next block into those same locations. If the `MOVE` instructions were placed after this `IN`, they would copy from locations $2000$-$2099$ while the new input operation was modifying them. The previous block would be destroyed before it had been completely transferred to locations $1000$-$1099$. Hence the sequence would not be correct.
