**Exercise 10.** [*21*] Repeat exercise 9, except with *four* buffers.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 6m02s

## Solution

There are twelve buffer assignments in the computation sequence. Denote the corresponding output blocks by

$$
B_1,B_2,\ldots,B_{12}.
$$

Each block requires $7500u$ for output.

The computation events occur at the following relative times:

$$
\begin{array}{c|c}
\text{Block} & \text{Assignment and release times} \\ \hline
B_1 & A:0,\ R:1000\\
B_2 & A:2000,\ R:3000\\
B_3 & A:4000,\ R:5000\\
B_4 & A:6000,\ R:7000\\
B_5 & A:8000,\ R:15000\\
B_6 & A:20000,\ R:27000\\
B_7 & A:32000,\ R:39000\\
B_8 & A:44000,\ R:51000\\
B_9 & A:56000,\ R:57000\\
B_{10} & A:58000,\ R:59000\\
B_{11} & A:60000,\ R:61000\\
B_{12} & A:62000,\ R:64000.
\end{array}
$$

With four buffers available, the first four assignments can be made immediately. At time $8000$, when $B_5$ is to be assigned, buffer 1 has already been freed because output of $B_1$ ended at time $8500$, and the assignment request can wait only until that instant. Proceeding in this manner gives the following schedule.

$$
\begin{array}{r|l}
\text{Time} & \text{Action} \\ \hline
0 & \text{ASSIGN(BUF1)}\\
1000 & \text{RELEASE,\ OUT BUF1}\\
2000 & \text{ASSIGN(BUF2)}\\
3000 & \text{RELEASE}\\
4000 & \text{ASSIGN(BUF3)}\\
5000 & \text{RELEASE}\\
6000 & \text{ASSIGN(BUF4)}\\
7000 & \text{RELEASE}\\
8000 & \text{ASSIGN (wait)}\\
8500 & \text{BUF1 assigned,\ OUT BUF2}\\
15000 & \text{RELEASE}\\
16000 & \text{OUT BUF3}\\
20000 & \text{ASSIGN(BUF2)}\\
23500 & \text{OUT BUF4}\\
27000 & \text{RELEASE}\\
31000 & \text{OUT BUF1}\\
32000 & \text{ASSIGN(BUF3)}\\
38500 & \text{OUT BUF2}\\
39000 & \text{RELEASE}\\
44000 & \text{ASSIGN(BUF4)}\\
46000 & \text{OUT BUF3}\\
51000 & \text{RELEASE}\\
53500 & \text{OUT BUF4}\\
56000 & \text{ASSIGN(BUF1)}\\
57000 & \text{RELEASE}\\
58000 & \text{ASSIGN(BUF2)}\\
59000 & \text{RELEASE}\\
60000 & \text{ASSIGN(BUF3)}\\
61000 & \text{RELEASE}\\
62000 & \text{ASSIGN(BUF4)}\\
64000 & \text{RELEASE}\\
68500 & \text{OUT BUF1}\\
76000 & \text{OUT BUF2}\\
83500 & \text{OUT BUF3}\\
91000 & \text{OUT BUF4}\\
98500 & \text{Output stops}
\end{array}
$$

The only delay suffered by the computer occurs from $8000$ to $8500$, while waiting for the completion of output of $B_1$. Hence the computer idle time is

$$
8500-8000=500u.
$$

The computation itself ends after block $B_{12}$ is released and the final $1000u$ of computation is performed:

$$
64000+1000=65000.
$$

Therefore the computer is active during

$$
65000+500=65500u.
$$

The output device is idle only at the beginning, from time $0$ until the first release at time $1000$. After that instant a continuous queue of completed blocks is always available. Hence the output-unit idle time is

$$
1000u.
$$

The last output operation begins at time $91000$ and requires $7500u$, so the total elapsed time is

$$
91000+7500=98500u.
$$

Thus

$$
\boxed{\text{Total time }=98500u,}
$$

$$
\boxed{\text{Computer idle time }=500u,}
$$

$$
\boxed{\text{Output-unit idle time }=1000u.}
$$

The four-buffer system therefore reduces the computer waiting time from $20500u$ in Exercise 9 to only $500u$, while the total running time becomes limited almost entirely by the output device.
