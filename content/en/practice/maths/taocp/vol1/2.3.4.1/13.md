[Section 2.3.4.1: Free Trees](../)

**Exercise 13.** [**] $$M24$$ Consider a graph with $n$ vertices and $m$ edges, in the notation of exercise 6. Show that it is possible to write any permutation of the integers $\{1,2,\ldots,n\}$ as a product of transpositions $(a_{k_1}\ b_{k_1})(a_{k_2}\ b_{k_2})\cdots(a_{k_t}\ b_{k_t})$ if and only if the graph is connected. (Hence there are sets of $n-1$ transpositions that generate all permutations on $n$ elements, but no set of $n-2$ will do so.)

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m51s  

---

Suppose first that the graph is connected. Let $(a,b)$ be the transposition corresponding to an edge joining the vertices $a$ and $b$. Since every permutation is a product of transpositions, it is enough to show that every transposition $(i,j)$ can be expressed as a product of edge-transpositions. By Theorem A(c), there is a simple path $(i=v_0,v_1,\ldots,v_r=j)$ from $i$ to $j$. Then

$$
(i,j)
=(v_0,v_1)(v_1,v_2)\cdots(v_{r-1},v_r)
(v_{r-2},v_{r-1})\cdots(v_0,v_1)
(v_1,v_2)\cdots(v_{r-1},v_r),
$$

and a straightforward computation shows that this product interchanges $v_0$ and $v_r$ while leaving every other vertex fixed. Hence every transposition, and therefore every permutation of ${1,\ldots,n}$, is generated by the transpositions corresponding to the edges of the graph.

Conversely, suppose the graph is not connected. Then its vertices split into at least two connected components. Every edge-transposition exchanges two vertices belonging to the same component, so any product of such transpositions leaves each connected component invariant as a set. Therefore no product of edge-transpositions can realize a transposition $(i,j)$ with $i$ and $j$ in different components. Hence not every permutation can be obtained. Thus the edge-transpositions generate all permutations if and only if the graph is connected. By Theorem A(e), a connected graph on $n$ vertices can have $n-1$ edges, namely a free tree, and such a set of $n-1$ transpositions generates all permutations. A graph with only $n-2$ edges cannot be connected by Theorem A(e), so no set of $n-2$ transpositions can generate all permutations. This completes the proof.
