**Exercise 4.** [**] $$M20$$ Let $G'$ be a finite free tree in which arrows have been drawn on its edges $e_1,\ldots,e_{n-1}$; let $E_1,\ldots,E_{n-1}$ be numbers satisfying Kirchhoff's law (1) in $G'$. Show that $E_1=\cdots=E_{n-1}=0$.

Let $G'$ be a finite free tree with $n$ vertices and $n-1$ edges, and assume Kirchhoff’s law (1) holds at every vertex with all vertex values equal to $0$, so that at each vertex the sum of $E$’s entering equals the sum of $E$’s leaving.

If $n=1$, there are no edges and the conclusion holds. Assume $n>1$. By the auxiliary result in Section 2.3.4.1, $G'$ contains a vertex $V_n$ adjacent to exactly one vertex $V_{n-1}$. Let $e_k$ be the unique edge incident to $V_n$. At $V_n$, either $e_k$ contributes to the incoming sum or to the outgoing sum, while all other incident contributions are absent. Kirchhoff’s law at $V_n$ reduces in either case to $E_k=0$.

Delete $V_n$ and the edge $e_k$. The remaining graph is again a free tree, since deleting a leaf from a tree preserves connectedness and introduces no cycles. The remaining edge variables still satisfy Kirchhoff’s law at all remaining vertices, since no other equation involves $E_k$.

Applying the same argument repeatedly removes one edge at each step and forces its corresponding value to be $0$. After $n-1$ deletions all edges have been removed, hence every $E_1,\ldots,E_{n-1}$ equals $0$.

This completes the proof. ∎
