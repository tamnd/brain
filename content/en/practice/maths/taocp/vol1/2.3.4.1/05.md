**Exercise 5.** [**] $$20$$ Using Eqs. (6), express the quantities $A,B,\ldots,S$ that appear inside the boxes of Fig. 31 in terms of the independent variables $E_2,E_5,\ldots,E_{25}$.

Let $G'$ be the chosen free subtree and let the independent variables be the values assigned to the non-tree edges $E_2,E_5,\ldots,E_{25}$, as in Eq. (6). By construction, every branch variable $E_j$ is a linear function of these independent variables with coefficients in ${ -1,0,1 }$, obtained by summing contributions of the fundamental cycles that contain $e_j$ with sign determined by the orientation of $e_j$ in the cycle representation.

For each box quantity $X \in {A,B,\ldots,S}$, Kirchhoff’s law at that box gives

$$
X = \sum (\text{incoming } E\text{'s}) = \sum (\text{outgoing } E\text{'s}),
$$

so $X$ is a linear expression in the branch variables $E_1,\ldots,E_{27}$. Substituting the expressions for each $E_j$ obtained from Eq. (6) eliminates all dependent variables, leaving $X$ expressed solely in terms of $E_2,E_5,\ldots,E_{25}$.

Thus each of $A,B,\ldots,S$ is uniquely determined as a linear combination of the independent variables $E_2,E_5,\ldots,E_{25}$ with integer coefficients induced by the incidence of each branch in the corresponding fundamental cycles. This substitution yields the required expressions for all box quantities, and no further dependencies remain once the free-subtree variables are eliminated. ∎
