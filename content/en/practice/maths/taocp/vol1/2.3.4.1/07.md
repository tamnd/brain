The construction in this exercise depends on the exact adjacency structure of the flow chart in Fig. 7, in particular which vertices each of $e_1,e_2,\ldots,e_9$ connect to and how $e_5,e_6,e_7,e_8$ are attached relative to the chosen free subtree.

Without the incidence information from the diagram, the fundamental cycles cannot be determined uniquely, since each cycle is defined by adding one non-tree edge and tracing the unique path in the selected subtree $\{e_1,e_2,e_3,e_4,e_9\}$.

Provide either the figure or a textual description of the graph (each edge $e_j$ as an ordered pair of vertices, or an adjacency list), and the fundamental cycles together with the expressions for $E_1,E_2,E_3,E_4,E_9$ in terms of $E_5,E_6,E_7,E_8$ can then be derived directly from Kirchhoff’s construction.
