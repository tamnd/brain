**Exercise 1.** [**] $$14$$ List all cycles from $B$ to $B$ that are present in the graph of Fig. 29.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m06s  

---

In Fig. 29 the vertices are $A,B,C,D,E$, and the edges are $AB$, $AC$, $BD$, $CD$, $BE$, and $DE$. A cycle from $B$ to $B$ is obtained by traversing a simple cycle and returning to the starting point.

There are exactly two such cycles. The first is the $4$-cycle

$$
(B,A,C,D,B),
$$

and the second is the $3$-cycle

$$
(B,D,E,B).
$$

No others exist, since every cycle must lie entirely within the subgraph on ${A,B,C,D}$ or within the subgraph on ${B,D,E}$, and these subgraphs contain only the cycles listed above. Therefore the cycles from $B$ to $B$ are

$$
\boxed{(B,A,C,D,B)\ \text{and}\ (B,D,E,B).}
$$
