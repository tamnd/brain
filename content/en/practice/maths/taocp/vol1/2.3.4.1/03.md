[Section 2.3.4.1: Free Trees](../)

**Exercise 3.** [**] $$15$$ What walk from Start to Stop is equivalent (in the sense of Theorem K) to one traversal of the fundamental path plus one traversal of cycle $C_2$ in Fig. 32?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m49s  

---

The fundamental path from Start to Stop is the path in the free subtree determined by the cycle $C_0$ with $e_0$ omitted:

$$
e_1+e_3+e_4+e_6+e_7+e_9+e_{10}+e_{11}+e_{12}+e_{14}.
$$

Adding one traversal of $C_2=e_2-e_4-e_3$ means that the segment $e_3+e_4$ of the fundamental path is replaced by the edge $e_2$, since traversing $e_2$ and then traversing $e_4$ and $e_3$ in the reverse direction returns to the same vertex of the path.

Hence the equivalent walk from Start to Stop is

$$
e_1+e_2+e_6+e_7+e_9+e_{10}+e_{11}+e_{12}+e_{14}.
$$

Therefore the walk is obtained by following $e_1$, then taking the branch $e_2$ instead of the detour $e_3,e_4$, and then continuing along $e_6,e_7,e_9,e_{10},e_{11},e_{12},e_{14}$ to Stop. The answer is

$$
\boxed{e_1+e_2+e_6+e_7+e_9+e_{10}+e_{11}+e_{12}+e_{14}}.
$$
