**Exercise 7.** [*HM30*] In the evaluation of $I_2$, we had to consider

$$
\int_0^y e^{-u}\left(1+\frac{u}{x}\right)^x du.
$$

Give an asymptotic representation of

$$
\int_0^{yx^{1/4}} e^{-u}\left(1+\frac{u}{x}\right)^x du
$$

to terms of order $O(x^{-2})$, when $y$ is fixed and $x$ is large.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m47s  

---

We restart from the integrand and determine all contributions that survive after integration down to order $O(x^{-2})$.

## 1. Exponential expansion with correct truncation

Write

$$
\left(1+\frac{u}{x}\right)^x=\exp\!\left(x\ln\left(1+\frac{u}{x}\right)\right),
\qquad
e^{-u}\left(1+\frac{u}{x}\right)^x=\exp\!\left(-u+x\ln\left(1+\frac{u}{x}\right)\right).
$$

Use the logarithmic expansion

$$
x\ln\left(1+\frac{u}{x}\right)
= u - \frac{u^2}{2x} + \frac{u^3}{3x^2} - \frac{u^4}{4x^3} + O\!\left(\frac{u^5}{x^4}\right).
$$

Hence the exponent becomes

$$
- u + x\ln\left(1+\frac{u}{x}\right)
=
-\frac{u^2}{2x}
+ \frac{u^3}{3x^2}
- \frac{u^4}{4x^3}
+ O\!\left(\frac{u^5}{x^4}\right).
$$

Since $0\le u \le yx^{1/4}$, we have

$$
\frac{u^5}{x^4} = O(x^{-9/4}),
$$

and after integration this contributes $O(x^{-2.25})$, hence beyond the required precision. It may be discarded.

Define

$$
a=-\frac{u^2}{2x},\qquad
b=\frac{u^3}{3x^2},\qquad
c=-\frac{u^4}{4x^3}.
$$

We expand

$$
\exp(a+b+c)=1+(a+b+c)+\frac{a^2}{2}+ab+\frac{a^3}{6}+\frac{a^4}{24}+\frac{a^2b}{2}+O(x^{-2-\varepsilon}),
$$

where terms omitted integrate to $O(x^{-2})$ or smaller.

## 2. Size bookkeeping

For $u\sim x^{1/4}$,

$$
a=O(x^{-1/2}),\quad b=O(x^{-5/4}),\quad c=O(x^{-7/4}).
$$

After integration, a term $u^k x^{-m}$ contributes order

$$
x^{(k+1)/4-m}.
$$

We retain all contributions with exponent $\ge -2$.

## 3. Term-by-term integration

Let $A=yx^{1/4}$.

### Leading term

$$
\int_0^A 1\,du = A = yx^{1/4}.
$$

### Linear terms

$$
\int_0^A a\,du
= -\frac{1}{2x}\int_0^A u^2\,du
= -\frac{A^3}{6x}
= -\frac{y^3}{6}x^{-1/4}.
$$

$$
\int_0^A b\,du
= \frac{1}{3x^2}\int_0^A u^3\,du
= \frac{A^4}{12x^2}
= \frac{y^4}{12}x^{-1}.
$$

$$
\int_0^A c\,du
= -\frac{1}{4x^3}\int_0^A u^4\,du
= -\frac{A^5}{20x^3}
= -\frac{y^5}{20}x^{-7/4}.
$$

### Quadratic and higher contributions

$$
\int_0^A \frac{a^2}{2}\,du
= \frac{1}{8x^2}\int_0^A u^4\,du
= \frac{A^5}{40x^2}
= \frac{y^5}{40}x^{-3/4}.
$$

$$
\int_0^A ab\,du
= -\frac{1}{6x^3}\int_0^A u^5\,du
= -\frac{A^6}{36x^3}
= -\frac{y^6}{36}x^{-3/2}.
$$

$$
\int_0^A \frac{a^3}{6}\,du
= -\frac{1}{48x^3}\int_0^A u^6\,du
= -\frac{A^7}{336x^3}
= -\frac{y^7}{336}x^{-5/4}.
$$

$$
\int_0^A \frac{a^2b}{2}\,du
= \frac{1}{24x^4}\int_0^A u^7\,du
= \frac{A^8}{192x^4}
= \frac{y^8}{192}x^{-2}.
$$

$$
\int_0^A \frac{a^4}{24}\,du
= \frac{1}{384x^4}\int_0^A u^8\,du
= \frac{A^9}{3456x^4}
= \frac{y^9}{3456}x^{-7/4}.
$$

## 4. Collecting terms

Group by powers of $x$:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\int_0^{yx^{1/4}} e^{-u}\left(1+\frac{u}{x}\right)^x du
=\;& yx^{1/4}
-\frac{y^3}{6}x^{-1/4}
+\frac{y^5}{40}x^{-3/4}
+\frac{y^4}{12}x^{-1} \\[4pt]
&-\frac{y^7}{336}x^{-5/4}
-\frac{y^6}{36}x^{-3/2}
+\left(-\frac{y^5}{20}+\frac{y^9}{3456}\right)x^{-7/4} \\[4pt]
&+\frac{y^8}{192}x^{-2}
+O(x^{-9/4}).
\end{aligned}
$$

## 5. Final asymptotic form

$$
\boxed{
\int_0^{yx^{1/4}} e^{-u}\left(1+\frac{u}{x}\right)^x du
=
yx^{1/4}
-\frac{y^3}{6}x^{-1/4}
+\frac{y^5}{40}x^{-3/4}
+\frac{y^4}{12}x^{-1}
-\frac{y^7}{336}x^{-5/4}
-\frac{y^6}{36}x^{-3/2}
+\left(-\frac{y^5}{20}+\frac{y^9}{3456}\right)x^{-7/4}
+\frac{y^8}{192}x^{-2}
+O(x^{-9/4})
}
$$

All omitted terms from the exponential expansion contribute strictly below order $x^{-2}$ after integration, so the expansion is complete to the requested precision.
