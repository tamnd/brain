**Exercise 3.** [*M20*] Derive Eq. (8) from Eq. (7).

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m26s

## Solution

Start from Eq. (7):

$$
\gamma(a,x)=\sum_{k\ge 0}\frac{(-1)^k x^{k+a}}{k!(k+a)}.
$$

Multiply both sides by $e^x=\sum_{m\ge 0}\frac{x^m}{m!}$:

$$
e^x\gamma(a,x)
= \left(\sum_{m\ge 0}\frac{x^m}{m!}\right)\left(\sum_{k\ge 0}\frac{(-1)^k x^{k+a}}{k!(k+a)}\right).
$$

Form the Cauchy product. The coefficient of $x^{n+a}$ arises from pairs $(m,k)$ with $m+k=n$:

$$
e^x\gamma(a,x)
= \sum_{n\ge 0} x^{n+a} \sum_{k=0}^n \frac{(-1)^k}{k!(n-k)!(k+a)}.
$$

Define

$$
A_n=\sum_{k=0}^n \frac{(-1)^k}{k!(n-k)!(k+a)}.
$$

The goal is to show

$$
A_n=\frac{1}{a(a+1)\cdots(a+n)}.
$$

For this, use the integral identity valid for $a>0$:

$$
\frac{1}{a(a+1)\cdots(a+n)}
= \frac{1}{n!}\int_0^1 t^{a-1}(1-t)^n\,dt.
$$

Expand $(1-t)^n$ by the binomial theorem:

$$
(1-t)^n=\sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k}(-t)^k.
$$

Substitute into the integral:

$$
\frac{1}{n!}\int_0^1 t^{a-1}(1-t)^n\,dt
= \frac{1}{n!}\sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k}(-1)^k \int_0^1 t^{a+k-1}\,dt.
$$

Evaluate the integral:

$$
\int_0^1 t^{a+k-1}\,dt=\frac{1}{a+k}.
$$

Hence

$$
\frac{1}{a(a+1)\cdots(a+n)}
= \sum_{k=0}^n \frac{(-1)^k}{k!(n-k)!(a+k)}.
$$

This matches $A_n$, so

$$
A_n=\frac{1}{a(a+1)\cdots(a+n)}.
$$

Substituting back gives

$$
e^x\gamma(a,x)
= \sum_{n\ge 0}\frac{x^{n+a}}{a(a+1)\cdots(a+n)}.
$$

Reindexing $n$ as $k$ yields Eq. (8):

$$
e^x\gamma(a,x)=\sum_{k\ge 0}\frac{x^{k+a}}{a(a+1)\cdots(a+k)}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
