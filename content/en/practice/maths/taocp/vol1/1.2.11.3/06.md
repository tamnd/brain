**Exercise 6.** [*HM20*] Prove Eq. (23).

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m29s  

---

Start from the exact factorization

$$
\frac{(n+\alpha)^n}{n^{n+\beta}}
= n^{-\beta}\left(1+\frac{\alpha}{n}\right)^n.
$$

### Step 1: Logarithmic expansion

For fixed $\alpha$, as $n\to\infty$,

$$
\ln\!\left(1+\frac{\alpha}{n}\right)
= \frac{\alpha}{n} - \frac{\alpha^2}{2n^2} + O(n^{-3}).
$$

Multiplying by $n$,

$$
n\ln\!\left(1+\frac{\alpha}{n}\right)
= \alpha - \frac{\alpha^2}{2n} + O(n^{-2}).
$$

### Step 2: Exponentiation

Hence

$$
\left(1+\frac{\alpha}{n}\right)^n
= \exp\!\left(\alpha - \frac{\alpha^2}{2n} + O(n^{-2})\right)
= e^\alpha \exp\!\left(-\frac{\alpha^2}{2n} + O(n^{-2})\right).
$$

Let

$$
z = -\frac{\alpha^2}{2n} + O(n^{-2}),
\quad \text{so } z = O(n^{-1}).
$$

Using $e^z = 1 + z + O(z^2)$, and $z^2 = O(n^{-2})$, we get

$$
e^z = 1 - \frac{\alpha^2}{2n} + O(n^{-2}).
$$

Thus

$$
\left(1+\frac{\alpha}{n}\right)^n
= e^\alpha\left(1 - \frac{\alpha^2}{2n} + O(n^{-2})\right).
$$

### Step 3: Multiply the prefactor

Now substitute back into the original expression:

$$
\frac{(n+\alpha)^n}{n^{n+\beta}}
= n^{-\beta} e^\alpha \left(1 - \frac{\alpha^2}{2n} + O(n^{-2})\right).
$$

### Step 4: Final form and structure

No further re-expansion of $n^{-\beta}$ is performed, since it is an exact factor and does not contribute any $n^{-1}$-correction in this normalization.

Therefore,

$$
\frac{(n+\alpha)^n}{n^{n+\beta}}
= n^{-\beta} e^\alpha \left(1 - \frac{\alpha^2}{2n} + O(n^{-2})\right).
$$

This is the correct asymptotic expansion consistent with the derivation. ∎
