**Exercise 1.** [*HM20*] Prove Eq. (5) by induction on $n$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m41s  

---

Let

$$
S_n(x)=\sum_{k=0}^{n}\frac{x^k}{k!}, \qquad
R_n(x)=\frac{1}{n!}\int_{0}^{x}(x-t)^n e^t\,dt.
$$

We prove that for every $n\ge 0$,

$$
e^x = S_n(x)+R_n(x).
\tag{5}
$$

## Step 1. Base case $n=0$

$$
S_0(x)=1,\qquad
R_0(x)=\int_0^x e^t\,dt = e^x-1.
$$

Hence

$$
S_0(x)+R_0(x)=1+(e^x-1)=e^x.
$$

## Step 2. Key identity relating successive remainders

We compute $R_n(x)$ by integration by parts. Let

$$
u=(x-t)^n,\quad dv=e^t dt.
$$

Then

$$
du=-n(x-t)^{n-1}dt,\quad v=e^t.
$$

Thus

$$
R_n(x)
=\frac{1}{n!}\left[(x-t)^n e^t\Big|_{0}^{x}
+ \int_0^x n(x-t)^{n-1} e^t\,dt\right].
$$

### Boundary term justification

At $t=x$, $(x-x)^n e^x=0$.

At $t=0$, the term is $-x^n$. Hence

$$
(x-t)^n e^t\Big|_{0}^{x} = 0 - x^n = -x^n.
$$

Therefore

$$
R_n(x)
=\frac{1}{n!}\left(-x^n + n\int_0^x (x-t)^{n-1}e^t\,dt\right).
$$

Rewrite the second term:

$$
\frac{n}{n!}=\frac{1}{(n-1)!}.
$$

So for $n\ge 1$,

$$
R_n(x)= -\frac{x^n}{n!} + \frac{1}{(n-1)!}\int_0^x (x-t)^{n-1}e^t\,dt.
$$

That is,

$$
R_n(x)=R_{n-1}(x)-\frac{x^n}{n!}.
\tag{1}
$$

## Step 3. Recurrence for $S_n(x)$

From the definition,

$$
S_n(x)=S_{n-1}(x)+\frac{x^n}{n!}.
\tag{2}
$$

## Step 4. Combine the recurrences

Add (1) and (2):

$$
S_n(x)+R_n(x)
=
\left(S_{n-1}(x)+\frac{x^n}{n!}\right)
+
\left(R_{n-1}(x)-\frac{x^n}{n!}\right).
$$

The $x^n/n!$ terms cancel, giving

$$
S_n(x)+R_n(x)=S_{n-1}(x)+R_{n-1}(x).
\tag{3}
$$

Thus $S_n(x)+R_n(x)$ is independent of $n$.

## Step 5. Conclude by the base case

From (3), for all $n$,

$$
S_n(x)+R_n(x)=S_0(x)+R_0(x)=e^x.
$$

## Conclusion

$$
e^x = S_n(x) + \frac{1}{n!}\int_{0}^{x}(x-t)^n e^t\,dt
$$

holds for all $n\ge 0$, which proves Eq. (5). ∎
