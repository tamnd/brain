**Exercise 2.** [*10*] The text points out that in many cases `CONTENTS(LOC(V)) = V`. Under what conditions do we have `LOC(CONTENTS(V)) = V`?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m07s  

---

For any variable $V$, the expression $\mathrm{CONTENTS}(\mathrm{LOC}(V)) = V$ holds whenever $V$ denotes a storage cell in the sense of Section 2.1, since $\mathrm{LOC}(V)$ is the address of that cell and $\mathrm{CONTENTS}(\mathrm{LOC}(V))$ is the value stored in it, which is $V$ itself.

For the reverse composition, $\mathrm{LOC}(\mathrm{CONTENTS}(V)) = V$ holds precisely when $\mathrm{CONTENTS}(V)$ denotes a variable (not a computed value) whose storage location is $V$, that is, when there exists a variable $X$ such that $\mathrm{CONTENTS}(V)=X$ and $\mathrm{LOC}(X)=V$. In this case $V$ is exactly the location of the variable obtained by dereferencing $V$, so applying $\mathrm{LOC}$ returns $V$ again. This completes the characterization. ∎
