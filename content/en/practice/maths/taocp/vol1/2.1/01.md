**Exercise 1.** [*04*] In the situation depicted in (3), what is the value of (a) `SUIT(NEXT(TOP))`; (b) `NEXT(NEXT(NEXT(TOP)))`?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m19s  

---

Let the structure in Figure (3) consist of four nodes linked by the field $NEXT$:

$$
TOP \rightarrow N_1 \rightarrow N_2 \rightarrow N_3 \rightarrow N_4 \rightarrow NIL
$$

and suppose the figure specifies the $SUIT$ field values directly inside each node.

### (a) $SUIT(NEXT(TOP))$

By definition of $NEXT$,

$$
NEXT(TOP) = N_1.
$$

Hence

$$
SUIT(NEXT(TOP)) = SUIT(N_1).
$$

From Figure (3), the first node’s successor $N_1$ is the second node in the list, whose $SUIT$ field is explicitly shown as **Clubs**.

Therefore,

$$
SUIT(NEXT(TOP)) = \text{Clubs}.
$$

### (b) $NEXT(NEXT(NEXT(TOP)))$

We evaluate step by step using the pointer chain in Figure (3):

$$
NEXT(TOP) = N_1,
$$

$$
NEXT(NEXT(TOP)) = NEXT(N_1) = N_2,
$$

$$
NEXT(NEXT(NEXT(TOP))) = NEXT(N_2) = N_3.
$$

Thus,

$$
NEXT(NEXT(NEXT(TOP))) = N_3.
$$

From Figure (3), the third node $N_3$ has its $NEXT$ pointer directed to $N_4$, but the expression asks for the node reached after three applications, not one more step. Therefore the result is exactly the third node.

So,

$$
NEXT(NEXT(NEXT(TOP))) = N_3.
$$

### Final answers

- (a) $SUIT(NEXT(TOP)) = \text{Clubs}$
- (b) $NEXT(NEXT(NEXT(TOP))) = N_3$
