**Exercise 7.** [*04*] In the text's example MIX program, (5), the link variable TOP is stored in the MIX computer word whose assembly language name is TOP. Given the field structure (1), which of the following sequences of code brings the quantity `NEXT(TOP)` into register A? Explain why the other sequence is incorrect.

```text
a) LDA TOP(NEXT)          b) LD1 TOP
                              LDA 0,1(NEXT)
```

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m04s  

---

Let TOP denote the word containing the link field of the top node. By the field structure (1), the notation TOP(NEXT) denotes the NEXT field of the word whose address is TOP, hence the quantity NEXT(TOP) is exactly the field value obtained by loading TOP(NEXT) into a register. The instruction

LDA TOP(NEXT)

therefore places NEXT(TOP) directly into register A, so sequence (a) is correct.

In sequence (b), the instruction LD1 TOP first loads index register $1$ with the contents of TOP, which is the address TOP of the node. The subsequent instruction

LDA 0,1(NEXT)

uses index register $1$ as the address of the node and the field specifier NEXT to select its NEXT field, so it loads the contents of NEXT(TOP) into register A. Thus sequence (b) also yields NEXT(TOP) in register A.

Both sequences therefore correctly bring NEXT(TOP) into register A. ∎
