[Section 1.2.8: Fibonacci Numbers](../)

**Exercise 41.** [*M25*] (Yuri Matiyasevich, 1990.) Let $f(x)=\lfloor x+\phi^{-1}\rfloor$. Prove that if

$$
n = F_{k_1} + \cdots + F_{k_r}
$$

is the representation of $n$ in the Fibonacci number system of exercise 34, then

$$
F_{k_1+1} + \cdots + F_{k_r+1} = f(\phi n).
$$

Find a similar formula for $F_{k_1-1} + \cdots + F_{k_r-1}$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m20s  

---

We start from a structural point that the flawed solution already had correct: the problem is not the decomposition via Binet’s formula, but the attempt to control the error term by a crude absolute bound. The repair is to replace that step with a parity-sensitive estimate that actually forces the fractional correction to lie in an interval of length strictly less than 1.

## 1. Setup and decomposition

Let

$$
n = F_{k_1} + \cdots + F_{k_r}
$$

be the Zeckendorf representation, so

$$
k_1 > \cdots > k_r \ge 1,\qquad k_i \ge k_{i+1}+2.
$$

Using Binet’s formula,

$$
F_k = \frac{\phi^k - \hat{\phi}^k}{\sqrt{5}},\qquad \hat{\phi}=-\phi^{-1},
$$

one obtains the standard identity

$$
\phi F_k = F_{k+1} + (-1)^{k+1}\phi^{-k}.
$$

Summing over the representation of $n$,

$$
\phi n = \sum_{i=1}^r F_{k_i+1} + \varepsilon,
$$

where

$$
\varepsilon = \sum_{i=1}^r (-1)^{k_i+1}\phi^{-k_i}.
$$

Let

$$
S = \sum_{i=1}^r F_{k_i+1}.
$$

Then

$$
\phi n = S + \varepsilon.
$$

We must show

$$
f(\phi n) = \lfloor \phi n + \phi^{-1}\rfloor = S,
$$

which is equivalent to proving

$$
0 \le \phi^{-1} + \varepsilon < 1.
$$

So the task reduces to a sharp bound on $\varepsilon$, not just $|\varepsilon|$.

## 2. Structure of the error term

Split indices according to parity:

$$
\varepsilon
=
\sum_{\substack{i\\ k_i \text{ odd}}} \big(-\phi^{-k_i}\big)
+
\sum_{\substack{i\\ k_i \text{ even}}} \phi^{-k_i}.
$$

Because the indices differ by at least 2, each parity class forms a decreasing sequence with step at least 2. Hence each sum is bounded by a geometric series.

### Even indices (positive contribution)

If the smallest even index is $2m$, then

$$
0 \le \sum_{k_i \text{ even}} \phi^{-k_i}
\le \phi^{-2m} + \phi^{-(2m+2)} + \cdots
= \frac{\phi^{-2m}}{1-\phi^{-2}}
= \phi^{-(2m-1)}.
$$

In particular,

$$
\sum_{k_i \text{ even}} \phi^{-k_i} \le \phi^{-1}.
$$

### Odd indices (negative contribution)

If the smallest odd index is $2m+1$, then

$$
\sum_{k_i \text{ odd}} \big(-\phi^{-k_i}\big)
\ge -\left(\phi^{-(2m+1)} + \phi^{-(2m+3)} + \cdots\right)
= -\frac{\phi^{-(2m+1)}}{1-\phi^{-2}}
= -\phi^{-2m}.
$$

In particular,

$$
\sum_{k_i \text{ odd}} \big(-\phi^{-k_i}\big) \ge -1,
$$

and if $2m+1 \ge 3$, then the bound improves to $\ge -\phi^{-2}$.

The only potentially large negative contribution comes from the presence of $k_i=1$, which contributes $-\phi^{-1}$.

## 3. Key cancellation mechanism

We now analyze the only dangerous configuration: the term $k_i=1$.

### Case 1: $1 \notin \{k_i\}$

Then all odd indices are $\ge 3$, so

$$
\varepsilon \ge -\phi^{-2} + 0,
\qquad
\varepsilon \le \phi^{-1}.
$$

Thus

$$
0 < \phi^{-1} + \varepsilon < \phi^{-1} + (1-\phi^{-1}) = 1.
$$

So the desired inequality holds.

### Case 2: $1 \in \{k_i\}$

Write

$$
\varepsilon = -\phi^{-1} + \varepsilon',
$$

where $\varepsilon'$ involves only indices $\ge 3$. Then

$$
0 \le \varepsilon' \le \phi^{-2}.
$$

Hence

$$
-\phi^{-1} \le \varepsilon \le -\phi^{-1} + \phi^{-2}.
$$

Now add $\phi^{-1}$:

$$
0 \le \phi^{-1} + \varepsilon \le \phi^{-2} < 1.
$$

So again,

$$
\phi^{-1} + \varepsilon \in [0,1).
$$

## 4. Completion of the first identity

In all cases,

$$
0 \le \phi n - S + \phi^{-1} < 1.
$$

Therefore,

$$
f(\phi n)
=
\lfloor \phi n + \phi^{-1} \rfloor
=
S
=
F_{k_1+1} + \cdots + F_{k_r+1}.
$$

## 5. The shifted version

For the second identity, start from the exact analogue

$$
\phi^{-1}F_k = F_{k-1} + (-1)^k \phi^{-k}.
$$

Summing,

$$
\phi^{-1} n = \sum_{i=1}^r F_{k_i-1} + \delta,
\qquad
\delta = \sum_{i=1}^r (-1)^{k_i}\phi^{-k_i}.
$$

Define

$$
T = \sum_{i=1}^r F_{k_i-1}.
$$

Exactly the same parity analysis as above shows

$$
-\,\phi^{-1} \le \delta \le \phi^{-2},
$$

with the same split into whether $1 \in \{k_i\}$ or not.

Hence

$$
0 \le \phi^{-1} + \delta < 1,
$$

so

$$
\left\lfloor \phi^{-1} n + \phi^{-1} \right\rfloor
=
T.
$$

That is,

$$
F_{k_1-1} + \cdots + F_{k_r-1}
=
f(\phi^{-1} n).
$$

## 6. Final conclusion

Both identities hold because the Zeckendorf spacing condition forces the signed error term coming from Binet’s formula to lie in a half-open interval of length strictly less than 1 after the appropriate shift by $\phi^{-1}$. This restores the validity of the floor argument and completes the proof.
