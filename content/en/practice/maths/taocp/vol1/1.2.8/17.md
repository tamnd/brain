[Section 1.2.8: Fibonacci Numbers](../)

**Exercise 17.** [*M24*] Using the conventions of exercise 8, prove the following generalization of Eq. (4):

$$
F_{n+k}F_{m-k} - F_nF_m = (-1)^n F_{m-n-k}F_k.
$$

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m  

---

## Solution

From identity (6) in Section 1.2.8,

$$
F_{r+s} = F_s F_{r+1} + F_{s-1}F_r,
$$

we obtain expansions of shifted Fibonacci numbers in terms of lower indices.

First expand $F_{n+k}$ by taking $r=n$ and $s=k$:

$$
F_{n+k} = F_k F_{n+1} + F_{k-1}F_n.
$$

Next expand $F_m$ by taking $r=m-k$ and $s=k$:

$$
F_m = F_k F_{m-k+1} + F_{k-1}F_{m-k}.
$$

Substituting these two expressions into the left-hand side gives

$$
F_{n+k}F_{m-k} - F_n F_m
= (F_k F_{n+1} + F_{k-1}F_n)F_{m-k}
- F_n(F_k F_{m-k+1} + F_{k-1}F_{m-k}).
$$

Expanding and collecting terms yields

$$
F_k F_{n+1}F_{m-k} + F_{k-1}F_n F_{m-k}
- F_n F_k F_{m-k+1} - F_n F_{k-1}F_{m-k}.
$$

The middle terms cancel, leaving

$$
F_k\bigl(F_{n+1}F_{m-k} - F_n F_{m-k+1}\bigr).
$$

From the Cassini-type relation in Exercise 8 conventions,

$$
F_{n+1}F_t - F_n F_{t+1} = (-1)^n F_{t-n},
$$

valid for all integers $t$, substitution $t=m-k$ gives

$$
F_{n+1}F_{m-k} - F_n F_{m-k+1} = (-1)^n F_{m-k-n}.
$$

Multiplying by $F_k$ gives

$$
F_{n+k}F_{m-k} - F_n F_m = (-1)^n F_k F_{m-n-k}.
$$

Rewriting the product on the right-hand side yields

$$
F_{n+k}F_{m-k} - F_nF_m = (-1)^n F_{m-n-k}F_k.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
