**Exercise 24.** [*HM20*] Evaluate the $n \times n$ determinant

$$
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & -1 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
1 & 1 & -1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & 1 & 1 & -1 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
\vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots \\
0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 1 & 1 & -1 \\
0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 1 & 1
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

**Solve time:** 1m17s

## Solution

Let $D_n$ denote the given $n \times n$ determinant. Expanding along the first row gives

$$
D_n
=
1 \cdot D_{n-1}
- (-1)\cdot M_{1,2},
$$

where $M_{1,2}$ is the minor obtained by deleting row $1$ and column $2$.

The minor $M_{1,2}$ has first column $(1,0,\dots,0)^{T}$, since only the entry in the second row and first column is nonzero among those remaining positions. Expanding $M_{1,2}$ along its first column reduces it to the determinant of the $(n-2)\times(n-2)$ matrix of the same form as the original, hence $M_{1,2}=D_{n-2}$.

The subdeterminant $D_{n-1}$ arises from deleting the first row and first column. The resulting matrix retains the same tridiagonal structure with diagonal entries $1$, superdiagonal entries $-1$, and subdiagonal entries $1$, so its determinant is $D_{n-1}$.

Therefore,

$$
D_n = D_{n-1} + D_{n-2}.
$$

The initial values are

$$
D_1 = 1,
\qquad
D_2 =
\begin{vmatrix}
1 & -1 \\
1 & 1
\end{vmatrix}
= 2.
$$

Let $F_n$ denote the Fibonacci numbers with $F_1=1$, $F_2=1$. The recurrence gives $F_3=2$, and the sequence $D_n$ matches the shifted Fibonacci values $F_{n+1}$ for $n=1,2$. Since both sequences satisfy $X_n = X_{n-1}+X_{n-2}$ for $n\ge 3$, they coincide for all $n$.

Thus,

$$
D_n = F_{n+1}.
$$

\boxed{F_{n+1}}
