**Exercise 26.** [*M20*] Using the previous exercise, show that $F_p \equiv 5^{(p-1)/2}$ (modulo $p$) if $p$ is an odd prime.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m43s  

---

## Solution

From Exercise 25 with $n=p$, where $p$ is an odd prime, we have

$$
2^p F_p = 2 \sum_{k\ \mathrm{odd}} \binom{p}{k} 5^{(k-1)/2}.
$$

For a prime $p$, the binomial coefficients satisfy

$$
\binom{p}{k} \equiv 0 \pmod p \quad (1 \le k \le p-1),
$$

since $p$ divides the numerator $p(p-1)\cdots(p-k+1)$ but does not divide $k!$. Hence, modulo $p$, every term in the sum vanishes except those with $k=0$ or $k=p$. The restriction $k$ odd excludes $k=0$, and since $p$ is odd, $k=p$ is included.

Thus

$$
\sum_{k\ \mathrm{odd}} \binom{p}{k} 5^{(k-1)/2} \equiv \binom{p}{p} 5^{(p-1)/2} \pmod p,
$$

and since $\binom{p}{p}=1$, this becomes

$$
\sum_{k\ \mathrm{odd}} \binom{p}{k} 5^{(k-1)/2} \equiv 5^{(p-1)/2} \pmod p.
$$

Substituting into the identity yields

$$
2^p F_p \equiv 2 \cdot 5^{(p-1)/2} \pmod p.
$$

Since $p$ is odd, $2$ is invertible modulo $p$, so division by $2$ is valid:

$$
2^{p-1} F_p \equiv 5^{(p-1)/2} \pmod p.
$$

By Fermat's little theorem,

$$
2^{p-1} \equiv 1 \pmod p.
$$

Multiplying the congruence by the inverse of $2^{p-1}$ gives

$$
F_p \equiv 5^{(p-1)/2} \pmod p.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
