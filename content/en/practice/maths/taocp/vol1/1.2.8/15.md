[Section 1.2.8: Fibonacci Numbers](../)

**Exercise 15.** [*M22*] Let $f(n)$ and $g(n)$ be arbitrary functions, and for $n \ge 0$ let

$$
\begin{aligned}
a_0&=0, & a_1&=1, & a_{n+2}&=a_{n+1}+a_n+f(n);\\
b_0&=0, & b_1&=1, & b_{n+2}&=b_{n+1}+b_n+g(n);\\
c_0&=0, & c_1&=1, & c_{n+2}&=c_{n+1}+c_n+xf(n)+yg(n).
\end{aligned}
$$

Express $c_n$ in terms of $x$, $y$, $a_n$, $b_n$, and $F_n$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m24s  

---

## Solution

Let $A_n$ and $B_n$ be defined by

$$
A_n = a_n - F_n, \qquad B_n = b_n - F_n.
$$

Using the recurrences for $a_n$, $b_n$, and $F_n$, for $n \ge 0$,

$$
a_{n+2} = a_{n+1} + a_n + f(n), \qquad F_{n+2} = F_{n+1} + F_n,
$$

hence

$$
A_{n+2} = a_{n+2} - F_{n+2}
= (a_{n+1} - F_{n+1}) + (a_n - F_n) + f(n)
= A_{n+1} + A_n + f(n).
$$

The initial conditions satisfy

$$
A_0 = a_0 - F_0 = 0, \qquad A_1 = a_1 - F_1 = 0.
$$

The same computation gives

$$
B_{n+2} = B_{n+1} + B_n + g(n), \qquad B_0 = 0, \qquad B_1 = 0.
$$

Define

$$
c_n = x a_n + y b_n + (1-x-y)F_n.
$$

Then

$$
c_0 = x a_0 + y b_0 + (1-x-y)F_0 = 0,
$$

and

$$
c_1 = x a_1 + y b_1 + (1-x-y)F_1 = x + y + (1-x-y) = 1.
$$

For $n \ge 0$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
c_{n+2}
&= x a_{n+2} + y b_{n+2} + (1-x-y)F_{n+2} \\
&= x(a_{n+1}+a_n+f(n)) + y(b_{n+1}+b_n+g(n)) + (1-x-y)(F_{n+1}+F_n) \\
&= (x a_{n+1} + y b_{n+1} + (1-x-y)F_{n+1}) \\
&\quad + (x a_n + y b_n + (1-x-y)F_n) \\
&\quad + x f(n) + y g(n) \\
&= c_{n+1} + c_n + x f(n) + y g(n).
\end{aligned}
$$

Thus $c_n$ satisfies the defining recurrence and initial conditions, hence coincides with the required sequence.

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{c_n = x a_n + y b_n + (1-x-y)F_n}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
