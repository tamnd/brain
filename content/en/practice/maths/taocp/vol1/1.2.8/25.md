[Section 1.2.8: Fibonacci Numbers](../)

**Exercise 25.** [*M21*] Show that

$$
2^nF_n = 2 \sum_{k\ \mathrm{odd}} \binom{n}{k} 5^{(k-1)/2}.
$$

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m14s  

---

## Solution

From Eq. (14), the closed form for Fibonacci numbers is

$$
F_n = \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}(\phi^n - \hat{\phi}^n),
$$

where $\phi = \frac{1}{2}(1+\sqrt{5})$ and $\hat{\phi} = \frac{1}{2}(1-\sqrt{5})$.

Multiplying both sides by $2^n$ gives

$$
2^n F_n = \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}\left((2\phi)^n - (2\hat{\phi})^n\right).
$$

Using $2\phi = 1+\sqrt{5}$ and $2\hat{\phi} = 1-\sqrt{5}$, this becomes

$$
2^n F_n = \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}\left((1+\sqrt{5})^n - (1-\sqrt{5})^n\right).
$$

Expand each term by the binomial theorem:

$$
(1+\sqrt{5})^n = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k} (\sqrt{5})^k,
$$

$$
(1-\sqrt{5})^n = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k} (-\sqrt{5})^k.
$$

Subtracting yields

$$
(1+\sqrt{5})^n - (1-\sqrt{5})^n
= \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k} \left((\sqrt{5})^k - (-\sqrt{5})^k\right).
$$

For even $k$, $(\sqrt{5})^k - (-\sqrt{5})^k = 0$. For odd $k$, $(-\sqrt{5})^k = -(\sqrt{5})^k$, hence the difference equals $2(\sqrt{5})^k$. Therefore

$$
(1+\sqrt{5})^n - (1-\sqrt{5})^n
= 2 \sum_{k\ \mathrm{odd}} \binom{n}{k} (\sqrt{5})^k.
$$

Dividing by $\sqrt{5}$ gives

$$
\frac{(1+\sqrt{5})^n - (1-\sqrt{5})^n}{\sqrt{5}}
= 2 \sum_{k\ \mathrm{odd}} \binom{n}{k} 5^{(k-1)/2}.
$$

Since the left-hand side equals $2^n F_n$, the identity follows:

$$
2^n F_n = 2 \sum_{k\ \mathrm{odd}} \binom{n}{k} 5^{(k-1)/2}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
