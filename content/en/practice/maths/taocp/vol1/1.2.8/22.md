[Section 1.2.8: Fibonacci Numbers](../)

**Exercise 22.** [*M20*] Show that $\sum_k \binom{n}{k}F_{m+k}$ is a Fibonacci number.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m27s  

---

Let

$$
M=\begin{pmatrix}1&1\\[2pt]1&0\end{pmatrix}.
$$

A standard identity for Fibonacci numbers is

$$
M^k=
\begin{pmatrix}
F_{k+1} & F_k\\
F_k & F_{k-1}
\end{pmatrix}
\qquad (k\ge 1),
$$

with the convention $F_0=0, F_1=1$. This follows by induction from the recurrence $F_{k+1}=F_k+F_{k-1}$.

Hence,

$$
F_{m+k} = (M^{m+k})_{1,2}.
$$

### Step 1: Rewrite the sum using matrix entries

Let $e_1=(1,0)^T$ and $e_2=(0,1)^T$. Then

$$
F_{m+k} = e_1^T M^{m+k} e_2.
$$

Therefore

$$
S:=\sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} F_{m+k}
= \sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} e_1^T M^{m+k} e_2.
$$

Factor out $M^m$:

$$
S = e_1^T M^m \left(\sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} M^k \right)e_2.
$$

### Step 2: Evaluate the binomial matrix sum

By the binomial theorem for matrices,

$$
\sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} M^k = (I+M)^n.
$$

Now compute $I+M$:

$$
I+M =
\begin{pmatrix}1&0\\0&1\end{pmatrix}
+
\begin{pmatrix}1&1\\1&0\end{pmatrix}
=
\begin{pmatrix}2&1\\1&1\end{pmatrix}.
$$

But

$$
M^2 =
\begin{pmatrix}1&1\\1&0\end{pmatrix}^2
=
\begin{pmatrix}2&1\\1&1\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Hence,

$$
I+M = M^2.
$$

Therefore,

$$
(I+M)^n = (M^2)^n = M^{2n}.
$$

So

$$
\sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} M^k = M^{2n}.
$$

### Step 3: Conclude the evaluation

Substitute back:

$$
S = e_1^T M^m M^{2n} e_2 = e_1^T M^{m+2n} e_2.
$$

But by the matrix–Fibonacci identity,

$$
e_1^T M^{m+2n} e_2 = F_{m+2n}.
$$

Thus,

$$
\sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} F_{m+k} = F_{m+2n}.
$$

### Final conclusion

The sum is itself a Fibonacci number:

$$
\boxed{\sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} F_{m+k} = F_{m+2n}.}
$$
