[Section 1.2.8: Fibonacci Numbers](../)

**Exercise 1.** [*10*] What is the answer to Leonardo Fibonacci's original problem: How many pairs of rabbits are present after a year?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m17s  

---

Let $R_n$ be the number of rabbit pairs after $n$ months, starting with one newborn pair at month $0$, and assume each pair becomes productive after one month and then produces one new pair every month thereafter.

Then $R_0=1$, $R_1=1$, and for $n\ge 0$ each month’s population is obtained by carrying forward all existing pairs and adding the new pairs produced by those that were already mature, giving the recurrence $R_{n+2}=R_{n+1}+R_n$. Hence $R_n=F_n$ under the standard initialization $F_0=0$, $F_1=1$ shifted by one step so that $R_1=F_1$ and $R_n=F_n$ for all $n\ge 1$.

After 12 months the number of pairs is therefore $R_{12}=F_{12}$. From the Fibonacci sequence $F_{12}=144$.

Thus the number of rabbit pairs after one year is $\boxed{144}$.
