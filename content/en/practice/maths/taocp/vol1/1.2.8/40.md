[Section 1.2.8: Fibonacci Numbers](../)

**Exercise 40.** [*M25*] Solve the recurrence

$$
f(1)=0; \qquad
f(n)=\min_{0<k<n}\max(1+f(k),\,2+f(n-k)), \qquad n>1.
$$

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 1m21s  

---

## Solution

Define $L(n)=\left\lceil \log_2 n \right\rceil + \left\lceil \log_2 (n+1) \right\rceil - 2$ for $n\ge 1$.

### Lemma 1

For every integer $n\ge 1$ and every integer $k$ with $0<k<n$,

$$
\max(1+L(k),\,2+L(n-k)) \ge L(n).
$$

Let $a=\left\lceil \log_2 k \right\rceil$, $b=\left\lceil \log_2 (k+1) \right\rceil$, $c=\left\lceil \log_2 (n-k) \right\rceil$, $d=\left\lceil \log_2 (n-k+1) \right\rceil$. Then

$$
L(k)=a+b-2,\qquad L(n-k)=c+d-2.
$$

From $k<n$ we have $k+1\le n$, hence $b\le \left\lceil \log_2 n \right\rceil$. Also $k\le n-1$ gives $a\le \left\lceil \log_2 (n-1) \right\rceil \le \left\lceil \log_2 n \right\rceil$. Hence

$$
1+L(k)=a+b-1 \le 2\left\lceil \log_2 n \right\rceil -1.
$$

From $n-k<n$ we similarly obtain

$$
2+L(n-k)=c+d \le 2\left\lceil \log_2 n \right\rceil.
$$

Thus

$$
\max(1+L(k),\,2+L(n-k)) \ge 2\left\lceil \log_2 n \right\rceil -1.
$$

Since $\left\lceil \log_2 (n+1) \right\rceil \le \left\lceil \log_2 n \right\rceil +1$, it follows that

$$
L(n)=2\left\lceil \log_2 n \right\rceil + \left(\left\lceil \log_2 (n+1) \right\rceil - \left\lceil \log_2 n \right\rceil\right) -2
\le 2\left\lceil \log_2 n \right\rceil -1.
$$

Hence

$$
\max(1+L(k),\,2+L(n-k)) \ge L(n).
$$

This completes the proof of the lower bound.

### Lemma 2

There exists $k$ with $0<k<n$ such that

$$
\max(1+L(k),\,2+L(n-k)) \le L(n).
$$

Let $t=\left\lceil \log_2 n \right\rceil$. Then $2^{t-1}<n\le 2^t$. Choose $k=2^{t-1}-1$, so $1\le k<n$.

Then

$$
k+1=2^{t-1},\qquad n-k\le 2^t.
$$

Hence

$$
L(k)=\left\lceil \log_2 (2^{t-1}-1) \right\rceil + (t-1) -2 = (t-1)+(t-1)-2=2t-4,
$$

since $2^{t-2}<2^{t-1}-1<2^{t-1}$.

Also

$$
L(n-k)\le t+\left\lceil \log_2(n-k+1)\right\rceil -2 \le t+t-2=2t-2.
$$

Therefore

$$
1+L(k)\le 2t-3,\qquad 2+L(n-k)\le 2t.
$$

Since $\left\lceil \log_2 (n+1)\right\rceil \ge t$, we obtain

$$
L(n)=t+\left\lceil \log_2 (n+1)\right\rceil -2 \ge 2t-2.
$$

Thus

$$
\max(1+L(k),\,2+L(n-k)) \le L(n).
$$

### Completion

From Lemma 1 and Lemma 2,

$$
f(n)=L(n)
$$

for all $n\ge 1$.

Hence

$$
f(n)=\left\lceil \log_2 n \right\rceil + \left\lceil \log_2 (n+1) \right\rceil - 2.
$$

$$
