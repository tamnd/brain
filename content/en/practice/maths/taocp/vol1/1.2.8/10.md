[Section 1.2.8: Fibonacci Numbers](../)

**Exercise 10.** [*15*] Is $\phi^n/\sqrt{5}$ greater than $F_n$ or less than $F_n$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m09s  

---

## Solution

From equation (14),

$$
F_n = \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}(\phi^n - \hat{\phi}^n),
\qquad \hat{\phi} = \frac{1}{2}(1-\sqrt{5}).
$$

Rearranging,

$$
\frac{\phi^n}{\sqrt{5}} - F_n = \frac{\hat{\phi}^n}{\sqrt{5}}.
$$

Since $\sqrt{5} > 0$, the sign of $\frac{\phi^n}{\sqrt{5}} - F_n$ is the sign of $\hat{\phi}^n$.

The number $\hat{\phi}$ is negative and satisfies $|\hat{\phi}| < 1$. Hence $\hat{\phi}^n > 0$ when $n$ is even and $\hat{\phi}^n < 0$ when $n$ is odd.

Therefore,

$$
\frac{\phi^n}{\sqrt{5}} > F_n \quad \text{if and only if } n \text{ is even},
$$

and

$$
\frac{\phi^n}{\sqrt{5}} < F_n \quad \text{if and only if } n \text{ is odd}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
