[Section 1.2.8: Fibonacci Numbers](../)

**Exercise 13.** [*M22*] Express the following sequences in terms of the Fibonacci numbers, when $r$, $s$, and $c$ are given constants:

a) $a_0=r$, $a_1=s$; $a_{n+2}=a_{n+1}+a_n$, for $n \ge 0$.

b) $b_0=0$, $b_1=1$; $b_{n+2}=b_{n+1}+b_n+c$, for $n \ge 0$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m37s  

---

## Solution

### (a)

Let $a_0=r$, $a_1=s$, and $a_{n+2}=a_{n+1}+a_n$ for $n\ge 0$. The Fibonacci numbers satisfy $F_0=0$, $F_1=1$, and $F_{n+2}=F_{n+1}+F_n$.

Consider the expression

$$
a_n = rF_{n-1} + sF_n \quad (n\ge 0),
$$

with the extension of Fibonacci numbers to negative indices determined by

$$
F_{n+1}F_{n-1}-F_n^2 = (-1)^n,
$$

which implies $F_{-1}=1$.

For $n=0$,

$$
rF_{-1}+sF_0 = r\cdot 1 + s\cdot 0 = r = a_0.
$$

For $n=1$,

$$
rF_0+sF_1 = 0+s = s = a_1.
$$

Assume $a_n=rF_{n-1}+sF_n$ and $a_{n+1}=rF_n+sF_{n+1}$. Then

$$
a_{n+2}=a_{n+1}+a_n
= rF_n+sF_{n+1}+rF_{n-1}+sF_n.
$$

Grouping terms,

$$
a_{n+2}=r(F_n+F_{n-1})+s(F_{n+1}+F_n).
$$

Using $F_{n+2}=F_{n+1}+F_n$,

$$
F_n+F_{n-1}=F_{n+1}, \quad F_{n+1}+F_n=F_{n+2},
$$

so

$$
a_{n+2}=rF_{n+1}+sF_{n+2}.
$$

This matches the proposed form with $n$ replaced by $n+2$, so the identity holds for all $n\ge 0$. Hence

$$
\boxed{a_n = rF_{n-1}+sF_n}.
$$

### (b)

Let $b_0=0$, $b_1=1$, and $b_{n+2}=b_{n+1}+b_n+c$.

Define

$$
u_n=b_n+c.
$$

Then

$$
u_{n+2}=b_{n+2}+c=b_{n+1}+b_n+c+c.
$$

Rewriting in terms of $u_n$,

$$
u_{n+2}=(u_{n+1}-c)+(u_n-c)+2c=u_{n+1}+u_n.
$$

Thus $u_n$ satisfies the Fibonacci recurrence

$$
u_{n+2}=u_{n+1}+u_n.
$$

The initial values are

$$
u_0=b_0+c=c, \quad u_1=b_1+c=1+c.
$$

By the representation from part (a),

$$
u_n = cF_{n-1} + (1+c)F_n.
$$

Since $b_n=u_n-c$,

$$
b_n = cF_{n-1} + (1+c)F_n - c.
$$

This expression satisfies the initial conditions:

for $n=0$,

$$
cF_{-1}+(1+c)F_0-c = c\cdot 1 + 0 - c = 0,
$$

and for $n=1$,

$$
cF_0+(1+c)F_1-c = 0+(1+c)-c = 1.
$$

Hence

$$
\boxed{b_n = cF_{n-1} + (1+c)F_n - c}.
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
