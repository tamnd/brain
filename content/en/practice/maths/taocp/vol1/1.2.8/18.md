[Section 1.2.8: Fibonacci Numbers](../)

**Exercise 18.** [*20*] Is $F_n^2 + F_{n+1}^2$ always a Fibonacci number?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m06s  

---

## Solution

Using identity (6), for any positive integers $n$ and $m$,

$$
F_{n+m} = F_m F_{n+1} + F_{m-1}F_n.
$$

Take $m = n+1$ and substitute into (6). Then

$$
F_{2n+1} = F_{n+1}F_{n+1} + F_n F_n.
$$

The right-hand side simplifies to

$$
F_{2n+1} = F_{n+1}^2 + F_n^2.
$$

Thus $F_n^2 + F_{n+1}^2$ is a Fibonacci number, namely $F_{2n+1}$, for every positive integer $n$.

This completes the proof. ∎
