[Section 1.2.8: Fibonacci Numbers](../)

**Exercise 5.** [*20*] Find all $n$ for which $F_n=n^2$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m22s  

---

## Solution

We use the standard initial values $F_0=0$, $F_1=1$, $F_2=1$, and the recurrence $F_{n+1}=F_n+F_{n-1}$ for $n\ge 1$.

First the small indices are checked directly from the definition. One has

$F_0=0=0^2$, hence $n=0$ is a solution. Also $F_1=1=1^2$ and $F_2=1=1^2$, hence $n=1$ and $n=2$ are solutions. For $n=3$, $F_3=2\ne 9$, so $n=3$ is not a solution.

For larger $n$, the growth estimate from Section 1.2.8 gives

$$
F_n \ge \phi^{n-2},
$$

where $\phi=\frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2}$.

For $n\ge 13$, comparison of successive ratios shows that the exponential term dominates the quadratic term. Indeed,

$$
\frac{(n+1)^2}{n^2}=1+\frac{2}{n}+\frac{1}{n^2}\le 1+\frac{2}{13}+\frac{1}{169}<1.157,
$$

while $\phi>1.618$. Hence for all $n\ge 13$,

$$
\frac{\phi^{n-1}}{\phi^{n-2}}=\phi > \frac{(n+1)^2}{n^2},
$$

so once an inequality of the form $\phi^{n-2}>n^2$ holds at some index, it persists for all larger indices. Direct evaluation at $n=13$ gives

$$
\phi^{11}\approx 198.6 > 169 = 13^2,
$$

so for every $n\ge 13$,

$$
F_n \ge \phi^{n-2} > n^2,
$$

and therefore no such $n$ can satisfy $F_n=n^2$ in this range.

It remains to check $3\le n\le 12$. Using the Fibonacci values

$$
F_3=2,\; F_4=3,\; F_5=5,\; F_6=8,\; F_7=13,\; F_8=21,\; F_9=34,\; F_{10}=55,\; F_{11}=89,\; F_{12}=144,
$$

none of these equals $n^2$ for $3\le n\le 11$, and for $n=12$ one has $F_{12}=144=12^2$.

Thus the complete list of solutions is $n=0,1,2,12$.

$$
\boxed{n\in\{0,1,2,12\}}
$$
