**Exercise 4.** [*14*] Find all $n$ for which $F_n=n$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m26s  

---

## Solution

The Fibonacci numbers are defined by $F_1=1$, $F_2=1$, and $F_{n+2}=F_{n+1}+F_n$ for $n \ge 1$.

We seek all positive integers $n$ such that $F_n=n$.

Direct computation from the recurrence gives

$$
F_1=1,\quad F_2=1,\quad F_3=2,\quad F_4=3,\quad F_5=5,\quad F_6=8.
$$

Hence $F_1=1$ and $F_5=5$ satisfy the equation, while $n=2,3,4,6$ do not.

We now show that no other solutions exist.

From the inequality in Section 1.2.8,

$$
\phi^{n-2} \le F_n \le \phi^{n-1},
$$

where $\phi=\tfrac{1}{2}(1+\sqrt{5})$.

For $n=6$, this gives

$$
F_6 \ge \phi^4.
$$

Since $\phi^2=\phi+1$, we have $\phi^3=2\phi+1$ and $\phi^4=3\phi+2$, hence

$$
\phi^4 = 3\phi+2 > 3\cdot 1 + 2 = 5.
$$

Thus $F_6 \ge 8 > 6$, so $F_6 \ne 6$.

For $n \ge 6$, we prove $F_n > n$ by induction using the lower bound $F_n \ge \phi^{n-2}$.

For the base case $n=6$, $\phi^4 > 6$ implies $F_6 > 6$.

Assume for some $n \ge 6$ that $\phi^{n-2} \ge n$. Then

$$
\phi^{n-1} = \phi \cdot \phi^{n-2} \ge \phi n.
$$

Since $\phi > 1$,

$$
\phi n - (n+1) = n(\phi-1) - 1.
$$

Because $\phi-1 = \tfrac{\sqrt{5}-1}{2} > 0.6$, for $n \ge 6$,

$$
n(\phi-1) - 1 \ge 6 \cdot 0.6 - 1 > 0.
$$

Hence $\phi n > n+1$, so $\phi^{n-1} > n+1$. Therefore

$$
F_{n+1} \ge \phi^{n-1} > n+1.
$$

This completes the induction, and yields $F_n > n$ for all $n \ge 6$.

Combining this with the direct check for $1 \le n \le 5$ shows that the only solutions occur at $n=1$ and $n=5$.

Thus

$$
\boxed{n \in \{1,5\}}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
