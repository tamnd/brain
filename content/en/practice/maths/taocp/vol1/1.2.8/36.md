**Exercise 36.** [*M32*] (*Fibonacci strings.*) Let $S_1=\text{"a"}$, $S_2=\text{"b"}$, and $S_{n+2}=S_{n+1}S_n$, $n>0$; in other words, $S_{n+2}$ is formed by placing $S_n$ at the right of $S_{n+1}$. We have $S_3=\text{"ba"}$, $S_4=\text{"bab"}$, $S_5=\text{"babba"}$, etc. Clearly $S_n$ has $F_n$ letters. Explore the properties of $S_n$. (Where do double letters occur? Can you predict the value of the $k$th letter of $S_n$? What is the density of the b's? And so on.)

Let $S_1=a$, $S_2=b$, and $S_{n+2}=S_{n+1}S_n$. Let $F_1=F_2=1$, $F_{n+2}=F_{n+1}+F_n$.

## 1. Length

We prove by induction that $|S_n|=F_n$.

For $n=1,2$, this holds by definition. If $|S_{n+1}|=F_{n+1}$ and $|S_n|=F_n$, then

$$
|S_{n+2}| = |S_{n+1}| + |S_n| = F_{n+1}+F_n = F_{n+2}.
$$

Thus $|S_n|=F_n$ for all $n\ge 1$.

## 2. First and last letters

### First letter

Let $f_n$ be the first letter of $S_n$. For $n\ge 1$, $S_{n+2}$ begins with $S_{n+1}$, hence

$$
f_{n+2}=f_{n+1}.
$$

With $f_1=a$, $f_2=b$, we obtain

$$
f_3=b,\quad f_4=b,\quad \dots
$$

Thus

$$
f_1=a,\qquad f_n=b \text{ for all } n\ge 2.
$$

### Last letter

Let $\ell_n$ be the last letter of $S_n$. Since $S_{n+2}=S_{n+1}S_n$,

$$
\ell_{n+2}=\ell_n.
$$

With $\ell_1=a$, $\ell_2=b$, it follows that

$$
\ell_n =
\begin{cases}
a & n \text{ odd},\\
b & n \text{ even}.
\end{cases}
$$

## 3. Letter counts

Let $A_n$ and $B_n$ be the number of $a$'s and $b$'s in $S_n$. From concatenation,

$$
A_{n+2}=A_{n+1}+A_n,\qquad B_{n+2}=B_{n+1}+B_n.
$$

Initial values:

$$
A_1=1,\ A_2=0,\qquad B_1=0,\ B_2=1.
$$

By induction this matches Fibonacci shifts:

$$
A_n = F_{n-2}\ (n\ge 3), \qquad B_n = F_{n-1}\ (n\ge 1),
$$

with the standard convention $F_0=0$.

## 4. Structure of adjacent pairs (key invariant)

We now analyze double letters rigorously.

### Lemma 1 (Inheritance of adjacent pairs)

For $n\ge 1$, every adjacent pair (length-2 substring) of $S_n$ is either:

1. an adjacent pair in $S_{n-1}$,
2. an adjacent pair in $S_{n-2}$, or
3. the boundary pair $\ell_{n-1} f_{n-2}$.

**Proof.** Since $S_n=S_{n-1}S_{n-2}$, any length-2 substring is either entirely inside $S_{n-1}$, entirely inside $S_{n-2}$, or crosses the concatenation boundary. ∎

Thus, new double letters can only arise at the boundary.

## 5. First structural consequence: no $aa$

### Lemma 2

No occurrence of $aa$ appears in any $S_n$ for $n\ge 1$.

**Proof by induction on $n$.**

For $n=1,2$, the claim is clear.

Assume $S_n$ and $S_{n-1}$ contain no $aa$. In $S_{n+1}=S_n S_{n-1}$, any new adjacency is either internal to $S_n$, internal to $S_{n-1}$, or the boundary pair $\ell_n f_{n-1}$.

By induction hypothesis, internal parts contain no $aa$. It remains to check the boundary:

- For $n=1$: $S_2S_1 = b a$, no $aa$.
- For $n\ge 2$: we have $f_{n-1}=b$, so the boundary is either $ab$ or $bb$, never $aa$.

Hence $aa$ never occurs. ∎

## 6. Complete classification of double letters

We now determine exactly when $bb$ occurs.

### Lemma 3 (First-letter stability)

For all $n\ge 2$, $f_n=b$.

This was proved earlier.

### Lemma 4 (Occurrence of $bb$)

For $n\ge 3$, the boundary of $S_n=S_{n-1}S_{n-2}$ is

$$
\ell_{n-1} f_{n-2}.
$$

Since $f_{n-2}=b$ for $n-2\ge 2$, i.e. $n\ge 4$, we consider cases:

- $n=3$: $S_3=ba$, no double letters.
- $n=4$: boundary is $\ell_3 f_2 = a b$, no $bb$.
- For $n\ge 5$: $f_{n-2}=b$, so boundary is $\ell_{n-1} b$.

Now:

- if $n-1$ is even, $\ell_{n-1}=b$, giving $bb$,
- if $n-1$ is odd, $\ell_{n-1}=a$, giving $ab$.

Thus:

$$
bb \text{ occurs in } S_n \iff n \ge 5 \text{ and } n \text{ is odd}.
$$

### Lemma 5 (No other double letters)

From Lemma 2, $aa$ never occurs. From Lemma 4, the only possible repeated pair is $bb$. Hence all double letters are exactly occurrences of $bb$.

## 7. Density of $b$'s

$$
\frac{B_n}{|S_n|} = \frac{F_{n-1}}{F_n}.
$$

Taking limits and using standard Fibonacci asymptotics,

$$
\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{F_{n-1}}{F_n} = \frac{1}{\varphi},
\qquad \varphi=\frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2}.
$$

Thus the density of $b$'s in the infinite Fibonacci word is

$$
\boxed{\frac{1}{\varphi}}.
$$

## 8. Summary of properties

- $|S_n|=F_n$.
- $S_{n+2}=S_{n+1}S_n$.
- Number of $b$'s is $F_{n-1}$, number of $a$'s is $F_{n-2}$.
- $S_n$ begins with $a$ for $n=1$, and with $b$ for all $n\ge 2$.
- Last letter alternates with parity of $n$.
- The only repeated adjacent pair is $bb$.
- $bb$ occurs first at $S_5$ and then precisely at those odd indices $n\ge 5$.
- The asymptotic density of $b$'s is $1/\varphi$.
