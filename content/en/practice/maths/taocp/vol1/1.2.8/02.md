[Section 1.2.8: Fibonacci Numbers](../)

**Exercise 2.** [*20*] In view of Eq. (15), what is the approximate value of $F_{1000}$? (Use logarithms found in Appendix A.)

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m18s  

---

## Solution

From equation (15),

$$
F_n = \frac{\phi^n}{\sqrt{5}} \text{ rounded to the nearest integer.}
$$

Taking base-10 logarithms gives

$$
\log_{10} F_n = n \log_{10}\phi - \tfrac{1}{2}\log_{10} 5 + \varepsilon_n,
$$

where the rounding error satisfies $|\varepsilon_n| < 1$ in the logarithmic scale and does not affect the leading digits.

For $n = 1000$,

$$
\log_{10} F_{1000} = 1000 \log_{10}\phi - \tfrac{1}{2}\log_{10} 5.
$$

From Appendix A,

$$
\log_{10}\phi \approx 0.20898764025, \qquad \log_{10} 5 \approx 0.69897.
$$

Hence,

$$
1000 \log_{10}\phi \approx 208.98764025,
$$

and

$$
\tfrac{1}{2}\log_{10} 5 \approx 0.349485.
$$

Subtraction yields

$$
\log_{10} F_{1000} \approx 208.98764025 - 0.349485 = 208.63815525.
$$

Write

$$
208.63815525 = 208 + 0.63815525.
$$

Then

$$
F_{1000} \approx 10^{208} \cdot 10^{0.63815525}.
$$

To evaluate the second factor, compute

$$
\ln(10^{0.63815525}) = 0.63815525 \ln 10 \approx 0.63815525 \cdot 2.302585 \approx 1.4694.
$$

Thus

$$
10^{0.63815525} \approx e^{1.4694} \approx 4.35.
$$

Therefore,

$$
F_{1000} \approx 4.35 \cdot 10^{208}.
$$

Since equation (15) prescribes rounding to the nearest integer,

$$
F_{1000} \approx 4.35 \times 10^{208}.
$$

$$
\boxed{4.35 \times 10^{208}}
$$
