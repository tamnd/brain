[Section 1.2.8: Fibonacci Numbers](../)

**Exercise 31.** [*M20*] Show that $F_{2n}\phi \bmod 1 = 1-\phi^{-2n}$ and $F_{2n+1}\phi \bmod 1 = \phi^{-2n-1}$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 4m30s  

---

Start from Binet’s formula in a consistent and fully controlled way.

Let

$$
\phi = \frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2}, \quad \psi = \frac{1-\sqrt{5}}{2} = -\phi^{-1}.
$$

Then

$$
F_n = \frac{\phi^n - \psi^n}{\sqrt{5}}, \quad \phi + \psi = 1, \quad \phi\psi = -1, \quad \psi = -\phi^{-1}.
$$

## Step 1: Compute $F_n \phi - F_{n+1}$

Using Binet’s formula,

$$
F_n \phi = \frac{\phi^{n+1} - \psi^n \phi}{\sqrt{5}}, \quad
F_{n+1} = \frac{\phi^{n+1} - \psi^{n+1}}{\sqrt{5}}.
$$

Subtract:

$$
F_n \phi - F_{n+1}
= \frac{-\psi^n \phi + \psi^{n+1}}{\sqrt{5}}
= \frac{\psi^n(\psi - \phi)}{\sqrt{5}}.
$$

Now compute $\psi - \phi$:

$$
\psi - \phi = (1-\sqrt{5})/2 - (1+\sqrt{5})/2 = -\sqrt{5}.
$$

So:

$$
F_n \phi - F_{n+1}
= \frac{\psi^n (-\sqrt{5})}{\sqrt{5}}
= -\psi^n.
$$

Hence:

$$
F_n \phi = F_{n+1} - \psi^n.
$$

Using $\psi = -\phi^{-1}$,

$$
\psi^n = (-1)^n \phi^{-n}.
$$

Therefore:

$$
F_n \phi = F_{n+1} + (-1)^{n+1}\phi^{-n}.
$$

## Step 2: Determine the fractional part

Since $F_{n+1}$ is an integer, the fractional part of $F_n \phi$ is determined by

$$
(-1)^{n+1}\phi^{-n},
\quad \text{with } 0 < \phi^{-n} < 1.
$$

### Case 1: $n = 2k$

Then $(-1)^{n+1} = -1$, so

$$
F_{2k}\phi = F_{2k+1} - \phi^{-2k}.
$$

This lies just below an integer, hence

$$
F_{2k}\phi \bmod 1 = 1 - \phi^{-2k}.
$$

### Case 2: $n = 2k+1$

Then $(-1)^{n+1} = 1$, so

$$
F_{2k+1}\phi = F_{2k+2} + \phi^{-(2k+1)}.
$$

This lies just above an integer, hence

$$
F_{2k+1}\phi \bmod 1 = \phi^{-(2k+1)}.
$$

## Conclusion

$$
F_{2n}\phi \bmod 1 = 1 - \phi^{-2n}, \quad
F_{2n+1}\phi \bmod 1 = \phi^{-2n-1}.
$$

∎
