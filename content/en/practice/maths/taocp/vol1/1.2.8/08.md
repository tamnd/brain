[Section 1.2.8: Fibonacci Numbers](../)

**Exercise 8.** [*15*] In many cases it is convenient to define $F_n$ for negative $n$, by assuming that $F_{n+2}=F_{n+1}+F_n$ for all integers $n$. Explore this possibility: What is $F_{-1}$? What is $F_{-2}$? Can $F_{-n}$ be expressed in a simple way in terms of $F_n$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m02s  

---

Define Fibonacci numbers for all integers $n$ by requiring

$$
F_{n+2}=F_{n+1}+F_n \quad \text{for all } n\in\mathbb{Z},
$$

together with $F_0=0$, $F_1=1$.

From the recurrence we also obtain the backward form

$$
F_n = F_{n+2}-F_{n+1},
$$

valid for all integers $n$.

### Computation of $F_{-1}$ and $F_{-2}$

Take $n=-1$:

$$
F_1 = F_0 + F_{-1}.
$$

Hence

$$
1 = 0 + F_{-1}, \quad \text{so } F_{-1}=1.
$$

Take $n=-2$:

$$
F_0 = F_{-1} + F_{-2}.
$$

Hence

$$
0 = 1 + F_{-2}, \quad \text{so } F_{-2}=-1.
$$

### Claim

For all integers $n \ge 0$,

$$
F_{-n} = (-1)^{n+1} F_n.
$$

### Proof by induction on $n$

**Base cases.**

For $n=0$,

$$
F_0 = 0,\quad (-1)^{1}F_0 = 0,
$$

so the identity holds.

For $n=1$,

$$
F_{-1}=1,\quad (-1)^2 F_1 = 1,
$$

so the identity holds.

Thus the statement is true for $n=0,1$.

**Inductive step.**

Assume the identity holds for $n$ and $n-1$, with $n \ge 1$:

$$
F_{-n} = (-1)^{n+1}F_n, \quad F_{-(n-1)} = (-1)^n F_{n-1}.
$$

Using the backward recurrence with index $-n$,

$$
F_{-(n+1)} = F_{-n+1} - F_{-n}.
$$

Substitute the induction hypotheses:

$$
F_{-(n+1)} = (-1)^n F_{n-1} - (-1)^{n+1} F_n.
$$

Factor $(-1)^n$:

$$
F_{-(n+1)} = (-1)^n (F_{n-1}+F_n).
$$

Using $F_{n+1}=F_n+F_{n-1}$,

$$
F_{-(n+1)} = (-1)^n F_{n+1}.
$$

Since $(-1)^n = (-1)^{(n+1)+1}$,

$$
F_{-(n+1)} = (-1)^{(n+1)+1} F_{n+1}.
$$

Thus the statement holds for $n+1$.

By induction, the formula holds for all $n \ge 0$.

### Conclusion

$$
F_{-1}=1,\quad F_{-2}=-1,
$$

and in general

$$
F_{-n} = (-1)^{n+1} F_n \quad (n \ge 0).
$$

This completes the corrected solution.
