[Section 1.2.8: Fibonacci Numbers](../)

**Exercise 12.** [*M26*] The "second order" Fibonacci sequence is defined by the rule

$$
\mathcal{F}_0 = 0, \qquad \mathcal{F}_1 = 1, \qquad \mathcal{F}_{n+2} = \mathcal{F}_{n+1} + \mathcal{F}_n + F_n.
$$

Express $\mathcal{F}_n$ in terms of $F_n$ and $F_{n+1}$. [Hint: Use generating functions.]

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m18s  

---

The issue in the previous submission is not the method but the validation: the reviewer’s claimed value $\mathcal{F}_5=11$ is incorrect. The correct sequence generated by the defining recurrence is

$$
0,\,1,\,1,\,3,\,5,\,10,\dots
$$

so the alleged contradiction is spurious.

We now give a complete derivation that avoids the flawed convolution step and produces a verified closed form.

## Step 1. Ansatz compatible with the homogeneous structure

The recurrence

$$
\mathcal{F}_{n+2}=\mathcal{F}_{n+1}+\mathcal{F}_n+F_n
$$

has homogeneous part identical to Fibonacci’s recurrence. Hence every homogeneous solution is a linear combination of $F_n$ and $F_{n+1}$. Since the forcing term is $F_n$, which already satisfies the homogeneous recurrence, we use a resonant ansatz:

$$
\mathcal{F}_n = (a+cn)F_n + (b+dn)F_{n+1}.
$$

## Step 2. Substitute into the recurrence

Define

$$
A_n=a+cn,\qquad B_n=b+dn,
$$

so

$$
\mathcal{F}_n = A_n F_n + B_n F_{n+1}.
$$

Using $F_{n+2}=F_{n+1}+F_n$ and $F_{n+3}=2F_{n+1}+F_n$, we compute:

### Left-hand side

$$
\mathcal{F}_{n+2} = (A_{n+2}+B_{n+2})F_n + (A_{n+2}+2B_{n+2})F_{n+1}.
$$

### Right-hand side

$$
\mathcal{F}_{n+1}+\mathcal{F}_n+F_n
$$

has coefficients

$$
F_n:\; B_{n+1}+A_n+1,
\qquad
F_{n+1}:\; (A_{n+1}+B_{n+1})+B_n.
$$

Equating coefficients yields:

### From $F_n$

$$
A_{n+2}+B_{n+2}=B_{n+1}+A_n+1.
$$

### From $F_{n+1}$

$$
A_{n+2}+2B_{n+2}=A_{n+1}+B_{n+1}+B_n.
$$

## Step 3. Reduce to linear equations in $c,d$

Using

$$
A_{n+1}=A_n+c,\quad A_{n+2}=A_n+2c,\quad
B_{n+1}=B_n+d,\quad B_{n+2}=B_n+2d,
$$

we substitute.

### First equation

$$
(A_n+2c)+(B_n+2d)=(B_n+d)+A_n+1
$$

so

$$
2c+d=1.
$$

### Second equation

$$
(A_n+2c)+2(B_n+2d)=(A_n+c)+(B_n+d)+B_n
$$

so

$$
c+3d=0.
$$

Solving,

$$
d=-\frac{1}{5},\qquad c=\frac{3}{5}.
$$

## Step 4. Determine constants $a,b$

### Initial condition $n=0$

$$
\mathcal{F}_0=0=bF_1 \Rightarrow b=0.
$$

### Initial condition $n=1$

$$
1=\mathcal{F}_1=(a+c)F_1+(b+d)F_2=(a+c)+(b+d),
$$

so

$$
a+\frac{3}{5}-\frac{1}{5}=1 \Rightarrow a=\frac{3}{5}.
$$

## Step 5. Final closed form

$$
\mathcal{F}_n=\left(\frac{3}{5}+\frac{3n}{5}\right)F_n-\frac{n}{5}F_{n+1}.
$$

Hence

$$
\boxed{
\mathcal{F}_n=\frac{3(n+1)F_n-nF_{n+1}}{5}
}.
$$

## Step 6. Verification against the recurrence

Using $F_{n+2}=F_{n+1}+F_n$, substitution shows the expression satisfies

$$
\mathcal{F}_{n+2}-\mathcal{F}_{n+1}-\mathcal{F}_n=F_n,
$$

and the initial conditions are satisfied as checked above.

A direct computation confirms:

$$
\mathcal{F}_0=0,\quad \mathcal{F}_1=1,\quad \mathcal{F}_2=1,\quad \mathcal{F}_3=3,\quad \mathcal{F}_4=5,\quad \mathcal{F}_5=10,
$$

matching the recurrence exactly.

## Conclusion

The correct expression is

$$
\boxed{\mathcal{F}_n=\frac{3(n+1)F_n-nF_{n+1}}{5}}.
$$

The earlier “counterexample” arose from an incorrect value of $\mathcal{F}_5$, not from the derived formula.
