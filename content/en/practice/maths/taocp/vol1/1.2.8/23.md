[Section 1.2.8: Fibonacci Numbers](../)

**Exercise 23.** [*M23*] Generalizing the preceding exercise, show that

$$
\sum_k \binom{n}{k} F_t^k F_{t-1}^{\,n-k} F_{m+k}
$$

is always a Fibonacci number.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m24s  

---

We restart from the definition and avoid splitting into $F_k$ and $F_{k-1}$, which is the source of the earlier error.

Let

$$
S=\sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} F_t^k F_{t-1}^{\,n-k} F_{m+k}.
$$

We use Binet’s formula:

$$
F_r=\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}(\phi^r-\hat{\phi}^r),
\quad \phi=\frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2},\quad \hat{\phi}=\frac{1-\sqrt{5}}{2}.
$$

Substitute into $S$:

$$
S=\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}\sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} F_t^k F_{t-1}^{\,n-k}
\left(\phi^{m+k}-\hat{\phi}^{m+k}\right).
$$

Split the sum:

$$
S=\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}\left(
\phi^m \sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} F_t^k F_{t-1}^{\,n-k}\phi^k
-
\hat{\phi}^m \sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} F_t^k F_{t-1}^{\,n-k}\hat{\phi}^k
\right).
$$

Rewrite each sum as a binomial expression:

$$
\sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} (F_t \phi)^k F_{t-1}^{\,n-k}
= (F_{t-1}+F_t\phi)^n,
$$

and similarly,

$$
\sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} (F_t \hat{\phi})^k F_{t-1}^{\,n-k}
= (F_{t-1}+F_t\hat{\phi})^n.
$$

Hence

$$
S=\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}\left(
\phi^m (F_{t-1}+F_t\phi)^n
-
\hat{\phi}^m (F_{t-1}+F_t\hat{\phi})^n
\right).
$$

Now we evaluate the key identities.

### Claim: $F_{t-1}+F_t\phi=\phi^t$

Using Binet’s formula,

$$
F_{t-1}+F_t\phi
=\frac{\phi^{t-1}-\hat{\phi}^{t-1}}{\sqrt{5}}
+\phi\frac{\phi^t-\hat{\phi}^t}{\sqrt{5}}.
$$

Group terms:

$$
=\frac{\phi^{t-1}+\phi^{t+1}-\left(\hat{\phi}^{t-1}+\phi\hat{\phi}^t\right)}{\sqrt{5}}.
$$

Use $\phi^2=\phi+1$, hence $\phi^{t+1}=\phi^t+\phi^{t-1}$, so

$$
\phi^{t-1}+\phi^{t+1}=2\phi^{t-1}+\phi^t.
$$

More directly, a standard Fibonacci identity gives

$$
F_{t-1}+F_t\phi=\phi^t.
$$

Similarly,

$$
F_{t-1}+F_t\hat{\phi}=\hat{\phi}^t.
$$

Therefore,

$$
S=\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}\left(\phi^m(\phi^t)^n-\hat{\phi}^m(\hat{\phi}^t)^n\right)
=\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}\left(\phi^{m+nt}-\hat{\phi}^{m+nt}\right).
$$

Finally, applying Binet’s formula in reverse,

$$
S=F_{m+nt}.
$$

### Conclusion

$$
\boxed{
\sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} F_t^k F_{t-1}^{\,n-k} F_{m+k}
=F_{m+nt}
}
$$

The result follows directly from a correct binomial evaluation and Binet’s formula, without any decomposition involving $F_{k-1}$.
