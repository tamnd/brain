[Section 1.2.8: Fibonacci Numbers](../)

**Exercise 33.** [*HM24*] Given that $z=\pi/2 + i\ln\phi$, show that $\sin nz/\sin z = i^{\,1-n}F_n$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m56s  

---

## Solution

Write

$$
z=\frac{\pi}{2}+i\ln\phi .
$$

From Euler’s formula,

$$
\sin t=\frac{e^{it}-e^{-it}}{2i}.
$$

### Step 1: compute $e^{iz}$ and $e^{-iz}$

$$
e^{iz}=e^{i\pi/2-\ln\phi}=e^{i\pi/2}\,e^{-\ln\phi}=i\phi^{-1},
$$

since $e^{i\pi/2}=i$ and $e^{-\ln\phi}=\phi^{-1}$.

Similarly,

$$
e^{-iz}=e^{-i\pi/2+\ln\phi}=e^{-i\pi/2}\,e^{\ln\phi}=(-i)\phi,
$$

since $e^{-i\pi/2}=-i$ and $e^{\ln\phi}=\phi$.

### Step 2: expression for $\sin z$

$$
\sin z=\frac{e^{iz}-e^{-iz}}{2i}
=\frac{i\phi^{-1}-(-i\phi)}{2i}
=\frac{i(\phi+\phi^{-1})}{2i}
=\frac{\phi+\phi^{-1}}{2}.
$$

Using $\phi^2=\phi+1$, one obtains $\phi+\phi^{-1}=\sqrt{5}$, hence

$$
\sin z=\frac{\sqrt{5}}{2}.
$$

### Step 3: expression for $\sin(nz)$

$$
e^{inz}=(e^{iz})^n=(i\phi^{-1})^n=i^n\phi^{-n},
\qquad
e^{-inz}=(e^{-iz})^n=(-i\phi)^n=(-1)^n i^n \phi^n.
$$

Thus

$$
\sin(nz)=\frac{i^n\phi^{-n}-(-1)^n i^n\phi^n}{2i}
=\frac{i^{n-1}}{2}\left(\phi^{-n}-(-1)^n\phi^n\right).
$$

### Step 4: form the ratio

$$
\frac{\sin(nz)}{\sin z}
=\frac{i^{n-1}\left(\phi^{-n}-(-1)^n\phi^n\right)}{\sqrt{5}}.
$$

Factor a minus sign:

$$
\phi^{-n}-(-1)^n\phi^n
=-\left(\phi^n-(-1)^n\phi^{-n}\right).
$$

Hence

$$
\frac{\sin(nz)}{\sin z}
=-\frac{i^{n-1}}{\sqrt{5}}\left(\phi^n-(-1)^n\phi^{-n}\right).
$$

Using Binet’s formula from Eq. (14),

$$
F_n=\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}\left(\phi^n-(-1)^n\phi^{-n}\right),
$$

so

$$
\frac{\sin(nz)}{\sin z}=-i^{n-1}F_n.
$$

Finally,

$$
-i^{n-1}=i^{1-n},
$$

therefore

$$
\frac{\sin(nz)}{\sin z}=i^{1-n}F_n.
$$

$$
\boxed{\frac{\sin(nz)}{\sin z}=i^{1-n}F_n}
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
