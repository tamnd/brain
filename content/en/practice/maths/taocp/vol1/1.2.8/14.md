[Section 1.2.8: Fibonacci Numbers](../)

**Exercise 14.** [*M28*] Let $m$ be a fixed positive integer. Find $a_n$, given that

$$
a_0 = 0, \qquad a_1 = 1, \qquad a_{n+2} = a_{n+1} + a_n + \binom{n}{m}, \quad n \ge 0.
$$

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m11s  

---

Let $F_n$ be the Fibonacci numbers:

$$
F_0=0,\quad F_1=1,\quad F_{n+2}=F_{n+1}+F_n.
$$

We solve

$$
a_0=0,\quad a_1=1,\quad a_{n+2}=a_{n+1}+a_n+\binom{n}{m}.
$$

## 1. Decomposition

Write

$$
a_n = F_n + b_n.
$$

Then $b_0=b_1=0$, and substituting into the recurrence gives

$$
F_{n+2}+b_{n+2} = (F_{n+1}+b_{n+1}) + (F_n+b_n) + \binom{n}{m}.
$$

Using $F_{n+2}=F_{n+1}+F_n$, we obtain the inhomogeneous recurrence

$$
b_{n+2}=b_{n+1}+b_n+\binom{n}{m},\quad b_0=b_1=0.
$$

So it suffices to solve for $b_n$.

## 2. Standard Fibonacci convolution lemma

Let $f_n$ be any sequence and define $b_n$ by

$$
b_0=b_1=0,\quad b_{n+2}=b_{n+1}+b_n+f_n.
$$

Then for all $n\ge 2$,

$$
b_n = \sum_{k=0}^{n-2} F_{n-1-k}\, f_k.
$$

### Proof

Define

$$
B_n = \sum_{k=0}^{n-2} F_{n-1-k} f_k \quad (n\ge 2),
$$

and set $B_0=B_1=0$.

Check the recurrence for $n\ge 0$:

$$
B_{n+2} = \sum_{k=0}^{n} F_{n+1-k} f_k.
$$

Split off the last term:

$$
B_{n+2} = \sum_{k=0}^{n-1} F_{n+1-k} f_k + f_n.
$$

Use the Fibonacci identity $F_{n+1-k}=F_{n-k}+F_{n-1-k}$:

$$
B_{n+2}
= \sum_{k=0}^{n-1} F_{n-k} f_k + \sum_{k=0}^{n-1} F_{n-1-k} f_k + f_n.
$$

Now identify the sums:

- Since $F_0=0$,

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n-1} F_{n-k} f_k = \sum_{k=0}^{n-2} F_{n-k} f_k = B_{n+1}.
$$

- And

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n-1} F_{n-1-k} f_k = B_n.
$$

Hence

$$
B_{n+2}=B_{n+1}+B_n+f_n.
$$

Also $B_0=B_1=0$. Therefore $B_n=b_n$ by uniqueness of solutions of the recurrence.

## 3. Apply $f_k=\binom{k}{m}$

Substitute $f_k=\binom{k}{m}$ into the lemma:

$$
b_n = \sum_{k=0}^{n-2} F_{n-1-k}\binom{k}{m}.
$$

## 4. Final expression for $a_n$

Since $a_n = F_n + b_n$,

$$
a_n = F_n + \sum_{k=0}^{n-2} F_{n-1-k}\binom{k}{m}.
$$

Equivalently, reindexing $j=n-1-k$,

$$
a_n = F_n + \sum_{j=1}^{n-1} F_j \binom{n-1-j}{m}.
$$

## Final answer

$$
\boxed{
a_n = F_n + \sum_{k=0}^{n-2} F_{n-1-k}\binom{k}{m}
}
$$

This satisfies the initial conditions and the recurrence for all $n\ge 0$.
