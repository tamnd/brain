[Section 1.2.8: Fibonacci Numbers](../)

**Exercise 20.** [*M16*] Express $\sum_{k=0}^{n} F_k$ in terms of Fibonacci numbers.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m  

---

## Solution

Let

$$
S_n = \sum_{k=0}^{n} F_k.
$$

Define

$$
T_n = F_{n+2} - 1.
$$

The Fibonacci recurrence $F_{n+2} = F_{n+1} + F_n$ gives

$$
T_n = F_{n+1} + F_n - 1 = (F_{n+1} - 1) + F_n,
$$

so

$$
T_n - T_{n-1} = F_n \quad \text{for } n \ge 1,
$$

since

$$
T_{n-1} = F_{n+1} - 1.
$$

For the partial sums,

$$
S_n = S_{n-1} + F_n \quad \text{for } n \ge 1.
$$

Thus both sequences $\langle S_n \rangle$ and $\langle T_n \rangle$ satisfy the same first-order recurrence with the same increment $F_n$.

For initial values,

$$
S_0 = F_0 = 0,
$$

and

$$
T_0 = F_2 - 1 = (F_1 + F_0) - 1 = 1 - 1 = 0.
$$

Since $S_0 = T_0$ and both sequences satisfy $X_n = X_{n-1} + F_n$, induction on $n$ gives $S_n = T_n$ for all $n \ge 0$.

Therefore,

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n} F_k = F_{n+2} - 1.
$$

$$
\boxed{F_{n+2} - 1}
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
