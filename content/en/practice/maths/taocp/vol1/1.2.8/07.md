**Exercise 7.** [*15*] If $n$ is not a prime number, $F_n$ is not a prime number (with one exception). Prove this and find the exception.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m37s  

---

We use two standard facts about Fibonacci numbers.

**Lemma 1.** If $m \mid n$, then $F_m \mid F_n$.

_Proof._ Write $n = km$. Using the identity

$$
F_{r+s} = F_{r+1}F_s + F_r F_{s-1},
$$

one proves by induction on $k$ that $F_m \mid F_{km}$. Hence $F_m \mid F_n$. ∎

Now consider a composite integer $n$. Then $n = ab$ with integers $a,b > 1$.

- If $a = b = 2$, then $n = 4$.
- Otherwise, at least one of $a$ or $b$ is $\ge 3$. Let $d$ be such a divisor of $n$, so $d \mid n$ and $d \ge 3$.

By Lemma 1,

$$
F_d \mid F_n.
$$

Now observe that for every $d \ge 3$,

$$
F_d \ge 2,
$$

indeed $F_3 = 2$, $F_4 = 3$, and the Fibonacci sequence is increasing thereafter. Also $d < n$, so $F_d < F_n$.

Thus $F_d$ is a nontrivial divisor of $F_n$, and $F_n$ cannot be prime.

It remains to check the excluded case $n = 4$. We have

$$
F_4 = 3,
$$

which is prime even though $4$ is not prime.

**Conclusion.** If $n$ is composite and $n \ne 4$, then $F_n$ is composite. The unique exception is

$$
n = 4,
$$

for which $F_4 = 3$ is prime.
