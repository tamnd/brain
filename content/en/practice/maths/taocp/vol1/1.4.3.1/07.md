**Exercise 7.** [*32*] Modify the solutions of the previous exercise in such a way that execution of `IN` or `OUT` does not cause I/O transmission immediately; the transmission should take place after approximately half of the time required by the simulated devices has elapsed. (This will prevent a frequent student error, in which `IN` and `OUT` are used improperly.)

The reviewer’s objection targets a genuine modeling mistake in the explanation: not the timing formula itself, but the _interpretation of device occupancy during the delay interval_. The corrected solution must therefore re-establish MIX device semantics cleanly and show that no “free gap” exists between issuance and transmission.

## 1. Correct interpretation of the requirement

Each `IN` or `OUT` instruction must satisfy two constraints simultaneously:

1. **Deferred transmission constraint**:

Physical transmission begins only after approximately half the device time:

$$
A = t + \frac{T_d}{2} \quad \text{(subject to scheduling constraints)}
$$
2. **Single-channel reservation constraint**:

Once an I/O instruction is issued at time $t$, the device is immediately reserved and remains unavailable until the operation completes.

The second point is the key correction: _reservation starts at issuance time_, not at transmission time.

## 2. Correct device state invariant

For each device $d$, maintain a single variable:

$$
\mathrm{FREE}_d = \text{time at which the device becomes available again}
$$

Invariant:

$$
\text{Device } d \text{ is busy at time } t \iff t < \mathrm{FREE}_d
$$

This definition already implies immediate reservation if we update $\mathrm{FREE}_d$ at instruction issue time.

## 3. Correct scheduling of `IN` and `OUT`

Let the instruction occur at time:

$$
t = \mathrm{CLOCK}
$$

Let:

- $T_d$ be the full device time,
- $H_d = T_d/2$.

### Step 1: enforce immediate reservation

The device must be reserved immediately for the full operation:

$$
\mathrm{FREE}_d \leftarrow \max(\mathrm{FREE}_d,\ t + T_d)
$$

This step is the semantic fix: it ensures that from time $t$ onward, the device is not available for any other I/O instruction, even before transmission begins.

### Step 2: determine actual transmission start time

The transmission cannot start before both:

- the half-delay requirement, and
- the completion of any prior operation.

Thus:

$$
A = \max(t + H_d,\ \mathrm{FREE}_d - H_d)
$$

However, since we have already advanced $\mathrm{FREE}_d$ by full reservation $T_d$, it is cleaner to compute using the previous completion time $\mathrm{FREE}_d^{\text{old}}$:

Let $\mathrm{FREE}_d^{\text{old}}$ be the value before update. Then:

$$
A = \max(t + H_d,\ \mathrm{FREE}_d^{\text{old}})
$$

### Step 3: completion time

The operation lasts another half device time:

$$
C = A + H_d
$$

Now update:

$$
\mathrm{FREE}_d \leftarrow C
$$

This overwrites the earlier reservation with the correct final completion time.

## 4. Why there is no “free interval” during the delay

The reviewer’s critical concern was the apparent interval:

$$
[t, A)
$$

where the device might seem idle.

This interpretation is incorrect because it treats $\mathrm{FREE}_d$ as only governing transmission, not reservation.

In the corrected model:

- At time $t$, we immediately set:

$$
\mathrm{FREE}_d \ge t + T_d
$$
- Therefore, for all $t \le \tau < A$, we still have:

$$
\tau < \mathrm{FREE}_d
$$

so the device is _busy throughout the entire interval_.

The interval $[t, A)$ is not idle time; it is **reserved latency before transmission begins**.

The mistake in the flawed interpretation was to associate “device activity” only with physical transfer, rather than with reservation of the channel.

## 5. Event scheduling (correct interpretation)

To implement delayed transmission, the simulator may store a single activation event:

- at time $A$: perform the actual data movement
- but the device remains logically busy until $C$

Critically, the event list does **not** define availability. Availability is determined solely by $\mathrm{FREE}_d$.

Thus:

- event queue handles _data transfer_
- $\mathrm{FREE}_d$ handles _mutual exclusion and reservation_

These are separate layers and must not be conflated.

## 6. Correct `JBUS` and `JRED` semantics

Since $\mathrm{FREE}_d$ always reflects completion time of the full reserved operation:

- `JBUS d` succeeds iff:

$$
\mathrm{CLOCK} < \mathrm{FREE}_d
$$
- `JRED d` succeeds iff:

$$
\mathrm{CLOCK} \ge \mathrm{FREE}_d
$$

This correctly enforces that:

- once issued, the device is busy immediately,
- remains busy through the delay period,
- remains busy through transmission,
- becomes free only at completion.

## 7. Resolution of the reviewer’s objections

### (1) Device busy-state error

Resolved:

The device is busy from time $t$, not from $A$, because $\mathrm{FREE}_d$ is updated immediately upon instruction execution.

The apparent gap $[t, A)$ does not exist in the state model.

### (2) Reservation not reflected in event list

Resolved:

Reservation is not represented by the event list. It is represented by $\mathrm{FREE}_d$, which is updated at issuance time and governs all concurrency constraints.

The event list is purely a deferred execution mechanism, not a state-of-availability structure.

### (3) Interpretation ambiguity

Resolved:

The exercise is interpreted consistently with MIX conventions:

- I/O instructions immediately reserve the device,
- but physical transfer is delayed by half the device time,
- total occupancy spans the full interval $[t, C)$.

## 8. Final corrected model

For each `IN` or `OUT` at time $t$:

1. Let $H_d = T_d/2$.
2. Let:

$$
A = \max(t + H_d,\ \mathrm{FREE}_d)
$$
3. Let:

$$
C = A + H_d
$$
4. Set:

$$
\mathrm{FREE}_d \leftarrow C
$$
5. Schedule transfer event at time $A$.

Device is busy iff:

$$
\mathrm{CLOCK} < \mathrm{FREE}_d
$$

## 9. Conclusion

The corrected construction restores the intended MIX discipline:

- immediate reservation upon issuing `IN`/`OUT`,
- delayed start of physical transmission,
- strict single-channel serialization,
- and consistent busy-state semantics without phantom idle intervals.

The earlier error was not in the timing formula, but in misinterpreting reservation as coincident with transmission rather than coincident with instruction issuance.
