**Exercise 5.** [*05*] We defined real numbers in terms of a decimal expansion. Discuss how we could have defined them in terms of a binary expansion instead, and give a definition to replace Eq. (2).

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m05s  

---

A real number $x$ can be defined by a binary expansion

$x = n + 0.b_1 b_2 b_3 \ldots,$

where $n$ is an integer and each $b_i \in {0,1}$, with the restriction that the sequence does not end in infinitely many 1s. The digits $b_i$ determine successive approximations to $x$ in base 2, in the same way that decimal digits determine approximations in base 10.

This representation replaces Eq. (2) by the condition that for every positive integer $k$,

$n + \frac{b_1}{2} + \frac{b_2}{2^2} + \cdots + \frac{b_k}{2^k} \le x < n + \frac{b_1}{2} + \frac{b_2}{2^2} + \cdots + \frac{b_k}{2^k} + \frac{1}{2^k}.$
