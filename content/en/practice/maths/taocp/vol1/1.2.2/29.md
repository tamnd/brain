**Exercise 29.** [*HM20*] Let $x$ be a real number greater than 1. (a) For what real number $b > 1$ is $b \log_b x$ a minimum? (b) For what *integer* $b > 1$ is it a minimum? (c) For what integer $b > 1$ is $(b + 1) \log_b x$ a minimum?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m20s  

---

## Solution

### (a)

For $b>1$,

$b \log_b x = b \frac{\ln x}{\ln b} = (\ln x)\frac{b}{\ln b}.$

Since $x>1$, the factor $\ln x$ is positive and independent of $b$, so minimizing $b \log_b x$ is equivalent to minimizing

$g(b)=\frac{b}{\ln b}, \quad b>1.$

Differentiate:

=\frac{\ln b - 1}{(\ln b)^2}.$$
The denominator is positive for $b>1$, so the sign of $g'(b)$ is determined by $\ln b - 1$. The equation $g'(b)=0$ gives $\ln b=1$, hence $b=e$. For $1<b<e$, $\ln b<1$ so $g'(b)<0$, and for $b>e$, $\ln b>1$ so $g'(b)>0$. Therefore $g(b)$ attains its minimum at $b=e$. Thus the minimum occurs at
$$\boxed{b=e}.$$
--- ### (b) For integer $b>1$, minimizing $b \log_b x$ is equivalent to minimizing
$$g(b)=\frac{b}{\ln b}.$$
The continuous minimum occurs at $b=e$, so the minimizing integer must be among the integers nearest to $e$, namely $b=2$ and $b=3$. Compute:
$$g(2)=\frac{2}{\ln 2}, \qquad g(3)=\frac{3}{\ln 3}.$$
Using $\ln 2 \approx 0.6931$ and $\ln 3 \approx 1.0986$,
$$g(2)\approx 2.885,\qquad g(3)\approx 2.730.$$
Thus $g(3)<g(2)$. For $b\ge 4$, the function $g(b)$ is increasing for $b>e$, so no larger integer yields a smaller value. Therefore the minimizing integer is
$$\boxed{b=3}.$$
--- ### (c) For integer $b>1$, minimize
$$(b+1)\log_b x = (\ln x)\frac{b+1}{\ln b}.$$
Since $\ln x>0$ is constant, it suffices to minimize
$$h(b)=\frac{b+1}{\ln b}, \quad b>1.$$
Compute values at relevant integers:
$$h(2)=\frac{3}{\ln 2}\approx 4.33,$$ $$h(3)=\frac{4}{\ln 3}\approx 3.64,$$ $$h(4)=\frac{5}{\ln 4}\approx \frac{5}{1.386}\approx 3.61,$$ $$h(5)=\frac{6}{\ln 5}\approx 3.73.$$
Thus the minimum among candidates is either $b=3$ or $b=4$, and the computed values give $h(4)<h(3)$. To exclude larger integers, consider the continuous extension
$$h'(b)=\frac{(\ln b)\cdot 1 - (b+1)\cdot \frac{1}{b}}{(\ln b)^2} =\frac{\ln b - 1 - \frac{1}{b}}{(\ln b)^2}.$$
The denominator is positive for $b>1$. Define
$$\phi(b)=\ln b - 1 - \frac{1}{b}.$$
Then
$$\phi(3)=\ln 3 - 1 - \frac{1}{3} \approx 1.0986 - 1.333 < 0,$$ $$\phi(4)=\ln 4 - 1 - \frac{1}{4} \approx 1.386 - 1.25 > 0.$$
Hence $h'(b)<0$ at $b=3$ and $h'(b)>0$ at $b=4$, so the continuous function decreases through $b=3$ and increases after $b=4$. The only integer where the minimum can occur is therefore $b=4$, and direct comparison confirms it is smaller than $b=3$. Thus the minimizing integer is
$$\boxed{b=4}.$$
