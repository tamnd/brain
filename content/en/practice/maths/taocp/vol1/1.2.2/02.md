**Exercise 2.** [*00*] Is $1 + 0.239999999\ldots$ a decimal expansion?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m05s  

---

The expression $1 + 0.239999999\ldots$ equals the real number whose fractional part is $0.239999999\ldots$, hence it represents $1.239999999\ldots$.

By the definition of a decimal expansion in Eq. (1), the digit sequence $d_1 d_2 d_3 \ldots$ is not allowed to end with infinitely many 9s. The fractional part $0.239999999\ldots$ has $d_k = 9$ for all sufficiently large $k$, so it violates this condition.

Thus $1 + 0.239999999\ldots$ is not a valid decimal expansion in the sense of the definition, although it denotes the same real number as $1.240000000\ldots$. ∎
