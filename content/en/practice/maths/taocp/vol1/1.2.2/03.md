**Exercise 3.** [*02*] What is $(-3)^{-3}$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 57s  

---

Let $b^n$ be defined for integer $n$ by the rules in (4). For $n=-3$ and $b=-3$,

$$
(-3)^{-3} = \frac{1}{(-3)^3}.
$$

Since $(-3)^3 = (-3)(-3)(-3) = 9 \cdot (-3) = -27$, we obtain

$$
(-3)^{-3} = \frac{1}{-27} = -\frac{1}{27}.
$$

$$
\boxed{-\frac{1}{27}}
$$
