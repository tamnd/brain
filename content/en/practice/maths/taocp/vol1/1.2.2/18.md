[Section 1.2.2: Numbers, Powers, and Logarithms](../)

**Exercise 18.** [*10*] Prove or disprove: $\log_8 x = \frac{1}{2} \lg x$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 57s  

---

Using the change of base formula (14) with base $2$,

$$
\log_8 x = \frac{\log_2 x}{\log_2 8}.
$$

Since $8 = 2^3$, the laws of exponents give $\log_2 8 = 3$, hence

$$
\log_8 x = \frac{\lg x}{3}.
$$

The proposed identity $\log_8 x = \frac{1}{2}\lg x$ is therefore false, since it contradicts the derived relation $\log_8 x = \frac{1}{3}\lg x$ for all $x>0$. This completes the proof. ∎
