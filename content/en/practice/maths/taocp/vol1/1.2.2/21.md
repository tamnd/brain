**Exercise 21.** [*15*] (*Logs of logs*.) Express $\log_b \log_b x$ in terms of $\ln \ln x$, $\ln \ln b$, and $\ln b$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m04s  

---

## Solution

Assume $b>0$, $b\ne 1$, and $x>1$ so that all logarithms and iterated logarithms are defined.

From the definition of natural logarithms, equation (15), and the change-of-base formula, equation (14),

$$
\log_b x = \frac{\ln x}{\ln b}.
$$

Applying $\log_b$ again,

$$
\log_b \log_b x = \frac{\ln(\log_b x)}{\ln b}.
$$

Substituting $\log_b x = \frac{\ln x}{\ln b}$ gives

$$
\log_b \log_b x = \frac{\ln\!\left(\frac{\ln x}{\ln b}\right)}{\ln b}.
$$

Using the law $\ln\left(\frac{u}{v}\right)=\ln u-\ln v$,

$$
\ln\!\left(\frac{\ln x}{\ln b}\right) = \ln \ln x - \ln \ln b.
$$

Substituting this into the previous expression yields

$$
\log_b \log_b x = \frac{\ln \ln x - \ln \ln b}{\ln b}.
$$

Thus,

$$
\boxed{\log_b \log_b x = \frac{\ln \ln x - \ln \ln b}{\ln b}}.
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
