[Section 1.2.2: Numbers, Powers, and Logarithms](../)

**Exercise 17.** &#9654; [*05*] What is $\lg 32$? $\log_\pi \pi$? $\ln e$? $\log_b 1$? $\log_b(-1)$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 55s  

---

$\lg 32 = \log_2 32 = 5$, because $2^5 = 32$.

$\log_\pi \pi = 1$, because $\pi^1 = \pi$.

$\ln e = \log_e e = 1$, because $e^1 = e$.

For $b > 0$ with $b \ne 1$, $\log_b 1 = 0$, because $b^0 = 1$. The expression $\log_b(-1)$ has no value in the real-number system, as logarithms are defined only for positive real arguments in this section.
