[Section 1.2.2: Numbers, Powers, and Logarithms](../)

**Exercise 16.** [*00*] How can $\log_{10} x$ be expressed in terms of $\ln x$ and $\ln 10$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m16s  

---

From equation (14), with $b = e$ and $c = 10$, one obtains

$\log_{10} x = \frac{\log_e x}{\log_e 10}.$

By definition (15), $\log_e x = \ln x$ and $\log_e 10 = \ln 10$, hence

$\log_{10} x = \frac{\ln x}{\ln 10}.$

Thus $\log_{10} x$ is expressed in terms of natural logarithms as $\displaystyle \boxed{\frac{\ln x}{\ln 10}}$. ∎
