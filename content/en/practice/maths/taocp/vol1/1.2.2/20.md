[Section 1.2.2: Numbers, Powers, and Logarithms](../)

**Exercise 20.** [*10*] Is there any simple relation between $\log_{10} 2$ and $\log_2 10$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 53s  

---

From equation (14), taking base $b=2$ and $c=10$, one obtains

$\log_{10} 2 = \frac{\log_2 2}{\log_2 10}.$

Since $\log_2 2 = 1$ by equation (9), this simplifies to

$\log_{10} 2 = \frac{1}{\log_2 10}.$

Equivalently, solving for $\log_2 10$ gives

$\log_2 10 = \frac{1}{\log_{10} 2}.$

Thus $\log_{10} 2$ and $\log_2 10$ are reciprocals of each other:

$\boxed{\log_{10} 2 = \frac{1}{\log_2 10}}.$
