**Exercise 10.** [*18*] Prove that $\log_{10} 2$ is not a rational number.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m10s  

---

## Solution

Assume that $\log_{10} 2$ is rational. Then there exist integers $p$ and $q$ with $q > 0$ such that

$$
\log_{10} 2 = \frac{p}{q}.
$$

By definition of logarithms,

$$
10^{p/q} = 2.
$$

Raising both sides to the $q$th power yields

$$
10^p = 2^q.
$$

Using $10 = 2 \cdot 5$, we obtain

$$
(2 \cdot 5)^p = 2^q,
$$

so

$$
2^p 5^p = 2^q.
$$

Dividing both sides by $2^p$ gives

$$
5^p = 2^{q-p}.
$$

The left-hand side is a power of $5$ and the right-hand side is a power of $2$. Since $p > 0$, the number $5^p$ has prime factor $5$, while $2^{q-p}$ has no factor $5$. Equality is impossible unless $p = 0$, which contradicts $q > 0$ and $\log_{10} 2 > 0$.

No integers $p, q$ satisfy $\log_{10} 2 = p/q$.

This completes the proof. ∎
