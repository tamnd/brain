**Exercise 22.** &#9654; [*20*] (R. W. Hamming.) Prove that

$$\lg x \approx \ln x + \log_{10} x,$$

with less than 1% error! (Thus a table of natural logarithms and of common logarithms can be used to get approximate values of binary logarithms as well.)

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m07s  

---

## Solution

For $x>0$, the binary, natural, and common logarithms are related by change of base:

$\lg x = \frac{\ln x}{\ln 2}, \qquad \log_{10} x = \frac{\ln x}{\ln 10}.$

Hence

$\ln x + \log_{10} x = \ln x \left(1 + \frac{1}{\ln 10}\right).$

We compare this expression with $\lg x$. Both are proportional to $\ln x$, so the relative error is independent of $x$ and depends only on the constants.

Define

$A = \frac{1}{\ln 2}, \qquad B = 1 + \frac{1}{\ln 10}.$

Then

$\lg x = A \ln x, \qquad \ln x + \log_{10} x = B \ln x.$

The relative error of using $B \ln x$ instead of $A \ln x$ is

$\frac{|A-B|}{A}.$

We compute the constants using $\ln 2 \approx 0.693147$, $\ln 10 \approx 2.302585$. Then

$A \approx \frac{1}{0.693147} \approx 1.442695,$

$\frac{1}{\ln 10} \approx 0.434294,$

$B \approx 1.434294.$

Thus

$|A-B| \approx 0.008401,$

and

$\frac{|A-B|}{A} \approx \frac{0.008401}{1.442695} \approx 0.00582.$

Since $0.00582 < 0.01$, the relative error is less than $1%$. Therefore

$\lg x \approx \ln x + \log_{10} x$

with error under $1%$ for all $x>0$.

This completes the proof. ∎
