**Exercise 30.** [*12*] Simplify the expression $(\ln x)^{\ln x / \ln \ln x}$, assuming that $x > 1$ and $x \ne e$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m05s  

---

## Solution

Let $t = \ln x$. Since $x > 1$, we have $t > 0$. The condition $x \ne e$ implies $t \ne 1$, so $\ln t = \ln \ln x$ is defined and nonzero.

The expression becomes

$$
(\ln x)^{\ln x / \ln \ln x} = t^{t / \ln t}.
$$

Using the identity $a^b = \exp(b \ln a)$ for $a > 0$, we obtain

$$
t^{t / \ln t} = \exp\!\left(\frac{t}{\ln t} \cdot \ln t\right).
$$

The factor $\ln t$ cancels, yielding

$$
\exp(t).
$$

Substituting back $t = \ln x$ gives

$$
\exp(\ln x) = x.
$$

Hence the simplified value is

$$
\boxed{x}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
