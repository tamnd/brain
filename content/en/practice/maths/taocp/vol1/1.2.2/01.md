**Exercise 1.** [*00*] What is the smallest positive rational number?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m19s  

---

Let $r$ be a positive rational number. Then $r/2$ is also a rational number, $r/2 > 0$, and $r/2 < r$ since multiplication by $1/2$ preserves order for positive reals. Thus every positive rational number admits a strictly smaller positive rational number.

No positive rational number can satisfy the defining property of being the smallest element of the set of positive rationals, since any candidate $r$ is exceeded by $r/2$ in the reverse order. Therefore the set of positive rational numbers has no smallest element. ∎
