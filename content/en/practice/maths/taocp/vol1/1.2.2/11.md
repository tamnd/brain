**Exercise 11.** &#9654; [*10*] If $b = 10$ and $x \approx \log_{10} 2$, to how many decimal places of accuracy will we need to know the value of $x$ in order to determine the first three decimal places of the decimal expansion of $b^x$? [*Note:* You may use the result of exercise 10 in your discussion.]

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m41s  

---

Let $y = 10^x$. Since $x = \log_{10} 2$, we have $y = 2$.

To determine the first three decimal places of $y$, it suffices to ensure that $y$ lies in the interval $[1.9995,, 2.0005]$, since any number in this interval has decimal expansion beginning with $2.000\ldots$.

Let $x + \delta$ be the available approximation to $x$. Then

$$
10^{x+\delta} - 10^x = 10^x\left(10^\delta - 1\right).
$$

By the mean value theorem applied to $10^t$, there exists $\xi$ between $0$ and $\delta$ such that

$$
10^\delta - 1 = (\ln 10)\,10^\xi \delta.
$$

Hence

$$
|10^{x+\delta} - 2| = 2 (\ln 10)\,10^\xi |\delta|.
$$

For sufficiently small $\delta$ (in particular for the accuracy required here), $10^\xi$ is close to $1$, so the error satisfies

$$
|10^{x+\delta} - 2| \approx 2(\ln 10)|\delta|.
$$

To force this error below $5 \cdot 10^{-4}$, it is sufficient that

$$
2(\ln 10)|\delta| < 5 \cdot 10^{-4},
$$

so

$$
|\delta| < \frac{5 \cdot 10^{-4}}{2 \ln 10} < 1.1 \cdot 10^{-4}.
$$

An accuracy of $10^{-4}$ in $x$ corresponds to four decimal places. Therefore, four decimal places of accuracy in $x$ suffice to determine the first three decimal places of $10^x$.

$$
\boxed{4\ \text{decimal places}}
$$
