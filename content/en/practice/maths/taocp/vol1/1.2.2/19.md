**Exercise 19.** &#9654; [*20*] If $n$ is an integer whose decimal representation is 14 digits long, will the value of $n$ fit in a computer word with a capacity of 47 bits and a sign bit?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m  

---

## Solution

A 14-digit decimal integer $n$ satisfies

$10^{13} \le n \le 10^{14} - 1.$

A computer word with 47 bits for magnitude and one sign bit can represent integers in the range

$- (2^{47} - 1) \le n \le 2^{47} - 1.$

It suffices to compare the upper bound $10^{14} - 1$ with $2^{47} - 1$. It is enough to show

$10^{14} < 2^{47}.$

Using standard power estimates,

$2^{10} = 1024 > 10^3.$

Raising both sides to the fourth power gives

$2^{40} > 10^{12}.$

Also,

$2^7 = 128 > 10^2.$

Multiplying these inequalities yields

$2^{47} = 2^{40} \cdot 2^7 > 10^{12} \cdot 10^2 = 10^{14}.$

Hence,

$2^{47} - 1 \ge 10^{14} - 1,$

so every integer with 14 decimal digits lies within the representable range of the 47-bit magnitude field.

Therefore a 14-digit decimal integer does fit in the given computer word.

$\boxed{\text{Yes}}$
