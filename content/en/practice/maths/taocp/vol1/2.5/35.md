[Section 2.5: Dynamic Storage Allocation](../)

**Exercise 35.** [**] [22] Contrast the dynamic storage allocation methods of this section
with the techniques for variable-size sequential lists discussed at the
end of Section 2.2.2.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m31s  

---

The dynamic storage allocation methods of Section 2.5 store records as linked structures in a storage pool. Nodes may occupy arbitrary locations in memory, and growth or shrinkage of a structure requires only the allocation or release of individual nodes. Accessibility is maintained by links, and garbage collection can reclaim storage that is no longer reachable.

The variable-size sequential lists of Section 2.2.2 keep elements in contiguous memory locations. Access by position is direct, but insertion, deletion, or expansion generally requires moving many elements and may require relocating the entire list when contiguous free space is unavailable. Dynamic storage allocation trades contiguous storage and direct indexing for greater flexibility in handling structures whose size changes during computation.
