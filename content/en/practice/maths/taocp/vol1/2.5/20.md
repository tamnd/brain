**Exercise 20.** [**] [00] Why is it desirable to have the `AVAIL[k]` lists in the buddy
system doubly linked instead of simply having straight linear lists?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 6m06s  

---

Doubly linking the `AVAIL[k]` lists in the buddy system allows a block to be removed from the list without traversing from the head. When a block of size $2^k$ is allocated or merged with its buddy, its predecessor and successor pointers immediately identify the adjacent blocks in the list. In contrast, a singly linked list requires scanning from the list head to find the previous block in order to update the links, which can take up to $\Theta(n)$ time for a list of length $n$.

The primary advantage is efficiency: insertion and deletion of blocks from the `AVAIL[k]` list can be done in $\Theta(1)$ time when the list is doubly linked, whereas singly linked lists require $\Theta(n)$ time in the worst case. This efficiency is particularly important in the buddy system because frequent splitting and coalescing of blocks occurs during dynamic storage allocation, and constant-time updates prevent the allocation algorithm from being dominated by list management.
