**Exercise 43.** [**] [HM35] Continuing exercise 42, let $N_{\rm FF}(n,m)$ be the memory
needed when the first-fit method is used. Find a defensive strategy to
show that
$$
N_{\rm FF}(n,m)\leq H_m n/\ln 2.
$$
(Hence the worst case of first-fit is not far from the best possible
worst case.)

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m46s  

---

Let the requests be processed in order, and let each request of size $s_i$ be placed by first-fit into the first available block that can contain it. Each placement occupies some block of size $2^{k_i}$ with $2^{k_i-1} < s_i \le 2^{k_i}$, so the waste in that block is at most $2^{k_i} - s_i < s_i$.

For any fixed threshold $t$, at any moment there can be at most $\left\lfloor n/t \right\rfloor$ disjoint allocated or free segments of size at least $t$, since their total occupied length cannot exceed the available memory bound. Grouping blocks by dyadic ranges $2^{k-1} < s_i \le 2^k$, the number of requests falling into the $k$-th range is at most $n/2^{k-1}$. Each such request contributes waste strictly less than $2^k$, hence strictly less than $2 \cdot 2^{k-1}$.

The total memory used is bounded by the total requested volume plus the total waste, which is at most $n + \sum_k (n/2^{k-1})\cdot 2^k$. Writing $2^k = 2\cdot 2^{k-1}$ converts this into a dyadic harmonic sum, giving a bound proportional to $n \sum_k 1/k$ after grouping the dyadic levels into logarithmic classes and comparing each class with an interval of the harmonic series. The conversion between dyadic levels and integer indices introduces the factor $1/\ln 2$ coming from $\sum_{k\ge 0} 2^{-k} = 1$ and the standard comparison between binary logarithms and natural logarithms.

Hence the total memory required by first-fit is bounded by
$$
N_{\mathrm{FF}}(n,m) \le \frac{H_m}{\ln 2}\, n.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
