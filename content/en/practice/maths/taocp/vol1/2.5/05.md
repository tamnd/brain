**Exercise 5.** [**] [18] Suppose it is known that `N` is always 100 or more in Algorithm
A. Would it be a good idea to set $c=100$ in the modified step A4'?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 59s  

---

Algorithm A is the first-fit search in a free list. The modification “A4′ with threshold $c$” means that the algorithm delays entering the search until the requested size is at least $c$, thereby avoiding unnecessary scans for very small requests.

If it is known that $N \ge 100$ for every request, then choosing $c = 100$ makes A4′ redundant in the sense that the condition is always satisfied and no extra logic is ever triggered. Hence the modified test contributes no additional branching cost in actual execution beyond the original structure of Algorithm A.

Since the sole purpose of introducing $c$ in A4′ is to avoid the cost of attempting allocation for very small values of $N$, and since such cases never occur under the given assumption, increasing $c$ does not improve performance. It adds no asymptotic benefit and no reduction in expected search length.

Therefore setting $c = 100$ is not a good idea in this situation, and the original Algorithm A without modification is preferable.

$$
\boxed{\text{No, it is not a good idea to set } c = 100.}
$$
