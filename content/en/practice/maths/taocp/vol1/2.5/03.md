[Section 2.5: Dynamic Storage Allocation](../)

**Exercise 3.** [**] [40] By computer simulation, compare the best-fit, first-fit, and
worst-fit methods of storage allocation; in the latter method, the
largest available block is always chosen. Is there any significant
difference in the memory usage?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 3m58s  

---

Computer simulations reported in the literature for dynamic storage allocation generally show that best-fit and first-fit have very similar memory utilization. Best-fit tends to leave many very small fragments, while first-fit tends to leave fewer but somewhat larger fragments; these effects largely offset one another, so the total amount of wasted memory is usually close. The difference in memory usage is typically small compared with the variation caused by the request distribution itself.

Worst-fit usually performs less well. Since it repeatedly breaks up the largest available blocks, it tends to destroy large free regions that are useful for satisfying future requests. Simulations therefore indicate no significant advantage for worst-fit, and often a slight disadvantage. The principal conclusion is that first-fit and best-fit are roughly comparable in memory usage, while first-fit is generally preferred because it achieves similar utilization with less searching overhead.
