Modify Algorithm A as follows. In step A1, initialize the `AVAIL` list to reflect the current boundary-tag structure, storing both the size and the preceding- and succeeding-block pointers in each free block. In step A2, when searching for a block of at least `N` cells, follow the improvement of exercise 6 by checking the block size before examining its successors. In step A4, replace the original block-splitting procedure with the boundary-tag convention: let `B` denote the chosen free block, and let `R` denote the remainder after allocating `N` cells. Set `B.size \leftarrow N` and mark it as allocated, then, if the remainder exists, set `R.size \leftarrow |B| - N` and adjust `R`’s boundary tags to point to its neighbors. Insert `R` into the `AVAIL` list in place of `B`, updating the predecessor and successor pointers accordingly. Each assignment to a size field, boundary tag, or pointer counts as one replacement; counting the updates to `B.size`, `R.size`, the allocation mark, and the predecessor and successor pointers gives a total of 5 replacements per split, plus 1 replacement for the initial allocation mark, yielding 6 replacements in the case where a split occurs. If no split occurs, only the allocation mark is updated, giving 1 replacement. This completes the construction. ∎
