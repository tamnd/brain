[Section 2.5: Dynamic Storage Allocation](../)

**Exercise 17.** [**] [10] What should the contents of `LOC(AVAIL)` and `LOC(AVAIL)+1` be
in (9) when there are no available blocks present?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m15s  

---

In formula (9), `AVAIL` serves as the header of the circular list of available blocks. When no available blocks exist, the list must still satisfy the circular-list convention used by Algorithm C. Therefore the header points to itself in both directions.

Hence the contents should be

$\operatorname{LINK}(\operatorname{AVAIL})=\operatorname{LOC}(\operatorname{AVAIL}),$

and

$\operatorname{LINK}(\operatorname{LOC}(\operatorname{AVAIL})+1)=\operatorname{LOC}(\operatorname{AVAIL}).$

In other words, both the forward link and the backward link of the header node contain the address $\operatorname{LOC}(\operatorname{AVAIL})$ itself, representing an empty circular doubly linked list.
