Algorithm A is modified by replacing the representation of blocks and by replacing its search and update steps while preserving its overall allocation and deallocation structure. The memory area is divided into blocks, and each block $B$ with starting address $P$ and length $L$ stores two boundary words. The left boundary word is located at address $P$ and the right boundary word at address $P+L-1$. Each boundary word contains the triple $(P,L,\text{status})$, where status is either free or allocated. From either boundary word the other end of the block is computed in $O(1)$ time by using $P$ and $L$, and adjacent blocks are found by examining the boundary word immediately before $P$ and immediately after $P+L-1$.

The free list `AVAIL` is a doubly linked list of free blocks in increasing address order. Each free block contains two link fields, $LLINK$ and $RLINK$, stored in its boundary-tag area. A pointer $R$ is maintained to a block of `AVAIL`, initially set to the head of `AVAIL` if it is nonempty.

Allocation replaces step A4 by step A4′ with roving search starting from $R$. The search proceeds cyclically through `AVAIL` in increasing address order. From the current block $X$, the next candidate is $RLINK(X)$; when $RLINK(X)$ is null, the search continues from the head of `AVAIL`. The search stops when either a block $X$ with $\text{size}(X)\ge N$ is found or the scan returns to the starting block $R$ without success. If no suitable block exists, allocation fails.

If a suitable block $X$ is found, it is removed from `AVAIL`. If $\text{size}(X)=N$, it is marked allocated and returned. If $\text{size}(X)>N$, the block is split into a front block $X_1$ of size $N$ and a remainder block $X_2$ of size $\text{size}(X)-N$. The block $X_1$ is marked allocated and removed from `AVAIL`. The block $X_2$ inherits the original position of $X$ in address order, is marked free, and is inserted into `AVAIL` by linking it between its predecessor and successor in address order. After this operation, the pointer $R$ is set to $RLINK(X_2)$ if $X_2$ exists, and otherwise to $RLINK(X)$’s former successor, ensuring that the next search begins immediately after the region affected by allocation.

Deallocation of a block $B$ beginning at address $P$ and of size $N$ uses boundary tags to obtain adjacent blocks in constant time. The left neighbor is the block whose right boundary word is at address $P-1$, and the right neighbor is the block whose left boundary word is at address $P+N$. If a neighbor exists and is free, it is removed from `AVAIL` by updating its $LLINK$ and $RLINK$ pointers. Coalescing is then performed by extending the leftmost starting address and the total length of the merged block and by rewriting the boundary words at both ends to reflect the new $(P,L,\text{free})$ descriptor. This merging step is repeated until neither adjacent block is free, producing a maximal free block. The resulting block is then inserted into `AVAIL` in address order by linking it between its predecessor and successor free blocks and updating the corresponding link fields.

These modifications implement boundary-tag storage as in (7)–(9), replace A4 by the cyclic roving-pointer search A4′, and incorporate the improvement of exercise 6 by maintaining and updating the pointer $R$ so that each search begins from the last successful position. This completes the modification. ∎
