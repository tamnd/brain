[Section 2.5: Dynamic Storage Allocation](../)

**Exercise 2.** [**] [HM23] (E. Wolman.) Suppose that we want to choose a fixed node size
for variable-length items, and suppose also that when each node has length
$k$ and when an item has length $l$, it takes $\lceil l/(k-b)\rceil$
nodes to store this item. (Here $b$ is a constant, signifying that $b$
words of each node contain control information, such as a link to the
next node.) If the average length $l$ of an item is $L$, what choice of
$k$ minimizes the average amount of storage space required? (Assume that
the average value of $(l/(k-b))\bmod 1$ is equal to $1/2$, for any fixed
$k$, as $l$ varies.)

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 5m32s  

---

Let

$$
x=\frac{l}{k-b}.
$$

The assumption that the average value of $x\bmod 1$ is $1/2$ implies

$$
\lceil x\rceil=x+\bigl(1-(x\bmod 1)\bigr),
$$

except when $x$ is an integer, a negligible case for the average. Hence the average number of nodes required is approximately

$$
\frac{L}{k-b}+\frac12.
$$

Therefore the average storage occupied by an item is

$$
S(k)=k\left(\frac{L}{k-b}+\frac12\right).
$$

To minimize $S(k)$, write

$$
S(k)=\frac{kL}{k-b}+\frac{k}{2}.
$$

Differentiating with respect to $k$ gives

$$
S'(k)=-\frac{Lb}{(k-b)^2}+\frac12.
$$

The minimum occurs when

$$
\frac12=\frac{Lb}{(k-b)^2,
}
$$

so

$$
(k-b)^2=2Lb,
$$

and therefore

$$
k=b+\sqrt{2Lb}.
$$

Since

$$
S''(k)=\frac{2Lb}{(k-b)^3}>0
\qquad (k>b),
$$

this critical point is the unique minimum. Thus the node size that minimizes the average storage requirement is

$$
\boxed{k=b+\sqrt{2Lb}}.
$$

For integral node lengths, choose the integer nearest to $b+\sqrt{2Lb}$.
