**Exercise 44.** [**] [M21] Suppose the distribution function $F(x)$ = (probability that a
block has size $\leq x$) is continuous. For example, $F(x)$ is
$(x-a)/(b-a)$ for $a\leq x\leq b$ if the sizes are uniformly distributed
between $a$ and $b$. Give a formula that expresses the sizes of the first
$N$ slots that should be set up when we use the distributed-fit method.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m19s  

---

Let $F$ be continuous and strictly increasing on its support, so it admits a generalized inverse $F^{-1}(u)=\inf\{x:F(x)\ge u\}$. In the distributed-fit method the $N$ slots are chosen so that each slot carries equal probability mass $1/N$, hence the cut points $x_k$ satisfy
$$
F(x_k)=\frac{k}{N}, \qquad k=1,2,\ldots,N,
$$
with $x_k=F^{-1}(k/N)$.

The sizes of the first $N$ slots are the successive differences
$$
x_k-x_{k-1}=F^{-1}\!\left(\frac{k}{N}\right)-F^{-1}\!\left(\frac{k-1}{N}\right), \qquad k=1,2,\ldots,N,
$$
where $x_0$ is the left endpoint of the support of $F$, characterized by $F(x_0)=0$. This construction assigns equal probability $1/N$ to each slot, and no other partition achieves this uniform distribution under the continuity assumption. ∎
