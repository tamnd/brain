**Exercise 5.** [**] [M25] (A. Cayley.) Let $c_n$ be the number of (unlabeled) oriented trees having $n$ leaves (namely, vertices with in-degree zero) and having at least two subtrees at every other vertex. Thus $c_3=2$, by virtue of the two trees

![Two oriented trees with three leaves](images/page_0418_two_oriented_leaf_trees.png)

Find a formula analogous to (3) for the generating function

$$
C(z)=\sum_n c_nz^n.
$$

**Solve time:** 4m56s

Let $C(z)=\sum_n c_n z^n$.

A tree counted by $c_n$ is a rooted unlabeled (oriented) tree in which every vertex is either a leaf or has at least two subtrees. In the language of Section 2.3.4.4, this means that at each vertex the multiset of subtrees is allowed to be empty (leaf case) or has size at least $2$.

Let $S(z)=\sum_{k\ge1}\frac{1}{k}C(z^k)$. The Pólya substitution principle for rooted unordered trees gives that the class of all multisets of subtrees has generating function $\exp(S(z))$. This includes the empty multiset (corresponding to a leaf) and singletons (corresponding to vertices with exactly one subtree). The present restriction excludes both of these cases at internal vertices.

Hence the contribution of admissible non-leaf structures at the root is

$$
\exp(S(z)) - 1 - C(z),
$$

where $1$ accounts for the empty multiset and $C(z)$ accounts for the case of exactly one subtree.

Since a tree is either a single vertex (contributing $z$) or has root degree at least $2$, we obtain

$$
C(z)= z + \bigl(\exp(S(z)) - 1 - C(z)\bigr).
$$

Rearranging yields

$$
2C(z)= z + \exp\!\left(\sum_{k\ge1}\frac{1}{k}C(z^k)\right) - 1.
$$

Thus the generating function satisfies

$$
\boxed{
C(z)=\frac{1}{2}\left(z + \exp\!\left(\sum_{k\ge1}\frac{1}{k}C(z^k)\right) - 1\right)
}.
$$

This is the required analogue of equation (3). ∎
