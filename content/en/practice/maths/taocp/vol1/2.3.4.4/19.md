[Section 2.3.4.4: Enumeration of Trees](../)

**Exercise 19.** [**] [M24] How many different labeled, oriented trees are there having $n$ vertices, $k$ of which are leaves (have in-degree zero)?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 5m34s  

---

By the correspondence of equation (16), every labeled oriented tree on ${1,\ldots,n}$ corresponds uniquely to an $(n-1)$-tuple $(x_1,\ldots,x_{n-1})$, where each $x_i$ is an integer between $1$ and $n$. In this correspondence, a vertex is a leaf if and only if its label does not appear in the tuple, since appearances count the incoming arcs of that vertex. Hence a tree has exactly $k$ leaves if and only if exactly $k$ labels from ${1,\ldots,n}$ are absent from $(x_1,\ldots,x_{n-1})$.

Choose the $k$ labels that are to be absent in $\binom{n}{k}$ ways. The remaining $n-k$ labels must all occur at least once among the $n-1$ positions. The number of $(n-1)$-tuples over these $n-k$ labels in which every label appears is, by inclusion-exclusion,

$$
\sum_{j=0}^{n-k}(-1)^j\binom{n-k}{j}(n-k-j)^{\,n-1}.
$$

Therefore the number of labeled, oriented trees having $n$ vertices and exactly $k$ leaves is

$$
\boxed{\binom{n}{k}
\sum_{j=0}^{n-k}
(-1)^j
\binom{n-k}{j}
(n-k-j)^{\,n-1}}.
$$

Equivalently, since the inner sum counts surjections from an $(n-1)$-set onto an $(n-k)$-set,

$$
\boxed{\binom{n}{k}(n-k)!\,S(n-1,n-k)},
$$

where $S(\cdot,\cdot)$ denotes a Stirling number of the second kind. ∎
