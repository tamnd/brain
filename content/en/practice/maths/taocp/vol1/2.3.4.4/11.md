[Section 2.3.4.4: Enumeration of Trees](../)

**Exercise 11.** [**] [M28] The text derives the number of different binary trees with $n$ nodes (14). Generalize this to find the number of different $t$-ary trees with $n$ nodes. (See exercise 2.3.1-35; a $t$-ary tree is either empty or consists of a root and $t$ disjoint $t$-ary trees.) *Hint:* Use Eq. (21) of Section 1.2.9.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m37s  

---

Let $b_n$ denote the number of $t$-ary trees with $n$ nodes, where each node has an ordered sequence of $t$ (possibly empty) disjoint $t$-ary subtrees. Let $b_0=1$ for the empty tree.

For $n\ge 1$, a tree with $n$ nodes consists of a root and an ordered $t$-tuple of subtrees whose total size is $n-1$. If the $j$th subtree has $n_j$ nodes with $n_1+\cdots+n_t=n-1$, then the number of such trees is $b_{n_1}\cdots b_{n_t}$. Summing over all compositions of $n-1$ yields

$$
b_n=\sum_{n_1+\cdots+n_t=n-1} b_{n_1}b_{n_2}\cdots b_{n_t}, \qquad n\ge 1.
$$

Let

$$
B(z)=\sum_{n\ge 0} b_n z^n.
$$

Multiplying the recurrence by $z^n$ and summing over $n\ge 1$ gives

$$
B(z)=1+zB(z)^t.
$$

The coefficient extraction from this functional equation is standard. Applying Lagrange inversion to $B(z)=1+zB(z)^t$ yields

$$
b_n=\frac{1}{(t-1)n+1}\binom{tn}{n}.
$$

Thus the number of $t$-ary trees with $n$ nodes is

$$
\boxed{\frac{1}{(t-1)n+1}\binom{tn}{n}}.
$$
