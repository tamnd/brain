[Section 2.3.4.4: Enumeration of Trees](../)

**Exercise 22.** [**] [M20] How many *labeled free trees* are possible with $n$ vertices? (In other words, if we are given $n$ vertices, there are $2^{\binom{n}{2}}$ possible graphs having these vertices, depending on which of the $\binom{n}{2}$ possible edges are incorporated into the graph; how many of these graphs are free trees?)

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m04s  

---

Let a labeled free tree mean a connected acyclic graph on the vertex set $\{1,2,\dots,n\}$. We count these objects directly.

## Step 1. Prüfer encoding

Given a labeled tree $T$ on $\{1,\dots,n\}$, construct a sequence $P(T)$ of length $n-2$ as follows.

For $i = 1,2,\dots,n-2$:

1. Let $v_i$ be the smallest labeled leaf of the current tree.
2. Let $w_i$ be the unique neighbor of $v_i$.
3. Record $w_i$ as the $i$-th entry of the sequence.
4. Delete $v_i$ and its incident edge.

After $n-2$ steps, two vertices remain and the procedure stops. The output is a sequence

$$
P(T) \in \{1,\dots,n\}^{n-2}.
$$

## Step 2. Prüfer decoding

Given any sequence

$$
(a_1,a_2,\dots,a_{n-2}) \in \{1,\dots,n\}^{n-2},
$$

we reconstruct a tree as follows.

Let $d(v)$ be $1$ plus the number of occurrences of $v$ in the sequence. Formally,

$$
d(v) = 1 + |\{i : a_i = v\}|.
$$

Start with the vertex set $\{1,\dots,n\}$. For $i = 1,2,\dots,n-2$:

1. Let $v_i$ be the smallest vertex with $d(v_i)=1$.
2. Add the edge $(v_i, a_i)$.
3. Decrease $d(v_i)$ and $d(a_i)$ by $1$.

After processing all entries, exactly two vertices remain with degree $1$, and we connect them by an edge.

This produces a tree $T(P)$.

## Step 3. Bijection

We show that the two procedures are inverses.

1. Starting from a tree $T$, the encoding produces a sequence $P(T)$.
2. Applying the decoding procedure to $P(T)$ reconstructs all edges removed during encoding in reverse order, hence returns the original tree.
3. Conversely, starting from any sequence, decoding produces a tree whose encoding recovers the same sequence because the degree bookkeeping matches the leaf removal process uniquely at each step.

Thus, the map

$$
T \longleftrightarrow P(T)
$$

is a bijection between labeled free trees on $n$ vertices and sequences of length $n-2$ over $\{1,\dots,n\}$.

## Step 4. Counting

The number of sequences of length $n-2$ with entries in $\{1,\dots,n\}$ is

$$
n^{n-2}.
$$

By the bijection, the same number counts labeled free trees.

## Conclusion

The number of labeled free trees on $n$ vertices is

$$
\boxed{n^{\,n-2}}.
$$
