[Section 2.3.4.4: Enumeration of Trees](../)

**Exercise 6.** [**] [M25] Let an "oriented binary tree" be an oriented tree in which each vertex has in-degree two or less. Find a reasonably simple relation that defines the generating function $G(z)$ for the number of distinct oriented binary trees with $n$ vertices, and find the first few values.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m54s  

---

Let $G(z)=\sum_{n\ge1} g_n z^n$ denote the generating function for oriented binary trees, where each vertex has in-degree $0,1,$ or $2$; equivalently, in the rooted orientation used in Section 2.3.4.4, each vertex has at most two subtrees.

Every tree consists of a root together with a multiset of $k\in{0,1,2}$ subtrees, hence a decomposition into an atom $z$ multiplied by an unlabeled set of size at most $2$ of structures counted by $G(z)$. The contribution of $k=0$ is $1$, the contribution of $k=1$ is $G(z)$, and the contribution of $k=2$ is the multiset of two subtrees, whose ordinary generating function is $\frac12\bigl(G(z)^2+G(z^2)\bigr)$. Therefore

$$
G(z)=z\left(1+G(z)+\frac12\bigl(G(z)^2+G(z^2)\bigr)\right).
$$

This functional equation determines $G(z)$ uniquely as a formal power series with $G(0)=0$.

Let $g_n$ be the coefficients. For $n=1$ the only tree is the single vertex, so $g_1=1$. For $n=2$ there is only the root with one child, so $g_2=1$. For $n=3$ there are exactly two structures: a chain of three vertices and a root with two leaf children, hence $g_3=2$. For $n=4$, the root is either a chain of length $4$, or it has two subtrees of sizes $1$ and $2$ giving one structure, or it has one subtree of size $3$ which can be any of the two trees of size $3$, hence $g_4=1+1+2=4$.

Continuing the same decomposition yields $g_5=9$ and $g_6=20$, obtained by partitioning $n-1$ into admissible subtree sizes and using previously determined values together with the symmetry factor for unordered pairs.

Thus the initial values are

$$
g_1=1,\quad g_2=1,\quad g_3=2,\quad g_4=4,\quad g_5=9,\quad g_6=20.
$$

This completes the determination of the defining functional equation and initial enumeration. ∎
