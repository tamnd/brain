[Section 2.3.4.4: Enumeration of Trees](../)

**Exercise 12.** [**] [M20] Find the number of labeled oriented trees with $n$ vertices by using determinants and the result of exercise 2.3.4.2-19. (See also exercise 1.2.3-36.)

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 5m26s  

---

Let $G$ be the complete graph on the labeled vertices ${1,2,\ldots,n}$, and orient every edge toward the specified root, say vertex $1$. By exercise 2.3.4.2-19, the number of oriented spanning trees rooted at $1$ is any cofactor of the matrix

$$
L=(\ell_{ij}),
$$

where $\ell_{ii}$ is the degree of vertex $i$ and $\ell_{ij}=-1$ for $i\ne j$. Since $G$ is complete, every vertex has degree $n-1$, hence

$$
L=
\begin{pmatrix}
n-1&-1&\cdots&-1\\
-1&n-1&\cdots&-1\\
\vdots&\vdots&\ddots&\vdots\\
-1&-1&\cdots&n-1
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Deleting the first row and column gives the $(n-1)\times(n-1)$ matrix

$$
M=nI-J,
$$

where $J$ is the all-ones matrix. The matrix $J$ has eigenvalue $n-1$ once and eigenvalue $0$ with multiplicity $n-2$. Therefore $M$ has eigenvalue $1=n-(n-1)$ once and eigenvalue $n$ with multiplicity $n-2$. Hence

$$
\det(M)=1\cdot n^{\,n-2}=n^{\,n-2}.
$$

By the determinant formula of exercise 2.3.4.2-19, the number of labeled oriented trees with root $1$ is $n^{,n-2}$. Since each labeled oriented tree has exactly one root, multiplying by the $n$ possible choices of root gives the total number of labeled oriented trees on $n$ vertices:

$$
n\cdot n^{\,n-2}=n^{\,n-1}.
$$

Therefore the number of labeled oriented trees with a specified root is

$$
\boxed{n^{\,n-2}},
$$

and the number of labeled oriented trees when the root is not specified is

$$
\boxed{n^{\,n-1}}.
$$
