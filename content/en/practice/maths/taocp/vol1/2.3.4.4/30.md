**Exercise 30.** [**] [M23] Let $n,x,y,z_1,\ldots,z_n$ be positive integers. Consider a set of $x+y+z_1+\cdots+z_n+n$ vertices $r_i,s_{jk},t_j$ ($1\le i\le x+y$, $1\le j\le n$, $1\le k\le z_j$), in which arcs have been drawn from $s_{jk}$ to $t_j$ for all $j$ and $k$. According to exercise 27, there are

$$
(x+y)(x+y+z_1+\cdots+z_n)^{n-1}
$$

ways to draw one arc from each of $t_1,\ldots,t_n$ to other

**Verified:** yes

Apply Exercise 27 with the vertices partitioned so that the $U$-set has $p=x+y$ elements and the remaining vertices play the role of the $V$-structure, with the fixed arcs $s_{jk}\to t_j$ forming a forest of $n$ components attached to the designated vertices $t_1,\ldots,t_n$. Each completion consists of choosing exactly one outgoing arc from each $t_j$ to one of the available vertices in ${U_i,s_{jk},t_\ell}$ subject to the constraint that no oriented cycle is created.

The canonical representation argument of Exercise 27 applies verbatim: each admissible completion corresponds uniquely to a sequence of choices in which the first $n-1$ selections range over all $x+y+z_1+\cdots+z_n$ available vertices and the final selection is restricted to the $x+y$ vertices in the $U$-set. Hence the number of admissible choices is

$$
(x+y)\bigl(x+y+z_1+\cdots+z_n\bigr)^{n-1}.
$$
