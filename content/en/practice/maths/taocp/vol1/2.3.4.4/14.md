[Section 2.3.4.4: Enumeration of Trees](../)

**Exercise 14.** [**] [10] True or false: The last entry, $f(V_{n-1})$, in the canonical representation of an oriented tree is always the root of that tree.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 5m23s  

---

After $n-2$ deletions in the construction, exactly two vertices remain: the root and $V_{n-1}$. The remaining graph is a tree on two vertices, hence consists of a single edge joining these two vertices.

Since every non-root vertex has exactly one outgoing arc to its parent, the vertex $V_{n-1}$ must be adjacent to the root, and the defining construction gives $f(V_{n-1})$ as the unique neighbor of $V_{n-1}$ in the remaining tree. Therefore $f(V_{n-1})$ equals the root.

This holds for every oriented tree, so the statement is true.

This completes the proof. ∎
