**Exercise 3.** [**] [M40] Write a computer program that determines the number of (unlabeled) free trees and of oriented trees with $n$ vertices, for $n\le100$. (Use the result of exercise 2.) Explore arithmetical properties of these numbers; can anything be said about their prime factors, or their residues modulo $p$?

A program based on exercise 2 computes the coefficients $a_n$ recursively from

$$
na_{n+1}=\sum_{k=1}^{n}k\,a_k\,s_{nk},
\qquad
s_{nk}=\sum_{1\le j\le n/k}a_{n+1-jk},
$$

starting with $a_1=1$. After obtaining $a_1,\ldots,a_{100}$, the numbers of free trees are obtained from equation (9), or equivalently from equation (8) together with the bicentroidal correction when $n$ is even. Since every $a_{n+1}$ is determined from previously computed values, the computation is straightforward and requires only integer arithmetic.

Experimentation with the resulting tables reveals no simple pattern in the prime factorizations of either the oriented-tree numbers $a_n$ or the free-tree numbers. Both primes and highly composite values occur irregularly. Similarly, the residues modulo a fixed prime $p$ appear to be distributed without any evident periodicity or simple congruence law. Unlike the Catalan numbers of equation (14), whose arithmetic properties are governed by an explicit binomial formula, the numbers $a_n$ and the coefficients of $F(z)$ arise from the recursive structure of equations (2), (8), and (9), and no elementary description of their prime factors or residue classes is suggested by the data for $n\le100$.
