[Section 2.3.4.4: Enumeration of Trees](../)

**Exercise 23.** [**] [M21] How many ordered trees are possible with $n$ labeled vertices? (Give a simple formula involving factorials.)

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 6m59s  

---

An ordered tree with $n$ vertices determines, by the correspondence in Section 2.3.4.4, a binary tree with $n-1$ nodes, and every binary tree with $n-1$ nodes determines exactly one ordered tree with $n$ vertices. Hence the number of ordered tree structures on $n$ vertices equals $b_{n-1}$.

From equation (14),

$$
b_{n-1}=\frac{1}{n}\binom{2n-2}{n-1}.
$$

To obtain ordered trees with $n$ labeled vertices, assign the labels ${1,2,\ldots,n}$ bijectively to the vertices of each fixed ordered tree structure. For any given structure there are $n!$ such assignments, since each vertex receives exactly one distinct label and every permutation of labels produces a distinct labeled tree.

The total number is therefore

$$
n!\,b_{n-1}=n!\cdot \frac{1}{n}\binom{2n-2}{n-1}=(n-1)!\binom{2n-2}{n-1}.
$$

Hence the number of ordered trees with $n$ labeled vertices is

\boxed{(n-1)!\binom{2n-2}{n-1}}. $$ ∎
