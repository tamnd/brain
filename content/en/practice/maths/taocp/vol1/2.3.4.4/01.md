[Section 2.3.4.4: Enumeration of Trees](../)

**Exercise 1.** [**] [M20] (G. Pólya.) Show that

$$
A(z)=z\cdot\exp\left(A(z)+\frac12A(z^2)+\frac13A(z^3)+\cdots\right).
$$

[*Hint:* Take logarithms of (3).]

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 3m56s  

---

Let $A(z)=\sum_{n\ge1} a_n z^n$, where $a_n$ is the number of rooted unlabeled trees with $n$ vertices.

A rooted tree consists of a root vertex together with a multiset of rooted subtrees attached to the root. If the root has subtrees of sizes $n_1,\dots,n_k$, then each subtree is again a rooted tree, contributing a factor $A(z^{n_i})$ after accounting for the size contribution $n_i$ in the exponent of $z$. The total size condition is $n_1+\cdots+n_k=n-1$.

The key issue is that subtrees are unordered, so a configuration with repeated identical subtrees has symmetries. To isolate these symmetries, consider a root with $k$ attached subtrees and let a permutation of the $k$ positions act on these subtrees. The structure is determined only up to such permutations.

A permutation of $k$ objects decomposes uniquely into cycles. If a cycle has length $j$, then along that cycle the same subtree structure is repeated $j$ times. This forces identification of $j$ identical rooted trees, and the contribution of such a cycle is obtained by treating it as a single rooted tree whose size is multiplied by $j$. This gives the weight $A(z^j)$, since every vertex in the repeated structure contributes a factor $z^j$ in total.

Thus a permutation with cycle structure containing $c_j$ cycles of length $j$ contributes the monomial

$$
\prod_{j\ge1} A(z^j)^{c_j}.
$$

Averaging over all permutations of $k$ subtrees gives the contribution of all configurations with exactly $k$ subtrees:

$$
\frac{1}{k!}\sum_{\sigma\in S_k}\prod_{j\ge1} A(z^j)^{c_j(\sigma)}.
$$

Now sum over all $k\ge0$. The expression

$$
\frac{1}{k!}\sum_{\sigma\in S_k}\prod_{j\ge1} x_j^{c_j(\sigma)}
$$

is the cycle index of the symmetric group $S_k$. The standard identity for symmetric groups states that

$$
\sum_{k\ge0} Z(S_k)=\exp\!\left(\sum_{j\ge1}\frac{x_j}{j}\right).
$$

Applying this identity with the substitution $x_j \leftarrow A(z^j)$ yields

$$
\sum_{k\ge0}\frac{1}{k!}\sum_{\sigma\in S_k}\prod_{j\ge1} A(z^j)^{c_j(\sigma)}
=
\exp\!\left(\sum_{j\ge1}\frac{A(z^j)}{j}\right).
$$

This exponential counts all multisets of rooted subtrees attached to the root.

Finally, each rooted tree contributes one distinguished root vertex, giving a factor $z$. Therefore

$$
A(z)=z\cdot \exp\!\left(\sum_{j\ge1}\frac{A(z^j)}{j}\right).
$$

Expanding the first term inside the exponential separates the $j=1$ contribution:

$$
\sum_{j\ge1}\frac{A(z^j)}{j}=A(z)+\frac12A(z^2)+\frac13A(z^3)+\cdots.
$$

Hence

$$
A(z)=z\cdot\exp\left(A(z)+\frac12A(z^2)+\frac13A(z^3)+\cdots\right).
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
