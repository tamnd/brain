**Exercise 28.** [**] [M22] (*Bipartite trees.*) Use the result of exercise 27 to enumerate the number of labeled free trees on vertices $U_1,\ldots,U_m,V_1,\ldots,V_n$, such that each edge joins $U_j$ to $V_k$ for some $j$ and $k$.

The error in the previous solution is structural: it attempts to replace the tree condition by independent parent choices. That step cannot be repaired locally. A correct argument must use a genuine characterization of trees, not an unconstrained encoding.

A clean way to finish the exercise is to recognize the problem as an instance of counting spanning trees of a complete bipartite graph and apply the Matrix–Tree Theorem.

## Step 1. Reformulation

A labeled free tree on vertex set

$$
U=\{U_1,\dots,U_m\}, \quad V=\{V_1,\dots,V_n\}
$$

in which every edge joins a vertex of $U$ to a vertex of $V$ is exactly a spanning tree of the complete bipartite graph $K_{m,n}$.

Indeed:

- all edges are required to go between $U$ and $V$,
- every such edge is allowed in $K_{m,n}$,
- and every spanning tree of $K_{m,n}$ uses only such edges.

Thus the problem is:

$$
\text{count spanning trees of } K_{m,n}.
$$

## Step 2. Laplacian of $K_{m,n}$

Let vertices in $U$ have degree $n$, and vertices in $V$ have degree $m$.

The Laplacian matrix $L$ has block form:

$$
L =
\begin{pmatrix}
n I_m & -J_{m\times n}\\
-J_{n\times m} & m I_n
\end{pmatrix},
$$

where $J$ is the all-ones matrix.

## Step 3. Spectrum of the Laplacian

We determine eigenvalues:

### (1) Eigenvalue $n$

Vectors supported on $U$ with coordinate sum $0$ satisfy

$$
Lx = n x,
$$

giving eigenvalue $n$ with multiplicity $m-1$.

### (2) Eigenvalue $m$

Vectors supported on $V$ with coordinate sum $0$ give eigenvalue $m$ with multiplicity $n-1$.

### (3) Zero eigenvalue

The all-ones vector is in the kernel, giving eigenvalue $0$.

Thus the nonzero eigenvalues are:

$$
\underbrace{n,\dots,n}_{m-1}, \quad \underbrace{m,\dots,m}_{n-1}.
$$

## Step 4. Matrix–Tree Theorem

Kirchhoff’s theorem states that the number of spanning trees is:

$$
\tau(K_{m,n}) = \frac{1}{m+n}\prod_{\lambda \neq 0} \lambda.
$$

Hence:

$$
\tau(K_{m,n})
= \frac{1}{m+n} \cdot n^{m-1} \cdot m^{n-1} \cdot (m+n).
$$

The factor $m+n$ cancels, giving:

$$
\tau(K_{m,n}) = m^{n-1} n^{m-1}.
$$

## Step 5. Conclusion

The number of labeled free trees on $U_1,\dots,U_m,V_1,\dots,V_n$ in which every edge joins $U$ to $V$ is:

$$
\boxed{m^{\,n-1} n^{\,m-1}}.
$$

## Remark on the failed argument

The previous solution failed because it treated “choose a parent in the opposite part” as an unconstrained product. That ignores the global consistency conditions that characterize trees. The correct structure theorem is encoded either in Prüfer codes or, more cleanly here, in the Laplacian spectrum of $K_{m,n}$, which enforces connectivity and acyclicity automatically.
