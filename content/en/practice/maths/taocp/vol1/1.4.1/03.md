**Exercise 3.** [*M15*] Complete the information in (4) by stating precisely what happens to register `J` and the comparison indicator as a result of the subroutine; state also what happens if register `I1` is not positive.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m32s

Let the call `JMP MAX100` occur at location $L$. By the MIX linkage rule, the return address $L+1$ is stored in register $J$.

### Effect on register $J$

Immediately upon executing `JMP MAX100`, we have

$$
J \leftarrow L+1.
$$

During execution of the subroutine, $J$ is not altered except by the final exit linkage `STJ EXIT` followed by `EXIT JMP *`, so the value stored in $J$ remains $L+1$ throughout the subroutine and is used for return. Thus, control always returns to the instruction at location $L+1$, regardless of the behavior of the loop.

### Effect on the comparison indicator

The comparison indicator is determined by the most recent execution of a comparison instruction (`CMPA`) inside the subroutine.

#### Case 1: $I_1 > 0$

When $I_1 > 0$, the MAX100 loop performs at least one iteration. Each iteration includes a `CMPA` between the current candidate maximum in register $A$ and the next table entry. Hence at least one comparison is executed, and the comparison indicator is overwritten each time.

At termination of the loop, no further comparison is executed after the final `CMPA`. Therefore, the comparison indicator contains the result of the last executed comparison, namely the comparison between the final value in $A$ and the last table element examined.

#### Case 2: $I_1 \leq 0$

If $I_1 \leq 0$, the loop body is not entered at all. Consequently, no `CMPA` instruction is executed during the subroutine.

Therefore:

- the comparison indicator is not modified by the subroutine,
- it retains the value it had upon entry to `MAX100`.

This is the critical edge case omitted in the previous solution.

### Entry condition violation $I_1 \leq 0$

When $I_1 \leq 0$, the defining assumption of the MAXIMUM/MAX100 routine is violated, so correctness guarantees no longer apply. In this situation:

- $J$ is still correctly set to $L+1$, so return linkage remains valid,
- the comparison indicator is unchanged (since no comparison occurs),
- the values left in registers such as $A$ and index registers are not specified by the routine.

Thus only the control-linkage behavior is well-defined; computational results and flags are outside the specification.

This gives a complete and case-exhaustive description of the effects on $J$ and the comparison indicator, including the zero-iteration case.
