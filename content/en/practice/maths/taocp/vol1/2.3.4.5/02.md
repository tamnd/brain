**Exercise 2.** [**] [17] Draw an extended binary tree with terminal nodes containing the
weights 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49, 64, 81, 100, having minimum weighted path
length.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 2m44s  

---

The construction follows Huffman’s algorithm applied to the weights in nondecreasing order.

Start with

$1,4,9,16,25,36,49,64,81,100$.

Each step replaces the two smallest available weights by their sum:

$1+4 \leftarrow 5$.

The multiset becomes $5,9,16,25,36,49,64,81,100$.

$5+9 \leftarrow 14$.

The multiset becomes $14,16,25,36,49,64,81,100$.

$14+16 \leftarrow 30$.

The multiset becomes $25,30,36,49,64,81,100$.

$25+30 \leftarrow 55$.

The multiset becomes $36,49,55,64,81,100$.

$36+49 \leftarrow 85$.

The multiset becomes $55,64,81,85,100$.

$55+64 \leftarrow 119$.

The multiset becomes $81,85,100,119$.

$81+85 \leftarrow 166$.

The multiset becomes $100,119,166$.

$100+119 \leftarrow 219$.

The multiset becomes $166,219$.

$166+219 \leftarrow 385$.

This produces the root weight $385$.

The corresponding extended binary tree is obtained by reversing the merges. Each internal node is labeled by the sum of its children, so the structure is determined by the nested combinations

$$
((((1,4),9),16,(25,((36,49),(64,81)))),100).
$$

More explicitly, the tree is formed by combining $(1,4)$ first, then attaching $9$, then $16$, then pairing $25$ with the result of $( (1,4),9,16 )$, then combining $(36,49)$ and $(64,81)$, then attaching $55$ with $64$, then pairing with $81$, then merging with $100$ in the final step.

This extended binary tree has minimum weighted path length among all trees with the given terminal weights. ∎
