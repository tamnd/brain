**Exercise 4.** [**] [M25] (E. S. Schwartz and B. Kallick.) Assume that
$w_1\le w_2\le\cdots\le w_m$. Show that there is an extended binary tree that
minimizes $\sum w_jl_j$ and for which the terminal nodes in left to right order
contain the respective values $w_1,w_2,\ldots,w_m$. [For example, tree (11) does
not meet this condition since the weights appear in the order 19, 23, 11, 13,
29, 2, 3, 5, 7, 17, 31, 37, 41. We seek a tree for which the weights appear in
ascending order, and this does not always happen with Huffman's construction.]

We prove the statement by induction on $m$.

Let $W=\{w_1,\dots,w_m\}$ with $w_1\le w_2\le\cdots\le w_m$. Let $L(T)=\sum_j w_j \ell_j$ denote the weighted path length of a tree $T$, where $\ell_j$ is the depth of the leaf carrying weight $w_j$.

## Induction hypothesis

We prove:

> For every multiset of $m$ nonnegative weights, there exists an optimal extended binary tree whose terminal nodes, read left to right, have nondecreasing weights.

No assumption is made about any special structure of intermediate instances; the hypothesis is purely existential.

## Base case $m=1$

Trivial: the single-node tree is optimal and its (only) leaf order is vacuously nondecreasing.

## Inductive step

Assume the statement holds for all multisets of size $m-1$. Let $W=\{w_1\le \cdots \le w_m\}$.

### 1. First reduction (Huffman sibling property)

By the standard Huffman greedy-choice property, there exists an optimal tree $T^*$ in which the two smallest weights $w_1,w_2$ are siblings at maximum depth. Let their common parent be replaced by a single leaf of weight

$$
w_{12}=w_1+w_2.
$$

This produces a reduced instance

$$
W'=\{w_{12}, w_3, w_4,\dots,w_m\}
$$

of size $m-1$, together with a correspondence:

- any tree for $W'$ expands uniquely to a tree for $W$,
- and if the depth of $w_{12}$ is $k$, then expanding it contributes

$$
(w_1+w_2)k + (w_1+w_2)
$$

instead of $(w_1+w_2)k$, hence increases total cost by exactly $w_1+w_2$, independent of the rest of the tree.

Therefore, optimality is preserved under contraction: a tree is optimal for $W'$ if and only if its expansion is optimal for $W$.

### 2. Apply induction to the reduced instance

By the induction hypothesis applied to the multiset $W'$, there exists an optimal tree $T'$ for $W'$ whose leaves appear left-to-right in nondecreasing order.

In that order, one leaf is labeled $w_{12}$. Replace this leaf by an internal node with two children labeled $w_1$ (left) and $w_2$ (right), forming a tree $T$.

### 3. Preservation of optimality

Let the depth of the contracted leaf $w_{12}$ in $T'$ be $k$. In $T'$, its contribution to cost is

$$
(w_1+w_2)k.
$$

After expansion, the contributions become

$$
w_1(k+1)+w_2(k+1)=(w_1+w_2)k+(w_1+w_2).
$$

Thus the total cost increases by the constant $w_1+w_2$, independent of the structure of $T'$. Hence $T$ is optimal for $W$.

### 4. Preservation of inorder ordering

In $T'$, the leaves appear in nondecreasing order, and the single occurrence of $w_{12}$ is replaced by two consecutive leaves $w_1,w_2$. Since

$$
w_1 \le w_2 \le w_3 \le \cdots \le w_m,
$$

the inorder sequence in $T$ is exactly

$$
w_1,w_2,\dots,w_m,
$$

which is nondecreasing.

## Conclusion

We have constructed an optimal extended binary tree for $w_1\le \cdots \le w_m$ whose terminal nodes appear from left to right in nondecreasing order.

Thus such a tree exists for every $m$, completing the induction. ∎
