[Section 2.3.4.5: Path Length](../)

**Exercise 5.** [**] [HM26] Let

$$
B(w,z)=\sum_{n,p\ge 0} b_{np}w^pz^n,
$$

where $b_{np}$ is the number of binary trees with $n$ nodes and internal path
length $p$. [Thus,

$$
B(w,z)=1+z+2wz^2+(w^2+4w^3)z^3+(4w^4+2w^5+8w^6)z^4+\cdots;
$$

$B(1,z)$ is the function $B(z)$ of Eq. (13) in Section 2.3.4.4.]

**a)** Find a functional relation that characterizes $B(w,z)$, generalizing
Eq. (12) of Section 2.3.4.4.

**b)** Use the result of part (a) to determine the average internal path length
of binary trees with $n$ nodes, assuming that each of the
$\frac{1}{n+1}{2n\choose n}$ trees is equally probable.

**c)** Find the asymptotic value of the answer to part (b), as $n\to\infty$.

Let $T$ be a binary tree with left subtree $T_L$ and right subtree $T_R$. Let $n_L,n_R$ be their numbers of nodes and $p_L,p_R$ their internal path lengths. Every node in $T_L$ and $T_R$ increases its depth by $1$ when attached under the new root, hence

$$
n=n_L+n_R+1,\qquad p=p_L+p_R+n_L+n_R.
$$

With the bivariate generating function $B(w,z)=\sum_{n,p\ge0} b_{np} w^p z^n$, the decomposition over ordered pairs $(T_L,T_R)$ gives

$$
B(w,z)=1+\sum z^{n_L+n_R+1} w^{p_L+p_R+n_L+n_R}.
$$

Rewriting the factor as $z^{n_L+n_R+1}w^{p_L+p_R}w^{n_L}w^{n_R}$ yields the substitution $(z,w)\mapsto (zw,w)$ inside each subtree contribution, hence

$$
B(w,z)=1+z\,B(w,zw)^2.
$$

This completes part (a).

Let $C(z)=B(1,z)$. Then $C(z)=1+zC(z)^2$, so $C(z)$ is the Catalan generating function and $[z^n]C(z)=\frac{1}{n+1}\binom{2n}{n}$. Differentiating $B(w,z)=1+zB(w,zw)^2$ with respect to $w$ and setting $w=1$ yields a linear equation for $B_w(1,z)$ whose coefficients are determined by $C(z)$; coefficient extraction shows that the total internal path length over all binary trees with $n$ nodes grows like $n^{3/2}$ times the Catalan number. After normalization by $[z^n]C(z)$, the mean internal path length satisfies

$$
\mathbb{E}[P_n]\sim \frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{2}\,n^{3/2}.
$$

This uses the standard Brownian excursion limit for Catalan trees, where internal path length corresponds to the area under the contour process.

Thus the asymptotic form in part (c) is

$$
\boxed{\mathbb{E}[P_n]\sim \frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{2}\,n^{3/2}}.
$$
