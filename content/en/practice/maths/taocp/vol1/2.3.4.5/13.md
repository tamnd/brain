[Section 2.3.4.5: Path Length](../)

**Exercise 13.** [**] [22] Design an algorithm that begins with $m$ weights
$w_1\le w_2\le\cdots\le w_m$ and constructs an extended binary tree having
minimum weighted path length. Represent the final tree in three arrays

$$
A[1]\ldots A[2m-1],\qquad L[1]\ldots L[m-1],\qquad R[1]\ldots R[m-1];
$$

here $L[i]$ and $R[i]$ point to the left and right children of internal node
$i$, the root is node 1, and $A[i]$ is the weight of node $i$. The original
weights should appear as the external node weights $A[m],\ldots,A[2m-1]$. Your
algorithm should make fewer than $2m$ weight-comparisons. Caution: Some or all
of the given weights may be negative!

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m08s  

---

Maintain two FIFO queues. Queue $Q_0$ initially contains the external nodes $m,m+1,\ldots,2m-1$ in the given order, with $A[m],\ldots,A[2m-1]$ equal to the prescribed weights. Queue $Q_1$ is initially empty. At each stage remove the two smallest available nodes by comparing only the fronts of the nonempty queues. If the chosen nodes are $u$ and $v$, create a new internal node numbered $k$, where $k$ runs from $m-1$ down to $1$. Set $L[k]\leftarrow u$, $R[k]\leftarrow v$, $A[k]\leftarrow A[u]+A[v]$, and append $k$ to the rear of $Q_1$. Since every newly created weight is appended in nondecreasing order, each queue remains ordered throughout the computation, even when some weights are negative. By the argument preceding the exercise, the repeated selection of the two least available weights therefore performs Huffman's construction and yields a tree of minimum weighted path length.

Each merge requires two selections. The first selection needs no comparison if one queue is empty, otherwise one comparison of the two queue fronts. The second selection is made after one node has been removed, so again at most one comparison is needed unless one queue has become empty. Hence each of the $m-1$ merges uses at most two weight-comparisons, but the final merge needs none after the first node has been determined, because only one node remains. Therefore the total number of weight-comparisons is at most

$$
2(m-1)-1=2m-3<2m.
$$

The replacement operations are

$$
L[k]\leftarrow u,\qquad
R[k]\leftarrow v,\qquad
A[k]\leftarrow A[u]+A[v],
$$

for each of the $m-1$ internal nodes. This is exactly $3(m-1)$ replacement operations. The completed arrays satisfy the required representation: node $1$ is the root, $L[i]$ and $R[i]$ are the children of internal node $i$, and the original weights remain in $A[m],\ldots,A[2m-1]$.
