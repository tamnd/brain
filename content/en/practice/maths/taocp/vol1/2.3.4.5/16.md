[Section 2.3.4.5: Path Length](../)

**Exercise 16.** [**] [M25] (F. K. Hwang.) Let $w_1\le\cdots\le w_m$ and
$w'_1\le\cdots\le w'_m$ be two sets of weights such that

$$
\sum_{j=1}^k w_j\le \sum_{j=1}^k w'_j,\qquad 1\le k\le m.
$$

Prove that the corresponding minimum weighted path lengths satisfy

$$
\sum_{j=1}^m w_jl_j\le \sum_{j=1}^m w'_jl'_j.
$$

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 13m21s  

---

Let $F(w_1,\ldots,w_m)$ denote the minimum weighted external path length over all extended binary trees with external weights $w_1,\ldots,w_m$. In any such tree, if two external nodes of weights $a$ and $b$ are attached as siblings of a new internal node, and these two nodes are replaced by a single node of weight $a+b$, then every path contributing $a$ or $b$ increases in length by $1$, while all other paths remain unchanged. The total increase in weighted path length is therefore $a+b$. Consequently,

$$
F(w_1,\ldots,w_m)=F(w_1,\ldots,w_{i-1},w_{i+1},\ldots,w_m,w_i+w_{i+1})+(w_i+w_{i+1})
$$

whenever $w_i$ and $w_{i+1}$ are combined as the two leaves of maximum depth in an optimal construction, as in Huffman's argument in Section 2.3.4.5.

Let $w_1\le \cdots \le w_m$ and $w'_1\le \cdots \le w'_m$ satisfy

$$
\sum_{j=1}^k w_j \le \sum_{j=1}^k w'_j,\qquad 1\le k\le m.
$$

Construct reduced sequences

$$
u=(w_1+w_2,w_3,\ldots,w_m),\qquad u'=(w'_1+w'_2,w'_3,\ldots,w'_m).
$$

For $k=1$, the defining hypothesis with $k=2$ gives $w_1+w_2\le w'_1+w'_2$, hence $u_1\le u'_1$ after reordering $u'$ if necessary. For $k\ge 2$,

$$
\sum_{j=1}^k u_j = (w_1+w_2)+\sum_{j=3}^{k+1} w_j = \sum_{j=1}^{k+1} w_j \le \sum_{j=1}^{k+1} w'_j.
$$

Since the right-hand side is at least the sum of the $k$ smallest components of $u'$, the same inequality holds after sorting, so the reduced sequences satisfy the same partial sum dominance condition.

Applying the same reduction inductively yields

$$
F(u)\le F(u').
$$

Using the decomposition of weighted path length under the first merge,

$$
F(w_1,\ldots,w_m)=F(u)+(w_1+w_2),\qquad F(w'_1,\ldots,w'_m)=F(u')+(w'_1+w'_2).
$$

Combining these relations with $w_1+w_2\le w'_1+w'_2$ and $F(u)\le F(u')$ gives

$$
F(w_1,\ldots,w_m)\le F(w'_1,\ldots,w'_m).
$$

Since $F$ equals the minimum weighted path length $\sum_{j=1}^m w_j l_j$ for optimal trees, the stated inequality follows.

This completes the proof. ∎
