**Exercise 9.** [**] [M21] The numbers appearing in the circular nodes of (11) are equal to
the sums of the numbers appearing in their external-node descendants. Show that
the sum of the circular node values is equal to the weighted path length.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 5m44s  

---

Let $T$ be an extended binary tree with external nodes carrying weights $w_1,\ldots,w_m$. For each internal node $V$, let $s(V)$ denote the sum of all weights attached to external nodes in the subtree rooted at $V$.

Then

$$
\sum_V s(V)
=
\sum_V \sum_{j \in \mathrm{ext}(V)} w_j,
$$

where $\mathrm{ext}(V)$ denotes the external nodes in the subtree of $V$. Interchanging the order of summation gives

$$
\sum_V s(V)
=
\sum_{j=1}^m w_j \cdot \#\{V : j \in \mathrm{ext}(V)\}.
$$

Fix an external node $j$ at depth $l_j$. The internal nodes $V$ for which $j \in \mathrm{ext}(V)$ are exactly the internal nodes on the unique path from the root to the parent of $j$. That path contains precisely $l_j$ internal nodes, since each edge on the path corresponds to entering a distinct internal node level, starting with the root and ending at the parent of $j$ at depth $l_j-1$.

Hence

$$
\#\{V : j \in \mathrm{ext}(V)\} = l_j,
$$

so

$$
\sum_V s(V) = \sum_{j=1}^m w_j l_j.
$$

The right-hand side is the weighted path length of the tree, and the left-hand side is the sum of the values of the circular (internal) nodes. Therefore the sum of the circular node values equals the weighted path length. This completes the proof. ∎
