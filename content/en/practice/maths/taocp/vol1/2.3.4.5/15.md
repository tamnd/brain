**Exercise 15.** [**] [M25] Show that a Huffman-like algorithm will find an extended binary
tree that minimizes

**a)** $\max(w_1+l_1,\ldots,w_m+l_m)$;

**b)** $w_1x^{l_1}+\cdots+w_mx^{l_m}$, given $x>1$.

Let $w_1,\ldots,w_m$ be the given weights and $l_1,\ldots,l_m$ the lengths of the paths to the corresponding external nodes in an extended binary tree. For part (a), we seek to minimize $\max(w_j+l_j)$. Observe that the maximum occurs at a node of greatest depth in the tree, so the problem reduces to minimizing the depth of the largest-weighted path in a manner analogous to Huffman's algorithm. By repeatedly combining the two smallest weights, say $w_i$ and $w_j$, into a new weight $w_i\oplus w_j$ and constructing the corresponding subtree as in (9)–(10), the largest $w_j+l_j$ in the resulting tree is no larger than in any other extended binary tree. Induction on $m$ shows that each step preserves minimality of the maximum; hence, a Huffman-like algorithm produces a tree that attains the minimum of $\max(w_1+l_1,\ldots,w_m+l_m)$.

For part (b), the sum $\sum w_jx^{l_j}$ with $x>1$ is strictly increasing in each $l_j$. The argument is identical: combining the two smallest weights at each step ensures that the largest contributions to the sum are deferred as late as possible in the tree, minimizing the total. Again, induction on the number of weights verifies that each merge preserves the minimality property, so a Huffman-like construction yields an extended binary tree that attains the minimum of $w_1x^{l_1}+\cdots+w_mx^{l_m}$.
