[Section 2.3.4.5: Path Length](../)

**Exercise 8.** [**] [M23] Prove Eq. (7).

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 1m31s  

---

Let the complete $t$-ary tree have internal nodes ${1,2,\ldots,n}$, and let

$$
q=\left\lfloor \log_t((t-1)n+1)\right\rfloor.
$$

In such a tree, all levels $0,1,\ldots,q-1$ are completely filled, while level $q$ is partially filled.

At level $k$ there are $t^k$ nodes, so the number of internal nodes strictly above level $q$ is

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{q-1} t^k=\frac{t^q-1}{t-1}.
$$

Hence the number of nodes on level $q$ is

$$
r=n-\frac{t^q-1}{t-1}.
$$

The internal path length $I$ is the sum of depths of all internal nodes, hence

$$
I=\sum_{k=0}^{q-1} k t^k + q r.
$$

For the geometric sum,

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{q-1} k t^k
=\frac{t - q t^q + (q-1)t^{q+1}}{(1-t)^2}
=\frac{(q-1)t^{q+1}-q t^q+t}{(t-1)^2}.
$$

Substituting $r$ gives

$$
I=\frac{(q-1)t^{q+1}-q t^q+t}{(t-1)^2}
+q\left(n-\frac{t^q-1}{t-1}\right).
$$

Expanding the second term,

$$
I=qn - q\frac{t^q-1}{t-1}
+\frac{(q-1)t^{q+1}-q t^q+t}{(t-1)^2}.
$$

Put everything over $(t-1)^2$:

$$
I=\frac{qn(t-1)^2 - q(t^q-1)(t-1) + (q-1)t^{q+1}-q t^q+t}{(t-1)^2}.
$$

Expanding and collecting terms, the coefficients of $t^{q+1}$ reduce to $-1$, all terms involving $t^q$ cancel, and the remaining constant terms combine with $qn(t-1)^2$ to yield

$$
I=\left(n+\frac{1}{t-1}\right)q-\frac{t^{q+1}-t}{(t-1)^2}.
$$

Thus the internal path length of the complete $t$-ary tree with $n$ internal nodes is

$$
\boxed{\left(n+\frac{1}{t-1}\right)q-\frac{t^{q+1}-t}{(t-1)^2}},\qquad q=\left\lfloor\log_t((t-1)n+1)\right\rfloor.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
