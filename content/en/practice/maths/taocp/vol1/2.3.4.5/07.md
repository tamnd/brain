[Section 2.3.4.5: Path Length](../)

**Exercise 7.** [**] [M21] What is the relation between the external and internal path length
in a $t$-ary tree? See exercise 6; this should be a generalization of Eq. (3).

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m37s  

---

Let $E$ and $I$ denote the external and internal path lengths of an extended $t$-ary tree, and let $n$ be the number of internal (circular) nodes. By exercise 6, if $s$ is the number of external (square) nodes, then

$$
s=(t-1)n+1.
$$

For each internal node $V$ at distance $k$ from the root, all $t$ of its children are one level deeper, and each contributes to $E$ if it is an external node. Replace $V$ by its $t$ children: the contribution to $I$ decreases by $k$, since $V$ is removed, while the contribution to $E$ changes by replacing a single node at depth $k$ with $t$ nodes at depth $k+1$, giving net change

$$
\Delta E = t(k+1)-k = (t-1)k+t.
$$

Summing this relation over all internal nodes and using $I=\sum k$ over internal nodes yields

$$
E=(t-1)I+tn.
$$

Since $tn = (t-1)n + n = s + n - 1 + 1$, the relation is consistent with the edge count identity $n+s-1$ for the underlying tree structure. Hence the external and internal path lengths satisfy

$$
E=(t-1)I+tn.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
