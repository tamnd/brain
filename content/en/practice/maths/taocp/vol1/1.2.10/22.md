**Exercise 22.** [*HM22*] Suppose $X$ has the generating function $(q_1+p_1z)(q_2+p_2z)\cdots(q_n+p_nz)$, where $p_k+q_k=1$ for $1 \le k \le n$. Let $\mu=EX=p_1+\cdots+p_n$.

a) Prove that

$$
\Pr(X \le \mu r) \le (r^{-r}e^{r-1})^\mu, \qquad 0<r\le 1;
$$

$$
\Pr(X \ge \mu r) \le (r^{-r}e^{r-1})^\mu, \qquad r\ge 1.
$$

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m40s  

---

Let $G(z)=\prod_{k=1}^n (q_k+p_k z)$ be the probability generating function of $X$. Then $E[X]=\mu=\sum_{k=1}^n p_k$.

Since $X$ is a sum of independent Bernoulli variables, for any real $t>0$,

$$
E[t^X]=G(t)=\prod_{k=1}^n (q_k+p_k t).
$$

## Upper tail ($r\ge 1$)

For $t>1$, Markov’s inequality applied to $t^X$ gives

$$
\Pr(X\ge a)=\Pr(t^X\ge t^a)\le \frac{E[t^X]}{t^a}=\frac{G(t)}{t^a}.
$$

Let $a=\mu r$, $r\ge 1$. Then

$$
\Pr(X\ge \mu r)\le G(t)\,t^{-\mu r}.
$$

For each factor and any real $t$,

$$
q_k+p_k t = 1+p_k(t-1)\le e^{p_k(t-1)}.
$$

Hence

$$
G(t)\le \exp\!\left((t-1)\sum_{k=1}^n p_k\right)=\exp(\mu(t-1)).
$$

Therefore

$$
\Pr(X\ge \mu r)\le \exp(\mu(t-1)-\mu r\ln t)
= \exp\!\bigl(\mu[(t-1)-r\ln t]\bigr).
$$

Minimize $\phi(t)=(t-1)-r\ln t$ over $t>1$. We compute

$$
\phi'(t)=1-\frac{r}{t}.
$$

Setting $\phi'(t)=0$ gives $t=r$, which lies in the admissible range $t>1$ since $r\ge 1$. Substituting $t=r$,

$$
\Pr(X\ge \mu r)\le \exp\!\bigl(\mu(r-1-r\ln r)\bigr)
=(r^{-r}e^{r-1})^\mu.
$$

## Lower tail ($0<r\le 1$)

For $0<t<1$, note that $t^x$ is decreasing in $x$, so

$$
X\le a \;\Longrightarrow\; t^X \ge t^a.
$$

Hence Markov’s inequality applies in the form

$$
\Pr(X\le a)=\Pr(t^X\ge t^a)\le \frac{E[t^X]}{t^a}=\frac{G(t)}{t^a}.
$$

Let $a=\mu r$, $0<r\le 1$. Then

$$
\Pr(X\le \mu r)\le G(t)\,t^{-\mu r}.
$$

As before,

$$
G(t)\le \exp(\mu(t-1)),
$$

so

$$
\Pr(X\le \mu r)\le \exp(\mu(t-1)-\mu r\ln t)
= \exp\!\bigl(\mu[(t-1)-r\ln t]\bigr).
$$

We again minimize $\phi(t)=(t-1)-r\ln t$, now over $0<t<1$. Its derivative is

$$
\phi'(t)=1-\frac{r}{t}.
$$

Setting $\phi'(t)=0$ gives $t=r$.

Since $0<r\le 1$, we have $t=r\in(0,1]$, so this critical point is admissible and yields the minimum. Substituting $t=r$,

$$
\Pr(X\le \mu r)\le \exp\!\bigl(\mu(r-1-r\ln r)\bigr)
=(r^{-r}e^{r-1})^\mu.
$$

## Conclusion

For all $r>0$,

$$
\Pr(X \ge \mu r) \le (r^{-r}e^{r-1})^\mu \quad (r\ge 1),
\qquad
\Pr(X \le \mu r) \le (r^{-r}e^{r-1})^\mu \quad (0<r\le 1).
$$

The lower-tail issue in the previous solution arose from an incorrect transformation leading to the wrong optimization variable; working directly with $t^X$ avoids this and yields the correct stationary point $t=r$ within the valid domain. ∎
