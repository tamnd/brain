**Exercise 3.** [*M15*] What are the minimum, maximum, average, and standard deviation of the number of times step M4 is executed, if we are using Algorithm M to find the maximum of 1000 randomly ordered, distinct items? (Give your answer as decimal approximations to these quantities.)

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m15s  

---

## Solution

For Algorithm M, the number of times step M4 is executed is the random variable $A$, whose distribution satisfies the results of Section 1.2.10. For input size $n$, equation (16) gives

$$
\min A = 0, \qquad \max A = n-1,
$$

$$
\operatorname{ave}(A) = H_n - 1, \qquad \operatorname{dev}(A) = \sqrt{H_n - H_n^{(2)}}.
$$

For $n = 1000$, these quantities are evaluated using standard asymptotic approximations for harmonic numbers.

The harmonic number satisfies

$$
H_n = \ln n + \gamma + \frac{1}{2n} - \frac{1}{12n^2} + O(n^{-4}),
$$

where $\gamma \approx 0.5772156649$. With $n = 1000$,

$$
\ln 1000 \approx 6.90775527898,
$$

so

$$
H_{1000} \approx 6.90775527898 + 0.5772156649 + 0.0005 - 0.0000000833.
$$

Hence

$$
H_{1000} \approx 7.48547086.
$$

The second-order harmonic number satisfies

$$
H_n^{(2)} = \sum_{k=1}^n \frac{1}{k^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{6} - \frac{1}{n} + O(n^{-2}),
$$

and since $\pi^2/6 \approx 1.64493406685$,

$$
H_{1000}^{(2)} \approx 1.64493406685 - 0.001 = 1.64393406685.
$$

### Minimum and maximum

The extremal cases are given directly by the algorithm analysis:

$$
\min A = 0,
\qquad
\max A = 999.
$$

### Average value

Using $A_n = H_n - 1$,

$$
A_{1000} \approx 7.48547086 - 1 = 6.48547086.
$$

### Standard deviation

Using $\operatorname{dev}(A) = \sqrt{H_n - H_n^{(2)}}$,

$$
H_{1000} - H_{1000}^{(2)} \approx 7.48547086 - 1.64393406685 = 5.84153679315.
$$

Hence

$$
\operatorname{dev}(A) \approx \sqrt{5.84153679315} \approx 2.4169.
$$

### Final answer

\boxed{ \min A = 0,\; \max A = 999,\; \operatorname{ave}(A) \approx 6.48547,\; \operatorname{dev}(A) \approx 2.4169 } $$ ∎
