**Exercise 5.** [*M13*] What are the mean and standard deviation of the distribution in Fig. 11?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m13s  

---

## Solution

The distribution in Fig. 11 corresponds to $n=12$ independent trials, each producing success with probability $p=\frac{3}{5}$ and failure with probability $q=\frac{2}{5}$. By Eq. (18), the generating function is

$$
G_{12}(z) = (q+pz)^{12}.
$$

From Eq. (12),

$$
\operatorname{mean}(G_{12}) = G_{12}'(1),
$$

and from the analysis of Eq. (18),

$$
\operatorname{mean}(G_{n}) = pn,\qquad \operatorname{var}(G_n)=pqn.
$$

Substituting $n=12$ gives

$$
\operatorname{mean} = 12 \cdot \frac{3}{5} = \frac{36}{5}.
$$

For the variance,

$$
\operatorname{var} = 12 \cdot \frac{3}{5} \cdot \frac{2}{5} = \frac{72}{25}.
$$

The standard deviation is

$$
\operatorname{dev} = \sqrt{\frac{72}{25}} = \frac{\sqrt{72}}{5} = \frac{6\sqrt{2}}{5}.
$$

Thus the distribution has mean $\frac{36}{5}$ and standard deviation $\frac{6\sqrt{2}}{5}$.

$$
\boxed{\text{mean } = \frac{36}{5}, \quad \text{standard deviation } = \frac{6\sqrt{2}}{5}}
$$
