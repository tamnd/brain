[Section 1.2.10: Analysis of an Algorithm](../)

**Exercise 1.** [*10*] Determine the value of $p_{n0}$ from Eqs. (4) and (5) and interpret this result from the standpoint of Algorithm M.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m18s  

---

From (4) with $k=0$,

$$
p_{n0}=\frac{1}{n}p_{n-1,-1}+\frac{n-1}{n}p_{n-1,0}.
$$

By the initial condition (5), $p_{n-1,-1}=0$, hence

$$
p_{n0}=\frac{n-1}{n}p_{n-1,0},
\qquad p_{10}=1.
$$

Iterating this relation gives

$$
p_{n0}=\frac{n-1}{n}\cdot\frac{n-2}{n-1}\cdots\frac{1}{2}\cdot p_{10}
=\frac{1}{n}.
$$

Thus

$$
\boxed{p_{n0}=\frac{1}{n}}.
$$

The event $A=0$ occurs exactly when step M4 is never executed, meaning the current maximum is never replaced while scanning $X[1],X[2],\ldots,X[n]$. This requires $X[1]$ to be the largest of all $X[k]$, since any later value exceeding $X[1]$ would force at least one update. Under the assumption that all $n!$ permutations are equally likely, each position is equally likely to contain the maximum element, so the probability that it lies in position $1$ is $1/n$. This matches the computed value. ∎
