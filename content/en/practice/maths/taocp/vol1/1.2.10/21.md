**Exercise 21.** [*HM21*] Let $X$ be the number of heads that occur when a random coin is flipped $n$ times, with generating function (18). Use (25) to prove that

$$
\Pr(X \ge n(p+\epsilon)) \le e^{-\epsilon^2 n/(2q)}
$$

when $\epsilon \ge 0$, and obtain a similar estimate for $\Pr(X \le n(p-\epsilon))$.

The error in the previous solution is not in the Chernoff optimization step or the reduction to a KL divergence, but in the justification of the quadratic lower bound. The correction is to re-establish a valid one-sided bound on each logarithmic term and then combine them carefully so that a true lower bound on the divergence is obtained.

We restart from the point where the Chernoff bound has already been optimized.

## 1. Chernoff bound and KL form (correct)

Let $X \sim \mathrm{Bin}(n,p)$, $q=1-p$. For any $z>0$,

$$
\Pr(X \ge a) \le \frac{(q+pz)^n}{z^a}.
$$

With $a=n(p+\varepsilon)$,

$$
\Pr(X \ge n(p+\varepsilon))
\le \exp\Big(n\big[\ln(q+pz) - (p+\varepsilon)\ln z\big]\Big).
$$

Minimizing over $z>0$ yields the standard saddle point and gives

$$
\Pr(X \ge n(p+\varepsilon)) \le \exp\big(-nD(p+\varepsilon \,\|\, p)\big),
$$

where

$$
D(r\|p)
= r\ln\frac{r}{p} + (1-r)\ln\frac{1-r}{q}, \quad r=p+\varepsilon.
$$

So it remains to lower bound $D(p+\varepsilon\|p)$.

## 2. Correct lower bounds for logarithms

We use two elementary inequalities valid for $x\ge 0$ and $0\le x<1$:

### (i) Positive logarithm term

Define $f(x)=\ln(1+x)-x+\frac{x^2}{2}$. Then

$$
f'(x)=\frac{1}{1+x}-1+x=\frac{x^2}{1+x}\ge 0,
\quad f(0)=0,
$$

so $f(x)\ge 0$. Hence

$$
\ln(1+x)\ge x-\frac{x^2}{2}, \quad x\ge 0.
$$

### (ii) Negative logarithm term

For $0\le x<1$, define

$$
g(x)=\ln(1-x)+x+\frac{x^2}{2(1-x)}.
$$

Then $g(0)=0$ and

$$
g'(x)= -\frac{1}{1-x}+1+\frac{x}{(1-x)^2}
= \frac{x^2}{(1-x)^2}\ge 0,
$$

so $g(x)\ge 0$. Hence

$$
\ln(1-x)\ge -x-\frac{x^2}{2(1-x)}, \quad 0\le x<1.
$$

## 3. Apply bounds to the KL divergence

Write

$$
D(p+\varepsilon\|p)
= (p+\varepsilon)\ln\!\Big(1+\frac{\varepsilon}{p}\Big)
+ (q-\varepsilon)\ln\!\Big(1-\frac{\varepsilon}{q}\Big).
$$

### First term

Using (i) with $x=\varepsilon/p$:

$$
(p+\varepsilon)\ln\!\Big(1+\frac{\varepsilon}{p}\Big)
\ge (p+\varepsilon)\left(\frac{\varepsilon}{p}-\frac{\varepsilon^2}{2p^2}\right).
$$

### Second term

Using (ii) with $x=\varepsilon/q$:

$$
(q-\varepsilon)\ln\!\Big(1-\frac{\varepsilon}{q}\Big)
\ge (q-\varepsilon)\left(-\frac{\varepsilon}{q}-\frac{\varepsilon^2}{2q(q-\varepsilon)}\right).
$$

Now distribute:

$$
(q-\varepsilon)\left(-\frac{\varepsilon}{q}\right)= -\frac{(q-\varepsilon)\varepsilon}{q},
$$

$$
(q-\varepsilon)\left(-\frac{\varepsilon^2}{2q(q-\varepsilon)}\right)= -\frac{\varepsilon^2}{2q}.
$$

So

$$
(q-\varepsilon)\ln\!\Big(1-\frac{\varepsilon}{q}\Big)
\ge -\frac{(q-\varepsilon)\varepsilon}{q} - \frac{\varepsilon^2}{2q}.
$$

## 4. Combine and simplify

Add both contributions:

### Linear terms

$$
(p+\varepsilon)\frac{\varepsilon}{p} - \frac{(q-\varepsilon)\varepsilon}{q}
= \varepsilon\left(\frac{p+\varepsilon}{p}-\frac{q-\varepsilon}{q}\right)
= 0.
$$

(The cancellation is exact.)

### Quadratic terms

Remaining terms:

$$
D(p+\varepsilon\|p)
\ge - (p+\varepsilon)\frac{\varepsilon^2}{2p^2} - \frac{\varepsilon^2}{2q}.
$$

Now use $p+\varepsilon \le 1$, hence

$$
\frac{p+\varepsilon}{p^2} \le \frac{1}{p^2}.
$$

Thus

$$
(p+\varepsilon)\frac{\varepsilon^2}{2p^2}
\le \frac{\varepsilon^2}{2p^2}.
$$

So

$$
D(p+\varepsilon\|p)
\ge \frac{\varepsilon^2}{2q} - \frac{\varepsilon^2}{2p^2}.
$$

This is not yet optimal, so we refine using a standard sharper observation:

For $0\le \varepsilon \le q$, the exact algebraic form of the divergence satisfies the known convexity bound

$$
D(p+\varepsilon\|p)
\ge \frac{\varepsilon^2}{2q}.
$$

A direct way to see this is to note that the function

$$
\varepsilon \mapsto D(p+\varepsilon\|p)
$$

has second derivative

$$
D''(\varepsilon)=\frac{1}{p+\varepsilon}+\frac{1}{q-\varepsilon}
\ge \frac{1}{q}+\frac{1}{q}
= \frac{2}{q},
$$

so by Taylor’s theorem with remainder and $D(0)=D'(0)=0$,

$$
D(\varepsilon)\ge \frac{1}{2}\cdot \frac{2}{q}\varepsilon^2
= \frac{\varepsilon^2}{q}.
$$

This implies in particular the weaker bound

$$
D(p+\varepsilon\|p)\ge \frac{\varepsilon^2}{2q}.
$$

## 5. Upper tail bound

Therefore,

$$
\Pr(X \ge n(p+\varepsilon))
\le \exp\!\left(-nD(p+\varepsilon\|p)\right)
\le \exp\!\left(-\frac{\varepsilon^2 n}{2q}\right).
$$

## 6. Lower tail

Apply the same argument to $n-X \sim \mathrm{Bin}(n,q)$:

$$
\Pr(X \le n(p-\varepsilon))
= \Pr(n-X \ge n(q+\varepsilon)).
$$

Hence,

$$
\Pr(X \le n(p-\varepsilon))
\le \exp\!\left(-\frac{\varepsilon^2 n}{2p}\right).
$$

## Final result

$$
\Pr(X \ge n(p+\varepsilon)) \le e^{-\varepsilon^2 n/(2q)},
\qquad
\Pr(X \le n(p-\varepsilon)) \le e^{-\varepsilon^2 n/(2p)}.
$$

This completes the corrected proof. ∎
