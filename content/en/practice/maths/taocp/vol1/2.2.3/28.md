## Correct formulation

Let $G=(V,E)$ be a finite directed graph. A position $x \in V$ is a predecessor of $y$ if $x \to y$ is an edge.

For each position $x$, define:

- $\mathrm{OUT}(x)=\{y : x \to y\}$
- $\mathrm{IN}(x)=\{u : u \to x\}$

We define three disjoint classes:

- $W$: winning positions (player to move can force a move to $L$)
- $L$: losing positions (all moves go to $W$)
- $U = V \setminus (W \cup L)$: undetermined positions (draw-like positions in the general graph model)

The intended fixed-point conditions are:

$$
x \in W \iff \exists y \in \mathrm{OUT}(x)\ \text{with } y \in L,
$$

$$
x \in L \iff \mathrm{OUT}(x) \neq \varnothing \ \text{and}\ \mathrm{OUT}(x) \subseteq W,
$$

and vertices not satisfying either condition remain in $U$.

## Algorithm (retrograde analysis)

We maintain:

- A label array $\mathrm{state}(x) \in \{W,L,U\}$, initially all $U$
- A counter $c(x)$, initially $c(x)=|\mathrm{OUT}(x)|$
- A queue $Q$ of newly discovered losing positions

### Step 1. Initialization

For every vertex $x$:

- If $|\mathrm{OUT}(x)|=0$, set $\mathrm{state}(x)=L$ and insert $x$ into $Q$

These are losing because the player has no move.

### Step 2. Backward propagation from losing positions

While $Q \neq \varnothing$, remove $v$ from $Q$. For each predecessor $u \in \mathrm{IN}(v)$:

1. If $\mathrm{state}(u)=U$, then mark $v$ as evidence that $u$ is winning:

- set $\mathrm{state}(u)=W$
2. If $u$ was just set to $W$, then for every predecessor $t \in \mathrm{IN}(u)$, decrement $c(t)$ only as bookkeeping (not needed for correctness of $W$, but useful for detecting $L$).

### Step 3. Completion of losing positions

After no more $W$-propagation from $L$, determine remaining losing vertices:

For each vertex $u$ with $\mathrm{state}(u)=U$, check:

- If every successor $y \in \mathrm{OUT}(u)$ satisfies $\mathrm{state}(y)=W$, then set $\mathrm{state}(u)=L$ and insert $u$ into $Q$.

Repeat Step 2 again for these newly found losing vertices.

### Step 4. Termination

Continue until no new vertices are added to $W$ or $L$. All remaining vertices stay in $U$.

## Correctness proof

### Lemma 1 (soundness of $W$)

If a vertex $x$ is placed into $W$, then it has a successor in $L$.

Proof:

A vertex is placed into $W$ only when a successor $y$ is already in $L$. Hence $\exists y \in \mathrm{OUT}(x)\cap L$. ∎

### Lemma 2 (soundness of $L$)

If a vertex $x$ is placed into $L$, then all successors of $x$ are in $W$.

Proof:

A vertex is placed into $L$ only when every successor has already been verified to be in $W$. Hence $\mathrm{OUT}(x)\subseteq W$. ∎

### Lemma 3 (no incorrect classification)

No vertex is ever placed into both $W$ and $L$.

Proof:

A vertex is assigned a label at the moment it first satisfies one condition. The conditions are mutually exclusive:

- $W$ requires existence of a successor in $L$
- $L$ requires all successors in $W$

If a vertex has a successor in $L$, it cannot satisfy the condition for $L$. ∎

### Lemma 4 (closure of $W \cup L$)

If a vertex satisfies the defining condition of $W$ or $L$, it will eventually be labeled.

Proof:

- If $x$ has a successor in $L$, that successor is processed in $Q$, so $x$ is discovered and placed in $W$.
- If all successors of $x$ are in $W$, then after those are labeled, the check in Step 3 eventually detects $x$ and places it into $L$.

Since $V$ is finite, this process stabilizes. ∎

### Lemma 5 (structure of remaining vertices $U$)

If $x \in U$, then:

1. No successor of $x$ lies in $L$
2. Not all successors of $x$ lie in $W$

Proof:

1. If a successor were in $L$, $x$ would have been placed into $W$, contradiction.
2. If all successors were in $W$, $x$ would have been placed into $L$, contradiction.

Hence neither winning nor losing condition holds. ∎

### Theorem (correctness and completeness)

The algorithm partitions $V$ into:

- $W$: positions from which the player can force a move into $L$
- $L$: positions where every move goes into $W$
- $U$: positions in strongly connected components with no forced progression to terminal structure

Moreover:

- Every vertex satisfying the winning condition is in $W$
- Every vertex satisfying the losing condition is in $L$
- Every remaining vertex is correctly left unclassified

Proof:

Lemmas 1–5 establish soundness, completeness for $W$ and $L$, and characterize exactly the remaining vertices. In particular, cycles with no access to terminal positions remain in $U$, resolving the gap in the previous solution. ∎

## Resolution of the original gap

The previous argument incorrectly assumed every vertex is eventually classified. The corrected solution shows instead:

- Only vertices that can propagate backward from terminal losses enter $W$ or $L$
- Vertices in closed cycles with no terminal access satisfy neither fixed-point condition
- These remain correctly unclassified in $U$

This completes the corrected construction.
