**Exercise 19.** [*18*] In Algorithm `T` we examine the front position of the queue in step `T5`, but do not remove that element from the queue until step `T7`. What would happen if we set `F ← QLINK[F]` at the conclusion of step `T5`, instead of in `T7`?

**Solve time:** 1m06s

## Solution

In Algorithm `T`, step `T5` accesses the front element of the queue via the pointer `F` without changing the queue structure, while step `T7` performs the structural update that removes that front element by executing

$$
F \leftarrow \text{QLINK}[F].
$$

Suppose instead that at the conclusion of `T5` we perform

$$
F \leftarrow \text{QLINK}[F],
$$

and still execute `T7` as originally defined.

Let the queue at the beginning of `T5` have front node at address $F$ and successor node at address $\text{QLINK}[F] = G$. After replacing `T5` as proposed, the value of $F$ becomes $G$ immediately after the examination step. The original front node, say $P$, is no longer referenced by the queue pointer.

When execution reaches `T7`, the algorithm applies the original deletion step, which assigns

$$
F \leftarrow \text{QLINK}[F].
$$

However, at this moment $F = G$, so `T7` sets

$$
F \leftarrow \text{QLINK}[G],
$$

which is the successor of the second node in the original queue position. The node $G$ is therefore never removed from the queue by `T7`, and the node $P$ has already been bypassed by the premature update in `T5`.

Thus two structural defects occur simultaneously. First, the original front node $P$ becomes unreachable from the queue without being properly processed by the deletion step, since no subsequent step restores or re-links it. Second, the deletion step at `T7` removes the second node of the original queue instead of the first, advancing the queue by two positions in one logical iteration.

The FIFO order maintained by Algorithm `T` is therefore destroyed, since the algorithm processes nodes in the order of their successors rather than the original front-to-back sequence. The queue pointer `F` no longer matches the element being examined during the remainder of the iteration, so any step between `T5` and `T7` that assumes `F` still refers to the examined node becomes inconsistent with the actual queue state.

This completes the proof. ∎
