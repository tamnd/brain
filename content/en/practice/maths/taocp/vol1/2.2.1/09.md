**Exercise 9.** [*M20*] Let $b_n$ be the number of permutations on $n$ elements obtainable by the use of an input-restricted deque. (Note that $b_4 = 22$, as shown in exercise 7.) Show that $b_n$ is also the number of permutations on $n$ elements with an output-restricted deque.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m09s  

---

## Solution

Fix a convention. In an input-restricted deque (IRD), insertions are allowed only at the right end, and deletions are allowed at both ends. In an output-restricted deque (ORD), insertions are allowed at both ends, and deletions are allowed only at the left end.

Let $\mathcal{P}_I(n)$ denote the set of permutations of $12\ldots n$ obtainable by an IRD, and let $\mathcal{P}_O(n)$ denote the set obtainable by an ORD.

Define the reversal map $R$ on permutations by

$$
p_1 p_2 \cdots p_n \mapsto p_n \cdots p_2 p_1.
$$

We construct a bijection between $\mathcal{P}_I(n)$ and $\mathcal{P}_O(n)$ by showing that

$$
p \in \mathcal{P}_I(n) \iff R(p) \in \mathcal{P}_O(n).
$$

Consider any valid IRD operation sequence producing a permutation $p_1 p_2 \cdots p_n$. Read the sequence of operations in reverse order, and replace each operation by its inverse operation type, with left and right ends interchanged.

In the original IRD, every insertion occurs at the right end. In the reversed process, these insertions become deletions, and they must occur at the left end in the reversed time evolution, since reversing the deque interchanges left and right positions of all elements. Since ORD permits deletions only at the left end, these reversed operations are admissible.

Similarly, every deletion in the IRD occurs at either end. In the reversed process, these become insertions, and the reversed model allows insertions at both ends, which matches the ORD rule.

Thus every IRD operation sequence corresponds to a valid ORD operation sequence acting on a horizontally reflected configuration of the deque.

Track the output order. Each element removed in the IRD is output in some order producing $p_1 p_2 \cdots p_n$. Under reversal of time and left-right symmetry, the removal order is reversed, producing $p_n \cdots p_2 p_1$. Hence the resulting permutation in the ORD model is $R(p)$.

The same transformation is reversible: applying the same reversal procedure to an ORD sequence yields a valid IRD sequence producing the reversed permutation again. This establishes a one-to-one correspondence between IRD-generated permutations and ORD-generated permutations via $R$.

Therefore the mapping $p \mapsto R(p)$ is a bijection from $\mathcal{P}_I(n)$ onto $\mathcal{P}_O(n)$, so $|\mathcal{P}_I(n)| = |\mathcal{P}_O(n)|$. Since $b_n = |\mathcal{P}_I(n)|$, the same number counts ORD permutations.

This completes the proof. ∎
