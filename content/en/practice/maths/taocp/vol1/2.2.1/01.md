[Section 2.2.1: Stacks, Queues, and Deques](../)

**Exercise 1.** [*06*] An input-restricted deque is a linear list in which items may be inserted at one end but removed from either end; clearly an input-restricted deque can operate either as a stack or as a queue, if we consistently remove all items from one of the two ends. Can an output-restricted deque also be operated either as a stack or as a queue?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m12s  

---

An output-restricted deque permits insertions at both ends and deletions at only one fixed end. Let this deletion end be called the front end.

To operate as a queue, we perform all insertions at the rear end and all deletions at the front end. These choices are consistent with the allowed operations, since insertion at the rear is permitted and deletion at the front is the only deletion operation available; thus the usual FIFO behavior is obtained.

To operate as a stack, we treat the front end as the top. Insertions are performed only at the front end, which is allowed since insertions are permitted at both ends, and deletions are also performed at the front end. This yields LIFO behavior.

$\boxed{\text{Yes, an output-restricted deque can operate as both a stack and a queue.}}$
