**Exercise 12.** [*HM34*] Compute the asymptotic values of the quantities $a_n$ and $b_n$ in exercises 4 and 11.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m07s  

---

## Setup

Let $a_n$ denote the number of permutations of $12\ldots n$ obtainable by a stack, as in Exercise 4. Let $b_n$ denote the number of permutations obtainable by an output-restricted deque, equivalently the number of admissible sequences of length $2n$ over the symbols $S$, $Q$, $X$ described in Exercise 11.

The goal is to determine asymptotic expressions for $a_n$ and $b_n$ as $n \to \infty$.

## Solution

### Asymptotics of $a_n$

Stack-generable permutations are counted by Catalan numbers. The standard decomposition of stack operations leads to the recurrence

$$
a_{n+1} = \sum_{k=0}^n a_k a_{n-k}, \quad a_0 = 1,
$$

so $a_n$ is the $n$th Catalan number

$$
a_n = C_n = \frac{1}{n+1} \binom{2n}{n}.
$$

Using Stirling’s formula

$$
n! \sim \sqrt{2\pi n}\left(\frac{n}{e}\right)^n,
$$

we obtain

$$
\binom{2n}{n}
= \frac{(2n)!}{(n!)^2}
\sim \frac{\sqrt{4\pi n}(2n)^{2n}e^{-2n}}{2\pi n \, n^{2n}e^{-2n}}
= \frac{4^n}{\sqrt{\pi n}}.
$$

Hence

$$
a_n = \frac{1}{n+1}\binom{2n}{n}
\sim \frac{1}{n}\cdot \frac{4^n}{\sqrt{\pi n}}
= \frac{4^n}{\sqrt{\pi}\,n^{3/2}}.
$$

Therefore,

$$
a_n \sim \frac{4^n}{\sqrt{\pi}\,n^{3/2}}.
$$

### Asymptotics of $b_n$

An admissible sequence contributing to $b_n$ has length $2n$, consisting of $n$ occurrences of $S$ or $Q$ (insertions) and $n$ occurrences of $X$ (deletions). The admissibility condition is that at every prefix, the number of deletions does not exceed the number of insertions, and the sequence ends with equality.

Ignoring the distinction between $S$ and $Q$, the underlying structure is a Dyck path of semilength $n$, hence counted by $C_n$.

For each insertion step, the symbol is either $S$ or $Q$, independently of the admissibility constraint, since both represent valid insertions at one of two ends and do not affect the balance condition. Thus each of the $n$ insertion steps carries two independent choices, contributing a factor $2^n$.

Hence

$$
b_n = 2^n C_n.
$$

Using the asymptotic form of $C_n$,

$$
C_n \sim \frac{4^n}{\sqrt{\pi}\,n^{3/2}},
$$

we obtain

$$
b_n \sim 2^n \cdot \frac{4^n}{\sqrt{\pi}\,n^{3/2}}
= \frac{8^n}{\sqrt{\pi}\,n^{3/2}}.
$$

## Verification

The Catalan asymptotic for $C_n$ follows directly from Stirling’s approximation applied to $\binom{2n}{n}/(n+1)$, with the factor $(n+1)^{-1}$ contributing only a multiplicative correction asymptotic to $n^{-1}$.

For $b_n$, the decomposition into a Dyck path with $n$ independent binary choices at insertion steps preserves bijectivity between admissible sequences and colored Dyck paths, since the coloring of $S$ and $Q$ does not affect feasibility constraints or matching structure with $X$ steps. The multiplicative factor $2^n$ therefore applies to each valid structure counted by $C_n$.

## Final results

$$
\boxed{a_n \sim \frac{4^n}{\sqrt{\pi}\,n^{3/2}}}
$$

$$
\boxed{b_n \sim \frac{8^n}{\sqrt{\pi}\,n^{3/2}}}
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
