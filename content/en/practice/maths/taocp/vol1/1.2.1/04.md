**Exercise 4.** [*20*] Prove that, in addition to Eq. (3), Fibonacci numbers satisfy $F_n \ge \phi^{n-2}$ for all positive integers $n$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m15s

## Solution

Let $P(n)$ denote the statement $F_n \ge \phi^{,n-2}$ for all positive integers $n$, where $F_0 = 0$, $F_1 = 1$, $F_{n+1} = F_n + F_{n-1}$, and $\phi = (1 + \sqrt{5})/2$.

For $n = 1$, $F_1 = 1$ and $\phi^{-1} = 1/\phi < 1$, so $F_1 \ge \phi^{1-2} = \phi^{-1}$.

For $n = 2$, $F_2 = 1$ and $\phi^0 = 1$, so $F_2 \ge \phi^{2-2}$.

Assume that for some integer $n \ge 2$, the inequalities $F_k \ge \phi^{k-2}$ hold for all $1 \le k \le n$. In particular,

$$
F_n \ge \phi^{n-2}, \quad F_{n-1} \ge \phi^{n-3}.
$$

Using the defining recurrence $F_{n+1} = F_n + F_{n-1}$, we obtain

$$
F_{n+1} \ge \phi^{n-2} + \phi^{n-3} = \phi^{n-3}(\phi + 1).
$$

From the identity $1 + \phi = \phi^2$, it follows that

$$
\phi^{n-3}(\phi + 1) = \phi^{n-3}\phi^2 = \phi^{n-1}.
$$

Hence $F_{n+1} \ge \phi^{n-1}$, which is $P(n+1)$.

By induction, $F_n \ge \phi^{n-2}$ holds for all positive integers $n$.

This completes the proof. ∎
