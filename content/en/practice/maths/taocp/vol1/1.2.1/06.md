[Section 1.2.1: Mathematical Induction](../)

**Exercise 6.** [*20*] Prove that if Eqs. (6) hold just before step E4, they hold afterwards also.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m14s  

---

## Solution

Before step E4, equations (6) state that

$a' m + b' n = c,$

$a m + b n = d.$

Step E2 supplies integers $q$ and $r$ such that

$c = qd + r.$

After step E4, the updated variables are

$a'_{\text{new}} = a,\quad b'_{\text{new}} = b,\quad c_{\text{new}} = d,$

$a_{\text{new}} = a' - qa,\quad b_{\text{new}} = b' - qb,\quad d_{\text{new}} = r.$

For the first relation after E4,

$a'_{\text{new}} m + b'_{\text{new}} n = a m + b n.$

Using the second equation in (6), this equals $d$. Since $c_{\text{new}} = d$, the first invariant relation becomes

$a'_{\text{new}} m + b'_{\text{new}} n = c_{\text{new}}.$

For the second relation after E4,

$$
\begin{aligned}
a_{\text{new}} m + b_{\text{new}} n
&= (a' - qa)m + (b' - qb)n \\
&= (a' m + b' n) - q(am + bn).
\end{aligned}
$$

Substituting the equations in (6) gives

$a_{\text{new}} m + b_{\text{new}} n = c - qd.$

Since $c = qd + r$, this reduces to

$a_{\text{new}} m + b_{\text{new}} n = r.$

With $d_{\text{new}} = r$, the second invariant relation becomes

$a_{\text{new}} m + b_{\text{new}} n = d_{\text{new}}.$

Both equations (6) therefore remain valid after step E4. This completes the proof. ∎
