[Section 1.2.1: Mathematical Induction](../)

**Exercise 3.** [*18*] The following proof by induction seems correct, but for some reason the equation for $n = 6$ gives $\frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{6} + \frac{1}{12} + \frac{1}{20} + \frac{1}{30} = \frac{5}{6}$ on the left-hand side, and $\frac{3}{2} - \frac{1}{6} = \frac{4}{3}$ on the right-hand side. Can you find a mistake? “**Theorem.**

$$\frac{1}{1 \times 2} + \frac{1}{2 \times 3} + \cdots + \frac{1}{(n - 1) \times n} = \frac{3}{2} - \frac{1}{n}.$$

*Proof.* We use induction on $n$. For $n = 1$, clearly $3/2 - 1/n = 1/(1 \times 2)$; and, assuming that the theorem is true for $n$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{1}{1 \times 2} + \cdots + \frac{1}{(n - 1) \times n} + \frac{1}{n \times (n + 1)} \\
= \frac{3}{2} - \frac{1}{n} + \frac{1}{n(n + 1)} &= \frac{3}{2} - \frac{1}{n} + \left( \frac{1}{n} - \frac{1}{n + 1} \right) = \frac{3}{2} - \frac{1}{n + 1} \text{.”}
\end{aligned}
$$

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m14s

## Solution

Let

$$
S_n = \frac{1}{1 \times 2} + \frac{1}{2 \times 3} + \cdots + \frac{1}{(n - 1)n}.
$$

The inductive step in the given argument transforms the last term using

$$
\frac{1}{n(n+1)} = \frac{1}{n} - \frac{1}{n+1},
$$

and hence produces a telescoping adjustment. This computation is correct and yields that if

$$
S_n = \frac{3}{2} - \frac{1}{n},
$$

then

$$
S_{n+1} = \frac{3}{2} - \frac{1}{n+1}.
$$

The failure occurs at the starting point. For $n = 1$, the sum $S_1$ has no terms, hence

$$
S_1 = 0.
$$

However, the proposed formula gives

$$
\frac{3}{2} - \frac{1}{1} = \frac{1}{2},
$$

so the claimed identity does not hold at $n = 1$.

Since induction requires both a correct base case and a correct inductive step applied to a true initial statement, the argument does not establish the theorem.

The numerical discrepancy at $n = 6$ reflects that the stated formula is false. In fact, the correct telescoping identity is

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{n-1} \frac{1}{k(k+1)} = 1 - \frac{1}{n},
$$

so the constant $\frac{3}{2}$ in the proposed statement is incorrect.

This completes the solution. ∎
