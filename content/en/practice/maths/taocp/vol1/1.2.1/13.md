**Exercise 13.** &#9654; [*M23*] Extend Algorithm E by adding a new variable $T$ and adding the operation “$T \leftarrow T + 1$” at the beginning of each step. (Thus, $T$ is like a clock, counting the number of steps executed.) Assume that $T$ is initially zero, so that assertion *A1* in Fig. 4 becomes “$m > 0$, $n > 0$, $T = 0$.” The additional condition “$T = 1$” should similarly be appended to *A2*. Show how to append additional conditions to the assertions in such a way that any one of *A1*, *A2*, $\ldots$, *A6* implies $T \le 3n$, and such that the inductive proof can still be carried out. (Hence the computation must terminate in at most $3n$ steps.)

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m30s

## Solution

Let the original Algorithm E be augmented so that at the beginning of every execution of each step E1, E2, E3, and E4, the operation $T \leftarrow T + 1$ is performed, with $T = 0$ initially.

Let $n$ denote the initial value of the variable $d$ after step E1, so initially $d = n$.

Define the invariant statement appended to each of the assertions $A_1, A_2, \ldots, A_6$ as

$$
T \le 3(n - d).
$$

This condition is attached uniformly to each assertion without altering the logical structure of the correctness proof for Algorithm E.

At initialization, after E1, we have $d = n$ and $T = 0$, so

$$
T = 0 = 3(n - n),
$$

hence the strengthened assertion holds at $A_1$.

Assume the assertion holds at the beginning of some execution of step E2 with current value $d$. One iteration of the loop performs the sequence E2, possibly E3, and if $r \ne 0$, then E4. Each of these steps increases $T$ by exactly $1$.

When E2 is executed, the quotient and remainder satisfy $c = qd + r$ with $0 \le r < d$. If $r = 0$, the algorithm terminates immediately after E3, so the loop body contributes exactly two increments of $T$ beyond the entry point to E2. If $r \ne 0$, then E4 is executed, contributing a third increment of $T$.

In both cases, one complete iteration of the Euclidean reduction strictly replaces the pair $(c, d)$ by $(d, r)$ with $0 \le r < d$, so the new value of $d$ decreases by at least $1$.

Let $d'$ denote the new value of $d$ after one iteration. Then $d' \le d - 1$, so

$$
n - d' \ge (n - d) + 1.
$$

Since at most three increments of $T$ occur during this iteration, the updated value $T'$ satisfies

$$
T' \le T + 3 \le 3(n - d) + 3 = 3(n - d + 1) \le 3(n - d').
$$

Thus the invariant $T \le 3(n - d)$ is preserved through each transition among assertions $A_2$ through $A_6$, since each of these assertions describes a configuration immediately before or after one of the steps E2, E3, or E4, and each such transition corresponds to either completion of one iteration or preparation of the next.

Since $d$ is a positive integer that strictly decreases whenever E4 is executed, at most $n$ such decreases can occur before termination. Each decrease is associated with at most three increments of $T$, so along any computation path leading to termination,

$$
T \le 3n.
$$

Every assertion $A_i$ implies $d \ge 0$, hence $n - d \le n$, and therefore

$$
T \le 3(n - d) \le 3n.
$$

This establishes that any of $A_1, A_2, \ldots, A_6$ implies $T \le 3n$, and the inductive verification of correctness for Algorithm E remains unchanged since the added variable $T$ evolves independently of the arithmetic relations among $a, b, c, d$. This completes the proof. ∎
