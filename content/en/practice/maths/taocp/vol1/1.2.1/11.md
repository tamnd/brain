\frac{1^3}{1^4 + 4} - \frac{3^3}{3^4 + 4} + \frac{5^3}{5^4 + 4} - \cdots + \frac{(-1)^n(2n + 1)^3}{(2n + 1)^4 + 4}.

### 1. Correcting the algebraic decomposition

The previous solution failed because the proposed identity was incorrect. We restart by finding the correct partial fraction decomposition of

$$
\frac{x^3}{x^4+4}, \qquad x \in \mathbb{R}.
$$

Factor

$$
x^4+4=(x^2+2x+2)(x^2-2x+2).
$$

Seek

$$
\frac{x^3}{x^4+4}=\frac{ax+b}{x^2+2x+2}+\frac{cx+d}{x^2-2x+2}.
$$

Multiplying through by $(x^2+2x+2)(x^2-2x+2)$ gives

$$
x^3=(ax+b)(x^2-2x+2)+(cx+d)(x^2+2x+2).
$$

Expanding,

$$
(ax+b)(x^2-2x+2)=a x^3+(-2a+b)x^2+(2a-2b)x+2b,
$$

$$
(cx+d)(x^2+2x+2)=c x^3+(2c+d)x^2+(2c+2d)x+2d.
$$

Comparing coefficients yields:

$$
a+c=1,\quad -2a+b+2c+d=0,\quad 2a-2b+2c+2d=0,\quad b+d=0.
$$

From $b+d=0$, let $d=-b$. Then

$$
-2a+b+2c-b=-2a+2c=0 \Rightarrow c=a,
$$

$$
a+c=1 \Rightarrow a=c=\frac12.
$$

Finally,

$$
2a-2b+2c+2d=2a+2c-4b=2-4b=0 \Rightarrow b=\frac12,\quad d=-\frac12.
$$

Hence the correct identity is

$$
\frac{x^3}{x^4+4}
=\frac12\frac{x+1}{x^2+2x+2}+\frac12\frac{x-1}{x^2-2x+2}.
$$

This corrects the critical error in the original solution.

### 2. Substitution $x_k=2k+1$

Let

$$
x_k=2k+1,\quad A_k=x_k^2-2x_k+2=4k^2+1,\quad B_k=x_k^2+2x_k+2=4k^2+8k+5.
$$

Note the shift relation

$$
B_k=A_{k+1}.
$$

Using the correct decomposition,

$$
\frac{x_k^3}{x_k^4+4}
=\frac12\left(\frac{x_k+1}{A_{k+1}}+\frac{x_k-1}{A_k}\right).
$$

Since $x_k=2k+1$,

$$
x_k+1=2k+2,\quad x_k-1=2k.
$$

Thus

$$
\frac{(2k+1)^3}{(2k+1)^4+4}
=\frac12\left(\frac{2k+2}{A_{k+1}}+\frac{2k}{A_k}\right).
$$

Multiplying by $(-1)^k$,

$$
(-1)^k\frac{(2k+1)^3}{(2k+1)^4+4}
=\frac12\left((-1)^k\frac{2k+2}{A_{k+1}}+(-1)^k\frac{2k}{A_k}\right).
$$

### 3. Telescoping structure

Split the sum

$$
S_n=\sum_{k=0}^n (-1)^k\frac{(2k+1)^3}{(2k+1)^4+4}
$$

into two parts:

$$
S_n=\frac12\sum_{k=0}^n (-1)^k\frac{2k+2}{A_{k+1}}+\frac12\sum_{k=0}^n (-1)^k\frac{2k}{A_k}.
$$

Reindex the first sum with $j=k+1$:

$$
\frac12\sum_{j=1}^{n+1} (-1)^{j-1}\frac{2j}{A_j}.
$$

Thus

$$
S_n=\frac12\left[\sum_{j=1}^{n+1} (-1)^{j-1}\frac{2j}{A_j}+\sum_{k=0}^n (-1)^k\frac{2k}{A_k}\right].
$$

Combine terms for $1\le k\le n$:

$$
(-1)^{k-1}\frac{2k}{A_k}+(-1)^k\frac{2k}{A_k}=0.
$$

Hence all interior terms cancel.

The remaining boundary terms are:

- from the second sum, $k=0$, which is $0$,
- from the first sum, $k=n+1$, giving $(-1)^n \frac{2(n+1)}{A_{n+1}}$.

Therefore,

$$
S_n=\frac12(-1)^n\frac{2(n+1)}{A_{n+1}}.
$$

### 4. Final simplification

Since $A_{n+1}=4(n+1)^2+1=(2n+2)^2+1$,

$$
S_n=\frac{(-1)^n(n+1)}{(2n+2)^2+1}.
$$

### Final answer

$$
\boxed{
S_n=\frac{(-1)^n(n+1)}{(2n+2)^2+1}
}
$$

This correct formula follows from a valid partial fraction decomposition and a complete telescoping cancellation argument.
