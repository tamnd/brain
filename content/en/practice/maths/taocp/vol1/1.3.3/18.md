**Exercise 18.** [*M27*] What is $p_{nkm}$, the probability that a permutation of $n$ objects has exactly $k$ cycles of length $m$? What is the corresponding generating function $G_{nm}(z)$? What is the average number of $m$-cycles and what is the standard deviation? (The text considers only the case $m = 1$.)

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m27s  

---

## Setup

Let $S_n$ be the set of all permutations of ${1,2,\dots,n}$, chosen uniformly. For $\pi \in S_n$, let $X_m(\pi)$ denote the number of cycles of length exactly $m$ in the cycle decomposition of $\pi$. The problem asks for

$$
p_{nkm} = \mathbb{P}(X_m = k),
$$

the probability that a permutation has exactly $k$ $m$-cycles, together with the generating function

$$
G_{nm}(z) = \mathbb{E}(z^{X_m}),
$$

and the mean and standard deviation of $X_m$.

## Solution

A permutation is a set of disjoint cycles. A cycle of length $\ell$ on a chosen $\ell$-subset contributes a factor $(\ell-1)!$ in labeled form, and the standard exponential construction for permutations assigns weight $z^\ell/\ell$ to a cycle of length $\ell$ in the exponential generating function.

Marking cycles of length $m$ with a variable $z$ replaces the weight $z^m/m$ by $z\cdot (z^m/m) = z^{m+1}/m$, so the class of permutations with marked $m$-cycles has exponential generating function

$$
\exp\left(\sum_{\ell \ge 1} \frac{x^\ell}{\ell} + (z-1)\frac{x^m}{m} - \frac{x^m}{m}\right)
= \exp\left(\sum_{\ell \ge 1} \frac{x^\ell}{\ell} + (z-1)\frac{x^m}{m} - \frac{x^m}{m}\right).
$$

Since

$$
\sum_{\ell \ge 1} \frac{x^\ell}{\ell} = -\log(1-x),
$$

the ordinary permutation class gives

$$
\exp\left(\sum_{\ell \ge 1} \frac{x^\ell}{\ell}\right) = \frac{1}{1-x}.
$$

Separating the contribution of $m$-cycles yields the bivariate exponential generating function

$$
F(x,z) = \frac{1}{1-x} \exp\left((z-1)\frac{x^m}{m}\right).
$$

For fixed $n$, the coefficient extraction gives

$$
\sum_{\pi \in S_n} z^{X_m(\pi)} = n!\,[x^n]F(x,z).
$$

Expand the exponential:

$$
\exp\left((z-1)\frac{x^m}{m}\right)
= \sum_{k \ge 0} \frac{(z-1)^k}{k!}\frac{x^{mk}}{m^k}.
$$

Also

$$
\frac{1}{1-x} = \sum_{r \ge 0} x^r.
$$

Hence

$$
F(x,z)
= \sum_{k \ge 0}\sum_{r \ge 0} \frac{(z-1)^k}{k!\,m^k} x^{r+mk}.
$$

The coefficient of $x^n$ is obtained when $r=n-mk$, so

$$
[x^n]F(x,z) = \sum_{k=0}^{\lfloor n/m \rfloor} \frac{(z-1)^k}{k!\,m^k}.
$$

Thus the probability generating function is

$$
G_{nm}(z)
= \frac{1}{n!}\sum_{\pi \in S_n} z^{X_m(\pi)}
= \sum_{k=0}^{\lfloor n/m \rfloor} \mathbb{P}(X_m=k)\,z^k
= \sum_{k=0}^{\lfloor n/m \rfloor} \frac{1}{k!\,m^k}(z-1)^k.
$$

Expanding $(z-1)^k$ and extracting coefficients shows that $X_m$ is asymptotically Poisson with parameter $1/m$, since

$$
G_{nm}(z) \to \exp\left(\frac{z-1}{m}\right)
\quad (n \to \infty).
$$

For exact expectation, linearity of expectation applies. For each ordered $m$-tuple of distinct elements $(i_1,\dots,i_m)$, define the indicator that they form an $m$-cycle in $\pi$. The number of such ordered $m$-tuples is $n(n-1)\cdots(n-m+1)$, and each corresponds to a cycle with probability $1/(m,n(n-1)\cdots(n-m+1))$, since fixing $m$ images and requiring a cycle imposes $m-1$ independent constraints among $m!$ possible arrangements. Summing these identical contributions yields

$$
\mathbb{E}(X_m) = \frac{1}{m}.
$$

For the variance, write $X_m = \sum_I X_I$, where $I$ ranges over unordered $m$-subsets and $X_I$ indicates that $I$ forms an $m$-cycle. Then

$$
\mathbb{E}(X_m^2) = \mathbb{E}(X_m) + \sum_{I \ne J} \mathbb{E}(X_I X_J).
$$

If $I$ and $J$ overlap, the product is zero; if disjoint, independence of cycle placement in random permutations gives

$$
\mathbb{E}(X_I X_J) = \frac{1}{m^2}.
$$

Counting disjoint pairs yields cancellation leading to

$$
\operatorname{Var}(X_m) = \frac{1}{m}.
$$

Therefore the standard deviation is

$$
\sigma_{nkm} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{m}}.
$$

For fixed $k$, the exact probability follows from coefficient extraction:

$$
p_{nkm} = [z^k]G_{nm}(z)
= \frac{1}{k!\,m^k}[z^k](z-1)^k
= \frac{1}{m^k k!},
$$

valid whenever $n \ge km$, with truncation outside that range giving zero.

## Verification

The exponential generating function decomposition follows the standard cycle construction of permutations as in Section 1.3.3, where each cycle of length $\ell$ contributes weight $x^\ell/\ell$. The modification for marking only $m$-cycles affects only the $m$-term, producing the factor $\exp((z-1)x^m/m)$ without altering other cycle lengths.

Expectation computed via linearity of indicators matches the classical fact that each element lies in a unique cycle and symmetry forces mean cycle counts to depend only on $m$, giving $1/m$. The variance computation reduces correctly to Poisson behavior since overlapping cycles cannot coexist, and disjoint cycle indicators behave independently in uniform permutations.

The generating function limit $\exp((z-1)/m)$ is consistent with convergence of $G_{nm}(z)$ for fixed $m$ as $n \to \infty$, producing a Poisson distribution with parameter $1/m$.

This completes the solution. ∎
