**Exercise 27.** [*M20*] Use the principle of inclusion and exclusion to count the number of integers $n$ in the range $0 \le n < am_1m_2\cdots m_t$ that are not divisible by any of $m_1, m_2, \ldots, m_t$. Here $m_1, m_2, \ldots, m_t$, and $a$ are positive integers, with $m_j \perp m_k$ when $j \ne k$.

## Solution

Let $M = m_1 m_2 \cdots m_t$. The condition $m_j \perp m_k$ for $j \ne k$ implies that for any subset $I \subseteq {1,\dots,t}$, the least common multiple of ${m_i : i \in I}$ equals $\prod_{i \in I} m_i$.

Let $S$ be the set of integers $n$ satisfying $0 \le n < aM$. For each $j$, let $A_j$ be the set of integers in $S$ divisible by $m_j$. The required count equals

$$
|S| - \left|\bigcup_{j=1}^t A_j\right|.
$$

The total size of $S$ is $|S| = aM$.

For each $j$, every multiple of $m_j$ in $S$ has the form $n = m_j k$ with $0 \le k < \frac{aM}{m_j}$, hence

$$
|A_j| = \frac{aM}{m_j}.
$$

For any distinct indices $j_1,\dots,j_r$, an integer lies in $A_{j_1} \cap \cdots \cap A_{j_r}$ precisely when it is divisible by $\prod_{s=1}^r m_{j_s}$. The number of such multiples in $S$ is

$$
|A_{j_1} \cap \cdots \cap A_{j_r}| = \frac{aM}{\prod_{s=1}^r m_{j_s}}.
$$

Applying the principle of inclusion and exclusion gives

$$
\left|\bigcup_{j=1}^t A_j\right|
=
\sum_{r=1}^t (-1)^{r-1}
\sum_{1 \le j_1 < \cdots < j_r \le t}
\frac{aM}{m_{j_1} \cdots m_{j_r}}.
$$

Hence the number of integers not divisible by any $m_j$ equals

$$
aM + \sum_{r=1}^t (-1)^r
\sum_{1 \le j_1 < \cdots < j_r \le t}
\frac{aM}{m_{j_1} \cdots m_{j_r}}.
$$

Factor $aM$:

$$
aM \left(
1 + \sum_{r=1}^t (-1)^r
\sum_{1 \le j_1 < \cdots < j_r \le t}
\frac{1}{m_{j_1} \cdots m_{j_r}}
\right).
$$

The inner expression expands as a product:

$$
\prod_{j=1}^t \left(1 - \frac{1}{m_j}\right),
$$

since expanding the product selects either $1$ or $-\frac{1}{m_j}$ from each factor and produces all subset terms with correct sign.

Thus the required count is

$$
aM \prod_{j=1}^t \left(1 - \frac{1}{m_j}\right).
$$

Substituting $M = \prod_{j=1}^t m_j$ yields

$$
a \prod_{j=1}^t (m_j - 1).
$$

Therefore the number of integers $n$ in $0 \le n < a m_1 m_2 \cdots m_t$ not divisible by any $m_j$ is

$$
\boxed{a \prod_{j=1}^t (m_j - 1)}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
