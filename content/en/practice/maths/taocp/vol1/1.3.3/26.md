## Solution

For each element $x$ in the underlying universe, let $t(x)$ denote the number of sets among $S_1,S_2,\ldots,S_M$ that contain $x$. The required quantity is the number of elements $x$ such that $t(x)=r$.

For each integer $k$ with $0 \le k \le M$, define

$$
F_k = \sum_{1 \le i_1 < i_2 < \cdots < i_k \le M} \left| S_{i_1} \cap S_{i_2} \cap \cdots \cap S_{i_k} \right|.
$$

Each element $x$ that lies in exactly $t(x)$ of the sets contributes to every intersection of $k$ of those sets, and it is counted in exactly $\binom{t(x)}{k}$ such $k$-fold intersections. Hence

$$
F_k = \sum_x \binom{t(x)}{k}.
$$

$$
F_k = \sum_{j=k}^M \binom{j}{k} g_j.
$$
