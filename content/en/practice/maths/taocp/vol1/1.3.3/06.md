**Exercise 6.** [*M28*] What changes are made to the timing of Program $A$ if we remove the assumption that all blank words occur at the extreme right?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 7m15s  

---

## Setup

Program A is analyzed in the text under the assumption that all blank words occur at the extreme right of the input. Under this assumption, every nonblank input word is followed only by nonblank words until the first blank is encountered, and the quantity

$$
Y=\text{number of nonblank input words}
$$

satisfies equation (14),

$$
J=Y-2B,
$$

where $J$ is the frequency of execution of line $78$ and $B$ is the number of left parentheses in the input.

The problem asks how the timing changes when blank words may occur anywhere in the input.

The timing formula before substitution is

$$
(7 + 5A + 6B + 7C + 2D + E + 3F + 4G + 8H + 6J + 3K + 4L + 3P + 4Q + 6R + 2S)u.
\tag{7}
$$

Using (8), (12), (13), (15), and (16), this becomes

$$
\begin{aligned}
T
&=(8+18B+18R+7G+7L+7P+2S+6J)u.
\end{aligned}
\tag{17}
$$

Hence only the interpretation of $J$ can be affected by allowing internal blanks.

## Solution

The only place where the assumption on blanks is used is in the evaluation of $J$.

Variable $J$ counts executions of line $78$:

```
78 CMP3 SIZE
```

inside the search loop consisting of lines $74$--$79$. Every pass through this loop corresponds to examination of one word of the input formula while searching for an occurrence of the current element.

Under the original assumption, the words examined by this loop are exactly the nonblank words. Since there are $Y$ nonblank words, and since each of the $2B$ parenthesis words cannot be matched by line $75$, equation (14) follows:

$$
J=Y-2B.
$$

When blanks may occur in arbitrary positions, line $75$

```
75 CMPX PERM,3(1:5)
```

must also compare against blank words. Such words contribute to the frequency count of line $78$, because the loop still advances through them one at a time. Therefore $J$ is no longer determined by the number of nonblank words alone.

Let

$$
Z=\text{total number of words in the input formula},
$$

including blank words and excluding the terminal word "=". Since every execution of the search loop examines one stored word, the same reasoning that produced (14) now yields

$$
J=Z-2B.
\tag{18}
$$

The difference is

$$
\Delta J=(Z-Y).
\tag{19}
$$

Since the coefficient of $J$ in (17) is $6$, the increase in running time is

$$
6(Z-Y)u.
\tag{20}
$$

Substituting (18) into (17) gives the revised timing formula

$$
T
=
\bigl(
8+6Z+6B+18R+7G+7L+7P+2S
\bigr)u.
\tag{21}
$$

Equivalently, relative to the original timing, each blank word that is not at the extreme right contributes an additional

$$
6u
$$

to the running time.

## Verification

Equation (18) counts exactly the iterations of the loop at lines $74$--$79$.

Each iteration executes line $78$ once. The loop advances $3$ through successive stored words until the end of the formula is reached. Internal blank words are neither skipped nor treated specially; they are examined exactly as parenthesis words and element words are examined. Hence every stored word contributes one unit to $J$, except the $2B$ parenthesis words, whose contribution is already removed in the derivation leading to (14). Replacing the count of nonblank words $Y$ by the total word count $Z$ gives (18).

Multiplying the change $\Delta J=Z-Y$ by the coefficient $6$ of $J$ in (17) yields the timing increase $6(Z-Y)u$, establishing (20).

This completes the proof.

∎

## Notes

The structure of Program A is unchanged. Allowing blanks in arbitrary positions affects only the frequency variable $J$, because the search loop must examine those blanks. All other frequency relations derived in the text remain valid.
