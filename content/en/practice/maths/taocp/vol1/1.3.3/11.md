**Exercise 11.** [*15*] Find a simple rule for writing $\pi^{-}$ in cycle form, if the permutation $\pi$ is given in cycle form.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 7m47s

## Solution

Let

$$
\pi=(x_1\,x_2\,\ldots\,x_n)
$$

be a cycle. By definition of cycle notation,

$$
x_1\mapsto x_2,\quad
x_2\mapsto x_3,\quad
\ldots,\quad
x_{n-1}\mapsto x_n,\quad
x_n\mapsto x_1.
$$

The inverse permutation $\pi^{-1}$ must reverse each of these mappings. Therefore

$$
x_2\mapsto x_1,\quad
x_3\mapsto x_2,\quad
\ldots,\quad
x_n\mapsto x_{n-1},\quad
x_1\mapsto x_n.
$$

This is precisely the cycle

$$
(x_1\,x_n\,x_{n-1}\,\ldots\,x_2).
$$

Indeed, in this cycle $x_1$ goes to $x_n$, $x_n$ goes to $x_{n-1}$, and so on, while $x_2$ goes to $x_1$.

Now suppose

$$
\pi
=
(x_1\,x_2\,\ldots\,x_n)
(y_1\,y_2\,\ldots\,y_m)
\cdots
(z_1\,z_2\,\ldots\,z_r)
$$

is written as a product of disjoint cycles. Since disjoint cycles act on disjoint sets of elements, they commute. The inverse of $\pi$ is obtained by inverting each cycle separately:

$$
\pi^{-1}
=
(x_1\,x_n\,x_{n-1}\,\ldots\,x_2)
(y_1\,y_m\,y_{m-1}\,\ldots\,y_2)
\cdots
(z_1\,z_r\,z_{r-1}\,\ldots\,z_2).
$$

Thus the rule is:

\[
\boxed{\text{To obtain }\pi^{-1}\text{ in cycle form, reverse the order of the elements in each cycle.}}
\]

Equivalently, each cycle

$$
(a_1\,a_2\,\ldots\,a_k)
$$

is replaced by

$$
(a_1\,a_k\,a_{k-1}\,\ldots\,a_2).
$$

This completes the proof.

∎
