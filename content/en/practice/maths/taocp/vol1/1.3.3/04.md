[Section 1.3.3: Applications to Permutations](../)

**Exercise 4.** [*10*] Express $(abd)(ef)(acf)(bd)$ as a product of disjoint cycles.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 9m44s  

---

Using the left-to-right convention of Section 1.3.3, trace the images of the elements under

$(abd)(ef)(acf)(bd).$

For $a$,

$$
a \to b \to b \to c \to c,
$$

so $a \to c$. Next,

$$
c \to c \to c \to f \to f,
$$

so $c \to f$. Next,

$$
f \to f \to e \to e \to e,
$$

so $f \to e$. Next,

$$
e \to e \to f \to a \to a,
$$

so $e \to a$.

Hence one cycle is $(acfe)$.

For $b$,

$$
b \to d \to d \to d \to b,
$$

so $b \to b$.

For $d$,

$$
d \to a \to a \to c \to c,
$$

so $d \to c,

but $c$ already belongs to the cycle $(acfe)$; continuing from $d$ gives

d \to c \to f \to e \to a \to d,

hence $d$ belongs to the same cycle and the complete cycle is

(adcfe).

Therefore $b$ is the only fixed point, and the permutation is

\boxed{(adcfe)}.

The singleton cycle $(b)$ is conventionally omitted.
