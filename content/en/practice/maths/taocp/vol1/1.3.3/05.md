**Exercise 5.** [*M10*] Equation (3) shows several equivalent ways to express the same permutation in cycle form. How many different ways of writing that permutation are possible, if all singleton cycles are suppressed?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 7m27s  

---

The permutation `(acf)(bd)` consists of a $3$-cycle and a $2$-cycle. Any cycle of length $n$ can be written in $n$ ways by choosing the starting element; thus the $3$-cycle `(acf)` can be written in $3$ ways: `(acf)`, `(cfa)`, `(fac)`. The $2$-cycle `(bd)` can be written in $2$ ways: `(bd)`, `(db)`. Since the cycles are disjoint, their relative order can be interchanged, giving $2! = 2$ possibilities. Therefore the total number of distinct representations is

$$
3 \times 2 \times 2 = 12.
$$

Hence there are exactly $\boxed{12}$ ways to write this permutation in cycle form when all singleton cycles are suppressed.
