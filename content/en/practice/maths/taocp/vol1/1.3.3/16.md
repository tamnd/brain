**Exercise 16.** [*M15*] Start with the permutation 1324 in linear notation; convert it to canonical cycle form and then remove the parentheses; repeat this process until arriving at the original permutation. What permutations occur during this process?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 4m12s  

---

Let $T$ be the operation described:

1. Start with a permutation in linear notation.
2. Convert it to canonical cycle form.
3. Remove parentheses by listing cycles in canonical order (including fixed points as 1-cycles).

This defines a map $T : S_4 \to S_4$ on linear permutations.

## Step 1: Start

$$
\pi_0 = 1324.
$$

This means

$$
\pi_0(1)=1,\quad \pi_0(2)=3,\quad \pi_0(3)=2,\quad \pi_0(4)=4.
$$

## Step 2: Convert to canonical cycle form

Decompose $\pi_0$:

- $1 \mapsto 1$ gives $(1)$,
- $2 \mapsto 3 \mapsto 2$ gives $(23)$,
- $4 \mapsto 4$ gives $(4)$.

Thus the canonical cycle form is

$$
(1)(23)(4).
$$

## Step 3: Remove parentheses

Canonical order places cycles by least element, so we concatenate:

$$
(1)(23)(4) \;\mapsto\; 1\,2\,3\,4.
$$

Hence

$$
T(1324)=1234.
$$

## Step 4: Apply $T$ to $1234$

Start with

$$
\pi_1 = 1234.
$$

This is the identity permutation:

$$
\pi_1(i)=i \quad \text{for all } i.
$$

Cycle form:

$$
(1)(2)(3)(4).
$$

Removing parentheses:

$$
(1)(2)(3)(4) \mapsto 1234.
$$

Thus

$$
T(1234)=1234.
$$

So $1234$ is a fixed point of $T$.

## Step 5: Iteration

$$
1324 \xrightarrow{T} 1234 \xrightarrow{T} 1234 \xrightarrow{T} \cdots
$$

No further distinct linear permutations arise after the first step.

## Final answer

The permutations that occur in linear notation during the process are:

$$
1324,\; 1234.
$$

After the first application of the procedure, the process stabilizes at $1234$, which is a fixed point of the transformation.
