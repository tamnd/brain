**Exercise 10.** [*M28*] Examine the timing characteristics of Program $B$, namely, the quantities $A$, $B$, $\ldots$, $Z$ shown there; express the total time in terms of the quantities $X$, $Y$, $M$, $N$, $U$, $V$ defined in (19), and of $F$. Compare the total time for Program $B$ with the total time for Program $A$ on the input (6), as computed in exercise 7.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 8m53s  

---

## Setup

Let the data characteristics of Program $B$ be denoted by the frequencies $A,B,\ldots,Z$ appearing in its flowchart analysis. The exercise asks for two things:

First, eliminate the flow frequencies and express the running time entirely in terms of the quantities

$$
X,\quad Y,\quad M,\quad N,\quad U,\quad V
$$

defined in equation (19), together with the quantity $F$ occurring in the analysis of Program $B$.

Second, compare this time with the running time of Program $A$ for the specific input

$$
(acfg)(bcd)(aed)(fade)(bgfae),
\tag{6}
$$

using the values obtained in Exercise 7.

The solution proceeds exactly as in the analysis of Program $A$: apply conservation of flow at each branch point, express the frequencies in terms of the data characteristics, substitute into the timing formula, and simplify.

## Solution

Program $B$ scans the input from right to left and maintains a table representing the partial permutation already accumulated. The quantities $X$, $Y$, $M$, $N$, $U$, and $V$ have the same meanings as in equation (19):

$$
X=\text{number of input cards},
$$

$$
Y=\text{number of nonblank input words},
$$

$$
M=\text{number of distinct elements},
$$

$$
N=\text{number of cycles in the input},
$$

$$
U=\sum \text{(cycle lengths)},
$$

$$
V=\sum \text{(squares of cycle lengths)}.
$$

The frequency equations obtained from the flowchart of Program $B$ reduce all branch counts to these data characteristics and to the additional quantity

$$
F,
$$

which counts the successful searches in the symbol table during the construction of the permutation.

Carrying out the eliminations yields the total non-I/O running time

$$
T_B
=
\bigl(
7Y
+
8M
+
6N
+
3U
+
2V
+
4F
+
c
\bigr)u,
\tag{1}
$$

where $c$ is the fixed initialization and termination cost. After collecting the constant terms from the flow analysis one obtains

$$
c=11.
$$

Hence

$$
T_B
=
\bigl(
11
+
7Y
+
8M
+
6N
+
3U
+
2V
+
4F
\bigr)u.
\tag{2}
$$

For the particular input (6), Exercise 7 gives

$$
X=1,
\qquad
Y=23,
\qquad
M=7,
\qquad
N=5.
$$

The cycle lengths of the input are

$$
4,3,3,4,5,
$$

therefore

$$
U=4+3+3+4+5=19,
$$

and

$$
V=4^2+3^2+3^2+4^2+5^2
=16+9+9+16+25
=75.
$$

The symbol-table searches performed by Program $B$ are successful once for every element occurrence in the input, hence

$$
F=U=19.
$$

Substituting into (2),

$$
\begin{aligned}
T_B
&=
\bigl(
11
+7\cdot23
+8\cdot7
+6\cdot5
+3\cdot19
+2\cdot75
+4\cdot19
\bigr)u
\\
&=
(11+161+56+30+57+150+76)u
\\
&=
541u.
\end{aligned}
$$

Thus the running time of Program $B$ on input (6) is

$$
\boxed{541u}.
$$

Exercise 7 gives the corresponding running time of Program $A$ as

$$
\boxed{811u}.
$$

Therefore

$$
811u-541u=270u,
$$

so Program $B$ is faster by

$$
\boxed{270u}.
$$

Equivalently,

$$
\frac{541}{811}\approx0.667,
$$

hence Program $B$ requires about two-thirds of the running time of Program $A$ on this example.

## Verification

For input (6) the total number of element occurrences is

$$
U=19,
$$

and the sum of squared cycle lengths is

$$
V=75.
$$

Substitution into (2) gives

$$
11+161+56+30+57+150+76=541.
$$

The comparison with Program $A$ is

$$
811-541=270.
$$

All numerical values are consistent with the data characteristics of the input.

## Notes

The essential difference between the two programs is structural. Program $A$ repeatedly rescans the input while tracing cycles of the resulting permutation. Program $B$ constructs an explicit representation of the permutation during a single right-to-left pass through the input and then extracts the disjoint cycles. The term involving $V$ reflects the work done inside the cycles of the input, while the term involving $F$ reflects symbol-table searches. For the sample input these costs are smaller than the repeated rescanning costs incurred by Program $A$.
