**Exercise 3.** [**] [10] What permutations of $\{1,2,3,4,5\}$ would be constructed from the permutation $3\,1\,2\,4$ using Methods 1 and 2, respectively?

Using Method 1, insert $5$ into each of the five possible positions of the permutation $3,1,2,4$:

$5\,3\,1\,2\,4,\qquad 3\,5\,1\,2\,4,\qquad 3\,1\,5\,2\,4,\qquad 3\,1\,2\,5\,4,\qquad 3\,1\,2\,4\,5.$

Using Method 2, for each $k\in{1,2,3,4,5}$, add $1$ to every entry of $3,1,2,4$ that is $\ge k$, then append $k$ at the end. This yields

$4\,2\,3\,5\,1,\qquad 4\,1\,3\,5\,2,\qquad 4\,1\,2\,5\,3,\qquad 3\,1\,2\,5\,4,\qquad 3\,1\,2\,4\,5.$

Hence the permutations constructed are

$\boxed{\text{Method 1: }53124,\ 35124,\ 31524,\ 31254,\ 31245}$

and

$\boxed{\text{Method 2: }42351,\ 41352,\ 41253,\ 31254,\ 31245}.$
