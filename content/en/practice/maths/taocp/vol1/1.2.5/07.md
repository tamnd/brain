**Exercise 7.** [**] [M10] Show that the "generalized termial" function in Eq. (10) satisfies the identity $x? = x + (x-1)?$ for all real numbers $x$.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 1s  

---

By Eq. (10), the generalized termial function is defined for all real numbers by

$$
x?=\frac12 x(x+1).
$$

Also,

$$
(x-1)?=\frac12 (x-1)x.
$$

Therefore

$$
x+(x-1)?
= x+\frac12(x-1)x
= \frac12\bigl(2x+x^2-x\bigr)
= \frac12(x^2+x)
= \frac12 x(x+1)
= x?.
$$

Hence the identity

$$
x?=x+(x-1)?
$$

holds for all real numbers $x$. This completes the proof.

∎
