**Exercise 5.** [**] [15] Estimate $8!$ using the following more exact version of Stirling's approximation:

$$
n! \approx \sqrt{2\pi n}\left(\frac{n}{e}\right)^n\left(1+\frac{1}{12n}\right).
$$

x 6. [17] Using Eq. (8), write $20!$ as a product of prime factors.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 1s  

---

Using the refined approximation with $n=8$,

$$
8!\approx \sqrt{2\pi\cdot 8}\left(\frac{8}{e}\right)^8\left(1+\frac1{12\cdot 8}\right)
=4\sqrt{\pi}\left(\frac{8}{e}\right)^8\frac{97}{96}.
$$

The text already computes

$$
4\sqrt{\pi}\left(\frac{8}{e}\right)^8 \approx 39902.
$$

Hence

$$
8!\approx 39902\cdot\frac{97}{96}
=39902+ \frac{39902}{96}
\approx 40317.65.
$$

Therefore the improved Stirling formula gives

$$
8!\approx 40318.
$$

Since the exact value is $8!=40320$, the error is about $2.35$, or roughly $0.006%$.

$$
\boxed{8!\approx 40318}
$$
