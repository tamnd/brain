[Section 1.2.5: Permutations and Factorials](../)

**Exercise 8.** [**] [HM15] Show that the limit in Eq. (13) does equal $n!$ when $n$ is a nonnegative integer.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 2s  

---

Let $n$ be a nonnegative integer. In Eq. (13),

$$
\frac{m^n m!}{(n+1)(n+2)\cdots(n+m)}
=
\frac{m^n\,m! \, n!}{(n+m)!},
$$

since

$$
(n+1)(n+2)\cdots(n+m)=\frac{(n+m)!}{n!}.
$$

Therefore

$$
\frac{m^n m!}{(n+1)(n+2)\cdots(n+m)}
=
n!\,\frac{m^n m!}{(n+m)!}
=
n!\prod_{k=1}^{n}\frac{m}{m+k},
$$

because

$$
\frac{m!}{(n+m)!}
=
\frac{1}{(m+1)(m+2)\cdots(m+n)}.
$$

For each fixed $k$,

$$
\frac{m}{m+k}=\frac{1}{1+k/m}\to 1
\qquad (m\to\infty).
$$

Since the product contains exactly $n$ factors and $n$ is fixed,

$$
\lim_{m\to\infty}\prod_{k=1}^{n}\frac{m}{m+k}
=
\prod_{k=1}^{n}\lim_{m\to\infty}\frac{m}{m+k}
=
1.
$$

Hence

$$
\lim_{m\to\infty}
\frac{m^n m!}{(n+1)(n+2)\cdots(n+m)}
=
n!\cdot 1
=
n!.
$$

Thus the limit in Eq. (13) agrees with the factorial defined by Eq. (4) for every nonnegative integer $n$. This completes the proof.

∎
