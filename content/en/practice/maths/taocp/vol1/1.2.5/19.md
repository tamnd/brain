[Section 1.2.5: Permutations and Factorials](../)

**Exercise 19.** [**] [HM22] Denote the quantity appearing after `$\lim_{m\to\infty}$` in Eq. (15) by $\Gamma_m(x)$. Show that

$$
\Gamma_m(x)
= \int_0^m \left(1-\frac{t}{m}\right)^m t^{x-1}\,dt
= m^x\int_0^1 (1-t)^m t^{x-1}\,dt,
\qquad \text{if } x>0.
$$

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 7m47s  

---

By Eq. (15), the quantity after $\lim_{m\to\infty)$ is

$$
\Gamma_m(x)=\frac{m^x\,m!}{x(x+1)\cdots(x+m)}.
$$

Integrating by parts,

$$
\int_0^m \left(1-\frac{t}{m}\right)^m t^{x-1}\,dt
=
\left[\frac{t^x}{x}\left(1-\frac{t}{m}\right)^m\right]_0^m
+\frac1x\int_0^m t^x\frac{d}{dt}\!\left(1-\frac{t}{m}\right)^m(-1)\,dt.
$$

Since the boundary term is $0$,

$$
\int_0^m \left(1-\frac{t}{m}\right)^m t^{x-1}\,dt
=
\frac1x\int_0^m t^x\left(1-\frac{t}{m}\right)^{m-1}dt.
$$

Repeating this process $m$ times yields

$$
\int_0^m \left(1-\frac{t}{m}\right)^m t^{x-1}\,dt
=
\frac{m!}{x(x+1)\cdots(x+m-1)}
\int_0^m \frac{t^{x+m-1}}{m^m}\,dt.
$$

Therefore

$$
\int_0^m \left(1-\frac{t}{m}\right)^m t^{x-1}\,dt
=
\frac{m!}{x(x+1)\cdots(x+m-1)}
\frac{m^x}{x+m}
=
\frac{m^x\,m!}{x(x+1)\cdots(x+m)}
=
\Gamma_m(x).
$$

For the second identity, make the change of variable $t=mu$, $dt=m,du$. Then

$$
\int_0^m \left(1-\frac{t}{m}\right)^m t^{x-1}\,dt
=
\int_0^1 (1-u)^m (mu)^{x-1}m\,du
=
m^x\int_0^1 (1-u)^m u^{x-1}\,du.
$$

Hence

$$
\Gamma_m(x)
=
\int_0^m \left(1-\frac{t}{m}\right)^m t^{x-1}\,dt
=
m^x\int_0^1 (1-t)^m t^{x-1}\,dt,
\qquad x>0.
$$

$$
\boxed{\Gamma_m(x)
=
\int_0^m \left(1-\frac{t}{m}\right)^m t^{x-1}\,dt
=
m^x\int_0^1 (1-t)^m t^{x-1}\,dt}
$$

∎
