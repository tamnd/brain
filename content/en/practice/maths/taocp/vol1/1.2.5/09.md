**Exercise 9.** [**] [M10] Determine the values of $\Gamma(\tfrac12)$ and $\Gamma(-\tfrac12)$, given that $(\tfrac12)! = \sqrt{\pi}/2$.

x 10. [HM20] Does the identity $\Gamma(x+1)=x\Gamma(x)$ hold for all real numbers $x$? (See exercise 7.)

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 8m10s  

---

By definition of the Gamma function, we have $\Gamma(x) = (x-1)!$ for positive real $x$, and $(\tfrac12)! = \Gamma(\tfrac12+1) = \Gamma(\tfrac32)$. Using the standard functional equation $\Gamma(x+1) = x\Gamma(x)$, we find

$\Gamma\Bigl(\frac12\Bigr) = \frac{\Gamma(\tfrac32)}{\tfrac12} = 2 \cdot (\tfrac12)! = 2 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{2} = \sqrt{\pi}.$

Next, to determine $\Gamma(-\tfrac12)$, we again apply the functional equation with $x=-\tfrac12$, giving

$\Gamma\Bigl(\frac12\Bigr) = -\frac12 \, \Gamma\Bigl(-\frac12\Bigr),$

so that

$\Gamma\Bigl(-\frac12\Bigr) = -2 \, \Gamma\Bigl(\frac12\Bigr) = -2 \sqrt{\pi}.$

Hence we have $\Gamma(\tfrac12) = \sqrt{\pi}$ and $\Gamma(-\tfrac12) = -2\sqrt{\pi}$. This completes the proof. ∎
