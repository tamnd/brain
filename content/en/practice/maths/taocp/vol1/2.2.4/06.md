[Section 2.2.4: Circular Lists](../)

**Exercise 6.** [*18*] Give diagrams of the list representation for the polynomials (a) $xz - 3$; (b) $0$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m35s  

---

The previous solution used a linear NIL-terminated list, but in TAOCP Section 2.2.4 the polynomial is represented as a circular list with a distinguished header (or head) node. Every polynomial, including the zero polynomial, is represented by such a header node, and the last term links back to the header rather than to NIL. The header node is not a term of the polynomial; it serves as a structural anchor for traversal and termination.

Each term node has the form $(COEF, A, B, C, LINK)$, and term nodes are arranged in strictly decreasing lexicographic order of $(A,B,C)$. The header node participates in the circular structure but does not represent a monomial.

### (a) $xz - 3$

We first express each term in exponent-vector form under variables $(x,y,z)$.

The term $xz$ corresponds to $x^1 y^0 z^1$, hence it is represented as

$$
(1,1,0,1).
$$

The constant term $-3$ corresponds to $x^0 y^0 z^0$, hence it is represented as

$$
(-3,0,0,0).
$$

We now order terms in decreasing lexicographic order of $(A,B,C)$. Since

$$
(1,0,1) > (0,0,0),
$$

the $xz$ term precedes the constant term.

Let $H$ denote the header node of the polynomial list. The correct circular list representation is therefore:

$$
H \rightarrow (1,1,0,1) \rightarrow (-3,0,0,0) \rightarrow H.
$$

In full node diagram form, emphasizing the link structure:

$$
H \xrightarrow{\text{LINK}} (1,1,0,1) \xrightarrow{\text{LINK}} (-3,0,0,0) \xrightarrow{\text{LINK}} H.
$$

Here the last term node does not terminate in NIL; instead, it closes the cycle by pointing back to the header node.

### (b) $0$

The zero polynomial is not represented by an empty pointer. In TAOCP’s representation it is still a circular list containing only the header node, with no term nodes present.

Thus the header node $H$ satisfies:

$$
H.\text{LINK} = H.
$$

The diagram is therefore a single self-loop:

$$
H \rightarrow H.
$$

Equivalently, the polynomial list contains exactly one node (the header), and traversal immediately returns to itself, indicating that there are no nonzero terms in the polynomial.
