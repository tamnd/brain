**Exercise 16.** [*M28*] Estimate the running time of the subroutine in exercise 15 in terms of some relevant parameters.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 55s  

---

## Setup

Let $p$ denote the number of nonzero terms in $P$, $m$ the number of nonzero terms in $M$, and $q$ the number of nonzero terms in the initial polynomial stored in $Q$. Let $K = mp$ denote the number of term products generated during multiplication.

Assume the standard unit-cost model in which each execution of a primitive MIX list operation, each comparison of `ABC` fields, each assignment to a field, and each pointer movement along a `LINK` field counts as one time unit. Memory allocation from `AVAIL` is also counted as one unit operation.

The subroutine in exercise 15 computes

$$
Q \leftarrow Q + M \times P
$$

by forming all $K$ products of terms from $M$ and $P$, then repeatedly inserting each resulting term into $Q$ using Algorithm $A$ for polynomial addition.

## Solution

Each term of $M$ is multiplied by each term of $P$, producing exactly $K = mp$ intermediate terms. Each such product produces one node allocation from `AVAIL`, one coefficient multiplication, and one exponent addition, all performed in constant time per product, contributing a total cost proportional to $K$.

Each generated term is inserted into $Q$ by executing Algorithm $A$ with input polynomial consisting of a single term. During each execution of Algorithm $A$, pointer $Q$ traverses the current polynomial in order of decreasing `ABC` values. If $Q$ initially contains $q$ terms, and if $i-1$ product terms have already been inserted, then the length of $Q$ at the $i$-th insertion is at most $q + i - 1$.

Algorithm $A$ advances through $Q$ by repeated execution of step $A2$, and each advancement changes either $Q$ or $Q1$ by one link traversal. Each comparison of `ABC(P)` and `ABC(Q)` occurs after at most one such traversal, so the cost of one insertion is bounded above by a constant multiple of the number of nodes scanned in $Q$ plus the number of local pointer updates needed for insertion or deletion at equality.

Thus the cost of the $i$-th insertion is bounded above by a constant multiple of $q + i$. Summing over all $K$ insertions gives

$$
\sum_{i=1}^{K} (q + i) = Kq + \frac{K(K+1)}{2}.
$$

All other work inside the multiplication loop contributes at most a constant multiple of $K$, since each product term is processed once.

Therefore the total running time $T$ satisfies

$$
T \le c_1 Kq + c_2 K^2 + c_3 K
$$

for fixed constants $c_1,c_2,c_3 > 0$ determined by the unit-cost model and the implementation of Algorithm $A$.

For a lower bound, each insertion into a list of size at least $q$ requires at least $q$ comparisons or pointer traversals in the case where the new term is placed after all existing terms or cancels only at the end of the scan. There are $K$ such insertions, hence

$$
T \ge c_4 Kq
$$

for some constant $c_4 > 0$. Since after $i$ insertions the list size is at least $i$, the cumulative traversal cost also contributes a term bounded below by a constant multiple of $\sum_{i=1}^K i = K(K+1)/2$.

Combining both bounds yields

$$
T = \Theta(Kq + K^2).
$$

Substituting $K = mp$ gives

$$
T = \Theta(mp\,q + m^2 p^2).
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
