[Section 2.2.4: Circular Lists](../)

**Exercise 2.** [*20*] Draw "before and after" diagrams illustrating the effect of the concatenation operation (3), assuming that `PTR₁` and `PTR₂` are $\ne \Lambda$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m04s

## Solution

Let

$$
L_1 = (a_1, a_2, \dots, a_k), \qquad PTR_1 = a_k,
$$

so that

$$
LINK(a_k) = a_1,
$$

and

$$
L_2 = (b_1, b_2, \dots, b_m), \qquad PTR_2 = b_m,
$$

so that

$$
LINK(b_m) = b_1.
$$

Both lists are nonempty circular lists.

### Before concatenation

The structure consists of two disjoint cycles:

```
L1:  a1 → a2 → ... → a_k
      ↑              ↓
      └──────────────┘
           PTR1 = a_k

L2:  b1 → b2 → ... → b_m
      ↑              ↓
      └──────────────┘
           PTR2 = b_m
```

### Effect of operation (3)

The operation performs

$$
LINK(PTR_1) \leftrightarrow LINK(PTR_2),
$$

so

$$
LINK(a_k) \leftrightarrow LINK(b_m),
$$

which replaces

$$
LINK(a_k) = a_1,\qquad LINK(b_m) = b_1
$$

by

$$
LINK(a_k) = b_1,\qquad LINK(b_m) = a_1.
$$

Then

$$
PTR_1 \leftarrow PTR_2 = b_m, \qquad PTR_2 \leftarrow \Lambda.
$$

### After concatenation

Starting from the new pointer $PTR_1 = b_m$, traversal proceeds as follows:

$$
b_m \to a_1 \to a_2 \to \cdots \to a_k \to b_1 \to b_2 \to \cdots \to b_m.
$$

Thus the two cycles become a single circular list containing all nodes.

Diagrammatically:

```
        b1 → b2 → ... → b_m
        ↑              ↓
        |              |
        |              v
        a1 → a2 → ... → a_k
        ↑              ↓
        └──────────────┘
             PTR1 = b_m
PTR2 = Λ
```

The list is now a single cycle in which the segment from $b_m$ leads into $a_1$, and the segment from $a_k$ leads back into $b_1$, completing the concatenation.

This completes the solution. ∎
