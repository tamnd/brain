**Exercise 6.** [*20*] What is $T_5$, the average number of times step E1 is performed when $n = 5$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m18s

## Solution

Let $T_5$ denote the average number of executions of step E1 of Algorithm E when the second input is fixed as $n=5$ and the first input $m$ ranges uniformly over the integers $1,2,3,4,5$.

For each value of $m$, the number of executions of step E1 is determined by following Algorithm E until termination.

### Case $m=1$

Start with $(m,n)=(1,5)$. Step E1 gives remainder $r=1$, since $1=0\cdot 5+1$. Step E3 sets $(m,n)\leftarrow (5,1)$. Step E1 is executed again and gives remainder $r=0$, since $5=5\cdot 1+0$, so the algorithm terminates. The number of executions of E1 is $2$.

### Case $m=2$

Start with $(m,n)=(2,5)$. Step E1 gives $r=2$, since $2=0\cdot 5+2$. Step E3 sets $(m,n)\leftarrow (5,2)$. Step E1 gives $r=1$, since $5=2\cdot 2+1$. Step E3 sets $(m,n)\leftarrow (2,1)$. Step E1 gives $r=0$, since $2=2\cdot 1+0$, and the algorithm terminates. The number of executions of E1 is $3$.

### Case $m=3$

Start with $(m,n)=(3,5)$. Step E1 gives $r=3$, since $3=0\cdot 5+3$. Step E3 sets $(m,n)\leftarrow (5,3)$. Step E1 gives $r=2$, since $5=1\cdot 3+2$. Step E3 sets $(m,n)\leftarrow (3,2)$. Step E1 gives $r=1$, since $3=1\cdot 2+1$. Step E3 sets $(m,n)\leftarrow (2,1)$. Step E1 gives $r=0$, since $2=2\cdot 1+0$, and the algorithm terminates. The number of executions of E1 is $4$.

### Case $m=4$

Start with $(m,n)=(4,5)$. Step E1 gives $r=4$, since $4=0\cdot 5+4$. Step E3 sets $(m,n)\leftarrow (5,4)$. Step E1 gives $r=1$, since $5=1\cdot 4+1$. Step E3 sets $(m,n)\leftarrow (4,1)$. Step E1 gives $r=0$, since $4=4\cdot 1+0$, and the algorithm terminates. The number of executions of E1 is $3$.

### Case $m=5$

Start with $(m,n)=(5,5)$. Step E1 gives $r=0$, since $5=1\cdot 5+0$, and the algorithm terminates immediately. The number of executions of E1 is $1$.

### Average value

The total number of executions of step E1 over all cases is

$$
2+3+4+3+1 = 13.
$$

Since there are $5$ equally likely values of $m$, the average is

$$
T_5 = \frac{13}{5}.
$$

$$
\boxed{\frac{13}{5}}
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
