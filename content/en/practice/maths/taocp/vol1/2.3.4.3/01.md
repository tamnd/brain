**Exercise 1.** [**] [M10] The text refers to a set $S$ containing finite sequences of positive integers, and states that this set is "essentially an oriented tree." What is the root of this oriented tree, and what are the arcs?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 2m38s  

---

The closure property (i) ensures that whenever a sequence $(x_1,\ldots,x_n)$ lies in $S$, its initial segment of length $0$ also lies in $S$. The unique sequence of length $0$, the empty sequence, is therefore contained in $S$ and serves as the root of the oriented tree.

The arcs of the tree are given by immediate extensions. For each $(x_1,\ldots,x_n)\in S$ and each positive integer $x_{n+1}$ such that $(x_1,\ldots,x_n,x_{n+1})\in S$, there is a directed edge from $(x_1,\ldots,x_n)$ to $(x_1,\ldots,x_n,x_{n+1})$. This parent–child relation organizes $S$ as a tree rooted at the empty sequence. ∎_
