[Section 1.2.3: Sums and Products](../)

**Exercise 34.** [*M25*] Prove that

$$\sum_{k=1}^n \frac{\prod_{1 \le r \le n, \, r \ne m} (x + k - r)}{\prod_{1 \le r \le n, \, r \ne k} (k - r)} = 1,$$

provided that $1 \le m \le n$ and $x$ is arbitrary. For example, if $n = 4$ and $m = 2$, then

$$\frac{x(x-2)(x-3)}{(-1)(-2)(-3)} + \frac{(x+1)(x-1)(x-2)}{(1)(-1)(-2)} + \frac{(x+2)x(x-1)}{(2)(1)(-1)} + \frac{(x+3)(x+1)x}{(3)(2)(1)} = 1.$$

**Verified:** yes

### Corrected Solution

Let

$$
P(t)=\prod_{r=1}^n (t-r).
$$

Then for each $k\in\{1,\dots,n\}$,

$$
P'(k)=\prod_{\substack{1\le r\le n\\ r\ne k}}(k-r)=D_k.
$$

Fix $x$, and define a polynomial in $t$,

$$
F(t)=\prod_{\substack{1\le r\le n\\ r\ne m}}(x+t-r).
$$

This is a polynomial in $t$ of degree $n-1$, and it is monic because each factor contributes leading term $t$, so

$$
F(t)=t^{n-1}+\text{(lower degree terms)}.
$$

Consider the rational function

$$
R(t)=\frac{F(t)}{P(t)}.
$$

Its partial fraction decomposition has the form

$$
\frac{F(t)}{P(t)}=\sum_{k=1}^n \frac{F(k)}{P'(k)}\cdot \frac{1}{t-k},
$$

since $P(t)$ has simple roots at $t=1,\dots,n$.

Now compare residues.

- The residue of $R(t)$ at $t=k$ is

$$
\operatorname{Res}_{t=k} R(t)=\frac{F(k)}{P'(k)}.
$$

- The sum of all residues of a rational function on the Riemann sphere is $0$. Hence

$$
\sum_{k=1}^n \frac{F(k)}{P'(k)} + \operatorname{Res}_{t=\infty} R(t)=0.
$$

We compute the residue at infinity from the asymptotic expansion.

Since $F(t)$ is monic of degree $n-1$ and $P(t)$ is monic of degree $n$,

$$
\frac{F(t)}{P(t)}=\frac{t^{n-1}+O(t^{n-2})}{t^n+O(t^{n-1})}
=\frac{1}{t}+O\!\left(\frac{1}{t^2}\right).
$$

Thus the coefficient of $1/t$ is $1$, so

$$
\operatorname{Res}_{t=\infty} R(t)=-1.
$$

Therefore,

$$
\sum_{k=1}^n \frac{F(k)}{P'(k)} - 1 = 0,
$$

so

$$
\sum_{k=1}^n \frac{F(k)}{P'(k)} = 1.
$$

Finally, substituting $F(k)$ and $P'(k)=D_k$,

$$
\sum_{k=1}^n \frac{\prod_{\substack{1\le r\le n\\ r\ne m}}(x+k-r)}{\prod_{\substack{1\le r\le n\\ r\ne k}}(k-r)}=1.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
