[Section 1.2.3: Sums and Products](../)

**Exercise 6.** [*HM20*] Prove that rule (d) is valid for an arbitrary infinite series, provided that any three of the four sums exist.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m43s  

---

Let $R,S \subseteq \mathbb{Z}$, and let $(a_j)$ be an arbitrary real-valued sequence. For each set $T \subseteq \mathbb{Z}$, define the truncated sets

$$
T_n = T \cap \{-n,-n+1,\dots,n\}.
$$

Whenever the limit exists, define

$$
\sum_{T} a_j \;=\; \lim_{n\to\infty} \sum_{j \in T_n} a_j.
$$

This definition reduces all infinite series over subsets of integers to limits of finite sums over increasing finite index sets.

For each $n$, all sets $R_n, S_n, (R\cup S)_n, (R\cap S)_n$ are finite. Hence the inclusion–exclusion identity for finite sums applies directly:

$$
\sum_{j \in R_n} a_j + \sum_{j \in S_n} a_j
=
\sum_{j \in (R_n \cup S_n)} a_j + \sum_{j \in (R_n \cap S_n)} a_j.
$$

Now observe that truncation commutes with union and intersection:

$$
(R \cup S)_n = R_n \cup S_n, \qquad (R \cap S)_n = R_n \cap S_n.
$$

Therefore, for every $n$,

$$
\sum_{j \in R_n} a_j + \sum_{j \in S_n} a_j
=
\sum_{j \in (R \cup S)_n} a_j + \sum_{j \in (R \cap S)_n} a_j.
$$

Define the sequences

$$
r_n = \sum_{j \in R_n} a_j,\quad
s_n = \sum_{j \in S_n} a_j,\quad
u_n = \sum_{j \in (R \cup S)_n} a_j,\quad
i_n = \sum_{j \in (R \cap S)_n} a_j.
$$

Then for all $n$,

$$
r_n + s_n = u_n + i_n.
$$

Now assume that any three of the four limits

$$
\lim r_n,\quad \lim s_n,\quad \lim u_n,\quad \lim i_n
$$

exist.

### Existence of the fourth limit

From the identity $r_n + s_n = u_n + i_n$, we may rewrite

$$
i_n = r_n + s_n - u_n,
$$

and similarly for the other variables.

If any three of the sequences $r_n, s_n, u_n, i_n$ converge, then the right-hand side of the corresponding identity is a sum and difference of convergent sequences. Hence it also converges, which forces the fourth sequence to converge as well.

Thus the fourth infinite sum exists.

### Identity of the limits

Taking limits in

$$
r_n + s_n = u_n + i_n
$$

and using convergence of all four sequences gives

$$
\lim r_n + \lim s_n = \lim u_n + \lim i_n.
$$

In the notation of infinite series,

$$
\sum_R a_j + \sum_S a_j = \sum_{R \cup S} a_j + \sum_{R \cap S} a_j.
$$

This proves that rule (d) is valid for arbitrary infinite series whenever any three of the four sums exist. ∎
