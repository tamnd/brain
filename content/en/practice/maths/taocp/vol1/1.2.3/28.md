[Section 1.2.3: Sums and Products](../)

**Exercise 28.** [*M22*] Find a simple formula for $\prod_{j=2}^n (1 - 1/j^2)$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m14s  

---

## Solution

For $j \ge 2$,

$$
1 - \frac{1}{j^2} = \frac{j^2 - 1}{j^2} = \frac{(j-1)(j+1)}{j^2} = \frac{j-1}{j} \cdot \frac{j+1}{j}.
$$

Hence

$$
\prod_{j=2}^n \left(1 - \frac{1}{j^2}\right)
= \prod_{j=2}^n \frac{j-1}{j} \cdot \prod_{j=2}^n \frac{j+1}{j}.
$$

First product:

$$
\prod_{j=2}^n \frac{j-1}{j}
= \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{2}{3} \cdots \frac{n-1}{n}.
$$

All intermediate factors cancel, leaving

$$
\prod_{j=2}^n \frac{j-1}{j} = \frac{1}{n}.
$$

Second product:

$$
\prod_{j=2}^n \frac{j+1}{j}
= \frac{3}{2} \cdot \frac{4}{3} \cdots \frac{n+1}{n}.
$$

All intermediate factors cancel, leaving

$$
\prod_{j=2}^n \frac{j+1}{j} = \frac{n+1}{2}.
$$

Multiplying the two results gives

$$
\prod_{j=2}^n \left(1 - \frac{1}{j^2}\right)
= \frac{1}{n} \cdot \frac{n+1}{2}
= \frac{n+1}{2n}.
$$

Thus,

$$
\boxed{\prod_{j=2}^n \left(1 - \frac{1}{j^2}\right) = \frac{n+1}{2n}}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
