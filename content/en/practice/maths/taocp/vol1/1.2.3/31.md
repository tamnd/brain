**Exercise 31.** [*M20*] Use Binet’s formula to express the sum $\sum_{1 \le j < k \le n} (u_j - u_k)(v_j - v_k)$ in terms of $\sum_{j=1}^n u_j v_j$, $\sum_{j=1}^n u_j$, and $\sum_{j=1}^n v_j$.

**Solve time:** 1m13s

## Solution

Let

$$
T = \sum_{1 \le j < k \le n} (u_j - u_k)(v_j - v_k).
$$

The summand is symmetric in the sense that replacing $(j,k)$ by $(k,j)$ leaves the value unchanged, since

$$
(u_k - u_j)(v_k - v_j) = (u_j - u_k)(v_j - v_k).
$$

Hence each unordered pair ${j,k}$ with $j \ne k$ contributes twice in the double sum over all ordered pairs. Therefore,

$$
T = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{\substack{1 \le j \le n \\ 1 \le k \le n \\ j \ne k}} (u_j - u_k)(v_j - v_k).
$$

Since the terms with $j=k$ vanish, this equals

$$
T = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{j=1}^n \sum_{k=1}^n (u_j - u_k)(v_j - v_k).
$$

Expand the product:

$$
(u_j - u_k)(v_j - v_k)
= u_j v_j - u_j v_k - u_k v_j + u_k v_k.
$$

Substitute into the double sum:

$$
\sum_{j=1}^n \sum_{k=1}^n (u_j - u_k)(v_j - v_k)
= A - B - C + D,
$$

where

$$
A = \sum_{j=1}^n \sum_{k=1}^n u_j v_j,\quad
B = \sum_{j=1}^n \sum_{k=1}^n u_j v_k,\quad
C = \sum_{j=1}^n \sum_{k=1}^n u_k v_j,\quad
D = \sum_{j=1}^n \sum_{k=1}^n u_k v_k.
$$

For $A$, the inner term does not depend on $k$, so

$$
A = \sum_{j=1}^n \left(u_j v_j \sum_{k=1}^n 1\right) = n \sum_{j=1}^n u_j v_j.
$$

For $D$, the inner term does not depend on $j$, so

$$
D = n \sum_{k=1}^n u_k v_k = n \sum_{j=1}^n u_j v_j.
$$

For $B$, the variables separate:

$$
B = \left(\sum_{j=1}^n u_j\right)\left(\sum_{k=1}^n v_k\right).
$$

For $C$, similarly,

$$
C = \left(\sum_{k=1}^n u_k\right)\left(\sum_{j=1}^n v_j\right)
= \left(\sum_{j=1}^n u_j\right)\left(\sum_{j=1}^n v_j\right).
$$

Hence

$$
A - B - C + D
= 2n \sum_{j=1}^n u_j v_j - 2\left(\sum_{j=1}^n u_j\right)\left(\sum_{j=1}^n v_j\right).
$$

Therefore,

$$
T = \frac{1}{2}\left(2n \sum_{j=1}^n u_j v_j - 2\left(\sum_{j=1}^n u_j\right)\left(\sum_{j=1}^n v_j\right)\right),
$$

which simplifies to

$$
\sum_{1 \le j < k \le n} (u_j - u_k)(v_j - v_k)
= n \sum_{j=1}^n u_j v_j - \left(\sum_{j=1}^n u_j\right)\left(\sum_{j=1}^n v_j\right).
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
