**Exercise 43.** [*M24*] What is the sum of all $n^2$ elements in the inverse of Vandermonde’s matrix? [Hint: Use exercise 33.]

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m22s

## Solution

Let $V$ be the Vandermonde matrix with entries $V_{ij} = x_i^{j-1}$ for $1 \le i, j \le n$, and let $B = V^{-1}$ with entries $b_{ij}$. The required sum is

$$
S = \sum_{i=1}^n \sum_{j=1}^n b_{ij}.
$$

Define

$$
a_i = \sum_{j=1}^n b_{ij}, \qquad 1 \le i \le n.
$$

Then $a_i$ is the $i$th component of the vector $a = B \mathbf{1}$, where $\mathbf{1}$ is the column vector with all entries equal to $1$.

Multiplying by $V$ gives

$$
V a = V(B \mathbf{1}) = (VB)\mathbf{1} = \mathbf{1}.
$$

Hence $a$ is the unique vector of coefficients of a polynomial $p$ of degree at most $n-1$ such that

$$
p(x_i) = 1, \qquad 1 \le i \le n,
$$

where

$$
p(x) = \sum_{j=1}^n a_j x^{j-1}.
$$

The constant polynomial $p(x) = 1$ satisfies these interpolation conditions. Uniqueness of interpolation in degree $< n$ implies $p(x) = 1$ for all $x$, hence

$$
a_1 = 1, \qquad a_2 = a_3 = \cdots = a_n = 0.
$$

Therefore,

$$
S = \sum_{i=1}^n a_i = 1.
$$

Thus,

$$
\boxed{1}
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
