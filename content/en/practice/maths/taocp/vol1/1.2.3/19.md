[Section 1.2.3: Sums and Products](../)

**Exercise 19.** [*20*] What is $\sum_{j=m}^n (a_j - a_{j-1})$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m05s  

---

## Solution

$\sum_{j=m}^n (a_j - a_{j-1}) = \sum_{j=m}^n a_j - \sum_{j=m}^n a_{j-1}.$

The first term is unchanged.

For the second term, apply the change of variable $i = j - 1$, which is of the form $p(j) = j - 1$ allowed by rule (b). When $j = m$, $i = m - 1$, and when $j = n$, $i = n - 1$. Hence

$\sum_{j=m}^n a_{j-1} = \sum_{i=m-1}^{n-1} a_i.$

Substituting gives

$\sum_{j=m}^n (a_j - a_{j-1}) = \sum_{j=m}^n a_j - \sum_{i=m-1}^{n-1} a_i.$

Rewrite both sums with a common index name:

$= \sum_{i=m}^n a_i - \sum_{i=m-1}^{n-1} a_i.$

Expand the ranges:

$= (a_m + a_{m+1} + \cdots + a_n) - (a_{m-1} + a_m + \cdots + a_{n-1}).$

All terms from $a_m$ through $a_{n-1}$ cancel, leaving

$a_n - a_{m-1}.$

Thus,

$\sum_{j=m}^n (a_j - a_{j-1}) = a_n - a_{m-1}.$

$\boxed{a_n - a_{m-1}}$
