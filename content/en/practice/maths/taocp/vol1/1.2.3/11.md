[Section 1.2.3: Sums and Products](../)

**Exercise 11.** [*03*] What should the right-hand side of Eq. (14) be if $x = 1$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m01s  

---

If $x = 1$, each term $a x^j$ in the sum $\sum_{0 \le j \le n} a x^j$ becomes $a$. The index $j$ ranges over all integers satisfying $0 \le j \le n$, so the number of terms is $n+1$. The sum therefore equals $a(n+1)$.

Hence the right-hand side of (14) must be $a(n+1)$ when $x = 1$.

$\boxed{a(n+1)}$
