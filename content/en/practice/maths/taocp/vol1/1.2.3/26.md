**Exercise 26.** [*25*] Show that $\prod_{i=0}^n \prod_{j=0}^i a_i a_j$ may be expressed in terms of $\prod_{i=0}^n a_i$ by manipulating the $\prod$-notation as stated in exercise 22.

**Solve time:** 1m19s  

---

## Solution

Let

$$
P=\prod_{i=0}^n \prod_{j=0}^i a_i a_j.
$$

For fixed $i$, the inner product expands as

$$
\prod_{j=0}^i a_i a_j
= \left(\prod_{j=0}^i a_i\right)\left(\prod_{j=0}^i a_j\right).
$$

The first factor contains $(i+1)$ identical terms $a_i$, hence

$$
\prod_{j=0}^i a_i = a_i^{i+1}.
$$

Thus

$$
\prod_{j=0}^i a_i a_j = a_i^{i+1} \prod_{j=0}^i a_j,
$$

and therefore

$$
P = \prod_{i=0}^n \left(a_i^{i+1} \prod_{j=0}^i a_j\right)
= \left(\prod_{i=0}^n a_i^{i+1}\right)\left(\prod_{i=0}^n \prod_{j=0}^i a_j\right).
$$

The second factor is a triangular product. Interchanging the order of multiplication over the finite index set yields

$$
\prod_{i=0}^n \prod_{j=0}^i a_j
= \prod_{j=0}^n \prod_{i=j}^n a_j.
$$

For fixed $j$, the inner product contains $(n-j+1)$ copies of $a_j$, hence

$$
\prod_{i=j}^n a_j = a_j^{n-j+1}.
$$

Therefore

$$
\prod_{i=0}^n \prod_{j=0}^i a_j
= \prod_{j=0}^n a_j^{n-j+1}.
$$

Substituting this into $P$ gives

$$
P = \left(\prod_{i=0}^n a_i^{i+1}\right)\left(\prod_{i=0}^n a_i^{n-i+1}\right).
$$

Combining exponents of $a_i$ yields

$$
P = \prod_{i=0}^n a_i^{(i+1)+(n-i+1)} = \prod_{i=0}^n a_i^{n+2}.
$$

Hence

$$
P = \left(\prod_{i=0}^n a_i\right)^{n+2}.
$$

$$
\boxed{\left(\prod_{i=0}^n a_i\right)^{n+2}}
$$
