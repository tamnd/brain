**Exercise 12.** [*10*] What is $1 + \frac{1}{7} + \frac{1}{49} + \frac{1}{343} + \cdots + \left(\frac{1}{7}\right)^n$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 56s  

---

Let

$$
S = 1 + \frac{1}{7} + \frac{1}{7^2} + \cdots + \left(\frac{1}{7}\right)^n.
$$

This is a finite geometric progression with first term $1$ and ratio $x = \frac{1}{7}$. By the geometric-sum formula (14),

$$
S = \frac{1 - x^{n+1}}{1 - x} = \frac{1 - \left(\frac{1}{7}\right)^{n+1}}{1 - \frac{1}{7}}.
$$

Since $1 - \frac{1}{7} = \frac{6}{7}$, this simplifies to

$$
S = \frac{7}{6}\left(1 - \left(\frac{1}{7}\right)^{n+1}\right).
$$

$$
\boxed{\,\frac{7}{6}\left(1 - \left(\frac{1}{7}\right)^{n+1}\right)\,}
$$
