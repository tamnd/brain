[Section 1.2.3: Sums and Products](../)

**Exercise 29.** &#9654; [*M30*] (a) Express $\sum_{i=0}^n \sum_{j=0}^i \sum_{k=0}^j a_i a_j a_k$ in terms of the multiple-sum notation explained at the end of the section. (b) Express the same sum in terms of $\sum_{i=0}^n a_i$, $\sum_{i=0}^n a_i^2$, and $\sum_{i=0}^n a_i^3$ [see Eq. (13)].

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 5m05s  

---

### (a) Multiple-sum notation

The iterated sum

$$
\sum_{i=0}^n \sum_{j=0}^i \sum_{k=0}^j a_i a_j a_k
$$

ranges over all integer triples satisfying

$$
0 \le k \le j \le i \le n.
$$

Hence, in Knuth’s multiple-sum notation, it is

$$
\sum_{0 \le k \le j \le i \le n} a_i a_j a_k.
$$

### (b) Expression in terms of $S, S_2, S_3$

Let

$$
S = \sum_{i=0}^n a_i, \quad
S_2 = \sum_{i=0}^n a_i^2, \quad
S_3 = \sum_{i=0}^n a_i^3.
$$

Define

$$
T = \sum_{0 \le k \le j \le i \le n} a_i a_j a_k.
$$

We partition $T$ according to index equalities:

$$
T
= \sum_i a_i^3
+ \sum_{i>j} a_i^2 a_j
+ \sum_{i>j} a_i a_j^2
+ \sum_{i>j>k} a_i a_j a_k.
$$

Hence,

$$
T = S_3 + (A_1 + A_2) + D,
$$

where

$$
A_1 + A_2 = \sum_{i>j} (a_i^2 a_j + a_i a_j^2), \quad
D = \sum_{i>j>k} a_i a_j a_k.
$$

#### Step 1: Relate $S S_2$ to $A_1 + A_2$

Expand:

$$
S S_2 = \left(\sum_i a_i\right)\left(\sum_j a_j^2\right)
= \sum_{i,j} a_i a_j^2.
$$

Split into $i=j$ and $i \ne j$:

- $i=j$ contributes $S_3$,
- $i \ne j$ contributes $\sum_{i\ne j} a_i a_j^2$.

Now split $i \ne j$ into $i>j$ and $i<j$. In the $i<j$ part, rename indices to obtain $a_i a_j^2$. Thus,

$$
\sum_{i\ne j} a_i a_j^2 = \sum_{i>j}(a_i^2 a_j + a_i a_j^2).
$$

Therefore,

$$
S S_2 = S_3 + (A_1 + A_2),
$$

so

$$
A_1 + A_2 = S S_2 - S_3.
$$

#### Step 2: Correct decomposition of $S^3$

Expand:

$$
S^3 = \sum_{i,j,k} a_i a_j a_k.
$$

Group terms by equality type:

- All equal: $S_3$.
- Exactly two equal: for distinct $p \ne q$, the triple $(p,p,q)$ has 3 permutations, so this contributes $3(A_1 + A_2)$.
- All distinct: $6D$.

Hence the correct identity is

$$
S^3 = S_3 + 3(A_1 + A_2) + 6D.
$$

Solve for $D$:

$$
6D = S^3 - S_3 - 3(A_1 + A_2).
$$

Substitute $A_1 + A_2 = S S_2 - S_3$:

$$
6D = S^3 - S_3 - 3S S_2 + 3S_3
= S^3 - 3S S_2 + 2S_3.
$$

Thus,

$$
D = \frac{S^3 - 3S S_2 + 2S_3}{6}.
$$

#### Step 3: Assemble $T$

We have

$$
T = S_3 + (A_1 + A_2) + D.
$$

Substitute:

$$
T = S_3 + (S S_2 - S_3) + \frac{S^3 - 3S S_2 + 2S_3}{6}.
$$

So

$$
T = S S_2 + \frac{S^3 - 3S S_2 + 2S_3}{6}.
$$

Bring to common denominator:

$$
T = \frac{6S S_2 + S^3 - 3S S_2 + 2S_3}{6}
= \frac{S^3 + 3S S_2 + 2S_3}{6}.
$$

### Final result

$$
\boxed{
\sum_{i=0}^n \sum_{j=0}^i \sum_{k=0}^j a_i a_j a_k
=
\sum_{0 \le k \le j \le i \le n} a_i a_j a_k
=
\frac{S^3 + 3S S_2 + 2S_3}{6}
}.
$$
