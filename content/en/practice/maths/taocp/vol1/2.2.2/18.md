[Section 2.2.2: Sequential Allocation](../)

**Exercise 18.** [*M30*] Show that the total running time for any sequence of $m$ insertions and/or deletions by Algorithms `G` and `R` is

$$
O\left(m + n\sum_{k=1}^{m}\frac{\alpha_k}{1-\alpha_k}\right),
$$

where $\alpha_k$ is the fraction of memory occupied on the most recent repacking previous to the $k$th operation; $\alpha_k = 0$ before the first repacking. (Therefore if the memory never gets more than, say, 90% full, each operation takes at most $O(n)$ units of time in an amortized sense, regardless of the total memory size.) Assume that $L_\infty - L_0 \ge n^2$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 4m18s  

---

The error in the previous solution is exactly the attempt to control the additive $O(n)$ per repacking by bounding the number of repackings. That is unnecessary and incorrect. The correct fix is to charge the $O(n)$ term _directly to the same structure that controls $\alpha_k$_, splitting only by whether $\alpha_k$ is large or small.

## Correct solution

Let $N = L_\infty - L_0$, so the total memory has capacity $N$ and $N \ge n^2$. Let repackings occur at times $t_1 < t_2 < \cdots < t_r$. For the $k$-th repacking, let $\alpha_k$ be the occupancy fraction immediately after the previous repacking, so just before the $k$-th repacking the number of occupied cells is $\alpha_k N$.

We split the cost of each repacking into two parts:

- Moving cost: $O(n \alpha_k N)$
- Overhead cost: $O(n)$

We handle these separately.

## 1. Amortization of the moving cost

Consider the interval between repacking $k-1$ and $k$. The number of occupied cells increases from $\alpha_k N$ up to $N$ (up to constant slack) before the next repacking is triggered. Since each operation changes occupancy by at most 1, this interval contains at least

$$
(1 - \alpha_k)N
$$

operations.

Thus we may charge the moving cost $O(n \alpha_k N)$ uniformly to these operations. The amortized cost per operation from repacking $k$ is

$$
\frac{n \alpha_k N}{(1 - \alpha_k)N}
= n \cdot \frac{\alpha_k}{1 - \alpha_k}.
$$

Summing over all repackings gives total moving cost

$$
O\left(n \sum_{k=1}^r \frac{\alpha_k}{1 - \alpha_k}\right).
$$

This part is standard and correct.

## 2. Correct handling of the $O(n)$ repacking overhead

We now treat the additive $O(n)$ cost per repacking.

### Case split

Fix $k$.

### Case 1: $\alpha_k \ge \tfrac{1}{2}$

Then

$$
\frac{\alpha_k}{1 - \alpha_k} \ge 1.
$$

Hence

$$
n = O\!\left(n \cdot \frac{\alpha_k}{1 - \alpha_k}\right).
$$

So for all repackings in this case, the overhead is absorbed directly into the main sum:

$$
\sum_{\alpha_k \ge 1/2} O(n)
\;\le\;
O\left(n \sum_{\alpha_k \ge 1/2} \frac{\alpha_k}{1 - \alpha_k}\right).
$$

No separate bound on the number of repackings is needed.

### Case 2: $\alpha_k < \tfrac{1}{2}$

Then $1 - \alpha_k \ge \tfrac{1}{2}$, so each such repacking interval has length at least

$$
(1 - \alpha_k)N \ge \frac{N}{2} \ge \frac{n^2}{2}.
$$

Thus each such repacking accounts for at least $\Omega(n^2)$ operations.

Since there are $m$ total operations, the number of repackings in this case satisfies

$$
r_{\text{small}} = O\!\left(\frac{m}{n^2}\right).
$$

Therefore the total overhead from these repackings is

$$
O(n r_{\text{small}})
=
O\!\left(n \cdot \frac{m}{n^2}\right)
=
O\!\left(\frac{m}{n}\right)
=
O(m).
$$

## 3. Combine overhead contributions

The total overhead cost is therefore

$$
O(m) + O\left(n \sum_{k=1}^r \frac{\alpha_k}{1 - \alpha_k}\right).
$$

## 4. Final combination with moving cost

Adding the moving cost from Section 1 yields total running time

$$
O\left(
m + n \sum_{k=1}^r \frac{\alpha_k}{1 - \alpha_k}
\right).
$$

Since each $k$ corresponds to a repacking and $\alpha_k$ is defined for every repacking, we may equivalently write the sum up to $m$ with the understanding that only repacking indices contribute.

## Conclusion

The total running time for any sequence of $m$ insertions and deletions under Algorithms $G$ and $R$ is

$$
O\left(m + n \sum_{k=1}^{m} \frac{\alpha_k}{1 - \alpha_k}\right),
$$

as required. ∎
