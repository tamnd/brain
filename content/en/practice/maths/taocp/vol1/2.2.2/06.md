## Correct framework (TAOCP multiple-stack memory)

We use Knuth’s standard model:

- Each stack $S_i$ occupies a contiguous region bounded below by $\mathrm{BASE}[i]$.
- $\mathrm{TOP}[i]$ is the current top element index.
- A deletion $D_i$ is legal iff

$$
\mathrm{TOP}[i] > \mathrm{BASE}[i],
$$

otherwise it causes **underflow**.
- An insertion $I_i$ requires one available cell from the global free list.
- **Overflow occurs iff the free list is empty at the moment an insertion is attempted**, or if the insertion would force stacks to cross boundaries (equivalently, no free cell can be allocated without violating ordering constraints).

Thus every part reduces to tracking:

1. Whether stack $i$ is empty at each deletion step.
2. Whether the global free list has at least $k$ cells available for $k$ insertions.
3. Whether any insertion would collide with adjacent stack regions.
