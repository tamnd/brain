[Section 2.2.2: Sequential Allocation](../)

**Exercise 7.** [*12*] Step `G4` of Algorithm `G` indicates a division by the quantity `INC`. Can `INC` ever be zero at that point in the algorithm?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m06s  

---

## Solution

The variable `OLDTOP[j]` is defined as the value of `TOP[j]` immediately after the previous allocation of memory. Between two allocations, `OLDTOP[j]` remains fixed for all $j$.

At the moment Algorithm G is invoked, overflow has occurred in stack $i$ according to step (9). That step performs the operation

$$
\text{TOP}[i] \leftarrow \text{TOP}[i] + 1
$$

before Algorithm G begins. Hence the value of `TOP[i]` at entry to Algorithm G exceeds its value at the previous allocation point, that is,

$$
\text{TOP}[i] > \text{OLDTOP}[i].
$$

In step G2, the quantity `D[j]` is defined by

$$
D[j] = \text{TOP}[j] - \text{OLDTOP}[j]
$$

when $\text{TOP}[j] > \text{OLDTOP}[j]$, and $D[j] = 0$ otherwise. Therefore for $j = i$,

$$
D[i] = \text{TOP}[i] - \text{OLDTOP}[i] \ge 1.
$$

The variable `INC` is initialized to $0$ in step G1 and then updated in step G2 by

$$
\text{INC} \leftarrow \text{INC} + D[j]
$$

for each $j$ with $\text{TOP}[j] > \text{OLDTOP}[j]$. Since at least the index $j = i$ satisfies this condition, the accumulated sum includes at least one positive contribution. Hence

$$
\text{INC} \ge D[i] \ge 1.
$$

Therefore `INC` cannot be zero at the time step G4 is reached.

This completes the proof. ∎
