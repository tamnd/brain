[Section 2.2.2: Sequential Allocation](../)

**Exercise 11.** [*M30*] Generalize the argument of exercise 9 so that the first $t$ insertions in any stack cause no movement, while subsequent insertions are unaffected. Thus if $t = 2$, the sequence in exercise 9 specifies $0 + 0 + 0 + 0 + 0 + 3 + 0 + 0 + 3 + 6 = 12$ moves. What is the average total number of moves under this assumption? [This is an approximation to the behavior of the algorithm when each stack starts with $t$ available spaces.]

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 1m13s  

---

## Setup

The mathematical model of Exercise 9 assigns a random sequence of stack operations and defines the total number of moves as a sum of contributions from individual insertions, where each insertion may force relocations depending on how many prior elements are already stored in adjacent regions. Let $m_{i,j}$ denote the number of moves caused by the $j$th insertion into stack $i$ under the original model of Exercise 9, so that the total number of moves is

$$
M=\sum_{i}\sum_{j\ge 1} m_{i,j}.
$$

In the modified setting, the first $t$ insertions into any stack produce no movement. For each stack $i$, the modified contribution of its $j$th insertion is

$$
m'_{i,j}=
\begin{cases}
0 & \text{if } j \le t,\\
m_{i,j} & \text{if } j > t.
\end{cases}
$$

The problem is to determine the average total number of moves

$$
\mathbb{E}[M'].
$$

## Solution

The total number of moves in the modified model is

$$
M'=\sum_{i}\sum_{j\ge 1} m'_{i,j}
=\sum_{i}\sum_{j>t} m_{i,j}.
$$

Taking expectations and using linearity,

$$
\mathbb{E}[M']
=\sum_{i}\sum_{j>t} \mathbb{E}[m_{i,j}].
$$

The expression $\mathbb{E}[m_{i,j}]$ is identical to the quantity appearing in Exercise 9, since the modification only affects whether a contribution is counted, not the probabilistic mechanism generating relocations after the $j$th insertion.

Hence the expected total number of moves in the modified scheme is obtained from the result of Exercise 9 by deleting the contributions from the first $t$ insertions of each stack:

$$
\mathbb{E}[M']
=\mathbb{E}[M]-\sum_{i}\sum_{j=1}^{t}\mathbb{E}[m_{i,j}].
$$

In the symmetric model of Exercise 9, the distribution of insertion positions depends only on the index $j$ and not on the stack label $i$, so the correction term is determined entirely by the expected contribution of the first $t$ insertions per stack. Writing $c_j=\mathbb{E}[m_{i,j}]$, independent of $i$, gives

$$
\mathbb{E}[M']
=\sum_{i}\sum_{j>t} c_j.
$$

If there are $n$ stacks, this becomes

$$
\mathbb{E}[M']=n\sum_{j>t} c_j.
$$

Thus the modification replaces the original expectation

$$
\mathbb{E}[M]=n\sum_{j\ge 1} c_j
$$

by truncating the first $t$ terms of each stack contribution.

Therefore the average total number of moves equals the expression of Exercise 9 with all terms corresponding to the first $t$ insertions in each stack removed.

This completes the proof. ∎
