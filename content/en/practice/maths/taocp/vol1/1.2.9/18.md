**Exercise 18.** [*M25*] Given positive integers $n$ and $r$, find a simple formula for the value of the following sums:

(a) $\sum_{1 \le k_1 < k_2 < \cdots < k_r \le n} k_1k_2\cdots k_r$;

(b) $\sum_{1 \le k_1 \le k_2 \le \cdots \le k_r \le n} k_1k_2\cdots k_r$.

(For example, when $n=3$ and $r=2$ the sums are, respectively, $1\cdot2 + 1\cdot3 + 2\cdot3$ and $1\cdot1 + 1\cdot2 + 1\cdot3 + 2\cdot2 + 2\cdot3 + 3\cdot3$.)

## Solution

Let

$$
P(z)=\prod_{k=1}^{n}(1+kz).
$$

Expanding each factor, a term of order $z^r$ arises by choosing $kz$ from exactly $r$ distinct factors and $1$ from the remaining $n-r$ factors. Each such choice corresponds uniquely to a subset ${k_1,\dots,k_r}\subseteq {1,\dots,n}$ with $k_1<\cdots<k_r$, and contributes the product $k_1k_2\cdots k_r z^r$. Hence the coefficient of $z^r$ in $P(z)$ is

$$
\sum_{1 \le k_1 < k_2 < \cdots < k_r \le n} k_1k_2\cdots k_r.
$$

Therefore,

$$
\sum_{1 \le k_1 < k_2 < \cdots < k_r \le n} k_1k_2\cdots k_r
= [z^r]\prod_{k=1}^{n}(1+kz).
$$

Equivalently, this is the $r$th elementary symmetric function in $1,2,\dots,n$.

For the second sum, consider instead

$$
Q(z)=\prod_{k=1}^{n}\frac{1}{1-kz}
=\prod_{k=1}^{n}(1+kz+(kz)^2+\cdots).
$$

In expanding $Q(z)$, each factor corresponding to $k$ contributes a term $(kz)^{m_k}$ for some $m_k \ge 0$. A term of total degree $r$ arises precisely when $m_1+\cdots+m_n=r$, and the resulting contribution is

$$
1^{m_1}2^{m_2}\cdots n^{m_n}\, z^r.
$$

Such a choice corresponds to a nondecreasing sequence $k_1 \le k_2 \le \cdots \le k_r$ in which each value $k$ appears exactly $m_k$ times, and the product is $k_1k_2\cdots k_r$. Hence the coefficient of $z^r$ in $Q(z)$ equals

$$
\sum_{1 \le k_1 \le k_2 \le \cdots \le k_r \le n} k_1k_2\cdots k_r.
$$

Therefore,

$$
\sum_{1 \le k_1 \le k_2 \le \cdots \le k_r \le n} k_1k_2\cdots k_r
= [z^r]\prod_{k=1}^{n}\frac{1}{1-kz}.
$$

This completes the derivation. ∎
