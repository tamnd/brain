[Section 1.2.9: Generating Functions](../)

**Exercise 20.** [*M21*] For what coefficients $c_{mk}$ is

$$
\sum_{n \ge 0} n^m z^n = \sum_{k=0}^{m} c_{mk}\frac{z^k}{(1-z)^{k+1}} \, ?
$$

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m20s  

---

## Solution

Let

$$
A_m(z)=\sum_{n\ge 0} n^m z^n.
$$

For $m=0$,

$$
A_0(z)=\sum_{n\ge 0} z^n=\frac{1}{1-z}.
$$

For $m\ge 0$, use

$$
z\frac{d}{dz} z^n = n z^n,
$$

so repeated application gives

$$
A_{m+1}(z)=z\frac{d}{dz}A_m(z).
$$

Define coefficients $c_{mk}$ by

$$
A_m(z)=\sum_{k=0}^m c_{mk}\frac{z^k}{(1-z)^{k+1}}.
$$

Differentiate one basis term. For $k\ge 0$,

$$
\frac{d}{dz}\left(\frac{z^k}{(1-z)^{k+1}}\right)
= k\frac{z^{k-1}}{(1-z)^{k+1}} + (k+1)\frac{z^k}{(1-z)^{k+2}}.
$$

Multiplying by $z$ gives

$$
z\frac{d}{dz}\left(\frac{z^k}{(1-z)^{k+1}}\right)
= k\frac{z^k}{(1-z)^{k+1}} + (k+1)\frac{z^{k+1}}{(1-z)^{k+2}}.
$$

Hence

$$
A_{m+1}(z)=\sum_{k\ge 0} c_{mk}\left[
k\frac{z^k}{(1-z)^{k+1}} + (k+1)\frac{z^{k+1}}{(1-z)^{k+2}}
\right].
$$

Reindex the second sum by replacing $k$ with $k-1$:

$$
\sum_{k\ge 1} c_{m,k-1}\,k\frac{z^k}{(1-z)^{k+1}}.
$$

Therefore, for $k\ge 1$, the coefficient of $\frac{z^k}{(1-z)^{k+1}}$ in $A_{m+1}(z)$ is

$$
k c_{mk} + k c_{m,k-1}.
$$

For $k=0$, only the first term contributes, giving

$$
c_{m+1,0}=0 \quad (m\ge 0).
$$

Thus the coefficients satisfy

$$
c_{m+1,k}=k c_{mk} + k c_{m,k-1}, \quad k\ge 1,
$$

with initial condition $c_{0,0}=1$ and $c_{0,k}=0$ for $k\ge 1$.

Let $\left{ {m \atop k} \right}$ denote Stirling numbers of the second kind. They satisfy

$$
\left\{ {m+1 \atop k} \right\}
= k\left\{ {m \atop k} \right\} + \left\{ {m \atop k-1} \right\}.
$$

Define

$$
c_{mk}=k!\left\{ {m \atop k} \right\}.
$$

Then

$$
c_{m+1,k}
= k!\left\{ {m+1 \atop k} \right\}
= k!\left(k\left\{ {m \atop k} \right\} + \left\{ {m \atop k-1} \right\}\right).
$$

This expands to

$$
k k!\left\{ {m \atop k} \right\} + k(k-1)!\left\{ {m \atop k-1} \right\}
= k c_{mk} + k c_{m,k-1},
$$

which matches the recurrence and initial condition. Hence the representation holds for all $m$.

Therefore,

$$
\sum_{n\ge 0} n^m z^n
=
\sum_{k=0}^m k!\left\{ {m \atop k} \right\}\frac{z^k}{(1-z)^{k+1}}.
$$

So the coefficients are

$$
\boxed{c_{mk}=k!\left\{ {m \atop k} \right\}}.
$$

This completes the proof. âˆŽ
