[Section 1.2.9: Generating Functions](../)

**Exercise 10.** [*M25*] An elementary symmetric function is defined by the formula

$$
e_m = \sum_{1 \le j_1 < \cdots < j_m \le n} x_{j_1}\cdots x_{j_m}.
$$

(This is the same as $h_m$ of Eq. (33), except that equal subscripts are not allowed.) Find the generating function for $e_m$, and express $e_m$ in terms of the $S_j$ in Eq. (34). Write out the formulas for $e_1,e_2,e_3,$ and $e_4$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m02s  

---

We restart from the exponential generating form and compute coefficients carefully, correcting the expansion of $A^2$ and the higher-order bookkeeping.

## 1. Generating function

Let

$$
e_m=\sum_{1\le j_1<\cdots< j_m\le n} x_{j_1}\cdots x_{j_m},\qquad e_0=1.
$$

Each factor $1+x_j z$ either contributes $1$ or selects $x_j z$ once, so every monomial with distinct indices appears exactly once. Hence

$$
E(z)=\sum_{m\ge 0} e_m z^m=\prod_{j=1}^n (1+x_j z).
$$

## 2. Expression in terms of power sums $S_k$

Let

$$
S_k=\sum_{j=1}^n x_j^k.
$$

Then

$$
\ln E(z)=\sum_{j=1}^n \ln(1+x_j z)
=\sum_{j=1}^n \sum_{k\ge 1}\frac{(-1)^{k+1}}{k}x_j^k z^k
=\sum_{k\ge 1}\frac{(-1)^{k+1}}{k}S_k z^k.
$$

Thus

$$
E(z)=\exp\!\left(\sum_{k\ge 1}\frac{(-1)^{k+1}}{k}S_k z^k\right).
$$

Write

$$
A(z)=S_1 z-\frac{S_2}{2}z^2+\frac{S_3}{3}z^3-\frac{S_4}{4}z^4+\cdots,
\qquad E(z)=\exp(A(z)).
$$

We expand

$$
E(z)=1+A+\frac{A^2}{2}+\frac{A^3}{6}+\frac{A^4}{24}+\cdots
$$

and extract coefficients.

## 3. Coefficients $e_1,e_2,e_3$

### $e_1$

$$
e_1=S_1.
$$

### $e_2$

From $A$ and $A^2/2$:

$$
A^2 = S_1^2 z^2 + \cdots
$$

so

$$
e_2=S_1 z^2\text{-term:}\quad -\frac{S_2}{2}+\frac{S_1^2}{2}
=\frac{S_1^2-S_2}{2}.
$$

### $e_3$

Terms contributing to $z^3$:

- From $A$: $\frac{S_3}{3}$
- From $A^2/2$: coefficient of $z^3$ in $A^2$ is $2S_1(-S_2/2)=-S_1S_2$, hence contribution $-\frac{S_1S_2}{2}$
- From $A^3/6$: $(S_1 z)^3$ gives $\frac{S_1^3}{6}$

Thus

$$
e_3=\frac{S_1^3-3S_1S_2+2S_3}{6}.
$$

## 4. Coefficient $e_4$

We compute each exponential term up to order $z^4$.

### Step 1: $A^2$ up to $z^4$

$$
A = S_1 z-\frac{S_2}{2}z^2+\frac{S_3}{3}z^3-\frac{S_4}{4}z^4+\cdots
$$

Compute contributions:

- $(S_1 z)^2 = S_1^2 z^2$
- $2(S_1 z)(-\frac{S_2}{2}z^2)= -S_1S_2 z^3$
- $2(S_1 z)(\frac{S_3}{3}z^3)=\frac{2}{3}S_1S_3 z^4$
- $(-\frac{S_2}{2}z^2)^2=\frac{S_2^2}{4}z^4$

So the $z^4$-part of $A^2$ is

$$
\frac{2}{3}S_1S_3+\frac{S_2^2}{4}.
$$

Hence

$$
\frac{A^2}{2}\Big|_{z^4}=\frac{S_1S_3}{3}+\frac{S_2^2}{8}.
$$

### Step 2: $A^3$ up to $z^4$

The only way to obtain $z^4$ is $1+1+2$:

$$
3\cdot (S_1 z)^2\left(-\frac{S_2}{2}z^2\right)
=-\frac{3}{2}S_1^2S_2 z^4.
$$

Thus

$$
\frac{A^3}{6}\Big|_{z^4}=-\frac{S_1^2S_2}{4}.
$$

### Step 3: $A^4$ up to $z^4$

Only $(S_1 z)^4$ contributes:

$$
\frac{A^4}{24}\Big|_{z^4}=\frac{S_1^4}{24}.
$$

### Step 4: Collect all contributions

From $A$:

$$
-\frac{S_4}{4}
$$

From $A^2/2$:

$$
\frac{S_1S_3}{3}+\frac{S_2^2}{8}
$$

From $A^3/6$:

$$
-\frac{S_1^2S_2}{4}
$$

From $A^4/24$:

$$
\frac{S_1^4}{24}
$$

Therefore

$$
e_4=
\frac{S_1^4}{24}
-\frac{S_1^2S_2}{4}
+\frac{S_1S_3}{3}
+\frac{S_2^2}{8}
-\frac{S_4}{4}.
$$

Putting over $24$,

$$
e_4=\frac{S_1^4-6S_1^2S_2+8S_1S_3+3S_2^2-6S_4}{24}.
$$

## Final formulas

$$
e_1=S_1,
$$

$$
e_2=\frac{S_1^2-S_2}{2},
$$

$$
e_3=\frac{S_1^3-3S_1S_2+2S_3}{6},
$$

$$
e_4=\frac{S_1^4-6S_1^2S_2+8S_1S_3+3S_2^2-6S_4}{24}.
$$

These agree with the Newton identities for elementary symmetric functions.
