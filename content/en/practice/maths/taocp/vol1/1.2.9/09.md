**Exercise 9.** [*M11*] In the notation of Eqs. (34) and (35), what is $h_4$ in terms of $S_1,S_2,S_3,$ and $S_4$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m42s  

---

## Solution

Let $S_k$ denote the power-sum quantities and $h_k$ the sequence defined by Eqs. (34) and (35), so that the generating function relation has the form

$$
\sum_{n \ge 0} h_n z^n
= \exp\!\left(\sum_{k \ge 1} \frac{S_k}{k} z^k\right).
$$

Write

$$
\sum_{k \ge 1} \frac{S_k}{k} z^k
= S_1 z + \frac{S_2}{2}z^2 + \frac{S_3}{3}z^3 + \frac{S_4}{4}z^4 + \cdots .
$$

The coefficient of $z^4$ in the exponential is determined by the fourth complete exponential Bell polynomial in the variables $S_1, S_2/2, S_3/3, S_4/4$. The expansion of the exponential up to fourth order is obtained by collecting all products whose total degree in $z$ is $4$.

Terms contributing to $z^4$ arise from:

$(S_1 z)^4,\quad (S_1 z)^2 \left(\frac{S_2}{2}z^2\right),\quad \left(\frac{S_2}{2}z^2\right)^2,\quad (S_1 z)\left(\frac{S_3}{3}z^3\right),\quad \frac{S_4}{4}z^4.$

Expanding each contribution and accounting for the multinomial coefficients from the exponential series gives

$$
h_4
= \frac{S_1^4}{24}
+ \frac{S_1^2 S_2}{4}
+ \frac{S_2^2}{8}
+ \frac{S_1 S_3}{3}
+ \frac{S_4}{4}.
$$

Putting everything over the common denominator $24$ yields

$$
h_4
= \frac{S_1^4 + 6 S_1^2 S_2 + 3 S_2^2 + 8 S_1 S_3 + 6 S_4}{24}.
$$

Hence

$$
\boxed{h_4 = \frac{S_1^4 + 6 S_1^2 S_2 + 3 S_2^2 + 8 S_1 S_3 + 6 S_4}{24}}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
