[Section 1.2.9: Generating Functions](../)

**Exercise 1.** [*M12*] What is the generating function for the sequence $2,5,13,35,\ldots = \langle 2^n+3^n \rangle$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 58s  

---

## Solution

Let $\langle a_n \rangle = 2^n + 3^n$. The generating function is

$$
G(z) = \sum_{n \ge 0} (2^n + 3^n) z^n.
$$

Split the sum into two series:

$$
G(z) = \sum_{n \ge 0} 2^n z^n + \sum_{n \ge 0} 3^n z^n.
$$

For the first series, write $2^n z^n = (2z)^n$. Hence

$$
\sum_{n \ge 0} 2^n z^n = \sum_{n \ge 0} (2z)^n.
$$

By Eq. (5) with $z$ replaced by $2z$,

$$
\sum_{n \ge 0} (2z)^n = \frac{1}{1-2z}.
$$

Similarly,

$$
\sum_{n \ge 0} 3^n z^n = \sum_{n \ge 0} (3z)^n = \frac{1}{1-3z}.
$$

Therefore,

$$
G(z) = \frac{1}{1-2z} + \frac{1}{1-3z}.
$$

Combine the fractions:

$$
G(z) = \frac{(1-3z) + (1-2z)}{(1-2z)(1-3z)}.
$$

Simplifying the numerator,

$$
(1-3z) + (1-2z) = 2 - 5z.
$$

Thus,

$$
G(z) = \frac{2-5z}{(1-2z)(1-3z)}.
$$

This completes the proof.

∎
