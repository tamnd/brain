**Exercise 25.** [*M23*] Evaluate the sum

$$
\sum_k \binom{n}{k} 2^{n-2k}(-2)^k
$$

by simplifying the equivalent formula

$$
\sum_k [w^k](1-2w)^n [z^{n-k}](1+z)^{2n-2k}.
$$

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 3m34s

## Solution

Consider

$$
\sum_k \binom{n}{k} 2^{\,n-2k}(-2)^k.
$$

Rewrite the factor depending on $k$:

$$
2^{\,n-2k}(-2)^k
= 2^n \cdot 2^{-2k} \cdot (-2)^k
= 2^n \cdot \frac{(-2)^k}{4^k}
= 2^n \cdot \left(-\frac{1}{2}\right)^k.
$$

Substituting into the sum gives

$$
\sum_k \binom{n}{k} 2^{\,n-2k}(-2)^k
= 2^n \sum_k \binom{n}{k}\left(-\frac{1}{2}\right)^k.
$$

Apply the binomial theorem in the form

$$
\sum_k \binom{n}{k} x^k = (1+x)^n
$$

with $x=-\frac{1}{2}$:

$$
\sum_k \binom{n}{k}\left(-\frac{1}{2}\right)^k
= \left(1-\frac{1}{2}\right)^n
= \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^n.
$$

Therefore,

$$
2^n \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^n = 1.
$$

Thus the value of the given sum is

\boxed{1}. $$ ∎
