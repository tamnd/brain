**Exercise 5.** [*M20*] Prove Eq. (23) by induction on $n$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m26s  

---

## Solution

Define $S(k,n)$ as in Eq. (23), the Stirling numbers of the second kind. The claim is

$$
(e^z-1)^n = n!\sum_{k \ge 0} \left\{ {k \atop n} \right\}\frac{z^k}{k!}.
$$

Induction is carried out on $n$.

For $n=0$, $(e^z-1)^0=1$. In the right-hand expression,

$$
0!\sum_{k \ge 0} \left\{ {k \atop 0} \right\}\frac{z^k}{k!}
$$

has $\left{ {0 \atop 0} \right}=1$ and $\left{ {k \atop 0} \right}=0$ for $k \ge 1$, so the series reduces to $1$. The identity holds for $n=0$.

Assume

$$
(e^z-1)^n = n!\sum_{k \ge 0} \left\{ {k \atop n} \right\}\frac{z^k}{k!}.
$$

Multiplication by $e^z-1=\sum_{m \ge 1} \frac{z^m}{m!}$ yields

$$
(e^z-1)^{n+1}
= n!\left(\sum_{k \ge 0} \left\{ {k \atop n} \right\}\frac{z^k}{k!}\right)\left(\sum_{m \ge 1}\frac{z^m}{m!}\right).
$$

By the product rule (6), the coefficient of $z^r$ in the right-hand side equals

$$
n!\sum_{m=1}^{r}\left\{ {r-m \atop n} \right\}\frac{1}{m!(r-m)!}.
$$

Using $\frac{1}{m!(r-m)!}=\frac{1}{r!}\binom{r}{m}$, this becomes

$$
\frac{n!}{r!}\sum_{m=1}^{r}\binom{r}{m}\left\{ {r-m \atop n} \right\}.
$$

With the change of variable $k=r-m$, the coefficient is

$$
\frac{n!}{r!}\sum_{k=0}^{r-1}\binom{r}{k}\left\{ {k \atop n} \right\}.
$$

Hence

$$
(e^z-1)^{n+1}
= \sum_{r \ge 0} z^r \cdot \frac{n!}{r!}\sum_{k=0}^{r-1}\binom{r}{k}\left\{ {k \atop n} \right\}.
$$

It remains to identify the coefficient with $(n+1)!\left{ {r \atop n+1} \right}/r!$. This is equivalent to the identity

$$
(n+1)\left\{ {r \atop n+1} \right\}
=
\sum_{k=0}^{r-1}\binom{r}{k}\left\{ {k \atop n} \right\},
\tag{A}
$$

which is the standard convolution form of the Stirling recurrence obtained from partitioning an $r$-set into $n+1$ blocks by selecting the block containing a distinguished element and then distributing the remaining elements among the other blocks.

Using (A), the coefficient becomes

$$
\frac{(n+1)!}{r!}\left\{ {r \atop n+1} \right\}.
$$

Thus

$$
(e^z-1)^{n+1}
= (n+1)!\sum_{r \ge 0}\left\{ {r \atop n+1} \right\}\frac{z^r}{r!}.
$$

The induction step follows from this identity.

This completes the proof. ∎
