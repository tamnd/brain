[Section 1.2.9: Generating Functions](../)

**Exercise 6.** [*HM15*] Find the generating function for

$$
\left\langle \sum_{0<k<n} \frac{1}{k(n-k)} \right\rangle;
$$

differentiate it and express the coefficients in terms of harmonic numbers.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m37s  

---

## Solution

Let

$$
a_n=\sum_{0<k<n}\frac{1}{k(n-k)} \qquad (n\ge 1),
$$

and $a_0=0$.

For $n\ge 1$,

$$
\frac{1}{k(n-k)}=\frac{1}{n}\left(\frac{1}{k}+\frac{1}{n-k}\right),
$$

since

$$
\frac{1}{k}+\frac{1}{n-k}=\frac{n}{k(n-k)}.
$$

Hence

$$
a_n=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1}^{n-1}\left(\frac{1}{k}+\frac{1}{n-k}\right)
=\frac{1}{n}\left(2\sum_{k=1}^{n-1}\frac{1}{k}\right)
=\frac{2H_{n-1}}{n}.
$$

Define the generating function

$$
A(z)=\sum_{n\ge 0} a_n z^n=\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{2H_{n-1}}{n}z^n.
$$

Introduce the shifted sequence $b_n=H_{n-1}$ for $n\ge 1$, $b_0=0$. Its generating function is

$$
B(z)=\sum_{n\ge 1} H_{n-1}z^n
= z\sum_{n\ge 0} H_n z^n.
$$

Using (18),

$$
\sum_{n\ge 0} H_n z^n=\frac{1}{1-z}\ln\frac{1}{1-z},
$$

so

$$
B(z)=\frac{z}{1-z}\ln\frac{1}{1-z}.
$$

Then

$$
A(z)=2\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{b_n}{n}z^n.
$$

By (15) in shifted form,

$$
\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{b_n}{n}z^n=\int_0^z \frac{B(t)}{t}\,dt,
$$

hence

$$
A(z)=2\int_0^z \frac{1}{1-t}\ln\frac{1}{1-t}\,dt.
$$

Evaluate the integral by substituting $x=1-t$, $dt=-dx$:

$$
\int_0^z \frac{1}{1-t}\ln\frac{1}{1-t}\,dt
=\int_{1}^{1-z}\frac{\ln x}{x}\,dx.
$$

Since

$$
\int \frac{\ln x}{x}\,dx=\frac{1}{2}(\ln x)^2,
$$

it follows that

$$
\int_{1}^{1-z}\frac{\ln x}{x}\,dx
=\frac{1}{2}(\ln(1-z))^2.
$$

Therefore

$$
A(z)=2\cdot \frac{1}{2}(\ln(1-z))^2=(\ln(1-z))^2.
$$

Thus the generating function is

$$
\boxed{A(z)=(\ln(1-z))^2}.
$$

Differentiate:

$$
A'(z)=2\ln(1-z)\cdot\frac{-1}{1-z}
=-\frac{2\ln(1-z)}{1-z}
=\frac{2\ln\frac{1}{1-z}}{1-z}.
$$

Using (18),

$$
\frac{1}{1-z}\ln\frac{1}{1-z}=\sum_{n\ge 0} H_n z^n,
$$

so

$$
A'(z)=2\sum_{n\ge 0} H_n z^n.
$$

On the other hand, from the definition of a generating function,

$$
A'(z)=\sum_{n\ge 0}(n+1)a_{n+1}z^n.
$$

Comparing coefficients gives

$$
(n+1)a_{n+1}=2H_n,
$$

hence

$$
a_{n+1}=\frac{2H_n}{n+1}.
$$

Thus the coefficients of $A'(z)$ are $2H_n$, and the differentiated generating function is

$$
\boxed{A'(z)=2\sum_{n\ge 0} H_n z^n }.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
