**Exercise 12.** [*M20*] Suppose we have a doubly subscripted sequence $\langle a_{mn} \rangle$ for $m,n=0,1,\ldots$; show how this double sequence can be represented by a single generating function of two variables, and determine the generating function for $\left\langle \binom{n}{m} \right\rangle$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m24s  

---

## Solution

Let $\langle a_{mn} \rangle$ be a doubly indexed sequence for $m,n \ge 0$. Introduce two independent parameters $x$ and $y$, and define the bivariate generating function

$$
A(x,y) = \sum_{m \ge 0} \sum_{n \ge 0} a_{mn} x^m y^n.
$$

This expression collects all coefficients $a_{mn}$ into a single formal power series in two variables. For fixed $n$, the coefficient of $y^n$ is the ordinary generating function in $x$ given by $\sum_{m \ge 0} a_{mn} x^m$, while for fixed $m$, the coefficient of $x^m$ is $\sum_{n \ge 0} a_{mn} y^n$. Thus the double sequence is encoded uniquely by $A(x,y)$ through coefficient extraction.

Given any $m,n \ge 0$, the coefficient of $x^m y^n$ in $A(x,y)$ equals $a_{mn}$, so

$$
a_{mn} = [x^m y^n] A(x,y),
$$

where $[x^m y^n]$ denotes coefficient extraction in the bivariate series expansion.

Now consider the sequence $a_{mn} = \binom{n}{m}$. The corresponding generating function is

$$
B(x,y) = \sum_{n \ge 0} \sum_{m \ge 0} \binom{n}{m} x^m y^n.
$$

For fixed $n$, the inner sum runs over $0 \le m \le n$ since $\binom{n}{m} = 0$ otherwise. Hence

$$
B(x,y) = \sum_{n \ge 0} y^n \sum_{m=0}^{n} \binom{n}{m} x^m.
$$

By the binomial theorem,

$$
\sum_{m=0}^{n} \binom{n}{m} x^m = (1+x)^n.
$$

Substituting this identity into the expression for $B(x,y)$ gives

$$
B(x,y) = \sum_{n \ge 0} \bigl(y(1+x)\bigr)^n.
$$

Using the geometric series expansion in Eq. (5),

$$
\sum_{n \ge 0} z^n = \frac{1}{1-z},
$$

with $z = y(1+x)$ yields

$$
B(x,y) = \frac{1}{1 - y(1+x)}.
$$

Thus the bivariate generating function for $\binom{n}{m}$ is

$$
\boxed{\frac{1}{1 - y - xy}}.
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
