**Exercise 2.** [*M13*] Prove Eq. (11).

## Solution

Let

$$
A(z)=\sum_{n\ge 0}\frac{a_n}{n!}z^n,\qquad B(z)=\sum_{n\ge 0}\frac{b_n}{n!}z^n.
$$

Their product expands as

$$
A(z)B(z)=\left(\sum_{k\ge 0}\frac{a_k}{k!}z^k\right)\left(\sum_{j\ge 0}\frac{b_j}{j!}z^j\right)
=\sum_{n\ge 0}z^n\sum_{k=0}^{n}\frac{a_k}{k!}\frac{b_{n-k}}{(n-k)!}.
$$

The coefficient of $z^n$ in $A(z)B(z)$ equals

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n}\frac{a_k b_{n-k}}{k!(n-k)!}.
$$

Define a sequence $\langle c_n\rangle$ by requiring that

$$
A(z)B(z)=\sum_{n\ge 0}\frac{c_n}{n!}z^n.
$$

Then comparison of coefficients gives

$$
\frac{c_n}{n!}=\sum_{k=0}^{n}\frac{a_k b_{n-k}}{k!(n-k)!}.
$$

Multiplying both sides by $n!$ yields

$$
c_n=\sum_{k=0}^{n}\frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!}a_k b_{n-k}.
$$

The factor satisfies

$$
\frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!}=\binom{n}{k}.
$$

Hence

$$
c_n=\sum_{k=0}^{n}\binom{n}{k}a_k b_{n-k}.
$$

This shows that

$$
\left(\sum_{n\ge 0}\frac{a_n}{n!}z^n\right)\left(\sum_{n\ge 0}\frac{b_n}{n!}z^n\right)
=\sum_{n\ge 0}\frac{c_n}{n!}z^n,
$$

where $c_n=\sum_k \binom{n}{k}a_k b_{n-k}$, which is Eq. (11).

This completes the proof. ∎
