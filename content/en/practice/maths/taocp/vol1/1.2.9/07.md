**Exercise 7.** [*M15*] Verify all the steps leading to Eq. (38).

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 5m20s  

---

Let $F_0=0$, $F_1=1$, and $F_{n+1}=F_n+F_{n-1}$. The derivation leading to Eq. (38) establishes the addition formula

$$
F_{m+n} = F_m F_{n+1} + F_{m-1} F_n.
$$

Each step in the derivation is justified as follows.

First fix $m \ge 1$ and prove the identity by induction on $n$.

For $n=0$, the right-hand side gives

$$
F_m F_1 + F_{m-1} F_0 = F_m \cdot 1 + F_{m-1} \cdot 0 = F_m,
$$

which equals $F_{m+0}$. This verifies the base case.

Assume now that for some $n \ge 0$,

$$
F_{m+n} = F_m F_{n+1} + F_{m-1} F_n.
$$

We verify the transition to $n+1$ using only the recurrence relation.

Start from the left-hand side:

$$
F_{m+n+1} = F_{m+n} + F_{m+n-1}.
$$

Substitute the induction hypothesis into both terms. For $F_{m+n}$, use the hypothesis directly. For $F_{m+n-1}$, apply the same identity with $n-1$:

$$
F_{m+n} = F_m F_{n+1} + F_{m-1} F_n,
$$

$$
F_{m+n-1} = F_m F_n + F_{m-1} F_{n-1}.
$$

Adding these expressions yields

$$
F_{m+n+1}
= F_m(F_{n+1}+F_n) + F_{m-1}(F_n+F_{n-1}).
$$

Now apply the Fibonacci recurrence $F_{n+2}=F_{n+1}+F_n$ and $F_{n+1}=F_n+F_{n-1}$. This gives

$$
F_{m+n+1} = F_m F_{n+2} + F_{m-1} F_{n+1}.
$$

This is exactly the same identity with $n$ replaced by $n+1$, completing the induction step.

Every transformation used above is justified solely by:

- the defining recurrence $F_{k+1}=F_k+F_{k-1}$,
- substitution of the induction hypothesis,
- and regrouping of terms using associativity and commutativity of integer addition.

Thus all steps leading to Eq. (38) are verified.
