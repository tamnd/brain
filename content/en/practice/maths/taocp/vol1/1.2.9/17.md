[Section 1.2.9: Generating Functions](../)

**Exercise 17.** [*M25*] What are the coefficients of $1/(1-z)^w$ if this function is expanded into a double power series in terms of both $z$ and $w$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m03s  

---

## Solution

Start from the identity

$$
\frac{1}{(1-z)^w} = (1-z)^{-w} = \exp\!\bigl(-w \ln(1-z)\bigr).
$$

Rewrite the logarithm in the form

$$
-\ln(1-z) = \ln\frac{1}{1-z}.
$$

Hence

$$
\frac{1}{(1-z)^w} = \exp\!\left(w \ln\frac{1}{1-z}\right).
$$

Expand the exponential series (22),

$$
\exp\!\left(w \ln\frac{1}{1-z}\right)
= \sum_{m \ge 0} \frac{w^m}{m!} \left(\ln\frac{1}{1-z}\right)^m.
$$

Apply identity (26),

$$
\left(\ln\frac{1}{1-z}\right)^m
= m! \sum_{n \ge m} \left[{n \atop m}\right] \frac{z^n}{n!},
$$

where $\left[{n \atop m}\right]$ denotes a Stirling number of the second kind.

Substituting this into the exponential expansion gives

$$
\frac{1}{(1-z)^w}
= \sum_{m \ge 0} \frac{w^m}{m!}
\left(m! \sum_{n \ge m} \left[{n \atop m}\right] \frac{z^n}{n!}\right).
$$

The factors $m!$ cancel, yielding

$$
\frac{1}{(1-z)^w}
= \sum_{m \ge 0} \sum_{n \ge m} \left[{n \atop m}\right] \frac{z^n w^m}{n!}.
$$

Reindexing as a double power series in $z$ and $w$,

$$
\frac{1}{(1-z)^w}
= \sum_{n \ge 0} \sum_{m=0}^{n} \left[{n \atop m}\right] \frac{z^n w^m}{n!}.
$$

Thus the coefficient of $z^n w^m$ is

$$
\frac{1}{n!}\left[{n \atop m}\right].
$$

Hence the double power series expansion is

$$
\boxed{\frac{1}{(1-z)^w} = \sum_{n \ge 0}\sum_{m=0}^{n} \left[{n \atop m}\right]\frac{z^n w^m}{n!}}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
