[Section 1.2.9: Generating Functions](../)

**Exercise 15.** [*M28*] By considering $H(w)=\sum_{n \ge 0} G_n(z)w^n$, find a closed form for the generating function

$$
G_n(z) = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n-k}{k} z^k
= \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{2k-n-1}{k} (-z)^k.
$$

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m27s  

---

## Setup

Let

$$
G_n(z) = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n-k}{k} z^k,
$$

and define the bivariate generating function

$$
H(w) = \sum_{n \ge 0} G_n(z)\, w^n.
$$

The goal is to find a closed form for $H(w)$ as a function of $w$ and the parameter $z$.

## Solution

Substituting the definition of $G_n(z)$ into $H(w)$ gives

$$
H(w) = \sum_{n \ge 0} \left( \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n-k}{k} z^k \right) w^n.
$$

Interchange the order of summation by writing $n = m + k$, where $m \ge 0$ and $k \ge 0$, which yields

$$
H(w) = \sum_{k \ge 0} \sum_{m \ge 0} \binom{m}{k} z^k w^{m+k}.
$$

Factor terms depending on $k$ and $m$:

$$
H(w) = \sum_{k \ge 0} z^k w^k \sum_{m \ge 0} \binom{m}{k} w^m.
$$

For fixed $k$, the inner sum is evaluated using the standard binomial generating function identity

$$
\sum_{m \ge k} \binom{m}{k} w^m = \frac{w^k}{(1-w)^{k+1}},
$$

and terms with $m < k$ vanish since $\binom{m}{k} = 0$ in that range. Hence

$$
\sum_{m \ge 0} \binom{m}{k} w^m = \frac{w^k}{(1-w)^{k+1}}.
$$

Substituting this into the expression for $H(w)$ gives

$$
H(w) = \sum_{k \ge 0} z^k w^k \frac{w^k}{(1-w)^{k+1}}.
$$

Combine factors:

$$
H(w) = \sum_{k \ge 0} \frac{z^k w^{2k}}{(1-w)^{k+1}}
= \frac{1}{1-w} \sum_{k \ge 0} \left( \frac{z w^2}{1-w} \right)^k.
$$

The remaining series is geometric, so

$$
\sum_{k \ge 0} \left( \frac{z w^2}{1-w} \right)^k
= \frac{1}{1 - \frac{z w^2}{1-w}}.
$$

Therefore

$$
H(w) = \frac{1}{1-w} \cdot \frac{1}{1 - \frac{z w^2}{1-w}}
= \frac{1}{1-w - z w^2}.
$$

Thus the closed form is

$$
\boxed{H(w) = \frac{1}{1 - w - z w^2}}.
$$

## Verification

From the closed form,

$$
(1 - w - z w^2)H(w) = 1.
$$

Expanding $H(w) = \sum_{n \ge 0} G_n(z) w^n$ gives

$$
\sum_{n \ge 0} G_n(z) w^n
- \sum_{n \ge 0} G_n(z) w^{n+1}
- z \sum_{n \ge 0} G_n(z) w^{n+2}
= 1.
$$

Reindexing the shifted sums yields

$$
G_0(z) + \sum_{n \ge 1} \bigl(G_n(z) - G_{n-1}(z) - z G_{n-2}(z)\bigr) w^n = 1,
$$

where $G_{-1}(z)$ is interpreted as $0$.

Matching coefficients gives $G_0(z) = 1$ and

$$
G_n(z) = G_{n-1}(z) + z G_{n-2}(z) \quad (n \ge 2).
$$

This recurrence is satisfied by

$$
G_n(z) = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n-k}{k} z^k,
$$

since direct substitution of the binomial identity reduces both sides to equal sums after shifting indices.
