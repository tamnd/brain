**Exercise 8.** [*M23*] Find the generating function for $p(n)$, the number of partitions of $n$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 56s  

---

## Solution

Let $p(n)$ denote the number of representations of $n$ as a sum of positive integers, where order is disregarded and repetition is allowed.

Each partition of $n$ is uniquely determined by a sequence $\langle a_1,a_2,a_3,\ldots\rangle$ of nonnegative integers such that
$$
\sum_{k \ge 1} k a_k = n,
$$
where $a_k$ is the number of parts equal to $k$.

For a fixed $k \ge 1$, the possible values of $a_k$ are $0,1,2,\ldots$. The contribution of parts of size $k$ to the generating function is therefore
$$
\sum_{a_k \ge 0} z^{k a_k} = 1 + z^k + z^{2k} + \cdots = \frac{1}{1 - z^k},
$$
by Eq. (5) applied with $z$ replaced by $z^k$.

The total contribution of all sizes $k \ge 1$ is obtained by combining independent choices for each $a_k$. Using the multiplication rule (6) iteratively yields the generating function
$$
P(z) = \prod_{k \ge 1} \frac{1}{1 - z^k}.
$$

To identify the coefficients, expand the product. A term in the expansion is obtained by choosing, for each $k \ge 1$, a term $z^{k a_k}$ from $\frac{1}{1-z^k}$. The resulting product is
$$
\prod_{k \ge 1} z^{k a_k} = z^{\sum_{k \ge 1} k a_k}.
$$

Hence the coefficient of $z^n$ in $P(z)$ equals the number of sequences $\langle a_1,a_2,\ldots\rangle$ satisfying $\sum_{k \ge 1} k a_k = n$, which equals $p(n)$ by definition.

Therefore
$$
\sum_{n \ge 0} p(n) z^n = \prod_{k \ge 1} \frac{1}{1 - z^k}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
