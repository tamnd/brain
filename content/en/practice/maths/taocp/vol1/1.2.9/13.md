**Exercise 13.** [*HM22*] The Laplace transform of a function $f(x)$ is the function

$$
Lf(s) = \int_0^\infty e^{-st}f(t)\,dt.
$$

Given that $a_0,a_1,a_2,\ldots$ is an infinite sequence having a convergent generating function, let $f(x)$ be the step function $\sum_k a_k [0 \le k \le x]$. Express the Laplace transform of $f(x)$ in terms of the generating function $G$ for this sequence.

**Solve time:** 3m15s

## Solution

Let $f(x)=\sum_k a_k [0 \le k \le x]$. For real $x \ge 0$, the condition $[0 \le k \le x]$ selects exactly those integers $k=0,1,\ldots,\lfloor x \rfloor$, so

$$
f(x)=\sum_{k=0}^{\lfloor x \rfloor} a_k.
$$

Define the partial sums

$$
S_n=\sum_{k=0}^{n} a_k, \qquad n \ge 0.
$$

Then $f(x)=S_n$ for $n \le x < n+1$. Hence

$$
Lf(s)=\int_0^\infty e^{-st} f(t)\,dt
=\sum_{n \ge 0} \int_n^{n+1} e^{-st} S_n\,dt.
$$

Since $S_n$ is constant on $[n,n+1)$,

$$
\int_n^{n+1} e^{-st} S_n\,dt
= S_n \int_n^{n+1} e^{-st}\,dt.
$$

A direct evaluation gives

$$
\int_n^{n+1} e^{-st}\,dt
=\left[-\frac{1}{s}e^{-st}\right]_{t=n}^{t=n+1}
=\frac{e^{-sn}-e^{-s(n+1)}}{s}
=\frac{1-e^{-s}}{s}e^{-sn}.
$$

Thus

$$
Lf(s)=\frac{1-e^{-s}}{s}\sum_{n \ge 0} S_n e^{-sn}.
$$

Now consider the generating function identity (7). Since

$$
\frac{1}{1-z}G(z)=\sum_{n \ge 0} S_n z^n,
$$

substitution $z=e^{-s}$ yields

$$
\sum_{n \ge 0} S_n e^{-sn}=\frac{1}{1-e^{-s}}G(e^{-s}).
$$

Substituting this into the expression for $Lf(s)$,

$$
Lf(s)=\frac{1-e^{-s}}{s} \cdot \frac{1}{1-e^{-s}}G(e^{-s})
=\frac{1}{s}G(e^{-s}).
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{Lf(s)=\frac{G(e^{-s})}{s}}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
