**Exercise 1.** [**] [21] Find a simple one-to-one correspondence between binary trees with
$n$ nodes and dissections of an $(n+2)$-sided convex polygon into $n$ triangles,
assuming that the sides of the polygon are distinct.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 6m13s  

---

Number the vertices of an $(n+2)$-sided convex polygon consecutively as $V_1,V_2,\ldots,V_{n+2}$ in clockwise order. Given a binary tree with $n$ nodes, embed it in the plane so that the root corresponds to a distinguished edge of the polygon, say $V_1V_2$, and proceed recursively: for each internal node, associate its left and right subtrees with two disjoint intervals of consecutive vertices along the polygon boundary, and draw a diagonal connecting the endpoints of each subtree interval to form a triangle. Each internal node generates exactly one diagonal, and each diagonal separates two smaller polygons corresponding to the left and right subtrees. Conversely, given a dissection of the polygon into $n$ triangles, select the triangle containing the distinguished edge $V_1V_2$ as the root; the diagonals of this triangle partition the remaining polygon into two smaller polygons, which correspond to the left and right subtrees. Repeating this process recursively produces a binary tree with $n$ nodes. This construction establishes a one-to-one correspondence between binary trees with $n$ nodes and triangulations of an $(n+2)$-gon.

This completes the proof. ∎
