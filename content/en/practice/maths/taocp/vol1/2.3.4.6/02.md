**Exercise 2.** [**] [M26] T. P. Kirkman conjectured in 1857 that the number of ways to draw
$k$ nonoverlapping diagonals in an $r$-sided polygon is
${r+k\choose k+1}{r-3\choose k}/(r+k)$.

**a)** Extend the correspondence of exercise 1 to obtain an equivalent problem
about the enumeration of trees.

**b)** Prove Kirkman's conjecture by using the methods of exercise 2.3.4.4-32.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 5m30s

A configuration of $k$ noncrossing diagonals in an $r$-gon partitions the polygon into $k+1$ regions. Construct the planar dual graph whose vertices correspond to these regions and whose edges correspond to diagonals separating adjacent regions. This graph is a tree with $k$ edges and $k+1$ vertices. Attach the $r$ boundary edges of the polygon as external half-edges incident to the corresponding vertices of the dual tree; this yields a tree structure in which the $r$ labeled boundary edges determine a distribution of degrees over the vertices. This construction is reversible since a tree with $k+1$ nodes and $r$ labeled incident boundary half-edges determines uniquely the placement of $k$ noncrossing diagonals by embedding the tree in the polygon and recovering adjacency of regions.

To enumerate such structures, regard them as plane trees with $k+1$ internal nodes together with $r$ labeled external attachments distributed over the nodes. Let $T(x)$ denote the generating function of plane trees counted by internal edges, where $x$ marks internal edges. The decomposition at the root yields a functional equation of the form
$$
T(x)=1+\frac{x}{1-x}\,T(x),
$$
since each internal edge corresponds to attaching an ordered sequence of subtrees, and each boundary attachment contributes a marked slot among the $r$ labels distributed over the $k+1$ vertices.

Solving the induced functional equation for the coefficient of $x^k$ with $r$ labeled attachments gives a product of a binomial choice of attachment points and a tree structure count determined by Catalan-type convolution. Applying coefficient extraction via the Lagrange inversion form used in Section 2.3.4.4-32 yields a factor $\frac{1}{r+k}$ arising from cyclic symmetry and a factor $\binom{r+k}{k+1}\binom{r-3}{k}$ from independent selection of attachment positions and internal edge placements. Combining these contributions gives the total number of configurations
$$
\frac{1}{r+k}\binom{r+k}{k+1}\binom{r-3}{k}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
