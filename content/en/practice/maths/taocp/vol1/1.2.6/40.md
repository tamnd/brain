**Exercise 40.** [*HM17*] The beta function $B(x,y)$ is defined for positive real numbers $x,y$ by

$$
B(x,y) = \int_0^1 t^{x-1}(1-t)^{y-1}\,dt.
$$

Show that:

1. $B(x,1)=B(1,x)=1/x$.
2. $B(x+1,y)+B(x,y+1)=B(x,y)$.
3. $B(x,y)=((x+y)/y)B(x,y+1)$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m23s

## Solution

### 1. Evaluation of $B(x,1)$ and $B(1,x)$

For $x>0$,

$$
B(x,1)=\int_0^1 t^{x-1}(1-t)^{0}\,dt=\int_0^1 t^{x-1}\,dt.
$$

The antiderivative of $t^{x-1}$ is $t^x/x$, hence

$$
B(x,1)=\left.\frac{t^x}{x}\right|_{0}^{1}=\frac{1}{x}.
$$

For $x>0$,

$$
B(1,x)=\int_0^1 t^{0}(1-t)^{x-1}\,dt=\int_0^1 (1-t)^{x-1}\,dt.
$$

With the substitution $u=1-t$, $du=-dt$, the limits interchange from $t=0,1$ to $u=1,0$, giving

$$
B(1,x)=\int_1^0 u^{x-1}(-du)=\int_0^1 u^{x-1}\,du=\frac{1}{x}.
$$

Thus,

$$
B(x,1)=B(1,x)=\frac{1}{x}.
$$

This completes part 1. ∎

### 2. Identity $B(x+1,y)+B(x,y+1)=B(x,y)$

Using the definition of the beta function,

$$
B(x+1,y)=\int_0^1 t^{x}(1-t)^{y-1}\,dt,\qquad
B(x,y+1)=\int_0^1 t^{x-1}(1-t)^{y}\,dt.
$$

Adding the integrands,

$$
t^{x}(1-t)^{y-1}+t^{x-1}(1-t)^{y}
= t^{x-1}(1-t)^{y-1}\bigl(t+(1-t)\bigr).
$$

Since $t+(1-t)=1$,

$$
t^{x}(1-t)^{y-1}+t^{x-1}(1-t)^{y}=t^{x-1}(1-t)^{y-1}.
$$

Integrating over $[0,1]$,

$$
B(x+1,y)+B(x,y+1)=\int_0^1 t^{x-1}(1-t)^{y-1}\,dt=B(x,y).
$$

This completes part 2. ∎

### 3. Identity $B(x,y)=\frac{x+y}{y}B(x,y+1)$

Start from the derivative of $t^x(1-t)^y$:

$$
\frac{d}{dt}\bigl(t^x(1-t)^y\bigr)
= x t^{x-1}(1-t)^y - y t^x(1-t)^{y-1}.
$$

Integrating over $[0,1]$,

$$
\int_0^1 \frac{d}{dt}\bigl(t^x(1-t)^y\bigr)\,dt
= \left.t^x(1-t)^y\right|_0^1=0.
$$

Hence,

$$
\int_0^1 x t^{x-1}(1-t)^y\,dt
=
\int_0^1 y t^x(1-t)^{y-1}\,dt.
$$

In beta notation,

$$
x\,B(x,y+1)=y\,B(x+1,y).
$$

Solving for $B(x+1,y)$,

$$
B(x+1,y)=\frac{x}{y}B(x,y+1).
$$

Substituting into part 2,

$$
B(x,y)=B(x+1,y)+B(x,y+1)
=\frac{x}{y}B(x,y+1)+B(x,y+1).
$$

Factoring,

$$
B(x,y)=\left(\frac{x+y}{y}\right)B(x,y+1).
$$

This completes part 3. ∎
