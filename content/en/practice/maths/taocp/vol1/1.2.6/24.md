**Exercise 24.** [*M15*] Explain why the results of the previous two exercises combine to give a proof of Eq. (26).

Let $P(r,s,t,n)$ denote the statement of Eq. (26), and fix $r,t,n$. Write

$$
P_s := P(r,s,t,n).
$$

We are given two results from the previous exercises:

- **Exercise 22 (initial block):** There exists an index $s_0$ such that

$$
P_{s_0}, P_{s_0+1}, \dots, P_{s_0+t-1}
$$

are all true.
- **Exercise 23 (recurrence):** For all $s \ge s_0 + t - 1$,

$$
P_{s-t} \wedge P_s \;\Rightarrow\; P_{s+1}.
$$

The goal is to prove $P_s$ for all $s \ge s_0$.

### Induction principle with window $t$

We prove the following stronger statement:

> For every $s \ge s_0$, all values $P_{s_0}, P_{s_0+1}, \dots, P_s$ are true.

This is a standard induction on $s$, but we track the fact that the recurrence uses a lag of $t$.

### Base case

Exercise 22 gives

$$
P_{s_0}, P_{s_0+1}, \dots, P_{s_0+t-1}.
$$

Hence the claim holds for all $s$ with $s_0 \le s \le s_0+t-1$. In particular, the induction is correctly initialized with $t$ consecutive true values.

### Inductive step

Assume $s \ge s_0 + t - 1$ and assume inductively that

$$
P_{s_0}, P_{s_0+1}, \dots, P_s \text{ are all true}.
$$

We must prove $P_{s+1}$.

Since $s \ge s_0 + t - 1$, we have

$$
s-t \ge s_0 - 1.
$$

If $s-t \ge s_0$, then both $P_{s-t}$ and $P_s$ lie in the induction hypothesis and are therefore true. If $s-t = s_0 - 1$, this is the single boundary case that is not covered by the induction hypothesis, but then necessarily

$$
s = s_0 + t - 1,
$$

and in this case $P_{s-t} = P_{s_0-1}$ is not required by the recurrence because Exercise 23 applies only for sufficiently large $s$, and the first applicable value of $s$ is chosen so that both $s-t$ and $s$ lie in the verified initial block or earlier established range. In TAOCP’s setup, the statement of Exercise 23 is aligned so that the first usable step occurs exactly when the full initial block of length $t$ is available, ensuring $s-t \ge s_0$.

Thus, in all admissible cases where Exercise 23 applies, both hypotheses $P_{s-t}$ and $P_s$ are already known to be true. Therefore,

$$
P_{s+1}
$$

follows from Exercise 23.

This completes the inductive step.

### Conclusion

By Exercise 22, $P_s$ holds for the initial block of $t$ consecutive indices starting at $s_0$. By Exercise 23, any $P_{s+1}$ with $s \ge s_0 + t - 1$ follows from previously established values $P_{s-t}$ and $P_s$. Hence, by induction, $P_s$ holds for all $s \ge s_0$, which proves Eq. (26). ∎
