**Exercise 54.** [*M21*] Consider Pascal's triangle as a matrix. What is the inverse of that matrix?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m25s  

---

## Solution

Let $P = (p_{ij})_{i,j \ge 0}$ denote Pascal’s triangle matrix defined by

$$
p_{ij} =
\begin{cases}
\binom{i}{j}, & i \ge j,\\
0, & i < j.
\end{cases}
$$

This is a lower triangular matrix with diagonal entries $p_{ii} = 1$.

Let $Q = (q_{ij})_{i,j \ge 0}$ be defined by

$$
q_{ij} =
\begin{cases}
(-1)^{i-j}\binom{i}{j}, & i \ge j,\\
0, & i < j.
\end{cases}
$$

The product $PQ$ is lower triangular, and its $(i,j)$ entry is

$$
(PQ)_{ij} = \sum_{k=j}^{i} \binom{i}{k}(-1)^{k-j}\binom{k}{j}.
$$

For $k \ge j$, use factorial form (5):

$$
\binom{i}{k}\binom{k}{j}
= \frac{i!}{k!(i-k)!} \cdot \frac{k!}{j!(k-j)!}
= \frac{i!}{j!(i-k)!(k-j)!}.
$$

Rewrite using binomial coefficients:

$$
\binom{i}{k}\binom{k}{j}
= \binom{i}{j}\binom{i-j}{k-j}.
$$

Substitution into the sum gives

$$
(PQ)_{ij}
= \binom{i}{j}\sum_{k=j}^{i} (-1)^{k-j}\binom{i-j}{k-j}.
$$

Let $t = k-j$. Then $t$ ranges from $0$ to $i-j$, hence

$$
(PQ)_{ij}
= \binom{i}{j}\sum_{t=0}^{i-j} (-1)^t \binom{i-j}{t}.
$$

By the binomial theorem (13),

$$
\sum_{t=0}^{n} \binom{n}{t}x^t = (1+x)^n,
$$

so with $x=-1$ and $n=i-j$,

$$
\sum_{t=0}^{i-j} (-1)^t \binom{i-j}{t} = (1-1)^{i-j}.
$$

Hence

$$
(PQ)_{ij} = \binom{i}{j}(1-1)^{i-j}.
$$

If $i=j$, then $(1-1)^0 = 1$ and $(PQ)_{ii} = 1$. If $i>j$, then $(1-1)^{i-j} = 0$ and $(PQ)_{ij} = 0$. If $i<j$, both matrices are triangular, so $(PQ)_{ij} = 0$.

Thus $PQ = I$, and $Q = P^{-1}$, where

$$
q_{ij} = (-1)^{i-j}\binom{i}{j} \quad (i \ge j).
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
