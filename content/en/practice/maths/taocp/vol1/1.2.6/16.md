[Section 1.2.6: Binomial Coefficients](../)

**Exercise 16.** [*M15*] Given that $n$ and $k$ are positive integers, prove the symmetrical identity

$$
(-1)^n\binom{-n}{k-1} = (-1)^k\binom{-k}{n-1}.
$$

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 3m01s  

---

## Solution

For integers $k \ge 0$, definition (3) gives

$$
\binom{-n}{k-1}
= \prod_{j=1}^{k-1} \frac{-n+1-j}{j}
= \prod_{j=1}^{k-1} \frac{-(n-1+j)}{j}.
$$

Each factor contributes a sign $(-1)$, hence

$$
\binom{-n}{k-1}
= (-1)^{k-1} \prod_{j=1}^{k-1} \frac{n-1+j}{j}
= (-1)^{k-1} \binom{n+k-2}{k-1},
$$

since the product matches the factorial form (5) for $\binom{n+k-2}{k-1}$.

Multiplying by $(-1)^n$ yields

$$
(-1)^n \binom{-n}{k-1}
= (-1)^{n+k-1} \binom{n+k-2}{k-1}.
$$

Interchanging the roles of $n$ and $k$ in the same argument gives

$$
\binom{-k}{n-1}
= (-1)^{n-1} \binom{n+k-2}{n-1},
$$

so

$$
(-1)^k \binom{-k}{n-1}
= (-1)^{n+k-1} \binom{n+k-2}{n-1}.
$$

Using the symmetry condition (6) with $N = n+k-2$,

$$
\binom{n+k-2}{k-1}
= \binom{n+k-2}{(n+k-2)-(k-1)}
= \binom{n+k-2}{n-1}.
$$

Both expressions therefore equal $(-1)^{n+k-1} \binom{n+k-2}{k-1}$, so

$$
(-1)^n\binom{-n}{k-1} = (-1)^k\binom{-k}{n-1}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
