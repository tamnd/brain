**Exercise 13.** [*M13*] Prove the summation formula, Eq. (10).

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m02s  

---

## Solution

Fix a real number $r$ and an integer $n \ge 0$. Define

$$
S_n = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{r+k}{k}.
$$

We prove by induction on $n$ that

$$
S_n = \binom{r+n+1}{n}.
$$

For $n=0$, the sum reduces to a single term,

$$
S_0 = \binom{r}{0}.
$$

By definition (3), $\binom{r}{0} = 1$. On the other hand,

$$
\binom{r+1}{0} = 1.
$$

Hence $S_0 = \binom{r+1}{0}$, which matches the required formula.

Assume for some integer $n \ge 0$ that

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{r+k}{k} = \binom{r+n+1}{n}.
$$

Then

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n+1} \binom{r+k}{k}
= \left(\sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{r+k}{k}\right) + \binom{r+n+1}{n+1}.
$$

Substituting the induction hypothesis gives

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n+1} \binom{r+k}{k}
= \binom{r+n+1}{n} + \binom{r+n+1}{n+1}.
$$

Apply the addition formula (9) with $r \leftarrow r+n+1$ and $k \leftarrow n+1$, giving

$$
\binom{r+n+2}{n+1}
= \binom{r+n+1}{n+1} + \binom{r+n+1}{n}.
$$

Reordering terms yields

$$
\binom{r+n+1}{n} + \binom{r+n+1}{n+1}
= \binom{r+n+2}{n+1}.
$$

Thus

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n+1} \binom{r+k}{k} = \binom{r+n+2}{n+1},
$$

which completes the induction step.

Therefore, for all integers $n \ge 0$,

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{r+k}{k} = \binom{r+n+1}{n}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎

## Notes

Identity (10) follows directly from repeated use of the Pascal relation (9), and the induction above formalizes that iterative construction into a single closed form.
