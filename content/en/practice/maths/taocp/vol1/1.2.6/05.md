**Exercise 5.** [*05*] Use Pascal's triangle to explain the fact that $11^4 = 14641$.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 2m11s  

---

From Pascal’s triangle, the row corresponding to $r=4$ is

$$
\binom{4}{0},\ \binom{4}{1},\ \binom{4}{2},\ \binom{4}{3},\ \binom{4}{4}
= 1,\ 4,\ 6,\ 4,\ 1.
$$

By the binomial theorem (13),

$$
11^4 = (10+1)^4 = \sum_{k=0}^{4} \binom{4}{k} 10^{4-k} 1^k.
$$

Substituting the coefficients from Pascal’s triangle gives

$$
11^4 = 1\cdot 10^4 + 4\cdot 10^3 + 6\cdot 10^2 + 4\cdot 10 + 1.
$$

Evaluating each term yields

$$
11^4 = 10000 + 4000 + 600 + 40 + 1 = 14641.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
