[Section 1.2.6: Binomial Coefficients](../)

**Exercise 47.** [*M21*] Given that $k$ is an integer, show that

$$
\binom{r}{k}\binom{r-1/2}{k}
= \binom{2r}{k}\binom{2r-k}{k}/4^k
= \binom{2r}{2k}\binom{2k}{k}/4^k.
$$

Give a simpler formula for the special case $r=-1/2$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m43s  

---

Let $k \ge 0$ be an integer and $r$ an arbitrary parameter.

## 1. Main identity

Start from the falling factorial definition

$$
\binom{r}{k}=\frac{r^{\underline{k}}}{k!}, \qquad r^{\underline{k}}=r(r-1)\cdots(r-k+1).
$$

Then

$$
\binom{r}{k}\binom{r-\tfrac12}{k}
=\frac{r^{\underline{k}}\,(r-\tfrac12)^{\underline{k}}}{(k!)^2}
=\frac{1}{(k!)^2}\prod_{j=0}^{k-1}(r-j)\left(r-\tfrac12-j\right).
$$

For each factor,

$$
(r-j)\left(r-\tfrac12-j\right)
=\frac{(2r-2j)(2r-2j-1)}{4}.
$$

Hence

$$
\prod_{j=0}^{k-1}(r-j)\left(r-\tfrac12-j\right)
=4^{-k}\prod_{j=0}^{k-1}(2r-2j)(2r-2j-1).
$$

The right-hand product is

$$
(2r)^{\underline{2k}}=(2r)(2r-1)\cdots(2r-2k+1),
$$

so

$$
\binom{r}{k}\binom{r-\tfrac12}{k}
=\frac{(2r)^{\underline{2k}}}{4^k (k!)^2}.
$$

## 2. First factorization

Use

$$
(2r)^{\underline{2k}}=(2r)^{\underline{k}}(2r-k)^{\underline{k}}.
$$

Then

$$
\frac{(2r)^{\underline{2k}}}{(k!)^2}
=\frac{(2r)^{\underline{k}}}{k!}\cdot \frac{(2r-k)^{\underline{k}}}{k!}
=\binom{2r}{k}\binom{2r-k}{k}.
$$

Thus

$$
\binom{r}{k}\binom{r-\tfrac12}{k}
=\frac{\binom{2r}{k}\binom{2r-k}{k}}{4^k}.
$$

## 3. Second factorization

Also,

$$
\binom{2r}{2k}\binom{2k}{k}
=\frac{(2r)^{\underline{2k}}}{(2k)!}\cdot \frac{(2k)!}{(k!)^2}
=\frac{(2r)^{\underline{2k}}}{(k!)^2}.
$$

Therefore,

$$
\binom{r}{k}\binom{r-\tfrac12}{k}
=\frac{\binom{2r}{2k}\binom{2k}{k}}{4^k}.
$$

## 4. Special case $r=-\tfrac12$

Start from the identity

$$
\binom{r}{k}\binom{r-\tfrac12}{k}
=\frac{\binom{2r}{2k}\binom{2k}{k}}{4^k}.
$$

Set $r=-\tfrac12$, so $2r=-1$:

$$
\binom{-\tfrac12}{k}\binom{-1}{k}
=\frac{\binom{-1}{2k}\binom{2k}{k}}{4^k}.
$$

Now evaluate the negative-integer binomial coefficients.

For $k>0$,

$$
\binom{-1}{k}=(-1)^k,
\qquad
\binom{-1}{2k}=(-1)^{2k}=1.
$$

Substitute:

$$
\binom{-\tfrac12}{k}(-1)^k
=\frac{\binom{2k}{k}}{4^k}.
$$

Hence

$$
\binom{-\tfrac12}{k}
=(-1)^k \frac{\binom{2k}{k}}{4^k}.
$$

## Final result

$$
\binom{r}{k}\binom{r-\tfrac12}{k}
= \frac{\binom{2r}{k}\binom{2r-k}{k}}{4^k}
= \frac{\binom{2r}{2k}\binom{2k}{k}}{4^k},
$$

and for $r=-\tfrac12$,

$$
\binom{-\tfrac12}{k}=(-1)^k \frac{\binom{2k}{k}}{4^k}.
$$
