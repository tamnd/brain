**Exercise 51.** [*M21*] Prove Abel's formula, Eq. (16), by writing $y=(x+y)-x$, expanding the right-hand side in powers of $(x+y)$, and applying the result of the previous exercise.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m58s  

---

Let $z=x+y$. We prove Abel’s formula

$$
(x+y)^{\overline r}
=
\sum_{k=0}^r \binom{r}{k}\, x^{\overline{r-k}}\, y^{\overline{k}}
$$

by showing that the right-hand side equals $z^{\overline r}$ as a polynomial in $z$.

Define

$$
F(z)
=
\sum_{k=0}^r \binom{r}{k}\, x^{\overline{r-k}}\, (z-x)^{\overline{k}}.
$$

We must show $F(z)=z^{\overline r}$.

### 1. Expand $(z-x)^{\overline k}$ in rising powers of $z$

We use the standard translation identity for rising factorials:

$$
(u+a)^{\overline k}
=
\sum_{i=0}^k \binom{k}{i}\, a^{\overline{k-i}}\, u^{\overline i}.
$$

With $u=z$ and $a=-x$, this gives

$$
(z-x)^{\overline k}
=
\sum_{i=0}^k \binom{k}{i}\, (-x)^{\overline{k-i}}\, z^{\overline i}.
$$

Substitute into $F(z)$:

$$
F(z)
=
\sum_{k=0}^r \binom{r}{k} x^{\overline{r-k}}
\sum_{i=0}^k \binom{k}{i} (-x)^{\overline{k-i}} z^{\overline i}.
$$

Swap the order of summation:

$$
F(z)
=
\sum_{i=0}^r z^{\overline i}
\sum_{k=i}^r \binom{r}{k}\binom{k}{i}
x^{\overline{r-k}} (-x)^{\overline{k-i}}.
$$

### 2. Simplify coefficients

Use

$$
\binom{r}{k}\binom{k}{i}
=
\binom{r}{i}\binom{r-i}{k-i}.
$$

Hence the coefficient of $z^{\overline i}$ is

$$
\binom{r}{i}
\sum_{k=i}^r \binom{r-i}{k-i}
x^{\overline{r-k}} (-x)^{\overline{k-i}}.
$$

Let $m=k-i$. Then $0 \le m \le r-i$, and $r-k = r-i-m$. The inner sum becomes

$$
\sum_{m=0}^{r-i}
\binom{r-i}{m}
x^{\overline{r-i-m}} (-x)^{\overline m}.
$$

### 3. Apply Exercise 1.2.6.50

From Exercise 1.2.6.50,

$$
\sum_{m=0}^{n}
\binom{n}{m}
x^{\overline{n-m}} (-x)^{\overline m}
=
\begin{cases}
1, & n=0,\\
0, & n \ge 1.
\end{cases}
$$

Apply this with $n=r-i$. Then:

- If $i < r$, so $r-i \ge 1$, the inner sum is $0$.
- If $i = r$, the inner sum equals $1$.

Therefore all coefficients vanish except the top one.

### 4. Identify the remaining term

For $i=r$, we have:

$$
\binom{r}{r} \cdot 1 = 1.
$$

Hence

$$
F(z) = z^{\overline r}.
$$

### 5. Substitute back $z=x+y$

Since $z=x+y$,

$$
\sum_{k=0}^r \binom{r}{k}\, x^{\overline{r-k}}\, (z-x)^{\overline{k}}
=
z^{\overline r}
$$

becomes

$$
\sum_{k=0}^r \binom{r}{k}\, x^{\overline{r-k}}\, y^{\overline{k}}
=
(x+y)^{\overline r}.
$$

This is Abel’s formula. ∎
