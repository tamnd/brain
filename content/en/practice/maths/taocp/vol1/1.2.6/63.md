**Exercise 63.** [*M30*] If $l,m,n$ are integers and $n \ge 0$, prove that

$$
\sum_{j,k} (-1)^{j+k}\binom{j+k}{k+l}\binom{r}{j}\binom{n}{k}\binom{s+n-j-k}{m-j}
= (-1)^l \binom{n+r}{n+l}\binom{s-r}{m-n-l}.
$$

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 4m59s  

---

The original solution fails because it introduces an incorrect algebraic factorization and then builds a decoupling argument on it. The correct approach must avoid any structural separation that is not algebraically justified and instead keep the coefficient extraction consistent throughout.

## Correct solution

Let

$$
S=\sum_{j,k} (-1)^{j+k}\binom{j+k}{k+l}\binom{r}{j}\binom{n}{k}\binom{s+n-j-k}{m-j},
\qquad n\ge 0.
$$

We use coefficient extraction in two auxiliary variables $u,v$.

## 1. Convert both binomial coefficients to coefficient form

$$
\binom{j+k}{k+l} = [u^{k+l}](1+u)^{j+k},
\qquad
\binom{s+n-j-k}{m-j} = [v^{m-j}](1+v)^{s+n-j-k}.
$$

Substitute into $S$ and interchange summation and coefficient extraction:

$$
S = [u^{l}v^{m}] \sum_{j,k} (-1)^{j+k}\binom{r}{j}\binom{n}{k}
(1+u)^{j+k}(1+v)^{s+n-j-k}
u^{-k}v^{j}.
$$

## 2. Factor terms independent of $j,k$

Extract the common factor $(1+v)^{s+n}$:

$$
S = [u^{l}v^{m}](1+v)^{s+n}
\sum_{j,k}\binom{r}{j}\binom{n}{k}(-1)^{j+k}
\left(\frac{(1+u)v}{1+v}\right)^j
\left(\frac{1+u}{u(1+v)}\right)^k.
$$

Now the double sum separates:

$$
\sum_j \binom{r}{j}(-1)^j A^j = (1-A)^r,
\qquad
\sum_k \binom{n}{k}(-1)^k B^k = (1-B)^n,
$$

with

$$
A=\frac{(1+u)v}{1+v}, \qquad B=\frac{1+u}{u(1+v)}.
$$

Thus

$$
S = [u^{l}v^{m}](1+v)^{s+n}(1-A)^r(1-B)^n.
$$

## 3. Simplify the algebra correctly (no factorization errors)

Compute each factor:

### First factor

$$
1-A = 1-\frac{(1+u)v}{1+v}
= \frac{1+v-(1+u)v}{1+v}
= \frac{1-uv}{1+v}.
$$

### Second factor

$$
1-B = 1-\frac{1+u}{u(1+v)}
= \frac{u(1+v)-(1+u)}{u(1+v)}
= \frac{uv-1}{u(1+v)}
= -\frac{1-uv}{u(1+v)}.
$$

Substitute:

$$
(1-A)^r(1-B)^n
= (1-uv)^{r+n}\,(1+v)^{-r-n}\,u^{-n}(-1)^n.
$$

Hence

$$
S = (-1)^n [u^{l}v^{m}]\, u^{-n}(1+v)^{s-r}(1-uv)^{r+n}.
$$

Rewriting coefficient extraction:

$$
S = (-1)^n [u^{l+n}v^{m}] (1+v)^{s-r}(1-uv)^{r+n}.
$$

## 4. Extract coefficients

Expand:

$$
(1-uv)^{r+n} = \sum_{t\ge 0} (-1)^t \binom{r+n}{t} u^t v^t.
$$

To obtain $u^{l+n}v^m$, we must take $t=l+n$. This forces the remaining $v$-power to be:

$$
v^{m-(l+n)}.
$$

Thus

$$
S = (-1)^n (-1)^{l+n}\binom{r+n}{l+n}
\,[v^{m-n-l}](1+v)^{s-r}.
$$

## 5. Final coefficient extraction

$$
[v^{m-n-l}](1+v)^{s-r} = \binom{s-r}{m-n-l}.
$$

Combine signs:

$$
(-1)^n(-1)^{l+n} = (-1)^l.
$$

## 6. Final result

$$
S = (-1)^l \binom{n+r}{n+l}\binom{s-r}{m-n-l}.
$$

## Conclusion

The corrected proof avoids the invalid factorization entirely and keeps all algebra within valid coefficient-extraction identities. The expression is reduced cleanly to a single bivariate generating function, followed by a direct coefficient extraction using a single binomial expansion, yielding the stated identity.
