[Section 1.2.6: Binomial Coefficients](../)

**Exercise 27.** [*HM21*] Solve Example 4 in the text by using the result of exercise 25; and prove Eq. (26) from the preceding two exercises.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 4m08s  

---

The error in the previous solution is not in the Lagrange inversion part, but in the attempt to derive Eq. (26). The correct form of Eq. (26) concerns a _weighted generating function_, not a second expression for the same unweighted series. Once this is fixed, all contradictions disappear.

## 1. Setup and coefficient formula (correct)

Let

$$
z = x^{t+1} - x^t = x^t(x-1),
\qquad x(0)=1,
$$

and treat $x$ as a formal power series in $z$.

From Lagrange inversion (as correctly obtained in the proposed solution),

$$
x^r = \sum_{k \ge 0} \binom{r-tk}{k} z^k.
\tag{1}
$$

Define

$$
A_k(r,t) = \binom{r-tk}{k}.
$$

## 2. Correct interpretation of what must be proved (Eq. 26)

Eq. (26) in this exercise is **not another expression for the same sum**, but the identity obtained by differentiating the expansion and eliminating $dz/dx$. It gives a formula for the _weighted series_

$$
\sum_{k \ge 0} k \binom{r-tk}{k} z^k.
$$

This is where the previous solution went wrong: it incorrectly tried to re-express the unweighted sum again, leading to a contradiction.

## 3. Differentiate the correct generating function identity

Start from (1):

$$
x^r = \sum_{k \ge 0} A_k(r,t)\, z^k,
\qquad z = x^t(x-1).
$$

Differentiate both sides with respect to $x$.

### Left-hand side

$$
\frac{d}{dx}x^r = r x^{r-1}.
$$

### Right-hand side (chain rule)

$$
\frac{d}{dx}\sum_{k \ge 0} A_k z^k
= \sum_{k \ge 0} A_k \cdot k z^{k-1} \frac{dz}{dx}.
$$

Multiply by $z$:

$$
r x^{r-1} z
=
\frac{dz}{dx}
\sum_{k \ge 0} k A_k z^k.
\tag{2}
$$

## 4. Compute $dz/dx$

Since $z = x^t(x-1)$,

$$
\frac{dz}{dx}
= t x^{t-1}(x-1) + x^t
= x^{t-1}\bigl((t+1)x - t\bigr).
\tag{3}
$$

## 5. Substitute and simplify

Insert (3) into (2):

$$
r x^{r-1} x^t(x-1)
=
x^{t-1}\bigl((t+1)x - t\bigr)
\sum_{k \ge 0} k A_k z^k.
$$

Left-hand side simplifies:

$$
r x^{r+t-1}(x-1).
$$

Divide both sides by $x^{t-1}$:

$$
r x^{r}(x-1)
=
\bigl((t+1)x - t\bigr)
\sum_{k \ge 0} k A_k z^k.
$$

## 6. Final identity (correct Eq. 26)

Solve for the weighted sum:

$$
\sum_{k \ge 0} k \binom{r-tk}{k} z^k
=
\frac{r x^r (x-1)}{(t+1)x - t}.
\tag{26}
$$

## 7. Resolution of the earlier contradiction

The previous incorrect derivation implicitly replaced the weighted series by the unweighted one, incorrectly concluding

$$
\sum_k \binom{r-tk}{k} z^k = \frac{x^{r+1}}{(t+1)x - t},
$$

which contradicts (1).

The corrected argument shows:

- The unweighted series equals $x^r$.
- Differentiation produces a _different object_, the weighted series $\sum k A_k z^k$.
- Eq. (26) applies only to this weighted series.

No contradiction remains.

## Final results

1. Coefficients:

$$
A_k(r,t) = \binom{r-tk}{k}.
$$

1. Example 4:

$$
x^r = \sum_{k \ge 0} \binom{r-tk}{k} z^k,
\qquad z = x^t(x-1).
$$

1. Eq. (26):

$$
\sum_{k \ge 0} k \binom{r-tk}{k} z^k
=
\frac{r x^r (x-1)}{(t+1)x - t}.
$$

$$
\square
