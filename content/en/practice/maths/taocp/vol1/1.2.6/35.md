## Solution

Let the Stirling numbers of the second kind $S(n,k)$ and the Stirling numbers of the first kind $s(n,k)$ be defined by Eqs. (44) and (45) of the text:

$$
x^n=\sum_k S(n,k)\,x^{\underline{k}}, \qquad
x^{\underline{n}}=\sum_k s(n,k)\,x^k,
$$

with summation over all integers $k$.

The addition formulas (46) express the values of these numbers at $m+n$ in terms of values at $m$ and $n$. Both are obtained by multiplying the defining expansions and rewriting products in the same basis.

### Stirling numbers of the second kind

Start from

$$
x^{m+n}=x^m x^n.
$$

Apply Eq. (44) to each factor:

$$
x^m=\sum_i S(m,i)\,x^{\underline{i}}, \qquad
x^n=\sum_j S(n,j)\,x^{\underline{j}}.
$$

Hence

$$
x^{m+n}=\sum_{i,j} S(m,i)S(n,j)\,x^{\underline{i}}x^{\underline{j}}.
$$

The product of falling factorials satisfies

$$
x^{\underline{i}}x^{\underline{j}}
=\sum_{t\ge 0}\binom{i}{t}\binom{j}{t}\,t!\,x^{\underline{i+j-t}},
$$

since choosing $t$ factors from the first block and $t$ from the second produces $t!$ pairings, and the remaining factors form $x^{\underline{i+j-t}}$.

Substituting this into the previous expression gives

$$
x^{m+n}
=\sum_{i,j} S(m,i)S(n,j)
\sum_{t\ge 0}\binom{i}{t}\binom{j}{t}\,t!\,x^{\underline{i+j-t}}.
$$

Reindex with $k=i+j-t$, so $t=i+j-k$. The coefficient of $x^{\underline{k}}$ is therefore

$$
S(m+n,k)
=\sum_{i,j}
S(m,i)S(n,j)
\binom{i}{i+j-k}\binom{j}{i+j-k}\,(i+j-k)!,
$$

where the summation is over all integers $i,j$ such that $i+j\ge k$.

This is the addition formula for Stirling numbers of the second kind.

This completes the derivation of the first part of (46). ∎

### Stirling numbers of the first kind

Start from the factorial-power identity

$$
x^{\underline{m+n}}=x^{\underline{m}}\, (x-m)^{\underline{n}}.
$$

Apply Eq. (45) to each falling factorial:

$$
x^{\underline{m}}=\sum_i s(m,i)x^i, \qquad
(x-m)^{\underline{n}}=\sum_j s(n,j)(x-m)^j.
$$

Expand $(x-m)^j$ using the binomial theorem:

$$
(x-m)^j=\sum_{t}\binom{j}{t}x^t(-m)^{j-t}.
$$

Hence

$$
x^{\underline{m+n}}
=\sum_{i,j,t} s(m,i)s(n,j)\binom{j}{t}(-m)^{j-t}x^{i+t}.
$$

Let $k=i+t$. The coefficient of $x^k$ is

$$
s(m+n,k)
=\sum_{i,j}
s(m,i)s(n,j)\binom{j}{k-i}(-m)^{j-(k-i)}.
$$

This is the addition formula for Stirling numbers of the first kind in the form corresponding to Eq. (46).

This completes the derivation of the second part of (46). ∎
