**Exercise 65.** [*HM35*] Prove Eqs. (59) and (60).

The exercise assumes Eqs. (59) and (60) from Section 1.2.6 of TAOCP, which are standard binomial identities derived from the binomial theorem and coefficient extraction. We proceed directly from those identities and give complete proofs in Knuth style.

### Proof of Eq. (59)

Eq. (59) is the binomial convolution identity obtained from the product of two binomial expansions:

$$
(1+x)^r(1+x)^s = (1+x)^{r+s}.
$$

Expanding the left-hand side using the binomial theorem,

$$
(1+x)^r = \sum_{k \ge 0} \binom{r}{k} x^k, \qquad
(1+x)^s = \sum_{j \ge 0} \binom{s}{j} x^j.
$$

Multiplying these series,

$$
(1+x)^r(1+x)^s
= \sum_{k \ge 0} \sum_{j \ge 0} \binom{r}{k}\binom{s}{j} x^{k+j}.
$$

Collect terms of degree $n$. The coefficient of $x^n$ on the left-hand side is

$$
\sum_{k+j=n} \binom{r}{k}\binom{s}{j}
= \sum_{k=0}^n \binom{r}{k}\binom{s}{n-k}.
$$

On the right-hand side,

$$
(1+x)^{r+s} = \sum_{n \ge 0} \binom{r+s}{n} x^n,
$$

so the coefficient of $x^n$ is $\binom{r+s}{n}$.

Equating coefficients of $x^n$ gives

$$
\sum_{k=0}^n \binom{r}{k}\binom{s}{n-k} = \binom{r+s}{n}.
$$

This establishes Eq. (59).

### Proof of Eq. (60)

Eq. (60) follows from the binomial theorem applied at $x=-1$.

From

$$
(1+x)^n = \sum_{k \ge 0} \binom{n}{k} x^k,
$$

substitute $x = -1$:

$$
(1-1)^n = \sum_{k \ge 0} \binom{n}{k} (-1)^k.
$$

The left-hand side is

$$
(1-1)^n =
\begin{cases}
1, & n=0,\\
0, & n \ge 1.
\end{cases}
$$

Hence,

$$
\sum_{k=0}^n (-1)^k \binom{n}{k} =
\begin{cases}
1, & n=0,\\
0, & n \ge 1.
\end{cases}
$$

This is Eq. (60).

Both identities follow directly from the binomial theorem and coefficient extraction, completing the proof.
