**Exercise 39.** [*M10*] What is the sum $\sum_k \left[{n \atop k}\right]$ of the numbers in each row of Stirling's first triangle? What is the sum of these numbers with alternating signs?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m11s  

---

The numbers $\left[{n \atop k}\right]$ count permutations of $n$ objects with exactly $k$ cycles. Summing over all possible numbers of cycles therefore counts every permutation exactly once, since each permutation has a unique cycle decomposition. Hence

$$
\sum_k \left[{n \atop k}\right] = n!.
$$

For the alternating sum, use the identity connecting Stirling numbers of the first kind to falling factorials:

$$
x^{\underline{n}} = \sum_k (-1)^{n-k} \left[{n \atop k}\right] x^k.
$$

Setting $x=1$ gives

$$
1^{\underline{n}} = \sum_k (-1)^{n-k} \left[{n \atop k}\right].
$$

For $n \ge 2$, the falling factorial $1^{\underline{n}} = 1 \cdot 0 \cdot (-1)\cdots = 0$, hence

$$
\sum_k (-1)^{n-k} \left[{n \atop k}\right] = 0.
$$

Multiplying by $(-1)^n$ yields

$$
\sum_k (-1)^k \left[{n \atop k}\right] = 0 \qquad (n \ge 2).
$$

For small cases, $n=0$ gives $[0\ 0]=1$, so the alternating sum is $1$, and $n=1$ gives $[1\ 1]=1$, so the alternating sum is $-1$. Thus

$$
\sum_k (-1)^k \left[{n \atop k}\right] =
\begin{cases}
1, & n=0,\\
-1, & n=1,\\
0, & n \ge 2.
\end{cases}
$$

This completes the answer. ∎
