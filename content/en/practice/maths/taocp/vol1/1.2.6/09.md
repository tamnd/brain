[Section 1.2.6: Binomial Coefficients](../)

**Exercise 9.** [*01*] What is the value of $\binom{n}{n}$? (Consider all integers $n$.)

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 54s  

---

Let $n \ge 0$ be an integer. From definition (3) with $r=n$ and $k=n$,

$$
\binom{n}{n}=\frac{n(n-1)\cdots(n-n+1)}{n!}.
$$

The numerator contains exactly the same $n$ factors as $n!$, in reverse order, hence the quotient equals $1$, so $\binom{n}{n}=1$.

Let $n<0$ be an integer. Then $k=n$ is a negative integer, and by definition (3),

$$
\binom{r}{k}=0 \quad \text{for integer } k<0.
$$

Hence $\binom{n}{n}=0$ in this case.

Therefore,

$$
\binom{n}{n}=
\begin{cases}
1, & n \ge 0,\\
0, & n < 0.
\end{cases}
$$

This completes the answer. ∎
