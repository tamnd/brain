**Exercise 17.** [*M18*] Prove the Chu--Vandermonde formula (21) from Eq. (15), using the idea that $(1+x)^{r+s} = (1+x)^r(1+x)^s$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m05s  

---

## Solution

Equation (15) gives the binomial expansion

$$
(1+x)^r = \sum_{k} \binom{r}{k} x^k,
$$

valid for all real $r$ and integer $k \ge 0$, and similarly

$$
(1+x)^s = \sum_{j} \binom{s}{j} x^j.
$$

Multiplying these two series yields

$$
(1+x)^r(1+x)^s
= \left(\sum_{k} \binom{r}{k} x^k\right)\left(\sum_{j} \binom{s}{j} x^j\right).
$$

Distributing products term by term gives

$$
(1+x)^r(1+x)^s
= \sum_{k}\sum_{j} \binom{r}{k}\binom{s}{j} x^{k+j}.
$$

Let $n = k+j$. For a fixed $n$, all contributing pairs $(k,j)$ satisfy $j = n-k$, hence

$$
(1+x)^r(1+x)^s
= \sum_{n} \left(\sum_{k} \binom{r}{k}\binom{s}{n-k}\right)x^n,
$$

where terms with $n-k<0$ contribute $0$ by definition of binomial coefficients.

The left-hand side simplifies to

$$
(1+x)^{r+s}.
$$

Applying Eq. (15) again,

$$
(1+x)^{r+s} = \sum_{n} \binom{r+s}{n} x^n.
$$

Both expressions represent the same formal power series in $x$, hence their coefficients of $x^n$ are equal for every integer $n$. Therefore,

$$
\binom{r+s}{n} = \sum_{k} \binom{r}{k}\binom{s}{n-k},
\qquad \text{integer } n \ge 0.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
