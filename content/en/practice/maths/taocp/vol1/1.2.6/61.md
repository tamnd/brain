**Exercise 61.** [*M25*] Evaluate the sum

$$
\sum_k \left[{n+1 \atop k+1}\right]\left\{{k \atop m}\right\}(-1)^{k-m},
$$

thereby obtaining a companion formula for Eq. (55).

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 5m14s  

---

### 1. Where the previous solution fails

The key failure is the incorrect “recognition step.” The quantity

$$
S(n,m)=\sum_k \left[{n+1 \atop k+1}\right]\left\{{k \atop m}\right\}(-1)^{k-m}
$$

is not another Stirling number of the second kind. The earlier argument incorrectly forced it into that form without correctly evaluating the convolution.

A correct approach must evaluate the convolution directly from the inverse relations, without inserting unsupported structural claims.

## 2. Start from the correct inverse relation

Recall the inverse Stirling relations:

$$
\sum_k (-1)^{n-k}\left[{n \atop k}\right]\left\{{k \atop m}\right\}=\delta_{nm}.
$$

Replace $n\to n+1$:

$$
\sum_k (-1)^{n+1-k}\left[{n+1 \atop k}\right]\left\{{k \atop m}\right\}=\delta_{n+1,m}.
$$

Now shift the index $k \mapsto k+1$. Then:

$$
\sum_k (-1)^{n-k}\left[{n+1 \atop k+1}\right]\left\{{k+1 \atop m}\right\}=\delta_{n+1,m}.
$$

## 3. Express $\{k+1 \atop m\}$

Use the Stirling recurrence:

$$
\left\{{k+1 \atop m}\right\}=\left\{{k \atop m-1}\right\}+m\left\{{k \atop m}\right\}.
$$

Substitute into the identity:

$$
\sum_k (-1)^{n-k}\left[{n+1 \atop k+1}\right]\left\{{k \atop m-1}\right\}
+
m\sum_k (-1)^{n-k}\left[{n+1 \atop k+1}\right]\left\{{k \atop m}\right\}
=\delta_{n+1,m}.
$$

## 4. Introduce the target sum

Define

$$
S(n,m)=\sum_k \left[{n+1 \atop k+1}\right]\left\{{k \atop m}\right\}(-1)^{k-m}.
$$

Rewrite the sign:

$$
(-1)^{n-k}=(-1)^{n-m}(-1)^{k-m}.
$$

Thus

$$
\sum_k (-1)^{n-k}\left[{n+1 \atop k+1}\right]\left\{{k \atop m}\right\}
=
(-1)^{n-m} S(n,m).
$$

Similarly,

$$
\sum_k (-1)^{n-k}\left[{n+1 \atop k+1}\right]\left\{{k \atop m-1}\right\}
=
(-1)^{n-m+1} S(n,m-1).
$$

Substitute into the recurrence:

$$
(-1)^{n-m+1} S(n,m-1) + m(-1)^{n-m} S(n,m) = \delta_{n+1,m}.
$$

Multiply by $(-1)^{n-m}$:

$$
- S(n,m-1) + m S(n,m) = (-1)^{n-m}\delta_{n+1,m}.
$$

## 5. Solve the recurrence

### (i) Case $m \neq n+1$

Then the right-hand side is $0$, so:

$$
m S(n,m) = S(n,m-1).
$$

This gives the forward recurrence:

$$
S(n,m)=\frac{1}{m}S(n,m-1).
$$

Iterating down to $m=0$:

$$
S(n,m)=\frac{S(n,0)}{m!}.
$$

### (ii) Evaluate $S(n,0)$

$$
S(n,0)=\sum_k \left[{n+1 \atop k+1}\right]\left\{{k \atop 0}\right\}(-1)^k.
$$

Since $\left\{{k \atop 0}\right\}=\delta_{k,0}$, only $k=0$ contributes:

$$
S(n,0)=\left[{n+1 \atop 1}\right]=n!.
$$

Thus:

$$
S(n,m)=\frac{n!}{m!}.
$$

## 6. Final check of the missing boundary case

When $m=n$, the formula gives:

$$
S(n,n)=\frac{n!}{n!}=1,
$$

which matches direct evaluation.

For $m>n$, Stirling numbers $\left\{{k \atop m}\right\}=0$, so $S(n,m)=0$.

## 7. Final result

$$
\boxed{
\sum_k \left[{n+1 \atop k+1}\right]\left\{{k \atop m}\right\}(-1)^{k-m}
=
\frac{n!}{m!}
}
$$

(valid for $0 \le m \le n$, and $0$ otherwise).

## 8. Interpretation (companion to Eq. (55))

This identity shows that the mixed Stirling convolution with the shift $k \mapsto k+1$ collapses not to another Stirling number, but to a simple factorial ratio. This is the correct companion formula to the inverse Stirling relation in Eq. (55).
