**Exercise 6.** [*10*] Pascal's triangle (Table 1) can be extended in all directions by use of the addition formula, Eq. (9). Find the three rows that go on top of Table 1 (i.e., for $r=-1$, $-2$, and $-3$).

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m05s  

---

For negative $r$, the entries are determined by Eq. (9) together with $\binom{r}{0}=1$ for all $r$. For $r=-1$, we apply

$$
\binom{-1}{k}=\binom{-2}{k}+\binom{-2}{k-1}
$$

inductively and obtain the closed form

$$
\binom{-1}{k}=\frac{(-1)(-2)\cdots(-k)}{k!}=(-1)^k.
$$

Hence the row for $r=-1$ is

$$
1,\ -1,\ 1,\ -1,\ 1,\ -1,\ \ldots
$$

For $r=-2$, Eq. (3) gives

$$
\binom{-2}{k}=\frac{(-2)(-3)\cdots(-k-1)}{k!}.
$$

The numerator equals $(-1)^k (k+1)!$, so

$$
\binom{-2}{k}=(-1)^k (k+1).
$$

Thus the row for $r=-2$ is

$$
1,\ -2,\ 3,\ -4,\ 5,\ -6,\ \ldots
$$

For $r=-3$, similarly

$$
\binom{-3}{k}=\frac{(-3)(-4)\cdots(-k-2)}{k!}
= (-1)^k \frac{(k+2)!}{2!\,k!}
= (-1)^k \binom{k+2}{2}.
$$

Therefore the row for $r=-3$ is

$$
1,\ -3,\ 6,\ -10,\ 15,\ -21,\ \ldots
$$

This completes the extension of Table 1 to the rows $r=-1,-2,-3$. ∎
