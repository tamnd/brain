[Section 1.2.6: Binomial Coefficients](../)

**Exercise 28.** [*M25*] Prove that

$$
\sum_k \binom{r+tk}{k}\binom{s-tk}{n-k}
= \sum_{k \ge 0} \binom{r+s-k}{n-k} t^k,
$$

if $n$ is a nonnegative integer.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m55s  

---

Let

$$
L_n(r,s,t)=\sum_{k\ge 0} \binom{r+tk}{k}\binom{s-tk}{n-k},\qquad
R_n(r,s,t)=\sum_{k\ge 0} \binom{r+s-k}{n-k}t^k.
$$

We prove $L_n=R_n$ for all nonnegative integers $n$, using a double induction on $n$ and $s$.

## 1. Initial condition $n=0$

For $n=0$, only the term $k=0$ contributes in both sums, since $\binom{a}{-k}=0$ for $k>0$.

Hence

$$
L_0(r,s,t)=\binom{r}{0}\binom{s}{0}=1,
\qquad
R_0(r,s,t)=\binom{r+s}{0}t^0=1.
$$

Thus $L_0=R_0=1$.

## 2. A common recurrence in $n$ and $s$

We use Pascalâ€™s identity in the form

$$
\binom{A}{B}=\binom{A-1}{B}+\binom{A-1}{B-1}.
$$

### 2.1 Recurrence for $L_n$

Apply Pascalâ€™s identity to $\binom{s-tk}{n-k}$:

$$
\binom{s-tk}{n-k}
=
\binom{(s-1)-tk}{n-k}
+
\binom{(s-1)-tk}{n-k-1}.
$$

Substitute into $L_n$:

$$
L_n(r,s,t)
=
\sum_{k\ge 0}\binom{r+tk}{k}\binom{(s-1)-tk}{n-k}
+
\sum_{k\ge 0}\binom{r+tk}{k}\binom{(s-1)-tk}{n-k-1}.
$$

The first sum is $L_n(r,s-1,t)$.

In the second sum, reindex $n-1$ (no change in $k$-range):

$$
\sum_{k\ge 0}\binom{r+tk}{k}\binom{(s-1)-tk}{(n-1)-k}
=
L_{n-1}(r,s-1,t).
$$

Thus

$$
L_n(r,s,t)=L_n(r,s-1,t)+L_{n-1}(r,s-1,t).
\tag{1}
$$

### 2.2 Recurrence for $R_n$

Apply Pascalâ€™s identity to $\binom{r+s-k}{n-k}$:

$$
\binom{r+s-k}{n-k}
=
\binom{r+(s-1)-k}{n-k}
+
\binom{r+(s-1)-k}{n-k-1}.
$$

Substitute into $R_n$:

$$
R_n(r,s,t)
=
\sum_{k\ge 0}\binom{r+(s-1)-k}{n-k}t^k
+
\sum_{k\ge 0}\binom{r+(s-1)-k}{n-k-1}t^k.
$$

The first sum is $R_n(r,s-1,t)$, and the second is $R_{n-1}(r,s-1,t)$. Hence

$$
R_n(r,s,t)=R_n(r,s-1,t)+R_{n-1}(r,s-1,t).
\tag{2}
$$

## 3. Induction

We prove $L_n(r,s,t)=R_n(r,s,t)$ for all $n,s\ge 0$.

- Base case: $n=0$ holds for all $s$.
- Inductive step: assume equality holds for all pairs $(n',s')$ with

either $n'<n$ or $n'=n$ and $s'<s$.

From (1) and (2),

$$
L_n(r,s,t)
=
L_n(r,s-1,t)+L_{n-1}(r,s-1,t),
$$

$$
R_n(r,s,t)
=
R_n(r,s-1,t)+R_{n-1}(r,s-1,t).
$$

By the induction hypothesis, the right-hand sides are equal term-by-term, since they involve either smaller $s$ or smaller $n$. Therefore

$$
L_n(r,s,t)=R_n(r,s,t).
$$

This completes the induction.

## 4. Conclusion

For all nonnegative integers $n$,

$$
\sum_{k\ge 0} \binom{r+tk}{k}\binom{s-tk}{n-k}
=
\sum_{k\ge 0} \binom{r+s-k}{n-k}t^k.
$$
