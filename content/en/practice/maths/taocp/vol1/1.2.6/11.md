**Exercise 11.** [*M20*] (E. Kummer, 1852.) Let $p$ be prime. Show that if $p^n$ divides $\binom{a+b}{a}$ but $p^{n+1}$ does not, then $n$ is equal to the number of carries that occur when $a$ is added to $b$ in the $p$-ary number system.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m05s

## Solution

Let $p$ be prime and write the base-$p$ expansions

$$
a = \sum_{i \ge 0} a_i p^i,\qquad b = \sum_{i \ge 0} b_i p^i,\qquad 0 \le a_i,b_i \le p-1.
$$

Let $s_p(x)$ denote the sum of the base-$p$ digits of $x$, and let $C$ denote the number of carries that occur when $a$ and $b$ are added in base $p$.

### Step 1: $p$-adic valuation of factorials

For $n \ge 0$, the exponent of $p$ in $n!$ satisfies

$$
v_p(n!) = \sum_{i \ge 1} \left\lfloor \frac{n}{p^i} \right\rfloor.
$$

Write each term using digit expansions. Each integer $n$ can be expressed in base $p$, and each quotient $\left\lfloor n/p^i \right\rfloor$ counts digits beyond position $i-1$. Summing over $i$ yields the identity

$$
v_p(n!) = \frac{n - s_p(n)}{p-1}.
$$

### Step 2: valuation of the binomial coefficient

Using $ \binom{a+b}{a} = \frac{(a+b)!}{a!,b!}$,

$$
v_p\!\binom{a+b}{a}
= v_p((a+b)!) - v_p(a!) - v_p(b!).
$$

Substituting the digit-sum formula,

$$
v_p\!\binom{a+b}{a}
= \frac{(a+b) - s_p(a+b)}{p-1}
- \frac{a - s_p(a)}{p-1}
- \frac{b - s_p(b)}{p-1}.
$$

The linear terms cancel, giving

$$
v_p\!\binom{a+b}{a}
= \frac{s_p(a) + s_p(b) - s_p(a+b)}{p-1}.
$$

### Step 3: interpretation via carries

Perform the addition of $a$ and $b$ digit by digit in base $p$. At each position $i$, a carry occurs exactly when

$$
a_i + b_i + c_i \ge p,
$$

where $c_i \in {0,1}$ is the incoming carry.

When a carry occurs, the digit sum of the resulting number decreases by $p$ at that position, while the combined digit sum $s_p(a)+s_p(b)$ contributes $a_i+b_i+c_i$ locally. Each carry reduces the total digit sum of the result by exactly $p-1$ relative to the sum of input digits.

Hence each carry contributes exactly $p-1$ to the quantity

$$
s_p(a) + s_p(b) - s_p(a+b).
$$

If $C$ carries occur in total, then

$$
s_p(a) + s_p(b) - s_p(a+b) = (p-1)C.
$$

### Step 4: conclusion

Combining the two expressions,

$$
v_p\!\binom{a+b}{a}
= \frac{(p-1)C}{p-1}
= C.
$$

Thus the exponent of $p$ dividing $\binom{a+b}{a}$ equals the number of carries that occur when $a$ and $b$ are added in base $p$.
