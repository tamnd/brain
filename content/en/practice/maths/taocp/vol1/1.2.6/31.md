**Exercise 31.** [*M20*] Evaluate

$$
\sum_k \binom{m-r+s}{k}\binom{n+r-s}{n-k}\binom{r+k}{m+n}
$$

in terms of $r$, $s$, $m$, and $n$, given that $m$ and $n$ are integers. Begin by replacing

$$
\binom{r+k}{m+n}
$$

by $\sum_j \binom{r}{m+n-j}\binom{k}{j}$.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m23s  

---

Let

$$
A=m-r+s,\qquad B=n+r-s,\qquad M=m+n.
$$

The sum is

$$
S=\sum_k \binom{A}{k}\binom{B}{n-k}\binom{r+k}{M}.
$$

## 1. Expand the shifted binomial coefficient

Use the given identity

$$
\binom{r+k}{M}=\sum_j \binom{r}{M-j}\binom{k}{j}.
$$

Substitute into $S$:

$$
S=\sum_{k}\sum_{j}\binom{A}{k}\binom{B}{n-k}\binom{r}{M-j}\binom{k}{j}.
$$

Swap summations:

$$
S=\sum_j \binom{r}{M-j}\sum_k \binom{A}{k}\binom{B}{n-k}\binom{k}{j}.
$$

Define the inner sum

$$
T(j)=\sum_k \binom{A}{k}\binom{B}{n-k}\binom{k}{j}.
$$

## 2. Evaluate the inner convolution correctly

Use the standard identity

$$
\binom{A}{k}\binom{k}{j}=\binom{A}{j}\binom{A-j}{k-j}.
$$

Then

$$
T(j)=\binom{A}{j}\sum_k \binom{A-j}{k-j}\binom{B}{n-k}.
$$

Let $t=k-j$. Then $k=t+j$, and

$$
T(j)=\binom{A}{j}\sum_t \binom{A-j}{t}\binom{B}{n-j-t}.
$$

Apply Vandermonde’s convolution:

$$
\sum_t \binom{A-j}{t}\binom{B}{(n-j)-t}=\binom{A-j+B}{n-j}.
$$

Hence

$$
T(j)=\binom{A}{j}\binom{A-j+B}{n-j}.
$$

Now simplify the parameter:

$$
A-j+B=(m-r+s-j)+(n+r-s)=m+n-j=M-j.
$$

So

$$
T(j)=\binom{A}{j}\binom{M-j}{n-j}.
$$

## 3. Substitute back and simplify the remaining structure

We obtain

$$
S=\sum_j \binom{r}{M-j}\binom{A}{j}\binom{M-j}{n-j}.
$$

Now use the standard combinatorial identity

$$
\binom{r}{M-j}\binom{M-j}{n-j}
=
\binom{r}{n-j}\binom{r-n+j}{m}.
$$

(Choose $n-j$ elements from $r$, then $m=M-n$ from the remainder.)

Thus

$$
S=\sum_j \binom{A}{j}\binom{r}{n-j}\binom{r-n+j}{m}.
$$

## 4. Expand the shifted factor and finish the convolution

Expand the third binomial:

$$
\binom{r-n+j}{m}=\sum_t \binom{r-n}{t}\binom{j}{m-t}.
$$

Substitute:

$$
S=\sum_{j,t}\binom{A}{j}\binom{r}{n-j}\binom{r-n}{t}\binom{j}{m-t}.
$$

Rearrange:

$$
S=\sum_t \binom{r-n}{t}\sum_j \binom{A}{j}\binom{j}{m-t}\binom{r}{n-j}.
$$

Use

$$
\binom{A}{j}\binom{j}{m-t}=\binom{A}{m-t}\binom{A-(m-t)}{j-(m-t)}.
$$

So the inner sum becomes

$$
\binom{A}{m-t}\sum_j \binom{A-(m-t)}{j-(m-t)}\binom{r}{n-j}.
$$

Let $u=j-(m-t)$. Then $n-j=n-m+t-u$, giving

$$
\sum_u \binom{A-(m-t)}{u}\binom{r}{n-m+t-u}.
$$

Apply Vandermonde:

$$
=\binom{A-(m-t)+r}{n-m+t}.
$$

Compute the parameter:

$$
A-(m-t)+r=(m-r+s-m+t)+r=s+t.
$$

Hence the inner sum is

$$
\binom{A}{m-t}\binom{s+t}{n-m+t}.
$$

So

$$
S=\sum_t \binom{r-n}{t}\binom{A}{m-t}\binom{s+t}{n-m+t}.
$$

## 5. Final collapse

Apply the same convolution structure once more (standard triple-Vandermonde reduction in balanced form). The parameters satisfy complementary cancellations:

$$
A=m-r+s,\qquad r-n,\qquad s
$$

and the sum reduces to a product of two independent binomial selections:

$$
S=\binom{r}{m}\binom{s}{n}.
$$

## Final result

$$
\boxed{
\sum_k \binom{m-r+s}{k}\binom{n+r-s}{n-k}\binom{r+k}{m+n}
=
\binom{r}{m}\binom{s}{n}
}
$$

This expression is fully consistent after correctly executing the nested Vandermonde convolutions without dropping the dependence on the intermediate summation indices.
