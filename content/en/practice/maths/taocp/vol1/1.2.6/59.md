[Section 1.2.6: Binomial Coefficients](../)

**Exercise 59.** [*M25*] A sequence of numbers $A_{nk}$ satisfies

$$
A_{n0}=1,\qquad A_{0k}=\delta_{0k},\qquad
A_{nk}=A_{n-1,k}+A_{n-1,k-1}+\binom{n}{k}.
$$

Find $A_{nk}$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 4m34s  

---

The core issue is that the recurrence is valid only for $k \ge 1$. The boundary condition $A_{n0}=1$ is not propagated by the recurrence and must be enforced separately throughout the generating-function derivation.

## 1. Correct formulation of the generating function

Define the full generating function

$$
A_n(x)=\sum_{k\ge 0} A_{nk}x^k.
$$

Apply the recurrence only for $k\ge 1$:

$$
A_{nk}=A_{n-1,k}+A_{n-1,k-1}+\binom{n}{k}.
$$

Sum over $k\ge 1$:

### Left-hand side

$$
\sum_{k\ge 1} A_{nk}x^k = A_n(x)-A_{n0} = A_n(x)-1.
$$

### First term

$$
\sum_{k\ge 1} A_{n-1,k}x^k = A_{n-1}(x)-A_{n-1,0}=A_{n-1}(x)-1.
$$

### Second term

$$
\sum_{k\ge 1} A_{n-1,k-1}x^k = xA_{n-1}(x).
$$

### Third term

$$
\sum_{k\ge 1} \binom{n}{k}x^k = (1+x)^n - 1.
$$

## 2. Functional recurrence

Combine all terms:

$$
A_n(x)-1 = (A_{n-1}(x)-1) + xA_{n-1}(x) + (1+x)^n - 1.
$$

Hence

$$
A_n(x) = (1+x)A_{n-1}(x) + (1+x)^n - 1,
\qquad A_0(x)=1.
$$

## 3. Solving the recurrence

Split into homogeneous and particular parts.

### Homogeneous part

$$
U_n(x)=(1+x)U_{n-1}(x) \quad \Rightarrow \quad U_n(x)=(1+x)^n.
$$

### Particular solution

Let $A_n(x)=(1+x)^n W_n(x)$. Substitute:

$$
(1+x)^n W_n = (1+x)^n W_{n-1} + (1+x)^n - 1.
$$

Divide by $(1+x)^n$:

$$
W_n = W_{n-1} + 1 - (1+x)^{-n}, \quad W_0=1.
$$

Iterate:

$$
W_n = 1 + \sum_{j=1}^n \left(1 - (1+x)^{-j}\right)
= n+1 - \sum_{j=1}^n (1+x)^{-j}.
$$

The geometric sum is

$$
\sum_{j=1}^n (1+x)^{-j} = \frac{1-(1+x)^{-n}}{x}.
$$

Thus

$$
W_n = n+1 - \frac{1-(1+x)^{-n}}{x}.
$$

Multiply back:

$$
A_n(x) = (1+x)^n\left(n+1 - \frac{1-(1+x)^{-n}}{x}\right).
$$

Simplify:

$$
A_n(x) = (n+1)(1+x)^n - \frac{(1+x)^n - 1}{x}.
$$

## 4. Extract coefficients

We use

$$
\frac{(1+x)^n - 1}{x}
= \sum_{k\ge 0} \binom{n}{k+1}x^k.
$$

Therefore

$$
A_{nk} = (n+1)\binom{n}{k} - \binom{n}{k+1}.
$$

## 5. Verification of boundary condition

For $k=0$:

$$
A_{n0} = (n+1)\binom{n}{0} - \binom{n}{1}
= (n+1) - n = 1.
$$

So the boundary is satisfied exactly.

## 6. Verification of recurrence (for $k\ge 1$)

Compute each term:

$$
A_{nk} = (n+1)\binom{n}{k} - \binom{n}{k+1}.
$$

$$
A_{n-1,k} = n\binom{n-1}{k} - \binom{n-1}{k+1},
\quad
A_{n-1,k-1} = n\binom{n-1}{k-1} - \binom{n-1}{k}.
$$

Using standard binomial identities and simplifying, the sum

$$
A_{n-1,k}+A_{n-1,k-1}+\binom{n}{k}
$$

reduces exactly to

$$
(n+1)\binom{n}{k} - \binom{n}{k+1} = A_{nk}.
$$

Thus the recurrence holds for all $k\ge 1$.

## Final answer

$$
\boxed{
A_{nk} = (n+1)\binom{n}{k} - \binom{n}{k+1}
}
$$

This expression satisfies both the recurrence for $k\ge 1$ and the boundary conditions $A_{n0}=1$, $A_{0k}=\delta_{0k}$.
