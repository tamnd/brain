[Section 1.2.6: Binomial Coefficients](../)

**Exercise 41.** [*HM22*] Prove that

$$
B(x,y)=\frac{\Gamma(x)\Gamma(y)}{\Gamma(x+y)}.
$$

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m05s  

---

## Solution

Let $x>0$ and $y>0$. The gamma function is defined by

$$
\Gamma(z)=\int_{0}^{\infty} t^{z-1} e^{-t}\,dt.
$$

Consider the product $\Gamma(x)\Gamma(y)$:

$$
\Gamma(x)\Gamma(y)
= \left(\int_{0}^{\infty} t^{x-1} e^{-t}\,dt\right)
  \left(\int_{0}^{\infty} s^{y-1} e^{-s}\,ds\right).
$$

This product equals the double integral

$$
\Gamma(x)\Gamma(y)
= \int_{0}^{\infty}\int_{0}^{\infty}
t^{x-1}s^{y-1}e^{-(t+s)}\,dt\,ds.
$$

Introduce the change of variables

$$
u=t+s,\qquad v=\frac{t}{t+s},
$$

so that

$$
t=\nu v,\qquad s=\nu(1-v).
$$

The mapping sends $(t,s)\in (0,\infty)^2$ to $(\nu,v)\in (0,\infty)\times(0,1)$. The Jacobian determinant satisfies

$$
\left|\frac{\partial(t,s)}{\partial(\nu,v)}\right|=\nu.
$$

Substituting these expressions into the double integral yields

$$
\Gamma(x)\Gamma(y)
= \int_{0}^{\infty}\int_{0}^{1}
(\nu v)^{x-1}(\nu(1-v))^{y-1}e^{-\nu}\,\nu\,dv\,d\nu.
$$

Collecting factors,

$$
(\nu v)^{x-1}(\nu(1-v))^{y-1}\nu
= \nu^{x+y-1} v^{x-1}(1-v)^{y-1}.
$$

Hence

$$
\Gamma(x)\Gamma(y)
= \int_{0}^{\infty}\int_{0}^{1}
\nu^{x+y-1} e^{-\nu} v^{x-1}(1-v)^{y-1}\,dv\,d\nu.
$$

The integrand separates into a function of $\nu$ times a function of $v$, so the integral factors:

$$
\Gamma(x)\Gamma(y)
=
\left(\int_{0}^{\infty} \nu^{x+y-1} e^{-\nu}\,d\nu\right)
\left(\int_{0}^{1} v^{x-1}(1-v)^{y-1}\,dv\right).
$$

The first factor is $\Gamma(x+y)$ and the second factor is $B(x,y)$ by definition. Thus

$$
\Gamma(x)\Gamma(y)=\Gamma(x+y)B(x,y).
$$

Dividing by $\Gamma(x+y)$ gives

$$
B(x,y)=\frac{\Gamma(x)\Gamma(y)}{\Gamma(x+y)}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
