**Exercise 12.** [*M22*] Are there any positive integers $n$ for which all the nonzero entries in the $n$th row of Pascal's triangle are odd? If so, find all such $n$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m02s  

---

## Solution

Let $n$ be a positive integer and consider the $n$th row of Pascal’s triangle, consisting of the numbers $\binom{n}{k}$ for $0 \le k \le n$. Since $n$ is a nonnegative integer, each $\binom{n}{k}$ is a positive integer, so “nonzero entries” refers to all entries in the row.

We seek all $n$ such that every $\binom{n}{k}$ is odd.

Work modulo $2$. Apply Lucas’s theorem in base $2$ (Exercise 1.2.6.10(6) with $p=2$). Write

$$
n = a_r2^r + \cdots + a_1 2 + a_0,\qquad
k = b_r2^r + \cdots + b_1 2 + b_0,
$$

where each $a_i, b_i \in {0,1}$. Then

$$
\binom{n}{k} \equiv \prod_{i=0}^{r} \binom{a_i}{b_i} \pmod 2.
$$

The values of $\binom{a_i}{b_i}$ modulo $2$ are determined by $a_i, b_i \in {0,1}$:

$$
\binom{0}{0}=1,\quad \binom{1}{0}=1,\quad \binom{1}{1}=1,\quad \binom{0}{1}=0.
$$

Hence the product is nonzero modulo $2$ exactly when $b_i \le a_i$ for every $i$, and in that case every factor equals $1$.

Assume every entry $\binom{n}{k}$ is odd. Fix an index $i$ with $a_i=0$. Choose $k=2^i$. Then $b_i=1$ and $b_j=0$ for $j \ne i$, so $b_i \nleq a_i$. The factor $\binom{a_i}{b_i}=\binom{0}{1}=0$ implies $\binom{n}{k} \equiv 0 \pmod 2$, contradicting the assumption. Hence every $a_i=1$, so $n$ has binary representation consisting entirely of $1$’s, and therefore

$$
n = 2^{r+1}-1
$$

for some integer $r \ge 0$.

Conversely, let $n = 2^{r+1}-1$, so every binary digit $a_i=1$. For any $k$, each $b_i \in {0,1}$ satisfies $b_i \le a_i$, hence every factor $\binom{a_i}{b_i}$ equals $1$. Therefore

$$
\binom{n}{k} \equiv 1 \pmod 2
$$

for all $0 \le k \le n$, so every entry in the row is odd.

Thus the required integers are exactly those of the form $n=2^m-1$ with $m \ge 1$. This completes the proof. ∎
