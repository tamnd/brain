[Section 1.2.6: Binomial Coefficients](../)

**Exercise 8.** [*00*] What property of Pascal's triangle is reflected in the symmetry condition, Eq. (6)?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m07s  

---

Equation (6) states that

$$
\binom{n}{k} = \binom{n}{n-k}.
$$

In Pascal’s triangle this expresses the mirror symmetry of each row: the entry in column $k$ equals the entry equally far from the right end, in column $n-k$. Thus every row is symmetric about its center.

Combinatorially, the same property reflects that choosing $k$ objects from $n$ leaves a complementary choice of $n-k$ objects, and each selection corresponds uniquely to its complement. This pairing produces identical counts on opposite sides of the row. ∎
