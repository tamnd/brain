**Exercise 44.** [*HM20*] Using the generalized binomial coefficient suggested in exercise 42, show that

$$
\binom{r}{1/2} = \frac{2^{2r+1}}{\binom{2r}{r}\pi}.
$$

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m16s  

---

## Solution

From the generalized binomial coefficient (Exercise 42), for real $r$ and real $k$ we use

$$
\binom{r}{k}=\frac{\Gamma(r+1)}{\Gamma(k+1)\Gamma(r-k+1)}.
$$

### Step 1: Evaluate $\binom{r}{\tfrac12}$

Substituting $k=\tfrac12$ gives

$$
\binom{r}{\tfrac12}
= \frac{\Gamma(r+1)}{\Gamma(\tfrac32)\Gamma(r+\tfrac12)}.
$$

Using $\Gamma(\tfrac32)=\frac12\Gamma(\tfrac12)$ and $\Gamma(\tfrac12)=\sqrt{\pi}$,

$$
\Gamma(\tfrac32)=\frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{2}.
$$

Hence

$$
\binom{r}{\tfrac12}
= \frac{2\,\Gamma(r+1)}{\sqrt{\pi}\,\Gamma(r+\tfrac12)}.
$$

### Step 2: Express $\binom{2r}{r}$

Again from the generalized factorial form,

$$
\binom{2r}{r}
= \frac{\Gamma(2r+1)}{\Gamma(r+1)^2}.
$$

### Step 3: Apply the duplication formula

The Gamma duplication formula states

$$
\Gamma(2r+1)=\frac{2^{2r}}{\sqrt{\pi}}\,\Gamma(r+1)\Gamma\!\left(r+\tfrac12\right).
$$

Substituting this into the expression for $\binom{2r}{r}$ gives

$$
\binom{2r}{r}
= \frac{\frac{2^{2r}}{\sqrt{\pi}}\Gamma(r+1)\Gamma(r+\tfrac12)}{\Gamma(r+1)^2}
= \frac{2^{2r}}{\sqrt{\pi}} \cdot \frac{\Gamma(r+\tfrac12)}{\Gamma(r+1)}.
$$

Solving for $\frac{\Gamma(r+1)}{\Gamma(r+\tfrac12)}$ yields

$$
\frac{\Gamma(r+1)}{\Gamma(r+\tfrac12)}
= \frac{2^{2r}}{\sqrt{\pi}\,\binom{2r}{r}}.
$$

### Step 4: Substitute into $\binom{r}{\tfrac12}$

Using the expression obtained in Step 1,

$$
\binom{r}{\tfrac12}
= \frac{2\,\Gamma(r+1)}{\sqrt{\pi}\,\Gamma(r+\tfrac12)}
= \frac{2}{\sqrt{\pi}} \cdot \frac{\Gamma(r+1)}{\Gamma(r+\tfrac12)}.
$$

Substituting the result from Step 3,

$$
\binom{r}{\tfrac12}
= \frac{2}{\sqrt{\pi}} \cdot \frac{2^{2r}}{\sqrt{\pi}\,\binom{2r}{r}}
= \frac{2^{2r+1}}{\pi\,\binom{2r}{r}}.
$$

Thus,

$$
\binom{r}{\tfrac12} = \frac{2^{2r+1}}{\binom{2r}{r}\pi}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
