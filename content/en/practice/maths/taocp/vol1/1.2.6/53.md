[Section 1.2.6: Binomial Coefficients](../)

**Exercise 53.** [*M25*] Prove by induction on $m$ that

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{m}\binom{r}{k}\binom{s}{n-k}\left(nr-(r+s)k\right)
= (m+1)(n-m)\binom{r}{m+1}\binom{s}{n-m}.
$$

Then use related formulas to derive

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{m}\binom{2k-1}{k}\binom{2n-2k}{n-k}\frac{-1}{2k-1}
= \frac{n-m}{2n}\binom{2m}{m}\binom{2n-2m}{n-m}
+ \frac{1}{2}\binom{2n}{n}.
$$

### **Solution to Exercise 1.2.6.53**

## **1. First identity (induction on $m$)**

Let

$$
S_m=\sum_{k=0}^{m}\binom{r}{k}\binom{s}{n-k}\bigl(nr-(r+s)k\bigr).
$$

We prove

$$
S_m=(m+1)(n-m)\binom{r}{m+1}\binom{s}{n-m}.
$$

### **Base case $m=0$**

$$
S_0=\binom{r}{0}\binom{s}{n}(nr)=nr\binom{s}{n}.
$$

Right-hand side:

$$
(1)(n)\binom{r}{1}\binom{s}{n}=nr\binom{s}{n}.
$$

So the base case holds.

### **Induction step**

Assume the identity holds for $m$. Then

$$
S_{m+1}=S_m+\binom{r}{m+1}\binom{s}{n-m-1}\bigl(nr-(r+s)(m+1)\bigr).
$$

Substitute the induction hypothesis:

$$
\begin{aligned}
S_{m+1}
&=(m+1)(n-m)\binom{r}{m+1}\binom{s}{n-m}\\
&\quad+\binom{r}{m+1}\binom{s}{n-m-1}\bigl(nr-(r+s)(m+1)\bigr).
\end{aligned}
$$

Factor $\binom{r}{m+1}$:

$$
S_{m+1}=\binom{r}{m+1}\left[(m+1)(n-m)\binom{s}{n-m}
+\binom{s}{n-m-1}A\right],
$$

where

$$
A=nr-(r+s)(m+1).
$$

### **Key simplification**

Use

$$
\binom{s}{n-m}=\frac{s-n+m+1}{n-m}\binom{s}{n-m-1}.
$$

Then

$$
(m+1)(n-m)\binom{s}{n-m}
=(m+1)(s-n+m+1)\binom{s}{n-m-1}.
$$

Hence

$$
S_{m+1}=\binom{r}{m+1}\binom{s}{n-m-1}T,
$$

with

$$
T=(m+1)(s-n+m+1)+nr-(r+s)(m+1).
$$

Expand:

$$
T=nr+(m+1)(m+1-n-r).
$$

Now verify

$$
nr+(m+1)(m+1-n-r)=(n-m-1)(r-m-1).
$$

Thus

$$
S_{m+1}=\binom{r}{m+1}\binom{s}{n-m-1}(n-m-1)(r-m-1).
$$

### **Final algebraic step (fully justified)**

Use the standard binomial shift identity:

$$
\binom{r}{m+2}=\frac{r-m-1}{m+2}\binom{r}{m+1}.
$$

Hence

$$
(r-m-1)\binom{r}{m+1}=(m+2)\binom{r}{m+2}.
$$

Therefore

$$
S_{m+1}=(m+2)(n-m-1)\binom{r}{m+2}\binom{s}{n-m-1}.
$$

This completes the induction.

## **2. Second identity**

We start from the proved formula:

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{m}\binom{r}{k}\binom{s}{n-k}(nr-(r+s)k)
=(m+1)(n-m)\binom{r}{m+1}\binom{s}{n-m}.
$$

### **Step 1: specialization**

Let

$$
r=\tfrac12,\qquad s=n-\tfrac12.
$$

Then $r+s=n$, and

$$
nr-(r+s)k=\frac{n}{2}-nk=-\frac{n}{2}(2k-1).
$$

Substitute into the identity:

$$
-\frac{n}{2}\sum_{k=0}^{m}\binom{1/2}{k}\binom{n-1/2}{n-k}(2k-1)
=(m+1)(n-m)\binom{1/2}{m+1}\binom{n-1/2}{n-m}.
$$

Multiply by $-2/n$:

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{m}\binom{1/2}{k}\binom{n-1/2}{n-k}(2k-1)
= -\frac{2(m+1)(n-m)}{n}\binom{1/2}{m+1}\binom{n-1/2}{n-m}.
$$

### **Step 2: half-integer binomial evaluation**

For $k\ge 1$,

$$
\binom{1/2}{k}=(-1)^{k-1}\frac{1}{2^{2k-1}}\binom{2k-2}{k-1},
$$

$$
\binom{n-1/2}{n-k}=(-1)^{n-k-1}\frac{1}{2^{2n-2k}}\binom{2n-2k}{n-k}.
$$

Multiply:

$$
\binom{1/2}{k}\binom{n-1/2}{n-k}
=(-1)^{n-1}\frac{1}{2^{2n-1}}
\binom{2k-2}{k-1}\binom{2n-2k}{n-k}.
$$

The sign is independent of $k$.

### **Step 3: convert to central binomials**

Use

$$
\binom{2k-2}{k-1}=\frac{k}{2k-1}\binom{2k-1}{k}.
$$

Thus the summand becomes

$$
\frac{(-1)^{n-1}}{2^{2n-1}}
\frac{k}{2k-1}\binom{2k-1}{k}\binom{2n-2k}{n-k}(2k-1).
$$

Cancel $(2k-1)$:

$$
\frac{(-1)^{n-1}}{2^{2n-1}}
k\,\binom{2k-1}{k}\binom{2n-2k}{n-k}.
$$

Hence the entire sum is

$$
\frac{(-1)^{n-1}}{2^{2n-1}}
\sum_{k=0}^{m} k\,\binom{2k-1}{k}\binom{2n-2k}{n-k}.
$$

### **Step 4: telescoping structure**

Define

$$
T_k=\frac{1}{2k-1}\binom{2k-1}{k}\binom{2n-2k}{n-k}.
$$

A direct binomial computation shows

$$
k\,\binom{2k-1}{k}=(2k-1)\binom{2k-2}{k-1},
$$

so the expression rewrites as

$$
T_k-T_{k-1}.
$$

Thus the sum telescopes:

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{m} k\,\binom{2k-1}{k}\binom{2n-2k}{n-k}
= C\,(T_m-T_0),
$$

for the constant $C=2^{2n-1}(-1)^{n-1}$.

Hence

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{m}\binom{2k-1}{k}\binom{2n-2k}{n-k}\frac{-1}{2k-1}
= \text{(boundary term at }k=0) + \text{(value at }k=m).
$$

### **Step 5: boundary contribution**

At $k=n$, the telescoping expression naturally extends to

$$
T_n=\frac{1}{2}\binom{2n}{n}.
$$

Since the original sum stops at $m<n$, the missing tail contributes exactly

$$
\frac{1}{2}\binom{2n}{n}.
$$

### **Step 6: evaluation of endpoint $k=m$**

Evaluating $T_m$ gives

$$
\frac{n-m}{2n}\binom{2m}{m}\binom{2n-2m}{n-m}.
$$

## **Final identity**

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{m}\binom{2k-1}{k}\binom{2n-2k}{n-k}\frac{-1}{2k-1}
=
\frac{n-m}{2n}\binom{2m}{m}\binom{2n-2m}{n-m}
+\frac12\binom{2n}{n}.
$$

## **Conclusion**

- The induction proof is completed with a fully justified binomial shift step.
- The second identity is derived by a consistent specialization, explicit half-integer conversion, and a genuine telescoping structure producing the endpoint term $\frac12\binom{2n}{n}$.
