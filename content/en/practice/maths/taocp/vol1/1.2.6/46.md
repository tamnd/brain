**Exercise 46.** [*M21*] Using Stirling's approximation, Eq. 1.2.5--(7), find an approximate value of $\binom{x+y}{y}$, assuming that both $x$ and $y$ are large. In particular, find the approximate size of $\binom{2n}{n}$ when $n$ is large.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m13s  

---

## Solution

Stirling’s approximation (Eq. 1.2.5–(7)) states

$$
n! \sim \sqrt{2\pi n}\left(\frac{n}{e}\right)^n
\qquad (n \to \infty).
$$

### Approximation of $\binom{x+y}{y}$

Starting from the factorial form (Eq. (5)),

$$
\binom{x+y}{y}=\frac{(x+y)!}{x!\,y!}.
$$

Apply Stirling’s approximation to each factorial:

$$
(x+y)! \sim \sqrt{2\pi(x+y)}\left(\frac{x+y}{e}\right)^{x+y},
$$

$$
x! \sim \sqrt{2\pi x}\left(\frac{x}{e}\right)^x,\qquad
y! \sim \sqrt{2\pi y}\left(\frac{y}{e}\right)^y.
$$

Substitution gives

$$
\binom{x+y}{y}
\sim
\frac{\sqrt{2\pi(x+y)}\left(\frac{x+y}{e}\right)^{x+y}}
{\sqrt{2\pi x}\left(\frac{x}{e}\right)^x \;\sqrt{2\pi y}\left(\frac{y}{e}\right)^y }.
$$

The factors $e^{-(x+y)}$ cancel with $e^{-x}e^{-y}$, leaving

$$
\binom{x+y}{y}
\sim
\frac{\sqrt{2\pi(x+y)}}{\sqrt{2\pi x}\,\sqrt{2\pi y}}
\cdot
\frac{(x+y)^{x+y}}{x^x y^y}.
$$

The square-root term simplifies as

$$
\frac{\sqrt{2\pi(x+y)}}{\sqrt{2\pi x}\,\sqrt{2\pi y}}
=
\sqrt{\frac{x+y}{2\pi x y}}.
$$

Hence

$$
\boxed{
\binom{x+y}{y}
\sim
\frac{(x+y)^{x+y}}{x^x y^y}
\sqrt{\frac{x+y}{2\pi x y}}
}.
$$

### Special case $\binom{2n}{n}$

Set $x=y=n$ in the general approximation:

$$
\binom{2n}{n}=\frac{(2n)!}{n!\,n!}.
$$

Using Stirling,

$$
(2n)! \sim \sqrt{4\pi n}\left(\frac{2n}{e}\right)^{2n},
\qquad
n! \sim \sqrt{2\pi n}\left(\frac{n}{e}\right)^n.
$$

Thus

$$
\binom{2n}{n}
\sim
\frac{\sqrt{4\pi n}\left(\frac{2n}{e}\right)^{2n}}
{2\pi n\left(\frac{n}{e}\right)^{2n}}.
$$

Cancellation of $e^{-2n}$ yields

$$
\binom{2n}{n}
\sim
\frac{\sqrt{4\pi n}}{2\pi n}\cdot \frac{(2n)^{2n}}{n^{2n}}.
$$

Since $\frac{(2n)^{2n}}{n^{2n}}=2^{2n}=4^n$ and $\frac{\sqrt{4\pi n}}{2\pi n}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi n}}$, we obtain

$$
\boxed{
\binom{2n}{n}
\sim
\frac{4^n}{\sqrt{\pi n}}
}.
$$

This completes the derivation. ∎
