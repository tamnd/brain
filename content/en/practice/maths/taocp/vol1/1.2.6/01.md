[Section 1.2.6: Binomial Coefficients](../)

**Exercise 1.** [*00*] How many combinations of $n$ things taken $n-1$ at a time are possible?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 57s  

---

The number of combinations of $n$ objects taken $n-1$ at a time is $\binom{n}{n-1}$. Using the factorial representation (5),

$$
\binom{n}{n-1}=\frac{n!}{(n-1)!\,1!}=\frac{n!}{(n-1)!}=n.
$$

Hence the number of such combinations is

$$
\boxed{n}.
$$
