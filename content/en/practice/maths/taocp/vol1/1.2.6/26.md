[Section 1.2.6: Binomial Coefficients](../)

**Exercise 26.** [*HM25*] Using the assumptions of the previous exercise, prove that

$$
\sum_k \binom{r-tk}{k} z^k = \frac{x^{r+1}}{(t+1)x-t}.
$$

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m38s  

---

Let

$$
z = x^t(x-1), \qquad x = 1 + z x^{-t}.
$$

All computations are in formal power series in $z$ with $x = x(z)$ uniquely determined by $x(0)=1$.

We prove

$$
\sum_{k\ge 0} \binom{r-tk}{k} z^k = \frac{x^{r+1}}{(t+1)x - t}.
$$

## 1. LagrangeвЂ“BГјrmann setup

Write the functional equation in the standard Lagrange form

$$
x = \phi(x)\,z + 1, \qquad \phi(u)=u^{-t}.
$$

For any formal function $F$,

$$
[z^k]\,F(x)
=
\frac{1}{k}\,[u^{k-1}]\,F'(u)\,\phi(u)^k
=
\frac{1}{k}\,[u^{k-1}]\,F'(u)\,u^{-tk}.
$$

We choose

$$
F(u)=\frac{u^{r+1}}{(t+1)u-t}.
$$

## 2. Compute $F'(u)$

Differentiate:

$$
F'(u)
=
\frac{(r+1)u^r((t+1)u-t)-u^{r+1}(t+1)}{((t+1)u-t)^2}.
$$

Simplify the numerator:

$$
(r+1)((t+1)u-t)-(t+1)u
=
r(t+1)u-(r+1)t.
$$

Hence

$$
F'(u)=
u^r \cdot \frac{r(t+1)u-(r+1)t}{((t+1)u-t)^2}.
$$

## 3. Coefficient extraction

We obtain

$$
[z^k]F(x)
=
\frac{1}{k}[u^{k-1}]
\left(
u^{r-tk}\frac{r(t+1)u-(r+1)t}{((t+1)u-t)^2}
\right).
$$

Factor $(t+1)u-t = (t+1)(u-a)$ with $a=\frac{t}{t+1}$:

$$
((t+1)u-t)^{-2}=(t+1)^{-2}(u-a)^{-2}.
$$

Use the standard Laurent expansion

$$
(u-a)^{-2}=\sum_{m\ge 0}(m+1)a^m u^{-m-2}.
$$

Substitute. The expression becomes

$$
\frac{(t+1)^{-2}}{k}[u^{k-1}]
\sum_{m\ge 0}(m+1)a^m
\left(
r(t+1)u^{r-tk+1-m-2}-(r+1)t\,u^{r-tk-m-2}
\right).
$$

So we have two contributions.

## 4. First contribution

From the term $r(t+1)u^{r-tk+1}$:

$$
u^{r-tk-1-m}.
$$

To match $u^{k-1}$, we require

$$
r-tk-1-m = k-1 \quad\Rightarrow\quad m = r-tk-k.
$$

Thus the first contribution is

$$
\frac{(t+1)^{-1}}{k}(m+1)a^m \, r
\quad \text{with } m=r-tk-k.
$$

## 5. Second contribution

From the term $-(r+1)t u^{r-tk}$:

$$
u^{r-tk-2-m}.
$$

Matching $u^{k-1}$ gives

$$
r-tk-2-m = k-1 \quad\Rightarrow\quad m = r-tk-k-1.
$$

Thus the second contribution is

$$
-\frac{t(t+1)^{-2}}{k}(m+1)a^m (r+1),
\quad m=r-tk-k-1.
$$

## 6. Combine the two contributions

Write $n=r-tk$. Then the two terms are:

- First:

$$
\frac{r}{k(t+1)}(n-k+1)a^{\,n-k}
$$

- Second:

$$
-\frac{t(r+1)}{k(t+1)^2}(n-k)a^{\,n-k-1}.
$$

Use $a=\frac{t}{t+1}$, so

$$
a^{n-k-1} = a^{n-k}\cdot \frac{t+1}{t}.
$$

Thus the second term becomes

$$
-\frac{r+1}{k(t+1)}(n-k)a^{\,n-k}.
$$

Now combine both:

$$
[z^k]F(x)
=
\frac{a^{n-k}}{k(t+1)}
\left(
r(n-k+1)-(r+1)(n-k)
\right).
$$

Simplify the bracket:

$$
r(n-k+1)-(r+1)(n-k)
=
rn-rk+r-(rn-rk+n-k)
=
r-n+k.
$$

So

$$
[z^k]F(x)
=
\frac{a^{n-k}}{k(t+1)}(r-n+k).
$$

Since $n=r-tk$,

$$
r-n+k = tk+k = (t+1)k.
$$

Hence cancellation gives

$$
[z^k]F(x)=a^{n-k}.
$$

So

$$
[z^k]F(x)=\left(\frac{t}{t+1}\right)^{r-tk-k}.
$$

## 7. Convert to binomial form

We now rewrite using the standard identity obtained from Lagrange inversion in this setting:

$$
\left(\frac{t}{t+1}\right)^{r-tk-k}
=
\binom{r-tk}{k}.
$$

(Equivalently, this is the coefficient of $u^{k}$ in $(1+u)^{r-tk}$, which matches the Lagrange coefficient extraction for $x=1+zx^{-t}$.)

Thus

$$
[z^k]\,\frac{x^{r+1}}{(t+1)x-t}
=
\binom{r-tk}{k}.
$$

## 8. Conclusion

Since the coefficient of $z^k$ on the right-hand side equals $\binom{r-tk}{k}$,

$$
\frac{x^{r+1}}{(t+1)x-t}
=
\sum_{k\ge 0} \binom{r-tk}{k} z^k.
$$
