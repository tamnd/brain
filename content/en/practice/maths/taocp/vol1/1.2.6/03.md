**Exercise 3.** [*00*] How many bridge hands (13 cards out of a 52-card deck) are possible?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 54s  

---

A bridge hand consists of choosing 13 cards from a deck of 52 distinct cards, where order is irrelevant. By the definition of binomial coefficients in (2), the number of such selections is

$$
\binom{52}{13}.
$$

Using the factorial representation (5),

$$
\binom{52}{13} = \frac{52!}{13!\,39!} = 635013559600.
$$

Hence the number of possible bridge hands is $\boxed{635013559600}$.
