[Section 1.2.6: Binomial Coefficients](../)

**Exercise 37.** [*M10*] From the answers to the preceding exercise, deduce the value of the sum of every other entry in a row, $\binom{n}{0} + \binom{n}{2} + \binom{n}{4} + \cdots$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m10s  

---

Let

$$
S=\binom{n}{0}+\binom{n}{2}+\binom{n}{4}+\cdots .
$$

From Exercise 1.2.6.36,

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n}\binom{n}{k}=2^{n},\qquad \sum_{k=0}^{n}(-1)^k\binom{n}{k}=(1-1)^n=0 \quad (n\ge 1).
$$

Split the total sum into even and odd parts,

$$
2^{n}=S+\sum_{\substack{0\le k\le n\\ k\ \text{odd}}}\binom{n}{k},
$$

and

$$
0=S-\sum_{\substack{0\le k\le n\\ k\ \text{odd}}}\binom{n}{k}.
$$

Adding these two equations eliminates the odd-index sum and yields

$$
2S=2^{n}.
$$

Hence, for $n\ge 1$,

$$
S=2^{n-1}.
$$

For $n=0$, the only term is $\binom{0}{0}=1$, so $S=1$. Therefore,

$$
\boxed{S=2^{n-1}\ (n\ge 1),\ \text{and } S=1\ (n=0).}
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
