**Exercise 10.** [*15*] List all the MIX operators that can possibly affect the setting of the comparison indicator.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m08s

## Solution

The comparison indicator is assigned only by instructions whose operational definition explicitly specifies a comparison between a register field and a memory field, producing one of $\text{LESS}$, $\text{EQUAL}$, or $\text{GREATER}$ as in the CMP family, or by the floating comparison operation.

From the instruction table, the integer comparison operators are those labeled $\text{CMP}$, since each of them has the form

$$
\text{CI} \leftarrow rR(F) : V,
$$

where $rR$ is one of the registers and $V = M(F)$. These instructions therefore determine the comparison indicator directly.

The accumulator comparison is given by $\text{CMPA}$, which sets $\text{CI}$ using $rA(F)$ and $V$. The index-register comparisons are given by $\text{CMP1}$, $\text{CMP2}$, $\text{CMP3}$, $\text{CMP4}$, $\text{CMP5}$, and $\text{CMP6}$, each comparing $rI_i(F)$ with $V$ and assigning the result to $\text{CI}$. The extension register comparison $\text{CMPX}$ performs the same role using $rX(F)$.

In addition to these integer comparisons, the floating-point comparison instruction $\text{FCMP}$ also assigns a value to the comparison indicator according to the comparison of floating-point operands.

No other instructions in the table specify an assignment to $\text{CI}$. Arithmetic operations such as $\text{ADD}$, $\text{SUB}$, $\text{MUL}$, and $\text{DIV}$ modify registers and may affect the overflow toggle, but they do not assign to the comparison indicator. Jump instructions test $\text{CI}$ but do not modify it.

The complete set of MIX operators that can possibly affect the comparison indicator is therefore

$$
\text{CMPA},\ \text{CMP1},\ \text{CMP2},\ \text{CMP3},\ \text{CMP4},\ \text{CMP5},\ \text{CMP6},\ \text{CMPX},\ \text{FCMP}.
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
