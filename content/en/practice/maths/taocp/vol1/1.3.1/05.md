[Section 1.3.1: Description of MIX](../)

**Exercise 5.** [*10*] What symbolic notation, analogous to (4), corresponds to (6) if (6) is regarded as a MIX instruction?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 2m14s  

---

If (6) is viewed as a machine word in the instruction format, it has the structure

$$
\pm\ AA\ I\ F\ C,
$$

where $C$ determines the operation code, $F$ is the field specification, $I$ is the index specification, and $\pm AA$ is the address field.

The corresponding symbolic notation replaces $C$ by its mnemonic operation name and interprets the remaining fields in the sense of (4). Hence the word (6) is written as

$$
\texttt{OP \ \ ADDRESS,I(F)},
$$

where $\texttt{OP}$ is the symbolic operation corresponding to code $C$, $\texttt{ADDRESS}$ is the signed address $\pm AA$, $I$ is omitted when $I=0$, and $(F)$ is omitted when $F$ is the normal field specification $(0:5)$. This is exactly the symbolic form analogous to (4). ∎
