**Exercise 18.** [*22*] After the following "number one" program has been executed, what changes to registers, toggles, and memory have taken place? (For example, what is the final setting of `rI1`? of `rX`? of the overflow and comparison indicators?)

```text
STZ 1
ENNX 1
STX 1(0:1)
SLAX 1
ENNA 1
INCX 1
ENT1 1
SRC 1
ADD 1
DEC1 -1
STZ 1
CMPA 1
MOVE -1,1(1)
NUM 1
CHAR 1
HLT 1
```

## Solution

Let $M = 1$ denote the address used by all instructions.

### Step 1: `STZ 1`

`STZ` stores zero into the addressed field of memory. Since the field is the whole word by default, the contents of location $1$ become

$$
\text{CONTENTS}(1) \leftarrow +0.
$$

All registers and indicators remain unchanged.

### Step 2: `ENNX 1`

`ENNX` loads register $rX$ with the negative of the specified field of $\text{CONTENTS}(1)$. Since $\text{CONTENTS}(1)=0$, the loaded value is $0$, hence

$$
rX \leftarrow 0.
$$

### Step 3: `STX 1(0:1)`

`STX` stores register $rX$ into the specified field of location $1$. Since $rX=0$, the sign and the first byte field $(0:1)$ are set consistently with zero. The word at location $1$ remains $0$.

### Step 4: `SLAX 1`

`SLAX` performs a left arithmetic shift of the combined registers $rA$ and $rX$ by one byte. Since initially $rA=0$ and $rX=0$, shifting a zero double register produces

$$
rA = 0, \quad rX = 0.
$$

### Step 5: `ENNA 1`

`ENNA` loads $rA$ with the negative of the specified field of $\text{CONTENTS}(1)$. Since $\text{CONTENTS}(1)=0$,

$$
rA \leftarrow 0.
$$

### Step 6: `INCX 1`

`INCX` increases $rX$ by $1$, hence

$$
rX \leftarrow 1.
$$

### Step 7: `ENT1 1`

`ENT1` enters the value $1$ into index register $rI1$, so

$$
rI1 \leftarrow 1.
$$

### Step 8: `SRC 1`

`SRC` performs a circular right shift of the combined registers $rA$ and $rX$ by one byte. At this moment,

$$
rA = 0,\quad rX = 1.
$$

Interpreting the 10-byte concatenation of $rA$ and $rX$, the single nonzero byte in $rX$ is rotated into the rightmost position of $rA$, while $rX$ becomes $0$. Thus after the shift,

$$
rA \neq 0 \text{ (contains the shifted byte)}, \quad rX = 0.
$$

### Step 9: `ADD 1`

`ADD` adds $\text{CONTENTS}(1)$ to $rA$. Since $\text{CONTENTS}(1)=0$, no change occurs:

$$
rA \text{ unchanged}, \quad rX = 0.
$$

### Step 10: `DEC1 -1`

`DEC1` decreases $rI1$ by $-1$, hence it increases $rI1$ by $1$:

$$
rI1 \leftarrow 2.
$$

### Step 11: `STZ 1`

Location $1$ is reset to zero again:

$$
\text{CONTENTS}(1) \leftarrow 0.
$$

### Step 12: `CMPA 1`

`CMPA` compares $rA$ with $\text{CONTENTS}(1)=0$. The comparison indicator becomes

$$
\text{GREATER, LESS, or EQUAL depending on } rA.
$$

From the preceding `SRC`, $rA$ contains a single nonzero byte (value $1$ in the shifted position), hence

$$
rA > 0,
$$

so the comparison indicator is set to

$$
\text{GREATER}.
$$

### Step 13: `MOVE -1,1(1)`

`MOVE` moves a block of words. With parameter $-1$, the move direction is negative and no effective transfer occurs in this context; registers remain unchanged.

### Step 14: `NUM 1`

`NUM` converts the contents of $rA$ and $rX$ from character form into numeric form using location $1$ as auxiliary storage. Since $rA$ contains a single nonzero byte and $rX=0$, the conversion yields a normalized numeric zero state in the registers:

$$
rA \leftarrow 0,\quad rX \leftarrow 0.
$$

### Step 15: `CHAR 1`

`CHAR` performs the inverse conversion. Since both registers are zero, no change occurs:

$$
rA = 0,\quad rX = 0.
$$

### Step 16: `HLT 1`

Execution halts. No further changes occur.

### Final state

Registers:

$$
rA = 0,\quad rX = 0,\quad rI1 = 2.
$$

Memory:

$$
\text{CONTENTS}(1) = 0.
$$

Comparison indicator:

$$
\text{GREATER}.
$$

Overflow toggle:

unchanged from initial state (remains off unless previously set).

This completes the solution. ∎
