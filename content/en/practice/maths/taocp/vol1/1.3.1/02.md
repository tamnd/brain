**Exercise 2.** [*02*] If a value to be represented within MIX may get as large as 99999999, how many adjacent bytes should be used to contain this quantity?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m09s  

---

A MIX byte is guaranteed to contain at least $64$ distinct values, so $k$ adjacent bytes can represent at most $64^k - 1$ different unsigned values. To represent all integers up to $99999999$, it is required that

$$
64^k - 1 \ge 99999999,
$$

equivalently

$$
64^k \ge 100000000.
$$

Compute successive powers:

$$
64^1 = 64,\quad 64^2 = 4096,\quad 64^3 = 262144,\quad 64^4 = 16777216,\quad 64^5 = 1073741824.
$$

The value $64^4$ is less than $100000000$, while $64^5$ exceeds it. Therefore four bytes are insufficient and five bytes are sufficient under the worst-case byte size allowed by MIX.

$\boxed{5\ \text{adjacent bytes}}$
