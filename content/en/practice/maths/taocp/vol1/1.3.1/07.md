**Exercise 7.** [*M15*] Give a precise definition of the results of the `DIV` instruction for all cases in which overflow does not occur, using the algebraic operations `X mod Y` and `\lfloor X/Y \rfloor`.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m13s

## Solution

Let the contents of register $rA$ be $A = s_A \cdot a$ and the contents of register $rX$ be $X = s_X \cdot x$, where $s_A, s_X \in {+1,-1}$ and $a, x \ge 0$ are the absolute values represented in the five-byte positional system of MIX with byte radix $\beta$ (where $\beta$ is the size of a byte base, unknown but fixed for a given machine).

The pair $(rA, rX)$ represents a single signed ten-byte integer obtained by concatenation of the two five-byte fields. Define

$$
N = s_N \cdot n
$$

where

$$
n = a \cdot \beta^5 + x,
$$

and $s_N$ is the sign of the representation of the combined word, taken as the sign of $rA$ if the machine convention treats the sign of $rA$ as governing the combined dividend.

Let the operand in memory be

$$
D = \text{CONTENTS}(M) = s_D \cdot d,
$$

with $d > 0$.

Assume $d \neq 0$ and no overflow occurs in the DIV operation, so both quotient and remainder are representable in their respective registers.

Define integers

$$
q = \left\lfloor \frac{n}{d} \right\rfloor, \qquad r = n \bmod d,
$$

so that

$$
n = qd + r, \qquad 0 \le r < d.
$$

The quotient and remainder signs are determined by the sign of the dividend and divisor. Define

$$
Q = s_N s_D \cdot q, \qquad R = s_N \cdot r.
$$

The DIV instruction replaces the contents of registers by

$$
rA \leftarrow Q, \qquad rX \leftarrow R.
$$

The quotient $Q$ is stored in $rA$ using the standard five-byte signed representation, and the remainder $R$ is stored in $rX$ using its standard five-byte signed representation. The sign of the remainder agrees with the sign of the dividend, and the quotient sign is the product of the signs of dividend and divisor.

This completes the definition of the DIV instruction under the assumption that no overflow occurs. ∎
