Let the original example on page 133 define a fixed MIX division

$$
rAX \leftarrow D,\quad V \text{ given}, \quad D = VQ + R,\quad 0 \le R < |V|.
$$

After execution of `DIV`, MIX produces

$$
rA = Q,\quad rX = R
$$

for the original dividend $D$, with the convention that the remainder is nonnegative.

## 1. What the modification actually changes

The given original value of `rX before` is

$$
+\,1235\ 0\ 3\ 1.
$$

This is a MIX word whose low half of the dividend is the `X` register. In MIX, `DIV` does **not** treat `rAX` as a single signed integer; it treats:

- `rA` as the high-order part (5 bytes plus sign),
- `rX` as the low-order 5 bytes,
- the sign of `rX` is irrelevant to the numeric value of the dividend.

So only the byte pattern of `rX` matters for the magnitude of the dividend.

The modified value is

$$
-\,1234\ 0\ 3\ 1.
$$

Comparing the two:

- the sign of `rX` is irrelevant for `DIV`,
- the magnitude changes from $12350\,31$ (in MIX byte form) to $12340\,31$,
- hence the low-order part decreases by exactly $1$.

Therefore the entire 10-byte dividend decreases by $1$:

$$
D' = D - 1.
$$

No other part of the machine state in the example is changed.

## 2. Effect on integer division

We now recompute the quotient and remainder of $D' = D - 1$ divided by $V$.

Start from

$$
D = VQ + R,\quad 0 \le R < |V|.
$$

Then

$$
D' = VQ + R - 1.
$$

We distinguish cases.

### Case 1: $R > 0$

Then

$$
D' = VQ + (R - 1),
\quad 0 \le R - 1 < |V|.
$$

This is already in normalized division form. Hence MIX produces

$$
rA = Q,\quad rX = R - 1.
$$

### Case 2: $R = 0$

Then

$$
D' = VQ - 1 = V(Q - 1) + (|V| - 1)
$$

in standard MIX unsigned remainder normalization.

Hence MIX produces

$$
rA = Q - 1,\quad rX = |V| - 1.
$$

## 3. Conclusion

After executing the unchanged `DIV` instruction with the modified `rX`:

- If the original remainder was nonzero, the quotient is unchanged and the remainder decreases by 1.
- If the original remainder was zero, the quotient decreases by 1 and the remainder becomes $|V| - 1$.

$$
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
R > 0 &: \quad rA = Q,\quad rX = R - 1,\\[4pt]
R = 0 &: \quad rA = Q - 1,\quad rX = |V| - 1.
\end{aligned}
}
$$

This fully determines the contents of registers $A$ and $X$ after `DIV` under the stated modification.
