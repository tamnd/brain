**Exercise 8.** [*M23*] Find the asymptotic value of $\ln((an^2+bn)!)$ with absolute error $O(n^{-2})$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m44s  

---

## Solution

Let

$$
m = an^2 + bn.
$$

The goal is to determine $\ln(m!)$ with absolute error $O(n^{-2})$.

Stirling’s expansion in logarithmic form is

$$
\ln(m!) = m\ln m - m + \frac{1}{2}\ln(2\pi m) + \frac{1}{12m} + O(m^{-3}).
$$

Since $m = \Theta(n^2)$, the remainder term satisfies $O(m^{-3}) = O(n^{-6})$, which is negligible for an error bound $O(n^{-2})$.

### Expansion of $\ln m$

Write

$$
m = an^2\left(1 + \frac{b}{an}\right).
$$

Then

$$
\ln m = \ln a + 2\ln n + \ln\!\left(1 + \frac{b}{an}\right).
$$

Using

$$
\ln(1+x) = x - \frac{x^2}{2} + O(x^3),
$$

with $x = \frac{b}{an}$,

$$
\ln m = \ln a + 2\ln n + \frac{b}{an} - \frac{b^2}{2a^2 n^2} + O(n^{-3}).
$$

### Expansion of $m\ln m$

Split into two parts.

First,

$$
an^2\ln m
= an^2(\ln a + 2\ln n)
+ an^2\left(\frac{b}{an} - \frac{b^2}{2a^2 n^2}\right)
+ O(n^{-1}),
$$

which simplifies to

$$
2a n^2\ln n + a n^2\ln a + bn - \frac{b^2}{2a} + O(n^{-1}).
$$

Second,

$$
bn\ln m
= bn(\ln a + 2\ln n)
+ bn\left(\frac{b}{an} - \frac{b^2}{2a^2 n^2}\right)
+ O(n^{-2}),
$$

which gives

$$
bn\ln a + 2bn\ln n + \frac{b^2}{a} - \frac{b^3}{2a^2 n} + O(n^{-2}).
$$

Adding both parts,

$$
m\ln m
= 2a n^2\ln n + a n^2\ln a
\+ 2bn\ln n + bn\ln a + bn
+ \frac{b^2}{2a}
- \frac{b^3}{2a^2 n}
+ O(n^{-2}).
$$

### Subtracting $m$

$$
m\ln m - m
= 2a n^2\ln n + a n^2(\ln a - 1)
\+ 2bn\ln n + bn\ln a
+ \frac{b^2}{2a}
- \frac{b^3}{2a^2 n}
+ O(n^{-2}).
$$

### Logarithmic correction term

$$
\frac{1}{2}\ln(2\pi m)
= \frac{1}{2}\ln(2\pi) + \frac{1}{2}\ln a + \ln n
+ \frac{b}{2a n}
- \frac{b^2}{4a^2 n^2}
+ O(n^{-3}).
$$

### Reciprocal correction term

Since

$$
\frac{1}{m}
= \frac{1}{an^2}\left(1 + \frac{b}{an}\right)^{-1}
= \frac{1}{an^2} + O(n^{-3}),
$$

we obtain

$$
\frac{1}{12m} = \frac{1}{12a n^2} + O(n^{-3}).
$$

### Final assembly

Combining all contributions,

$$
\ln((an^2+bn)!)
=
2a n^2\ln n + a n^2(\ln a - 1)
\+ 2bn\ln n + bn\ln a
+ \ln n
$$

$$
+ \frac{1}{2}\ln(2\pi a)
+ \frac{b^2}{2a}
- \frac{b^3}{2a^2 n}
+ \frac{b}{2a n}
+ \left(\frac{1}{12a} - \frac{b^2}{4a^2}\right)\frac{1}{n^2}
+ O(n^{-2}).
$$

All omitted contributions are $O(n^{-2})$ in absolute size, since every further Stirling term is $O(n^{-6})$ and all higher-order expansions of $\ln m$ contribute at most $O(n^{-2})$ after multiplication by $m$ or by bounded prefactors.

$$
\boxed{\ln((an^2+bn)!)\ \text{given by the expression above}}
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
