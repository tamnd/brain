[Section 1.2.11.2: Euler's Summation Formula](../)

**Exercise 5.** [*HM30*] Given that

$$
n! = \kappa \sqrt{n}\left(\frac{n}{e}\right)^n\left(1+O\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)\right),
$$

show that $\kappa=\sqrt{2\pi}$ by using Wallis's product.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m23s  

---

## Setup

Assume

$$
n! = \kappa \sqrt{n}\left(\frac{n}{e}\right)^n\left(1+O\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)\right)
$$

for some constant $\kappa$ independent of $n$.

Wallis’s product yields the asymptotic form of the central binomial coefficient,

$$
\binom{2n}{n} = \frac{4^n}{\sqrt{\pi n}}\left(1+O\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)\right).
$$

The goal is to determine $\kappa$.

## Solution

Apply the assumed factorial approximation to $(2n)!$ and $(n!)^2$.

For the numerator,

$$
(2n)! = \kappa \sqrt{2n}\left(\frac{2n}{e}\right)^{2n}\left(1+O\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)\right).
$$

For the denominator,

$$
(n!)^2 = \kappa^2 n \left(\frac{n}{e}\right)^{2n}\left(1+O\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)\right).
$$

Form the quotient:

$$
\binom{2n}{n}
= \frac{(2n)!}{(n!)^2}.
$$

Substituting the expressions gives

$$
\binom{2n}{n}
=
\frac{\kappa \sqrt{2n}\left(\frac{2n}{e}\right)^{2n}\left(1+O\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)\right)}
{\kappa^2 n \left(\frac{n}{e}\right)^{2n}\left(1+O\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)\right)}.
$$

The exponential factors simplify as

$$
\frac{(2n)^{2n}}{n^{2n}} = 2^{2n} = 4^n.
$$

The remaining algebraic factor simplifies as

$$
\frac{\kappa \sqrt{2n}}{\kappa^2 n}
= \frac{\sqrt{2}}{\kappa}\cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{n}}.
$$

Combining terms yields

$$
\binom{2n}{n}
=
\frac{4^n}{\sqrt{n}}
\cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{\kappa}
\left(1+O\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)\right).
$$

Wallis’s product gives the asymptotic form

$$
\binom{2n}{n}
=
\frac{4^n}{\sqrt{\pi n}}
\left(1+O\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)\right).
$$

Comparison of the multiplicative constants in front of $\frac{4^n}{\sqrt{n}}$ yields

$$
\frac{\sqrt{2}}{\kappa} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi}}.
$$

Solving for $\kappa$ gives

$$
\kappa = \sqrt{2\pi}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎

## Verification

The powers of $n$ in $(2n)!$ and $(n!)^2$ cancel exactly after substitution, leaving only a factor proportional to $n^{-1/2}$ times $4^n$, matching the structure required by Wallis’s product. The remaining constant factor is uniquely determined by equating the coefficient of $n^{-1/2}$ in both asymptotic expressions, forcing $\kappa = \sqrt{2\pi}$.

## Notes

The same constant arises if Stirling’s formula is derived first for $\Gamma(n)$ and then specialized to integers, since $n! = \Gamma(n+1)$. The present argument isolates the constant directly from the central binomial coefficient via Wallis’s product, avoiding independent evaluation of $\Gamma(1/2)$.
