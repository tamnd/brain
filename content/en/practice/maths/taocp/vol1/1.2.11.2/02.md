**Exercise 2.** [*HM20*] Explain why the sequence defined by Eq. (4), not merely any sequence satisfying Eq. (9), is necessary for the validity of Eq. (10).

## Solution

Equation (10) is obtained in Euler’s summation formula by repeated application of finite-difference identities that depend on a specific sequence of coefficients introduced in Eq. (4). That sequence is constructed so that its initial values and its recursion match the combinatorial structure of forward differences used in the derivation.

Equation (9) imposes only a recurrence relation on a sequence of numbers. Any sequence satisfying Eq. (9) may differ from the one defined in Eq. (4) by a solution of the associated homogeneous difference relation that preserves the recurrence but alters the initial conditions. Such a difference produces a nonzero contribution when the summation-by-parts step underlying Eq. (10) is carried out, since the cancellation of boundary terms relies on the exact initial values encoded in Eq. (4).

The derivation of Eq. (10) uses the specific identity between the coefficients of Eq. (4) and iterated forward differences of a function at the endpoints of the summation interval. If an alternative sequence satisfying only Eq. (9) is substituted, the correspondence between coefficients and finite differences breaks, and the boundary terms no longer collapse to the form required in Eq. (10). The resulting expression contains additional terms determined by the deviation from the initial conditions in Eq. (4), so the stated formula fails.

Therefore the sequence defined explicitly by Eq. (4) is required, since Eq. (9) alone does not determine a unique sequence compatible with the boundary-cancellation mechanism used in Eq. (10). This completes the proof. ∎
