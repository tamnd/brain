**Exercise 6.** [**] [00] The quantitative discussion at the end of this section says that the
cost of garbage collection is approximately $c_1N+c_2M$ units of time; where
does the "$c_2M$" term come from?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m58s  

---

The term $c_2M$ arises from the need to traverse all the pointers contained within the nodes of the Lists during the marking phase of garbage collection. Here $M$ denotes the total number of pointer fields in all nodes, which includes the `DLINK` and `RLINK` fields of each node, as well as any references stored in the `REF` fields for sub-Lists. Each such pointer must be examined to determine whether it refers to a node that has already been marked; if not, the referenced node is marked and its pointers are subsequently processed.

Since each of these $M$ pointer fields requires a constant amount of work, proportional to examining and possibly following the pointer, the total cost of handling all pointer fields is proportional to $M$. Hence the contribution $c_2M$ to the overall cost $c_1N + c_2M$ reflects the time spent following pointers and marking reachable nodes, in addition to the $c_1N$ term, which accounts for the time spent visiting each of the $N$ nodes themselves. This completes the explanation. ∎
