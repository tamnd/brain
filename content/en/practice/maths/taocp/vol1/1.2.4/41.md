**Exercise 41.** [*M23*] Let $a_1,a_2,a_3,\ldots$ be the sequence $1,2,2,3,3,3,4,4,4,4,\ldots$; find an expression for $a_n$ in terms of $n$, using the floor and/or ceiling function.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m16s  

---

## Solution

Let $T_k = \frac{k(k+1)}{2}$ for $k \ge 1$. The sequence $1,2,2,3,3,3,4,4,4,4,\ldots$ is characterized by the property that the value $k$ occurs exactly for those indices $n$ satisfying

$T_{k-1} < n \le T_k,$

where $T_0 = 0$.

Hence $a_n = k$ if and only if

$\frac{(k-1)k}{2} < n \le \frac{k(k+1)}{2}.$

The upper inequality is equivalent to

$k^2 + k - 2n \ge 0.$

The positive root of $k^2 + k - 2n = 0$ is

$\frac{-1 + \sqrt{1 + 8n}}{2}.$

Since the quadratic is increasing for $k \ge 0$, the least integer $k$ satisfying the inequality is

$k = \left\lceil \frac{\sqrt{8n+1} - 1}{2} \right\rceil.$

This expression yields $a_n$ for every $n \ge 1$, since it selects the unique integer $k$ such that $n$ lies in the interval $(T_{k-1}, T_k]$.

Thus,

$\boxed{a_n = \left\lceil \frac{\sqrt{8n+1} - 1}{2} \right\rceil}.$

An equivalent form follows from rewriting the interval condition as

$T_{k-1} < n \le T_k \iff k = \left\lfloor \frac{\sqrt{8n-7} + 1}{2} \right\rfloor,$

so

$\boxed{a_n = \left\lfloor \frac{\sqrt{8n-7} + 1}{2} \right\rfloor}.$ ∎
