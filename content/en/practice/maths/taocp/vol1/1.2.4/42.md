[Section 1.2.4: Integer Functions and Elementary Number Theory](../)

**Exercise 42.** [*M24*] (a) Prove that

$$\sum_{k=1}^n a_k = na_n - \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} k(a_{k+1}-a_k),\qquad \text{if } n>0.$$

(b) The preceding formula is useful for evaluating certain sums involving the floor function. Prove that, if $b$ is an integer $\ge 2$,

$$
\sum_{k=1}^n \lfloor \log_b k \rfloor
= (n+1)\lfloor \log_b n \rfloor - \frac{b^{\lfloor \log_b n \rfloor +1}-b}{b-1}.
$$

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m29s

## Solution

### (a)

Let

$$
S = n a_n - \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} k(a_{k+1}-a_k).
$$

Expand the second term:

$$
S = n a_n - \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} k a_{k+1} + \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} k a_k.
$$

In the first sum substitute $j=k+1$, so $k=j-1$ and $j$ runs from $2$ to $n$:

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{n-1} k a_{k+1} = \sum_{j=2}^{n} (j-1)a_j.
$$

Hence

$$
S = n a_n - \sum_{j=2}^{n} (j-1)a_j + \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} k a_k.
$$

Rewrite the last sum with index $j$:

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{n-1} k a_k = \sum_{j=1}^{n-1} j a_j.
$$

Now collect coefficients of each $a_j$.

For $j=1$, only the last sum contributes, giving coefficient $1$.

For $2 \le j \le n-1$, the coefficient is

$$
j - (j-1) = 1.
$$

For $j=n$, the coefficient is

$$
n - (n-1) = 1.
$$

Therefore

$$
S = \sum_{j=1}^{n} a_j.
$$

This establishes

$$
\sum_{k=1}^n a_k = n a_n - \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} k(a_{k+1}-a_k).
$$

This completes the proof. âˆŽ

### (b)

Let

$$
a_k = \lfloor \log_b k \rfloor,
$$

and let

$$
L = \lfloor \log_b n \rfloor.
$$

Then $b^L \le n < b^{L+1}$, and $a_n = L$.

Apply part (a):

$$
\sum_{k=1}^n a_k = nL - \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} k(a_{k+1}-a_k).
$$

The difference $a_{k+1}-a_k$ equals $1$ exactly when $a_{k+1} = a_k + 1$, which occurs exactly when $k+1$ is a power of $b$. Hence

$$
a_{k+1}-a_k =
\begin{cases}
1, & k = b^m - 1 \text{ for some integer } m \ge 1,\\
0, & \text{otherwise}.
\end{cases}
$$

Only values $m$ with $b^m \le n$ contribute, so $m=1,2,\ldots,L$. Therefore

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{n-1} k(a_{k+1}-a_k)
= \sum_{m=1}^{L} (b^m - 1).
$$

Compute this sum:

$$
\sum_{m=1}^{L} (b^m - 1)
= \sum_{m=1}^{L} b^m - L
= \frac{b^{L+1}-b}{b-1} - L.
$$

Substitute into the formula from part (a):

$$
\sum_{k=1}^n \lfloor \log_b k \rfloor
= nL - \left(\frac{b^{L+1}-b}{b-1} - L\right).
$$

Simplify:

$$
= (n+1)L - \frac{b^{L+1}-b}{b-1}.
$$

Thus

$$
\sum_{k=1}^n \lfloor \log_b k \rfloor
= (n+1)\lfloor \log_b n \rfloor - \frac{b^{\lfloor \log_b n \rfloor +1}-b}{b-1}.
$$

This completes the proof. âˆŽ
