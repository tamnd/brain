**Exercise 28.** &#9654; [*M25*] Show that the method used to prove Theorem F can be used to prove the following extension, called *Euler’s theorem*: $a^{\varphi(m)} \equiv 1$ (modulo $m$), for any positive integer $m$, when $a \perp m$. (In particular, the number $n'$ in exercise 19 may be taken to be $n^{\varphi(m)-1} \bmod m$.)

## Solution

Let $m$ be a positive integer and let $a \perp m$. Let $r_1, r_2, \ldots, r_{\varphi(m)}$ be the integers in ${0,1,\ldots,m-1}$ that are relatively prime to $m$.

Consider the set of residues

$$
a r_1 \bmod m,\quad a r_2 \bmod m,\quad \ldots,\quad a r_{\varphi(m)} \bmod m.
$$

If $a r_i \bmod m = a r_j \bmod m$, then $a r_i \equiv a r_j \pmod m$, and since $a \perp m$, Law B gives $r_i \equiv r_j \pmod m$. Because both $r_i$ and $r_j$ lie in ${0,1,\ldots,m-1}$, this implies $r_i = r_j$. Hence the residues $a r_i \bmod m$ are all distinct, and each is relatively prime to $m$ since any common divisor of $a r_i$ and $m$ must divide $r_i$.

Therefore the numbers $a r_1 \bmod m, \ldots, a r_{\varphi(m)} \bmod m$ form a permutation of $r_1, \ldots, r_{\varphi(m)}$, so

$$
(a r_1)(a r_2)\cdots(a r_{\varphi(m)}) \equiv r_1 r_2 \cdots r_{\varphi(m)} \pmod m.
$$

The left-hand side expands to

$$
a^{\varphi(m)} (r_1 r_2 \cdots r_{\varphi(m)}) \equiv r_1 r_2 \cdots r_{\varphi(m)} \pmod m.
$$

Each $r_i$ satisfies $r_i \perp m$, hence the product $R = r_1 r_2 \cdots r_{\varphi(m)}$ also satisfies $R \perp m$. By repeated application of Law B, $R$ can be canceled from both sides of the congruence, yielding

$$
a^{\varphi(m)} \equiv 1 \pmod m.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
