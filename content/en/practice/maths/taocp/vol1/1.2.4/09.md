[Section 1.2.4: Integer Functions and Elementary Number Theory](../)

**Exercise 9.** [*05*] What are $5 \bmod (-3)$, $18 \bmod (-3)$, $-2 \bmod (-3)$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 59s  

---

For $y<0$, the definition is $x \bmod y = x - y\lfloor x/y \rfloor$, and the quotient is determined by the floor of the real number $x/y$.

For $5 \bmod (-3)$, we compute $5/(-3) = -\frac{5}{3}$, hence $\lfloor 5/(-3) \rfloor = -2$. Then

$5 \bmod (-3) = 5 - (-3)(-2) = 5 - 6 = -1$.

For $18 \bmod (-3)$, $18/(-3) = -6$ and $\lfloor -6 \rfloor = -6$. Thus

$18 \bmod (-3) = 18 - (-3)(-6) = 18 - 18 = 0$.

For $-2 \bmod (-3)$, $-2/(-3) = \frac{2}{3}$ so $\lfloor -2/(-3) \rfloor = 0$. Hence

$-2 \bmod (-3) = -2 - (-3)\cdot 0 = -2$.

Therefore,

$\boxed{5 \bmod (-3) = -1,\quad 18 \bmod (-3) = 0,\quad -2 \bmod (-3) = -2}$.
