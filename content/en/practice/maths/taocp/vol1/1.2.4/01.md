[Section 1.2.4: Integer Functions and Elementary Number Theory](../)

**Exercise 1.** [*00*] What are $\lfloor 1.1 \rfloor$, $\lceil -1.1 \rceil$, $\lfloor -1.1 \rfloor$, $\lfloor 0.99999 \rfloor$, and $\lfloor \lg 35 \rfloor$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 59s  

---

$\lfloor 1.1 \rfloor = 1$ since $1 \le 1.1 < 2$.

$\lceil -1.1 \rceil = -1$ since $-1 \ge -1.1$ and $-2 < -1.1$.

$\lfloor -1.1 \rfloor = -2$ since $-2 \le -1.1 < -1$.

$\lfloor 0.99999 \rfloor = 0$ since $0 \le 0.99999 < 1$.

For $\lfloor \lg 35 \rfloor$, with $\lg x = \log_2 x$, we have $2^5 = 32 \le 35 < 64 = 2^6$, hence $5 \le \log_2 35 < 6$, so $\lfloor \lg 35 \rfloor = 5$.

$\boxed{1,,-1,,-2,,0,,5}$
