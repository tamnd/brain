[Section 1.2.4: Integer Functions and Elementary Number Theory](../)

**Exercise 8.** [*00*] What are $100 \bmod 3$, $100 \bmod 7$, $-100 \bmod 7$, $-100 \bmod 0$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 58s  

---

Using $x \bmod y = x - y\lfloor x/y \rfloor$ for $y \ne 0$ and $x \bmod 0 = x$:

For $100 \bmod 3$, one has $\lfloor 100/3 \rfloor = 33$, hence $100 \bmod 3 = 100 - 3 \cdot 33 = 1$.

For $100 \bmod 7$, one has $\lfloor 100/7 \rfloor = 14$, hence $100 \bmod 7 = 100 - 7 \cdot 14 = 2$.

For $-100 \bmod 7$, one has $\lfloor -100/7 \rfloor = -15$, since $-15 \le -100/7 < -14$. Hence $-100 \bmod 7 = -100 - 7(-15) = -100 + 105 = 5$.

Finally, by definition $-100 \bmod 0 = -100$.

Thus the values are $\boxed{1, 2, 5, -100}$.
