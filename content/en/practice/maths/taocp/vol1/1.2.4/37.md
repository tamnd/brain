**Exercise 37.** &#9654; [*M30*] Let $m$ and $n$ be integers, $n>0$. Show that

$$
\sum_{0 \le k < n} \left\lfloor \frac{mk+x}{n} \right\rfloor
= \frac{(m-1)(n-1)}{2} + \frac{d-1}{2} + d\left\lfloor \frac{x}{d} \right\rfloor,
$$

where $d$ is the greatest common divisor of $m$ and $n$, and $x$ is any real number.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m54s  

---

Let $d=\gcd(m,n)$ and write

$$
m=d m_1,\qquad n=d n_1,\qquad \gcd(m_1,n_1)=1.
$$

Define

$$
S=\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\left\lfloor \frac{mk+x}{n}\right\rfloor
=\sum_{k=0}^{dn_1-1}\left\lfloor \frac{dm_1k+x}{dn_1}\right\rfloor.
$$

## 1. Decomposition of $k$

Write each $k$ uniquely as

$$
k=a+t n_1,\qquad 0\le a<n_1,\quad 0\le t<d.
$$

Then

$$
mk = d m_1(a+t n_1)=d m_1 a + d t m_1 n_1,
$$

so

$$
\frac{mk+x}{n}
=\frac{d m_1 a + d t m_1 n_1 + x}{d n_1}
= t m_1 + \frac{m_1 a + x/d}{n_1}.
$$

Hence

$$
\left\lfloor \frac{mk+x}{n}\right\rfloor
= t m_1 + \left\lfloor \frac{m_1 a + x/d}{n_1}\right\rfloor.
$$

Summing over $a,t$,

$$
S=\sum_{a=0}^{n_1-1}\sum_{t=0}^{d-1} t m_1
\;+\;
d\sum_{a=0}^{n_1-1}\left\lfloor \frac{m_1 a + x/d}{n_1}\right\rfloor.
$$

## 2. The linear $t$-term (corrected)

$$
\sum_{a=0}^{n_1-1}\sum_{t=0}^{d-1} t m_1
= n_1 \cdot m_1 \cdot \frac{d(d-1)}{2}
= m_1 n_1 \frac{d(d-1)}{2}.
$$

Now substitute $m_1=m/d$, $n_1=n/d$:

$$
m_1 n_1 \frac{d(d-1)}{2}
= \frac{m}{d}\cdot\frac{n}{d}\cdot \frac{d(d-1)}{2}
= \frac{mn(d-1)}{2d}.
$$

Thus

$$
S=\frac{mn(d-1)}{2d}
+
dT,
\quad
T=\sum_{a=0}^{n_1-1}\left\lfloor \frac{m_1 a + x/d}{n_1}\right\rfloor.
$$

## 3. Evaluation of $T$

Write

$$
\frac{x}{d}=q+u,\qquad q=\left\lfloor \frac{x}{d}\right\rfloor,\quad 0\le u<1.
$$

Then

$$
T=\sum_{a=0}^{n_1-1}\left\lfloor \frac{m_1 a + q + u}{n_1}\right\rfloor.
$$

Split into fractional and integer parts:

$$
\left\lfloor \frac{m_1 a + q + u}{n_1}\right\rfloor
=
\left\lfloor \frac{m_1 a + u}{n_1} + \frac{q}{n_1}\right\rfloor.
$$

Let

$$
b_a=\left\lfloor \frac{m_1 a + u}{n_1}\right\rfloor,\qquad
\theta_a=\left\{\frac{m_1 a + u}{n_1}\right\}.
$$

Then

$$
\frac{m_1 a + u}{n_1}=b_a+\theta_a,\quad 0\le \theta_a<1,
$$

so

$$
\left\lfloor \frac{m_1 a + q + u}{n_1}\right\rfloor
=
b_a + \left\lfloor \frac{q + n_1\theta_a}{n_1}\right\rfloor.
$$

Since $0\le n_1\theta_a<n_1$, the second term equals $1$ exactly when

$$
n_1\theta_a \ge n_1-q \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \theta_a \ge 1-\frac{q}{n_1}.
$$

Because $\gcd(m_1,n_1)=1$, the values $\theta_a$ are a permutation of

$$
\left\{0,\frac1{n_1},\dots,\frac{n_1-1}{n_1}\right\},
$$

so exactly $q$ indices satisfy the inequality. Hence

$$
T = \sum_{a=0}^{n_1-1} b_a + q.
$$

Now evaluate the base sum:

$$
\sum_{a=0}^{n_1-1} b_a
=
\sum_{a=0}^{n_1-1}\left\lfloor \frac{m_1 a + u}{n_1}\right\rfloor.
$$

The fractional shift $u\in[0,1)$ does not change the total floor sum over a complete residue system (it only permutes which $a$ cross integer boundaries, without changing the total count of crossings). Hence

$$
\sum_{a=0}^{n_1-1}\left\lfloor \frac{m_1 a + u}{n_1}\right\rfloor
=
\sum_{a=0}^{n_1-1}\left\lfloor \frac{m_1 a}{n_1}\right\rfloor
=
\frac{(m_1-1)(n_1-1)}{2}.
$$

Therefore

$$
T=\frac{(m_1-1)(n_1-1)}{2}+\left\lfloor \frac{x}{d}\right\rfloor.
$$

## 4. Final assembly

Substitute into $S$:

$$
S=\frac{mn(d-1)}{2d}
+
d\left(\frac{(m_1-1)(n_1-1)}{2}+\left\lfloor \frac{x}{d}\right\rfloor\right).
$$

Expand:

$$
S=\frac{mn(d-1)}{2d}
+\frac{d(m_1-1)(n_1-1)}{2}
+d\left\lfloor \frac{x}{d}\right\rfloor.
$$

Now rewrite:

$$
d(m_1-1)(n_1-1)
= d\left(\frac{m}{d}-1\right)\left(\frac{n}{d}-1\right)
=\frac{(m-d)(n-d)}{d}.
$$

So

$$
S=\frac{mn(d-1)}{2d}
+\frac{(m-d)(n-d)}{2d}
+d\left\lfloor \frac{x}{d}\right\rfloor.
$$

Combine the first two terms:

$$
\frac{mn(d-1)+(m-d)(n-d)}{2d}
=
\frac{mn d - mn + mn - md - nd + d^2}{2d}
=
\frac{(m-1)(n-1)d + (d-1)d}{2d}.
$$

Hence

$$
S=\frac{(m-1)(n-1)}{2}+\frac{d-1}{2}+d\left\lfloor \frac{x}{d}\right\rfloor.
$$

## Conclusion

$$
\sum_{0 \le k < n} \left\lfloor \frac{mk+x}{n} \right\rfloor
= \frac{(m-1)(n-1)}{2} + \frac{d-1}{2} + d\left\lfloor \frac{x}{d} \right\rfloor,
$$

where $d=\gcd(m,n)$. ∎
