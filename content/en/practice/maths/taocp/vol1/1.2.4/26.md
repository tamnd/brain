**Exercise 26.** [*M15*] Let $p$ be an odd prime number, let $a$ be any integer, and let $b = a^{(p-1)/2}$. Show that $b \bmod p$ is either 0 or 1 or $p-1$. [Hint: Consider $(b+1)(b-1)$.]

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m03s  

---

## Solution

Let $p$ be an odd prime and let $a$ be an integer. Define $b = a^{(p-1)/2}$.

If $a \bmod p = 0$, then $p \mid a$, hence $p \mid b$, so $b \bmod p = 0$.

Assume now $a \bmod p \ne 0$. Then $a \perp p$ since $p$ is prime. By Fermat’s theorem,

$a^{p-1} \equiv 1 \pmod p.$

With $b = a^{(p-1)/2}$, this gives

$b^2 \equiv 1 \pmod p.$

Hence

$(b-1)(b+1) \equiv 0 \pmod p,$

so $p \mid (b-1)(b+1)$.

Since $p$ is prime, $p \mid (b-1)$ or $p \mid (b+1)$. Therefore

$b \equiv 1 \pmod p \quad \text{or} \quad b \equiv -1 \pmod p.$

Thus $b \bmod p$ equals $1$ or $p-1$ in these two cases.

Combining both cases, $b \bmod p$ is $0$, $1$, or $p-1$.

This completes the proof. ∎
