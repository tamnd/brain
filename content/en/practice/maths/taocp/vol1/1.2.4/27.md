**Exercise 27.** [*M15*] Given that $n$ is a positive integer, let $\varphi(n)$ be the number of values among $\{0,1,\ldots,n-1\}$ that are relatively prime to $n$. Thus $\varphi(1)=1$, $\varphi(2)=1$, $\varphi(3)=2$, $\varphi(4)=2$, etc. Show that $\varphi(p)=p-1$ if $p$ is a prime number; and evaluate $\varphi(p^e)$, when $e$ is a positive integer.

We restart from the definition.

Let $n$ be a positive integer. The function $\varphi(n)$ counts the integers in

$$
\{0,1,\dots,n-1\}
$$

that are relatively prime to $n$, i.e. those integers $k$ such that $\gcd(k,n)=1$.

## 1. Case $n=p$, where $p$ is prime

We examine the set $\{0,1,\dots,p-1\}$.

- For $k=1,2,\dots,p-1$, since $p$ is prime, the only positive divisors of $p$ are $1$ and $p$. Any integer $k$ with $1 \le k \le p-1$ cannot share a nontrivial common divisor with $p$. Hence

$$
\gcd(k,p)=1 \quad \text{for all } k=1,\dots,p-1.
$$
- For $k=0$, we have $\gcd(0,p)=p \ne 1$, so $0$ is not counted.

Thus exactly the $p-1$ integers $1,2,\dots,p-1$ are relatively prime to $p$. Therefore,

$$
\varphi(p)=p-1.
$$

## 2. Case $n=p^e$, where $p$ is prime and $e \ge 1$

We consider integers $k \in \{0,1,\dots,p^e-1\}$.

A key observation is:

$$
\gcd(k,p^e)=1 \iff p \nmid k.
$$

Indeed, since $p^e$ has no prime factors other than $p$, any common divisor greater than $1$ must include $p$. Thus a number is coprime to $p^e$ exactly when it is not divisible by $p$.

So we count the complement: integers divisible by $p$ in $\{0,1,\dots,p^e-1\}$.

Every such integer has the form $k = pm$, where

$$
0 \le m \le p^{e-1}-1,
$$

because

$$
p m \le p^e - 1 \implies m \le p^{e-1} - \frac{1}{p}.
$$

Hence $m$ takes exactly the integer values $0,1,\dots,p^{e-1}-1$.

Therefore there are exactly $p^{e-1}$ multiples of $p$ in the set $\{0,1,\dots,p^e-1\}$.

Since these are precisely the integers not coprime to $p^e$, we obtain:

$$
\varphi(p^e) = p^e - p^{e-1}.
$$

This can also be written as

$$
\varphi(p^e) = p^{e-1}(p-1).
$$

## Conclusion

$$
\boxed{\varphi(p)=p-1 \text{ for prime } p,}
$$

$$
\boxed{\varphi(p^e)=p^e-p^{e-1}=p^{e-1}(p-1).}
$$
