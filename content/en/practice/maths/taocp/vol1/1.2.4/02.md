[Section 1.2.4: Integer Functions and Elementary Number Theory](../)

**Exercise 2.** &#9654; [*01*] What is $\lceil \lfloor x \rfloor \rceil$?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 2m04s  

---

Let $n = \lfloor x \rfloor$. By definition of the floor function, $n$ is an integer and $n \le x < n+1$.

Since $n$ is already an integer, the ceiling of $n$ is $n$ itself, that is, $\lceil n \rceil = n$.

Substituting $n = \lfloor x \rfloor$ gives

$$
\lceil \lfloor x \rfloor \rceil = \lfloor x \rfloor.
$$

Thus,

$$
\boxed{\lceil \lfloor x \rfloor \rceil = \lfloor x \rfloor}.
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
