[Section 1.2.4: Integer Functions and Elementary Number Theory](../)

**Exercise 20.** [*M15*] Use the law of inverses and Law A to prove Law B.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m26s  

---

Since $a \perp m$, the law of inverses yields an integer $a'$ such that

$$
aa' \equiv 1 \pmod m.
$$

From the hypothesis $a \equiv b \pmod m$, apply Law A (multiplication) with $a'$ to obtain

$$
aa' \equiv ba' \pmod m.
$$

Since also $aa' \equiv 1 \pmod m$, transitivity of congruence gives

$$
ba' \equiv 1 \pmod m.
$$

Now start from the given congruence

$$
ax \equiv by \pmod m.
$$

Apply Law A (multiplication) with $a'$ to both sides:

$$
(ax)a' \equiv (by)a' \pmod m,
$$

so by associativity,

$$
aa'x \equiv ba'y \pmod m.
$$

We now replace each side using Law A again, not substitution.

From $aa' \equiv 1 \pmod m$, apply Law A with $x$:

$$
(aa')x \equiv 1\cdot x \pmod m,
$$

hence

$$
aa'x \equiv x \pmod m.
$$

From $ba' \equiv 1 \pmod m$, apply Law A with $y$:

$$
(ba')y \equiv 1\cdot y \pmod m,
$$

hence

$$
ba'y \equiv y \pmod m.
$$

Thus we have

$$
aa'x \equiv x \pmod m
\quad \text{and} \quad
aa'x \equiv ba'y \pmod m
\quad \text{and} \quad
ba'y \equiv y \pmod m.
$$

By transitivity of congruence,

$$
x \equiv y \pmod m.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
