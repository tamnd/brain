[Section 1.2.4: Integer Functions and Elementary Number Theory](../)

**Exercise 15.** [*10*] Prove that $z(x \bmod y) = (zx) \bmod (zy)$. [Law C is an immediate consequence of this distributive law.]

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m03s  

---

If $y \ne 0$ and $z \ne 0$, then by definition (1),

$$
x \bmod y = x - y\left\lfloor \frac{x}{y} \right\rfloor.
$$

Multiplying by $z$ gives

$$
z(x \bmod y) = zx - zy\left\lfloor \frac{x}{y} \right\rfloor.
$$

Since $z \ne 0$, we have

$$
\frac{zx}{zy} = \frac{x}{y},
$$

hence

$$
\left\lfloor \frac{zx}{zy} \right\rfloor = \left\lfloor \frac{x}{y} \right\rfloor.
$$

Applying definition (1) again,

$$
(zx) \bmod (zy) = zx - zy\left\lfloor \frac{zx}{zy} \right\rfloor = zx - zy\left\lfloor \frac{x}{y} \right\rfloor,
$$

which matches the previous expression.

If $y=0$, then $x \bmod 0 = x$ and $(zx) \bmod 0 = zx$ by definition, so both sides equal $zx$. If $z=0$, then $z(x \bmod y)=0$ and $(zx) \bmod (zy)=0 \bmod 0=0$, so both sides equal $0$. This completes the proof. ∎
