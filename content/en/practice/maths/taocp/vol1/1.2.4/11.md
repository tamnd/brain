[Section 1.2.4: Integer Functions and Elementary Number Theory](../)

**Exercise 11.** [*00*] What does “$x \equiv y \pmod 0$” mean by our conventions?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m  

---

By definition (5), $x \equiv y \pmod z$ means $x \bmod z = y \bmod z$. When $z = 0$, the definition of “mod” in (1) gives $x \bmod 0 = x$ and $y \bmod 0 = y$. Hence $x \equiv y \pmod 0$ holds exactly when $x = y$.
