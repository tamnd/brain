**Exercise 50.** [*M15*] $[M15]$ Interpret the value $E(D_1, \ldots, D_n)$ in the text's Monte Carlo algorithm.

We restart the argument from the underlying random process so that all executions of the Monte Carlo backtracking algorithm are included in the probability space.

Let the algorithm proceed from level $1$ to level $n$. At level $\ell$, given a prefix $(x_1,\ldots,x_{\ell-1})$, it forms the admissible set $S_\ell(x_1,\ldots,x_{\ell-1})$. If this set is empty, the computation terminates immediately (a dead end). Otherwise the algorithm chooses $x_\ell$ uniformly from this set and continues.

Thus every execution produces a unique terminal outcome, which is either:

1. a full assignment $t=(t_1,\ldots,t_n)$, or
2. a dead-end prefix $u=(u_1,\ldots,u_k)$ such that $S_{k+1}(u_1,\ldots,u_k)=\varnothing$.

Let $\Omega$ be the set of all such terminal outcomes.

The random variable produced by the algorithm is

$$
X = \prod_{\ell=1}^{K} |S_\ell|,
$$

where $K=n$ for full assignments and $K=k$ for a dead end occurring at level $k$. In particular, if termination occurs because $S_{k+1}=\varnothing$, then the process includes the factor $|S_{k+1}|=0$, so $X=0$ for every dead-end outcome.

Hence:

- If the outcome is a full solution $t \in T$, then

$$
X(t)=\prod_{\ell=1}^{n} |S_\ell(t_1,\ldots,t_{\ell-1})|.
$$
- If the outcome is a dead end, then $X=0$.

### Step 1: Probability of a terminal outcome

Fix any terminal outcome $u \in \Omega$ of length $k$. The probability that the algorithm follows exactly this sequence of choices is

$$
\Pr(u) = \prod_{\ell=1}^{k} \frac{1}{|S_\ell(u_1,\ldots,u_{\ell-1})|},
$$

since at each step the choice is uniform over the admissible set.

### Step 2: Contribution to the expectation

For a full solution $t \in T$,

$$
\Pr(t)\,X(t)
=
\left(\prod_{\ell=1}^{n} \frac{1}{|S_\ell|}\right)
\left(\prod_{\ell=1}^{n} |S_\ell|\right)
=1.
$$

For a dead-end outcome $u$, we have $X(u)=0$, hence

$$
\Pr(u)\,X(u)=0.
$$

### Step 3: Partition of the sample space

The set $\Omega$ of terminal outcomes forms a complete partition of all executions of the algorithm: every run ends in exactly one element of $\Omega$, and these events are disjoint.

Therefore,

$$
\mathbb{E}[X]
=
\sum_{u \in \Omega} \Pr(u)\,X(u).
$$

Splitting into full solutions and dead ends,

$$
\mathbb{E}[X]
=
\sum_{t \in T} \Pr(t)\,X(t)
+
\sum_{\substack{u \in \Omega \\ u \notin T}} \Pr(u)\,X(u).
$$

The second sum is zero, and the first sum equals

$$
\sum_{t \in T} 1
=
|T|.
$$

### Conclusion

$$
E(D_1,\ldots,D_n)=|T|.
$$

### Interpretation

The value $E(D_1,\ldots,D_n)$ is the number of satisfying complete assignments of $P_n$. Equivalently, the Monte Carlo backtracking algorithm defines an unbiased estimator of the solution count: every full successful path contributes exactly $1$ in expectation, while every dead-end execution contributes $0$, and the expectation over all executions equals $|T|$.
