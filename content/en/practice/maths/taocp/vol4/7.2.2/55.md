**Exercise 55.** [*M30*] If all costs $c(x_1, \ldots, x_l)$ are positive, show that the biased probabilities of exercise 54 can be chosen in such a way that the estimate $S$ is always exact.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m25s  

---

## Setup

Let a node in Algorithm $E$ be a partial sequence $v = (x_1, \ldots, x_l)$, and let $\mathcal{D}(v)$ denote the set of children of $v$, corresponding to all choices $x_{l+1} \in D_{l+1}$ that satisfy the cutoff condition $P_{l+1}(x_1, \ldots, x_l, x_{l+1})$.

For each node $v$, define its total cost

$$
C(v) = \sum c(x_1, \ldots, x_n),
$$

where the sum is over all completions $(x_{l+1}, \ldots, x_n)$ such that $P_n(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ holds.

Let the root be $r = ()$. Then $C(r)$ is the quantity estimated by Algorithm $E$.

Algorithm $E$ maintains a random estimate $S$ that is updated along a single root-to-leaf path according to transition probabilities chosen at each level. Exercise 54 allows arbitrary positive conditional probabilities

$$
\Pr\{x_{l+1} = t \mid x_1, \ldots, x_l\} = p_v(t) > 0,
$$

with a modified update rule ensuring $\mathbb{E}[S] = C(r)$.

## Solution

Assume $C(v) > 0$ for every node $v$ that is reachable under the cutoff rules, which holds because all costs are positive.

For each nonterminal node $v$, define probabilities

$$
p_v(u) = \frac{C(u)}{C(v)} \quad \text{for } u \in \mathcal{D}(v).
$$

Since every completion of $v$ is partitioned uniquely according to its first step, we have

$$
C(v) = \sum_{u \in \mathcal{D}(v)} C(u),
$$

so these values form a valid probability distribution.

Define the estimate $S$ so that at the root,

$$
S = C(r),
$$

and whenever the algorithm moves from a node $v$ to a child $u$, the update rule of Algorithm $E$ from exercise 54 produces

$$
S \leftarrow S \cdot \frac{C(u)}{C(v) p_v(u)}.
$$

Substituting the chosen probabilities gives

$$
S \leftarrow S \cdot \frac{C(u)}{C(v)} \cdot \frac{C(v)}{C(u)} = S.
$$

Thus the value of $S$ does not change at any transition, and therefore remains equal to $C(r)$ along every root-to-leaf path.

At termination, Algorithm $E$ outputs $S$, so every run produces

$$
S = C(r)
$$

exactly.

## Verification

At each node $v$, positivity of costs implies $C(v) > 0$, so $p_v(u)$ is well-defined and strictly positive for all admissible children $u$.

The normalization

$$
\sum_{u \in \mathcal{D}(v)} p_v(u) = \frac{1}{C(v)} \sum_{u \in \mathcal{D}(v)} C(u) = 1
$$

ensures that the transition rule is a valid probability distribution.

The update factor in Algorithm $E$ cancels exactly because the numerator $C(u)$ in the weighting term matches the numerator in the probability definition, and the denominator $C(v)$ matches the normalization factor. Hence the multiplicative change in $S$ equals $1$ at every step.

Since the initial value is $S = C(r)$ and no update changes it, the final output equals $C(r)$ for every sample path.

## Conclusion

Choosing transition probabilities

$$
p_v(u) = \frac{C(u)}{C(v)}
$$

forces every realization of Algorithm $E$ to satisfy $S = C(r)$ identically, so the estimator is exact without randomness. ∎
