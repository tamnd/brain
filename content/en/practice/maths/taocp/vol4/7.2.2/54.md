**Exercise 54.** [*M21*] Instead of choosing each $y_i$ in step E5 with probability $1/d$, we could use a biased distribution where $\Pr\{I = i \mid X_1, \ldots, X_{l-1}\} = p_{X_1 \cdots X_{l-1}}(i) > 0$. How should the estimate $S$ be modified so that its expected value in this general scheme is still $C(t)$?

## Solution

Let $X_1, X_2, \ldots, X_l$ denote the random choices produced by step E5 under the modified scheme, where

$\Pr\{X_k = i \mid X_1,\ldots,X_{k-1}\} = p_{X_1\cdots X_{k-1}}(i), \qquad p_{X_1\cdots X_{k-1}}(i) > 0.$

For a complete feasible sequence $x_1 x_2 \cdots x_n$ that satisfies the constraints of Algorithm E, the probability of generating this sequence is

$\Pr(x_1,\ldots,x_n) = \prod_{k=1}^n p_{x_1\cdots x_{k-1}}(x_k).$

Let $C(t)$ denote the quantity computed by Algorithm E, namely the total contribution of all complete solutions consistent with the backtracking constraints at target level $t$. In the original unbiased algorithm, each feasible sequence contributes weight $1$ to $C(t)$, and the estimator compensates by multiplying by the inverse of the uniform path probability.

Under the biased scheme, define the modified estimator $S$ by assigning to each completed solution $x_1\cdots x_n$ the weight

$w(x_1,\ldots,x_n) = \prod_{k=1}^n \frac{1}{p_{x_1\cdots x_{k-1}}(x_k)}.$

If a branch terminates without reaching a full solution, its contribution is $0$. Thus the estimator produced by a run of the algorithm is

\begin{cases} \prod_{k=1}^n \dfrac{1}{p_{X_1\cdots X_{k-1}}(X_k)}, & \text{if a complete solution is reached},\\[6pt] 0, & \text{otherwise}. \end{cases}$$
To compute the expectation, sum over all complete feasible sequences:
$$\mathbb{E}[S] = \sum_{x_1,\ldots,x_n} \Pr(x_1,\ldots,x_n)\, w(x_1,\ldots,x_n).$$
Substituting the expressions for $\Pr$ and $w$ gives
$$\mathbb{E}[S] = \sum_{x_1,\ldots,x_n} \left(\prod_{k=1}^n p_{x_1\cdots x_{k-1}}(x_k)\right) \left(\prod_{k=1}^n \frac{1}{p_{x_1\cdots x_{k-1}}(x_k)}\right).$$
Each product cancels termwise, so every feasible sequence contributes $1$:
$$\mathbb{E}[S] = \sum_{x_1,\ldots,x_n} 1 = C(t).$$ Thus the estimator remains unbiased under the biased transition probabilities when each run is weighted by the inverse path probability. This completes the proof. ∎ ## Notes The modification is the standard importance-sampling correction applied along the decision path of Algorithm E, replacing the uniform factor $d$ at each step by the reciprocal of the conditional probability used in step E5.
