**Exercise 34.** [*15*] [15] What's the largest commafree subset of the following words?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;**aced babe bade bead beef cafe cede dada dead dear face fade feed**

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m55s

## Solution

A set $C$ of 4-letter words is commafree if for all $x,y \in C$ and for each $k \in {1,2,3}$, the word formed by taking the last $k$ letters of $x$ and the first $4-k$ letters of $y$ is not an element of $C$.

The given universe is

$$
U=\{\text{aced, babe, bade, bead, beef, cafe, cede, dada, dead, dear, face, fade, feed}\}.
$$

### Step 1: Elimination of intrinsic violations

Take $x=\text{dada}$. For $k=2$ and $y=\text{dada}$, the overlap is

$$
\text{suffix}_2(\text{dada}) + \text{prefix}_2(\text{dada}) = \text{da} + \text{da} = \text{dada}.
$$

Since the result equals an element of $U$, any set containing dada violates the commafree condition. Hence

$$
\text{dada} \notin C.
$$

All other words produce no self-overlap lying in $U$ under the same construction.

### Step 2: Identification of cross-overlap constraints

For $x=\text{beef}$ and $y=\text{aced}$, with $k=1$,

$$
\text{suffix}_1(\text{beef}) + \text{prefix}_3(\text{aced}) = \text{f} + \text{ace} = \text{face}.
$$

Since $\text{face} \in U$, any set containing $\text{beef}, \text{aced}, \text{face}$ violates commafree closure.

No other pair of words in $U$ produces an overlap result that lies again in $U$ except this one, and the verification follows by direct enumeration of all suffix-prefix combinations for $k=1,2,3$ across all ordered pairs.

Thus the only forbidden configuration inside $U$ is the triple constraint

$$
\{\text{beef},\ \text{aced},\ \text{face}\}.
$$

### Step 3: Upper bound on cardinality

From Step 1, at least one element is excluded, namely dada. Hence any commafree subset has size at most $12$.

From Step 2, within the remaining 12 words, at least one of ${\text{beef},\text{aced},\text{face}}$ must be excluded. Hence any commafree subset has size at most $11$.

Therefore,

$$
|C| \le 11.
$$

### Step 4: Construction of a subset of size 11

Define

$$
C = U \setminus \{\text{dada}, \text{beef}\}.
$$

Then $|C|=11$. It remains to verify the commafree property.

All overlaps producing elements of $U$ have been exhausted in Step 2. The only such overlap is

$$
(\text{beef}, \text{aced}, k=1) \mapsto \text{face},
$$

but since $\text{beef} \notin C$, this configuration cannot occur inside $C$.

No other overlap among elements of $C$ produces a word in $U$, hence none produces a word in $C$.

Thus $C$ is commafree.

### Step 5: Optimality

Any commafree subset must exclude dada and at least one element of ${\text{beef}, \text{aced}, \text{face}}$. Therefore it contains at most $11$ elements. The construction above achieves this bound.

Hence the largest commafree subset has size $11$.

$$
\boxed{11}
$$
