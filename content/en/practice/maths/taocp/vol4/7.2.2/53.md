**Exercise 53.** &#9654; [*M30*] $[M30]$ Extend Algorithm E so that it also computes the minimum, maximum, mean, and variance of the Monte Carlo estimates $S$ produced by Algorithm E.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 1m02s  

---

## Setup

Let Algorithm E produce, on its $r$-th independent execution, a real-valued output $S^{(r)}$. For $m \ge 1$, define the sample minimum and maximum

$$
S_{\min}(m) = \min_{1 \le r \le m} S^{(r)}, \qquad
S_{\max}(m) = \max_{1 \le r \le m} S^{(r)}.
$$

Define the empirical mean

$$
\mu_m = \frac{1}{m}\sum_{r=1}^m S^{(r)}.
$$

Define the empirical variance

$$
\sigma_m^2 = \frac{1}{m}\sum_{r=1}^m \left(S^{(r)} - \mu_m\right)^2.
$$

The task is to extend Algorithm E so that after each execution it updates $S_{\min}(m)$, $S_{\max}(m)$, $\mu_m$, and $\sigma_m^2$ without storing all values $S^{(r)}$.

## Solution

Introduce four accumulators maintained across executions of Algorithm E: an integer counter $m$, real-valued variables $s$ and $q$, and two additional variables $S_{\min}$ and $S_{\max}$. Initialize

$$
m \leftarrow 0, \quad s \leftarrow 0, \quad q \leftarrow 0, \quad S_{\min} \leftarrow +\infty, \quad S_{\max} \leftarrow -\infty.
$$

Each time Algorithm E completes one Monte Carlo trial, let the output be $S$. The update sequence performs exactly one increment of $m$ and one update of each accumulator.

Set

$$
m \leftarrow m+1,
$$

then update the running sum

$$
s \leftarrow s + S,
$$

and the running quadratic sum

$$
q \leftarrow q + S^2.
$$

Update extremal values by

$$
S_{\min} \leftarrow \min(S_{\min}, S), \qquad S_{\max} \leftarrow \max(S_{\max}, S).
$$

After these updates, define

$$
\mu_m = \frac{s}{m},
$$

and expand the variance expression

$$
\sigma_m^2 = \frac{1}{m}\sum_{r=1}^m S^{(r)2} - \mu_m^2.
$$

Since $q = \sum_{r=1}^m S^{(r)2}$ and $s = \sum_{r=1}^m S^{(r)}$, substitution yields

$$
\sigma_m^2 = \frac{q}{m} - \left(\frac{s}{m}\right)^2.
$$

Thus the maintained state $(m,s,q,S_{\min},S_{\max})$ determines minimum, maximum, mean, and variance after each execution of Algorithm E without retaining past samples.

## Verification

After $m$ executions, $s$ equals $\sum_{r=1}^m S^{(r)}$ because it starts at $0$ and receives exactly one addition of $S^{(r)}$ per execution. The same invariant holds for $q$ since each update adds $S^{(r)2}$ exactly once.

The variable $S_{\min}$ is initialized above all possible outputs and updated by taking the minimum with each new sample, so after $m$ steps it equals the minimum of the first $m$ outputs. The same argument applies to $S_{\max}$.

The identity

$$
\sum_{r=1}^m (S^{(r)} - \mu_m)^2
= \sum_{r=1}^m S^{(r)2} - 2\mu_m \sum_{r=1}^m S^{(r)} + m\mu_m^2
$$

combined with $\sum_{r=1}^m S^{(r)} = s$ and $\mu_m = s/m$ simplifies to

$$
\sigma_m^2 = \frac{q}{m} - \left(\frac{s}{m}\right)^2.
$$

Each quantity used in the computation depends only on current state variables, so no historical storage is required.

This completes the proof. ∎
