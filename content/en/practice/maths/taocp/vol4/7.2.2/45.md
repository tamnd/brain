**Exercise 45.** &#9654; [*28*] $[28]$ Continuing exercise 44, spell out the details of step C3 when $x \ge 0$.

a) What updates should be done to MEM when a blue word $x$ becomes red?

b) What updates should be done to MEM when a blue word $x$ becomes green?

c) Step C3 finishes its job by making $x$ green as in part (b). Explain how it should finish its job by updating the poison list.

Let $U$ be the set of words and let each word be colored blue, green, or red. For each class $c$, MEM stores a value $b(c)$, the number of blue words in $C(c)$, together with the incidence structure that allows $\mathrm{store}(n,c)$ to decrease $b(c)$ when a word leaves the blue set.

For each word $x$, let $\Pi(x)$ be its poison list. When a word becomes green, every word in its poison list must be forced to red.

The key point is that “blue to red” is purely a removal from future consideration, while “blue to green” is a selection event that also satisfies all classes containing the word. That difference must be reflected in MEM.

## (a) When a blue word $x$ becomes red

When $x \in B$ becomes red, it is simply excluded from all blue counts.

For every class $c$ with $x \in C(c)$, MEM performs

$$
b(c) \leftarrow b(c) - 1
$$

via the mechanism $\mathrm{store}(n,c)$ applied to each such class $c$.

No other structural change is made. In particular:

- no class is marked satisfied,
- no additional propagation is triggered,
- $x$ plays no further role in MEM.

This is purely a deletion from the blue accounting.

## (b) When a blue word $x$ becomes green

When $x \in B$ is chosen to become green, the update is not only a removal from blue counts. It is also a commitment that $x$ satisfies every class that contains it.

Two distinct MEM effects occur.

### 1. Blue count updates (same local decrement)

For every class $c$ with $x \in C(c)$, perform

$$
b(c) \leftarrow b(c) - 1
$$

using $\mathrm{store}(n,c)$, exactly as in the red case.

This removes $x$ from blue availability.

### 2. Class satisfaction marking

In addition, for every class $c$ with $x \in C(c)$, MEM must record that $c$ is now satisfied by a green word.

Introduce a class status bit $s(c)$ with values:

- $s(c) = 0$: not yet satisfied by a green word,
- $s(c) = 1$: already satisfied.

When $x$ becomes green, set

$$
s(c) \leftarrow 1 \quad \text{for all } c \text{ with } x \in C(c).
$$

Once $s(c)=1$, further blue deletions in $c$ remain numerically tracked through $b(c)$, but the class is no longer relevant for future “must-be-satisfied-by-blue” reasoning in the algorithm. In particular, later updates via $\mathrm{store}(n,c)$ do not affect any decision logic for satisfied classes.

Finally, $x$ is marked as chosen (green) and removed from further consideration as a candidate.

This extra satisfaction marking is the essential difference from the red case.

## (c) Completion of Step C3 via poison list processing

After $x$ becomes green, Step C3 must enforce incompatibility constraints encoded in the poison list $\Pi(x)$.

The rule is immediate propagation:

For each $y \in \Pi(x)$, do:

### Case 1: $y$ is blue

Change $y$ from blue to red. Then for every class $c$ with $y \in C(c)$,

$$
b(c) \leftarrow b(c) - 1
$$

via $\mathrm{store}(n,c)$.

### Case 2: $y$ is red

No action is required.

### Case 3: $y$ is green

This is a failure condition.

A green word represents a committed choice. If it appears in $\Pi(x)$, it means two chosen words are incompatible. This is not ignored and not deferred. The algorithm must terminate the current branch of the search (backtrack failure).

Thus:

$$
y \in G \cap \Pi(x) \;\Rightarrow\; \text{contradiction, trigger backtracking}.
$$

### Finalization of Step C3

After all poison-list elements have been processed:

- all forced red changes have been fully propagated into MEM,
- all affected blue counts have been updated,
- all incompatibilities of $x$ have been enforced,
- and $x$ remains permanently green.

No additional clearing of $\Pi(x)$ is conceptually required beyond noting that its effect has been completely exhausted for the current choice.

This completes the precise specification of Step C3 when $x \ge 0$, with the necessary distinction between red and green updates in MEM and full propagation semantics of the poison list.
