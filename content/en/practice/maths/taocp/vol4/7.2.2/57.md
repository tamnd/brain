[Section 7.2.2: Backtracking](../)

**Exercise 57.** [*HM21*] Algorithm E can potentially follow $M$ different paths $X_1 \cdots X_{l-1}$ before it terminates, where $M$ is the number of leaves of the backtrack tree. Suppose the final values of $D$ at those leaves are $D^{(1)}, \ldots, D^{(M)}$. Prove that $(D^{(1)} \cdots D^{(M)})^{1/M} \ge M$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m17s  

---

Let $M$ be the number of leaves of the backtrack tree, and for each leaf $i$ let $D^{(i)}$ denote the product of the branching factors along the unique root-to-leaf path leading to that leaf.

At each internal node of Algorithm E, the next branch is chosen uniformly among the available $D$ alternatives. Therefore, if a particular leaf $i$ is reached through a sequence of branching factors $D_1, D_2, \ldots, D_k$, the probability of following exactly that path is

$$
\frac{1}{D_1 D_2 \cdots D_k} = \frac{1}{D^{(i)}}.
$$

Distinct leaves correspond to disjoint events, and every execution path terminates at exactly one leaf. Hence these probabilities sum to 1:

$$
\sum_{i=1}^M \frac{1}{D^{(i)}} = 1.
$$

All $D^{(i)}$ are positive real numbers, so we may apply the arithmetic–geometric mean inequality to the $M$ numbers $\frac{1}{D^{(1)}}, \ldots, \frac{1}{D^{(M)}}$. This gives

$$
\frac{1}{M}\sum_{i=1}^M \frac{1}{D^{(i)}} \;\ge\; \left(\prod_{i=1}^M \frac{1}{D^{(i)}}\right)^{1/M}.
$$

Substituting $\sum_{i=1}^M \frac{1}{D^{(i)}} = 1$ yields

$$
\frac{1}{M} \ge \left(\prod_{i=1}^M \frac{1}{D^{(i)}}\right)^{1/M}.
$$

Rewriting the right-hand side,

$$
\left(\prod_{i=1}^M \frac{1}{D^{(i)}}\right)^{1/M}
= \left(\prod_{i=1}^M D^{(i)}\right)^{-1/M}.
$$

Thus,

$$
\frac{1}{M} \ge \left(\prod_{i=1}^M D^{(i)}\right)^{-1/M}.
$$

Taking reciprocals (which reverses the inequality since both sides are positive) gives

$$
M \le \left(\prod_{i=1}^M D^{(i)}\right)^{1/M}.
$$

Hence,

$$
\left(D^{(1)} D^{(2)} \cdots D^{(M)}\right)^{1/M} \ge M,
$$

as required. ∎
