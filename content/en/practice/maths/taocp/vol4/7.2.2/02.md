### Interpretation of the statement

We are asked:

> Can we choose $D_j$ so that $P_l(x_l)$ is always true?

In TAOCP backtracking notation, $P_l$ is a feasibility predicate at level $l$. In this exercise it is explicitly written as a unary condition $P_l(x_l)$, so it depends only on the single variable $x_l$.

Thus the question is purely about whether we can redefine each domain so that the predicate becomes identically true on that domain.

### Construction

For each $l = 1,\dots,n$, define a new domain

$$
D_l' = \{x_l \in D_l : P_l(x_l) \text{ is true}\}.
$$

Now consider any $x_l \in D_l'$. By definition of $D_l'$, we immediately have

$$
P_l(x_l) \text{ is true}.
$$

Hence $P_l(x_l)$ holds for every element of the domain $D_l'$, i.e. it is identically true on $D_l'$.

### Conclusion

Such a choice of domains always exists (possibly producing empty domains, which is allowed in the backtracking framework unless otherwise specified).

Therefore, the statement is **true**.
