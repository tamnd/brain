**Exercise 35.** &#9654; [*22*] [22] Let $w_1, w_2, \ldots, w_n$ be four-letter words on an $m$-letter alphabet. Design an algorithm that accepts or rejects each $w_j$, according as $w_j$ is commafree or not with respect to the accepted words of $\{w_1, \ldots, w_{j-1}\}$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 7m41s  

---

### Correct solution

#### 1. Correct definition

Let $S$ be a set of four-letter words over an $m$-letter alphabet.

Write each word as $u = u_1u_2u_3u_4$, $v = v_1v_2v_3v_4$.

In the concatenation $uv = u_1u_2u_3u_4v_1v_2v_3v_4$, the length-4 substrings are

$$
u_1u_2u_3u_4,\;
u_2u_3u_4v_1,\;
u_3u_4v_1v_2,\;
u_4v_1v_2v_3,\;
v_1v_2v_3v_4.
$$

A set $S$ is **commafree** iff for all $u,v \in S$, none of the three cross-boundary substrings

$$
u_2u_3u_4v_1,\quad
u_3u_4v_1v_2,\quad
u_4v_1v_2v_3
$$

belongs to $S$.

Equivalently, if we define

$$
c_1(u,v)=u_2u_3u_4v_1,\quad
c_2(u,v)=u_3u_4v_1v_2,\quad
c_3(u,v)=u_4v_1v_2v_3,
$$

then $S$ is commafree iff

$$
c_i(u,v) \notin S \quad \text{for all } u,v \in S,\; i=1,2,3.
$$

Crucially, the substrings $u$ and $v$ themselves are not part of the restriction; only cross-boundary substrings matter.

#### 2. Problem setting

We process words $w_1,\dots,w_n$ sequentially.

Let $S_j$ be the set of accepted words among $w_1,\dots,w_{j-1}$. We maintain the invariant:

$$
S_j \text{ is commafree.}
$$

We must decide whether to accept $w_j$, producing $S_{j+1} = S_j \cup \{w_j\}$.

#### 3. Data structure

Maintain a Boolean table

$$
A[x] =
\begin{cases}
1 & x \in S_j \\
0 & x \notin S_j
\end{cases}
$$

indexed over all four-letter words $x$. Membership queries take $O(1)$ time.

#### 4. What must be checked

To ensure $S_j \cup \{w\}$ is commafree, we must verify that for all $u,v \in S_j \cup \{w\}$,

$$
c_i(u,v) \notin S_j \cup \{w\}, \quad i=1,2,3.
$$

Since $S_j$ is already commafree, all pairs $u,v \in S_j$ are safe.

Thus only pairs involving $w$ need to be checked:

1. $(u,w)$ for $u \in S_j$
2. $(w,u)$ for $u \in S_j$
3. $(w,w)$

For these checks, we must ensure the generated substring is not already in $S_j$, and also not equal to $w$ (since $w$ is not yet in $S_j$).

#### 5. Acceptance test

Given candidate $w$, compute:

For every $u \in S_j$:

- Check $(u,w)$:

$$
c_i(u,w) \notin S_j \cup \{w\}, \quad i=1,2,3.
$$
- Check $(w,u)$:

$$
c_i(w,u) \notin S_j \cup \{w\}, \quad i=1,2,3.
$$

Also check:

- $(w,w)$:

$$
c_i(w,w) \notin S_j \cup \{w\}, \quad i=1,2,3.
$$

In implementation, since $w \notin S_j$, this is equivalent to testing:

- $A(c_i(\cdot,\cdot)) = 0$, and
- $c_i(\cdot,\cdot) \neq w$.

If all tests pass, accept $w$ and set $A(w) \gets 1$.

#### 6. Correctness proof

We prove that after acceptance, $S_{j+1} = S_j \cup \{w\}$ is commafree.

By the inductive hypothesis, all pairs $u,v \in S_j$ satisfy the commafree condition.

Thus it suffices to consider pairs involving $w$.

- **Case $(u,w)$, $u \in S_j$:**

Every cross-boundary substring $c_i(u,w)$ is explicitly checked not to lie in $S_j \cup \{w\}$. Hence no violation occurs.
- **Case $(w,u)$, $u \in S_j$:**

Symmetric to the previous case; all cross-boundary substrings are checked.
- **Case $(w,w)$:**

All cross-boundary substrings of $ww$ are checked not to lie in $S_j \cup \{w\}$.

Therefore no concatenation of two words in $S_{j+1}$ produces a cross-boundary substring that is itself a codeword in $S_{j+1}$. Hence $S_{j+1}$ is commafree.

#### 7. Complexity

Each insertion of $w_j$ requires checking all $u \in S_j$, and for each pair only a constant number of substrings.

Thus the total work is

$$
\sum_{j=1}^n O(|S_j|) = O(n^2).
$$

#### Conclusion

The corrected formulation fixes the key issue: a commafree set forbids only **cross-boundary occurrences** of codewords in concatenations, not the trivial occurrences of the concatenated words themselves. The incremental test then reduces correctly to checking only pairs involving the newly inserted word.
