**Exercise 29.** [*20*] *[20]* Do any $5 \times 6$ word rectangles contain fewer than 11 different words?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m15s  

---

We restate the definition. A $5\times 6$ word rectangle consists of an array $(a_{ij})$ such that each row $r_i = a_{i1}\cdots a_{i6}$ is a word of length $6$, each column $c_j = a_{1j}\cdots a_{5j}$ is a word of length $5$, and the symbols agree at intersections. There is no requirement that the words $r_i$ or $c_j$ be distinct.

We now show that such rectangles can contain fewer than $11$ different words.

Consider an alphabet containing at least one symbol, say $a$. Let every entry of the rectangle be $a$, that is,

$$
a_{ij} = a \quad \text{for all } 1 \le i \le 5,\; 1 \le j \le 6.
$$

Then every row word is

$$
r_i = aaaaaa \quad (6 \text{ times } a),
$$

and every column word is

$$
c_j = aaaaa \quad (5 \text{ times } a).
$$

Thus the set of words appearing in the rectangle consists of at most two distinct words:

$$
\{ aaaaaa,\; aaaaa \}.
$$

Both are valid words under the definition (they are simply length-6 and length-5 strings over the alphabet). The rectangle conditions are satisfied because every row and every column is uniform and consistent.

Hence this $5\times 6$ word rectangle contains exactly $2$ different words, which is strictly fewer than $11$.

Therefore, $5\times 6$ word rectangles can indeed contain fewer than $11$ different words. ∎
