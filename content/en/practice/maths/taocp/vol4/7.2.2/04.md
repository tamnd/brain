**Exercise 4.** [**] [16] Using a chessboard and eight coins to represent queens, one can follow the steps of Algorithm B and essentially traverse the tree of Fig. 68 by hand in about three hours. Invent a trick to save half of the work.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 28s  

---

The $n$ queens problem is invariant under reflection in the vertical axis of the board. If $x_1x_2\ldots x_n$ is a solution, then $(n+1-x_1)(n+1-x_2)\ldots(n+1-x_n)$ is also a solution, and the correspondence is one-to-one. The same symmetry applies to every node of the backtrack tree, because condition (3) is preserved when each column number $x_k$ is replaced by $n+1-x_k$.

Therefore, when $n=8$, it is sufficient to execute Algorithm B only for initial choices

$$
x_1\in\{1,2,3,4\}.
$$

The cases $x_1=5,6,7,8$ are the mirror images of the cases $x_1=4,3,2,1$, respectively, so they need not be explored separately. After all solutions with $x_1\le4$ have been generated, obtain the remaining solutions by reflecting each one in the vertical axis. This reduces the hand search to essentially one half of the original work.
