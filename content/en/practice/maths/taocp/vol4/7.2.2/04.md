[Section 7.2.2: Backtracking](../)

**Exercise 4.** [**] [16] Using a chessboard and eight coins to represent queens, one can follow the steps of Algorithm B and essentially traverse the tree of Fig. 68 by hand in about three hours. Invent a trick to save half of the work.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m11s  

---

A horizontal reflection of the $8 \times 8$ board about its vertical midline preserves the constraint (3), since it maps each column $x_k$ to $9-x_k$ and preserves both equality and diagonal differences $|x_k-x_j|$. Hence every partial placement beginning with $x_1 = c$ has a symmetric counterpart beginning with $x_1 = 9-c$ that generates a mirror copy of the same subtree of the backtrack tree.

During the hand execution of Algorithm B, it suffices to explore only one representative from each symmetric pair of choices for $x_1$, for instance restricting $x_1 \in {1,2,3,4}$. Whenever a placement with $x_1 \in {5,6,7,8}$ would be entered, its mirror is already covered by a previously explored branch, so the corresponding subtree can be skipped entirely. This eliminates exactly half of the level-1 branching and therefore halves the total traversal work of the tree in Fig. 68. ∎
