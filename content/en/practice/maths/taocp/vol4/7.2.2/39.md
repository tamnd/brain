**Exercise 39.** [*18*] $[18]$ Why can't a commafree code of length $(m^4 - m^2)/4$ contain 0001 and 2000?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 7m52s  

---

Let $C$ be a commafree code of length $4$ over an $m$-letter alphabet. Recall the defining property:

A set $C$ of 4-letter words is commafree iff for any $x,y \in C$, no nontrivial overlap between a suffix of $x$ and a prefix of $y$ can produce a valid codeword alignment inside the concatenation $xy$. Equivalently, for $1 \le t \le 3$, no suffix of length $t$ of a codeword in $C$ can equal a prefix of length $t$ of another codeword in $C$, since such an equality would create a codeword starting at a non-boundary position in a concatenation.

### Step 1. Identify the relevant prefix–suffix structure

Consider the two words:

$$
x = 2000, \quad y = 0001.
$$

Compute their length-3 suffix and prefix:

- Suffix of $x$ of length 3:

$$
\operatorname{suf}_3(2000) = 000.
$$

- Prefix of $y$ of length 3:

$$
\operatorname{pre}_3(0001) = 000.
$$

Thus,

$$
\operatorname{suf}_3(2000) = \operatorname{pre}_3(0001).
$$

### Step 2. Show why this violates commafreeness

Assume for contradiction that both $2000$ and $0001$ belong to a commafree code $C$.

Form the concatenation:

$$
2000\,0001.
$$

Because the last three symbols of the first word equal the first three symbols of the second word, the overlap

$$
000
$$

creates an ambiguity in segmentation.

Specifically, in the string

$$
20000001,
$$

the block of 4 symbols starting at position 2 is

$$
0000,
$$

and the block starting at position 3 is

$$
0000,
$$

while more importantly, the overlap equality $000 = 000$ implies that the boundary between the two codewords is not uniquely recoverable from a sliding window of length 4. One can shift the decomposition by one position inside the overlap without changing local 3-prefix/3-suffix consistency, producing a valid alternative parsing window structure.

This is exactly what commafreeness forbids: a codeword boundary must always be identifiable from local length-4 structure, and no overlap of length $1,2,$ or $3$ between a suffix and a prefix of codewords is allowed.

Thus the equality

$$
\operatorname{suf}_3(2000) = \operatorname{pre}_3(0001)
$$

violates the commafree condition.

### Step 3. Conclude

Since the existence of both words forces a forbidden length-3 overlap between a suffix of one codeword and a prefix of another, they cannot simultaneously belong to a commafree code.

Therefore, a commafree code of length $(m^4 - m^2)/4$ cannot contain both $0001$ and $2000$.

$$
\boxed{\text{They cannot both appear because they form a forbidden 3-symbol overlap.}}
$$
