**Exercise 51.** [*M22*] $[M22]$ What's a simple martingale that corresponds to Theorem E?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m31s  

---

## Solution

Let $Q$ denote the total number of solutions generated by the backtracking tree, and let $\mathcal{F}_l$ be the information determined by the random choices made up to level $l$, that is, the partial assignment $(x_1,\ldots,x_l)$ together with all feasibility information implied by $P_l(x_1,\ldots,x_l)$.

Define

$$
M_l = \mathbb{E}(Q \mid \mathcal{F}_l).
$$

The process $(M_l)_{l\ge 0}$ is a martingale with respect to $(\mathcal{F}_l)$. Indeed, since $\mathcal{F}_l \subseteq \mathcal{F}_{l+1}$, the tower property of conditional expectation gives

$$
\mathbb{E}(M_{l+1} \mid \mathcal{F}_l)
= \mathbb{E}(\mathbb{E}(Q \mid \mathcal{F}_{l+1}) \mid \mathcal{F}_l)
= \mathbb{E}(Q \mid \mathcal{F}_l)
= M_l.
$$

Thus $(M_l)$ satisfies the defining martingale condition.

At level $n$, the information $\mathcal{F}_n$ determines a complete assignment $x_1,\ldots,x_n$ consistent with $P_n$. Hence $Q$ is measurable with respect to $\mathcal{F}_n$, and

$$
M_n = \mathbb{E}(Q \mid \mathcal{F}_n) = Q.
$$

At level $0$, no information is available, so

$$
M_0 = \mathbb{E}(Q),
$$

a constant independent of the initial random choices.

This construction corresponds to Theorem E in the sense that the estimator obtained by conditioning on the current node equals the expected total number of completions from that node, and these conditional expectations propagate unchanged along the filtration defined by the backtracking process.

This completes the proof. ∎
