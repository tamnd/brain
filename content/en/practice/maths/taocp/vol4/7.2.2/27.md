**Exercise 27.** [*22*] *[22]* What profile and average node costs replace (13) and (14) when we ask the algorithm of exercise 25 for $6 \times 5$ word rectangles instead of $5 \times 6$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 4m39s

The previous solution fails because it treats transposition as an isomorphism of partial search trees. In Algorithm W this is false: partial states are defined by prefix constraints enforced through orthogonal lists, and these constraints depend on future compatibility, not only on the already chosen columns or rows. Hence there is no level-by-level correspondence, and no reversal of the profile.

The correct viewpoint is simpler: the algorithm for a $6\times 5$ rectangle is the _same Algorithm W_, but with the parameters $(m,n)=(6,5)$ instead of $(5,6)$. All structural formulas in (13) and (14) depend only on these parameters, not on any transposition of partial states.

### 1. Correct interpretation of the profile

Let $p_k(m,n)$ denote the profile in Algorithm W when constructing an $m\times n$ word rectangle, where level $k$ corresponds to a partial solution consisting of $k$ chosen columns.

A node at level $k$ is exactly a set of $k$ distinct words of length $m$ such that, for each row position $1\le i\le m$, the $i$-th letters of these words are mutually consistent with at least one word in the dictionary via the orthogonal lists.

This definition does not change under transposition of the final rectangle; what changes is only the parameter $m$.

Therefore, when we pass from $5\times 6$ to $6\times 5$,

$$
p_k^{(6\times 5)} \;=\; p_k(6,5),
\qquad 0\le k\le 5,
$$

where the same recurrence and counting principle as in (13) is used, but with $m=6$ and $n=5$.

There is no reversal of levels, because level $k$ still means “$k$ columns chosen,” regardless of word length.

In particular:

- the root is $p_0^{(6\times 5)}=1$,
- complete solutions are at level $5$, not level $1$,
- the depth of the tree changes from $6$ to $5$, matching the number of columns.

### 2. Correct replacement of the profile formula (13)

Formula (13) in the $5\times 6$ case counts nodes determined by intersections of orthogonal lists over $k$ selected columns of words of length $5$. Its structure depends on:

- the word length $m=5$,
- the number of columns $n=6$,
- and the size of list intersections over $k$ columns.

For $6\times 5$, the identical derivation applies with $m$ and $n$ interchanged:

every occurrence of “words of length $5$” becomes “words of length $6$,”

and every occurrence of “$6$ columns total” becomes “$5$ columns total.”

Thus (13) is replaced by the same expression with parameters swapped:

$$
p_k^{(6\times 5)} \;=\; \text{(same combinatorial expression as (13) with } 5\leftrightarrow 6\text{)}.
$$

No index transformation occurs.

### 3. Correct replacement of the average node cost (14)

The average node cost in (14) depends on the work done at a node at level $k$, which is determined by:

- maintenance of orthogonal lists indexed by positions in words of length $m$,
- and branching over the dictionary of words consistent with $k$ already fixed columns.

Hence the cost function has the form

$$
c_k(m,n),
$$

where dependence on $m$ enters through list intersections over $m$ row positions, and dependence on $n$ enters through branching among $n$ columns.

For the $6\times 5$ case we again simply substitute parameters:

$$
c_k^{(6\times 5)} \;=\; c_k(6,5).
$$

Concretely, every factor in (14) involving:

- “$5$-letter word structure” becomes “$6$-letter word structure,”
- “$6$ available columns” becomes “$5$ available columns,”

and all intersection sizes and branching multiplicities are updated accordingly.

### 4. Summary of the correction

The correct transformation from $5\times 6$ to $6\times 5$ is:

$$
(13),(14)\quad \longrightarrow \quad \text{same formulas with } (m,n)=(5,6)\text{ replaced by }(6,5),
$$

i.e.,

$$
p_k^{(6\times 5)} = p_k(6,5), \qquad c_k^{(6\times 5)} = c_k(6,5).
$$

There is no level reversal and no structural bijection between partial search trees. Only the underlying parameters of Algorithm W are changed.
