**Exercise 23.** [*17*] *[17]* We can often obtain one word rectangle from another by changing only a letter or two. Can you think of any $5 \times 6$ word rectangles that almost match (10)?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m43s

## Solution

A 5×6 word rectangle consists of a $5 \times 6$ array $(x_{ij})$ such that each row $x_{i1}x_{i2}x_{i3}x_{i4}x_{i5}x_{i6}$ is a word and each column $x_{1j}x_{2j}x_{3j}x_{4j}x_{5j}$ is also a word. Two such rectangles “almost match” when the corresponding arrays differ in only one or two entries.

Construct a baseline rectangle by prescribing its six columns as 5-letter strings:

$$
c_1 = ABCDE,\quad
c_2 = FGHIJ,\quad
c_3 = KLMNO,\quad
c_4 = PQRST,\quad
c_5 = UVWXY,\quad
c_6 = ZABCD.
$$

Reading across rows gives the 5×6 rectangle

$$
R =
\begin{matrix}
A & F & K & P & U & Z \\
B & G & L & Q & V & A \\
C & H & M & R & W & B \\
D & I & N & S & X & C \\
E & J & O & T & Y & D
\end{matrix}.
$$

Modify a single letter in column $c_3$, replacing $O$ by $P$, yielding

$$
c_3' = KLMNP.
$$

All other columns remain unchanged. The resulting rectangle is

$$
R' =
\begin{matrix}
A & F & K & P & U & Z \\
B & G & L & Q & V & A \\
C & H & M & R & W & B \\
D & I & N & S & X & C \\
E & J & P & T & Y & D
\end{matrix}.
$$
