[Section 7.2.2: Backtracking](../)

**Exercise 19.** [**] *[M10]* What are the domains $D_l$ in Langford's problem (7)?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m50s  

---

In Langford’s formulation, the variables are the sequence positions $x_1,x_2,\ldots,x_{2n}$, and each $x_j$ is required to be a permutation element of the multiset ${-1,-2,\ldots,-n,1,2,\ldots,n}$. Thus the universal set is

$U=\{-n,\ldots,-1,1,\ldots,n\}.$

At level $l$ of Algorithm B, the value $x_l$ may be any element of $U$ that has not already been used in positions $1,\ldots,l-1$. Therefore the domain is

$D_l = U \setminus \{x_1,x_2,\ldots,x_{l-1}\}, \qquad 1 \le l \le 2n.$

Each domain is thus obtained by deleting from $U$ the symbols already assigned earlier in the construction of the partial permutation.
