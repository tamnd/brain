[Section 7.2.2: Backtracking](../)

**Exercise 3.** [**] [20] Let $T$ be any tree. Is it possible to define domains $D_k$ and cutoff properties $P_l(x_1, \ldots, x_l)$ so that $T$ is the backtrack tree traversed by Algorithm B?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m59s  

---

Yes.

Root the tree $T$ at an arbitrary node $r$. For each $k \ge 1$, let $D_k$ be the set of vertices of $T$ at depth $k$ (distance $k$ from $r$). For a sequence $x_1, \ldots, x_l$ with $x_k \in D_k$, define $P_l(x_1, \ldots, x_l)$ to hold iff $x_1$ is a child of $r$ and, for every $1 \le j < l$, the vertex $x_{j+1}$ is a child of $x_j$ in $T$.

Then every prefix satisfying $P_l$ corresponds to a unique simple path in $T$ starting at $r$, and every such path is generated by the conditions on parent-child adjacency. Conversely, any violation of adjacency causes failure of $P_l$ at the first index where the edge is not present, so no sequence outside the root-to-node paths can be extended further in Algorithm B. Hence the backtrack tree consists exactly of the root-to-node paths in $T$, and is isomorphic to $T$ (with the root corresponding to level $0$). This completes the construction. ∎
