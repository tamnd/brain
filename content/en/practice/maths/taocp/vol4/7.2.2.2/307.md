Prove that any sequence $(N_1, N_2, \ldots)$ must have $\mathop{\rm E} X \ge \frac{1}{n} \bar{m} H_n - \frac{1}{2n} = \frac{1}{n}[H_1 + \cdots + H_n] - \frac{1}{2n}$ on at least one of those $n$ inputs. *Consequence*: the smallest $N^*$ is best, for each $m$, the probability is $\ge \frac{1}{2}$ that $t$ trial runs suffice; show that $\ge n/(2m)$ of $\{N_1, \ldots, N_t\}$ are $\ge m$.
