[Section 7.2.2.2: Satisfiability](../)

**Exercise 187.** [*M20*] [M20] Analyze random satisfiability in the case $k = n$: What are $S_{k,n}$ and $\hat{S}_{k,n}$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m12s

## Solution

For $k=n$, every clause contains all $n$ variables, with one choice of polarity for each variable. Hence the possible clauses are in one-to-one correspondence with the $2^n$ binary assignments to $x_1,\ldots,x_n$.

Fix an assignment $a=(a_1,\ldots,a_n)$. Among the $2^n$ possible $n$-clauses, exactly one clause is false under $a$, namely the clause whose literals are the complements of the values in $a$. Consequently, a family of $n$-clauses is unsatisfiable if and only if it contains every possible $n$-clause. If even one of the $2^n$ clauses is absent, the assignment falsifying only that missing clause satisfies all clauses that are present.

In the model without repeated clauses, $m$ clauses are chosen uniformly from the $2^n$ possible clauses. The formula is unsatisfiable only when

$m=2^n.$

For every

$0\le m<2^n,$

the formula is satisfiable. Thus

$S_{n,n}(m)=1 \qquad (0\le m<2^n),$

and

$S_{n,n}(2^n)=0.$

In the model with replacement, the only unsatisfiable outcome is that all $2^n$ possible clauses have appeared among the $m$ independent choices. Inclusion-exclusion gives the probability of this event:

$$
\Pr(\text{unsatisfiable})
=
\frac{1}{(2^n)^m}
\sum_{j=0}^{2^n}(-1)^j
\binom{2^n}{j}(2^n-j)^m .
$$

The term $j=0$ counts all sequences of $m$ clauses. For a fixed set of $j$ clauses excluded, $(2^n-j)^m$ sequences remain. Inclusion-exclusion over the excluded clauses gives the displayed expression.

Therefore the satisfiability probability in the with-replacement model is

$$
\hat S_{n,n}(m)
=
1-
\frac{1}{2^{nm}}
\sum_{j=0}^{2^n}(-1)^j
\binom{2^n}{j}(2^n-j)^m .
$$

Equivalently, the stopping time is the coupon-collector time for the $2^n$ possible clauses, because unsatisfiability occurs precisely when the last missing clause has been collected.

Hence the two random satisfiability functions in the case $k=n$ are

$$
\boxed{
S_{n,n}(m)=
\begin{cases}
1,&0\le m<2^n,\\
0,&m=2^n,
\end{cases}
}
$$

and

$$
\boxed{
\hat S_{n,n}(m)
=
1-
\frac{1}{2^{nm}}
\sum_{j=0}^{2^n}(-1)^j
\binom{2^n}{j}(2^n-j)^m .
}
$$
