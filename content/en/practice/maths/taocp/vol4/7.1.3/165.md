## Solution

Let the cellular configuration at time $t$ be an $m \times n$ Boolean array $X(t)$, encoded in memory as a word-array so that each cell corresponds to one bit. For a cell $(i,j)$, let $X_{i,j}(t)\in{0,1}$ denote its value. The given rule is

$$
X(t+1)=\sim X(t)\ \&\ (X_N(t)\mid X_W(t)\mid X_E(t)\mid X_S(t)),
$$

where each subscript denotes the configuration shifted one step in the indicated direction.

The definition means that a cell becomes $1$ at time $t+1$ if and only if it is $0$ at time $t$ and at least one of its four orthogonal neighbors is $1$ at time $t$.

To compute this rule in bitwise parallel form, define four shifted versions of $X(t)$:

The northern shift $X_N(t)$ is the array with

$$
(X_N(t))_{i,j} = X_{i+1,j}(t),
$$

the western shift $X_W(t)$ is

$$
(X_W(t))_{i,j} = X_{i,j+1}(t),
$$

the eastern shift $X_E(t)$ is

$$
(X_E(t))_{i,j} = X_{i,j-1}(t),
$$

and the southern shift $X_S(t)$ is

$$
(X_S(t))_{i,j} = X_{i-1,j}(t),
$$

with boundary conditions interpreted as zero outside the grid.

Under a standard packed representation in which each row is a binary word and adjacent rows are stored consecutively, these shifts correspond to word-level operations: vertical shifts are implemented by moving whole words, and horizontal shifts are implemented by $\ll 1$ and $\gg 1$ together with masking of boundary bits.

Define

$$
Y(t)=X_N(t)\mid X_W(t)\mid X_E(t)\mid X_S(t).
$$

Then the update rule becomes

$$
X(t+1)=\sim X(t)\ \&\ Y(t).
$$

The computation of $Y(t)$ uses three binary operations at word level, namely two applications of $\mid$ to combine four operands. The construction of $X(t+1)$ from $X(t)$ and $Y(t)$ uses one bitwise complement and one bitwise AND.

Thus each update step consists of the following sequence of word operations:

First compute the four shifted configurations $X_N(t), X_W(t), X_E(t), X_S(t)$ by shifts and row moves. Next form $Y(t)$ by combining them using two applications of $\mid$. Finally compute $X(t+1)$ by applying one bitwise complement to $X(t)$ and one bitwise AND with $Y(t)$.

Since each operation acts on all bits of a word simultaneously, the update of the entire $m \times n$ configuration is performed in constant word-operation depth independent of $m$ and $n$.

This completes the computation of the cellular automaton rule in terms of enriched bitwise arithmetic. ∎
