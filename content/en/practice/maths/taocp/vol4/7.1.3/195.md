**Exercise 195.** &#9654; [*HM25*] [HM25] Let A be a binary matrix having rows
α1, . . . , αm of length n.
Explain how to use bitwise operations to compute the
rank m −r of A over the binary field {0, 1}, and to find linearly independent binary
vectors θ1, . . . , θr of length m such that θjA = 0 . . . 0 for 1 ≤j ≤r. Hint: See the
“triangularization” algorithm for null spaces, Algorithm 4.6.2N.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m15s  

---

## Solution

Represent each row $\alpha_i$ of $A$ as an $n$-bit integer, so that addition over the field ${0,1}$ is implemented by bitwise exclusive-or $\oplus$ and scalar multiplication is trivial since the only scalars are $0$ and $1$. The system is then manipulated by elementary row operations over $\mathrm{GF}(2)$: swapping two rows and replacing a row $x$ by $x \oplus y$.

Maintain two synchronized arrays. The first array stores the current rows $a_1,\ldots,a_m$, initially $a_i=\alpha_i$. The second array stores $m$-bit vectors $\theta_1,\ldots,\theta_m$, initially $\theta_i$ is the $i$-th unit vector in ${0,1}^m$. Every row operation applied to the array $a_i$ is applied identically to the corresponding $\theta_i$; this invariant ensures that at every stage each current row satisfies

$a_i = \theta_i A.$

Gaussian elimination over $\mathrm{GF}(2)$ is performed column by column. Let the current pivot row index be $i=1$. For each column $k$ from $1$ to $n$, a pivot row $p \ge i$ is sought such that the $k$-th bit of $a_p$ equals $1$. If no such $p$ exists, the column is skipped. If such a $p$ exists, rows $a_i$ and $a_p$ are swapped, and simultaneously $\theta_i$ and $\theta_p$ are swapped. Then every row $j \ne i$ with a $1$ in column $k$ is replaced by $a_j \leftarrow a_j \oplus a_i$ and $\theta_j \leftarrow \theta_j \oplus \theta_i$. After this operation, all rows below and above the pivot row have a $0$ in column $k$, and the pivot row has a $1$ in column $k$. The index $i$ is then increased by $1$.

When no further pivot rows can be selected, the process terminates. Let $r$ denote the number of pivot rows created. The rank of $A$ over ${0,1}$ equals $r$ because each pivot introduces a linearly independent constraint over $\mathrm{GF}(2)$ and all row operations are invertible, hence preserve the row space dimension.

For each row $a_i$ that becomes the zero vector during elimination, the corresponding vector $\theta_i$ satisfies $\theta_i A = a_i = 0\cdots 0$, so each such $\theta_i$ lies in the left nullspace of $A$. Distinct zero rows yield distinct $\theta_i$, and linear independence follows because if a linear combination over $\mathrm{GF}(2)$ of such $\theta_i$ were zero, applying the same combination to the invariant identity $a_i=\theta_i A$ would produce a nontrivial linear dependence among zero rows, which is impossible in a row-echelon form where elimination steps are invertible and zero rows remain zero under all subsequent operations.

The number of zero rows equals $m-r$, since exactly $r$ rows remain nonzero in echelon form and all other rows are reduced to zero during elimination. Therefore the set of all $\theta_i$ corresponding to zero rows forms a basis of the solution space of $\theta A = 0\cdots 0$, and this space has dimension $m-r$.

The algorithm thus produces both the rank of $A$ and a basis $\theta_1,\ldots,\theta_{m-r}$ of the left nullspace, using only bitwise operations on $n$-bit words and XOR-based row operations over $\mathrm{GF}(2)$.

This completes the proof. ∎
