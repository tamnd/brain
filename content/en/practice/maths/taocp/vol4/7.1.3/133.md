**Exercise 133.** &#9654; [*20*] [20] Given a graph G, how can the algorithm of exercise 132 be used to find
(a) all maximal independent sets of vertices? (b) all minimal vertex covers (sets that
hit every edge)?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 51s  

---

## Solution

Let $G = (V, E)$ with $V = {0,1,\dots,n-1}$. In exercise 132, a clique in $G$ is characterized via the bitwise construction using

$$
\rho_v = \sum_{u \in V,\; (u=v \;\vee\; u-v)} 2^u,\qquad 
\delta_v = \sum_{u \ne v} 2^u = 2^n - 1 - 2^v,
$$

and intersections of the form

$$
\tau(U) = \bigwedge_{0 \le u < n} (u \in U ? \rho_u : \delta_u).
$$

### (a) Maximal independent sets

A set $I \subseteq V$ is an independent set in $G$ if and only if it is a clique in the complement graph $\overline{G}$, since

$$
u-v \notin E(G) \;\;\Longleftrightarrow\;\; u-v \in E(\overline{G}).
$$

Hence maximal independent sets of $G$ are exactly maximal cliques of $\overline{G}$.

To apply exercise 132, it suffices to construct the reflexive adjacency masks of $\overline{G}$. For each vertex $v$, let $\rho^{\ast}_v$ denote the reflexive adjacency mask in $\overline{G}$. A vertex $u \ne v$ belongs to $\rho^{\ast}_v$ if and only if $u-v \notin E(G)$. Therefore

$$
\rho^{\ast}_v \setminus \{2^v\} = \delta_v - (\rho_v - 2^v),
$$

since $\delta_v$ contains all vertices except $v$, while $\rho_v - 2^v$ contains exactly the neighbors of $v$ in $G$ excluding $v$. Adding the reflexive term gives

$$
\rho^{\ast}_v = 2^v \;+\; \bigl(\delta_v \,\&\, \overline{\rho_v - 2^v}\bigr).
$$

Substituting $\rho^{\ast}_v$ for $\rho_v$ in the construction of exercise 132 yields all maximal cliques of $\overline{G}$, hence all maximal independent sets of $G$.

### (b) Minimal vertex covers

A set $C \subseteq V$ is a vertex cover if every edge of $G$ has at least one endpoint in $C$. Equivalently, $V \setminus C$ contains no edge, so $V \setminus C$ is an independent set.

Let $I = V \setminus C$. Then

$$
C \text{ is a minimal vertex cover} \;\;\Longleftrightarrow\;\; I \text{ is a maximal independent set}.
$$

If $I$ were not maximal independent, there exists $v \notin I$ such that $I \cup {v}$ is independent. Then $C \setminus {v}$ is still a vertex cover, contradicting minimality of $C$. Conversely, if $I$ is maximal independent, then removing any vertex from $C$ enlarges $I$, destroying independence, so $C$ is minimal.

Thus all minimal vertex covers are obtained by taking complements of maximal independent sets computed in part (a).

This completes the solution. ∎
