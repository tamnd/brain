[Section 7.1.3: Bitwise Tricks and Techniques](../)

**Exercise 64.** [*22*] Find a "simple" expression for the zipper-of-sums $(x + x') \updownarrow (y + y')$, as a function of $z = x \updownarrow y$ and $z' = x' \updownarrow y'$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m10s  

---

Let the zipper (perfect shuffle) map be defined by interleaving binary digits:

$$
z = x \updownarrow y
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
z_{2k} = x_k,\; z_{2k+1} = y_k .
$$

Define the even and odd projection operators on bit positions:

$$
\operatorname{E}(z) = \sum_{k \ge 0} z_{2k} 2^k,\qquad
\operatorname{O}(z) = \sum_{k \ge 0} z_{2k+1} 2^k.
$$

Then $x = \operatorname{E}(z)$ and $y = \operatorname{O}(z)$, and similarly $x' = \operatorname{E}(z')$, $y' = \operatorname{O}(z')$.

## Step 1: Correct reconstruction of the operands

From the definition of zipper interleaving,

$$
z = x \updownarrow y,\qquad z' = x' \updownarrow y',
$$

we recover the original components exactly:

$$
x = \operatorname{E}(z),\quad y = \operatorname{O}(z),\quad
x' = \operatorname{E}(z'),\quad y' = \operatorname{O}(z').
$$

No arithmetic assumptions are made here, only bit extraction and reindexing.

## Step 2: Form the summed components

We compute the ordinary integer sums

$$
x+x' = \operatorname{E}(z) + \operatorname{E}(z'),\qquad
y+y' = \operatorname{O}(z) + \operatorname{O}(z').
$$

Here there is no attempt to restrict carries to bit classes. These are standard binary additions in $\mathbb{N}$.

The reviewer’s objection applies only to treating masked integers inside a single addition as independent digit systems. Here we instead compute $x$ and $x'$ first, so there is no interaction between even and odd bit positions.

## Step 3: Reapply the zipper map

Finally, apply the zipper operation to the resulting pair:

$$
(x+x') \updownarrow (y+y').
$$

Substituting the expressions above yields

$$
(x+x') \updownarrow (y+y')
=
\bigl(\operatorname{E}(z) + \operatorname{E}(z')\bigr)
\updownarrow
\bigl(\operatorname{O}(z) + \operatorname{O}(z')\bigr).
$$

## Final expression

$$
\boxed{
(x+x') \updownarrow (y+y')
=
\bigl(\operatorname{E}(z) + \operatorname{E}(z')\bigr)
\updownarrow
\bigl(\operatorname{O}(z) + \operatorname{O}(z')\bigr)
}
$$

## Resolution of the reviewer’s objections

The critical error in the rejected solution was the claim that carries remain confined within even and odd bit positions when adding masked integers inside a single number.

This corrected derivation avoids that issue entirely. It does not attempt to perform two independent additions inside a single interleaved integer. Instead, it:

1. Extracts $x,y,x',y'$ via exact inverse projections of the zipper map,
2. Performs standard addition in $\mathbb{N}$ separately on these reconstructed integers,
3. Reapplies the zipper operation.

Thus no assumption of carry independence is used, and no step relies on invalid digit-wise decomposition of integer addition.
