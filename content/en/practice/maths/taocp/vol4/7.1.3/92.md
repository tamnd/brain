**Exercise 92.** &#9654; [*21*] [21] What happens if the second line of (88) is changed to $z \leftarrow (x \mid y) - z$"?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m22s  

---

## Solution

Let the original recipe (88) be applied bytewise, so for each byte position $j$ we write $a = x_j$, $b = y_j$. The construction in (88) is

$$
t \leftarrow x \oplus y,
\qquad
z \leftarrow (x \,\&\, y) + (t \gg 1),
$$

so that for each byte

$$
z_j = \left\lfloor \frac{a+b}{2} \right\rfloor.
$$

The modified rule replaces the second line by

$$
z \leftarrow (x \mid y) - z.
$$

For each byte,

$$
z'_j = (a \mid b) - \left\lfloor \frac{a+b}{2} \right\rfloor.
$$

Using the identity (7.1.3–(6) in bitwise form, applied within each byte)

$$
a \mid b = a + b - (a \,\&\, b),
$$

we obtain

$$
z'_j = a + b - (a \,\&\, b) - \left\lfloor \frac{a+b}{2} \right\rfloor.
$$

Let $s = a+b$ and write $s = 2q + \varepsilon$ with $\varepsilon \in {0,1}$. Then $\lfloor s/2 \rfloor = q$, hence

$$
z'_j = (2q + \varepsilon) - (a \,\&\, b) - q = q + \varepsilon - (a \,\&\, b).
$$

Therefore

$$
z'_j =
\begin{cases}
\frac{a+b}{2} - (a \,\&\, b), & a+b \text{ even},\\[6pt]
\frac{a+b+1}{2} - (a \,\&\, b), & a+b \text{ odd}.
\end{cases}
$$

Equivalently,

$$
z'_j = \left\lceil \frac{a+b}{2} \right\rceil - (a \,\&\, b).
$$

Thus the modified operation replaces the bytewise average (rounded down) by the bytewise average (rounded up) and then subtracts the bytewise intersection mask $a ,&, b$. Each byte of the result measures the excess of the bitwise union over the midpoint of the arithmetic sum. This completes the analysis. ∎
