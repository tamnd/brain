**Exercise 18.** [*M25*] The curious pixel pattern shown here has $(x^2 y \gg 11) \& 1$ in row $x$ and column $y$, for $1 \le x, y \le 256$. Is there any simple way to explain some of its major characteristics mathematically?

## Solution

Let

$f(x,y) = \bigl((x^2 y \gg 11)\bigr)\,\&\,1,$

for $1 \le x,y \le 256$. This extracts the $11$th binary digit (counting from $2^0$) of the integer $x^2 y$, equivalently the coefficient of $2^{11}$ in the binary expansion of $x^2 y$.

Write the binary decomposition of $x$ as

$x = 2^a u,$

where $u$ is odd and $a = \nu_2(x)$. Then

$x^2 = 2^{2a} u^2,$

so

$x^2 y = 2^{2a}(u^2 y).$

The function becomes

$f(x,y) = \bigl((u^2 y \gg (11 - 2a))\bigr)\,\&\,1$

when $2a \le 11$, since shifting right by $11$ after multiplying by $2^{2a}$ is equivalent to shifting $u^2 y$ by $11-2a$. When $2a \ge 12$, the factor $2^{2a}$ contributes at least $2^{12}$, so $x^2 y$ is divisible by $2^{12}$ for all $y$, hence the $11$th bit is always $0$, giving

$f(x,y) = 0.$

Thus all rows with $x \ge 64$ (since $2^6 = 64$) are identically zero, producing a sharp horizontal cutoff in the bitmap.

For $2a \le 11$, fix $x$. The function depends only on $u^2 y$ shifted by a constant amount, so as a function of $y$ it is periodic with period dividing a power of two determined by $11 - 2a$. More precisely, multiplying $y$ by $u^2$ permutes residues modulo powers of two, since $u^2$ is odd and hence invertible modulo $2^k$ for every $k$. Therefore the map

$y \mapsto (u^2 y \gg (11 - 2a)) \,\&\, 1$

is a linear bit-extraction on $y$ with dyadic period $2^{12-2a}$, truncated by the fixed bit position $11$.

As $a$ increases by $1$, the exponent $11 - 2a$ decreases by $2$, so the effective scale in the $y$-direction halves every time $x$ is multiplied by $2$. This produces nested self-similar bands: low $2$-adic valuation of $x$ yields slowly varying horizontal patterns, while higher valuation compresses the variation in $y$ until it vanishes entirely once $x$ is divisible by $64$.

The dependence on $u^2$ mod powers of two introduces structured irregularity within each band. Since squaring an odd number preserves invertibility modulo $2^k$ and distributes residues in a deterministic way, the patterns for fixed $a$ are not random but reindexed versions of a base dyadic pattern.

The major visible characteristics are therefore a staircase of horizontal regimes determined by powers of two in $x$, complete extinction of the pattern for $x \ge 64$, and self-similar dyadic repetition in $y$ within each regime, with resolution doubling whenever $x$ is halved. This explains the block structure and scaling symmetries of the image. ∎
