**Exercise 52.** [*22*] Find hexadecimal constants $(\theta_0, \theta_1, \theta_2, \theta_3, \theta_4, \theta_5, \tilde{\theta}_4, \tilde{\theta}_3, \tilde{\theta}_2, \tilde{\theta}_1, \tilde{\theta}_0)$ that change (71) to produce the following important 64-bit permutations, based on the binary representation $j = (j_5 j_4 j_3 j_2 j_1 j_0)_2$: (a) $j\pi = (j_0 j_1 j_2 j_3 j_4 j_5)_2$; (b) $j\pi = (j_3 j_1 j_4 j_2 j_5 j_0)_2$; (c) $j\pi = (j_1 j_0 j_3 j_2 j_5 j_4)_2$; (d) $j\pi = (j_0 j_5 j_2 j_3 j_4 j_1)_2$. [Case (a) is the "perfect shuffle" (175) that takes $(x_{63}\ldots x_{33}x_{32}x_{31}\ldots x_1x_0)_2$ into $(x_{63}x_{31}\ldots x_{33}x_1x_{32}x_0)_2$; case (b) transposes an $8 \times 8$ matrix of bits; case (c), similarly, transposes a $4 \times 16$ matrix; and case (d) arises in connection with "fast Fourier transforms," see exercise 4.6.4–14.]

Scheme (71) uses 64-bit control words in which bit position $j$ selects, at stage $k$, whether the $k$-th address bit of the datum located at index $j$ is taken from the “upper” or “lower” branch of the interconnection pattern. For a pure bit permutation, each output bit is equal to exactly one input bit, so each stage control word is one of the fixed hierarchical masks

$$
(\mu_{6,0},\mu_{6,1},\mu_{6,2},\mu_{6,3},\mu_{6,4},\mu_{6,5})
=
(\texttt{AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA},\texttt{CCCCCCCCCCCCCCCC},\texttt{F0F0F0F0F0F0F0F0},
\texttt{FF00FF00FF00FF00},\texttt{FFFF0000FFFF0000},\texttt{FFFFFFFF00000000}).
$$

and $\tilde{\theta}_k=\overline{\theta_k}$.

For a permutation $j\pi=(j_{\pi(5)}\,j_{\pi(4)}\,j_{\pi(3)}\,j_{\pi(2)}\,j_{\pi(1)}\,j_{\pi(0)})$, define the induced bit-source map $\sigma$ by

$$
\text{output bit }k = \text{input bit }\sigma(k).
$$

Then scheme (71) requires

$$
\theta_k=\mu_{6,\sigma(k)}, \qquad \tilde{\theta}_k=\overline{\theta_k}.
$$

## (a) $j\pi=(j_0\,j_1\,j_2\,j_3\,j_4\,j_5)$

Here $\sigma(k)=5-k$. Hence

$$
(\sigma(5),\sigma(4),\sigma(3),\sigma(2),\sigma(1),\sigma(0))=(0,1,2,3,4,5).
$$

Therefore

$$
\begin{aligned}
\theta_0&=\mu_{6,5}=\texttt{FFFFFFFF00000000}\\
\theta_1&=\mu_{6,4}=\texttt{FFFF0000FFFF0000}\\
\theta_2&=\mu_{6,3}=\texttt{FF00FF00FF00FF00}\\
\theta_3&=\mu_{6,2}=\texttt{F0F0F0F0F0F0F0F0}\\
\theta_4&=\mu_{6,1}=\texttt{CCCCCCCCCCCCCCCC}\\
\theta_5&=\mu_{6,0}=\texttt{AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA}
\end{aligned}
$$

## (b) $j\pi=(j_3\,j_1\,j_4\,j_2\,j_5\,j_0)$

Thus

$$
\sigma(5)=3,\;\sigma(4)=1,\;\sigma(3)=4,\;\sigma(2)=2,\;\sigma(1)=5,\;\sigma(0)=0.
$$

So

$$
\begin{aligned}
\theta_0&=\mu_{6,0}=\texttt{AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA}\\
\theta_1&=\mu_{6,5}=\texttt{FFFFFFFF00000000}\\
\theta_2&=\mu_{6,2}=\texttt{F0F0F0F0F0F0F0F0}\\
\theta_3&=\mu_{6,4}=\texttt{FFFF0000FFFF0000}\\
\theta_4&=\mu_{6,1}=\texttt{CCCCCCCCCCCCCCCC}\\
\theta_5&=\mu_{6,3}=\texttt{FF00FF00FF00FF00}
\end{aligned}
$$

## (c) $j\pi=(j_1\,j_0\,j_3\,j_2\,j_5\,j_4)$

Thus

$$
\sigma(5)=1,\;\sigma(4)=0,\;\sigma(3)=3,\;\sigma(2)=2,\;\sigma(1)=5,\;\sigma(0)=4.
$$

Hence

$$
\begin{aligned}
\theta_0&=\mu_{6,4}=\texttt{FFFF0000FFFF0000}\\
\theta_1&=\mu_{6,5}=\texttt{FFFFFFFF00000000}\\
\theta_2&=\mu_{6,2}=\texttt{F0F0F0F0F0F0F0F0}\\
\theta_3&=\mu_{6,3}=\texttt{FF00FF00FF00FF00}\\
\theta_4&=\mu_{6,0}=\texttt{AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA}\\
\theta_5&=\mu_{6,1}=\texttt{CCCCCCCCCCCCCCCC}
\end{aligned}
$$

## (d) $j\pi=(j_0\,j_5\,j_2\,j_3\,j_4\,j_1)$

Thus

$$
\sigma(5)=0,\;\sigma(4)=5,\;\sigma(3)=2,\;\sigma(2)=3,\;\sigma(1)=4,\;\sigma(0)=1.
$$

Therefore

$$
\begin{aligned}
\theta_0&=\mu_{6,1}=\texttt{CCCCCCCCCCCCCCCC}\\
\theta_1&=\mu_{6,4}=\texttt{FFFF0000FFFF0000}\\
\theta_2&=\mu_{6,3}=\texttt{FF00FF00FF00FF00}\\
\theta_3&=\mu_{6,2}=\texttt{F0F0F0F0F0F0F0F0}\\
\theta_4&=\mu_{6,5}=\texttt{FFFFFFFF00000000}\\
\theta_5&=\mu_{6,0}=\texttt{AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA}
\end{aligned}
$$

## Complements

For all cases,

$$
\tilde{\theta}_k = \texttt{FFFFFFFFFFFFFFFF} \oplus \theta_k,
$$

giving the corresponding complements of the listed constants.

This fully specifies the required hexadecimal control constants for scheme (71) in all four permutations.
