[Section 7.1.3: Bitwise Tricks and Techniques](../)

**Exercise 200.** [*20*] [20] Interpret the contents of register $3 after the following three MMIX instruc-
tions have been executed: MOR $1,$0,#94; MXOR $2,$0,#94; SUBU $3,$1,$2.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 53s  

---

## Solution

Let $MOR$ denote bitwise OR with a constant mask and $MXOR$ denote bitwise exclusive-or with a constant mask, applied to the contents of the specified register and the immediate hexadecimal constant.

The initial state has $r_0 = 0$ in register $0$.

After the first instruction

$$
\text{MOR } \$1,\$0,\#94,
$$

register $1$ receives

$$
r_1 = r_0 \mid \#94.
$$

Since $r_0 = 0$, identity (10) gives $0 \mid x = x$, hence

$$
r_1 = \#94.
$$

After the second instruction

$$
\text{MXOR } \$2,\$0,\#94,
$$

register $2$ receives

$$
r_2 = r_0 \oplus \#94.
$$

Using identity (10), $0 \oplus x = x$, hence

$$
r_2 = \#94.
$$

After the third instruction

$$
\text{SUBU } \$3,\$1,\$2,
$$

register $3$ receives the unsigned difference

$$
r_3 \equiv r_1 - r_2 \pmod{2^{64}}.
$$

Since $r_1 = r_2 = #94$, ordinary integer subtraction yields $0$, hence the residue modulo $2^{64}$ is also $0$:

$$
r_3 = 0.
$$

Therefore the contents of register $3$ after execution of the three instructions is

$$
\boxed{0}.
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
