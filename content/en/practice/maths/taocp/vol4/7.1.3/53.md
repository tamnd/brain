**Exercise 53.** &#9654; [*M25*] The permutations in exercise 52 are said to be "induced by a permutation of index digits," because we obtain $j\pi$ by permuting the binary digits of $j$. Suppose $j\pi = (j_{(d-1)\nu}\ldots j_{1\nu}j_{0\nu})_2$, where $\upsilon$ is a permutation of $\{0, 1, \ldots, d-1\}$. Prove that if $\upsilon$ has $t$ cycles, the $2^d$-bit permutation $x \mapsto x^*$ can be obtained with only $d - t$ swaps. In particular, show that this observation speeds up all four cases of exercise 52.

## Solution

Let $\upsilon$ be a permutation of ${0,1,\ldots,d-1}$ with $t$ cycles. Write the cycles of $\upsilon$ as

$$
C_1, C_2, \ldots, C_t,
$$

where each $C_i$ has the form

$$
C_i = (a_{i,0}\ a_{i,1}\ \ldots\ a_{i,k_i-1}),
\quad k_i \ge 1,
$$

and $\sum_{i=1}^t k_i = d$.

The permutation of indices $j \mapsto j\pi$ is defined by permuting binary digits,

$$
j = (j_{d-1}\ldots j_1 j_0)_2
\quad \mapsto \quad
j\pi = (j_{(d-1)\upsilon}\ldots j_{1\upsilon} j_{0\upsilon})_2.
$$

Thus $\upsilon$ permutes coordinate positions in binary representations of indices in ${0,1}^d$, and $\pi$ is the induced permutation on the set of $2^d$ indices.

A swap of coordinates $a$ and $b$ is the operation that transforms every index

$$
(j_{d-1}\ldots j_a \ldots j_b \ldots j_0)_2
$$

into

$$
(j_{d-1}\ldots j_b \ldots j_a \ldots j_0)_2.
$$

This operation is a single transposition on the set ${0,1}^d$ of all $2^d$ indices.

Fix a cycle $C = (a_0\ a_1\ \ldots\ a_{k-1})$. Consider the sequence of coordinate swaps

$$
(a_0,a_1), (a_0,a_2), \ldots, (a_0,a_{k-1}).
$$

Each swap exchanges the corresponding binary digit positions in every index simultaneously.

After the first swap $(a_0,a_1)$, positions $a_0$ and $a_1$ are interchanged in all indices. After the second swap $(a_0,a_2)$, the digit originally in position $a_2$ is moved into position $a_0$, while the previous content of $a_0$ is moved into $a_2$. Continuing this process, after executing all swaps $(a_0,a_i)$ for $1 \le i \le k-1$, the digit initially at position $a_0$ moves to $a_1$, the digit at $a_1$ moves to $a_2$, and so on, while the digit at $a_{k-1}$ moves to $a_0$. This realizes the cyclic permutation

$$
a_0 \mapsto a_1 \mapsto \cdots \mapsto a_{k-1} \mapsto a_0.
$$

Hence the cycle $C$ is implemented by exactly $k-1$ coordinate swaps.

Applying this construction independently to each cycle $C_i$ yields a realization of $\upsilon$ as a composition of coordinate swaps. The total number of swaps used is

$$
\sum_{i=1}^t (k_i - 1)
= \left(\sum_{i=1}^t k_i\right) - t
= d - t.
$$

Since each coordinate swap induces the corresponding transposition on the $2^d$-bit indexed word, the induced permutation $x \mapsto x^*$ is obtained using exactly $d - t$ swaps.
