[Section 7.1.3: Bitwise Tricks and Techniques](../)

**Exercise 4.** &#9654; [*M16*] Let $x^C = \bar{x}$, $x^N = -x$, $x^P = x + 1$, and $x^P = x - 1$ denote the complement, the negative, the successor, and the predecessor of an infinite-precision integer $x$. Then we have $x^{CC} = x^{NN} = x^{SP} = x^{PS} = x$. What are $x^{CN}$ and $x^{NC}$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 39s  

---

## Solution

By equation (16),

$$
-x=\bar{x}+1.
$$

With the notation of the exercise,

$$
x^C=\bar x,\qquad x^N=-x,\qquad x^P=x+1,\qquad x^S=x-1.
$$

For the composition $CN$,

$$
x^{CN}=(x^C)^N=-(\bar x).
$$

Applying equation (16) with $x$ replaced by $\bar x$,

$$
-\bar x=\overline{\bar x}+1=x+1=x^P.
$$

Hence

$$
\boxed{x^{CN}=x^P=x+1.}
$$

For the composition $NC$,

$$
x^{NC}=(x^N)^C=\overline{-x}.
$$

By equation (17),

$$
-x=\overline{x-1},
$$

therefore

$$
\overline{-x}=\overline{\overline{x-1}}=x-1=x^S.
$$

Hence

$$
\boxed{x^{NC}=x^S=x-1.}
$$

Thus the missing compositions are

$$
\boxed{x^{CN}=x^P,\qquad x^{NC}=x^S.}
$$
