**Exercise 215.** &#9654; [*21*] [21] What’s a quick way for MMIX to test if a 64-bit number is a multiple of 3?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m14s  

---

## Solution

Since

$$
2 \equiv -1 \pmod 3,
$$

every power of two satisfies

$$
2^k \equiv (-1)^k \pmod 3.
$$

Hence a 64-bit integer

$$
x=\sum_{k=0}^{63}x_k2^k,\qquad x_k\in\{0,1\},
$$

satisfies

$$
x\equiv
\sum_{k\text{ even}}x_k-
\sum_{k\text{ odd}}x_k
\pmod3.
$$

Therefore $x$ is divisible by $3$ if and only if the numbers of $1$-bits in the even and odd bit positions are congruent modulo $3$.

MMIX provides the instruction `SADD`, which counts the $1$-bits of a masked operand. Let

$$
E=\mathtt{0x5555555555555555},
\qquad
O=\mathtt{0xAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA}.
$$

Then

- `SADD $1,$0,E` computes the number of $1$-bits in the even positions;
- `SADD $2,$0,O` computes the number of $1$-bits in the odd positions.

Both counts lie between $0$ and $32$. Their difference therefore lies between $-32$ and $32$. Reducing this small integer modulo $3$ determines whether the original 64-bit integer is a multiple of $3$. Thus the test is obtained by

$$
c_e=\operatorname{popcount}(x\mathbin{\&}E),\qquad
c_o=\operatorname{popcount}(x\mathbin{\&}O),
$$

followed by checking whether

$$
c_e-c_o\equiv0\pmod3.
$$

The reduction modulo $3$ is inexpensive because the argument is confined to the interval $[-32,32]$.

Therefore a fast MMIX test for divisibility by $3$ is to count the $1$-bits in the even and odd positions separately with two `SADD` instructions, subtract the counts, and test whether the result is congruent to $0$ modulo $3$.

$$
\boxed{\text{A 64-bit integer is a multiple of }3\iff
\operatorname{popcount}(x\mathbin{\&}0x5555555555555555)
\equiv
\operatorname{popcount}(x\mathbin{\&}0xAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA)
\pmod3.}
$$
