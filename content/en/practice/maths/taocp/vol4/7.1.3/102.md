**Exercise 102.** [*25*] Discuss routines for the addition and subtraction of polynomials modulo 5, when (a) 16 4-bit coefficients or (b) 21 3-bit coefficients are packed into a 64-bit word.

## Solution

Let the coefficients be elements of $\{0,1,2,3,4\}$. The packed operations must perform independent arithmetic on the coefficient fields and then reduce the resulting field values modulo $5$.

The two packing schemes are different because the field widths allow different amounts of temporary overflow.

### (a) Sixteen 4-bit coefficients

Write

$$
a(x)=\sum_{j=0}^{15}a_jx^j,\qquad b(x)=\sum_{j=0}^{15}b_jx^j
$$

and pack them as

$$
A=\sum_{j=0}^{15}a_j16^j,\qquad
B=\sum_{j=0}^{15}b_j16^j .
$$

Define

$$
M=\sum_{j=0}^{15}15\cdot16^j
$$

and

$$
H=\sum_{j=0}^{15}8\cdot16^j .
$$

For addition,

$$
0\le a_j+b_j\le8 .
$$

Since $8<16$, the ordinary word addition

$$
S=A+B
$$

produces the sums in the individual fields without carries between fields.

It remains to reduce each field. For a field value $c\in\{0,\ldots,8\}$, add $11$. The field values become

$$
\begin{array}{c|ccccccccc}
c&0&1&2&3&4&5&6&7&8\\
\hline
c+11\bmod16&11&12&13&14&15&0&1&2&3 .
\end{array}
$$

The top bit of the field is $1$ exactly when $c<5$. Therefore the fields with $c\ge5$ are selected by

$$
T=\left(\overline{S+11M}\mathbin{\&}H\right)\gg3 .
$$

The value $T$ has a $1$ in the low bit of every field requiring subtraction of $5$. Multiplying by $5$ gives the correction amount, so the addition routine is

$$
\boxed{
A+B-5\left(\left(\overline{A+B+11M}\mathbin{\&}H\right)\gg3\right)
}.
$$

For subtraction, compute

$$
a_j-b_j\equiv a_j+5-b_j\pmod5 .
$$

The intermediate field values satisfy

$$
1\le a_j+5-b_j\le9 .
$$

Since $9<16$, no carries occur between fields. Hence

$$
D=A+5M-B
$$

contains the biased differences. Applying the same reduction gives

$$
\boxed{
D-5\left(\left(\overline{D+11M}\mathbin{\&}H\right)\gg3\right)
}.
$$

This completes the 4-bit case.

### (b) Twenty-one 3-bit coefficients

Now pack

$$
A=\sum_{j=0}^{20}a_j8^j,\qquad
B=\sum_{j=0}^{20}b_j8^j .
$$

Let

$$
M=\sum_{j=0}^{20}7\cdot8^j
$$

and

$$
H=\sum_{j=0}^{20}4\cdot8^j .
$$

The top bit mask $H$ selects the high bit of each 3-bit field.

#### Addition

The ordinary sums satisfy

$$
0\le a_j+b_j\le8 .
$$

A 3-bit field cannot hold $8$, so first take

$$
S=(A+B)\mathbin{\&}M .
$$

Each field of $S$ is

$$
c_j=(a_j+b_j)\bmod8 .
$$

If the true sum is $0,\ldots,4$, the field is already correct. If the true sum is $5,6,$ or $7$, the field contains $5,6,$ or $7$ and needs subtraction of $5$. If the true sum is $8$, the field contains $0$, which is already equal to $8\bmod5=3$, so it also needs correction.

Thus the values needing correction are detected by adding $3$ to each field. For a field value $c$,

$$
c+3\ge8
$$

exactly when

$$
c\ge5 .
$$

The carry into the high bit position of the field is therefore captured by

$$
R=\left((S+3M)\mathbin{\&}H\right)\gg2 .
$$

The bit pattern in $R$ marks precisely the fields whose residue modulo $5$ is too large by $5$, except that the value $8$ has already wrapped to $0$. For the value $8$, the carry also occurs, so the same mask marks it. Therefore the reduction is

$$
\boxed{
S-5R
}
$$

for the addition routine.

#### Subtraction

A direct reuse of the 4-bit bias fails because adding $5$ can create values too large for 3-bit fields. A different construction is needed.

Use the identity

$$
a_j-b_j\equiv a_j+4-b_j\pmod5 .
$$

Define

$$
D=A+4M-B .
$$

Every mathematical field value satisfies

$$
0\le a_j+4-b_j\le8 .
$$

The only value that cannot fit into a 3-bit field is $8$. The representation of $8$ in a packed word is not a high bit in the same field. Instead it is a carry into the next field. Therefore it must be detected before masking.

Add one more bit of bias:

$$
D'=A+5M-B .
$$

Now each mathematical field value satisfies

$$
1\le a_j+5-b_j\le9 .
$$

The values $8$ and $9$ are exactly the cases where a carry leaves a field. Because the minimum field value is $1$, there is no borrow and no other source of bits outside the fields. The carry-out mask is therefore

$$
C=(D'\mathbin{\&}\neg M).
$$

To align those carry bits with their original fields, shift them down by four positions:

$$
E=(C\gg4)\mathbin{\&}M .
$$

This places a single bit in the low position of each field whose biased value was $8$ or $9$.

Now mask away the carry bits:

$$
S=D'\mathbin{\&}M .
$$

The value $S$ contains the low three bits of the biased differences. The overflow cases are

$$
8\quad\hbox{and}\quad9 .
$$

The field values after masking are

$$
8\mapsto0,\qquad 9\mapsto1 .
$$

The required corrections are therefore

$$
8\mapsto3,\qquad 9\mapsto3 .
$$

Adding $3$ to the fields selected by $E$ repairs both cases:

$$
T=S+3E .
$$

The corrected fields now lie in

$$
1,\ldots,7
$$

and represent

$$
a_j+5-b_j\pmod8
$$

with the value $8$ or $9$ replaced by its proper residue modulo $5$.

Now reduce modulo $5$. For a 3-bit field value $c\in\{0,\ldots,7\}$, adding $3$ creates a carry into the top bit exactly when $c\ge5$. Hence

$$
Q=\left((T+3M)\mathbin{\&}H\right)\gg2
$$

marks the fields needing subtraction of $5$.

The subtraction routine is therefore

$$
\boxed{
T-5Q
}
$$

where

$$
D'=A+5M-B,
$$

$$
S=D'\mathbin{\&}M,
$$

$$
E=\left((D'\mathbin{\&}\neg M)\gg4\right)\mathbin{\&}M,
$$

$$
T=S+3E,
$$

and

$$
Q=\left((T+3M)\mathbin{\&}H\right)\gg2 .
$$

All additions, subtractions, masks, and shifts act on the entire packed word. The construction keeps every temporary value within a range that prevents unwanted interaction between coefficient fields, and the final words contain exactly the coefficients of the polynomial sum or difference modulo $5$. $\square$
