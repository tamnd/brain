[Section 7.1.3: Bitwise Tricks and Techniques](../)

**Exercise 47.** [*10*] Can the general δ-swap (69) also be done with a method like (67)?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m06s  

---

The δ-swap (69) is an affine involution of the form

$$
(x,y) \mapsto (y,\; x-\delta)
$$

(or equivalently a shift of the ordinary swap by a fixed constant $\delta$). As in method (67), it can be decomposed into three reversible updates using only addition and subtraction.

Starting from a pair $(x,y)$, perform

$$
x \leftarrow x + y
$$

$$
y \leftarrow x - y - \delta
$$

$$
x \leftarrow x - y - \delta
$$

After the first step, $x = x + y$. After the second step,

$$
y = (x+y) - y - \delta = x - \delta.
$$

After the third step,

$$
x = (x+y) - (x-\delta) - \delta = y.
$$

Thus the final state is $(y,; x-\delta)$, which is exactly the δ-swap. This has the same three-step structure as (67), obtained by replacing the XOR-based self-inverse operation with the additive group operation and adjusting by the constant $\delta$. ∎
