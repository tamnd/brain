At what bit position (from the left) does the pointer for node j end?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m04s  

---

Start from the definition of the encoding.

Each node $i \ge 2$ contributes a binary string equal to the standard binary representation of $\lfloor i/2 \rfloor$, with no leading zeros. Let

$$
\lambda(x)=\lfloor \log_2 x \rfloor + 1
$$

be the bit-length of $x \ge 1$.

Let $P(j)$ be the position (from the left, starting at $1$) of the last bit of the pointer for node $j$. Then

$$
P(j)=\sum_{i=2}^{j} \lambda(\lfloor i/2 \rfloor).
$$

## Step 1: group indices correctly (fixing the boundary error)

Let $m=\lfloor j/2 \rfloor$.

We group terms by $k=\lfloor i/2 \rfloor$, i.e., by nodes $k$.

For each $k \ge 1$, the relevant indices are:

$$
i=2k \quad \text{and} \quad i=2k+1,
$$

provided they are $\le j$.

### Case 1: $1 \le k \le m-1$

If $k \le m-1$, then

$$
2k+1 \le 2(m-1)+1 = 2m-1 \le j,
$$

so both indices $2k$ and $2k+1$ are included.

Hence contribution:

$$
2\lambda(k).
$$

### Case 2: $k=m$

We always have $2m \le j$, so $i=2m$ is always included.

However, $2m+1 \le j$ holds only when $j=2m+1$ (i.e., $j$ is odd).

Thus contribution from $k=m$ is:

$$
\lambda(m) + \mathbf{1}_{j \text{ odd}}\,\lambda(m).
$$

## Step 2: correct decomposition of $P(j)$

Combining both cases:

$$
P(j)=2\sum_{k=1}^{m-1}\lambda(k)+\lambda(m)+\mathbf{1}_{j \text{ odd}}\,\lambda(m),
\quad m=\left\lfloor \frac{j}{2}\right\rfloor.
$$

This avoids the overcounting error in the previous solution.

## Step 3: clean simplification (separating parity)

Let

$$
S(m)=\sum_{k=1}^{m}\lambda(k), \quad S(0)=0.
$$

### If $j=2m$ (even)

Only $i=2m$ contributes from the last pair, so:

$$
P(2m)=2\sum_{k=1}^{m-1}\lambda(k)+\lambda(m)
=2S(m-1)+\lambda(m).
$$

### If $j=2m+1$ (odd)

Both $2m$ and $2m+1$ contribute:

$$
P(2m+1)=2\sum_{k=1}^{m}\lambda(k)=2S(m).
$$

## Step 4: explicit closed form for $S(m)$

Let

$$
L=\lfloor \log_2 m \rfloor + 1.
$$

Then grouping by bit-length:

$$
S(m)=\sum_{t=1}^{L-1} t\,2^{t-1} + L\bigl(m-2^{L-1}+1\bigr).
$$

## Final corrected result

$$
\boxed{
P(2m)=2S(m-1)+\lambda(m)
}
$$

$$
\boxed{
P(2m+1)=2S(m)
}
$$

where

$$
S(m)=\sum_{t=1}^{\lfloor \log_2 m \rfloor} t\,2^{t-1}
+ (\lfloor \log_2 m \rfloor+1)\bigl(m-2^{\lfloor \log_2 m \rfloor}+1\bigr),
\quad S(0)=0.
$$

The correction is entirely in the boundary handling at $k=m$: the last block contributes either one or two copies of $\lambda(m)$, depending on the parity of $j$, and must not be doubled unconditionally.
