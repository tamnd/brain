## Solution

In formulas (55) and (56), the value $\lambda 0$ is determined solely by the constant base case used in those definitions, since no nontrivial bit propagation occurs when the input is $0$.

In the present form of the construction, the constant $0 = (\ldots 0000)_2$ is acting as the absorbing or initial word. Under the bitwise identities of (12)–(13), this forces all operations that propagate from the zero input to remain zero, hence $\lambda 0 = 0$.

To obtain $\lambda 0 = -1 = (\ldots 1111)_2$, the construction must instead use the all-ones word as the base case. This is the unique bitwise element that satisfies

$x \,\&\, (-1) = x, \qquad x \mid (-1) = -1,$

so it propagates through the same recursive structure as $0$ does in the original formulas, but with all bits equal to $1$.

Therefore, in both (55) and (56), every occurrence of the constant $0$ that serves as the initial or boundary value must be replaced by $-1$. Equivalently, the initialization of the construction defining $\lambda x$ is changed from

$\lambda 0 = 0$

to

$\lambda 0 = -1,$

with all subsequent occurrences of the base constant adjusted consistently in the recursive expressions.

This substitution preserves the structural rules of (55) and (56) while changing the fixed point of the construction from the zero word to the all-ones word, yielding the required value.
