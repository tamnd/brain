**Exercise 89.** [*23*] [23] Given $x = (x_{31} \ldots x_1 x_0)_4$ and $y = (y_{31} \ldots y_1 y_0)_4$, compute $z = (z_{31} \ldots z_1 z_0)_4$, where $z_j = \lfloor x_j/y_j \rfloor$ for $0 \le j < 32$, assuming that no $y_j$ is zero.

We correct the solution by first making explicit the bit-parallel model that was implicitly assumed, then rebuilding every step so that all operations act independently on each 2-bit base-4 digit.

## Correct solution

We represent the 32 base-4 digits in a single word $w$ of $64$ bits, grouped into 2-bit lanes. For each $j$, bits $2j$ and $2j+1$ encode $x_j$ or $y_j$.

Define the standard lane masks

$$
B_0 = \sum_{j=0}^{31} (1 \ll 2j), \qquad
B_1 = 2 B_0 = \sum_{j=0}^{31} (1 \ll (2j+1)).
$$

Thus $B_0$ selects the low bit of each lane and $B_1$ selects the high bit.

For any word $u$, define its per-lane bit decomposition

$$
u^{(0)} = u \,\&\, B_0, \qquad
u^{(1)} = (u \,\&\, B_1) \gg 1.
$$

Then in each lane $j$,

$$
u_j = 2u^{(1)}_j + u^{(0)}_j.
$$

We apply this to $x$ and $y$, so each $x_j, y_j \in \{0,1,2,3\}$, and by assumption $y_j \neq 0$.

## Step 1: Per-digit classification of $y_j$

We construct lane-wise masks indicating whether $y_j = 1,2,3$.

First compute bitwise comparisons in each lane:

- $y = 1$ corresponds to $(y^{(1)}, y^{(0)}) = (0,1)$
- $y = 2$ corresponds to $(1,0)$
- $y = 3$ corresponds to $(1,1)$

Define per-lane equality indicators (each is a full 2-bit mask per lane, i.e. either $00_2$ or $11_2$):

$$
E_1 = \neg\big((y^{(1)} \oplus 0) \lor (y^{(0)} \oplus 1)\big),
$$

$$
E_2 = \neg\big((y^{(1)} \oplus 1) \lor (y^{(0)} \oplus 0)\big),
$$

$$
E_3 = \neg\big((y^{(1)} \oplus 1) \lor (y^{(0)} \oplus 1)\big),
$$

where all operations are interpreted bitwise and then lifted per lane, and the final negation is extended to all bits of each lane so that each lane becomes either $00_2$ or $11_2$.

These masks are disjoint and satisfy, in every lane,

$$
E_1 \lor E_2 \lor E_3 = 11_2.
$$

## Step 2: Per-digit computation of $x_j$-dependent values

We similarly use the bit-plane decomposition of $x$:

$$
x^{(0)}, x^{(1)}.
$$

Define the per-lane values:

- $a_1 = x$
- $a_2 = \lfloor x/2 \rfloor$ per lane, i.e.

$$
a_2^{(1)} = 0, \quad a_2^{(0)} = x^{(1)}
$$
- $a_3 = [x = 3]$, i.e. indicator that $x_j = 3$, computed as

$$
a_3 = E_{x=3}.
$$

This is valid because $x_j = 3$ iff $x^{(1)}_j = 1$ and $x^{(0)}_j = 1$.

Thus:

$$
a_3 = x^{(1)} \,\&\, x^{(0)} \quad \text{(lifted to full 2-bit lanes)}.
$$

## Step 3: Correct digitwise quotient logic

We now analyze

$$
z_j = \left\lfloor \frac{x_j}{y_j} \right\rfloor.
$$

Since $y_j \in \{1,2,3\}$, we have:

- If $y_j = 1$, then $z_j = x_j$.
- If $y_j = 2$, then $z_j = \lfloor x_j/2 \rfloor$.
- If $y_j = 3$, then:

$$
z_j =
\begin{cases}
1 & x_j = 3, \\
0 & \text{otherwise}.
\end{cases}
$$

Thus the per-lane expression is:

$$
z = (E_1 \,\&\, x)\; \lor\; (E_2 \,\&\, \lfloor x/2 \rfloor)\; \lor\; (E_3 \,\&\, [x=3]).
$$

## Step 4: Assembly of the final word

All operations are lane-independent because:

- each $E_k$ is a full 2-bit lane mask,
- each operand $x$, $\lfloor x/2 \rfloor$, and $[x=3]$ is defined per lane via bit-plane arithmetic,
- and the masks are disjoint.

Therefore OR-combination does not mix lanes.

We obtain a word $z$ whose $j$-th lane equals $z_j$.

## Conclusion

The constructed word $z$ satisfies, for every $0 \le j < 32$,

$$
z_j = \left\lfloor \frac{x_j}{y_j} \right\rfloor,
$$

hence

$$
z = (z_{31} \ldots z_1 z_0)_4.
$$

This completes the corrected solution. ∎
