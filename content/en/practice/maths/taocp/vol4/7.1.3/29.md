**Exercise 29.** [*20*] (V. R. Pratt.) Express the magic mask $\mu_k$ of (47) in terms of $\mu_{k+1}$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 4m48s  

---

Let $T = 2^k$.

Recall the definitions:

- $\mu_{k+1}$ is the infinite bit mask

$$
1^{2T} 0^{2T} 1^{2T} 0^{2T} \cdots
$$
- $\mu_k$ is

$$
1^{T} 0^{T} 1^{T} 0^{T} \cdots
$$

We must express the finer alternation at scale $T$ using only bitwise operations and shifts applied to $\mu_{k+1}$.

## Step 1: Align $\mu_{k+1}$ with a shifted copy

Let $S = \mu_{k+1} \gg T$, i.e., a right shift by $T$.

Consider one period of $\mu_{k+1}$:

$$
\underbrace{1^{2T}}_{[0,2T)} \ \underbrace{0^{2T}}_{[2T,4T)}
$$

Now compare $\mu_{k+1}$ and $S$ on the interval $[0,2T)$:

- On $[0,T)$:

$\mu_{k+1} = 1$, and $S = \mu_{k+1}[T,2T] = 1$
- On $[T,2T)$:

$\mu_{k+1} = 1$, and $S = \mu_{k+1}[2T,3T] = 0$

So within the first $2T$-block we get:

$$
\mu_{k+1} = 1^T 1^T, \quad S = 1^T 0^T
$$

## Step 2: Extract the transition pattern

Compute XOR:

$$
A = \mu_{k+1} \oplus S
$$

On $[0,2T)$:

- first $T$: $1 \oplus 1 = 0$
- next $T$: $1 \oplus 0 = 1$

Hence:

$$
A = 0^T 1^T \ \text{(on each }2T\text{-block)}
$$

So $A$ is exactly the pattern “zeros then ones”, i.e., the complement of $\mu_k$ on each block.

## Step 3: Recover $\mu_k$

Since $\mu_k$ is $1^T 0^T$, we have:

$$
\mu_k = \overline{A}
$$

Thus:

$$
\mu_k = \overline{\mu_{k+1} \oplus (\mu_{k+1} \gg T)}
$$

Equivalently, using all-ones mask $\mathbf{1}$:

$$
\mu_k = \mathbf{1} \oplus \mu_{k+1} \oplus (\mu_{k+1} \gg T)
$$

## Step 4: Verification over all blocks

Both $\mu_{k+1}$ and its shift by $T$ are periodic with period $4T$. The XOR construction produces within every interval $[2mT, 2(m+1)T)$:

- first $T$: $0$
- second $T$: $1$

Flipping gives:

- first $T$: $1$
- second $T$: $0$

which is exactly $\mu_k$.

## Final expression

$$
\boxed{
\mu_k \;=\; \overline{\mu_{k+1} \oplus (\mu_{k+1} \gg 2^k)}
}
$$

or equivalently

$$
\boxed{
\mu_k \;=\; \mathbf{1} \oplus \mu_{k+1} \oplus (\mu_{k+1} \gg 2^k)
}
$$
