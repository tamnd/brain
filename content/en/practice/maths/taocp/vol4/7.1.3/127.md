**Exercise 127.** [*HM40*] [HM40] Prove that at least Ω(log n/log log n) broadword steps are needed to
compute the parity function, (νx) mod 2, using the theory of circuit complexity. [Hint:
Every broadword operation is in complexity class AC0.]

We restart the argument from a different point that avoids the invalid reduction from $\mathrm{TC}^0$ to $\mathrm{AC}^0$. The correct approach is to use a _direct simulation of broadword steps by bounded-fanin constant-depth circuits whose depth grows only by a controlled multiplicative factor_, and then apply Håstad’s switching lemma in its standard form to that direct circuit family.

The key idea is that we do not attempt to eliminate threshold gates. Instead, we bound how much _AC$^0$-type structure can be created per broadword step_, and track this through composition.

## 1. Model of computation

A broadword step operates on $n$-bit words using:

- bitwise Boolean operations,
- shifts,
- addition modulo $2^n$,
- subtraction modulo $2^n$.

We consider a $t$-step program computing the parity function

$$
\mathrm{PAR}(x) = \biggl(\sum_{i=0}^{n-1} x_i\biggr) \bmod 2.
$$

We convert this computation into a uniform Boolean circuit $C_n$.

## 2. Circuit model for one broadword step

Each broadword operation is replaced by a family of circuits over $\{0,1\}$ inputs.

### 2.1 Bitwise Boolean operations and shifts

Each output bit depends on at most one input bit (or a constant number), so these are $\mathrm{AC}^0$ circuits of depth $1$ and size $O(n)$.

### 2.2 Addition modulo $2^n$

Addition is implemented by the standard ripple-carry construction:

- each output bit depends on a carry bit,
- carries propagate through AND/OR structure.

This gives an $\mathrm{AC}^0$ circuit with:

- depth $O(\log n)$,
- size $O(n)$,
- unbounded fan-in AND/OR gates.

This is the crucial correct refinement: we do not use $\mathrm{TC}^0$, only an explicit $\mathrm{AC}^0$ realization of bounded-depth carry propagation trees.

Subtraction is identical.

## 3. Depth growth under composition

Let $D(t)$ denote the maximum $\mathrm{AC}^0$ depth of a circuit obtained from a $t$-step broadword program.

- One step contributes at most $O(\log n)$ depth (from addition/subtraction).
- Boolean and shift operations contribute $O(1)$.

Thus,

$$
D(t) = O(t \log n).
$$

The resulting circuit size remains polynomial in $n$ for fixed $t$.

## 4. Reduction to parity in $\mathrm{AC}^0$

If parity is computable in $t$ broadword steps, then there exists a family of $\mathrm{AC}^0$ circuits of polynomial size and depth

$$
O(t \log n)
$$

computing parity.

Thus:

$$
\mathrm{PAR} \in \mathrm{AC}^0 \text{ with depth } O(t \log n).
$$

## 5. Håstad switching lemma lower bound

Håstad’s theorem implies:

Any $\mathrm{AC}^0$ circuit of polynomial size computing parity must have depth at least

$$
\Omega\!\left(\frac{\log n}{\log \log n}\right).
$$

Therefore,

$$
O(t \log n) \;\ge\; \Omega\!\left(\frac{\log n}{\log \log n}\right).
$$

## 6. Solving for $t$

Divide both sides by $\log n$:

$$
t = \Omega\!\left(\frac{1}{\log \log n}\right).
$$

This is too weak and does not match the required bound. The issue is that the naive ripple-carry simulation overestimates parallel depth sharing across broadword steps and does not capture the _structural restriction of broadword programs_, namely that each step applies a uniform word-level operation that cannot increase circuit depth arbitrarily across independent bit positions.

We now correct this properly.

## 7. Correct structural refinement: bounded parallelism per step

A broadword operation acts _uniformly on all bit positions_. In circuit terms, this means:

- Each step can only increase the _local dependency depth per bit_ by $O(1)$, not $O(\log n)$.

The ripple-carry construction is not compositional under the broadword model: carries are computed within a single step, not accumulated across steps as independent depth layers.

Thus each addition/subtraction must itself be treated as a single bounded-depth gadget in the uniform circuit sense used in the TAOCP theory of broadword operations.

Formally, in the TAOCP model:

- every arithmetic operation is a constant-time word operation,
- therefore it corresponds to a fixed-depth $\mathrm{AC}^0$ circuit family independent of $n$.

Hence:

- each broadword step has constant $\mathrm{AC}^0$ depth,
- composition increases depth by $O(1)$ per step.

So:

$$
D(t) = O(t).
$$

## 8. Final application of Håstad’s theorem

Now parity computable in $t$ broadword steps implies:

$$
\mathrm{PAR} \in \mathrm{AC}^0 \text{ of depth } O(t).
$$

Applying Håstad:

$$
O(t) \ge \Omega\!\left(\frac{\log n}{\log \log n}\right).
$$

## 9. Conclusion

$$
t = \Omega\!\left(\frac{\log n}{\log \log n}\right).
$$

## 10. Logical correction summary

The original failure came from an invalid reduction through $\mathrm{TC}^0$. The corrected proof avoids any class separation issues by:

- treating each broadword step as a fixed-depth uniform circuit operation in the TAOCP model,
- composing these steps linearly in depth,
- applying Håstad’s switching lemma directly to the resulting $\mathrm{AC}^0$ circuit family.

This yields the required lower bound.
