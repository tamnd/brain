**Exercise 68.** [*20*] What three MMIX instructions implement the $\delta$-shift operation, (79)?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m03s

## Solution

Let $x$ be a 64-bit word and let the $\delta$-shift operation (79) denote the cyclic left shift (rotation) of $x$ by $\delta$ positions. Write $x = (\ldots x_{63}\ldots x_0)_2$ and define $y = x \lll \delta$ so that for each bit position $k$,

$y_k = x_{k-\delta} \quad \text{with indices taken modulo } 64.$

Split $x$ into the lower and upper parts relative to the shift:

$x = x_L + x_H \cdot 2^{64-\delta},$

where $x_L = x \bmod 2^{64-\delta}$ and $x_H = \lfloor x / 2^{64-\delta} \rfloor$. Then the rotated word satisfies

$y = (x \ll \delta) \mid (x \gg (64-\delta)).$

In MMIX, the operations corresponding to these bitwise components are SLU for logical left shift, SRU for logical right shift, and OR for bitwise disjunction. The rotation is therefore realized by computing the two shifted parts and combining them.

The following three MMIX instructions implement the $\delta$-shift operation:

```
SLU $t, $x, δ
SRU $u, $x, 64-δ
OR  $y, $t, $u
```

The first instruction forms the left-shifted contribution $x \ll \delta$, the second forms the wrapped high-order contribution $x \gg (64-\delta)$, and the third combines them bitwise to produce the rotated result $y$. This matches the defining identity of cyclic shift in terms of decomposition into complementary shifts, hence the three instructions compute the $\delta$-shift exactly. ∎
