**Exercise 180.** &#9654; [*M24*] [M24] Digitize the hyperbola y2 = x2 + 13 by hand, for 0 < y ≤7.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 1m54s  

---

The task is not to find integer solutions of $y^2 = x^2 + 13$, but to construct a discrete (pixel) representation of the curve in the strip $0 < y \le 7$. We therefore treat the image as a bitmap on the integer lattice and, for each integer row $y$, determine which integer $x$-positions best represent the continuous curve in that row.

The curve is

$$
y^2 = x^2 + 13,
\qquad
x = \pm \sqrt{y^2 - 13}.
$$

For a fixed integer $y$, let

$$
r(y) = \sqrt{y^2 - 13}.
$$

When $y^2 < 13$, no real intersection occurs, so no pixels are drawn in those rows.

For $y \ge 4$, the curve intersects row $y$ near $x = \pm r(y)$. In digitization, each row $y$ is represented by pixels whose unit squares intersect the curve, equivalently by choosing the integer $x$ closest to $r(y)$, i.e. $x = \lfloor r(y) + \tfrac12 \rfloor$, with symmetry about the $y$-axis.

Now compute row by row.

### Row $y = 1,2,3$

$$
y^2 - 13 < 0,
$$

so no real $x$. No pixels are plotted.

### Row $y = 4$

$$
r = \sqrt{16 - 13} = \sqrt{3} \approx 1.732.
$$

Nearest integer is $2$. Hence pixels:

$$
(x,y) = (\pm 2, 4).
$$

### Row $y = 5$

$$
r = \sqrt{25 - 13} = \sqrt{12} \approx 3.464.
$$

Nearest integer is $3$. Hence:

$$
(x,y) = (\pm 3, 5).
$$

### Row $y = 6$

$$
r = \sqrt{36 - 13} = \sqrt{23} \approx 4.796.
$$

Nearest integer is $5$. Hence:

$$
(x,y) = (\pm 5, 6).
$$

### Row $y = 7$

$$
r = \sqrt{49 - 13} = \sqrt{36} = 6.
$$

Exact lattice alignment gives:

$$
(x,y) = (\pm 6, 7).
$$

### Digitized hyperbola for $0 < y \le 7$

The bitmap representation consists of the lattice points:

$$
(\pm 2,4),\ (\pm 3,5),\ (\pm 5,6),\ (\pm 6,7).
$$

These points form two symmetric discrete branches approximating the continuous hyperbola in the given window.
