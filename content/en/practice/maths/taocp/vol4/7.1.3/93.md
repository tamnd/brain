[Section 7.1.3: Bitwise Tricks and Techniques](../)

**Exercise 93.** [*18*] [18] What basic formula for subtraction is analogous to formula (8q) for addition?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m25s  

---

From formula (8q) for addition,

$$
x+y=(x\oplus y)+2(x\&y),
$$

we first isolate the exclusive-or term:

$$
x\oplus y = x+y-2(x\&y).
$$

We also use the standard identity for two’s complement negation,

$$
-y=\bar y+1,
$$

so that

$$
x-y = x+\bar y+1.
$$

We now compute $x+\bar y$ using (8q). Applying the addition formula,

$$
x+\bar y = (x\oplus \bar y) + 2(x\&\bar y).
$$

Hence

$$
x-y = (x\oplus \bar y) + 2(x\&\bar y) + 1.
$$

We now rewrite all terms in a form involving $x\oplus y$ and $\bar x \& y$.

### Step 1: Rewrite $x\oplus \bar y$

Bitwise complement flips every bit of $y$, so

$$
x\oplus \bar y = \overline{x\oplus y}.
$$

In integer arithmetic this means flipping all bits, i.e. adding $-1$:

$$
x\oplus \bar y = (x\oplus y) \oplus (-1).
$$

We do not need this explicitly; instead we eliminate XOR entirely using the identity derived above:

$$
x\oplus y = x+y-2(x\&y).
$$

### Step 2: Rewrite the target expression directly

Consider

$$
(x\oplus y) - 2(\bar x \& y).
$$

Substitute $x\oplus y = x+y-2(x\&y)$:

$$
(x+y-2(x\&y)) - 2(\bar x \& y).
$$

Group terms:

$$
x+y - 2\bigl((x\&y)+(\bar x \& y)\bigr).
$$

Now simplify the parenthesis bitwise. For each bit position $k$,

$$
(x_k\&y_k)+(\bar x_k\&y_k)
= (x_k + (1-x_k))y_k
= y_k.
$$

Hence, as integers,

$$
(x\&y)+(\bar x \& y)=y.
$$

Therefore,

$$
(x\oplus y) - 2(\bar x \& y)
= x+y - 2y
= x-y.
$$

### Conclusion

We obtain the desired subtraction analogue of (8q):

$$
\boxed{x-y = (x\oplus y) - 2(\bar x \& y).}
$$

The term $\bar x \& y$ marks exactly the bit positions where a borrow is generated, and the factor $2$ shifts these borrows into the next higher bit position, mirroring the carry propagation structure in (8q).
