**Exercise 81.** [*21*] Find a formula for "scattered difference," to go with the "scattered sum" $(86)$.

**Verified:** no

Let the “scattered sum” (86) be the binomially weighted transform

$$
g(n)=\sum_{k=0}^{n}\binom{n}{k} f(k).
$$

The “scattered difference” is the inverse operation: recover $f$ from $g$.

## Scattered difference formula

We claim

$$
f(n)=\sum_{k=0}^{n}(-1)^{\,n-k}\binom{n}{k} g(k).
$$

## Derivation

Start from the scattered sum definition

$$
g(k)=\sum_{j=0}^{k}\binom{k}{j} f(j).
$$

Substitute this into the proposed inverse expression:

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n}(-1)^{n-k}\binom{n}{k} g(k)
=
\sum_{k=0}^{n}(-1)^{n-k}\binom{n}{k}
\sum_{j=0}^{k}\binom{k}{j} f(j).
$$

Rearrange the double sum by collecting terms with fixed $j$:

$$
=
\sum_{j=0}^{n} f(j)
\sum_{k=j}^{n} (-1)^{n-k}\binom{n}{k}\binom{k}{j}.
$$

Use the standard identity

$$
\binom{n}{k}\binom{k}{j}
=
\binom{n}{j}\binom{n-j}{k-j}.
$$

Thus the inner sum becomes

$$
\binom{n}{j}
\sum_{k=j}^{n} (-1)^{n-k}\binom{n-j}{k-j}.
$$

Let $m=n-j$ and $i=k-j$. Then

$$
=
\binom{n}{j} \sum_{i=0}^{m} (-1)^{m-i}\binom{m}{i}
=
\binom{n}{j}(-1)^m \sum_{i=0}^{m}(-1)^i\binom{m}{i}.
$$

But

$$
\sum_{i=0}^{m}(-1)^i\binom{m}{i} = (1-1)^m =
\begin{cases}
1 & m=0,\\
0 & m>0.
\end{cases}
$$

Hence the whole inner sum equals $1$ when $j=n$ and $0$ otherwise. Therefore the double sum reduces to

$$
f(n).
$$

## Result

The scattered difference (inverse of the scattered sum) is

$$
\boxed{
f(n)=\sum_{k=0}^{n}(-1)^{n-k}\binom{n}{k} g(k)
}
$$

This is the binomial inversion formula corresponding to the scattered sum (86).
