**Exercise 185.** &#9654; [*23*] [23] Design an algorithm that properly digitizes a straight line from (ξ, η) to
(ξ′, η′), when ξ, η, ξ′, and η′ are rational numbers, by simplifying Algorithm T.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 4m15s  

---

Let

$$
(\xi,\eta)=\left(\frac{a}{D},\frac{b}{D}\right), \qquad
(\xi',\eta')=\left(\frac{a'}{D},\frac{b'}{D}\right),
$$

where $a,b,a',b'\in\mathbb Z$ and $D>0$ is a common denominator.

Algorithm T decides the next pixel using orientation tests of the form “which side of the line a current lattice point lies on”. Such tests can be written as the sign of an expression

$$
F(u,v)
= (\eta'-\eta)(u-\xi) - (\xi'-\xi)(v-\eta),
$$

where $(u,v)\in\mathbb Z^2$ is the current pixel.

## 1. Correct algebraic form of the decision predicate

Substitute the rational endpoints:

$$
\xi=\frac{a}{D},\quad \eta=\frac{b}{D},\quad
\xi'=\frac{a'}{D},\quad \eta'=\frac{b'}{D}.
$$

Then

$$
\eta'-\eta=\frac{b'-b}{D}, \qquad \xi'-\xi=\frac{a'-a}{D}.
$$

Hence

$$
F(u,v)
=
\frac{b'-b}{D}\left(u-\frac{a}{D}\right)
-
\frac{a'-a}{D}\left(v-\frac{b}{D}\right).
$$

Expand:

$$
F(u,v)
=
\frac{1}{D}(b'-b)u - \frac{1}{D^2}(b'-b)a
-
\frac{1}{D}(a'-a)v + \frac{1}{D^2}(a'-a)b.
$$

Group terms:

$$
F(u,v)
=
\frac{1}{D}(b'-b)u - \frac{1}{D}(a'-a)v
+
\frac{1}{D^2}\bigl((a'-a)b-(b'-b)a\bigr).
$$

Multiply by $D^2>0$:

$$
D^2 F(u,v)
=
D(b'-b)u - D(a'-a)v + (a'b-ab').
$$

Define the integer-valued function

$$
F^*(u,v)=D^2 F(u,v).
$$

Then $F^*(u,v)\in\mathbb Z$ for all integer $(u,v)$, and crucially

$$
\operatorname{sign}(F(u,v))=\operatorname{sign}(F^*(u,v))
$$

because $D^2>0$.

## 2. Why the earlier scaling argument was insufficient

The previous solution incorrectly claimed a uniform factor $1/D$. The correct situation is:

- different terms introduce different powers of $D$,
- but every decision predicate in Algorithm T is affine in $(u,v)$,
- so after substitution it always becomes a rational expression with a _single common positive denominator_ after clearing.

The key correction is that we do not track the raw expression, only its sign, and the sign is preserved after multiplication by $D^2$.

## 3. Structure of all Algorithm T tests

Every comparison used by Algorithm T is ultimately one of the following equivalent forms:

1. A signed area/orientation test between three points, or
2. A comparison derived from such an orientation test.

Each such test has the determinant form

$$
\det
\begin{pmatrix}
\xi' - \xi & \eta' - \eta \\
u - \xi & v - \eta
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Expanding any such determinant produces an expression linear in $u,v$ with coefficients depending only on endpoints. After substitution of rational endpoints, every coefficient lies in $\frac{1}{D^2}\mathbb Z$. Therefore every predicate has the form

$$
F(u,v)=\frac{1}{D^2}G(u,v),
\quad G(u,v)\in\mathbb Z.
$$

Thus every decision reduces to the sign of an integer expression $G(u,v)$.

## 4. Invariance of Algorithm T

At each step, Algorithm T performs a finite sequence of decisions of the form

$$
F(u,v) \gtrless 0.
$$

Replacing $F$ by $G = D^2 F$ does not change any outcome because multiplication by the positive constant $D^2$ preserves sign.

Therefore:

- every branch decision in Algorithm T is identical under the transformation $F \mapsto G$,
- the update rules of Algorithm T depend only on these branch decisions,
- hence the entire sequence of moves is unchanged.

## 5. No geometric rescaling is required

A critical clarification: we do **not** rescale the integer grid or the output pixels.

We only replace rational arithmetic in the decision procedure by equivalent integer arithmetic. The lattice $\mathbb Z^2$ remains fixed, and the output pixel sequence is unchanged.

The “clearing denominators” step is purely algebraic inside the predicate evaluation.

## 6. Algorithm

Given rational endpoints $(\xi,\eta)$, $(\xi',\eta')$:

1. Choose $D>0$ such that

$$
\xi=\frac{a}{D},\ \eta=\frac{b}{D},\ 
\xi'=\frac{a'}{D},\ \eta'=\frac{b'}{D}.
$$
2. Replace every orientation test in Algorithm T by the integer equivalent obtained by multiplying by $D^2$, e.g.

$$
F(u,v)=0 \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad G(u,v)=0.
$$
3. Run Algorithm T unchanged in structure, but evaluate all predicates using $G(u,v)\in\mathbb Z$.
4. Output the resulting sequence of pixels $(u_k,v_k)\in\mathbb Z^2$.

## 7. Correctness conclusion

All decision predicates of Algorithm T are sign conditions of affine expressions in the current pixel coordinates. After substituting rational endpoints, each predicate becomes a rational function with a uniform positive denominator after clearing, hence equivalent to an integer expression with identical sign behavior.

Therefore Algorithm T executes identically for rational endpoints when implemented with cleared-denominator arithmetic, and it correctly digitizes the line segment from $(\xi,\eta)$ to $(\xi',\eta')$.
