**Exercise 97.** [*23*] Leslie Lamport's paper in 1975 presented the following "problem taken from an actual compiler optimization algorithm": Given octabytes $x = (x_7 \ldots x_0)_{256}$ and $y = (y_7 \ldots y_0)_{256}$, compute $t = (t_7 \ldots t_0)_{256}$ and $z = (z_7 \ldots z_0)_{256}$ so that $t_j \ne 0$ if and only if $x_j \ne 0$, $x_j \ne \texttt{'*'}$, and $x_j \ne y_j$; and $z_j = (x_j = 0?$  $y_j$: $(x_j \ne \texttt{'*'} \land x_j \ne y_j ?$ $\texttt{'*'}$ : $x_j)$).

We work bytewise, so all definitions are per component $j$. All operations $(+,-,\&,|,\sim,\gg)$ are on octabytes but interpreted independently on each byte.

We use boolean masks taking values in $\{0,1\}$ per byte.

## 1. Basic byte predicates

### Nonzero test

Define

$$
\operatorname{nz}(x) = \bigl((x \mid -x) \gg 7\bigr)\ \&\ 1.
$$

Then for each byte $x_j$,

$$
\operatorname{nz}(x_j)=
\begin{cases}
0, & x_j=0,\\
1, & x_j\neq 0.
\end{cases}
$$

Hence define the zero test as

$$
\operatorname{z}(x) = 1 - \operatorname{nz}(x).
$$

### Inequality test

Define

\operatorname{neq}(x,y) = \bigl((x-y)\mid (y-x)\bigr \gg 7)\ \&\ 1.

Then $\operatorname{neq}(x_j,y_j)=1$ iff $x_j\neq y_j$.

### Wildcard constant

Let $c = (\texttt{'*'})_{256}$.

Define

$$
\operatorname{neqc}(x) = \operatorname{neq}(x,c),
\quad
\operatorname{eqc}(x)=1-\operatorname{neqc}(x).
$$

## 2. Construction of $t$

The required condition is:

$$
x_j \neq 0,\quad x_j \neq c,\quad x_j \neq y_j.
$$

Define the boolean mask

$$
g = \operatorname{nz}(x)\ \&\ \operatorname{neqc}(x)\ \&\ \operatorname{neq}(x,y).
$$

Then set

$$
t = g.
$$

Thus $t_j=1$ iff all three conditions hold, and $t_j=0$ otherwise, which satisfies the requirement $t_j\neq 0$ exactly in the desired case.

## 3. Correct partition for $z$

We must implement:

$$
z_j =
\begin{cases}
y_j, & x_j=0,\\
\texttt{'*'}, & x_j\neq 0,\ x_j\neq c,\ x_j\neq y_j,\\
x_j, & \text{otherwise}.
\end{cases}
$$

Define three disjoint masks.

### Case 0: $x_j = 0$

$$
m_0 = \operatorname{z}(x).
$$

### Case 1: “good substitution” case

$$
m_1 = \operatorname{nz}(x)\ \&\ \operatorname{neqc}(x)\ \&\ \operatorname{neq}(x,y).
$$

### Case 2: remainder

$$
m_2 = 1 - m_0 - m_1.
$$

These are disjoint and satisfy $m_0+m_1+m_2=1$ per byte.

## 4. Construction of $z$

Now combine by selection:

$$
z = m_0 \cdot y \;+\; m_1 \cdot c \;+\; m_2 \cdot x.
$$

All products are bytewise: multiplying by a mask in $\{0,1\}$ selects or suppresses each byte.
