[Section 7.1.3: Bitwise Tricks and Techniques](../)

**Exercise 161.** [*20*] [20] The cells of a chessboard can be colored black and white, so that neighboring
cells have different colors. Does the pentagrid also have this property?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m10s  

---

The goal is to decide whether the faces (cells) of the pentagrid admit a black–white coloring such that adjacent faces receive opposite colors. This is equivalent to asking whether the face-adjacency graph (the dual graph) is bipartite.

Let $G$ be the planar graph of the pentagrid $\{5,4\}$, and let $G^\ast$ be its planar dual. Vertices of $G^\ast$ are pentagons, and edges connect pentagons sharing a full edge. A valid coloring of cells is exactly a proper 2-coloring of $G^\ast$, so the task is to show that $G^\ast$ is bipartite.

We prove this by working through the primal graph $G$.

### 1. Even degrees imply all edge cuts have even size

Every vertex of the pentagrid graph $G$ has degree $4$, hence every vertex degree is even.

Let $S \subseteq V(G)$ be any set of vertices, and let $\delta(S)$ be the set of edges with exactly one endpoint in $S$.

Consider the sum of degrees of vertices in $S$:

$$
\sum_{v \in S} \deg(v).
$$

Since every $\deg(v)$ is even, this sum is even.

On the other hand, each edge entirely inside $S$ contributes $2$ to the sum, and each edge in $\delta(S)$ contributes $1$. Hence

$$
\sum_{v \in S} \deg(v) = 2|E(S)| + |\delta(S)|.
$$

The left-hand side is even, and $2|E(S)|$ is even, so $|\delta(S)|$ must also be even. Therefore:

$$
|\delta(S)| \equiv 0 \pmod{2}
\quad \text{for every } S \subseteq V(G).
$$

So every edge cut in $G$ has even cardinality.

### 2. Relation between cycles in the dual and cuts in the primal

In a planar embedded graph, cycles in the dual graph correspond to edge cuts in the primal graph.

More precisely:

- A simple cycle $C^\ast$ in $G^\ast$ separates the sphere (or plane) into an interior and exterior region.
- Let $S$ be the set of vertices of $G$ lying in the interior region.
- Then the edges of $G$ crossed by $C^\ast$ are exactly the edges with one endpoint in $S$ and one outside $S$, that is:

$$
|C^\ast| = |\delta(S)|.
$$

Thus every cycle in $G^\ast$ corresponds to an edge cut in $G$, and its length equals the size of that cut.

This correspondence is standard for planar duality: cycles in the dual are precisely boundaries of vertex-induced regions in the primal.

### 3. Cycles in the dual have even length

From Step 1, every edge cut $\delta(S)$ in $G$ has even size.

From Step 2, every cycle $C^\ast$ in $G^\ast$ corresponds to such a cut, so its length is even:

$$
|C^\ast| = |\delta(S)| \equiv 0 \pmod{2}.
$$

Hence every cycle in the dual graph $G^\ast$ has even length.

### 4. Bipartiteness of the dual graph

A graph is bipartite if and only if it contains no cycle of odd length. Since every cycle in $G^\ast$ has even length, $G^\ast$ is bipartite.

Therefore its vertices (the pentagons) admit a 2-coloring such that adjacent pentagons receive opposite colors.

### 5. Conclusion

The face-adjacency graph of the pentagrid is bipartite, so its cells can be colored black and white with adjacent cells having different colors.

$$
\boxed{\text{The pentagrid does admit a black–white coloring of its cells.}}
$$
