[Section 7.1.3: Bitwise Tricks and Techniques](../)

**Exercise 148.** [*M21*] [M21] When preprocessing for Algorithm V, is it possible to have βx3 −−→∗
βy2 −−→∗βx2 −−→∗βy1 −−→∗βx1 in S when x3 −−→x2 −−→x1 −−→Λ and y2 −−→y1 −−→Λ in
the forest? (If so, two different trees are “entangled” in S.)

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 47s  

---

## Solution

Assume that a relation $\beta_u \to^{_} \beta_v$ holds in $S$. By construction of the preprocessing for Algorithm V, each vertex $\beta_x$ is associated with a unique vertex $x$ of the underlying forest, and every arc in $S$ is induced by structural relations inside a single tree of that forest. Hence, if $\beta_u \to^{_} \beta_v$ holds, then $u$ and $v$ lie in the same tree of the forest.

Consider a sequence

$$
\beta_{x_3} \to^{*} \beta_{y_2} \to^{*} \beta_{x_2} \to^{*} \beta_{y_1} \to^{*} \beta_{x_1}
$$

in $S$. By the preceding implication applied to each reachability segment, $x_3$ and $y_2$ lie in the same tree, $y_2$ and $x_2$ lie in the same tree, $x_2$ and $y_1$ lie in the same tree, and $y_1$ and $x_1$ lie in the same tree.

Transitivity of “being in the same tree” implies that $x_3, y_2, x_2, y_1, x_1$ all lie in a single tree of the forest.

This contradicts the hypothesis that $x_3 \to x_2 \to x_1 \to \Lambda$ and $y_2 \to y_1 \to \Lambda$ are two distinct trees in the forest, since vertices from different trees cannot be connected by any arc in $S$ and hence cannot appear in a single reachability chain.

Therefore no such alternating chain can occur in $S$.

This completes the proof. ∎
