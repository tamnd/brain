**Exercise 212.** [*M32*] [M32] Suppose a = (a63 . . . a1a0)2 represents the polynomial
a(x) = (a63 . . . a1a0)x = a63x63 + · · · + a1x + a0.
Discuss using MXOR to compute the product c(x) = a(x)b(x), modulo x64 and mod 2.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m05s  

---

## Setup

Let

$$
a(x)=\sum_{i=0}^{63} a_i x^i,\qquad b(x)=\sum_{j=0}^{63} b_j x^j,
$$

where each $a_i,b_j \in \{0,1\}$. Arithmetic on coefficients is in $\mathbb{F}_2$, so addition is XOR and multiplication is AND.

Let

$$
c(x)=a(x)b(x)\bmod x^{64}
$$

so

$$
c(x)=\sum_{k=0}^{63} c_k x^k,\qquad c_k=\bigoplus_{i+j=k} a_i b_j.
$$

The task is to describe computation of $c(x)$ using MXOR (bitwise XOR on registers), interpreted as word-level addition in $\mathbb{F}_2^{64}$.

## Solution

Represent $a(x)$ and $b(x)$ as 64-bit words

$$
a=(a_{63}\ldots a_0)_2,\qquad b=(b_{63}\ldots b_0)_2.
$$

For each index $i$ with $0\le i\le 63$, the term $a_i b(x)x^i$ contributes to the product. Since $a_i\in\{0,1\}$, multiplication by $a_i$ is either the zero polynomial or a copy of $b(x)x^i$.

Thus

$$
c(x)=\bigoplus_{i=0}^{63} a_i \cdot (b(x)x^i),
$$

where the outer sum is XOR in $\mathbb{F}_2[x]/(x^{64})$.

At the word level, multiplication by $x^i$ corresponds to a left shift by $i$ bits, with truncation modulo $x^{64}$. Define

$$
T_i(b)=b \ll i \;\; \text{(shift left by \(i\), discard overflow beyond bit 63)}.
$$

Then

$$
c=\bigoplus_{i=0}^{63} \left(a_i \cdot T_i(b)\right),
$$

where $a_i \cdot T_i(b)$ is either $T_i(b)$ if $a_i=1$, or $0$ if $a_i=0$.

A register-level computation proceeds as follows. Initialize $c\leftarrow 0$. For each $i$ from $0$ to $63$, extract bit $a_i$. If $a_i=1$, compute $t \leftarrow b \ll i$ and update

$$
c \leftarrow c \mathbin{\text{MXOR}} t.
$$

Each MXOR corresponds exactly to addition in $\mathbb{F}_2^{64}$, since bitwise addition mod 2 is XOR. Therefore each coefficient satisfies

$$
c_k = \bigoplus_{i=0}^{k} a_i b_{k-i},
$$

which matches the definition of polynomial multiplication in $\mathbb{F}_2[x]/(x^{64})$.

## Verification

For fixed $k$,

$$
c = \bigoplus_{i=0}^{63} a_i (b \ll i)
$$

implies the $k$-th bit of $c$ equals the XOR of all contributions from shifts $i$ such that bit $k$ of $b\ll i$ is 1. This condition is equivalent to $b_{k-i}=1$, hence

$$
c_k = \bigoplus_{i=0}^{k} a_i b_{k-i}.
$$

Each pair $(i,j)$ with $i+j=k$ contributes exactly once via $i$, so no term is duplicated or omitted. Reduction modulo $x^{64}$ removes all terms with $k\ge 64$, which are never stored in the 64-bit register representation.

Each MXOR operation combines disjoint partial polynomials, so associativity of XOR ensures the final register equals the full convolution result.

This completes the proof. ∎

## Notes

The computation is the carryless convolution of two 64-bit words. In hardware terms it is multiplication in the ring $\mathbb{F}_2[x]/(x^{64})$. MXOR provides the additive structure, while shifts implement multiplication by powers of $x$. A fully optimized implementation replaces the bit-by-bit loop by decomposition into smaller word blocks or table-based methods, but the algebraic structure remains identical: multiplication is a sum of shifted copies combined by MXOR.
