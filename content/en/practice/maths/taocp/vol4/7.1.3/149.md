## Solution

Let $F$ denote the forest that underlies Algorithm V, with parent function $\pi_v$ defined for every non-root vertex $v$, and let $\Lambda$ denote the root of each tree as in the statement of Algorithm V.

The preprocessing constructs a depth-first traversal of $F$ and assigns to each vertex $v$ two integers $\beta_v$ and $\alpha_v$ together with a global ordering of the arcs $\tau_j$ induced by first traversal of each arc $(v,\pi_v)$. The construction is defined recursively on each tree of $F$.

For each root $r$, perform a depth-first search starting at $r$, and when a vertex $v$ is first encountered assign

$\beta_v$ as the next unused integer in ${1,2,\ldots,n}$. After all descendants of $v$ have been completely processed, assign

$\alpha_v$ as the next unused integer in the same sequence. Each edge $(v,\pi_v)$ is assigned a unique index $\tau_j$ at the moment it is traversed from child to parent during backtracking.

This construction ensures that for every vertex $v$, all descendants of $v$ in the forest are visited in a contiguous interval of the DFS, so the set of vertices in the subtree rooted at $v$ is exactly

${u : \beta_v \le \beta_u \le \alpha_u \le \alpha_v}$.

For any arc $x \to y$ in $F$, the DFS ordering implies that either $y$ is the parent of $x$ or lies in a different subtree; hence the intervals defined by $(\beta_v,\alpha_v)$ are either nested or disjoint. In particular, no two distinct subtrees can interleave in the sense of a pattern

$\beta_{x_3} \to \beta_{y_2} \to \beta_{x_2} \to \beta_{y_1} \to \beta_{x_1}$ along increasing DFS time, since such a pattern would require simultaneous partial expansion of two distinct descendant chains. The DFS stack invariant forbids this, since at any moment the active vertices form a single root-to-current-node path.

Define $\lambda_v$ as the depth of $v$ in its tree. Then $\lambda_v$ increases by exactly $1$ along each arc $(v,\pi_v)$ and is constant outside ancestor relations. This gives the relation used in Algorithm V between depth differences and traversal indices, since each traversal step corresponds to exactly one increment or decrement along the stack height.

The preprocessing also constructs $\rho$ as the last descendant time of each vertex, which coincides with $\alpha_v$, and $\pi_v$ as the unique parent pointer discovered during DFS. The mapping $v \mapsto \pi_v$ is well defined because each non-root vertex is discovered exactly once and attached to the vertex that first discovered it.

Correctness of the preprocessing follows from the DFS stack invariant. At every stage of the search the active vertices form a chain in $F$, so every vertex outside this chain is either completely unvisited or completely finished. Therefore all structural relations required by Algorithm V between $\pi_v$, $\beta_v$, $\alpha_v$, and $\tau_j$ are consistent, and no interleaving of distinct tree paths occurs.

This completes the construction of a preprocessing procedure satisfying the structural requirements of Algorithm V. ∎
