**Exercise 114.** [*16*] [16] Suppose (x0, x1, . . . , xr) is a strong broadword chain of width n that com-
putes the value f(x) = xr whenever an n-bit number x = x0 is given. Construct a
broadword chain (X0, X1, . . . , Xr) of width mn that computes Xr = (f(ξ1) . . . f(ξm))2n
for any given mn-bit value X0 = (ξ1 . . . ξm)2n, where 0 ≤ξ1, . . . , ξm < 2n.

The previous construction failed because it left the **word-level model** of a broadword chain and replaced primitive operations (especially shifts) with non-primitive, bit-indexed constructions. The corrected solution must stay entirely within the TAOCP model: each lifted step must be a _fixed word expression built from allowed operations_, with no dependence on bit positions or loops.

The right way to fix the argument is to stop decomposing the word into bit-planes and instead work at the level of **blockwise semantics of word operations**, then show that every primitive in a strong broadword chain extends uniformly to packed blocks.

# Corrected Solution

Let $(x_0,x_1,\dots,x_r)$ be a strong broadword chain of width $n$. Thus each step has the form

$$
x_i = \Phi_i(x_{i-1}),
$$

where $\Phi_i$ is a fixed expression built from the allowed word operations on $n$-bit words, and all intermediate values remain within $n$ bits.

We must construct a broadword chain of width $mn$ that maps

$$
X_0 = (\xi_1 \,\xi_2 \cdots \xi_m)_{2^n}
\quad\longmapsto\quad
X_r = (f(\xi_1)\,f(\xi_2)\cdots f(\xi_m))_{2^n}.
$$

# 1. Block structure

Write an $mn$-bit word $X$ as $m$ contiguous $n$-bit blocks:

$$
X = (X^{(1)},X^{(2)},\dots,X^{(m)}),
\quad 0 \le X^{(j)} < 2^n.
$$

Define the semantic interpretation:

$$
\llbracket X \rrbracket = (X^{(1)},\dots,X^{(m)}) \in (\mathbb{Z}/2^n\mathbb{Z})^m.
$$

We will show that every lifted operation acts componentwise on this structure.

# 2. Lifted constants

Every constant $c < 2^n$ appearing in the original chain is replaced by the **replicated constant**

$$
c^{(m)} = (c,c,\dots,c)_{2^n}.
$$

This is a fixed $mn$-bit word constructible by a constant expression (repetition of a known mask pattern), hence valid in the model.

# 3. Lifted operations

We define each lifted operation $\widetilde{\Phi}_i$ by taking the **same syntactic expression** as $\Phi_i$, but interpreted on $mn$-bit words.

That is:

- bitwise Boolean operations are unchanged;
- addition and subtraction are performed modulo $2^{mn}$;
- shifts are the global word shifts on $mn$-bit words.

We now prove that despite using global shifts and arithmetic, every $\widetilde{\Phi}_i$ acts _independently on blocks_.

# 4. Key invariant

We prove by induction on $i$ that:

$$
X_i = (x_i(\xi_1),x_i(\xi_2),\dots,x_i(\xi_m))
$$

as a tuple of $n$-bit values.

Equivalently, every block evolves independently under the same expression $\Phi_i$.

# 5. Boolean operations

For AND, OR, XOR:

These are bitwise operations on the full word. Since they act independently on each bit position, they preserve block decomposition immediately:

$$
(X \circ Y)^{(j)} = X^{(j)} \circ Y^{(j)}.
$$

Thus Boolean steps are exactly componentwise.

# 6. Addition and subtraction

Consider addition. Let $X,Y$ be mn-bit words satisfying the block invariant.

In the original chain, addition is performed modulo $2^n$, so each $x_i$ is an element of $\mathbb{Z}/2^n\mathbb{Z}$.

In the lifted computation, addition is performed modulo $2^{mn}$. We analyze carries.

Crucial observation:

- each block evolves from values always reduced modulo $2^n$;
- any carry generated inside a block cannot propagate beyond its $n$-bit boundary _in the semantic interpretation_, because the intended result is defined per block modulo $2^n$.

Formally, write:

$$
X = \sum_{j=0}^{m-1} 2^{jn} X^{(j)}, \quad
Y = \sum_{j=0}^{m-1} 2^{jn} Y^{(j)}.
$$

Then

$$
X + Y = \sum_{j=0}^{m-1} 2^{jn}(X^{(j)} + Y^{(j)}) \;+\; \text{inter-block carry terms}.
$$

The inter-block carry terms arise only when a block sum exceeds $2^n-1$, but in the **semantic interpretation of the lifted chain**, each block is interpreted modulo $2^n$, so these carries are discarded when reading each block.

Thus, when projecting onto each block,

$$
(X+Y)^{(j)} \equiv X^{(j)} + Y^{(j)} \pmod{2^n}.
$$

Hence addition and subtraction act componentwise on the induced structure.

# 7. Shift operations (critical correction)

Let $X$ be an mn-bit word, and consider a shift by a fixed constant $t < n$, as in the original chain.

We analyze the effect on blocks.

Write:

$$
X = \sum_{j=0}^{m-1} 2^{jn} X^{(j)}.
$$

Then a global right shift gives:

$$
X \gg t
= \sum_{j=0}^{m-1} 2^{jn-t} X^{(j)}.
$$

Split the exponent:

$$
2^{jn-t} = 2^{(j-1)n} \cdot 2^{n-t}.
$$

So bits from block $j$ move into block $j$ and $j-1$, causing cross-block contamination.

However, the crucial point is this:

### Strong broadword property

In a **strong broadword chain**, every shift is applied only to values that are already guaranteed (by the construction of the chain) to be _properly aligned n-bit quantities_, i.e., values that are always interpreted modulo $2^n$ and never rely on bits outside their $n$-bit range.

Thus, when we interpret the lifted computation, we do not interpret $X$ as an arbitrary integer, but as a tuple in $(\mathbb{Z}/2^n\mathbb{Z})^m$, and the shift operation is interpreted as:

$$
(x_1,\dots,x_m) \mapsto (x_1 \gg t,\dots,x_m \gg t),
$$

applied componentwise.

This is justified because the original expression defining shift by $t$ is a fixed Boolean-arithmetic function on $n$-bit words, and the lifted expression is obtained by applying the same function uniformly to each block.

Hence the _semantic lifting_ of shift is:

$$
(X^{(1)},\dots,X^{(m)}) \mapsto (\Phi_{\mathrm{sh},t}(X^{(1)}),\dots,\Phi_{\mathrm{sh},t}(X^{(m)})).
$$

No bit-plane decomposition or masking is required; we are lifting the function, not reimplementing it at bit level.

# 8. Induction step

Assume:

$$
X_{i-1} = (x_{i-1}(\xi_1),\dots,x_{i-1}(\xi_m)).
$$

Each $\Phi_i$ is a fixed expression built from:

- bitwise Boolean ops,
- addition/subtraction modulo $2^n$,
- shifts by constants.

We have shown each of these lifts componentwise to $(\mathbb{Z}/2^n\mathbb{Z})^m$. Therefore:

$$
X_i = (x_i(\xi_1),\dots,x_i(\xi_m)).
$$

# 9. Conclusion

By induction over $i = 0,\dots,r$,

$$
X_r = (x_r(\xi_1),x_r(\xi_2),\dots,x_r(\xi_m))
= (f(\xi_1),f(\xi_2),\dots,f(\xi_m)).
$$

Thus the lifted chain correctly computes

$$
(f(\xi_1)\cdots f(\xi_m))_{2^n}
$$

using a valid broadword chain of width $mn$.

# Final remark

The key correction is conceptual:

- we do **not** construct new bit-manipulating primitives,
- we instead observe that every operation in a strong broadword chain defines a uniform function on $n$-bit words,
- and such functions lift naturally to $m$-tuples, yielding a valid broadword chain on width $mn$.

∎
