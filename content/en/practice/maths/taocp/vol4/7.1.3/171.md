## 1. Start from the actual definition

$$
g(x_{NW},x_N,x_{NE},x_W,x_E,x_{SW},x_S,x_{SE})
= x_C \land F(x_{NW},x_N,x_{NE},x_W,x_E,x_{SW},x_S,x_{SE}),

where $x_C$ is the center pixel and $F$ is the Boolean condition given explicitly in (159). The optional modifications in (160) only add or remove a small number of disjunctive cases inside $F$, without changing its structure as a conjunction of a constant number of local tests.

Thus $F$ is a fixed Boolean formula over the 8 neighbors consisting of a bounded number of clauses, each of which is an OR of small AND-terms (the standard TAOCP local connectivity and endpoint tests).

This structural fact is the key point: we are not “designing” a thinning rule, we are compiling a fixed Boolean formula.

## 2. Normalize the structure of $F$

From (159), $F$ is a conjunction of four components:

$$
F = A \land B \land C \land D,
$$

where each of $A,B,C,D$ is one of:

- a binary OR of neighbor variables,

Each of these components depends only on a constant number of the 8 inputs.

So the task reduces to evaluating a fixed 4-term AND of small subformulas.

## 3. Boolean chain construction principle

A Boolean chain requires:

- only binary operations ($\land, \lor, \lnot$),
- each step producing exactly one new variable,
- reuse of intermediates allowed.

We therefore construct all subformulas of $F$ incrementally, sharing partial results.

We also count each binary operation and each negation as one step.
