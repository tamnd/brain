[Section 7.1.3: Bitwise Tricks and Techniques](../)

**Exercise 65.** [*M16*] The binary polynomial $u(x) = u_0 + u_1 x + \cdots + u_{n-1} x^{n-1} \pmod{2}$ can be represented by the integer $u = (u_{n-1} \ldots u_1 u_0)_2$. If $u(x)$ and $v(x)$ correspond to integers $u$ and $v$ in this way, what polynomial corresponds to $u \updownarrow v$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 54s  

---

## Solution

Let the zipper function $w = u \updownarrow v$ be defined in the binary sense described in Section 7.1.3, so that the bits of $u$ and $v$ are interleaved. Write

$u = (u_{n-1}\ldots u_1 u_0)_2,\qquad v = (v_{n-1}\ldots v_1 v_0)_2,$

and let $w = (w_{2n-1}\ldots w_1 w_0)_2$ denote $u \updownarrow v$. By definition of the zipper operation, the bit at position $2i$ of $w$ comes from $u_i$, and the bit at position $2i+1$ comes from $v_i$, so

$w_{2i} = u_i,\qquad w_{2i+1} = v_i,\qquad 0 \le i < n.$

The binary polynomial corresponding to an integer $a = (a_k)_2$ is $A(x) = \sum_{k \ge 0} a_k x^k \pmod{2}$. Applying this to $w$ gives

$W(x) = \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} w_{2i} x^{2i} + \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} w_{2i+1} x^{2i+1}.$

Substituting the definitions of the bits yields

$W(x) = \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} u_i x^{2i} + \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} v_i x^{2i+1}.$

Factorizing powers of $x$,

$W(x) = \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} u_i (x^2)^i + x \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} v_i (x^2)^i.$

Recognizing the original polynomials $U(x) = \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} u_i x^i$ and $V(x) = \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} v_i x^i$, this becomes

$W(x) = U(x^2) + x V(x^2).$

Thus the polynomial corresponding to $u \updownarrow v$ is obtained by substituting $x^2$ for $x$ in each input polynomial and shifting the second by a factor of $x$.

$\boxed{U(x^2) + x V(x^2)}$

This completes the solution. ∎
