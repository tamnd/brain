**Exercise 103.** &#9654; [*22*] Sometimes it's convenient to represent small numbers in *unary* notation, so that 0, 1, 2, 3, ..., $k$ appear respectively as $(0)_2$, $(1)_2$, $(11)_2$, $(111)_2$, ..., $2^k - 1$ inside the computer. Then max and min are easily implemented as $|$ and $\&$.

Suppose the bytes of $x = (x_7 \ldots x_0)_{256}$ are such unary numbers, while the bytes of $y = (y_7 \ldots y_0)_{256}$ are all either 0 or 1. Explain how to "add" $y$ to $x$ or "subtract" $y$ from $x$, giving $u = (u_7 \ldots u_0)_{256}$ and $v = (v_7 \ldots v_0)_{256}$ where

$$u_j = 2^{\min(8,\lg(x_j+1)+y_j)} - 1 \quad \text{and} \quad v_j = 2^{\max(0,\lg(x_j+1)-y_j)} - 1.$$

Let each byte $x_j$ be a unary number, so there exists an integer $k_j \in \{0,\dots,8\}$ such that

$$
x_j = 2^{k_j}-1,
\qquad\text{equivalently}\qquad
x_j+1 = 2^{k_j},\;\; k_j = \lg(x_j+1).
$$

Thus each byte is exactly a string of $k_j$ low-order 1-bits.

The byte $y_j \in \{0,1\}$ specifies whether we increment or decrement this unary length.

## 1. Meaning of the formulas

From

$$
u_j = 2^{\min(8,\lg(x_j+1)+y_j)} - 1,
\qquad
v_j = 2^{\max(0,\lg(x_j+1)-y_j)} - 1,
$$

substitute $k_j = \lg(x_j+1)$:

- $u_j$ corresponds to $k_j \mapsto \min(8,k_j+y_j)$, i.e. increment by 1 if $y_j=1$, with saturation at 8.
- $v_j$ corresponds to $k_j \mapsto \max(0,k_j-y_j)$, i.e. decrement by 1 if $y_j=1$, with floor at 0.

Hence we are updating the _length of a run of 1s_.

So the task is to implement:

- $k \leftarrow k+1$ (capped at 8),
- $k \leftarrow k-1$ (floored at 0),

using only bitwise operations on the unary representation $x = 2^k-1$.

## 2. Key unary identities

If $x = 2^k-1$ (binary: $k$ ones), then:

- Left shift:

$$
x \ll 1 = 2x = 2^{k+1}-2
$$

- Right shift:

$$
x \gg 1 = 2^{k-1}-1
\quad (k \ge 1)
$$

Now observe:

### OR produces max in unary scale

If $x = 2^a-1$, $z = 2^b-1$, then

$$
x \,|\, z = 2^{\max(a,b)} - 1.
$$

### AND produces min in unary scale

x \,&\, z = 2^{\min(a,b)} - 1.

This holds because both operands are contiguous blocks of 1s.

## 3. Implementing increment ($y_j=1$)

We want to transform

$$
2^k - 1 \mapsto 2^{k+1}-1.
$$

Note:

$$
x \,|\, (x \ll 1)
= (2^k-1)\,|\, (2^{k+1}-2)
= 2^{k+1}-1.
$$

Thus:

$$
u_j =
\begin{cases}
x_j, & y_j=0,\\[4pt]
(x_j \;|\; (x_j \ll 1)) \;\&\; 255, & y_j=1.
\end{cases}
$$

The mask $255 = 2^8-1$ enforces the saturation at 8 bits.

## 4. Implementing decrement ($y_j=1$)

We want:

$$
2^k - 1 \mapsto 2^{k-1}-1.
$$

Use:

x \,&\, (x \gg 1) = (2^k-1)\,&\,(2^{k-1}-1) = 2^{k-1}-1.

Thus:

$$
v_j =
\begin{cases}
x_j, & y_j=0,\\[4pt]
x_j \,&\, (x_j \gg 1), & y_j=1.
\end{cases}
$$

This correctly implements flooring at 0 since $0 \gg 1 = 0$.

## 5. Unified bitwise form

Since $y_j \in \{0,1\}$, we can write:

### Addition (increment)

$$
u_j = \bigl(x_j \;|\; (x_j \ll y_j)\bigr)\;\&\;255.
$$

- If $y_j=0$: $x_j \ll 0 = x_j$, so $u_j=x_j$.
- If $y_j=1$: gives incremented unary value.

### Subtraction (decrement)

v_j = x_j \,&\, (x_j \gg y_j).

- If $y_j=0$: $x_j \gg 0 = x_j$, so $v_j=x_j$.
- If $y_j=1$: gives decremented unary value.

## 6. Connection with max/min viewpoint

Since unary numbers are of the form $2^k-1$, we have:

- $x \,|\, z = \max_{\text{unary}}(x,z)$,
- x \,&\, z = \min_{\text{unary}}(x,z).

The operations above implement:

- increment: combine two unary levels $k$ and $k+1$ via max,
- decrement: combine $k$ and $k-1$ via min.

Thus addition/subtraction of $y_j$ corresponds to shifting the unary “prefix length” by one and then taking max/min structure induced by bitwise OR/AND.

## Conclusion

Each byte $x_j$ encodes a small integer $k_j$ as a block of $k_j$ ones. Adding or subtracting $y_j \in \{0,1\}$ is implemented by shifting this block left or right and then using:

u_j = (x_j \;|\; (x_j \ll y_j)) \& 255, \qquad v_j = x_j \,&\, (x_j \gg y_j),

which exactly realize the given formulas

$$
2^{\min(8,k_j+y_j)}-1,\quad 2^{\max(0,k_j-y_j)}-1.
$$
