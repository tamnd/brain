**Exercise 70.** &#9654; [*31*] (Guy L. Steele Jr., 1994.) What's a good way to compute the masks $\theta_0$, $\theta_1$, $\ldots$, $\theta_{d-1}$ that are needed in the general compression procedure (80), given $\chi \ne 0$?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 1m01s

## Setup

Let $\chi \ne 0$ be a mask. The compression procedure (80) requires masks $\theta_0, \theta_1, \ldots, \theta_{d-1}$ such that the $\theta_k$ are pairwise disjoint, their bitwise OR equals $\chi$, and each $\theta_k$ selects exactly those bit positions of $\chi$ that are destined for the $k$th compressed position in (80). In particular, the construction must partition the set of 1-bits of $\chi$ into $d$ disjoint classes in increasing order of significance, using only bitwise operations.

The task is to compute such a family ${\theta_k}$ from $\chi$.

## Solution

Define a sequence of masks $\chi^{(0)}, \chi^{(1)}, \ldots$ by setting $\chi^{(0)} = \chi$ and, whenever $\chi^{(k)} \ne 0$, defining

$\theta_k = \chi^{(k)} \,\&\, (-\chi^{(k)}),$

and then

$\chi^{(k+1)} = \chi^{(k)} \oplus \theta_k.$

The expression $\chi^{(k)} ,&, (-\chi^{(k)})$ isolates the least significant 1-bit of $\chi^{(k)}$, since $-\chi^{(k)} = \overline{\chi^{(k)} - 1}$ by (17), and thus $\chi^{(k)} ,&, (-\chi^{(k)})$ preserves exactly the lowest position where $\chi^{(k)}$ has a 1 and clears all higher bits. Hence each $\theta_k$ contains exactly one bit position of $\chi^{(k)}$.

The update $\chi^{(k+1)} = \chi^{(k)} \oplus \theta_k$ removes that bit from $\chi^{(k)}$ while leaving all other bits unchanged, since $\theta_k$ has exactly one 1-bit and is disjoint from $\chi^{(k)} \oplus \theta_k$ at that position. Therefore the set of 1-bits strictly decreases at each step, and the process terminates after finitely many steps with $\chi^{(d)} = 0$.

Each $\theta_k$ is nonzero until termination, and the construction guarantees pairwise disjointness because every step removes the unique bit selected at that step from all subsequent masks. Consequently,

$\chi = \theta_0 \mid \theta_1 \mid \cdots \mid \theta_{d-1}.$

The ordering is determined by increasing significance of the isolated bits, since each step extracts the least significant remaining 1-bit.

Thus the masks $\theta_k$ required in (80) are obtained by iteratively extracting the lowest 1-bit of the current residual mask and removing it.

This completes the construction of $\theta_0, \theta_1, \ldots, \theta_{d-1}$. ∎

## Verification

At each stage, $\theta_k ,&, \theta_{k'} = 0$ for $k \ne k'$ because $\theta_k$ is removed from $\chi^{(k)}$ before forming $\chi^{(k+1)}$. The identity $\chi^{(k+1)} = \chi^{(k)} \oplus \theta_k$ ensures no other bits are altered, so previously chosen masks remain unchanged.

Termination follows because each step eliminates at least one 1-bit of $\chi^{(k)}$, and $\chi$ has finitely many 1-bits in its binary representation. Hence the process yields exactly $d$ masks covering all 1-bits of $\chi$.

## Notes

The same construction can be described as repeatedly applying the operation “extract lowest set bit” to a running residual mask. In implementations, $\chi ,&, (-\chi)$ is the standard idiom for this extraction, and $\oplus$ serves as a safe removal operation because the extracted bit is guaranteed not to overlap with any other active bits in the current mask.
