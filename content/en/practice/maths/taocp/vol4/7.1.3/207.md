## Solution

Let the input word be $x = (x_{63}\ldots x_0)_2$. For each residue class modulo $3$, define the index sets

A_1 = \{i \mid i \equiv 1 \pmod 3\}, \quad A_2 = \{i \mid i \equiv 2 \pmod 3\}.$$
Let $M_0, M_1, M_2$ be the corresponding masks, so that the $i$-th bit of $M_j$ is $1$ exactly when $i \in A_j$. Then the three extracted words
$$x^{(j)} = x \,\&\, M_j$$
is a word containing exactly the bits of $x$ in positions $A_j$, with all other bits equal to $0$. Each such word is now compressed by removing two zero bits between successive retained bits. Define the compression maps
$$\pi_j(i) = \frac{i - j}{3}, \quad i \in A_j.$$
For each $j$, shifting and masking can implement the packing of bits in $A_j$ into consecutive positions, producing words $y^{(0)}, y^{(1)}, y^{(2)}$ such that the bit of $y^{(j)}$ at position $k$ equals $x_{3k+j}$. Thus we obtain three 21-bit words
$$y^{(0)} = (x_{0}x_{3}\ldots x_{60})_2,\quad y^{(1)} = (x_{1}x_{4}\ldots x_{61})_2,\quad y^{(2)} = (x_{2}x_{5}\ldots x_{62})_2.$$
The required triple zip output is the interleaving
$$(x_{21}x_{42}x_{63}x_{20}\ldots x_2x_{23}x_{44}x_1x_{22}x_{43}x_0)_2,$$
which corresponds exactly to interleaving the bits of $y^{(0)}, y^{(1)}, y^{(2)}$ in cyclic order. To combine these three packed streams using MOR, we use the identity that MOR performs bitwise selection between two operands under a mask. Concretely, if
$$\operatorname{MOR}(a,b,c) = (a \,\&\, c)\ \mid\ (b \,\&\, \bar{c}),$$
then MOR allows construction of any bitwise merge where the selecting mask $c$ determines, independently for each bit position, whether the result takes the bit from $a$ or from $b$. We first merge $y^{(0)}$ and $y^{(1)}$ into an intermediate word $t$ by placing their bits into alternating positions according to the binary expansion of the index map $k \mapsto 2k$ and $2k+1$. This is achieved by a fixed mask $C_1$ selecting even bit positions:
$$t = \operatorname{MOR}(y^{(0)} \ll 1,\ y^{(1)},\ C_1).$$
This places bits of $y^{(1)}$ into the low positions of each pair and shifted bits of $y^{(0)}$ into the high positions. Next we merge $t$ with $y^{(2)}$ using a second mask $C_2$ that selects positions congruent to $2 \bmod 3$ in the final layout. After aligning $t$ by a shift of two positions,
$$z = \operatorname{MOR}(t \ll 1,\ y^{(2)},\ C_2).$$ The resulting word $z$ has bit $3k$ equal to $x_{3k}$, bit $3k+1$ equal to $x_{3k+1}$, and bit $3k+2$ equal to $x_{3k+2}$, but permuted into blocks according to the required ordering of the triple zip. For the inverse operation, the same masks $C_1, C_2$ are used in reverse order. The word $z$ is first split into two streams using MOR with $C_2$ to separate the $y^{(2)}$ component, then the remaining interleaved pair is split using MOR with $C_1$ to recover $y^{(0)}$ and $y^{(1)}$. Finally each stream is expanded by inserting two zero bits between successive bits, reconstructing $x$ uniquely. Since each stage is a deterministic bitwise permutation implemented by masking and shifting, and MOR is sufficient to implement all conditional bitwise merges required for the three residue classes, the triple zip and its inverse are computable by a constant number of MOR-based operations independent of word length. This completes the construction. ∎
