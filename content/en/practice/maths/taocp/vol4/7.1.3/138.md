We restart from the formal structure used in TAOCP Section 7.1.3.

A three-state-to-two-bit encoding chooses an injective map

$$
\varphi:\{a,b,c\}\to \{0,1\}^2.
$$

Write the two coordinate projections as $\pi_1,\pi_2$, and for each symbol $x\in\{a,b,c\}$ define its coordinate bits

$$
\varphi(x)=(x_1,x_2).
$$

## 1. What class $V_a$ means in this setting

In Knuth’s model for this section, a key distinction between classes is whether the encoding allows the two bit positions to be used independently when implementing arbitrary binary operations on $\{a,b,c\}$.

Class $V_a$ is characterized by the existence of an ordering of the symbols such that the induced subsets

$$
S_i^{(k)}=\{x\in\{a,b,c\} : x_k = i\}, \quad k\in\{1,2\},\ i\in\{0,1\},
$$

are nested in the sense that, after possibly complementing coordinates and relabeling symbols, one coordinate refines the other:

$$
S_1^{(1)} \subset S_1^{(2)} \quad \text{or the reverse}.
$$

Equivalently (in this 3-element, 2-bit case), $V_a$ consists exactly of those encodings where the three points in $\{0,1\}^2$ lie on a monotone chain in the coordinatewise partial order.

This is because such encodings permit one bit to act as a refinement of the other, so symbol selection can be decomposed hierarchically rather than requiring interaction between coordinates.

## 2. Classification of all 3-point subsets of $\{0,1\}^2$

Up to symmetry of $\{0,1\}^2$, there are only two types of 3-element subsets:

1. **Chain type** (totally ordered under coordinatewise $\le$):

$$
00 \le 01 \le 11 \quad \text{(or symmetric variants)}
$$
2. **Non-chain type** (an antichain pattern):

$$
00,\ 01,\ 10.
$$

Only the first type has the refinement property required for $V_a$.

## 3. Construction of a $V_a$ encoding

Take the chain encoding

$$
a=00,\qquad b=01,\qquad c=11.
$$

Then the coordinate subsets are

$$
S_1^{(1)}=\{c\}, \qquad S_1^{(2)}=\{b,c\},
$$

hence

$$
S_1^{(1)} \subset S_1^{(2)}.
$$

Thus coordinate 1 is a refinement of coordinate 2 (or equivalently, coordinate 2 coarsens coordinate 1 after relabeling). This places the encoding in class $V_a$ by definition.

## 4. Why this encoding is in $V_a$

Because of the nesting

$$
\{c\} \subset \{b,c\} \subset \{a,b,c\},
$$

each symbol can be identified by a hierarchical test:

- the second bit distinguishes $\{a\}$ from $\{b,c\}$,
- the first bit refines only the subset $\{b,c\}$.

Formally, every indicator function $[x = a], [x = b], [x = c]$ can be written so that one coordinate partitions the domain and the other only refines a single part of that partition. No test requires simultaneous use of both bits in an essential way.

This is exactly the structural condition defining $V_a$: one coordinate system suffices as a coarse selector, and the second acts only as a refinement.

## 5. Why non-chain encodings are not in $V_a$

Consider any non-chain encoding, for example

$$
a=00,\quad b=01,\quad c=10.
$$

Here the subsets

$$
S_1^{(1)}=\{b\}, \qquad S_1^{(2)}=\{c\}
$$

are incomparable:

$$
S_1^{(1)} \nsubseteq S_1^{(2)}, \qquad S_1^{(2)} \nsubseteq S_1^{(1)}.
$$

Thus neither coordinate refines the other.

Consequently, the indicator of a single symbol, say $[x=b]$, cannot be expressed using a single coordinate test plus refinement. Any representation must distinguish $b$ from both $a$ and $c$ using different coordinates simultaneously. This forces essential coupling between the two bit positions in at least one coordinate function when implementing arbitrary binary operations.

Hence such encodings do not satisfy the structural condition for $V_a$.

## 6. Optimality: why $V_a$ is best

We now show that the chain encoding achieves the minimal possible structural complexity among all three-state-to-two-bit encodings.

The key observation is that in any encoding, the three codewords in $\{0,1\}^2$ induce a poset under coordinatewise order. There are only two possibilities:

- **Chain case:** the three points are totally ordered. Then one coordinate refines the other, giving maximal decomposability of symbol tests.
- **Non-chain case:** the points contain an antichain of size 2, forcing incomparability of coordinate partitions.

Only in the chain case can the indicator functions of symbols be generated by a hierarchical (refinement) structure. In the non-chain case, no coordinate can be eliminated or subordinated to the other, so every implementation of a general binary operation must, in the worst case, combine both coordinates in at least one essential decision.

Thus:

- Chain encodings minimize cross-coordinate interaction,
- Non-chain encodings force unavoidable interaction in some binary operations,
- Therefore the chain encoding achieves the best possible class, namely $V_a$.

## 7. Conclusion

The encoding

$$
a=00,\quad b=01,\quad c=11
$$

lies in class $V_a$ because its coordinate projections form a chain of nested subsets, giving a hierarchical refinement structure.

No non-chain encoding admits such refinement, and those encodings necessarily require cross-coordinate interaction in implementing arbitrary binary operations.

Therefore this encoding realizes class $V_a$, and since all other encodings fall into strictly less structured cases, class $V_a$ is best.

$$
\boxed{a=00,\ b=01,\ c=11 \text{ is an example where class } V_a \text{ is best.}}
$$
