**Exercise 101.** &#9654; [*22*] Two octabytes $x$ and $y$ contain amounts of time, represented in five fields that respectively signify days (3 bytes), hours (1 byte), minutes (1 byte), seconds (1 byte), and milliseconds (2 bytes). Can you add and subtract them quickly, without converting from this mixed-radix representation to binary and back again?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 57s  

---

## Solution

The five fields occupy disjoint bit positions inside the octabyte. Let the field widths in bytes be $3,1,1,1,2$, so the corresponding bit widths are $24,8,8,8,16$. Define the masks

$$
A=(2^{24}-1)\ll 40,\qquad
B=255\ll 32,\qquad
C=255\ll 24,
$$

$$
D=255\ll 16,\qquad
E=2^{16}-1 .
$$

For a time value $x$, the fields are extracted by

$$
x_D=x\mathbin{\&}A,\quad
x_H=x\mathbin{\&}B,\quad
x_M=x\mathbin{\&}C,\quad
x_S=x\mathbin{\&}D,\quad
x_T=x\mathbin{\&}E .
$$

The same definitions apply to $y$.

To add $x$ and $y$, first add the two least significant fields. The milliseconds field has radix $2^{16}$, so the carry into seconds is

$$
c_S=(x_T+y_T)\gg16 .
$$

The new milliseconds field is

$$
u_T=(x_T+y_T)\mathbin{\&}E .
$$

The seconds field must include this carry. Hence

$$
s=x_S+y_S+c_S .
$$

The carry into minutes is

$$
c_M=s\gg8 ,
$$

and the seconds field is

$$
u_S=s\mathbin{\&}D .
$$

Continuing with the same mixed-radix rule gives

$$
m=x_M+y_M+c_M ,
$$

$$
c_H=m\gg8 ,
$$

$$
u_M=m\mathbin{\&}C ,
$$

$$
h=x_H+y_H+c_H ,
$$

$$
c_D=h\gg8 ,
$$

$$
u_H=h\mathbin{\&}B ,
$$

and finally

$$
u_D=(x_D+y_D+c_D)\mathbin{\&}A .
$$

The complete sum is therefore

$$
u=u_D\mid u_H\mid u_M\mid u_S\mid u_T .
$$

Each step uses only bitwise extraction, shifts, additions, and masks. No conversion to an ordinary binary time value occurs.

For subtraction, the same procedure is applied with borrows instead of carries. The least significant field gives the initial borrow. Since a field of width $k$ bits represents a residue modulo $2^k$, the borrow from milliseconds is

$$
b_S=[x_T<y_T].
$$

The milliseconds result is

$$
v_T=(x_T-y_T)\mathbin{\&}E .
$$

The seconds difference is

$$
s=x_S-y_S-b_S .
$$

The borrow into minutes is

$$
b_M=[s<0],
$$

and

$$
v_S=s\mathbin{\&}D .
$$

Proceeding upward,

$$
m=x_M-y_M-b_M ,
$$

$$
b_H=[m<0],
$$

$$
v_M=m\mathbin{\&}C ,
$$

$$
h=x_H-y_H-b_H ,
$$

$$
b_D=[h<0],
$$

$$
v_H=h\mathbin{\&}B ,
$$

and

$$
v_D=(x_D-y_D-b_D)\mathbin{\&}A .
$$

The final difference is

$$
v=v_D\mid v_H\mid v_M\mid v_S\mid v_T .
$$

The masks guarantee that every field remains in its original position, and the carry or borrow variables account exactly for the radix change between adjacent fields. Therefore both addition and subtraction are performed directly in the mixed-radix representation.

$$
\boxed{\text{Addition and subtraction are possible using only masks, shifts, and ordinary integer operations.}}
$$
