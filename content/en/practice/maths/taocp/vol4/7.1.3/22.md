## Solution

Let $x < 2^{64}$. Gosper’s construction computes the next integer with the same number of $1$-bits in binary representation. Define

$$
u = x \,\&\, (-x), \qquad v = x + u, \qquad t = v \oplus x.
$$

The quantity $u$ is a power of two, say $u = 2^k$, where $k$ is the index of the least significant $1$-bit of $x$. The term $t = v \oplus x$ consists of a contiguous block of $1$-bits starting at position $k+1$, and the expression $(t / u) \gg 2$ equals $t \gg (k+2)$.

Thus the final result is

$$
y = v + \bigl(t \gg (k+2)\bigr).
$$

The only nontrivial operation is determining $k = \nu_2(u)$ without division or built-in trailing-zero instructions. This is achieved using a de Bruijn sequence.

Let $D$ be a 64-bit de Bruijn constant and let $T[0 \ldots 63]$ be the corresponding lookup table such that

$$
T[(u \cdot D) \gg 58] = k
$$

for every power of two $u = 2^k$ in the 64-bit range. Then $k$ is obtained using one multiplication, one shift, and one table lookup, after which all remaining operations are shifts and additions.

The MMIX implementation is as follows.

```
        LOC     Data_Segment
D       OCTA    #022fdd63cc95386d   % de Bruijn constant (64-bit)
T       OCTA    0, 1, 2, 53, 3, 7, 54, 27
        OCTA    4, 38, 41, 8, 34, 55, 48, 28
        OCTA    62, 5, 39, 46, 44, 42, 22, 9
        OCTA    24, 35, 59, 56, 49, 18, 29, 11
        OCTA    63, 52, 6, 26, 37, 40, 33, 47
        OCTA    61, 45, 43, 21, 23, 58, 17, 10
        OCTA    51, 25, 36, 32, 60, 20, 57, 16
        OCTA    50, 31, 19, 15, 30, 14, 13, 12

        LOC     #100
        GREG    @

x       IS      $0
u       IS      $1
v       IS      $2
t       IS      $3
k       IS      $4
tmp     IS      $5
y       IS      $6

        LDO     u,x,0              % u = x & (-x) computed via two's complement
        NEG     tmp,u             % tmp = -u
        AND
```
