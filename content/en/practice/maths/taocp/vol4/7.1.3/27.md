**Exercise 27.** [*21*] In the notation of Eqs. (32)–(43), how would you compute (a) $(\alpha\,10^*01^k)_2$? (b) $(\alpha\,10^*11^k)_2$? (c) $(\alpha 00^*01^k)_2$? (d) $(0^\infty 11^*00^k)_2$? (e) $(0^\infty 01^*00^k)_2$? (f) $(0^\infty 11^*11^k)_2$?

**Solve time:** 2m25s

Let concatenation be interpreted as binary string concatenation. For a finite binary string $x$, write $|x|$ for its length and $(x)_2$ for its value. Then

$$
(\alpha x)_2 = \alpha \cdot 2^{|x|} + (x)_2.
$$

Starred blocks are interpreted as Kleene stars: $0^*$ means $0^t$ for some $t \ge 0$, and similarly $1^*$ means $1^t$.

## (a) $(\alpha\,10^*01^k)_2$

Write $0^* = 0^t$. Then the suffix is

$$
10^t01^k = 1\,0^t\,0\,1^k.
$$

Its length is $t + k + 2$.

Its value is:

- the leading $1$ contributes $2^{t+k+1}$,
- the block $0^t01^k$ contributes $0^t01^k = 2^k - 1$.

Thus

$$
(10^t01^k)_2 = 2^{t+k+1} + (2^k - 1).
$$

So

$$
(\alpha\,10^t01^k)_2
= \alpha \cdot 2^{t+k+2} + 2^{t+k+1} + (2^k - 1).
$$

## (b) $(\alpha\,10^*11^k)_2$

Write $0^* = 0^t$. Then

$$
10^t11^k = 1\,0^t\,1^{k+1}.
$$

Length is $t+k+2$.

Value:

- leading $1$: $2^{t+k+1}$,
- trailing $1^{k+1}$: $2^{k+1} - 1$.

Hence

$$
(10^t11^k)_2 = 2^{t+k+1} + (2^{k+1} - 1).
$$

Therefore

$$
(\alpha\,10^t11^k)_2
= \alpha \cdot 2^{t+k+2} + 2^{t+k+1} + 2^{k+1} - 1.
$$

## (c) $(\alpha\,00^*01^k)_2$

Write $0^* = 0^t$. Then

$$
00^*01^k = 0^{t+2}1^k.
$$

This is $t+2$ zeros followed by $k$ ones, so its value is simply

$$
(00^*01^k)_2 = 2^k - 1.
$$

Its length is $t+k+2$. Therefore

$$
(\alpha\,00^*01^k)_2
= \alpha \cdot 2^{t+k+2} + (2^k - 1).
$$

## (d) $(0^\infty 11^*00^k)_2$

Because of $0^\infty$, only the finite suffix matters.

Write $1^* = 1^t$. Then

$$
11^*00^k = 1\,1^t\,0^{k+1} = 1^{t+2}0^{k+1}.
$$

This is $t+2$ ones followed by $k+1$ zeros.

Hence

$$
(11^t00^k)_2 = (2^{t+2} - 1)\cdot 2^{k+1}.
$$

So

$$
(0^\infty 11^*00^k)_2 = (2^{t+2} - 1)\cdot 2^{k+1}.
$$

## (e) $(0^\infty 01^*00^k)_2$

Write $1^* = 1^t$. Then

$$
01^*00^k = 0\,1^t\,0^{k+1}.
$$

The leading 0 does not affect value, so this is equivalent to

$$
1^t0^{k+1}.
$$

Thus

$$
(01^t00^k)_2 = (2^t - 1)\cdot 2^{k+1}.
$$

So

$$
(0^\infty 01^*00^k)_2 = (2^t - 1)\cdot 2^{k+1}.
$$

## (f) $(0^\infty 11^*11^k)_2$

Write $1^* = 1^t$. Then

$$
11^*11^k = 1\,1^t\,1^{k+1} = 1^{t+k+2}.
$$

A block of $m$ ones has value $2^m - 1$. Hence

$$
(11^*11^k)_2 = 2^{t+k+2} - 1.
$$

Therefore

$$
(0^\infty 11^*11^k)_2 = 2^{t+k+2} - 1.
$$

## Summary of corrections

- (b): trailing block is $1^{k+1}$, not mixed powers.
- (d): the string has $t+2$ leading ones and $k+1$ trailing zeros.
- (f): total number of ones is $t+k+2$, not $t+k+1$.

All results now follow directly from correct block expansion and standard binary evaluation rules.
