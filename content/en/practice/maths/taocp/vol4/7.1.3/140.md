**Exercise 140.** [*27*] [27] Design an economical full adder for balanced ternary numbers: Show how
to compute signed bits u and v such that 3u + v = x+ y + z when x, y, z ∈{0, +1, −1}.

## Setup

Let $x,y,z \in {-1,0,1}$. Define

$s = x + y + z,$

so $s \in {-3,-2,-1,0,1,2,3}$.

We seek signed bits $u,v \in {-1,0,1}$ such that

$3u + v = s.$

Equivalently, we must define a deterministic mapping from $s$ to $(u,v)$ satisfying this identity for every possible value of $s$.

## Solution

Partition the range of $s$ according to whether its magnitude exceeds $1$.

Define $u$ by

$$
u =
\begin{cases}
1, & s \ge 2,\\
-1, & s \le -2,\\
0, & -1 \le s \le 1.
\end{cases}
$$

Define $v$ by

$$
v = s - 3u.
$$

We verify that this construction is well-defined in the sense that $v \in {-1,0,1}$ for every admissible $s$.

For each possible value of $s$:

When $s=3$, the definition gives $u=1$, hence $v = 3 - 3 = 0$, and $3u+v=3$.

When $s=2$, the definition gives $u=1$, hence $v = 2 - 3 = -1$, and $3u+v=2$.

When $s=1$, the definition gives $u=0$, hence $v = 1$, and $3u+v=1$.

When $s=0$, the definition gives $u=0$, hence $v = 0$, and $3u+v=0$.

When $s=-1$, the definition gives $u=0$, hence $v = -1$, and $3u+v=-1$.

When $s=-2$, the definition gives $u=-1$, hence $v = -2 + 3 = 1$, and $3u+v=-2$.

When $s=-3$, the definition gives $u=-1$, hence $v = -3 + 3 = 0$, and $3u+v=-3$.

In every case $v$ lies in ${-1,0,1}$ and satisfies $3u+v=s$.

Thus $u$ and $v$ form a valid balanced-ternary decomposition of the sum $x+y+z$.

This completes the proof. ∎

## Verification

For each $s \in {-3,-2,-1,0,1,2,3}$, the construction assigns a unique pair $(u,v)$ and directly satisfies $v=s-3u$, so $3u+v=s$ holds identically. The case analysis covers the full domain of $s$, so no additional values occur outside the verified set.

## Notes

The construction is equivalent to selecting the nearest multiple of $3$ in ${-3,0,3}$ as $3u$, with $v$ as the residual digit constrained to ${-1,0,1}$. This yields a canonical carry rule for balanced ternary addition of three digits, and it is the minimal-range adjustment ensuring digit closure under base-$3$ representation.
