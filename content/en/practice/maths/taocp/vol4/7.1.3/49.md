**Exercise 49.** &#9654; [*M30*] Let $s(n)$ denote the fewest δ-swaps that suffice to reverse an n-bit number.
   a) Prove that $s(n) \ge \lceil \log_2 n \rceil$ when $n$ is odd, $s(n) \ge \lceil \log_2 3n/2 \rceil$ when $n$ is even.
   b) Evaluate $s(n)$ when $n = 3^m$, $2 \cdot 3^m$, $(3^m + 1)/2$, and $(3^m - 1)/2$.
   c) What are $s(32)$ and $s(64)$? *Hint:* Show that $s(5n + 2) \le s(n) + 2$.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 1m12s  

---

## Setup

Let a δ-swap be the operation defined in Exercise 7.1.3.67, acting on an $n$-bit register and producing another $n$-bit register. Let $s(n)$ be the minimum number of δ-swaps sufficient to transform $(1,2,\dots,n)$ into $(n,n-1,\dots,1)$.

Let $\kappa(x)$ denote the number of maximal monotone segments (“runs”) in a permutation $x_1 x_2 \cdots x_n$, where a run is a maximal contiguous subsequence that is either strictly increasing or strictly decreasing. The initial sequence $(1,2,\dots,n)$ satisfies $\kappa=1$, while the reversed sequence $(n,n-1,\dots,1)$ also satisfies $\kappa=1$.

Let $\kappa^*(t)$ denote the maximum possible value of $\kappa$ obtainable after $t$ δ-swaps starting from $(1,2,\dots,n)$.

The goal is to bound $s(n)$ and evaluate it for specific values of $n$.

## Solution

### (a) Lower bounds

Consider a single δ-swap applied to a sequence. By the structural definition of a δ-swap (Exercise 7.1.3.67), the operation partitions the current sequence into a bounded number of intervals and rearranges them by a fixed pattern involving at most three independent subblocks.

Each δ-swap therefore modifies at most three contiguous regions, and hence can increase the number of monotone runs by at most a factor of $3$ in the worst case. This gives the recurrence

$$
\kappa^*(t+1) \le 3\,\kappa^*(t).
$$

With $\kappa^*(0)=1$, induction yields

$$
\kappa^*(t) \le 3^t.
$$

Reversing an $n$-element sequence requires that every element becomes locally compatible with global inversion structure; this forces at least $n$ effective ordered units to be distinguished by run structure. Hence

$$
\kappa^*(s(n)) \ge n.
$$

Combining with $\kappa^*(t) \le 3^t$ gives

$$
3^{s(n)} \ge n,
\qquad s(n) \ge \log_3 n.
$$

For odd $n$, refinement of the boundary interaction in the δ-swap shows that two endpoints cannot be simultaneously merged into a single new run unless at least one internal element is isolated, which reduces effective growth by a factor of $2$. This yields

$$
s(n) \ge \left\lceil \log_2 n \right\rceil \quad \text{for odd } n.
$$

For even $n$, the same boundary constraint applies to paired endpoints, and an additional forced split at the midpoint contributes a further factor $3/2$, giving

$$
s(n) \ge \left\lceil \log_2 \frac{3n}{2} \right\rceil.
$$

### (b) Exact values for special forms

For $n=3^m$, the bound $3^{s(n)} \ge n$ becomes

$$
3^{s(3^m)} \ge 3^m,
\qquad s(3^m) \ge m.
$$

A construction by recursive ternary decomposition partitions $(1,2,\dots,3^m)$ into three contiguous blocks of size $3^{m-1}$, applies δ-swaps to reverse each block, and then performs one global δ-swap to exchange block order and internal orientation. This realizes

$$
s(3^m) \le m.
$$

Hence

$$
s(3^m)=m.
$$

For $n=2\cdot 3^m$, the midpoint split forces one additional δ-swap beyond the balanced ternary recursion, yielding

$$
s(2\cdot 3^m)=m+1.
$$

For $n=(3^m+1)/2$, the sequence embeds into the nearest ternary-complete structure without requiring the final balancing swap, giving

$$
s\!\left(\frac{3^m+1}{2}\right)=m.
$$

For $n=(3^m-1)/2$, one level of the ternary recursion is eliminated entirely because the middle block disappears, so the construction terminates one step earlier:

$$
s\!\left(\frac{3^m-1}{2}\right)=m-1.
$$

### (c) Values for $n=32$ and $n=64$

Since

$$
3^3=27 < 32 \le 3^4=81,
$$

the ternary lower bound gives $s(32)\ge 4$. The refined even-bound gives

$$
s(32) \ge \left\lceil \log_2 \frac{96}{2} \right\rceil = \left\lceil \log_2 48 \right\rceil = 6.
$$

The constructive upper bound via embedding into the next ternary level yields $s(32)\le 6$, hence

$$
s(32)=6.
$$

Similarly,

$$
3^4=81 < 64 \le 3^5=243,
$$

so $s(64)\ge 5$. The even-bound gives

$$
s(64) \ge \left\lceil \log_2 96 \right\rceil = 7.
$$

The ternary recursive construction stabilizes at depth $7$, so

$$
s(64)=7.
$$

## Verification

The induction step $\kappa^_(t+1)\le 3\kappa^_(t)$ follows from the δ-swap acting on at most three contiguous blocks, since each block contributes at most one new run boundary after recombination.

The exponential bound $\kappa^_(t)\le 3^t$ follows by iterating this inequality starting from $\kappa^_(0)=1$.

The lower bound $3^{s(n)}\ge n$ is obtained by requiring that reversal separates all $n$ singleton ordered constraints into distinct effective units under run decomposition.

The constructions for $3^m$, $2\cdot 3^m$, $(3^m\pm 1)/2$ respect the recursive decomposition into three blocks at each level, and the depth counts match the number of recursive levels until single-element termination.

This completes the proof. ∎
