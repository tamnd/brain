**Exercise 25.** &#9654; [*15*] Four volumes sit side by side on a bookshelf. Each of them contains exactly 500 pages, printed on 250 sheets of paper 0.1 mm thick; each book also has a front and back cover whose thicknesses are 1 mm each. A bookworm gnaws its way from page 1 of Volume 1 to page 500 of Volume 4. How far does it travel while doing so?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m08s  

---

## Solution

Each volume contains $500$ pages printed on $250$ sheets. Each sheet is $0.1\ \mathrm{mm}$ thick, so the paper block in one volume has thickness

$$
250 \cdot 0.1 = 25\ \mathrm{mm}.
$$

Each book also has a front cover and a back cover, each of thickness $1\ \mathrm{mm}$.

The bookworm starts at page $1$ of Volume $1$. Page $1$ lies on the first sheet inside the front cover, so the front cover of Volume $1$ is not traversed. The worm ends at page $500$ of Volume $4$, which lies on the last sheet immediately before the back cover, so the back cover of Volume $4$ is not traversed.

Within each volume, the worm traverses the full paper block, contributing

$$
4 \cdot 25 = 100\ \mathrm{mm}.
$$

Between consecutive volumes, the worm passes through the interface consisting of the back cover of the left volume and the front cover of the right volume. Each such interface contributes

$$
1 + 1 = 2\ \mathrm{mm}.
$$

There are three such interfaces between four volumes, hence their total contribution is

$$
3 \cdot 2 = 6\ \mathrm{mm}.
$$

Adding all contributions gives the total distance

$$
100 + 6 = 106\ \mathrm{mm}.
$$

$$
\boxed{106\ \mathrm{mm}}
$$
