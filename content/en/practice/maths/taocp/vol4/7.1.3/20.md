**Exercise 20.** &#9654; [*21*] [21] *(Gosper's hack.)* The following seven operations produce a useful function $y$ of $x$, when $x$ is a positive integer. Explain what this function is and why it is useful.

$$u \leftarrow x \,\&\, -x; \qquad v \leftarrow x + u; \qquad y \leftarrow v + (((v \oplus x)/u) \gg 2).$$

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m50s  

---

Let $x>0$. Define

$$
u = x \,\&\, (-x),
$$

so $u = 2^t$, where $t$ is the index of the least significant $1$-bit of $x$. Hence the correct structural fact is:

- bits $0,1,\dots,t-1$ of $x$ are $0$,
- bit $t$ is $1$.

Above bit $t$, there may be a consecutive run of $1$s. Let this run have length $k+1$, starting at position $t$. Thus there exists $k \ge 0$ such that

$$
x = (\cdots a\,0\,\underbrace{1\cdots 1}_{k+1\ \text{bits}}\,\underbrace{0\cdots 0}_{t\ \text{bits}})_2,
$$

where:

- the lowest $t$ bits are $0$,
- bits $t,t+1,\dots,t+k$ are all $1$,
- bit $t+k+1$ is $0$,
- $a$ denotes the higher prefix.

### Step 1: Compute $v = x+u$

We add $u=2^t$ to $x$. This flips bit $t$ from $1$ to $0$ and propagates a carry through the entire block of consecutive $1$s above it.

- Bit $t$: $1 \to 0$, carry continues.
- Bits $t+1,\dots,t+k$: each $1 \to 0$.
- Bit $t+k+1$: $0 \to 1$, carry stops.

Thus

$$
v = (\cdots a\,1\,\underbrace{0\cdots 0}_{k+1\ \text{bits}}\,\underbrace{0\cdots 0}_{t\ \text{bits}})_2.
$$

So the block of $k+1$ ones has been replaced by a single $1$ one position higher.

### Step 2: Compute $v \oplus x$

Comparing $x$ and $v$:

- At positions $t,\dots,t+k$: $x$ has $1$, $v$ has $0$,
- At position $t+k+1$: $x$ has $0$, $v$ has $1$.

Hence

$$
v \oplus x = (\underbrace{1\cdots 1}_{k+2\ \text{bits}} \ll t)
= (2^{k+2}-1)\,2^t.
$$

### Step 3: Divide by $u$

Since $u=2^t$,

$$
\frac{v \oplus x}{u} = 2^{k+2}-1.
$$

### Step 4: Shift right by 2

$$
\left(\frac{v \oplus x}{u}\right)\gg 2
= \frac{2^{k+2}-1}{4}
= 2^k - 1.
$$

This is an integer because $2^{k+2}-1 = 4(2^k-1)+3$, and the low-order structure ensures the shift corresponds exactly to truncation of the two least significant bits in this construction.

### Step 5: Compute $y$

$$
y = v + (2^k - 1).
$$

Now observe what this does in binary:

- In $v$, the lowest $t+k+1$ bits are all $0$,
- we add $2^k-1 = (k \text{ ones})_2$, placed starting at bit $0$.

Thus we insert $k$ ones into the lowest available positions.

So $y$ has:

- the same high prefix as $x$,
- the same number of $1$-bits as $x$,
- and is the smallest integer larger than $x$ with that property.

## Final characterization

The function computed is:

$$
y = \text{next integer after } x \text{ with the same number of } 1\text{-bits in binary}.
$$

Equivalently, it is the **next lexicographic binary combination of the same weight**.

## Why this is useful (Gosper’s hack)

This sequence generates the next subset of a fixed-size set in colexicographic (binary counting by weight) order.

If integers are interpreted as characteristic vectors of subsets of $\{0,\dots,n-1\}$, then:

- popcount($x$) is subset size,
- the operation computes the next subset of the same size.

Thus Gosper’s hack allows efficient enumeration of all $k$-element subsets of an $n$-element set using only a few bit operations per step, without branching or searching.

This is widely used in combinatorial generation and bitmask dynamic programming.
