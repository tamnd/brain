**Exercise 5.** [*M21*] Prove or disprove the following conjectured laws concerning binary shifts:
a) $(x \ll j) \ll k = x \ll (j + k)$;
b) $(x \gg j) \mathbin{\&} (y \ll k) = ((x \gg (j + k)) \mathbin{\&} y) \ll k = (x \mathbin{\&} (y \ll (j + k))) \gg j$.

## Solution

Recall the TAOCP definition of binary shifts:

$$
x\ll j=\lfloor 2^j x\rfloor,\qquad j\in\mathbb Z,
$$

where $x$ is an integer. Positive $j$ denotes a left shift and negative $j$ denotes a right shift.

### (a)

We prove that

$$
(x\ll j)\ll k=x\ll(j+k)
$$

for all integers $j,k$.

By definition,

$$
(x\ll j)\ll k
=
\left\lfloor
2^k\lfloor2^j x\rfloor
\right\rfloor.
$$

The proof is based on two elementary identities.

#### Lemma 1

For every real number $t$,

$$
\left\lfloor\frac{\lfloor t\rfloor}{2}\right\rfloor
=
\left\lfloor\frac t2\right\rfloor.
$$

**Proof.**

Write

$$
n=\lfloor t\rfloor.
$$

Then

$$
n\le t<n+1.
$$

Dividing by $2$,

$$
\frac n2\le\frac t2<\frac n2+\frac12.
$$

The interval has length $1/2<1$, so its endpoints have the same integer part. Hence

$$
\left\lfloor\frac t2\right\rfloor
=
\left\lfloor\frac n2\right\rfloor
=
\left\lfloor\frac{\lfloor t\rfloor}{2}\right\rfloor.
$$

This proves the lemma.

#### Lemma 2

For every integer $r\ge0$,

$$
\left\lfloor2^{-r}\lfloor t\rfloor\right\rfloor
=
\lfloor2^{-r}t\rfloor.
$$

**Proof.**

For $r=0$ the identity is immediate.

Assume it holds for $r$. Then

$$
\begin{aligned}
\left\lfloor2^{-(r+1)}\lfloor t\rfloor\right\rfloor
&=
\left\lfloor
\frac12\left(2^{-r}\lfloor t\rfloor\right)
\right\rfloor \\
&=
\left\lfloor
\frac{\lfloor2^{-r}\lfloor t\rfloor\rfloor}{2}
\right\rfloor
\qquad
(\text{since }2^{-r}\lfloor t\rfloor\text{ is real})\\
&=
\left\lfloor
\frac{\lfloor2^{-r}t\rfloor}{2}
\right\rfloor
\qquad
(\text{induction hypothesis})\\
&=
\left\lfloor2^{-(r+1)}t\right\rfloor
\qquad
(\text{Lemma 1}).
\end{aligned}
$$

Thus the identity holds for every $r\ge0$.

Now we prove the shift law.

### Case 1: $k\ge0$

Since $2^k$ is an integer,

$$
\left\lfloor2^k\lfloor2^j x\rfloor\right\rfloor
=
2^k\lfloor2^j x\rfloor.
$$

If $j\ge0$, then $2^j x$ is an integer, so

$$
2^k\lfloor2^j x\rfloor
=
2^{j+k}x
=
\lfloor2^{j+k}x\rfloor.
$$

If $j=-m<0$, then

$$
\begin{aligned}
2^k\lfloor2^{-m}x\rfloor
&=
\lfloor2^k(2^{-m}x)\rfloor,
\end{aligned}
$$

because multiplying by the integer $2^k$ commutes with the floor:

$$
2^k\lfloor a\rfloor=\lfloor2^k a\rfloor.
$$

Therefore

$$
2^k\lfloor2^{-m}x\rfloor
=
\lfloor2^{k-m}x\rfloor
=
\lfloor2^{j+k}x\rfloor.
$$

Thus

$$
(x\ll j)\ll k
=
x\ll(j+k).
$$

### Case 2: $k=-\ell<0$

Then

$$
(x\ll j)\ll k
=
\left\lfloor
2^{-\ell}\lfloor2^j x\rfloor
\right\rfloor.
$$

Applying Lemma 2 with $t=2^j x$,

$$
\left\lfloor
2^{-\ell}\lfloor2^j x\rfloor
\right\rfloor
=
\left\lfloor
2^{j-\ell}x
\right\rfloor
=
\lfloor2^{j+k}x\rfloor.
$$

Hence

$$
(x\ll j)\ll k
=
x\ll(j+k).
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{(x\ll j)\ll k=x\ll(j+k).}
$$

### (b)

Under the TAOCP model, integers are represented by infinite-precision two's-complement bit strings. This bit interpretation is equivalent to the floor definition of shifts:

- the bit in position $i$ of $x\gg j$ is $x_{i+j}$;
- the bit in position $i$ of $x\ll k$ is $x_{i-k}$.

Let

$$
A=(x\gg j)\,\&\,(y\ll k).
$$

The bit in position $i$ is therefore

$$
A_i=x_{i+j}\wedge y_{i-k}.
$$

Now consider

$$
B=((x\gg(j+k))\,\&\,y)\ll k.
$$

Before the final left shift, bit $i-k$ equals

$$
x_{(i-k)+(j+k)}\wedge y_{i-k}
=
x_{i+j}\wedge y_{i-k}.
$$

After shifting left by $k$, this becomes bit $i$. Hence

$$
B_i=x_{i+j}\wedge y_{i-k}=A_i.
$$

Finally consider

$$
C=(x\,\&\,(y\ll(j+k)))\gg j.
$$

Before the final right shift, bit $i+j$ is

$$
x_{i+j}\wedge
y_{(i+j)-(j+k)}
=
x_{i+j}\wedge y_{i-k}.
$$

After shifting right by $j$, this becomes bit $i$. Thus

$$
C_i=x_{i+j}\wedge y_{i-k}=A_i.
$$

Since corresponding bits agree in all three expressions,

$$
\boxed{
(x\gg j)\,\&\,(y\ll k)
=
((x\gg(j+k))\,\&\,y)\ll k
=
(x\,\&\,(y\ll(j+k)))\gg j.
}
$$

Hence both conjectured identities are true.
