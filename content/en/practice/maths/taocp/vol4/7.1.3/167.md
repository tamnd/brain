## 1. Correct reduction of the rule

Let

$$
N = x_{NW}+x_N+x_{NE}+x_W+x_E+x_{SW}+x_S+x_{SE}.
$$

The given condition is

$$
2 < N + \tfrac{1}{2}x < 4.
$$

Multiply by 2:

$$
4 < 2N + x < 8.
$$

Let

$$
T = 2N + x.
$$

Since $T$ is integer,

$$
T \in \{5,6,7\}.
$$

Case analysis:

- If $x=0$, then $T=2N$, so $2N\in\{6\}\Rightarrow N=3$.
- If $x=1$, then $T=2N+1$, so $2N+1\in\{5,7\}\Rightarrow N\in\{2,3\}$.

Hence

$$
f = (N=3)\ \vee\ (x \wedge N=2).
$$

All later work reduces to computing $N$ and testing equality.

## 2. Boolean chain construction (≤ 26 steps)

### Model

A Boolean chain consists of layers. At each step, any number of new variables may be computed, each being an arbitrary Boolean function of variables from earlier steps. Depth is the number of sequential layers.

Thus XOR, majority, etc., are single-step operations once inputs exist.

## 3. Popcount of 8 bits in a correct chain

We compute $N = a_1+\cdots+a_8$.

We use a carry-save tree (3:2 compressors), which is standard in TAOCP-style Boolean circuit depth arguments.

### Step 1: First compression (2 CSA blocks)

Define two carry-save compressors:

From triples:

$$
(a,b,c)\mapsto (s,c') \quad \text{where } s=a\oplus b\oplus c,\; c'=ab\vee ac\vee bc.
$$

Compute in parallel:

- CSA1 on $(a_1,a_2,a_3)\to (s_1,c_1)$
- CSA2 on $(a_4,a_5,a_6)\to (s_2,c_2)$
- For the remaining pair, pad with 0:

$(a_7,a_8,0)\to (s_3,c_3)$

After Step 1 we have:

$$
s_1,s_2,s_3,\quad c_1,c_2,c_3.
$$

Interpretation:

$$
N = (s_1+s_2+s_3) + 2(c_1+c_2+c_3).
$$

### Step 2: Second compression of sums

Now reduce the three $s_i$ again with a CSA:

$$
(s_1,s_2,s_3)\to (s',c_s)
$$

and similarly compress carries:

$$
(c_1,c_2,c_3)\to (c',c_c)
$$

This is valid in one step because all outputs depend only on Step 1 values.

Now:

$$
N = s' + 2c_s + 2c' + 4c_c.
$$

We now have a redundant binary representation with at most 4 weighted bit-positions, each still Boolean-valued.

### Step 3: Final normalization to binary (4-bit value)

We now add the four contributions using a bounded-width adder.

We combine:

- $s'$
- $c'\ll 1$
- $c_s\ll 1$
- $c_c\ll 2$

This is a 4-input addition on 1-bit signals with fixed shifts.

We compute in two layers:

**Step 3a:** pairwise half-add:

- combine $(s',c')$
- combine $(c_s,c_c)$

Each produces sum and carry bits (all Boolean functions of Step 2).

**Step 3b:** final combine of the two results into binary $n_0,n_1,n_2,n_3$.

After Step 3 we have full binary representation of $N$.

So:

$$
N \in \{0,\dots,8\}
$$

is available in **3 Boolean chain steps**.

## 6. Broadword evaluation (≤ 23 steps)

Now each $x_i$ is a full machine word (bitmap). All operations are bitwise across words.

We use standard broadword primitives: bitwise AND, OR, XOR, NOT, and addition. Each counts as one step.

We compute the same 8-way sum in parallel across all bit positions.

## 7. Broadword popcount of 8 one-bit fields

Let $a_1,\dots,a_8$ be words.

### Step 1–2: first CSA layer

Form three carry-save reductions:

- $(a_1,a_2,a_3)\to (s_1,c_1)$

- $(a_7,a_8,0)\to (s_3,c_3)$

- majority

Both are single broadword steps, so:

- Step 1: CSA outputs $s_i$
- Step 2: CSA outputs $c_i$

### Step 3–4: second CSA layer

Compress:

- $s_1,s_2,s_3 \to s'$
