**Exercise 44.** &#9654; [*23*] (E. Freed, 1983.) What's a fast way to evaluate the *weighted* bit sum $\sum j x_j$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m13s  

---

## Solution

Let $x = (\ldots x_2 x_1 x_0)_2$, where each $x_j \in {0,1}$. The target quantity is

$$
W(x) = \sum_{j \ge 0} j x_j.
$$

Each term corresponds to a single set bit of $x$, weighted by its position. The computation is therefore reduced to locating successive $1$-bits and summing their indices.

For any nonzero integer $x$, define

$$
t = x \,\&\, (-x).
$$

By (11) and the two’s complement identity (16), $-x = \bar{x} + 1$, hence $t$ isolates the least significant $1$-bit of $x$. Thus $t = 2^j$ where $j$ is the unique index such that $x_j = 1$ and $x_i = 0$ for $i < j$.

The index $j$ is obtained by repeatedly shifting $t$ right until it becomes $1$. Define $y = t$ and initialize $j = 0$. Each application of $y \gg 1$ decreases the exponent of the unique power of two represented by $y$ by $1$, and after exactly $j$ such shifts one reaches $1$. Therefore $j$ is uniquely determined by $t$.

To remove the processed bit from $x$, use $x \leftarrow x - t$. Since $t$ has exactly one nonzero bit and that bit is present in $x$, subtraction cancels that bit without affecting other positions, so the remaining bits of $x$ are unchanged.

The procedure is thus:

starting with $x$, repeatedly extract $t = x ,&, (-x)$, compute its index $j$ by repeated right shifts of $t$, and accumulate $j$ into the running sum, then replace $x$ by $x - t$. Each iteration removes exactly one $1$-bit of the original $x$, because $t$ contains exactly one nonzero bit and no other bit has that property for the same extraction step.

Termination occurs when $x = 0$, since every step reduces the number of $1$-bits by exactly one. The accumulated sum equals $\sum j x_j$, because every original $1$-bit at position $j$ contributes $j$ exactly once, and no other operation introduces or duplicates contributions.

Hence the procedure evaluates the weighted bit sum correctly, yielding

$$
\boxed{\sum_{j \ge 0} j x_j}.
$$
