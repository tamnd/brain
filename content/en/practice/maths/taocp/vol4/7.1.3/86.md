**Exercise 86.** [*M27*] [M27] An array of $2^p \times 2^q \times 2^r$ elements is to be allocated by putting $a[i, j, k]$ into a location whose bits are the $p + q + r$ bits of $(i, j, k)$, permuted in some fashion. Furthermore, this array is to be stored in an external memory using pages of size $2^s$. (Exercise 85 considers the case $p = q = r = 5$ and $s = 7$.) What allocation strategy of this kind minimizes the number of times that $a[i, j, k]$ is on a different page from $a[i', j', k']$, summed over all $i$, $j$, $k$, $i'$, $j'$, and $k'$ such that $|i-i'|+|j-j'|+|k-k'|=1$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m34s  

---

### Corrected Solution

We recompute the objective carefully and then minimize it.

## 1. Bit allocation model

Write each index in binary:

$$
i=(i_{p-1}\ldots i_0)_2,\quad
j=(j_{q-1}\ldots j_0)_2,\quad
k=(k_{r-1}\ldots k_0)_2.
$$

A legal allocation permutes these $p+q+r$ bits into an address. The low $s$ bits determine the page offset, so the allocation is equivalent to choosing which $s$ bits are used for page offset.

As in the original solution, any optimal permutation may be assumed to select:

- $a$ bits from $i$,
- $b$ bits from $j$,
- $c$ bits from $k$,

with

$$
a+b+c=s,\quad 0\le a\le p,\ 0\le b\le q,\ 0\le c\le r.
$$

The remaining bits determine the page number.

## 2. Structure of pages

Fix $(a,b,c)$. In each coordinate:

- $i$-values are grouped into blocks of size $2^a$,
- $j$-values into blocks of size $2^b$,
- $k$-values into blocks of size $2^c$.

Thus each page corresponds to a block

$$
2^a \times 2^b \times 2^c.
$$

A neighbor pair crosses a page boundary exactly when it crosses a block boundary in the corresponding coordinate.

## 3. Correct counting of boundary crossings

### (a) $i$-direction

Fix $(j,k)$. Consider the sequence $i=0,1,\dots,2^p-1$.

Within each block of length $2^a$, there is exactly one potential boundary at the transition

$$
\ell = 2^a-1 \to 0
$$

in block-local coordinates, but the last block has no outgoing edge.

Hence the number of valid crossing edges in the $i$-direction is:

$$
2^{p-a}-1 \quad \text{per fixed } (j,k).
$$

Since there are $2^{q+r}$ choices of $(j,k)$,

$$
E_i = 2^{q+r}(2^{p-a}-1).
$$

### (b) $j$- and $k$-directions

By the same argument:

$$
E_j = 2^{p+r}(2^{q-b}-1),\qquad
E_k = 2^{p+q}(2^{r-c}-1).
$$

## 4. Total cost

Add contributions:

$$
E(a,b,c)=2^{q+r}(2^{p-a}-1)+2^{p+r}(2^{q-b}-1)+2^{p+q}(2^{r-c}-1).
$$

Expand:

$$
E(a,b,c)
=2^{p+q+r}\left(2^{-a}+2^{-b}+2^{-c}\right)
-\left(2^{q+r}+2^{p+r}+2^{p+q}\right).
$$

The second term is independent of $a,b,c$. Therefore minimizing $E$ is equivalent to minimizing

$$
F(a,b,c)=2^{-a}+2^{-b}+2^{-c}
\quad \text{subject to } a+b+c=s.
$$

## 5. Exact minimization

We now minimize

$$
F(a,b,c)=2^{-a}+2^{-b}+2^{-c},\quad a+b+c=s.
$$

### Key exchange argument

Assume $a\ge b+2$. Consider shifting one bit from $a$ to $b$:

$$
(a,b)\to (a-1,b+1).
$$

The change in $F$ is:

$$
\Delta = (2^{-(a-1)}+2^{-(b+1)})-(2^{-a}+2^{-b}).
$$

Compute:

$$
2^{-(a-1)} = 2\cdot 2^{-a},\quad 2^{-(b+1)}=\tfrac12 2^{-b}.
$$

Thus

$$
\Delta = (2\cdot 2^{-a}+\tfrac12 2^{-b})-(2^{-a}+2^{-b})
=2^{-a}-\tfrac12 2^{-b}.
$$

Since $a\ge b+2$,

$$
2^{-a}\le 2^{-(b+2)}=\tfrac14 2^{-b},
$$

so

$$
\Delta \le \tfrac14 2^{-b}-\tfrac12 2^{-b}<0.
$$

Thus the transfer strictly decreases $F$. Therefore no optimal solution can have a gap $\ge 2$ between any two variables.

Hence:

$$
|a-b|\le 1,\quad |b-c|\le 1,\quad |a-c|\le 1.
$$

## 6. Optimal structure

Let

$$
s=3t+\delta,\quad \delta\in\{0,1,2\}.
$$

Then the only integer triples satisfying balance are:

- $\delta=0$: $(t,t,t)$
- $\delta=1$: permutation of $(t+1,t,t)$
- $\delta=2$: permutation of $(t+1,t+1,t)$

All such allocations are optimal.

## 7. Interpretation

The optimal strategy is:

> Distribute the $s$ low-order address bits as evenly as possible among the $i,j,k$ coordinates.

Any permutation of bits achieving this balance minimizes the number of cross-page nearest-neighbor pairs.

## Final answer

The minimizing allocation is obtained by choosing $a,b,c$ satisfying $a+b+c=s$ and differing by at most 1, i.e.

$$
(a,b,c)\in \{t,t,t\},\ \{t+1,t,t\},\ \{t+1,t+1,t\}
$$

depending on $s \bmod 3$, with arbitrary permutation among coordinates.
