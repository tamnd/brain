**Exercise 75.** &#9654; [*32*] The method of Chung and Wong replicates bit $l$ of a register exactly $c_l$ times, but it produces results in scrambled order. For example, the case $(c_0, \ldots, c_7) = (1, 2, 0, 2, 0, 2, 0, 1)$ unblends the bits of the produces $(x_7x_3x_5x_3x_5x_1x_5x_0)_2$. In some applications this can be a disadvantage; we might prefer to have the bits retain their original order, namely $(x_7x_5x_5x_3x_3x_1x_1x_0)_2$ in that example.

Prove that the permutation network $P(2^d)$ of Fig. 12 can be modified to achieve this goal, given any sequence of counts $(c_0, c_1, \ldots, c_{2^d-1})$. If we replace the $d \cdot 2^{d-1}$ crossbar modules in the right-hand half by general $2 \times 2$ *mapping modules*. (A crossbar module with inputs $(a, b)$ produces either $(a, b)$ or $(b, a)$ as output; a mapping module can also produce $(a, a)$ or $(b, b)$.)
