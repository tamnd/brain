### Corrected Solution

The key mistake in the previous attempt is the introduction of automaton-style evolution over prefixes. In a shift-free 2-adic chain, there is no mechanism that propagates information between bit positions. This forces a much stronger locality principle than the one assumed earlier.

## 1. Locality in shift-free 2-adic chains

We work in the model of shift-free 2-adic chains where all operations are built from

$$
\&, \ \mid, \ \oplus, \ \neg
$$

applied to 2-adic integers and fixed rational 2-adic constants, with no shift operators.

### Lemma (bitwise independence)

For every expression $z$ constructed in such a chain, there exists a function $F$ and a period $m \ge 1$ such that for every position $j$,

$$
z_j = F\bigl(x_j, j \bmod m\bigr),
$$

where the dependence on $j \bmod m$ comes only from the ultimately periodic bit sequences of rational 2-adic constants.

#### Proof

We proceed by structural induction on the construction of $z$.

- For the input $x$, we have $x_j$ depends only on $x_j$.
- For any rational 2-adic constant $c$, its bit sequence $(c_j)$ is ultimately periodic, hence depends only on $j \bmod m$ for some $m$ after absorbing a finite prefix into a larger period.
- The operations $\&, \mid, \oplus, \neg$ are applied pointwise:

$$
(u \circ v)_j = u_j \circ v_j.
$$

Hence they preserve the property that the $j$-th bit depends only on $x_j$ and $j \bmod m$.

Thus every constructed variable satisfies the claim. ∎

## 2. Consequence for the output bit

Let $y$ be the output of the chain. Then there exist a period $m$ and a Boolean function

$$
f_r : \{0,1\} \to \{0,1\}, \quad r \in \mathbb{Z}/m\mathbb{Z},
$$

such that

$$
y_j = f_{j \bmod m}(x_j).
$$

In other words, each output bit depends only on:

- the corresponding input bit $x_j$, and
- the position class $j \bmod m$,

and nothing else.

There is no dependence on $x_{j-1}, x_{j-2}, \dots$, hence no prefix information can be formed.

## 3. Translating Exercise 116

Exercise 116 states that for such a function there exists a language $L \subseteq \{0,1\}^*$ such that

$$
y_j = [x_j x_{j-1} \cdots x_0 \in L].
$$

Let $w = x_j x_{j-1} \cdots x_0$, so $|w| = j+1$.

Then the condition becomes

$$
w \in L \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad f_{(|w|-1)\bmod m}(w_{|w|-1}) = 1.
$$

Define $a = w_{|w|-1}$, the last symbol of $w$, and $r = (|w|-1) \bmod m$. Then:

$$
w \in L \iff (a \in A_r),
$$

where

$$
A_r = \{a \in \{0,1\} : f_r(a)=1\}.
$$

## 4. Characterization of $L$

We obtain the following necessary and sufficient form.

### Theorem

A regular language $L$ arises from a shift-free 2-adic chain if and only if there exists a period $m \ge 1$ and subsets $A_0, \dots, A_{m-1} \subseteq \{0,1\}$ such that

$$
L = \{ w \in \{0,1\}^* : w_{|w|-1} \in A_{(|w|-1)\bmod m} \}.
$$

Equivalently,

$$
L = \bigcup_{r=0}^{m-1} \{ w : |w| \equiv r \!\!\!\pmod m,\ w_{|w|-1} \in A_r \}.
$$

## 5. Sufficiency

Given such a description, a shift-free chain can realize $L$ directly:

- Use a rational 2-adic constant whose bit sequence cycles with period $m$ to encode the phase $r = j \bmod m$.
- For each $r$, define a Boolean function $f_r(x_j)$ using $\&, \mid, \oplus, \neg$.
- Output $y_j = f_r(x_j)$.

This matches the required form.

## 6. Exclusion of more complex regular languages

Languages such as

$$
0^*(10^*10^*)^*
$$

do not satisfy this characterization because membership depends on internal structure of the word, not solely on:

- its length modulo $m$, and
- its last symbol.

Since shift-free chains cannot access any information beyond the current bit position and periodic constants, they cannot express such languages.

## 7. Final conclusion

Shift-free 2-adic chains do not generate automaton-like temporal memory. They are strictly pointwise periodic Boolean transformations. The induced languages are exactly those whose membership depends only on the last symbol of the word together with the word length modulo a fixed period.

∎
