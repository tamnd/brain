**Exercise 90.** [*20*] [20] The bytewise averaging rule (88) always rounds downward when $x_j + y_j$ is odd. Make it less biased by rounding to the nearest odd integer in such cases.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m26s  

---

## Solution

Let each bytewise operation be performed in base $256$, so that addition of two packed words $x$ and $y$ produces a word whose $j$th component is $(x_j + y_j)$ without interaction between distinct bytes, as in exercise 88.

The standard averaging rule produces

$$
a = \left\lfloor \frac{x + y}{2} \right\rfloor,
$$

so that each byte satisfies $a_j = \lfloor (x_j + y_j)/2 \rfloor$. When $x_j + y_j$ is odd, write

$$
x_j + y_j = 2k + 1,
$$

so $a_j = k$. The alternative integer is $k+1$, and exactly one of $k, k+1$ is odd. If $k$ is even, then $k+1$ is odd; if $k$ is odd, then $k$ is already odd.

Let

$$
s = x + y,\qquad a = s \gg 1,
$$

and let $o = s ,&, 1$, so $o$ is the bytewise parity indicator, with $o_j = 1$ exactly when $x_j + y_j$ is odd.

The correction term must be applied exactly when $o_j = 1$ and $a_j$ is even, since then the odd choice is $a_j + 1$. The condition “$a_j$ is even” is expressed by $a ,&, 1 = 0$ in the corresponding byte position. Hence the adjustment mask is

$$
t = o \,\&\, \overline{(a \,\&\, 1)}.
$$

The final result is therefore

$$
z = a + t.
$$

Substituting $a = (x + y) \gg 1$ and $o = (x + y) ,&, 1$ gives the compact form

$$
z = (x + y) \gg 1 \;+\; \bigl((x + y) \,\&\, 1 \,\&\, \overline{((x + y) \gg 1 \,\&\, 1)}\bigr).
$$

Each byte $z_j$ equals the nearer of $\lfloor (x_j + y_j)/2 \rfloor$ and $\lceil (x_j + y_j)/2 \rceil$, with the odd integer chosen when the sum is odd.

This completes the construction. ∎
