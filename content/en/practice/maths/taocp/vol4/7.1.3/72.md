## Solution

Let $\pi$ denote the gather-flip operation $(81')$. By the definition of (81′), $\pi$ is obtained by composing a gather transformation with a flip transformation, both acting on bit positions of a word. Each of these transformations is a permutation of the bit indices ${0,1,\ldots,2^d-1}$ determined entirely by bitwise structure.

A routing in an $\Omega$-network (in the sense of exercise 58) assigns to each input wire a path through $d$ stages of $2\times 2$ switches, where at stage $k$ the decision depends only on the $k$-th bit of the destination address. A permutation is $\Omega$-routable precisely when such a stagewise consistent assignment exists for all inputs simultaneously.

The flip operation sends an index $i = (i_{d-1}\ldots i_0)_2$ to its bit-reversal $\overline{i} = (i_0\ldots i_{d-1})_2$. This mapping preserves the property that at stage $k$, the $k$-th destination bit is determined solely by a single source bit, namely $i_{d-1-k}$. Therefore, at each stage the routing decision is well-defined and independent of conflicts, so flip is $\Omega$-routable.

The gather operation acts by selecting bits according to a fixed pattern determined by a mask $\chi \ne 0$, and packing selected bits into contiguous positions.
