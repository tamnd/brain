**Exercise 74.** [*22*] Given counts $(c_0, c_1, \ldots, c_{2^d-1})$ for the Chung-Wong procedure, explain why an appropriate cyclic 1-shift can always produce new counts $(c'_0, c'_1, \ldots, c'_{2^d-1})$ for which $\sum c'_{2t} = \sum c'_{2t+1}$, thus allowing the recursion to proceed.

## Solution

Let the counts be indexed by residues modulo $2^d$, and for a cyclic 1-shift by $k$ define

$$
c'_i = c_{i-k \bmod 2^d}.
$$

Let the imbalance after shift $k$ be

$$
D(k) = \sum_{\substack{0 \le i < 2^d \\ i \equiv 0 \,(\mathrm{mod}\,2)}} c_{i-k} \;-\; \sum_{\substack{0 \le i < 2^d \\ i \equiv 1 \,(\mathrm{mod}\,2)}} c_{i-k}.
$$

This is the difference between the total mass in even and odd positions after the shift.

Rewrite $D(k)$ by changing variables $j = i-k \pmod{2^d}$, giving

$$
D(k) = \sum_{j=0}^{2^d-1} \sigma_k(j)\, c_j,
$$

where $\sigma_k(j) \in {+1,-1}$ depends on whether $j+k \equiv 0 \pmod{2}$ or $1 \pmod{2}$.

When $k$ increases by $1$, the parity of $j+k$ flips exactly when a carry occurs in the addition $j \mapsto j+1 \pmod{2^d}$. This happens precisely for those $j$ in the interval ${2^{d-1},\dots,2^d-1}$, since these are exactly the indices whose least significant bit flips under the cyclic increment. Hence the parity classification changes only for the two boundary points of the induced cyclic cut, which are $k$ and $k+2^{d-1}$.

Therefore the change in imbalance satisfies

$$
D(k+1) - D(k) = c_k - c_{k+2^{d-1}}.
$$

Summing this identity over all $k$ modulo $2^d$ gives

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{2^d-1} (D(k+1)-D(k)) = \sum_{k=0}^{2^d-1} (c_k - c_{k+2^{d-1}}) = 0,
$$

since $k \mapsto k+2^{d-1}$ is a permutation of the index set. Thus $D(k)$ has total net change zero over a full cycle.

If all values $D(k)$ were strictly positive, then every difference $D(k+1)-D(k)$ would have to sum to a positive total contradiction with the telescoping sum being $0$. The same contradiction arises if all $D(k)$ were strictly negative. Hence there exist indices $k_1,k_2$ such that $D(k_1) \ge 0$ and $D(k_2) \le 0$.

Since each increment $D(k+1)-D(k)$ is an integer combination of counts, $D(k)$ changes in integer steps, so along the cyclic progression from $k_1$ to $k_2$ the value must pass through $0$. Therefore there exists a shift $k$ such that $D(k)=0$.

For this shift,

$$
\sum c'_{2t} = \sum c'_{2t+1},
$$

so the even and odd totals are equal and the Chung–Wong recursion can proceed.

This completes the proof. ∎
