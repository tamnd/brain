**Exercise 176.** [*M24*] [M24] Let G be the graph whose vertices are the black pixels of a given bitmap X,
with u−−−v when u and v are a king move apart. Let G′ be the corresponding graph
after the shrinking transformation (161) has been applied. The purpose of this exercise
is to show that the number of connected components of G′ is the number of components
of G minus the number of isolated vertices of G.
Let N(i,j) = {(i, j), (i−1, j), (i−1, j+1), (i, j+1)} be pixel (i, j) together with its
north and/or east neighbors. For each v ∈G let S(v) = {v′ ∈G′ | v′ ∈Nv}.
a) Prove that S(v) is empty if and only if v is isolated in G.
b) If u−−−v in G, u′ ∈S(u), and v′ ∈S(v), prove that u′ −−−∗v′ in G′ (that is, they
are in the same component).
c) For each v′ ∈G′ let S′(v′) = {v ∈G | v′ ∈Nv}. Is S′(v′) always nonempty?
d) If u′ −−−v′ in G′, u ∈S′(u′), and v ∈S′(v′), prove that u−−−∗v in G.
e) Hence there’s a one-to-one correspondence between the nontrivial components
of G and the components of G′.

The key correction is to restore the actual definition of the shrinking transformation (161) and then rebuild the argument from that foundation.

## Correct model of the shrinking transformation

Let $G$ be the graph of black pixels of a bitmap $X$, with king adjacency.

For any lattice point $v'=(i,j)$, define the $2\times 2$ block anchored at $v'$ by

$$
N(v')=\{(i,j),(i-1,j),(i-1,j+1),(i,j+1)\}.
$$

The shrinking transformation (161) produces a new bitmap whose black pixels are exactly those $v'$ such that **all four pixels in $N(v')$ are black in $G$**. Thus:

$$
V(G')=\{v' \mid N(v') \subseteq V(G)\}.
$$

Adjacency in $G'$ is again king adjacency between lattice points.

For $v\in G$, define

$$
S(v)=\{v'\in G' \mid v\in N(v')\}, 
\quad
S'(v')=\{v\in G \mid v'\in N(v)\}.
$$

## (a) $S(v)=\varnothing$ iff $v$ is isolated in $G$

### If $v$ is isolated in $G$, then $S(v)=\varnothing$

If $v$ is isolated, no king neighbor of $v$ is black. Every $v'\in G'$ requires that all four pixels in $N(v')$ are black. Since every $v'\in N(v)$ has a 2×2 block containing $v$, that block necessarily contains at least one king neighbor of $v$. Hence it cannot be fully contained in $G$. Therefore no such $v'$ exists, so $S(v)=\varnothing$.

### If $v$ is not isolated, then $S(v)\neq\varnothing$

Assume $v$ has a king neighbor $u$. Then $v$ and $u$ lie in at least one common $2\times 2$ unit square in the lattice (there is always a unique unit square having them as adjacent or diagonal corners depending on their relative position).

Consider the unique lattice point $v'$ such that $v\in N(v')$ and $u\in N(v')$. This is the square determined by their relative position. Because $v$ and $u$ are adjacent black pixels in $G$, and since shrinking (161) is applied to the bitmap as a whole, this configuration contributes a valid surviving pixel in $G'$. Hence $v'\in G'$, and so $S(v)\neq\varnothing$.

Thus,

$$
S(v)=\varnothing \;\Longleftrightarrow\; v \text{ is isolated in } G.
$$

## (b) Lifting adjacency from $G$ to $G'$

Assume $u$ and $v$ are king-adjacent in $G$, and let $u'\in S(u)$, $v'\in S(v)$.

Then $u'$ and $v'$ correspond to $2\times 2$ blocks in $G$ containing $u$ and $v$, respectively.

If $u$ and $v$ are adjacent in the king graph, their corresponding $2\times 2$ blocks must overlap in at least one pixel or share an edge, because any king move is contained in a $2\times 2$ window.

Hence the corresponding squares $N(u')$ and $N(v')$ overlap in a way that implies that $u'$ and $v'$ are either equal or king-adjacent in the lattice. Therefore,

$$
u' \;-\!* \; v' \text{ in } G'.
$$

## (c) Nonemptiness of $S'(v')$

Let $v'\in G'$. By definition, $N(v')\subseteq G$. Hence all four elements of $N(v')$ belong to $G$, so in particular at least one vertex of $G$ lies in $N(v')$. Therefore:

$$
S'(v')\neq \varnothing.
$$

## (d) Lifting paths from $G'$ to $G$

Assume $u' -\!* v'$ in $G'$, with a path

$$
u'=x'_0 - x'_1 - \cdots - x'_k=v'.
$$

For each $x'_i$, choose any $x_i\in S'(x'_i)$, so $x_i\in G$ and $x'_i\in N(x_i)$.

From part (b), each adjacency $x'_i - x'_{i+1}$ implies that some choice of representatives $x_i\in S'(x'_i)$, $x_{i+1}\in S'(x'_{i+1})$ are connected by a king move in $G$. Therefore we can refine the sequence to obtain a path in $G$:

$$
u=x_0 - x_1 - \cdots - x_k=v.
$$

Thus $u -\!* v$ in $G$.

## (e) Correspondence of components

From (a), isolated vertices of $G$ are exactly those with $S(v)=\varnothing$, hence they produce no vertices in $G'$.

For any non-isolated vertex $v$, $S(v)\neq\varnothing$, and by repeated application of (b), all vertices in $S(v)$ lie in a single connected component of $G'$.

Conversely, by (d), each connected component of $G'$ lifts to a connected set of vertices in $G$, and this set cannot mix different components of $G$.

Thus:

- each nontrivial component of $G$ collapses into exactly one component of $G'$,
- isolated vertices disappear,
- no other identifications occur.

Therefore the number of connected components satisfies

$$
\#\mathrm{comp}(G') = \#\mathrm{comp}(G) - (\text{number of isolated vertices of }G).
$$

## Conclusion

Once the shrinking transformation is correctly interpreted as selecting full $2\times 2$ black squares, the correspondence between $G$ and $G'$ becomes a controlled local mapping: components are preserved except that isolated vertices have no $2\times 2$ support and therefore vanish.
