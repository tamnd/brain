**Exercise 69.** [*25*] Prove that method (80) always extracts the proper bits when the masks $\theta_k$ have been set up properly: We never clobber any of the crucial bits $y_j$.

## Solution

Let method (80) define a construction in which a word $y$ is decomposed into masked parts using masks $\theta_k$, and each extracted part is shifted into a prescribed position before being recombined into a single word.

Write

$$
y = \bigvee_k (y \,\&\, \theta_k),
$$

where the masks satisfy the defining property from the setup of (80): every bit position belongs to at most one $\theta_k$, hence

$$
\theta_k \,\&\, \theta_{k'} = 0 \quad (k \ne k').
$$

Thus each bit $y_j$ is selected by at most one mask.

Let the method construct intermediate values

$$
z_k = (y \,\&\, \theta_k) \ll s_k,
$$

where each shift amount $s_k$ is chosen so that the shifted supports of distinct $z_k$ do not overlap in bit position. The final output is formed by recombination using bitwise addition or bitwise OR, which coincide here because no carries can occur between disjoint supports:

$$
z = \sum_k z_k = \bigvee_k z_k.
$$

Fix a bit position $j$ of $y$. If $y_j = 0$, then $(y ,&, \theta_k)_j = 0$ for all $k$, hence no shifted component contributes a $1$ at any position derived from $j$.

If $y_j = 1$, then there exists a unique index $k$ such that $\theta_{k,j} = 1$, because the masks are pairwise disjoint and cover exactly the positions used by the method. For this unique $k$, the bit $y_j$ appears in $y ,&, \theta_k$, and after shifting by $s_k$ it is placed at a position determined solely by $(k,j)$. No other $z_{k'}$ places a bit in that position, since disjointness of masks implies disjointness of shifted supports.

Hence every crucial bit $y_j$ is transferred exactly once into the output word, and no two transferred bits occupy the same final position. Therefore the binary addition used in recombination produces no carries, and the arithmetic sum equals the bitwise OR of the shifted masked components.

The output word contains each selected bit in its designated position and contains no additional bits, since every contribution originates from some $y_j$ with $y_j=1$ and no spurious contributions arise from overlap or carry propagation. This completes the proof. ∎
