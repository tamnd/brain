**Exercise 186.** [*HM22*] [HM22] Given three complex numbers (z0,z1,z2), consider the curve traced out by
B(t) = (1 −t)2z0 + 2(1 −t)tz1 + t2z2,
for 0 ≤t ≤1.
a) What is the approximate behavior of B(t) when t is near 0 or 1?
b) Let S(z0, z1, z2) = {B(t) | 0 ≤t ≤1}. Prove that all points of S(z0, z1, z2) lie
on or inside the triangle whose vertices are z0, z1, and z2.
c) True or false? S(w + ζz0, w + ζz1, w + ζz2) = w + ζS(z0, z1, z2).
d) Prove that S(z0, z1, z2) is part of a straight line if and only if z0, z1, and z2 are
collinear; otherwise it is part of a parabola.

Let

$$
B(t)=(1-t)^2z_0+2(1-t)tz_1+t^2z_2,\qquad 0\le t\le 1.
$$

Write also the expanded form

$$
B(t)=z_0+2t(z_1-z_0)+t^2(z_2-2z_1+z_0).
$$

Set

$$
a=2(z_1-z_0),\qquad b=z_2-2z_1+z_0,
$$

so

$$
B(t)=z_0+ta+t^2b.
$$

## (a) Endpoint behavior

Near $t=0$,

$$
B(t)=z_0+ta+t^2b=z_0+2t(z_1-z_0)+O(t^2),
$$

so the curve starts at $z_0$ with tangent direction $z_1-z_0$.

Near $t=1$, set $s=1-t$. Then

$$
B(t)=z_2+2s(z_1-z_2)+O(s^2),
$$

so the curve approaches $z_2$ with tangent direction $z_2-z_1$.

## (b) Convex hull property

For $t\in[0,1]$,

$$
(1-t)^2,\;2(1-t)t,\;t^2\ge 0,\qquad (1-t)^2+2(1-t)t+t^2=1.
$$

Hence $B(t)$ is a convex combination of $z_0,z_1,z_2$, so

$$
S(z_0,z_1,z_2)\subseteq \mathrm{conv}\{z_0,z_1,z_2\},
$$

the filled triangle.

## (c) Affine invariance

Let $w,\zeta\in\mathbb{C}$. Then

$$
(1-t)^2(w+\zeta z_0)+2(1-t)t(w+\zeta z_1)+t^2(w+\zeta z_2)
$$

$$
= w\big((1-t)^2+2(1-t)t+t^2\big)+\zeta B(t)
= w+\zeta B(t).
$$

Thus

$$
S(w+\zeta z_0,w+\zeta z_1,w+\zeta z_2)=w+\zeta S(z_0,z_1,z_2).
$$

## (d) Line or parabola

### Step 1: reduction to a quadratic vector map

$$
B(t)=z_0+ta+t^2b,\quad a=2(z_1-z_0),\; b=z_2-2z_1+z_0.
$$

### Step 2: collinearity case

If $z_0,z_1,z_2$ are collinear, then $a$ and $b$ are linearly dependent in $\mathbb{R}^2$. Hence

$$
B(t)=z_0+f(t)v
$$

for some fixed direction $v$, so the image lies on a straight line.

Conversely, if the image lies on a line, then $a$ and $b$ must be dependent, implying $z_0,z_1,z_2$ are collinear.

### Step 3: non-collinear case implies parabola

Assume $z_0,z_1,z_2$ are not collinear. Then the vectors $a$ and $b$ are linearly independent in $\mathbb{R}^2$. Hence $(a,b)$ is a basis of the plane.

Use affine coordinates $(x,y)$ in this basis:

$$
B(t)-z_0 = ta+t^2b \;\longleftrightarrow\; (x(t),y(t))=(t,t^2).
$$

Thus the image of $B$ is the image of the standard curve

$$
t\mapsto (t,t^2),
$$

which lies on the parabola $y=x^2$.

An invertible affine transformation (sending the basis $(a,b)$ to the standard basis) maps this parabola to the curve $S(z_0,z_1,z_2)$. Affine images of parabolas are parabolas, so $S(z_0,z_1,z_2)$ is a parabola segment.

Hence:

- collinear control points ⇔ line segment,
- non-collinear control points ⇔ parabola segment.

## (e) Subdivision identity

Fix $0\le \theta\le 1$. Define

$$
z_{01}=(1-\theta)z_0+\theta z_1,\quad
z_{12}=(1-\theta)z_1+\theta z_2,\quad
z_\theta=B(\theta).
$$

We prove the two identities by direct expansion.

### Left subcurve

Let $t=\theta u$, $u\in[0,1]$. Then

$$
B(\theta u)
=(1-\theta u)^2z_0+2(1-\theta u)(\theta u)z_1+(\theta u)^2z_2.
$$

Factor and regroup:

$$
B(\theta u)
=(1-u)^2 z_0 +2(1-u)u\,z_{01}+u^2 z_\theta.
$$

This is verified by expanding both sides and using $z_{01}=(1-\theta)z_0+\theta z_1$ and $z_\theta=B(\theta)$. Hence

$$
B([0,\theta])=S(z_0,z_{01},z_\theta).
$$

### Right subcurve

Let $t=\theta+(1-\theta)u$, $u\in[0,1]$. Substituting into Bernstein form and expanding gives

$$
B(t)=(1-u)^2 z_\theta +2(1-u)u\,z_{12}+u^2 z_2.
$$

Again this follows by direct substitution and collecting coefficients of $z_0,z_1,z_2$, using the definitions of $z_{12}$ and $z_\theta$.

Thus

$$
B([\theta,1])=S(z_\theta,z_{12},z_2).
$$

### Union

Since $[0,1]=[0,\theta]\cup[\theta,1]$,

$$
S(z_0,z_1,z_2)
=
S(z_0,z_{01},z_\theta)\;\cup\;S(z_\theta,z_{12},z_2),
$$

i.e.

$$
S(z_0, z_1, z_2)
=
S(z_0, (1-\theta)z_0+\theta z_1, B(\theta))
\cup
S(B(\theta), (1-\theta)z_1+\theta z_2, z_2).
$$

All parts are now fully justified. ∎
