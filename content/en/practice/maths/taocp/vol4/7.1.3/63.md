**Exercise 63.** [*19*] For what integers $w$, $x$, $y$, and $z$ does the zipper function satisfy (i) $x\updownarrow y = y\updownarrow x$? (ii) $(x\updownarrow y)\gg z = (x\gg \lceil z/2 \rceil)\updownarrow(y\gg \lfloor z/2 \rfloor)$? (iii) $(w\updownarrow x)\&(y\updownarrow z) = (w\&y)\updownarrow(x\&z)$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m04s

## Solution

Let $x = (\ldots x_2 x_1 x_0)_2$ and $y = (\ldots y_2 y_1 y_0)_2$. The zipper function $x \updownarrow y$ is defined by interleaving bits so that the binary expansion of $x \updownarrow y$ is

$$
x \updownarrow y = (\ldots x_2\, y_2\, x_1\, y_1\, x_0\, y_0)_2,
$$

equivalently, the bit in position $2k$ equals $x_k$ and the bit in position $2k+1$ equals $y_k$.

### (i) Commutativity condition

For all $k \ge 0$, the bit in position $2k$ of $x \updownarrow y$ equals $x_k$, while the bit in position $2k$ of $y \updownarrow x$ equals $y_k$. Equality $x \updownarrow y = y \updownarrow x$ implies $x_k = y_k$ for all $k \ge 0$, since equality of integers in two’s complement form requires equality of all bit positions.

Conversely, if $x_k = y_k$ for all $k \ge 0$, then the interleavings coincide bitwise, so $x \updownarrow y = y \updownarrow x$.

Hence the condition is equivalent to $x = y$.

$$
\boxed{x \updownarrow y = y \updownarrow x \;\;\text{iff}\;\; x = y.}
$$

### (ii) Interaction with right shifts

Let $z \ge 0$ be fixed. Write $z = 2t$ or $z = 2t+1$, where $t = \lfloor z/2 \rfloor$, so $\lceil z/2 \rceil = t$ when $z$ is even and $t+1$ when $z$ is odd.

Shifting $x \updownarrow y$ right by $z$ removes the lowest $z$ bits. The bit in position $k$ of $(x \updownarrow y)\gg z$ is the bit in position $k+z$ of $x \updownarrow y$. That bit equals:

- $x_{(k+z)/2}$ if $k+z$ is even,
- $y_{(k+z-1)/2}$ if $k+z$ is odd.

The parity of $k+z$ depends on the parity of $z$. If $z=2t$, then positions split so that:

$$
(x \updownarrow y)\gg 2t
$$

has even positions corresponding to $x_{k+t}$ and odd positions corresponding to $y_{k+t}$, which is exactly

$$
(x\gg t)\updownarrow(y\gg t).
$$

If $z=2t+1$, then the first bit removed comes from the $y$-stream, so the $x$-stream is shifted by $t+1$ while the $y$-stream is shifted by $t$, yielding

$$
(x \updownarrow y)\gg (2t+1) = (x\gg (t+1))\updownarrow(y\gg t).
$$

These two cases combine into

$$
(x \updownarrow y)\gg z = (x\gg \lceil z/2 \rceil)\updownarrow (y\gg \lfloor z/2 \rfloor),
$$

which holds for all integers $x,y,z$ under the bitwise definitions of shifts.

$$
\boxed{\text{Identity (ii) holds for all integers } x,y,z.}
$$

### (iii) Compatibility with bitwise AND

Let $w,x,y,z$ be integers. The $2k$-th bit of $w \updownarrow x$ equals $w_k$, and the $2k$-th bit of $y \updownarrow z$ equals $y_k$. Therefore the $2k$-th bit of $(w \updownarrow x),&,(y \updownarrow z)$ equals $w_k \wedge y_k$.

Similarly, the $(2k+1)$-st bit of $(w \updownarrow x),&,(y \updownarrow z)$ equals $x_k \wedge z_k$.

On the other hand,

$$
(w \& y)\updownarrow(x \& z)
$$

has $2k$-th bit $(w & y)_k = w_k \wedge y_k$ and $(2k+1)$-st bit $(x & z)_k = x_k \wedge z_k$.

Thus every bit position agrees between the two expressions, so the integers are equal.

This argument does not impose any restriction on $w,x,y,z$.

$$
\boxed{\text{Identity (iii) holds for all integers } w,x,y,z.}
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
