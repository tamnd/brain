## Solution

Let $f(x_1,\dots,x_n)$ be a Boolean function and define

$$
g_k(x_0,x_1,\dots,x_n)
= f(x_0,\dots,x_{k-2}, x_{k-1}\oplus x_k, x_{k+1},\dots,x_n).
$$

Fix an assignment to all variables except $x_{k-1},x_k$. The value of $g_k$ depends on $f$ evaluated at the single derived bit $z=x_{k-1}\oplus x_k$. The four possible pairs $(x_{k-1},x_k)$ map to $z$ as $00\mapsto 0$, $01\mapsto 1$, $10\mapsto 1$, $11\mapsto 0$. Hence, for every fixed context of the other variables, the length-$4$ subtable of $g_k$ in the variables $(x_{k-1},x_k)$ is obtained from the corresponding length-$2$ subtable of $f$ in the single variable $z$ by duplication along this parity pattern.

Let $\tau$ be any subtable of $f$ at the level where the variable $x_{k-1}$ is first exposed, so that $\tau$ splits as $\tau=\tau_0\tau_1$, where $\tau_0$ and $\tau_1$ are the subtables corresponding to $x_{k-1}=0$ and $x_{k-1}=1$. At the next variable $x_k$, each of $\tau_0$ and $\tau_1$ further splits into $\tau_{00},\tau_{01}$ and $\tau_{10},\tau_{11}$ respectively. The subtable of $g_k$ corresponding to this same context is obtained by identifying the cases $00$ and $11$ with input $0$ for $z$, and the cases $01$ and $10$ with input $1$ for $z$. Therefore the induced subtable of $g_k$ is

$$
\tau^{(g)} = (\tau_{00}\tau_{11})\,(\tau_{01}\tau_{10}).
$$

This is exactly the effect of replacing the pair of variables $(x_{k-1},x_k)$ by a single variable $z=x_{k-1}\oplus x_k$ in the truth-table decomposition.

In terms of BDD structure, the evaluation rule for XOR,

$$
x_{k-1}\oplus x_k =
\begin{cases}
x_k, & x_{k-1}=0,\\
\neg x_k, & x_{k-1}=1,
\end{cases}
$$

determines how the decision nodes are modified. At every node labeled $x_{k-1}$ in the BDD of $f$, the LO successor corresponds to setting $x_{k-1}=0$, so the remaining computation proceeds exactly as in $f$ with variable $x_k$ unchanged. The HI successor corresponds to $x_{k-1}=1$, so every subsequent occurrence of $x_k$ must be interpreted through complementation of the resulting subfunction.

Let $u$ be the root of the sub-BDD of $f$ at variable $x_k$. Then in the BDD of $g_k$, the LO edge from any $x_{k-1}$-node points to $u$, while the HI edge points to the BDD obtained from $u$ by swapping the sinks $\bot\leftrightarrow\top$ and propagating this swap through all terminal reductions, which is the standard BDD representation of Boolean complementation.

Thus the BDD of $g_k$ is obtained from the BDD of $f$ by replacing every local two-level fragment involving $x_{k-1}$ followed by $x_k$ with a single $x_{k-1}$ node whose LO branch enters the original $x_k$-subgraph and whose HI branch enters its complemented copy. This preserves ordering since all paths still test $x_{k-1}$ before $x_k$, and it preserves reduction because complementation does not introduce duplicate $(V,\mathrm{LO},\mathrm{HI})$ triples.

This completes the proof. ∎
