**Exercise 96.** [*20*] [20] Show how to achieve (a) universal quantification ∀xj1 . . . ∀xjm f = f Ag, and
(b) differential quantification
xj1 . . .
xjm f = f D g, by modifying (65).

**Solve time:** 1m17s

## Solution

Let (65) denote the recursive BDD procedure that, for a Boolean function $f$ and a control function $g$, constructs the quantified function by descending the BDD of $f$ and combining cofactors at nodes where the variable is selected by $g$. At a node labeled $x_j$ with low and high cofactors $f_0$ and $f_1$, the rule in (65) forms the result from recursively computed subresults according to whether $x_j$ is to be eliminated.

Write $Q(f,g)$ for the result produced by (65). Then existential quantification corresponds to replacing the combining operation at eliminated variables by disjunction, since

$\exists x_j\, f = f_0 \lor f_1.$

Define $Q_{\exists}(f,g)$ by modifying (65) so that at every node $k_j$ with $g(x_j)=1$, the recursive results $u = Q_{\exists}(f_0,g)$ and $v = Q_{\exists}(f_1,g)$ are combined as

$u \lor v,$

while if $g(x_j)=0$ the node is preserved in the standard BDD form with children $u$ and $v$. Terminal nodes $\bot,\top$ remain unchanged.

For universal quantification, use the identity

$\forall x_j\, f = f_0 \land f_1.$

Define $Q_{\forall}(f,g)$ by the same recursion as in (65), except that at eliminated variables $x_j$ the combining operation is replaced by conjunction:

$u \land v.$

For differential quantification, define

$f \, D \, x_j = f_0 \oplus f_1,$

corresponding to symmetric difference of cofactors. Define $Q_{D}(f,g)$ by modifying (65) so that at eliminated variables $x_j$ the recursive results are combined as

$u \oplus v.$

In all three cases, if $g(x_j)=0$, the node is rebuilt with unchanged structure from the recursively computed low and high subgraphs; if $g(x_j)=1$, the node is removed and replaced by the appropriate binary operator applied to the two recursive results. Memoization of previously computed pairs $(f,g)$ is preserved exactly as in (65), since the subproblems depend only on sub-BDDs and the restriction of $g$.

Each modified procedure computes the corresponding quantified function because the BDD semantics at a node $x_j$ represent decomposition into the two cofactors $f_0$ and $f_1$, and existential, universal, and differential quantification are defined pointwise by applying $\lor$, $\land$, and $\oplus$ to these cofactors respectively.

This completes the proof. ∎
