**Exercise 90.** [*M20*] [M20] When l = m = n = 3, Eq. (64) corresponds to the MOR operation of MMIX.
Is there an analogous formula that corresponds to MXOR (matrix multiplication mod 2)?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m23s  

---

## Solution

Equation (64) in Section 7.1.4 expresses Boolean matrix composition in a form where a product entry is obtained by a MOR reduction over pairwise ANDs of input bits. In the case $l = m = n = 3$, each matrix entry is a single bit, and MOR corresponds to the Boolean operation $\lor$ applied across the intermediate index.

For MXOR, the algebra changes from the Boolean semiring $({0,1}, \lor, \land)$ to the field $\mathbb{F}_2 = ({0,1}, \oplus, \land)$, where addition is XOR and multiplication is AND. The structural form of the composition remains unchanged, since distributivity of $\land$ over $\oplus$ replaces distributivity over $\lor$ used in the MOR case.

Let $A = (a_{ik})$, $B = (b_{kj})$, and $C = (c_{ij})$ be $3 \times 3$ matrices with entries in ${0,1}$. The MXOR analogue of Equation (64) defines each entry of $C$ by reducing over the intermediate index $k$ using XOR instead of OR:

$$
c_{ij} = \bigoplus_{k=1}^{3} (a_{ik} \land b_{kj}).
$$

This expression corresponds exactly to matrix multiplication over $\mathbb{F}_2$, since each product term $a_{ik} \land b_{kj}$ contributes $1$ precisely when both bits are $1$, and the XOR reduction counts these contributions modulo $2$.

At the level of MMIX operations, MOR-based evaluation accumulates partial results via bitwise OR, while MXOR-based evaluation accumulates them via bitwise XOR, leaving the bitwise AND structure of partial products unchanged. Thus Equation (64) admits a direct analogue in which every MOR reduction is replaced by MXOR, yielding the standard Boolean matrix product over $\mathbb{F}_2$.

Therefore the required analogue exists and is given by the XOR-reduction form above.

$$
\boxed{c_{ij} = \bigoplus_{k=1}^{3} (a_{ik} \land b_{kj})}
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
