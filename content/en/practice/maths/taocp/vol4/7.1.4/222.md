**Exercise 222.** &#9654; [*27*] [27] When family algebra is applied to five-letter words as in (131), the 130
variables are called a1, b1, . . . , z5 instead of x1, x2, . . . , x130; and the corresponding
elementary families are denoted by the symbols a1, b1, . . . , z5 instead of e1, e2, . . . , e130.

F = (w1 ⊔h2 ⊔i3 ⊔c4 ⊔h5) ∪· · · ∪(f1 ⊔u2 ⊔n3 ⊔n4 ⊔y5) ∪· · · ∪(p1 ⊔u2 ⊔p3 ⊔a4 ⊔l5).

Let $U=\{a_1,b_1,\dots,z_5\}$, $F\subseteq \mathcal P(U)$ the family of SGB words, and $X_j=\{a_j,\dots,z_j\}$, $V_j=\{a_j,e_j,i_j,o_j,u_j\}$.

## (a)

$\mathcal P=\mathcal P(U)$ is the universal family of all subsets of $U$. Since $F\subseteq \mathcal P$,

$$
F \sqcap \mathcal P = F.
$$

## (b)

$$
X = X_1 \sqcup X_2 \sqcup X_3 \sqcup X_4 \sqcup X_5
$$

is the family of all 5-letter words over the alphabet. Since every SGB word is such a word,

$$
F \sqcap X = F.
$$

## (c)

The pattern $t*u*h$ is the family

$$
P = \{t_1\} \sqcup X_2 \sqcup \{u_3\} \sqcup X_4 \sqcup \{h_5\}.
$$

Hence the required set is

$$
F \sqcap P.
$$

## (d) Words with exactly $k$ vowels

A word has vowels only from $V_j=\{a_j,e_j,i_j,o_j,u_j\}$. Define for each $S\subseteq\{1,2,3,4,5\}$ with $|S|=k$,

$$
P_S
=
\left(\bigsqcup_{j\in S} V_j\right)
\sqcup
\left(\bigsqcup_{j\notin S} (X_j \setminus V_j)\right).
$$

Then the family of all SGB words with exactly $k$ vowels is

$$
\left(\bigcup_{|S|=k} P_S\right)\sqcap F.
$$

## (e) Three-specified patterns matched by at least one SGB word

Fix a set $S\subseteq\{1,2,3,4,5\}$, $|S|=3$, and a pattern $c\in \{a,\dots,z\}^S$. The corresponding pattern family is

$$
P(S,c)=\left(\bigsqcup_{j\in S}\{c_j\}\right)\sqcup \left(\bigsqcup_{j\notin S} X_j\right).
$$

A cleaner TAOCP formulation uses elimination (quotienting out unspecified positions). The set of realizable 3-letter patterns on coordinates $S$ is

$$
\pi_S(F) \equiv F / \left(\bigsqcup_{j\notin S} X_j\right),
$$

which is the family of all assignments on $S$ extendable to a word in $F$.

Hence the number of patterns of this type matched by at least one SGB word is

$$
\sum_{|S|=3} \left|\, F / \left(\bigsqcup_{j\notin S} X_j\right)\,\right|.
$$

## (f) Patterns matched at least twice

For fixed $S,c$, the multiplicity is

$$
|F \sqcap P(S,c)|.
$$

Thus patterns matched at least twice are counted by

$$
\sum_{|S|=3}
\left|
\left\{
c\in \{a,\dots,z\}^S :
|F \sqcap P(S,c)| \ge 2
\right\}
\right|.
$$

No further simplification is possible without structural information about $F$. In contrast to (e), this depends on fiber sizes of the projection $F \to \{a,\dots,z\}^S$, not just its image.

## (g) Words invariant under $b \mapsto o$ (corrected)

Let $\sigma$ be the substitution on letters defined by

$$
\sigma(b_j)=o_j,\qquad \sigma(x_j)=x_j \ (x\neq b).
$$

Extend $\sigma$ to words and families elementwise.

We require words $w\in F$ such that after substitution the result is still in $F$:

$$
w\in F \quad \text{and} \quad \sigma(w)\in F.
$$

This is exactly

$$
F \sqcap \sigma^{-1}(F).
$$

Here $\sigma^{-1}(F)=\{w : \sigma(w)\in F\}$ is the preimage family under substitution. Therefore the required family is

$$
\boxed{F \sqcap \sigma^{-1}(F)}.
$$

This correctly enforces that the _transformed word_ remains in $F$, rather than incorrectly requiring membership in the image family.

## (h) Meaning of $F / V_2$

The quotient $F/V_2$ eliminates position 2 while allowing any letter from $V_2$ there. Thus it is the family of all 4-position structures on $\{1,3,4,5\}$ that can be extended at position 2 by a vowel to form a word in $F$:

$$
x \in F/V_2 \iff \exists y\in V_2 \text{ such that } x\sqcup\{y_2\}\in F.
$$

So $F/V_2$ is the projection of $F$ with position 2 restricted to vowels.

## (i) Comparison

### First expression

$$
(X_1 \sqcup V_2 \sqcup V_3 \sqcup V_4 \sqcup X_5)\cap F
$$

This is the family of SGB words whose positions $2,3,4$ are restricted to vowels.

### Second expression

$$
(X_1 \sqcup X_5)\setminus \left((\mathcal P\setminus F)/(V_2 \sqcup V_3 \sqcup V_4)\right).
$$

Here:

- $\mathcal P\setminus F$ are non-words,
- quotient eliminates positions $2,3,4$ under vowel restriction,
- subtraction removes boundary patterns that can be extended to a non-word.

Thus this describes 1–5 position pairs that force membership in $F$ under all vowel fillings of the middle positions.

## Summary of correction

- (g) replaced image condition $F \sqcap \sigma(F)$ with correct preimage condition $F \sqcap \sigma^{-1}(F)$.
- (e) rewritten using quotient/projection $F/( \bigsqcup_{j\notin S} X_j)$.
- (f) clarified as fiber-size condition over the projection, showing why no simplification is available.
- (d) notation clarified as positional join/product structure.

All operations remain within TAOCP family algebra.
