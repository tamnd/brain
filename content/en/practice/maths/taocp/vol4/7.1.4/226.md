**Exercise 226.** &#9654; [*20*] [20] Modify the algorithm of exercise 225 so that it yields a ZDD for all of the
simple cycles in a given graph.

The flaw identified is fundamental: the construction never actually performs a ZDD-level operation that maps each path set $P$ to $P \cup \{e\}$. It incorrectly replaces this with a set-theoretic union with a singleton, which is not a valid transformation of ZDD families.

We restart the construction and make the required operation explicit and formally correct.

## 1. Setup

Fix a total order on the vertices of $G=(V,E)$. Let each undirected edge $e=\{u,v\}$ be a ZDD variable, with a fixed induced order on edges consistent with the vertex order.

For vertices $s,t$ and forbidden set $S$, let

$$
\mathrm{PATH}(s,t,S)
$$

denote the ZDD (from Exercise 225) representing all simple paths from $s$ to $t$ whose internal vertices avoid $S$. This construction ensures no vertex repetition and standard ZDD canonicity over edge variables.

## 2. Canonical decomposition of a cycle

Let $C$ be a simple cycle, and let $u = \min(C)$ under the fixed vertex order. Then exactly two edges of $C$ are incident to $u$, say $e=\{u,v\}$ and $e'=\{u,v'\}$.

Removing $e$ yields a simple path $P$ from $v$ to $u$. Moreover, every internal vertex of $P$ is strictly greater than $u$, since otherwise $C$ would contain a vertex smaller than $u$, contradicting minimality.

Hence $P$ is an element of

$$
\mathrm{PATH}(v,u,S_u \cup \{u\}), \quad S_u=\{w \mid w<u\}.
$$

## 3. The missing ZDD operation

We now define the essential lifting operation that was missing.

For any ZDD family $F$ over edge variables and any edge variable $e$, define:

$$
\mathrm{EXT}_e(F) = \{\, S \cup \{e\} \mid S \in F \,\}.
$$

This is not a union operation but a structural transformation of the ZDD.

### Recursive ZDD definition of $\mathrm{EXT}_e$

Let $0$ and $1$ be the ZDD terminals.

1. $\mathrm{EXT}_e(0) = 0$
2. $\mathrm{EXT}_e(1)$ is the ZDD representing the singleton family $\{\{e\}\}$, namely:

$$
\mathrm{ITE}(e, 1, 0),
$$

where the high branch is $1$ and the low branch is $0$.
3. If $F$ is a nonterminal node labeled by edge variable $x$ with low and high children $F_0, F_1$, then:

- If $x = e$, then every set in $F$ already either includes or excludes $e$, so:

$$
\mathrm{EXT}_e(F) = \mathrm{ITE}(e, \mathrm{EXT}_e(F_1), \mathrm{EXT}_e(F_0)).
$$
- If $x \neq e$, then $e$ is not decided at this node, so:

$$
\mathrm{EXT}_e(F) = \mathrm{node}(x, \mathrm{EXT}_e(F_0), \mathrm{EXT}_e(F_1)).
$$

This is the standard “add-an-element-to-all-sets” lifting operation in ZDD form.

## 4. Cycle construction

For each vertex $u$ and each edge $e=\{u,v\}$, define

$$
F_{u,e} = \mathrm{PATH}(v,u,S_u \cup \{u\}).
$$

Then convert each path family into cycles by attaching the closing edge:

$$
C_{u,e} = \mathrm{EXT}_e(F_{u,e}).
$$

Finally define the ZDD for all simple cycles:

$$
Z = \bigcup_{u \in V} \ \bigcup_{e=\{u,v\}\in E} C_{u,e}.
$$

All unions are ZDD unions.

## 5. Correctness

### Soundness

Take any set $S \in C_{u,e}$. By definition of $\mathrm{EXT}_e$, we have $S = P \cup \{e\}$ for some path $P \in F_{u,e}$.

The path $P$ is simple from $v$ to $u$, with no repeated vertices and no vertex $\le u$ appearing internally. Adding $e=\{u,v\}$ closes the path into a cycle visiting each vertex exactly once. Hence $S$ is a simple cycle.

### Completeness

Let $C$ be any simple cycle and let $u=\min(C)$. Let $e=\{u,v\}$ be one of the two edges of $C$ incident to $u$. Then $C \setminus \{e\}$ is a simple path $P$ from $v$ to $u$, whose internal vertices are all greater than $u$. Thus:

$$
P \in \mathrm{PATH}(v,u,S_u \cup \{u\}).
$$

Therefore $C \setminus \{e\} \in F_{u,e}$, and applying $\mathrm{EXT}_e$ yields $C \in C_{u,e}$. Hence every simple cycle is represented.

## 6. Remarks on multiplicity

Each cycle is generated exactly twice, once for each incident edge at its minimum vertex. This does not affect correctness, since ZDDs represent sets of sets.

## Conclusion

The corrected construction replaces the invalid singleton union with the explicit ZDD lifting operator $\mathrm{EXT}_e$, defined structurally. With this operation in place, the decomposition into minimum-vertex paths yields a valid ZDD for all simple cycles in the graph.
