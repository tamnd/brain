N(v,f_0,f_1),

# 1. Structural equality of families

We first define equality as a **primitive ZDD recursion**, not a relational object.

### Definition (EQ)

$$
\operatorname{EQ}(f,g)
$$

is a Boolean-valued recursive procedure defined by simultaneous descent on the roots of $f$ and $g$.

Base cases:

- $\operatorname{EQ}(\emptyset,\emptyset)=\text{true}$
- $\operatorname{EQ}(\emptyset,N(v,g_0,g_1))=\text{false}$
- $\operatorname{EQ}(N(v,f_0,f_1),\emptyset)=\text{false}$

Recursive step (let $v=\min(V(f)\cup V(g))$):

We align missing nodes using the ZDD convention:

- if $f$ does not test $v$, replace $f$ by $N(v,f,f)$, and similarly for $g$.

Then:

$$
\operatorname{EQ}(f,g)=\operatorname{EQ}(f_0,g_0)\ \wedge\ \operatorname{EQ}(f_1,g_1).
$$

This is a standard memoized traversal on pairs $(f,g)$ and is fully ZDD-computable.

# 2. Strict subset relation (constructive ZDD form)

We define:

$$
\alpha \subset \beta \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad \alpha \subseteq \beta \ \wedge\ \neg(\alpha=\beta).
$$

We implement this as a **recursive ZDD procedure producing a family of pairs is not needed**; instead we define a family operator directly.

### Definition (SSUB family filter)

$$
\operatorname{SSUB}(f,g)
= \{ \alpha \in f \mid \exists \beta \in g:\ \alpha \subset \beta \}.
$$

We compute it by recursion on $v=\min(V(f)\cup V(g))$, using only ZDD restriction operations.

At variable $v$, decompose:

$$
f = N(v,f_0,f_1), \quad g = N(v,g_0,g_1).
$$

A strict subset witness requires either:

- a witness already in a recursive branch, or
- inclusion of $v$ in $g$ but not in $f$, which guarantees strictness.

Thus:

$$
\operatorname{SSUB}(f,g)
=
\operatorname{SSUB}(f_0, g_0 \cup g_1)
\ \cup\
\operatorname{SSUB}(f_1, g_1)
\ \cup\
\operatorname{SUB}(f, g_1 \setminus g_0).
$$

All operations on the right are standard ZDD operations (union, difference, restriction), and recursion is memoized on $(f,g)$.

No equality test is required.

# 3. Strict superset relation

Dually:

$$
\operatorname{SSUP}(f,g)
= \{ \alpha \in f \mid \exists \beta \in g:\ \alpha \supset \beta \}.
$$

We compute it by symmetry:

$$
\operatorname{SSUP}(f,g)=\operatorname{SSUB}(g,f)
\quad \text{with roles reversed and interpretation adjusted.}
$$

Thus SSUP is not an independent construction.

# 4. Maximal elements $f^{\uparrow}$

A set $\alpha \in f$ is maximal iff no strict superset exists in $f$.

Define the dominated family:

$$
D(f)=\operatorname{SSUB}(f,f).
$$

Then:

$$
f^{\uparrow} = f \setminus D(f).
$$

This is correct because:

- $\operatorname{SSUB}(f,f)$ contains exactly those $\alpha\in f$ that are strictly contained in another element of $f$,
- removing them leaves precisely maximal elements.

All operations are ZDD-recursive.

# 5. Minimal elements $f^{\downarrow}$

Symmetrically:

$$
f^{\downarrow} = f \setminus \operatorname{SSUP}(f,f).
$$

Equivalently:

$$
f^{\downarrow} = f \setminus \operatorname{SSUB}(f,f)^{\text{dual}}.
$$

# 6. Nonsubset and nonsuperset operations

These are standard and already ZDD-valid:

$$
f \nearrow g = f \setminus \operatorname{SUB}(f,g),
\qquad
f \searrow g = f \setminus \operatorname{SUP}(f,g),
$$

where SUB and SUP are the exercise 236 implementations (non-strict filters), computed by memoized recursion.

No modification is needed.

# 7. Hitting sets (transversal family) $\operatorname{TRANS}(f)$

We now give a fully ZDD-correct construction in the style of exercise 205.

A set $T$ is a hitting set iff:

$$
\forall \beta \in f,\quad T \cap \beta \neq \emptyset.
$$

Let $v=\min(V(f))$. Partition $f$ by presence of $x_v$:

$$
f_0 = f|_{x_v=0}, \qquad f_1 = f|_{x_v=1}.
$$

These are **ZDD restriction operations**, implemented directly:

- $f|_{x_v=0}$: delete all sets containing $x_v$
- $f|_{x_v=1}$: remove $x_v$ from all sets containing it

Now we construct transversals.

### Case 1: include $x_v$

If $x_v \in T$, then all sets in $f_1$ are automatically hit. Only $f_0$ remains:

$$
T = \{x_v\} \cup T', \quad T' \in \operatorname{TRANS}(f_0).
$$

Contribution:

$$
x_v \cup \operatorname{TRANS}(f_0).
$$

### Case 2: exclude $x_v$

If $x_v \notin T$, then every set in $f_1$ must still be hit using other variables. After exclusion, those sets become:

$$
f_1' = f_1|_{x_v=0},
$$

which in ZDD terms is exactly removing $x_v$ from all sets.

Thus remaining constraints are:

$$
f' = f_0 \cup f_1'.
$$

Contribution:

$$
\operatorname{TRANS}(f').
$$

### Correct recurrence

$$
\operatorname{TRANS}(f)
=
\bigl(\{x_v\} \cup \operatorname{TRANS}(f_0)\bigr)
\ \cup\
\operatorname{TRANS}(f_0 \cup f_1').
$$

### Base cases

- If $f=\emptyset$, then:

$$
\operatorname{TRANS}(f)=\{\emptyset\}.
$$

- If $\emptyset \in f$, then:

$$
\operatorname{TRANS}(f)=\emptyset,
$$

since an empty set cannot be hit.

# 8. Final construction $f^{\sharp}$

The family of minimal hitting sets is:

$$
f^{\sharp} = \operatorname{MIN}(\operatorname{TRANS}(f)),
$$

where:

$$
\operatorname{MIN}(h)=h \setminus \operatorname{SSUP}(h,h).
$$
