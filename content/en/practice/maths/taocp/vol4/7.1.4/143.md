## Solution

Let

$$
f=\left\langle
x_1x_2x_3^{2}x_4^{4}x_5^{8}x_6^{16}x_7^{32}x_8^{64}x_9^{128}x_{10}^{256}
x_{11}^{512}x_{12}^{768}x_{13}^{896}x_{14}^{960}x_{15}^{992}
x_{16}^{1008}x_{17}^{1016}x_{18}^{1020}x_{19}^{1022}x_{20}^{1023}
\right\rangle .
$$

The weights are

$$
1,1,2,4,8,16,32,64,128,256,512,
768,896,960,992,1008,1016,1020,1022,1023,
$$

which are exactly the weights of exercise 142(a) with $m=10$.

Exercise 142(a) proves that the natural ordering

$$
x_1,x_2,\ldots,x_{20}
$$

produces exponentially many distinct threshold states, while another ordering gives a BDD of quadratic size. The optimum ordering is obtained by placing complementary weights consecutively.

For $2\le k\le10$,

$$
w_k=2^{k-2},
\qquad
w_{21-k}=2^{10}-2^{k-2}=1024-w_k.
$$

Hence the complementary pairs are

$$
\begin{aligned}
&(1,1023),\\
&(1,1023),\\
&(2,1022),\\
&(4,1020),\\
&(8,1016),\\
&(16,1008),\\
&(32,992),\\
&(64,960),\\
&(128,896),\\
&(256,768).
\end{aligned}
$$

The first pair uses the two unit weights $x_1$ and $x_2$. Every remaining pair consists of one small weight and its complement.

An optimum variable ordering is therefore

$$
\boxed{
x_1,\,
x_{20},\,
x_2,\,
x_{19},\,
x_3,\,
x_{18},\,
x_4,\,
x_{17},\,
x_5,\,
x_{16},\,
x_6,\,
x_{15},\,
x_7,\,
x_{14},\,
x_8,\,
x_{13},\,
x_9,\,
x_{12},\,
x_{10},\,
x_{11}
}.
$$

Equivalently, the variables are ordered so that each weight is immediately followed by the variable whose weight is its complement with respect to $1024$.

The proof given in exercise 142(a) shows that after each complementary pair has been processed, the remaining threshold can assume only $O(n)$ distinct values. Consequently the total number of distinct BDD nodes is

$$
O(n^2),
$$

which matches the upper bound established there. Exercise 142(a) also proves that no ordering can produce fewer asymptotic states for this family, so this ordering is optimum.

Therefore the optimum ordering for the given $20$-variable function is

$$
\boxed{
x_1,\,
x_{20},\,
x_2,\,
x_{19},\,
x_3,\,
x_{18},\,
x_4,\,
x_{17},\,
x_5,\,
x_{16},\,
x_6,\,
x_{15},\,
x_7,\,
x_{14},\,
x_8,\,
x_{13},\,
x_9,\,
x_{12},\,
x_{10},\,
x_{11}
}.
$$
