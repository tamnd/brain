**Exercise 120.** [*18*] [18] Explain the hidden-permuted-weighted-bit formula (94).

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 4m

Let $x=(x_1,\dots,x_n)\in\{0,1\}^n$ and let

$$
w(x)=\sum_{i=1}^n x_i \in \{0,1,\dots,n\}.
$$

Introduce the standard sentinel convention

$$
x_0 := 0.
$$

This is not part of the input; it is only a device to make indexing uniform.

## Correct form of formula (94)

Let $\pi$ be a permutation of $\{1,\dots,n\}$. The hidden-permuted-weighted-bit function is defined by the piecewise rule

$$
\operatorname{hpwb}_\pi(x)=
\begin{cases}
0, & w(x)=0,\\[4pt]
x_{\pi(w(x))}, & w(x)\ge 1.
\end{cases}
$$

This is the correct interpretation of formula (94).

## Why this is the correct structure

### 1. Role of the weight

The quantity $w(x)$ depends only on the number of 1s in the input, not their positions. Hence it partitions $\{0,1\}^n$ into weight classes

$$
W_k=\{x : w(x)=k\}, \quad k=0,1,\dots,n.
$$

Any function depending only on $w(x)$ is symmetric in the sense of invariance under permutations of coordinates.

### 2. Why $w(x)=0$ must be special

If $w(x)=0$, then $x=(0,\dots,0)$. There is no meaningful “selected 1-position” in this case. TAOCP fixes this by forcing the output to be

$$
x_0 = 0.
$$

This keeps the function Boolean-valued and consistent with the intended behavior of hidden weighted bit constructions.

### 3. Correct role of the permutation

The permutation $\pi$ acts only on $\{1,\dots,n\}$, not on $\{0,\dots,n\}$.

Its purpose is to relabel the nonzero weight classes:

$$
k \mapsto \pi(k), \quad k\ge 1.
$$

Thus, when $w(x)=k\ge 1$, the function selects the $\pi(k)$-th input bit:

$$
\operatorname{hpwb}_\pi(x)=x_{\pi(k)}.
$$

This preserves the structure of the hidden weighted bit function while scrambling which coordinate is chosen for each weight.

### 4. Avoiding the incorrect extension

One must not treat $\pi$ as acting on $0$. Including $0$ in the permutation domain would incorrectly allow the weight class $0$ to be mapped into a nonzero index, which would contradict the defining convention that the all-zero input must produce output $0$.

Instead, the construction separates:

- $w(x)=0$: fixed output $0$
- $w(x)\in\{1,\dots,n\}$: permuted selection via $\pi$

### 5. Connection to hidden weighted bit

If $\pi$ is the identity permutation, then for $w(x)\ge 1$,

$$
\operatorname{hpwb}_\pi(x)=x_{w(x)},
$$

and for $w(x)=0$, the output is $0=x_0$. Hence we recover the standard hidden weighted bit function:

$$
\operatorname{hwb}(x)=x_{w(x)} \quad \text{with } x_0=0.
$$

Thus formula (94) generalizes hidden weighted bit by permuting the nonzero weight classes while preserving the distinguished zero case.

## Conclusion

The hidden-permuted-weighted-bit function is a symmetric Boolean function determined solely by the Hamming weight, with the additional structure that weight classes $1,\dots,n$ are relabeled by a permutation $\pi$, while weight $0$ is fixed to output $0$. This yields exactly formula (94) in TAOCP.
