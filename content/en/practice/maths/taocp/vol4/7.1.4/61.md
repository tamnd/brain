## Setup

Let $f$ and $g$ be Boolean functions with BDDs $B(f)$ and $B(g)$. Let $\alpha$ be a node of $B(f)$ and $\beta$ a node of $B(g)$. Let $\alpha \diamond \beta$ denote the corresponding node in the melded BDD $f \diamond g$, defined by the recursive construction (37), in which branch nodes are formed in pairs and sinks are paired in the natural way, with reduction applied afterward.

For any node $v$ in a BDD, let $\mathrm{in}(v)$ denote its in-degree, defined as the number of incoming LO or HI edges from branch nodes (the root has $\mathrm{in}(\mathrm{root})=1$ by convention).

The goal is to prove

$$
\mathrm{in}(\alpha \diamond \beta) \le \mathrm{in}(\alpha)\,\mathrm{in}(\beta).
$$

## Solution

Each node of a melded BDD is obtained from a pair of nodes, one from $B(f)$ and one from $B(g)$, and each directed edge in the melded structure arises from simultaneously following corresponding edges in the two component BDDs under the recursive construction (37).

Let $\mathrm{Pred}_f(\alpha)$ be the set of predecessors of $\alpha$ in $B(f)$, meaning nodes $\alpha'$ such that there is an LO or HI edge $\alpha' \to \alpha$, and define $\mathrm{Pred}_g(\beta)$ analogously.

Every predecessor of $\alpha \diamond \beta$ arises from a node pair $(\alpha',\beta')$ such that $\alpha' \in \mathrm{Pred}_f(\alpha)$ and $\beta' \in \mathrm{Pred}_g(\beta)$, and such that both edges $\alpha' \to \alpha$ and $\beta' \to \beta$ correspond to the same branch choice (LO or HI) at the same variable level. Under the meld construction, this produces an edge

$$
(\alpha' \diamond \beta') \to (\alpha \diamond \beta).
$$

Define a mapping from incoming edges of $\alpha \diamond \beta$ to pairs of incoming edges $(e_f,e_g)$, where $e_f$ is an edge into $\alpha$ and $e_g$ is an edge into $\beta$, determined by projecting each melded edge onto its two component BDD edges. This mapping is well-defined because every edge in the melded BDD is generated from a unique pair of corresponding edges in the two input BDDs at the same variable index.

If two distinct incoming edges into $\alpha \diamond \beta$ projected to the same pair $(e_f,e_g)$, then they would originate from the same pair of predecessor nodes $(\alpha',\beta')$ and the same branch label, hence they would coincide as edges in the construction, contradicting their distinctness. This establishes injectivity of the projection from incoming edges of $\alpha \diamond \beta$ into the Cartesian product of incoming edges of $\alpha$ and $\beta$.

Therefore the number of incoming edges satisfies

$$
\mathrm{in}(\alpha \diamond \beta)
\le |\mathrm{Pred}_f(\alpha)| \cdot |\mathrm{Pred}_g(\beta)|
= \mathrm{in}(\alpha)\,\mathrm{in}(\beta).
$$

Reduction of the BDD cannot increase in-degree, since it only merges identical nodes or deletes redundant tests, which removes edges rather than introducing new ones. Hence the inequality remains valid in the reduced melded BDD.

This completes the proof. âˆŽ

## Verification

Each incoming edge of $\alpha \diamond \beta$ is generated only from synchronized traversal of one incoming edge to $\alpha$ and one incoming edge to $\beta$, since the meld construction preserves variable ordering and pairs corresponding LO/HI choices. This defines an injection from incoming edges of $\alpha \diamond \beta$ into ordered pairs of incoming edges of $\alpha$ and $\beta$. The size of a set injects into the size of its image, so the in-degree bound follows. Reduction steps remove edges or identify nodes, so they cannot increase in-degree and preserve the inequality.

## Notes

The argument extends to any DAG product construction in which edges are formed by synchronized pairing of labeled transitions. In BDD terminology, the result expresses that local fan-in in the product graph is bounded by the product of fan-in values in the factor graphs, independent of global sharing effects introduced by reduction.
