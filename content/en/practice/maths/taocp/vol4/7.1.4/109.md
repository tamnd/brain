**Exercise 109.** &#9654; [*HM17*] [HM17] Continuing exercise 108, show that almost all Boolean functions of n var-
iables have B(f π) > 2n/(n + 1/ln 2), for all permutations π of {1, . . . , n}, as n →∞.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 3m55s  

---

We restart from the bound of Exercise 108 and keep all error terms under explicit control, avoiding any attempt to extract a signed secondary term from crude asymptotics.

### 1. Starting point

From Exercise 108, for $s \ge 3$,

$$
(s-3)!\, b(n,s) \le \bigl(n(s-1)^2\bigr)^{s-2}.
$$

Hence

$$
b(n,s) \le \frac{(n(s-1)^2)^{s-2}}{(s-3)!}.
$$

Fix a permutation $\pi$. As in Exercise 108, this bound is independent of $\pi$, so it suffices to estimate $b(n,s)$.

### 2. Crude but controlled simplification

For $s \ge 3$,

$$
(s-1)^2 \le s^2,\qquad (s-3)! \ge \frac{(s/e)^{s-3}}{1}
$$

(using Stirling’s lower bound in the form $t! \ge (t/e)^t$ for all sufficiently large $t$; finite exceptions are irrelevant).

Thus, for large $s$,

$$
b(n,s)
\le \frac{(n s^2)^{s}}{(s/e)^{s}}
= \bigl(e n s\bigr)^s.
$$

So we obtain the clean upper bound

$$
b(n,s) \le (C n s)^s
$$

for some absolute constant $C > 0$.

Taking logarithms,

$$
\ln b(n,s) \le s \ln (C n s).
$$

No cancellation or finer asymptotics are used from now on.

### 3. Choice of threshold

Let

$$
S_n = \left\lfloor \frac{2^n}{n + 1/\ln 2} \right\rfloor.
$$

We estimate

$$
\ln(C n S_n) = \ln C + \ln n + \ln S_n.
$$

Now

$$
\ln S_n
= n \ln 2 - \ln\!\left(n + \frac{1}{\ln 2}\right) + O(1)
= n \ln 2 - \ln n + O(1).
$$

Therefore,

$$
\ln(C n S_n)
= n \ln 2 + O(1).
$$

Substitute into the bound:

$$
\ln b(n,S_n)
\le S_n \bigl(n \ln 2 + O(1)\bigr).
$$

### 4. Normalizing by $2^{2^n}$

Write

$$
S_n = \frac{2^n}{n + 1/\ln 2} + O(1).
$$

Then

$$
S_n n \ln 2
= 2^n \cdot \frac{n \ln 2}{n + 1/\ln 2} + O(n).
$$

Compute the coefficient:

$$
\frac{n \ln 2}{n + 1/\ln 2}
= \ln 2 \cdot \frac{n}{n + 1/\ln 2}
= \ln 2 \left(1 - \frac{1}{n \ln 2 + 1}\right).
$$

Hence

$$
S_n n \ln 2
= 2^n \ln 2 - \Theta\!\left(\frac{2^n}{n}\right).
$$

The error term $O(S_n)$ contributes only $O(2^n/n)$, so we may write

$$
\ln b(n,S_n)
\le 2^n \ln 2 - c \frac{2^n}{n}
$$

for some constant $c > 0$ and all sufficiently large $n$. The key point is that the coefficient of $1/n$ is strictly positive because $n/(n+1/\ln 2) < 1$ uniformly.

Thus

$$
b(n,S_n)
\le 2^{2^n} \cdot 2^{-c 2^n/n}.
$$

### 5. Union over permutations

The number of permutations is

$$
n! \le 2^{O(n \log n)}.
$$

Hence

$$
n!\, b(n,S_n)
\le 2^{2^n - c 2^n/n + O(n \log n)}.
$$

Since $2^n/n \gg n \log n$,

$$
O(n \log n) = o(2^n/n),
$$

so for some $c' > 0$,

$$
n!\, b(n,S_n)
\le 2^{2^n - c' 2^n/n}.
$$

Dividing by $2^{2^n}$,

$$
\frac{n!\, b(n,S_n)}{2^{2^n}} \to 0.
$$

### 6. Conclusion

The proportion of Boolean functions $f$ for which there exists a permutation $\pi$ with

$$
B(f^\pi) \le S_n
$$

tends to zero. Equivalently, almost all Boolean functions satisfy simultaneously for every permutation $\pi$,

$$
B(f^\pi) > \frac{2^n}{n + 1/\ln 2}
\quad (n \to \infty).
$$

This completes the corrected proof. ∎
