## Solution

Let $f(x_1,\dots,x_n)$ be a monotone Boolean function. As in (49), write

$$
f(x_1,\dots,x_n) = (g,h),
$$

where $g$ and $h$ are monotone functions of $x_2,\dots,x_n$ defined by

$$
g(x_2,\dots,x_n)=f(0,x_2,\dots,x_n), \quad h(x_2,\dots,x_n)=f(1,x_2,\dots,x_n),
$$

with $g \le h$ pointwise.

Self-duality means

$$
f(x_1,\dots,x_n)=\neg f(\bar x_1,\dots,\bar x_n),
$$

where $\bar x_i = 1-x_i$. Substituting $x_1=0$ and $x_1=1$ gives

$$
g(x_2,\dots,x_n)=\neg h(\bar x_2,\dots,\bar x_n),
$$

$$
h(x_2,\dots,x_n)=\neg g(\bar x_2,\dots,\bar x_n).
$$

Hence $h$ is uniquely determined by $g$ via

$$
h = g^{*}, \quad g^{*}(x_2,\dots,x_n)=\neg g(\bar x_2,\dots,\bar x_n).
$$

The monotonicity constraint $g \le h$ becomes

$$
g(x) \le \neg g(\bar x)
$$

for all $x \in {0,1}^{n-1}$.

Conversely, any monotone $g$ satisfying this inequality determines a monotone pair $(g,h)$ with $h=g^{*}$, and the resulting $f$ is self-dual.

Thus the counting of self-dual monotone functions reduces to counting monotone functions $g$ of $n-1$ variables satisfying the pointwise constraint $g \le g^{*}$.

Let $S(n)$ denote the number of self-dual monotone Boolean functions of $n$ variables. Then the modification of recurrence (49) replaces the sum over all pairs $(g,h)$ with $g \le h$ by a sum over single functions $g$ with $g \le g^{*}$. Hence

$$
S(n)=\#\{g \in \mathcal{M}_{n-1} : g \le g^{*}\},
$$

where $\mathcal{M}_{n-1}$ denotes the set of monotone Boolean functions on $n-1$ variables.

In BDD terms, if (49) counts all admissible ordered pairs of compatible sub-BDDs for the two cofactors, the modification identifies the HI-sub-BDD with the dual of the LO-sub-BDD, leaving only those sub-BDDs that are consistent under the involution induced by complementation of variables and swapping of $\bot$ and $\top$. The recurrence is obtained by restricting the combinatorial sum in (49) to fixed points of this involution.

This completes the derivation of the modified counting procedure. ∎
