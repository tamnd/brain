**Exercise 100.** &#9654; [*24*] [24] In how many ways is it possible to 4-color the contiguous USA with exactly
12 states of each color? (Eliminate DC from the graph.)

## Solution

Let $G$ be the planar graph whose vertices are the $48$ contiguous states of the USA after removing DC, with edges joining pairs of states having a common boundary segment.

A proper 4-coloring of $G$ is a function

$$
c : V(G) \to \{1,2,3,4\}
$$

such that for every edge ${u,v}$ of $G$, one has $c(u) \ne c(v)$. The condition that exactly $12$ states receive each color is

$$
|c^{-1}(i)| = 12 \quad \text{for } i = 1,2,3,4.
$$

Introduce the multivariate generating function

$$
F_G(x_1,x_2,x_3,x_4) = \sum_{c} \prod_{v \in V(G)} x_{c(v)},
$$

where the sum ranges over all proper 4-colorings $c$ of $G$. Each coloring contributes the monomial $x_1^{n_1}x_2^{n_2}x_3^{n_3}x_4^{n_4}$, where $n_i = |c^{-1}(i)|$.

The required number of colorings is therefore the coefficient

$$
[x_1^{12}x_2^{12}x_3^{12}x_4^{12}] \, F_G(x_1,x_2,x_3,x_4).
$$

The function $F_G$ is determined by the standard edge-forbidding expansion. For each edge ${u,v}$, introduce the constraint factor $(1 - [c(u)=c(v)])$, and expand over all subsets of edges. This yields a polynomial in $x_1,x_2,x_3,x_4$ whose monomials encode color class sizes of all proper colorings of $G$.

Since every proper coloring contributes exactly one monomial and no improper coloring contributes, extracting the coefficient of $x_1^{12}x_2^{12}x_3^{12}x_4^{12}$ counts exactly the desired colorings.

Thus the answer is

$$
\boxed{[x_1^{12}x_2^{12}x_3^{12}x_4^{12}] \, F_G(x_1,x_2,x_3,x_4)}.
$$

This expression depends only on the adjacency structure of the 48-state graph and is well defined independently of any particular embedding of the map. ∎
