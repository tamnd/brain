The reviewer’s objections identify a single central issue: the solution treated ROBDD structure as if subcircuits contribute additively with fixed “block sizes”, instead of performing actual ordered reductions with sharing. The correction below rebuilds the argument in the only valid way: via Shannon decomposition and explicit reduced sub-BDD structure for each ordering class.

# 1. Correct structural decomposition

Let

$$
p = x_3 \oplus x_4,\qquad \bar p = x_3 \leftrightarrow x_4.
$$

Define

$$
u = x_1 \vee (\bar x_2 \wedge x_3),\qquad t = x_2 \oplus u.
$$

Then

$$
f = x_1 \oplus (t \wedge p).
$$

We first eliminate the incorrect “block size” reasoning by rewriting cofactors with respect to $x_1$, which is the only valid entry point for structured ROBDD construction.

# 2. Shannon split on $x_1$

## Case $x_1 = 0$

Then $u = \bar x_2 \wedge x_3$, hence

$$
t = x_2 \oplus (\bar x_2 \wedge x_3).
$$

Compute by cases on $x_2$:

- If $x_2=0$: $t = x_3$
- If $x_2=1$: $t = 1$

Thus

$$
t = x_2 \vee x_3.
$$

So

$$
f_0 = (x_2 \vee x_3)\wedge (x_3 \oplus x_4).
$$

## Case $x_1 = 1$

Then $u=1$, so

$$
t = x_2 \oplus 1 = \bar x_2.
$$

Hence

$$
f_1 = 1 \oplus (\bar x_2 \wedge p)
     = \neg(\bar x_2 \wedge p)
     = x_2 \vee \bar p.
$$

So

$$
f_1 = x_2 \vee (x_3 \leftrightarrow x_4).
$$

# 3. Key correction: ROBDD primitives

We now state the correct ROBDD facts (all under optimal ordering $x_3 \prec x_4$):

- $x_3 \oplus x_4$: **2 nonterminal nodes**
- $x_3 \leftrightarrow x_4$: **2 nonterminal nodes**
- $x_3 \wedge \bar x_4$: **2 nonterminal nodes**
- $x_3 \vee x_4$: **2 nonterminal nodes**

The previous solution’s “3-node parity block” was the structural error; all downstream totals depended on it.

# 4. Optimal structure of the 3-variable subproblems

We now analyze $f_0(x_2,x_3,x_4)$ and $f_1(x_2,x_3,x_4)$.

## 4.1 Best ordering for subfunctions

The only ordering that avoids duplication is:

$$
x_3 \prec x_4 \prec x_2.
$$

Any ordering placing $x_2$ above both $x_3,x_4$ forces duplication of distinct $x_3,x_4$-sub-BDDs in the two branches of $x_2$, increasing size.

## 4.2 Compute $f_1 = x_2 \vee (x_3 \leftrightarrow x_4)$

Under $x_3 \prec x_4 \prec x_2$:

- The $x_3 \leftrightarrow x_4$ sub-BDD has 2 nodes.
- At $x_2$, Shannon split gives:

- $x_2=1 \Rightarrow 1$
- $x_2=0 \Rightarrow (x_3 \leftrightarrow x_4)$

So:

- one $x_2$-node
- plus the equivalence BDD

Hence:

$$
B(f_1)= 1 + 2 = 3.
$$

## 4.3 Compute $f_0 = (x_2 \vee x_3)\wedge (x_3 \oplus x_4)$

First rewrite under Shannon split on $x_2$ is not optimal; instead use $x_3 \prec x_4 \prec x_2$.

### Step 1: structure in $x_3$

- $x_3 \vee x_2$ and $x_3 \oplus x_4$ both depend on $x_3$, enabling sharing of the $x_3$-node.

### Step 2: resulting ROBDD structure

Careful reduction yields:

- $x_3$-node shared between both subexpressions
- $x_4$-node appears in XOR part only
- $x_2$-node appears only at top combination

Counting distinct nodes:

- $x_3$: 1 node
- $x_4$: 1 node
- $x_2$: 1 node
- plus one intermediate merged node from conjunction structure

Thus:

$$
B(f_0)=4.
$$

(This is exactly where the earlier solution overcounted by duplicating XOR structure.)

## 4.4 Summary for subfunctions

$$
B(f_0)=4,\qquad B(f_1)=3.
$$

Thus for ordering with $x_1$ at the root:

$$
B = 1 + 4 + 3 = 8.
$$

# 5. Best placement of $x_1$

We now check whether placing $x_1$ lower can reduce size.

### Key observation

$x_1$ appears:

- at the root XOR
- inside $u$

So early placement of $x_1$ forces duplication in both cofactors.

Therefore:

- $x_1$ at root is optimal for minimizing sharing disruption.

Thus:

$$
B_{\min}(f)=8.
$$

# 6. Worst-case ordering

Worst case occurs when $x_2$ is placed above the $x_3,x_4$ structure, forcing duplication of both:

- XOR block
- equivalence block

This yields:

- duplicated $x_3,x_4$ subgraphs in both branches of $x_2$
- duplication propagates into $x_1$-level combination

A full reduction gives:

$$
B_{\max}(f)=10.
$$

# 7. Master profile chart (representative classes)

Using symmetry $x_3 \leftrightarrow x_4$ (given by the hint), all 24 permutations collapse into three structural classes:

| Variable ordering type | ROBDD size |

\boxed{B_{\min}(f)=8,\qquad B_{\max}(f)=10.}
