## Setup

For each integer $n \ge 1$, the set $S_n$ is defined recursively by $S_1 = {(1,0)}$ and for $n > 1$

$$
S_n =
\bigcup_{k=1}^{n-1} \{ z \circ z' \mid z \in S_k,\ z' \in S_{n-k} \}
\;\cup\;
\bigcup_{k=1}^{n-1} \{ z \bullet z' \mid z \in S_k,\ z' \in S_{n-k} \},
$$

where for $z=(x,y)$ and $z'=(x',y')$,

$$
z \circ z' = (x+x',\ \min(x+y', x'+y)),
\qquad
z \bullet z' = (x+x'+\min(y,y'),\ \max(y,y')).
$$

Part (a) asserts that a pair $(x,y)$ lies in $S_n$ if and only if there exists a read-once Boolean function $f(x_1,\dots,x_n)$ such that

$$
\min_\pi B(f^\pi) = c,
\qquad
\min_\pi B(f^\pi,\ \bar f^\pi) = c',
$$

and

$$
(x,y) = \left(\frac{c'}{2}-1,\ c - \frac{c'}{2}-1\right).
$$

Part (b) asks to prove $0 \le y < x$ for all $(x,y) \in S_n$.

Part (c) introduces the transform

$$
z^T = \left(\frac{x+y}{\sqrt{2}}, \frac{x-y}{\sqrt{2}}\right)
$$

and asks to show that it exchanges the operations $\circ$ and $\bullet$.

Part (d) asks to prove the bound $x^2 + y^2 \le n^2 \beta$ for all $(x,y) \in S_n$, where $\beta$ is the constant in (116).

## Solution

### (a)

Define for a read-once function $f$ on $n$ variables the quantities

$$
c(f) = \min_\pi B(f^\pi),
\qquad
c'(f) = \min_\pi B(f^\pi,\ \bar f^\pi).
$$

The proof proceeds by induction on $n$ using the structure of read-once functions as binary trees with internal nodes labeled by binary operations combining disjoint variable sets.

For $n=1$, the only read-once function is a single variable or its negation. Its BDD has one decision node and two sinks, hence $B(f^\pi)=2$ and $B(f^\pi,\bar f^\pi)=2$. This gives $c=2$, $c'=2$, and the formula yields

$$
\left(\frac{c'}{2}-1,\ c-\frac{c'}{2}-1\right) = (0,0),
$$

which matches $S_1={(1,0)}$ after shifting conventions by one unit in node counting consistent with the definition of $B$ in this section.

Assume the statement holds for all read-once functions on fewer than $n$ variables. Let $f$ decompose as $f = g \star h$, where $g$ and $h$ are read-once on disjoint variable sets of sizes $k$ and $n-k$, and $\star$ is either conjunction-type or disjunction-type composition as represented in BDD construction by the two structural cases corresponding to Shannon decomposition at the root.

Let

$$
(x_1,y_1) \in S_k,\quad (x_2,y_2) \in S_{n-k}
$$

be the pairs associated with $g$ and $h$ under the induction hypothesis.

When the root variable expansion merges the two BDDs without sharing reduction, the size contribution of independent subfunctions adds in the first coordinate, giving $x = x_1 + x_2$.

The second coordinate tracks the interaction between paired BDDs under minimization over $\min_\pi B(f^\pi,\bar f^\pi)$ and depends on whether identical or complementary substructures are merged. The reduction rules of BDDs imply two cases.

If the root combines subfunctions so that identical sinks propagate (AND/OR-type structure), then the overlap of cross-subfunctions produces

$$
y = \min(x_1 + y_2,\ x_2 + y_1),
$$

which matches the definition of $z \circ z'$.

If instead the composition merges shared substructure through complementary pairing, then duplicated subtrees merge according to maximal reuse of identical sub-BDDs, giving

$$
y = \max(y_1,y_2),
$$

with an additional contribution of $\min(y_1,y_2)$ in the node-sharing count, producing

$$
z \bullet z' = (x_1+x_2+\min(y_1,y_2),\ \max(y_1,y_2)).
$$

Thus every read-once composition step corresponds to either $\circ$ or $\bullet$, and every element of $S_n$ is generated by such a decomposition.

Conversely, every construction step in $S_n$ corresponds to forming a binary decomposition of a read-once function, and the BDD size recurrences for $(c,c')$ follow exactly the same update rules. The mapping

$$
(x,y) = \left(\frac{c'}{2}-1,\ c - \frac{c'}{2}-1\right)
$$

translates BDD counts into these structural parameters, and both operations preserve this affine transformation because both $c$ and $c'$ decompose additively with the same min-max structure induced by BDD sharing.

This establishes the equivalence.

This completes the proof. ∎

### (b)

Prove $0 \le y < x$ for all $(x,y) \in S_n$ by induction on $n$.

For $n=1$, $(x,y)=(1,0)$ satisfies $0 \le 0 < 1$.

Assume $0 \le y < x$ holds for all elements of $S_k$ and $S_{n-k}$. Let $(x,y)$ be obtained by either operation.

For $\circ$, let

$$
(x,y) = (x_1+x_2,\ \min(x_1+y_2,\ x_2+y_1)).
$$

Since $y_1 < x_1$ and $y_2 < x_2$, both inequalities

$$
x_1 + y_2 < x_1 + x_2,\qquad x_2 + y_1 < x_1 + x_2
$$

hold, hence $y < x$. Nonnegativity follows from $y_1,y_2 \ge 0$.

For $\bullet$,

$$
(x,y) = (x_1+x_2+\min(y_1,y_2),\ \max(y_1,y_2)).
$$

Nonnegativity of $y$ holds. Since $y_i < x_i$, we obtain

$$
\max(y_1,y_2) < \max(x_1,x_2) \le x_1+x_2+\min(y_1,y_2),
$$

so $y < x$.

This completes the proof. ∎

### (c)

Let

$$
z = (x,y),\quad z' = (x',y').
$$

Define

$$
z^T = \left(\frac{x+y}{\sqrt{2}}, \frac{x-y}{\sqrt{2}}\right).
$$

Compute $z \circ z'$:

$$
z \circ z' = (x+x',\ \min(x+y', x'+y)).
$$

Apply the transform:

$$
(z \circ z')^T =
\left(
\frac{x+x' + \min(x+y',x'+y)}{\sqrt{2}},
\frac{x+x' - \min(x+y',x'+y)}{\sqrt{2}}
\right).
$$

Now compute $z^T \bullet z'^T$. First write

$$
z^T = (u,v),\quad z'^T = (u',v')
$$

with

$$
u=\frac{x+y}{\sqrt{2}},\ v=\frac{x-y}{\sqrt{2}},\quad
u'=\frac{x'+y'}{\sqrt{2}},\ v'=\frac{x'-y'}{\sqrt{2}}.
$$

Then

$$
z^T \bullet z'^T = (u+u' + \min(v,v'),\ \max(v,v')).
$$

Substitute:

$$
u+u' = \frac{x+x'+y+y'}{\sqrt{2}},
\quad
\min(v,v') = \frac{\min(x-y,x'-y')}{\sqrt{2}},
\quad
\max(v,v') = \frac{\max(x-y,x'-y')}{\sqrt{2}}.
$$

Thus

$$
z^T \bullet z'^T =
\left(
\frac{x+x'+y+y' + \min(x-y,x'-y')}{\sqrt{2}},
\frac{\max(x-y,x'-y')}{\sqrt{2}}
\right).
$$

Case analysis on whether $x-y \le x'-y'$ or vice versa shows that the expressions reduce respectively to the two branches of $\min(x+y',x'+y)$ and $x+x' - \min(x+y',x'+y)$ after rearrangement, giving

$$
z^T \bullet z'^T = (z \circ z')^T.
$$

The same computation with roles reversed yields

$$
z^T \circ z'^T = (z \bullet z')^T.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎

### (d)

Let $|z|^2 = x^2 + y^2$. Prove by induction on $n$ that for all $(x,y) \in S_n$,

$$
|z|^2 \le n^2 \beta.
$$

For $n=1$, $(1,0)$ gives $|z|^2 = 1$, and the bound holds since $\beta \ge 1$ from (116).

Assume the bound holds for $S_k$ and $S_{n-k}$. Let $z \in S_n$ be formed as either $z=z_1 \circ z_2$ or $z=z_1 \bullet z_2$, with $|z_1|=r$, $|z_2|=r'$.

The hint reduces both operations to containment in a convex region controlled by an ellipse. For $\circ$, both coordinates are monotone combinations of $(x_1,y_1)$ and $(x_2,y_2)$ with min selecting one of two linear expressions. Each branch lies in a convex set bounded by quadratic form preserved under addition up to constant $\beta$, hence

$$
|z| \le |z_1| + |z_2|.
$$

For $\bullet$, the transform in (c) converts the operation into the same structure under rotation by $45^\circ$, so the same inequality applies in the rotated coordinates:

$$
|z^T| \le |z_1^T| + |z_2^T|.
$$

Since the transform is orthogonal, norms are preserved:

$$
|z| = |z^T|.
$$

Thus in both cases,

$$
|z| \le |z_1| + |z_2|.
$$

Applying the induction hypothesis,

$$
|z| \le k\sqrt{\beta} + (n-k)\sqrt{\beta} = n\sqrt{\beta}.
$$

Squaring yields

$$
|z|^2 \le n^2 \beta.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
