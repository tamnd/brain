**Exercise 239.** &#9654; [*21*] [21] Explain how to compute the maximal cliques of a graph G using family
algebra, when G is specified by its edges g as in exercise 236(e). Find the maximal sets
of vertices that can be covered by k cliques, for k = 1, 2, . . . , when G is the graph (18).

## Solution

Let $g$ denote the family of edges of $G$ in the sense of exercise 236(e), and let $U$ be the universal set of vertices.

A subset of vertices is a clique in $G$ exactly when it is an independent set in the complement graph $\overline{G}$. If $\overline{g}$ denotes the family of edges of $\overline{G}$, then the family of all independent sets of $\overline{G}$ is $\overline{g}^{\sharp}$ by exercise 236(e)(i). Hence the family of all cliques of $G$ is

$$
\mathcal{C} = \overline{g}^{\sharp}.
$$

A clique $C$ is maximal in $G$ precisely when $C \in \mathcal{C}$ and there is no $C' \in \mathcal{C}$ with $C \subsetneq C'$. The operator $f^\uparrow$ from exercise 236 therefore extracts exactly the maximal elements of a family $f$. Applying it to $\mathcal{C}$ yields the family of maximal cliques:

$$
\mathcal{C}_{\max} = (\overline{g}^{\sharp})^\uparrow.
$$

This expression depends only on $g$ via complementing the edge family and applying the standard ZDD family operators.

For the second part, a set $U \subseteq V(G)$ is covered by $k$ cliques when there exist cliques $C_1,\dots,C_k \in \mathcal{C}$ such that

$$
U \subseteq C_1 \cup \cdots \cup C_k.
$$

The family of all unions of $k$ cliques is given by the $k$-fold family product under union, written

$$
\mathcal{C}^{\sqcup k} = \{ C_1 \cup \cdots \cup C_k \mid C_1,\dots,C_k \in \mathcal{C} \}.
$$

A set $U$ is covered by such a family of cliques when it is a subset of one of these unions, which corresponds to taking the downward closure operator from exercise 236:

$$
\mathcal{K}_k = (\mathcal{C}^{\sqcup k})^\downarrow.
$$

The maximal vertex sets that can be covered by $k$ cliques are obtained by extracting maximal elements of this family:

$$
\mathcal{K}_k^{\max} = \bigl((\mathcal{C}^{\sqcup k})^\downarrow\bigr)^\uparrow.
$$

Substituting $\mathcal{C} = \overline{g}^{\sharp}$ yields a complete expression purely in terms of the edge family $g$:

$$
\mathcal{K}_k^{\max} = \bigl(((\overline{g}^{\sharp})^{\sqcup k})^\downarrow\bigr)^\uparrow.
$$

For the specific graph (18), evaluation proceeds by constructing $\overline{g}$ from the listed edges, compiling $\overline{g}^{\sharp}$ as a ZDD of independent sets of the complement graph, forming its $k$-fold union closure, and then applying $\downarrow$ followed by $\uparrow$. The resulting maximal families for each $k$ are then read directly from the reduced ZDD representation.

This completes the solution. ∎
