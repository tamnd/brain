**Exercise 188.** [*16*] [16] Express the 16 Boolean functions f(x1, x2) as families of subsets of {1, 2}.

**Verified:** no

## Solution

Let $U={\varnothing,{1},{2},{1,2}}$.

For a Boolean function $f(x_1,x_2)$, identify each input pair $(x_1,x_2)\in{0,1}^2$ with the subset

$$
S(x_1,x_2)=\{\,i\in\{1,2\}\mid x_i=1\,\}.
$$

Thus

$$
(0,0)\leftrightarrow \varnothing,\quad (0,1)\leftrightarrow \{2\},\quad (1,0)\leftrightarrow \{1\},\quad (1,1)\leftrightarrow \{1,2\}.
$$

The family of subsets associated with $f$ is

$$
\mathcal{F}(f)=\{\,S(x_1,x_2)\in U \mid f(x_1,x_2)=1\,\}.
$$

Write the truth table of $f$ in lexicographic order as

$$
(f(0,0), f(0,1), f(1,0), f(1,1)) = (a_0,a_1,a_2,a_3).
$$

Then

$$
\mathcal{F}(f)=\{\varnothing : a_0=1\}\cup\{\{2\}: a_1=1\}\cup\{\{1\}: a_2=1\}\cup\{\{1,2\}: a_3=1\}.
$$

Each of the 16 Boolean functions corresponds uniquely to one subset of $U$, hence to one 4-bit string.

The correspondence is as follows.

$0000 \mapsto \varnothing$.

$0001 \mapsto {{1,2}}$.

$0010 \mapsto {{1}}$.

$0011 \mapsto {{1},{1,2}}$.

$0100 \mapsto {{2}}$.

$0101 \mapsto {{2},{1,2}}$.

$0110 \mapsto {{2},{1}}$.

$0111 \mapsto {{2},{1},{1,2}}$.

$1000 \mapsto {\varnothing}$.

$1001 \mapsto {\varnothing,{1,2}}$.

$1010 \mapsto {\varnothing,{1}}$.

$1011 \mapsto {\varnothing,{1},{1,2}}$.

$1100 \mapsto {\varnothing,{2}}$.

$1101 \mapsto {\varnothing,{2},{1,2}}$.

$1110 \mapsto {\varnothing,{2},{1}}$.

$1111 \mapsto U$.

This establishes a bijection between Boolean functions on ${x_1,x_2}$ and families of subsets of ${1,2}$. ∎
