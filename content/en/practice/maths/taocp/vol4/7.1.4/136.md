**Exercise 136.** &#9654; [*M34*] [M34] What is the master profile chart of the median-of-medians function
⟨⟨x11x12 . . . x1n⟩⟨x21x22 . . . x2n⟩. . . ⟨xm1xm2 . . . xmn⟩⟩,
when m and n are odd integers? What is the best ordering? (There are mn variables.)

The previous argument failed because it collapsed a full threshold profile into a 3-state system and then incorrectly treated inner and outer BDDs as additively independent. The corrected analysis restores the actual profile structure of majority functions and then applies it carefully to the composed function.

## 1. Structure of the function

Let

$$
f=\Big\langle \langle x_{11},\dots,x_{1n}\rangle,\;\langle x_{21},\dots,x_{2n}\rangle,\;\dots,\;\langle x_{m1},\dots,x_{mn}\rangle \Big\rangle,
$$

with $m,n$ odd. Let

$$
t=\frac{n+1}{2},\qquad s=\frac{m+1}{2}.
$$

Each row function is a majority:

$$
g_i = \langle x_{i1},\dots,x_{in}\rangle,
\quad
f = \langle g_1,\dots,g_m\rangle.
$$

Thus $f$ is a majority of $m$ majority blocks of size $n$.

## 2. Correct state of a partially evaluated majority

Fix a row $i$. After reading $k$ of its variables, suppose $a$ of them are $1$.

The correct residual information is not ternary. It is the interval of possible completions:

- Minimum possible ones: $a$
- Maximum possible ones: $a+(n-k)$

Thus the row is determined by the pair $(a,k)$, and two prefixes yield the same subfunction iff they induce the same feasible interval with respect to the threshold $t$.

As in the standard majority profile analysis, this yields exactly

$$
k+2
$$

distinct subfunctions after $k$ variables of a row have been fixed.

These correspond to the possible values of the “critical deficit”

$$
d = t-a,
\quad \text{truncated to feasibility bounds}.
$$

So a row evolves through a chain of length $n+2$ over its full evaluation.

## 3. Master profile chart for a row-by-row ordering

Consider the ordering

$$
(x_{11},\dots,x_{1n},x_{21},\dots,x_{2n},\dots,x_{m1},\dots,x_{mn}).
$$

### 3.1 Within a single row

After processing $k$ variables of a row, the number of distinct subfunctions is

$$
k+2.
$$

This is the standard majority profile chart.

### 3.2 After completing full rows

After finishing $j$ full rows, each row contributes a Boolean value $g_i \in \{0,1\}$. The remaining function is a majority on $j$ bits, hence depends only on the number of ones among the completed rows.

Thus the number of distinct subfunctions after completing $j$ rows is

$$
j+2.
$$

### 3.3 Master profile chart (row-major ordering)

The master profile chart is therefore:

- within row $i$: $1,2,\dots,n+2$
- across rows: $1,2,\dots,m+2$

No blow-up occurs beyond the standard majority growth at each level.

## 4. Why interleaving cannot improve the profile

Suppose variables from different rows are interleaved.

After any prefix, each partially exposed row is in one of its $k+2$ majority states. A full global subfunction is determined by the vector of row states.

However, the outer majority depends only on the feasible intervals of the rows, and these intervals evolve independently only when rows are fully separated in the ordering.

If rows are interleaved, then many rows remain partially evaluated simultaneously, and the state space becomes strictly a refinement of the Cartesian product of independent row chains. This increases, not decreases, the number of distinguishable residual subfunctions.

Thus any interleaving can only refine the profile chart beyond the row-major chain structure and cannot reduce the maximum width.

Hence row-major ordering is optimal.

## 5. BDD size computation

A reduced ordered BDD for a majority function on $k$ variables has $k+2$ nodes (including sinks) under an optimal ordering.

In row-major ordering:

- Each row contributes a chain of $n+2$ nodes.
- These chains are reused only in structure, but not merged across different levels because variable indices differ by row.

Thus each of the $m$ rows contributes $n+1$ internal decision nodes plus shared sinks.

The two sinks $\bot,\top$ are shared globally.

Therefore the total number of nodes is

$$
m(n+1) + 2.
$$

## 6. Optimality

Any ordering that interleaves rows forces simultaneous partial evaluation of multiple majority blocks. This increases the number of distinct residual threshold intervals that must be tracked at the same depth.

Since each row already requires a linear chain of $n+2$ distinct majority states, and these cannot be merged across different variable levels, interleaving introduces additional incomparable partial states rather than collapsing them.

Thus no ordering can reduce the linear per-row contribution, and row-by-row ordering is optimal.

## Final answer

### Master profile chart

Under optimal ordering (row-by-row),

- within each row: profile sizes $k+2$, $0 \le k \le n$,
- across rows: profile sizes $j+2$, $0 \le j \le m$.

So the master profile chart is the standard majority chart applied at both levels without cross-coupling.

### Best ordering

$$
x_{11},\dots,x_{1n},\;x_{21},\dots,x_{2n},\;\dots,\;x_{m1},\dots,x_{mn}.
$$

### BDD size

$$
\boxed{m(n+1)+2}
$$

including the two sink nodes $\bot$ and $\top$.
