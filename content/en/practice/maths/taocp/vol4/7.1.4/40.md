**Exercise 40.** &#9654; [*22*] [22] Let g be the “condensation” of f obtained by setting xk+1 ←xk as in (27).

## Solution

Let $f(x_1,\dots,x_n)$ have BDD size $B(f)$, and let $g$ be obtained from $f$ by setting $x_{k+1} \leftarrow x_k$.

For every subfunction of $g$, fix a partial assignment $(x_1,\dots,x_k,x_{k+2},\dots,x_n)$. The induced function in the remaining variables equals the subfunction of $f$ obtained under the same assignment together with the constraint $x_{k+1}=x_k$. Thus every subtable of $g$ arises by taking a subtable $\sigma$ of $f$ and replacing each occurrence of $x_{k+1}$ by $x_k$, producing a binary string $\sigma^{*}$ of length $2^{n-1}$.

Let $\tau$ be a bead of $g$. Let $\sigma$ be the corresponding subtable of $f$ that yields $\tau$ under the substitution $x_{k+1}\leftarrow x_k$. If $\sigma$ were not a bead, then $\sigma=\alpha\alpha$ for some string $\alpha$. The substitution $x_{k+1}\leftarrow x_k$ preserves equality of the two halves, since both halves of $\sigma$ are affected identically. Hence $\tau$ would also satisfy $\tau=\beta\beta$ for the corresponding reduced string $\beta$, contradicting that $\tau$ is a bead. Therefore $\sigma$ is a bead of $f$.

The mapping that sends each bead $\tau$ of $g$ to the corresponding bead $\sigma$ of $f$ is injective, since $\tau$ uniquely determines $\sigma$ by reintroducing the duplicated variable $x_{k+1}$ in the positions of $x_k$. Hence the number of beads of $g$ does not exceed the number of beads of $f$, giving

$$
B(g)\le B(f).
$$

This completes the proof. ∎

Now let $h$ be obtained from $f$ by setting $x_{k+2}\leftarrow x_k$.

The inequality $B(h)\le B(f)$ does not hold in general. The substitution identifies variables that are separated in the ordering of the BDD, and this can increase the number of distinct subtables that arise at intermediate levels of the ordered decomposition.

To see this, consider that in a reduced ordered BDD, node sharing depends on equality of subtables with respect to the fixed variable order $x_1,\dots,x_n$. When $x_{k+2}$ is replaced by $x_k$, the dependency structure of the function can be altered so that previously identical subtables split according to different combinations of the shared variable $x_k$. This can create additional distinct beads at intermediate levels of the decomposition, increasing the number of nodes required in the reduced dag.

Thus no monotone bound of the form $B(h)\le B(f)$ holds for this operation in general. ∎
