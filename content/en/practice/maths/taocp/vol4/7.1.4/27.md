**Exercise 27.** &#9654; [*M26*] [M26] Suppose F(p1, . . . , pn) and G(p1, . . . , pn) are the reliability polynomials
for Boolean functions f(x1, . . . , xn) and g(x1, . . . , xn), where f ̸= g. Let q be a prime
number, and choose independent random integers q1, . . . , qn, uniformly distributed
in the range 0 ≤qk < q. Prove that F(q1, . . . , qn) mod q ̸= G(q1, . . . , qn) mod q with
probability ≥(1−1/q)n. (In particular, if n = 1000 and q = 231 −1, different functions
lead to different “hash values” under this scheme with probability at least 0.9999995.)

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m26s  

---

## Setup

Let

$$
F(p_1,\dots,p_n)=\sum_{x_1=0}^1 \cdots \sum_{x_n=0}^1 \Bigl(\prod_{k=1}^n (1-p_k)^{1-x_k}p_k^{x_k}\Bigr) f(x_1,\dots,x_n)
$$

and similarly define $G(p_1,\dots,p_n)$ from $g(x_1,\dots,x_n)$.

Let

$$
H(p_1,\dots,p_n)=F(p_1,\dots,p_n)-G(p_1,\dots,p_n).
$$

Since $f\ne g$, there exists $x\in{0,1}^n$ such that $f(x)\ne g(x)$, hence $H$ is not the zero polynomial over the integers and therefore not the zero polynomial over $\mathbb{Z}_q$.

Each variable $p_k$ appears only in factors $(1-p_k)$ and $p_k$, so $H$ is a multilinear polynomial over $\mathbb{Z}_q$.

We choose $q_1,\dots,q_n$ independently and uniformly in ${0,\dots,q-1}$ and evaluate $H(q_1,\dots,q_n)\bmod q$.

It must be proved that

$$
\Pr\bigl[H(q_1,\dots,q_n)\ne 0 \bmod q\bigr]\ge (1-\tfrac{1}{q})^n.
$$

## Solution

The proof proceeds by induction on the number of variables $n$.

Let $P(n)$ be the statement: every nonzero multilinear polynomial $H(p_1,\dots,p_n)$ over $\mathbb{Z}_q$ satisfies

$$
\Pr[H(q_1,\dots,q_n)\ne 0]\ge (1-\tfrac{1}{q})^n.
$$

### Base case $n=1$

A nonzero multilinear polynomial in one variable has the form

$$
H(p_1)=ap_1+b,\quad (a,b)\not=(0,0).
$$

If $a\ne 0$, there exists exactly one value $p_1=-b a^{-1}\pmod q$ where $H(p_1)=0$. If $a=0$, then $b\ne 0$ and $H$ is never zero.

In either case,

$$
\Pr[H(q_1)\ne 0]\ge 1-\frac{1}{q}=(1-\tfrac{1}{q})^1.
$$

### Inductive step

Assume $P(n-1)$ holds for all $n-1\ge 1$. Let $H(p_1,\dots,p_n)$ be a nonzero multilinear polynomial.

Write $H$ as a polynomial in $p_n$:

$$
H(p_1,\dots,p_n)=A(p_1,\dots,p_{n-1})+p_n B(p_1,\dots,p_{n-1}),
$$

where $A$ and $B$ are multilinear in $n-1$ variables and not both identically zero.

Fix a random choice of $q_1,\dots,q_{n-1}$. Define

$$
A' = A(q_1,\dots,q_{n-1}),\quad B' = B(q_1,\dots,q_{n-1}).
$$

Three cases occur.

If $B'\ne 0$, then as a function of $p_n$,

$$
H = A' + p_n B'
$$

has exactly one root in $\mathbb{Z}_q$, so

$$
\Pr[H(q_1,\dots,q_n)=0 \mid B'\ne 0]=\frac{1}{q},
$$

and hence

$$
\Pr[H(q_1,\dots,q_n)\ne 0 \mid B'\ne 0]=1-\frac{1}{q}.
$$

If $B'=0$ and $A'\ne 0$, then $H$ is constant nonzero in $p_n$, so

$$
\Pr[H(q_1,\dots,q_n)\ne 0 \mid B'=0, A'\ne 0]=1.
$$

If $A'=B'=0$, then $H$ evaluates to the zero polynomial at this restriction, contributing no success probability.

Now split according to whether $B$ is identically zero.

#### Case 1: $B$ is not identically zero

Then $B$ is a nonzero multilinear polynomial in $n-1$ variables, so by the induction hypothesis,

$$
\Pr[B'\ne 0]\ge (1-\tfrac{1}{q})^{n-1}.
$$

Therefore,

$$
\Pr[H\ne 0]\ge \Pr[B'\ne 0]\cdot (1-\tfrac{1}{q})
\ge (1-\tfrac{1}{q})^{n-1}(1-\tfrac{1}{q})
= (1-\tfrac{1}{q})^n.
$$

#### Case 2: $B$ is identically zero

Then $H=A$ and $A$ is a nonzero multilinear polynomial in $n-1$ variables. By the induction hypothesis,

$$
\Pr[H\ne 0]=\Pr[A'\ne 0]\ge (1-\tfrac{1}{q})^{n-1}.
$$

Since $0<1-\tfrac{1}{q}\le 1$,

$$
(1-\tfrac{1}{q})^{n-1}\ge (1-\tfrac{1}{q})^n.
$$

Thus in all cases,

$$
\Pr[H(q_1,\dots,q_n)\ne 0]\ge (1-\tfrac{1}{q})^n.
$$

This completes the induction.

Since $H=F-G$, the event $F(q_1,\dots,q_n)\equiv G(q_1,\dots,q_n)\pmod q$ is exactly $H(q_1,\dots,q_n)=0$, so the complementary probability bound yields the claim.

This completes the proof. ∎

## Verification

The decomposition $H=A+p_nB$ uses multilinearity, since every monomial contains $p_n$ to exponent $0$ or $1$ only, so coefficients separate uniquely into terms independent of $p_n$ and terms containing $p_n$.

For fixed $q_1,\dots,q_{n-1}$ with $B'\ne 0$, the equation $A'+p_nB'=0$ has a unique solution in $\mathbb{Z}_q$ because $q$ is prime and $B'$ is invertible, giving exactly one failure among $q$ equally likely values.

The induction preserves nonzeroness: if $H$ is nonzero, then either $B$ is nonzero or $A$ is nonzero when $B$ is zero, ensuring the inductive hypothesis applies in each branch.

The inequality $(1-\frac{1}{q})^{n-1}\ge (1-\frac{1}{q})^n$ follows from $0<1-\frac{1}{q}\le 1$.

## Notes

The bound is stronger than the classical Schwartz–Zippel estimate $\Pr[H=0]\le n/q$ for multilinear polynomials, since

$$
(1-\tfrac{1}{q})^n = 1-\frac{n}{q}+O(q^{-2})
$$

for large $q$, matching the leading-order behavior while retaining a multiplicative structure induced by sequential variable exposure.
