The flaw in the proposed solution is that it treats every edge as if it advances exactly one variable. In a reduced ordered BDD, a child of a node labeled $x_j$ may instead be labeled $x_k$ with $k>j+1$. The variables $x_{j+1},\dots,x_{k-1}$ are then _skipped_, meaning they are free and must be summed over explicitly. Exercise 31 is precisely designed to handle this situation via an abstract “combination over skipped variables.” We now repair the construction accordingly and restate the solution in the required general form.

## Correct formulation of the state

For each BDD node $u$, let $\operatorname{top}(u)=x_j$ be its variable index. We associate three quantities:

$$
N(u)=\sum_{f_u(x)=1} 1,
\quad
S(u)=\sum_{f_u(x)=1}\sum_{k=j}^n w_k x_k,
\quad
Q(u)=\sum_{f_u(x)=1}\left(\sum_{k=j}^n w_k x_k\right)^2,
$$

where $f_u$ is the subfunction represented by $u$, and assignments range over all variables in its scope.

The sinks are:

$$
N(\bot)=S(\bot)=Q(\bot)=0,
\qquad
N(\top)=1,\; S(\top)=0,\; Q(\top)=0.
$$

The only missing ingredient is how to combine a node with a child whose top variable is not the next one in sequence.

## Contribution of skipped variables

Assume a node $u$ is labeled $x_j$, and one of its children $v$ is labeled $x_k$ with $k>j+1$. Let the skipped block be

$$
W = \{x_j, x_{j+1}, \dots, x_{k-1}\}, \quad m = k-j.
$$

We must account for all assignments to these free variables.

Define:

$$
W_W = \sum_{i=j}^{k-1} w_i.
$$

For the skipped block alone:

- Number of assignments:

$$
A(W)=2^m.
$$

- First moment over all assignments:

$$
S(W)=\sum_{a \in \{0,1\}^m} \sum_{i=j}^{k-1} w_i a_i = 2^{m-1} W_W.
$$

- Second moment:

$$
Q(W)=\sum_{a} \left(\sum_{i=j}^{k-1} w_i a_i\right)^2
=2^{m-2}\left(W_W^2+\sum_{i=j}^{k-1} w_i^2\right).
$$

## Skip-extension operator

Let $(N,S,Q)$ be the values at child $v$, which already accounts for variables $x_k,\dots,x_n$. We extend these values back over the skipped block $x_j,\dots,x_{k-1}$.

The extended quantities are:

### Number of solutions

$$
N' = A(W)\,N = 2^m N.
$$

### First moment

Split the weight sum into skipped and child parts:

$$
S' = \sum (S_W + S_v).
$$

Thus:

$$
S' = S(W)\,N + A(W)\,S
= 2^{m-1} W_W\,N + 2^m S.
$$

### Second moment

$$
(S_W + S_v)^2 = S_W^2 + 2S_W S_v + S_v^2.
$$

Summing over all assignments:

$$
Q' = Q(W)\,N + 2S(W)\,S + A(W)\,Q.
$$

So:

$$
Q' =
2^{m-2}\left(W_W^2+\sum_{i=j}^{k-1} w_i^2\right)N
+ 2\cdot 2^{m-1} W_W S
+ 2^m Q.
$$

## Branch combination rule

Now let a node $u$ labeled $x_j$ have low and high successors $u_0,u_1$, whose top variables may be $x_k$ and $x_\ell$ respectively.

We first apply the skip-extension from $j$ to the child variable:

$$
(N_0,S_0,Q_0) = \operatorname{Skip}(j,k; N(u_0),S(u_0),Q(u_0)),
$$

$$
(N_1,S_1,Q_1) = \operatorname{Skip}(j,\ell; N(u_1),S(u_1),Q(u_1)).
$$

Then the node recurrences become:

$$
N(u)=N_0+N_1,
$$

$$
S(u)=S_0+S_1 + w_j N_1,
$$

$$
Q(u)=Q_0+Q_1 + 2w_j S_1 + w_j^2 N_1.
$$

These formulas are now correct for arbitrary reduced ordered BDDs because all skipped variables have already been accounted for by the Skip operator.

## Relation to Exercise 31

This is exactly a specialization of Exercise 31: the abstract combination operator on edges is instantiated as the Skip-extension of a triple $(N,S,Q)$, followed by the standard branching combination at $x_j$. All operations remain constant-time per edge using precomputed prefix sums of the weights.

## Conclusion

The corrected algorithm processes each BDD node once. Each edge contributes one Skip transformation plus one constant-time merge, so the total running time is linear in the size of the BDD.

At the root node $r$, the required quantities are:

$$
S(r)=\sum_{f(x)=1}(w_1x_1+\cdots+w_nx_n),
\qquad
Q(r)=\sum_{f(x)=1}(w_1x_1+\cdots+w_nx_n)^2.
$$

These are obtained directly from the corrected propagation scheme.
