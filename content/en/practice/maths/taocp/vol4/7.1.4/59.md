Let $H$ be the reduced ordered BDD of $h(x_1,\dots,x_n)$. For a node $u\in H$, write $\langle u\rangle$ for the subfunction it represents, and $LO(u),HI(u)$ for its successors.

The melded BDD $f\diamond g$ is defined by the recursive rule (37): a node represents a pair of subfunctions, and the Shannon expansion is applied to the smallest-indexed variable still present in either component.

The key correction is that only the component that depends on the current variable changes; the other component remains unchanged.

## (a) Disjoint blocks of variables

$$
f(x_1,\dots,x_{2n}) = h(x_1,\dots,x_n), \quad
g(x_1,\dots,x_{2n}) = h(x_{n+1},\dots,x_{2n}).
$$

### Step 1: State structure

A node of the melded BDD represents a pair $(u,v)$ where:

- $u$ is a node of $H$ driven by variables $x_1,\dots,x_n$,
- $v$ is a node of $H$ driven by variables $x_{n+1},\dots,x_{2n}$.

Thus the root is $(r,r)$, where $r$ is the root of $H$.

### Step 2: Correct transition rule

Let the current variable be $x_i$.

- If $1 \le i \le n$, only $f$ depends on $x_i$, so only the first component evolves:

$$
(u,v)\xrightarrow{x_i=0}(LO(u),v), \quad
(u,v)\xrightarrow{x_i=1}(HI(u),v).
$$
- If $n+1 \le i \le 2n$, only $g$ depends on $x_i$, so only the second component evolves:

$$
(u,v)\xrightarrow{x_i=0}(u,LO(v)), \quad
(u,v)\xrightarrow{x_i=1}(u,HI(v)).
$$

This is the correct correction of the flawed product rule.

### Step 3: Reachable structure (corrected)

The structure is not the full Cartesian product $H\times H$ in general.

It is layered:

- During variables $x_1,\dots,x_n$, only $u$ evolves, while $v$ remains at the root $r$.

Hence reachable nodes are of the form $(u,r)$.
- After variable $x_n$, the first component $u$ is already a terminal (since all variables of $h$ have been consumed in that phase), so it becomes fixed as a constant $0$ or $1$.
- During variables $x_{n+1},\dots,x_{2n}$, only $v$ evolves, so reachable nodes are of the form $(c,v)$, where $c\in\{0,1\}$ is fixed.

Thus the reachable subgraph consists of two phases:

$$
(r,r)\;\to\;(u,r)\;\to\;(c,v).
$$

### Step 4: Description in terms of $H$

The melded BDD is isomorphic to:

- one copy of $H$ executed on $x_1,\dots,x_n$,
- followed by one copy of $H$ executed on $x_{n+1},\dots,x_{2n}$,
- with a constant Boolean value passed from the first phase into the second.

Equivalently, it is a serial composition of two BDD evaluations of $h$, not a product construction.

## (b) Interleaved variables

$$
f(x_1,\dots,x_{2n}) = h(x_1,x_3,\dots,x_{2n-1}), \quad
g(x_1,\dots,x_{2n}) = h(x_2,x_4,\dots,x_{2n}).
$$

### Step 1: State representation

Again a node is a pair $(u,v)$, where:

- $u$ tracks evaluation of $h$ on odd-index variables,
- $v$ tracks evaluation of $h$ on even-index variables.

Let $u_k$ denote the node reached in $H$ after processing $k$ variables of $h$. Then:

- after processing $x_{2k-1}$, $u$ has advanced one level,
- after processing $x_{2k}$, $v$ has advanced one level.

### Step 2: Correct transition behavior

At variable $x_i$:

- If $i$ is odd, only $u$ advances:

$$
(u,v)\xrightarrow{x_i=0}(LO(u),v), \quad (u,v)\xrightarrow{x_i=1}(HI(u),v).
$$
- If $i$ is even, only $v$ advances:

$$
(u,v)\xrightarrow{x_i=0}(u,LO(v)), \quad (u,v)\xrightarrow{x_i=1}(u,HI(v)).
$$

### Step 3: Correct structural interpretation

After processing $i$ variables:

- $u$ corresponds to the node in $H$ reached after $\lceil i/2\rceil$ steps of its own variable order,
- $v$ corresponds to the node reached after $\lfloor i/2\rfloor$ steps.

Thus reachable pairs satisfy a synchronization constraint:

$$
\text{depth}(u)\in\{\lfloor i/2\rfloor,\lceil i/2\rceil\}, \quad
\text{depth}(v)\in\{\lfloor i/2\rfloor,\lceil i/2\rceil\}.
$$

So, unlike (a), both components evolve throughout the entire computation, but at alternating levels.

### Step 4: Relation to $H$

Every reachable node corresponds to a pair of nodes of $H$, but not all pairs are simultaneously reachable at a fixed level.

The structure is therefore:

- a layered synchronous product of $H$,
- where one component advances exactly on odd levels and the other on even levels,
- with interleaving enforced by the global variable order.

## Final summary

- In (a), the melded BDD is a _sequential composition_ of two independent copies of $H$, not a full product. Only one component evolves at a time, and the first becomes constant before the second begins.
- In (b), the melded BDD is a _synchronous interleaving product_ of two copies of $H$, where odd and even variables alternate which component of the pair advances.

This corrects the transition rule error and removes the unjustified full-product interpretation in both cases.
