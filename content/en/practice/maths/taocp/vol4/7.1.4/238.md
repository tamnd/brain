## Solution

Let $G=(V,E)$ denote the contiguous-USA graph of (18). Let $f$ be the family of all subsets $U \subseteq V$ such that the induced subgraph $G|U$ is bipartite.

A subset $U$ is bipartite if and only if $G|U$ contains no cycle of odd length. Thus $f$ can be characterized as the family of all $U$ satisfying a global parity constraint over every cycle of $G|U$.

To represent $f$ by a ZDD, introduce for each vertex $v \in V$ a Boolean variable $x_v$ indicating whether $v \in U$. For each odd cycle $C \subseteq G$, impose the constraint that $C$ does not appear entirely inside $U$ with an odd cycle structure. Equivalently, enforce 2-colorability constraints: there exists a labeling $\chi : U \to {0,1}$ such that for every edge ${u,v} \in E$ with $u,v \in U$, we have $\chi(u) \ne \chi(v)$.

This constraint is representable in ZDD form by constructing a family $f$ as the solution set of a binary constraint system over edges, where each edge enforces inequality of endpoint colors, and color consistency is enforced implicitly by traversal of the decision diagram.

Let $g \subseteq 2^V$ denote the family of all subsets $U$ that admit a valid 2-coloring extension. Then $g$ is obtained by dynamic programming over the graph structure using the standard ZDD product construction over edges:

for each edge ${u,v}$, refine the family by eliminating assignments where $x_u=x_v=1$ violates bipartiteness under a shared component coloring constraint. The resulting ZDD represents exactly $f$.

The maximal induced bipartite subgraphs correspond to the maximal elements of $f$, namely

$$
f^{\uparrow} = \{U \in f \mid \nexists W \in f \text{ such that } U \subset W\}.
$$

To compute $f^{\uparrow}$ in ZDD form, construct $f$ and then apply the maximal-element operator from exercise 236:

$$
f^{\uparrow} = \{ \alpha \in f \mid \beta \in f \ \text{and}\ \alpha \subseteq \beta \Rightarrow \alpha = \beta \}.
$$

This is implemented by computing subset intersections via ZDD product and subtracting non-maximal elements:

$$
f^{\uparrow} = f \setminus (f \sqcap f_{\supset})
$$

where $f_{\supset}$ denotes the family of strict supersets generated by the standard ZDD superset operator.

The number of such sets is

$$
|f^{\uparrow}| = \text{card}(f^{\uparrow}),
$$

computed directly from the reduced ZDD representing $f^{\uparrow}$ by counting sink-reachable paths corresponding to $\top$.

The smallest maximal induced bipartite subgraphs are exactly the inclusion-minimal vertex sets that are maximal under bipartiteness; these correspond to maximal bipartite induced subgraphs where removing any vertex introduces an odd cycle obstruction or disconnects a required bipartite extension constraint. The largest such sets are the maximal elements of $f$ with maximum cardinality; these correspond to maximal bipartite induced subgraphs of maximum size, equivalently complements of minimum vertex deletions that destroy all odd-cycle obstructions.

Since the structure of $G$ in (18) is fixed but not specified here, the explicit cardinality of $f^{\uparrow}$ and concrete representatives of smallest and largest maximal sets are determined by the evaluated ZDD for that specific graph and cannot be derived without the edge list of $G$.

The same construction extends to maximal induced tripartite subgraphs by introducing three-valued color variables and enforcing inequality constraints on every edge. The resulting family $f_3$ is obtained by a ternary extension of the ZDD construction, and maximal tripartite induced subgraphs are given by $f_3^{\uparrow}$. Their enumeration proceeds identically via ZDD reduction and maximal-element extraction.
