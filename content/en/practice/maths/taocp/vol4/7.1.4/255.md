**Exercise 255.** &#9654; [*25*] [25] A multifamily of sets, in which members of f are allowed to occur more
than once, can be represented as a sequence of ZDDs (f0, f1, f2, . . . ) in which fk is the
family of sets that occur ( . . . a2a1a0)2 times in f where ak = 1. For example, if α
appears exactly 9 = (1001)2 times in the multifamily, α would be in f3 and f0.
a) Explain how to insert and delete items from this representation of a multifamily.
b) Implement the multiset union h = f ⊎g for multifamilies.

## Solution

A multifamily $f$ assigns to each set $\alpha$ a multiplicity $m_f(\alpha) \in \mathbb{N}$. The representation $(f_0,f_1,f_2,\dots)$ encodes this multiplicity in binary form by

$$
m_f(\alpha)=\sum_{k\ge 0} a_k(\alpha)2^k,\qquad a_k(\alpha)\in\{0,1\},
$$

with the defining condition

$$
\alpha \in f_k \;\Longleftrightarrow\; a_k(\alpha)=1.
$$

Each $f_k$ is a ZDD representing a family of sets.

### (a) Insertion and deletion

Insertion of one occurrence of a set $\alpha$ replaces $m_f(\alpha)$ by $m_f(\alpha)+1$. In binary form this is standard increment with carries applied to the digit sequence $(a_0(\alpha),a_1(\alpha),\dots)$, hence it can be implemented by updating the ZDD layers $(f_k)$ locally at $\alpha$.

At level $k=0$, membership of $\alpha$ in $f_0$ is toggled. If $\alpha \notin f_0$, then after insertion it enters $f_0$ and no carry occurs. If $\alpha \in f_0$, then it is removed from $f_0$ and a carry propagates to level $k=1$.

At a general level $k \ge 0$, assuming a carry reaches $k$, the same rule applies. If $\alpha \notin f_k$, then $\alpha$ is inserted into $f_k$ and the carry stops. If $\alpha \in f_k$, then $\alpha$ is removed from $f_k$ and the carry continues to level $k+1$.

This procedure implements binary addition of $1$ to $m_f(\alpha)$, since the carry propagation exactly matches the rule $1+1=0$ with carry $1$ and $0+1=1$ with no carry. Each level updates only membership of $\alpha$, so the resulting families still satisfy the defining binary encoding condition.

Deletion of one occurrence of $\alpha$ replaces $m_f(\alpha)$ by $m_f(\alpha)-1$, which is binary subtraction with borrow propagation.

At level $k=0$, if $\alpha \in f_0$, then it is removed and the process stops, since $1-1=0$ produces no borrow. If $\alpha \notin f_0$, then $\alpha$ is inserted into $f_0$ and a borrow is propagated to level $k=1$, since $0-1$ forces compensation from higher bits.

At a general level $k \ge 0$, when a borrow arrives, if $\alpha \in f_k$ then it is removed and the borrow terminates. If $\alpha \notin f_k$, then $\alpha$ is inserted into $f_k$ and the borrow continues to level $k+1$.

This matches binary subtraction digitwise, since the propagated borrow enforces the identity $0-1=1$ with borrow $1$ and $1-1=0$.

### (b) Multiset union

For multifamilies $f$ and $g$, the union $h=f \uplus g$ satisfies

$$
m_h(\alpha)=m_f(\alpha)+m_g(\alpha).
$$

Write

$$
m_f(\alpha)=\sum_{k\ge 0} f_k(\alpha)2^k,\qquad m_g(\alpha)=\sum_{k\ge 0} g_k(\alpha)2^k,
$$

where $f_k(\alpha),g_k(\alpha)\in{0,1}$. The addition is performed bitwise with carry.

Define a carry family $c_k$ with $c_0=\emptyset$, interpreted as $c_0(\alpha)=0$ for all $\alpha$. For each level $k$, the sum bit is

$$
h_k(\alpha)= f_k(\alpha)\oplus g_k(\alpha)\oplus c_k(\alpha),
$$

and the next carry is

$$
c_{k+1}(\alpha)=\big(f_k(\alpha)+g_k(\alpha)+c_k(\alpha)\ge 2\big),
$$

meaning that at least two of the three bits are $1$.

Expressed in ZDD operations, letting symmetric difference be $\triangle$, union be $\cup$, and intersection be $\cap$, this becomes

$$
s_k = f_k \triangle g_k \triangle c_k,
$$

and the carry family is the set of $\alpha$ for which at least two of $f_k,g_k,c_k$ contain $\alpha$, that is

$$
c_{k+1} = (f_k \cap g_k)\;\cup\;(f_k \cap c_k)\;\cup\;(g_k \cap c_k).
$$

The construction proceeds inductively on $k$. At each level $k$, $h_k$ is determined solely by the three input families at that level, while $c_{k+1}$ records precisely the positions where binary addition generates a carry to the next digit. Since each $\alpha$ evolves independently under this digitwise addition, the resulting sequence $(h_0,h_1,\dots)$ satisfies

$$
\sum_{k\ge 0} h_k(\alpha)2^k = m_f(\alpha)+m_g(\alpha),
$$

so it is the required representation of $f \uplus g$.

This completes the solution. ∎
