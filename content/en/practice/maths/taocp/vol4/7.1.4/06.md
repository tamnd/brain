**Exercise 6.** [*10*] [10] Let g(x1, x2, x3, x4) = f(x4, x3, x2, x1), where f has the BDD in (6). What

The truth table of $g$ is already correct. We begin from it and rebuild the bead structure without excluding square nodes.

## 1. Truth table of $g$

Given

$$
f = 1100100100001111,
\qquad
g(x_1,x_2,x_3,x_4)=f(x_4,x_3,x_2,x_1),
$$

we reverse the binary index of each entry of $f$. This yields

$$
g = 1011000110010011.
$$

## 2. Meaning of beads in this decomposition

A bead is any distinct subfunction appearing in the full recursive decomposition of the BDD, including square nodes (where LO = HI). Square nodes are not excluded; they represent constant or repeated subfunctions and remain valid beads.

Thus, every distinct subtable produced by recursive halving is included.

## 3. Recursive decomposition of $g$

### Order 4 (root)

$$
g = 10110001 \;\; 10010011.
$$

This gives the order-4 bead:

$$
1011000110010011.
$$

### Order 3

Left and right halves:

$$
10110001,\quad 10010011.
$$

Both are distinct beads.

### Order 2

Split each 8-bit table:

$$
10110001 = 1011 \;\; 0001,
\quad
10010011 = 1001 \;\; 0011.
$$

Thus the order-2 beads are:

$$
1011,\; 0001,\; 1001,\; 0011.
$$

### Order 1

Split each 4-bit table:

$$
10,\; 11,\; 00,\; 01.
$$

Here:

- $11$ and $00$ are square nodes (constant subfunctions),
- but they are still valid beads and must be included.

## 4. Complete bead set

Collecting all distinct subfunctions from all levels:

$$
\{
1011000110010011,\;
10110001,\;
10010011,\;
1011,\;
0001,\;
1001,\;
0011,\;
10,\;
11,\;
00,\;
01
\}.
$$

## 5. Correction of the earlier error

The previous solution incorrectly excluded square nodes at order 1. In the BDD/bead construction, square nodes are not discarded; they represent valid subfunctions and must appear in the bead set. The correct bead set is therefore the full set of distinct subfunctions produced by the recursive decomposition, including $00$ and $11$.
