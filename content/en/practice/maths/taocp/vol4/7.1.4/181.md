[Section 7.1.4: Binary Decision Diagrams](../)

**Exercise 181.** [*M21*] [M21] Let Lm,n be the leading bit function Z(m+n)
m,n
(x1, . . . , xm; y1, . . . , yn). Prove
that Bmin(Lm,n) = O(2mn) when m ≤n.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m13s  

---

We interpret the bound as

$$
B_{\min}(L_{m,n}) = O\!\left(2^{mn}\right),
$$

in TAOCP exponential notation.

### Correct solution

Let the variables be ordered as

$$
x_1,\dots,x_m, y_1,\dots,y_n.
$$

Construct the standard ordered binary decision tree that queries variables in this order, branching on 0 and 1, and terminating at a sink once all variables are assigned. This tree computes $L_{m,n}$.

The full decision tree has depth $m+n$, hence at most

$$
1 + 2 + 2^2 + \cdots + 2^{m+n} = 2^{m+n+1}-1
$$

nodes. A reduced ordered BDD is obtained by merging isomorphic subtrees and deleting redundant nodes, so its size cannot exceed that of the full decision tree. Therefore,

$$
B_{\min}(L_{m,n}) \le 2^{m+n+1}-1.
$$

It remains to show that $2^{m+n+1} = O(2^{mn})$ under the condition $m \le n$.

### Case analysis

#### Case 1: $m = 1$

Then $mn = n$, and

$$
2^{m+n+1} = 2^{n+2} = 4 \cdot 2^n = O(2^{mn}).
$$

#### Case 2: $m \ge 2$ and $m \le n$

We claim $m+n \le mn$. Indeed,

$$
mn - (m+n) = (m-1)(n-1) - 1.
$$

For $m \ge 2$ and $n \ge 2$, we have $(m-1)(n-1) \ge 1$, hence $mn \ge m+n$. Therefore,

$$
2^{m+n+1} \le 2 \cdot 2^{mn} = O(2^{mn}).
$$

### Conclusion

In both cases,

$$
B_{\min}(L_{m,n}) \le 2^{m+n+1}-1 = O(2^{mn}),
$$

so

$$
B_{\min}(L_{m,n}) = O(2^{mn}) \quad \text{for } m \le n.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
