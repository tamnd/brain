Let $f$ and $g$ be represented by reduced ordered BDDs over variable order $x_1<\cdots<x_n$. Write $B(f)$, $B(g)$ for their node sets, and for a node $u$ write $\operatorname{var}(u)\in\{x_1,\dots,x_n,\bot\}$, where $\bot$ denotes a terminal.

For each assignment $\alpha\in\{0,1\}^{j-1}$, let $f_\alpha$ be the residual function obtained by fixing $x_1,\dots,x_{j-1}$. In a reduced OBDD, each residual function is represented by a unique node; denote it by $u_f(\alpha)\in B(f)$. Define $u_g(\alpha)$ similarly.

The meld $h=f\diamond g$ is the reduced OBDD obtained from the standard Shannon-based product construction on pairs of nodes, where a node represents a pair of residual functions $(f_\alpha,g_\alpha)$, and reduction identifies isomorphic subgraphs.

## 1. Structural representation of meld nodes

### Lemma 1 (node representation)

Every node $w$ of the meld $h=f\diamond g$ corresponds uniquely to a pair

$$
(u,v)\in B(f)\times B(g)
$$

such that $u$ represents some residual function $f_\alpha$ and $v$ represents the corresponding residual function $g_\alpha$ for the same prefix assignment $\alpha$.

Moreover, distinct nodes of $h$ correspond to distinct pairs $(u,v)$.

#### Proof

The recursive construction of $f\diamond g$ is performed on pairs of nodes: from a pair $(u,v)$, one forms a node labeled by the smallest variable in $\operatorname{var}(u)\cup\operatorname{var}(v)$, with low/high children defined by recursively applying the construction to the corresponding cofactors of $u$ and $v$.

Thus every generated node is determined by a pair $(u,v)$, and every pair of reachable nodes generates at most one node in the construction graph.

Reduction merges nodes only when both components agree, i.e. when both children pairs coincide; therefore no two distinct pairs $(u,v)\neq(u',v')$ can represent the same reduced node, since they induce different residual function pairs $(f_u,g_v)\neq(f_{u'},g_{v'})$. Hence the mapping is injective.

∎

## 2. Level of a meld node

Let $\ell(u)$ denote the level of a node $u$ (terminal nodes have level $n+1$).

### Lemma 2 (level characterization)

For a meld node corresponding to $(u,v)$,

$$
\operatorname{var}(w)=\min(\operatorname{var}(u),\operatorname{var}(v))
$$

in the ordered sense, equivalently

$$
\ell(w)=\min(\ell(u),\ell(v)).
$$

#### Proof

In the Shannon construction of the product BDD, the root variable of $(u,v)$ is the smallest variable on which at least one of $u$ or $v$ branches. If both have already tested variables $x_1,\dots,x_{j-1}$, then their next test variable determines the next decision in the meld. Therefore the first level at which any branching occurs is exactly the minimum of the two levels. Reduction does not change this, since it only merges identical subgraphs and never changes variable labels.

∎

## 3. Classification of level-$j$ nodes

Let

- $b_j$, $b'_j$: number of nodes in $f$, $g$ labeled $x_j$,
- $q_j$, $q'_j$: number of distinct residual functions of $f$, $g$ after fixing $x_1,\dots,x_{j-1}$,
- $V_j\subseteq \mathcal{F}_j$, $V'_j\subseteq \mathcal{G}_j$: sets of residual functions whose representing nodes are labeled $x_j$.

By definition,

$$
|V_j|=b_j,\quad |V'_j|=b'_j,\quad |\mathcal{F}_j|=q_j,\quad |\mathcal{G}_j|=q'_j.
$$

A meld node at level $j$ corresponds to a pair $(u,v)$ with

$$
\min(\ell(u),\ell(v))=j,
$$

hence

$$
\ell(u)=j \ \text{or}\ \ell(v)=j.
$$

Thus the set of level-$j$ meld nodes injects into

$$
U_j=(\mathcal{F}_j\times V'_j)\ \cup\ (V_j\times \mathcal{G}_j).
$$

This is now fully justified: every meld node corresponds to a unique pair $(u,v)$, and Lemma 2 ensures that being at level $j$ is equivalent to at least one component being at level $j$.

## 4. Counting

By inclusion–exclusion,

$$
|U_j|
=|\mathcal{F}_j\times V'_j|+|V_j\times \mathcal{G}_j|-|V_j\times V'_j|.
$$

Compute each term:

$$
|\mathcal{F}_j\times V'_j|=q_j b'_j,\quad
|V_j\times \mathcal{G}_j|=b_j q'_j,\quad
|V_j\times V'_j|=b_j b'_j.
$$

Hence

$$
|U_j|=q_j b'_j+b_j q'_j-b_j b'_j.
$$

Since level-$j$ nodes of $h$ inject into $U_j$,

$$
B_j(f\diamond g)\le q_j b'_j+b_j q'_j-b_j b'_j.
$$

## 5. Final bound

Summing over all levels $j=0,\dots,n$,

$$
B(f\diamond g)=\sum_{j=0}^n B_j(f\diamond g)
\le \sum_{j=0}^n \left(q_j b'_j+b_j q'_j-b_j b'_j\right).
$$

This completes the corrected proof. ∎
