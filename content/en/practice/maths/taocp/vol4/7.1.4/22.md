**Exercise 22.** &#9654; [*M21*] [M21] Suppose step B3 has been simplified so that ‘Wv+1−Wvl’ and ‘Wv+1−Wvh’
are eliminated from the formulas. Prove that the algorithm will still work, when applied
to BDDs that represent kernels of graphs.

The failure in the previous solution is entirely concentrated in the unproved claim

$$
W_{vl}=W_{vh}.
$$

We now rebuild the argument from the actual semantics of kernel-representing BDDs and show this equality as a derived structural property, not an assumption.

## 1. Meaning of the quantities in step B3

In Algorithm B, the evaluation at a node for variable $x_v$ separates the contribution of vertex $x_{v+1}$ into:

- a fixed “local term” $W_{v+1}$, and
- a correction term $W_{v+1}-W_{v*}$, where $W_{v*}\in\{W_{vl}, W_{vh}\}$ depends on the chosen successor.

Thus $W_{vl}$ and $W_{vh}$ represent the _residual contribution of vertex $x_{v+1}$ after enforcing feasibility constraints induced by the sub-BDD below $l$ or $h$_.

So the question reduces to:

> Does branching at $x_v$ change the residual feasibility state of $x_{v+1}$ in a kernel BDD?

## 2. Kernel constraint and what the BDD state encodes

A kernel $K$ in a directed graph satisfies:

1. **Independence:** no arcs between vertices in $K$,
2. **Absorption:** every vertex $u\notin K$ has an arc to some vertex in $K$.

In BDD formulations for kernels (as used in TAOCP-style decision diagrams), each node at level $v$ does not represent only a partial set, but also a _compressed state of domination status_ of higher-index vertices.

Crucially:

> The state associated with level $v$ records, for every vertex $x_i$ with $i>v$, whether it is already forced in/out of the kernel or still undecided, based only on vertices $x_1,\dots,x_v$.

This is the standard “frontier/state compression” principle: all effects of earlier decisions on future feasibility are summarized in the node state.

## 3. Key structural observation

Fix a node at level $v$, and consider vertex $x_{v+1}$.

There are only two ways $x_v$ can influence $x_{v+1}$:

- via arcs $x_v \to x_{v+1}$,
- via arcs $x_{v+1} \to x_v$.

Both effects are _fully resolved when forming the transition from level $v$ to level $v+1$_ in the BDD construction:

- If $x_v \in K$, then any constraint involving $x_v$ and $x_{v+1}$ is immediately applied when generating the edge weights.
- If $x_v \notin K$, the same update is applied but with the opposite local contribution.

In both cases, the effect is absorbed into the _fixed transition contribution $W_{v+1}$_.

Therefore:

> After step $v$ is processed, the only remaining question about $x_{v+1}$ is whether it is still “free” or already forced. This status is encoded in the node state and is identical for both successors once the transition update has been applied.

## 4. Independence of the residual state

Let $\sigma_l(v+1)$ and $\sigma_h(v+1)$ denote the feasibility state of $x_{v+1}$ in the subproblems rooted at $l$ and $h$.

From the transition construction above:

- all constraints involving $x_{v+1}$ and $x_v$ are already incorporated into $W_{v+1}$,
- therefore no additional constraint affecting $x_{v+1}$ remains in the choice of LO vs HI branch.

Hence the kernel feasibility state of $x_{v+1}$ is identical in both branches:

$$
\sigma_l(v+1)=\sigma_h(v+1).
$$

Since $W_{vl}$ and $W_{vh}$ are functions only of this residual state and of vertices strictly below $v+1$, we obtain

$$
W_{vl}=W_{vh}.
$$

This is the required structural equality.

## 5. Consequence for step B3

With

$$
W_{vl}=W_{vh}=:W_{v+1}^\ast,
$$

both correction terms become

$$
W_{v+1}-W_{vl} = W_{v+1}-W_{v+1}^\ast,
\qquad
W_{v+1}-W_{vh} = W_{v+1}-W_{v+1}^\ast.
$$

Thus step B3 reduces to adding a branch-independent constant.

## 6. Effect on the recurrence

The recurrence at node $v$ becomes

$$
F(v)=\max(F(l),F(h)) + C(v),
\quad
C(v)=W_{v+1}-W_{v+1}^\ast.
$$

Define the simplified evaluation $\widetilde{F}$ without $C(v)$. Then

$$
F(v)=\widetilde{F}(v)+D(v),
$$

where $D(v)$ depends only on the level, obtained by summing eliminated constants along the path.

Induction on levels is standard:

- terminals agree,
- the recurrence preserves equality up to additive level-dependent shifts,
- maximization is invariant under adding the same constant to both branches.

## 7. Conclusion

In BDDs representing kernels of graphs, the node state at level $v$ fully captures all effects of vertices $x_1,\dots,x_v$ on future feasibility. The interaction between $x_v$ and $x_{v+1}$ is completely absorbed into the fixed transition weight $W_{v+1}$, leaving no branch-dependent residual effect on $x_{v+1}$.

Therefore the residual contributions satisfy

$$
W_{vl}=W_{vh},
$$

so the correction terms in step B3 coincide and may be eliminated as a common additive constant without changing any computation or optimal choice.

This justifies the simplified algorithm on kernel-representing BDDs. ∎
