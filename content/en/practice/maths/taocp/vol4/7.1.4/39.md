[Section 7.1.4: Binary Decision Diagrams](../)

**Exercise 39.** [*M20*] [M20] What is the size of the BDD for the threshold function [x1 + · · · + xn ≥k]?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m30s  

---

Let

$$
f(x_1,\dots,x_n) = [x_1+\cdots+x_n \ge k]
$$

with variable order $x_1 < x_2 < \cdots < x_n$, and consider the reduced ordered BDD.

## 1. Structure of subfunctions

After reading the first $i$ variables, let $t$ be the number of 1s among $x_1,\dots,x_i$. The remaining subfunction is

$$
f_{i,t}(x_{i+1},\dots,x_n)
=
[t + (x_{i+1}+\cdots+x_n) \ge k].
$$

Equivalently, letting $r = k-t$, this is a threshold function on the suffix:

$$
f_{i,t} = [(x_{i+1}+\cdots+x_n) \ge r]
\quad \text{on } n-i \text{ variables}.
$$

Hence the subfunction is completely determined by the pair $(i,t)$.

## 2. Correctness of state identification

If $(i,t) \ne (i',t')$, then the corresponding subfunctions differ.

Indeed:

- If $i \ne i'$, the functions depend on different numbers of remaining variables, so they cannot be identical as Boolean functions.
- If $i = i'$ but $t \ne t'$, then the required residual thresholds $k-t$ and $k-t'$ differ, so one function accepts assignments that the other rejects (e.g. take a suffix with sum exactly between the two thresholds).

Thus each feasible pair $(i,t)$ corresponds to a distinct BDD node.

## 3. Feasible pairs and node count per level

A pair $(i,t)$ is nonterminal exactly when:

$$
0 \le t \le i,\quad
t < k,\quad
t + (n-i) \ge k.
$$

The last condition ensures the function is not identically $0$, and $t<k$ ensures it is not identically $1$.

Thus for fixed $i$,

$$
\max(0, k-(n-i)) \le t \le \min(i,k-1).
$$

Let

$$
N_i = \max\!\Bigl(0,\ \min(i,k-1) - \max(0,k-(n-i)) + 1\Bigr).
$$

This is exact, but we will only use asymptotics.

## 4. Asymptotics of $N_i$

### Upper bound

For all $i$,

$$
N_i \le k.
$$

Indeed, $t \le k-1$, so there are at most $k$ possible values.

### Lower bound in the central region

Assume

$$
k-1 \le i \le n-k+1.
$$

Then:

- $i \ge k-1$, so $\min(i,k-1) = k-1$,
- $n-i \ge k-1$, so $k-(n-i) \le 1$, hence $\max(0,k-(n-i)) \le 1$.

Therefore,

$$
N_i \ge (k-1) - 1 + 1 = k-1,
$$

and also $N_i \le k$, so

$$
N_i = \Theta(k)
\quad \text{uniformly in the central region.}
$$

The earlier claim that $N_i = k$ exactly is incorrect because boundary cases shift the lower cutoff between $0$ and $1$, producing $k-1$ or $k$ values.

### Boundary regions

If $i < k-1$, then $N_i \le i+1$.

If $i > n-k+1$, then $N_i \le n-i+1$.

Thus the size tapers linearly at both ends.

## 5. Total size

The BDD size is

$$
B(f) = 2 + \sum_{i=0}^{n} N_i.
$$

Split into regions.

### Central region

There are $\Theta(n)$ indices $i$ with

$$
k-1 \le i \le n-k+1
$$

(provided $k \le n/2$; otherwise roles swap symmetrically).

Each contributes $\Theta(k)$, hence total contribution:

$$
\Theta(nk).
$$

### Boundary regions

Each boundary contributes at most a triangular sum:

$$
\sum_{i=0}^{k} O(i) = O(k^2),
\quad
\sum_{i=n-k}^{n} O(n-i) = O(k^2),
$$

which is lower order when $k \le n/2$. If $k > n/2$, replace $k$ by $n-k$.

## 6. Final result

Let $m = \min(k,n-k)$. Then

$$
B(f) = \Theta(nm).
$$

Including the two sinks does not change the asymptotics.

## Final answer

$$
\boxed{B(f) = \Theta\!\bigl(n \cdot \min(k,n-k)\bigr)}.
$$
