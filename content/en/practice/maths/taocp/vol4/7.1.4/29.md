[Section 7.1.4: Binary Decision Diagrams](../)

**Exercise 29.** [*HM20*] [HM20] Modify Algorithm C so that it computes the reliability polynomial F(p)
of exercise 28 and also its derivative F ′(p), given p and the BDD for f.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m04s  

---

## Solution

Let $v$ be a node of the ordered reduced BDD for $f$, with sink nodes $\bot$ and $\top$. For a fixed parameter $p$, let $F(v)$ denote the reliability contribution of the subfunction represented by $v$, and let $F'(v)$ denote its derivative with respect to $p$.

For sink nodes,

$$
F(\bot)=0,\quad F'(\bot)=0,\qquad F(\top)=1,\quad F'(\top)=0.
$$

Let $v$ be a branch node labeled by variable $x_i$, with LO successor $u$ and HI successor $w$. Because the BDD is ordered, every path from $v$ to its successors skips no variables except those whose contributions cancel in the specialization $p_1=\cdots=p_n=p$, so each missing variable contributes a factor $(1-p)+p=1$. Hence the reliability recurrence is

$$
F(v)=(1-p)F(u)+pF(w).
$$

Differentiate this identity. Writing $A=F(u)$ and $B=F(w)$,

$$
F(v)=(1-p)A+pB.
$$

Since $A$ and $B$ depend on $p$,

$$
F'(v)=-(A)+(1-p)A'+B+pB'.
$$

Substituting back $A=F(u)$ and $B=F(w)$ gives

$$
F'(v)=(F(w)-F(u))+(1-p)F'(u)+pF'(w).
$$

To compute both quantities on the DAG, define a memoized evaluation function that assigns to each node $v$ the pair $(F(v),F'(v))$. Each node is evaluated once because the BDD is reduced and every node is reachable from the root.

Let $\mathrm{Eval}(v)$ return this pair.

For sink nodes,

$$
\mathrm{Eval}(\bot)=(0,0),\qquad \mathrm{Eval}(\top)=(1,0).
$$

For a branch node $v$ with LO successor $u$ and HI successor $w$, compute

$$
(F_u,F'_u)=\mathrm{Eval}(u),\qquad (F_w,F'_w)=\mathrm{Eval}(w),
$$

and return

$$
F(v)=(1-p)F_u+pF_w,
$$

$$
F'(v)=(F_w-F_u)+(1-p)F'_u+pF'_w.
$$

The reliability polynomial and its derivative are obtained at the root $r$ as $\mathrm{Eval}(r)=(F(r),F'(r))$, so

$$
F(p)=F(r),\qquad F'(p)=F'(r).
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
