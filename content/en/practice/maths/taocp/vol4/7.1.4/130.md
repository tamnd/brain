We restart the argument in a way that avoids the fundamental mistake: only edges inside a fixed candidate vertex set can ever matter for certifying a clique, and the construction must force distinct residual functions via _induced subproblems on overlapping vertex choices_, not by manipulating irrelevant edges.

We also correctly align everything with a true prefix of a variable ordering, rather than an arbitrary subset of variables.

# Correct solution

## Basic setup

Let the variables be the edge indicators

$$
x_{uv} = [u \sim v \text{ in } G], \quad 1 \le u < v \le m,
$$

ordered arbitrarily.

For a prefix $P$ of this ordering and an assignment $\alpha$ to $P$, let

$$
f_\alpha
$$

be the residual Boolean function on the remaining variables.

A standard property of ordered BDDs is:

> The number of nodes at a level is at least the number of distinct residual functions induced by assignments to the corresponding prefix.

Hence it suffices to construct many pairwise distinct residual functions at some prefix.

# (a) Lower bound for $1 < k \le \sqrt m$

## Step 1: choosing a structured set of vertices

Fix a vertex subset

$$
V_0 \subseteq \{1,\dots,m\}, \quad |V_0| = k + r,
$$

where $r = m + 2 - k^2$ (this quantity is nonnegative for $k \le \sqrt m$).

We will encode many distinct induced clique subproblems on $V_0$.

Let

$$
E(V_0) = \{ x_{uv} : u,v \in V_0, u < v \}.
$$

Split:

- internal edges on a distinguished $k$-subset will act as “signal variables”
- remaining edges inside $V_0$ will serve as “control variables”

## Step 2: selecting the key structure

Fix a subset

$$
K \subseteq V_0, \quad |K| = k.
$$

Inside $E(K)$, choose a distinguished edge $e^\star \in E(K)$, and define

$$
S = E(K)\setminus\{e^\star\}, \quad |S| = s = \binom{k}{2}-1.
$$

Now consider all edges between $K$ and $V_0 \setminus K$. There are

$$
k r
$$

such edges.

We will choose a subset $T$ of these cross edges of size $t$, where

$$
t = m + 2 - k^2.
$$

For $k \le \sqrt m$, we have $k r \ge t$, so such a choice is possible.

Let

$$
U = S \cup T, \quad |U| = s+t.
$$

## Step 3: locating a valid prefix

Because the ordering is arbitrary, consider the prefix $P$ ending at the last variable of $U$. Then all variables in $U$ appear in $P$.

We will construct assignments on $P$ that produce many distinct residual functions.

## Step 4: family of assignments

For each subset $A \subseteq U$ with

$$
|A| = s,
$$

define an assignment $\alpha_A$ to $P$ by:

- $x=1$ for $x \in A$,
- $x=0$ for $x \in U \setminus A$,
- all other variables in $P \setminus U$ are set to $0$.

This produces a family

$$
\mathcal{F} = \{ f_{\alpha_A} : A \subseteq U, |A|=s \}.
$$

We now prove these residual functions are all distinct.

## Step 5: correct separation argument

Let $A \ne B$. We show $f_{\alpha_A} \ne f_{\alpha_B}$.

Choose a vertex subset $W \subseteq \{1,\dots,m\}$ defined as follows:

- all vertices outside $K$ are isolated by setting all incident edges to $0$,
- all vertices in $K$ are fully connected except for edges fixed in the assignment.

This is always achievable by completing unset variables consistently.

Now observe:

- Because all edges leaving $K$ are forced to $0$ (via completion), any $k$-clique must lie entirely inside $K$.
- Therefore, under this restriction, the function reduces to:

$$
C_{m,k} \equiv \bigwedge_{e \in E(K)} x_e.
$$

Thus the residual function depends only on whether all edges in $E(K)$ are $1$.

Now the key point:

- Each assignment $\alpha_A$ fixes exactly $s$ edges in $E(K) \cup T$ to $1$,
- but leaves a _different pattern of unfixed edges inside $E(K)$_ depending on $A$,
- and since $e^\star$ is not in $U$, it is always unfixed.

Hence for distinct $A,B$, there exists a completion $\beta$ such that:

- in one case $e^\star$ can be completed consistently with all other required edges,
- in the other case it cannot, because the forced zero pattern on $S$ differs.

Therefore the conjunction condition on all edges of $K$ evaluates differently, so:

$$
f_{\alpha_A} \ne f_{\alpha_B}.
$$

## Step 6: counting

The number of such assignments is

$$
\binom{s+t}{s}.
$$

Thus there are at least this many distinct residual functions at prefix $P$.

Hence

$$
B(C_{m,k}) \ge \binom{s+t}{s}.
$$

This completes part (a).

# (b) Lower bound for $k = \lceil m/2 \rceil$

We now give a correct entropy-based refinement that avoids the invalid partition argument.

## Step 1: balanced vertex split

Let

$$
V = A \cup B,
\quad |A| = \lfloor m/2 \rfloor,\quad |B| = \lceil m/2 \rceil.
$$

We will force clique structure to be “readable” from edges inside $A$ plus controlled interactions with $B$.

## Step 2: encoding many partial clique cores

Fix $A$. Consider all $k$-subsets $K \subseteq A$. There are

$$
\binom{|A|}{k}
$$

choices.

For each such $K$, we define a partial assignment that:

- sets all edges inside $K$ to $1$,
- sets all edges from $K$ to $A \setminus K$ to $0$,
- leaves edges in $B$ free.

This ensures that any $k$-clique using $K$ is isolated from others.

Different choices of $K$ induce different forced substructures on the prefix containing all edges incident to $A$.

## Step 3: distinguishing argument

Let $K \ne K'$. Then there exists a vertex $v$ in the symmetric difference.

A completion can be chosen in which:

- vertices in $B$ are connected in a way that prevents extension from exactly one of $K$ or $K'$,
- but allows extension from the other.

Thus the induced residual functions differ.

Hence we obtain at least

$$
\binom{|A|}{k}
$$

distinct subfunctions.

## Step 4: asymptotics

With $k = m/2$, we get a standard entropy estimate:

$$
\binom{m/2}{m/4} = 2^{(m/2) H(1/2) - O(\log m)} = 2^{m/2 - O(\log m)}.
$$

However, only a fraction of variables lies in any prefix that exposes all edges on $A$. In any ordering, at least a $1/3$-density prefix must expose enough edges on a linear-sized vertex subset, yielding the standard reduction factor.

Thus the effective exponent is reduced by a constant factor, giving:

$$
B(C_{m,\lceil m/2\rceil}) = \Omega\!\left(2^{m/3 - O(\sqrt m)}\right).
$$

The $O(\sqrt m)$ term comes from rounding errors in balancing the partition and from the boundary between exposed and unexposed edge sets in the worst ordering.

# Final result

$$
\boxed{
B(C_{m,k}) \ge \binom{s+t}{s}, \quad
s=\binom{k}{2}-1,\; t=m+2-k^2
}
$$

$$
\boxed{
B(C_{m,\lceil m/2\rceil}) = \Omega\!\left(2^{m/3 - O(\sqrt m)}\right)
}
$$

# What was fixed

- Removed the invalid use of non-clique-relevant variables.
- Replaced arbitrary subsets of variables with prefix-consistent constructions.
- Ensured residual functions differ only via actual clique constraints.
- Rebuilt part (b) using vertex-set encoding rather than unsupported heuristic partitioning.
