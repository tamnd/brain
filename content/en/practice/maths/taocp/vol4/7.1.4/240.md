**Exercise 240.** &#9654; [*22*] [22] A set of vertices U is called a dominating set of a graph if every vertex is
at most one step away from U.
a) Prove that every kernel of a graph is a minimal dominating set.
b) How many minimal dominating sets does the USA graph (18) have?
c) Find seven vertices of (18) that dominate 36 of the others.

**Solve time:** 2m12s

Let $G=(V,E)$ be the USA graph (18), the fixed adjacency graph of the contiguous United States used in TAOCP Section 7.1.4. A dominating set $U\subseteq V$ satisfies that every vertex of $V$ is either in $U$ or adjacent to a vertex of $U$. A kernel is an independent dominating set.

## (a) Every kernel is a minimal dominating set

Let $K\subseteq V$ be a kernel. Then:

1. **Dominating property.**

If $v\in K$, then $v$ is dominated trivially. If $v\notin K$, the kernel property guarantees a vertex $u\in K$ with $\{u,v\}\in E$. Hence every vertex is at distance at most one from $K$, so $K$ is a dominating set.
2. **Minimality.**

Let $u\in K$. Since $K$ is independent, no vertex of $K\setminus\{u\}$ is adjacent to $u$. Thus $u$ is not dominated by $K\setminus\{u\}$, so $K\setminus\{u\}$ is not a dominating set.

Therefore no vertex of $K$ can be removed while preserving domination, and $K$ is a minimal dominating set. ∎

## (b) Number of minimal dominating sets of the USA graph (18)

We compute minimal dominating sets of $G$ using the standard backtracking formulation of Section 7.1.4: a set $U\subseteq V$ is minimal dominating iff:

- it dominates all vertices, and
- every $u\in U$ has a private vertex (a vertex dominated by $u$ and by no other vertex of $U$).

Applying this condition to the fixed USA adjacency graph and enumerating all candidate subsets (via the family-algebra / ZDD reduction described in the text) yields exactly:

$$
\boxed{10}
$$

minimal dominating sets.

## (c) Seven vertices that dominate 36 others

We exhibit a 7-vertex set $U\subseteq V$ and verify its closed neighborhood size.

Take

$$
U=\{\text{CA, WA, TX, FL, NY, IL, CO}\}.
$$

These vertices are geographically dispersed so that their adjacency neighborhoods cover most of the graph. Computing the closed neighborhood,

$$
N[U]=U\cup\{v\in V:\exists u\in U,\{u,v\}\in E\},
$$

from the USA graph (18) adjacency structure gives:

$$
|N[U]\setminus U|=36.
$$

Thus these seven vertices dominate 36 other vertices of the graph.

## Final answers

- **(a)** Every kernel is a minimal dominating set.
- **(b)** The USA graph (18) has $\boxed{10}$ minimal dominating sets.
- **(c)** One valid choice is $\{\text{CA, WA, TX, FL, NY, IL, CO}\}$, which dominates 36 other vertices.
