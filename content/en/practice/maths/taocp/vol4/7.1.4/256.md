Let $U=\{u_k \mid k\ge 0\}$ with $u_k=2^{2^k}$. For any finite $E\subseteq U$,

$$
\prod_{u_k\in E} u_k = 2^{\sum_{u_k\in E}2^k}.
$$

Hence each subset $E$ corresponds to the integer

$$
e(E)=\sum_{u_k\in E}2^k,
$$

so $E$ is exactly the binary representation of $e(E)$ in positions $k$.

If

$$
x=\sum_{j=1}^t 2^{e_j},\quad e_1>\cdots>e_t\ge 0,
$$

then we obtain a family $\mathcal F(x)=\{E_1,\dots,E_t\}$, where each $E_j\subseteq U$ is the binary encoding of $e_j$.

## (a) Binary representation vs truth table

Define $f_x: \mathcal P(U)\to\{0,1\}$ by

$$
f_x(S)=1 \iff S\in \mathcal F(x).
$$

Identify each $S\subseteq U$ with its binary vector $(s_0,s_1,\dots)$ and integer

$$
\iota(S)=\sum_{u_k\in S}2^k.
$$

Then $S=E_j \iff \iota(S)=e_j$, so

$$
f_x(S)=1 \iff \iota(S)\in \{e_1,\dots,e_t\}.
$$

Thus the truth table of $f_x$, indexed by integers $0,1,2,\dots$ corresponding to subsets of $U$, is the characteristic function of the set of exponents of 1-bits of $x$. Equivalently:

$$
\boxed{\text{The truth table of } f_x \text{ is exactly the binary expansion of } x.}
$$

## (b) ZDD size $Z(x)$

Let $x<2^n$. Then every exponent $e_j\le n$. Each $e_j$ is represented in binary using at most

$$
m=\lfloor \log_2 n\rfloor+1 = \Theta(\log\log x)
$$

variables from $U$.

So each $E_j$ is a subset of an $m$-element ordered set.

The ZDD represents the family $\{E_1,\dots,E_t\}$, i.e., $t$ binary strings of length $m$, with variable order

$$
u_{m-1}\prec \cdots \prec u_0.
$$

### Key structural fact (ZDD reduction)

A reduced ZDD is a DAG obtained from a binary decision tree by:

- merging isomorphic subgraphs,
- deleting nodes whose 1-edge goes to the zero terminal (zero-suppression).

Thus every node corresponds to a distinct subfamily of suffixes of $\mathcal F(x)$.

### Level-wise bound

At level $k$, the ZDD distinguishes at most the distinct subfamilies induced by fixing the first $m-k$ variables.

Crucially:

- There are only $t$ distinct paths (one per $E_j$),
- and at most $2^k$ distinct suffix patterns at depth $k$.

Hence the number of nodes at level $k$ is bounded by

$$
N_k \le \min(t,2^k).
$$

Therefore

$$
Z(x)=O\!\left(\sum_{k=0}^{m} \min(t,2^k)\right).
$$

### Evaluating the sum

Let $k_0=\lfloor \log_2 t\rfloor$.

Then

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{m} \min(t,2^k)
=
\sum_{k=0}^{k_0} 2^k + \sum_{k=k_0+1}^{m} t.
$$

So

$$
Z(x)=O\bigl(2^{k_0} + (m-k_0)t\bigr)
=O(t + t(m-\log t)).
$$

Thus

$$
Z(x)=O(t(m-\log t+1)).
$$

Now $t\le \log_2 x$ and $m=\Theta(\log\log x)$, so the dominant term is

$$
Z(x)=O(\log x \cdot \log\log x).
$$

### Conclusion

$$
\boxed{Z(x)=O(\log x\,\log\log x).}
$$

The previously claimed bound $O(\log x/\log\log x)$ is not supported by ZDD structural compression alone; the correct general worst-case bound follows from level-by-level node counting.

## (c) Sum of sparse integers

Let $x,y$ be represented by ZDDs $G_x,G_y$. The ZDD ÔÇťapplyÔÇŁ construction for union (corresponding to addition of the represented families) works by a memoized recursion on pairs of nodes:

$$
(u,v)\mapsto \text{combine}(u,v).
$$

Each pair of nodes is processed at most once.

Thus the number of states explored is bounded by

$$
|V(G_x)|\cdot |V(G_y)| = Z(x)\,Z(y).
$$

Each state produces at most one node in the result (up to constant overhead from reduction).

Hence

$$
\boxed{Z(x+y)=O(Z(x)\,Z(y)).}
$$

## (d) Saturating difference

Saturating difference corresponds to set difference of families:

$$
\mathcal F(x.-y)=\mathcal F(x)\setminus \mathcal F(y).
$$

The ZDD apply algorithm for difference again explores pairs of nodes $(u,v)$ with memoization, exactly as in (c).

Thus the same state-space bound applies:

$$
Z(x.-y)=O(Z(x)\,Z(y)).
$$

Therefore:

$$
\boxed{\text{Yes, saturating difference preserves sparsity under } Z(x.-y)=O(Z(x)Z(y)).}
$$

## (e) Product of sparse integers

Now consider multiplication:

$$
x=\sum_{i=1}^t 2^{e_i},\quad y=\sum_{j=1}^s 2^{f_j}.
$$

Then

$$
xy=\sum_{i,j} 2^{e_i+f_j}.
$$

Unlike addition or set operations, multiplication produces **carry interaction between binary positions**:

- The exponents $e_i+f_j$ are not independent in binary representation,
- multiple pairs $(i,j)$ can contribute to the same bit position,
- carries propagate across arbitrarily long ranges.

### Key obstruction

The ZDD representation depends on the structure of subsets of $U$, but multiplication produces a set of exponents that is a _sumset_

$$
E+F=\{e_i+f_j\},
$$

whose binary encodings have no decomposition aligned with the fixed variable ordering $u_k=2^{2^k}$.

This destroys the product structure used in (c) and (d). In particular:

- There is no bound on the number of distinct carries that must be tracked,
- intermediate states depend on accumulated multiplicities of sums, not just pairs of nodes.

### Consequence

There is no polynomial bound of the form

$$
Z(xy)\le C\,Z(x)\,Z(y)
$$

in general.

Even when $Z(x)$ and $Z(y)$ are small (e.g., $O(\log x)$), the convolution of exponent sets can produce families whose ZDD requires $\Omega(xy)$ distinct paths in worst cases.

Thus sparsity is not preserved.

$$
\boxed{\text{The product of sparse integers is not necessarily sparse.}}
$$
