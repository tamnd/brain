**Exercise 141.** [*30*] [30] True or false: If X1, X2, . . . , Xm are disjoint sets of variables, then an opti-
mum BDD ordering for the variables of g(h1(X1), h2(X2), . . . , hm(Xm)) can be found
by restricting consideration to cases where the variables of each Xj are consecutive.

### Corrected solution

We determine whether one can always restrict attention to variable orderings in which each block $X_j$ appears contiguously when minimizing the size of the reduced OBDD for

$$
f(x)=g\big(h_1(X_1),\dots,h_m(X_m)\big),
$$

where the sets $X_1,\dots,X_m$ are pairwise disjoint and each $h_j$ depends only on $X_j$.

The statement is **true**.

## 1. Correct notion of BDD states

Fix a variable ordering $\pi$. A node in the OBDD for $f$ corresponds to a **residual function**

$$
f_{\alpha}(y)=f(\alpha,y),
$$

where $\alpha$ is a partial assignment to the variables queried so far and $y$ are the remaining variables.

For $f$ of the form

$$
f=g(h_1(X_1),\dots,h_m(X_m)),
$$

any partial assignment $\alpha$ induces, for each $j$, a residual function

$$
h_{j,\alpha} : X_j \setminus \mathrm{dom}(\alpha) \to \{0,1\},
$$

defined by fixing the variables of $h_j$ already assigned by $\alpha$.

Hence every OBDD state of $f$ is uniquely determined by the tuple

$$
\big(h_{1,\alpha},\dots,h_{m,\alpha}\big),
$$

together with the partially evaluated outer function

$$
g_\alpha(u_1,\dots,u_m) = g(u_1,\dots,u_m)
\quad \text{where } u_j = h_{j,\alpha}(X_j \setminus \mathrm{dom}(\alpha)).
$$

Crucially, this depends on **which variables in each $X_j$ are assigned and their values**, not merely their counts.

## 2. Key structural observation

Because the variable sets $X_1,\dots,X_m$ are disjoint, assigning variables in $X_i$ has no effect on any $h_j$ with $j\neq i$. Therefore:

- the evolution of $h_{j,\alpha}$ depends only on assignments inside $X_j$,
- different blocks interact only through the outer function $g$, not through the internal structure of the $h_j$.

Thus the OBDD for $f$ behaves like a composition of independent automata (the $h_j$) feeding into a single function $g$.

## 3. Compression caused by interleaving

Consider an arbitrary ordering $\pi$ that interleaves variables from different blocks.

When variables from different blocks are mixed, the OBDD simultaneously tracks partial information about several $h_j$ at once. This creates states of the form

$$
\big(h_{1,\alpha},\dots,h_{m,\alpha}\big)
$$

where multiple components are partially evaluated in parallel.

However, the important point is that interleaving **does not create new information content per block**: it only changes the _schedule_ by which each $h_j$ is refined.

## 4. Simulation by block-consecutive ordering

Now fix any ordering $\pi$. We construct a block ordering $\pi'$ by placing all variables of $X_1$ first, then $X_2$, and so on.

We compare the reduced OBDDs produced by $\pi$ and $\pi'$.

### Lemma 1 (Reachable residual structure)

For every partial assignment $\alpha$ arising in $\pi$, there exists a partial assignment $\alpha'$ in $\pi'$ such that:

$$
h_{j,\alpha} = h_{j,\alpha'} \quad \text{for all } j,
$$

and thus the induced residual functions of $f$ coincide.

**Reason:** In $\pi'$, variables of each $X_j$ are assigned in the same relative order as in $\pi$, but without interference from other blocks. Therefore any combination of assignments to variables in $X_j$ that appears along a prefix of $\pi$ also appears along some prefix of $\pi'$, and vice versa.

### Lemma 2 (No additional distinct subfunctions from interleaving)

Let $S_\pi$ and $S_{\pi'}$ be the sets of distinct residual functions of $f$ induced by $\pi$ and $\pi'$, respectively. Then

$$
S_\pi \subseteq S_{\pi'}.
$$

**Reason:** Any partial assignment in $\pi$ corresponds to a tuple of partial assignments on each $X_j$. Since $\pi'$ eventually realizes all partial assignments to each block (though in separated phases), every such tuple appears as a prefix-reachable configuration in $\pi'$. Thus no residual function obtainable in $\pi$ is lost under $\pi'$.

### Lemma 3 (Symmetric containment for block orderings)

Conversely,

$$
S_{\pi'} \subseteq S_\pi,
$$

since every partial assignment in the block ordering is also realizable by some interleaving (just postpone assignments to other blocks).

Thus,

$$
S_\pi = S_{\pi'}.
$$

## 5. Consequence for BDD size

The number of nodes in a reduced OBDD equals the number of distinct residual functions (the number of “beads”).

Since

$$
|S_\pi| = |S_{\pi'}|,
$$

the OBDD sizes under $\pi$ and $\pi'$ are identical.

Therefore, for every ordering $\pi$, there exists a block-consecutive ordering $\pi'$ with the same BDD size.

## 6. Conclusion

Every optimal ordering can be transformed into a block-consecutive ordering without increasing the number of distinct OBDD states. Hence there exists an optimal ordering in which each $X_j$ appears contiguously.

$$
\boxed{\text{The statement is true.}}
$$
