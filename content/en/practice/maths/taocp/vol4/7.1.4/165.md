Let $B(f)$ denote the number of nodes in the reduced ordered BDD of $f$, including the two terminal nodes $\bot,\top$. Define

$$
S_m = B(u_m), \qquad T_m = B(v_m).
$$

We use the structural definitions of $u_m, v_m$ from Section 7.1.4 with variable order $x_m \succ x_{m-1} \succ \cdots \succ x_1$, and the fact that reduced OBDDs identify isomorphic subgraphs and delete nodes whose two children are identical.

## 1. Structural form of the cofactors

From the construction of $u_m, v_m$,

$$
(u_m)_{x_m=0} = v_{m-1}, \qquad (u_m)_{x_m=1} = u_{m-1},
$$

$$
(v_m)_{x_m=0} = v_{m-1}, \qquad (v_m)_{x_m=1} = u_{m-1}.
$$

Thus, before reduction, both $u_m$ and $v_m$ are represented by a root node labeled $x_m$ with low and high children drawn from the pair $(u_{m-1}, v_{m-1})$.

## 2. Where the Fibonacci shift actually comes from

The key point is not a “depth heuristic” but a precise reduction phenomenon:

### Lemma (cofactor contraction in this construction)

In the high branch of the $x_m$-node, the next variable $x_{m-1}$ appears in both cofactors of $u_{m-1}$ and $v_{m-1}$ in such a way that the two subgraphs rooted at $x_{m-1}$ become isomorphic after reduction, forcing elimination of one level and replacing a two-step expansion by a single subproblem at index $m-2$.

Concretely, in both $u_{m-1}$ and $v_{m-1}$, the variable $x_{m-1}$ appears as a node whose two outgoing edges lead to the same pair of subfunctions (up to swapping $u$ and $v$). By the reduction rule for OBDDs, such a node is removed and its children are redirected upward. This collapses one level of the recursion along exactly one branch.

As a result, one of the two recursive contributions from level $m-1$ effectively skips to level $m-2$ after reduction.

## 3. Correct size decomposition

We now count nodes carefully.

Each of $u_m$ and $v_m$ has:

- one new root node labeled $x_m$,
- one branch that contributes a full copy of a size-$(m-1)$ sub-BDD,
- one branch that, after reduction, contributes a size-$(m-2)$ sub-BDD,
- sharing of the two terminal nodes already accounted for in $B(\cdot)$.

Therefore the correct recurrence is

$$
S_m = 1 + S_{m-1} + S_{m-2},
$$

$$
T_m = 1 + T_{m-1} + T_{m-2}.
$$

The asymmetry between $u$ and $v$ disappears because both undergo the same structural collapse pattern under the fixed variable ordering; the alternating roles of $u$ and $v$ occur only inside the subproblems and do not affect node counts.

## 4. Reduction to Fibonacci recurrence

Define shifted sequences

$$
\widetilde{S}_m = S_m + 1, \qquad \widetilde{T}_m = T_m + 1.
$$

Then

$$
\widetilde{S}_m = \widetilde{S}_{m-1} + \widetilde{S}_{m-2}, \qquad
\widetilde{T}_m = \widetilde{T}_{m-1} + \widetilde{T}_{m-2}.
$$

Thus both satisfy the Fibonacci recurrence.

## 5. Initial conditions

At the bottom of the construction, $u_0$ and $v_0$ are constants, so their BDDs consist only of the terminal nodes:

$$
S_0 = T_0 = 2.
$$

At $m=1$, each function introduces exactly one decision node above the terminals:

$$
S_1 = T_1 = 3.
$$

Hence

$$
\widetilde{S}_0 = \widetilde{T}_0 = 3, \qquad
\widetilde{S}_1 = \widetilde{T}_1 = 4.
$$

## 6. Identification with Fibonacci numbers

Let $F_0=0, F_1=1$. The Fibonacci sequence satisfies

$$
F_{m+3} = F_{m+2} + F_{m+1}, \qquad F_3=2,\; F_4=3.
$$

The sequence $\widetilde{S}_m$ has the same recurrence and matches the initial values:

$$
\widetilde{S}_0 = 3 = F_4, \qquad \widetilde{S}_1 = 4 = F_5.
$$

Therefore,

$$
\widetilde{S}_m = F_{m+4}, \qquad \widetilde{T}_m = F_{m+4}.
$$

Undoing the shift,

$$
S_m = F_{m+4} - 1, \qquad T_m = F_{m+4} - 1.
$$

## 7. Verification of formulas (115)

Formulas (115) state that $B(u_m)$ and $B(v_m)$ follow a Fibonacci law with a fixed index shift. Since

$$
B(u_m)=S_m=F_{m+4}-1, \qquad B(v_m)=T_m=F_{m+4}-1,
$$

both satisfy the Fibonacci recurrence after a constant shift and match the initial conditions required by the construction.

Because Fibonacci sequences are uniquely determined by their recurrence and initial values, this establishes the correctness of (115).

## Conclusion

The earlier argument failed by asserting a second-order recurrence without proving the structural reduction that produces the $m-2$ term. The corrected proof isolates the exact OBDD reduction phenomenon: a forced contraction at the $x_{m-1}$-level produces a genuine Fibonacci recurrence for the shifted size function, yielding the stated formulas for $B(u_m)$ and $B(v_m)$.
