**Exercise 111.** [*M22*] [M22] Verify the summation formula (79) in Theorem U.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m23s  

---

From Theorem U, formula (79) expresses a finite sum in telescoping form. Write it as

$$
S_n = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \bigl(T_{n,k} - T_{n,k-1}\bigr),
$$

where the convention is $T_{n,-1} = 0$. The theorem asserts that this sum simplifies to

$$
S_n = T_{n,n}.
$$

To verify (79), start from the left-hand side and expand the summation:

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n} \bigl(T_{n,k} - T_{n,k-1}\bigr)
= \sum_{k=0}^{n} T_{n,k} - \sum_{k=0}^{n} T_{n,k-1}.
$$

Reindex the second sum by letting $j = k-1$. Then as $k$ runs from $0$ to $n$, $j$ runs from $-1$ to $n-1$, so

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n} T_{n,k-1} = \sum_{j=-1}^{n-1} T_{n,j}.
$$

Using $T_{n,-1} = 0$, this becomes

$$
\sum_{j=0}^{n-1} T_{n,j}.
$$

Hence,

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n} T_{n,k} - \sum_{k=0}^{n} T_{n,k-1}
= \left(\sum_{k=0}^{n} T_{n,k}\right) - \left(\sum_{k=0}^{n-1} T_{n,k}\right).
$$

All intermediate terms cancel, leaving only the final term:

$$
S_n = T_{n,n}.
$$

This establishes formula (79).
