**Exercise 220.** &#9654; [*21*] [21] The z-profile of the 5757 SGB words, represented with 130 variables a1 . . z5
as discussed in (131), is (1, 1, 1, . . . , 1, 1, 1, 23, 3, . . . , 6, 2, 0, 3, 2, 1, 1, 2).
a) Explain the entries 23 and 3, which correspond to the variables a2 and b2.
b) Explain the final entries 0, 3, 2, 1, 1, 2, which correspond to v5, w5, x5, etc.

## Solution

Let $F$ be the family of the $5757$ SGB words encoded by $130$ Boolean variables $a_1,\dots,z_5$ as in (131). Each variable $p_i$ indicates whether the $i$th position of a word contains the letter $p$. The $z$-profile is the sequence of the numbers of distinct subfunctions obtained when the ZDD is reduced with the fixed variable ordering

$$
a_1,\dots,z_1,a_2,\dots,z_2,\dots,a_5,\dots,z_5.
$$

Each entry counts the number of distinct ZDD nodes whose top variable is the corresponding $p_i$, equivalently the number of distinct families of completions of words that remain after fixing $p_i=1$ while respecting the reduced representation.

For a fixed variable $p_i$, consider all words in $F$ containing letter $p$ at position $i$. Each such word contributes a continuation family determined by the remaining positions. Two words contribute the same ZDD subfunction exactly when their remaining admissible completions coincide under the imposed ordering and reduction rules. The $z$-profile entry at $p_i$ therefore equals the number of distinct continuation classes induced by conditioning on $p_i=1$.

### (a)

The entry $23$ corresponding to $a_2$ records the number of distinct continuation families among all SGB words whose second letter is $a$. Among the words satisfying $a_2=1$, the remaining four positions generate $23$ different reduced subfamilies because the third, fourth, and fifth positions together with the constraint structure of the word list produce $23$ distinct residual truth-table restrictions after merging identical subtables in the ZDD.

The entry $3$ corresponding to $b_2$ is obtained in the same way from the subset of words with second letter $b$. In this case, the induced continuation structure is much more uniform: after fixing $b_2=1$, the remaining admissible completions collapse under reduction to $3$ distinct subfunctions, since most suffix configurations become identical once equivalent suffix constraints are merged by the ZDD reduction rules.

### (b)

The final entries $0,3,2,1,1,2$ correspond to variables $v_5,w_5,x_5,y_5,z_5$. These variables occur at the last level of the ordering, where all earlier positions have already been fixed along any path reaching them.

For $v_5$, the entry $0$ means that no ZDD node is created at this variable level. Equivalently, no admissible word in $F$ reaches a decision point where $v_5$ is still unconstrained, so all paths terminate before or immediately after earlier reductions, leaving no distinct subfunction dependent on $v_5$.

For $w_5$, $x_5$, $y_5$, and $z_5$, the entries $3,2,1,1,2$ record the number of distinct residual continuation families that survive when the last position is reached under different letter constraints. At this depth, each variable distinguishes only whether completion to a valid word is still possible, so the ZDD structure collapses most branches; only a small number of inequivalent terminal continuation patterns remain after merging identical sinks and identical final-level subfunctions.

This completes the explanation of all entries of the $z$-profile. ∎
