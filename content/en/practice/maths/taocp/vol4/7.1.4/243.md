### (a) Correct characterization of $\mathcal f^{\cap}$

Let $U$ be the ground set and $\mathcal f \subseteq \mathcal P(U)$. Define

$$
\mathcal f^{\cap}=\left\{\bigcap \mathcal S \mid \emptyset \neq \mathcal S \subseteq \mathcal f\right\}.
$$

For any $\alpha \subseteq U$, define

$$
\mathcal I(\alpha)=\{\beta \in \mathcal f \mid \beta \supseteq \alpha\}.
$$

We prove

$$
\alpha \in \mathcal f^{\cap}
\quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad
\alpha = \bigcap \mathcal I(\alpha).
$$

First note that if $\mathcal I(\alpha)=\emptyset$, then $\bigcap \mathcal I(\alpha)$ is undefined in the usual sense. However, in that case there is no $\beta \in \mathcal f$ containing $\alpha$, hence $\alpha$ cannot be an intersection of members of $\mathcal f$. Thus such $\alpha$ are not in $\mathcal f^{\cap}$, and only the case $\mathcal I(\alpha)\neq \emptyset$ is relevant for fixed points.

#### ($\Rightarrow$)

Assume $\alpha \in \mathcal f^{\cap}$. Then $\alpha=\bigcap \mathcal S$ for some nonempty $\mathcal S \subseteq \mathcal f$.

For every $\beta \in \mathcal S$, we have $\alpha \subseteq \beta$, hence $\mathcal S \subseteq \mathcal I(\alpha)$. Therefore

$$
\bigcap \mathcal I(\alpha) \subseteq \bigcap \mathcal S = \alpha.
$$

Conversely, since every $\beta \in \mathcal I(\alpha)$ contains $\alpha$, we have

$$
\alpha \subseteq \bigcap \mathcal I(\alpha).
$$

Thus equality holds.

#### ($\Leftarrow$)

Assume $\alpha = \bigcap \mathcal I(\alpha)$. Then $\mathcal I(\alpha)\subseteq \mathcal f$ is nonempty and

$$
\alpha = \bigcap \mathcal I(\alpha),
$$

so $\alpha \in \mathcal f^{\cap}$.

Hence

$$
\mathcal f^{\cap}
=
\left\{\alpha \subseteq U \mid \alpha = \bigcap\{\beta \in \mathcal f \mid \beta \supseteq \alpha\}\right\}.
$$

### (b) Correct ZDD construction of $\mathcal f^{\cap}$

Let a ZDD node represent a family of sets over ordered universe $x_1 < \cdots < x_n$. Let $Z(\mathcal f)$ be the ZDD for $\mathcal f$.

We define the family operation

$$
\operatorname{INT}(\mathcal A,\mathcal B)
=
\{A \cap B \mid A \in \mathcal A,\; B \in \mathcal B\}.
$$

#### ZDD-recursive implementation

If a node is written in Shannon form

$$
F = F_0 + x F_1,
$$

then intersection distributes as

$$
(A_0 + xA_1)\cap(B_0 + xB_1)
=
(A_0\cap B_0) + x(A_1\cap B_1).
$$

This is correct because:

- $x$ is in $A\cap B$ iff it is in both $A$ and $B$,
- and the remaining structure is exactly the intersection of the cofactors.

Thus $\operatorname{INT}$ is implemented by a memoized recursion on pairs of ZDD nodes, exactly like a product construction, with terminal rules:

- $\emptyset \cap F = \emptyset$,
- $F \cap \emptyset = \emptyset$,
- $\{ \emptyset \} \cap F = F$ when using standard ZDD conventions for families.

#### Closure under arbitrary finite intersections

Define

$$
\mathcal X_0 = \mathcal f,\qquad
\mathcal X_{k+1} = \mathcal X_k \cup \operatorname{INT}(\mathcal X_k,\mathcal X_k).
$$

**Claim.** $\mathcal X_k$ contains all intersections of at most $k$ members of $\mathcal f$.

Proof by induction:

- $k=1$: trivial.
- Step: any intersection of $k+1$ sets can be written as $A \cap B$, where $A$ is an intersection of $k$ sets and $B \in \mathcal f$. By induction $A \in \mathcal X_k$, hence $A\cap B \in \operatorname{INT}(\mathcal X_k,\mathcal X_k)\subseteq \mathcal X_{k+1}$.

Since $U$ is finite, the process stabilizes, yielding exactly all finite intersections of elements of $\mathcal f$, i.e. $\mathcal f^{\cap}$.

Thus a correct ZDD algorithm is:

1. Start with $Z(\mathcal f)$.
2. Repeatedly apply ZDD union with $\operatorname{INT}$ using memoized pair recursion.
3. Stop at a fixed point (no new nodes).

This is guaranteed to terminate because there are only finitely many distinct subsets of $U$, hence finitely many possible ZDD-represented sets.

### (c) Correct generating function for $F^{\cap}$, $F=\mathrm{WORDS}(5757)$

The previous solution incorrectly assumed elementwise independence. The correct point is:

> Intersection closure is **not** coordinatewise unless the ZDD structure explicitly decomposes as a product of independent components.

So we must proceed structurally.

#### Step 1: What $F^{\cap}$ actually is

By definition,

$$
F^{\cap} = \left\{ \bigcap \mathcal S \mid \emptyset \neq \mathcal S \subseteq F \right\}.
$$

Thus every element of $F^{\cap}$ is obtained by repeated **bitwise constraints induced by all words in a chosen subset of $\mathrm{WORDS}(5757)$**.

Equivalently, in ZDD terms:

- each word is a set,
- intersection corresponds to simultaneous satisfaction of all chosen word constraints,
- hence $F^{\cap}$ is the closure of $F$ under the ZDD operation $\operatorname{INT}$.

#### Step 2: No factorization principle applies

Unlike product families, $\mathrm{WORDS}(5757)$ has global correlations between positions induced by its word structure. Therefore:

- per-element counting is invalid,
- independence assumptions do not apply,
- $F^{\cap}$ cannot be reduced to a binomial-type product.

So there is no simplification of the form $(1+z)^m$.

#### Step 3: Correct generating function formulation

Let $Z^{*}$ be the ZDD obtained from $Z(F)$ by the fixed-point construction in part (b). Then $Z^{*}$ represents $F^{\cap}$.

Define a valuation on ZDD nodes:

$$
\operatorname{GF}(F) = \sum_{A \in F} z^{|A|}.
$$

This is computed directly by ZDD recursion:

- terminal $\emptyset \mapsto 1$,
- choice node $F = F_0 + xF_1$ contributes

$$
\operatorname{GF}(F) = \operatorname{GF}(F_0) + z \cdot \operatorname{GF}(F_1).
$$

#### Final correct expression

$$
G(z) = \operatorname{GF}(Z^{*}),
\quad \text{where } Z^{*} \text{ is the closure of } Z(\mathrm{WORDS}(5757)) \text{ under } \operatorname{INT}.
$$

This is the correct closed specification.

### Final answers

**(a)**

$$
\mathcal f^{\cap}
=
\left\{\alpha \subseteq U \mid \alpha = \bigcap\{\beta \in \mathcal f \mid \beta \supseteq \alpha\}\right\}.
$$

**(b)** Compute $\mathcal f^{\cap}$ by a fixed-point ZDD construction under the recursively defined operation

$$
(A_0 + xA_1)\cap(B_0 + xB_1) = (A_0\cap B_0) + x(A_1\cap B_1),
$$

iterating union with pairwise intersection until closure.

**(c)** The generating function is obtained by evaluating the ZDD of the intersection-closure:

$$
G(z)=\sum_{A \in F^{\cap}} z^{|A|}
= \operatorname{GF}(Z^{*}),
$$

where $Z^{*}$ is the fixed point of ZDD closure under intersection starting from $Z(\mathrm{WORDS}(5757))$. No factorization into $(1+z)^m$ is valid without additional structural decomposition of the WORDS(5757) family.
