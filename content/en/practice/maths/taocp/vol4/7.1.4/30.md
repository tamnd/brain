## Solution

Let a minterm corresponding to an assignment $x_1 \dots x_n$ be

$$
M(x_1,\dots,x_n)=\prod_{i=1}^n (1-p_i)^{1-x_i}p_i^{x_i}.
$$

Maximizing this product is equivalent to maximizing its logarithm, since $\log$ is strictly increasing. Define

$$
W(x_1,\dots,x_n)=\sum_{i=1}^n \bigl(x_i \log p_i + (1-x_i)\log(1-p_i)\bigr).
$$

Expanding,

$$
W(x_1,\dots,x_n)=\sum_{i=1}^n \log(1-p_i) + \sum_{i=1}^n x_i \log\frac{p_i}{1-p_i}.
$$

The first sum is independent of the assignment, so maximizing $W$ reduces to maximizing

$$
W'(x_1,\dots,x_n)=\sum_{i=1}^n x_i \log\frac{p_i}{1-p_i}.
$$

Define the weight

$$
a_i=\log\frac{p_i}{1-p_i}.
$$

Then the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of $f(x_1,\dots,x_n)=1$ maximizing $\sum_{i=1}^n a_i x_i$.

Let the BDD be ordered and reduced, and let each node $v$ be labeled by $V(v)=j$, with LO and HI successors $LO(v)$ and $HI(v)$, corresponding to setting $x_j=0$ and $x_j=1$ respectively.

Define a value function $B(v)$ on BDD nodes as the maximum contribution of any partial assignment consistent with reaching $v$, considering only variables tested along paths from $v$ to a sink.

For sink nodes,

$$
B(\bot)=-\infty,\quad B(\top)=0.
$$

For a branch node $v$ with $V(v)=j$, any feasible assignment extending through $v$ must choose either $x_j=0$ or $x_j=1$. In the first case, the contribution from $x_j$ is $0$, and the continuation is through $LO(v)$. In the second case, the contribution from $x_j$ is $a_j$, and the continuation is through $HI(v)$. Therefore,

$$
B(v)=\max\bigl(B(LO(v)),\, a_j + B(HI(v))\bigr).
$$

Since the BDD is acyclic and ordered, this recurrence is well-defined and can be evaluated in reverse topological order from sinks to root. The value $B(\text{root})$ gives the maximum contribution from all variables that actually appear in the BDD structure.

Variables that do not occur on any root-to-sink path are independent of the constraint $f(x_1,\dots,x_n)=1$. For such a variable $x_i$, the contribution to $W'$ is maximized by choosing $x_i=1$ if $a_i\ge 0$ and $x_i=0$ if $a_i<0$. The maximal contribution of such a variable is

$$
\max(0,a_i).
$$

Thus, once $B(\text{root})$ is computed, a complete maximizing assignment is obtained by tracing a path from the root that realizes the maximum in the recurrence at each node, and then assigning every variable not encountered on that path according to the rule above.

The resulting assignment maximizes the original reliability contribution over all $x_1,\dots,x_n$ satisfying $f(x_1,\dots,x_n)=1$. This completes the construction and the optimality proof follows from the fact that each term $a_i x_i$ is optimized locally at every branching choice, and all satisfying assignments are represented by root-to-$\top$ paths in the BDD.
