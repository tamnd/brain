**Exercise 177.** [*M22*] [M22] Complete the proof of Theorem A by bounding the entire quasi-profile.

Let $(b_0,\dots,b_n)$ be the quasi-profile of the reduced ordered BDD of $f(x_1,\dots,x_n)$, where $b_j$ is the number of nodes labeled $j$. The total number of nodes is

$$
B(f)=\sum_{j=0}^{n} b_j .
$$

We correct the analysis by treating the terminal level and the internal levels separately.

### Level $j=0$ (terminal level)

By convention for reduced ordered BDDs, level $0$ consists of the terminal nodes representing the constant functions $0$ and $1$. These are distinct and always present. Hence

$$
b_0 = 2.
$$

### Levels $j \ge 1$

Fix $j \in \{1,\dots,n\}$. Each node $v$ at level $j$ represents a subfunction

$$
f_v(x_j,\dots,x_n)
$$

obtained by fixing a prefix assignment $(x_1,\dots,x_{j-1})$ along some path from the root to $v$.

#### Step 1: Every node is induced by a prefix assignment

Because the BDD is rooted and acyclic, every node $v$ is reachable from the root. Along any root-to-$v$ path, the variables $x_1,\dots,x_{j-1}$ are tested in order, with each edge corresponding to fixing a value in $\{0,1\}$. Hence this path defines a unique assignment

$$
a \in \{0,1\}^{j-1}
$$

that leads to $v$. Therefore every node at level $j$ arises from at least one prefix assignment.

#### Step 2: Nodes correspond to distinct subfunctions

If two distinct nodes $u \neq v$ at level $j$ induced the same subfunction on $(x_j,\dots,x_n)$, then they would be isomorphic as BDD nodes: they would have identical low and high successors and represent the same function. This contradicts reducedness of the BDD, which merges all isomorphic subgraphs. Hence distinct nodes at level $j$ correspond to distinct subfunctions.

Therefore, the number $b_j$ of nodes at level $j$ is bounded by the number of distinct subfunctions induced by assignments to $(x_1,\dots,x_{j-1})$.

#### Step 3: Counting possible subfunctions

There are exactly $2^{j-1}$ assignments to the variables $(x_1,\dots,x_{j-1})$. Each such assignment induces a (possibly repeated) subfunction on $(x_j,\dots,x_n)$. Hence the number of distinct induced subfunctions is at most $2^{j-1}$. Since each node at level $j$ corresponds to a distinct induced subfunction,

$$
b_j \le 2^{j-1}.
$$

### Total bound on the quasi-profile

Now sum over all levels:

$$
B(f)= b_0 + \sum_{j=1}^{n} b_j
\le 2 + \sum_{j=1}^{n} 2^{j-1}.
$$

Compute the geometric sum:

$$
\sum_{j=1}^{n} 2^{j-1} = 2^n - 1.
$$

Thus

$$
B(f) \le 2 + (2^n - 1) = 2^n + 1.
$$

### Conclusion

The quasi-profile of any reduced ordered BDD satisfies

$$
\sum_{j=0}^{n} b_j \le 2^n + 1,
$$

which completes the required global bound in Theorem A. ∎
