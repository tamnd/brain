**Exercise 7.** [*21*] [21] Given a Boolean function f(x1, . . . , xn), let
gk(x0, x1, . . . , xn) = f(x0, . . . , xk−2, xk−1∨xk, xk+1, . . . , xn)
for 1 ≤k ≤n.
Find a simple relation between (a) the truth tables and (b) the BDDs of f and gk.

Let

$$
g_k(x_0,\dots,x_n)=f(x_0,\dots,x_{k-2},\,x_{k-1}\lor x_k,\,x_{k+1},\dots,x_n),
\quad 1\le k\le n.
$$

Fix the variable order $x_0<x_1<\cdots<x_n$.

## (a) Truth tables

Let $a$ be an assignment to all variables except $x_{k-1},x_k$. Define the unary function

$$
h_a(z)=f(a_0,\dots,a_{k-2},z,a_{k+1},\dots,a_n), \quad z\in\{0,1\}.
$$

Then

$$
g_k(a, x_{k-1},x_k)=h_a(x_{k-1}\lor x_k).
$$

Now evaluate by cases:

- If $(x_{k-1},x_k)=(0,0)$, then $x_{k-1}\lor x_k=0$, hence

$$
g_k(a,0,0)=h_a(0)=f(a_0,\dots,a_{k-2},0,a_{k+1},\dots,a_n).
$$
- If $(x_{k-1},x_k)\in\{(0,1),(1,0),(1,1)\}$, then $x_{k-1}\lor x_k=1$, hence

$$
g_k(a,x_{k-1},x_k)=h_a(1)=f(a_0,\dots,a_{k-2},1,a_{k+1},\dots,a_n).
$$

Thus each 4-entry block of the truth table of $f$ determined by fixing all variables except $x_{k-1},x_k$,

$$
\big(f_a(0,0),\,f_a(0,1),\,f_a(1,0),\,f_a(1,1)\big),
$$

is transformed into the corresponding block of $g_k$,

$$
\big(f_a(0,0),\,f_a(1,1),\,f_a(1,1),\,f_a(1,1)\big).
$$

Equivalently: in every such block, the value depends only on whether $x_{k-1}\lor x_k$ is $0$ or $1$.

## (b) BDDs

Let $B(f)$ be the reduced ordered BDD of $f$. Define the subfunction

$$
h(z, x_0,\dots,x_{k-2},x_{k+1},\dots,x_n)
= f(x_0,\dots,x_{k-2},z,x_{k+1},\dots,x_n).
$$

Then

$$
g_k = h(x_{k-1}\lor x_k,\; x_0,\dots,x_{k-2},x_{k+1},\dots,x_n).
$$

### Step 1: cofactors at $x_{k-1}$

For the function $g_k$, compute cofactors with respect to $x_{k-1}$:

- If $x_{k-1}=0$, then $x_{k-1}\lor x_k = x_k$, so

$$
(g_k)_{x_{k-1}=0} = h(x_k,\; x_0,\dots,x_{k-2},x_{k+1},\dots,x_n).
$$
- If $x_{k-1}=1$, then $x_{k-1}\lor x_k = 1$, so

$$
(g_k)_{x_{k-1}=1} = h(1,\; x_0,\dots,x_{k-2},x_{k+1},\dots,x_n).
$$

Thus the ROBDD of $g_k$ is determined by two functions:

$$
g_k^{(0)} = h(x_k,\dots), \qquad g_k^{(1)} = h(1,\dots).
$$

### Step 2: interpretation in the BDD of $f$

In the ROBDD of $f$, every node labeled $x_{k-1}$ has:

- low child representing $h(0,\dots)$,
- high child representing $h(1,\dots)$.

Hence:

- $g_k^{(1)}$ is exactly the high child subfunction of each $x_{k-1}$-node.
- $g_k^{(0)}$ is obtained from the low child subfunction by replacing the variable $x_{k-1}$ by $x_k$ (since the OR makes $x_k$ take the role of $x_{k-1}$ only in the zero-branch).

Concretely, the transformation is:

- **At each $x_{k-1}$-node** with children $(L,H)$:

- the new high branch remains $H$,
- the new low branch is obtained from $L$ by redirecting the decision on $x_k$ so that it becomes $x_{k-1}\lor x_k$, i.e.:

- paths where $x_k=1$ behave as if $x_{k-1}=1$,
- paths where $x_k=0$ behave as the original low structure.

### Step 3: clean structural description

A correct global construction is:

1. Traverse $B(f)$.
2. Replace every node labeled $x_{k-1}$ by a node whose cofactors are:

$$
\text{LO} = \mathrm{ROBDD}(h(x_k,\dots)), \quad
\text{HI} = \mathrm{ROBDD}(h(1,\dots)).
$$
3. Reduce the resulting diagram using ROBDD reduction rules.

### Step 4: key simplification insight

Because $h(1,\dots)$ does not depend on $x_k$, all dependence on the $x_k$-sub-BDD disappears in the high cofactor of $x_{k-1}$. Therefore:

- in the **high branch of $x_{k-1}$**, the entire $x_k$-subgraph collapses to a constant subgraph determined by fixing $x_{k-1}=1$,
- in the **low branch**, the $x_k$-structure is preserved exactly as in $f$.

## Final statement

### (a) Truth tables

For every fixed assignment to the other variables, each $(x_{k-1},x_k)$-block transforms as

$$
(f(0,0),f(0,1),f(1,0),f(1,1))
\mapsto
(f(0,0),f(1,1),f(1,1),f(1,1)),
$$

because $g_k$ depends on $(x_{k-1},x_k)$ only through $x_{k-1}\lor x_k$.

### (b) BDDs

Let $h$ be $f$ with its $k$-th argument isolated. Then:

- the cofactor at $x_{k-1}=0$ becomes $h(x_k,\dots)$,
- the cofactor at $x_{k-1}=1$ becomes $h(1,\dots)$,

so the ROBDD of $g_k$ is obtained by replacing every $x_{k-1}$-node in $B(f)$ by a decision node whose two children are the ROBDDs of these two cofactors, followed by standard reduction. The key effect is that the high cofactor eliminates all dependence on $x_k$, while the low cofactor preserves it unchanged.
