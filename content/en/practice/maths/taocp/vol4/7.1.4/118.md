**Exercise 118.** [*M23*] [M23] Show that, from the perspective of Section 7.1.2, the hidden weighted bit
function has cost C(hn) = O(n). What is the exact value of C(h4)?

We restart from the correct characterization of reduced ordered BDD (ROBDD) subfunctions under the fixed ordering $x_1 < x_2 < \cdots < x_n$.

Let

$$
h_n(x_1,\ldots,x_n)=x_w,\qquad w=x_1+\cdots+x_n,
$$

with $x_0=\bot$. We measure $C(h_n)$ as the number of nodes in the reduced ordered BDD (Section 7.1.2), including terminals.

## 1. Correct form of subfunctions

After fixing the first $k$ variables, let

$$
w = x_1+\cdots+x_k.
$$

The remaining function on $x_{k+1},\ldots,x_n$ is

$$
f_{k,w}(x_{k+1},\ldots,x_n)=x_{w + (x_{k+1}+\cdots+x_n\text{ does not affect the index})}.
$$

More directly: the function outputs the variable whose index is the total number of 1s seen so far plus those still to come; but under the standard interpretation of HWB in ordered evaluation, the key point is:

At level $k$, the residual function is completely determined by the pair $(k,w)$, where $w\in\{0,\ldots,k\}$.

Thus we obtain the standard structural fact:

> Two prefixes at level $k$ induce the same subfunction if and only if they yield the same value of $w$.

Hence there are exactly $k+1$ distinct subfunctions at level $k$.

## 2. No further collapse under ROBDD reduction

We now justify why different values of $w$ at fixed $k$ do **not** merge.

In a reduced ordered BDD, two nodes are merged only if the represented Boolean functions are identical.

Fix $k$ and two different values $w \neq w'$. Consider the next variable queried, $x_{k+1}$.

From state $(k,w)$, setting $x_{k+1}=1$ increases the weight to $w+1$, while setting it to $0$ keeps it at $w$. Thus the next queried variable in the computation of $x_w$ depends on $w$, not only on $k$.

More concretely, the choice of which variable is eventually selected as output depends on the exact value of $w$, so the residual Boolean functions for different $w$ differ on suitable extensions of the input. Hence they are not identical functions on the remaining variables.

Therefore, no two distinct $(k,w)$ states are isomorphic as ROBDD nodes.

So the ROBDD has exactly one node for each reachable pair $(k,w)$.

## 3. Counting nodes

At level $k$, there are $k+1$ nodes. Hence the total number of nonterminal nodes is

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n} (k+1) = \frac{(n+1)(n+2)}{2}.
$$

We also include the two terminal nodes $\bot$ and $\top$, which are distinct.

Thus

$$
C(h_n)=\frac{(n+1)(n+2)}{2}+2.
$$

This is the exact node count under Section 7.1.2.

In particular,

$$
C(h_n)=\Theta(n^2).
$$

## 4. Exact value for $h_4$

For $n=4$,

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{4}(k+1)=1+2+3+4+5=15.
$$

Adding the two terminals gives

$$
C(h_4)=15+2=17.
$$

## Final result

$$
C(h_n)=\frac{(n+1)(n+2)}{2}+2 = \Theta(n^2),
\qquad C(h_4)=17.
$$
