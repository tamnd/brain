**Exercise 104.** &#9654; [*21*] [21] Devise an efficient way to decide whether f ≤g or f ≥g or f ∥g, where
f ∥g means that f and g are incomparable, given the BDDs for f and g.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m53s  

---

We restart from a correct and standard formulation based on product BDD traversal for satisfiability of conjunctions.

## Correct solution

Let $f,g$ be Boolean functions given by reduced ordered BDDs over the same variable order $x_1<\cdots<x_n$. Let $T_f, T_g$ denote their root nodes. We write each node $u$ with variable index $V(u)$, low child $LO(u)$, high child $HI(u)$, and terminals $\bot,\top$ with $V(\bot)=V(\top)=+\infty$.

We want to decide whether

$$
f \le g,\quad f \ge g,\quad \text{or } f \parallel g.
$$

## Reduction to witness existence

Define two witness conditions:

$$
A \equiv \exists x\,(f(x)=1 \land g(x)=0),
\quad
B \equiv \exists x\,(f(x)=0 \land g(x)=1).
$$

Then:

- $f \le g \iff A$ is false,
- $f \ge g \iff B$ is false,
- $f=g \iff \neg A \land \neg B$,
- $f \parallel g \iff A \land B$.

Thus it suffices to test satisfiability of

$$
f \land \neg g,\quad \neg f \land g.
$$

We compute these using a correct product traversal over BDD nodes without explicitly building complements.

## Key construction: product reachability with labeled values

We define a function that checks satisfiability of a pair of BDD nodes under an assignment of values to the represented subfunctions.

Define a predicate:

$$
\mathrm{SAT}(u,v,a,b),
$$

where:

- $u$ is a node in $f$,
- $v$ is a node in $g$,
- $a,b \in \{0,1\}$,

and the intended meaning is:

$$
\mathrm{SAT}(u,v,a,b)=\text{true}
$$

iff there exists an assignment to variables below $u,v$ such that:

$$
f_u(x)=a,\quad g_v(x)=b,
$$

where $f_u,g_v$ are the subfunctions represented by nodes $u,v$.

We will only use:

- $(a,b)=(1,0)$ for $f \land \neg g$,
- $(a,b)=(0,1)$ for $\neg f \land g$.

## Base cases

At terminals:

### 1. Both terminals

If $u,v \in \{\bot,\top\}$, then satisfiability reduces to consistency of constants.

- $\top \equiv 1$, $\bot \equiv 0$.

Thus:

$$
\mathrm{SAT}(u,v,a,b)=
\begin{cases}
\text{true} & \text{if } (u=a) \land (v=b),\\
\text{false} & \text{otherwise}.
\end{cases}
$$

More explicitly:

- if $u=\top$, then $a$ must be $1$,
- if $u=\bot$, then $a$ must be $0$,

and similarly for $v,b$.

## Recursive step (correct product Shannon decomposition)

Let $i=V(u)$, $j=V(v)$, with $V(\bot)=V(\top)=+\infty$.

We branch on the smallest indexed variable.

### Case 1: $i<j$

Then $u$ depends on $x_i$ but $v$ does not. We expand $u$:

$$
\mathrm{SAT}(u,v,a,b)
=
\mathrm{SAT}(LO(u),v,a,b)
\;\lor\;
\mathrm{SAT}(HI(u),v,a,b).
$$

### Case 2: $j<i$

Symmetrically:

$$
\mathrm{SAT}(u,v,a,b)
=
\mathrm{SAT}(u,LO(v),a,b)
\;\lor\;
\mathrm{SAT}(u,HI(v),a,b).
$$

### Case 3: $i=j$

This is the critical corrected case.

Both nodes test the same variable $x_i$. Any assignment must choose exactly one of:

$$
x_i=0 \quad \text{or} \quad x_i=1.
$$

Hence we must synchronize branches:

- if $x_i=0$, move to $(LO(u),LO(v))$,
- if $x_i=1$, move to $(HI(u),HI(v))$.

Thus:

$$
\mathrm{SAT}(u,v,a,b)
=
\mathrm{SAT}(LO(u),LO(v),a,b)
\;\lor\;
\mathrm{SAT}(HI(u),HI(v),a,b).
$$

This is correct because in any valuation, both BDDs evaluate the same variable at this level, so mixed pairs like $(LO(u),HI(v))$ correspond to contradictory assignments and cannot represent a consistent valuation.

## Memoization and complexity

The state space is:

$$
(u,v,a,b) \in |f|\cdot|g|\cdot 4,
$$

so $O(|f||g|)$ states.

Each state performs $O(1)$ work plus two recursive calls.

Thus:

$$
T = O(|f||g|), \quad S = O(|f||g|).
$$

## Computing the ordering relation

We now test:

### Forward witness

$$
A \equiv \mathrm{SAT}(T_f,T_g,1,0),
$$

which checks $f=1, g=0$, i.e. $f \land \neg g$.

### Reverse witness

$$
B \equiv \mathrm{SAT}(T_f,T_g,0,1),
$$

which checks $f=0, g=1$, i.e. $\neg f \land g$.

## Final classification

- If $\neg A \land \neg B$, then $f=g$.
- If $\neg A \land B$, then $f \le g$.
- If $A \land \neg B$, then $f \ge g$.
- If $A \land B$, then $f \parallel g$.

## Why this fixes the previous errors

1. The complement issue is eliminated: no $\sigma$ flag is used. Negation is encoded explicitly in the pair $(a,b)$.
2. The recursion is a standard correct BDD product construction for reachability in the synchronous product graph.
3. The $i=j$ case is now correctly constrained to consistent variable assignments, removing invalid cross-branching.
4. The invariant is explicit: $\mathrm{SAT}(u,v,a,b)$ corresponds exactly to existence of assignments realizing the sub-BDD constraints.

This yields a correct and efficient $O(|f||g|)$ decision procedure for $f \le g$, $f \ge g$, or incomparability $f \parallel g$.
