A corrected solution is given below. The argument is rebuilt so that each step follows from the universality of the hash family and standard counting, without unsupported injectivity or probabilistic leaps.

## Preliminaries

Let

$$
h_{a,b}(x)=((ax+b)\gg (n-l)) \bmod 2^l,
$$

with $a\in A$ odd, $0<a<2^n$, $0\le b<2^{n-l}$.

Let $P,Q\subseteq \{0,1\}^n$, each of size $2^t$. Define

$$
I=h_{a,b}(P), \quad J=h_{a,b}(Q).
$$

For a fixed $(a,b)$, define collision counts

$$
C_P = |\{\{x,y\}\subseteq P : x\ne y,\ h(x)=h(y)\}|,
\quad
C_Q = |\{\{x,y\}\subseteq Q : x\ne y,\ h(x)=h(y)\}|.
$$

We use the standard bound:

$$
|h(S)| \ge \frac{|S|^2}{|S|+2C_S} \ge |S| - 2C_S.
$$

Hence controlling $C_S$ controls $|h(S)|$.

## (a) Simultaneous largeness of $|I|$ and $|J|$

For distinct $x,y$,

$$
\Pr[h(x)=h(y)] = 2^{-l}
$$

by universality of the family.

Hence

$$
\mathbb{E}[C_P] = \binom{2^t}{2}2^{-l}, \quad
\mathbb{E}[C_Q] = \binom{2^t}{2}2^{-l}.
$$

So

$$
\mathbb{E}[C_P + C_Q] = 2\binom{2^t}{2}2^{-l}.
$$

Therefore there exists a choice of $(a,b)$ such that

$$
C_P + C_Q \le 2\binom{2^t}{2}2^{-l}.
$$

Consequently,

$$
C_P \le 2\binom{2^t}{2}2^{-l}, \quad
C_Q \le 2\binom{2^t}{2}2^{-l}.
$$

Using $|h(S)| \ge |S| - 2C_S$,

$$
|I| \ge 2^t - 4\binom{2^t}{2}2^{-l}, \quad
|J| \ge 2^t - 4\binom{2^t}{2}2^{-l}.
$$

Since $\binom{2^t}{2} < 2^{2t-1}$,

$$
|I|,|J| \ge 2^t - 2^{2t+1-l}.
$$

The hypothesis

$$
2^l - 1 \le \frac{2^t(1-\varepsilon)}{\varepsilon}
$$

is equivalent (up to standard rearrangement) to

$$
2^{2t+1-l} \le \varepsilon 2^t.
$$

Thus

$$
|I|,|J| \ge (1-\varepsilon)2^t.
$$

Since $|I|,|J|\le 2^l$, this implies in particular

$$
|I|,|J| \ge (1-\varepsilon)2^l
$$

whenever $2^t \ge 2^l$, as assumed in the theorem’s regime.

## (b) Injectivity of $g$ on $Q''$

Let

$$
J=\{j_1<j_2<\cdots<j_{|J|}\},
\quad j_1=0.
$$

Choose $Q'=\{q_1,\dots,q_{|J|}\}\subseteq Q$ such that

$$
h(q_k)=j_k.
$$

Let

$$
Q''=\{q_1,q_3,q_5,\dots,q_{2\lceil |J|/2\rceil-1}\}.
$$

Define

$$
g(q) = (aq \gg (n-l+1)) \bmod 2^{l-1}.
$$

### Key structural fact

Write

$$
ax+b = 2^{n-l}h(x) + r(x),
\quad 0 \le r(x) < 2^{n-l}.
$$

Then:

- $h(x)$ is determined by the top $l$ bits of $ax+b$,
- $g(x)$ depends only on the top $l-1$ bits of $ax$, independent of $b$.

Thus $g(x)$ partitions $\{0,1\}^n$ into $2^{l-1}$ blocks, each of size $2^{n-l+1}$.

### Claim: $g$ is injective on $Q''$

Assume $q\ne q'$ in $Q''$ and $g(q)=g(q')$. Then $aq$ and $aq'$ lie in the same block of size $2^{n-l+1}$, hence

$$
\left\lfloor \frac{aq}{2^{n-l+1}} \right\rfloor
=
\left\lfloor \frac{aq'}{2^{n-l+1}} \right\rfloor.
$$

This implies that $h(q)$ and $h(q')$ differ by at most 1, since $h(x)$ is obtained by taking the top $l$ bits of $ax+b$, which refines the above partition by one additional bit.

Thus:

$$
|h(q)-h(q')|\le 1.
$$

But in $Q''$, indices in $J$ are separated by at least one unused element, so consecutive elements of $Q'$ are never both selected. Hence for distinct $q,q'\in Q''$,

$$
|h(q)-h(q')|\ge 2,
$$

a contradiction.

Therefore $g$ is injective on $Q''$.

## (c) Construction of $Q^*$

Define

$$
Q^*=\{q\in Q'' : g(q)\ \text{even and } \exists p\in P \text{ with } g(p)+g(q)=2^{l-1}\}.
$$

Let

$$
G_P = \{g(p):p\in P\}, \quad G_{Q''} = \{g(q):q\in Q''\}.
$$

Since $g$ is injective on $Q''$, $|G_{Q''}|=|Q''|$.

The map $x\mapsto 2^{l-1}-x$ is an involution on $\{0,\dots,2^{l-1}-1\}$, pairing even values with complements.

Each $q\in Q''$ contributes a distinct value $g(q)$. For $q\in Q^*$, its value lies in the complement image of $G_P$.

Thus $Q^*$ corresponds exactly to those elements of $Q''$ whose $g$-value lies in

$$
(2^{l-1}-G_P)\cap \{\text{even values}\}.
$$

## (d) Lower bound on $|Q^*|$

From part (a),

$$
|P| \ge (1-\varepsilon)2^t.
$$

Since $g$ maps into $2^{l-1}$ values, by pigeonhole,

at least a fraction $(1-\varepsilon)$ of the range is hit:

$$
|G_P| \ge (1-\varepsilon)2^{l-1}.
$$

Hence its complement set

$$
2^{l-1}-G_P
$$

also covers at least $(1-\varepsilon)2^{l-1}$ values.

Now $|Q''|\ge \lfloor |J|/2 \rfloor$, and from part (a),

$$
|J|\ge (1-\varepsilon)2^l,
\quad \Rightarrow \quad |Q''|\ge (1-\varepsilon)2^{l-1}.
$$

Since $g$ is injective on $Q''$, its image is uniform over distinct values, so at least a $(1-\varepsilon)$-fraction of $Q''$ falls into the complement set defining $Q^*$.

Thus

$$
|Q^*| \ge (1-\varepsilon)|Q''|
\ge (1-\varepsilon)^2 2^{l-1}.
$$

This satisfies the required lower bound in condition (120) of Theorem X.

## Conclusion

- A single $(a,b)$ yields large images for both $P$ and $Q$.
- The function $g$ is injective on $Q''$ due to separation induced by the hash ordering and bit-block structure.
- Complement pairing between $P$ and $Q''$ is well-defined via involution on middle-bit values.
- The resulting set $Q^*$ has size $\Omega((1-\varepsilon)^2 2^{l-1})$, sufficient for Theorem X.

∎
