## Solution

Let the generic recurrence of Exercise 31 be

$$
v=\bar x_j\bullet v_{\mathrm{LO}}\circ x_j\bullet v_{\mathrm{HI}},
$$

where the $0$-sink has value $\bot$, the $1$-sink has value $\top$, and the meanings of

$$
\circ,\qquad
\bullet,\qquad
\bot,\qquad
\top,\qquad
\bar x_j,\qquad
x_j
$$

are to be chosen appropriately. For each of Exercises 25, 26, 29, and 30, we specify a carrier together with interpretations of these six symbols so that the generic recurrence becomes the corresponding specialized algorithm.

### Exercise 25

Take the carrier to be the Boolean algebra

$$
\{\mathrm{FALSE},\mathrm{TRUE}\}.
$$

Interpret

$$
\circ=\lor,\qquad
\bullet=\land,
$$

with

$$
\bot=\mathrm{FALSE},\qquad
\top=\mathrm{TRUE}.
$$

For a fixed assignment $c_1,\ldots,c_n$, let

$$
\bar x_j=\neg c_j,\qquad
x_j=c_j.
$$

Then each branch node evaluates

$$
(\neg c_j\land v_{\mathrm{LO}})
\lor
(c_j\land v_{\mathrm{HI}}).
$$

If $c_j=0$, this equals $v_{\mathrm{LO}}$; if $c_j=1$, it equals $v_{\mathrm{HI}}$. Thus the generic recurrence is exactly the evaluation algorithm of Exercise 25.

### Exercise 26

Take the carrier to be the nonnegative integers.

Interpret

$$
\circ=+,\qquad
\bullet=\times,
$$

with

$$
\bot=0,\qquad
\top=1.
$$

Suppose the LO successor of a node labeled $x_j$ is labeled $x_\ell$, where $\ell=n+1$ if the successor is a sink. Likewise suppose the HI successor is labeled $x_m$, where $m=n+1$ for a sink.

Define

$$
\bar x_j=2^{\,\ell-j-1},
\qquad
x_j=2^{\,m-j-1}.
$$

Since every BDD edge goes from level $j$ to a strictly larger level, we have $\ell,m\ge j+1$, so the exponents are nonnegative. The factors

$$
2^{\,\ell-j-1},
\qquad
2^{\,m-j-1}
$$

are exactly the skipped-variable multiplicities used in Algorithm C of Exercise 26.

The recurrence therefore becomes

$$
v
=
2^{\,\ell-j-1}v_{\mathrm{LO}}
+
2^{\,m-j-1}v_{\mathrm{HI}},
$$

which is precisely the recurrence of Exercise 26.

### Exercise 29

Take the carrier to be ordered pairs

$$
(f,f'),
$$

where $f$ is the reliability polynomial and $f'$ is its derivative.

Interpret addition componentwise:

$$
(a,a')\circ(b,b')
=
(a+b,\;a'+b').
$$

Interpret multiplication by the product rule:

$$
(c,c')\bullet(d,d')
=
(cd,\;cd'+c'd).
$$

This is multiplication of dual numbers.

Let

$$
\bot=(0,0),\qquad
\top=(1,0).
$$

Finally interpret the edge labels by

$$
\bar x_j=(1-p_j,-1),
\qquad
x_j=(p_j,1).
$$

Then

$$
\bar x_j\bullet(f,f')
=
\bigl((1-p_j)f,\;
(1-p_j)f'-f\bigr),
$$

because

$$
((1-p_j),-1)\bullet(f,f')
=
\left((1-p_j)f,\;
(1-p_j)f'+(-1)f\right).
$$

Similarly,

$$
x_j\bullet(f,f')
=
\bigl(p_jf,\;
p_jf'+f\bigr),
$$

because

$$
(p_j,1)\bullet(f,f')
=
\left(p_jf,\;
p_jf'+f\right).
$$

These are exactly the two updates used in Exercise 29. Since $\circ$ adds the contributions from the two outgoing edges, the generic recurrence specializes to the simultaneous computation of the reliability polynomial and its derivative.

### Exercise 30

To obtain the algorithm of Exercise 30, the assignment must be propagated as part of the algebra itself. It cannot be added afterward as an external procedural step.

Take the carrier to be pairs

$$
(w,A),
$$

where $w$ is a probability and $A$ is the partial assignment accumulated from the current node to a terminal.

Represent the edge labels as pairs consisting of the edge probability together with the corresponding bit:

$$
\bar x_j=(1-p_j,0),
\qquad
x_j=(p_j,1).
$$

Interpret $\bullet$ by

$$
(\alpha,b)\bullet(w,A)
=
(\alpha w,\,(b)\mathbin{\|}A),
$$

where $(b)\mathbin{\|}A$ denotes the sequence obtained by prefixing the bit $b$ to the assignment $A$.

Interpret $\circ$ by selecting the larger probability:

$$
(w_1,A_1)\circ(w_2,A_2)
=
\begin{cases}
(w_1,A_1),&w_1\ge w_2,\\
(w_2,A_2),&w_2>w_1.
\end{cases}
$$

Finally let

$$
\bot=(0,\varepsilon),\qquad
\top=(1,\varepsilon),
$$

where $\varepsilon$ is the empty assignment.

At a node labeled $x_j$, the recurrence becomes

$$
(\bar x_j\bullet v_{\mathrm{LO}})
\circ
(x_j\bullet v_{\mathrm{HI}}).
$$

The operation $\bullet$ both multiplies the probability by the appropriate edge weight and prefixes the correct decision bit, $0$ on the LO edge and $1$ on the HI edge. The operation $\circ$ then chooses the pair having the larger probability. Consequently, the value propagated upward is exactly the maximum attainable probability together with an assignment that attains it, with all assignment bookkeeping performed entirely by the interpretations of the six abstract symbols.

Thus, in each of Exercises 25, 26, 29, and 30, the specialized algorithm is obtained solely by choosing appropriate interpretations of

$$
\circ,\qquad
\bullet,\qquad
\bot,\qquad
\top,\qquad
\bar x_j,\qquad
x_j.
$$
