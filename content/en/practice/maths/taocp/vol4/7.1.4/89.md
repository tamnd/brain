**Exercise 89.** [*15*] [15] Are the following quantified formulas true or false? (a) ∃x1∃x2f = ∃x2∃x1f.
(b) ∀x1∀x2f = ∀x2∀x1f. (c) ∀x1∃x2f ≤∃x2∀x1f. (d) ∀x1∃x2f ≥∃x2∀x1f.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 1m07s

## Solution

Let $f(x_1,x_2)$ be a Boolean function. Each quantified expression reduces to a constant in ${0,1}$ since both variables are bound.

For existential quantification,

$$
\exists x_1 \exists x_2 f = \max_{x_1,x_2} f(x_1,x_2),
\qquad
\exists x_2 \exists x_1 f = \max_{x_2,x_1} f(x_1,x_2).
$$

Both expressions take the maximum over the same set of four values of $f$, hence they are equal. This establishes (a).

For universal quantification,

$$
\forall x_1 \forall x_2 f = \min_{x_1,x_2} f(x_1,x_2),
\qquad
\forall x_2 \forall x_1 f = \min_{x_2,x_1} f(x_1,x_2).
$$

Both expressions take the minimum over the same set of values, hence they are equal. This establishes (b).

For (c), interpret $g \le h$ as implication between Boolean constants, equivalently $g \Rightarrow h$. Let

$$
g = \forall x_1 \exists x_2 f,
\qquad
h = \exists x_2 \forall x_1 f.
$$

Take the matrix

$$
f(0,0)=1,\quad f(0,1)=0,\quad f(1,0)=0,\quad f(1,1)=1.
$$

For $x_1=0$ there exists $x_2$ with $f=1$, and for $x_1=1$ there exists $x_2$ with $f=1$, hence $g=1$. For every $x_2$, neither column is all $1$, so no $x_2$ satisfies $\forall x_1 f$, hence $h=0$. Therefore $g \le h$ fails in general, so (c) is false.

For (d), consider implication $h \Rightarrow g$. If $\exists x_2 \forall x_1 f$ holds, then there exists a fixed $x_2^\ast$ such that $f(x_1,x_2^\ast)=1$ for both $x_1=0$ and $x_1=1$. For each $x_1$, choosing $x_2=x_2^\ast$ yields $f(x_1,x_2)=1$, hence $\forall x_1 \exists x_2 f$ holds. Thus $h \Rightarrow g$ holds for all $f$, so $g \ge h$ is true.

This completes the proof. ∎
