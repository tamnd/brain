**Exercise 4.** [*21*] [21] Suppose three fields
V
LO
HI
have been packed into a 64-bit word x,
where V occupies 8 bits and the other two fields occupy 28 bits each. Show that five
bitwise instructions will transform x →
x′, where x′ is equal to x except that a LO or
HI value of 0 is changed to 1 and vice versa. (Repeating this operation on every branch
node x of a BDD for f will produce the BDD for the complementary function, ¯f.)

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m42s  

---

Let the 64-bit word $x$ be partitioned as

$$
x = (V, LO, HI),
$$

where the bit layout is assumed to be

$$
x = V \,\|\, LO \,\|\, HI,
$$

with $V$ occupying bits $63$–$56$, $LO$ occupying bits $55$–$28$, and $HI$ occupying bits $27$–$0$.

Thus the two low fields form a single contiguous block of $56$ bits.

The required transformation is:

- leave $V$ unchanged,
- complement every bit in $LO$ and $HI$.

This is achieved by XOR with a mask that has:

- 0 in bits $63$–$56$,
- 1 in bits $55$–$0$.

### Construction of the mask

Let $T$ be the 64-bit word consisting of all 1s:

$$
T = (1\ldots 1)_2.
$$

Then shifting right by 8 positions yields

$$
M = T \gg 8,
$$

so that:

- bits $63$–$56$ become 0,
- bits $55$–$0$ remain 1.

Hence $M$ is exactly the mask for the union of the $LO$ and $HI$ fields.

### Correctness of the transformation

Applying XOR with $M$,

$$
x' = x \oplus M,
$$

has the following effects:

- On the $V$ field: $M$ has 0s, so bits of $V$ are unchanged.
- On the $LO$ and $HI$ fields: $M$ has 1s, so each bit is complemented.

Therefore $x'$ agrees with $x$ except that every bit in $LO$ and $HI$ is flipped, as required.

### Instruction sequence

A valid sequence of five bitwise instructions (indeed fewer suffice) is:

```
T   ← 0
T   ← NOT T
M   ← T >> 8
x   ← x XOR M
```

This uses only bitwise operations (complement, shift, XOR) and constructs the required mask without isolating subfields separately.

### Conclusion

The key correction is that $LO$ and $HI$ need not be constructed separately. Since they form a contiguous 56-bit region, a single shift of an all-ones word produces the correct mask directly. The transformation is therefore implemented correctly with XOR against $T \gg 8$. ∎
