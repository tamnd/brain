**Exercise 193.** [*M21*] [M21] Continuing exercise 192, what is SZ
k (x1, . . . , xn) when 0 ≤k ≤n?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m31s

Let $S_k(x_1,\ldots,x_n)$ be the symmetric Boolean function

$$
S_k(x_1,\ldots,x_n)=1 \;\Longleftrightarrow\; x_1+\cdots+x_n = k.
$$

We write an input $x=(x_1,\ldots,x_n)$ as a subset $X \subseteq [n]$, where $i \in X \iff x_i=1$, so $|X|$ is the Hamming weight.

### Definition of the $Z$-transform (from Exercise 192)

Let $f:\{0,1\}^n \to \{0,1\}$. The $Z$-transform $f^Z$ is defined on truth tables by the extension rule:

$$
f^Z(X)=1 \;\Longleftrightarrow\; \exists Y \supseteq X \text{ such that } f(Y)=1.
$$

Equivalently, $f^Z$ is the upward closure of the set of satisfying assignments of $f$ with respect to the subset order.

### Application to $S_k$

We compute $S_k^Z(X)$.

By definition,

$$
S_k^Z(X)=1 \;\Longleftrightarrow\; \exists Y \supseteq X \text{ with } |Y|=k.
$$

So the question reduces to when a set $X$ can be extended to a set of size exactly $k$.

### Necessary and sufficient condition

Let $|X|=i$.

- (**Necessity**) If there exists $Y \supseteq X$ with $|Y|=k$, then $i \le k$, since $|Y| \ge |X|$.
- (**Sufficiency**) If $i \le k$, then we can choose $k-i$ elements from $[n]\setminus X$ (possible whenever $k \le n$) and form $Y \supseteq X$ with $|Y|=k$.

Thus,

$$
\exists Y \supseteq X \text{ with } |Y|=k \;\Longleftrightarrow\; |X| \le k.
$$

### Conclusion

Therefore,

$$
S_k^Z(x_1,\ldots,x_n)=1 \;\Longleftrightarrow\; x_1+\cdots+x_n \le k.
$$

Equivalently, $S_k^Z$ is the symmetric threshold function “at most $k$”.

$$
\boxed{S_k^Z(x_1,\ldots,x_n)=\mathbf{1}[x_1+\cdots+x_n \le k].}
$$
