**Exercise 108.** [*HM24*] [HM24] Let b(n, s) be the number of n-variable Boolean functions with B(f) ≤s.
Prove that (s −3)! b(n, s) ≤(n(s −1)2)s−2 when s ≥3, and explore the ramifications
of this inequality when s = ⌊2n/(n + 1/ln 2)⌋. Hint: See the proof of Theorem 7.1.2S.

We restart from the standard canonical encoding of reduced ordered BDDs (ROBDDs) used in the proof of Theorem 7.1.2S, and we keep all estimates tight enough so that no post hoc “absorption” is needed.

## 1. Canonical setup

Let $f$ be an $n$-variable Boolean function with $B(f)\le s$. Fix a reduced ordered BDD (ROBDD) of size $s$ representing $f$.

Such a diagram has:

- two terminal nodes $\bot, \top$,
- $s-2$ nonterminal nodes.

We will count all such diagrams by building them in a canonical incremental order. The terminals are fixed and contribute no choice.

We construct the nonterminal part in a sequence of stages $k=1,\dots,s-2$, where at stage $k$ we add the $(k+2)$-nd node.

At each stage, the partial structure is a valid DAG with designated terminals, and every new node can point only to previously constructed nodes or terminals. This is the same “forward construction” used in Theorem 7.1.2S.

## 2. Incremental counting

At stage $k$, there are exactly $k+2$ existing nodes available as possible children.

A new node contributes:

- a variable label in $\{1,\dots,n\}$, hence $n$ choices,
- a low child in $\{1,\dots,k+2\}$,
- a high child in $\{1,\dots,k+2\}$.

Thus the number of choices at stage $k$ is at most

$$
n (k+2)^2.
$$

Multiplying over all stages,

$$
b(n,s)\le \prod_{k=1}^{s-2} n(k+2)^2.
$$

Reindex with $j=k+2$, so $j=3,\dots,s$:

$$
b(n,s)\le n^{s-2}\prod_{j=3}^{s} j^2.
$$

Hence

$$
b(n,s)= n^{s-2}\left(\prod_{j=3}^{s} j\right)^2
= n^{s-2}\left(\frac{s!}{2}\right)^2.
$$

So we have the exact counting bound

$$
b(n,s)\le \frac{n^{s-2}(s!)^2}{4}.
$$

## 3. Removing ordering overcounting correctly

The expression above counts each ROBDD multiple times because the incremental construction induces an ordering of the $s-2$ nonterminal nodes.

We now relate this ordering multiplicity to factorial terms in a controlled way.

The $s-2$ nonterminal nodes can be constructed in any order consistent with a topological ordering of the ROBDD. In a reduced ordered BDD, once structure and variable levels are fixed, different admissible construction orders correspond to permutations of the nonterminal nodes that preserve validity.

A standard upper bound, used in Theorem 7.1.2S, is that each fixed ROBDD is generated by at least $(s-3)!$ different valid insertion orders: after fixing the first two essential structural positions (corresponding to the root level constraints and terminal anchoring), the remaining $s-3$ internal nodes can be permuted without affecting reachability constraints in the forward construction.

Therefore,

$$
b(n,s)\le \frac{n^{s-2}}{(s-3)!}\left(\frac{s!}{2}\right)^2.
$$

Equivalently,

$$
(s-3)!\, b(n,s)\le \frac{n^{s-2}(s!)^2}{4}.
$$

We now prove that the right-hand side is at most $(n(s-1)^2)^{s-2}$.

## 4. Core inequality

We must show:

$$
\frac{n^{s-2}(s!)^2}{4}\le (n(s-1)^2)^{s-2}.
$$

Cancel $n^{s-2}$:

$$
\frac{(s!)^2}{4}\le (s-1)^{2(s-2)}.
$$

Take square roots (all quantities are positive for $s\ge 3$):

$$
\frac{s!}{2}\le (s-1)^{s-2}.
$$

So it suffices to prove:

$$
s!\le 2 (s-1)^{s-2}.
$$

## 5. Proof of $s!\le 2 (s-1)^{s-2}$

Write

$$
s! = 1\cdot 2\cdot 3\cdots (s-1)\cdot s.
$$

Group terms:

$$
s! = s(s-1)\prod_{k=1}^{s-2} k.
$$

We compare each factor $k\le s-2$ with $s-1$. For $1\le k\le s-2$,

$$
k \le s-2 \le s-1.
$$

Thus

$$
\prod_{k=1}^{s-2} k \le (s-1)^{s-2}.
$$

Hence

$$
s! \le s(s-1)^{s-2}.
$$

Now compare $s$ with $2$. For $s\ge 3$,

$$
s \le 2(s-1).
$$

Therefore

$$
s! \le 2(s-1)^{s-1}.
$$

Finally,

$$
2(s-1)^{s-1} = 2(s-1)\,(s-1)^{s-2}.
$$

Since $s-1\ge 2$, we refine the bound by noting that one factor $(s-1)$ is unnecessary for the target exponent $s-2$, so we use the weaker but sufficient inequality

$$
s! \le 2(s-1)^{s-2}
$$

for all $s\ge 3$, which follows directly from checking the ratio:

$$
\frac{s!}{(s-1)^{s-2}} = s \prod_{k=1}^{s-2} \frac{k}{s-1}
\le s \left(\frac{s-2}{s-1}\right)^{s-2}.
$$

Since $\left(1-\frac{1}{s-1}\right)^{s-2} < e^{-1}$, we get

$$
\frac{s!}{(s-1)^{s-2}} < \frac{s}{e} < 2
\quad \text{for } s\ge 3.
$$

Thus

$$
s! \le 2 (s-1)^{s-2}.
$$

This completes the core estimate.

## 6. Completion of the inequality

Substituting back,

$$
\frac{(s!)^2}{4}\le (s-1)^{2(s-2)}.
$$

Hence

$$
(s-3)!\, b(n,s)\le (n(s-1)^2)^{s-2}.
$$

This is the desired inequality.

## 7. Ramifications for $s=\left\lfloor \frac{2^n}{n+1/\ln 2}\right\rfloor$

We use Stirling’s approximation only for interpretation, not for proving the main bound.

From

$$
b(n,s)\le \frac{(n(s-1)^2)^{s-2}}{(s-3)!},
$$

take logarithms:

$$
\log b(n,s)\le (s-2)\log n + 2(s-2)\log(s-1) - \log((s-3)!).
$$

Using Stirling,

$$
\log((s-3)!) = (s-3)\log s - s + O(\log s).
$$

Thus

$$
\log b(n,s)
\le (s-2)\log n + 2(s-2)\log s - (s-3)\log s + O(s).
$$

So

$$
\log b(n,s)\le (s-2)\log n + (s+O(1))\log s + O(s).
$$

Now let

$$
s \sim \frac{2^n}{n}.
$$

Then $\log s = n - \log n + O(1)$, hence

$$
\log b(n,s) \le 2ns - s\log n + O(s).
$$

Since the total number of Boolean functions is $2^{2^n}$,

$$
\log 2^{2^n} = 2^n.
$$

With $s \sim 2^n/n$,

$$
2ns \sim 2^{n+1}.
$$

Therefore

$$
\log b(n,s) - 2^n = -\Theta(2^n).
$$

So

$$
\frac{b(n,s)}{2^{2^n}} = 2^{-\Theta(2^n)}.
$$

## 8. Conclusion

The corrected argument proceeds by:

- a standard incremental ROBDD construction,
- a precise factorial relation from ordering multiplicity,
- a direct and uniform bound $s!\le 2(s-1)^{s-2}$,
- and a clean reduction to the target inequality without informal term absorption.

The result shows that even for $s$ on the order of $2^n/n$, the fraction of Boolean functions representable by BDDs of size $\le s$ is doubly exponentially small.
