## Solution

Let a pair of five-letter words be fixed as in the word pairing scheme of (23), and let $z$ be the associated 5-letter encoding used in step W2. For each $0 \le j < 5$, the mask

$m_j = z \;\&\; (25^{j+1}-1)$

selects a subset of positions determined by a base-$25$ representation of letters, so that each position of the resulting word is taken from exactly one of the two words in the pair.

Each position admits two independent choices, namely the letter from the first word or the letter from the second word. The five positions are independent under the masking rule, so the construction produces exactly

$2^5 = 32$

distinct five-letter words from a single fixed pair.

In the original scheme for the full list (23), these $32$ words are all valid dictionary entries, so every generated combination corresponds to a word in the allowed set.

After restricting attention to only the 3000 most common five-letter words, the list (23) is modified by removing exactly the 13 words

$\text{ducky, duces, dunks, dinks, dinky, dices, dicey, dicky, dicks, picky, pinky, punky, pucks}.$

Each of these removed words corresponds to one specific selection of the five positions from the two words in the pair, because the masking construction assigns a unique binary choice vector $(\varepsilon_1,\dots,\varepsilon_5) \in {0,1}^5$ to each generated word, and different vectors produce different outputs. Hence each removed word eliminates exactly one previously valid outcome of the 32 generated words, and no additional collisions arise under the scheme.

The number of valid words remaining from a single pair equals the original $32$ outputs minus the $13$ removed valid words:

$32 - 13 = 19.$

Thus each pair continues to generate exactly

$\boxed{19}$

valid five-letter words under the restricted dictionary. ∎
