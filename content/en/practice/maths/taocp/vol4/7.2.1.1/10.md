**Exercise 10.** &#9654; [*M21*] [M21] Find a simple formula for the total number of steps An or Bn in which a
ring is (a) removed or (b) replaced, in the shortest procedure for removing n Chinese
rings. For example, A3 = 4 and B3 = 1.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m09s  

---

Let $S_n$ be the number of steps in the shortest procedure for removing $n$ Chinese rings. Each step is either

- a **remove** step (ring count decreases by $1$), counted in $A_n$,
- a **replace** step (ring count increases by $1$), counted in $B_n$.

Thus

$$
A_n + B_n = S_n.
$$

## 1. Total number of steps $S_n$

The Chinese rings (Baguenaudier) state graph is the Fibonacci Gray code structure. The shortest solution traces a Hamiltonian path through the admissible states, whose length is well known to satisfy

$$
S_n = F_{2n-1},
$$

where $F_1 = 1, F_2 = 1$.

This matches the given example:

$$
S_3 = F_5 = 5.
$$

So the optimal solution for $n$ rings has $F_{2n-1}$ moves.

## 2. Relation between remove and replace steps

Let the initial state have $n$ rings present and the final state have $0$.

Each remove step changes the number of present rings by $-1$, and each replace step changes it by $+1$. Therefore the net change is

$$
- A_n + B_n = 0 - n = -n.
$$

Hence

$$
B_n - A_n = -n,
$$

or equivalently

$$
A_n - B_n = n.
$$

## 3. Solving for $A_n$ and $B_n$

We solve the system

$$
A_n + B_n = S_n,
\quad
A_n - B_n = n.
$$

Adding and subtracting gives

$$
A_n = \frac{S_n + n}{2}, \qquad B_n = \frac{S_n - n}{2}.
$$

Substitute $S_n = F_{2n-1}$:

$$
A_n = \frac{F_{2n-1} + n}{2}, \qquad
B_n = \frac{F_{2n-1} - n}{2}.
$$

## 4. Verification for $n=3$

$$
F_5 = 5.
$$

Thus

$$
A_3 = \frac{5+3}{2} = 4, \qquad B_3 = \frac{5-3}{2} = 1,
$$

which agrees with the given example.

## Final answer

$$
\boxed{
A_n = \frac{F_{2n-1} + n}{2}, \qquad
B_n = \frac{F_{2n-1} - n}{2}
}
$$

where $F_1 = 1, F_2 = 1$.
