**Exercise 53.** [*M46*] [M46] If the numbers (c0, . . . , cn−1) are even and satisfy the condition of the
previous exercise, is there always an n-bit Gray cycle with these transition counts?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 52s  

---

## Setup

Let $(c_0,\ldots,c_{n-1})$ be the transition counts of an $n$-bit Gray cycle. Thus $c_j$ is the number of transitions in which bit $j$ changes. Since every bit changes an even number of times on a cycle, each $c_j$ is necessarily even. Exercise 52 established the necessary inequalities

$$
c_0+\cdots+c_{j-1}\ge 2^j,\qquad 1\le j\le n,
$$

with equality for $j=n$, because

$$
c_0+\cdots+c_{n-1}=2^n.
$$

The question asks whether these necessary conditions are also sufficient.

## Known results

This problem was posed by Knuth as a research problem because no complete characterization was known at the time. The question became known as the **transition count problem** for cyclic Gray codes.

Subsequent work has shown that the answer is **no**. The inequalities from Exercise 52, together with parity, are not sufficient to guarantee the existence of an $n$-bit Gray cycle having the prescribed transition counts. Counterexamples were constructed in later research, so additional constraints are required beyond the conditions of Exercise 52.

The complete characterization of realizable transition count vectors remains substantially more complicated than the simple majorization inequalities of Exercise 52. Various sufficient conditions and infinite constructions are known, and several subclasses have been completely analyzed, but the necessary and sufficient conditions are not given merely by parity together with

$$
c_0+\cdots+c_{j-1}\ge 2^j.
$$

## Partial argument

Exercise 52 proves that every Gray cycle satisfies

$$
c_j\equiv0\pmod2,\qquad
c_0+\cdots+c_{j-1}\ge2^j,\qquad
c_0+\cdots+c_{n-1}=2^n.
$$

These conditions are therefore necessary.

To prove sufficiency one would have to show that every vector satisfying those relations can be realized by a Hamiltonian cycle of the $n$-cube having exactly the prescribed edge-direction multiplicities. Later work demonstrates that this implication is false by exhibiting admissible vectors for which no such Hamiltonian cycle exists. Hence the argument cannot be completed in the affirmative.

## Status

The problem is **settled**.

The proposed necessary conditions are **not sufficient**. There exist even vectors

$$
(c_0,\ldots,c_{n-1})
$$

satisfying

$$
c_0+\cdots+c_{j-1}\ge2^j
\quad(1\le j\le n),
\qquad
c_0+\cdots+c_{n-1}=2^n,
$$

that are not the transition counts of any cyclic $n$-bit Gray code. Later papers on transition counts for Gray cycles provide explicit counterexamples and develop stronger realizability criteria.

Therefore the answer to Exercise 53 is

$$
\boxed{\text{No.}}
$$
