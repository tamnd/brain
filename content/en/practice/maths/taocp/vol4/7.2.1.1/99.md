## Solution

Let $f_n(k)$ denote the $(k+1)$st bit of the binary de Bruijn cycle of order $n$ produced by Algorithms $R$ and $D$ for $m=2$. The cycle is indexed modulo $2^n$, so $f_n(k)$ is defined for all integers $k$ with periodicity $2^n$.

The previous exercise establishes that the construction of $f_{2n}$ from $f_n$ is governed by a doubling structure in which the $2n$-cycle is obtained by interleaving two affine transforms of the $n$-cycle. Concretely, there exists $\delta_n \in {\pm 1}$ such that, after identifying indices modulo $2^n$,

$$
f_{2n}(2k) = f_n(k), \qquad
f_{2n}(2k+1) = 1 + f_n(k+\delta_n) \pmod 2.
$$

The function $k \mapsto 2k$ and $k \mapsto 2k+1$ partitions the $2n$ positions into even and odd indices, and the second branch differs from the first by both a shift and a bit complement.

The structure of Algorithm $S$ is based on recursively combining two instances of a smaller cycle via such a decomposition. If the composition $f \circ g$ degenerates to a trivially constant function, then the recursion step that would normally distinguish the two interleaved branches loses its discriminating information. In that situation, the only remaining consistent choice is to eliminate the dependence on the inner structure entirely, since both branches of the recursion produce identical output.

In Algorithm $S$, step $S1$ is the initialization that determines how the recursion proceeds when a split into two nontrivial substructures is possible. If $f \circ g$ is constant, then no distinction between the two recursive subcalls can be made, and both branches yield the same value for every argument. This implies that the intended decomposition into two complementary or shifted components collapses into a single uniform sequence.

In that case, step $S1$ must set the result of the composed construction equal to this constant value and terminate the recursive refinement. Since the codomain is binary in the present setting, the only possible constant outcomes are $0$ or $1$, and the construction in Algorithm $D$ and Algorithm $R$ implies that the initial segment of every cycle begins with $0$ values before the first occurrence of $1$ at position $j_n$. Thus the only consistent constant compatible with the base initialization of the de Bruijn construction is the value $0$.

Therefore, when $f \circ g$ is trivially constant, step $S1$ replaces the recursive decomposition by direct assignment of the constant output:

$$
S1:\ \text{set the output of } f \circ g \text{ to } 0 \text{ for all inputs and return}.
$$

This choice preserves consistency with the base case of the construction and avoids introducing an artificial split that would violate the collapse of the interleaving structure.

This completes the construction of the missing case in Algorithm $S$. ∎
