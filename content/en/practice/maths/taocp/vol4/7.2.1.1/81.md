## Setup

Let $f$ be a read-once Boolean function of $2^{2m+1}$ variables. Let $f^\pi$ denote $f$ under a permutation $\pi$ of its variables, and let $B(f^\pi)$ be the size of the reduced ordered binary decision diagram (BDD) of $f^\pi$ in the sense of Section 7.1.4, including both sink nodes.

Let $v_k$ denote the standard extremal read-once family from Section 7.1.4, where $B(v_k)$ is defined recursively by the bead decomposition corresponding to complete binary composition of variables under alternating $\land,\lor$ structure. The quantity $B(v_{2m+1})$ is the benchmark BDD size for the canonical worst-case read-once construction at height $2m+1$.

The problem asks whether, for every read-once $f$ on $2^{2m+1}$ variables,

$$
\min_\pi B(f^\pi) \le B(v_{2m+1}).
$$

Equivalently, it asks whether the canonical alternating full binary read-once function gives an upper bound on the best possible BDD size achievable after optimal variable reordering, uniformly over all read-once functions of this size.

## Known results

For read-once functions, each variable appears exactly once in the underlying formula tree, so every such $f$ corresponds to a rooted binary tree whose internal nodes are binary Boolean operators and whose leaves are variables. Section 7.1.4 establishes that for such functions, BDD size is governed by the propagation and merging of subfunctions, and is therefore controlled by the pattern of repeated subtables (beads) induced by the evaluation order.

For any fixed ordering $\pi$, $B(f^\pi)$ depends on how subfunctions induced by prefixes of $\pi$ coincide. In particular, if two different subtrees induce identical truth subtables at some level, the reduced BDD merges them, decreasing size. Conversely, imbalance in the decomposition tends to increase distinct beads and therefore increases $B(f^\pi)$.

For perfectly balanced alternating read-once trees (the $v_k$ family), the structure is extremal in the sense that at each level of decomposition the number of distinct subfunctions is maximized among symmetric compositions. This is consistent with the bead-growth mechanism described in Section 7.1.4, since each split produces new subtables with minimal duplication until late stages, yielding maximal node retention in the BDD.

Special cases are known:

1. For monotone read-once AND/OR trees of fixed shape, certain variable orders achieve BDD size equal to the corresponding $v_k$ construction.
2. For degenerate read-once functions (essentially paths), optimal ordering yields linear BDD size, strictly below $B(v_{2m+1})$.
3. The general problem of minimizing BDD size over variable orderings is known to be NP-hard even for restricted classes of Boolean functions; read-once restriction simplifies structure but does not eliminate ordering complexity.

No theorem in the literature establishes a uniform upper bound of the form

$$
\min_\pi B(f^\pi) \le B(v_{2m+1})
$$

for all read-once $f$ of $2^{2m+1}$ variables.

## Partial argument

Let $T_f$ be the formula tree of $f$. Any permutation $\pi$ induces a sequence of variable eliminations along the root-to-leaf decomposition determined by $\pi$. At each stage, the induced subfunctions correspond to partial evaluations of $T_f$ where some leaves are fixed and others remain symbolic.

The BDD size $B(f^\pi)$ equals the number of distinct subfunctions that arise as beads in this decomposition. Thus bounding $B(f^\pi)$ reduces to bounding the number of distinct residual read-once functions produced by cutting $T_f$ along an ordering $\pi$.

For $v_{2m+1}$, the balanced alternating structure ensures that at each split the number of active subtrees is as large as possible while still preserving read-once constraints. Any other tree shape introduces structural asymmetry: at some level, at least one branch becomes strictly smaller, which forces earlier merging of identical subtables under any ordering that respects that imbalance. This suggests that deviations from the balanced shape cannot systematically increase the number of distinct beads at all levels simultaneously.

However, a complete proof would require showing that for every read-once tree $T_f$ and every ordering $\pi$, the multiset of induced subfunctions can be embedded into that of $v_{2m+1}$ in a dominance relation preserving bead counts at every depth. No such embedding theorem is currently established, and known invariants such as pathwidth or communication complexity only provide partial bounds that are not tight enough to compare uniformly with $B(v_{2m+1})$.

## Status

The inequality

$$
\min_\pi B(f^\pi) \le B(v_{2m+1})
$$

is not known to be resolved in full generality for all read-once Boolean functions of $2^{2m+1}$ variables.

The statement is best classified as open in the strong uniform form asked here. Partial results support the plausibility of the bound for broad subclasses (balanced monotone trees, symmetric compositions), but no general extremal theorem establishes $v_{2m+1}$ as a universal upper bound for optimal BDD size under variable reordering in the read-once regime.
