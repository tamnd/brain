[Section 7.2.1.1: Generating All n-Tuples](../)

**Exercise 32.** [*M20*] [M20] True or false: Walsh functions satisfy wk(−x) = (−1)kwk(x).

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 5m26s  

---

## Solution

Let $x \in [0,1)$ and write its dyadic expansion

$$
x = \sum_{j \ge 1} x_j 2^{-j}, \qquad x_j \in \{0,1\}.
$$

Let the Rademacher functions be defined by

$$
r_j(x) = (-1)^{x_j}.
$$

In Paley ordering, the Walsh functions are

$$
w_k(x) = \prod_{j \ge 1} r_j(x)^{k_j},
$$

where $k = \sum_{j \ge 1} k_j 2^{j-1}$ and each $k_j \in {0,1}$.

The dyadic reflection used for Walsh functions on $[0,1)$ is $-x \equiv 1-x \pmod 1$, so

$$
w_k(-x) = w_k(1-x).
$$

The binary digits of $1-x$ are obtained by complementing each digit $x_j$, except for the terminating carry ambiguity on a null set, which does not affect Walsh functions. Thus

$$
(1-x)_j = 1 - x_j.
$$

Hence

$$
r_j(1-x) = (-1)^{1-x_j} = -(-1)^{x_j} = -r_j(x).
$$

Substituting into the product definition,

$$
w_k(1-x)
= \prod_{j \ge 1} (-r_j(x))^{k_j}
= \prod_{j \ge 1} (-1)^{k_j} \prod_{j \ge 1} r_j(x)^{k_j}.
$$

The second factor equals $w_k(x)$. The first factor simplifies as

$$
\prod_{j \ge 1} (-1)^{k_j} = (-1)^{\sum_{j \ge 1} k_j}.
$$

Since $\sum_{j \ge 1} k_j$ is the binary digit sum of $k$, its parity equals $k \bmod 2$, hence

$$
(-1)^{\sum_{j \ge 1} k_j} = (-1)^k.
$$

Therefore

$$
w_k(-x) = w_k(1-x) = (-1)^k w_k(x).
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
