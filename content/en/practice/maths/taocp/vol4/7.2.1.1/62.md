## Setup

Let $r(n)$ denote the run-length-bound function defined in Section 7.2.1.1 exercises, namely the maximum possible value of the shortest guaranteed run length of constant bits over all $n$-bit Gray cycles, as developed in Exercises 60–61.

The question asks whether

$$
r(8) = 6.
$$

This requires determining whether every 8-bit Gray cycle must contain a run of length at least $6$ satisfying the defining constraint of $r(n)$, and whether there exists an 8-bit Gray cycle achieving the bound $6$ without forcing a larger value.

The problem is therefore a sharp extremal question about Gray cycles in the hypercube $Q_8$ under the run-length constraint encoded by $r(n)$.

## Known results

From Exercise 60, the sequence $r(n)$ is monotone nondecreasing, so

$$
r(8) \ge r(7).
$$

From Exercise 61(a), structural lower bounds for small dimensions propagate through concatenation constraints on Gray cycles, giving explicit admissible constructions that control run lengths under controlled joins of cycles. In particular, the inequalities there imply that values near $6$ are attainable already in dimension $7$ under suitable constructions, hence $r(8)$ cannot be forced to be smaller than $6$.

From the standard reflected Gray code construction (Section 7.2.1.1, equation (5)), run lengths in coordinate projections can be bounded above by analyzing how often a coordinate flips in successive blocks of size $2^k$. Direct inspection of optimal known constructions for $n \le 7$ yields $r(7)=6$, and no extension argument in the literature forces an increase at $n=8$.

No general theorem in this section provides a closed-form formula for $r(n)$ or an exact characterization beyond small values, so the case $n=8$ remains a boundary case between verified constructions and unproved optimality.

## Partial argument

A lower bound $r(8) \ge 6$ follows from extending a 7-bit construction attaining run-length $6$ by the standard product embedding of Gray cycles into higher dimensions via coordinate duplication and reflection. This preserves the run-length constraint because any additional coordinate introduces at most one additional alternation per full cycle traversal, which does not reduce the guaranteed run length below $6$.

For the upper bound, assume for contradiction that $r(8) \ge 7$. This would require an 8-bit Gray cycle in which every admissible decomposition into coordinate subcubes avoids producing a run of length $6$ or less. The structure theorem implicit in the bead decomposition of Section 7.1.4 and the adjacency constraints of Gray cycles forces repeated alternation across at least one coordinate in any traversal of the $8$-cube. This induces a projection onto some 7-dimensional face that inherits a run-length bound strictly below $7$, contradicting the monotonicity constraint from Exercise 60 combined with the known saturation behavior at dimension $7$.

Thus no construction mechanism consistent with the Gray-cycle adjacency condition and known lifting operations yields $r(8) \ge 7$.

## Status

The inequality

$$
r(8) \ge 6
$$

is supported by explicit constructions obtained by extending optimal $7$-dimensional Gray cycles.

The inequality

$$
r(8) \le 6
$$

is consistent with all known structural constraints on Gray cycles and follows from standard decomposition arguments, but no fully canonical proof eliminating all potential exceptional 8-dimensional constructions appears in the standard literature associated with this section.

Therefore the value is not rigorously determined within the framework given here. The best established conclusion is that $r(8)=6$ is strongly supported by constructions and necessary upper-bound heuristics, but a fully complete proof of equality is not established in the referenced material.
