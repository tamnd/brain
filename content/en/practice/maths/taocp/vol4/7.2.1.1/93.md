## Solution

Let the digit sequence be

$$
1\,2\,3\,4\,5\,6\,7\,8\,9\,8\,7\,6\,5\,4\,3\,2\,1,
$$

and let a valid expression be obtained by inserting either “+”, “−”, or concatenation (no sign) between consecutive digits. This produces a partition of the sequence into blocks, each block being a decimal integer formed by concatenation of consecutive digits, together with a choice of sign $+$ or $-$ for each block.

Let the resulting value be

$$
S = \sum_{i=1}^{t} \varepsilon_i x_i,
$$

where each $x_i$ is a positive integer formed from a contiguous block of digits, each $\varepsilon_i \in {+1,-1}$, and the blocks form a disjoint cover of the full sequence in order.

Let

$$
N = 12345678987654321,
$$

the integer obtained when no signs are inserted and the whole sequence is taken as a single block.

For any valid signed partition, each block $x_i$ is a number formed from a substring of digits of $N$, hence $0 < x_i \le N$. Since the blocks are disjoint and preserve order, they form a partition of the digit string, and each digit contributes exactly once to exactly one block.

Write

$$
P = \sum_{\varepsilon_i = +1} x_i, \qquad Q = \sum_{\varepsilon_i = -1} x_i,
$$

so that $S = P - Q$ and $P + Q$ equals the sum of all block values.

Each block value $x_i$ is obtained by replacing a segment of $N$ by a base-$10$ decomposition; merging blocks strictly increases digit-length compatibility only when signs are removed. Replacing any multi-block partition by a single concatenation removes leading structure and yields a number whose value is exactly the integer represented by the full digit string. Therefore the full concatenation dominates every block decomposition in total digit weight in the sense that each digit contributes positively to at most one $x_i$, and carries increase magnitude only within the fixed digit sequence.

Since all digits of $N$ are nonnegative and the representation is positional in base $10$, the maximum possible value of any signed expression occurs when every block is taken with positive sign and no splits are introduced. Indeed, introducing a split replaces a single integer $ab$ by two integers $a$ and $b$ with

$$
10^{k}a + b = ab \ge a + b,
$$

for any positive integers $a,b$ formed from adjacent digit blocks, so splitting cannot increase the total positive contribution, and introducing negative signs can only decrease the value.

Hence for every valid construction,

$$
S \le N.
$$

Equality is achieved by taking a single block and no inserted signs, yielding $S = N$.

No construction can exceed $N$, since every valid expression evaluates to a signed sum of non-overlapping substrings of the fixed decimal expansion of $N$, and any deviation from the single-block positive form either introduces subtraction or replaces a longer base-$10$ block by a decomposition that does not increase total value.

Therefore the maximum representable integer is

$$
\boxed{12345678987654321}.
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
