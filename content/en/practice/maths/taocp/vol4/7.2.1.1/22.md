## Solution

A right subcube in the sense of the statement is determined by a prefix $p \in {0,1}^k$ and a suffix of $n-k$ stars, so the subcube consists of all binary $n$-tuples of the form $p\alpha$ where $\alpha \in {0,1}^{n-k}$. In a binary trie, each internal node corresponds to a fixed prefix, and each leaf corresponds to a right subcube obtained by stopping at some depth and leaving all remaining coordinates free.

Construct the Gray binary trie by starting from a binary trie and, at every internal node whose subtrie is itself a right subcube partition, interchanging its left and right subtries recursively from the root downward. This operation preserves the set of leaves, so every leaf still represents the same right subcube as before, but the left-to-right order of leaves changes.

Let the leaves in the resulting Gray binary trie be $L_1, L_2, \dots, L_m$, and let their corresponding fixed prefixes be $p_1, p_2, \dots, p_m$. Traversing leaves from left to right is exactly the same as listing these prefixes in the order induced by the recursive rule defining Gray binary code: at each node, the left subtree is visited in the same order as the parent, while the right subtree is visited in the reversed order of its own Gray traversal because of the swap at that node.

This produces a standard Gray ordering on the prefixes: if two consecutive leaves correspond to prefixes $p_i$ and $p_{i+1}$, then $p_i$ and $p_{i+1}$ differ in exactly one bit position, namely the highest level at which their paths in the trie diverge. This follows from the recursive structure of Gray order, since each level of swapping reverses exactly one coordinate block and preserves Gray order inside each block.

Now consider the corresponding subcubes $S_i$ and $S_{i+1}$ defined by $p_i$ and $p_{i+1}$. Let $t$ be the first position at which $p_i$ and $p_{i+1}$ differ. Then $p_i$ and $p_{i+1}$ agree on all coordinates to the left of $t$, and differ only at coordinate $t$. Since both subcubes leave all coordinates to the right of their defining prefixes free, one can choose a vertex $u \in S_i$ and a vertex $v \in S_{i+1}$ that agree on all free coordinates and on all fixed coordinates except at position $t$. These vertices differ in exactly one coordinate, so they are adjacent in the hypercube.

Thus $S_i$ and $S_{i+1}$ contain adjacent vertices, so the subcubes are adjacent. This holds for every consecutive pair of leaves in the left-to-right traversal of the Gray binary trie.

This completes the proof. ∎
