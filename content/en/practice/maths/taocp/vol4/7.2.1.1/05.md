## Solution

Let the array be indexed by binary integers of $n$ bits. The initial configuration places the value

$$
A[(b_0b_1\ldots b_{n-1})_2]
$$

at position

$$
(b_{n-1}\ldots b_1b_0)_2.
$$

The desired rearrangement therefore applies the permutation

$$
r(b_{n-1}\ldots b_1b_0)
=(b_0b_1\ldots b_{n-1}),
$$

where $r$ denotes bit reflection.

The permutation $r$ is an involution, because

$$
r(r(x))=x.
$$

Hence every cycle has length $1$ or $2$. Therefore each required exchange is between a pair of distinct indices $i$ and $r(i)$ satisfying $i<r(i)$. Indices fixed by $r$ require no action.

Algorithm M generates the indices in lexicographic order. A reflected version generates them in increasing order of their reflected values. This is obtained by interchanging the roles of the least and most significant digits. The reflected algorithm is

**R1.** Set $a_j\leftarrow0$ for $0\le j\le n$, and set $m_0\leftarrow2$, $m_j\leftarrow2$ for $1\le j\le n$.

**R2.** Let

$$
i=(a_1a_2\ldots a_n)_2,\qquad
j=(a_na_{n-1}\ldots a_1)_2.
$$

If $i<j$, interchange $A[i]$ and $A[j]$.

**R3.** Set $k\leftarrow1$.

**R4.** If $a_k=m_k-1$, set $a_k\leftarrow0$, $k\leftarrow k+1$, and repeat this step.

**R5.** If $k=n+1$, terminate the algorithm. Otherwise set $a_k\leftarrow a_k+1$ and return to step R2.

The only difference from Algorithm M is that carries propagate from the least significant position toward the most significant position. Consequently the sequence $(a_1,\ldots,a_n)$ is generated in the order

$$
000\ldots0,\;
100\ldots0,\;
010\ldots0,\;
110\ldots0,\;
\ldots,
$$

which is lexicographic in the reflected bit strings.

During the visit to $(a_1,\ldots,a_n)$, the algorithm simultaneously knows the index

$$
i=(a_1\ldots a_n)_2
$$

and its reflected partner

$$
j=(a_n\ldots a_1)_2=r(i).
$$

Since $r$ is an involution, exactly one of the inequalities $i<j$ or $j<i$ holds whenever $i\ne j$. Thus every transposition is performed exactly once, and fixed points with $i=j$ are left unchanged.

After all visits, every element originally stored at position $r(i)$ has been moved to position $i$. Hence the array is restored to the correct order.

\boxed{\text{Exchange }A[i]\text{ and }A[r(i)]\text{ whenever }i<r(i),\text{ generating }i\text{ with the reflected form of Algorithm M.}
