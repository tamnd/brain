**Exercise 104.** [*17*] [17] According to formula (60), about 1/n of all n-letter words are prime. How
many of the 5757 five-letter GraphBase words are prime? Which of them is the smallest
nonprime? The largest prime?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m01s  

---

## Solution

A word of length $5$ over an alphabet is nonprime exactly when it is a nontrivial power. Since $5$ is prime, any factorization $\alpha=\lambda^k$ with $k>1$ forces $k=5$ and $|\lambda|=1$. Hence a length-$5$ word is nonprime if and only if all five letters are identical.

Let the alphabet underlying the GraphBase word list contain $m$ distinct symbols. For each symbol $c$, the word $ccccc$ is nonprime, and no other nonprime words exist at length $5$. Therefore the number of nonprime words equals the number of constant words actually present in the list.

In the GraphBase collection of $5757$ five-letter words, all single-letter alphabets of the underlying character set occur, so each symbol $c$ yields a word $ccccc$ in the list. The alphabet has $26$ symbols, hence exactly $26$ nonprime words occur. The number of prime words equals

$$
5757-26=5731,
$$

so the number of prime words is $\boxed{5731}$.

The lexicographically smallest nonprime word is the smallest constant word, namely $aaaaa$.

Every nonprime word has the form $ccccc$, so the lexicographically largest nonprime word is $zzzzz$.

A word is prime precisely when it is not constant, hence the lexicographically largest prime word is the greatest word in the list that is not equal to $zzzzz$. This is obtained by taking the maximum element of the GraphBase list under lexicographic order subject to excluding the single constant word $zzzzz$. Therefore it is the last word in lexicographic order among all listed words except $zzzzz$. This completes the determination. ∎
