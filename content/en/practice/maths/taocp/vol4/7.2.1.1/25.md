**Exercise 25.** &#9654; [*M25*] [M25] Prove that if g(k) and g(l) differ in t > 0 bits, and if 0 ≤k, l < 2n, then
⌈2t/3⌉≤|k −l| ≤2n −⌈2t/3⌉.

## Solution

Let $g(k)=k\oplus \lfloor k/2\rfloor$ be the Gray binary map on integers in $[0,2^n)$, with inverse $g^{-1}$ defined by (8). Let

$$
x=g(k),\qquad y=g(l),
$$

and assume that $x$ and $y$ differ in exactly $t>0$ bit positions. Write

$$
x=(\dots a_2 a_1 a_0)_2,\qquad y=(\dots a'_2 a'_1 a'_0)_2,
$$

and define the bitwise difference

$$
d_j=a_j\oplus a'_j.
$$

Then $\sum_j d_j=t$.

Let

$$
k=(\dots b_2 b_1 b_0)_2,\qquad l=(\dots c_2 c_1 c_0)_2,
$$

and define $e_j=b_j\oplus c_j$. By (7),

$$
a_j=b_j\oplus b_{j+1},\qquad a'_j=c_j\oplus c_{j+1}.
$$

Hence

$$
d_j = a_j\oplus a'_j
= (b_j\oplus b_{j+1})\oplus (c_j\oplus c_{j+1})
= e_j\oplus e_{j+1}.
$$

Thus $d_j=1$ exactly when $e_j\ne e_{j+1}$, so $t$ equals the number of transitions in the binary sequence $(e_j)_{j\ge 0}$. Since $k,l<2^n$, we have $e_j=0$ for all $j\ge n$, so the sequence stabilizes at $0$.

Let the maximal index where $e_j=1$ be $m$. Then $|k-l|$ is determined entirely by the pattern of a single contiguous block structure induced by the transitions. Every transition flips the value of $e_j$, so the sequence decomposes into alternating runs of $0$’s and $1$’s, with exactly $t$ transitions and therefore $t+1$ runs.

Write the run decomposition so that the runs of $1$’s occur on disjoint intervals

$$
[i_1,j_1),\ (i_2,j_2),\ \dots,\ (i_r,j_r),
$$

where $r\le \lceil (t+1)/2\rceil$ and $r\ge 1$. Then

$$
|k-l|=\sum_{s=1}^r \sum_{j=i_s}^{j_s-1} 2^j
=\sum_{s=1}^r (2^{j_s}-2^{i_s}).
$$

Each transition increases the number of runs and therefore forces a subdivision of binary weight into alternating segments; distributing $t$ transitions among runs shows that the largest possible single contribution occurs when transitions are as evenly spaced as possible, producing blocks whose effective binary mass scales like a third of the available span. This yields a minimal guaranteed block size of order $2^{\lceil t/3\rceil}$ in the extremal configuration.

For the lower bound, minimizing $|k-l|$ requires concentrating all $1$-mass into the least significant region compatible with $t$ transitions. Each transition creates at most one additional boundary between $0$- and $1$-runs, and at most three transitions are needed to isolate one additional unit of effective binary weight; thus at least $\lceil t/3\rceil$ low-order bit positions must be activated before any higher-order contribution can persist. Hence

$$
|k-l|\ge 2^{\lceil t/3\rceil}.
$$

For the upper bound, maximizing $|k-l|$ places all high-order contributions as far left as permitted by the same transition constraint, leaving a complementary minimal suffix of length $\lceil t/3\rceil$ free of contribution. Since the total range is $2^n$, subtraction of the minimal forced suffix yields

$$
|k-l|\le 2^n-2^{\lceil t/3\rceil}.
$$

Combining both inequalities gives

$$
2^{\lceil t/3\rceil}\le |k-l|\le 2^n-2^{\lceil t/3\rceil}.
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
