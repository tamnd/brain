**Exercise 34.** [*M21*] [M21] The Paley functions pk(x) are defined by
p0(x) = 1
and
pk(x) = (−1)⌊2x⌋kp⌊k/2⌋(2x).
Show that pk(x) has a simple expression in terms of Rademacher functions, analogous
to (17), and relate Paley functions to Walsh functions.

**Solve time:** 4m57s

## Solution

Let $k = (\ldots b_2 b_1 b_0)_2$ with $b_j \in {0,1}$. Let the Rademacher functions be

$$
r_j(x) = (-1)^{\lfloor 2^j x \rfloor}, \quad j \ge 0.
$$

The Paley functions satisfy

$$
p_0(x)=1,\qquad p_k(x)=(-1)^{\lfloor 2x \rfloor k}\, p_{\lfloor k/2 \rfloor}(2x).
$$

The factor $(-1)^{\lfloor 2x \rfloor k}$ equals $((-1)^{\lfloor 2x \rfloor})^k$, hence

$$
(-1)^{\lfloor 2x \rfloor k} = r_0(x)^k.
$$

Writing $k = 2\lfloor k/2 \rfloor + (k \bmod 2)$ gives

$$
r_0(x)^k = r_0(x)^{k \bmod 2},
$$

since $r_0(x)^2 = 1$. Therefore the recursion becomes

$$
p_k(x) = r_0(x)^{b_0}\, p_{\lfloor k/2 \rfloor}(2x),
$$

where $b_0 = k \bmod 2$.

Apply the same transformation to $p_{\lfloor k/2 \rfloor}(2x)$. The definition yields

$$
p_{\lfloor k/2 \rfloor}(2x)
= r_0(2x)^{b_1}\, p_{\lfloor k/4 \rfloor}(4x).
$$

Since $r_0(2x) = (-1)^{\lfloor 4x \rfloor} = r_1(x)$, this becomes

$$
p_{\lfloor k/2 \rfloor}(2x) = r_1(x)^{b_1}\, p_{\lfloor k/4 \rfloor}(4x).
$$

Iterating this substitution $t$ times produces

$$
p_k(x)
= \left(\prod_{j=0}^{t} r_j(x)^{b_j}\right)\, p_{\lfloor k/2^{t+1} \rfloor}(2^{t+1}x).
$$

For sufficiently large $t$, $\lfloor k/2^{t+1} \rfloor = 0$, so the remaining factor equals $p_0(2^{t+1}x)=1$. This yields the closed form

$$
p_k(x) = \prod_{j \ge 0} r_j(x)^{b_j}.
$$

The Walsh functions are defined in the same section by the product formula corresponding to equation (17),

$$
w_k(x) = \prod_{j \ge 0} r_j(x)^{b_j},
$$

with the same binary expansion $k = (\ldots b_2 b_1 b_0)_2$. Comparison of the two expressions gives

$$
p_k(x) = w_k(x).
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
