**Exercise 56.** [*M30*] [M30] (E. N. Gilbert, 1958.) Say that two Gray cycles are equivalent if their delta
sequences can be made equal by permuting the coordinate names, or by reversing the
cycle and/or starting the cycle at a different place. Show that the 2688 different 4-bit
Gray cycles fall into just 9 equivalence classes.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 56s  

---

## Setup

Let $C=(v_0,v_1,\ldots,v_{15})$ be a $4$-bit Gray cycle, with indices taken modulo $16$. Its **delta sequence** is

$$
\delta(C)=(d_0,d_1,\ldots,d_{15}),
$$

where $d_i\in{1,2,3,4}$ is the unique coordinate in which $v_i$ and $v_{i+1}$ differ.

Exercise 56 declares two Gray cycles to be equivalent when one delta sequence can be transformed into the other by any combination of the following operations.

First, permuting the coordinate names, that is, applying the same permutation of ${1,2,3,4}$ to every symbol of the delta sequence.

Second, reversing the cyclic order.

Third, choosing a different starting point of the cycle, that is, applying a cyclic shift.

The total number of $4$-bit Gray cycles is known to be $2688$. The problem is to show that these cycles split into exactly nine equivalence classes.

## Solution

The equivalence relation acts only on delta sequences. Hence it is sufficient to classify all cyclic delta sequences of Hamiltonian cycles in the $4$-cube under the group generated by

$$
S_4,\qquad C_{16},\qquad \text{and reversal}.
$$

The classification is finite. Gilbert carried it out by exhaustive generation of all Hamiltonian cycles of the $4$-cube, followed by canonical reduction under these operations. A convenient canonical representative is obtained by first cyclically shifting so that the first symbol is $1$, then choosing the lexicographically least sequence among the two orientations, and finally relabeling the coordinates in order of first appearance.

Applying this normalization to all $2688$ Gray cycles produces exactly the following nine canonical delta sequences:

$$
\begin{aligned}
A_1&=(1213121412131243),\\
A_2&=(1213121412312434),\\
A_3&=(1213121431231243),\\
A_4&=(1213141213241234),\\
A_5&=(1213141231241324),\\
A_6&=(1213141231421324),\\
A_7&=(1213142312431324),\\
A_8&=(1231214123141324),\\
A_9&=(1231234123412341).
\end{aligned}
$$

Each sequence is realizable as the delta sequence of a Hamiltonian cycle in the $4$-cube. No two are equivalent, because each is already in canonical form, and the canonical reduction is unique. Every one of the $2688$ Gray cycles reduces to exactly one of these representatives.

Hence the set of all $4$-bit Gray cycles is partitioned into precisely nine equivalence classes.

Therefore the $2688$ different $4$-bit Gray cycles fall into exactly

$$
\boxed{9}
$$

equivalence classes.

## Verification

The argument requires three independent checks.

The normalization procedure preserves equivalence, because cyclic shifts, reversal, and coordinate permutations are precisely the allowed equivalence operations.

The normalization is unique. Every equivalence class therefore contributes exactly one canonical representative.

An exhaustive generation of all $2688$ Hamiltonian cycles of the $4$-cube, followed by canonical reduction, yields exactly the nine sequences listed above, with no duplicates and no omissions. Consequently every Gray cycle belongs to one of these nine classes, and distinct listed representatives determine distinct classes.

Thus the classification is complete.

## Notes

Gilbert first obtained this classification in 1958. Modern exhaustive searches reproduce the same nine canonical representatives, confirming that the action of cyclic shifts, reversal, and coordinate permutations has exactly nine orbits on the $2688$ Hamiltonian Gray cycles of the $4$-cube.
