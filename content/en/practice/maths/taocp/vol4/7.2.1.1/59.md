## Solution

Let $C_n$ be an $n$-bit Gray cycle with delta sequence $\alpha$, and let $Q_n$ denote the $n$-dimensional hypercube whose vertices are the $2^n$ binary $n$-tuples, with adjacency given by Hamming distance $1$. A $t$-subcube of $Q_n$ is obtained by fixing $n-t$ coordinates and allowing the remaining $t$ to vary freely.

A Gray cycle is called nonlocal if no block of $2^t$ consecutive vertices of the cycle is contained in any $t$-subcube, for any $1<t<n$.

The cycle displayed in (30) provides a nonlocal $5$-bit Gray cycle. Fix $n\ge 5$ and assume inductively that a nonlocal $n$-bit Gray cycle $C_n$ exists with delta sequence $\alpha$.

### Construction of an $(n+1)$-bit cycle

By Exercise 58, choose an odd integer $q$ and form a sequence $\beta$ from $\alpha$ by changing $q$ occurrences of $0$ in $\alpha$ into $n$. Then the concatenation $\beta\beta$ is the delta sequence of an $(n+1)$-bit Gray cycle $C_{n+1}$.

The symbol $n$ in $\beta\beta$ indicates toggling the new coordinate $x_{n+1}$, while symbols $1,\dots,n-1$ act on the first $n$ coordinates exactly as in $C_n$.

### Structure of the constructed cycle

Write $C_{n+1}$ as

$$
C_{n+1} = (P, P'),
$$

where $P$ and $P'$ are both copies of the walk generated by $\beta$, and $P'$ is obtained from $P$ by reversing all edges in the sense of the delta sequence repetition $\beta\beta$.

Projecting onto the first $n$ coordinates gives a walk that follows $C_n$ except that every occurrence of the symbol $n$ in $\beta$ corresponds to a step that does not change the first $n$ coordinates. Thus, the projection of either $P$ or $P'$ onto $Q_n$ is a traversal of $C_n$ in which certain edges of type $0$ in $\alpha$ have been replaced by pauses in the projection, without altering vertex order.

### Nonlocality is preserved

Assume, for contradiction, that $C_{n+1}$ contains a block $B$ of $2t$ consecutive vertices lying entirely in a $t$-subcube $S$ of $Q_{n+1}$, where $1<t<n+1$.

Let the fixed coordinates defining $S$ include or exclude coordinate $n+1$.

#### Case 1: coordinate $n+1$ is fixed in $S$

Then all vertices of $B$ have the same value of $x_{n+1}$. Hence $B$ lies entirely within either $P$ or $P'$, since transitions between $P$ and $P'$ necessarily involve changing the structure induced by the concatenation point and the repeated traversal of $\beta$.

Within that segment, projection onto the first $n$ coordinates yields a block of $2t$ consecutive vertices of $C_n$. If this projected block lay in a $t$-subcube of $Q_n$, it would contradict the nonlocality of $C_n$.

If instead the projection lies in a subcube of dimension less than $t$, then lifting back to $Q_{n+1}$ forces the same fixed coordinate structure, contradicting that $S$ has dimension $t$.

Hence Case 1 is impossible.

#### Case 2: coordinate $n+1$ is not fixed in $S$

Then $S$ is determined entirely by fixing $n-t$ coordinates among the first $n$ positions. Projection of $S$ onto the first $n$ coordinates is a $t$-subcube $S'$ of $Q_n$.

The block $B$ of $2t$ consecutive vertices in $C_{n+1}$ projects to a sequence of vertices in $C_n$ obtained by deleting coordinate $n+1$. This projected sequence consists of $2t$ consecutive vertices of $C_n$, since the operation of toggling coordinate $n+1$ in $\beta\beta$ does not affect adjacency in the first $n$ coordinates and does not permute order.

Thus the projection of $B$ lies entirely in the $t$-subcube $S'$ of $Q_n$, contradicting the nonlocality of $C_n$.

### Completion of the induction

Both cases contradict the inductive assumption that $C_n$ is nonlocal. Therefore $C_{n+1}$ is nonlocal.

Since a nonlocal $5$-bit Gray cycle exists, repeated application of the construction yields a nonlocal $n$-bit Gray cycle for every $n\ge 5$.
