## Solution

Let $k$ have binary expansion

$$
k = (. . . b_2 b_1 b_0)_2,
$$

so that

$$
k = \sum_{j \ge 0} b_j 2^j,\quad b_j \in \{0,1\}.
$$

From (9) in the section,

$$
g(k) = k \oplus \left\lfloor \frac{k}{2} \right\rfloor.
$$

Write

$$
\left\lfloor \frac{k}{2} \right\rfloor = \sum_{j \ge 0} b_{j+1} 2^j,
$$

which follows by shifting the binary expansion of $k$ one position to the right.

Let

$$
g(k) = (. . . a_2 a_1 a_0)_2.
$$

The operation $\oplus$ is bitwise addition modulo $2$ without carries, so each binary digit of $g(k)$ is the sum modulo $2$ of the corresponding digits of $k$ and $\lfloor k/2 \rfloor$. Hence for each $j \ge 0$,

$$
a_j = b_j \oplus b_{j+1}.
$$

This matches the digitwise structure induced by $g(k) = k \oplus \lfloor k/2 \rfloor$, since the $j$-th bit of $k$ contributes $b_j$ and the $j$-th bit of $\lfloor k/2 \rfloor$ contributes $b_{j+1}$, with no interaction between positions under bitwise XOR.

This completes the proof. ∎
