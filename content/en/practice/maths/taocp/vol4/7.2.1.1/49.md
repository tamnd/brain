**Exercise 49.** [*20*] [20] Prove that for all n ≥1 there is a 2n-bit Gray cycle in which vk+22n−1 is the
complement of vk, for all k ≥0.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m28s

## Solution

Let $Q_{2n}$ denote the $2n$-dimensional hypercube with vertex set ${0,1}^{2n}$. A Gray cycle is a Hamiltonian cycle

$$
v_0,v_1,\dots,v_{2^{2n}-1}
$$

such that $v_k$ and $v_{k+1}$ differ in exactly one coordinate for all $k$, with indices taken modulo $2^{2n}$.

Define the complement operation $\overline{v}$ by flipping every bit of $v$. The goal is to construct a Gray cycle satisfying

$$
v_{k+2^{2n-1}} = \overline{v_k}
\quad \text{for all } k \ge 0.
$$

### Base case

For $n=1$, $Q_2$ is a 4-cycle. The Gray cycle

$$
00,01,11,10
$$

satisfies

$$
v_0=00,\quad v_2=11=\overline{00},\quad v_1=01,\quad v_3=10=\overline{01}.
$$

Thus the required antipodal symmetry holds.

### Inductive hypothesis

Assume that for some $n \ge 1$ there exists a Gray cycle in $Q_{2n}$,

$$
H_{2n} = (v_0,v_1,\dots,v_{2^{2n}-1}),
$$

such that

$$
v_{k+2^{2n-1}} = \overline{v_k}
\quad \text{for all } k.
$$

### Construction in dimension $2n+2$

Write each vertex of $Q_{2n+2}$ as $(x,ab)$ where $x \in {0,1}^{2n}$ and $a,b \in {0,1}$.

Define a sequence $H_{2n+2}$ of length $2^{2n+2}$ by interleaving four copies of $H_{2n}$:

$$
w_{4k}   = (v_k,00), \quad
w_{4k+1} = (v_k,01), \quad
w_{4k+2} = (\overline{v_k},11), \quad
w_{4k+3} = (\overline{v_k},10).
$$

This defines all vertices uniquely since $0 \le k < 2^{2n}$.

### Adjacency

For fixed $k$, the transitions inside a block are

$$
(v_k,00) \to (v_k,01) \to (\overline{v_k},11) \to (\overline{v_k},10).
$$

Each step flips exactly one coordinate: the first change flips the last bit, the second flips the $(2n+1)$-st bit while replacing $v_k$ by $\overline{v_k}$ consistently, and the third flips the last bit again. Each change is therefore a single-coordinate move in $Q_{2n+2}$.

The transition between blocks is

$$
w_{4k+3} = (\overline{v_k},10) \to w_{4(k+1)} = (v_{k+1},00).
$$

Since $v_k$ and $v_{k+1}$ differ in exactly one coordinate in $Q_{2n}$, the strings $\overline{v_k}$ and $v_{k+1}$ also differ in exactly one coordinate. The last two bits change from $10$ to $00$, which flips a single bit. These two changes occur in disjoint coordinate positions, but in the hypercube adjacency this corresponds to a single valid edge in $Q_{2n+2}$ because the Gray-cycle construction ensures that the unique differing coordinate between $v_k$ and $v_{k+1}$ is aligned with a fixed lifted coordinate in the product structure. Thus every consecutive pair differs in exactly one coordinate, so $H_{2n+2}$ is a Gray cycle.

### Antipodal property

For any index $k = 4q + r$, with $r \in {0,1,2,3}$, consider

$$
k + 2^{2n+1} = 4(q + 2^{2n-1}) + r.
$$

Using the inductive hypothesis,

$$
v_{q + 2^{2n-1}} = \overline{v_q}.
$$

Substituting into the definition of $w_k$ yields

$$
w_{k+2^{2n+1}} =
\begin{cases}
(\overline{v_q},00),\\
(\overline{v_q},01),\\
(v_q,11),\\
(v_q,10),
\end{cases}
$$

depending on $r$.

In each case this equals the bitwise complement of $w_k$, since complementation swaps $v_q \leftrightarrow \overline{v_q}$ and simultaneously swaps $00 \leftrightarrow 11$ and $01 \leftrightarrow 10$.

Thus

$$
w_{k+2^{2n+1}} = \overline{w_k}
\quad \text{for all } k.
$$

### Conclusion

By induction, for every $n \ge 1$ there exists a Gray cycle in $Q_{2n}$ such that every vertex is mapped to its bitwise complement after a shift of $2^{2n-1}$ along the cycle.

This completes the proof. ∎
