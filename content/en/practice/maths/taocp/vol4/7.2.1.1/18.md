## Solution

Define a map $G:{0,1,2,3}\to {0,1}^2$ by

$$
G(0)=00,\quad G(1)=01,\quad G(2)=11,\quad G(3)=10.
$$

Extend this coordinatewise to vectors $u=(u_1,\dots,u_n)$ by

$$
u'=(u'_1,\dots,u'_{2n})=(G(u_1),\dots,G(u_n)).
$$

The Lee weight on a single coordinate satisfies

$$
\nu_L(a)=\min(a,4-a)
$$

for $a\in{0,1,2,3}$, giving

$$
\nu_L(0)=0,\quad \nu_L(1)=1,\quad \nu_L(2)=2,\quad \nu_L(3)=1.
$$
