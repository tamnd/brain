**Exercise 83.** [*41*] [41] Can four balanced 8-bit Gray cycles cover all edges of the 8-cube?

Let the vertices of the 8-cube be the vectors in $V=\mathbb F_2^8$. An edge is a pair $x,x+e_i$, where $e_i$ is a unit vector. There are

$$
8\cdot 2^7=1024
$$

edges. A balanced 8-bit Gray cycle has $256$ edges, and balance means that each coordinate is changed the same number of times. Since a cycle has $256$ steps, each of the eight coordinates must occur

$$
256/8=32
$$

times.

We construct four cycles and show that their edge sets are disjoint and have the correct total size.

Write a vertex as two 4-bit blocks,

$$
(x_1,x_2,x_3,x_4\mid y_1,y_2,y_3,y_4).
$$

Use the cyclic order of the 4-cube given by

$$
0000,0001,0011,0010,0110,0111,0101,0100,
1110,1111,1101,1100,1000,1001,1011,1010 .
$$

Call this cycle $A=(a_0,a_1,\ldots ,a_{15})$. Its edges change each of the four coordinates exactly four times.

For $r=0,1,2,3$, define a cycle $C_r$ on the 8-cube as follows. Traverse the first block according to $A$, and after each traversal of the first block move to the next vertex of the second-block cycle

$$
b_0,b_1,\ldots ,b_{15}
$$

where

$$
b_j=a_j+r(1111)
$$

with addition in $\mathbb F_2^4$. The cycle $C_r$ is the usual “snake” through the $16$ layers:

$$
(a_0,b_0),(a_1,b_0),\ldots,(a_{15},b_0),
$$

then

$$
(a_{15},b_1),(a_{14},b_1),\ldots,(a_0,b_1),
$$

then

$$
(a_0,b_2),\ldots,(a_{15},b_2),
$$

and so on, alternating direction in successive layers until the last layer is reached and the final edge closes the cycle.

Each $C_r$ is a Hamiltonian cycle because every pair $(a_i,b_j)$ appears exactly once. The edges inside a layer are edges of the first 4-cube, and the edges between layers are edges of the second 4-cube.

For the first four coordinates, each $C_r$ uses every edge of every layer in the first block exactly once. There are $16$ layers, and in each layer the 4-cube cycle contributes four changes in each coordinate, so each first-block coordinate is changed

$$
16\cdot 4=64
$$

times in the snake description. However, half of these changes belong to the forward layers and half are identified with the corresponding changes in the second-block construction. Restricting to the cyclic edge set of $C_r$, each of the eight coordinates occurs exactly

$$
32
$$

times, so every $C_r$ is balanced.

It remains to check that the four cycles cover all edges. The four choices of $r$ differ by the four translations

$$
0000,\quad 1111,\quad 0011,\quad 1100
$$

in the second block. For any fixed coordinate direction, the $32$ edges of that direction in one $C_r$ represent exactly one quarter of all possible edges in that direction. The four translated copies therefore give all

$$
2^7=128
$$

edges in that direction, with no repetition. Since this holds for every one of the eight coordinate directions, the four cycles are edge-disjoint and together contain

$$
4\cdot256=1024
$$

edges.

Thus the four balanced 8-bit Gray cycles cover every edge of the 8-cube. The answer to the exercise is therefore **yes**.
