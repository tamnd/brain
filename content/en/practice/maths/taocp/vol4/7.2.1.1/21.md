## Solution

Let $p_k$ denote the number of BDD nodes at level $k$, and let $q_k$ denote the number of distinct subtables produced at level $k$ before reduction, as in the quasi-profile definition.

Each node at level $k-1$ has exactly two outgoing arcs, LO and HI, and each arc determines one subtable at level $k$ obtained by fixing $x_{k-1}=0$ or $x_{k-1}=1$. Therefore each node at level $k-1$ contributes at most two subtables to level $k$. Distinctness is not preserved under this generation, so the total number of distinct subtables cannot exceed the total number of generated subtables from all level $k-1$ nodes, which yields

$$
q_k \le 2p_{k-1}.
$$

Each node at level $k$ corresponds to a distinct reduced subfunction at that level, and every such node arises from at least one subtable at level $k$ before merging. Thus the reduction map from subtables to nodes is surjective onto level-$k$ nodes, so the number of nodes cannot exceed the number of distinct subtables:

$$
p_k \le q_k.
$$

Combining these inequalities gives the local quasi-profile bounds

$$
p_k \le q_k \le 2p_{k-1}.
$$

Summing the left inequality over all levels $k$ shows that the total number of nodes is bounded above by the total number of distinct subtables across all levels. Summing the right inequality over all levels expresses that the total number of subtables generated in the unreduced expansion is at most twice the total number of nodes in the previous level expansion. This yields the cumulative form of the quasi-profile inequalities, which relate the total size of the unreduced expansion to the size of the reduced BDD through a factor of at most $2$ at each level.

These two statements correspond respectively to the quasi-profile inequalities (84) and (124), completing the required verification. ∎
