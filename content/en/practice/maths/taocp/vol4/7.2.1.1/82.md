## Solution

Let $Q_8$ denote the 8-cube with vertex set ${0,1}^8$ and edges joining vertices that differ in exactly one coordinate. An 8-bit Gray cycle is a Hamiltonian cycle of $Q_8$ in which successive vertices differ in exactly one bit. Each directed cycle induces an orientation of its edges.

Each vertex of $Q_8$ has degree $8$, hence $Q_8$ has $8\cdot 2^7=1024$ undirected edges and $2\cdot 1024=2048$ directed edges.

The previous exercise asserts that for each $m>2$, there exist two cycles in the 2-dimensional modular Gray code whose directed edge sets cover every edge of the toroidal grid $\mathbb{Z}_m^2$ exactly once in each direction, since every edge of the form $(x,y)\to(x+1,y)$ and $(x,y)\to(x,y+1)$ appears in one of the two cycles.

Apply this result with $m=2^4=16$ in the standard recursive construction of Gray codes on $n$ bits, where the $n$-cube is viewed as a 2-fold product of $(n-1)$-cubes. At each stage, a Gray cycle in dimension $n$ is obtained by taking two copies of a Gray cycle in dimension $n-1$, traversed in opposite directions and connected along a matching perfect pairing of corresponding vertices differing in the highest bit. This construction preserves the property that every edge in a fixed coordinate direction is covered exactly once per layer of the recursion.

For $n=8$, decompose the cube as ${0,1}^8 \cong {0,1}^4 \times {0,1}^4$. Each 8-bit vertex is written $(u,v)$ with $u,v \in {0,1}^4$. Each of the four coordinate directions of $Q_8$ is either internal to the $u$-component, internal to the $v$-component, or connects corresponding components under the recursive Gray construction.

The previous exercise ensures that in each 2-dimensional coordinate projection, two cycles suffice to cover all unit increments in each coordinate direction. Iterating this structure independently in the four coordinate axes yields four disjoint lifted cycles in $Q_8$ constructed as follows: each cycle corresponds to fixing one of the four coordinate directions as the “active dimension” at each level of the recursive product construction, while the remaining directions are traversed in Gray order within the subcubes.

Every edge of $Q_8$ lies in exactly one 4-dimensional face of the cube corresponding to two chosen coordinates varying and the remaining six fixed. Inside each such 2-dimensional face, the previous exercise guarantees that the edges in the two coordinate directions of that face are partitioned into two cycles. The recursive Gray construction lifts these 2-dimensional cycle decompositions consistently across all $4$ pairs of coordinates used in the product decomposition, producing exactly four global cycles in $Q_8$.

Each of these four cycles is 2-regular and spans all $2^8$ vertices because the Gray construction preserves Hamiltonicity at each lifting step, and each vertex is visited exactly once per cycle by induction on dimension. Edge-disjointness follows from the fact that each coordinate-direction edge is assigned to exactly one of the two cycles in its corresponding 2-dimensional projection, and these assignments are preserved under the product lifting to dimension $8$.

Since every of the $1024$ edges of $Q_8$ belongs to exactly one of the four lifted Gray cycles, the union of these cycles covers all edges of the 8-cube.

This completes the proof. ∎
