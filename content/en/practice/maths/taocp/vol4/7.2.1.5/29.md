**Exercise 29.** [*M26*] [M26] Continuing the previous exercise, let $H_r(a_1, \ldots, a_m) = [x^r] R(a_1, \ldots, a_m)$ be the polynomial in $y$ that enumerates free cells when $r$ rooks are placed.

a) Show that the number of ways to place $r$ rooks on an $m \times n$ board, leaving $f$ cells free, is the number of permutations of $\{1, \ldots, n\}$ that have $f$ inversions. Thus, by Eq. 5.1.1–(8) and exercise 5.1.2–16, we have

$$R_n\bigl(\underbrace{n, \ldots, n}_{m}\bigr) = n!_y = \prod_{j=1}^{n}(1 + y + \cdots + y^{j-1}).$$

b) What is $H_r(\underbrace{n, \ldots, n}_{m})$, the generating function for $r$ rooks on an $m \times n$ board?

c) If $a_1 \ge \cdots \ge a_m \ge 0$ and $t$ is a nonnegative integer, prove the general formula

$$\prod_{j=1}^{m} \frac{1 - y^{t + j + a_j + \cdots + a_m}}{1 - y} = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{n!_y}{(t-k)!_y} H_k(a_1, \ldots, a_m).$$

[*Note:* The quantity $\binom{t}{k}_y = \prod_{j=1}^{k}\bigl((1-y^{t-j+1})/(1-y^j)\bigr)$ is zero when $k > t \ge 0$. Thus, for example, when $t = 0$ the right-hand side reduces to $R_m(a_1, \ldots, a_m)$. We can compute $R_m, R_{m-1}, \ldots, R_0$ by successively setting $t = 0, 1, \ldots, m$.]

d) If $a_1 \ge a_2 \ge \cdots \ge a_m \ge 0$ and $a'_1 \ge a'_2 \ge \cdots \ge a'_m \ge 0$, show that we have $R(a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_m) = R(a'_1, a'_2, \ldots, a'_m)$ if and only if the associated multisets $\{a_1+1, a_2+2, \ldots, a_m+m\}$ and $\{a'_1+1, a'_2+2, \ldots, a'_m+m\}$ are the same.
