**Exercise 67.** [*26*] [26] Continuing the previous exercise, find a first-element-swap Gray cycle for
n = 5 in which each star transposition (1 j) occurs 30 times, for 2 ≤j ≤5.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 7m50s

## Setup

Let $S_5$ be generated by the star transpositions

$$
g_j = (1\,j), \quad 2 \le j \le 5.
$$

A first-element-swap Gray cycle is a Hamiltonian cycle in the Cayley graph of $S_5$ with respect to ${g_2,g_3,g_4,g_5}$, hence a cyclic sequence

$$
\pi_0, \pi_1, \dots, \pi_{120}=\pi_0
$$

such that $\pi_{k+1} = g_{j_k}\pi_k$ for some $j_k \in {2,3,4,5}$.

The goal is to construct such a cycle in which each generator $g_j$ occurs exactly $30$ times.

## Solution

Let $\Gamma$ be the Cayley graph of $S_5$ under ${g_2,g_3,g_4,g_5}$. Each vertex has degree $4$, and each edge is labeled by exactly one generator $g_j$. A Hamiltonian cycle in $\Gamma$ uses exactly $120$ edges, since $|S_5|=120$.

Let $H$ be a Hamiltonian cycle. For each $j$, let $N_j(H)$ denote the number of edges of $H$ labeled by $g_j$. Then

$$
N_2(H)+N_3(H)+N_4(H)+N_5(H)=120.
$$

Define an automorphism of $\Gamma$ by any permutation $\sigma$ of ${2,3,4,5}$ acting on generators via $g_j \mapsto g_{\sigma(j)}$. This action preserves adjacency and maps Hamiltonian cycles to Hamiltonian cycles. Hence $H^\sigma$ is again a Hamiltonian cycle and satisfies

$$
N_j(H^\sigma)=N_{\sigma^{-1}(j)}(H).
$$

Let $G \cong S_4$ be the full permutation group of ${2,3,4,5}$. The orbit of $H$ under $G$ consists of Hamiltonian cycles, and for fixed $j$,

$$
\sum_{\sigma \in G} N_j(H^\sigma)
=
\sum_{\sigma \in G} N_{\sigma^{-1}(j)}(H).
$$

For fixed $k$ and $j$, the number of $\sigma \in S_4$ satisfying $\sigma^{-1}(j)=k$ equals $6$, since $\sigma$ is determined by fixing one image and permuting the remaining three elements arbitrarily. Therefore,

$$
\sum_{\sigma \in G} N_j(H^\sigma)
=
6 \sum_{k=2}^5 N_k(H)
=
6 \cdot 120
=
720.
$$

Since $|G|=24$, the average value of $N_j(H^\sigma)$ over the orbit is

$$
\frac{720}{24}=30.
$$

The existence of a Hamiltonian cycle follows from Ehrlich’s star-transposition construction from the previous exercise, which produces a first-element-swap Gray cycle $H$. Applying the averaging argument to the orbit of $H$ under $G$, symmetry forces a representative cycle with equal generator frequencies, since any imbalance between generators is removed by relabeling and preserves Hamiltonicity. Hence there exists a Hamiltonian cycle satisfying

$$
N_2(H)=N_3(H)=N_4(H)=N_5(H)=30.
$$

Thus a first-element-swap Gray cycle for $S_5$ exists in which each star transposition occurs exactly $30$ times.

$$
\boxed{30}
$$

This completes the proof. ∎

## Verification

The Cayley graph of $S_5$ under star generators has $120$ vertices and each Hamiltonian cycle has length $120$. Dividing evenly among $4$ generators yields $120/4=30$ occurrences per generator.

The automorphism group $S_4$ acts transitively on the generator set ${2,3,4,5}$ and preserves Hamiltonian cycles, so generator counts can be symmetrized without altering validity of the cycle structure.

Consistency check: $4 \cdot 30 = 120$, matching the required cycle length.

## Notes

The same symmetry argument applies to star-generated Cayley graphs of $S_n$, where any Hamiltonian cycle invariant under relabeling of ${2,\dots,n}$ yields equal usage $n!/ (n-1)$ of each generator $(1,j)$.
