## Solution

Let the distinct symbols be ordered $1<2<3<4<5<6<9$ with multiplicities

$$
n_1=2,\quad n_2=1,\quad n_3=1,\quad n_4=1,\quad n_5=2,\quad n_6=1,\quad n_7=1,
$$

so $n=9$. The permutation is

$$
a_1a_2\ldots a_9 = 3\,1\,4\,1\,5\,9\,2\,6\,5.
$$

Under Algorithm L for multisets, the rank coincides with the lexicographic rank induced by the same ordering, since the generation proceeds through the mixed-radix inversion system of Section 7.2.1.1, with admissible digit ranges determined by remaining multiplicities. The rank is computed by summing, at each position $i$, the number of admissible smaller choices multiplied by the number of completions of the remaining multiset.

For a remaining multiset with counts $m_1,\dots,m_t$ and total size $r$, the number of permutations equals

$$
\frac{r!}{m_1!\cdots m_t!},
$$

by the multinomial counting argument.

### Position 1

At position $1$, the remaining multiset is the full one. Symbols smaller than $3$ are $1,2$.

For $1$ at position $1$, remaining counts become

$$
(1,1,1,1,2,1,1),\quad r=8,
$$

so the number of completions is

$$
\frac{8!}{2!}=20160.
$$

For $2$ at position $1$, remaining counts become

$$
(2,0,1,1,2,1,1),\quad r=8,
$$

so the number of completions is

$$
\frac{8!}{2!\,2!}=10080.
$$

The contribution at position $1$ is

$$
20160+10080=30240.
$$

### Position 2

After fixing $3$, the remaining symbols are $1,2,4,5,5,6,9$. The chosen symbol is $1$, the smallest available, so no smaller symbol contributes. The contribution is $0$.

### Position 3

Remaining multiset is $1,2,4,5,5,6,9$. The chosen symbol is $4$. Symbols smaller than $4$ are $1,2$.

For $1$ at position $3$, remaining counts become $(0,1,1,2,1,1)$ with $r=6$, giving

$$
\frac{6!}{2!}=360.
$$

For $2$ at position $3$, remaining counts become $(1,0,1,2,1,1)$ with $r=6$, giving the same value

$$
\frac{6!}{2!}=360.
$$

Contribution:

$$
360+360=720.
$$

### Position 4

Remaining multiset is $1,2,5,5,6,9$. The chosen symbol is $1$, hence no smaller contribution.

### Position 5

Remaining multiset is $2,5,5,6,9$. The chosen symbol is $5$. The only smaller symbol is $2$.

Placing $2$ leaves $(5,5,6,9)$, so

$$
\frac{4!}{2!}=12.
$$

Contribution: $12$.

### Position 6

Remaining multiset is $2,5,6,9$. The chosen symbol is $9$. Smaller symbols are $2,5,6$.

Each choice leaves three distinct symbols, hence each yields

$$
3!=6.
$$

Contribution:

$$
6+6+6=18.
$$

### Position 7

Remaining multiset is $2,5,6$. The chosen symbol is $2$, the smallest available, so contribution is $0$.

### Position 8

Remaining multiset is $5,6$. The chosen symbol is $6$. The only smaller symbol is $5$.

Placing $5$ leaves a single symbol, so the completion count is $1$.

Contribution: $1$.

### Position 9

No contribution.

### Total rank

Summing all contributions,

$$
30240+720+12+18+1 = 30991.
$$

This is the lexicographic rank of the permutation under Algorithm L for the given multiset.

$$
\boxed{30991}
$$
