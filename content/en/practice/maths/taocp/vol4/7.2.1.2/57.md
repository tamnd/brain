**Exercise 57.** [*HM22*] [HM22] What is the average number of interchanges made in step E5?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 7m26s

## Solution

Let $X(m)$ denote the number of interchanges performed in step E5 when the algorithm processes the integer $m$, with $0 \le m < n!$.

In Algorithm E, step E5 performs a cyclic shift of a block of length $k$, where $k = \rho!(m) + 1$. The execution of a cyclic shift of $k$ elements by adjacent transpositions requires exactly $k-1$ interchanges, since each adjacent interchange moves one element one position, and transforming a cycle of length $k$ into its shifted form reduces to decomposing the cycle into $k-1$ adjacent swaps.

Hence

$$
X(m) = k - 1 = \rho!(m).
$$

The average number of interchanges over the full run of the algorithm equals

$$
\frac{1}{n!}\sum_{m=0}^{n!-1} \rho!(m).
$$

For each integer $r \ge 1$, define the indicator variable

$$
I_r(m) =
\begin{cases}
1, & r! \mid m, \\
0, & \text{otherwise}.
\end{cases}
$$

By definition of $\rho!(m)$,

$$
\rho!(m) \ge r \iff r! \mid m,
$$

so

$$
\rho!(m) = \sum_{r \ge 1} I_r(m),
$$

where the sum is finite because $r! > n!$ implies $I_r(m)=0$ for all $m$.

Summing over all $m$ gives

$$
\sum_{m=0}^{n!-1} \rho!(m)
= \sum_{r \ge 1} \sum_{m=0}^{n!-1} I_r(m).
$$

For fixed $r$, the condition $r! \mid m$ holds exactly for the multiples of $r!$ in ${0,1,\dots,n!-1}$. Their number is

$$
\frac{n!}{r!},
$$

since $n!$ is divisible by $r!$ whenever $r \le n$.

Therefore,

$$
\sum_{m=0}^{n!-1} I_r(m) = \frac{n!}{r!}.
$$

Substitution yields

$$
\sum_{m=0}^{n!-1} \rho!(m)
= \sum_{r=1}^{n-1} \frac{n!}{r!}.
$$

Dividing by $n!$ gives the average number of interchanges in step E5:

$$
\frac{1}{n!}\sum_{m=0}^{n!-1} \rho!(m)
= \sum_{r=1}^{n-1} \frac{1}{r!}.
$$

Thus the required average is

$$
\boxed{\sum_{r=1}^{n-1} \frac{1}{r!}}.
$$
