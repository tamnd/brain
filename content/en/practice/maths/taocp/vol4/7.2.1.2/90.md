[Section 7.2.1.2: Generating All Permutations](../)

**Exercise 90.** [*M21*] [M21] Algorithm V can be used to produce all permutations that are h-ordered
for all h in a given set, namely all a′
1 . . . a′
n such that a′
j < a′
j+h for 1 ≤j ≤n −h
(see Section 5.2.1). Analyze the running time of Algorithm V when it generates all
permutations that are both 2-ordered and 3-ordered.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 56s

## Solution

Let the required relations be

$$
a'_j<a'_{j+2}\qquad(1\le j\le n-2),
$$

and

$$
a'_j<a'_{j+3}\qquad(1\le j\le n-3).
$$

Algorithm V generates all inverse topological sorts of the corresponding partial order. Its running time is governed by the quantities

$$
t_0=t_1=1,\qquad
t_j=t_{j-1}+t_{j-r},
$$

whenever the largest predecessor of $j$ is $j-r$; if $j$ has no predecessor then

$$
t_j=2t_{j-1},
$$

as in equation (51).

The first task is therefore to determine the predecessor relation.

For $j\ge4$, the element $j$ has incoming relations

$$
j-2\prec j,\qquad j-3\prec j.
$$

Since

$$
j-3\prec j-1\prec j
$$

by the $2$-ordering relation, the edge $j-3\prec j$ is implied transitively. Hence the only immediate predecessor of $j$ is $j-2$.

For $j=2$, there is no predecessor.

For $j=3$, the only predecessor is $1$.

Thus the Hasse diagram consists of the two independent chains

$$
1\prec3\prec5\prec7\prec\cdots,
$$

$$
2\prec4\prec6\prec8\prec\cdots.
$$

Consequently,

$$
t_0=t_1=t_2=1,
$$

and for every $j\ge3$,

$$
t_j=t_{j-1}+t_{j-2}.
$$

Therefore the sequence is

$$
1,1,1,2,3,5,8,13,\ldots,
$$

so

$$
t_j=F_j,
$$

where $F_0=F_1=1$ and

$$
F_j=F_{j-1}+F_{j-2}\qquad(j\ge2).
$$

Equation (51) shows that the total number of executions of the principal loop is

$$
M=\sum_{j=1}^n t_j.
$$

Since

$$
\sum_{j=1}^nF_j=F_{n+2}-2,
$$

we obtain

$$
M=F_{n+2}-2.
$$

The number of generated permutations is the number of linear extensions of two independent chains. If

$$
m=\Bigl\lceil\frac n2\Bigr\rceil,\qquad
\ell=\Bigl\lfloor\frac n2\Bigr\rfloor,
$$

the chains have lengths $m$ and $\ell$. Every linear extension is determined by choosing the positions occupied by the elements of one chain, hence

$$
N=\binom{n}{m}.
$$

To compare $M$ with $N$, use the asymptotic formulas

$$
F_{n+2}
=
\frac{\phi^{\,n+2}}{\sqrt5}
+O(\phi^{-n}),
\qquad
\phi=\frac{1+\sqrt5}{2},
$$

and

$$
\binom{n}{\lfloor n/2\rfloor}
\sim
\frac{2^n}{\sqrt{\pi n/2}}.
$$

Therefore

$$
\frac{M}{N}
\sim
\frac{\phi^2}{\sqrt5}\,
\sqrt{\frac{\pi n}{2}}
\left(\frac{\phi}{2}\right)^n.
$$

Since

$$
\frac{\phi}{2}=0.809016\ldots<1,
$$

this ratio decreases exponentially to $0$.

Hence the total running time of Algorithm V is

$$
\Theta(M+N)=\Theta(N),
$$

because $M=o(N)$.

The algorithm therefore requires constant amortized time per generated permutation. More explicitly,

$$
M=F_{n+2}-2,\qquad
N=\binom{n}{\lceil n/2\rceil},
$$

and

$$
\boxed{\frac{M}{N}\longrightarrow0,\qquad
\text{so the running time is }\Theta\!\left(\binom{n}{\lceil n/2\rceil}\right).}
$$
