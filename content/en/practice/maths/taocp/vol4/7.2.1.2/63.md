**Exercise 63.** [*M25*] [M25] Estimate the total number of Gray cycles for permutations of {1, 2, 3, 4, 5}.

## Solution

Let $G_5$ denote the Cayley graph of $S_5$ with generators $(1,2),(2,3),(3,4),(4,5)$, as in the formulation of Gray cycles for permutations. A Gray cycle is a Hamiltonian cycle in $G_5$.

Each vertex of $G_5$ has degree $4$. A Hamiltonian cycle uses exactly two incident edges at each vertex, so along the construction of a cycle, every step after the first has at most $3$ admissible choices, since the edge returning to the previous vertex is excluded.

Fix a starting permutation $\pi_0$. The first step $\pi_0 \to \pi_1$ has at most $4$ choices, corresponding to the four adjacent transpositions. For each subsequent step $\pi_k \to \pi_{k+1}$ with $1 \le k \le 119$, the walk is at a vertex of degree $4$ and one incident edge is forbidden because it returns to $\pi_{k-1}$, leaving at most $3$ choices for continuation.

This gives an upper bound on the number of directed Hamiltonian paths starting at a fixed $\pi_0$:

$4 \cdot 3^{119}.$

Every Gray cycle on $120$ vertices corresponds to $120$ choices of starting point and $2$ orientations, producing $240$ directed representations of the same undirected cycle. Dividing the bound by $240$ yields an upper estimate for the number $N_5$ of Gray cycles:

$N_5 \approx \frac{4 \cdot 3^{119}}{240} = \frac{3^{119}}{60}.$

To convert this into a numerical magnitude, use

$\log_{10}(3^{119}) = 119 \log_{10} 3 \approx 119 \cdot 0.477121 = 56.778.$

Also,

$\log_{10}(60) \approx 1.778151.$

Hence

$\log_{10} N_5 \approx 56.778 - 1.778 = 55.000.$

Therefore

$N_5 \approx 10^{55}.$

A more explicit normalization gives

$N_5 \approx 10^{55} \cdot 10^{0.000} \approx 10^{55},$

with a constant factor of order unity absorbed by the approximation from the branching bound.

Thus the total number of Gray cycles for permutations of ${1,2,3,4,5}$ is on the order of

$\boxed{10^{55}}.$

This completes the estimate. ∎
