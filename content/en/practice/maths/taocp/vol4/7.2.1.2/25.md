**Exercise 25.** &#9654; [*M21*] [M21] Devise a fast way to compute min(a · s) and max(a · s) over all valid
permutations a1 . . . a10 of {0, . . . , 9}, given the signature vector s = (s1, . . . , s10) and
the first-letter set F of an alphametic problem. (Such a procedure makes it possible
to rule out many cases quickly when a large family of alphametics is being considered,
as in several of the exercises that follow, because a solution can exist only when
min(a · s) ≤0 ≤max(a · s).)

## Solution

Let the ten letters be indexed by $1,\dots,10$ with signature vector $s=(s_1,\dots,s_{10})$. A valid assignment is a permutation $a=(a_1,\dots,a_{10})$ of ${0,1,\dots,9}$ such that $a_j\neq 0$ for all $j\in F$. The objective is to optimize the linear form

$a\cdot s=\sum_{j=1}^{10} a_j s_j.$

### Unconstrained extremal structure

For any two indices $i,k$, exchanging $a_i$ and $a_k$ changes the value by

$(a_k-a_i)(s_i-s_k).$

Hence, by the rearrangement inequality, among all unrestricted permutations, the maximum is achieved by sorting $s_1,\dots,s_{10}$ in nonincreasing order and assigning digits $9,8,\dots,0$ in that order; the minimum is obtained by reversing the digit order.

Let $\sigma$ be a permutation such that

$s_{\sigma(1)}\ge s_{\sigma(2)}\ge \cdots \ge s_{\sigma(10)}.$

Define the unconstrained maximizer $a^_$ by

$a^*_{\sigma(k)} = 10-k,$

and the unconstrained minimizer $b^_$ by

$b^*_{\sigma(k)} = k-1.$

### Effect of the leading-letter constraint

The constraint only forbids $0$ from appearing in positions in $F$. Since $0$ contributes nothing to $a\cdot s$, the location of $0$ is determined entirely by which placement causes the least damage to the remaining weighted assignment.

In the unconstrained maximizer $a^*$, the digit $0$ is placed at the index

$i_0=\sigma(10),$

the position of smallest signature.

If $i_0\notin F$, then $a^*$ is feasible and is optimal.

Assume $i_0\in F$. Let

$N=\{1,\dots,10\}\setminus F.$

Among indices in $N$, choose

$j=\arg\min_{k\in N} s_k.$

This index exists since $N\neq\emptyset$ (otherwise the problem has no feasible assignment).

In $a^*$, index $j$ carries some digit $d_j>0$. Construct a modified assignment $a$ by swapping the digits at $i_0$ and $j$:

$a_{i_0}=d_j,\qquad a_j=0,$

and all other entries unchanged.

### Optimality of the modification

Compare $a$ with $a^*$:

$a\cdot s - a^*\cdot s = d_j s_{i_0} - d_j s_j = d_j(s_{i_0}-s_j).$

Since $i_0$ is the index of minimum signature over all indices and $j\in N$ is chosen among indices with $s_j\ge s_{i_0}$, the quantity satisfies $s_{i_0}\le s_j$, hence

$a\cdot s \le a^*\cdot s.$

Any feasible permutation must place $0$ at some index in $N$. In any optimal unconstrained solution, all digits except $0$ are already optimally assigned to maximize contribution. Moving $0$ away from $i_0$ forces it into some $j\in N$ and necessarily replaces a strictly larger digit at $j$ by $0$, while giving that digit to $i_0$, whose signature is minimal. The exchange argument above shows that among all feasible placements of $0$, choosing $j$ with minimal $s_j$ over $N$ minimizes the loss relative to the unconstrained optimum, hence yields the global constrained maximum.

Therefore the constrained maximum is obtained by the construction above.

### Minimization

For the minimum, apply the same argument to $-s$. Maximizing $a\cdot(-s)$ is equivalent to minimizing $a\cdot s$. Thus we reverse the digit ordering.

Let

$\tau(1),\dots,\tau(10)$

be such that

$s_{\tau(1)}\le s_{\tau(2)}\le \cdots \le s_{\tau(10)}.$

Define the unconstrained minimizer $b^*$ by

$b^*_{\tau(k)} = 10-k.$

Let

$i_0'=\tau(10),$

the index of largest signature. If $i_0'\notin F$, this is optimal.

Otherwise choose

$j'=\arg\max_{k\in N} s_k,$

since swapping $0$ away from the most negative impact requires placing it where $s_k$ is largest among allowed positions.

Construct $b$ by swapping digits at $i_0'$ and $j'$ as above. The same exchange computation shows this modification yields the minimal feasible value.

### Result

The extremal values are obtained by a single sorting step of the signatures followed by one correction swap if the unconstrained placement of $0$ violates the leading-letter restriction. The correction uses only extremal elements of $s$ within and outside $F$, so no further rearrangement can improve the objective.

This completes the proof. ∎
