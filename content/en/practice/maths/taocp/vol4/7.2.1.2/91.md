[Section 7.2.1.2: Generating All Permutations](../)

**Exercise 91.** [*HM21*] [HM21] Analyze the running time of Algorithm V when it is used with the
relations (49) to find perfect matchings.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 55s  

---

## Solution

Algorithm V generates all inverse topological sorts of the partial order defined by the relations (49). In the application to perfect matchings, the relations (49) describe the Ferrers diagram associated with the bipartite graph, therefore each inverse topological sort corresponds to one perfect matching, and every perfect matching is generated exactly once.

The analysis required here is the same as the analysis leading to equation (51), specialized to this family of partial orders.

Let $N$ denote the number of perfect matchings. Let $t_j$ be the quantities defined before equation (51), and let

$$
M=\sum_{j=1}^n t_j.
$$

Equation (51) states that Algorithm V performs

$$
O(M)
$$

total work while generating all $N$ objects, and the amortized cost per generated object is

$$
\frac{M}{N}.
$$

For the relations (49), every principal interval $[1\mathinner{\ldotp\ldotp}j]$ again represents a Ferrers diagram. Removing the largest element of an inverse topological sort corresponds to deleting an outer corner of that diagram. Consequently, the quantity $t_j$ is precisely the number of perfect matchings of the subproblem determined by the first $j$ elements.

The sequence ${t_j}$ therefore satisfies the same recurrence as the numbers of perfect matchings of nested Ferrers diagrams. Every perfect matching of the $j$-element instance extends to at most two perfect matchings after the next element is introduced. Hence

$$
t_j\le 2t_{j-1}
\qquad(1\le j\le n),
$$

with $t_0=1$. By induction,

$$
t_j\le 2^j.
$$

More importantly, every perfect matching of the full instance contributes to at most one value of $t_j$ for each level $j$, so

$$
t_j\le N.
$$

Combining these estimates gives

$$
t_j\le\min(2^j,N).
$$

Therefore

$$
M
 =\sum_{j=1}^n t_j
 \le
 \sum_{j=1}^{\lfloor\lg N\rfloor}2^j
 +
 \sum_{j=\lfloor\lg N\rfloor+1}^{n}N.
$$

Since

$$
\sum_{j=1}^{\lfloor\lg N\rfloor}2^j
<
2N,
$$

it follows that

$$
M
<
2N+nN
=
(n+2)N.
$$

Thus Algorithm V requires

$$
O(nN)
$$

time to generate all perfect matchings.

The initialization and termination costs are $O(n)$, which are absorbed into this bound. Hence the amortized running time is

$$
O(n)
$$

per perfect matching generated.

The final result is

$$
\boxed{\text{Algorithm V generates all }N\text{ perfect matchings in }O(nN)\text{ time,}
\text{ with }O(n)\text{ amortized time per matching.}
}
$$
