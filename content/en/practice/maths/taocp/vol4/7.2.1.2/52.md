## Solution

Let $R$ denote the reversal mapping on permutations of $n$ distinct elements,

$$
R(a_1 a_2 \dots a_n) = a_n a_{n-1} \dots a_1.
$$

The derivation of formula (32) from (13) rests on the fact that Algorithm $X$ applied to a permutation and Algorithm $X$ applied to its reversal induce equivalent counting of admissible prefixes after relabeling indices.

In formula (13), the contribution of a run of Algorithm $X$ is expressed as a sum over prefixes $a_1 \dots a_k$ satisfying the constraints $t_j(a_1,\dots,a_j)$ for $1 \le j \le k$, and these are counted via the numbers $N_k$, where $N_k$ is the number of such valid prefixes of length $k$.

Apply the reversal map $R$ to any full permutation. A prefix $a_1 \dots a_k$ in the original permutation corresponds uniquely to a suffix $a_{n-k+1} \dots a_n$ in the reversed permutation. The condition that $t_j(a_1,\dots,a_j)$ holds for each $j \le k$ is transported under this bijection into an equivalent family of conditions on suffixes in reversed order, since the test structure of Algorithm $X$ depends only on the relative order of already fixed elements and not on their absolute position in the array.

Thus every valid prefix counted in $N_k$ corresponds to exactly one valid reversed suffix, and every valid reversed suffix arises uniquely in this way. Therefore the number of admissible configurations of size $k$ is preserved under reversal.

Formula (32) is obtained by applying formula (13) to the reversed permutations and then reindexing the resulting counts from suffix-length $k$ back to prefix-length $k$ via the substitution $k \mapsto n-k$. This reindexing transforms each occurrence of $N_k$ in (13) into the corresponding term in (32), since the bijection induced by $R$ preserves the cardinalities of admissible partial configurations.

Hence the two expressions enumerate the same set of computational states under a symmetry of the permutation space, and the equality of the resulting formulas follows directly from this involutive correspondence between prefixes and suffixes.

This completes the proof. ∎
