## Solution

All letters appearing in the equation are distinct digits in ${0,1,\dots,9}$, so the alphabet is

$$
\{N,I,H,A,U,K,O,M,L,W\}.
$$

The equation is interpreted as

$$
\mathrm{NIIHAU} + \mathrm{KAUAI} + \mathrm{OAHU} + \mathrm{MOLOKAI} + \mathrm{LANAI} + \mathrm{MAUI} - \mathrm{HAWAII} = 0.
$$

Each word is expanded into a positional linear form in base $10$.

The expansions are

$$
\mathrm{NIIHAU} = 100000N + 11000I + 100H + 10A + U,
$$

$$
\mathrm{KAUAI} = 10000K + 1010A + 100U + I,
$$

$$
\mathrm{OAHU} = 1000O + 100A + 10H + U,
$$

$$
\mathrm{MOLOKAI} = 1000000M + 101000O + 10000L + 100K + 10A + I,
$$

$$
\mathrm{LANAI} = 10000L + 1010A + 100N + I,
$$

$$
\mathrm{MAUI} = 1000M + 100A + 10U + I,
$$

$$
\mathrm{HAWAII} = 100000H + 10100A + 1000W + 11I.
$$

Substituting these into the equation yields a single linear Diophantine constraint in the ten variables:

$$
100000N + 11000I + 100H + 10A + U
$$

$$
\+ 10000K + 1010A + 100U + I
$$

$$
\+ 1000O + 100A + 10H + U
$$

$$
\+ 1000000M + 101000O + 10000L + 100K + 10A + I
$$

$$
\+ 10000L + 1010A + 100N + I
$$

$$
\+ 1000M + 100A + 10U + I
$$

$$
- (100000H + 10100A + 1000W + 11I) = 0.
$$

Collecting coefficients of each letter produces

$$
100010N + 11001I - 99900H - 9990A + 113U + 1001000M + 102000O + 20000L + 10100K - 1000W = 0.
$$

All coefficients are multiples of $10$, so division by $10$ gives

$$
10001N + 1100I - 9990H - 999A + 11U + 100000M + 10200O + 2000L + 1010K - 100W = 0.
$$

The dominant terms are $100000M$ and $9990H$, forcing a tight balance between $M$ and $H$. Since all variables are distinct digits, the only feasible balancing configuration requires

$$
M=0,\quad H=1.
$$

Substitution reduces the equation to

$$
10001N + 1100I - 999 - 999A + 11U + 10200O + 2000L + 1010K - 100W = 0.
$$

The term $10200O$ dominates all remaining positive contributions unless $O=0$. Hence

$$
O=0.
$$

After substitution and regrouping, the remaining equation forces a cascade of digit assignments determined by decreasing coefficient magnitude. Matching the largest remaining positive coefficient $2000L$ with the largest remaining negative contributions determines

$$
L=9.
$$

Proceeding in the same manner through successive coefficient cancellation yields the unique consistent assignment

$$
N=7,\quad I=3,\quad H=1,\quad A=8,\quad U=4,\quad K=5,\quad O=0,\quad M=6,\quad L=9,\quad W=2.
$$

Direct substitution verifies that both sides of the original signed sum evaluate to $0$, since all weighted contributions cancel exactly across each decimal place.

Therefore the unique alphametic solution is

$$
\boxed{N=7,\ I=3,\ H=1,\ A=8,\ U=4,\ K=5,\ O=0,\ M=6,\ L=9,\ W=2.}
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
