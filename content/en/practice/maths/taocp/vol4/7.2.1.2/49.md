## Solution

Algorithm X constructs permutations by backtracking over prefixes $a_1,\dots,a_k$ subject to the tests $t_1,\dots,t_k$. When all tests $t_j(a_1,\dots,a_j)$ are always true, every prefix is admissible, so every node of the implicit search tree is visited.

For each $k$, the number $N_k$ of admissible prefixes of length $k$ equals the number of ways to choose and order $k$ distinct elements from $n$, hence

$$
N_k = n(n-1)\cdots(n-k+1) = \frac{n!}{(n-k)!}.
$$

Algorithm X performs work at each admissible prefix of length $k$ proportional to the local control actions associated with level $k$. From the structure of the algorithm, each such prefix contributes a constant number of step executions depending only on $k$, so the total number of step executions is proportional to

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n} N_k.
$$

Substituting the expression for $N_k$ gives

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{n} \frac{n!}{(n-k)!}
= n!\sum_{k=0}^{n}\frac{1}{(n-k)!}.
$$

Reindexing with $j=n-k$ yields

$$
n!\sum_{j=0}^{n}\frac{1}{j!}.
$$

The partial sum $\sum_{j=0}^{n} \frac{1}{j!}$ increases monotonically to $e$, and for every $n\ge 1$ it satisfies

$$
\sum_{j=0}^{n}\frac{1}{j!} < e < \sum_{j=0}^{n}\frac{1}{j!} + \frac{1}{n\cdot n!}.
$$

Hence the total running time of Algorithm X in this case is

$$
n!\sum_{j=0}^{n}\frac{1}{j!} = (e + o(1))\,n!.
$$

Algorithm L generates all permutations in lexicographic order by transforming each permutation into the next one through a fixed local update procedure. Each transition modifies a bounded number of array positions determined by the rightmost ascent and subsequent swap-and-reversal structure, and every permutation except the last is produced by one such transition. Therefore the total number of structural updates performed by Algorithm L is exactly $n!-1$, and the total running time is proportional to $n!$.

Comparing the two totals, Algorithm X performs asymptotically $e n!$ units of work while Algorithm L performs asymptotically $n!$ units of work. The ratio of running times satisfies

$$
\frac{T_X}{T_L} \to e.
$$

This completes the comparison. ∎
