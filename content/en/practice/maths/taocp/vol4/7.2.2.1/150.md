This is a finite exact cover problem with an added degree constraint enforcing that every used endpoint has degree 2 in the resulting connection graph.

The key structural consequence of the specific 19th-century chain set in Figure 150 (the standard TAOCP instance) is that:

## 3. Structural reduction

Because each endpoint must be matched exactly once, the solution graph is a disjoint union of cycles. The “endless chain” condition forces this union to be a single cycle covering all pieces.

In the specific instance of Figure 150, the local geometry of the pieces eliminates all branching:
