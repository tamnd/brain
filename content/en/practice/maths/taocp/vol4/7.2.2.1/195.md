[Section 7.2.2.1: Dancing Links](../)

**Exercise 195.** &#9654; [*M22*] Approximately how many updates does Algorithm X perform, when it is asked to find all of the perfect matchings of the graph (8g)?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 7m24s  

---

The original argument fails because it tries to infer global work from root-to-leaf paths and an ill-defined “average level” of the search tree. In Algorithm X, work is not organized by disjoint solution paths; it is accumulated over a single DFS traversal in which partial solutions are shared across many complete matchings. The correct approach is to count nodes by depth and weight each node by the actual local update cost.

We restart the analysis from the exact cover structure of perfect matchings in $K_{2q}$.

## 1. Exact cover formulation and structure of the search

For $K_{2q}$:

- items are vertices,
- options are edges,
- a solution is a perfect matching.

A node at depth $k$ in the search corresponds to a partial matching of size $k$, i.e., $k$ disjoint edges covering $2k$ vertices.

The number of such partial matchings is:

$$
N_k
= \binom{2q}{2k}(2k-1)!!.
$$

Using $(2k-1)!! = \frac{(2k)!}{2^k k!}$, this becomes

$$
N_k
= \frac{(2q)!}{(2q-2k)! \, 2^k k!}.
$$

This counts each node exactly once in the DFS search tree of Algorithm X.

## 2. Cost of updates at a node

At a node where $k$ edges have been chosen, the remaining graph has

$$
2(q-k)
$$

vertices. Each vertex has degree $2(q-k)-1$, so each cover or uncover operation costs

$$
\Theta(q-k)
$$

pointer updates.

Each node performs a constant number of such cover/uncover steps per branching decision, so the cost per node at depth $k$ is:

$$
\Theta(q-k).
$$

This is the correct local cost model; the earlier solution incorrectly used a per-path sum instead of per-node cost.

## 3. Total number of updates

The total number of updates is therefore

$$
T_q
= \sum_{k=0}^{q-1} N_k \cdot \Theta(q-k).
$$

We now identify the dominant contribution.

## 4. Asymptotic dominance of near-leaf levels

We compare adjacent levels.

At the top level:

$$
N_q = (2q-1)!!.
$$

At level $q-1$:

$$
N_{q-1}
= \binom{2q}{2}(2q-3)!!
= \frac{(2q)!}{2\cdot 2^{q-1}(q-1)!}.
$$

Using $(2q-1)!! = \frac{(2q)!}{2^q q!}$, we get

$$
\frac{N_{q-1}}{N_q}
= q.
$$

So level $q-1$ alone already contains $q \cdot (2q-1)!!$ nodes.

Its cost per node is $\Theta(1)$, hence its total contribution is:

$$
\Theta\!\big(q(2q-1)!!\big).
$$

Now consider lower levels $k = q-j$. One can similarly rewrite:

$$
N_{q-j}
= \frac{(2q)!}{(2j)! \, 2^{q-j} (q-j)!}
= (2q-1)!! \cdot \Theta\!\left(\frac{q^j}{j!}\right).
$$

The per-node cost at that level is $\Theta(j)$. Hence level $q-j$ contributes

$$
(2q-1)!! \cdot \Theta\!\left(\frac{q^j}{j!} \cdot j\right).
$$

This is maximized at $j=1$, and higher $j$ terms decay factorially. Therefore the dominant contribution comes from level $q-1$.

## 5. Total update count

Summing over all levels:

- level $q-1$: $\Theta(q(2q-1)!!)$,
- all deeper levels: strictly lower-order multiples of $(2q-1)!!$.

Thus,

$$
T_q = \Theta\!\big(q(2q-1)!!\big).
$$

## Final answer

$$
\boxed{\Theta\!\big(q(2q-1)!!\big)}
$$

Algorithm X performs on the order of $q(2q-1)!!$ pointer updates when enumerating all perfect matchings of $K_{2q}$.
