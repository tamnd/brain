## Solution

Let the original exact cover instance of Fig. 73 contain two options corresponding to the two flipped-over Y pentomino placements, labeled $8$ and $b$. The requirement is to forbid selecting both of these options simultaneously.

Introduce a new item $V_{1b}$, declared as a _secondary item_ in the sense of Algorithm X, and modify the incidence structure as follows. In the option representation, append the item $V_{1b}$ to each of the two forbidden options, replacing

$$
8 \quad \text{by} \quad 8\, V_{1b}, \qquad b \quad \text{by} \quad b\, V_{1b}.
$$

All other options remain unchanged, and no additional occurrences of $V_{1b}$ are introduced.

In the corresponding 0–1 matrix, this construction adds a new column indexed by $V_{1b}$, with entries $1$ precisely in the rows corresponding to options $8$ and $b$, and $0$ elsewhere.

In Algorithm X, $V_{1b}$ is treated as a secondary item, so it is not required to be covered. However, it still participates in the dancing-links structure exactly as other items, so any selected option that contains $V_{1b}$ covers that item via the usual operation $\text{cover}(V_{1b})$ defined in (12). Consequently, if both options $8$ and $b$ were selected in a purported solution, then $V_{1b}$ would be covered twice, which contradicts the requirement that each item is covered at most once in the maintained exact-cover state. Therefore the two flipped-over Y placements cannot both appear in any valid solution.

All other solutions of the original problem remain valid, since the added item $V_{1b}$ imposes no additional restriction beyond excluding the simultaneous choice of the two specified options.

This completes the construction. ∎
