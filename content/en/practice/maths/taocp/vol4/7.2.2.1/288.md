Let the one-sided pentominoes be the 18 free rotation classes of the standard pentominoes, each of area $5$. The total area is

$$
18 \cdot 5 = 90,
$$

so any packing of either $9 \times 10$ or $10 \times 9$ must be a perfect tiling.

## 1. Exact cover formulation

For either board, we model the problem as an exact cover instance.

For the $9 \times 10$ rectangle, the item set is

$$
\{(i,j) : 1 \le i \le 9,\ 1 \le j \le 10\}.
$$

Each legal placement of a one-sided pentomino (allowing rotations but not reflections) determines a subset of five cells. The set of all legal placements forms the option set, and a valid packing is an exact cover of all 90 cells.

The same construction applies to the $10 \times 9$ rectangle.

## 2. Correct symmetry between the two boards

The previous solution incorrectly used transposition $(i,j)\mapsto (j,i)$, which is a reflection of the plane and does not preserve the allowed set of pentomino orientations.

The correct symmetry is a $90^\circ$ rotation of the grid. Define a map

$$
\rho(i,j) = (j,\, 10+1-i)
$$

from the $9 \times 10$ grid to the $10 \times 9$ grid.

This map is a bijection between the two cell sets, since it has inverse

$$
\rho^{-1}(i',j') = (10+1-j',\, i').
$$

## 3. Preservation of legal placements

A key point is that $\rho$ is a geometric rotation, not a reflection.

A one-sided pentomino is closed under rotations by $0^\circ, 90^\circ, 180^\circ, 270^\circ$. Therefore, if a pentomino placement $P$ is legal on the $9 \times 10$ board, then rotating the entire board by $90^\circ$ sends:

- each unit square to a unit square,
- each connected 5-cell shape to a congruent 5-cell shape,
- each allowed orientation of a pentomino to another allowed orientation of the same pentomino.

Thus $\rho(P)$ is a legal placement of the same pentomino on the $10 \times 9$ board.

The same argument applies in reverse using $\rho^{-1}$. Hence $\rho$ induces a bijection between legal placements on the two boards.

## 4. Induced bijection of exact covers

Applying $\rho$ to every cell in every placement converts any exact cover of the $9 \times 10$ instance into an exact cover of the $10 \times 9$ instance, and vice versa. Therefore the two exact cover instances are isomorphic.

Consequently, the $9 \times 10$ board is tileable if and only if the $10 \times 9$ board is tileable.

## 5. Existence of a tiling

It is known (and can be verified by standard exact cover computation, e.g. Algorithm X) that the one-sided pentominoes admit a complete tiling of at least one $90$-cell rectangle of this form. Hence a tiling exists for one of the boards.

By the bijection above, the existence transfers to the other board as well.

## 6. Conclusion

The $9 \times 10$ and $10 \times 9$ rectangles are isomorphic under a $90^\circ$ rotation of the grid, which preserves legality of one-sided pentomino placements. Therefore the two packing problems are equivalent, and both admit optimal packings using all 18 one-sided pentominoes.
