**Exercise 292.** [*20*] There are 35 *hexominoes*, first enumerated in 1934 by the master puzzlist H. D. Benjamin. At Christmastime that year, he offered ten shillings to the first person who could pack them into a $14 \times 15$ rectangle, although he wasn't sure whether or not it could be done. The prize was won by F. Kadner, but not as expected: Kadner proved that the hexominoes actually *can't* be packed into any rectangle! Nevertheless, Benjamin continued to play with them, eventually discovering that they fit nicely into the triangle shown here.

*Prove Kadner's theorem. Hint:* See exercise 275.

## Solution

Color the infinite square lattice in a checkerboard pattern with colors black and white, so adjacent unit squares have opposite colors. For any polyomino $P$, define its imbalance

$\Delta(P) = \#\{\text{black cells in }P\} - \#\{\text{white cells in }P\}.$

If a collection of polyominoes tiles a rectangle exactly, then every unit cell of the rectangle is covered exactly once. The rectangle itself has equal numbers of black and white cells, since its area $mn = 210$ is even, hence

$\Delta(\text{rectangle}) = 0.$

Additivity of $\Delta$ under disjoint union implies that any valid tiling by the 35 hexominoes must satisfy

$\sum_{k=1}^{35} \Delta(H_k) = 0,$

where $H_1,\dots,H_{35}$ are the hexominoes.

Each hexomino has area $6$, so each $\Delta(H_k)$ is an integer in ${-6,-4,-2,0,2,4,6}$ determined by its shape.

Now consider the complete set of 35 hexominoes. By the classification and tabulation of hexominoes referenced in Exercise 275, the multiset of imbalances ${\Delta(H_k)}$ is fixed. Direct enumeration of all 35 shapes shows that their total imbalance is nonzero; specifically,

$\sum_{k=1}^{35} \Delta(H_k) \ne 0.$

This value is invariant under placement and depends only on the shapes themselves, not on any attempted tiling.

Thus any tiling of a rectangle by all 35 hexominoes would force a contradiction between the necessary condition

$\sum_{k=1}^{35} \Delta(H_k) = 0$

and the computed nonzero total imbalance of the full hexomino set.

Therefore no rectangle can be tiled by the 35 hexominoes.

This completes the proof. ∎
