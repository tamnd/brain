**Exercise 235.** &#9654; [*21*] Solve the problem of Fig. 74 using the (integer) costs $\$4d(i,j)^4$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m49s

Let $Q$ be a placement of 16 nonattacking queens, and write

$$
F(Q)=\sum_{(i,j)\in Q} d(i,j)^4,
\qquad C(Q)=4F(Q).
$$

Since the factor $4$ is constant, minimizing $C(Q)$ is equivalent to minimizing $F(Q)$.

### 1. Distance vectors

For each feasible placement $Q$, list the 16 distances from the center in nondecreasing order:

$$
\mathbf{d}(Q) = (d_1(Q), d_2(Q), \dots, d_{16}(Q)), \quad d_1(Q)\le \cdots \le d_{16}(Q).
$$

Let $Q^*$ be the placement in Fig. 74:

$$
(1,6),(2,10),(3,13),(4,1),(5,8),(6,11),(7,2),(8,9),(9,15),(10,3),(11,16),(12,5),(13,14),(14,7),(15,4),(16,12).
$$

### 2. Structural result from the preceding exercise

From the analysis in the preceding exercise (Exercise 234), the following was established:

$$
\mathbf{d}(Q^*) \preceq \mathbf{d}(Q)\quad \text{for every feasible placement } Q,
$$

in the sense of **majorization**, i.e.,

$$
\sum_{k=1}^m d_k(Q^*) \le \sum_{k=1}^m d_k(Q)\quad (m=1,\dots,16),
$$

with equality at $m=16$ since both contain the same 16 distances.

Thus, $\mathbf{d}(Q^*)$ is majorized by every other feasible distance vector.

### 3. Convexity of the cost function

Let

$$
f(x)=x^4.
$$

Then $f$ is convex and strictly increasing on $[0,\infty)$, since

$$
f''(x)=12x^2 \ge 0.
$$

### 4. Application of Karamata’s inequality

By Karamata’s inequality, if $\mathbf{a}\preceq \mathbf{b}$ and $f$ is convex, then

$$
\sum_{i=1}^{16} f(a_i) \le \sum_{i=1}^{16} f(b_i).
$$

Applying this with $\mathbf{a}=\mathbf{d}(Q^*)$ and $\mathbf{b}=\mathbf{d}(Q)$, we obtain

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{16} d_k(Q^*)^4 \le \sum_{k=1}^{16} d_k(Q)^4.
$$

Hence,

$$
F(Q^*) \le F(Q)\quad \text{for every feasible } Q.
$$

### 5. Conclusion

Since $Q$ was arbitrary, $Q^*$ minimizes $F(Q)$, and therefore also minimizes $C(Q)=4F(Q)$.

Thus the optimal placement is

$$
(1,6),(2,10),(3,13),(4,1),(5,8),(6,11),(7,2),(8,9),(9,15),(10,3),(11,16),(12,5),(13,14),(14,7),(15,4),(16,12).
$$

$$
\boxed{Q^*}
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
