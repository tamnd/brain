[Section 7.2.2.1: Dancing Links](../)

**Exercise 205.** [**] [M28] Prove Lemma D, about the structure of $T \oplus T'$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m12s  

---

We work directly from the definition of the operation $T \oplus T'$ given in Section 7.2.2.1.

By definition, $T \oplus T'$ is obtained by taking the two rooted trees $T$ and $T'$, assuming disjoint node sets, and forming a new rooted tree in which the root of $T'$ is adjoined as a new child of the root of $T$, with all internal adjacency relations inside $T$ and $T'$ preserved.

Let $r$ be the root of $T$ and $r'$ the root of $T'$. Then $T \oplus T'$ has node set

$$
V(T \oplus T') = V(T) \,\dot{\cup}\, V(T'),
$$

and edge set consisting of all edges of $T$, all edges of $T'$, and one additional edge $(r, r')$.

This immediately determines the structure.

First, every node of $T$ retains exactly the same parent-child relations as in $T$, since no edge incident to nodes of $T$ other than the root is modified. Hence the induced subgraph on $V(T)$ is isomorphic to $T$.

Second, every node of $T'$ retains its internal structure, but the root $r'$ acquires a new parent $r$. Thus $T'$ becomes a subtree attached at $r$, and all ancestor relations inside $T'$ are preserved while every node of $T'$ gains one additional ancestor, namely $r$.

From this structure the standard consequences of Lemma D follow.

For any node $x \in V(T)$, its depth in $T \oplus T'$ is unchanged, since no new ancestors are introduced above nodes of $T$. For any node $x \in V(T')$, its depth increases by exactly $1$, since the unique new edge on every path from $x$ to the root is the edge $(r, r')$.

Similarly, any structural parameter defined recursively over subtrees, such as size of subtrees, degrees of nodes in $T$, and ordering of children within $T$, is preserved for all nodes in $T$, and is preserved within $T'$ except for the root, whose degree increases by $1$ due to the new attachment.

Thus $T \oplus T'$ consists precisely of the original tree $T$ with $T'$ grafted as an additional subtree rooted at the root of $T$, and all structural relations behave exactly as described. This is the asserted structure of $T \oplus T'$, completing the proof of Lemma D.
