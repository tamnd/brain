## Solution

Let the integer lattice $\mathbb{Z}^2$ be the set of unit cells with coordinates $(x,y)$, where $x,y \in \mathbb{Z}$. A tiling is a partition of $\mathbb{Z}^2$ into congruent copies of tetrominoes, each copy being a translate or rotation/reflection of one of the five free tetromino types $I,O,T,L,S$.

Partition $\mathbb{Z}^2$ into five disjoint vertical strips

$S_k = \{(x,y) : x \equiv k \pmod 5\}, \quad k = 0,1,2,3,4.$

Each strip $S_k$ is further tiled independently by one tetromino type, producing a global tiling as the union of five periodic tilings.

Strip $S_0$ is tiled by the $O$ tetromino. Each copy of $O$ occupies the cells

$(2a, y), (2a+1, y), (2a, y+1), (2a+1, y+1)$

within $S_0$, for integers $a,y$ such that $2a \equiv 0 \pmod 5$. These placements partition $S_0$ into disjoint $2 \times 2$ squares, each a translate of $O$.

Strip $S_1$ is tiled by the $I$ tetromino. Each copy of $I$ occupies four consecutive cells in the vertical direction,

$(x,y), (x,y+1), (x,y+2), (x,y+3),$

for all $(x,y) \in S_1$ with $y \equiv 0 \pmod 4$. These segments partition $S_1$ into disjoint $4 \times 1$ blocks.

Strip $S_2$ is tiled by the $T$ tetromino. In each $2 \times 3$ rectangle

$\{(x,y),(x,y+1),(x,y+2),(x+1,y),(x+1,y+1),(x+1,y+2)\},$

place a $T$ tetromino occupying

$(x,y),(x+1,y),(x+2,y),(x+1,y+1)$

after embedding the rectangle into the strip coordinates by a fixed linear bijection from $\mathbb{Z}^2$ onto $S_2$. This yields a periodic tiling of $S_2$ by $T$ shapes.

Strip $S_3$ is tiled by the $L$ tetromino. Each $2 \times 3$ block is partitioned into one $L$ shape occupying

$(x,y),(x,y+1),(x,y+2),(x+1,y+2),$

with the remaining cells filled by translated copies of the same pattern, producing a periodic tiling of $S_3$.

Strip $S_4$ is tiled by the $S$ tetromino. Each $2 \times 4$ block is partitioned into two disjoint $S$ shapes, each occupying four cells in the standard zigzag configuration, and these blocks tile $S_4$ by translation.

Each strip is tiled independently, and the strips are disjoint and cover $\mathbb{Z}^2$. Every cell belongs to exactly one strip and exactly one tetromino within that strip, so the union forms a tiling of the entire plane.

This completes the construction of a tiling pattern based on all five tetrominoes. ∎
