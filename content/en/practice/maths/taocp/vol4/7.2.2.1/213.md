**Exercise 213.** [*M21*] [M21] If $\pi$ and $\pi'$ are two partitions of the same set, say that $\pi < \pi'$ if the restricted growth string of $\pi$ is lexicographically less than the restricted growth string

of $\pi'$. Let $(\alpha, \beta; \alpha', \beta')$ be a canonical bipair in the sense of exercise 212. Also let $\pi$ be a partition of the items such that $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are two of its parts, and let $\pi'$ be the same partition but with $\alpha'$ and $\beta'$ substituted. Is $\pi < \pi'$?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m01s  

---

Let $U=\alpha\cup\beta=\alpha'\cup\beta'$. All other blocks of $\pi$ and $\pi'$ are identical, so the comparison of restricted growth strings reduces entirely to the restriction to $U$.

## 1. Structure of the comparison

Let $r(\pi)$ and $r(\pi')$ be the restricted growth strings (RGS). For any element $x\notin U$, both partitions agree, hence

$$
r(\pi)(x)=r(\pi')(x).
$$

Therefore, any lexicographic difference must occur at the first element of $U$ (in increasing order) where the block assignments differ.

So it suffices to compare the induced 2-block partitions of $U$:

- in $\pi$: blocks $\alpha,\beta$,
- in $\pi'$: blocks $\alpha',\beta'$.

## 2. What “canonical bipair” means in this context

From the definition in Exercise 212, a **canonical bipair** $(\alpha,\beta;\alpha',\beta')$ means:

- $\alpha\cup\beta=\alpha'\cup\beta'=U$,
- $|\alpha|=|\alpha'|$, $|\beta|=|\beta'|$,
- $(\alpha',\beta')$ is the **canonical 2-partition of $U$** in the sense of restricted growth strings: when elements of $U$ are scanned in increasing order, the assignment that produces $(\alpha',\beta')$ is the greedy RGS construction, always giving the smallest admissible label.

Equivalently, $r(\alpha',\beta')$ is the lexicographically smallest RGS among all 2-block partitions of $U$ with the same block sizes.

Thus, for every other admissible bipartition of $U$, including $(\alpha,\beta)$,

$$
r(\alpha',\beta') \le r(\alpha,\beta),
$$

with strict inequality unless $(\alpha,\beta)$ is already canonical.

## 3. Effect on the full partition

Since $\pi'$ differs from $\pi$ only by replacing $(\alpha,\beta)$ with the canonical version $(\alpha',\beta')$, we have:

- Outside $U$: identical contributions to the RGS.
- Inside $U$: the RGS of $\pi'$ is lexicographically minimal among all such replacements.

Hence, at the first position $x\in U$ where the assignments differ,

$$
r(\pi')(x) < r(\pi)(x),
$$

because the canonical construction assigns the smallest possible valid label at the earliest possible deviation point.

This guarantees a strict lexicographic improvement.

## 4. Conclusion

Therefore,

$$
r(\pi') < r(\pi),
$$

and hence

$$
\pi' < \pi.
$$

## Final answer

$$
\boxed{\text{No, } \pi < \pi' \text{ does not hold. In fact, } \pi' < \pi \text{ always holds for a canonical bipair substitution.}}
$$
