[Section 7.2.2.1: Dancing Links](../)

**Exercise 227.** [*10*] What cost should be assigned to option $\langle 16 \rangle$, to minimize (a) $\Sigma_2$? (b) $S$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m11s  

---

In the exact-cover encoding used in this section, each option ⟨k⟩ corresponds to choosing the value $a_k = 1$ and leaving all other $a_i = 0$. Thus the cost assigned to an option is obtained by evaluating the objective function on the partial solution it represents.

Here we consider option ⟨16⟩, which corresponds to

$$
a_{16} = 1,\qquad a_k = 0 \text{ for } k \neq 16.
$$

### (a) Minimizing $\Sigma_2$

$$
\Sigma_2 = \sum_{k=1}^{2n} k a_k^2.
$$

Substituting the assignment of option ⟨16⟩ gives

$$
\Sigma_2 = 16 \cdot (1)^2 + \sum_{k \neq 16} k \cdot 0^2 = 16.
$$

Hence the cost assigned to option ⟨16⟩ for minimizing $\Sigma_2$ is

$$
\boxed{16}.
$$

### (b) Minimizing $S$

$$
S = \sum_{k=1}^{2n} k a_k^3.
$$

Substituting the same assignment gives

$$
S = 16 \cdot (1)^3 + \sum_{k \neq 16} k \cdot 0^3 = 16.
$$

Hence the cost assigned to option ⟨16⟩ for minimizing $S$ is

$$
\boxed{16}.
$$

### Conclusion

For option ⟨16⟩,

$$
\text{cost for } \Sigma_2 = 16, \qquad \text{cost for } S = 16.
$$
