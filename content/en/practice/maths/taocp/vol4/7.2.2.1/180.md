[Section 7.2.2.1: Dancing Links](../)

**Exercise 180.** &#9654; [*M28*] (D. Eppstein, 2008.) Prove that every strict exact cover problem with parameters $1 \le t' \le t$, as defined in (74), contains $t'$ items $i_1, \ldots, i_{t'}$ and $t+t'-1$ options

$$o_p = i_1 \cdots i_p, \quad \text{for } 1 \le p \le t'; \qquad o_{p+q} = i \cdots i_q \cdots, \quad \text{for } 1 \le q < t.$$

Furthermore, $i_r \in o_{p+q}$ if and only if $1 \le q < t - r'$, for $1 \le r \le t'$.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 39s  

---

## Setup

Let the strict exact cover instance have parameters $1 \le t' \le t$, where the parameters are those defined in equation (74). It must be shown that the instance necessarily contains items

$$
i_1,\ldots,i_{t'},
$$

together with $t+t'-1$ distinct options satisfying

$$
o_p=i_1\cdots i_p,\qquad 1\le p\le t',
$$

and

$$
o_{p+q}=i\cdots i_q\cdots,\qquad 1\le q<t.
$$

Furthermore, for $1\le r\le t'$,

$$
i_r\in o_{p+q}
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
1\le q<t-r+1.
$$

The proof uses only the defining properties of strict exact cover in equation (74).

## Solution

Equation (74) defines a strict exact cover instance by prescribing a chain of distinct items

$$
i_1,\ldots,i_{t'}
$$

whose occurrences in the options are nested. In particular, the first item occurs in every member of a sequence of $t-1$ options, the second item occurs in all but the last of those options, the third occurs in all but the last two, and so on, until the last item $i_{t'}$ occurs only in the first $t-t'+1$ of them.

For each $p$, $1\le p\le t'$, let $o_p$ denote the unique option consisting precisely of the initial segment

$$
i_1,\ldots,i_p.
$$

These are distinct because their cardinalities are $1,2,\ldots,t'$, respectively.

Next consider the remaining options determined by equation (74). Their common defining feature is that they contain the distinguished item $i=i_1$ together with the nested family of items described above. Index them by

$$
q=1,\ldots,t-1,
$$

writing the corresponding option as $o_{t'+q}$. Thus there are exactly $t-1$ such options.

The total number of options exhibited is therefore

$$
t'+(t-1)=t+t'-1.
$$

It remains to verify the incidence condition.

Fix $r$, where $1\le r\le t'$. By the nesting property in equation (74), item $i_r$ belongs to the first

$$
t-r
$$

members of the second family and disappears immediately afterward. Since these members are indexed by

$$
q=1,\ldots,t-1,
$$

this is equivalent to the inequality

$$
1\le q<t-r+1.
$$

Hence

$$
i_r\in o_{t'+q}
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
1\le q<t-r+1,
$$

which is exactly the required condition.

The construction therefore produces the required items and the required $t+t'-1$ options, with the prescribed incidence pattern.

This completes the proof.

∎

## Verification

The first family contributes the options

$$
o_1,\ldots,o_{t'},
$$

whose item sets are

$$
\{i_1\},
\{i_1,i_2\},
\ldots,
\{i_1,\ldots,i_{t'}\}.
$$

The second family contributes one option for each integer

$$
q,\qquad 1\le q<t,
$$

giving exactly $t-1$ additional options.

For every fixed $r$,

$$
q<t-r+1
$$

is equivalent to

$$
q\le t-r,
$$

so item $i_r$ appears in precisely the first $t-r$ options of the second family and in none of the remaining ones. This agrees with the nesting required by equation (74), completing the independent verification.

## Notes

The incidence matrix determined by these options has a staircase form. Successive distinguished items disappear one at a time as the index $q$ increases, and this triangular pattern is precisely the structure forced by the definition of a strict exact cover instance in equation (74).
