## 1. Basic structure and symmetry

A Langford pairing of order $n=16$ is a sequence $(a_1,\dots,a_{32})$ such that each $i\in\{1,\dots,16\}$ appears twice, and if its positions are $p_i<q_i$, then

$$
q_i - p_i = i+1.
$$

Define the reversal map

$$
(a_1,\dots,a_{32}) \mapsto (a_{32},\dots,a_1).
$$

If $i$ occurs at $(p_i,q_i)$, then after reversal it occurs at $(33-q_i, 33-p_i)$, and

$$
(33-p_i) - (33-q_i) = q_i - p_i = i+1,
$$

so reversal preserves Langford validity.

Hence the solution set is closed under reversal, and therefore:

- the lexicographically largest solution is the reversal of the lexicographically smallest solution.

So it suffices to determine only the lexicographically smallest pairing.
