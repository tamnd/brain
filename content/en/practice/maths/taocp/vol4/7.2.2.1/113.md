**Exercise 113.** [*21*] [21] An 'alphabet block' is a cube whose six faces are marked with letters. Is there a set of five alphabet blocks that are able to spell the 25 words TREES, NODES, STACK, AVAIL, FIRST, RIGHT, ORDER, LIST, GIVEN, LINKS, QUEUE, GRAPH, TIMES, BLOCK, VALUE, TABLE, FIELD, EDGE, ABOVE, POINT, THREE, LINK, HENCE, QUITE, DEBUG? (Each of these words appears more than 50 times in Chapter 2.)

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m30s  

---

## Solution

Let each alphabet block be identified with a color in ${1,2,3,4,5}$. Assign to each letter $x$ a color $c(x)\in{1,2,3,4,5}$ corresponding to a block containing that letter.

Each word requires that its letters lie on distinct blocks, hence for any two distinct letters $x,y$ appearing together in a word, one must have $c(x)\ne c(y)$. Define a graph $G$ whose vertices are the letters appearing in the 25 words, with an edge between two letters if and only if they appear together in at least one word. The condition for the blocks is equivalent to a proper vertex coloring of $G$ with at most 5 colors.

Let $A,T,R,E,S,I$ be letters. Each pair among these letters occurs together in at least one of the listed words, as verified from the given set.

First, among ${A,T,R,E,S}$, every pair occurs in a word:

$A$ with $T$ in STACK or TABLE, $A$ with $R$ in GRAPH, $A$ with $E$ in AVAIL or TABLE or ABOVE or VALUE, $A$ with $S$ in STACK, $T$ with $R$ in TREES, $T$ with $E$ in TREES, $T$ with $S$ in STACK, $R$ with $E$ in TREES, $R$ with $S$ in TREES, $E$ with $S$ in TREES.

Next, letter $I$ forms edges to all five of these letters:

$I$ with $A$ in AVAIL, $I$ with $T$ in TIMES or FIRST or RIGHT, $I$ with $R$ in RIGHT, $I$ with $E$ in FIELD or GIVEN, $I$ with $S$ in FIRST.

Each pair in ${A,T,R,E,S,I}$ is therefore adjacent in $G$, so these six vertices induce a complete subgraph $K_6$.

A proper coloring of a complete graph $K_6$ requires at least 6 distinct colors. Since each block provides only one color, five blocks supply at most 5 colors, contradicting the existence of such a coloring.

No assignment of five alphabet blocks can therefore realize all 25 words.

This completes the proof. ∎
