## Solution

Place the plane on the triangular lattice generated by basis vectors

$$
\mathbf{a}=(1,0), \qquad \mathbf{b}=\left(\tfrac12,\tfrac{\sqrt3}{2}\right).
$$

Every lattice point has integer coordinates $(i,j)$ and represents the point

$$
P(i,j)=i\mathbf{a}+j\mathbf{b}.
$$

Each fundamental parallelogram spanned by $(i,j)$, $(i+1,j)$, $(i,j+1)$, $(i+1,j+1)$ is subdivided into two unit equilateral triangles by the diagonal joining $P(i+1,j)$ to $P(i,j+1)$. This produces a regular tiling by equilateral triangles.

Each triangle is specified by a triple $(i,j,\sigma)$ where $(i,j)\in\mathbb{Z}^2$ and $\sigma\in{0,1}$. The value $\sigma=0$ denotes the triangle with vertices

$$
P(i,j),\ P(i+1,j),\ P(i,j+1),
$$

and the value $\sigma=1$ denotes the triangle with vertices

$$
P(i+1,j+1),\ P(i+1,j),\ P(i,j+1).
$$

Two triangles are adjacent if and only if they share an edge, which occurs exactly in the following cases.

For each $(i,j)$, the triangle $(i,j,0)$ is adjacent to $(i,j,1)$ along the diagonal edge between $P(i+1,j)$ and $P(i,j+1)$. It is also adjacent to $(i-1,j,1)$, sharing the edge between $P(i,j)$ and $P(i,j+1)$, and to $(i,j-1,1)$, sharing the edge between $P(i,j)$ and $P(i+1,j)$.

Symmetrically, the triangle $(i,j,1)$ is adjacent to $(i,j,0)$ and to $(i+1,j,0)$ and $(i,j+1,0)$ by the corresponding shared edges.

Each edge of the triangulation is uniquely represented by a pair $(t,t')$ of adjacent triangles, ordered arbitrarily but consistently stored in both directions. A coordinate representation for edges is obtained by labeling each edge by its midpoint in the lattice and a direction index.

The midpoint of any edge is of the form

$$
M(i,j,k)=\tfrac12(P(u)+P(v))
$$

for its endpoints $P(u),P(v)$. Each midpoint lies either at an integer lattice point or at a half-integer translate of $\mathbb{Z}^2$ in the $(\mathbf{a},\mathbf{b})$ basis, so edges fall into three orientation classes according to direction parallel to $\mathbf{a}$, $\mathbf{b}$, or $\mathbf{b}-\mathbf{a}$.

Thus each edge is represented by a pair $(i,j,\tau)$ where $(i,j)\in\mathbb{Z}^2$ is the unique lattice point associated with its midpoint projection and $\tau\in{0,1,2}$ specifies whether the edge is parallel to $\mathbf{a}$, $\mathbf{b}$, or $\mathbf{b}-\mathbf{a}$. The two incident triangles of an edge are recovered from $(i,j,\tau)$ by the adjacency rules above.

This coordinate system assigns every triangle a unique discrete label $(i,j,\sigma)$ and every edge a unique discrete label $(i,j,\tau)$, with adjacency given by constant finite translations in $\mathbb{Z}^2$. ∎
