# (b) Dual solutions are inverses

A solution of the bounded permutation problem is a choice of pairs $(j,\pi(j))$ such that:

- each row $j$ is used exactly once,
- each column $k$ is used exactly once,
- and $1 \le \pi(j) \le a_j$.

Thus solutions correspond to perfect matchings in the bipartite graph with edges

$$
(j,k) \text{ if } k \le a_j.
$$

Define the inverse matching by

$$
\pi^{-1}(k)=j \iff (j,k)\text{ is chosen}.
$$

Now row condition becomes “each $k$ is used once”. The allowable rows in column $k$ are those with $k \le a_j$, i.e.

$$
a_j \ge k.
$$

Rewrite this in dual Ferrers form: column $k$ in the original corresponds to row condition $n+1-k$ in the conjugate diagram. Hence the number of available rows for column $k$ is exactly

$$
b_{n+1-k}.
$$

Thus inversion transforms the incidence matrix with row bounds $a_j$ into the transposed Ferrers-conjugate system with column bounds $b_k$. The construction is involutive, hence bijective.

Therefore solutions of the dual problem are exactly inverses of solutions of the original.

$$
\boxed{\text{Solutions correspond bijectively via } \pi \leftrightarrow \pi^{-1}.}
$$

# (c) Correct DLX update count at depth 1

We now compute updates correctly using the DLX mechanism.

At the root, we branch on row $X_1$. Choosing column $k$ corresponds to selecting row $(1,k)$.

## Key DLX fact

Covering a column removes:

- the column header,
- all rows containing a 1 in that column,
- and in those rows, all other column incidences.

Thus **updates are counted by deleted 1-entries in the exact cover matrix**.

We count deletions in two stages.

## Step 1: deletions from covering $X_1$

Column $X_1$ contains exactly the nodes:

$$
(1,1),(1,2),\dots,(1,a_1),
$$

so covering $X_1$ deletes exactly $a_1$ nodes.

## Step 2: choosing $(1,k)$ and covering $Y_k$

Selecting row $(1,k)$ removes:

- the single node $(1,k)$ from column $Y_k$,
- and triggers covering of $Y_k$.

Column $Y_k$ contains exactly $b_k$ rows $j$ such that $k \le a_j$.

Thus covering $Y_k$ deletes:

- $b_k$ nodes in column $Y_k$,
- and for each such row $j$, all other incident nodes $(j,\ell)$, i.e. $a_j-1$ additional nodes.

Hence total deletions caused by covering $Y_k$ are:

$$
b_k + \sum_{j: a_j \ge k}(a_j-1).
$$

## Step 3: total updates at depth 1

For each branch $k$, total updates are

$$
a_1 + \Bigl(b_k + \sum_{j: a_j \ge k}(a_j-1)\Bigr).
$$

Summing over $k=1,\dots,a_1$:

$$
\boxed{
U_1
=
\sum_{k=1}^{a_1}
\left[
a_1 + b_k + \sum_{j: a_j \ge k}(a_j-1)
\right].
}
$$

This expression correctly counts each DLX deletion exactly once.

\boxed{\#\text{solutions}=\operatorname{perm}(A)}, \quad A_{j,k}=\mathbf{1}[k \le a_j].

# (e) Correct global update formula under fixed branching order

Assume Algorithm X always branches on $X_j$ at depth $j-1$.

At depth $j$, we select column $Y_k$ among remaining feasible choices.

We count updates as total number of deleted 1-entries in the exact cover matrix during execution.

## Key reformulation

Each solution corresponds to a permutation $\pi$ with $\pi(j)=k$.

During execution:

- selecting row $(j,k)$ removes:

- the row-node $(j,k)$,
- all other occurrences of column $k$,
- and all other column incidences in row $j$.

Thus cost contributed by choosing $(j,k)$ is:

$$
1 + (b_k-1) + (a_j-1)
= a_j + b_k - 1.
$$

This is now correct because overlap at $(j,k)$ is removed.

## Total updates

Summing over all solution paths:

$$
\boxed{
U
=
\sum_{\pi}
\sum_{j=1}^n
\bigl(a_j + b_{\pi(j)} - 1\bigr).
}
$$

We separate terms:

- row part:

$$
\sum_{\pi}\sum_{j=1}^n a_j
= \operatorname{perm}(A)\sum_{j=1}^n a_j
$$

- column part:

$$
\sum_{\pi}\sum_{j=1}^n b_{\pi(j)}
= \operatorname{perm}(A)\sum_{k=1}^n b_k
$$

- correction:

$$
\sum_{\pi}\sum_{j=1}^n 1
= n\operatorname{perm}(A)
$$

Thus:

$$
\boxed{
U
=
\operatorname{perm}(A)
\left(
\sum_{j=1}^n a_j
+
\sum_{k=1}^n b_k
-
n
\right).
}
$$

# (f) Full permutation case $a_j=n+1-j$

Then every permutation is valid, so:

$$
\operatorname{perm}(A)=n!.
$$

We compute sums:

$$
\sum_{j=1}^n a_j = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}.
$$

Since $b_k = n+1-k$, also:

$$
\sum_{k=1}^n b_k = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}.
$$

Thus:

$$
U = n!\left(n(n+1) - n\right)
= n!\,n^2.
$$

$$
\boxed{U = n^2 n!}.
$$

# (g) Case $a_j=\min(j+1,n)$

We compute:

### Row sum

$$
\sum_{j=1}^n a_j
=
\sum_{j=1}^{n-1}(j+1) + n
=
\frac{n(n+1)}{2}.
$$

### Column sums

For $k \le n-1$, $b_k=n-k+1$, and $b_n=n$.

Thus:

$$
\sum_{k=1}^n b_k = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}.
$$

### Solutions

This is the standard staircase Ferrers diagram; every permutation is valid:

$$
\operatorname{perm}(A)=n!.
$$

Thus:

$$
U = n!\,(n(n+1)-n)=n!\,n^2.
$$

$$
\boxed{U=n^2 n!}.
$$

# (h) Case $a_j=\min(2j,n)$

### Row sum

Split at $2j=n$, i.e. $j \le n/2$:

$$
\sum a_j
=
\sum_{j \le n/2} 2j + \sum_{j>n/2} n.
$$

This gives:

$$
\sum a_j
=
\frac{(n/2)(n/2+1)}{1} + n(n/2)
=
\frac{n^2}{2} + \frac{n}{2} + \frac{n^2}{2}
=
n^2 + \frac{n}{2}.
$$

### Column sum

By symmetry of conjugation:

$$
\sum b_k = \sum a_j = n^2 + \frac{n}{2}.
$$

### Solutions

This Ferrers shape is again full-rank monotone and admits all permutations:

$$
\operatorname{perm}(A)=n!.
$$

Thus:

$$
U = n!\,(2n^2 + n - n)
= 2n^2 n!.
$$

$$
\boxed{U = 2n^2 n!}.
$$

# (i) Why the naive assumption fails and correct principle

The assumption that costs depend only on local column sizes is incorrect because DLX updates count **row–column incidence deletions**, not just column removals.

The correct invariant is:

> Each update corresponds to deletion of a 1-entry in the incidence matrix.

Thus the correct global formula is:

$$
\boxed{
U
=
\sum_{\text{all selected rows }(j,k)}
\left(a_j + b_k - 1\right)
}
$$

aggregated over all solutions under the fixed branching rule.

This works because:

- each row contributes $a_j$ incidences,
- each column contributes $b_k$,
- intersection $(j,k)$ is counted twice and corrected by $-1$.
