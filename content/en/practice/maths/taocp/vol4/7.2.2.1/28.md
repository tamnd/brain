**Exercise 28.** [*M23*] For what integers $c_0, t_0, c_1, t_1, \ldots, c_t, t_t$ with $1 \le c_j \le t_j$ does the text's formula (27) for estimated completion ratio give the value (a) $1/27$ (b) $1/37$?

Formula (27) in TAOCP §7.2.2.1 defines the estimated completion ratio as the product

$$
R=\prod_{j=0}^{t}\frac{c_j}{t_j},
\qquad 1 \le c_j \le t_j.
$$

We rewrite this as

$$
R=\frac{\prod_{j=0}^{t} c_j}{\prod_{j=0}^{t} t_j}.
$$

Thus the problem is to characterize all finite integer sequences $(c_0,t_0,\dots,c_t,t_t)$ such that this ratio equals $1/27$ or $1/37$.

Equivalently,

$$
\prod_{j=0}^{t} \frac{t_j}{c_j} = 27 \quad \text{or} \quad 37.
$$

## (a) Case $R = \frac{1}{27}$

We require

$$
\prod_{j=0}^{t} \frac{t_j}{c_j} = 27 = 3^3.
$$

$$
\Delta_p = \sum_{j=0}^{t} \bigl(v_p(t_j) - v_p(c_j)\bigr),
$$

where $v_p(x)$ is the exponent of $p$ in $x$.

Then the condition is:

$$
\Delta_3 = 3, \qquad \Delta_p = 0 \text{ for all primes } p \ne 3.
$$

Because $1 \le c_j \le t_j$, each term $\frac{t_j}{c_j} \ge 1$, so every factor can only contribute nonnegative net valuation to primes.

\prod_{j=0}^{t} \frac{t_j}{c_j} = 37.

Thus the only possible net contribution is a single unit of $37$ in the numerator when all ratios are multiplied together.
