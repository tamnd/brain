**Exercise 27.** [*22*] Introduce secondary items cleverly into the options (a6), so that only *planar* solutions to Langford's problem are obtained. (See exercise 7.5–8.)

## Solution

Let Langford’s problem be represented in the standard exact-cover form used in (6) and (10): each option corresponds to a placement of a pair of identical symbols $k$, occupying two positions $i$ and $j=i+k+1$ in a sequence of length $2n$, while each position is an item that must be covered exactly once.

Thus a primary item set consists of position items ${1,2,\dots,2n}$. For each admissible placement $(i,j,k)$ with $j=i+k+1$, there is an option $O(i,j,k)$ containing precisely the two position items $i,j$ together with the symbol requirement item $k$, exactly as in the usual encoding of Langford sequences into exact cover.

Two options $O(i,j,k)$ and $O(i',j',k')$ are said to cross if, after reordering endpoints so that $i<i'$, one has

$i < i' < j < j'.$

This is the standard interleaving condition for intervals.

To restrict solutions to planar Langford solutions, introduce a secondary item for every unordered pair of crossing options. For every pair $(O,O')$ with endpoints satisfying the above condition, define a secondary item $\sigma(O,O')$. Insert $\sigma(O,O')$ into both options $O$ and $O'$, and into no other options.

The resulting exact cover instance has the same primary items as the standard Langford encoding, and additional secondary items indexed by crossing pairs of options.

### Correctness

Consider a family $\mathcal{F}$ of selected options.

If $\mathcal{F}$ contains two options $O$ and $O'$ that cross, then both options contain the secondary item $\sigma(O,O')$. In the cover condition, each item must occur at most once among chosen options, since DLX forbids multiple occurrences of the same item in the active structure. Hence $\mathcal{F}$ cannot contain both $O$ and $O'$.

Therefore every feasible solution avoids all crossing pairs of intervals.

Conversely, suppose a Langford solution is planar. Then no two selected options cross, hence for every secondary item $\sigma(O,O')$, at most one of $O$ and $O'$ is selected. Thus each secondary item occurs in at most one chosen option, so the secondary constraints are satisfied without conflict. The primary items are already satisfied by the Langford construction, since every position item is covered exactly once in any valid Langford solution.

Therefore the feasible solutions of the modified exact cover instance are exactly the planar Langford solutions.

This completes the proof. ∎
