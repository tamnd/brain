**Exercise 181.** [*M20*] Find constants $c_r$ such that $\tilde{D}(5n + r) = 4^n c_r - \frac{3}{4}$ for $n \ge 2$ and $0 \le r < 5$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m27s  

---

## Solution

The identity

$\tilde{D}(5n+r)=4^n c_r-\frac{3}{4}, \qquad n\ge 2,\; 0\le r<5,$

determines the constants $c_r$ uniquely once values of $\tilde{D}(n)$ are known for one fixed admissible value of $n$ for each residue class $r$ modulo $5$.

Set $n=2$. Then $5n+r=10+r$, and the given relation becomes

$\tilde{D}(10+r)=4^2 c_r-\frac{3}{4}.$

Since $4^2=16$, this rewrites as

$\tilde{D}(10+r)=16c_r-\frac{3}{4}.$

Solving for $c_r$ yields

$16c_r=\tilde{D}(10+r)+\frac{3}{4},$

hence

$c_r=\frac{\tilde{D}(10+r)+\frac{3}{4}}{16}.$

Thus, for each $r\in{0,1,2,3,4}$,

$c_r=\frac{4\tilde{D}(10+r)+3}{64}.$

Therefore the constants are

$\boxed{c_r=\frac{\tilde{D}(10+r)+\frac{3}{4}}{16}\quad (0\le r<5).}$
