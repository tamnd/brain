[Section 7.2.2.1: Dancing Links](../)

**Exercise 192.** [*M29*] What's the asymptotic value of $g_n$ when $n$ is large?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m05s  

---

## Setup

Let

$G(z)=\int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-t}(1+z)^t\,dt -1=\sum_{n\ge 1} g_n z^n.$

For $\Re(1-\log(1+z))>0$, rewrite the integral as

$G(z)=\int_{0}^{\infty} \exp\!\bigl(-t + t\log(1+z)\bigr)\,dt -1,$

so

$G(z)=\int_{0}^{\infty} \exp\!\bigl(-t(1-\log(1+z))\bigr)\,dt -1.$

Hence

$G(z)=\frac{1}{1-\log(1+z)}-1=\frac{\log(1+z)}{1-\log(1+z)}.$

Let $\alpha=e-1$. The dominant singularity occurs when $\log(1+z)=1$, hence at $z=\alpha$.

The goal is to determine the asymptotic behavior of $g_n$ as $n\to\infty$.

## Solution

Write

$G(z)=\frac{\log(1+z)}{1-\log(1+z)}.$

Let

$\phi(z)=\log(1+z), \qquad z_0=e-1,$

so that $\phi(z_0)=1$ and $1-\phi(z)$ vanishes at $z_0$.

Expand $\phi$ at $z_0$. Since

$\phi'(z)=\frac{1}{1+z},$

we have

$\phi'(z_0)=\frac{1}{e}.$

Thus

$\phi(z)=1+\frac{z-z_0}{e}+O((z-z_0)^2).$

It follows that

$1-\phi(z)= -\frac{z-z_0}{e}+O((z-z_0)^2).$

Also,

$\phi(z)=1+O(z-z_0).$

Substitute into $G(z)$:

$G(z)=\frac{1+O(z-z_0)}{-\frac{z-z_0}{e}+O((z-z_0)^2)}.$

Factor the linear term in the denominator:

$G(z)=\frac{1+O(z-z_0)}{-\frac{z-z_0}{e}\bigl(1+O(z-z_0)\bigr)}.$

Hence

$G(z)= -\frac{e}{z-z_0}\bigl(1+O(z-z_0)\bigr).$

This gives the principal singular part

$G(z)=\frac{e}{z_0-z}+O(1).$

Rewrite the pole in standard transfer form:

$\frac{1}{z_0-z}=\frac{1}{z_0}\cdot \frac{1}{1-z/z_0}.$

Therefore

$G(z)=\frac{e}{z_0}\cdot \frac{1}{1-z/z_0}+O(1).$

Since $z_0=e-1$, the principal term is

$\frac{e}{e-1}\cdot \frac{1}{1-z/(e-1)}.$

Extract coefficients. The series expansion

$\frac{1}{1-z/z_0}=\sum_{n\ge 0} z^n z_0^{-n-1}$

implies

$[z^n]\frac{1}{1-z/z_0}=z_0^{-n-1}.$

Thus the dominant contribution to $g_n$ is

$g_n \sim \frac{e}{e-1}\,(e-1)^{-n-1}.$

Equivalently,

$g_n \sim \frac{e}{(e-1)^{n+1}}.$

This completes the derivation of the asymptotic form.

## Verification

The only singularity of $\log(1+z)$ in the finite complex plane is at $z=-1$, and the equation $1-\log(1+z)=0$ yields the nearest singularity to the origin at $z=e-1$, so this singularity determines the exponential growth rate.

The expansion of $\phi(z)=\log(1+z)$ is analytic at $z_0$, and $\phi'(z_0)=1/e\neq 0$, so the zero of $1-\phi(z)$ is simple, giving a simple pole for $G(z)$.

The conversion

$\frac{1}{z_0-z}=\frac{1}{z_0}\frac{1}{1-z/z_0}$

produces a standard geometric series whose coefficient extraction yields $z_0^{-n-1}$, matching the stated asymptotic term.

## Result

$\boxed{g_n \sim \frac{e}{(e-1)^{n+1}}}$
