## 1. Correct geometry of the regions

### 1.1 Aztec diamonds from checkerboards

Consider a $(2n+1)\times(2n+1)$ checkerboard with black corners, i.e. the cell $(1,1)$ is black and colors alternate by parity. A cell is white exactly when $i+j$ is odd.

Let $W_n$ be the number of white cells. Count by rows:

- There are $n+1$ odd-indexed rows and $n$ even-indexed rows.
- In an odd row $i$, white cells occur at even columns, of which there are $n$.
- In an even row $i$, white cells occur at odd columns, of which there are $n+1$.

Hence

$$
W_n = (n+1)n + n(n+1) = 2n(n+1).
$$

This is the standard Aztec diamond of order $n+\tfrac12$.

Thus:

$$
|A_{n+\frac12}| = 2n(n+1).
$$
