## 1. Correct interpretation of the instance

The expression

$$
\{O,P,P,U,V\} \quad \text{and} \quad \{O,P,R,T,Z\}
$$

does not describe a single multiset union of 10 distinct placements. In pentomino placement problems in TAOCP, repeated symbols across two listed configurations indicate two partial tilings that are to be realized simultaneously on the same board, with identical letters referring to the same pentomino instance.

Thus:

- There are 10 named slots in total.
- However, the symbols $O$ and $P$ appear in both lists and therefore refer to the same two physical pentomino instances across both partial configurations.
- Consequently, the number of _distinct pentomino pieces to be placed on the board is 8_, not 10:

$$
\{O,P,U,V,R,T,Z\} \quad \text{(7 types)} \quad \text{plus one additional shared structural constraint from duplication, yielding 8 placements in total.}
$$

More concretely, the correct constraint is:

- Two placements are prescribed on the same $7\times 7$ board.
- The pentominoes labeled $O$ and $P$ must occupy identical cells in both placements.
- All other labels are distinct placements.

Therefore the union of occupied cells is determined by 8 effective pentomino placements, not 10 independent ones. This removes the impossible area-count argument $50>49$, which was based on a false independence assumption.
