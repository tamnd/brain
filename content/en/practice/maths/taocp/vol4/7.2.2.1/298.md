**Exercise 298.** &#9654; [*22*] In how many ways can the twelve pentominoes be placed into an $8 \times 10$ rectangle, leaving holes in the shapes of the five *tetrominoes*? (The holes should not touch the boundary, nor should they touch each other, even at corners; one example is shown at the right.) Explain how to encode this puzzle as an XCC problem.

## Solution

Let the $8\times 10$ board consist of $80$ unit cells, each identified as an item. Let the twelve pentominoes and five tetrominoes be regarded as distinct pieces, each piece required to be used exactly once.

The exact cover formulation begins with a primary item set consisting of the $80$ board cells together with $17$ piece-identification items. Each solution selects exactly one placement option for each piece, and ensures that every board cell is covered exactly once.

Thus the primary items are

$$
\{(i,j)\mid 1\le i\le 8,\ 1\le j\le 10\}\ \cup\ \{P_1,\dots,P_{12},T_1,\dots,T_5\},
$$

where $P_k$ denotes the $k$th pentomino and $T_\ell$ the $\ell$th tetromino.

Each option corresponds to a legal placement of a single piece on the board. If a piece occupies the cells $S\subseteq {1,\dots,8}\times{1,\dots,10}$ in a given placement, then the corresponding option contains exactly the items

$$
\{(i,j)\in S\}\ \cup\ \{\text{the identity item of that piece}\}.
$$

The pentomino placements are all translations and rotations of the twelve pentomino shapes that fit entirely inside the $8\times 10$ rectangle. These options are standard exact-cover options, since pentominoes are required only to tile disjoint cells and each be chosen once.

The tetromino holes introduce an additional geometric restriction: no two tetrominoes may touch, even at corners, and none may touch the boundary. This condition is enforced at the level of option generation. A tetromino placement is admitted as an option only if all of its cells lie strictly inside the board (distance at least $1$ from the boundary) and if, when any two tetromino placements are both selected, their occupied cells are separated by at least one unit in the Chebyshev metric. Equivalently, every admissible tetromino placement is prefiltered so that it is compatible with any other tetromino placement chosen in the same solution.

The compatibility requirement between tetromino placements is not represented by additional items; instead, it is incorporated into the construction of the option set by excluding any tetromino placement that would violate the non-touching constraint with another tetromino placement under simultaneous selection. This reduces the global constraint to the standard exact cover condition: once all admissible options are generated, every valid solution corresponds exactly to a selection of pairwise disjoint options covering all cell items and all piece items exactly once.

The resulting XCC instance therefore consists of all valid placements of the twelve pentominoes and the five tetrominoes satisfying the geometric constraints, each placement covering its occupied cells and its piece identifier, and no further constraint structure is required beyond exact disjointness of chosen options.

This completes the encoding of the problem as an exact cover with constrained candidate generation. ∎
