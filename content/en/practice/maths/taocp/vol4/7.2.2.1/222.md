## Corrected Solution

Let item $i$ be the item to be deleted in step P7. Let the vertical list of item $i$ consist of all nodes $p$ such that $\mathrm{TOP}(p)=i$, linked by the vertical pointers $\mathrm{ULINK}$ and $\mathrm{DLINK}$. Each node $p$ lies in a unique option $O(p)$, represented by a circular doubly linked horizontal list of nodes.

Let $S$ be the stack of already chosen items. For an option $O$, write $O \cap S \neq \emptyset$ if at least one item in $S$ occurs in $O$.

The operation proceeds by scanning the current vertical list of item $i$. Since this list is modified during processing, we preserve correctness by always saving the next node before any modification.

### Step 1: Scan of item $i$'s vertical list

Set

$$
p \leftarrow \mathrm{DLINK}(i).
$$

While $p \neq i$, do:

Let

$$
p_{\text{next}} \leftarrow \mathrm{DLINK}(p),
$$

so that $p_{\text{next}}$ is the next node in the original vertical traversal order.

Let $O = O(p)$ be the option containing $p$.

### Step 2: Case analysis on the option $O$

#### Case 1: $O \cap S \neq \emptyset$

In this case, item $i$ must be removed from the option, but the option itself is retained.

We perform two low-level actions on node $p$:

1. **Unlink $p$ from the vertical list of item $i$:**

Let

$$
u = \mathrm{ULINK}(p), \quad d = \mathrm{DLINK}(p).
$$

Update:

$$
\mathrm{DLINK}(u) \leftarrow d, \qquad \mathrm{ULINK}(d) \leftarrow u.
$$

This removes $p$ from the circular list of item $i$, preserving the invariant that every node in the column of $i$ satisfies $\mathrm{TOP}(x)=i$.

1. **Convert $p$ into a spacer:**

Set

$$
\mathrm{TOP}(p) \leftarrow 0.
$$

No horizontal links inside the option are changed, since $p$ remains a structural spacer separating surviving option segments.

After this transformation, $p$ is no longer part of any item list, including the list of item $i$, and will not be encountered in further column traversals.

#### Case 2: $O \cap S = \emptyset$

In this case, the entire option $O$ must be deleted.

For every node $q \in O$, do:

1. **Remove $q$ from its item column (if applicable):**

If $\mathrm{TOP}(q)=x>0$, then unlink $q$ from the vertical list of item $x$ using:

$$
\mathrm{DLINK}(\mathrm{ULINK}(q)) \leftarrow \mathrm{DLINK}(q), \qquad
\mathrm{ULINK}(\mathrm{DLINK}(q)) \leftarrow \mathrm{ULINK}(q),
$$

and update

$$
\mathrm{LEN}(x) \leftarrow \mathrm{LEN}(x) - 1.
$$

1. The node $q$ remains in memory but is no longer part of any column list.

### Step 3: Advance traversal

Set

$$
p \leftarrow p_{\text{next}}.
$$

Continue until $p = i$.

### Step 4: Delete item $i$ itself

Finally, remove item $i$ from the horizontal item list:

$$
\mathrm{RLINK}(l) \leftarrow r, \qquad \mathrm{LLINK}(r) \leftarrow l,
$$

where $l = \mathrm{LLINK}(i)$ and $r = \mathrm{RLINK}(i)$.

### Invariants maintained

- Every node with $\mathrm{TOP}(x)>0$ appears in exactly one vertical item list.
- Converting a node to a spacer requires explicit removal from its vertical list; setting $\mathrm{TOP}(p)=0$ alone is insufficient.
- Traversal remains well-defined because the next pointer is saved before any structural modification of the current node.

This completes the corrected low-level specification of the deletion process for item $i$ in step P7. ∎
