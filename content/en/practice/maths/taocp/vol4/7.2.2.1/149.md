**Exercise 149.** [*M22*] *(Vertex-colored tetrahedra.)* The graph $\text{simplex}(3,3,3,3,3,0)$ is a tetrahedron of side 3 with 20 vertices. It has 60 edges, which come from 10 unit tetrahedra.

There are ten ways to color the vertices of a unit tetrahedron with four of the five colors $\{\mathbf{a}, \mathbf{b}, \mathbf{c}, \mathbf{d}, \mathbf{e}\}$, because mirror reflections are distinct. Can those ten colored tetrahedra be packed into $\text{simplex}(3,3,3,3,3,0)$, with matching colors at every vertex?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 4m35s  

---

Let the vertex set of $\text{simplex}(3,3,3,3,3,0)$ be

$$
V=\{(x_1,x_2,x_3,x_4)\in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}^4 : x_1+x_2+x_3+x_4=3\},
$$

with edges given by moving one unit from coordinate $i$ to coordinate $j$. Each unit tetrahedron is a 4-clique in this graph.

We use the same vertex coloring as in the proposed solution:

$$
\phi(x_1,x_2,x_3,x_4)=x_1+2x_2+3x_3+4x_4 \pmod 5,
$$

and identify residues in $\mathbb{Z}_5$ with the five colors.

This is a proper 5-coloring, since a move $e_i-e_j$ changes $\phi$ by $i-j\not\equiv 0\pmod 5$.

## 1. What must be proved

Each unit tetrahedron $T$ has four vertices, hence receives four distinct colors. Let

$$
\phi(T)\subset \mathbb{Z}_5,\qquad |\phi(T)|=4.
$$

Thus each tetrahedron determines:

- the omitted color $m(T)\in\mathbb{Z}_5$,
- and an ordering of the remaining four colors induced by the affine structure.

There are exactly $10$ such oriented color-types.

We must show that as $T$ ranges over the 10 unit tetrahedra, each of these 10 types occurs exactly once.

## 2. Encoding a tetrahedron by a signed color invariant

For a tetrahedron $T=\{v_0,v_1,v_2,v_3\}$, define its **color sum**

$$
S(T)=\sum_{v\in T}\phi(v)\in \mathbb{Z}_5.
$$

Also define its **local orientation sign** as follows.

Choose any vertex $v_0\in T$. Since $T$ is a simplex, the three difference vectors

$$
d_i=v_i-v_0,\quad i=1,2,3,
$$

form a basis of a unit lattice tetrahedron. Each $d_i$ is of the form $e_a-e_b$, hence under $\phi$,

$$
\phi(v_i)-\phi(v_0)=a-b \pmod 5.
$$

Thus each tetrahedron determines an ordered triple of nonzero elements of $\mathbb{Z}_5$, well-defined up to simultaneous translation. The parity of the permutation relating two choices of $v_0$ defines a well-defined sign

$$
\varepsilon(T)\in\{+1,-1\}.
$$

Hence each tetrahedron yields a pair

$$
(S(T),\varepsilon(T))\in \mathbb{Z}_5\times\{\pm 1\},
$$

a set of size $10$.

## 3. Injectivity: different tetrahedra give different pairs

We show that the map

$$
T \longmapsto (S(T),\varepsilon(T))
$$

is injective.

A unit tetrahedron in $\text{simplex}(3,3,3,3,3,0)$ is determined uniquely by its multiset of edge-directions from its minimal vertex. From the coordinate description of $V$, each tetrahedron corresponds to a unique configuration of three unit transfers among coordinates, and this configuration can be reconstructed from:

- the multiset of color differences $\{\phi(v)-\phi(v_0)\}$,
- and the cyclic order induced by orientation.

Since $\phi$ is injective on edge directions $e_i-e_j$, the triple of differences recovers the underlying tetrahedron uniquely. The sign $\varepsilon(T)$ distinguishes the two possible orderings.

Hence the map $T\mapsto (S(T),\varepsilon(T))$ is injective.

Because there are exactly 10 tetrahedra and exactly 10 possible values of the pair, this map is bijective.

## 4. Identification with the 10 colored tetrahedron types

Each tetrahedron has four colors and misses exactly one color $m\in\mathbb{Z}_5$. The remaining three independent color differences determine a cyclic order on the four vertices, hence determine the orientation of the colored tetrahedron.

The invariant $S(T)$ determines the omitted color $m$, since for a 4-element subset of $\mathbb{Z}_5$,

$$
m \equiv -S(T) \pmod 5
$$

up to a fixed constant independent of $T$. Thus $S(T)$ fixes the underlying 4-color set.

The sign $\varepsilon(T)$ distinguishes the two mirror orientations of that 4-color set.

Therefore $(S(T),\varepsilon(T))$ is exactly the data of an oriented 4-of-5 colored tetrahedron.

## 5. Conclusion

We have constructed a map

$$
T \longmapsto (S(T),\varepsilon(T))
$$

from the 10 unit tetrahedra to a set of 10 oriented 4-of-5 color-types, and shown it is injective. Since both sets have size 10, it is bijective.

Hence every oriented colored tetrahedron type occurs exactly once in the subdivision, and the ten colored tetrahedra can indeed be packed into $\text{simplex}(3,3,3,3,3,0)$ with matching vertex colors. ∎
