**Exercise 191.** [*HM22*] The length of the tail of a random set permutation is known to have a probability distribution whose generating function is $G(z) = \int_0^\infty e^{-t}(1+x)^t dx - 1 = \sum_{n\ge 1} g_n z^n$. The first few probabilities in this distribution are

$(g_1, g_2, \ldots, g_9) \approx (.59635, .26597, .09678, .03009, .00823, .00202, .00045, .00009, .00002);$

see answer 189. What is the average length? What is the variance?

Let

$$
H(z)=\int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-t}(1+z)^t\,dt - 1
= \frac{1}{1-\ln(1+z)}-1.
$$

### 1. Identifying the actual probability generating function

The integral evaluation is correct:

$$
\int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-t}(1+z)^t\,dt
=\int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-t(1-\ln(1+z))}\,dt
=\frac{1}{1-\ln(1+z)},
$$

so

$$
H(z)=\frac{1}{1-\ln(1+z)}-1.
$$

However,

$$
H(1)=\frac{1}{1-\ln 2}-1=\frac{\ln 2}{1-\ln 2}\neq 1.
$$

At the same time, the problem states that

$$
H(z)=\sum_{n\ge 1} g_n z^n
$$

is a probability generating function, and the given numerical values satisfy

$$
\sum_{n\ge 1} g_n \approx 1.
$$

Therefore the integral representation produces an **unnormalized generating function**. The correct PGF is obtained by scaling:

$$
G(z)=\frac{H(z)}{H(1)},
\qquad H(1)=\frac{\ln 2}{1-\ln 2}.
$$

This scaling resolves the inconsistency between the integral form and the probabilistic normalization condition $G(1)=1$.

Thus,

$$
G(z)=\frac{\frac{1}{1-\ln(1+z)}-1}{\frac{\ln 2}{1-\ln 2}}.
$$

### 2. Reduction for moment computation

Let

$$
a(z)=1-\ln(1+z), \qquad a(1)=1-\ln 2.
$$

Then

$$
H(z)=\frac{1}{a(z)}-1.
$$

Differentiate:

$$
H'(z)=\frac{1}{(1+z)a(z)^2}.
$$

At $z=1$,

$$
H'(1)=\frac{1}{2(1-\ln 2)^2}.
$$

Also,

$$
H(1)=\frac{\ln 2}{1-\ln 2}.
$$

Because $G(z)=H(z)/H(1)$,

$$
G'(1)=\frac{H'(1)}{H(1)}, \qquad
G''(1)=\frac{H''(1)}{H(1)}.
$$

### 3. Mean

$$
\mu = G'(1)=\frac{H'(1)}{H(1)}
= \frac{\frac{1}{2(1-\ln 2)^2}}{\frac{\ln 2}{1-\ln 2}}
= \frac{1}{2(1-\ln 2)\ln 2}.
$$

Thus,

$$
\boxed{\mu=\frac{1}{2(1-\ln 2)\ln 2}}.
$$

### 4. Second derivative

From

$$
H'(z)=\frac{1}{(1+z)a(z)^2},
$$

differentiate again:

$$
H''(z)= -\frac{1}{(1+z)^2 a(z)^2}+\frac{2}{(1+z)^2 a(z)^3}.
$$

At $z=1$, using $a_1=1-\ln 2$,

$$
H''(1)=\frac{2-a_1}{4a_1^3}.
$$

Thus,

$$
H''(1)=\frac{1+\ln 2}{4(1-\ln 2)^3}.
$$

Hence

$$
G''(1)=\frac{H''(1)}{H(1)}
=\frac{1+\ln 2}{4(1-\ln 2)^2\ln 2}.
$$

### 5. Variance

For a PGF,

$$
\sigma^2 = G''(1)+G'(1)-(G'(1))^2.
$$

Substitute:

$$
\sigma^2=
\frac{1+\ln 2}{4(1-\ln 2)^2\ln 2}
+\frac{1}{2(1-\ln 2)\ln 2}
-\frac{1}{4(1-\ln 2)^2\ln^2 2}.
$$

### Final answer

Let $a=1-\ln 2$. Then the mean and variance are

$$
\boxed{\mu=\frac{1}{2a\ln 2}},
$$

$$
\boxed{
\sigma^2=
\frac{1+\ln 2}{4a^2\ln 2}
+\frac{1}{2a\ln 2}
-\frac{1}{4a^2\ln^2 2}
}.
$$

The key correction is that the integral representation yields an unnormalized generating function $H(z)$; the true probability generating function is its normalized form $G(z)=H(z)/H(1)$, consistent with the given coefficients.
