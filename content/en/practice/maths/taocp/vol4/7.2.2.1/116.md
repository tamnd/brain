**Exercise 116.** &#9654; [*M25*] Given a graph $G$ on vertices $V$, let $\mu(G)$ be obtained by (i) adding new vertices $V' = \{v' \mid v \in V\}$, with $u' \mathbin{-\!\!-} v$ when $u \mathbin{-\!\!-} v$; and also (ii) adding another vertex $w$, with $w \mathbin{-\!\!-} v'$ for all $v' \in V'$. If $G$ has $m$ edges and $n$ vertices, $\mu(G)$ has $3m+n$ edges and $2n+1$ vertices. The *Mycielski graphs* $M_c$ are defined for all $c \ge 2$ by setting $M_2 = K_2$ and $M_{c+1} = \mu(M_c)$; they have $\frac{1}{2} \cdot 3^c - \frac{3}{2} \cdot 2^c + 1$ edges and $\frac{3}{2} \cdot 2^c - 1$ vertices:

$$M_2 = \circ\!\!-\!\!\circ; \qquad M_3 = \bigtriangleup; \qquad M_4 = \cdots$$

a) Prove that each $M_c$ is triangle-free (contains no subgraph $K_3$).
b) Prove that the chromatic number $\chi(M_c) = c$.
c) Prove that each $M_c$ is in fact "$\chi$-critical": Removing any edge decreases $\chi$.

### (a) Triangle-freeness of $M_c$

Induction on $c$.

For $c=2$, $M_2=K_2$ is triangle-free.

Assume $M_c$ is triangle-free and construct $M_{c+1}=\mu(M_c)$ with parts $V$, $V'=\{v':v\in V\}$, and $w$.

Edges are:

- those of $M_c$ inside $V$,
- $u'-v$ iff $u-v\in E(M_c)$,
- $w$ adjacent to all vertices in $V'$,
- no edges inside $V'$, and no edges from $w$ to $V$.

Now consider any triangle in $M_{c+1}$.

- A triangle entirely in $V$ would be a triangle in $M_c$, impossible.
- A triangle entirely in $V'$ is impossible since $V'$ is independent.
- A triangle containing $w$: then the other two vertices lie in $V'$, but $V'$ has no edges.

So no triangle exists. Hence $M_{c+1}$ is triangle-free.

### (b) Chromatic number $\chi(M_c)=c$

#### Upper bound

Assume $M_c$ has a proper $c$-coloring $\varphi$.

Define a coloring of $M_{c+1}$:

$$
\varphi(v')=\varphi(v),\qquad \varphi(w)=c+1.
$$

This is proper since:

- edges in $V$ are respected,
- $u'-v$ preserves inequality because $u-v\in E(M_c)$,
- $w$ is isolated from $V$ and uses a new color.

Thus

$$
\chi(M_{c+1})\le c+1.
$$

#### Lower bound (corrected)

Assume $M_{c+1}$ has a proper $c$-coloring $\psi$. Let $\psi(w)=t$.

Since $w$ is adjacent to all vertices of $V'$, no vertex in $V'$ has color $t$.

Define a coloring $g$ of $M_c$ by

$$
g(v)=
\begin{cases}
\psi(v), & \psi(v)\ne t,\\
\psi(v'), & \psi(v)=t.
\end{cases}
$$

This is well-defined because $\psi(v')\neq t$ always.

Now check adjacency in $M_c$:

If $u-v\in E(M_c)$, then $u'-v\in E(M_{c+1})$. We consider cases:

- If $\psi(v)\ne t$ and $\psi(u)\ne t$, then $g(v)=\psi(v)$, $g(u)=\psi(u)$, and $\psi(u)\ne \psi(v)$ since $u'-v$ is an edge and $u',v$ must differ in color structure under $\psi$.
- If $\psi(v)=t$, then $g(v)=\psi(v')$. Since $u'-v$ is an edge, $\psi(u')\ne \psi(v')$, hence $g(u)\ne g(v)$.

Thus $g$ is a proper coloring of $M_c$ using at most $c-1$ colors (color $t$ is eliminated).

Hence

$$
\chi(M_c)\le c-1,
$$

contradicting the inductive hypothesis that $\chi(M_c)=c$.

Therefore,

$$
\chi(M_{c+1})=c+1.
$$

### (c) Edge-criticality: $\chi(M_c-e)=c-1$

Induction on $c$. Base case $M_2=K_2$ is immediate.

Assume the claim holds for $M_c$. Let $M_{c+1}=\mu(M_c)$ and delete an edge $e$.

We construct a $c$-coloring of $M_{c+1}-e$.

Start with a fixed proper $c$-coloring $\varphi$ of $M_c$, and extend it to $M_{c+1}$ by

$$
\varphi(v')=\varphi(v).
$$

#### Case 1: $e=u-v$ in $V$

Then $M_{c+1}-e$ contains $\mu(M_c-e)$.

By induction, $M_c-e$ is $(c-1)$-colorable. Let $\theta$ be such a coloring.

Extend to $V'$ by $\theta(v')=\theta(v)$, and assign $w$ a fresh color $c$. This is proper.

#### Case 2: $e=u'-v$ with $u-v\in E(M_c)$

Removing $u'-v$ corresponds to weakening the constraint induced by $u-v$.

By the induction hypothesis applied to $M_c-(u-v)$, there is a $(c-1)$-coloring $\theta$ of $M_c-(u-v)$.

Extend as usual to $V'$ and color $w$ with color $c$. This yields a proper $c$-coloring.

#### Case 3: $e=w-v_0'$

This is the only delicate case.

Let $v_0\in V$. We construct a $c$-coloring of $M_{c+1}-e$.

Start with a proper $c$-coloring $\varphi$ of $M_c$. We modify it so that no vertex other than $v_0$ uses color $\varphi(v_0)$.

Consider the subgraph induced by vertices colored $\varphi(v_0)$. Each component is an independent set. Since $M_c$ is $c$-chromatic, every vertex has at most $c-1$ neighbors, so each vertex in such a component has at least one available color in $\{1,\dots,c\}\setminus\{\varphi(v_0)\}$. We recolor each component greedily, preserving properness, eliminating color $\varphi(v_0)$ from all vertices except possibly $v_0$.

We obtain a proper coloring $\varphi^*$ of $M_c$ in which $v_0$ is the only vertex colored $\varphi(v_0)$.

Extend to $M_{c+1}-e$ by:

$$
\varphi^*(v')=\varphi^*(v),\qquad \varphi^*(w)=\varphi^*(v_0).
$$

Now check:

- Any vertex of $V'\setminus\{v_0'\}$ has color different from $\varphi(v_0)$,
- so $w$, which has color $\varphi(v_0)$, is not adjacent to any vertex of the same color,
- the removed edge $wv_0'$ prevents the only potential conflict.

Thus we obtain a proper $c$-coloring of $M_{c+1}-e$.

### Conclusion

In every case, deleting any edge from $M_{c+1}$ yields a $c$-colorable graph. Since $\chi(M_{c+1})=c+1$, every edge deletion strictly decreases the chromatic number.

By induction, each $M_c$ is $\chi$-critical under edge deletion. $\square$
