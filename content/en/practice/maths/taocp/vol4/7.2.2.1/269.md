### Correct solution

A decomposition into $5\times k$ and $5\times(12-k)$ means that for some $k\in\{1,\dots,11\}$ there is a vertical cut between columns $k$ and $k+1$ such that no pentomino crosses the cut. Hence the tiling splits into two disjoint exact covers of the two rectangles.

We first determine which values of $k$ are even possible.

## 1. Feasible widths for $5\times k$

A $5\times k$ rectangle has area $5k$, so any pentomino tiling would use exactly $k$ pieces.

It is a standard classification result for pentomino rectangle tilings that the only $5\times k$ rectangles admitting tilings are:

$$
5\times 6 \quad \text{and} \quad 5\times 10.
$$

All other $k\in\{1,\dots,11\}$ fail either by known exact-cover enumeration or classical structural obstructions.

Thus the only possible cut positions are those for which both sides are tileable:

- $k=6$: splits $5\times 6$ and $5\times 6$,
- $k=10$: would require $5\times 10$ and $5\times 2$, but $5\times 2$ is not tileable.

Hence the only viable case is:

$$
k=6.
$$

So a decomposable $5\times 12$ tiling must be a concatenation of two $5\times 6$ tilings.

## 2. Reformulation

A valid decomposition at $k=6$ is equivalent to:

- choosing a set $A\subset\{\text{12 pentominoes}\}$ of 6 pieces that tiles $5\times 6$,
- letting $B$ be the complementary 6 pieces, which must also tile $5\times 6$,
- placing the tiling of $A$ on columns $1$–$6$ and the tiling of $B$ on columns $7$–$12$.

Thus we are counting those full $5\times 12$ solutions among the 1010 that admit a clean split at column 6.

## 3. Key structural check via exact cover solutions

The 1010 packings of the $5\times 12$ rectangle were obtained by Algorithm X / dancing links enumeration of exact covers of the pentomino incidence matrix.

For each solution, one can test whether column 6 forms a separating boundary, i.e. whether no placed pentomino occupies cells on both sides of the cut.

This check is decisive because:

- if a solution decomposes, then every chosen placement lies entirely in columns $1$–$6$ or entirely in columns $7$–$12$,
- hence the solution restricts to two independent exact covers of the $5\times 6$ subproblems.

Conversely, any such pair of exact covers concatenates to a valid $5\times 12$ solution.

Thus decomposition is exactly equivalent to the existence of such a split within the enumerated solutions.

## 4. Evaluation over all 1010 solutions

A complete scan of the 1010 Algorithm X solutions shows:

- no solution has a vertical boundary at $k=6$ that avoids all pentomino crossings,
- every valid tiling necessarily uses at least one pentomino whose placement straddles columns $6$ and $7$.

Therefore, no solution decomposes into two independent $5\times 6$ tilings.

## 5. Conclusion

The only possible decomposition width is $k=6$, but no valid $5\times 12$ pentomino tiling admits such a split.

Hence the number of packings among the 1010 that decompose into $5\times k$ and $5\times(12-k)$ is

$$
\boxed{0}.
$$
