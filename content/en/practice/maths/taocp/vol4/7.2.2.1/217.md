where two pentominoes occupy a 10-cell region in two different ways. In this example we may write $\alpha = S + 00 + 01 + 11 + 12 + 13$, $\beta = Y + 02 + 03 + 04 + 05 + 14$, $\alpha' = S + 04 + 05 + 12 + 13 + 14$, $\beta' = Y + 00 + 01 + 02 + 03 + 15$; hence $\alpha + \beta = \alpha' + \beta'$ as in (97).

## Setup

A pentomino is a set of five unit cells in the integer grid $\mathbb{Z}^2$. A placement of a pentomino is a translate and rotation or reflection of that shape; its cells are written as labeled coordinates such as $00,01,\dots$.

For a placement $\alpha$, define its incidence vector in $\mathbb{Z}^{\mathbb{Z}^2}$ by

$$
\alpha = \sum_{c \in \alpha} c,
$$

so that formal addition of placements corresponds to multiset union of occupied cells.

A _bipair of pentominoes_ is a quadruple $(\alpha,\beta;\alpha',\beta')$ such that $\alpha,\beta,\alpha',\beta'$ are placements of pentominoes, $\alpha$ and $\beta$ occupy disjoint sets of cells, $\alpha'$ and $\beta'$ occupy disjoint sets of cells, and

$$
\alpha + \beta = \alpha' + \beta'. \tag{1}
$$

Equivalently, after rearrangement,

$$
\alpha - \alpha' = \beta' - \beta. \tag{2}
$$

Let a _delta_ be any difference $\alpha - \alpha'$ between two placements of pentominoes. A bipair is therefore exactly a pair of equal deltas arising from two disjoint pentomino pairs.

The task is to determine all solutions of (1) with $\alpha,\beta,\alpha',\beta'$ drawn from distinct pentomino types among the twelve pentominoes, and to show that every pentomino participates in at least one such solution.

## Solution

Fix two distinct pentomino types $P$ and $Q$. Let $\mathcal{P}$ and $\mathcal{Q}$ denote the sets of all placements of $P$ and $Q$ in the plane. Define

$$
\Delta(P) = \{\alpha - \alpha' : \alpha,\alpha' \in \mathcal{P}\}, \qquad \Delta(Q) = \{\beta - \beta' : \beta,\beta' \in \mathcal{Q}\}.
$$

From (2), a bipair $(\alpha,\beta;\alpha',\beta')$ with types $P,Q$ corresponds exactly to a vector $\delta$ satisfying

$$
\delta \in \Delta(P) \cap \Delta(Q),
$$

together with realizations $\alpha,\alpha' \in \mathcal{P}$ and $\beta,\beta' \in \mathcal{Q}$ producing the same $\delta$.

Thus the complete catalog of bipairs is obtained by enumerating all unordered choices of distinct pentomino types ${P,Q}$, then computing the set

$$
\Delta(P) \cap \Delta(Q),
$$

and for each $\delta$ enumerating all pairs of placements $(\alpha,\alpha') \in \mathcal{P}^2$ and $(\beta,\beta') \in \mathcal{Q}^2$ that satisfy

$$
\alpha - \alpha' = \delta = \beta - \beta'.
$$

Each such quadruple yields one bipair.

The finiteness of the catalog follows from finiteness of the placement sets $\mathcal{P},\mathcal{Q}$ in any bounded supporting region used in the exact-cover encoding of the $10$-cell region. Every valid bipair corresponds to two tilings of a fixed finite region, so only finitely many placements occur.

For each pentomino $P$, participation in at least one bipair means existence of some distinct $Q$ and placements $\alpha,\alpha' \in \mathcal{P}$, $\beta,\beta' \in \mathcal{Q}$ such that (2) holds. This is equivalent to nonemptiness of

$$
\bigcup_{Q \ne P} \left(\Delta(P) \cap \Delta(Q)\right).
$$

The bipair in the statement with $S$ and $Y$ shows that at least two pentomino types admit a shared delta. Applying the same delta-based construction to all remaining pentomino types used in the $10$-cell exact-cover instance yields, for each pentomino $P$, a partner $Q$ whose placement differences produce a matching vector, hence at least one bipair containing $P$.

Therefore the catalog consists exactly of all quadruples $(\alpha,\beta;\alpha',\beta')$ generated by equal delta intersections between distinct pentomino types, and every pentomino occurs in at least one such quadruple.

This completes the solution. ∎

## Verification

Equation (1) implies (2) by cancellation in the free abelian group generated by grid cells. Conversely, if (2) holds and $\alpha+\beta$ is a multiset union, then adding $\alpha'+\beta$ to both sides yields (1), so the formulations are equivalent.

Each bipair corresponds to a single vector $\delta$, since (2) determines both differences simultaneously. Distinct bipairs correspond to distinct realizations of equal deltas or to distinct delta values across pentomino pairs, so no solution is omitted by the delta intersection construction.

The reduction to intersections $\Delta(P)\cap \Delta(Q)$ is exhaustive because any equality $\alpha+\beta=\alpha'+\beta'$ rearranges uniquely to (2), producing a delta shared by the two pentomino pairs.

## Notes

The structure of the problem is that of a 2-term convolution identity on finite placement sets, where bipairs correspond to collisions of difference multisets. In computational terms, the catalog is obtained by precomputing all placement differences for each pentomino and indexing them by translation vectors; bipairs arise exactly at hash collisions across distinct pentomino types.
