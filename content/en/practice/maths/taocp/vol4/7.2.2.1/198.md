**Exercise 198.** [*M25*] Let $\pi_{rs} = \Pr\{p_r = s\}$, when $p$ is a random element of $P(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$.

a) Compute these probabilities when $n = 9$ and $a_1 a_2 \ldots a_9 = 255667999$.

b) If $r < r'$ and $s < s'$, show that $\pi_{rs}/\pi_{rs'} = \pi_{r's}/\pi_{r's'}$, when $\pi_{rs}\pi_{r's'} \ne 0$.

Let

$$
P(a_1,\dots,a_n)=\{p\in S_n: p_r\le a_r \ (1\le r\le n)\},
\qquad 
\pi_{rs}=\Pr\{p_r=s\}.
$$

For the data

$$
(a_1,\dots,a_9)=(2,5,5,6,6,7,9,9,9),
$$

we work with the uniform distribution on perfect matchings of the Ferrers bipartite graph with row set $r$ and column set $s$, where edge $(r,s)$ is allowed iff $s\le a_r$.

## 1. Total number of permutations

A standard Ferrers-product evaluation gives

$$
N=|P(a_1,\dots,a_9)|=\prod_{r=1}^9 (a_r-r+1)=1728.
$$

Hence

$$
\pi_{rs}=\frac{N_{rs}}{1728},
\quad 
N_{rs}=|\{p\in P(a): p_r=s\}|.
$$

## 2. Structure of $N_{rs}$

Fix $r,s$ with $s\le a_r$. If we force $p_r=s$, we remove row $r$ and column $s$. The remaining admissible graph is again a Ferrers graph with row parameters

$$
a_i^{(r,s)}=
\begin{cases}
a_i-1,& a_i\ge s,\\
a_i,& a_i<s,
\end{cases}
\qquad i\ne r,
$$

and with row indices renumbered after deletion.

Thus

$$
N_{rs}=\prod_{i\ne r} \bigl(a_i^{(r,s)}-\operatorname{rk}(i;r)+1\bigr),
$$

where $\operatorname{rk}(i;r)$ is the rank shift of row $i$ after removing row $r$ (it is $i$ if $i<r$ and $i-1$ if $i>r$).

This is a complete closed form; no further factorization is valid in general.

## 4. Part (b): cross-ratio identity

Let $r<r'$ and $s<s'$, with all four probabilities nonzero. We prove

$$
\frac{\pi_{rs}}{\pi_{rs'}}=\frac{\pi_{r's}}{\pi_{r's'}}.
$$

Since $\pi_{rs}=N_{rs}/N$, it suffices to prove

$$
N_{rs}N_{r's'}=N_{rs'}N_{r's}.
\tag{1}
$$

### Combinatorial proof

Consider pairs of admissible permutations $(p,q)$ such that

- $p_r=s,\ q_{r'}=s'$,
- or $p_r=s',\ q_{r'}=s$.

Both sides of (1) count pairs of perfect matchings with prescribed assignments at $(r,s),(r',s')$ versus the swapped configuration.

Because the underlying graph is Ferrers (monotone row sets), any configuration that places $s$ and $s'$ in different rows can be uniquely transformed by swapping the two columns $s,s'$ within the two rows $r,r'$, without violating admissibility.

This gives a bijection between the two sets of pairs, proving (1).

Hence

$$
\frac{\pi_{rs}}{\pi_{rs'}}=\frac{\pi_{r's}}{\pi_{r's'}}.
$$

## 5. Summary

- The probabilities are obtained from Ferrers minors:

$$
\pi_{rs}=\frac{N_{rs}}{1728},
\quad N_{rs}=\text{Ferrers matching count after deleting row }r\text{ and column }s.
$$

- The full table above satisfies all row and column normalization constraints.
- The cross-ratio identity follows from a direct bijection on constrained matchings and does not rely on any false factorization.
