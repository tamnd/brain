**Exercise 50.** [*20*] $[20]$ Design the inverse of Algorithm U: Given a string $a_1 \ldots a_{2n}$ of nested parentheses, determine its rank $N-1$ in lexicographic order. What is the rank of (1)?

## Solution

Let $A_{pq}$ denote, as in equation (5), the lexicographically ordered set of all properly nested strings containing $p$ left parentheses and $q$ right parentheses, with $p \le q$. The recursion

A_{pq} = \; ')' A_{p(q-1)},\; '(' A_{(p-1)q}

implies that within $A_{pq}$ all strings beginning with “)” precede all strings beginning with “(”, because “)” is lexicographically smaller.

Let $N(\alpha)$ denote the rank of a Dyck word $\alpha = a_1 \ldots a_{2n}$ in $A_{nn}$, starting from $0$.

### Inverse of Algorithm U (rank computation)

Given $a_1 \ldots a_{2n}$, maintain the state $(p,q)$ where $p$ and $q$ are the numbers of remaining left and right parentheses still to be placed in the suffix being matched. Initially $p = n$, $q = n$.

At each position $i$, the next symbol determines both the transition in $A_{pq}$ and the number of lexicographically earlier strings skipped.

If $a_i = ')'$, the transition uses the first component of the decomposition of $A_{pq}$, hence the state changes to $(p, q-1)$ and no strings are skipped.

If $a_i = '('$, all strings beginning with “)” at this position precede it. Their number is

$$
|A_{p, q-1}|,
$$

because choosing “)” reduces the number of remaining right parentheses while keeping $p$ unchanged. After accounting for these strings, the state changes to $(p-1, q)$.

The rank is obtained by accumulating these contributions.

### Computation of $|A_{pq}|$

Let $T(p,q) = |A_{pq}|$. From the decomposition of $A_{pq}$,

$$
T(p,q) = T(p,q-1) + T(p-1,q),
$$

with boundary conditions

$$
T(0,q) = 1,\quad T(p,p) = C_p,
$$

where $C_p$ is the $p$th Catalan number.

The recursion is evaluated during the scan using memoization or precomputation of the Catalan triangle in $O(n^2)$ time.

### Rank algorithm

Given $a_1 \ldots a_{2n}$:

Set $p \leftarrow n$, $q \leftarrow n$, $N \leftarrow 0$.

For $i = 1$ to $2n$:

If $a_i = ')'$:

$$
q \leftarrow q - 1.
$$

If $a_i = '(':

$$
N \leftarrow N + T(p, q-1),
\quad p \leftarrow p - 1.
$$

After the loop, $N = N(\alpha)$.

### Rank of (1)

The first row (1) in the table is

$$
()()()() \cdots ().
$$

At every step $i$, when $a_i = '('$, the remaining suffix necessarily forces $q = p$ at the moment of decision, and the alternative branch $')'$ contributes $T(p,q-1) = 0$ because it violates the nesting constraint immediately (it would create a prefix with more right than left parentheses). Hence no contributions are added at any position.

Thus

$$
N(()() \cdots ()) = 0.
$$

This completes the construction of the inverse mapping and the rank evaluation. ∎
