**Exercise 5.** &#9654; [*20*] x
**5.** [20] What is a good way to generate all of the integer solutions to the following
systems of inequalities?
a) n > xt ≥xt−1 > xt−2 ≥xt−3 > · · · > x1 ≥0, when t is odd.
b) n ≫xt ≫xt−1 ≫· · · ≫x2 ≫x1 ≫0, where a ≫b means a ≥b + 2.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 3m13s

## Solution

### (a)

The system is

$$
n > x_t \ge x_{t-1} > x_{t-2} \ge x_{t-3} > \cdots > x_1 \ge 0,
$$

with $t$ odd.

From the pattern, the inequalities alternate, starting with a weak inequality at $(x_t, x_{t-1})$ and ending with a weak inequality at $(x_1,0)$. Writing $t=2m+1$, the strict inequalities occur exactly at indices $i$ where $i$ is even:

$$
x_i \ge x_{i-1}+1 \quad (i \text{ even}), \qquad x_i \ge x_{i-1} \quad (i \text{ odd},\, i>1).
$$

Define transformed variables

$$
y_i = x_i - \left\lfloor \frac{i}{2} \right\rfloor \qquad (1 \le i \le t).
$$

For $i$ even, $i=2k$, the relation $x_{2k} \ge x_{2k-1}+1$ gives

$$
y_{2k} = x_{2k}-k \ge x_{2k-1}+1-k = x_{2k-1}-(k-1)=y_{2k-1}.
$$

For $i$ odd, $i=2k+1$, the relation $x_{2k+1} \ge x_{2k}$ gives

$$
y_{2k+1} = x_{2k+1}-k \ge x_{2k}-k = y_{2k}.
$$

Hence

$$
0 \le y_1 \le y_2 \le \cdots \le y_t.
$$

From $x_1 \ge 0$ follows $y_1 \ge 0$.

Since $x_t < n$ and $t=2m+1$, one has $\lfloor t/2 \rfloor = m$, so

$$
y_t = x_t - m \le (n-1) - m = n-1-\frac{t-1}{2}.
$$

Thus the transformed variables satisfy

$$
0 \le y_1 \le \cdots \le y_t \le s,
\qquad s = n-1-\frac{t-1}{2}.
$$

This is exactly the representation of a $t$-combination with repetition allowed from ${0,1,\dots,s}$. Each such nondecreasing sequence corresponds to a unique solution of the original system via

$$
x_i = y_i + \left\lfloor \frac{i}{2} \right\rfloor.
$$

A correct generation method is therefore to enumerate all multisets $y_t \ge \cdots \ge y_1 \ge 0$ (or equivalently $0 \le y_1 \le \cdots \le y_t \le s$) in lexicographic order using the standard combination-with-repetition algorithm of Section 7.2.1.3, and then apply the inverse shift above.

This completes part (a). ∎

### (b)

The system is

$$
n \gg x_t \gg x_{t-1} \gg \cdots \gg x_1 \gg 0,
$$

where $a \gg b$ means $a \ge b+2$. Hence

$$
x_i \ge x_{i-1}+2 \qquad (1 \le i \le t), \quad \text{with } x_0=0.
$$

Define

$$
y_i = x_i - 2(i-1) \qquad (1 \le i \le t).
$$

Then, for $i \ge 1$,

$$
y_i = x_i - 2(i-1) \ge x_{i-1}+2 - 2(i-1) = x_{i-1} - 2(i-2) = y_{i-1}.
$$

Hence

$$
0 \le y_1 \le y_2 \le \cdots \le y_t.
$$

From $x_1 \ge 0$ follows $y_1 \ge 0$.

From $x_t < n$ follows

$$
y_t = x_t - 2(t-1) \le (n-1) - 2(t-1) = n-1-2(t-1).
$$

Thus

$$
0 \le y_1 \le \cdots \le y_t \le s,
\qquad s = n-1-2(t-1).
$$

This again is a $t$-combination with repetition from ${0,1,\dots,s}$. Generation proceeds by lexicographic enumeration of all nondecreasing sequences $y_1,\dots,y_t$ in this range, followed by reconstruction

$$
x_i = y_i + 2(i-1).
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
