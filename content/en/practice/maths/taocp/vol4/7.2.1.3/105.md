Let the cycle have length

$$
m=\binom{n}{t}.
$$

Write it as a cyclic sequence

$$
a_0,a_1,\dots,a_{m-1}
\quad (a_i\in \{0,1,\dots,n-1\}).
$$

For each starting position $i$, the block

$$
W_i=\{a_i,a_{i+1},\dots,a_{i+t-1}\}
$$

(indices mod $m$) is a $t$-combination, and by assumption every $t$-subset of $\{0,1,\dots,n-1\}$ appears exactly once among the $W_i$.

### Step 1: Count symbol occurrences in windows

Fix a symbol $x\in\{0,1,\dots,n-1\}$.

Let $k_x$ be the number of indices $i$ such that $a_i=x$, i.e., the number of occurrences of $x$ in the cycle.

Each occurrence of $x$ lies in exactly $t$ windows $W_i$: those starting at positions $i-t+1,i-t+2,\dots,i$ (mod $m$). Hence, the total number of pairs $(i, x\in W_i)$ is

$$
t\,k_x.
$$

On the other hand, counting by windows, each window contributes exactly one count for each of its $t$ elements, so the total number of such pairs is

$$
t\binom{n}{t}.
$$

Summing over all symbols gives

$$
\sum_x t\,k_x = t\binom{n}{t}.
$$

### Step 2: Show all symbols occur equally often

The set of all $t$-combinations is invariant under any permutation of the symbols $\{0,1,\dots,n-1\}$. Therefore, no symbol is distinguished by the collection $\{W_i\}$. It follows that all $k_x$ are equal; write

$$
k_x = k \quad \text{for all } x.
$$

Thus,

$$
\sum_x k_x = nk = m = \binom{n}{t}.
$$

### Step 3: Conclude the divisibility condition

We obtain

$$
n k = \binom{n}{t},
$$

so $k$ is an integer. Therefore,

$$
n \mid \binom{n}{t}.
$$

### Conclusion

A universal cycle of $t$-combinations forces every symbol in $\{0,1,\dots,n-1\}$ to appear equally often in the cycle. This implies that the total length $\binom{n}{t}$ must be divisible by $n$, proving the claim.
