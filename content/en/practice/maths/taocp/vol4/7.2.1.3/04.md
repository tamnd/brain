**Exercise 4.** [*16*] [16] Show that every composition qt . . . q0 of s into t + 1 nonnegative parts corre-
sponds to a composition rs . . . r0 of t into s + 1 nonnegative parts. What composition
corresponds to 10224000001010 under this correspondence?

## Solution

An (s, t)-composition is represented in Section 7.2.1.3 by

$$
s=q_t+\cdots+q_1+q_0,\qquad q_j\ge0,
$$

and the corresponding bit string is given by equation (14):

$$
a_{n-1}\cdots a_1a_0
=
0^{q_t}1\,0^{q_{t-1}}1\cdots 1\,0^{q_1}1\,0^{q_0},
$$

where $n=s+t$.

The same bit string also determines the complementary combination obtained by interchanging $0$ and $1$. Reading the complementary string in the form of equation (14) yields a composition of $t$ into $s+1$ nonnegative parts. Hence there is a correspondence

$$
(q_t,\ldots,q_0)\longleftrightarrow (r_s,\ldots,r_0),
$$

where

$$
1^{r_s}0\,1^{r_{s-1}}0\cdots 0\,1^{r_1}0\,1^{r_0}
=
1^{q_t}0\,1^{q_{t-1}}0\cdots
$$

is obtained simply by complementing every bit of the original string.

To describe $r_s,\ldots,r_0$ directly, write

$$
q_t+\cdots+q_0=s.
$$

Each $q_j$ is the length of a block of $0$s between consecutive $1$s. After complementing the bits, these become blocks of $1$s. The inserted $1$s become separating $0$s. Therefore $r_i$ is the number of consecutive separators between the $i$-th and $(i+1)$-st original zeros. Equivalently, the correspondence is obtained by complementing the bit string and applying equation (14) again. Since complementation is an involution, this correspondence is one-to-one.

For the composition

$$
10224000001010,
$$

the parts are

$$
(q_{13},q_{12},\ldots,q_0)
=
(1,0,2,2,4,0,0,0,0,0,1,0,1,0).
$$

By equation (14) the corresponding bit string is

$$
0100100100001111101101.
$$

Its complement is

$$
1011011011110000010010.
$$

Applying equation (14) to this complementary string gives the successive lengths of the blocks of $0$s between $1$s:

$$
(r_{10},r_9,\ldots,r_0)
=
(0,1,0,0,1,0,5,0,0,1,1).
$$

Their sum is

$$
0+1+0+0+1+0+5+0+0+1+1=9=t,
$$

so this is indeed a composition of $t=9$ into $s+1=11$ nonnegative parts.

Thus the required corresponding composition is

$$
\boxed{01001050011}.
$$
