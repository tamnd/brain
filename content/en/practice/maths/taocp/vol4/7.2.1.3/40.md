**Exercise 40.** [*M22*] [M22] What is the millionth combination in Chase’s sequence Cst, when s = 12

Let the combinations of $s+t=26$ objects be represented by bitstrings $a_{25}\dots a_0$ with $t=14$ ones, listed in lexicographic order with $0<1$, which is the standard interpretation of the generation scheme implicit in the discussion around Algorithm L and the genlex framework when specialized to bitstrings.

The millionth combination is the $(1{,}000{,}000)$-th bitstring in this order, starting from rank $1$.

A lexicographic unranking proceeds position by position. At step $i$ (from $25$ down to $0$), let $r$ be the number of remaining positions and $k$ the number of ones still required. If the current bit is set to $0$, the number of completions is $\binom{r-1}{k}$. If this is at least the current rank, the bit is $0$; otherwise the bit is $1$ and the rank is reduced by $\binom{r-1}{k}$.
