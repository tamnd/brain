**Exercise 70.** [*M25*] [M25] What is the maximum value of κtN −N, for N ≥0?

## Solution

Represent $N$ as a binary string $a_{n-1}\dots a_1a_0$ containing exactly $t$ ones, with value

$$
N=\sum_{k=0}^{n-1} a_k 2^k.
$$

The operator $\kappa_t N$ is the lexicographically next binary string with the same number $t$ of ones, as defined in the lexicographic generation scheme for $(s,t)$-combinations in Section 7.2.1.3. The quantity $\kappa_t N - N$ is the increase in integer value produced by this successor step.

Let $a_{n-1}\dots a_1a_0$ be any non-final configuration. The lexicographic successor identifies the rightmost position $j$ such that $a_j=0$ and some $a_i=1$ occurs to its right, then performs a local transformation that moves a block of ones leftward and resets trailing positions to their lexicographically minimal configuration. In particular, a block of consecutive ones is shifted from a lower-weight region to a higher-weight region, and all bits to its right are minimized subject to preserving the total number $t$ of ones.

To produce large values of $\kappa_t N - N$, it suffices to place the movable structure arbitrarily far to the left. Fix an integer $k\ge t$. Consider the configuration

$$
N_k = 2^k + 2^{k-1} + \cdots + 2^{k-t+1},
$$

which corresponds to the bitstring consisting of $t$ consecutive ones in positions $k,k-1,\dots,k-t+1$, followed by zeros.

In this configuration, the lexicographic successor moves the rightmost available pattern and shifts the block of ones one position to the left, producing a leading structure beginning at position $k+1$ while resetting lower positions to their minimal admissible arrangement. The resulting integer has leading contribution at least $2^{k+1}$, while $N_k$ has leading contribution $2^k$ and all remaining terms are strictly less than $2^k$.

Hence the difference satisfies

$$
\kappa_t(N_k) - N_k \ge 2^{k+1} - \left(2^k + 2^{k-1} + \cdots + 2^{k-t+1}\right).
$$

The geometric sum evaluates to

$$
2^k + 2^{k-1} + \cdots + 2^{k-t+1} = 2^k\left(1-2^{-t}\right)\frac{1}{1-1/2} = 2^{k+1}\left(1-2^{-t}\right),
$$

so

$$
\kappa_t(N_k) - N_k \ge 2^{k+1} - 2^{k+1}(1-2^{-t}) = 2^{k+1-t}.
$$

The lower bound $2^{k+1-t}$ grows without bound as $k\to\infty$ with fixed $t$. Therefore the set of values $\kappa_t N - N$ over all $N\ge 0$ is unbounded above, and no finite maximum exists.

The supremum of $\kappa_t N - N$ is infinite.

$$
\boxed{\text{There is no finite maximum; } \sup_{N\ge 0}(\kappa_t N - N)=\infty.}
$$

This completes the proof. ∎
