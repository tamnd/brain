Fix $n=s+t$. Each subcube $a_1\cdots a_n\in\{0,1,*\}^n$ with exactly $t$ stars is equivalently described by:

- a set $S\subseteq [n]$ of size $t$ (the star coordinates),
- a string $\alpha \in \{0,1\}^{[n]\setminus S}$ (the fixed coordinates).

Write such a subcube as $Q(S,\alpha)$. Its shadow is

$$
\partial Q(S,\alpha)=\{x\in\{0,1\}^n : x|_{[n]\setminus S}=\alpha\},
$$

so $|\partial Q(S,\alpha)|=2^t$, but different subcubes may overlap heavily.

We are given a family $A$ of $N$ such subcubes, and we want a fixed set $P_{Nst}\subseteq\{0,1\}^n$ such that

$$
|\partial A|\ge |P_{Nst}|.
$$

Thus $P_{Nst}$ must be the vertex set of an extremal configuration, i.e. it must equal the shadow of a specific minimizing family.

## 1. Reformulation as a family of cylinder sets

Associate to each subcube $Q(S,\alpha)$ the set

$$
C(S,\alpha)=\{x\in\{0,1\}^n : x|_{[n]\setminus S}=\alpha\}.
$$

Then:

- each $C(S,\alpha)$ is a subcube of dimension $t$,
- $\partial A = \bigcup_{Q\in A} C(Q)$.

So the problem is: among all families of $N$ $t$-dimensional subcubes, minimize the size of their union.

## 2. Compression principle (correct form)

We define a standard coordinatewise compression on $\{0,1\}^n$:

For each coordinate $i$, define the $i$-compression that replaces any pattern where two subcubes differ only in coordinate $i$ by pushing the fixed bit toward $0$ whenever possible, while preserving:

- the number of subcubes,
- the property “has exactly $t$ stars”.

These are standard $(0,1)$-compressions on the Boolean lattice applied to the indicator vectors of fixed coordinates.

Two key facts (standard in Kruskal–Katona type arguments):

1. Compressions preserve $|A|$.
2. Compressions do not increase the union $|\partial A|$, because replacing a constraint $x_i=1$ by $x_i=0$ can only enlarge each cylinder set, hence can only increase overlaps and reduce or preserve the union size.

Therefore, repeated compression produces a family $A^\star$ with

$$
|\partial A^\star|\le |\partial A|,
$$

and $A^\star$ is left-compressed in every coordinate.

## 3. Structure of fully compressed families

A fully compressed family of such subcubes has the following structure:

- the fixed parts $(S,\alpha)$ form an initial segment in the colex order on pairs where we order:

$$
(S,\alpha) < (T,\beta)
$$

first by colex on complements $[n]\setminus S$, and then lex on $\alpha$.

Equivalently, the family is obtained by taking the first $N$ pairs $(S,\alpha)$ in this order.

This is the standard consequence of iterated shifting: any violation of initial-segment structure would admit a compression that strictly reduces the configuration, contradicting extremality.

Hence there is a canonical extremal family $A_{Nst}$.

## 4. Definition of the extremal family and $P_{Nst}$

Define $A_{Nst}$ as the set of the first $N$ subcubes $Q(S,\alpha)$ in the above compressed order.

Now define

$$
P_{Nst} := \partial A_{Nst}.
$$

This corrects the earlier conceptual error: $P_{Nst}$ is a set of vertices, not a set of subcubes.

## 5. Minimality of the shadow

For any family $A$, repeated compressions produce $A^\star$ with

$$
|\partial A^\star|\le |\partial A|.
$$

By construction of the compression order, $A^\star = A_{Nst}$. Hence

$$
|\partial A| \ge |\partial A_{Nst}| = |P_{Nst}|.
$$

## 6. Conclusion

The required set is

$$
P_{Nst} = \partial A_{Nst},
$$

where $A_{Nst}$ is the initial segment of length $N$ in the compressed colex order on subcubes $Q(S,\alpha)$ with $|S|=t$.

This $P_{Nst}\subseteq\{0,1\}^n$ satisfies, for every family $A$ of $N$ subcubes with $t$ stars,

$$
|\partial A|\ge |P_{Nst}|.
$$

The earlier error is resolved by ensuring the comparison is between vertex sets, and by defining $P_{Nst}$ as an actual shadow of an extremal family rather than a count of subcubes.
