## Solution

Let $b_s b_{s-1} \dots b_1$ denote the dual representation of an $(s,t)$-combination, where

$$
n > b_s > b_{s-1} > \cdots > b_1 \ge 0.
$$

Lexicographic order on these sequences means comparison from left to right, hence decreasing lexicographic order corresponds to generating all strictly decreasing $s$-tuples over ${0,\dots,n-1}$ by repeatedly decreasing the rightmost position that can be decreased while maintaining strict decrease, then resetting all positions to its right to their maximal admissible values.

The maximal admissible completion after fixing a prefix $b_s \dots b_j$ is obtained by

$$
b_{j-1} = b_j - 1,\quad b_{j-2} = b_j - 2,\quad \dots,\quad b_1 = b_j - (j-1),
$$

which preserves strict decrease and yields the largest possible suffix under the ordering constraint.

The algorithm therefore maintains a current valid tuple $b_s \dots b_1$ and repeatedly produces the next tuple in decreasing lexicographic order by finding the smallest index $j$ such that $b_j$ can be decreased while keeping feasibility of the completion.

The feasibility condition for decreasing $b_j$ is that after replacement $b_j \leftarrow b_j - 1$, the chain inequality

$$
b_j - 1 > b_{j-1}
$$

remains satisfiable with strict decreasing completion. Since the completion forces $b_{j-1} = b_j - 2$ at maximum, feasibility reduces to

$$
b_j - 1 > b_{j-1}.
$$

Thus the correct update rule is to decrease the rightmost index $j$ such that $b_j - 1 > b_{j-1}$ holds, where $b_0$ is treated as $-1$ for boundary convenience.

After decreasing $b_j$, the suffix is reset to its maximal decreasing completion

$$
b_{j-1} \leftarrow b_j - 1,\; b_{j-2} \leftarrow b_j - 2,\; \dots,\; b_1 \leftarrow b_j - (j-1).
$$

This leads directly to the following procedure, written in Knuth’s style.

Algorithm D (Dual lexicographic combinations).

D1. [Initialize.] Set $b_j \leftarrow n - j$ for $1 \le j \le s$.

D2. [Visit.] Visit the combination $b_s b_{s-1} \dots b_1$.

D3. [Find change position.] Set $j \leftarrow 1$. While $j \le s$ and $b_j = s - j$, increase $j \leftarrow j + 1$.

D4. [Done?] If $j > s$, terminate the algorithm.

D5. [Decrease.] Set $b_j \leftarrow b_j - 1$.

D6. [Reset suffix.] For $k \leftarrow j-1, j-2, \dots, 1$, set $b_k \leftarrow b_{k+1} - 1$.

D7. Return to D2.

Correctness follows from the characterization of lexicographically maximal decreasing suffixes: after step D5, the suffix $b_{j-1},\dots,b_1$ is recomputed as the unique maximal strictly decreasing sequence compatible with the new prefix, hence no combination lies between successive outputs in decreasing lexicographic order. Every valid $b_s \dots b_1$ is reached exactly once because each step strictly decreases the leftmost possible coordinate in a well-founded ordering on $\sum_{j=1}^s b_j$, ensuring termination after all $\binom{n}{s}$ combinations are exhausted.

This completes the construction. ∎
