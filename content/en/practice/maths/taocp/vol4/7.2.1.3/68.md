[Section 7.2.1.3: Generating All Combinations](../)

**Exercise 68.** [*M01*] [M01] If α is a t-combination, what is (a) ∂tα? (b) ∂t+1α?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 15m33s  

---

Let $\alpha$ be a $t$-combination of an $n$-set, so $\alpha$ is a $t$-element subset.

The operator $\partial_t \alpha$ is the family of all $(t-1)$-combinations obtained by deleting exactly one element of $\alpha$. If $\alpha = {c_t, \dots, c_1}$ in decreasing order, then

$$
\partial_t \alpha = \{\alpha \setminus \{c_j\} : 1 \le j \le t\},
$$

which consists of exactly $t$ distinct $(t-1)$-combinations.

The operator $\partial_{t+1} \alpha$ is the family of all $(t+1)$-combinations that contain $\alpha$. Since $n = s + t$, there are $s$ elements not in $\alpha$, and each such element $x \notin \alpha$ produces a unique superset $\alpha \cup {x}$. Hence

$$
\partial_{t+1} \alpha = \{\alpha \cup \{x\} : x \notin \alpha\},
$$

which consists of exactly $s$ distinct $(t+1)$-combinations. This completes the description. ∎
