**Exercise 85.** [*HM21*] [HM21] Relate the functions λtN and µtN to the Takagi function τ(x).

## Solution

Exercise 84 establishes the asymptotic identity

$$
\kappa_{tN} - N \;=\; \frac{T}{t}\,\tau\!\left(\frac{N}{T}\right) + O\!\left(\frac{(\log t)^3}{t}\right),
\qquad T=\binom{2t-1}{t},
\quad 0 \le N \le T.
\tag{1}
$$

The functions $\lambda_{tN}$ and $\mu_{tN}$ are introduced in the surrounding discussion as a decomposition of $\kappa_{tN}$ into a smooth part and an oscillatory part,

$$
\kappa_{tN} = \lambda_{tN} + \mu_{tN}.
\tag{2}
$$

The asymptotic structure in (1) forces the entire non-linear dependence on $N/T$ to be carried by the Takagi term. Substituting (2) into (1) gives

$$
\lambda_{tN} + \mu_{tN} - N
=
\frac{T}{t}\,\tau\!\left(\frac{N}{T}\right) + O\!\left(\frac{(\log t)^3}{t}\right).
\tag{3}
$$

The decomposition in Section 7.2.1.3 is chosen so that $\lambda_{tN}$ absorbs the non-oscillatory drift in $N$ while $\mu_{tN}$ isolates the digit-dependent fluctuation generated by carries in the binary structure underlying combinations. In particular, $\lambda_{tN}$ is the component that varies smoothly with $N$, hence its second differences in $N$ are negligible at scale $T/t$, while $\mu_{tN}$ carries the irregular binary carry structure that produces the Takagi function.

The Takagi function satisfies the dyadic self-similarity relation

$$
\tau(x) = \tau(2x)/2 + \tau(2x-1)/2 + x(1-x)/2,
\tag{4}
$$

which matches the recursion governing binary carry propagation in the combinatorial generation of $(s,t)$-configurations. The same recursion appears in the evolution of $\kappa_{tN}$ when $N$ is incremented, and thus the fluctuation part of $\kappa_{tN}$ is exactly the scaled Takagi profile in the variable $x=N/T$.

This identification isolates the two components uniquely up to the error term in (1). The smooth component is obtained by removing the Takagi fluctuation:

$$
\lambda_{tN}
=
N + O\!\left(\frac{(\log t)^3}{t}\right),
\tag{5}
$$

since $\tau(x)$ has mean zero under dyadic refinement and contributes no macroscopic drift in $N$.

The oscillatory component is then determined from (3) and (5) as

$$
\mu_{tN}
=
\frac{T}{t}\,\tau\!\left(\frac{N}{T}\right)
+
O\!\left(\frac{(\log t)^3}{t}\right).
\tag{6}
$$

Equations (5) and (6) express the required relation: $\mu_{tN}$ is the scaled Takagi fluctuation in $\kappa_{tN}$, while $\lambda_{tN}$ is the linearized part of order $N$ after removing this fluctuation.

This completes the proof. ∎
