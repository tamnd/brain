## Solution

The Erdős–Lehner distribution (43) in Section 7.2.1.4 gives the asymptotic law of the normalized random variable $x$ arising from the number of parts (equivalently, the largest part) in a uniformly random partition of $n$. In this normalization the distribution converges to the standard Gumbel law with cumulative distribution function

$$
F(x)=\exp\!\left(-e^{-x}\right),
$$

and probability density

$$
f(x)=F'(x)=e^{-x}\exp\!\left(-e^{-x}\right).
$$

### (a) Most probable value

The most probable value is the maximizer of $f(x)$. Differentiating,

$$
f'(x)=\left(-e^{-x}\right)\exp(-e^{-x}) + e^{-x}\exp(-e^{-x})\cdot e^{-x}.
$$

Factoring,

$$
f'(x)=e^{-x}\exp(-e^{-x})\left(-1+e^{-x}\right).
$$

The critical point satisfies $-1+e^{-x}=0$, hence $e^{-x}=1$ and $x=0$. The density increases for $x<0$ and decreases for $x>0$, so this is the unique maximum.

The most probable value is $\boxed{0}$.

### (b) Median

The median $x_{1/2}$ satisfies $F(x_{1/2})=\tfrac{1}{2}$:

$$
\exp(-e^{-x_{1/2}})=\frac{1}{2}.
$$

Taking logarithms,

$$
-e^{-x_{1/2}}=-\log 2,
$$

so

$$
e^{-x_{1/2}}=\log 2.
$$

Taking logarithms again,

$$
x_{1/2}=-\log(\log 2).
$$

The median is $\boxed{-\log(\log 2)}$.

### (c) Mean

The expectation of the standard Gumbel distribution is known to equal Euler’s constant $\gamma$. This follows from the identity

$$
\mathbb{E}[x]=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x e^{-x}e^{-e^{-x}}\,dx,
$$

and the standard substitution $t=e^{-x}$, which transforms the integral into the classical representation of $\gamma$:

$$
\gamma = -\int_0^\infty e^{-t}\log t\,dt.
$$

Thus the mean of $x$ is

$$
\boxed{\gamma}.
$$

### (d) Standard deviation

The variance of the standard Gumbel distribution is

$$
\operatorname{Var}(x)=\frac{\pi^2}{6}.
$$

This follows from the second moment identity

$$
\mathbb{E}[x^2]=\gamma^2+\frac{\pi^2}{6},
$$

combined with $\mathbb{E}[x]=\gamma$. Hence

$$
\operatorname{Var}(x)=\mathbb{E}[x^2]-\mathbb{E}[x]^2=\frac{\pi^2}{6}.
$$

The standard deviation is therefore

$$
\boxed{\frac{\pi}{\sqrt{6}}}.
$$

This completes the solution. ∎
