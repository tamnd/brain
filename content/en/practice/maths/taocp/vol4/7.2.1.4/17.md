**Exercise 17.** [*M26*] [M26] A joint partition of n is a pair of sequences (a1, . . . , ar; b1, . . . , bs) of
positive integers for which we have
a1 ≥· · · ≥ar,
b1 > · · · > bs,
and
a1 + · · · + ar + b1 + · · · + bs = n.
Thus it is an ordinary partition if s = 0, and a partition into distinct parts if r = 0.
a) Find a simple formula for the generating function ur+svszn, summed over all
joint partitions of n with r ordinary parts ai and s distinct parts bj.
b) Similarly, find a simple formula for vszn when the sum is over all joint partitions
that have exactly r+s = t total parts, given the value of t. For example, the answer
when t = 2 is (1 + v)(1 + vz)z2/((1 −z)(1 −z2)).
c) What identity do you deduce?

## Setup

A joint partition of $n$ consists of two sequences

$$
(a_1,\dots,a_r;\; b_1,\dots,b_s)
$$

with $a_1 \ge \cdots \ge a_r$, $b_1 > \cdots > b_s$, and

$$
\sum_{i=1}^r a_i + \sum_{j=1}^s b_j = n.
$$

The $a$-sequence is an ordinary partition, the $b$-sequence is a partition into distinct parts. The variable $u$ marks each ordinary part, $v$ marks each distinct part, and $z$ marks total sum.

Let $r$ denote the number of $a$-parts and $s$ the number of $b$-parts.

## Solution

### (a) Generating function for joint partitions with weights $u^r v^s z^n$

Fix a part size $k \ge 1$. In the $a$-component, $k$ may appear any number of times; if it appears $m_k \ge 0$ times, it contributes $u^{m_k} z^{k m_k}$. Summing over $m_k$ gives a geometric series

$$
\sum_{m_k \ge 0} (u z^k)^{m_k} = \frac{1}{1 - u z^k}.
$$

Multiplying over all $k \ge 1$ yields the ordinary-part contribution

$$
\prod_{k \ge 1} \frac{1}{1 - u z^k}.
$$

In the $b$-component, each $k$ may appear either zero or one time. If present, it contributes $v z^k$, and if absent it contributes $1$, giving factor

$$
1 + v z^k.
$$

Multiplying over all $k \ge 1$ yields the distinct-part contribution

$$
\prod_{k \ge 1} (1 + v z^k).
$$

Independence of the two structures implies multiplication of generating functions, so the full generating function is

$$
\sum_{n,r,s \ge 0} a_{n,r,s} u^r v^s z^n
=
\prod_{k \ge 1} \frac{1 + v z^k}{1 - u z^k},
$$

where $a_{n,r,s}$ counts joint partitions of $n$ with $r$ ordinary parts and $s$ distinct parts.

Hence

$$
\boxed{\prod_{k \ge 1} \frac{1 + v z^k}{1 - u z^k}}.
$$

### (b) Fixed total number of parts $t = r + s$

A uniform marking for both types of parts is obtained by replacing each ordinary part and each distinct part with the same marker $x$. The resulting bivariate generating function is

$$
F(x,z)
=
\prod_{k \ge 1} \frac{1 + x z^k}{1 - x z^k}.
$$

In this series, the exponent of $x$ counts $r+s$, so the desired generating function is the coefficient of $x^t$:

$$
F_t(z) = [x^t] \prod_{k \ge 1} \frac{1 + x z^k}{1 - x z^k}.
$$

A more symmetric form follows from the identity

$$
\frac{1 + x z^k}{1 - x z^k}
=
\frac{1 - x^2 z^{2k}}{(1 - x z^k)^2},
$$

since

$$
(1 - x z^k)(1 + x z^k) = 1 - x^2 z^{2k}.
$$

Therefore

$$
F(x,z)
=
\prod_{k \ge 1} \frac{1 - x^2 z^{2k}}{(1 - x z^k)^2}.
$$

Hence

$$
\boxed{
F_t(z)
=
[x^t]\prod_{k \ge 1} \frac{1 + x z^k}{1 - x z^k}
=
[x^t]\prod_{k \ge 1} \frac{1 - x^2 z^{2k}}{(1 - x z^k)^2}
}.
$$

### (c) Deduced identity

Summing over all possible total numbers of parts removes the restriction $r+s=t$, hence corresponds to setting $x=1$ in the two-variable generating function:

$$
\sum_{t \ge 0} F_t(z)
=
F(1,z)
=
\prod_{k \ge 1} \frac{1 + z^k}{1 - z^k}.
$$

On the other hand, by definition of coefficient extraction,

$$
\sum_{t \ge 0} F_t(z)
=
\sum_{t \ge 0} [x^t] \prod_{k \ge 1} \frac{1 + x z^k}{1 - x z^k}.
$$

Equating the two expressions yields the identity

$$
\boxed{
\sum_{t \ge 0} [x^t]\prod_{k \ge 1} \frac{1 + x z^k}{1 - x z^k}
=
\prod_{k \ge 1} \frac{1 + z^k}{1 - z^k}
}.
$$

Equivalently, the product on the right is the ordinary generating function for all joint partitions when the distinction between ordinary and distinct parts is forgotten after weighting by total number of parts. This completes the derivation. ∎
