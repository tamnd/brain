[Section 7.2.1.4: Generating All Partitions](../)

**Exercise 13.** &#9654; [*M23*] [M23] (F. Franklin, 1882.)
Find a one-to-one correspondence α ↔β between
partitions of n such that α has exactly k parts repeated more than once if and only if β
has exactly k even parts. (For example, the partition 64421111 has two repeated parts
{4, 1} and three even parts {6, 4, 2}. The case k = 0 corresponds to Euler’s result.)

## Solution

Let $\alpha$ be a partition of $n$ written in nonincreasing form

$\alpha = a_1 a_2 \cdots a_m.$

For each integer value $t \ge 1$, let $\mu(t)$ denote the multiplicity of $t$ among the parts of $\alpha$. A part value $t$ is called repeated if $\mu(t) \ge 2$. Let $R(\alpha)$ be the number of such values $t$.

The goal is to construct a partition $\beta$ of $n$ such that the number of even parts of $\beta$ equals $R(\alpha)$, and such that the construction is invertible.

The construction is performed on the Ferrers diagram of $\alpha$. The diagram consists of left-justified rows of lengths $a_1 \ge a_2 \ge \cdots \ge a_m$. Rows of equal length form contiguous blocks; each repeated part value corresponds to a block of at least two identical rows.

For each distinct part value $t$ with multiplicity $\mu(t) \ge 2$, consider the block of rows of length $t$. Select the bottom two rows in this block and pair their cells columnwise. In each column $1 \le j \le t$, both selected rows contain a cell, so the pair contributes a vertical domino of height $2$. These dominoes are then reinterpreted as a single row of length $t$ in a new Ferrers-type diagram in which each domino contributes one unit of size $2$ rather than two unit cells. This produces a part of size $2t$, which is even. This defines one even part of $\beta$ for each such $t$.

All remaining rows of $\alpha$ are treated without pairing. Each unpaired row of length $t$ remains a row of length $t$ in $\beta$, except that whenever rows are involved in a pairing step, only two of them are consumed to create the even part $2t$, and the remaining $\mu(t)-2$ rows of length $t$ are left unchanged and contribute parts equal to $t$.

This construction preserves total weight. Each paired block of two rows of length $t$ contributes $2t$ to $\alpha$, and is replaced by the single part $2t$ in $\beta$. Each unpaired row contributes the same part $t$ in both $\alpha$ and $\beta$. Hence the sum of parts is preserved, so $\beta$ is a partition of $n$.

Each distinct repeated value $t$ contributes exactly one paired construction step and therefore exactly one even part $2t$ in $\beta$. No other even parts are created, since all unpaired parts remain unchanged and therefore inherit the parity of their original values, and no unpaired transformation introduces a factor of $2$.

Thus the number of even parts in $\beta$ equals the number of distinct values $t$ with $\mu(t)\ge 2$, which is exactly $R(\alpha)$.

The mapping is injective because the pairing is reversible: in $\beta$, each even part $2t$ identifies uniquely the value $t$ and the unique paired block from which it originated. Removing all even parts and splitting each $2t$ back into two rows of length $t$ reconstructs the original Ferrers diagram of $\alpha$. The remaining parts are unchanged in both directions, so the entire transformation is invertible.

Therefore the correspondence $\alpha \leftrightarrow \beta$ is a bijection between partitions of $n$ with exactly $k$ repeated part values and partitions of $n$ with exactly $k$ even parts.

This completes the proof. ∎
