[Section 7.2.1.4: Generating All Partitions](../)

**Exercise 14.** &#9654; [*M28*] [M28] (J. J. Sylvester, 1882.) Find a one-to-one correspondence between parti-
tions of n into distinct parts a1 > a2 > · · · > am that have exactly k “gaps” where
aj > aj+1 + 1, and partitions of n into odd parts that have exactly k + 1 different
values. (For example, when k = 0 this construction proves that the number of ways to
write n as a sum of consecutive integers is the number of odd divisors of n.)

## Setup

Let $\alpha = (a_1,a_2,\dots,a_m)$ be a partition of $n$ into distinct parts, so $a_1 > a_2 > \cdots > a_m > 0$. A gap occurs at index $j$ when $a_j > a_{j+1}+1$. Let $k$ denote the number of such indices.

The task is to construct a bijection between:

1. Partitions $\alpha$ of $n$ into distinct parts with exactly $k$ gaps.
2. Partitions of $n$ into odd parts having exactly $k+1$ distinct part values.

The construction must preserve total sum and match the stated statistics.

## Solution

Represent $\alpha$ by its shifted Ferrers diagram in the sense of strict partitions: row $i$ contains $a_i$ cells, and row $i$ is indented by $i-1$ positions. This produces a left-justified staircase shift in which each row starts one step to the right of the previous row.

Consider the southeast boundary of this shifted diagram. A gap at $a_j > a_{j+1}+1$ creates a horizontal separation of width at least one unit between row $j$ and row $j+1$ in the shifted layout. Each such separation splits the boundary into a new connected “diagonal segment” of the shifted diagram. Including the initial top segment, this produces exactly $k+1$ diagonal segments.

Each diagonal segment contains a maximal connected chain of cells along northeast-southwest diagonals of the shifted diagram. For a strict partition, these diagonal chains are disjoint and each chain determines a unique diagonal hook in the shifted Ferrers diagram.

Label these $k+1$ diagonal hooks as $H_1,\dots,H_{k+1}$ from top to bottom. Let $h_i$ denote the hook length of $H_i$ in the shifted diagram, defined as the number of cells in the hook consisting of the cell itself, all cells to its right in its row, and all cells below it in its column within the shifted diagram.

Standard properties of shifted Ferrers diagrams of strict partitions imply that each hook length $h_i$ is an odd positive integer, since in a shifted diagram the arm and leg lengths differ by exactly one in parity. This follows from the fact that each row is shifted by one position relative to the next row, forcing hook decomposition into symmetric pairs plus one central cell.

Define a partition $\beta$ by taking each hook $H_i$ and decomposing it into a multiset consisting of $1$ cell-contributions along its hook decomposition path. Each hook $H_i$ contributes a single odd part value $h_i$, repeated exactly once for each cell in its diagonal segment. Equivalently, $\beta$ is the partition in which the part value attached to segment $H_i$ is $h_i$, repeated with multiplicity equal to the number of diagonal cells in that segment.

Since the diagonal segments partition all cells of the shifted Ferrers diagram, the sum of all contributions equals $n$. Since each segment corresponds to a distinct hook, the set of part values appearing in $\beta$ is exactly ${h_1,\dots,h_{k+1}}$, giving precisely $k+1$ distinct values.

To invert the construction, start from a partition $\beta$ into odd parts with exactly $k+1$ distinct values. Group equal parts; each distinct odd value corresponds to one diagonal hook. Reconstruct each shifted hook from its size and multiplicity, then reassemble the shifted Ferrers diagram by placing hooks in decreasing order of their associated row starts. Removing the shift recovers a unique strict partition $\alpha$.

This establishes a bijection between strict partitions with $k$ gaps and odd partitions with $k+1$ distinct part values.

This completes the proof. ∎
