Let $G$ be a median graph containing the edge $r-s$. Write

$$
L=\{x : d(x,r)<d(x,s)\}, \qquad R=\{x : d(x,s)<d(x,r)\}.
$$

Because $r$ and $s$ are adjacent, no vertex satisfies equality, so $V(G)=L\sqcup R$. We first establish the structural properties needed for the rank definition.

## Lemma 1 (Uniqueness of projections onto convex sets)

Let $C\subseteq V(G)$ be convex in a median graph. For every vertex $x$, there exists a unique vertex $p\in C$ minimizing $d(x,p)$.

**Proof.** Existence follows from finiteness of distances.

For uniqueness, suppose $u,v\in C$ both minimize $d(x,\cdot)$ with $u\neq v$. Let $m$ be the median of $(u,v,x)$. In a median graph, $m\in I(u,v)\cap I(u,x)\cap I(v,x)$. Since $C$ is convex and $u,v\in C$, every vertex on a shortest $u$-$v$ path lies in $C$, hence $m\in C$.

Moreover, $m\neq u$ and $m\neq v$, so

$$
d(x,m) < d(x,u)=d(x,v),
$$

contradicting minimality. Hence the projection is unique. ∎

We call this unique vertex the gate of $x$ in $C$.
