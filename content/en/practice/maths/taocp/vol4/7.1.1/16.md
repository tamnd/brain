**Exercise 16.** [*15*] [15] Can we replace each $\vee$ by $\oplus$ in a full disjunctive normal form?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m18s  

---

## Solution

A full disjunctive normal form expresses a Boolean function $f(x_1,\ldots,x_n)$ as a disjunction of all minterms corresponding exactly to those input assignments on which $f$ takes the value $1$. Each minterm has the form

$$
m_a(x_1,\ldots,x_n) = \prod_{j=1}^n x_j^{a_j}(1-x_j)^{1-a_j},
$$

where $a = (a_1,\ldots,a_n) \in {0,1}^n$, and the product is interpreted using $\wedge$.

For distinct assignments $a \ne b$, the corresponding minterms $m_a$ and $m_b$ cannot both be true under any common input. Indeed, if $a \ne b$, then there exists an index $j$ with $a_j \ne b_j$. At any fixed input $(x_1,\ldots,x_n)$, the condition $m_a=1$ forces $x_j=a_j$, while $m_b=1$ forces $x_j=b_j$, which is impossible. Hence

$$
m_a \wedge m_b = 0 \quad \text{for } a \ne b.
$$

Thus, in a full disjunctive normal form,

$$
f = \bigvee_{a \in A} m_a,
$$

where $A$ is the set of assignments for which $f(a)=1$, the minterms are pairwise disjoint. At any input, at most one term $m_a$ evaluates to $1$.

For two bits $u,v \in {0,1}$ with $u \wedge v = 0$, the operations $\vee$ and $\oplus$ agree, since the only possible pairs $(u,v)$ are $(0,0)$, $(1,0)$, and $(0,1)$, and in each case both $u \vee v$ and $u \oplus v$ equal $u+v$. Therefore, for any finite family of pairwise disjoint Boolean terms, the disjunction equals their iterated exclusive-or.

Applying this to the minterms, the expression

$$
\bigvee_{a \in A} m_a
$$

coincides with

$$
\bigoplus_{a \in A} m_a,
$$

because at every input at most one summand equals $1$, so both operations return $1$ exactly when that unique minterm is active, and return $0$ otherwise.

Therefore replacing each $\vee$ by $\oplus$ in a full disjunctive normal form does not change the represented Boolean function.

$$
\boxed{\text{Yes}}
$$

This completes the solution.
