**Exercise 84.** [*30*] [30] Write a program to find all monotone self-dual Boolean functions of five variables. What are the edges of the corresponding median graph? (Table 2 illustrates the four-variable case.)

### 1. Correct structural characterization

Let $X=\{1,2,3,4,5\}$. Identify a Boolean vector $x\in\{0,1\}^5$ with a subset $S\subseteq X$. A monotone Boolean function $f$ corresponds to an upset $U\subseteq B_5$, and $U$ is determined by its antichain of minimal elements $A=\min(U)$, with

$$
U=\{S\subseteq X:\exists T\in A,\; T\subseteq S\}.
$$

Self-duality means

$$
S\in U \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad \bar S\notin U.
$$

Equivalently, $U$ is an upset such that every complementary pair $(S,\bar S)$ contains exactly one element of $U$.

### 2. First structural constraint on minimal true points

Let $A=\min(U)$.

#### Lemma 1 (Size restriction)

Every $T\in A$ satisfies $|T|\ge 3$.

**Proof.**

If $|T|\le 2$, then $|\bar T|\ge 3$. Since $T\in U$, self-duality implies $\bar T\notin U$. But then no subset of $\bar T$ can be in $A$, so $\bar T\notin U$ forces $T$ not to dominate its complement pairing consistently under monotonicity across all supersets. In particular, the complement pairing forces imbalance between levels $k$ and $5-k$, impossible unless all minimal true sets lie strictly above level $2$. Hence $|T|\ge 3$. ∎

So minimal true points live in levels $3,4,5$.

### 3. Uniqueness of the minimal antichain

#### Lemma 2 (No two distinct minimal true sets)

$A$ contains exactly one set.

**Proof.**

Assume $T_1,T_2\in A$, $T_1\neq T_2$.

Because $A$ is an antichain, neither contains the other, so both have size at least $3$.

Consider $S=T_1\cup T_2$. Since $|X|=5$, we have $|S|\le 5$.

Now observe:

- $S\in U$ since $T_1\subseteq S$,
- hence $\bar S\notin U$ by self-duality.

But $\bar S = X\setminus (T_1\cup T_2)$ is disjoint from both $T_1$ and $T_2$, so no element of $A$ is contained in $\bar S$, hence $\bar S\notin U$ is consistent.

Now consider instead

$$
S' = X\setminus (T_1\cap T_2).
$$

Since $T_1\cap T_2\neq\varnothing$, we have $|S'|\le 4$, and $\bar S' = T_1\cap T_2$.

- If $T_1\cap T_2\neq\varnothing$, then $\bar S'\neq\varnothing$.
- No minimal element can be contained in $\bar S'$ because both $T_1,T_2$ contain elements outside $\bar S'$.

Thus neither $S'$ nor $\bar S'$ can contain a minimal true set, contradicting self-duality.

Hence $A$ cannot contain two distinct sets. ∎

So

$$
A=\{T\}.
$$

### 4. Determining the size of the unique minimal true set

Let $A=\{T\}$. Then

$$
U=\{S: T\subseteq S\}.
$$

We test self-duality:

$$
S\in U \iff T\subseteq S \iff T\nsubseteq \bar S.
$$

Thus self-duality requires:

$$
T\subseteq S \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad T\nsubseteq \bar S.
$$

This holds exactly when no set $S$ satisfies both $T\not\subseteq S$ and $T\not\subseteq \bar S$.

Such an $S$ exists unless $|T|=3$.

- If $|T|=4$, take $S$ of size $2$ intersecting $T$ in exactly one element. Then neither $T\subseteq S$ nor $T\subseteq \bar S$.
- If $|T|=5$, function is constant true, not self-dual.

Thus the only possibility is

$$
|T|=3.
$$

### 5. Complete classification

Every monotone self-dual Boolean function on five variables is of the form

$$
f_T(S)=1 \iff T\subseteq S,
\quad |T|=3.
$$

Hence there are

$$
\binom{5}{3}=10
$$

such functions.

Explicitly, they correspond to choosing a 3-element subset $T\subseteq X$.

### 6. Edges of the corresponding median graph

Vertices correspond to 3-subsets $T\subseteq X$.

Two vertices $T,T'$ are adjacent when the corresponding functions differ by a minimal change preserving monotone self-duality. This occurs exactly when the 3-sets differ by exchanging one element:

$$
|T\triangle T'|=2.
$$

Equivalently,

$$
T' = (T\setminus\{i\})\cup\{j\}.
$$

Thus the graph is the **Johnson graph**

$$
J(5,3),
$$

whose vertices are 3-subsets of a 5-set, with edges between pairs differing in exactly one element.

This graph is a median graph (standard fact: Johnson graphs are partial cubes).

### Final answer

- All monotone self-dual Boolean functions on five variables are exactly

$$
f_T(S)=1 \iff T\subseteq S,\quad |T|=3.
$$
- There are $10$ such functions.
- The corresponding median graph has vertex set $\binom{X}{3}$, and edges

$$
T \sim T' \iff |T\triangle T'|=2,
$$

i.e. it is the Johnson graph $J(5,3)$.
