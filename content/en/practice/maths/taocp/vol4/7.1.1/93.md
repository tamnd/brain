**Exercise 93.** [*M20*] [M20] Prove that every retract $X$ of a graph $G$ is an isometric subgraph of $G$. (In other words, distances in $X$ are the same as in $G$; see exercise 80.)

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m02s  

---

## Solution

Let $G$ be a graph and let $X$ be a retract of $G$. Then there exists a retraction mapping $r: V(G) \to V(X)$ such that $r(x) = x$ for every $x \in V(X)$ and such that $r$ preserves adjacency: if $uv$ is an edge of $G$, then $r(u)r(v)$ is either an edge of $X$ or a degenerate edge with $r(u)=r(v)$.

Let $d_G$ and $d_X$ denote graph distances in $G$ and $X$ respectively.

Take arbitrary vertices $u,v \in V(X)$.

Every path in $X$ is also a path in $G$ since $X$ is a subgraph of $G$, hence every $u$–$v$ path in $X$ has the same length when viewed in $G$. Therefore

$$
d_G(u,v) \le d_X(u,v).
$$

To obtain the reverse inequality, let

$$
P: u = v_0, v_1, \dots, v_k = v
$$

be a shortest path in $G$, so $k = d_G(u,v)$.

Apply the retraction $r$ to each vertex of $P$, obtaining a sequence

$$
r(v_0), r(v_1), \dots, r(v_k).
$$

For each $i$, since $v_{i-1}v_i$ is an edge of $G$, either $r(v_{i-1}) = r(v_i)$ or $r(v_{i-1})r(v_i)$ is an edge of $X$. Thus the sequence $r(v_0), \dots, r(v_k)$ is a walk in $X$ from $u$ to $v$ of length at most $k$ after deleting consecutive repetitions.

Removing every maximal block of equal consecutive vertices produces a path in $X$ from $u$ to $v$ whose length does not exceed $k$. Hence

$$
d_X(u,v) \le k = d_G(u,v).
$$

Combining both inequalities gives

$$
d_X(u,v) = d_G(u,v)
$$

for all $u,v \in V(X)$, so $X$ is an isometric subgraph of $G$.

This completes the proof. ∎
