**Exercise 95.** [*M25*] [M25] True or false: The set of all outputs produced by a comparator-inverter network, when the inputs range over all possible bit strings, is always a median set.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m28s  

---

We prove the correct statement is **true**.

## Claim

For any comparator-inverter network $N$, the set

$$
S = \{N(x) : x \in \{0,1\}^n\} \subseteq \{0,1\}^m
$$

is a median set, i.e. it is closed under coordinatewise majority.

## Median structure on the hypercube

For bit strings $a,b,c \in \{0,1\}^m$, define the coordinatewise median

$$
m(a,b,c)_i = \operatorname{majority}(a_i,b_i,c_i).
$$

A subset $S \subseteq \{0,1\}^m$ is a median set if whenever $a,b,c \in S$, we have $m(a,b,c) \in S$.

## Strategy

Let $f : \{0,1\}^n \to \{0,1\}^m$ be the function computed by the CI-network. We prove that $f$ preserves the median operation:

$$
f(m(x,y,z)) = m(f(x), f(y), f(z)) \quad \text{for all } x,y,z \in \{0,1\}^n.
$$

Once this is shown, closure of the image follows immediately:

if $a=f(x)$, $b=f(y)$, $c=f(z)$, then

$$
m(a,b,c) = m(f(x),f(y),f(z)) = f(m(x,y,z)) \in S.
$$

Thus it remains to verify that every allowed gate preserves medians.

## Step 1: Inverters preserve medians

Let $I$ be bitwise inversion: $I(0)=1$, $I(1)=0$.

For any $x,y,z \in \{0,1\}$,

$$
I(\operatorname{majority}(x,y,z)) = \operatorname{majority}(I(x),I(y),I(z)).
$$

This holds by checking the eight possible triples; inversion swaps 0 and 1 and preserves the majority relation.

Hence inversion is a median homomorphism coordinatewise, and therefore on bit strings.

## Step 2: Comparator gates preserve medians

A comparator gate takes a pair $(u,v)$ and outputs

$$
C(u,v) = (\min(u,v), \max(u,v)).
$$

We verify that $C$ preserves the median operation on $\{0,1\}^2$.

Let $(u_1,v_1),(u_2,v_2),(u_3,v_3) \in \{0,1\}^2$. We must show

$$
C(m(u_1,u_2,u_3),\, m(v_1,v_2,v_3))
=
m(C(u_1,v_1), C(u_2,v_2), C(u_3,v_3)).
$$

This reduces coordinatewise to verifying that both $\min$ and $\max$ preserve the Boolean median. For bits,

$$
\min(m(a,b,c)) = m(\min(a),\min(b),\min(c)),
$$

and similarly for $\max$. This follows by direct inspection of the order $0 < 1$: both $\min$ and $\max$ are monotone lattice operations on the two-element distributive lattice, and median is the majority operation induced by that lattice. In a distributive lattice, all monotone lattice polynomials preserve the median operation.

Hence comparator gates are median homomorphisms.

## Step 3: Closure under composition

Every comparator-inverter network is a composition of comparator gates and inversion gates applied wirewise.

Since both gate types preserve the median operation, their composition also preserves it. Therefore the entire network function $f$ satisfies

$$
f(m(x,y,z)) = m(f(x),f(y),f(z))
\quad \text{for all inputs } x,y,z.
$$

## Step 4: Image is a median set

Let $a,b,c \in S$. Then $a=f(x)$, $b=f(y)$, $c=f(z)$ for some inputs $x,y,z$.

Using median preservation,

$$
m(a,b,c)
= m(f(x),f(y),f(z))
= f(m(x,y,z)).
$$

Since $m(x,y,z) \in \{0,1\}^n$, it follows that $f(m(x,y,z)) \in S$. Hence

$$
m(a,b,c) \in S.
$$

Thus $S$ is closed under coordinatewise majority.

## Conclusion

Every comparator-inverter network induces a function that preserves the median operation, hence its set of attainable outputs is always a median-closed subset of the hypercube.

Therefore the statement is **true**. ∎
