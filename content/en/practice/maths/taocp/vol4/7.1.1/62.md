**Exercise 62.** [*25*] [25] (C. Schensted.) If $f(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ is a monotone Boolean function and $n \ge 3$, prove the median expansion formula

$$f(x_1, \ldots, x_n) = \langle f(x_1, x_2, x_3, x_4, \ldots, x_n) \, f(x_1, x_2, \bar{x}_3, x_4, \ldots, x_n) \, f(x_1, x_2, x_3, x_4, \ldots, x_n) \rangle.$$

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m17s

## Solution

Fix $(x_1,\ldots,x_n)$ with $n\ge 3$ and write

$$
a = f(x_1,x_2,1,x_4,\ldots,x_n), \qquad b = f(x_1,x_2,0,x_4,\ldots,x_n), \qquad c = x_3.
$$

Monotonicity of $f$ in the third argument implies $b \le a$ in the Boolean order, since replacing $x_3=0$ by $x_3=1$ can only increase the value of a monotone Boolean function.

The median operation is

$$
\langle a\, b\, c\rangle = (a \wedge b)\,\vee\,(b \wedge c)\,\vee\,(a \wedge c).
$$

Substitute $c=1$. Then

$$
\langle a\, b\, 1\rangle = (a \wedge b)\vee b \vee a.
$$

Since $b \le a$, one has $a \wedge b = b$ and $b \vee a = a$, hence

$$
\langle a\, b\, 1\rangle = a.
$$

Substitute $c=0$. Then

$$
\langle a\, b\, 0\rangle = (a \wedge b)\vee 0 \vee 0 = a \wedge b.
$$

Again $b \le a$ implies $a \wedge b = b$, hence

$$
\langle a\, b\, 0\rangle = b.
$$

Thus the expression $\langle a, b, c\rangle$ agrees with $a$ when $x_3=1$ and with $b$ when $x_3=0$. By definition of $a$ and $b$, this yields

$$
f(x_1,\ldots,x_n) = \langle f(x_1,x_2,1,x_4,\ldots,x_n)\; f(x_1,x_2,0,x_4,\ldots,x_n)\; x_3\rangle.
$$

This completes the proof.
