**Exercise 94.** [*M21*] [M21] Prove that every retract $X$ of a hypercube is a set of median labels, if we suppress coordinates that are constant for all $x \in X$.

Let the hypercube be $Q_t=\{0,1\}^t$ with Hamming distance $d$, and let $X\subseteq Q_t$ be a retract. Thus there exists a graph homomorphism

$$
r:Q_t\to X,\quad r(x)=x \text{ for all } x\in X.
$$

We first suppress all coordinates that are constant on $X$. After this, for every coordinate $i$ there exist $x,y\in X$ with $x_i\neq y_i$.

We prove that for all $x,y,z\in X$, the coordinatewise majority

$$
m=m(x,y,z),\quad m_i=\operatorname{majority}(x_i,y_i,z_i)
$$

lies in $X$.

### 1. Distance structure in the hypercube

For any $u,v\in Q_t$,

$$
d(u,v)=\sum_{i=1}^t |u_i-v_i|.
$$

Hence for any $u,v,w$,

$$
d(u,w)=d(u,v)+d(v,w)
$$

holds if and only if, for every coordinate $i$, the triple $(u_i,v_i,w_i)$ does not have $v_i$ differing from both $u_i$ and $w_i$. Equivalently, in each coordinate, $v_i$ lies “between” $u_i$ and $w_i$, meaning $v_i\in\{u_i,w_i\}$.

Thus the interval $I(u,w)$ consists exactly of all vertices obtained by choosing in each coordinate either $u_i$ or $w_i$.

### 2. Retractions preserve endpoints and do not increase distance

Since $r$ is a graph homomorphism of a hypercube,

$$
d(r(a),r(b))\le d(a,b)\quad \text{for all }a,b\in Q_t,
$$

and $r$ fixes every point of $X$.

### 3. Convexity of $X$

We prove the key fact:

> If $x,y\in X$ and $v\in I(x,y)$, then $v\in X$.

Let $x,y\in X$ and $v\in I(x,y)$. Then

$$
d(x,y)=d(x,v)+d(v,y).
$$

Apply $r$. Since $r(x)=x$ and $r(y)=y$,

$$
d(x,y)=d(r(x),r(y)) \le d(r(x),r(v)) + d(r(v),r(y)).
$$

Using contractivity again,

$$
d(r(x),r(v)) \le d(x,v),\quad d(r(v),r(y)) \le d(v,y).
$$

Hence

$$
d(x,y)\le d(x,v)+d(v,y)=d(x,y),
$$

so equality holds throughout. Therefore,

$$
d(r(x),r(v)) = d(x,v),\quad d(r(v),r(y)) = d(v,y).
$$

We now use additivity of Hamming distance coordinatewise. If $r(v)\neq v$, then there exists a coordinate $i$ with $r(v)_i\neq v_i$. Changing a coordinate flips the contribution of that coordinate to distance. Since $v\in I(x,y)$, in each coordinate $i$, the value $v_i$ is one of $x_i,y_i$. Replacing $v_i$ by the opposite bit strictly increases at least one of $d(x,v)$ or $d(v,y)$, because in that coordinate $v_i$ already minimizes the sum

$$
|x_i - t| + |t - y_i| \quad (t\in\{0,1\}).
$$

Thus changing $v_i$ forces

$$
d(x,r(v)) + d(r(v),y) > d(x,v)+d(v,y),
$$

contradicting the equality obtained above.

Hence no coordinate can change, so $r(v)=v$, and therefore $v\in X$. Thus $X$ is convex in $Q_t$.

### 4. Closure under majority

Let $x,y,z\in X$ and let $m=\operatorname{majority}(x,y,z)$. In each coordinate $i$, $m_i\in\{x_i,y_i\}$, hence

$$
m \in I(x,y),\quad m \in I(y,z),\quad m \in I(z,x)
$$

in the hypercube.

By convexity proved above, each of these interval memberships implies $m\in X$.

Thus $X$ is closed under coordinatewise majority.

### 5. Suppression of constant coordinates

After removing coordinates constant on $X$, every coordinate is active, and the majority operation is non-degenerate. Hence $X$, in its reduced representation, is closed under the median operation of the hypercube, so its elements are precisely median labels.

### Conclusion

After suppressing coordinates constant on $X$, the retract $X$ is closed under coordinatewise majority. Therefore $X$ is a set of median labels. ∎
