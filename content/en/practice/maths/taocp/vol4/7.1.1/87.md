## Solution

In the construction, each constraint of the median structure is expressed as a pair of implications so that every edge of the underlying median graph is enforced by logical compatibility of adjacent variables.

For the free tree $\langle 63\rangle$, the median structure is already a tree, so every adjacency relation is a primitive constraint rather than a derived one. In the proof of Theorem F, each edge $u$–$v$ of the tree is converted into the pair of implications

$$
u \supset v,\qquad v \supset u,
$$

which is equivalent to the Boolean condition $u \equiv v$. This forces equality of the two endpoints in every admissible assignment.
