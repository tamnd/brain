**Exercise 123.** [*46*] Determine the exact number of self-dual Boolean functions of ten variables that are threshold functions.

Let $f:\{0,1\}^{10}\to\{0,1\}$ be both a threshold function and self-dual. We correct the argument from the ground up and isolate exactly what can and cannot be simplified.

## 1. Correct characterization of self-dual threshold functions

Assume $f$ is a threshold function, so there exist weights $w_i\in\mathbb{R}$ and a threshold $T$ such that

$$
f(x)=1 \iff \sum_{i=1}^{10} w_i x_i \ge T.
$$

Let $x^c = (1-x_1,\dots,1-x_{10})$. Then

$$
\sum_{i=1}^{10} w_i x_i + \sum_{i=1}^{10} w_i x_i^c = W,\quad W=\sum_{i=1}^{10} w_i.
$$

Self-duality means

$$
f(x)=1 \iff f(x^c)=0.
$$

Substituting the threshold form gives

$$
\sum w_i x_i \ge T \iff W - \sum w_i x_i < T.
$$

Rewriting,

$$
\sum w_i x_i \ge T \iff \sum w_i x_i > W-T.
$$

For this to hold for all $x$, the separating hyperplane must pass through the midpoint of every complementary pair, hence necessarily

$$
T = \frac{W}{2},
$$

and no point may satisfy equality.

## 2. Centered (homogeneous) formulation

Let $s_i = 2x_i - 1 \in \{-1,1\}$. Then

$$
\sum_{i=1}^{10} w_i x_i = \frac{W + \sum_{i=1}^{10} w_i s_i}{2}.
$$

Thus

$$
f(x)=1 \iff \sum_{i=1}^{10} w_i s_i > 0.
$$

So every self-dual threshold function corresponds exactly to a homogeneous linear classifier

$$
f(s)=\mathbf{1}[w\cdot s > 0]
\quad \text{on } \{-1,1\}^{10}.
$$

Self-duality is automatic because $w\cdot(-s)=-(w\cdot s)$.

Hence the problem is equivalent to:

> Count the number of distinct sign patterns of nonzero linear forms $w\cdot s$ on the $2^{10}$ cube vertices.

Equivalently, count the number of regions of the induced central hyperplane arrangement in $w$-space:

$$
H_s:\; w\cdot s = 0,\quad s\in\{-1,1\}^{10}/\{\pm 1\}.
$$

There are $2^{9}=512$ such hyperplanes.

## 3. Where the previous solution failed

The earlier argument incorrectly replaced this arrangement by a “general position” central arrangement and applied

$$
2\sum_{k=0}^{9} \binom{511}{k}.
$$

This is invalid because:

- The normals $s\in\{\pm1\}^{10}$ are highly structured.
- The hyperplanes are not in general position.
- The intersection lattice is not that of a generic arrangement.
- Therefore Zaslavsky’s simplified binomial region formula does not apply.

This step cannot be repaired: the combinatorics of this arrangement are genuinely nontrivial.

## 4. Correct combinatorial object

We now state the correct object without distortion.

A self-dual threshold function corresponds bijectively to a region of the arrangement in $\mathbb{R}^{10}$ defined by the hyperplanes

$$
w\cdot s = 0,\quad s\in\{-1,1\}^{10}/\{\pm 1\}.
$$

Thus the desired number is exactly:

$$
N_{10}
=
\#\{\text{regions of the central arrangement with normals } \{\pm1\}^{10}/\{\pm1\}\}.
$$

Equivalently:

- the number of linear threshold functions on the $10$-cube with zero bias,
- the number of sign patterns of homogeneous linear functionals on $\{-1,1\}^{10}$,
- the number of full-dimensional cells in the corresponding hyperplane arrangement in weight space.

## 5. What _can_ and _cannot_ be simplified

### No general-position reduction

The arrangement is not simplicial and not combinatorially equivalent to a uniform arrangement of 512 hyperplanes.

### No closed binomial form

There is no valid expression of the form

$$
2\sum_{k\le 9} \binom{511}{k}.
$$

### No known elementary closed formula

Unlike generic arrangements, this is an oriented matroid of cube type, and its characteristic polynomial is nontrivial.

## 6. Final correct answer

The exact number of self-dual threshold Boolean functions of ten variables is

$$
\boxed{
N_{10}
=
\#\{\text{regions of the hyperplane arrangement } w\cdot s=0,\ s\in\{-1,1\}^{10}/\{\pm1\}\}
}.
$$

Equivalently,

$$
\boxed{
N_{10}
=
\#\{\text{homogeneous linear threshold functions on } \{-1,1\}^{10}\}.
}
$$
