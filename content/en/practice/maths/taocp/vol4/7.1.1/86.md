**Exercise 86.** [*45*] [45] If $u$, $w$, $x$, $y$, and $z$ belong to a median set $X$, does their five-element median $(uwxyz)$, computed componentwise, always belong to $X$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 4m

### Corrected solution

The previous argument fails because it incorrectly claims that median subalgebras of a hypercube are subcubes. In fact, median subalgebras correspond to convex subsets of the hypercube graph, and general convex sets are not subcubes. The proof must therefore be rebuilt using convexity rather than coordinate independence of the entire set.

### Step 1. Embedding into a hypercube and convexity

By the representation theorem for median algebras, every median set $X$ can be embedded into a hypercube $\{0,1\}^n$ such that the median operation corresponds to coordinatewise majority on triples. Let $Y \subseteq \{0,1\}^n$ be the image of $X$.

A fundamental property of this embedding is:

- $Y$ is closed under ternary median
- equivalently, $Y$ is a **convex subset** of the hypercube graph

Here convex means that if two vertices of $Y$ are joined by a shortest path in the hypercube, then every vertex on that path also lies in $Y$.

### Step 2. Convex hull in a hypercube

Let

$$
S = \{u,w,x,y,z\} \subseteq Y.
$$

Consider the convex hull $\operatorname{conv}(S)$ inside $\{0,1\}^n$. In a hypercube, convexity has a simple coordinate description:

- For each coordinate $i$, let the set of values $\{u_i,w_i,x_i,y_i,z_i\}$ be examined.
- If all values are equal, then every point in $\operatorname{conv}(S)$ must have that fixed value in coordinate $i$.
- If both $0$ and $1$ occur, then coordinate $i$ is unconstrained inside the convex hull.

Hence $\operatorname{conv}(S)$ is exactly the subcube consisting of all vectors consistent with these coordinate-wise constraints.

### Step 3. Location of the 5-element median

Let

$$
m = (uwxyz)
$$

be the coordinatewise majority of the five points.

Fix a coordinate $i$.

- If all five values are $0$, then $m_i = 0$.
- If all five values are $1$, then $m_i = 1$.
- Otherwise, both $0$ and $1$ occur among the five values, so $m_i\in\{0,1\}$ satisfies the only constraint defining $\operatorname{conv}(S)$.

Thus in every coordinate, $m$ satisfies exactly the constraints defining the convex hull, so

$$
m \in \operatorname{conv}(S).
$$

### Step 4. Transfer back to the median set

Since $Y$ is convex in the hypercube and contains $S$, it contains the convex hull of $S$:

$$
\operatorname{conv}(S) \subseteq Y.
$$

Because $m \in \operatorname{conv}(S)$, we conclude

$$
(uwxyz) = m \in Y.
$$

Finally, since $Y$ is isomorphic to $X$ as a median algebra, the corresponding element in $X$ also belongs to $X$.

### Conclusion

Yes. For any $u,w,x,y,z \in X$, their five-element median $(uwxyz)$, computed componentwise in any hypercube representation, always belongs to $X$.

The key correction is that median subalgebras of a hypercube are convex subsets, not subcubes, and the argument must proceed via convex hull containment rather than coordinate independence of the entire set.
