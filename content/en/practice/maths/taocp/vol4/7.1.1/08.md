**Exercise 8.** [*24*] [24] Which of the $16^2$ pairs of operations $(\circ, \bullet)$ satisfy the left-distributive law $x \circ (y \bullet z) = (x \circ y) \bullet (x \circ z)$?

We restart from the correct structural reduction and complete the classification rigorously.

## 1. Correct reformulation

Let a binary operation be a function

$$
f:\{0,1\}^2 \to \{0,1\}.
$$

Write

$$
\bullet(x,y)=b(x,y), \quad \circ(x,y)=c(x,y).
$$

Define the left translations of $\circ$:

$$
L_x(y)=c(x,y).
$$

The identity

$$
x\circ (y\bullet z)=(x\circ y)\bullet (x\circ z)
$$

is equivalent to:

$$
L_x(y\bullet z)=L_x(y)\bullet L_x(z)
\quad \text{for each } x\in\{0,1\}.
$$

Hence:

> For each $x$, the unary map $L_x$ is an endomorphism of $(\{0,1\},\bullet)$.

Conversely, any choice of two endomorphisms $L_0,L_1$ uniquely defines $\circ$, and the identity holds.

So the problem becomes:

> For each binary operation $\bullet$, determine its unary endomorphisms, then count all pairs $(L_0,L_1)$.

## 2. Unary endomorphisms on $\{0,1\}$

There are only four unary maps:

- $I$: $0\mapsto 0, 1\mapsto 1$
- $N$: $0\mapsto 1, 1\mapsto 0$
- $C_0$: constant $0$
- $C_1$: constant $1$

Let the truth table of $\bullet$ be

$$
(a,b,c,d)=(\bullet(0,0),\bullet(0,1),\bullet(1,0),\bullet(1,1)).
$$

### Endomorphism conditions

- $I$ is always an endomorphism.
- $C_0$ is an endomorphism  iff

$$
a=0.
$$
- $C_1$ is an endomorphism iff

$$
d=1.
$$
- $N$ is an endomorphism iff

$$
a=1-d,\quad b=1-c.
$$

Thus:

$$
\mathrm{End}(\bullet)\subseteq \{I,C_0,C_1,N\}
$$

is completely determined by these constraints.

## 3. Construction of $\circ$

Each row of $\circ$ is an endomorphism:

$$
L_0, L_1 \in \mathrm{End}(\bullet).
$$

Hence:

$$
\#\circ = |\mathrm{End}(\bullet)|^2.
$$

We now classify all 16 operations $\bullet$.
