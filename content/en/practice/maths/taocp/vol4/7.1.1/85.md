### Corrected solution

Let $D$ be an antisymmetric implication digraph on literals corresponding to a 2CNF formula, and let $\preceq$ be the reachability relation in $D$:

$$
u \preceq v \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad \text{there is a directed path } u \leadsto v.
$$

Because $D$ is antisymmetric, $\preceq$ is a partial order.

By Theorem S, satisfying assignments correspond exactly to sets of vertices that are upward closed in this partial order, and the associated median set is the collection of all such assignments.

We determine precisely when the digraph is _reduced_, meaning no edge is logically redundant.

## 1. Meaning of redundancy

An edge $u \to v$ is **redundant** if removing it does not change the set of satisfying assignments. Equivalently, every assignment that is upward closed with respect to all other edges already satisfies $u \Rightarrow v$.

Thus:

$$
u \to v \text{ is redundant} \iff u \preceq v \text{ holds via another directed path}.
$$

We prove both directions carefully.

## 2. If there is an alternative path, the edge is redundant

Assume there is a directed path

$$
u = w_0 \to w_1 \to \cdots \to w_k = v, \quad k \ge 2.
$$

Let $D'$ be the digraph obtained by removing the edge $u \to v$. Take any satisfying assignment $X$ of $D'$. Then $X$ is upward closed under all remaining edges.

If $u \in X$, then closure under edges implies

$$
w_1 \in X, \; w_2 \in X, \; \dots, \; v \in X.
$$

Hence $u \Rightarrow v$ holds in every model of $D'$. Therefore removing $u \to v$ does not change the solution set, so the edge is redundant.

## 3. If no alternative path exists, the edge is essential

Assume there is **no directed path from $u$ to $v$** in $D'$, where $D'$ is obtained by deleting the edge $u \to v$.

We construct a satisfying assignment of $D'$ that violates $u \Rightarrow v$.

Define

$$
A = \{ w : u \leadsto w \text{ in } D' \}.
$$

### Claim 1: $A$ is upward closed in $D'$

If $w \in A$, then there is a path $u \leadsto w$. If $w \to z$ is any edge in $D'$, then concatenating gives a path

$$
u \leadsto w \to z,
$$

so $u \leadsto z$, hence $z \in A$. Therefore $A$ is upward closed.

### Claim 2: $A$ is a satisfying assignment of $D'$

Let all vertices in $A$ be set to true. Since $A$ is upward closed under all edges of $D'$, every implication $x \to y$ in $D'$ is respected. Hence this assignment satisfies all clauses of $D'$.

### Claim 3: $u \in A$ and $v \notin A$

- $u \in A$ because $u \leadsto u$ trivially.
- $v \notin A$ because by assumption there is no path $u \leadsto v$ in $D'$.

Thus the assignment makes $u$ true and $v$ false, so it violates $u \Rightarrow v$.

Therefore removing $u \to v$ strictly enlarges the solution set, so the edge is not redundant.

## 4. Characterization of reduced digraphs

An edge $u \to v$ is redundant exactly when $u \preceq v$ holds via a path of length at least $2$. Hence reduced digraphs are precisely those in which no edge is implied by reachability through other edges.

Equivalently, every edge is a **cover relation** in the partial order $(V, \preceq)$.

## 5. Final conclusion

The reduced implication digraph is exactly the transitive reduction of the reachability poset. Therefore:

$$
\boxed{\text{Reduced median sets correspond exactly to Hasse diagrams of the reachability partial order.}}
$$

This is the unique minimal representation of the median set associated with the 2CNF formula.
