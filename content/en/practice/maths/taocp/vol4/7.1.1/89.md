**Exercise 89.** [*24*] [24] When the construction $\langle 73\rangle$ appends a new cluster of modules to enforce the condition $u \to v$, for some literals $u$ and $v$, prove that it preserves all previously enforced conditions $u' \to v'$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m07s

## Solution

Let $N$ be the network obtained before the application of construction $\langle 73\rangle$, and let $N'$ be the network after appending the new cluster that enforces $u \to v$.

Each previously enforced condition $u' \to v'$ is realized in $N$ by a fixed subnetwork whose input and output terminals are connected to the literals $u'$ and $v'$, respectively. Construction $\langle 73\rangle$ augments $N$ by attaching an additional cluster that introduces new modules and wires used only to enforce the new condition $u \to v$. This cluster is connected to the existing network only through the terminals corresponding to $u$ and $v$; no existing wire, gate, or module in the subnetwork implementing any earlier condition $u' \to v'$ is modified.

Let an assignment of Boolean values to all literals satisfy $N'$. Its restriction to the components of $N$ must satisfy every module of $N$ whose structure is unchanged in $N'$, since these modules appear unchanged as subgraphs of $N'$. Hence the same assignment, when considered as an assignment on $N$, satisfies all previously enforced conditions $u' \to v'$.

Conversely, any violation of a previously enforced condition $u' \to v'$ in $N'$ would require a violation within the original subnetwork that enforces $u' \to v'$ in $N$, since the evaluation of that subnetwork depends only on its internal wires and the literals $u'$ and $v'$. The appended cluster introduces no new paths into that subnetwork and does not alter its internal evaluation, so no new violation can arise.

Therefore every condition $u' \to v'$ that held in $N$ continues to hold in $N'$ after the addition of the cluster enforcing $u \to v$.

This completes the proof. ∎
