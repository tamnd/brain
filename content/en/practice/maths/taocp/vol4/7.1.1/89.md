**Exercise 89.** [*24*] [24] When the construction $\langle 73\rangle$ appends a new cluster of modules to enforce the condition $u \to v$, for some literals $u$ and $v$, prove that it preserves all previously enforced conditions $u' \to v'$.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m03s

## Solution

Let $G$ be the CI-net obtained before the application of construction $\langle 73\rangle$, and let $G^+$ be the net after appending the new cluster enforcing $u \to v$. A previously enforced condition $u' \to v'$ means that in $G$ there exists a directed path from the output corresponding to $u'$ to the output corresponding to $v'$.

Construction $\langle 73\rangle$ introduces a new cluster of modules whose internal wires and external connections are incident only to the literals $u$ and $v$, together with newly created auxiliary nodes used to implement the implication gadget. Every edge and vertex belonging to the original net $G$ remains unchanged in $G^+$, since the construction acts by adjoining a disjoint subnetwork and attaching it locally at the terminals corresponding to $u$ and $v$ without altering any preexisting connection.

Let $P$ be a directed path in $G$ from $u'$ to $v'$. Since no edge of $G$ is removed in forming $G^+$, every edge of $P$ is still present in $G^+$. Hence $P$ is also a directed path in $G^+$ from $u'$ to $v'$. The existence of the additional cluster cannot destroy $P$, since destruction of a directed path would require removal of at least one of its edges, and no such removal occurs in the construction.

It remains to exclude the possibility that the newly added cluster alters the interpretation of previously existing connections in a way that invalidates the enforced implication. The construction introduces only new vertices and new edges; it does not redirect, split, or rewire any edge already present in $G$. Therefore every previously existing adjacency relation among original vertices is preserved identically in $G^+$.

Consequently, every implication $u' \to v'$ that held in $G$ continues to hold in $G^+$, since its witnessing directed path remains intact.

This completes the proof. ∎
