**Exercise 41.** [*20*] (*The pigeonhole principle.*) The island of San Serriffe contains $m$ pigeons and $n$ holes. Find a conjunctive normal form that is satisfiable if and only if each pigeon can be the sole occupant of at least one hole.

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 1m56s  

---

## Solution

Let $p_{ij}$ be a Boolean variable with the intended meaning that pigeon $i$ occupies hole $j$, where $1 \le i \le m$ and $1 \le j \le n$. Introduce auxiliary variables $q_{ij}$ intended to represent that hole $j$ is a _valid sole-occupancy witness_ for pigeon $i$, meaning that pigeon $i$ occupies hole $j$ and no other pigeon occupies hole $j$.

The required condition is that for each pigeon $i$ there exists at least one hole $j$ such that $q_{ij}=1$, hence the global condition is expressed by

$$
\bigwedge_{i=1}^m \left( \bigvee_{j=1}^n q_{ij} \right).
$$

It remains to enforce, in conjunctive normal form, that $q_{ij}=1$ can hold only when pigeon $i$ occupies hole $j$ and no other pigeon occupies hole $j$. This is encoded by the implications

$$
q_{ij} \to p_{ij},
$$

and

$$
q_{ij} \to \bar{p}_{kj} \quad \text{for all } k \ne i.
$$

Each implication is converted to a clause using $a \to b \equiv \bar{a} \vee b$, yielding the clauses

$$
\bar{q}_{ij} \vee p_{ij},
$$

and

$$
\bar{q}_{ij} \vee \bar{p}_{kj} \quad \text{for all } k \ne i.
$$

Let $F$ be the conjunction of all these clauses together with the requirement that each pigeon has a witness hole:

$$
F \;=\;
\bigwedge_{i=1}^m \left[
\left( \bigvee_{j=1}^n q_{ij} \right)
\;\wedge\;
\bigwedge_{j=1}^n (\bar{q}_{ij} \vee p_{ij})
\;\wedge\;
\bigwedge_{j=1}^n \bigwedge_{k \ne i} (\bar{q}_{ij} \vee \bar{p}_{kj})
\right].
$$

If $F$ is satisfiable, then for each $i$ at least one $q_{ij}=1$, and every such chosen $j$ satisfies $p_{ij}=1$ and $p_{kj}=0$ for all $k \ne i$, so pigeon $i$ is the sole occupant of hole $j$. Conversely, if each pigeon has a hole in which it is the unique occupant, set $p_{ij}$ accordingly and set $q_{ij}=1$ exactly for such witnessing pairs; all clauses are then satisfied. Hence $F$ is satisfiable if and only if each pigeon can be the sole occupant of at least one hole.

This completes the proof. ∎
