Let $\barwedge$ denote NAND:

$$
x \barwedge y = \neg(x \wedge y).
$$

We derive all operations of Table 1 using only $\barwedge$.

## 1. Negation

$$
\neg x = x \barwedge x.
$$

Indeed,

$$
x \barwedge x = \neg(x \wedge x) = \neg x.
$$

## 2. Conjunction

$$
x \wedge y = (x \barwedge y)\barwedge(x \barwedge y).
$$

Proof:

$$
(x \barwedge y)\barwedge(x \barwedge y)
= \neg(\neg(x \wedge y))
= x \wedge y.
$$

## 3. Disjunction

By De Morgan,

$$
x \vee y = \neg(\neg x \wedge \neg y).
$$

Using NAND for negation,

$$
\neg x = x \barwedge x,\quad \neg y = y \barwedge y.
$$

Then

$$
x \vee y
= (x \barwedge x)\ \barwedge\ (y \barwedge y).
$$

Indeed,

$$
(x \barwedge x)\barwedge(y \barwedge y)
= \neg(\neg x \wedge \neg y)
= x \vee y.
$$

## 4. Constants

Define truth and falsity using NAND.

### Truth

$$
\top = x \barwedge (x \barwedge x).
$$

Then

$$
x \barwedge (x \barwedge x)
= \neg(x \wedge \neg x)
= \neg 0
= 1.
$$

Hence it is independent of $x$.

### Falsehood

$$
\bot = \top \barwedge \top = \neg(1 \wedge 1) = 0.
$$

## 5. Projections

The projection functions are identity functions:

$$
\mathsf{L}(x,y)=x,\quad \mathsf{R}(x,y)=y.
$$

No NAND construction is required, since variables themselves are admissible as basic expressions representing projections in the functional system.

## 6. Implication and converse implication

### Implication

$$
x \supset y = \neg x \vee y.
$$

Substitute:

$$
\neg x = x \barwedge x,\quad a \vee b = (a \barwedge a)\barwedge(b \barwedge b).
$$

Let $a=\neg x$, $b=y$:

$$
x \supset y
= ((x \barwedge x)\barwedge(x \barwedge x)) \barwedge (y \barwedge y).
$$

Since

$$
(x \barwedge x)\barwedge(x \barwedge x)=x,
$$

we obtain

$$
x \supset y = x \barwedge (y \barwedge y).
$$

### Converse implication

$$
x \mathsf{C} y = \neg y \vee x = y \barwedge (x \barwedge x).
$$

## 7. Nonimplication

$$
x \not\supset y = \neg(x \supset y).
$$

Thus

$$
x \not\supset y
= (x \barwedge (y \barwedge y)) \barwedge (x \barwedge (y \barwedge y)).
$$

Similarly,

$$
x \not\mathsf{C} y
= (y \barwedge (x \barwedge x)) \barwedge (y \barwedge (x \barwedge x)).
$$

## 8. Exclusive-or

Use

$$
x \oplus y = (x \vee y)\wedge \neg(x \wedge y).
$$

We already have

$$
x \wedge y = (x \barwedge y)\barwedge(x \barwedge y),
\quad
\neg(x \wedge y)=x \barwedge y,
$$

and

$$
x \vee y = (x \barwedge x)\barwedge(y \barwedge y).
$$

Hence

$$
x \oplus y
=
\bigl((x \vee y)\barwedge(x \barwedge y)\bigr)
\barwedge
\bigl((x \vee y)\barwedge(x \barwedge y)\bigr).
$$

## 9. Equivalence

$$
x \equiv y = \neg(x \oplus y)
= (x \oplus y)\barwedge(x \oplus y).
$$

## 10. NOR (corrected critical step)

NOR is defined as

$$
x \downarrow y = \neg(x \vee y).
$$

We already have

$$
x \vee y = (x \barwedge x)\barwedge(y \barwedge y).
$$

Therefore,

$$
x \downarrow y
= (x \vee y)\barwedge(x \vee y).
$$

Substituting the NAND form of OR gives the fully expanded NAND expression:

$$
x \downarrow y
=
\Bigl((x \barwedge x)\barwedge(y \barwedge y)\Bigr)
\barwedge
\Bigl((x \barwedge x)\barwedge(y \barwedge y)\Bigr).
$$

This is the correct NOR construction.

## Conclusion

All logical operations in Table 1 are expressible using only $\barwedge$, via:

$$
\neg x = x \barwedge x,\quad
x \wedge y = (x \barwedge y)\barwedge(x \barwedge y),\quad
x \vee y = (x \barwedge x)\barwedge(y \barwedge y).
$$

Hence NAND is functionally complete for all listed operations. ∎
