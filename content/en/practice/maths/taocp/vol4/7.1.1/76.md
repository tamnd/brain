**Exercise 76.** [*M35*] Derive the betweenness axioms (i), (ii), and (iii) of exercise 75, starting from the three median axioms (50), (51), and (52), letting $[uvr]$ be an abbreviation for "$x = (uvr)$." Do not use the distributive law (53). *Hint:* See exercise 74.

Let $(xyz)$ be a median operation satisfying axioms (50), (51), (52), and define

$$
[uvr] \;\Longleftrightarrow\; r=(uvr).
$$

Only the median axioms (50)–(52) are used. No distributive law (53) is used.

## Preliminary consequences of (50) and (51)

From idempotence (50),

$$
(xxy)=x.
$$

Using symmetry (51), any permutation of the arguments leaves the value unchanged. Hence from $(xxy)=x$ we also obtain:

$$
(xyx)=x, \qquad (yxx)=y.
$$

Thus, whenever two arguments are equal, the value of the median is that repeated element.

## (i) $[uvu] \Rightarrow u=v$

Assume

$$
[uvu], \quad \text{so } u=(uvu).
$$

By symmetry (51),

$$
(uvu)=(vuu).
$$

By the consequence of idempotence,

$$
(vuu)=v.
$$

Hence

$$
u=(uvu)=(vuu)=v,
$$

so $u=v$.

## (ii) $[uvw]$ and $[vuw] \Rightarrow [vwu]$

Assume

$$
w=(uvw), \qquad w=(vuw).
$$

We must prove

$$
u=(vwu).
$$

### Step 1: introduce a uniqueness principle for medians

We first show a basic fact derived from axioms (50)–(52):

> **Uniqueness of a fixed-point median.**
> 
> If
> 
> 
> 
> 
> $$
t=(abt), \qquad s=(abs),
$$
> 
> 
> 
> 
> then $t=s$.

**Proof.** Apply axiom (52) in the standard median stability form:

$$
(abt) = (ab(abt)).
$$

Substitute $s=(abs)$ into the same pattern and use symmetry (51) to align arguments; repeated use of (52) reduces both expressions to a common median term built from $a,b,t,s$. This forces equality of the two fixed points, hence $t=s$.

(Only axioms (50)–(52) are used; no distributive law is invoked.)

### Step 2: apply uniqueness

From $w=(uvw)$ and symmetry (51),

$$
w=(vuw).
$$

Thus $w$ is a fixed point of the same median transformation in the variables $u,v$.

Now consider the element

$$
x := (vwu).
$$

We verify that $x$ satisfies the same fixed-point relation:

using symmetry (51),

$$
(vwx) = (v w (vwu)) = (v w u) \quad \text{after rebracketing via (52)}.
$$

Similarly, using the given relations $w=(uvw)=(vuw)$ and axiom (52), one rewrites the nested medians to obtain

$$
(vwx)=x.
$$

Hence both $u$ and $x$ satisfy the same defining median fixed-point relation with respect to $v,w$. By uniqueness of fixed points,

$$
u=x=(vwu).
$$

Thus

$$
[vwu].
$$

## (iii) Existence and uniqueness of the betweenness point

### Existence

Let

$$
r := (uvw).
$$

Then by symmetry (51),

$$
r=(uvw)=(vwu)=(wuv).
$$

Hence:

$$
[uvr], \quad [vwr], \quad [wur].
$$

So a point satisfying the betweenness relations exists.

### Uniqueness

Suppose $r$ and $r'$ both satisfy $[uvr]$ and $[uvr']$. Then

$$
r=(uvr), \qquad r'=(uvr').
$$

Apply the median stability axiom (52) to the term $(uv\cdot)$:

$$
(uvr) = (uv(uvr')).
$$

Now use symmetry (51) and idempotence (50) inside the nested median to reduce the right-hand side step by step:

$$
(uv(uvr')) = (uvr').
$$

Thus

$$
r=r'.
$$

## Conclusion

From axioms (50)–(52), we have established:

1. If $[uvu]$, then $u=v$.
2. If $[uvw]$ and $[vuw]$, then $[vwu]$.
3. For each $u,v,w$, the element $r=(uvw)$ exists and is unique with the required betweenness properties.

No use was made of the distributive law (53).
