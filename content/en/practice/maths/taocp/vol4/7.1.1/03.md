**Exercise 3.** &#9654; [*19*] [19] Suppose logical values were respectively $-1$ for falsehood and $+1$ for truth, instead of 0 and 1. What operations $\circ$ in Table 1 would then correspond to (a) $\max(x, y)$? (b) $\min(x, y)$? (c) $-x$? (d) $x \cdot y$?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 59s  

---

## Solution

Let logical values be encoded by ${-1, +1}$, where $+1$ represents truth and $-1$ represents falsehood. Connect this encoding to the standard ${0,1}$ system by the bijection

$$
b = \frac{x+1}{2}, \qquad x = 2b-1.
$$

Under this map, $x=-1$ corresponds to $b=0$ and $x=+1$ corresponds to $b=1$.

### (a) $\max(x,y)$

For $x,y \in {-1,+1}$, the function $\max(x,y)$ returns $+1$ if at least one input is $+1$, and returns $-1$ only when both inputs are $-1$. Under the correspondence $x = 2b-1$, this is exactly the behavior of Boolean disjunction $b \vee c$, since $b \vee c = 1$ if and only if at least one of $b,c$ equals $1$.

Thus $\max(x,y)$ corresponds to the operation $x \vee y$ in Table 1.

$$
\boxed{\max(x,y) \equiv x \vee y}
$$

### (b) $\min(x,y)$

For $x,y \in {-1,+1}$, the function $\min(x,y)$ returns $+1$ only when both inputs are $+1$, and returns $-1$ otherwise. Under the same correspondence, this matches Boolean conjunction $b \wedge c$, which equals $1$ exactly when both inputs are $1$.

Thus $\min(x,y)$ corresponds to the operation $x \wedge y$ in Table 1.

$$
\boxed{\min(x,y) \equiv x \wedge y}
$$

### (c) $-x$

For $x \in {-1,+1}$, the transformation $-x$ exchanges $+1$ and $-1$. Under the map $b = (x+1)/2$, this induces $b \mapsto 1-b$, which is Boolean complementation.

In Table 1 this is the unary negation operation $\bar{x}$ (also denoted $\neg x$), corresponding to left complementation.

$$
\boxed{-x \equiv \bar{x}}
$$

### (d) $x \cdot y$

For $x,y \in {-1,+1}$, the product satisfies

$$
x \cdot y =
\begin{cases}
+1, & x=y,\\
-1, & x \ne y.
\end{cases}
$$

Thus $x \cdot y$ is $+1$ exactly when both inputs have the same truth value and $-1$ otherwise.

In Boolean terms this is equivalence $x \equiv y$ (XNOR), whose truth table is $1001$ in Table 1.

$$
\boxed{x \cdot y \equiv x \equiv y}
$$

This completes the identification of the required operations. ∎
