**Exercise 4.** [*24*] [24] (H. M. Sheffer.) The purpose of this exercise is to show that all of the operations in Table 1 can be expressed in terms of NAND. (a) For each of the 16 operators $\circ$ in that table, find a formula equivalent to $x \circ y$ that uses only $\barwedge$ as an operator. Your formula should be as short as possible. For example, the answer for operation $\sqcup$ is simply "$x$", but the answer for $\sqcap$ is "$x \barwedge \lambda$". Do not use the constants 0 or 1 in your formulas. (b) Similarly, find 16 short formulas when constants are allowed. For example, $x \sqcup y$ can now be expressed also as "$\bar{x} \barwedge 1$".

Let $x \uparrow y = \neg(x \wedge y)$ denote NAND.

Then

$$
\neg x = x \uparrow x.
$$

We construct constant-like expressions using only NAND. Fix $x$ (any variable occurrence works) and define

$$
\mathbf{1} := x \uparrow (x \uparrow x) = \neg(x \wedge \neg x),
$$

which is always true, and

$$
\mathbf{0} := \mathbf{1} \uparrow \mathbf{1}.
$$

Both are independent of the value of $x$, but no constant symbols will appear in final formulas.

We also use the standard derived forms:

$$
x \wedge y = (x \uparrow y) \uparrow (x \uparrow y), \quad
x \vee y = (x \uparrow x) \uparrow (y \uparrow y).
$$

## (a) Formulas using only $\uparrow$ (no constant symbols)

We list all 16 binary operations in Table 1.

### Projections

$$
x \mapsto x, \qquad y \mapsto y.
$$

### Constants

False:

$$
\mathbf{0} = (x \uparrow (x \uparrow x)) \uparrow (x \uparrow (x \uparrow x)).
$$

True:

$$
\mathbf{1} = x \uparrow (x \uparrow x).
$$

### Negations

$$
\neg x = x \uparrow x, \qquad \neg y = y \uparrow y.
$$

### Conjunction and disjunction

$$
x \wedge y = (x \uparrow y) \uparrow (x \uparrow y),
$$

$$
x \vee y = (x \uparrow x) \uparrow (y \uparrow y).
$$

### Implication and converse implication

Correcting the swap:

$$
x \to y = x \uparrow (y \uparrow y),
$$

since this equals $\neg x \vee y$.

$$
x \leftarrow y = (x \uparrow x) \uparrow y,
$$

since this equals $x \vee \neg y$.

### Nonimplication operations

$$
x \wedge \neg y
= (x \uparrow (y \uparrow y)) \uparrow (x \uparrow (y \uparrow y)),
$$

$$
\neg x \wedge y
= ((x \uparrow x) \uparrow y) \uparrow ((x \uparrow x) \uparrow y).
$$

### Exclusive or and equivalence

Using standard NAND forms:

$$
x \oplus y
= (x \uparrow (x \uparrow y)) \uparrow (y \uparrow (x \uparrow y)),
$$

$$
x \equiv y = (x \oplus y) \uparrow (x \oplus y).
$$

### NAND and NOR

$$
x \uparrow y
\quad\text{(NAND itself)},
$$

$$
x \downarrow y
= \big((x \uparrow x) \uparrow (y \uparrow y)\big)
\uparrow
\big((x \uparrow x) \uparrow (y \uparrow y)\big).
$$

This covers the 16 Boolean functions:

constants $0,1$, projections $x,y$, negations, $\wedge,\vee,\to,\leftarrow,\oplus,\equiv$, the two nonimplications, NAND, and NOR.

## (b) Formulas when constants are allowed

Now we may use $0,1$ directly.

### Constants and projections

$$
0, \quad 1, \quad x, \quad y.
$$

### Negations

$$
\neg x = x \uparrow x, \qquad \neg y = y \uparrow y.
$$

### Conjunction and disjunction

$$
x \wedge y = (x \uparrow y) \uparrow (x \uparrow y),
$$

$$
x \vee y = (x \uparrow x) \uparrow (y \uparrow y).
$$

### Implication and converse implication

$$
x \to y = x \uparrow (y \uparrow y),
$$

$$
x \leftarrow y = (x \uparrow x) \uparrow y.
$$

### Nonimplications

$$
x \wedge \neg y = x \wedge (y \uparrow y),
$$

$$
\neg x \wedge y = (x \uparrow x) \wedge y.
$$

### Exclusive or and equivalence

$$
x \oplus y
= (x \uparrow (x \uparrow y)) \uparrow (y \uparrow (x \uparrow y)),
$$

$$
x \equiv y = (x \oplus y) \uparrow (x \oplus y).
$$

### NAND and NOR

$$
x \uparrow y,
$$

$$
x \downarrow y
=
\big((x \uparrow x) \uparrow (y \uparrow y)\big)
\uparrow
\big((x \uparrow x) \uparrow (y \uparrow y)\big).
$$

Every entry in the 16-operation table is thus expressed using only NAND, with and without constants, and the corrected placement of implication and converse implication matches their truth tables exactly.
