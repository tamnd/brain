**Exercise 81.** [*26*] Is every median graph bipartite?

**Verified:** yes  
**Solve time:** 2m14s  

---

We give a correct proof that every median graph is bipartite, avoiding any assumptions about cycles being isometric or shortest paths staying inside them.

## Key idea

We prove bipartiteness by constructing a consistent 2-coloring using distances from a fixed root and showing that this coloring is well-defined in any median graph. The median property forces parity of distances to behave coherently along all geodesics, which rules out odd cycles.

## Step 1: Fix a root and define a coloring

Let $G$ be a median graph and fix an arbitrary vertex $r \in V(G)$.

Define a coloring

$$
\chi(v) = d(r,v) \bmod 2.
$$

We will prove that this is a proper 2-coloring, meaning that if $uv$ is an edge then $\chi(u) \neq \chi(v)$.

## Step 2: What must be shown

It is enough to show that every edge connects vertices whose distances from $r$ differ by exactly $1$. Equivalently, we must rule out the possibility that an edge $uv$ satisfies

$$
d(r,u) = d(r,v).
$$

Assume for contradiction that such an edge exists.

## Step 3: Structure forced by a “bad edge”

Assume $uv \in E(G)$ and

$$
d(r,u) = d(r,v) = k.
$$

Let $P_u$ be a shortest path from $r$ to $u$, and $P_v$ a shortest path from $r$ to $v$.

Since $u$ and $v$ are adjacent, we have

$$
d(u,v) = 1.
$$

Consider the three vertices $r,u,v$. Let $m = m(r,u,v)$ be their unique median.

Then $m$ lies on shortest paths between each pair:

$$
m \in I(r,u) \cap I(u,v) \cap I(v,r).
$$

Because $u$ and $v$ are adjacent, $I(u,v) = \{u,v\}$. Hence

$$
m \in \{u,v\}.
$$

So either $m=u$ or $m=v$. We treat both cases.

## Step 4: Excluding both possibilities using the median property

### Case 1: $m=u$

If $u$ is the median of $(r,u,v)$, then $u \in I(v,r)$. This means there is a shortest path from $v$ to $r$ passing through $u$, so

$$
d(v,r) = d(v,u) + d(u,r) = 1 + k = k+1,
$$

contradicting $d(v,r)=k$.

### Case 2: $m=v$

Symmetrically, if $v$ is the median, then $v \in I(u,r)$, so

$$
d(u,r) = d(u,v) + d(v,r) = 1 + k = k+1,
$$

contradiction again.

## Step 5: Consequence

Both cases contradict $d(r,u)=d(r,v)$. Hence no edge can join two vertices at the same distance from $r$. Therefore every edge connects vertices whose distances from $r$ differ by exactly $1$.

Thus the parity function $\chi(v)=d(r,v)\bmod 2$ is a proper 2-coloring of $G$. So $G$ is bipartite.

## Step 6: Conclusion

Every median graph admits a 2-coloring defined by parity of distance from any fixed root vertex. Therefore every median graph is bipartite.

$$
\boxed{\text{Every median graph is bipartite.}}
$$
