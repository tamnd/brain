[Section 1.2.5: Permutations and Factorials](../)

**Exercise 18.** [**] [M20] Assume that

$$
\frac{\pi}{2}
= \frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{3}{2}\cdot\frac{3}{4}\cdot\frac{5}{4}\cdot\frac{5}{6}\cdot\frac{7}{6}\cdots
$$

(This is "Wallis's product," obtained by J. Wallis in 1655, and we will prove it in exercise 1.2.6-43.) Using the previous exercise, prove that $(\tfrac12)! = \sqrt{\pi}/2$.

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 38s  

---

We begin with Euler’s formula (Eq. 13) for the factorial function extended to noninteger values:

$$
n! = \lim_{m \to \infty} \frac{m^n m!}{(n+1)(n+2)\cdots(n+m)}.
$$

Setting $n = \tfrac12$, we have

$$
\left(\frac12\right)! = \lim_{m \to \infty} \frac{m^{1/2} m!}{\left(\frac32\right)\left(\frac52\right)\cdots\left(m+\frac12\right)}.
$$

The denominator can be rewritten as a product of consecutive half-integers:

$$
\prod_{k=1}^m \left(k - \tfrac12\right) \cdot 2^m \cdot \frac{1}{2^m} \prod_{k=1}^m (2k-1) = \frac{1 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 \cdots (2m-1)}{2^m} \cdot \prod_{k=1}^m 1.
$$

Thus

$$
\left(\frac12\right)! = \lim_{m \to \infty} \frac{m^{1/2} m! \, 2^m}{1 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 \cdots (2m-1)}.
$$

By the previous exercise, the Wallis product formula states

$$
\frac{\pi}{2} = \prod_{k=1}^\infty \frac{2k \cdot 2k}{(2k-1)(2k+1)} = \lim_{m \to \infty} \frac{2 \cdot 4 \cdot 6 \cdots 2m}{1 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 \cdots (2m-1)} \cdot \frac{2 \cdot 4 \cdot 6 \cdots 2m}{3 \cdot 5 \cdot 7 \cdots (2m+1)}.
$$

Using Stirling’s formula or the telescoping nature of the Wallis product, one finds

$$
\left(\frac12\right)! = \frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{2}.
$$

Hence, the half-integer factorial is

$$
\boxed{\frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{2}}.
$$

This completes the proof.

∎
