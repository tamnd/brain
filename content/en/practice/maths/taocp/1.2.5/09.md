[Section 1.2.5: Permutations and Factorials](../)

**Exercise 9.** [**] [M10] Determine the values of $\Gamma(\tfrac12)$ and $\Gamma(-\tfrac12)$, given that $(\tfrac12)! = \sqrt{\pi}/2$.

x 10. [HM20] Does the identity $\Gamma(x+1)=x\Gamma(x)$ hold for all real numbers $x$? (See exercise 7.)

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 30s  

---

By definition, $\Gamma(x) = (x-1)!$ for positive integers and extends the factorial function to real numbers. Since $(\tfrac12)! = \sqrt{\pi}/2$, we have

$\Gamma\Bigl(\tfrac12\Bigr) = (\tfrac12-1)! = (-\tfrac12)! = \sqrt{\pi}.$

Using the functional equation $\Gamma(x+1) = x\Gamma(x)$, we obtain

$\Gamma\Bigl(-\tfrac12\Bigr) = \frac{\Gamma(\tfrac12)}{-\tfrac12} = \frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{-1/2} = -2\sqrt{\pi}.$

Hence the values are

$\boxed{\Gamma\Bigl(\tfrac12\Bigr) = \sqrt{\pi},\qquad \Gamma\Bigl(-\tfrac12\Bigr) = -2\sqrt{\pi}}.$
