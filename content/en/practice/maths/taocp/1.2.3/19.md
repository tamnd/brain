[Section 1.2.3: Sums and Products](../)

**Exercise 19.** [*20*] What is $\sum_{j=m}^n (a_j - a_{j-1})$?

**Verified:** no  
**Solve time:** 36s  

---

## Solution

By rule (d), Eq. (11), with

$$
R(j) : m \le j \le n, \qquad S(j) : m-1 \le j \le n-1,
$$

we have

$$
\sum_{j=m}^n a_j - \sum_{j=m-1}^{n-1} a_j
=
\sum_{R(j)} a_j - \sum_{S(j)} a_j.
$$

The sets defined by $R(j)$ and $S(j)$ satisfy

$$
R(j) \text{ and } S(j) \iff m \le j \le n-1,
$$

while

$$
R(j) \text{ or } S(j) \iff m-1 \le j \le n.
$$

Hence Eq. (11) gives

$$
\sum_{j=m}^n a_j + \sum_{j=m-1}^{n-1} a_j
=
\sum_{j=m-1}^n a_j + \sum_{j=m}^{n-1} a_j.
$$

Rearranging,

$$
\sum_{j=m}^n a_j - \sum_{j=m-1}^{n-1} a_j
=
a_n - a_{m-1},
$$

because

$$
\sum_{j=m-1}^n a_j
=
a_{m-1} + \sum_{j=m}^{n-1} a_j + a_n.
$$

Now

$$
\sum_{j=m}^n (a_j-a_{j-1})
=
\sum_{j=m}^n a_j - \sum_{j=m}^n a_{j-1}.
$$

By the change of variable $k=j-1$, using Eq. (6),

$$
\sum_{j=m}^n a_{j-1}
=
\sum_{k=m-1}^{n-1} a_k.
$$

Therefore

$$
\sum_{j=m}^n (a_j-a_{j-1})
=
\sum_{j=m}^n a_j - \sum_{k=m-1}^{n-1} a_k
=
a_n-a_{m-1}.
$$

Thus

$$
\boxed{\sum_{j=m}^n (a_j-a_{j-1}) = a_n-a_{m-1}.}
$$
