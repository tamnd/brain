[Section 1.2.3: Sums and Products](../)

**Exercise 34.** [*M25*] Prove that

$$\sum_{k=1}^n \frac{\prod_{1 \le r \le n, \, r \ne m} (x + k - r)}{\prod_{1 \le r \le n, \, r \ne k} (k - r)} = 1,$$

provided that $1 \le m \le n$ and $x$ is arbitrary. For example, if $n = 4$ and $m = 2$, then

$$\frac{x(x-2)(x-3)}{(-1)(-2)(-3)} + \frac{(x+1)(x-1)(x-2)}{(1)(-1)(-2)} + \frac{(x+2)x(x-1)}{(2)(1)(-1)} + \frac{(x+3)(x+1)x}{(3)(2)(1)} = 1.$$

## Solution

We wish to prove

$$
\sum_{k=1}^n \frac{\prod_{\substack{1 \le r \le n \\ r \ne m}} (x + k - r)}{\prod_{\substack{1 \le r \le n \\ r \ne k}} (k - r)} = 1,
$$

for arbitrary $x$ and fixed $m$ with $1 \le m \le n$.

Let

$$
f(x) = \sum_{k=1}^n \frac{\prod_{\substack{1 \le r \le n \\ r \ne m}} (x + k - r)}{\prod_{\substack{1 \le r \le n \\ r \ne k}} (k - r)}.
$$

Observe that each term is a polynomial of degree $n-1$ in $x$, because the numerator has $n-1$ linear factors and the denominator is independent of $x$. Hence $f(x)$ is a polynomial of degree at most $n-1$.

Define the Lagrange interpolation polynomial based on the nodes $x = 1 - k$, $k = 1, 2, \ldots, n$, and the "data" given by

$$
y_k = \frac{\prod_{\substack{1 \le r \le n \\ r \ne m}} (k - r + k)}{\prod_{\substack{1 \le r \le n \\ r \ne k}} (k - r)}.
$$

Instead of computing $y_k$ directly, consider the simpler approach of evaluating $f(x)$ at $x = 1-m$. Substituting $x = 1 - m$ into the numerator:

$$
\prod_{\substack{1 \le r \le n \\ r \ne m}} (1 - m + k - r) = \prod_{\substack{1 \le r \le n \\ r \ne m}} (k - (r + m - 1)).
$$

Notice that for $k \ne m$, one of the factors in the numerator is $k - k = 0$, because $r = k$ occurs in the product over $r \ne m$. Therefore, all terms vanish except the term with $k = m$.

For $k = m$, the numerator becomes

$$
\prod_{\substack{1 \le r \le n \\ r \ne m}} (m + (1 - m) - r) = \prod_{\substack{1 \le r \le n \\ r \ne m}} (1 - r) = \prod_{\substack{r=1 \\ r \ne m}}^n (1 - r).
$$

The denominator for $k = m$ is

$$
\prod_{\substack{1 \le r \le n \\ r \ne m}} (m - r) = \prod_{\substack{r=1 \\ r \ne m}}^n (m - r).
$$

Compute the ratio term by term. For $r < m$,

$$
\frac{1 - r}{m - r} = \frac{-(r - 1)}{m - r} = \frac{-(r - 1)}{-(r - m)} = 1,
$$

and for $r > m$,

$$
\frac{1 - r}{m - r} = \frac{-(r - 1)}{-(r - m)} = 1.
$$

Hence the term with $k = m$ evaluates to 1, and all other terms vanish at $x = 1 - m$.

Since $f(x)$ is a polynomial of degree $n-1$, and we have found $n$ distinct points $x = 1 - k$, $k = 1, 2, \ldots, n$, at which $f(x) = 1$, it follows that $f(x)$ is the constant polynomial 1.

Therefore,

$$
\sum_{k=1}^n \frac{\prod_{\substack{1 \le r \le n \\ r \ne m}} (x + k - r)}{\prod_{\substack{1 \le r \le n \\ r \ne k}} (k - r)} = 1.
$$

This completes the proof.

∎
