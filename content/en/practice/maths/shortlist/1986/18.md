# IMO 1986 SL 18

**Origin:** TUR

## Problem

Let AX, BY, CZ be three cevians concurrent at an inte-
rior point D of a triangle ABC. Prove that if two of the quadrangles
DY AZ, DZBX, DXCY are circumscribable, so is the third.

## Solution

We shall use the following criterion for a quadrangle to be circumscribable.
Lemma. The quadrangle AY DZ is circumscribable if and only if DB −
DC = AB −AC.
