# IMO 1986 SL 14

**Origin:** IRE

## Problem

The circle inscribed in a triangle ABC touches the sides
BC, CA, AB in D, E, F, respectively, and X, Y, Z are the midpoints of
EF, FD, DE, respectively. Prove that the centers of the inscribed circle
and of the circles around XY Z and ABC are collinear.

## Solution

We shall use the following simple fact.
Lemma. If <k is the image of a circle k under an inversion centered at a
point Z, and O1, O2 are centers of k and <k, then O1, O2, and Z are
collinear.
Proof. The result follows immediately from the symmetry with respect
to the line ZO1.
Let I be the center of the inscribed circle i. Since IX \cdot IA = IE2, the
inversion with respect to i takes points A into X, and analogously B, C
into Y, Z respectively. It follows from the lemma that the center of circle
ABC, the center of circle XY Z, and point I are collinear.
