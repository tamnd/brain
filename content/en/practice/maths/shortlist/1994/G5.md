# IMO 1994 SL G5

**Origin:** CYP  |  **Category:** Geometry

## Problem

A line l does not meet a circle \omega with center O. E is the
point on l such that OE is perpendicular to l. M is any point on l other
than E. The tangents from M to \omega touch it at A and B. C is the point on
MA such that EC is perpendicular to MA. D is the point on MB such
that ED is perpendicular to MB. The line CD cuts OE at F. Prove that
the location of F is independent of that of M.
