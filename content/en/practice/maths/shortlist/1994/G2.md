# IMO 1994 SL G2

**Origin:** UKR  |  **Category:** Geometry

## Problem

ABCD is a quadrilateral with BC parallel to AD. M is the
midpoint of CD, P that of MA and Q that of MB. The lines DP and
CQ meet at N. Prove that N is not outside triangle ABM.8

## Solution

Although it does not seem to have been noticed at the jury, the state-
ment of the problem is false. For A(0, 0), B(0, 4), C(1, 4), D(7, 0), we have
M(4, 2), P(2, 1), Q(2, 3) and N(9/2, 1/2) ̸\in\triangleABM.

inside ABCD and goes as follows: The case AD = BC is trivial, so let
