# IMO 1993 SL 16

**Origin:** MCD

## Problem

Let n \inN, n \geq2, and A0 = (a01, a02, . . . , a0n) be any n-tuple
of natural numbers such that 0 \leqa0i \leqi−1, for i = 1, . . . , n. The n-tuples
A1 = (a11, a12, . . . , a1n), A2 = (a21, a22, . . . , a2n), . . . are deﬁned by
ai+1,j = Card{ai,l | 1 \leql \leqj−1, ai,l \geqai,j},
for i \inN and j = 1, . . . , n.
Prove that there exists k \inN, such that Ak+2 = Ak.
