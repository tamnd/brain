# IMO 1983 SL 4

**Origin:** BEL

## Problem

On the sides of the triangle ABC, three similar isosceles tri-
angles ABP (AP = PB), AQC (AQ = QC), and BRC (BR = RC) are
constructed. The ﬁrst two are constructed externally to the triangle ABC,
but the third is placed in the same half-plane determined by the line BC
as the triangle ABC. Prove that APRQ is a parallelogram.

## Solution

The rotational homothety centered at C that sends B to R also sends A
to Q; hence the triangles ABC and QRC are similar. For the same reason,

\triangleABC and \trianglePBR are similar. Moreover, BR = CR; hence \triangleCRQ ∼=
\triangleRBP. Thus PR = QC = AQ and QR = PB = PA, so APQR is a
parallelogram.
