# IMO 1970 SL 11

**Origin:** SWE

## Problem

Let P, Q, R be polynomials and let S(x) = P(x3) + xQ(x3) +
x2R(x3) be a polynomial of degree n whose roots x1, . . . , xn are distinct.
Construct with the aid of the polynomials P, Q, R a polynomial T of degree
n that has the roots x3
1, x3
2, . . . , x3
n.

\omega2xQ(x3) + \omegax2R(x3), the above expression reduces to
T (x3) = P 3(x3) + x3Q3(x3) + x6R3(x3) −3P(x3)Q(x3)R(x3).
