# IMO 1977 SL 13

**Origin:** POL

## Problem

Let B be a set of k sequences each having n terms equal to 1 or
−1. The product of two such sequences (a1, a2, . . . , an) and (b1, b2, . . . , bn)
is deﬁned as (a1b1, a2b2, . . . , anbn). Prove that there exists a sequence
(c1, c2, . . . , cn) such that the intersection of B and the set containing all
sequences from B multiplied by (c1, c2, . . . , cn) contains at most k2/2n
sequences.
