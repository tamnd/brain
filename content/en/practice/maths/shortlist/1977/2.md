# IMO 1977 SL 2

**Origin:** CZS

## Problem

A lattice point in the plane is a point both of whose coordinates
are integers. Each lattice point has four neighboring points: upper, lower,
left, and right. Let k be a circle with radius r \geq2, that does not pass
through any lattice point. An interior boundary point is a lattice point
lying inside the circle k that has a neighboring point lying outside k.
Similarly, an exterior boundary point is a lattice point lying outside the
circle k that has a neighboring point lying inside k. Prove that there are
four more exterior boundary points than interior boundary points.
