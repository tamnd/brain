# IMO 1977 SL 14

## Problem

(FIN 2‘) Let E be a ﬁnite set of points such that E is not contained in
a plane and no three points of E are collinear. Show that at least one of
the following alternatives holds:
(i) E contains ﬁve points that are vertices of a convex pyramid having
no other points in common with E;
(ii) some plane contains exactly three points from E.
