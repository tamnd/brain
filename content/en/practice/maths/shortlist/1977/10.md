# IMO 1977 SL 10

**Origin:** NET

## Problem

Let n be an integer greater than 2. Deﬁne V = {1 + kn |
k = 1, 2, . . . }. A number p \inV is called indecomposable in V if it is not
possible to ﬁnd numbers q1, q2 \inV such that q1q2 = p. Prove that there
exists a number N \inV that can be factorized into indecomposable factors
in V in more than one way.
