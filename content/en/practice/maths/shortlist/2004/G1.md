# IMO 2004 SL G1

**Origin:** ROM  |  **Category:** Geometry

## Problem

Let ABC be an acute-angled triangle with AB ̸= AC.
The circle with diameter BC intersects the sides AB and AC at M and
N, respectively. Denote by O the midpoint of BC. The bisectors of the
angles BAC and MON intersect at R. Prove that the circumcircles of the
triangles BMR and CNR have a common point lying on the line segment
BC.

## Solution

Note that \triangleANM ∼\triangleABC and consequently AM ̸= AN. Since OM =
ON, it follows that OR is a perpendicular bisector of MN. Thus, R is the
common point of the median of MN and the bisector of \angleMAN. Then it
follows from a well-known fact that R lies on the circumcircle of \triangleAMN.
Let K be the intersection of AR and BC. We then have \angleMRA =
\angleMNA = \angleABK and \angleNRA = \angleNMA = \angleACK, from which we
conclude that RMBK and RNCK are cyclic. Thus K is the desired in-
tersection of the circumcircles of \triangleBMR and \triangleCNR and it indeed lies
on BC.
