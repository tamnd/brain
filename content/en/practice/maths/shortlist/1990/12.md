# IMO 1990 SL 12

**Origin:** IRE

## Problem

Let ABC be a triangle and L the line through C parallel to
the side AB. Let the internal bisector of the angle at A meet the side BC
at D and the line L at E and let the internal bisector of the angle at B
meet the side AC at F and the line L at G. If GF = DE, prove that
AC = BC.

## Solution

Let d(X, l) denote the distance of a point X from a line l. Using the
elementary facts that AF : FC = c : a and BD : DC = c : b, we obtain
d(F, L) =

a+chc and d(D, L) =

b+chc, where ha is the altitude of \triangleABC

DE = d(D, L)
sin(\alpha/2)
and
FG = d(F, L)
sin(\beta/2).
(1)
Now suppose that a > b. Since the function f(x) =
x
x+c is strictly increas-
ing, we deduce d(F, L) > d(D, L). Furthermore, sin(\alpha/2) > sin(\beta/2), so
we get from (1) that FG > DE.
Similarly, a < b implies FG < DE. Hence we must have a = b, i.e.,
AC = BC.
