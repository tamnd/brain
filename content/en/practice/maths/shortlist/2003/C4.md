## Solution

Denote by bij the entries of the matrix B. Suppose the contrary, i.e., that
there is a pair (i0, j0) such that ai0,j0 ̸= bi0,j0. We may assume without
loss of generality that ai0,j0 = 0 and bi0,j0 = 1.
Since the sums of elements in the i0th rows of the matrices A and B
are equal, there is some j1 for which ai0,j1 = 1 and bi0,j1 = 0. Similarly,
from the fact that the sums in the j1th columns of the matrices A and
B are equal, we conclude that there exists i1 such that ai1,j1 = 0 and
bi1,j1 = 1. Continuing this procedure, we construct two sequences ik, jk
