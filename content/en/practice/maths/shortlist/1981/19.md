# IMO 1981 SL 19

**Origin:** YUG

## Problem

A ﬁnite set of unit circles is given in a plane such that the area
of their union U is S. Prove that there exists a subset of mutually disjoint
circles such that the area of their union is greater that 2S
9 .

## Solution

Consider the partition of plane \pi into regular hexagons, each having in-
radius 2. Fix one of these hexagons, denoted by \gamma. For any other hexagon
x in the partition, there exists a unique translation \taux taking it onto \gamma.
Deﬁne the mapping ϕ : \pi \to\gamma as follows: If A belongs to the interior of a
hexagon x, then ϕ(A) = \taux(A) (if A is on the border of some hexagon, it
does not actually matter where its image is).
The total area of the images of the union of the given circles equals S,
while the area of the hexagon \gamma is 8
