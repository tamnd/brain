# IMO 1984 SL 5

**Origin:** FRG

## Problem

Let x, y, z be nonnegative real numbers with x+y +z = 1.
Show that
0 \leqxy + yz + zx −2xyz \leq7
27.

## Solution

Let f(x, y, z) = xy + yz + zx −2xyz. The ﬁrst inequality follows imme-
diately by adding xy \geqxyz, yz \geqxyz, and zx \geqxyz (in fact, a stronger
inequality xy + yz + zx −9xyz \geq0 holds).
Assume w.l.o.g. that z is the smallest of x, y, z. Since xy \leq(x + y)2/4 =
(1 −z)2/4 and z \leq1/2, we have
xy + yz + zx −2xyz = (x + y)z + xy(1 −2z)
\leq(1 −z)z + (1 −z)2(1 −2z)
= 7
27 −(1 −2z)(1 −3z)2
\leq7
27.
