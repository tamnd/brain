# IMO 1984 SL 17

**Origin:** FRG

## Problem

In a permutation (x1, x2, . . . , xn) of the set 1, 2, . . . , n we call
a pair (xi, xj) discordant if i < j and xi > xj. Let d(n, k) be the number
of such permutations with exactly k discordant pairs. Find d(n, 2) and
d(n, 3).
