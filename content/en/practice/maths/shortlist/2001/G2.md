# IMO 2001 SL G2

**Origin:** KOR  |  **Category:** Geometry

## Problem

In acute triangle ABC with circumcenter O and altitude
AP, ∡C \geq∡B + 30◦. Prove that ∡A + ∡COP < 90◦.

## Solution

Since \angleOCP = 90◦−\angleA, we are led to showing that \angleOCP > \angleCOP,
i.e., OP > CP. By the triangle inequality it suﬃces to prove CP < 1
2CO.
Let CO = R. The law of sines yields CP = AC cos \gamma = 2R sin \beta cos \gamma <
2R sin \beta cos(\beta + 30◦). Finally, we have
2 sin \beta cos(\beta + 30◦) = sin(2\beta + 30◦) −sin 30◦\leq1
2,
which completes the proof.
