# IMO 1992 SL 11

**Origin:** JAP

## Problem

In a triangle ABC, let D and E be the intersections of the bisec-
tors of \angleABC and \angleACB with the sides AC, AB, respectively. Determine
the angles \angleA, \angleB, \angleC if
∡BDE = 24◦,
∡CED = 18◦.

## Solution

Let I be the incenter of \triangleABC. Since 90◦+ \alpha/2 = \angleBIC = \angleDIE =
138◦, we obtain that \angleA = 96◦.

Let D′ and E′ be the points symmetric to D and E with respect to CE
and BD respectively, and let S be the intersection point of ED′ and BD.
Then \angleBDE′ = 24◦and \angleD′DE′ = \angleD′DE −\angleE′DE = 24◦,which
means that DE′ bisects the angle SDD′. Moreover, \angleE′SB = \angleESB =

that E′ is the excenter of \triangleD′DS and consequently \angleD′DC = \angleDD′C =
\angleSD′E′ = (180◦−72◦)/2 = 54◦. Finally, \angleC = 180◦−2 \cdot 54◦= 72◦and
\angleB = 12◦.
