# IMO 1971 SL 6

**Origin:** HUN

## Problem

Let n \geq2 be a natural number. Find a way to assign nat-
ural numbers to the vertices of a regular 2n-gon such that the following
conditions are satisﬁed:
(1) only digits 1 and 2 are used;
(2) each number consists of exactly n digits;
(3) diﬀerent numbers are assigned to diﬀerent vertices;
(4) the numbers assigned to two neighboring vertices diﬀer at exactly one
digit.

## Solution

The proof goes by induction on n. For n = 2, the following numeration
satisﬁes the conditions (a)–(d): C1 = 11, C2 = 12, C3 = 22, C4 = 21.
Suppose that n > 2, and that the numeration C1, C2, . . . , C2n−1 of a reg-
ular 2n−1-gon, in cyclical order, satisﬁes (i)–(iv). Then one can assign to
the vertices of a 2n-gon cyclically the following numbers:
1C1, 1C2, . . . , 1C2n−1, 2C2n−1, . . . , 2C2, 2C1.
The conditions (i), (ii) obviously hold, while (iii) and (iv) follow from the
inductive assumption.
