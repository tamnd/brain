# IMO 1971 SL 16

**Origin:** USS

## Problem

Given a convex polyhedron P1 with 9 vertices A1, . . . , A9,
let us denote by P2, P3, . . . , P9 the images of P1 under the translations
mapping the vertex A1 to A2, A3, . . . , A9 respectively. Prove that among
the polyhedra P1, . . . , P9 at least two have a common interior point.

## Solution

Denote by P ′ the polyhedron deﬁned as the image of P under the homo-
thety with center at A1 and coeﬃcient of similarity 2. It is easy to see
that all Pi, i = 1, . . . , 9, are contained in P ′ (indeed, if M \inPk, then

that not all Pi have disjoint interiors.
