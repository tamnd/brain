# IMO 1996 SL G8

**Origin:** RUS  |  **Category:** Geometry

## Problem

Let ABCD be a convex quadrilateral, and let RA, RB, RC,
and RD denote the circumradii of the triangles DAB, ABC, BCD, and
CDA respectively. Prove that RA + RC > RB + RD if and only if
\angleA + \angleC > \angleB + \angleD.

## Solution

Let the diagonals AC and BD meet in X. Either \angleAXB or \angleAXD is
geater than or equal to 90◦, so we assume w.l.o.g. that \angleAXB \geq90◦. Let
\alpha, \beta, \alpha′, \beta′ denote \angleCAB, \angleABD, \angleBDC, \angleDCA. These angles are all
acute and satisfy \alpha + \beta = \alpha′ + \beta′. Furthermore,
RA =
AD
2 sin \beta ,
RB =
BC
2 sin \alpha,
RC =
BC
2 sin \alpha′ ,
RD =
AD
2 sin \beta′ .
Let \angleB + \angleD = 180◦. Then A, B, C, D are concyclic and trivially RA +
RC = RB + RD.
Let \angleB +\angleD > 180◦. Then D lies within the circumcircle of ABC, which
implies that \beta > \beta′. Similarly \alpha < \alpha′, so we obtain RA < RD and
RC < RB. Thus RA + RC < RB + RD.
Let \angleB + \angleD < 180◦. As in the previous case, we deduce that RA > RD
and RC > RB, so RA + RC > RB + RD.
