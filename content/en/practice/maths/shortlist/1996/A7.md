Prove that f is a periodic function (that is, there exists a nonzero real

## Solution

We are given that f(x+a+b)−f(x+a) = f(x+b)−f(x), where a = 1/6
and b = 1/7. Summing up these equations for x, x+b, . . . , x+6b we obtain
f(x + a + 1) −f(x + a) = f(x + 1) −f(x). Summing up the new equations
for x, x + a, . . . , x + 5a we obtain that
f(x + 2) −f(x + 1) = f(x + 1) −f(x).
It follows by induction that f(x + n) −f(x) = n[f(x + 1) −f(x)]. If
f(x + 1) ̸= f(x), then f(x + n) −f(x) will exceed in absolute value
an arbitrarily large number for a suﬃciently large n, contradicting the
assumption that f is bounded. Hence f(x + 1) = f(x) for all x.
