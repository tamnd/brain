# IMO 1996 SL A6

**Origin:** IRE  |  **Category:** Algebra

## Problem

Let n be an even positive integer. Prove that there exists a
positive integer k such that
k = f(x)(x + 1)n + g(x)(xn + 1)
for some polynomials f(x), g(x) having integer coeﬃcients. If k0 denotes
the least such k, determine k0 as a function of n.
A6′ Let n be an even positive integer. Prove that there exists a positive
integer k such that
k = f(x)(x + 1)n + g(x)(xn + 1)
for some polynomials f(x), g(x) having integer coeﬃcients. If k0 denotes
the least such k, show that k0 = 2q, where q is the odd integer determined
by n = q2r, r \inN.
A6′′
Prove that for each positive integer n, there exist polynomials
f(x), g(x) having integer coeﬃcients such that
f(x)(x + 1)2n + g(x)(x2n + 1) = 2.

f(x)(x + 1)n + g(x)(xn + 1) = k0.

B(x) = x2r(m−1) −x2r(m−2) + \cdot \cdot \cdot −x2r + 1. Moreover, B(−1) = 1; hence

for some polynomials c(x) and R(x).
