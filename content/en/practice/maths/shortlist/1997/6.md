# IMO 1997 SL 6

**Origin:** IRE

## Problem

(a) Let n be a positive integer. Prove that there exist distinct
positive integers x, y, z such that
xn−1 + yn = zn+1.
(b) Let a, b, c be positive integers such that a and b are relatively prime
and c is relatively prime either to a or to b. Prove that there exist
inﬁnitely many triples (x, y, z) of distinct positive integers x, y, z such
that
xa + yb = zc.
Original formulation: Let a, b, c, n be positive integers such that n is odd
and ac is relatively prime to 2b. Prove that there exist distinct positive
integers x, y, z such that
(i) xa + yb = zc, and
(ii) xyz is relatively prime to n.

## Solution

(a) One solution is
x = 2n23n+1,
y = 2n2−n3n,
z = 2n2−2n+23n−1.
(b) Suppose w.l.o.g. that gcd(c, a) = 1. We look for a solution of the form
x = pm,
y = pn,
z = qpr,
p, q, m, n, r \inN.
Then xa +yb = pma +pnb and zc = qcprc, and we see that it is enough
to assume ma −1 = nb = rc (there are inﬁnitely many such triples
(m, n, r)) and qc = p + 1.
