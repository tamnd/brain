# IMO 1997 SL 20

**Origin:** IRE

## Problem

Let D be an internal point on the side BC of a triangle ABC.
The line AD meets the circumcircle of ABC again at X. Let P and Q be
the feet of the perpendiculars from X to AB and AC, respectively, and
let \gamma be the circle with diameter XD. Prove that the line PQ is tangent
to \gamma if and only if AB = AC.

## Solution

To avoid dividing into cases regarding the position of the point X, we use
oriented angles.
Let R be the foot of the perpendicular from X to BC. It is well known
that the points P, Q, R lie on the corresponding Simson line. This line is
a tangent to \gamma (i.e., the circle XDR) if and only if \anglePRD = \angleRXD. We
have
\anglePRD = \anglePXB = 90◦−\angleXBA = 90◦−\angleXBC + \angleABC
= 90◦−\angleDAC + \angleABC
and
\angleRXD = 90◦−\angleADB = 90◦+ \angleBCA −\angleDAC;
hence \anglePRD = \angleRXD if and only if \angleABC = \angleBCA, i.e, AB = AC.
