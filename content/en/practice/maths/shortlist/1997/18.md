# IMO 1997 SL 18

**Origin:** GBR

## Problem

The altitudes through the vertices A, B, C of an acute-angled
triangle ABC meet the opposite sides at D, E, F, respectively. The line
through D parallel to EF meets the lines AC and AB at Q and R, re-
spectively. The line EF meets BC at P. Prove that the circumcircle of
the triangle PQR passes through the midpoint of BC.

## Solution

By symmetry, assume that AB > AC. The point D lies between M and
P as well as between Q and R, and if we show that DM \cdotDP = DQ\cdotDR,
it will imply that M, P, Q, R lie on a circle.
Since the triangles ABC, AEF, AQR are similar, the points B, C, Q, R lie
on a circle. Hence DB \cdot DC = DQ \cdot DR, and it remains to prove that
DB \cdot DC = DM \cdot DP.
