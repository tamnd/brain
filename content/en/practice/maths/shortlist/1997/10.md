# IMO 1997 SL 10

**Origin:** CZE

## Problem

Find all positive integers k for which the following statement is
true:
If F(x) is a polynomial with integer coeﬃcients satisfying the condition
0 \leqF(c) \leqk
for each c \in{0, 1, . . ., k + 1},
then F(0) = F(1) = \cdot \cdot \cdot = F(k + 1).

## Solution

Suppose that k \geq4. Consider any polynomial F(x) with integer coeﬃ-
cients such that 0 \leqF(x) \leqk for x = 0, 1, . . . , k+1. Since F(k+1)−F(0)
is divisible by k + 1, we must have F(k + 1) = F(0). Hence
F(x) −F(0) = x(x −k −1)Q(x)
for some polynomial Q(x) with integer coeﬃcients. In particular, F(x) −
F(0) is divisible by x(k + 1 −x) > k + 1 for every x = 2, 3, . . . , k −1, so
F(x) = F(0) must hold for any x = 2, 3, . . ., k −1. It follows that
F(x) −F(0) = x(x −2)(x −3) \cdot \cdot \cdot (x −k + 1)(x −k −1)R(x)

for some polynomial R(x) with integer coeﬃcients. Thus k \geq|F(1) −
F(0)| = k(k −2)!|R(1)|, although k(k −2)! > k for k \geq4. In this case
we have F(1) = F(0) and similarly F(k) = F(0). Hence, the statement is
true for k \geq4.
