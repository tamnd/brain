# IMO 1985 SL 11

## Problem

3a.(USS 3) Find a method by which one can compute the coeﬃcients
of P(x) = x6 + a1x5 + \cdot \cdot \cdot + a6 from the roots of P(x) = 0 by performing
not more than 15 additions and 15 multiplications.

## Solution

Let −x1, . . . , −x6 be the roots of the polynomial. Let sk,i (k \leqi \leq6)
denote the sum of all products of k of the numbers x1, . . . , xi. By Vieta’s
formula we have ak = sk,6 for k = 1, . . . , 6. Since sk,i = sk−1,i−1xi +
sk,i−1, one can compute the ak by the following scheme (the horizontal
and vertical arrows denote multiplications and additions respectively):
