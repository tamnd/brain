# IMO 1985 SL 21

**Origin:** IRE

## Problem

triangle ABC meet at X. Let M be the midpoint of BC. Prove that
(a) \angleBAM = \angleCAX, and

## Solution

Let B1 and C1 be the points on the rays AC and AB respectively such
that XB1 = XC = XB = XC1. Then \angleXB1C = \angleXCB1 = \angleABC and
\angleXC1B = \angleXBC1 = \angleACB, which imply that B1, X, C1 are collinear
and \triangleAB1C1 ∼\triangleABC. Moreover, X is the midpoint of B1C1 because
XB1 = XC = XB = XC1, from which we conclude that \triangleAXC1 ∼
\triangleAMC. Therefore \angleBAX = \angleCAM and
