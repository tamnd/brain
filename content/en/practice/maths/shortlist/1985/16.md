# IMO 1985 SL 16

## Problem

5b.(BEL 2)
If possible, construct an equilateral triangle whose three
vertices are on three given circles.

## Solution

Let the three circles be \alpha(A, a), \beta(B, b), and \gamma(C, c), and assume c \leqa, b.
We denote by RX,ϕ the rotation around X through an angle ϕ. Let PQR
be an equilateral triangle, say of positive orientation (the case of negatively
oriented \trianglePQR is analogous), with P \in\alpha, Q \in\beta, and R \in\gamma. Then
Q = RP,−60◦(R) \inRP,−60◦(\gamma) \cap\beta.
Since the center of RP,−60◦(\gamma) is RP,−60◦(C) = RC,60◦(P) and it lies on
RC,60◦(\alpha), the union of circles RP,−60◦(\gamma) as P varies on \alpha is the annulus
U with center A′ = RC,60◦(A) and radii a −c and a + c. Hence there is a
solution if and only if U \cap\beta is nonempty.
