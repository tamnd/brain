# IMO 1999 SL C2

**Origin:** CAN  |  **Category:** Combinatorics

## Problem

(a) If a 5 \times n rectangle can be tiled using n pieces like those
shown in the diagram, prove that n is even.
(b) Show that there are more than 2\cdot3k−1 ways to tile a ﬁxed 5\times2k rect-
angle (k \geq3) with 2k pieces. (Symmetric constructions are considered
to be diﬀerent.)

## Solution

(a) Color the ﬁrst, third, and ﬁfth row red, and the remaining squares
white. There in total n pieces and 3n red squares. Since each piece
can cover at most three red squares, it follows that each piece colors
exactly three red squares. Then it follows that the two white squares
it covers must be on the same row; otherwise, the piece has to cover

at least three. Hence, each white row can be partitioned into pairs of
squares belonging to the same piece. Thus it follows that the number
of white squares in a row, which is n, must be even.
