# IMO 2000 SL G1

**Origin:** NET  |  **Category:** Geometry

## Problem

In the plane we are given two circles intersecting at X and Y .
Prove that there exist four points A, B, C, D with the following property:
For every circle touching the two given circles at A and B, and meeting the
line XY at C and D, each of the lines AC, AD, BC, BD passes through
one of these points.

## Solution

We shall consider the case that k3 is inside k1 and k2, since the other case
is analogous.

P and R, and BC and BD meet k2
at Q and S respectively. We claim
that PQ and RS are the common
