# IMO 1987 SL 17

**Origin:** ROM

## Problem

Prove that there exists a four-coloring of the set M =
{1, 2, . . ., 1987} such that any arithmetic progression with 10 terms in
the set M is not monochromatic.
Alternative formulation. Let M = {1, 2, . . ., 1987}. Prove that there is a
function f : M \to{1, 2, 3, 4} that is not constant on every set of 10 terms
from M that form an arithmetic progression.

## Solution

number of arithmetic progressions in M with 10 terms. The number of col-
orings containing a monochromatic arithmetic progression with 10 terms
is less than 4A \cdot 41977. So, if A < 49, then there exist 4-colorings with the
required property.
