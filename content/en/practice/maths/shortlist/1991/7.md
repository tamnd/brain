# IMO 1991 SL 7

**Origin:** CHN

## Problem

Let O be the center of the circumsphere of a tetrahedron
ABCD. Let L, M, N be the midpoints of BC, CA, AB respectively, and
assume that AB+BC = AD+CD, BC+CA = BD+AD, and CA+AB =
CD + BD. Prove that \angleLOM = \angleMON = \angleNOL.

## Solution

The given equations imply AB = CD, AC = BD, AD = BC. Let L1,
M1, N1 be the midpoints of AD, BD, CD respectively. Then the above

equalities yield
L1M1 = AB/2 = LM,
L1M1 \parallelAB \parallelLM;
L1M = CD/2 = LM1,
L1M \parallelCD \parallelLM1.
Thus L, M, L1, M1 are coplanar and
LML1M1 is a rhombus as well as
MNM1N1 and LNL1N1. Then the
