# IMO 1989 SL 28

**Origin:** ROM

## Problem

Consider in a plane \Pi the points O, A1, A2, A3, A4 such that
\sigma(OAiAj) \geq1 for all i, j = 1, 2, 3, 4, i ̸= j. Prove that there is at least
one pair i0, j0 \in{1, 2, 3, 4} such that \sigma(OAi0Aj0) \geq
\sqrt
2.
(We have denoted by \sigma(OAiAj) the area of triangle OAiAj.)

## Solution

Assume w.l.o.g. that the rays OA1, OA2, OA3, OA4 are arranged clock-
wise. Setting OA1 = a, OA2 = b, OA3 = c, OA4 = d, and \angleA1OA2 = x,
\angleA2OA3 = y, \angleA3OA4 = z, we have
S1 = \sigma(OA1A2) = 1
2ab| sin x|, S2 = \sigma(OA1A3) = 1
2ac| sin(x + y)|,
S3 = \sigma(OA1A4) = 1
2ad| sin(x + y + z)|, S4 = \sigma(OA2A3) = 1
2bc| sin y|,
S5 = \sigma(OA2A4) = 1
2bd| sin(y + z)|, S6 = \sigma(OA3A4) = 1
2cd| sin z|.
Since sin(x+y +z) siny + sin x sin z = sin(x+y) sin(y +z), it follows that
there exists a choice of k, l \in{0, 1} such that
S1S6 + (−1)kS2S5 + (−1)lS3S4 = 0.
