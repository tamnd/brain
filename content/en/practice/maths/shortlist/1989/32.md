# IMO 1989 SL 32

**Origin:** USA

## Problem

The vertex A of the acute triangle ABC is equidistant from
the circumcenter O and the orthocenter H. Determine all possible values
for the measure of angle A.

## Solution

Let CC′ be an altitude, and R the circumradius. Then, since AH = R,
we have AC′ = |R sin B| and hence (1) CC′ = |R sin B tan A|. On the
other hand, CC′ = |BC sin B| = 2|R sin A sin B|, which together with (1)
yields 2| sin A| = | tan A| ⇒| cosA| = 1/2. Hence, \angleA is 60◦. (Without
the condition that the triangle is acute, \angleA could also be 120◦.)

Third Solution.
Let A1 be the midpoint of BC. It is well known that
AH = 2OA1, and since AH = AO = BO, it means that in the right-
angled triangle BOA1 the relation BO = 2OA1 holds. Thus \angleBOA1 =
\angleA = 60◦.
