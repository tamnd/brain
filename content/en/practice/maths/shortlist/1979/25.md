# IMO 1979 SL 25

**Origin:** USA

## Problem

Given a point P in a given plane \pi and also a given point
Q not in \pi, show how to determine a point R in \pi such that QP+PR
QR
is a
maximum.

## Solution

Let us ﬁrst look for such a point R on a line l in \pi going through P. Let
\angleQPR = 2\theta. Consider a point Q′ on l such that Q′P = QP. Then we
have
QP + PR
QR
= RQ′
QR = sin Q′QR
sin QQ′R .
Since QQ′P is ﬁxed, the maximal value of the expression occurs when
\angleQQ′R = 90◦. In this case (QP + PR)/QR = 1/sin \theta. Looking at all
possible lines l, we see that \theta is minimized when l equals the projection
of PQ onto \pi. Hence, the point R is the intersection of the projection of
PQ onto \pi and the plane through Q perpendicular to PQ.
