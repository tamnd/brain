# IMO 1979 SL 5

**Origin:** CZS

## Problem

Let n \geq2 be an integer. Find the maximal cardinality of a set
M of pairs (j, k) of integers, 1 \leqj < k \leqn, with the following property:
If (j, k) \inM, then (k, m) ̸\inM for any m.

## Solution

Let A = {x | (x, y) \inM} and B = {y | (x, y) \inM. Then A and B are
disjoint and hence
|M| \leq|A| \cdot |B| \leq(|A| + |B|)2
\leq
n2

.
These cardinalities can be achieved for M = {(a, b) | a = 1, 2, . . . , [n/2],
b = [n/2] + 1, . . . , n} .
