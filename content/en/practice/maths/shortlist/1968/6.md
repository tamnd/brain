# IMO 1968 SL 6

**Origin:** HUN

## Problem

If ai (i = 1, 2, . . ., n) are distinct non-zero real numbers, prove
that the equation
a1
a1 −x +
a2
a2 −x + \cdot \cdot \cdot +
an
an −x = n
has at least n −1 real roots.

## Solution

We deﬁne f(x) =
a1
a1−x +
a2
a2−x + \cdot \cdot \cdot +
an
an−x. Let us assume w.l.o.g.
a1 < a2 < \cdot \cdot \cdot < an. We note that for all 1 \leqi < n the function f
is continuous in the interval (ai, ai+1) and satisﬁes limx\toai f(x) = −\infty
and limx\toai+1 f(x) = \infty. Hence the equation f(x) = n will have a real
solution in each of the n −1 intervals (ai, ai+1).
Remark.
In fact, this equation has exactly n solutions, and hence they
are all real. Moreover, the solutions are distinct if all ai are of the same
sign, since x = 0 is an evident solution.
