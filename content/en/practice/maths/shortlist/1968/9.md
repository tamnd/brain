# IMO 1968 SL 9

**Origin:** ROM

## Problem

Let ABC be an arbitrary triangle and M a point inside it. Let
da, db, dc be the distances from M to sides BC, CA, AB; a, b, c the lengths
of the sides respectively, and S the area of the triangle ABC. Prove the
inequality
abdadb + bcdbdc + cadcda \leq4S2
3 .
Prove that the left-hand side attains its maximum when M is the centroid
of the triangle.

## Solution

We note that Sa = ada/2, Sb = bdb/2, and Sc = cdc/2 are the areas of
the triangles MBC, MCA, and MAB respectively. The desired inequality
now follows from
SaSb + SbSc + ScSa \leq1
3(Sa + Sb + Sc)2 = S2
3 .
Equality holds if and only if Sa = Sb = Sc, which is equivalent to M being
the centroid of the triangle.
