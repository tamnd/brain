# IMO 1974 SL 12

## Problem

II 6 (USS 1) In a certain language words are formed using an alphabet
of three letters. Some words of two or more letters are not allowed, and
any two such distinct words are of diﬀerent lengths. Prove that one can
form a word of arbitrary length that does not contain any nonallowed
word.

## Solution

We say that a word is good if it doesn’t contain any nonallowed word. Let
an be the number of good words of length n. If we prolong any good word
of length n by adding one letter to its end (there are 3an words that can
be so obtained), we get either
(i) a good word of length n + 1, or
