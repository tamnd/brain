# IMO 2002 SL N5

**Origin:** IRN  |  **Category:** Number Theory

## Problem

Let m, n \geq2 be positive integers, and let a1, a2, . . . , an
be integers, none of which is a multiple of mn−1. Show that there exist
integers e1, e2, . . . , en, not all zero, with |ei| < m for all i, such that
e1a1 + e2a2 + \cdot \cdot \cdot + enan is a multiple of mn.
