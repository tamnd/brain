# IMO 2002 SL N1

**Origin:** UZB  |  **Category:** Number Theory

## Problem

What is the smallest positive integer t such that there exist
integers x1, x2, . . . , xt with
x3
1 + x3
2 + \cdot \cdot \cdot + x3
t = 20022002?

## Solution

Consider the given equation modulo 9. Since each cube is congruent to
either −1, 0 or 1, whereas 20022002 \equiv42002 = 4 \cdot 64667 \equiv4 (mod 9), we
conclude that t \geq4.
On the other hand, 20022002 = 2002 \cdot (2002667)3 = (103 + 103 + 13 +
13)(2002667)3 is a solution with t = 4. Hence the answer is 4.
