**Category:** Geometry

## Problem

Let ABC be a fixed triangle, and let A1, B1, C1 be the midpoints of sides BC, CA, AB,
respectively. Let P be a variable point on the circumcircle. Let lines PA1, PB1, PC1 meet the
circumcircle again at A0
, B0
, C0
respectively. Assume that the points A, B, C, A0
, B0
, C0
are
distinct, and lines AA0
, BB0
, CC0
form a triangle. Prove that the area of this triangle does not
depend on P.
(United Kingdom)
