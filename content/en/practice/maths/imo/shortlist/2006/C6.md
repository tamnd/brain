**Category:** Combinatorics

## Problem

A holey triangle is an upward equilateral triangle of side length n with n upward unit
triangular holes cut out. A diamond is a 60◦
−120◦
unit rhombus. Prove that a holey triangle T
can be tiled with diamonds if and only if the following condition holds: Every upward equilateral
triangle of side length k in T contains at most k holes, for 1 ≤ k ≤ n.
(Colombia)
