## Novice Texts: Specially Written and Bridge Texts

### LLPSI Familia Romana — Hans Ørberg

The gold standard beginning reader. Entirely written in Latin (no English). ~1,800 words introduced through a sustained narrative about a Roman family in 2nd-century AD Rome. 35 chapters. Grammar introduced inductively.

**Read**: From chapter 1; aim to finish all 35 chapters before moving on.
**Available**: Hackett Publishing (~$30); audio from Latinum Institute and Latinitium.

---

### Latin Novellas

Short books written in simple Latin with very limited vocabulary (100–200 unique words). A fast-growing genre since ~2015, created by Latin teachers for comprehensible input. Serve as supplementary reading alongside LLPSI.

Key authors and examples:
- **Rachel Ash**: *Plinia: A Story of Roman Britain* (~200 unique words)
- **Lance Piantaggini** (magisterp.com): *Piso Ille Poetulus* and others (~100–150 unique words)
- **Miriam Patrick**: *Itinera Petri* and others
- **Evan Millner**: adapted classical stories

**Find**: Teachers Pay Teachers, Amazon, the author websites. Many are under $10.

---

### LLPSI Supplements (all in Latin)

- **Colloquia Personarum**: Conversational dialogues; good for Active Latin practice alongside Familia Romana
- **Fabellae Latinae**: Brief fables as additional reading (slightly easier than Familia Romana main text)
- **Fabulae Syrae**: Additional narratives parallel to Familia Romana
- **Exercitia Latina I**: Grammar workbook for each chapter

### Eutropius, *Breviarium* (Bridge Text)

**Date**: ~369 CE | **Level**: Late Novice / Early Intermediate | **Genre**: Abbreviated Roman history

Written by Eutropius, secretary to the Roman emperor, as a brief Roman history for a non-specialist audience. Clear, simple declarative sentences, limited vocabulary, consistent style throughout.

**Why it's the ideal bridge text**: Written in plain, unadorned Latin without Ciceronian complexity; vocabulary and style are more accessible than any classical author while still being authentic Latin.

**Available**:
- DCC annotated edition: [dcc.dickinson.edu](https://dcc.dickinson.edu)
- Multiple free editions online; inexpensive print editions

---

### Cornelius Nepos, *Lives* (Bridge Text)

**Date**: ~1st century BCE | **Level**: Intermediate (strong bridge) | **Genre**: Biography

Short biographies of famous Greek and Roman generals (*Miltiades, Themistocles, Alcibiades, Epaminondas, Hannibal*). Clear, accessible prose with a vocabulary that overlaps heavily with Caesar's.

**Why use Nepos**: Shorter individual texts (1–5 pages per biography), historical content that contextualizes later reading, vocabulary between Eutropius and Caesar in difficulty.

**Available**: Free on Perseus; DCC vocabulary list; inexpensive print editions with vocabulary support.
