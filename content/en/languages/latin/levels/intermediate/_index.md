Intermediate Latin spans Latin II–III in school (approximately 400–1,000 hours total). By the end of this stage you can read adapted classical prose with minimal dictionary use and understand most syntax constructions.

## What You Can Do at Intermediate Level

- Read LLPSI *Familia Romana* and *Roma Aeterna* completely
- Read Eutropius and Cornelius Nepos with occasional dictionary use
- Read Caesar's *Gallic War* with a dictionary (not fluently, but steadily)
- Parse all verb forms (all tenses in indicative and subjunctive, active and passive)
- Recognize and produce ablative absolute, indirect statement, purpose and result clauses
- Identify all six conditional types

## Grammar at This Level

| Grammar Topic | Coverage |
|---------------|---------|
| Perfect system | Perfect, pluperfect, future perfect (active + passive) |
| Passive voice | All tenses; PPP + esse construction |
| Subjunctive | All 4 tenses; purpose, result, fear, indirect question, cum clauses |
| Verbal nouns | Gerund, gerundive, passive periphrastic (*mihi eundum est*) |
| Complex syntax | Ablative absolute, ACI (indirect statement), all 6 conditional types |
| Comparison | Comparative and superlative of adjectives and adverbs |
| Deponent verbs | Passive form, active meaning (loquor, sequor, morior, etc.) |

## Vocabulary Target

- **DCC Core 1–500**: All 500 highest-frequency words
- **Caesar-specific vocabulary**: ~300 additional words common in *Gallic War*
- Total active vocabulary: ~700–1,000 words

## What to Read

| Text | Level | Why |
|------|-------|-----|
| LLPSI *Roma Aeterna* (ch. 36+) | Core transition | Moves from adapted to authentic classical texts |
| **Eutropius**, *Breviarium* | First authentic text | Concise Roman history, very clear simple prose |
| **Cornelius Nepos**, *Lives* | First authentic text | Short biographies, excellent bridge to Caesar |
| **Caesar**, *Gallic War* Books 1, 4, 5 | Intermediate core | Traditional first authentic text for US students |
| **Ovid**, selected *Metamorphoses* | Poetry intro | Engaging stories; hexameter |
| **Catullus** (shorter poems: 1, 2, 3, 5, 51) | Poetry intro | Short, varied difficulty |

## The "Bridge" Texts — Why They Matter

Most learners jump from LLPSI/adapted texts directly to Caesar and find it brutal. The bridge texts are the solution:

1. **Eutropius**: Simple declarative sentences, limited vocabulary, historical content to contextualize
2. **Cornelius Nepos**: Slightly longer sentences, vocabulary overlap with Caesar
3. **Easy Caesar** (Book 4 naval section, Book 1 opening): Most accessible Caesar passages
