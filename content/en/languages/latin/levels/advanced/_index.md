Advanced Latin (C1) corresponds to AP Latin or Latin IV in school (approximately 1,000–1,500 total hours). At this level you can read unadapted classical prose with a dictionary and begin to appreciate Latin poetry.

## What You Can Do at Advanced Level

- Read unadapted Caesar fluently with dictionary
- Read Cicero's speeches and philosophical works with steady dictionary use
- Read Ovid's *Metamorphoses* and selected Catullus without excessive difficulty
- Sight-read unseen Latin passages (AP Latin tests this)
- Recognize all syntax constructions automatically during reading
- Understand basic verse scansion (dactylic hexameter, elegiac couplet)
- Begin reading Virgil's *Aeneid* (the hardest step up)

## AP Latin Requirements

The AP Latin exam (College Board) requires:
- **Vergil's *Aeneid***: Books 1, 2, 4, 6 in Latin; Books 1, 2, 4, 6, 8, 12 in English
- **Caesar's *Gallic War***: Selections from Books 1, 4, 5, 6 in Latin; Books 1, 6, 7 in English
- **Sight translation** of an unseen Latin passage
- **Free-response** analysis essays in English

## What to Read

| Text | Why |
|------|-----|
| **Caesar** *Gallic War* (all standard books) | Master level; fluent reading benchmark |
| **Cicero** *In Catilinam* I–IV | Classic rhetoric; complex periods |
| **Cicero** *Pro Archia* | Shorter; literary theme; ideal first Cicero speech |
| **Cicero** *De Amicitia*, *De Senectute* | Philosophical; less rhetorical complexity |
| **Ovid** *Metamorphoses* | Hexameter; engaging; great breadth of mythology |
| **Catullus** (selected, including odes) | Varied meters; introduces lyric |
| **Pliny the Younger** letters (esp. 6.16, 6.20) | Personal tone; intermediate bridge to advanced |
| **Virgil** *Aeneid* Books 1, 2, 4 | The pinnacle; hardest step up — expect slowness initially |

## Resources for Advanced Level

- **Grammar**: A&G's New Latin Grammar chapters on complex syntax; Woodcock's *A New Latin Syntax*
- **Commentaries**: Dickinson College Commentaries for Caesar, Tacitus *Agricola*, others
- **Dictionary**: Oxford Latin Dictionary (institutional) or Lewis & Short (Perseus)
- **Scansion**: DCC scansion guide; Bennett's prosody chapter; practice scanning Virgil
- **YouTube**: [Latin Per Diem](../../resources/youtube/) — short daily classical author readings
- **Podcast**: [Quomodo Dicitur?](../../resources/podcasts/) — advanced conversational Latin
