Scholar Latin (C2+) is the level of graduate reading proficiency, where you can read most classical and post-classical texts without aids and begin to produce Latin prose. This requires 2,000+ hours of dedicated study.

## What You Can Do at Scholar Level

- Read most classical Latin texts without a dictionary
- Read Tacitus (the hardest classical Latin prose) with steady comprehension
- Read Horace's *Odes* in complex lyric meters (Sapphic, Alcaic, Asclepiadean)
- Produce formal Latin prose in imitation of classical style
- Translate unseen Latin passages under examination conditions (2 hours, dictionary permitted)
- Read Medieval Latin (Bede, Thomas Aquinas) with adjustment
- Compose in the style of specific classical authors

## Graduate Reading Proficiency Standard

Most US PhD programs in Classics require a reading proficiency examination:
- Translate 2–3 paragraphs of unseen Latin in 2 hours
- Latin-English dictionary permitted
- This is the practical C2+ threshold

## What to Read

| Author | Work | Why Difficult |
|--------|------|---------------|
| **Tacitus** | *Annals*, *Histories*, *Agricola* | Most compressed classical prose; archaic, dense |
| **Horace** | *Odes* | Complex lyric meters; dense allusion |
| **Seneca** | *Moral Letters*, *Tragedies* | Silver Age style; neologisms; tragedies have complex meter |
| **Sallust** | *Catiline*, *Jugurtha* | Archaic, compressed; influenced Tacitus |
| **Juvenal** | *Satires* | Dense, allusive; scathing social commentary |
| **Persius** | *Satires* | Notoriously obscure — hardest classical Latin |
| **Augustine** | *Confessions* | Late Latin style; philosophical depth |
| **Boethius** | *Consolation of Philosophy* | Alternating prose and verse |
| **Medieval Latin** | Bede, Aquinas, chronicles | Different conventions from classical norms |

## Latin Prose Composition Resources

- **Bradley's Arnold Latin Prose Composition** — the classic composition manual
- **North and Hillard Latin Prose Composition** — alternative approach
- **Gwynne's Latin** — modern revival with traditional method
- Terence Tunberg's approach at University of Kentucky *Conventiculum Latinum*
