The Novice stage corresponds to approximately the first year of Latin study (Latin I in school, or ~200–400 hours of self-study). By the end of this stage you can read simple Latin sentences and adapted texts.

## What You Can Do at Novice Level

- Read and understand LLPSI *Familia Romana* chapters 1–20 without constant lookups
- Identify and parse all five noun declensions in nominative, genitive, and accusative
- Conjugate all four verb conjugations in present, imperfect, and future tense (active)
- Recognize *esse* and its compounds; use it correctly
- Read Latin novellas (100–200 unique word vocabulary)
- Identify simple prepositional phrases, purpose clauses, and basic subordinate clauses

## Grammar at This Level

| Grammar Topic | Coverage |
|---------------|---------|
| Noun declensions | All 5 (nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, ablative, vocative) |
| Adjective agreement | 1st–2nd declension adjectives agree with noun |
| Pronouns | Personal (ego, tu, is/ea/id), demonstrative (hic, ille) |
| Verb conjugations | All 4 + irregular esse; present, imperfect, future (indicative, active) |
| Basic syntax | Prepositional phrases, simple relative clauses, indirect statement (intro) |

## Vocabulary Target

- **DCC Core 1–200**: The 200 most frequent Latin words — learn all of these by end of Novice
- From LLPSI Familia Romana: ~800 words introduced through chapters 1–20
- Recommended: learn DCC 1–200 with Anki SRS (15 min/day)

## What to Read

| Text | Level | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| LLPSI *Familia Romana* ch. 1–20 | Core | The gold standard beginning reader |
| Latin novellas (Pliny letters adapted) | Supplementary | 100–200 unique words each |
| *Fabulae Syrae* (LLPSI supplement) | Supplementary | Simple additional narratives |
| *Fabellae Latinae* (LLPSI supplement) | Supplementary | Brief fables |

## Study Routine

- **Daily**: 15 min Anki (DCC 1–200), 20–30 min reading (LLPSI chapter + exercises)
- **Weekly**: Grammar review (one declension or conjugation paradigm)
- **Monthly**: Self-test: can you read last month's chapter without aids?

## Resources for Novice Level

## Common Mistakes at Novice Level

1. **Ignoring macrons (vowel length)** — *mālo* (I prefer) ≠ *malo* (evil, dat./abl.); macrons matter
2. **Confusing dative and ablative** — both often translated "to/from"; context and case markers differ
3. **Translating instead of reading** — try to understand meaning directly, not by English word order
4. **Skipping paradigm memorization** — you must know the endings cold before they become automatic
5. **Neglecting *esse*** — *sum/es/est/sumus/estis/sunt* appears on every page; master it first
