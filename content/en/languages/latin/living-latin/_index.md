The Active Latin (or Living Latin) movement treats Latin as a spoken, living language rather than a subject for grammar-translation exercises alone. Drawing on second-language acquisition research — particularly Krashen's comprehensible input hypothesis — practitioners argue that meaningful exposure to spoken and written Latin builds reading fluency more efficiently than explicit grammar drilling alone.

## Core Philosophy

**Research finding**: Students encounter **~60 words per minute** of spoken Latin vs. **5–10 words per minute** through written translation — a 6–12× speed advantage for input quantity.

**Key principles**:
- Use Latin as a communication medium — speak, read, and listen as primary activities
- Provide **comprehensible input** (content slightly above current level) rather than word-by-word translation
- Develop intuitive feel for syntax rather than consciously parsing every form
- Build automaticity through massive exposure — not just drilling paradigms

This approach doesn't abandon grammar — grammar knowledge matters. But it adds an input-rich environment that grammar-translation alone cannot provide.

## Key Figures

### Luke Amadeus Ranieri (ScorpioMartianus)
US Army helicopter pilot and linguist; Latin and Greek Fellow at the Ancient Language Institute; speaks on Vatican Radio in Latin. Produces hundreds of YouTube videos entirely in Latin (Classical pronunciation). The largest single popularizer of spoken Classical Latin online, with ~148,000+ YouTube subscribers. Also speaks Ancient Greek, Egyptian, and several modern languages.

### Justin Slocum Bailey
Active Latin practitioner; co-host of *Quomodo Dicitur?* podcast; runs immersion events and produces pedagogical resources for spoken Latin in the classroom.

### Luigi Miraglia
Director of Accademia Vivarium Novum in Frascati, Italy. One of the world's most renowned Living Latin teachers; has trained hundreds of Latin teachers in the spoken Latin method. Teaches exclusively in Latin — no other language used on campus.

### Terence Tunberg and Milena Minkova
University of Kentucky professors; founded the *Conventiculum Latinum* annual spoken Latin seminar; authors of *Latin for the New Millennium*. Among the most rigorous scholarly practitioners of Active Latin.

### Evan Millner
Produced the Latinum podcast and extensive audio recordings of *Familia Romana* and other Latin texts. Made spoken Latin accessible via audio for the first time at scale.

### Daniel Pettersson (Latinitium)
Swedish Latinist; co-runs [latinitium.com](https://latinitium.com); produces high-quality written and audio Latin content in Classical pronunciation.

## Major Programs and Institutions

### Paideia Institute
**Website**: [paideiainstitute.org](https://www.paideiainstitute.org)
**Location**: New York (based); programs in Rome and online
**Type**: Non-profit educational organization

Programs:
- **Living Latin in Rome** (summer, ~3 weeks): Classes at Cornell's Rome campus; on-site visits to ancient ruins connected to the readings. ~$2,500 (includes some housing, site admissions, group meals). Prerequisite: ~1 year college or 2 years high school Latin. Scholarships available.
- **Living Latin Online** (summer intensive): Intensive online program; multiple levels from beginner to advanced. Focused on Seneca's *Moral Letters* (and other texts).
- **Living Latin in New York City** (annual conference): Two-day conference at Regis High School, Manhattan. Lectures on Latin pedagogy; small group speaking practice; optional spoken Ancient Greek sessions.

---

### Accademia Vivarium Novum
**Website**: [vivariumnovum.net](https://vivariumnovum.net)
**Location**: Frascati, near Rome (campus); also online seminars
**Director**: Luigi Miraglia

The most intensive residential Latin immersion program in the world. Latin is the **sole language of instruction and daily life** on campus — no other language spoken at any time.

**Summer courses** (in-person, Frascati campus):
- Full immersion: lectures, meals, recreation, all in Latin
- LLPSI *Familia Romana* is the core text for beginners

**Online summer seminars**:
- 4-week seminars; €400; 2 hours, 3×/week + 4 hours independent work
- Covers ancient and Renaissance authors with composition exercises
- 10 scholarships available per session (non-profit model)

---

### SALVI — Septentrionale Americanum Latinitatis Vivae Institutum
**Website**: [latin.org](https://www.latin.org)
**Type**: North American Institute for Living Latin Studies

**Rusticatio Virginiana** (flagship event):
- Annual week-long immersion retreat in West Virginia
- Latin spoken at all times: meals, recreation, classes, free conversation
- All levels welcome; no prior spoken Latin experience required
- Prerequisite: ~2 years college or 4 years high school Latin (reading ability)

**Biduum events** (3-day weekends):
- ~20 hours of Latin immersion over a weekend
- Held throughout the year across the US: New York, Virginia, Pennsylvania, Colorado, Georgia, Minnesota, Oklahoma, Los Angeles, and others
- Also *Biduum Graecum* for Ancient Greek
- Cost: ~$300/person all-inclusive (lodging, meals, instruction)
- Prerequisite: same as Rusticatio

---

### Conventiculum Latinum
**Host**: University of Kentucky (Terence Tunberg & Milena Minkova)
**Type**: Annual week-long spoken Latin seminar for scholars
**Level**: Advanced to scholar level

All Latin spoken throughout. High academic level. Participants include professors, graduate students, and serious independent learners.

---

### Ancient Language Institute
**Website**: [ancientlanguage.com](https://ancientlanguage.com)
**Type**: Online courses (flexible scheduling)

Online courses in Latin, Greek, Hebrew, and other ancient languages. Luke Ranieri is affiliated as Latin and Greek Fellow. Focus on intensive online instruction in speaking/reading ancient languages.

---

### Veterum Sapientia Institute (VSI)
**Website**: [veterumsapientia.org](https://veterumsapientia.org)

Summer Latin immersion workshops; instructors trained at Vivarium Novum.

## Getting Started with Active Latin

1. **Start reading LLPSI** — the Natural Method approach is the classroom equivalent of Active Latin
2. **Listen to ScorpioMartianus** and Latinitium Podcast — exposure to spoken Latin even before you can speak it
3. **Watch Magister Craft** and Comprehensible Antiquity — comprehensible video input
4. **Attend a Biduum** (SALVI) — the most accessible entry point to spoken Latin immersion; no speaking experience needed
5. **Use LLPSI Colloquia Personarum** — conversational Latin dialogues designed for classroom speaking practice
