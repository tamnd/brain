Latin verbs are classified into 4 conjugations identified by the ending of the **present active infinitive**. Every Latin verb has **4 principal parts** from which all forms are derived.

## The 4 Principal Parts

The dictionary entry for a regular verb lists 4 principal parts:
1. **1st person singular present active indicative**: *amō* — "I love"
2. **Present active infinitive**: *amāre* — "to love" (identifies the conjugation)
3. **1st person singular perfect active indicative**: *amāvī* — "I loved"
4. **Perfect passive participle (PPP)**: *amātus* — "loved" (or future active participle *amātūrus* for intransitives)

From these 4 parts you can form every one of the 130+ verb forms.

## The 4 Conjugations

| Conjugation | Infinitive Ending | Vowel | Example | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1st** | -**ā**re | ā | *amāre* | to love |
| **2nd** | -**ē**re (long ē) | ē | *habēre* | to have |
| **3rd** | -**e**re (short e) | e | *dūcere* | to lead |
| **3rd-io** | -**e**re (io-stem) | e | *capere* | to take |
| **4th** | -**ī**re | ī | *audīre* | to hear |

*Warning*: 2nd (*habēre*) and 3rd (*dūcere*) differ only by the length of the **e** in the infinitive. Always check the dictionary entry carefully.

## Present Stem Tenses (Present, Imperfect, Future)

### Present Active Indicative

| Person | 1st Conj. (*amō*) | 2nd Conj. (*habeō*) | 3rd Conj. (*dūcō*) | 3rd-io (*capiō*) | 4th Conj. (*audiō*) |
|--------|------------------|--------------------|--------------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 1 sg. | am**ō** | habe**ō** | dūc**ō** | capi**ō** | audi**ō** |
| 2 sg. | amā**s** | habē**s** | dūci**s** | capi**s** | audī**s** |
| 3 sg. | amā**t** | habē**t** | dūci**t** | capi**t** | audī**t** |
| 1 pl. | amā**mus** | habē**mus** | dūci**mus** | capi**mus** | audī**mus** |
| 2 pl. | amā**tis** | habē**tis** | dūci**tis** | capi**tis** | audī**tis** |
| 3 pl. | amā**nt** | habē**nt** | dūcu**nt** | capiu**nt** | audiu**nt** |

### Imperfect Active Indicative (sign: **-bā-**)

| Person | 1st | 2nd | 3rd | 3rd-io | 4th |
|--------|-----|-----|-----|--------|-----|
| 1 sg. | amā**bam** | habē**bam** | dūcē**bam** | capiē**bam** | audiē**bam** |
| 3 sg. | amā**bat** | habē**bat** | dūcē**bat** | capiē**bat** | audiē**bat** |
| 3 pl. | amā**bant** | habē**bant** | dūcē**bant** | capiē**bant** | audiē**bant** |

### Future Active Indicative (sign: **-bi-/-bo-** for 1st/2nd; **-a-/-e-** for 3rd/4th)

|--------|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 1 sg. | amā**bō** | habē**bō** | dūc**am** | audi**am** |

## Perfect Stem Tenses (Perfect, Pluperfect, Future Perfect)

**Perfect stem** = 3rd principal part minus -ī: *amāv-*, *habu-*, *dūx-*

### Perfect Active Indicative (endings: -ī, -istī, -it, -imus, -istis, -ērunt/-ēre)

| Person | Any Conjugation (model: *amāvī*) |
|--------|----------------------------------|
| 1 sg. | amāv**ī** |
| 2 sg. | amāv**istī** |
| 3 sg. | amāv**it** |
| 1 pl. | amāv**imus** |
| 2 pl. | amāv**istis** |
| 3 pl. | amāv**ērunt** / amāv**ēre** |

### Pluperfect Active (sign: **-era-**)

| Person | Model (*amāveram*) |
|--------|-------------------|
| 1 sg. | amāv**eram** |
| 3 sg. | amāv**erat** |
| 3 pl. | amāv**erant** |

### Future Perfect Active (sign: **-eri-**)

| Person | Model |
|--------|-------|
| 1 sg. | amāv**erō** |
| 3 sg. | amāv**erit** |
| 3 pl. | amāv**erint** |

## Passive Voice

**Present passive** replaces active endings with passive endings: -or, -ris, -tur, -mur, -minī, -ntur

**Perfect passive** uses **PPP + forms of *esse***: *amātus sum, amātus es, amātus est...* (PPP agrees in gender/number with subject)

**Pluperfect passive**: PPP + imperfect of *esse*: *amātus eram, amātus erat...*

## Subjunctive Mood

### Present Subjunctive (sign: vowel change — "CABS" mnemonic: Conj. 1→**ē**, 2-4→**a**)

| Person | 1st Conj. | 2nd Conj. | 3rd Conj. | 4th Conj. |
|--------|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| 1 sg. | am**em** | habe**am** | dūc**am** | audi**am** |
| 3 sg. | am**et** | habe**at** | dūc**at** | audi**at** |

### Imperfect Subjunctive (sign: present active infinitive + personal endings)

*amāre-m, amāre-s, amāre-t, amāre-mus, amāre-tis, amāre-nt*

Perfect Subj.: perfect stem + -eri- + endings: *amāverim, amāveris...*
Pluperfect Subj.: perfect stem + -isse- + endings: *amāvissem, amāvisses...*

## The 8 Irregular Verbs

### *esse* (to be) — Most important irregular verb

| Tense | 1 sg. | 2 sg. | 3 sg. | 1 pl. | 2 pl. | 3 pl. |
|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| Pres. | **sum** | **es** | **est** | **sumus** | **estis** | **sunt** |
| Impf. | **eram** | **erās** | **erat** | **erāmus** | **erātis** | **erant** |
| Fut. | **erō** | **eris** | **erit** | **erimus** | **eritis** | **erunt** |
| Perf. | **fuī** | fuistī | fuit | fuimus | fuistis | fuērunt |
| Plupf. | **fueram** | — | fuerat | — | — | fuerant |

Pres. Subj.: *sim, sīs, sit, sīmus, sītis, sint*
Impf. Subj.: *essem, essēs, esset...*

**Compounds of *esse***: *abesse* (to be away), *adesse* (to be present), *prōdesse* (to benefit — prōd- + esse), *superesse* (to survive), *posse* (to be able)

### *posse* (to be able) — *pot-* + *esse*

Pres.: *possum, potes, potest, possumus, potestis, possunt*
Impf.: *poteram...*; Fut.: *poterō...*; Perf.: *potuī...*
Pres. Subj.: *possim...*; Impf. Subj.: *possem...*

### *īre* (to go)

Pres.: *eō, is, it, īmus, ītis, eunt*
Impf.: *ībam...*; Fut.: *ībō...*; Perf.: *iī* (or *īvī*)
Compounds: *abeō, adeō, exeō, ineō, intereō, redeō, trānseō*

### *velle* (to want), *nōlle* (to not want), *mālle* (to prefer)

| Form | *volō* | *nōlō* | *mālō* |
|------|--------|--------|--------|
| 1 sg. | *volō* | *nōlō* | *mālō* |
| 2 sg. | *vīs* | *nōn vīs* | *māvīs* |
| 3 sg. | *vult* | *nōn vult* | *māvult* |
| 1 pl. | *volumus* | *nōlumus* | *mālumus* |
| 3 pl. | *volunt* | *nōlunt* | *mālunt* |

*Nōlī/Nōlīte + infinitive* = standard way to express a **negative command** (not *ne* + imperative)

### *ferre* (to carry) — shows suppletion (3 roots)

Pres.: *ferō, fers, fert, ferimus, fertis, ferunt*
Infinitives: *ferre* (pres. act.), *ferrī* (pres. pass.)
Perf.: *tulī*; PPP: *lātus*

### *fierī* (to become/be made) — passive of *facere*

Pres.: *fiō, fīs, fit, fīmus, fītis, fiunt*
Infinitive: *fierī*; Perf.: *factus sum*

## Deponent Verbs

Deponent verbs are **passive in form but active in meaning**. They have no active forms. Examples:
- *loquor, loquī, locūtus sum* — to speak
- *sequor, sequī, secūtus sum* — to follow
- *morior, morī, mortuus sum* — to die
- *proficiscor, proficiscī, profectus sum* — to set out
- *arbitror, arbitrārī, arbitrātus sum* — to think, judge

Semideponents are active in present system, deponent (passive form) in perfect: *audeō, audēre, ausus sum* (to dare).
