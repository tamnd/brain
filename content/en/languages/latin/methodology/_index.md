## Step 1: Choose Your Approach

The most important decision: **reading-first (LLPSI)** or **grammar-first (Wheelock)**?

| | LLPSI Familia Romana | Wheelock's Latin |
|---|---|---|
| **Method** | All in Latin; reading-based | English explanations; grammar-based |
| **Best for** | Self-learners; reading fluency goal | Academic Latin I-II; grammar mastery goal |
| **Grammar** | Inductive (you figure it out from context) | Explicit (grammar rules presented first) |
| **Reading start** | Day 1 | Chapter 15+ (after grammar foundation) |
| **Active Latin** | Natural fit | Possible addition |
| **Difficulty** | Easy at first, gradually harder | Grammar-heavy early; authentic Latin from ch. 1 |

**Verdict**: LLPSI produces faster reading fluency; Wheelock produces deeper early grammar analysis. Both work. If you stall with one, try the other.

**Hybrid approach** (recommended by many experienced learners): Use LLPSI as your main course + read Allen & Greenough's grammar explanations for the topic of the current chapter.

## Step 2: The Learning Roadmap

### Novice Phase (Months 1–12, ~200–400 hours)

**Month 1–2** (Orientation):
- Choose textbook and start from chapter 1
- Learn Classical or Ecclesiastical pronunciation (choose now, stay consistent)
- Install Anki; download DCC Core Vocabulary deck; start with Band 1 (1–200)
- Bookmark Perseus and Logeion; use for every unknown word

**Month 3–6** (Grammar Foundation):
- Master all 5 noun declensions (nom., gen., dat., acc., abl., voc.)
- Learn 1st and 2nd conjugations in present, imperfect, future (active)
- Learn *esse* and its compounds
- Begin adjective agreement (1st-2nd declension)
- LLPSI: chapters 1–20 (or Wheelock ch. 1–20)

**Month 7–12** (Grammar Completion):
- 3rd, 4th, 5th conjugations; all tenses
- Perfect passive system (PPP + *esse*)
- Pronouns (demonstrative, relative)
- Subjunctive (present, imperfect)
- Purpose and result clauses
- LLPSI: chapters 21–35 (Familia Romana complete)

### Intermediate Phase (Year 2, ~400–1,000 hours)

**Year 2 priorities**:
1. Complete LLPSI Roma Aeterna or continue Wheelock supplements
2. Read Eutropius completely (bridge text)
3. Read selected Cornelius Nepos biographies
4. Start Caesar *Gallic War* Book 1
5. Learn all 6 conditional sentence types
6. Master ablative absolute and ACI (indirect statement)
7. Learn gerund and gerundive; passive periphrastic
8. DCC Core Vocabulary Bands 1–2 (words 1–500)

### Advanced Phase (Year 3–4, ~1,000–1,500 hours)

1. Caesar fluently (all AP books)
2. Cicero *Pro Archia* + *In Catilinam* I
3. Ovid *Metamorphoses* selections
4. Begin Virgil *Aeneid* Book 1 (slow at first; persevere)
5. Scansion of dactylic hexameter
6. DCC Core Vocabulary Band 3 (1–1,000 complete)
7. Allen & Greenough — work through syntax sections

### Scholar Phase (Year 5+, ~2,000+ hours)

1. Virgil complete (all 12 books)
2. Cicero's philosophical works
3. Tacitus *Agricola* → *Annals*
4. Horace *Odes* (hardest step)
5. Seneca *Moral Letters*
6. Latin prose composition (Bradley's Arnold or North & Hillard)

## Step 3: Daily Study Routine

### Novice Routine (1 hour/day)

| Activity | Time | Purpose |
|----------|------|---------|
| Anki (DCC vocabulary) | 15 min | Systematic vocabulary |
| Textbook reading (LLPSI/Wheelock) | 30 min | Grammar + comprehension |
| Grammar review (one paradigm) | 10 min | Form memorization |
| Free re-reading (yesterday's chapter) | 5 min | Fluency practice |

### Intermediate Routine (1.5 hours/day)

| Activity | Time | Purpose |
|----------|------|---------|
| Anki (DCC + author-specific) | 15 min | Vocabulary maintenance |
| Reading authentic Latin | 45 min | Fluency building |
| Grammar study (A&G section) | 20 min | Syntax mastery |
| Latin podcast/video | 10 min | Listening input |

### Advanced Routine (2 hours/day)

| Activity | Time | Purpose |
|----------|------|---------|
| Anki (maintenance only) | 10 min | Keep vocabulary fresh |
| Sustained reading (1 author) | 60 min | Fluency and depth |
| Grammar or commentary work | 30 min | Precision |
| Spoken Latin (optional) | 20 min | Active production |

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Skipping Vowel Quantities (Macrons)
Macrons signal vowel length, which determines accent, meter, and sometimes meaning (*mālo* vs. *malo*). Always learn vocabulary with macrons. Use a source that marks them (LLPSI, DCC, or a good dictionary).

### 2. Translating Instead of Reading
Translating every word into English is a trap — it's slow (5-10 words/minute) and trains dependence on English rather than Latin intuition. Practice reading paragraphs for meaning, then check translation later.

### 3. Ignoring Noun Gender
Every Latin noun has a grammatical gender. You must memorize it with each word. Without gender, you cannot make adjectives agree correctly. The dictionary entry always gives gender: *puella, -ae, **f.***

### 4. Stalling at the Grammar-Authentic Latin Gap
The jump from adapted texts (LLPSI, textbooks) to authentic Latin (Caesar) is the most dangerous point. Use Eutropius and Nepos as bridges. Don't skip from LLPSI directly to Cicero.

### 5. Doing Too Many Resources
Latin learner brain disease: collecting 15 textbooks, 10 apps, 20 YouTube channels. You need one main course + one dictionary + Anki. Everything else is supplementary. Depth beats breadth.

### 6. Not Using Audio
Latin is no longer "dead" — there are hundreds of hours of spoken Latin content. Even 10 minutes/day of listening (ScorpioMartianus, Quomodo Dicitur?) accelerates intuitive grammar feel.

### 7. Stopping After One Year
Most Latin learners who quit do so at the end of year 1 (after finishing a textbook). This is exactly when the payoff begins — the reading finally becomes fluid. Push through to Eutropius and Nepos.

## SRS Strategy for Latin

**Tool**: Anki (free desktop/Android; ~$25 iOS)
**Primary deck**: DCC Latin Core Vocabulary

**Card format** (front / back):
- Front: Latin word (*amō, amāre, amāvī, amātus* — present, infinitive, perfect, PPP)
- Back: English definition + one example sentence

**Settings**: 
- New cards/day: 15–20 (no more than 30)
- Maximum interval: 365 days
- Graduating interval: 3 days

**When you encounter a DCC word in reading and don't recognize it**: search for it in Anki, mark it as "again" to reset interval.

**Text-specific vocabulary**: After finishing DCC Band 1-2, add text-specific decks (Caesar-specific vocab, Cicero-specific vocab) when you commit to reading those authors seriously.
