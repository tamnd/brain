A curated guide to the best Latin learning resources. Organized by type and annotated with level guidance.

## Textbooks

### For Beginners

#### LLPSI — Lingua Latina per se Illustrata (Hans Ørberg)
The gold standard. Written entirely in Latin — no English. Inductive grammar (you figure out the patterns from context). Two volumes: *Familia Romana* (35 chapters, novice–intermediate) and *Roma Aeterna* (30 chapters, intermediate–advanced).

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Approach** | Natural/reading method; entirely in Latin |
| **Level** | Novice (ch. 1) through Advanced (Roma Aeterna) |
| **Publisher** | Focus Publishing / Hackett |
| **Price** | ~$30 per volume; annotated edition ~$45 |
| **Supplements** | *Colloquia Personarum* (conversations), *Fabellae Latinae*, *Fabulae Syrae*, *Exercitia Latina* |
| **Audio** | Latinum Institute recordings; Latinitium has free and premium audio |
| **Verdict** | Best choice for most learners; immersive; high retention |

**Best supplements**:
- *Exercitia Latina* I & II — grammar exercises keyed to each chapter
- *Colloquia Personarum* — spoken dialogue supplement for conversational practice
- *Roma Aeterna* — Pars II; bridges to authentic classical texts

#### Wheelock's Latin (Frederic M. Wheelock)
The dominant US university textbook for 70 years. Systematic grammar presentation (one chapter per topic) with readings from actual classical authors. 40 chapters cover all grammar.

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Approach** | Grammar-translation; deductive (rules explained, then applied) |
| **Level** | Novice through Intermediate |
| **Publisher** | HarperCollins |
| **Edition** | 7th edition (2011) or 8th (latest) |
| **Supplements** | *Workbook for Wheelock's Latin*; *38 Latin Stories* (graded readers keyed to Wheelock) |
| **Verdict** | Works well; less engaging than LLPSI but very thorough |

#### Cambridge Latin Course (CLC)
Story-driven approach following a Roman family in Pompeii and across the empire. Popular in UK schools. Strong cultural context. Less explicit grammar instruction than Wheelock.

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Approach** | Reading method; context-first with cultural emphasis |
| **Level** | Novice through Intermediate (5 units) |
| **Publisher** | Cambridge University Press |
| **Verdict** | Engaging stories; weaker on explicit grammar — supplement with grammar reference |

#### Oxford Latin Course (OLC)
Similar to CLC but with more explicit grammar. Follows Marcus Tullius and family through Roman life. Common in schools.

#### Ecce Romani
School textbook series (6 books). Gradual progression. Less popular now but still used in some US schools.

#### Learn Latin the Natural Way (Miraglia/Balme)
Italian natural-method textbook, structured more explicitly than LLPSI but still largely in Latin.

### For Intermediate Learners

#### Eutropius: Breviarium ab Urbe Condita (DCC Edition)
The standard bridge from adapted to authentic texts. Eutropius wrote in the 4th century AD in clear, simple Latin. Dickinson College Commentaries provides a free annotated edition online.

**URL**: [dcc.dickinson.edu/eutropius/eutropius-introduction](https://dcc.dickinson.edu/eutropius/eutropius-introduction)

#### Ritchie's Fabulae Facilae (Ritchie)
Stories from Greek mythology adapted into easy Latin. Good transition reader. Available free at Perseus.

#### Cornelius Nepos — Lives
Brief biographies of Greek and Roman commanders. Clear, straightforward prose. Good for building fluency before Caesar.

#### Bradley's Arnold: Latin Prose Composition
The classic composition manual. Systematic exercises covering every Latin construction. **Start after completing LLPSI or Wheelock.**

### Reference Grammars

#### Allen & Greenough's New Latin Grammar (A&G)
The standard scholarly reference grammar. Exhaustive treatment of every construction with examples from classical authors. Available free online at Dickinson College Commentaries.

**URL**: [dcc.dickinson.edu/grammar/latin/introduction](https://dcc.dickinson.edu/grammar/latin/introduction)

#### Woodcock's A New Latin Syntax (E.C. Woodcock)
Superb treatment of Latin syntax from a semantic/functional perspective rather than just listing rules. For advanced learners.

#### Bennett's New Latin Grammar
Shorter than A&G; useful quick reference.

#### Gildersleeve and Lodge Latin Grammar
Another thorough reference grammar; less commonly used now.

## Dictionaries

### For Learners

#### Lewis's Elementary Latin Dictionary (Charlton T. Lewis)
**Best learner's dictionary**. Abridged from L&S for student use. Available free online at Perseus and Logeion. Print edition available (~$30).

#### Cassell's Latin Dictionary
Reliable, compact, affordable. Good for beginners. Contains English–Latin and Latin–English sections (useful for composition).

### For Advanced Learners

#### Lewis and Short: A Latin Dictionary (L&S)
The standard English-language Latin dictionary. 50,000+ entries with classical citations. Available free at Perseus. Very large print edition.

**URL**: [perseus.tufts.edu](https://www.perseus.tufts.edu) → Latin texts → lexicon tools

#### Oxford Latin Dictionary (OLD)
The most authoritative Latin dictionary in English. Based on primary sources. 10 volumes (now in one). Institutional library access typically required for print; available on Oxford Reference Online.

**For serious advanced study**: The OLD is preferred by scholars over L&S as it doesn't include post-classical Latin and is more precise.

#### Georges Handwörterbuch (German)
The standard dictionary for German-speaking Latinists. *Ausführliches Handwörterbuch Lateinisch-Deutsch*. Very thorough.
