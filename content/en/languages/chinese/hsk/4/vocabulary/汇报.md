## 汇报 (huìbào)

汇报 means to report work progress or findings to a superior, or the act of making such a report.

## Meanings

1. **[verb]** to report; to give a report; to brief (a superior)
2. **[noun]** a report; a briefing

## Example Sentences

**他每周向经理汇报工作情况。** Tā měi zhōu xiàng jīnglǐ huìbào gōngzuò qíngkuàng. He reports his work progress to the manager every week.

**请你明天汇报一下项目进展。** Qǐng nǐ míngtiān huìbào yīxià xiàngmù jìnzhǎn. Please give a report on the project progress tomorrow.

**这次汇报进行得非常顺利。** Zhè cì huìbào jìnxíng de fēicháng shùnlì. This briefing went very smoothly.

**她准备了一份详细的工作汇报。** Tā zhǔnbèi le yī fèn xiángxì de gōngzuò huìbào. She prepared a detailed work report.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 工作汇报 (gōngzuò huìbào) | work report |
| 汇报情况 (huìbào qíngkuàng) | to report the situation |
| 向上级汇报 (xiàng shàngjí huìbào) | to report to a superior |
| 做汇报 (zuò huìbào) | to give a report/presentation |

## Usage Notes

汇报 specifically refers to reporting upward in a hierarchy, such as an employee reporting to a manager. It is different from 报告 (bàogào), which can refer to a report document or a lecture-style presentation. 汇报 emphasizes the communicative act of informing a superior and is common in workplace Chinese.

## Memory Hook

汇 (to converge/gather information) + 报 (to report) — you gather all the information and then report it up the chain.
