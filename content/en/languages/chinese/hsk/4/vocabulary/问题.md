## 问题 (wèntí)

问题 covers both a question asked to seek information and a problem or issue that needs to be resolved, making it one of the most frequently used nouns in Chinese.

## Meanings

1. **[noun]** Problem; issue; matter that needs resolving.
2. **[noun]** Question (asked to obtain information or in a test).

## Example Sentences

**这台电脑出了问题，无法正常运行。**
Zhè tái diànnǎo chū le wèntí, wúfǎ zhèngcháng yùnxíng.
This computer has a problem and cannot run normally.

**老师问了一个很难的问题。**
Lǎoshī wèn le yī gè hěn nán de wèntí.
The teacher asked a very difficult question.

**我们需要尽快解决这个问题。**
Wǒmen xūyào jǐnkuài jiějué zhège wèntí.
We need to solve this problem as soon as possible.

**有什么问题可以随时问我。**
Yǒu shénme wèntí kěyǐ suíshí wèn wǒ.
If you have any questions, feel free to ask me anytime.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 解决问题 | to solve a problem |
| 提出问题 | to raise a question / issue |
| 存在问题 | there exists a problem |
| 没问题 | no problem; sure |
| 出问题 | to have a problem; something goes wrong |

## Usage Notes

没问题 is an extremely common expression meaning "no problem" or "sure." 出问题 means something has gone wrong. When asking "is there a problem?", Chinese speakers often say 有问题吗? The word is used in both formal and casual contexts.

## Memory Hook

问 means "to ask/question" and 题 means "topic/subject"; together they make the subject that you need to ask about or solve.
