## 热情 (rèqíng)

热情 describes a warm, enthusiastic attitude toward people or activities, and is one of the highest compliments you can give someone's personality in Chinese.

## Meanings

1. **[adjective]** enthusiastic, warm, passionate
2. **[noun]** enthusiasm, warmth, passion

## Example Sentences

**她对工作非常热情。** Tā duì gōngzuò fēicháng rèqíng. She is very enthusiastic about her work.

**他热情地欢迎了每一位客人。** Tā rèqíng de huānyíng le měi yī wèi kèrén. He warmly welcomed every guest.

**老师的热情影响了全班同学。** Lǎoshī de rèqíng yǐngxiǎng le quán bān tóngxué. The teacher's enthusiasm influenced the whole class.

**我们应该用热情去面对生活中的挑战。** Wǒmen yīnggāi yòng rèqíng qù miàn duì shēnghuó zhōng de tiǎozhàn. We should face life's challenges with enthusiasm.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 热情好客 (rèqíng hàokè) | warm and hospitable |
| 充满热情 (chōngmǎn rèqíng) | full of enthusiasm |
| 热情款待 (rèqíng kuǎndài) | to entertain warmly |
| 失去热情 (shīqù rèqíng) | to lose enthusiasm |

## Usage Notes

热情 can function as both an adjective and a noun. As an adjective it often precedes 地 (de) to form an adverb: 热情地帮助 (rèqíng de bāngzhù, "to help enthusiastically"). It carries a positive connotation and emphasizes both warmth toward others and personal passion for a pursuit.

## Memory Hook

Picture a blazing fire (热 = heat) inside someone's heart (情 = feeling/emotion) — that burning inner fire is their 热情.
