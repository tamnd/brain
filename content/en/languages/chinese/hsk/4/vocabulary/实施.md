## 实施 (shíshī)

实施 means to implement or put into effect a plan, policy, law, or measure. It is a formal verb that emphasizes the transition from planning to action. It is commonly used in government, legal, business, and project management contexts.

## Meanings

1. **[verb]** To implement; to carry out; to put into effect; to execute.

## Example Sentences

**新法规将于下个季度正式实施。**
Xīn fǎguī jiāng yú xià gè jìdù zhèngshì shíshī.
The new regulations will officially come into effect next quarter.

**政府宣布将实施一系列措施来刺激经济增长。**
Zhèngfǔ xuānbù jiāng shíshī yī xìliè cuòshī lái cìjī jīngjì zēngzhǎng.
The government announced that it would implement a series of measures to stimulate economic growth.

**这项改革方案经过详细论证后，终于开始全面实施。**
Zhè xiàng gǎigé fāng'àn jīngguò xiángxì lùnzhèng hòu, zhōngyú kāishǐ quánmiàn shíshī.
After detailed deliberation, this reform plan finally began full-scale implementation.

**项目团队按计划实施了每个阶段的任务，进展顺利。**
Xiàngmù tuánduì àn jìhuà shíshī le měi gè jiēduàn de rènwù, jìnzhǎn shùnlì.
The project team carried out the tasks at each stage as planned, with smooth progress.

## Usage Notes

实施 is more formal and action-oriented than 执行 (zhíxíng), which also means "to execute" but tends to focus on following orders or carrying out instructions. 实施 often pairs with abstract objects like 政策 (policy), 法规 (regulations), 计划 (plan), and 措施 (measures). A near-synonym 落实 (luòshí) means "to put into practice" and emphasizes ensuring that something actually happens, while 实施 focuses on the action of beginning execution.

## Memory Hook

实 means "solid/real" and 施 means "to apply/carry out." To 实施 is to make something real and solid by actually applying it in the world, moving from paper to practice.
