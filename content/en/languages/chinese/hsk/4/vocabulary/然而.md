## 然而 (rán'ér)

然而 is a formal conjunction meaning however, but, or nevertheless, used to introduce a contrasting statement.

## Meanings

1. **[conjunction]** however; but; nevertheless; used to introduce a contrast or unexpected turn

## Example Sentences

**他努力工作，然而结果并不理想。** Tā nǔlì gōngzuò, rán'ér jiéguǒ bìng bù lǐxiǎng. He worked hard; however, the result was not ideal.

**计划听起来不错，然而实施起来并不容易。** Jìhuà tīng qǐlái búcuò, rán'ér shíshī qǐlái bìng bù róngyì. The plan sounds good; however, it is not easy to implement.

**这个方法被广泛使用，然而它也有明显的缺点。** Zhège fāngfǎ bèi guǎngfàn shǐyòng, rán'ér tā yě yǒu míngxiǎn de quēdiǎn. This method is widely used; however, it also has obvious drawbacks.

**她很聪明，然而有时候过于自信。** Tā hěn cōngmíng, rán'ér yǒu shíhou guòyú zìxìn. She is very intelligent; however, she is sometimes overly confident.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 然而事实上 | however, in reality |
| 然而结果 | however, the result |
| 虽然...然而 | although... however... |
| 然而并不 | however, not at all |

## Usage Notes

然而 is more formal and literary than 但是 (but) or 可是 (but/however). It appears primarily in written Chinese, academic essays, and formal speech. It typically begins a new clause or sentence and always marks a contrast with what came before.

## Memory Hook

然 means "so/thus" and 而 means "yet/but": 然而 is "thus yet," the elegant pivot that says "things were going one way, yet now they turn."
