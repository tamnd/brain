## 选举 (xuǎnjǔ)

选举 refers to the process of choosing a person for an office or position through voting. It functions both as a noun meaning "election" and as a verb meaning "to elect."

## Meanings
1. **n** election, vote
2. **v** to elect, to vote for

## Example Sentences
**这次总统选举吸引了全国人民的关注。** Zhè cì zǒngtǒng xuǎnjǔ xīyǐn le quánguó rénmín de guānzhù. This presidential election attracted the attention of the entire nation.

**学生们选举出了新的班长。** Xuéshengmen xuǎnjǔ chū le xīn de bānzhǎng. The students elected a new class monitor.

**公平、公正的选举是民主的基础。** Gōngpíng, gōngzhèng de xuǎnjǔ shì mínzhǔ de jīchǔ. Fair and just elections are the foundation of democracy.

**他被选举为委员会主席。** Tā bèi xuǎnjǔ wéi wěiyuánhuì zhǔxí. He was elected as the chairman of the committee.

## Collocations
| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 举行选举 (jǔxíng xuǎnjǔ) | to hold an election |
| 参加选举 (cānjiā xuǎnjǔ) | to participate in an election |
| 总统选举 (zǒngtǒng xuǎnjǔ) | presidential election |
| 选举结果 (xuǎnjǔ jiéguǒ) | election results |

## Usage Notes
选举 combines 选 (to select/choose) and 举 (to raise/nominate). It is typically used for formal institutional voting. For everyday choices, 选择 (xuǎnzé) is preferred. The passive structure 被选举为... means "to be elected as..."

## Memory Hook
选 means "to choose" and 举 means "to raise (one's hand)" — an election is raising your hand to choose someone.
