## 血压 (xuèyā)

血压 refers to the pressure of blood as it flows through the arteries. It is an important medical term used in health check-ups and discussions about cardiovascular health.

## Meanings

1. **[noun]** blood pressure; the force exerted by blood against artery walls

## Example Sentences

**他的血压有点高，医生让他少吃盐。** Tā de xuèyā yǒudiǎn gāo, yīshēng ràng tā shǎo chī yán. His blood pressure is a bit high, so the doctor told him to eat less salt.

**量血压是体检的基本项目之一。** Liáng xuèyā shì tǐjiǎn de jīběn xiàngmù zhī yī. Measuring blood pressure is one of the basic items in a physical exam.

**老年人应该定期检查血压。** Lǎonián rén yīnggāi dìngqī jiǎnchá xuèyā. Elderly people should regularly check their blood pressure.

**压力太大会导致血压升高。** Yālì tài dà huì dǎozhì xuèyā shēnggāo. Too much stress can cause blood pressure to rise.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 量血压 | to measure blood pressure |
| 高血压 | high blood pressure; hypertension |
| 低血压 | low blood pressure; hypotension |
| 血压升高 | blood pressure rises |

## Usage Notes

血压 is a common medical noun. 高血压 (gāo xuèyā, hypertension) and 低血压 (dī xuèyā, hypotension) are very frequently used compound terms. The verb used with 血压 for measuring is 量 (liáng) or 测 (cè), not 看 (kàn). Regular check-ups (定期检查) and blood pressure monitoring are common topics in health discussions at the B1 level.

## Memory Hook

血 (blood) + 压 (pressure) — blood pressure is literally the "pressure of blood." Simple and direct.
