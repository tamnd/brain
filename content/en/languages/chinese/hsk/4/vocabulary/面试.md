## 面试 (miànshì)

面试 means "interview" or "to interview" and specifically refers to a face-to-face evaluation, most commonly for a job or academic admission.

## Meanings

1. **[noun]** interview; oral examination
2. **[verb]** to interview; to have an interview

## Example Sentences

**明天我有一个重要的面试。** Míngtiān wǒ yǒu yīgè zhòngyào de miànshì. I have an important interview tomorrow.

**他面试了三家公司，最后选择了其中一家。** Tā miànshì le sān jiā gōngsī, zuìhòu xuǎnzé le qízhōng yī jiā. He interviewed at three companies and finally chose one of them.

**面试时要注意穿着和言谈举止。** Miànshì shí yào zhùyì chuānzhuó hé yántán jǔzhǐ. During an interview, pay attention to your attire and behavior.

**她顺利通过了面试，得到了这份工作。** Tā shùnlì tōngguò le miànshì, dédào le zhè fèn gōngzuò. She successfully passed the interview and got the job.

## Usage Notes

面试 literally means "face test" and specifically refers to in-person interviews, as opposed to written exams or phone calls. 面试官 (miànshì guān) is the interviewer. Preparing for a 面试 is a major topic in Chinese workplace culture, and the word appears frequently in job-seeking and HR contexts. The phrase 参加面试 (to attend an interview) is very common.

## Memory Hook

面 (miàn) means "face" and 试 (shì) means "to test": a 面试 is literally a "face test," meeting someone face to face to be evaluated.
