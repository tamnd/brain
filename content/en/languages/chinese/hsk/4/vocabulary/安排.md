## 安排 (ānpái)

安排 refers to deliberately organizing people, events, or resources into a coherent plan, and can function as both the act of arranging and the resulting arrangement itself.

## Meanings

1. **[verb]** To arrange, to plan, to schedule, to assign.
2. **[noun]** An arrangement, a plan, a schedule.

## Example Sentences

**经理已经安排好了明天的会议室。**
Jīnglǐ yǐjīng ānpái hǎo le míngtiān de huìyìshì.
The manager has already arranged tomorrow's meeting room.

**请你提前安排好旅行中的每一个细节。**
Qǐng nǐ tíqián ānpái hǎo lǚxíng zhōng de měi yī gè xìjié.
Please arrange every detail of the trip in advance.

**这次活动的安排非常周到，大家都很满意。**
Zhè cì huódòng de ānpái fēicháng zhōudào, dàjiā dōu hěn mǎnyì.
The arrangements for this event were very thorough, and everyone was satisfied.

**公司安排新员工参加入职培训。**
Gōngsī ānpái xīn yuángōng cānjiā rùzhí péixùn.
The company arranged for new employees to attend onboarding training.

## Usage Notes

安排 implies intentional, often careful organization rather than a casual plan. When used as a noun, it often appears with 做出安排 (make arrangements) or as a modifier (安排不合理 — the arrangements are unreasonable). It differs from 计划, which emphasizes the plan itself, while 安排 emphasizes the act of assigning or placing things into order.

## Memory Hook

安 means "peace/settle" and 排 means "to line up in a row" — when you line everything up peacefully, you have a proper arrangement.
