## 礼貌 (lǐmào)

礼貌 describes polite behavior and good manners, and is both a noun (politeness) and an adjective (polite) in Chinese.

## Meanings

1. **[adjective]** polite, courteous, well-mannered
2. **[noun]** politeness, courtesy, good manners

## Example Sentences

**他说话很有礼貌。** Tā shuōhuà hěn yǒu lǐmào. He speaks very politely.

**礼貌是与人交往的基本要求。** Lǐmào shì yǔ rén jiāowǎng de jīběn yāoqiú. Politeness is a basic requirement for getting along with others.

**孩子们应该从小学会礼貌待人。** Háizimen yīnggāi cóng xiǎo xuéhuì lǐmào dài rén. Children should learn to treat people politely from a young age.

**她的礼貌让所有人都对她印象很好。** Tā de lǐmào ràng suǒyǒu rén dōu duì tā yìnxiàng hěn hǎo. Her politeness gave everyone a very good impression of her.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 有礼貌 (yǒu lǐmào) | to be polite |
| 不礼貌 (bù lǐmào) | impolite, rude |
| 礼貌用语 (lǐmào yòngyǔ) | polite expressions |
| 讲礼貌 (jiǎng lǐmào) | to observe good manners |

## Usage Notes

礼貌 is most naturally used in the pattern 有礼貌 (yǒu lǐmào, "to be polite") or 没有礼貌 / 不礼貌 (to be impolite). As an adjective, it cannot directly modify nouns without 的: 礼貌的行为 (lǐmào de xíngwéi, "polite behavior"). 礼貌 differs from 客气 (kèqi), which refers more specifically to being modest and deferential in social situations.

## Memory Hook

礼 means "ritual/etiquette" and 貌 means "appearance/manner" — your 礼貌 is the outward appearance of your etiquette.
