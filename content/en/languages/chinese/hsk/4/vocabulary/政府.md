## 政府 (zhèngfǔ)

政府 refers to the government as an institution — the body responsible for administering and governing a country, province, or municipality. It is used at all levels from central to local.

## Meanings
1. **n** government

## Example Sentences
**政府出台了新的环保政策。** Zhèngfǔ chūtái le xīn de huánbǎo zhèngcè. The government introduced new environmental protection policies.

**市民希望政府能解决交通拥堵问题。** Shìmín xīwàng zhèngfǔ néng jiějué jiāotōng yōngdǔ wèntí. Citizens hope the government can solve traffic congestion problems.

**中央政府负责制定全国性的法规。** Zhōngyāng zhèngfǔ fùzé zhìdìng quánguó xìng de fǎguī. The central government is responsible for formulating national regulations.

**这项工程得到了政府的大力支持。** Zhè xiàng gōngchéng dédào le zhèngfǔ de dàlì zhīchí. This project received strong support from the government.

## Collocations

## Usage Notes
政府 can be used as a subject taking verbs like 出台 (to introduce/issue), 制定 (to formulate), or 支持 (to support). It can be modified by adjectives of scale: 中央 (central), 地方 (local), 市 (municipal). Note: 当局 (dāngjú) is a more formal synonym often seen in news.

## Memory Hook
政 means "governance/politics" and 府 means "official residence/office" — the office of governance is the government.
