## 全面 (quánmiàn)

全面 means comprehensive or all-around, indicating that something covers every aspect of a subject or situation without omission. It is used to describe analyses, plans, reforms, understanding, and abilities that are thorough and complete.

## Meanings

1. **[adjective]** Comprehensive; all-around; thorough; all-encompassing.

## Example Sentences

**这份报告对市场现状进行了全面而深入的分析。**
Zhè fèn bàogào duì shìchǎng xiànzhuàng jìnxíng le quánmiàn ér shēnrù de fēnxī.
This report conducted a comprehensive and in-depth analysis of the current market situation.

**我们需要全面了解问题，才能制定有效的解决方案。**
Wǒmen xūyào quánmiàn liǎojiě wèntí, cái néng zhìdìng yǒuxiào de jiějué fāng'àn.
We need a comprehensive understanding of the problem before we can formulate an effective solution.

**教育改革旨在培养学生全面发展，而不仅仅是学业成绩。**
Jiàoyù gǎigé zhǐ zài péiyǎng xuéshēng quánmiàn fāzhǎn, ér bù jǐnjǐn shì xuéyè chéngjì.
Educational reform aims to cultivate students' all-around development, not just academic performance.

**该计划涵盖了社会、经济和环境三个方面，是一个全面的发展框架。**
Gāi jìhuà hángài le shèhuì, jīngjì hé huánjìng sān gè fāngmiàn, shì yīgè quánmiàn de fāzhǎn kuàngjià.
The plan covers three dimensions — social, economic, and environmental — making it a comprehensive development framework.

## Usage Notes

全面 emphasizes leaving nothing out, while 深入 (shēnrù, in-depth) emphasizes going deep into a subject. The two are often paired: 全面而深入的分析 (comprehensive and in-depth analysis). In Chinese political discourse, 全面建设 (comprehensive construction) and 全面深化改革 (comprehensively deepening reform) are frequent collocations. As an adverb: 全面提升能力 (comprehensively raise one's abilities).

## Memory Hook

全 means "complete/whole" and 面 means "face/side/aspect." Something 全面 shows all its faces and sides completely — nothing is hidden or overlooked.
