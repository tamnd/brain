## 承认 (chéngrèn)

承认 covers two related senses: admitting something (often a fault or uncomfortable truth) and officially recognizing something (such as a government or credential).

## Meanings

1. **[verb]** To admit, to confess (a fault or truth one might prefer to hide).
2. **[verb]** To acknowledge, to recognize, to accept as valid or legitimate.

## Example Sentences

**他终于承认自己在这件事上犯了一个严重的错误。**
Tā zhōngyú chéngrèn zìjǐ zài zhè jiàn shì shàng fàn le yī gè yánzhòng de cuòwù.
He finally admitted that he had made a serious mistake in this matter.

**该协议要求各方相互承认对方颁发的学历证书。**
Gāi xiéyì yāoqiú gè fāng xiānghù chéngrèn duìfāng bānfā de xuélì zhèngshū.
The agreement requires all parties to mutually recognize academic credentials issued by each other.

**尽管证据充分，他始终不肯承认自己参与了这件事。**
Jǐnguǎn zhèngjù chōngfèn, tā shǐzhōng bù kěn chéngrèn zìjǐ cānyù le zhè jiàn shì.
Despite the substantial evidence, he refused to acknowledge his involvement in the matter.

**承认自己的局限性，是一种成熟和自信的表现。**
Chéngrèn zìjǐ de júxiànxìng, shì yī zhǒng chéngshú hé zìxìn de biǎoxiàn.
Acknowledging one's own limitations is a sign of maturity and confidence.

## Usage Notes

承认 carries a sense of reluctance or significance — you acknowledge something that matters or that you might have resisted. In diplomatic or legal contexts it means formal recognition (承认某国政府 — to recognize a government). In everyday speech, it often implies admitting something negative. Contrast with 确认 (to confirm), which is neutral and simply verifies a fact.

## Memory Hook

承 means "to receive/accept" and 认 means "to recognize" — you receive and recognize something as true, even if it is uncomfortable.
