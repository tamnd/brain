## 举办 (jǔbàn)

举办 means to hold or host an organized event such as a conference, exhibition, competition, or ceremony. It implies that the subject is the organizer and host of a formal activity. It is more formal than 开 and more specific than 组织 in that it always refers to events.

## Meanings

1. **[verb]** To hold; to host; to organize and run (an event or activity).

## Example Sentences

**这座城市将于明年举办国际马拉松赛事。**
Zhè zuò chéngshì jiāng yú míngnián jǔbàn guójì mǎlāsōng sàishì.
This city will host an international marathon next year.

**学校每年都会举办一次科技创新大赛，鼓励学生发挥创造力。**
Xuéxiào měinián dōu huì jǔbàn yī cì kējì chuàngxīn dàsài, gǔlì xuéshēng fāhuī chuàngzào lì.
The school holds a science and technology innovation competition every year to encourage students to demonstrate creativity.

**博物馆正在举办一场关于古代丝绸之路的专题展览。**
Bówùguǎn zhèngzài jǔbàn yī chǎng guānyú gǔdài sīchóu zhī lù de zhuāntí zhǎnlǎn.
The museum is currently hosting a thematic exhibition on the ancient Silk Road.

**两国政府共同举办了一次双边文化交流论坛。**
Liǎng guó zhèngfǔ gòngtóng jǔbàn le yī cì shuāngbiān wénhuà jiāoliú lùntán.
The governments of both countries jointly hosted a bilateral cultural exchange forum.

## Usage Notes

举办 is always used with formal, organized events — you cannot use it for casual gatherings. Compare 举办会议 (hold a formal conference) with 开会 (have a meeting, more everyday). A useful formal pattern is X 将于 Y 地点举办 Z 活动 (X will hold Z event at Y location), frequently seen in official announcements and news reports.

## Memory Hook

举 means "to raise/lift up" and 办 means "to handle/run." To 举办 an event is to lift it up and run it — raising it to the level of a formal occasion.
