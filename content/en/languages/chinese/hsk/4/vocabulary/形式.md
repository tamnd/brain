## 形式 (xíngshì)

形式 refers to the form, format, or structure of something, as opposed to its content or substance. It can also mean "formality" when something is done for appearance rather than substance.

## Meanings

1. **[noun]** form; format; structure; shape
2. **[noun]** formality; outward appearance (as opposed to substance)

## Example Sentences

**这次活动的形式很新颖，吸引了很多人。** Zhè cì huódòng de xíngshì hěn xīnyǐng, xīyǐnle hěn duō rén. The format of this event was very novel and attracted many people.

**我们要改变学习的形式，不能只靠背诵。** Wǒmen yào gǎibiàn xuéxí de xíngshì, bù néng zhǐ kào bèisòng. We need to change the form of learning; we cannot rely only on memorization.

**这个会议只是形式，没有实质内容。** Zhège huìyì zhǐshì xíngshì, méiyǒu shízhì nèiróng. This meeting is just a formality; there is no substantive content.

**艺术可以用很多不同的形式表达。** Yìshù kěyǐ yòng hěn duō bùtóng de xíngshì biǎodá. Art can be expressed in many different forms.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 形式多样 | diverse in form |
| 走形式 | to go through the motions |
| 内容与形式 | content and form |
| 组织形式 | organizational form |

## Usage Notes

形式 is often contrasted with 内容 (content) or 实质 (substance). The phrase 走形式 (to go through the motions) has a negative connotation, meaning something is done superficially. At B1 level, 形式 appears frequently in discussions of art, education, and organization.

## Memory Hook

形 means "shape/form" and 式 means "style/pattern." So 形式 is the shape and style of something: its outward form.
