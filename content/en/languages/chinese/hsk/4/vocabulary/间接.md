## 间接 (jiànjiē)

间接 means "indirect" or "roundabout" and describes something that does not happen directly but through an intermediate step or agent.

## Meanings

1. **[adjective]** indirect; not direct; roundabout

## Example Sentences

**他间接地表达了自己的不满。** Tā jiànjiē de biǎodá le zìjǐ de bùmǎn. He expressed his dissatisfaction indirectly.

**污染对健康的间接影响不可忽视。** Wūrǎn duì jiànkāng de jiànjiē yǐngxiǎng bù kě hūshì. The indirect effects of pollution on health cannot be ignored.

**他们通过间接渠道获取了信息。** Tāmen tōngguò jiànjiē qúdào huòqǔ le xìnxī. They obtained information through indirect channels.

**这次事故的间接原因是管理不善。** Zhè cì shìgù de jiànjiē yuányīn shì guǎnlǐ bùshàn. The indirect cause of this accident was poor management.

## Usage Notes

间接 contrasts with 直接 (zhíjiē, direct). It is used in many contexts including communication styles (indirect speech), cause-and-effect analysis, and taxation (间接税, indirect tax). In Chinese culture, indirect communication is often associated with politeness and face-saving, making this a culturally relevant word.

## Memory Hook

间 (jiān) means "between or gap" and 接 (jiē) means "to connect": 间接 describes a connection that goes through a gap or intermediary, making it indirect.
