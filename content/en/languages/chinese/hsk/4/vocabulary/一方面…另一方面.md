## 一方面…另一方面 (yī fāngmiàn… lìng yī fāngmiàn)

This is a paired grammar pattern used to present two sides, aspects, or reasons of a situation, similar to "on one hand… on the other hand" in English.

## Meanings

1. **[conjunction pattern]** on one hand… on the other hand; used to balance or contrast two points

## Example Sentences

**一方面，工作很有意思；另一方面，压力也很大。** Yī fāngmiàn, gōngzuò hěn yǒu yìsi; lìng yī fāngmiàn, yālì yě hěn dà. On one hand, the job is very interesting; on the other hand, the pressure is also great.

**一方面要学好中文，另一方面也不能忽视英语。** Yī fāngmiàn yào xuéhǎo Zhōngwén, lìng yī fāngmiàn yě bù néng hūshì Yīngyǔ. On one hand, you need to learn Chinese well; on the other hand, you cannot neglect English either.

**一方面城市生活很方便，另一方面空气污染也比较严重。** Yī fāngmiàn chéngshì shēnghuó hěn fāngbiàn, lìng yī fāngmiàn kōngqì wūrǎn yě bǐjiào yánzhòng. On one hand, city life is very convenient; on the other hand, air pollution is also quite serious.

**一方面他想去旅游，另一方面他又没有足够的钱。** Yī fāngmiàn tā xiǎng qù lǚyóu, lìng yī fāngmiàn tā yòu méiyǒu zúgòu de qián. On one hand, he wants to travel; on the other hand, he does not have enough money.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 一方面…另一方面 | on one hand… on the other hand |
| 一方面原因 | one reason / one aspect of the reason |
| 另一方面来说 | speaking from another aspect |
| 从另一方面看 | looking from another angle |

## Usage Notes

This pattern is commonly used in spoken and written Chinese to acknowledge complexity or to give balanced arguments. Both clauses often share the same subject, but the subject can differ. It is a useful structure for expressing nuanced opinions at the B1 level and above.

## Memory Hook

Picture a balance scale: one pan is labeled "一方面" and the other "另一方面" — always weighing two sides at once.
