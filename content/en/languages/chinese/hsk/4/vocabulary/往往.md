## 往往 (wǎngwǎng)

往往 describes something that tends to happen regularly or as a rule based on past pattern, meaning "often," "frequently," or "more often than not."

## Meanings

1. **[adverb]** Often; frequently; as a rule (based on an observed pattern or tendency).

## Example Sentences

**冬天往往比夏天更难坚持锻炼。**
Dōngtiān wǎngwǎng bǐ xiàtiān gèng nán jiānchí duànliàn.
Winter is often harder to keep up with exercise than summer.

**成功的人往往比别人更努力。**
Chénggōng de rén wǎngwǎng bǐ biérén gèng nǔlì.
Successful people tend to work harder than others.

**他考试前往往睡不好觉。**
Tā kǎoshì qián wǎngwǎng shuì bù hǎo jiào.
He often cannot sleep well before exams.

**好的机会往往只有一次，要好好把握。**
Hǎo de jīhuì wǎngwǎng zhǐ yǒu yī cì, yào hǎohǎo bǎwò.
Good opportunities often come only once; you must seize them well.

## Collocations

## Usage Notes

往往 is based on a past pattern or tendency and implies "this is how things tend to go." It cannot be used for one-time future arrangements. 常常 and 经常 are near-synonyms but simply mean "often/frequently" without the implication of a pattern based on experience.

## Memory Hook

往 means "to go toward (the past)" and the reduplication 往往 suggests going that way again and again; things keep heading in the same direction.
