## 诚实 (chéngshí)

诚实 means honest or truthful, describing a person who says what they truly think and does not deceive others.

## Meanings

1. **[adjective]** honest; truthful; sincere; straightforward

## Example Sentences

**诚实是最重要的品质之一。** Chéngshí shì zuì zhòngyào de pǐnzhì zhī yī. Honesty is one of the most important qualities.

**你应该诚实地告诉他真相。** Nǐ yīnggāi chéngshí de gàosù tā zhēnxiàng. You should honestly tell him the truth.

**他是一个非常诚实的人，从不说谎。** Tā shì yīgè fēicháng chéngshí de rén, cóng bù sāhuǎng. He is a very honest person who never lies.

**诚实的态度会赢得别人的信任。** Chéngshí de tàidu huì yíngdé biérén de xìnrèn. An honest attitude will earn the trust of others.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 诚实的人 (chéngshí de rén) | an honest person |
| 诚实回答 (chéngshí huídá) | to answer honestly |
| 诚实守信 (chéngshí shǒuxìn) | honest and trustworthy |
| 诚实待人 (chéngshí dài rén) | to treat others honestly |

## Usage Notes

诚实 is a core virtue in Chinese culture and frequently appears in discussions of character, education, and professional ethics. Its antonym is 虚伪 (xūwěi) or 说谎 (sāhuǎng, to lie). The phrase 诚实守信 is a common four-character idiom used in formal settings to describe integrity.

## Memory Hook

诚 (sincere/genuine) + 实 (real/true) — to be honest is to be both sincere and real, with no gap between what you feel and what you say.
