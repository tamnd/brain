## 权利 (quánlì)

权利 refers to a right or entitlement that a person holds, whether legal, civil, or human. It is distinct from 权力 (quánlì with a different character), which means "power or authority."

## Meanings
1. **n** right, entitlement, legal right

## Example Sentences
**每个人都有受教育的权利。** Měi gè rén dōu yǒu shòu jiàoyù de quánlì. Everyone has the right to receive education.

**公民的基本权利受到法律的保护。** Gōngmín de jīběn quánlì shòudào fǎlǜ de bǎohù. Citizens' basic rights are protected by law.

**她坚决维护自己的合法权利。** Tā jiānjué wéihù zìjǐ de héfǎ quánlì. She firmly defended her legal rights.

**人权包括生命、自由和平等的权利。** Rénquán bāokuò shēngmìng, zìyóu hé píngděng de quánlì. Human rights include the rights to life, freedom, and equality.

## Usage Notes
Do not confuse 权利 (quánlì, right/entitlement) with 权力 (quánlì, power/authority). The second character is different: 利 means "benefit/interest" while 力 means "force/power." A useful test: if it can be "enjoyed," it is 权利; if it can be "exercised over others," it is 权力.

## Memory Hook
权利 — your 权 (entitlement) brings you 利 (benefit). Rights are entitlements that benefit you.
