## 理性 (lǐxìng)

理性 refers to rationality as a noun, or describes something as rational and reasonable as an adjective.

## Meanings

1. **[noun]** rationality; reason; the capacity for logical and reasoned thinking
2. **[adjective]** rational; reasonable; guided by reason rather than emotion

## Example Sentences

**面对问题，我们应该保持理性。** Miànduì wèntí, wǒmen yīnggāi bǎochí lǐxìng. When facing problems, we should remain rational.

**做决定时需要理性思考，而不仅仅靠感情。** Zuò juédìng shí xūyào lǐxìng sīkǎo, ér bù jǐnjǐn kào gǎnqíng. When making decisions, rational thinking is needed, not just emotions.

**消费者应该理性消费，不要盲目跟风。** Xiāofèizhě yīnggāi lǐxìng xiāofèi, bùyào mángmù gēn fēng. Consumers should consume rationally and not blindly follow trends.

**他处理矛盾的方式非常理性。** Tā chǔlǐ máodùn de fāngshì fēicháng lǐxìng. His way of handling conflicts is very rational.

## Usage Notes

理性 contrasts with 感性 (gǎnxìng), which means emotional or intuitive. In Chinese discourse, being 理性 is generally valued as a sign of maturity and good judgment. It is used both in everyday speech and formal writing.

## Memory Hook

理 means "principle/reason" and 性 means "nature/character": 理性 is having a nature governed by reason and principles.
