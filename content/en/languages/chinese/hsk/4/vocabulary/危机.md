## 危机 (wēijī)

危机 refers to a dangerous or critical turning point in a situation, often requiring urgent action.

## Meanings

1. **[noun]** crisis; critical juncture; emergency situation

## Example Sentences

**公司正面临一场严重的财务危机。** Gōngsī zhèng miànlín yī chǎng yánzhòng de cáiwù wēijī. The company is currently facing a serious financial crisis.

**政府迅速采取措施解决了这次危机。** Zhèngfǔ xùnsù cǎiqǔ cuòshī jiějuéle zhè cì wēijī. The government quickly took measures to resolve the crisis.

**危机往往也是转机的开始。** Wēijī wǎngwǎng yěshì zhuǎnjī de kāishǐ. A crisis is often also the beginning of a turning point.

**他在危机时刻表现出了冷静和智慧。** Tā zài wēijī shíkè biǎoxiànchūle lěngjìng hé zhìhuì. He showed calmness and wisdom at the moment of crisis.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 面临危机 | to face a crisis |
| 经济危机 | economic crisis |
| 危机管理 | crisis management |
| 解决危机 | to resolve a crisis |

## Usage Notes

危机 is a formal noun often used in news and academic contexts. The word carries the sense that danger and opportunity coexist, which is why Chinese speakers sometimes say 危机 contains both 危 (danger) and 机 (opportunity). It can be modified by adjectives to specify the type of crisis.

## Memory Hook

危 means "danger" and 机 means "turning point/opportunity" -- in every crisis (危机) there is both danger and a critical opportunity to turn things around.
