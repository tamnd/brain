## 推测 (tuīcè)

推测 means to draw a conclusion or make an inference based on available evidence or reasoning.

## Meanings

1. **[verb]** to infer; to speculate; to deduce; to conjecture
2. **[noun]** inference; speculation; conjecture

## Example Sentences

**根据这些数据，我们可以推测市场的未来走向。** Gēnjù zhèxiē shùjù, wǒmen kěyǐ tuīcè shìchǎng de wèilái zǒuxiàng. Based on this data, we can infer the future direction of the market.

**他推测天气可能会变，出门带了雨伞。** Tā tuīcè tiānqì kěnéng huì biàn, chūmén dàile yǔsǎn. He speculated that the weather might change, so he brought an umbrella when going out.

**警察根据现场证据推测案发时间。** Jǐngchá gēnjù xiànchǎng zhèngjù tuīcè àn fā shíjiān. The police inferred the time of the crime based on evidence at the scene.

**这只是我的推测，不一定是事实。** Zhè zhǐshì wǒ de tuīcè, bù yīdìng shì shìshí. This is just my speculation; it is not necessarily the truth.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 合理推测 | reasonable inference |
| 根据…推测 | to infer based on... |
| 大胆推测 | bold speculation |
| 仅是推测 | merely speculation |

## Usage Notes

推测 is used when making an educated guess or deduction based on clues, data, or logic. It is more analytical than 猜 (to guess), which implies a random or intuitive guess without reasoning. The phrase 根据…推测 (to infer based on...) is a very useful structure in writing and speaking.

## Memory Hook

推 means "to push forward" and 测 means "to measure/assess" -- 推测 is pushing your reasoning forward to measure a conclusion, i.e., to infer or speculate.
