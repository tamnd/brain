## 传统 (chuántǒng)

传统 refers to customs, beliefs, and practices that have been inherited and passed down through generations. As an adjective it means "traditional." It carries a generally positive connotation of cultural continuity, though it can also imply something outdated in certain contexts.

## Meanings

1. **[noun]** Tradition; convention; heritage.
2. **[adjective]** Traditional; conventional; classic.

## Example Sentences

**春节是中国最重要的传统节日，有着几千年的历史。**
Chūnjié shì Zhōngguó zuì zhòngyào de chuántǒng jiérì, yǒuzhe jǐ qiān nián de lìshǐ.
The Spring Festival is China's most important traditional holiday, with a history of several thousand years.

**这家餐厅以传统烹饪方法著称，坚持使用天然食材。**
Zhè jiā cāntīng yǐ chuántǒng pēngrèn fāngfǎ zhùchēng, jiānchí shǐyòng tiānrán shícái.
This restaurant is famous for its traditional cooking methods and insists on using natural ingredients.

**他们既尊重传统，也勇于接受新的思想和变化。**
Tāmen jì zūnzhòng chuántǒng, yě yǒng yú jiēshòu xīn de sīxiǎng hé biànhuà.
They both respect tradition and are willing to embrace new ideas and changes.

**现代社会中，如何平衡传统价值观与现代生活方式是一个重要课题。**
Xiàndài shèhuì zhōng, rúhé pínghéng chuántǒng jiàzhíguān yǔ xiàndài shēnghuó fāngshì shì yīgè zhòngyào kètí.
In modern society, how to balance traditional values and modern lifestyles is an important issue.

## Usage Notes

传统 used as an adjective always precedes the noun it modifies: 传统食物 (traditional food), 传统医学 (traditional medicine). As a noun it often appears after verbs like 尊重 (respect), 继承 (inherit), 保护 (preserve), and 打破 (break). In contemporary discussion, 传统 is frequently contrasted with 现代 (modern) or 创新 (innovation).

## Memory Hook

传 means "to pass on/transmit" and 统 means "unified/continuous thread." Tradition is the continuous thread passed from one generation to the next, keeping the fabric of culture intact.
