## 特定 (tèdìng)

特定 means specific, particular, or designated — referring to a particular thing or condition that has been defined or set in advance. It is often used in formal and academic contexts to indicate that something applies to a particular set of circumstances rather than in general.

## Meanings

1. **[adjective]** Specific; particular; designated; given; predetermined.

## Example Sentences

**这条规定只适用于特定情况，不能一概而论。**
Zhè tiáo guīdìng zhǐ shìyòng yú tèdìng qíngkuàng, bù néng yīgài ér lùn.
This rule only applies to specific situations and cannot be generalized.

**研究人员在特定的实验条件下重复了这个试验。**
Yánjiū rényuán zài tèdìng de shíyàn tiáojiàn xià chóngfù le zhège shìyàn.
The researchers repeated this experiment under specific experimental conditions.

**语言在特定的文化背景下才能被准确理解。**
Yǔyán zài tèdìng de wénhuà bèijǐng xià cái néng bèi zhǔnquè lǐjiě.
Language can only be accurately understood within a specific cultural context.

**公司为每个岗位设定了特定的工作职责和绩效指标。**
Gōngsī wèi měi gè gǎngwèi shèdìng le tèdìng de gōngzuò zhízé hé jìxiào zhǐbiāo.
The company has set specific job responsibilities and performance indicators for each position.

## Usage Notes

特定 is more precise than 特别 (tèbié, special/especially). 特定 always refers to something that has been specifically defined or designated — it answers "which particular one?" In research and legal writing, 特定 is preferred: 特定人群 (a specific population), 特定时期 (a specific period). It cannot usually be used predicatively (you would not say 情况是特定的 in normal speech).

## Memory Hook

特 means "special" and 定 means "fixed/settled." Something 特定 is specially fixed or designated — not just any example, but the particular one that has been set apart and defined.
