HSK 7, 8, and 9 form a single **Advanced band (C2)** that shares the same vocabulary pool of ~10,877 words. The levels differ in task complexity and depth, not vocabulary scope.

## Level Overview

| Metric | Count |
|--------|-------|
| Cumulative vocabulary | 10,877 words |
| New vocabulary (vs HSK 6) | ~5,480 words — largest jump in the system |
| Recognition characters | 3,088 |
| Must-write characters | 1,200 |
| Grammar points | 586 (cumulative) |
| CEFR | C2 |
| Skills tested | All 6: Listening, Reading, Speaking, Writing, Translation + Oral Defense |
| Estimated study hours | 3,000–5,000+ |
| Time at 10 hrs/week | 5+ years from zero; requires immersion |

## What Distinguishes HSK 7, 8, and 9

All three levels draw from the same ~10,877-word pool. What differs:

| Level | Focus |
|-------|-------|
| HSK 7 | C2 foundational: near-native comprehension and expression |
| HSK 8 | Academic and professional register; complex analysis |
| HSK 9 | Full native-level: literary, archaic, technical, oral defense |

At HSK 7–9, proficiency is assessed by **depth and nuance**, not simply vocabulary breadth.

## The HSK 6→7 Jump

The jump from HSK 6 (5,397 words) to the HSK 7–9 pool (10,877 words) adds **~5,480 new words** — the largest increment in the entire HSK system. This level is designed for:
- Students studying in Chinese-medium universities
- Professionals working in Chinese-speaking environments
- Researchers reading Chinese academic literature

## Vocabulary Profile at HSK 7–9

| Domain | Examples |
|--------|---------|
| Academic | 论述 (expound), 阐释 (explain/interpret), 范畴 (category), 诠释 (interpret) |
| Literary | 意境 (artistic conception), 意蕴 (connotation), 韵味 (charm/flavor) |
| Archaic/Classical | 乃 (thus), 兹 (here, now), 盖 (generally speaking, for) |
| Technical | Domain-specific vocabulary across law, medicine, science, engineering |
| Chengyu (advanced) | Rare and literary 4-character idioms beyond HSK 6 |
| Formal written | 就…而言, 从某种意义上说, 无论如何 |

## What You Can Do at HSK 7–9

- Read any modern Chinese text: novels, academic papers, legal documents, technical manuals
- Participate in university seminars and professional conferences in Chinese
- Write research papers, formal reports, and creative essays in Chinese
- Understand regional accents, informal registers, and fast colloquial speech
- Recognize classical Chinese (文言文) influences in modern writing
- Handle translation between Chinese and other languages professionally

## Skills Tested

| Skill | Format at HSK 7–9 |

| Writing | Analytical essays, research summaries, formal documents |

## Grammar at C2

HSK 7–9 includes all 586 grammar points. Key additions beyond C1:

- **Classical structures in modern writing**: 焉, 乎, 者, 之 in formal contexts
- **Full chengyu competence**: idiomatic use, not just recognition
- **Academic hedging language**: 可能, 大概, 有可能, 似乎, 据…看来
- **Complex embedded clauses**: multiple relative clauses, nominalization chains
- **Discourse markers**: marking logical structure across paragraphs
