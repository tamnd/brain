## 你好 (nǐ hǎo)

你好 is the standard everyday greeting in Mandarin, equivalent to "hello" or "hi." It is used when greeting one person in an informal or neutral setting.

## Meanings

1. **[phrase]** Hello; hi. A general greeting directed at one person.

## Example Sentences

**你好！很高兴认识你。**
Nǐ hǎo! Hěn gāoxìng rènshí nǐ.
Hello! Nice to meet you.

**你好，请问你叫什么名字？**
Nǐ hǎo, qǐngwèn nǐ jiào shénme míngzì?
Hello, may I ask your name?

**A: 你好！ B: 你好！**
A: Nǐ hǎo! B: Nǐ hǎo!
A: Hello! B: Hello!

**老师，您好！**
Lǎoshī, nín hǎo!
Hello, teacher! (using polite form for teacher)

## Usage Notes

你好 is appropriate for greeting any one person in daily life — classmates, shopkeepers, new acquaintances. The response is simply to say 你好 back. When greeting more than one person, use 你们好 (nǐmen hǎo). When greeting an elder, a superior, or someone deserving extra respect, switch to 您好 (nín hǎo), which uses the formal "you." 你好 is not typically used to greet close friends multiple times a day — in that case a simple 嗯 or 哎 is more natural, but 你好 is always correct and polite.

## Collocations or Common Phrases

| Phrase | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 你好吗？ | how are you? |
| 你们好 | hello (to a group) |
| 您好 | hello (formal/respectful) |
| 大家好 | hello everyone |
| 早上好 | good morning |

## Memory Hook

你好 literally means "you good" — you are wishing the other person well the moment you meet.
