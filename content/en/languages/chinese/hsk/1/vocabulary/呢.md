## 呢 (ne)

呢 is a sentence-final particle used to ask a follow-up question ("and what about you/it?"), to soften a statement, or to indicate that a situation is ongoing.

## Meanings

1. **[particle]** Continuation question: "And what about...?" — bounces the topic back to the listener.
2. **[particle]** Softener: makes a statement or question sound less abrupt.
3. **[particle]** Ongoing state: indicates that a situation is still in progress (e.g., 他睡觉呢, He is sleeping).

## Example Sentences

**我很好，你呢？**
Wǒ hěn hǎo, nǐ ne?
I am doing well, and you?

**他在哪里呢？**
Tā zài nǎlǐ ne?
Where is he? (softened, wondering tone)

**你的书呢？**
Nǐ de shū ne?
What about your book? / Where is your book?

**他睡觉呢，别打扰他。**
Tā shuìjiào ne, bié dǎrǎo tā.
He is sleeping — do not disturb him.

## Usage Notes

呢 is most commonly used after a topic to ask "and what about [topic]?" without repeating the full question. This is called the 呢 question. Unlike 吗, which requires a full sentence before it, 呢 can follow a single word or noun phrase to create an implied question. 呢 also softens the tone of a question, making it sound curious or gentle rather than demanding. It cannot be used for simple yes/no questions — use 吗 for those.

## Collocations or Common Phrases

| Phrase | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 你呢？ | and you? what about you? |
| 他呢？ | what about him? |
| 在哪呢？ | where is it/he? |
| 怎么还没来呢？ | why hasn't (he) come yet? |
| 还没好呢 | it's still not ready |

## Memory Hook

呢 has the mouth radical — it is the soft murmur at the end of a question, as if you are thinking aloud and asking gently.
