## 火车 (huǒchē)

A train. 火 means "fire" and 车 means "vehicle," a reference to the steam-powered locomotives of early railway history. Today it refers to any train.

## Meanings

1. **[noun]** Train. A series of connected railway cars pulled by a locomotive.

## Example Sentences

**我坐火车去上海。**
Wǒ zuò huǒchē qù Shànghǎi.
I am taking the train to Shanghai.

**火车几点到？**
Huǒchē jǐ diǎn dào?
What time does the train arrive?

**火车票比飞机票便宜。**
Huǒchē piào bǐ fēijī piào piányí.
Train tickets are cheaper than airplane tickets.

**火车站在城市中心。**
Huǒchē zhàn zài chéngshì zhōngxīn.
The train station is in the city center.

## Memory Hook

火 is "fire" and 车 is "vehicle." The original trains were fire-powered steam engines, so the Chinese name preserves this historical image. Picture a vehicle powered by fire racing along the tracks.
