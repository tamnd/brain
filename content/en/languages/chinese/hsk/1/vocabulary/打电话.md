## 打电话 (dǎ diànhuà)

A verb phrase meaning "to make a phone call." It combines 打 (dǎ, to strike/hit, used for many actions) and 电话 (diànhuà, telephone). Together they form the standard expression for making a call.

## Meanings

1. **[verb phrase]** To make a phone call, to call someone by phone.

## Example Sentences

**我想给妈妈打电话。**
Wǒ xiǎng gěi māma dǎ diànhuà.
I want to make a phone call to my mom.

**他正在打电话，请等一下。**
Tā zhèngzài dǎ diànhuà, qǐng děng yīxià.
He is on the phone right now, please wait a moment.

**你可以给我打电话吗？**
Nǐ kěyǐ gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà ma?
Can you call me?

**我每天晚上打电话给朋友。**
Wǒ měitiān wǎnshàng dǎ diànhuà gěi péngyou.
I call my friend every evening.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 给……打电话 | to call someone (give someone a call) |
| 打长途电话 | to make a long-distance call |
| 打国际电话 | to make an international call |
| 接电话 | to answer the phone |
| 挂电话 | to hang up the phone |
| 用手机打电话 | to call using a mobile phone |

## Memory Hook

Think of "striking" (打) the phone buttons to dial — you hit the keys to make the call.
