## 好吃 (hǎochī)

An adjective meaning delicious or tasty. It literally combines 好 (good) and 吃 (eat), making it one of the most transparent and memorable compounds in Chinese.

## Meanings

1. **[adjective]** Delicious, tasty, good to eat.

## Example Sentences

**这个菜真好吃！**
Zhège cài zhēn hǎochī!
This dish is truly delicious!

**妈妈做的饭最好吃了。**
Māma zuò de fàn zuì hǎochī le.
The food Mom makes is the most delicious.

**你觉得这个蛋糕好吃吗？**
Nǐ juéde zhège dàngāo hǎochī ma?
Do you think this cake tastes good?

**这家餐厅的饺子非常好吃，很多人来这里吃。**
Zhè jiā cāntīng de jiǎozi fēicháng hǎochī, hěn duō rén lái zhèlǐ chī.
The dumplings at this restaurant are incredibly delicious and many people come here to eat them.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 很好吃 (hěn hǎochī) | very delicious |
| 真好吃 (zhēn hǎochī) | really tasty |
| 最好吃 (zuì hǎochī) | the most delicious |
| 不好吃 (bù hǎochī) | not tasty, bad-tasting |
| 好吃的东西 (hǎochī de dōngxi) | delicious food, tasty stuff |
| 难吃 (nán chī) | tastes bad (opposite) |

## Memory Hook

好 (good) plus 吃 (eat) equals exactly what it means: good to eat. The characters are the definition. Contrast with the opposite 难吃 (nán chī), which means tastes bad.
