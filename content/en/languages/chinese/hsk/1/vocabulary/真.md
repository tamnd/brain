## 真 (zhēn)

真 is an intensifying adverb meaning "really" or "truly." It expresses genuine emotion or strong emphasis, and also serves as an adjective meaning "real" or "true."

## Meanings

1. **[adverb]** Really; truly; genuinely (intensifier before adjectives or verbs).
2. **[adjective]** Real; authentic; true.

## Example Sentences

**这个菜真好吃！**
Zhège cài zhēn hǎo chī!
This dish is really delicious!

**你真漂亮。**
Nǐ zhēn piàoliang.
You are truly beautiful.

**这是真的吗？**
Zhè shì zhēn de ma?
Is this real?

**他真的不知道。**
Tā zhēn de bù zhīdào.
He really does not know.

## Usage Notes

真 as an intensifying adverb is placed directly before the adjective or verb it modifies, similar to 很 (very) and 非常 (extremely). It is stronger and more emotional than 很 and adds a sense of sincerity or surprise. The phrase 真的 (zhēn de) on its own means "really" and is often used for confirmation or emphasis mid-sentence.

## Collocations or Common Phrases

| Phrase | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 真好 | really good |
| 真的吗 | really? is that so? |
| 真漂亮 | truly beautiful |
| 说真的 | to be honest; speaking truthfully |
| 真正 | genuine; truly (more formal) |

## Memory Hook

真 contains the character 直 (straight) at its core — a straight-talking person tells you what is truly real.
