## 开始 (kāishǐ)

A word meaning "to start" or "to begin" as a verb, and "the beginning" or "the start" as a noun. It is one of the most common time-related words in Mandarin. Its antonym is 结束 (jiéshù), meaning "to end."

## Meanings

1. **[verb]** To start, to begin (an action or event).
2. **[noun]** The beginning, the start (of something).

## Example Sentences

**电影几点开始？**
Diànyǐng jǐ diǎn kāishǐ?
What time does the movie start?

**我们开始上课吧。**
Wǒmen kāishǐ shàngkè ba.
Let's start the class.

**从开始到现在，他一直很努力。**
Cóng kāishǐ dào xiànzài, tā yīzhí hěn nǔlì.
From the beginning until now, he has always worked hard.

**比赛已经开始了。**
Bǐsài yǐjīng kāishǐ le.
The competition has already started.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 开始工作 | to start work |
| 开始学习 | to start studying |
| 从……开始 | starting from... |
| 重新开始 | to start over |
| 开始到结束 | from start to finish |
| 比赛开始 | the game/competition starts |

## Memory Hook

开 means "to open" and 始 means "beginning" — opening something is the moment it begins. When you open a door, things start.
