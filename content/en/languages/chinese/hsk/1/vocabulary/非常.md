## 非常 (fēicháng)

非常 is an adverb meaning "extremely" or "exceptionally." It is a stronger intensifier than 很 (very) and is neutral in register — appropriate in both spoken and written Chinese.

## Meanings

1. **[adverb]** Extremely; exceptionally; very much so.

## Example Sentences

**她非常聪明。**
Tā fēicháng cōngmíng.
She is extremely intelligent.

**我非常喜欢中国菜。**
Wǒ fēicháng xǐhuan Zhōngguó cài.
I like Chinese food very much.

**这个问题非常重要。**
Zhège wèntí fēicháng zhòngyào.
This question is extremely important.

**今天的天气非常好。**
Jīntiān de tiānqì fēicháng hǎo.
The weather today is exceptionally good.

## Usage Notes

非常 precedes the adjective or verb it modifies and does not require 了 at the end of the sentence (unlike 太). Among the common intensifiers, the rough order of strength is: 有点儿 (a little) < 比较 (relatively) < 很 (very) < 非常 (extremely) < 太 (too/extremely). 非常 does not carry the negative "too much" implication that 太 sometimes does, so it is always safe to use for strong positive or neutral emphasis.

## Collocations or Common Phrases

| Phrase | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 非常好 | extremely good |
| 非常感谢 | thank you very much |
| 非常重要 | extremely important |
| 非常高兴 | extremely pleased |
| 非常漂亮 | exceptionally beautiful |

## Memory Hook

非常 literally means "not ordinary" (非 = not, 常 = ordinary/usual) — something that is not ordinary is extraordinary.
