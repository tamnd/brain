## 太 (tài)

太 is an adverb meaning "too" or "excessively." It expresses that a quality exceeds an acceptable or desired level, and in everyday speech it also functions as a strong intensifier expressing delight.

## Meanings

1. **[adverb]** Too; excessively (beyond an acceptable limit).
2. **[adverb]** Extremely; so very (in exclamatory sentences expressing strong feeling).

## Example Sentences

**这件衣服太贵了。**
Zhè jiàn yīfu tài guì le.
This piece of clothing is too expensive.

**今天太热了！**
Jīntiān tài rè le!
It is so hot today!

**你来得太晚了。**
Nǐ lái de tài wǎn le.
You came too late.

**太好了！谢谢你。**
Tài hǎo le! Xièxiè nǐ.
That is wonderful! Thank you.

## Usage Notes

太 almost always appears together with 了 at the end of the adjective phrase or sentence, forming the pattern 太 + adjective + 了. Omitting 了 makes the sentence sound incomplete. In negative contexts (something is too much of a bad thing) the meaning is critical, but in positive exclamations like 太好了, 太棒了, the meaning is purely enthusiastic. Do not use 太 in formal comparisons — use 太 only for absolute degree, not relative comparison.

## Collocations or Common Phrases

| Phrase | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 太好了 | wonderful; great |
| 太贵了 | too expensive |
| 太多了 | too many/much |
| 太难了 | too difficult |
| 太棒了 | awesome; fantastic |

## Memory Hook

太 looks like 大 (big) with an extra dot pushing it over the top — it has gone one dot too far.
