## 什么 (shénme)

The most versatile interrogative word in Chinese, used to ask "what" about anything: things, names, types, reasons, and more; it occupies the same sentence position as the noun it replaces.

## Meanings

1. **[pronoun]** What (asking about identity or type of a thing).
2. **[determiner]** What kind of (before a noun: 什么 + noun).
3. **[pronoun]** Anything, whatever (in a negative or open-ended context).

## Example Sentences

**你叫什么名字？**
Nǐ jiào shénme míngzi?
What is your name?

**这是什么？**
Zhè shì shénme?
What is this?

**你想买什么东西？**
Nǐ xiǎng mǎi shénme dōngxi?
What things do you want to buy?

**你在说什么？**
Nǐ zài shuō shénme?
What are you saying?

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 什么时候 (shénme shíhou) | when (what time) |
| 什么地方 (shénme dìfang) | where (what place) |
| 为什么 (wèishénme) | why (for what reason) |
| 什么样 (shénme yàng) | what kind/type |
| 没什么 (méi shénme) | nothing, never mind |
| 什么都 (shénme dōu) | everything, anything |

## Memory Hook

什么 sounds like "shen-muh." Imagine asking a confused "hm?" and stretching it out: "what IS that shen-muh thing?" Every time you are confused and need to ask what something is, reach for 什么.
