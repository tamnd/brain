## 去 (qù)

去 means "to go," indicating movement away from the speaker's current position toward a destination. It contrasts with 来 (lái), which means movement toward the speaker. Use 去 when you are heading away; use 来 when you or someone else is heading to where the speaker is.

## Meanings

1. **[verb]** To go (movement away from the speaker).

## Example Sentences

**我要去超市买东西。**
Wǒ yào qù chāoshì mǎi dōngxi.
I want to go to the supermarket to buy things.

**你去哪里？**
Nǐ qù nǎlǐ?
Where are you going?

**明天我们去公园吧。**
Míngtiān wǒmen qù gōngyuán ba.
Let's go to the park tomorrow.

**他昨天去了北京。**
Tā zuótiān qù le Běijīng.
He went to Beijing yesterday.

## Memory Hook

去 looks like a person walking out of a box. The person is departing, going away from where they were.
