## 错 (cuò)

An adjective meaning wrong, incorrect, or mistaken. It is the direct antonym of 对 (duì). It is also used as a noun to mean a mistake or error.

## Meanings

1. **[adjective]** Wrong, incorrect, mistaken.
2. **[noun]** A mistake, an error.

## Example Sentences

**这道题你做错了。**
Zhè dào tí nǐ zuò cuò le.
You did this problem incorrectly.

**对不起，我说错了，请原谅我。**
Duìbuqǐ, wǒ shuō cuò le, qǐng yuánliàng wǒ.
Sorry, I said something wrong, please forgive me.

**这不是我的错，是他先开始的。**
Zhè bú shì wǒ de cuò, shì tā xiān kāishǐ de.
This is not my mistake, he started it first.

**你走错路了，应该向左转。**
Nǐ zǒu cuò lù le, yīnggāi xiàng zuǒ zhuǎn.
You took the wrong road, you should turn left.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 做错 (zuò cuò) | do something wrong |
| 说错 (shuō cuò) | say something wrong |
| 走错 (zǒu cuò) | go the wrong way |
| 写错 (xiě cuò) | write incorrectly |
| 我的错 (wǒ de cuò) | my fault, my mistake |
| 不错 (bùcuò) | not bad, pretty good |

## Memory Hook

Note that 不错 (not wrong) is the common way to say "not bad" or "pretty good" in Chinese. Knowing 错 unlocks this everyday phrase immediately.
