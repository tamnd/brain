## 对 (duì)

A versatile word with two key uses. As an adjective, it means correct or right. As a preposition, it means towards, to, or regarding. Both uses are common at the HSK 1 level and appear in everyday conversation.

## Meanings

1. **[adjective]** Correct, right. The opposite of 错 (cuò).
2. **[preposition]** Towards, to, directed at, regarding.

## Example Sentences

**你说得对，这道题的答案是五。**
Nǐ shuō de duì, zhè dào tí de dá'àn shì wǔ.
You are right, the answer to this question is five.

**这个字写对了吗？**
Zhège zì xiě duì le ma?
Was this character written correctly?

**他对我很好。**
Tā duì wǒ hěn hǎo.
He treats me very well. (He is very good towards me.)

**你对这件事怎么看？**
Nǐ duì zhè jiàn shì zěnme kàn?
What do you think about this matter?

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 对了 (duì le) | that's right, correct |
| 说得对 (shuō de duì) | said correctly, you're right |
| 对不对 (duì bu duì) | is it correct or not? |
| 对…来说 (duì...lái shuō) | as for, from the perspective of |
| 对不起 (duìbuqǐ) | sorry (lit. cannot face someone) |
| 正确 vs 对 (zhèngquè vs duì) | formal vs. everyday word for "correct" |

## Memory Hook

When something is 对, it points in exactly the right direction. When it is a preposition, it still points, but at a person or thing.
