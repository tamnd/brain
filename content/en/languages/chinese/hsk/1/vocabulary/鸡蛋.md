## 鸡蛋 (jīdàn)

A chicken egg. 鸡 means "chicken" and 蛋 means "egg." While 蛋 alone can mean any egg, 鸡蛋 specifically refers to hen's eggs, which are the most common in Chinese cooking.

## Meanings

1. **[noun]** Chicken egg. The egg laid by a hen, used widely in Chinese cooking.

## Example Sentences

**我每天早上吃一个鸡蛋。**
Wǒ měitiān zǎoshang chī yī gè jīdàn.
I eat one egg every morning.

**冰箱里有鸡蛋吗？**
Bīngxiāng lǐ yǒu jīdàn ma?
Are there eggs in the refrigerator?

**她会做炒鸡蛋。**
Tā huì zuò chǎo jīdàn.
She knows how to make scrambled eggs.

**一个鸡蛋多少钱？**
Yī gè jīdàn duōshao qián?
How much is one egg?

## Memory Hook

鸡 is "chicken" and 蛋 is "egg." A 鸡蛋 is simply a "chicken egg." The character 蛋 has a bug (虫) component at the bottom, an old reference to small creatures hatching from eggs.
