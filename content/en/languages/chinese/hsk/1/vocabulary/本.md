## 本 (běn)

A measure word used specifically for books, notebooks, and other bound volumes. 本 also means "root," "origin," or "this" in other contexts, but as a measure word it always refers to bound materials.

## Meanings

1. **[measure word]** Classifier for books, notebooks, and bound volumes.
2. **[noun]** Root, origin, fundamental. Used in other compound words.

## What 本 counts

本 is used with:

- 书 (shū): books — 一本书 (one book)
- 杂志 (zázhì): magazines — 一本杂志 (one magazine)
- 词典 (cídiǎn): dictionaries — 一本词典 (one dictionary)
- 笔记本 (bǐjìběn): notebooks — 一本笔记本 (one notebook)
- 漫画 (mànhuà): comic books — 一本漫画 (one comic book)

## Example Sentences

**我有一本汉语书。**
Wǒ yǒu yī běn Hànyǔ shū.
I have one Chinese language book.

**她买了两本小说。**
Tā mǎile liǎng běn xiǎoshuō.
She bought two novels.

**这本词典很有用。**
Zhè běn cídiǎn hěn yǒuyòng.
This dictionary is very useful.

**图书馆里有很多本书。**
Túshūguǎn lǐ yǒu hěn duō běn shū.
There are many books in the library.

## Common Learner Mistake

Do not use 个 for books. Saying 一个书 is incorrect. The correct measure word for books and bound publications is always 本. The same applies to notebooks and magazines.
