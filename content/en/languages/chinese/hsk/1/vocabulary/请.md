## 请 (qǐng)

A versatile word meaning "please" when used as a polite adverb before a verb, and "to invite" or "to treat someone to something" when used as a main verb. It is one of the most common politeness words in Mandarin.

## Meanings

1. **[adverb]** Please (used to make a polite request).
2. **[verb]** To invite someone; to treat someone to a meal or drink.

## Example Sentences

**请坐，不要客气。**
Qǐng zuò, bùyào kèqi.
Please sit down, make yourself at home.

**请问，洗手间在哪里？**
Qǐngwèn, xǐshǒujiān zài nǎlǐ?
Excuse me, where is the restroom?

**今天我请你吃饭。**
Jīntiān wǒ qǐng nǐ chīfàn.
Today I will treat you to a meal.

**他请我去他家玩。**
Tā qǐng wǒ qù tā jiā wán.
He invited me to come to his home.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 请坐 | please sit down |
| 请问 | excuse me / may I ask |
| 请进 | please come in |
| 请客 | to treat someone, to be the host |
| 请吃饭 | to treat someone to a meal |
| 请帮我 | please help me |

## Memory Hook

Imagine a host bowing at the door saying "qǐng" — "please, come in!" The word always carries warmth and courtesy.
