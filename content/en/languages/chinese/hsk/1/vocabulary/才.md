## 才 (cái)

才 is a timing adverb meaning "not until," "only then," or "just now." It signals that the action happened later, slower, or with more effort than expected.

## Meanings

1. **[adverb]** Not until; only after (implying lateness or difficulty).
2. **[adverb]** Just now; only a moment ago.
3. **[adverb]** Only; merely (limiting quantity or degree).

## Example Sentences

**他九点才到。**
Tā jiǔ diǎn cái dào.
He didn't arrive until nine o'clock.

**我昨天才知道这件事。**
Wǒ zuótiān cái zhīdào zhè jiàn shì.
I only found out about this yesterday.

**她学了三年才会说汉语。**
Tā xué le sān nián cái huì shuō Hànyǔ.
She studied for three years before she could finally speak Chinese.

**这里才有五个人。**
Zhèlǐ cái yǒu wǔ gè rén.
There are only five people here.

## Usage Notes

才 contrasts with 就. 就 suggests something happened sooner than expected (praise or efficiency), while 才 suggests it happened later than expected (mild criticism or effort). In sentences with 才, do not add 了 after the verb to mark completion — the lateness feeling and 了 conflict. 才 can also mean "only/merely" when limiting a number or extent.

## Collocations or Common Phrases

| Phrase | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 才来 | just arrived / didn't come until now |
| 才知道 | only just found out |
| 这才对 | now that's correct |
| 才一个人 | only one person |
| 学完才走 | won't leave until finished studying |

## Memory Hook

才 sounds like "tie" — you have been tied up with obstacles, and you only managed to break free just now.
