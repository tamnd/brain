## 条 (tiáo)

A measure word used for long, narrow, or flexible objects and for certain categories of things like trousers, fish, rivers, and roads. 条 captures the idea of something elongated or strip-like.

## Meanings

1. **[measure word]** Classifier for long, narrow, or flexible objects.
2. **[noun]** Strip, piece, item. Used in compound words like 面条 (noodles).

## What 条 counts

条 is used with:

- 鱼 (yú): fish — 一条鱼 (one fish)
- 裤子 (kùzi): trousers — 一条裤子 (one pair of trousers)
- 路 (lù): road — 一条路 (one road)
- 河 (hé): river — 一条河 (one river)
- 消息 (xiāoxi): news/message — 一条消息 (one piece of news)

## Example Sentences

**我买了一条鱼。**
Wǒ mǎile yī tiáo yú.
I bought a fish.

**他穿了一条新裤子。**
Tā chuānle yī tiáo xīn kùzi.
He is wearing a new pair of trousers.

**这条路很长。**
Zhè tiáo lù hěn cháng.
This road is very long.

**前面有一条河。**
Qiánmiàn yǒu yī tiáo hé.
There is a river ahead.

## Common Learner Mistake

Use 条 for trousers and fish, not 件 or 个. A very common error is saying 一件裤子 (wrong) instead of 一条裤子 (correct). The logic is that trousers are long with two leg "strips," fitting the 条 category.
