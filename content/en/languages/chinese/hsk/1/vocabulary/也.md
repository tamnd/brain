## 也 (yě)

An adverb meaning "also" or "too." 也 adds the same quality, action, or condition to the subject being talked about. It always comes before the verb or adjective, never at the end of a sentence.

## Meanings

1. **[adverb]** Also, too. Indicates that the subject shares the same attribute or action as something previously mentioned.

## 也 vs. 都: the key contrast

- **也** applies to each subject individually, one at a time. It says "this person/thing ALSO does X."
- **都** applies to all subjects collectively. It says "ALL of them do X."

| Sentence | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| 我喜欢茶，他也喜欢茶。 | I like tea, and he also likes tea. (individually) |
| 我们都喜欢茶。 | We all like tea. (collectively) |

也 focuses on adding a subject to a statement. 都 focuses on covering all subjects at once.

## Example Sentences

**我喜欢咖啡，他也喜欢咖啡。**
Wǒ xǐhuān kāfēi, tā yě xǐhuān kāfēi.
I like coffee, and he also likes coffee.

**她会说汉语，也会说英语。**
Tā huì shuō Hànyǔ, yě huì shuō Yīngyǔ.
She can speak Chinese, and she can also speak English.

**我也不知道。**
Wǒ yě bù zhīdào.
I don't know either.

**这家饭馆的菜很好，价格也不贵。**
Zhè jiā fànguǎn de cài hěn hǎo, jiàgé yě bù guì.
The food at this restaurant is good, and the price is also not expensive.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 也是 | is also |
| 也有 | also have / there is also |
| 也不 | also not / not either |
| 也可以 | is also okay / can also |
| 也好 | that's fine too |

## Memory Hook

也 is about one subject joining a club. "I like X. You also like X." Each sentence focuses on one person at a time. Position is critical: 也 always comes right before the verb, never at the sentence end.
