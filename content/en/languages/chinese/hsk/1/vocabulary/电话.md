## 电话 (diànhuà)

A noun meaning telephone or phone call. It combines 电 (electricity) and 话 (speech, words). In modern Chinese, 电话 most often refers to the act of calling or a phone call, rather than the physical device. For the device in your pocket, use 手机 (shǒujī). The phrase 打电话 (make a phone call) is extremely common.

## Meanings

1. **[noun]** Telephone, phone (especially landline).
2. **[noun]** Phone call.

## Example Sentences

**我给他打了一个电话，但他没接。**
Wǒ gěi tā dǎle yīgè diànhuà, dàn tā méi jiē.
I gave him a phone call, but he did not pick up.

**你的电话号码是多少？**
Nǐ de diànhuà hàomǎ shì duōshao?
What is your phone number?

**她正在打电话，等一下再说。**
Tā zhèngzài dǎ diànhuà, děng yīxià zài shuō.
She is on a phone call right now, let us talk in a moment.

**家里有一部电话，但我们都用手机。**
Jiā lǐ yǒu yī bù diànhuà, dàn wǒmen dōu yòng shǒujī.
There is a landline at home, but we all use mobile phones.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 打电话 (dǎ diànhuà) | make a phone call |
| 接电话 (jiē diànhuà) | answer the phone |
| 电话号码 (diànhuà hàomǎ) | phone number |
| 挂电话 (guà diànhuà) | hang up the phone |
| 来电话 (lái diànhuà) | receive a call |

## Memory Hook

电 (electricity) plus 话 (speech): electricity carrying your words. That is what a telephone does. Compare with 手机 (hand machine), which is the physical device you carry.
