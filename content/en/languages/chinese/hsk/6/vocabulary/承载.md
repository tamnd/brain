## 承载 (chéngzài)

承载 means to carry or bear a load — both physically (a bridge bearing weight) and metaphorically (a culture carrying history, a person bearing responsibility). The metaphorical use is particularly rich and common in Chinese literary and academic language, where objects, places, and institutions are said to 承载 historical memory, cultural meaning, or social expectations.

## Meanings
1. **[v]** to carry; to bear (a physical load or weight)
2. **[v]** to carry; to embody; to be the vehicle for (meaning, memory, history, expectation)

## Example Sentences
**这座古城承载着数百年的历史记忆与文化积淀，不可轻易拆毁。** Zhè zuò gǔchéng chéngzàizhe shùbǎi nián de lìshǐ jìyì yǔ wénhuà jīdiàn, bù kě qīngyì chāihuǐ. This ancient city carries centuries of historical memory and cultural accumulation — it cannot be easily demolished.

**青年一代承载着民族复兴的历史使命，肩负时代赋予的重大责任。** Qīngnián yīdài chéngzàizhe mínzú fùxīng de lìshǐ shǐmìng, jiānfù shídài fùyǔ de zhòngdà zérèn. The younger generation bears the historical mission of national rejuvenation, shouldering the great responsibilities bestowed by the era.

**这条铁路不仅承载着物资运输，更承载着边疆地区对发展的深切期盼。** Zhè tiáo tiělù bùjǐn chéngzàizhe wùzī yùnshū, gèng chéngzàizhe biānjiāng dìqū duì fāzhǎn de shēnqiè qīpàn. This railway carries not only the transport of goods, but also the deep hopes of border regions for development.

**大桥的设计承载能力经过严格测算，可安全承受单向六车道的满载通行。** Dàqiáo de shèjì chéngzài nénglì jīngguò yángé cèsuàn, kě ānquán chéngshòu dānxiàng liù chēdào de mǎnzài tōngxíng. The bridge's designed load-bearing capacity has been rigorously calculated and can safely handle full traffic flow on six lanes in one direction.

**语言是文化的载体，每一种濒危语言的消失都意味着一种独特世界观的失落。** Yǔyán shì wénhuà de zàitǐ, měi yī zhǒng bīnyù yǔyán de xiāoshī dōu yìwèizhe yī zhǒng dútè shìjièguān de shīluò. Language is the vehicle of culture — the disappearance of every endangered language means the loss of a unique worldview.

**文学作品之所以能够跨越时空，是因为它承载着普世的人类情感与道德关怀。** Wénxué zuòpǐn zhīsuǒyǐ nénggòu kuàyuè shíkōng, shì yīnwèi tā chéngzàizhe pǔshì de rénlèi qínggǎn yǔ dàodé guānhuái. Literary works can transcend time and space because they carry universal human emotions and moral concerns.

## Usage Notes

承载 is a highly expressive metaphor in Chinese. While 承受 (chéngshòu) means to endure or tolerate (often pain or pressure), 承载 implies actively carrying something of value or significance. The distinction is important: 承受痛苦 (to endure pain) vs. 承载历史 (to carry history).

The word is extremely common in political speeches, literary writing, and cultural commentary. The phrase X承载着Y的期望/希望/记忆 (X carries the hopes/memories of Y) is a template worth mastering. In engineering, 承载能力 (load-bearing capacity) is a technical term. 承载 can also be used for digital media: 这个平台承载了大量用户生成的内容 (this platform carries a large volume of user-generated content).

## Memory Hook

承 (to receive, to take on — hands held up in offering) + 载 (to carry, to load): "receiving something and carrying it forward" — the responsibility of being a vessel for something meaningful.
