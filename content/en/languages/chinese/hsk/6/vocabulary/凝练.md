## 凝练 (nínliàn)

凝练 describes language or expression that is both concise and dense with meaning — each word carrying concentrated depth. As a verb, it means to distill or condense ideas into compact form. As an adjective, it describes writing that is not merely brief but rich with compressed meaning. It is a high praise in Chinese literary criticism, suggesting both economy and profundity.

## Meanings
1. **[adj]** concise and profound; refined and dense with meaning
2. **[v]** to distill; to condense into concentrated, meaningful form

## Example Sentences
**鲁迅的小说语言极为凝练，寥寥数语便能勾勒出一个时代的精神困境。** Lǔ Xùn de xiǎoshuō yǔyán jíwéi nínliàn, liáoliáo shùyǔ biàn néng gōulè chū yīgè shídài de jīngshén kùnjìng. Lu Xun's novel language is extremely refined and condensed — a few words can sketch the spiritual predicament of an entire era.

**他花了数年时间将田野调研的发现凝练成一套普适性理论框架。** Tā huāle shù nián shíjiān jiāng tiányě diàoyán de fāxiàn nínliàn chéng yī tào pǔshìxìng lǐlùn kuàngjià. He spent several years distilling the findings of field research into a universally applicable theoretical framework.

**好的学术摘要应该凝练而不失准确，在有限篇幅内传达最大的信息量。** Hǎo de xuéshù zhāiyào yīnggāi nínliàn ér bù shī zhǔnquè, zài yǒuxiàn piānfú nèi chuándá zuìdà de xìnxī liàng. A good academic abstract should be concise without sacrificing accuracy, conveying maximum information within limited space.

**这段碑文凝练地概括了这位历史人物一生的功过是非。** Zhè duàn bēiwén nínliàn de gàikuòle zhè wèi lìshǐ rénwù yīshēng de gōngguò shìfēi. This inscription concisely and densely summarized the merits and faults of this historical figure's life.

**将复杂的哲学思想凝练成通俗易懂的语言，是哲学普及工作的核心挑战。** Jiāng fùzá de zhéxué sīxiǎng nínliàn chéng tōngsú yìdǒng de yǔyán, shì zhéxué pǔjí gōngzuò de héxīn tiǎozhàn. Distilling complex philosophical ideas into accessible language is the core challenge of popularizing philosophy.

**他的诗风以凝练著称，常以十四字道尽人生百态的沧桑感悟。** Tā de shī fēng yǐ nínliàn zhùchēng, cháng yǐ shísì zì dàojìn rénshēng bǎitài de cāngsāng gǎnwù. His poetic style is renowned for its condensed depth — often using only fourteen characters to express the vicissitudes and insights of a lifetime.

## Usage Notes

凝练 is almost exclusively a literary and aesthetic term. It is the highest praise for written or spoken language in Chinese literary criticism, suggesting that words are not just few but weighted with depth. It is commonly used to describe classical Chinese poetry, great prose, and distilled academic writing.

The key distinction from 精炼 (jīngliàn): 精炼 means refined/purified (free of excess), while 凝练 implies condensed profundity — the meaning is concentrated and rich, not just brief. A 精炼 sentence removes the unnecessary; a 凝练 sentence packs maximum meaning into minimal words. In practice, great writing can be both, but the emphasis is different.

## Memory Hook

凝 (to congeal, to concentrate — like liquid condensing) + 练 (to refine, to train): "concentrating distilled essence into a small, dense form" — the beauty of few words bearing great weight.
