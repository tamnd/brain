## 融合 (rónghé)

融合 refers to the process of blending, merging, or integrating different elements — cultures, technologies, ideas, or communities — into a unified whole. It emphasizes mutual assimilation and the creation of something new from the combination, rather than mere coexistence.

## Meanings
1. **[v]** to fuse, to integrate, to blend (elements into a unified whole)
2. **[n]** fusion, integration (the state or process of merging)

## Example Sentences
**中西医学的融合是现代医学发展中最具挑战性、也最具潜力的方向之一。** Zhōng xī yīxué de rónghé shì xiàndài yīxué fāzhǎn zhōng zuì jù tiǎozhàn xìng, yě zuì jù qiánlì de fāngxiàng zhī yī. The integration of Chinese and Western medicine is one of the most challenging and promising directions in modern medical development.

**这座城市因移民群体的汇聚而形成了独特的多元文化融合景观。** Zhè zuò chéngshì yīn yímín qúntǐ de huìjù ér xíngchéng le dútè de duōyuán wénhuà rónghé jǐngguān. This city has formed a unique multicultural fusion landscape through the convergence of immigrant communities.

**科技与艺术的融合正在催生一批颠覆传统美学边界的新型创作形式。** Kējì yǔ yìshù de rónghé zhèngzài cuīshēng yī pī diānfù chuántǒng měixué biānjìe de xīnxíng chuàngzuò xíngshì. The fusion of technology and art is generating a new wave of creative forms that subvert traditional aesthetic boundaries.

**民族融合是中华文明多元一体格局形成的历史基础。** Mínzú rónghé shì Zhōnghuá wénmíng duōyuán yītǐ géjú xíngchéng de lìshǐ jīchǔ. Ethnic integration is the historical foundation for the formation of the diverse yet unified pattern of Chinese civilization.

**线上与线下融合的商业模式正在重塑零售业的竞争格局。** Xiànshàng yǔ xiànxià rónghé de shāngyè móshì zhèngzài chóngsù língshòu yè de jìngzhēng géjú. The online-offline fusion business model is reshaping the competitive landscape of the retail industry.

**这部作品成功地将戏剧张力与哲学沉思融合为一体，令观众叹为观止。** Zhè bù zuòpǐn chénggōng de jiāng xìjù zhānglì yǔ zhéxué chénsī rónghé wéi yītǐ, lìng guānzhòng tàn wéi guān zhǐ. This work successfully fuses dramatic tension with philosophical contemplation into a unified whole, leaving audiences in awe.

## Usage Notes

融合 is a more active and transformative term than 结合 (jiéhé, to combine/join): 结合 can simply mean two things working together without full merging, while 融合 implies that the elements have genuinely blended and lost their separateness. Compare also 整合 (zhěnghé, to integrate/consolidate), which is more organizational and systematic.

In social and cultural contexts, 融合 often has a positive connotation, suggesting the creation of a richer whole. In demographic or political contexts, however, the term can be contested, as it may imply assimilation at the expense of distinct identities.

## Memory Hook

融 (to melt/dissolve) + 合 (to combine) — 融合 is melting together, like metals fusing in a forge to become something new and stronger.
