## 打电话 (dǎ diànhuà)

打电话 is a verb-object phrase meaning "to make a phone call." 打 (to hit/do) is used with many communication and activity verbs in Chinese: 打电话 (phone call), 打篮球 (play basketball), 打招呼 (greet). The person you call is introduced with 给 (gěi): 给他打电话 (call him).

## Meanings

1. **[verb phrase]** To make a phone call; to call someone. Used for initiating a telephone conversation.

## Example Sentences

**我可以用你的手机打电话吗？**
Wǒ kěyǐ yòng nǐ de shǒujī dǎ diànhuà ma?
May I use your phone to make a call?

**他刚才给我打电话了。**
Tā gāngcái gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà le.
He called me just now.

**如果有问题，请给我打电话。**
Rúguǒ yǒu wèntí, qǐng gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà.
If there is a problem, please call me.

**我每天晚上都给父母打电话。**
Wǒ měitiān wǎnshang dōu gěi fùmǔ dǎ diànhuà.
I call my parents every evening.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 给...打电话 (gěi...dǎ diànhuà) | to call someone |
| 接电话 (jiē diànhuà) | to answer the phone |
| 挂电话 (guà diànhuà) | to hang up the phone |
| 打错电话 (dǎ cuò diànhuà) | to call the wrong number |
| 手机打电话 (shǒujī dǎ diànhuà) | to call by mobile phone |

## Memory Hook

打 literally means "to hit" or "to do." In Chinese, you "hit" a phone call just like you "hit" a game of basketball. Whenever you take action to start something physical or communicative, you use 打.
