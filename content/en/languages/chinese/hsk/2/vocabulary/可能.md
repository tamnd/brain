## 可能 (kěnéng)

可能 expresses possibility. It works as an adverb meaning "maybe" (placed before a verb or adjective) or as an adjective meaning "possible" (used with 有/没有). Both uses are common.

## Meanings

1. **[adverb]** Maybe, possibly (modifying a verb or adjective: 可能来 "may come").
2. **[adjective]** Possible (used with 有/没有: 有可能 "there is a possibility").

## Example Sentences

**他可能明天来。**
Tā kěnéng míngtiān lái.
He may come tomorrow.

**这件事有可能发生。**
Zhè jiàn shì yǒu kěnéng fāshēng.
This thing may possibly happen.

**你可能不知道这件事。**
Nǐ kěnéng bù zhīdào zhè jiàn shì.
You probably don't know about this.

**没有可能，他不会同意的。**
Méiyǒu kěnéng, tā bù huì tóngyì de.
It's not possible; he won't agree.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 有可能 | there is a possibility |
| 没有可能 | impossible, no possibility |
| 可能性 | possibility (as a noun) |
| 尽可能 | as much as possible |
| 不可能 | impossible |

## Memory Hook

可 (can/permitted) + 能 (able): when something "can be able" to happen, it is possible. Stack both words that mean "can" and you get "maybe."
