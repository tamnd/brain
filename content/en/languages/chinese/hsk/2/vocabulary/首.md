## 首 (shǒu)

首 is the measure word for songs (歌) and poems (诗). It counts each complete musical or poetic composition. While 首 literally means "head" (as in 首都 — capital city), as a measure word it is reserved for songs and poetry. Use 首 where English uses "a song" or "a poem."

## Meanings

1. **[mw]** Song — counts individual songs as complete pieces.
2. **[mw]** Poem — counts individual poems as complete literary works.

## Example Sentences

**我想学这首歌。**
Wǒ xiǎng xué zhè shǒu gē.
I want to learn this song.

**她唱了一首很好听的歌。**
Tā chàng le yī shǒu hěn hǎotīng de gē.
She sang a very beautiful song.

**这首诗是李白写的。**
Zhè shǒu shī shì Lǐ Bái xiě de.
This poem was written by Li Bai.

**请给我推荐一首中文歌曲。**
Qǐng gěi wǒ tuījiàn yī shǒu Zhōngwén gēqǔ.
Please recommend a Chinese song to me.

## Memory Hook

首 means "head" — a song or poem is the "head" of artistic expression, leading the emotions of its listeners.
