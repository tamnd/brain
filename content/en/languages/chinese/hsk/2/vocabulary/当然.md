## 当然 (dāngrán)

当然 expresses that something is obvious, expected, or goes without saying. It functions like "of course," "naturally," or "certainly" in English and typically appears at the start of a sentence or before a verb.

## Meanings

1. **[adverb]** Of course; naturally; certainly. Indicates that the statement is self-evident or expected.

## Example Sentences

**你能来吗？— 当然能！**
Nǐ néng lái ma? — Dāngrán néng!
Can you come? — Of course I can!

**当然，我很喜欢吃中国菜。**
Dāngrán, wǒ hěn xǐhuan chī Zhōngguó cài.
Of course, I love eating Chinese food.

**学好一门语言当然需要时间。**
Xué hǎo yī mén yǔyán dāngrán xūyào shíjiān.
Of course, learning a language well takes time.

**他当然不知道，你又没告诉他。**
Tā dāngrán bù zhīdào, nǐ yòu méi gàosu tā.
Of course he doesn't know — you never told him.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 当然了 (dāngrán le) | of course! (emphatic) |
| 当然可以 (dāngrán kěyǐ) | of course you can |
| 当然不 (dāngrán bù) | of course not |
| 这当然 (zhè dāngrán) | this is of course... |
| 那当然 (nà dāngrán) | naturally; that goes without saying |

## Memory Hook

当 means "at that moment/as it is" and 然 means "so/thus." 当然 literally means "as it naturally is" — of course things are this way, it is obvious.
