## 还是 (háishì)

还是 is used to present two alternatives in a question, asking which one applies. This is a KEY learner confusion: 还是 belongs in questions, while 或者 (huòzhě) belongs in statements. Never swap them.

## Meanings

1. **[conjunction]** Or (used in questions offering a choice between A and B).
2. **[adverb]** Still, nevertheless (in a different usage: 还是来吧, "you should still come").

## Example Sentences

**你喝茶还是咖啡？**
Nǐ hē chá háishì kāfēi?
Do you drink tea or coffee?

**你去还是不去？**
Nǐ qù háishì bù qù?
Are you going or not?

**这是你的还是他的？**
Zhè shì nǐ de háishì tā de?
Is this yours or his?

**你喜欢夏天还是冬天？**
Nǐ xǐhuān xiàtiān háishì dōngtiān?
Do you prefer summer or winter?

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| A还是B | A or B (in a question) |
| 还是你来 | you had better come (adverb use) |
| 还是算了 | forget it, better not |
| 还是不错 | still pretty good |
| 你说还是我说 | you speak or I speak |

## Memory Hook

还 also means "still," so picture still asking: "Is it A, or is it still B?" That "still questioning" feeling signals 还是 lives in questions.
