## 可以 (kěyǐ)

可以 expresses permission ("you may") or general possibility ("it is okay to"). Key contrast: 能 (néng) expresses physical ability or learned skill ("I can swim"), while 可以 expresses whether something is allowed or feasible ("you can leave now").

## Meanings

1. **[modal verb]** May, can (permission: you are allowed to).
2. **[modal verb]** Can, be able to (possibility: it is feasible).

## Example Sentences

**你可以在这里停车。**
Nǐ kěyǐ zài zhèlǐ tíng chē.
You may park here.

**这里可以拍照吗？**
Zhèlǐ kěyǐ pāizhào ma?
Is it okay to take photos here?

**我现在可以走了吗？**
Wǒ xiànzài kěyǐ zǒu le ma?
May I leave now?

**这个问题可以用两种方法解决。**
Zhège wèntí kěyǐ yòng liǎng zhǒng fāngfǎ jiějué.
This problem can be solved in two ways.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 不可以 | not allowed, cannot |
| 可以吗 | is it okay? |
| 可以用 | can use |
| 可以走了 | can go now |
| 可以帮我吗 | can you help me? |

## Memory Hook

可 (permitted) + 以 (by means of): something is 可以 when it is permitted by the situation. Permission first, ability second. For pure ability, reach for 能.
