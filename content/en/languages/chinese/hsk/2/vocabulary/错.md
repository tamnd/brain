## 错 (cuò)

错 means wrong or mistaken. It is used to describe an incorrect answer, action, or situation. In colloquial speech, 不错 (bú cuò) is a common expression meaning "not bad" or "pretty good," which is its own useful phrase to learn alongside 错.

## Meanings

1. **[adjective]** Wrong; incorrect; mistaken. Describes something that does not match the expected or correct version.
2. **[noun]** Mistake; error. Used in expressions about faults and corrections.

## Example Sentences

**这个答案是错的。**
Zhège dá'àn shì cuò de.
This answer is wrong.

**对不起，我说错了。**
Duìbuqǐ, wǒ shuō cuò le.
Sorry, I said it incorrectly.

**这道菜味道不错！**
Zhè dào cài wèidào bú cuò!
This dish tastes pretty good!

**你没错，是我的问题。**
Nǐ méi cuò, shì wǒ de wèntí.
You are not wrong; it is my problem.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 不错 (bú cuò) | not bad; pretty good |
| 说错 (shuō cuò) | to say something wrong |
| 走错 (zǒu cuò) | to go the wrong way |
| 做错 (zuò cuò) | to do something wrong |
| 犯错 (fàn cuò) | to make a mistake |

## Memory Hook

错 has the gold/metal radical (钅) on the left, suggesting something refined that has gone off-track. When precious metal is miscast, it comes out 错 (wrong). Even valuable things can go wrong.
