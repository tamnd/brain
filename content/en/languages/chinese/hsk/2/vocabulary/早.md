## 早 (zǎo)

Means early or pertaining to the morning. Used as an adjective describing time, as an adverb modifying a verb, and as an informal morning greeting (早！ = Morning!). The opposite is 晚 (wǎn, late).

## Meanings

1. **[adj]** Early, not late.
2. **[adv]** Early on, long ago already.
3. **[greeting]** Good morning (short for 早上好).

## Example Sentences

**你今天来得很早。**
Nǐ jīntiān lái de hěn zǎo.
You arrived very early today.

**我早就知道这件事了。**
Wǒ zǎo jiù zhīdào zhè jiàn shì le.
I knew about this a long time ago.

**早！你吃早饭了吗？**
Zǎo! Nǐ chī zǎofàn le ma?
Morning! Have you had breakfast?

**要早点睡，明天还要上班。**
Yào zǎo diǎn shuì, míngtiān hái yào shàngbān.
Go to sleep earlier. You still have work tomorrow.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 早上 | morning |
| 早饭 | breakfast |
| 早点 | earlier, a bit early |
| 早已 | already long ago |
| 早睡早起 | sleep early and rise early |

## Memory Hook

早 is made of 日 (sun) over 十 (ten): the sun has barely risen past the ten-mark — it is still 早, early morning.
