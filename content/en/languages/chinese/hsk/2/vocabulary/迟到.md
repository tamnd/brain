## 迟到 (chídào)

迟到 is a verb-object compound: 迟 (chí) means "late/slow" and 到 (dào) means "to arrive." Together they express arriving or showing up after the expected time. Because it is a verb-object compound, time expressions and 了 attach after the whole word.

## Meanings

1. **[verb]** To be late; to arrive late. Used for being late to a scheduled event, class, meeting, or appointment.

## Example Sentences

**对不起，我迟到了。**
Duìbuqǐ, wǒ chídào le.
Sorry, I am late.

**不要迟到，会议八点开始。**
Bùyào chídào, huìyì bā diǎn kāishǐ.
Don't be late; the meeting starts at eight.

**他今天迟到了三十分钟。**
Tā jīntiān chídào le sānshí fēnzhōng.
He was thirty minutes late today.

**你为什么迟到？因为堵车了。**
Nǐ wèishénme chídào? Yīnwèi dǔchē le.
Why were you late? Because of a traffic jam.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 经常迟到 (jīngcháng chídào) | to be late often |
| 迟到了 (chídào le) | was/arrived late |
| 不要迟到 (bùyào chídào) | don't be late |
| 迟到的原因 (chídào de yuányīn) | the reason for being late |
| 无故迟到 (wúgù chídào) | to be late without reason |

## Memory Hook

迟 looks heavy and slow, like it is dragging its feet. 到 means to arrive. Put them together and you have someone who dragged their feet and arrived late.
