## 从 (cóng)

从 marks the starting point of a journey, a period of time, or a sequence of events. It is placed before the starting location or time, and the destination or end point usually follows later in the sentence.

## Meanings

1. **[preposition]** From (a place). Marks the point of departure or origin in space.
2. **[preposition]** From; since (a time). Marks the starting point of a time period.

## Example Sentences

**我从北京来。**
Wǒ cóng Běijīng lái.
I come from Beijing.

**从这里到学校要走二十分钟。**
Cóng zhèlǐ dào xuéxiào yào zǒu èrshí fēnzhōng.
It takes twenty minutes to walk from here to school.

**他从早上九点工作到晚上六点。**
Tā cóng zǎoshang jiǔ diǎn gōngzuò dào wǎnshang liù diǎn.
He works from nine in the morning to six in the evening.

**从现在开始，我要每天练习汉语。**
Cóng xiànzài kāishǐ, wǒ yào měitiān liànxí Hànyǔ.
Starting from now, I will practice Chinese every day.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 从...到... (cóng...dào...) | from...to... |
| 从来 (cónglái) | ever; always (usually negative: 从来不/没) |
| 从小 (cóng xiǎo) | since childhood |
| 从现在开始 (cóng xiànzài kāishǐ) | starting from now |
| 从哪里来 (cóng nǎlǐ lái) | where do you come from? |

## Memory Hook

从 is made of two 人 (person) characters — one person following another. Picture someone following a path from a starting point: that follower is always moving 从 (from) somewhere toward somewhere else.
