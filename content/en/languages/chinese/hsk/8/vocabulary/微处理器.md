## 微处理器 (wēi chǔlǐqì)

微处理器 is a miniaturized digital computer central processing unit fabricated on a single semiconductor integrated circuit. It executes stored instructions and is the computational core of computers, smartphones, embedded systems, and virtually all modern digital devices. Microprocessor performance and architecture are central topics in computer science and hardware engineering.

## Meanings

1. **[noun]** microprocessor; a single-chip integrated circuit that performs the functions of a central processing unit

## Usage Notes

微处理器 is the formal technical term for microprocessor. In everyday Chinese, 芯片 (chip) or 处理器 (processor) are more commonly used colloquially, while 微处理器 is preferred in academic papers, technical specifications, and engineering documentation. The term is distinct from 微控制器 (microcontroller, MCU), which integrates processor, memory, and peripherals on a single chip for embedded applications.

Key architectural concepts include 指令集架构 (ISA), 流水线 (pipeline), 缓存 (cache), and 主频 (clock frequency). In Chinese national strategy documents, 高端微处理器 (high-end microprocessors) represent a critical technology for 自主可控 (autonomous controllability).

## Memory Hook

微 (micro/tiny) + 处理器 (processor) — a tiny processor that fits on a chip yet drives an entire computer.
