## 集成电路 (jíchéng diànlù)

集成电路 is an electronic circuit in which all components — transistors, resistors, capacitors, and their interconnections — are fabricated on a single semiconductor substrate, typically silicon. Invented in the late 1950s, integrated circuits enabled the miniaturization and mass production of complex electronic systems and are the foundation of modern computing and telecommunications.

## Meanings

1. **[noun]** integrated circuit (IC); a miniaturized electronic circuit fabricated on a single semiconductor chip

**集成电路的发明彻底改变了电子技术的发展轨迹。** Jíchéng diànlù de fāmíng chèdǐ gǎibiànle diànzǐ jìshù de fāzhǎn guǐjì. The invention of the integrated circuit fundamentally transformed the trajectory of electronic technology development.

## Usage Notes

集成电路 is abbreviated as 集电 in some contexts but more commonly abbreviated as IC in mixed Chinese-English technical writing. The scale of integration is described by terms such as 小规模集成电路 (SSI), 中规模集成电路 (MSI), 大规模集成电路 (LSI), 超大规模集成电路 (VLSI), and 极大规模集成电路 (ULSI).

In Chinese industrial policy, 集成电路 and 芯片 (chip) are often used interchangeably in policy documents, reflecting their strategic importance. The 国家集成电路产业投资基金 (National IC Industry Investment Fund) is a major state vehicle for funding domestic chip development.

## Memory Hook

集成 (integrated/assembled together) + 电路 (electric circuit) — all the circuits assembled together onto one tiny piece of silicon.
