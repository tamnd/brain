HSK 9 is the **highest level** in the HSK 3.0 system. It is part of the **HSK 7–9 Advanced band** (same ~10,877-word pool) but assessed at maximum depth and complexity.

HSK 9 specifically requires:
- Full command of literary and archaic vocabulary
- Professional translation proficiency
- Oral defense: present and defend a research topic or complex argument in Chinese
- Production of near-native level written texts across all registers

Very few non-native speakers reach HSK 9. It is comparable to C2+ — a level that typically requires years of living and working in a Chinese-speaking environment.
