## 公平的 (gōngpíng de)

公平的 describes something that is fair, just, and impartial -- where everyone is treated equally according to the same rules or standards. It is used across legal, social, educational, and economic contexts.

## Meanings
1. **[adj]** fair; equitable; just; impartial; treating everyone equally

## Example Sentences
**建立公平的竞争环境是市场经济的核心原则。** Jiànlì gōngpíng de jìngzhēng huánjìng shì shìchǎng jīngjì de héxīn yuánzé. Establishing a fair competitive environment is a core principle of the market economy.

**公平的教育机会对每个孩子的发展至关重要。** Gōngpíng de jiàoyù jīhuì duì měi gè háizi de fāzhǎn zhìguān zhòngyào. Equal educational opportunities are crucial to the development of every child.

**裁判应该以公平的态度对待每一位运动员。** Cáipàn yīnggāi yǐ gōngpíng de tàidù duìdài měi yī wèi yùndòng yuán. Referees should treat every athlete with fairness.

**税收政策是否公平的，直接影响社会的稳定。** Shuìshōu zhèngcè shìfǒu gōngpíng de, zhíjiē yǐngxiǎng shèhuì de wěndìng. Whether tax policies are equitable directly affects social stability.

**她认为这项决定并不公平，并向上级提出了申诉。** Tā rènwéi zhè xiàng juédìng bìng bù gōngpíng, bìng xiàng shàngjí tíchūle shēnsù. She thought the decision was unfair and filed an appeal with her superiors.

**公平的法律体系是保障公民权利的基础。** Gōngpíng de fǎlǜ tǐxì shì bǎozhàng gōngmín quánlì de jīchǔ. A fair legal system is the foundation for protecting citizens' rights.

## Usage Notes
公平的 is the attributive adjective form. When used predicatively, it appears as 很公平 or 不公平 (e.g., 这个决定不公平 -- this decision is not fair). The standalone noun 公平 (fairness) is common in formal writing.

A subtle distinction: 公平 emphasizes equal treatment under the same rules (procedural fairness), while 公正 (gōngzhèng) emphasizes moral rightness or justice. The two are often used together as 公平公正.

## Memory Hook
公 means "public/shared equally" and 平 means "level/equal" -- 公平 is a level playing field for everyone.
