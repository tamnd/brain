## 论据 (lùnjù)

论据 refers to the evidence, data, or reasoning used to support a thesis or argument, and is a key term in academic writing and formal debate in Chinese.

## Meanings
1. **[noun]** argument; grounds; evidence supporting a thesis

## Example Sentences
**这篇文章的论据充分，令人信服。** Zhè piān wénzhāng de lùnjù chōngfèn, lìngrén xìnfú. The arguments in this article are substantial and convincing.

**他在辩论中提出了有力的论据。** Tā zài biànlùn zhōng tíchū le yǒulì de lùnjù. He put forward strong arguments in the debate.

**没有充足的论据，很难支撑这个论点。** Méiyǒu chōngzú de lùnjù, hěn nán zhīchēng zhège lùndiǎn. Without sufficient evidence, it is hard to support this thesis.

**作者用历史数据作为论据，证明了自己的观点。** Zuòzhě yòng lìshǐ shùjù zuòwéi lùnjù, zhèngmíng le zìjǐ de guāndiǎn. The author used historical data as grounds to prove his viewpoint.

**这个理论的论据经不起仔细推敲。** Zhège lǐlùn de lùnjù jīng bù qǐ zǐxì tuīqiāo. The grounds for this theory do not stand up to careful scrutiny.

**提出论点时，必须同时给出有力的论据。** Tíchū lùndiǎn shí, bìxū tóngshí gěichū yǒulì de lùnjù. When making a claim, you must also provide strong supporting arguments.

## Usage Notes
In Chinese argumentation structure, 论点 (lùndiǎn, thesis/claim), 论据 (lùnjù, evidence/grounds), and 论证 (lùnzhèng, reasoning/demonstration) form a classic triad. 论据 is the supporting evidence that backs up a 论点. It can be factual data, examples, expert opinions, or logical reasoning.

Mastering this triad is essential for writing Chinese argumentative essays (议论文, yìlùn wén) at B2 level and above.

## Memory Hook
论 (argument/theory) + 据 (basis/evidence): the basis on which an argument stands -- its supporting evidence.
