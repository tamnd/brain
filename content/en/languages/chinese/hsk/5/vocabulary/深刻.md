## 深刻 (shēnkè)

深刻 describes something that is profound, deeply meaningful, or incisive -- it is used for ideas, impressions, experiences, and analyses that go beyond the surface and leave a lasting or significant impact.

## Meanings
1. **[adj]** profound; deep; incisive; penetrating; deeply felt

## Example Sentences
**这部电影留给我非常深刻的印象。** Zhè bù diànyǐng liú gěi wǒ fēicháng shēnkè de yìnxiàng. This film left a very profound impression on me.

**他对社会问题的分析深刻而犀利。** Tā duì shèhuì wèntí de fēnxī shēnkè ér xīlì. His analysis of social issues is profound and incisive.

**这次旅行让我对中国文化有了更深刻的理解。** Zhè cì lǚxíng ràng wǒ duì Zhōngguó wénhuà yǒule gèng shēnkè de lǐjiě. This trip gave me a deeper understanding of Chinese culture.

**那场地震给当地居民留下了深刻的心理创伤。** Nà chǎng dìzhèn gěi dāngdì jūmín liúxiàle shēnkè de xīnlǐ chuāngshāng. That earthquake left deep psychological trauma on local residents.

**这位哲学家提出了许多深刻的问题，引发了广泛讨论。** Zhè wèi zhéxuéjiā tíchūle xǔduō shēnkè de wèntí, yǐnfāle guǎngfàn tǎolùn. This philosopher raised many profound questions that sparked widespread discussion.

**经历了失败之后，他对成功有了更深刻的认识。** Jīnglìle shībài zhīhòu, tā duì chénggōng yǒule gèng shēnkè de rènshí. After experiencing failure, he gained a deeper understanding of success.

## Usage Notes
深刻 is often paired with 印象 (impression) to form 深刻印象 (deep impression) -- one of the most common collocations. It is also frequently combined with 理解, 认识, and 影响 to express intellectual or emotional depth.

Distinguish 深刻 from 深入: 深入 focuses on the act of going deep (thorough investigation, going deep into a topic), while 深刻 focuses on the quality of depth (a profound idea, a deep impression). 深刻 is usually used with abstract nouns relating to understanding, impact, and meaning; 深入 is used for actions and processes.

## Memory Hook
深 = deep, 刻 = to engrave/carve -- something so deep it is engraved -- leaving a profound, lasting mark.
