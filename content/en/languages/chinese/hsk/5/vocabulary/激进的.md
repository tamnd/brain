## 激进的 (jījìn de)

激进的 describes views, actions, or people that advocate for drastic, rapid, or extreme change -- going far beyond mainstream or moderate positions. It can carry a neutral analytical tone in academic writing or a negative connotation in everyday speech.

## Meanings
1. **[adj]** radical; extreme; advocating drastic or rapid change

## Example Sentences
**激进的改革方案可能会引发社会的强烈反对。** Jījìn de gǎigé fāng'àn kěnéng huì yǐnfā shèhuì de qiángliè fǎnduì. Radical reform proposals may trigger strong social opposition.

**他的观点过于激进，难以得到多数人的认可。** Tā de guāndiǎn guòyú jījìn, nányǐ dédào duōshù rén de rènkě. His views are too radical to gain the approval of the majority.

**激进的环保主义者认为，温和的措施已经不够。** Jījìn de huánbǎo zhǔyì zhě rènwéi, wēnhé de cuòshī yǐjīng bùgòu. Radical environmentalists believe that moderate measures are no longer sufficient.

**该运动起初是激进的，后来逐渐走向温和。** Gāi yùndòng qǐchū shì jījìn de, hòulái zhújiàn zǒuxiàng wēnhé. The movement was initially radical and gradually became more moderate.

**激进的技术变革可能让许多传统行业难以适应。** Jījìn de jìshù biàngé kěnéng ràng xǔduō chuántǒng hángyè nányǐ shìyìng. Radical technological change may make it difficult for many traditional industries to adapt.

**学界对激进的货币政策是否有效仍存在争议。** Xuéjiè duì jījìn de huòbì zhèngcè shìfǒu yǒuxiào réng cúnzài zhēngyì. There is still debate in academia about whether radical monetary policy is effective.

## Usage Notes
激进的 often appears in political and social analysis. Unlike English "radical" which can sometimes be neutral or positive (as in "radical change"), 激进的 in Chinese generally carries a cautionary or negative nuance, suggesting going too far or too fast.

Do not confuse with 积极的 (jījí de -- positive/proactive), which sounds similar but has a completely different meaning. The key distinction is 激 (intense/激烈) vs. 积 (accumulate/积累).

## Memory Hook
激 means "intense/fierce" (like waves crashing) and 进 means "advance" -- 激进 is advancing with fierce intensity, far beyond the norm.
