## 落实 (luòshí)

落实 describes the act of turning plans, policies, or promises into concrete reality, and is one of the most common verbs in Chinese administrative and business language.

## Meanings
1. **[verb]** to implement; to put into effect; to follow through on
2. **[verb]** to verify; to confirm (a detail or arrangement)

## Example Sentences
**政府正在落实新的环保政策。** Zhèngfǔ zhèngzài luòshí xīn de huánbǎo zhèngcè. The government is implementing the new environmental policy.

**我们需要落实每一个细节。** Wǒmen xūyào luòshí měi yīgè xìjié. We need to nail down every detail.

**计划已经制定，现在是落实的时候了。** Jìhuà yǐjīng zhìdìng, xiànzài shì luòshí de shíhòu le. The plan has been drawn up; now it is time to put it into action.

**领导要求各部门尽快落实这项决定。** Lǐngdǎo yāoqiú gè bùmén jǐnkuài luòshí zhè xiàng juédìng. The leadership asked each department to implement this decision as soon as possible.

**资金问题还没有落实，项目无法启动。** Zījīn wèntí hái méiyǒu luòshí, xiàngmù wúfǎ qǐdòng. The funding issue has not yet been confirmed, so the project cannot start.

**他保证会落实承诺，不让大家失望。** Tā bǎozhèng huì luòshí chéngnuò, bù ràng dàjiā shīwàng. He promised to follow through on his commitments and not let everyone down.

## Collocations
| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 落实政策 (luòshí zhèngcè) | to implement a policy |
| 落实责任 (luòshí zérèn) | to pin down responsibility |
| 落实措施 (luòshí cuòshī) | to put measures into effect |
| 尽快落实 (jǐnkuài luòshí) | to implement as soon as possible |
| 落实到位 (luòshí dàowèi) | to implement thoroughly |

## Usage Notes
落实 is strongly associated with official and organizational language. It implies that something moves from the abstract (plans, words, policies) to the concrete (actions, results). It is frequently used in government reports, company meetings, and news articles.

The complement 到位 (dàowèi, "in place") is often appended to stress thorough execution: 落实到位. Contrast with 实施 (shíshī), which also means "to implement" but is more neutral and less tied to following through on a specific prior commitment.

## Memory Hook
Think of 落 (to land/fall) + 实 (solid/real): a plan that finally "lands on solid ground" -- it has been made real.
