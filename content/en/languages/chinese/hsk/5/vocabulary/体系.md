## 体系 (tǐxì)

体系 refers to an organized, interconnected system or framework in which all parts work together coherently, and is used for both abstract conceptual systems and concrete organizational structures.

## Meanings

1. **[noun]** system, framework, organized structure (of interconnected elements)
2. **[noun]** a coherent body (of knowledge, rules, institutions)

## Example Sentences

**中国正在积极参与全球治理体系的改革与完善。** Zhōngguó zhèngzài jījí cānyù quánqiú zhìlǐ tǐxì de gǎigé yǔ wánshàn. China is actively participating in the reform and improvement of the global governance system.

**完善的法律体系是维护社会公平正义的基础。** Wánshàn de fǎlǜ tǐxì shì wéihù shèhuì gōngpíng zhèngyì de jīchǔ. A sound legal system is the foundation for maintaining social fairness and justice.

**这位哲学家构建了一套严密的理论体系。** Zhè wèi zhéxué jiā gòujiàn le yī tào yánmì de lǐlùn tǐxì. This philosopher constructed a rigorous theoretical system.

**国家正在加快建设现代化经济体系。** Guójiā zhèngzài jiākuài jiànshè xiàndàihuà jīngjì tǐxì. The country is accelerating the building of a modern economic system.

**教育体系的改革直接关系到人才培养的质量。** Jiàoyù tǐxì de gǎigé zhíjiē guānxi dào réncái péiyǎng de zhìliàng. Reform of the education system is directly related to the quality of talent development.

**她在博士论文中提出了一个新的分析体系。** Tā zài bóshì lùnwén zhōng tíchū le yīgè xīn de fēnxī tǐxì. She proposed a new analytical framework in her doctoral dissertation.

## Usage Notes

体系 emphasizes the systematic, structured, and interconnected nature of the components within it. It differs from 系统 (another word for "system") in that 体系 often refers to a conceptual or institutional framework with a theoretical dimension, while 系统 is more commonly used for technical or operational systems (e.g., computer systems, management systems). The phrase 构建体系 (to build a system/framework) is very common in academic and policy writing.

At B2 level, 体系 is essential for discussing governance, education, law, theory, and economic structures.

## Memory Hook

体 means body/whole and 系 means to connect or a system -- 体系 is the connected body, like a skeleton that holds all the bones of knowledge together in an organized structure.
