## 呈现 (chéngxiàn)

呈现 means to take on or display a certain state, appearance, or quality, often emphasizing that something comes into view or becomes apparent before observers.

## Meanings

1. **[verb]** to present; to exhibit; to appear as; to take on (a state or quality)
2. **[verb]** to emerge; to come into view; to manifest visually

## Example Sentences

**夕阳下，整座城市呈现出金色的光芒。** Xīyáng xià, zhěng zuò chéngshì chéngxiàn chū jīnsè de guāngmáng. Under the setting sun, the entire city took on a golden glow.

**随着经济发展，社会呈现出多元化的趋势。** Suízhe jīngjì fāzhǎn, shèhuì chéngxiàn chū duōyuánhuà de qūshì. With economic development, society has taken on a trend of diversification.

**这次展览呈现了艺术家最新的创作成果。** Zhè cì zhǎnlǎn chéngxiànle yìshùjiā zuìxīn de chuàngzuò chéngguǒ. This exhibition presented the artist's latest creative achievements.

**数据图表直观地呈现了十年间的变化趋势。** Shùjù túbiǎo zhíguān de chéngxiànle shí nián jiān de biànhuà qūshì. The data charts intuitively presented the trends of change over ten years.

**舞台上，演员们呈现了一场精彩绝伦的演出。** Wǔtái shàng, yǎnyuánmen chéngxiànle yī chǎng jīngcǎi juélún de yǎnchū. On stage, the performers presented a dazzling show.

**这首诗呈现出一种宁静而深邃的意境。** Zhè shǒu shī chéngxiàn chū yī zhǒng níngjìng ér shēnsuì de yìjìng. This poem presents a tranquil and profound artistic conception.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 呈现出 (chéngxiàn chū) | to present; to take on |
| 直观呈现 (zhíguān chéngxiàn) | intuitively present |
| 呈现趋势 (chéngxiàn qūshì) | show a trend |
| 呈现状态 (chéngxiàn zhuàngtài) | take on a state |
| 生动呈现 (shēngdòng chéngxiàn) | vividly present |
| 呈现形式 (chéngxiàn xíngshì) | form of presentation |

## Usage Notes

呈现 often describes the state or appearance something takes on, with a somewhat passive or emergent quality, as if the thing naturally reveals or manifests itself before the observer. It is frequently followed by 出 (呈现出) to indicate emergence into view. Compared with 展现 (zhǎnxiàn, to display/unfold), which implies an active showing, 呈现 is more neutral and can describe both intentional presentation and natural appearance.

In academic and descriptive writing, 呈现……的趋势/状态 (to take on a trend/state) is a very common construction. At B2, learners should use 呈现 when describing how phenomena appear to observers.

## Memory Hook

CHENG (呈, to offer up/present) + XIAN (现, to appear/emerge) = offering something up so it appears before the viewer.
