## 可再生 (kě zàishēng)

可再生 means "renewable" and is primarily used to describe energy sources and natural resources that can be naturally replenished, as opposed to finite fossil fuels.

## Meanings

1. **[adjective]** renewable; regenerable (describing resources or energy that can be naturally replenished)

## Example Sentences

**太阳能和风能都是可再生能源。** Tàiyángnéng hé fēngnéng dōu shì kě zàishēng néngyuán. Solar energy and wind energy are both renewable energy sources.

**发展可再生能源是应对气候变化的关键措施。** Fāzhǎn kě zàishēng néngyuán shì yìngduì qìhòu biànhuà de guānjiàn cuòshī. Developing renewable energy is a key measure to address climate change.

**许多国家正在加大对可再生能源的投资。** Xǔduō guójiā zhèngzài jiādà duì kě zàishēng néngyuán de tóuzī. Many countries are increasing their investment in renewable energy.

**可再生资源的利用有助于减少碳排放。** Kě zàishēng zīyuán de lìyòng yǒuzhù yú jiǎnshǎo tàn páifàng. The use of renewable resources helps reduce carbon emissions.

**与化石燃料相比，可再生能源更加清洁。** Yǔ huàshí ránliào xiāngbǐ, kě zàishēng néngyuán gèngjiā qīngjié. Compared with fossil fuels, renewable energy is cleaner.

**政府制定了到2030年可再生能源占比达到50%的目标。** Zhèngfǔ zhìdìng le dào 2030 nián kě zàishēng néngyuán zhànbǐ dádào 50% de mùbiāo. The government set a target for renewable energy to account for 50% of the share by 2030.

## Usage Notes

可再生 is nearly always followed by 能源 (energy) or 资源 (resources) in practice. The phrase 可再生能源 has become a central term in environmental policy and green economy discussions. Its antonym is 不可再生 (non-renewable) or 化石燃料 (fossil fuels).

The word is composed of 可 (can/able to), 再 (again), and 生 (grow/regenerate), making the meaning transparent: "able to grow again." This is an important B2-level term for discussing environmental topics in Chinese.

## Memory Hook

可 = "can," 再 = "again," 生 = "grow/live" -- 可再生 means "can grow again," just like renewable resources that replenish themselves naturally.
