## 期待 (qīdài)

To look forward to something hoped-for with positive anticipation -- warmer and more emotionally charged than neutral 期望 or 预期.

## Meanings
1. **[verb]** to look forward to, to eagerly anticipate, to expect with hope
2. **[noun]** expectation, anticipation, hope

## Example Sentences
**孩子们都非常期待明天的春游活动。** Háizimen dōu fēicháng qīdài míngtiān de chūnyóu huódòng. The children are all very much looking forward to tomorrow's spring outing.

**观众对这部续集电影抱有很高的期待。** Guānzhòng duì zhè bù xùjí diànyǐng bàoyǒu hěn gāo de qīdài. The audience has very high expectations for this sequel film.

**我期待着与您在下次会议上再次见面。** Wǒ qīdàizhe yǔ nín zài xià cì huìyì shàng zàicì jiànmiàn. I look forward to meeting with you again at the next meeting.

**她满怀期待地打开了那封来自大学的录取通知。** Tā mǎnhuái qīdài de dǎkāile nà fēng láizì dàxué de lùqǔ tōngzhī. She opened the university acceptance letter with great anticipation.

**这项技术未能达到公众的普遍期待，令人有些失望。** Zhè xiàng jìshù wèi néng dádào gōngzhòng de pǔbiàn qīdài, lìng rén yǒuxiē shīwàng. This technology failed to meet public expectations, which was somewhat disappointing.

**父母对子女的期待往往会影响孩子的职业选择。** Fùmǔ duì zǐnǚ de qīdài wǎngwǎng huì yǐngxiǎng háizi de zhíyè xuǎnzé. Parents' expectations of their children often influence the children's career choices.

## Usage Notes
期待 emphasizes hopeful, emotionally positive anticipation. It contrasts with 期望 (qīwàng), which has a stronger sense of "hoping that" something will happen (often used by superiors toward subordinates), and 预期 (yùqī), which is a more neutral, analytical expectation used in business and academic contexts.

期待 is natural in personal and social contexts: looking forward to an event, a meeting, or a result. In letters and emails, 期待您的回复 (looking forward to your reply) is a standard polite closing phrase.

## Memory Hook
期 means "a set period of time" or "to hope/expect." 待 means "to wait." Together, 期待 is "waiting for a set time" -- that eager, hopeful waiting for something you really want to arrive.
