## 垄断 (lǒngduàn)

垄断 means monopoly or to monopolize -- exclusive control over a market, resource, or activity by one entity, preventing competition.

## Meanings

1. **[noun]** monopoly; exclusive control; domination
2. **[verb]** to monopolize; to dominate exclusively; to corner (a market)

## Example Sentences

**反垄断法旨在防止企业过度集中市场权力。** Fǎn lǒngduàn fǎ zhǐ zài fángzhǐ qǐyè guòdù jízhōng shìchǎng quánlì. Antitrust law aims to prevent companies from concentrating market power excessively.

**这家公司垄断了该地区的电信市场。** Zhè jiā gōngsī lǒngduàn le gāi dìqū de diànxìn shìchǎng. This company monopolized the telecommunications market in the region.

**国家对某些行业实行垄断，以确保国家安全。** Guójiā duì mǒuxiē hángyè shíxíng lǒngduàn, yǐ quèbǎo guójiā ānquán. The state maintains monopolies in certain industries to ensure national security.

**垄断行为损害了消费者的利益和市场竞争。** Lǒngduàn xíngwéi sǔnhài le xiāofèi zhě de lìyì hé shìchǎng jìngzhēng. Monopolistic behavior harms the interests of consumers and market competition.

**打破垄断有助于激励创新和降低价格。** Dǎpò lǒngduàn yǒuzhù yú jīlì chuàngxīn hé jiàngdī jiàgé. Breaking up monopolies helps stimulate innovation and lower prices.

**历史上，许多重要资源曾被少数人垄断。** Lìshǐ shàng, xǔduō zhòngyào zīyuán céng bèi shǎoshù rén lǒngduàn. Historically, many important resources were monopolized by a small number of people.

## Usage Notes

垄断 is an important term in economics, law, and business. In Chinese economic policy, 反垄断 (antitrust/anti-monopoly) has become increasingly prominent with the regulation of large tech companies. 垄断 can describe market monopolies, information monopolies, or even exclusive social influence.

The characters 垄 (ridge between fields) and 断 (to cut off) historically referred to merchants who set up shop on a ridge to control trade and cut off competition -- an evocative origin for the word "monopoly."

## Memory Hook

垄 is a raised ridge that blocks passage, and 断 means to cut off. 垄断 originally meant to stand on a ridge and cut off others from the market -- which is exactly what a monopoly does.
