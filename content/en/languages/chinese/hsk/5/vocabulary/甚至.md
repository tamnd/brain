## 甚至 (shènzhì)

甚至 is used to present an extreme example that exceeds what has just been stated, adding emphasis to the degree or extent of something.

## Meanings
1. **[adverb/conjunction]** even; even to the point that; so much so that

## Example Sentences
**他学习非常努力，甚至周末也不休息。** Tā xuéxí fēicháng nǔlì, shènzhì zhōumò yě bù xiūxi. He studies very hard -- he even skips rest on weekends.

**这道题太难了，甚至老师也做不出来。** Zhè dào tí tài nán le, shènzhì lǎoshī yě zuò bu chūlái. This problem is so difficult that even the teacher cannot solve it.

**天气太冷，甚至河面都结冰了。** Tiānqì tài lěng, shènzhì hémiàn dōu jiébīng le. The weather was so cold that even the river surface froze.

**她喜欢读书，甚至吃饭时也拿着书。** Tā xǐhuān dúshū, shènzhì chīfàn shí yě ná zhe shū. She loves reading so much that she even holds a book while eating.

**空气污染越来越严重，甚至影响到了儿童的健康。** Kōngqì wūrǎn yuèlái yuè yánzhòng, shènzhì yǐngxiǎng dào le értóng de jiànkāng. Air pollution is getting worse and worse, even affecting children's health.

**那场演讲非常精彩，甚至让反对者也改变了看法。** Nà chǎng yǎnjiǎng fēicháng jīngcǎi, shènzhì ràng fǎnduìzhě yě gǎibiàn le kànfǎ. That speech was so impressive that even opponents changed their views.

## Collocations
| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 甚至连……都 (shènzhì lián...dōu) | even...also (reinforced) |
| 甚至于 (shènzhìyú) | even to the extent that (formal) |
| 不仅……甚至 (bùjǐn...shènzhì) | not only...but even |
| 甚至可以说 (shènzhì kěyǐ shuō) | one could even say |
| 甚至更 (shènzhì gèng) | even more |

## Usage Notes
甚至 introduces a clause or noun phrase that represents an extreme or surprising case that goes beyond what was previously mentioned. It can appear before a noun, verb phrase, or clause. When paired with 连...也/都, the emphasis is further strengthened: 甚至连他都不知道 means "even he didn't know."

甚至 differs from 即使 (jíshǐ, meaning "even if") in that 甚至 presents a real, factual extreme situation, not a hypothetical one. It is common in both spoken and written Chinese and is considered a neutral, versatile connector.

## Memory Hook
甚 means "very/extreme" and 至 means "to reach" -- it reaches even the extreme case.
