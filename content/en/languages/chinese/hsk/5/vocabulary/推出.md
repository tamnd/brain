## 推出 (tuīchū)

推出 means to launch, roll out, or introduce something to the public or market, and is widely used in business, policy, media, and cultural contexts.

## Meanings

1. **[verb]** to launch; to roll out (a product, policy, or service)
2. **[verb]** to introduce; to put forward; to bring out (a new offering)

## Example Sentences

**该品牌近期推出了一款全新的智能手机产品。** Gāi pǐnpái jìnqī tuīchūle yī kuǎn quánxīn de zhìnéng shǒujī chǎnpǐn. The brand recently launched a brand-new smartphone product.

**政府将推出一系列扶持中小企业的优惠政策。** Zhèngfǔ jiāng tuīchū yī xìliè fúchí zhōngxiǎo qǐyè de yōuhuì zhèngcè. The government will roll out a series of preferential policies to support small and medium enterprises.

**这家流媒体平台每月都会推出新的原创剧集。** Zhè jiā liúméitǐ píngtái měi yuè dōu huì tuīchū xīn de yuánchuàng jùjí. This streaming platform rolls out new original drama series every month.

**该软件公司宣布将于下季度推出升级版本。** Gāi ruǎnjiàn gōngsī xuānbù jiāng yú xià jìdù tuīchū shēngjí bǎnběn. The software company announced it will launch an upgraded version in the next quarter.

**学校推出了一项新的奖学金计划，以吸引优秀学生。** Xuéxiào tuīchūle yī xiàng xīn de jiǎngxuéjīn jìhuà, yǐ xīyǐn yōuxiù xuésheng. The school launched a new scholarship program to attract outstanding students.

**为应对市场需求变化，公司及时推出了多款新产品。** Wèi yìngduì shìchǎng xūqiú biànhuà, gōngsī jíshí tuīchūle duō kuǎn xīn chǎnpǐn. To respond to changing market demands, the company promptly launched multiple new products.

## Usage Notes

推出 combines 推 (to push) and 出 (out), evoking the image of pushing something out into public view. It is the standard verb for product launches, policy rollouts, and the release of creative works. It is more dynamic and marketing-oriented than 发布 (fābù, to release/publish), which is used more for official announcements and the release of information.

推出 is widely used in business journalism and official communications. It can refer to both tangible products and intangible offerings (policies, programs, services). At B2, learners should use 推出 for market and policy launches.

## Memory Hook

TUI (推, to push) + CHU (出, out) = to push something out for the public to see and use.
