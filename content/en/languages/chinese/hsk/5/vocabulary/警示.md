## 警示 (jǐngshì)

警示 means to warn or to serve as a warning, and is commonly used in safety, legal, and educational contexts to alert people to dangers or consequences.

## Meanings
1. **[verb]** to warn; to caution; to alert
2. **[noun]** a warning; a caution; an alert

## Example Sentences
**这个案例对其他企业具有重要的警示意义。** Zhège ànlì duì qítā qǐyè jùyǒu zhòngyào de jǐngshì yìyì. This case has important warning significance for other enterprises.

**政府在危险路段设置了警示标志。** Zhèngfǔ zài wēixiǎn lùduàn shèzhì le jǐngshì biāozhì. The government erected warning signs on dangerous road sections.

**这场灾难警示我们要重视自然保护。** Zhè chǎng zāinàn jǐngshì wǒmen yào zhòngshì zìrán bǎohù. This disaster warns us to take nature conservation seriously.

**他的失败经历是对年轻人的一次警示。** Tā de shībài jīnglì shì duì niánqīng rén de yīcì jǐngshì. His experience of failure is a warning to young people.

**医生向患者发出了健康警示。** Yīshēng xiàng huànzhě fāchū le jiànkāng jǐngshì. The doctor issued a health warning to the patient.

**香烟包装上印有警示图片，提醒人们戒烟。** Xiāngyān bāozhuāng shàng yìn yǒu jǐngshì túpiàn, tíxǐng rénmen jièyān. Warning images are printed on cigarette packaging to remind people to quit smoking.

## Usage Notes
警示 is more formal and serious than 警告 (jǐnggào). While 警告 can be a direct personal rebuke or official reprimand, 警示 tends to have a broader educational or societal function -- it warns people collectively about a type of risk or consequence. It is common in government announcements, safety campaigns, and moral/educational commentary.

The phrase 具有警示意义 (jùyǒu jǐngshì yìyì, "has cautionary significance") is a fixed expression frequently used in news analysis.

## Memory Hook
警 (alert/police) + 示 (to show/indicate): showing an alert -- a visible warning signal.
