## 大数据 (dà shùjù)

Big data refers to extremely large data sets that are analyzed computationally to reveal patterns, trends, and associations, especially relating to human behavior and interactions.

## Meanings

1. **[noun]** big data; massive collections of digital information processed for insight

## Example Sentences

**大数据正在改变我们的生活方式。** Dà shùjù zhèngzài gǎibiàn wǒmen de shēnghuó fāngshì. Big data is changing the way we live.

**政府利用大数据来预测交通拥堵。** Zhèngfǔ lìyòng dà shùjù lái yùcè jiāotōng yōngdǔ. The government uses big data to predict traffic congestion.

**大数据分析帮助企业了解消费者需求。** Dà shùjù fēnxī bāngzhù qǐyè liǎojiě xiāofèizhě xūqiú. Big data analysis helps businesses understand consumer needs.

**在医疗领域，大数据可以加速新药的研发。** Zài yīliáo lǐngyù, dà shùjù kěyǐ jiāsù xīnyào de yánfā. In the medical field, big data can accelerate the development of new drugs.

**保护个人隐私是大数据时代的重要课题。** Bǎohù gèrén yǐnsī shì dà shùjù shídài de zhòngyào kètí. Protecting personal privacy is an important issue in the age of big data.

**他在大学专攻大数据技术。** Tā zài dàxué zhuāngōng dà shùjù jìshù. He majors in big data technology at university.

## Usage Notes

大数据 is a compound noun formed from 大 (big/large) and 数据 (data). It is primarily used in technology, business, and academic contexts. The term has gained wide usage in China as the country invests heavily in data-driven industries.

At B2 level, learners should note that 大数据 often collocates with verbs like 利用 (to use/leverage), 分析 (to analyze), 处理 (to process), and 挖掘 (to mine). It is a formal term most common in written and professional speech.

## Memory Hook

Think of 大 (big) + 数据 (data) -- a giant mountain of numbers that, when examined, reveals hidden truths about the world.
