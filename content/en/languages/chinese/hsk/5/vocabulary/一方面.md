## 一方面 (yī fāngmiàn)

一方面 is a discourse marker used to introduce one aspect of a situation, typically paired with 另一方面 (lìng yī fāngmiàn) to contrast two perspectives at B2 level.

## Meanings

1. **[conjunction]** on one hand; in one respect (introduces the first of two contrasting points)
2. **[adverb]** from one angle or perspective

## Example Sentences

**一方面，经济发展很重要；另一方面，环境保护也不能忽视。** Yī fāngmiàn, jīngjì fāzhǎn hěn zhòngyào; lìng yī fāngmiàn, huánjìng bǎohù yě bù néng hūshì. On one hand, economic development is very important; on the other hand, environmental protection cannot be ignored.

**一方面他想出国留学，另一方面又舍不得离开家人。** Yī fāngmiàn tā xiǎng chūguó liúxué, lìng yī fāngmiàn yòu shěbude líkāi jiārén. On one hand he wants to study abroad; on the other hand, he is reluctant to leave his family.

**这项政策一方面降低了成本，另一方面提高了效率。** Zhè xiàng zhèngcè yī fāngmiàn jiàngdīle chéngběn, lìng yī fāngmiàn tígāole xiàolǜ. This policy on one hand reduced costs, and on the other hand improved efficiency.

**一方面要完成工作，另一方面还要照顾孩子，她压力很大。** Yī fāngmiàn yào wánchéng gōngzuò, lìng yī fāngmiàn hái yào zhàogù háizi, tā yālì hěn dà. On one hand she has to finish work, and on the other hand take care of her child — she is under a lot of pressure.

**一方面是资金不足，另一方面是人才匮乏，项目进展缓慢。** Yī fāngmiàn shì zījīn bùzú, lìng yī fāngmiàn shì réncái kuìfá, xiàngmù jìnzhǎn huǎnmàn. On one hand there is insufficient funding, on the other hand there is a lack of talent, so the project is progressing slowly.

**一方面要保持传统文化，另一方面也要接受新的理念。** Yī fāngmiàn yào bǎochí chuántǒng wénhuà, lìng yī fāngmiàn yě yào jiēshòu xīn de lǐniàn. On one hand we must preserve traditional culture, and on the other hand we must also accept new ideas.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 一方面...另一方面 | on one hand ... on the other hand |
| 一方面...同时 | on one hand ... at the same time |
| 从一方面来看 | looking at it from one side |
| 一方面的原因 | one reason/aspect |
| 两方面 | two aspects/sides |

## Usage Notes

一方面 almost always appears in the paired structure 一方面...另一方面, presenting a balanced two-part argument or comparison. It is common in written Chinese, academic essays, and formal speeches. Unlike 但是 or 然而, which signal contrast, 一方面...另一方面 often presents complementary or simultaneously true points rather than strict opposites.

At B2 level, learners should practice using this structure to add nuance to their arguments, especially in writing tasks where presenting multiple perspectives is expected.

## Memory Hook

Think of "one side of a coin" — 一 (one) + 方面 (side/aspect) — you always need to flip the coin to see 另一方面 (the other side).
