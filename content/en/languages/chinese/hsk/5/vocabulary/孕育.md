## 孕育 (yùnyù)

To nurture or give rise to something means to create the conditions for it to grow and develop, often used figuratively for ideas, movements, or civilizations.

## Meanings

1. **[verb]** to nurture; to breed; to give rise to; to incubate (ideas, culture, life)

## Example Sentences

**这片土地孕育了灿烂的古代文明。** Zhè piàn tǔdì yùnyùle càn làn de gǔdài wénmíng. This land gave rise to a brilliant ancient civilization.

**良好的教育环境孕育了无数优秀人才。** Liánghǎo de jiàoyù huánjìng yùnyùle wúshù yōuxiù réncái. A good educational environment has nurtured countless talented individuals.

**改革开放孕育了中国经济的腾飞。** Gǎigé kāifàng yùnyùle Zhōngguó jīngjì de téngfēi. The reform and opening up gave rise to the takeoff of the Chinese economy.

**长期的思考孕育了他独特的哲学观点。** Chángqī de sīkǎo yùnyùle tā dútè de zhéxué guāndiǎn. Years of thinking nurtured his unique philosophical perspective.

**大自然孕育了地球上丰富的生命形式。** Dà zìrán yùnyùle dìqiú shàng fēngfù de shēngmìng xíngshì. Nature has given rise to the rich forms of life on Earth.

**这段历史孕育了民族团结的精神。** Zhè duàn lìshǐ yùnyùle mínzú tuánjié de jīngshén. This period of history nurtured the spirit of national unity.

## Usage Notes

孕育 literally means "to be pregnant and rear" (孕 = pregnant; 育 = to raise/nurture). It is almost always used metaphorically at B2 level and above to describe how environments, historical periods, or processes produce great outcomes. It carries a positive, formal tone.

This verb is common in literary, historical, and academic writing. It implies a gradual, organic process rather than a sudden creation. Avoid using it for trivial everyday things -- it is reserved for significant developments, civilizations, ideals, or life itself.

## Memory Hook

孕 means "pregnant" and 育 means "to raise a child" -- together, 孕育 is the full journey from conception to raising greatness, used figuratively for any powerful nurturing process.
