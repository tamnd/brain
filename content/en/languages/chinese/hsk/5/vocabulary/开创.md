## 开创 (kāichuàng)

To pioneer or create something new means to be the first to establish it, breaking new ground and bringing something previously nonexistent into being.

## Meanings

1. **[verb]** to pioneer; to found; to create; to open up (a new era, field, or path)

## Example Sentences

**他开创了现代中国画的新风格。** Tā kāichuàngle xiàndài Zhōngguó huà de xīn fēnggé. He pioneered a new style of modern Chinese painting.

**改革开放开创了中国历史上的新局面。** Gǎigé kāifàng kāichuàngle Zhōngguó lìshǐ shàng de xīn júmiàn. Reform and opening up opened up a new situation in Chinese history.

**他们希望开创一个更加公平、包容的社会。** Tāmen xīwàng kāichuàng yīgè gèngjiā gōngpíng, bāoróng de shèhuì. They hope to pioneer a more equitable and inclusive society.

**这位科学家开创了基因编辑领域的先河。** Zhè wèi kēxuéjiā kāichuàngle jīyīn biānjí lǐngyù de xiānhé. This scientist pioneered the field of gene editing.

**公司正在努力开创新的市场，寻找增长机会。** Gōngsī zhèngzài nǔlì kāichuàng xīn de shìchǎng, xúnzhǎo zēngzhǎng jīhuì. The company is working hard to open up new markets and find growth opportunities.

**年轻一代有责任开创更加美好的未来。** Niánqīng yīdài yǒu zérèn kāichuàng gèngjiā měihǎo de wèilái. The younger generation has the responsibility to pioneer a better future.

## Usage Notes

开创 emphasizes the act of originating something new, suggesting a first-of-its-kind achievement. It is more formal and elevated than 开始 (to begin) and implies historical or significant impact. The phrase 开创先河 (literally "to open the source of a river") means to set a precedent for the first time.

开创性 as an adjective means "pioneering" or "groundbreaking": 开创性的工作 (pioneering work). This is especially common in academic and scientific contexts.

## Memory Hook

开 (to open) + 创 (to create/found) -- opening a door to something entirely new, creating the very path others will follow.
