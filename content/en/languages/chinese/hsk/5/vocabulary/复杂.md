## 复杂 (fùzá)

复杂 describes something that has many interrelated parts, is difficult to understand or deal with, and cannot be reduced to simple terms -- complex or complicated.

## Meanings

1. **[adjective]** complex; complicated; intricate; involved

## Example Sentences

**人际关系有时非常复杂，难以处理。** Rénjì guānxi yǒushí fēicháng fùzá, nán yǐ chǔlǐ. Interpersonal relationships are sometimes very complicated and difficult to handle.

**这个问题比看起来复杂得多。** Zhège wèntí bǐ kàn qǐlái fùzá de duō. This problem is much more complex than it appears.

**全球经济形势错综复杂。** Quánqiú jīngjì xíngshì cuòzōng fùzá. The global economic situation is tangled and complex.

**修复这台机器需要复杂的技术知识。** Xiūfù zhè tái jīqì xūyào fùzá de jìshù zhīshì. Repairing this machine requires complex technical knowledge.

**气候变化背后的原因十分复杂。** Qìhòu biànhuà bèihòu de yuányīn shífēn fùzá. The reasons behind climate change are extremely complex.

**他的内心世界非常复杂，外人很难理解。** Tā de nèixīn shìjiè fēicháng fùzá, wàirén hěn nán lǐjiě. His inner world is very complex and difficult for outsiders to understand.

## Usage Notes

复杂 is the standard adjective for "complex" or "complicated" in Chinese. 复 means "multiple/repeated" and 杂 means "mixed/disordered," combining to suggest something with many mixed and intertwined elements.

The antonym is 简单 (jiǎndān, simple). A common four-character idiom is 错综复杂 (cuòzōng fùzá), meaning "tangled and complicated," often used to describe intricate situations in politics, economics, or relationships.

## Memory Hook

复 means "repeated/many" and 杂 means "mixed up." 复杂 is many things mixed up together -- that is exactly what "complex" or "complicated" means.
