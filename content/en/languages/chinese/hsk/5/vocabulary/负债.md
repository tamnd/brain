## 负债 (fùzhài)

负债 describes the state of having debts or owing money, and in financial accounting it refers specifically to liabilities, making it essential vocabulary for finance, business reporting, and economic discussion.

## Meanings
1. **[verb]** to be in debt; to owe money
2. **[noun]** debt; liabilities; indebtedness

## Example Sentences
**这家公司因过度扩张而负债累累。** Zhè jiā gōngsī yīn guòdù kuòzhāng ér fùzhài lěilěi. This company is deeply in debt due to overexpansion.

**负债与资产的比率是评估企业财务健康的关键指标。** Fùzhài yǔ zīchǎn de bǐlǜ shì pínggū qǐyè cáiwù jiànkāng de guānjiàn zhǐbiāo. The ratio of debt to assets is a key indicator for assessing a company's financial health.

**许多年轻人因为房贷而背负大量负债。** Xǔduō niánqīng rén yīnwèi fángdài ér bēifù dàliàng fùzhài. Many young people carry heavy debt burdens due to housing mortgages.

**政府的外债负债水平引发了国际社会的担忧。** Zhèngfǔ de wàizhài fùzhài shuǐpíng yǐnfā le guójì shèhuì de dānyōu. The government's external debt level has raised concern in the international community.

**他通过理财逐步清偿了所有负债。** Tā tōngguò lǐcái zhúbù qīngcháng le suǒyǒu fùzhài. He gradually paid off all his debts through financial planning.

**合理的负债可以帮助企业实现快速扩张。** Hélǐ de fùzhài kěyǐ bāngzhù qǐyè shíxiàn kuàisù kuòzhāng. Reasonable debt can help a company achieve rapid expansion.

## Usage Notes
负债 functions both as a verb (to be in debt / to owe) and a noun (debts / liabilities). In financial accounting it is the standard term for "liabilities" on a balance sheet, contrasting with 资产 (zīchǎn, assets). The ratio 资产负债率 (asset-liability ratio) is a core metric in business Chinese.

The compound 负债累累 (fùzhài lěilěi) is a set phrase meaning "burdened with mountains of debt."

## Memory Hook
负 (to bear/carry a burden) + 债 (debt): carrying the burden of debt -- to be in debt.
