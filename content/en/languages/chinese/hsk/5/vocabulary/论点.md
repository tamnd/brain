## 论点 (lùndiǎn)

论点 is the specific claim, proposition, or thesis that a writer or speaker sets out to prove, and is the centerpiece of any structured argument in Chinese.

## Meanings
1. **[noun]** thesis; argument; point; proposition

## Example Sentences
**这篇文章的中心论点是技术进步有利于社会发展。** Zhè piān wénzhāng de zhōngxīn lùndiǎn shì jìshù jìnbù yǒulì yú shèhuì fāzhǎn. The central thesis of this article is that technological progress benefits social development.

**他的论点很有说服力，让听众信服。** Tā de lùndiǎn hěn yǒu shuōfú lì, ràng tīngzhòng xìnfú. His argument is very persuasive and convinced the audience.

**在写议论文时，要先确定明确的论点。** Zài xiě yìlùn wén shí, yào xiān quèdìng míngquè de lùndiǎn. When writing an argumentative essay, you should first establish a clear thesis.

**对方提出了几个有趣的论点，值得深思。** Duìfāng tíchū le jǐ gè yǒuqù de lùndiǎn, zhídé shēnsī. The other side raised several interesting points worth pondering.

**这个论点缺乏充分的论据支撑。** Zhège lùndiǎn quēfá chōngfèn de lùnjù zhīchēng. This argument lacks sufficient supporting evidence.

**她成功地反驳了对手的主要论点。** Tā chénggōng de fǎnbó le duìshǒu de zhǔyào lùndiǎn. She successfully rebutted her opponent's main argument.

## Usage Notes
论点 is the claim or proposition to be proven. In Chinese rhetoric, the classic essay structure is: 论点 (claim) supported by 论据 (evidence) and demonstrated through 论证 (reasoning). Understanding this triad is essential for Chinese argumentative writing.

At B2 level, note that 观点 (guāndiǎn, viewpoint/opinion) is more general and personal, while 论点 is specifically a claim being argued in a structured way.

## Memory Hook
论 (argument/discussion) + 点 (point): the specific point being argued -- the thesis.
