## 进口 (jìnkǒu)

进口 refers to the importation of goods or services from another country, or the imported goods themselves, and is a fundamental term in trade, economics, and commerce.

## Meanings

1. **[verb]** to import (goods or services)
2. **[noun]** imports; imported goods

## Example Sentences

**这批进口设备大大提升了工厂的生产效率。** Zhè pī jìnkǒu shèbèi dàdà tíshēngle gōngchǎng de shēngchǎn xiàolǜ. This batch of imported equipment greatly improved the factory's production efficiency.

**该国的石油主要依赖进口，国内储量有限。** Gāi guó de shíyóu zhǔyào yīlài jìnkǒu, guónèi chǔliàng yǒuxiàn. The country relies mainly on imports for oil as domestic reserves are limited.

**政府决定降低部分消费品的进口关税。** Zhèngfǔ juédìng jiàngdī bùfèn xiāofèipǐn de jìnkǒu guānshuì. The government decided to reduce import tariffs on some consumer goods.

**进口汽车在高端市场上仍然占据重要地位。** Jìnkǒu qìchē zài gāoduān shìchǎng shàng réngrán zhànjù zhòngyào dìwèi. Imported cars still occupy an important position in the high-end market.

**贸易逆差意味着一个国家的进口超过了出口。** Màoyì nìchā yìwèizhe yī gè guójiā de jìnkǒu chāoguòle chūkǒu. A trade deficit means that a country's imports exceed its exports.

**严格的进口检疫措施有助于防止外来病虫害入侵。** Yángé de jìnkǒu jiǎnyì cuòshī yǒuzhù yú fángzhǐ wàilái bìng chóng hài rùqīn. Strict import quarantine measures help prevent the invasion of foreign pests and diseases.

## Usage Notes

进口 is the antonym of 出口 (chūkǒu, export). Together they form the core vocabulary of international trade. Note that 进口 also has a literal spatial meaning of "entrance" (the mouth or opening you enter through), which is its older, non-trade usage.

In trade contexts, 进口 is always used as a verb or attributive noun (进口商品, imported goods), not as a standalone predicate without an object. Learners should practise the pair 进口/出口 together with trade-related collocations.

## Memory Hook

进 (to enter) + 口 (mouth/opening) = goods that enter through the country's mouth -- imports.
