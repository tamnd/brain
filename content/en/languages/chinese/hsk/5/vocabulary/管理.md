## 管理 (guǎnlǐ)

管理 means to manage, administer, or supervise people, resources, or systems in an organised and responsible way. It functions as both a verb and a noun in Chinese.

## Meanings

1. **[verb]** to manage; to administer; to oversee
2. **[noun]** management; administration

## Example Sentences

**企业要想持续发展，必须建立科学有效的管理制度。** Qǐyè yào xiǎng chíxù fāzhǎn, bìxū jiànlì kēxué yǒuxiào de guǎnlǐ zhìdù. For an enterprise to develop sustainably, it must establish a scientific and effective management system.

**他在公司担任人力资源管理工作已有十年。** Tā zài gōngsī dānrèn rénlì zīyuán guǎnlǐ gōngzuò yǐ yǒu shí nián. He has worked in human resources management at the company for ten years.

**政府加强了对食品安全的管理，严厉打击违规行为。** Zhèngfǔ jiāqiángle duì shípǐn ānquán de guǎnlǐ, yánlì dǎjí wéiguī xíngwéi. The government has tightened food safety management and cracked down hard on violations.

**良好的时间管理能力是职场成功的重要因素之一。** Liánghǎo de shíjiān guǎnlǐ nénglì shì zhíchǎng chénggōng de zhòngyào yīnsù zhī yī. Good time-management skills are one of the key factors for success in the workplace.

**这座城市推行智慧城市管理平台，大幅提升了公共服务效率。** Zhè zuò chéngshì tuīxíng zhìhuì chéngshì guǎnlǐ píngtái, dàfú tíshēngle gōnggòng fúwù xiàolǜ. This city has rolled out a smart city management platform, significantly improving public service efficiency.

**学生宿舍实行严格的门禁管理，要求晚上十一点前返回。** Xuéshēng sùshè shíxíng yángé de ménjìn guǎnlǐ, yāoqiú wǎnshàng shíyī diǎn qián fǎnhuí. The student dormitory enforces strict access management, requiring residents to return by eleven o'clock at night.

## Usage Notes

管理 is one of the most productive words in formal Chinese. As a verb it takes a direct object: 管理公司 (manage a company), 管理资源 (manage resources). As a noun it combines freely: 管理者 (manager), 管理层 (management tier), 管理学 (management studies).

At B2 level, learners should distinguish 管理 (manage/administer, implies authority and system) from 管 (to be in charge of, more colloquial) and 负责 (to be responsible for, emphasising duty rather than authority).

## Memory Hook

管 (tube/pipe/control) + 理 (organise/reason) -- channelling things through a structured pipe to keep them in order; that is management.
