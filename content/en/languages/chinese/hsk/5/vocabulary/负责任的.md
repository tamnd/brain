## 负责任的 (fùzérèn de)

负责任的 describes a person, organization, or system that takes responsibility for its actions and their consequences. It implies both a willingness to answer for one's conduct and a moral commitment to doing what is right.

## Meanings
1. **[adj]** accountable; responsible; willing to take responsibility for one's actions

## Example Sentences
**负责任的政府必须对公民的需求做出回应。** Fùzérèn de zhèngfǔ bìxū duì gōngmín de xūqiú zuòchū huíyìng. A responsible government must respond to the needs of its citizens.

**企业应该以负责任的方式使用自然资源。** Qǐyè yīnggāi yǐ fùzérèn de fāngshì shǐyòng zìrán zīyuán. Enterprises should use natural resources in a responsible manner.

**作为负责任的公民，我们有义务遵守法律。** Zuòwéi fùzérèn de gōngmín, wǒmen yǒu yìwù zūnshǒu fǎlǜ. As responsible citizens, we have an obligation to obey the law.

**她以负责任的态度处理了这次危机，获得了广泛好评。** Tā yǐ fùzérèn de tàidù chǔlǐle zhè cì wēijī, huòdéle guǎngfàn hǎopíng. She handled the crisis in a responsible manner and received widespread praise.

**培养负责任的领导者是教育的重要目标之一。** Péiyǎng fùzérèn de lǐngdǎozhě shì jiàoyù de zhòngyào mùbiāo zhī yī. Cultivating responsible leaders is one of the important goals of education.

**媒体也应扮演负责任的角色，避免传播错误信息。** Méitǐ yě yīng bànyǎn fùzérèn de juésè, bìmiǎn chuánbō cuòwù xìnxī. Media should also play a responsible role and avoid spreading misinformation.

## Usage Notes
负责任的 combines 负 (to bear/carry), 责任 (responsibility), and the adjective marker 的. Note that 负责 (fùzé) alone is a common verb meaning "to be in charge of / to be responsible for" something specific, while 负责任的 as an adjective describes a general character trait of accountability.

In formal contexts, 问责 (wèn zé -- to hold accountable) is the related verb used in governance and institutional settings, while 负责任的 describes a quality someone possesses.

## Memory Hook
负 is "to carry/bear", 责任 is "responsibility" -- 负责任的 is someone who carries their responsibility, i.e., accountable.
