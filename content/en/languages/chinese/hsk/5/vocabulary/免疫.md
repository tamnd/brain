## 免疫 (miǎnyì)

免疫 refers to the biological capacity of an organism to resist disease, or more broadly, to being protected from or unaffected by something harmful.

## Meanings

1. **[noun]** immunity (biological or metaphorical)
2. **[verb]** to be immune to, to have immunity against

## Example Sentences

**接种疫苗可以增强人体对疾病的免疫能力。** Jiēzhòng yìmiáo kěyǐ zēngqiáng réntǐ duì jíbìng de miǎnyì nénglì. Vaccination can enhance the body's immunity against disease.

**他的免疫系统因长期压力而变得脆弱。** Tā de miǎnyì xìtǒng yīn chángqī yālì ér biàn de cuìruò. His immune system became weakened due to long-term stress.

**这种病毒变异非常快，让人们难以产生有效免疫。** Zhè zhǒng bìngdú biànyì fēicháng kuài, ràng rénmen nán yǐ chǎnshēng yǒuxiào miǎnyì. This virus mutates so quickly that it is difficult for people to develop effective immunity.

**经历了那么多失败，他似乎对批评产生了免疫。** Jīnglì le nàme duō shībài, tā sìhū duì pīpíng chǎnshēng le miǎnyì. Having experienced so many failures, he seemed to have become immune to criticism.

**群体免疫是应对传染病的重要策略之一。** Qúntǐ miǎnyì shì yìngduì chuánrǎn bìng de zhòngyào cèlüè zhī yī. Herd immunity is one of the important strategies for dealing with infectious diseases.

**研究人员正在努力开发针对这种病毒的免疫疗法。** Yánjiū rényuán zhèngzài nǔlì kāifā zhēnduì zhè zhǒng bìngdú de miǎnyì liáofǎ. Researchers are working hard to develop immunotherapy against this virus.

## Usage Notes

免疫 is primarily a medical and biological term but is widely used metaphorically in everyday Chinese. The phrase 对...产生免疫 (to develop immunity to) can describe becoming desensitized to anything -- criticism, bad news, or even advertisements. This metaphorical use is common in informal writing and speech at B2 level.

The compound 免疫系统 (immune system) is extremely common in health-related content. 免疫力 (immunity, immune strength) is the everyday spoken variant, while 免疫能力 is slightly more formal.

## Memory Hook

免 means "to be exempt from" and 疫 means "epidemic/plague" -- 免疫 is being exempt from the plague, spared from disease.
