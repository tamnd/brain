## 新能源 (xīn néngyuán)

Energy derived from sources other than traditional fossil fuels, including solar, wind, hydroelectric, and hydrogen power -- central to global climate and energy policy discussions.

## Meanings
1. **[noun]** new energy, renewable energy, clean energy

## Example Sentences
**中国已成为全球最大的新能源汽车生产国。** Zhōngguó yǐ chéngwéi quánqiú zuì dà de xīn néngyuán qìchē shēngchǎn guó. China has become the world's largest producer of new energy vehicles.

**政府计划大幅增加新能源在能源结构中的比重。** Zhèngfǔ jìhuà dàfú zēngjiā xīn néngyuán zài néngyuán jiégòu zhōng de bǐzhòng. The government plans to significantly increase the share of new energy in the energy mix.

**太阳能和风能是目前应用最广泛的新能源形式。** Tàiyángnéng hé fēngnéng shì mùqián yìngyòng zuì guǎngfàn de xīn néngyuán xíngshì. Solar and wind energy are currently the most widely used forms of new energy.

**投资新能源领域既环保又有长期经济回报。** Tóuzī xīn néngyuán lǐngyù jì huánbǎo yòu yǒu chángqī jīngjì huíbào. Investing in the new energy sector is both environmentally friendly and offers long-term economic returns.

**新能源技术的突破是实现碳中和目标的关键。** Xīn néngyuán jìshù de tūpò shì shíxiàn tàn zhōnghé mùbiāo de guānjiàn. Breakthroughs in new energy technology are key to achieving carbon neutrality goals.

**农村地区推广新能源有助于改善能源贫困问题。** Nóngcūn dìqū tuīguǎng xīn néngyuán yǒuzhù yú gǎishàn néngyuán pínkùn wèntí. Promoting new energy in rural areas helps address the problem of energy poverty.

## Usage Notes
In Chinese policy discourse, 新能源 specifically emphasizes alternatives to coal, oil, and gas. The most prominent sub-category is 新能源汽车 (NEV, new energy vehicles), which has become a flagship phrase for China's industrial policy and is widely used in news.

Note that 可再生能源 (kě zài shēng néngyuán, renewable energy) is a more technical synonym, while 新能源 is the common policy and media term. B2 learners reading about climate change, industry, or China's green initiatives will encounter 新能源 constantly.

## Memory Hook
新 = new. 能源 = energy source. New energy is simply all the energy sources beyond the traditional fossil fuels -- the new alternatives powering the future.
