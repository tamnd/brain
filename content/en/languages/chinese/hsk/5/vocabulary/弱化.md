## 弱化 (ruòhuà)

弱化 means to weaken or dilute something, describing a process by which strength, intensity, or prominence is reduced, either deliberately or as a natural consequence.

## Meanings

1. **[verb]** to weaken, to dilute, to reduce in strength or prominence
2. **[verb]** to downplay, to de-emphasize (in discourse or policy)

## Example Sentences

**这项政策可能会弱化地方政府的自主权。** Zhè xiàng zhèngcè kěnéng huì ruòhuà dìfāng zhèngfǔ de zìzhǔquán. This policy may weaken the autonomy of local governments.

**长期缺乏锻炼会弱化肌肉力量和心肺功能。** Cháng qī quēfá duànliàn huì ruòhuà jīròu lìliàng hé xīn fèi gōngnéng. Long-term lack of exercise will weaken muscle strength and cardiopulmonary function.

**一些学者认为全球化有弱化民族文化的倾向。** Yīxiē xuézhě rènwéi quánqiú huà yǒu ruòhuà mínzú wénhuà de qīngxiàng. Some scholars believe that globalization has a tendency to weaken ethnic cultures.

**在这篇文章中，作者刻意弱化了政治因素的影响。** Zài zhè piān wénzhāng zhōng, zuòzhě kèyì ruòhuà le zhèngzhì yīnsù de yǐngxiǎng. In this article, the author deliberately downplayed the influence of political factors.

**语言接触会导致某些语法特征逐渐弱化。** Yǔyán jiēchù huì dǎozhì mǒu xiē yǔfǎ tèzhēng zhújiàn ruòhuà. Language contact can cause certain grammatical features to gradually weaken.

**管理层担心频繁的人事变动会弱化团队凝聚力。** Guǎnlǐ céng dānyōu pínfán de rénsì biàndòng huì ruòhuà tuánduì níngjùlì. Management fears that frequent personnel changes will weaken team cohesion.

## Usage Notes

弱化 is the antonym of 强化 and describes a reduction in strength, influence, or prominence. It can describe both intentional downplaying (刻意弱化 -- to deliberately dilute) and unintentional weakening due to external forces. In linguistic contexts, 弱化 refers to sound reduction or phonological weakening. In social and political contexts, it often carries a slightly negative connotation, suggesting undesirable loss of strength.

At B2 level, 弱化/强化 as an antonym pair is useful for nuanced academic and journalistic writing.

## Memory Hook

弱 means weak and 化 means to transform -- 弱化 is to transform something toward weakness, like diluting strong tea with too much water until the flavor fades.
