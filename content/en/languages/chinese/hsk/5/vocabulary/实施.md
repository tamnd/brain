## 实施 (shíshī)

To implement or carry out means to put a plan, policy, decision, or law into actual practice or effect.

## Meanings

1. **[verb]** to implement; to carry out; to put into effect; to execute (a plan or policy)

## Example Sentences

**新的环保法规将于明年开始实施。** Xīn de huánbǎo fǎguī jiāng yú míngnián kāishǐ shíshī. The new environmental regulations will come into effect next year.

**政府决定实施一系列经济刺激措施。** Zhèngfǔ juédìng shíshī yī xìliè jīngjì cìjī cuòshī. The government decided to implement a series of economic stimulus measures.

**这项改革方案已经实施了两年，效果显著。** Zhè xiàng gǎigé fāng'àn yǐjīng shíshīle liǎng nián, xiàoguǒ xiǎnzhù. This reform plan has been implemented for two years with remarkable results.

**在实施新政策之前，需要做好充分的准备工作。** Zài shíshī xīn zhèngcè zhīqián, xūyào zuòhǎo chōngfèn de zhǔnbèi gōngzuò. Before implementing the new policy, thorough preparation work needs to be done.

**该项目按计划顺利实施，达到了预期目标。** Gāi xiàngmù àn jìhuà shùnlì shíshī, dádào le yùqī mùbiāo. The project was implemented smoothly according to plan and achieved the expected goals.

**学校正在实施一套新的教学评估体系。** Xuéxiào zhèngzài shíshī yī tào xīn de jiàoxué pínggū tǐxì. The school is implementing a new teaching evaluation system.

## Usage Notes

实施 is a formal verb used primarily in institutional, governmental, and legal contexts. It implies that a decision or plan has been officially adopted and is now being put into action. It is stronger and more formal than 进行 (to carry out/conduct) and implies a degree of official authority behind the action.

Common collocates include policies (政策), laws (法律), plans (计划), measures (措施), and reforms (改革). The related noun form 实施方案 (implementation plan) is very common in official documents and project management.

## Memory Hook

实 (real/actual) + 施 (to apply/carry out) -- 实施 is to actually apply something in the real world, turning paper plans into action.
