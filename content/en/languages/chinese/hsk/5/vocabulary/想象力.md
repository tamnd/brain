## 想象力 (xiǎngxiànglì)

想象力 refers to the mental capacity or power to form images, ideas, and concepts not present to the senses, i.e., the imagination, especially in creative, artistic, or intellectual pursuits.

## Meanings

1. **[noun]** imagination; imaginative power; creative capacity

## Example Sentences

**儿童时期是培养想象力最关键的阶段，丰富的游戏和阅读体验能激发孩子的创造潜能。** Értóng shíqī shì péiyǎng xiǎngxiànglì zuì guānjiàn de jiēduàn, fēngfù de yóuxì hé yuèdú tǐyàn néng jīfā háizi de chuàngzào qiánnéng. Childhood is the most critical stage for developing imagination; rich play and reading experiences can stimulate children's creative potential.

**这位作家拥有惊人的想象力，他的小说总能带领读者进入一个充满奇幻色彩的世界。** Zhè wèi zuòjiā yōngyǒu jīngrén de xiǎngxiànglì, tā de xiǎoshuō zǒng néng dài lǐng dúzhě jìnrù yī gè chōngmǎn qíhuàn sècǎi de shìjiè. This writer has an astonishing imagination; his novels always lead readers into a world full of fantastical elements.

**科学的进步往往源于科学家们大胆的想象力，爱因斯坦曾说想象力比知识更重要。** Kēxué de jìnbù wǎngwǎng yuányú kēxuéjiāmen dǎdǎn de xiǎngxiànglì, Àiyīnsītǎn céng shuō xiǎngxiànglì bǐ zhīshì gèng zhòngyào. Scientific progress often stems from scientists' bold imagination; Einstein once said imagination is more important than knowledge.

**过度依赖电子设备可能会限制孩子的想象力和独立思考能力。** Guòdù yīlài diànzǐ shèbèi kěnéng huì xiànzhì háizi de xiǎngxiànglì hé dúlì sīkǎo nénglì. Excessive reliance on electronic devices may limit children's imagination and ability to think independently.

**艺术创作需要丰富的想象力，但也需要扎实的技术功底来加以实现。** Yìshù chuàngzuò xūyào fēngfù de xiǎngxiànglì, dàn yě xūyào zhāshí de jìshù gōngdǐ lái jiāyǐ shíxiàn. Artistic creation requires a rich imagination, but also solid technical skills to bring it to life.

**这部电影充分展示了导演的想象力，画面精美绝伦，令观众叹为观止。** Zhè bù diànyǐng chōngfèn zhǎnshì le dǎoyǎn de xiǎngxiànglì, huàmiàn jīngměi juélún, lìng guānzhòng tàn wéi guān zhǐ. This film fully demonstrates the director's imagination; the visuals are breathtakingly beautiful, leaving audiences in awe.

## Usage Notes

想象力 is formed from 想象 (to imagine) plus the suffix 力 (power/ability/force). This -力 suffix pattern is common for forming nouns meaning "capacity" or "ability" in Chinese: 创造力 (creativity), 判断力 (judgment), 执行力 (ability to execute).

想象力 is distinct from 想象 alone: 想象 is the verb (to imagine) or abstract noun (imagination as an act), while 想象力 specifically refers to the capacity or strength of one's imagination. It is frequently used in educational and artistic contexts.

## Memory Hook

想象 (to imagine) + 力 (power) — 想象力 is the power of imagining, the engine of creativity.
