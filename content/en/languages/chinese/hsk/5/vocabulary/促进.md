## 促进 (cùjìn)

促进 means to actively promote, advance, or facilitate something, implying that an agent is pushing a positive process forward, and is one of the most important formal verbs in Chinese official and academic language.

## Meanings

1. **[verb]** to promote; to advance; to facilitate
2. **[verb]** to further; to spur on; to accelerate a positive process

## Example Sentences

**经济发展有助于促进社会的整体进步。** Jīngjì fāzhǎn yǒuzhù yú cùjìn shèhuì de zhěngtǐ jìnbù. Economic development helps to promote the overall progress of society.

**这项协议旨在促进两国之间的贸易往来。** Zhè xiàng xiéyì zhǐ zài cùjìn liǎng guó zhījiān de màoyì wǎnglái. This agreement aims to promote trade between the two countries.

**良好的营养摄入可以促进儿童的身体发育。** Liánghǎo de yíngyǎng shèrù kěyǐ cùjìn értóng de shēntǐ fāyù. Good nutritional intake can promote children's physical development.

**政府出台了一系列措施来促进就业，稳定经济。** Zhèngfǔ chūtāile yī xìliè cuòshī lái cùjìn jiùyè, wěndìng jīngjì. The government introduced a series of measures to promote employment and stabilize the economy.

**国际交流促进了不同文化之间的相互理解。** Guójì jiāoliú cùjìnle bùtóng wénhuà zhījiān de xiānghù lǐjiě. International exchange has promoted mutual understanding between different cultures.

**技术创新是促进生产力发展的关键动力。** Jìshù chuàngxīn shì cùjìn shēngchǎnlì fāzhǎn de guānjiàn dònglì. Technological innovation is a key driving force in promoting the development of productivity.

## Usage Notes

促进 is one of the most characteristic verbs of formal Chinese, especially in government documents, policy texts, and academic writing. It always implies a positive, beneficial process being advanced by an active agent. It is distinct from 推动 (tuīdòng), which also means to promote/drive but carries a stronger sense of giving a push to something that needed impetus, while 促进 suggests facilitating and nurturing.

The construction 促进……的发展 (to promote the development of...) is a ubiquitous phrase in Chinese official language. At B2, learners should use 促进 when describing beneficial facilitation in economic, social, cultural, or political contexts.

## Memory Hook

CU (促, to urge/push) + JIN (进, to advance/move forward) = to urge something to move forward.
