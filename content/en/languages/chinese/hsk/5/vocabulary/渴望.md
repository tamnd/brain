## 渴望 (kěwàng)

渴望 expresses a deep, intense desire or longing for something -- much stronger than simply wanting. It conveys an emotional urgency comparable to physical thirst.

## Meanings
1. **[verb]** to yearn for; to long for; to crave; to thirst for
2. **[noun]** yearning; longing; strong desire

## Example Sentences
**她渴望有一天能够环游世界，探索不同的文化。** Tā kěwàng yǒu yītiān nénggòu huányóu shìjiè, tànsuǒ bùtóng de wénhuà. She yearns for the day she can travel around the world and explore different cultures.

**孩子们渴望父母的陪伴和关爱。** Háizimenrén kěwàng fùmǔ de péibàn hé guān'ài. Children deeply long for their parents' company and affection.

**他对知识的渴望驱使他不断学习新事物。** Tā duì zhīshi de kěwàng qūshǐ tā bùduàn xuéxí xīn shìwù. His thirst for knowledge drives him to constantly learn new things.

**经过多年的努力，她终于实现了自己渴望已久的梦想。** Jīngguò duō nián de nǔlì, tā zhōngyú shíxiànle zìjǐ kěwàng yǐ jiǔ de mèngxiǎng. After years of hard work, she finally achieved the dream she had long yearned for.

**人们普遍渴望和平与稳定的生活环境。** Rénmen pǔbiàn kěwàng hépíng yǔ wěndìng de shēnghuó huánjìng. People generally yearn for a peaceful and stable living environment.

**他的眼神中流露出对自由的强烈渴望。** Tā de yǎnshén zhōng liúlùchū duì zìyóu de qiángliè kěwàng. His eyes revealed an intense longing for freedom.

## Usage Notes
渴望 is emotionally stronger than 希望 (xīwàng, hope/wish) and 想要 (xiǎngyào, want). The character 渴 literally means "thirsty" (water radical + 曷), emphasizing the urgency and intensity of the desire -- like being physically parched and desperate for water.

渴望 can take a noun or a verb phrase as its object: 渴望自由 (yearn for freedom), 渴望成功 (long for success), or 渴望能见到你 (long to see you). It is used in both literary and everyday contexts, though it carries a more emotional and formal register than simply saying 想 (want).

## Memory Hook
渴 = thirsty (the desperation of physical thirst), 望 = look/hope -- like being thirsty and desperately looking for water -- to intensely yearn for something.
