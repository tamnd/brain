## 证明 (zhèngmíng)

证明 is both the act of proving something through reasoning or evidence and a formal document that certifies a fact, making it a versatile word in legal, academic, and everyday administrative contexts.

## Meanings
1. **[verb]** to prove; to demonstrate; to certify
2. **[noun]** proof; certificate; certification document

## Example Sentences
**他的成功证明了努力终将得到回报。** Tā de chénggōng zhèngmíng le nǔlì zhōng jiāng dédào huíbào. His success proves that hard work will eventually be rewarded.

**你需要提供收入证明才能申请贷款。** Nǐ xūyào tígōng shōurù zhèngmíng cái néng shēnqǐng dàikuǎn. You need to provide proof of income to apply for a loan.

**实验结果证明了这一假设的正确性。** Shíyàn jiéguǒ zhèngmíng le zhè yī jiǎshè de zhèngquèxìng. The experimental results proved the correctness of this hypothesis.

**无法证明某人有罪，就应假定其无罪。** Wúfǎ zhèngmíng mǒu rén yǒuzuì, jiù yīng jiǎdìng qí wúzuì. If you cannot prove someone is guilty, you should presume them innocent.

**他向银行出示了身份证明文件。** Tā xiàng yínháng chūshì le shēnfèn zhèngmíng wénjiàn. He showed the bank his identity verification document.

**历史证明，封闭的国家很难实现持续发展。** Lìshǐ zhèngmíng, fēngbì de guójiā hěn nán shíxiàn chíxù fāzhǎn. History proves that closed countries find it difficult to achieve sustained development.

## Usage Notes
证明 has two distinct grammatical functions. As a verb it means "to prove" and is followed by a clause or noun phrase. As a noun it means "proof" or "certificate" and is frequently found in administrative contexts (开证明, to get a certificate; 出示证明, to present a certificate).

In academic and argumentative writing the structure [事实/研究/数据] + 证明 + [conclusion] is very common, similar to 表明 (biǎomíng) but with a stronger sense of definitive proof.

## Memory Hook
证 (to certify/verify) + 明 (to clarify/make clear): certifying something until it is perfectly clear -- to prove.
