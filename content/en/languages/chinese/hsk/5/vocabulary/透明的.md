## 透明的 (tòumíng de)

透明的 describes something that is open, clear, and visible -- whether physically transparent (like glass) or figuratively transparent (like an open government process). In social and political contexts, it refers to accessibility of information and accountability.

## Meanings
1. **[adj]** transparent; clear and open; not hidden or secretive

## Example Sentences
**政府需要建立透明的财政管理制度。** Zhèngfǔ xūyào jiànlì tòumíng de cáizhèng guǎnlǐ zhìdù. The government needs to establish a transparent financial management system.

**这家企业以透明的运营方式赢得了投资者的信任。** Zhè jiā qǐyè yǐ tòumíng de yùnyíng fāngshì yíngdéle tóuzīzhě de xìnrèn. This company has won investors' trust with its transparent operations.

**透明的信息披露是资本市场健康运行的前提。** Tòumíng de xìnxī pīlù shì zīběn shìchǎng jiànkāng yùnxíng de qiántí. Transparent information disclosure is a prerequisite for the healthy operation of capital markets.

**科学研究的过程应该是透明的，以便他人重复验证。** Kēxué yánjiū de guòchéng yīnggāi shì tòumíng de, yǐbiàn tārén chóngfù yànzhèng. The process of scientific research should be transparent so that others can replicate and verify it.

**透明的采购程序有助于减少腐败现象。** Tòumíng de cǎigòu chéngxù yǒuzhù yú jiǎnshǎo fǔbài xiànxiàng. Transparent procurement procedures help reduce corruption.

**公众要求政府在决策上更加透明。** Gōngzhòng yāoqiú zhèngfǔ zài juécè shàng gèngjiā tòumíng. The public demands that the government be more transparent in its decision-making.

## Usage Notes
透明的 can describe both physical transparency (e.g., 透明的玻璃 -- transparent glass) and figurative transparency (e.g., 透明的政策 -- transparent policy). In governance, business, and academia, the figurative use is far more common at B2 level and above.

The related noun 透明度 (tòumíngdù -- transparency/degree of transparency) is frequently used in formal writing, e.g., 提高透明度 (increase transparency).

## Memory Hook
透 means "to penetrate/pass through" and 明 means "bright/clear" -- 透明 is something light passes through clearly, whether glass or information.
