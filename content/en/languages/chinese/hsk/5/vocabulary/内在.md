## 内在 (nèizài)

内在 describes qualities, forces, or characteristics that exist within something inherently, as opposed to those imposed or visible from the outside.

## Meanings

1. **[adjective]** intrinsic, internal, inherent, inner

## Example Sentences

**真正的美来自内在，而不仅仅是外表。** Zhēnzhèng de měi láizì nèizài, ér bù jǐnjǐn shì wàibiǎo. True beauty comes from within, not just from appearance.

**我们需要理解事物发展的内在规律。** Wǒmen xūyào lǐjiě shìwù fāzhǎn de nèizài guīlǜ. We need to understand the intrinsic laws governing the development of things.

**内在动机比外在奖励更能持久地激励人。** Nèizài dòngjī bǐ wàizài jiǎnglì gèng néng chíjiǔ de jīlì rén. Intrinsic motivation can inspire people more sustainably than external rewards.

**这两个概念之间存在内在的逻辑联系。** Zhè liǎng gè gàiniàn zhījiān cúnzài nèizài de luójí liánxì. There is an intrinsic logical connection between these two concepts.

**艺术家致力于挖掘人类内在的情感世界。** Yìshùjiā zhìlì yú wājué rénlèi nèizài de qínggǎn shìjiè. The artist is dedicated to excavating the inner emotional world of humanity.

**提升内在修养比追求物质财富更能带来持久的幸福感。** Tíshēng nèizài xiūyǎng bǐ zhuīqiú wùzhì cáifù gèng néng dài lái chíjiǔ de xìngfú gǎn. Improving inner cultivation brings more lasting happiness than pursuing material wealth.

## Usage Notes

内在 is the antonym of 外在 (external, extrinsic). Together, this pair is very commonly used in philosophical, psychological, and educational writing to contrast what comes from within versus what comes from outside. The phrase 内在动机 vs. 外在动机 (intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation) is standard terminology in educational psychology.

内在 is typically used before nouns as an attributive adjective (内在联系, 内在规律) or in the phrase 来自内在 (comes from within). It is more literary and formal than saying 里面的 for "inside," which is a spatial description rather than a philosophical one.

## Memory Hook

内 means "inside/inner" and 在 means "to be/exist" -- 内在 is what "exists inside," the hidden essence that dwells within, not the surface that the world sees.
