## 权利 (quánlì)

A legal or moral entitlement that an individual or group possesses -- the right to do something or to be treated in a certain way, as guaranteed by law or ethics.

## Meanings
1. **[noun]** right, entitlement, legal right

## Example Sentences
**每个公民都有接受教育的基本权利。** Měi gè gōngmín dōu yǒu jiēshòu jiàoyù de jīběn quánlì. Every citizen has the basic right to receive education.

**法律明确保障了劳动者的合法权利。** Fǎlǜ míngquè bǎozhàngle láodòngzhě de héfǎ quánlì. The law clearly protects the legal rights of workers.

**消费者有权利要求退款或换货。** Xiāofèizhě yǒu quánlì yāoqiú tuìkuǎn huò huànhuò. Consumers have the right to demand a refund or exchange.

**保护人权是国际社会的共同责任。** Bǎohù rénquán shì guójì shèhuì de gòngtóng zérèn. Protecting human rights is a shared responsibility of the international community.

**她坚持维护自己的知识产权，不允许他人侵犯。** Tā jiānchí wéihù zìjǐ de zhīshí chǎnquán, bù yǔnxǔ tārén qīnfàn. She insists on protecting her intellectual property rights and does not allow others to infringe on them.

**联合国《世界人权宣言》列举了每个人应享有的基本权利。** Liánhéguó "Shìjiè Rénquán Xuānyán" lièjǔle měi gè rén yīng xiǎngyǒu de jīběn quánlì. The United Nations' Universal Declaration of Human Rights enumerates the basic rights that every person should enjoy.

## Usage Notes
权利 (rights/entitlements) must be carefully distinguished from its near-homophone 权力 (quánlì, power/authority). Though identical in pronunciation, their meanings are opposite in social register: 权利 is what citizens or individuals are owed; 权力 is the power or authority that institutions or officials hold. Confusing them is a very common learner error.

A useful test: if you can substitute "power" in English, use 权力. If you can substitute "rights," use 权利.

## Memory Hook
Both 权利 and 权力 share 权 (rights/authority). The key is the second character: 利 (benefit/profit) -- rights are the benefits you are owed. 力 (strength/force) -- power is the force you wield. Rights benefit you; power enables you.
