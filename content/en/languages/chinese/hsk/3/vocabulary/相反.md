## 相反 (xiāngfǎn)

相反 means "opposite" or "contrary." As an adjective it describes two things that are diametrically opposed. As an adverb (often 相反地 or as a sentence connector), it introduces a statement that is the reverse of what was just said — similar to English "on the contrary."

## Meanings

1. **[adjective]** Opposite; contrary; reverse — describes two things that point in opposite directions.
2. **[adverb]** On the contrary; instead — introduces a statement that is the reverse of the previous one.

## Example Sentences

**我以为他会生气，相反，他笑了。**
Wǒ yǐwéi tā huì shēngqì, xiāngfǎn, tā xiào le.
I thought he would be angry; on the contrary, he smiled.

**他们两人性格完全相反。**
Tāmen liǎng rén xìnggé wánquán xiāngfǎn.
The two of them have completely opposite personalities.

**这两种方法方向相反，不能同时使用。**
Zhè liǎng zhǒng fāngfǎ fāngxiàng xiāngfǎn, bù néng tóngshí shǐyòng.
These two methods go in opposite directions and cannot be used at the same time.

**多说不一定有用，相反，有时候安静更重要。**
Duō shuō bù yīdìng yǒuyòng, xiāngfǎn, yǒushíhòu ānjìng gèng zhòngyào.
Talking a lot is not necessarily helpful; on the contrary, sometimes being quiet is more important.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 恰恰相反 | exactly the opposite |
| 与…相反 | contrary to; opposite to |
| 方向相反 | in opposite directions |
| 性格相反 | opposite in character |
| 结果相反 | the opposite result |

## Memory Hook

相 = "mutually"; 反 = "to flip/reverse." 相反 = two things flipping against each other — a perfect mirror image of opposition.
