## 钥匙 (yàoshi)

钥匙 is the everyday word for a key — the physical object used to lock and unlock doors, cars, cabinets, and so on. The second character 匙 (spoon / key) carries a neutral tone in speech; the full word is pronounced yàoshi. It is also used metaphorically for the "key" to solving a problem.

## Meanings

1. **[noun]** Key (for a lock); a physical key.
2. **[noun]** Key (figurative) — the crucial element that unlocks a solution.

## Example Sentences

**我找不到钥匙了，进不了门。**
Wǒ zhǎo bù dào yàoshi le, jìn bù liǎo mén.
I can't find the key; I can't get through the door.

**请把钥匙放在桌子上。**
Qǐng bǎ yàoshi fàng zài zhuōzi shàng.
Please put the key on the table.

**他忘记带钥匙了，只好等朋友回来。**
Tā wàngjì dài yàoshi le, zhǐhǎo děng péngyǒu huílái.
He forgot to bring his key and had no choice but to wait for his friend to come back.

**努力是成功的钥匙。**
Nǔlì shì chénggōng de yàoshi.
Hard work is the key to success.

## Memory Hook

钥 has the metal radical 钅 (for metallic objects) and 匙 looks like a tiny spoon shape — which is exactly what an old-fashioned key resembles: a metal spoon that fits a lock.
