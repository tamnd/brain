## 毕竟 (bìjìng)

毕竟 means "after all" in the sense of pointing to an essential, underlying, or final truth. It is used to justify, explain, or concede by invoking a fundamental fact that cannot be argued with. It often softens criticism or explains why something is the way it is.

## Meanings

1. **[adverb]** After all; all in all; ultimately; when all is said and done — introduces the fundamental reason or unavoidable truth behind a situation.

## Example Sentences

**他毕竟还是个孩子，不要太严格了。**
Tā bìjìng hái shì gè háizi, bú yào tài yángé le.
After all, he is still a child; don't be too strict with him.

**这道菜不太好吃，毕竟是我第一次做。**
Zhè dào cài bú tài hǎochī, bìjìng shì wǒ dì yī cì zuò.
The dish does not taste great, but after all it was my first time making it.

**你毕竟认识他多年，相信他的判断吧。**
Nǐ bìjìng rènshi tā duō nián, xiāngxìn tā de pànduàn ba.
After all, you have known him for many years; trust his judgment.

**毕竟，健康比金钱更重要。**
Bìjìng, jiànkāng bǐ jīnqián gèng zhòngyào.
When all is said and done, health is more important than money.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 毕竟是 | after all, it is… |
| 毕竟还是 | in the end, still… |
| 毕竟不同 | after all, it is different |
| 毕竟有限 | ultimately limited |
| 毕竟年轻 | after all, (one is) young |

## Memory Hook

毕 means "to complete/finish" and 竟 means "to the end / after all." Together: "having finished and gone to the very end" — that final, unavoidable truth you reach after everything is considered.
