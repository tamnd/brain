## 接 (jiē)

接 is a versatile verb with several related meanings: to pick someone up (from a place), to answer a phone call, or to receive something passed to you. All meanings share the core idea of bringing something toward you or making a connection.

## Meanings

1. **[verb]** To pick up a person; to go and collect someone.
2. **[verb]** To answer (a phone call): 接电话.
3. **[verb]** To receive or catch something handed over.

## Example Sentences

**我去机场接朋友。**
Wǒ qù jīchǎng jiē péngyou.
I am going to the airport to pick up my friend.

**他正在接电话，请稍等。**
Tā zhèngzài jiē diànhuà, qǐng shāo děng.
He is answering a phone call; please wait a moment.

**她接住了我扔过去的球。**
Tā jiēzhù le wǒ rēng guòqù de qiú.
She caught the ball I threw to her.

**孩子放学了，你去接他吗？**
Háizi fàngxué le, nǐ qù jiē tā ma?
School is out. Are you going to pick the child up?

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 接电话 | to answer the phone |
| 接机 | to pick someone up at the airport |
| 接孩子 | to pick up a child (from school) |
| 接受 | to accept; to receive |
| 接着 | continuing; following (then) |

## Memory Hook

接 has a hand radical (扌). Picture your hand reaching out to connect with someone or something coming toward you, whether a person, a call, or a ball.
