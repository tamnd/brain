## 打折 (dǎzhé)

打折 means to give a discount or sell at a reduced price. 折 refers to a fraction of the original price, so 八折 means 80% of the price, which is a 20% discount. It is commonly used in shopping contexts.

## Meanings

1. **[verb]** To give a discount; to sell at a reduced price.
2. **[verb]** To fall short of a standard (figurative, less common).

## Example Sentences

**这家店今天所有商品打八折。**
Zhè jiā diàn jīntiān suǒyǒu shāngpǐn dǎ bā zhé.
This shop has a 20% discount on all items today.

**你们有打折活动吗？**
Nǐmen yǒu dǎzhé huódòng ma?
Do you have any discount promotions?

**这件衣服打折以后只要一百块。**
Zhè jiàn yīfu dǎzhé yǐhòu zhǐ yào yī bǎi kuài.
After the discount, this piece of clothing is only 100 yuan.

**商场节假日经常打折。**
Shāngchǎng jiéjià rì jīngcháng dǎzhé.
Shopping malls often have discounts during holidays.

## Memory Hook

打 means "to hit" and 折 means "to fold." Imagine folding the price tag in half to hit a lower number — that is a discount. In Chinese pricing, 八折 means 80% of the original price (a 20% discount), 七折 means 70% (30% off), and 五折 means half price. The number before 折 is the percentage you actually pay, not the amount you save.
