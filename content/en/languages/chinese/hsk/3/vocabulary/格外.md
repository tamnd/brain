## 格外 (géwài)

格外 is an adverb meaning "especially," "particularly," or "unusually," used when something stands out as noticeably beyond the ordinary or expected level.

## Meanings

1. **[adverb]** especially; particularly; unusually (indicates a quality is markedly beyond what is normal or expected in the current context)

## Usage Note

格外 is placed before an adjective or verb and implies a comparison with a baseline. It emphasizes that in this particular situation, the quality is notably stronger than usual. It often carries a slightly subjective, experiential tone.

Compare with 特别 (tèbié) and 尤其 (yóuqí):
- 格外 focuses on "beyond the usual baseline in this moment" (e.g., the air feels unusually fresh today)
- 特别 is a general intensifier similar to "especially / very"
- 尤其 means "in particular," used to single out one item from a group

## Example Sentences

**节日的时候，街上格外热闹。** Jiérì de shíhou, jiē shàng géwài rènào. During the holiday, the streets are unusually lively.

**雨后的空气格外清新。** Yǔ hòu de kōngqì géwài qīngxīn. The air after rain is especially fresh.

**离家在外，才格外想念父母。** Lí jiā zài wài, cái géwài xiǎngniàn fùmǔ. When away from home, one misses one's parents all the more.

**他今天格外安静，不知道发生了什么事。** Tā jīntiān géwài ānjìng, bù zhīdào fāshēng le shénme shì. He is unusually quiet today; I wonder what happened.

## Memory Hook

格 means "standard / pattern" and 外 means "outside / beyond." Something that is 格外 is outside the standard pattern, meaning it exceeds what is normal, making it "especially" or "unusually" so.
