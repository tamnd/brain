## 其实 (qíshí)

其实 means "actually" or "in fact." It introduces the real truth of a situation, often gently correcting a mistaken belief, assumption, or appearance. The speaker signals: what you think is not quite right — here is what is truly the case.

## Meanings

1. **[adverb]** Actually; in fact; as a matter of fact; in reality — reveals a truth that contrasts with appearance or assumption.

## Example Sentences

**他看起来很严肃，其实很幽默。**
Tā kàn qǐlái hěn yánsù, qíshí hěn yōumò.
He looks very serious, but he is actually very humorous.

**这道题看起来难，其实很简单。**
Zhè dào tí kàn qǐlái nán, qíshí hěn jiǎndān.
This question looks difficult, but in fact it is quite easy.

**我其实早就知道了，只是没有说。**
Wǒ qíshí zǎo jiù zhīdào le, zhǐshì méiyǒu shuō.
I actually knew long ago; I just did not say anything.

**其实我们并不陌生，以前见过面。**
Qíshí wǒmen bìng bù mòshēng, yǐqián jiànguò miàn.
Actually, we are not strangers at all; we have met before.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 其实不然 | actually that is not the case |
| 其实很简单 | actually quite simple |
| 其实并不 | actually not at all |
| 说其实 | to be honest; to tell the truth |
| 其实是这样 | the truth of the matter is this |

## Memory Hook

其 = "that/the"; 实 = "reality/substance." 其实 points directly at "the reality of things" — the real substance behind what appears on the surface.
