## 菜 (cài)

菜 is a broad word covering both dishes of food (cooked) and vegetables (raw produce). As a dish it means any course served at a meal. As a vegetable it is the general everyday term. For the specific category of vegetables, 蔬菜 is more precise.

## Meanings

1. **[noun]** Dish; course; a prepared food item served at a meal.
2. **[noun]** Vegetable; greens; produce (general and colloquial).

## Example Sentences

**这道菜真好吃！**
Zhè dào cài zhēn hǎo chī!
This dish is really delicious!

**妈妈每天去市场买菜。**
Māma měitiān qù shìchǎng mǎi cài.
Mom goes to the market to buy vegetables every day.

**你会做哪些中国菜？**
Nǐ huì zuò nǎ xiē zhōngguó cài?
Which Chinese dishes can you cook?

**桌上的菜都是我妈妈做的。**
Zhuō shang de cài dōu shì wǒ māma zuò de.
All the dishes on the table were made by my mom.

## Collocations

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 做菜 | to cook a dish |
| 买菜 | to buy vegetables / groceries |
| 点菜 | to order dishes (at a restaurant) |
| 中国菜 | Chinese cuisine |
| 一道菜 | one dish (measure word 道) |

## Memory Hook

菜 has 草 (grass/plant) at the top, showing its plant origin. Whether you are talking about a plate of stir-fried greens or an elaborate dinner course, 菜 is the food that starts from the garden.
