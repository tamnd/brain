The 214 Kangxi radicals (康熙部首, Kāngxī bùshǒu) are the indexing system for Chinese characters, established in the 1716 Kangxi Dictionary. Every character is classified under one radical.

## Why Learn Radicals

1. **Dictionary lookup** — Traditional method for looking up characters without knowing their pronunciation
2. **Pattern recognition** — Group ~50,000 characters into 214 families
3. **Meaning hints** — Semantic radicals indicate the meaning category of a character
4. **Memory scaffold** — Learn 214 units instead of memorizing 3,000+ characters in isolation

## How to Use Radicals

**For dictionary lookup**: If you don't know the pronunciation of a character, count its strokes, identify its radical, then look up the remaining strokes.

**For learning**: When you encounter a new character, ask:
1. What radical does it use? (semantic hint)
2. What is the phonetic component? (pronunciation hint)
3. What other characters share this radical?

**Example analysis — 清 qīng (clear/pure)**:
- 氵(water) = semantic: relates to water/liquids
- 青 qīng = phonetic: suggests pronunciation qīng
- Related characters: 请qǐng (please), 情qíng (emotion), 晴qíng (sunny)

## Component vs. Radical

A **radical** (部首) is the one component used to **index** a character in a dictionary.
A **component** (部件) is any recurring structural element.

Not every character has an obvious semantic radical — some radicals were assigned based on obscure ancient forms. For learning, focus on components that appear frequently and hint at meaning.
