## Family Members (家庭成员 jiātíng chéngyuán)

> **Note on Chinese address terms:** Chinese distinguishes between paternal and maternal relatives. Your father's parents are 爷爷/奶奶 (yéye/nǎinai), while your mother's parents are 外公/外婆 (wàigōng/wàipó). Siblings are also addressed differently based on relative age: 哥哥 (older brother) vs 弟弟 (younger brother).

## Key Grammar Notes

**Expressing possession:** Use 的 (de) between pronoun and family term:
- 我的爸爸 (wǒ de bàba) — my father
- 他的妻子 (tā de qīzi) — his wife

**Counting family members:** Use 口 (kǒu) as measure word:
- 我家有三口人。(Wǒ jiā yǒu sān kǒu rén.) — My family has three people.

**Addressing vs. referring:** In Chinese, you call your father 爸爸 to his face but may say 我爸 (wǒ bà) when referring to him in conversation.

**Paternal vs. Maternal distinction:**
| Relative | Paternal Side | Maternal Side |
|----------|---------------|---------------|
| Grandfather | 爷爷 yéye | 外公 wàigōng / 姥爷 lǎoye |
| Grandmother | 奶奶 nǎinai | 外婆 wàipó / 姥姥 lǎolao |
| Uncle | 叔叔 shūshu / 伯伯 bóbo | 舅舅 jiùjiu |
| Aunt | 姑姑 gūgu | 阿姨 āyí |
| Cousin | 堂哥/堂弟/堂姐/堂妹 | 表哥/表弟/表姐/表妹 |
