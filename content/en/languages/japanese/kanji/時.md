## 時

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Meaning | time, hour |
| Strokes | 10 |
| JLPT Level | N5 |
| On-reading (音読み) | ジ |
| Kun-reading (訓読み) | とき |
| Radical | 日 (Sun/Day, U+65E5) |

## Stroke Order

The stroke order follows the standard rule of top-to-bottom and left-to-right.
1. **Left side (日):** Vertical stroke (1) → corner stroke (2) → horizontal middle (3) → bottom closure (4).
2. **Right side (寺):** Top horizontal (5) → vertical (6) → horizontal (7) → horizontal (8) → vertical hook (9) → final dot (10).

## Radical Analysis

The kanji **時** is a phono-semantic compound:
*   **Radical:** **日** (sun/day). This provides the semantic meaning related to time and the sun's movement.
*   **Component:** **寺** (temple). This acts as the phonetic component (providing the *ji* sound) and also carries the connotation of a place where time was traditionally measured (by bells or sun-shadows).

## Mnemonic

Imagine the **Sun** (**日**) standing behind the **Temple** (**寺**). As the sun moves across the temple roof, the shadow changes, telling us exactly what **time** (**時**) it is.

## Common Confusion Pairs

| Kanji | Reading | Meaning | How to distinguish |
|-------|---------|---------|-------------------|
| **時** | ジ / とき | Time | Contains the **日** (sun) radical. |
| **寺** | ジ / てら | Temple | Lacks the **日** radical; represents a building. |
| **持** | ジ / も(つ) | Hold | Contains the **扌** (hand) radical; used for "holding." |

## Usage Frequency Note

**時** is one of the most frequently used kanji in the Japanese language. It appears in almost every daily conversation, news report, and written document. Because it is essential for expressing time, duration, and specific moments, it is one of the first kanji learners should master. It is equally common in formal writing and casual speech.
