## 天

| Property | Value |

| Meaning | heaven, sky |
| Strokes | 4 |
| JLPT Level | N5 |
| On-reading (音読み) | テン |
| Kun-reading (訓読み) | あめ、あま |
| Radical | 大 (dai - big) [U+5927] |

## Stroke Order

The character **天** follows standard Japanese stroke order rules: top-to-bottom and left-to-right.

1. **Stroke 1:** A short horizontal line at the top.
2. **Stroke 2:** A second, slightly longer horizontal line below the first.
3. **Stroke 3:** A left-slanting stroke (払 - *harai*) starting from the center of the second stroke.
4. **Stroke 4:** A right-slanting stroke (捺 - *natsu*) starting from the same intersection as stroke 3.

## Radical Analysis

The radical for **天** is **大** (big). Historically, **天** is a pictograph of a person (*大*) with a line drawn over their head to represent the vast, infinite sky or heaven above them. The "big" radical here emphasizes the vastness of the heavens.

## Mnemonic

Imagine a person (**大**) standing on the ground. They are so tall that they can reach up and touch the sky (**天**) with their head. The two horizontal lines represent the layers of the clouds or the heavens above the person.

## Common Confusion Pairs

| Kanji | Reading | Meaning | How to distinguish |
|-------|---------|---------|-------------------|
| **天** | テン | Heaven/Sky | Has a horizontal line above the "person" shape. |
| **夫** | フ / おっと | Husband | The top stroke is a horizontal line, but it does not have the second horizontal line; it looks like a person with a "head" bar. |
| **夭** | ヨウ | Young/Die young | The top part is a "katakana" *no* (ノ) shape, not a horizontal line. |

## Usage Frequency Note

**天** is a high-frequency kanji. It is essential for daily life, primarily appearing in the word *tenki* (weather). It is used in both formal writing and casual speech. You will encounter it daily in weather reports, news, and general conversation.
