## Stroke Order

The kanji **雨** follows standard top-to-bottom, left-to-right rules. It is a "crown" (かんむり) radical, meaning it sits atop other characters.

1. **Stroke 1:** Top horizontal line (slightly slanted).
2. **Stroke 2:** The left vertical line.
3. **Stroke 3:** The top-right corner (horizontal then vertical).
4. **Stroke 4:** The middle horizontal line inside the box.
5. **Stroke 5:** The central vertical line.
6. **Stroke 6:** The left dot.
7. **Stroke 7:** The middle dot.
8. **Stroke 8:** The right dot (or the final stroke of the internal design).

## Radical Analysis

The kanji **雨** is a **pictograph**. It represents a window or the sky with raindrops falling down. 
*   **The Radical:** The entire character is the radical itself (雨部). When used as a component on top of other kanji, it is called *ame-kanmuri*.
*   **Meaning Contribution:** Any kanji containing this radical usually relates to weather or atmospheric phenomena (e.g., 雪 *yuki* - snow, 雲 *kumo* - cloud, 雷 *kaminari* - thunder).

## Mnemonic

Imagine the top horizontal line is the sky. The box is a window frame, and the four dots inside are raindrops falling down through the window pane. When you see this shape, think: "Rain is falling through the window!"

## Common Confusion Pairs

| Kanji | Reading | Meaning | How to distinguish |
|-------|---------|---------|-------------------|
| **雨** | あめ | rain | Has 4 dots inside representing rain. |
| **雲** | くも | cloud | Has the rain radical on top, but a "cloud" base (ム) below. |
| **雪** | ゆき | snow | Has the rain radical on top, but a "broom" (ヨ) base below. |

## Usage Frequency Note

**雨** is a high-frequency kanji used daily in weather reports, casual conversation, and literature. It is one of the first kanji learned by students. Because it is a radical, mastering its shape is essential for learning many other weather-related kanji.
