## Stroke Order

The kanji **毎** is written in 6 strokes. Follow the standard rule of top-to-bottom and left-to-right:

1. **Stroke 1**: The short horizontal line at the top.
2. **Stroke 2**: The vertical line with a hook at the bottom.
3. **Stroke 3**: The left dot.
4. **Stroke 4**: The right dot.
5. **Stroke 5**: The horizontal line connecting the dots.
6. **Stroke 6**: The final horizontal line at the bottom.

*Note: The radical 母 (mother) is formed by strokes 3 through 6. The two dots inside represent the breasts of a mother, emphasizing the "nurturing" or "recurring" nature of the character.*

## Radical Analysis

The main radical is **母** (mother). Originally, this character depicted a person with breasts, symbolizing a mother. In **毎**, the top part (the horizontal line and the vertical hook) acts as a phonetic or structural modifier, while the "mother" radical provides the base. Over time, the meaning shifted to "every" because a mother's care is constant and recurring.

## Mnemonic

Imagine a **mother** (母) who is so busy that she has to do everything **every** (毎) single day. She has a "to-do" list that starts with a checkmark at the top (the first two strokes) and then she repeats her chores for her children, day after day.

## Common Confusion Pairs

| Kanji | Reading | Meaning | How to distinguish |
|-------|---------|---------|-------------------|
| **毎** | マイ | Every | Has two dots inside the bottom part. |
| **母** | はは | Mother | The base character without the top horizontal/vertical bar. |
| **海** | うみ | Sea | Contains the water radical (氵) on the left. |

## Usage Frequency Note

**毎** is one of the most frequently used kanji in the Japanese language. It is essential for daily communication, appearing in almost every conversation regarding schedules, habits, and time. It is used equally in formal writing and casual speech.
