## 左

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Meaning | left |
| Strokes | 5 |
| JLPT Level | N5 |
| On-reading (音読み) | サ |
| Kun-reading (訓読み) | ひだり |
| Radical | 工 (たくみ/こう) [U+5DE5] |

## Stroke Order

The stroke order for 左 follows the rule of writing horizontal strokes before vertical/diagonal ones, and top-to-bottom.

1. **Stroke 1**: A short horizontal stroke starting from the top left.
2. **Stroke 2**: A long, sweeping diagonal stroke starting from the top center, moving down to the left.
3. **Stroke 3**: A horizontal stroke forming the top of the "工" shape.
4. **Stroke 4**: A vertical stroke connecting the center of the horizontal strokes.
5. **Stroke 5**: A horizontal base stroke at the bottom.

## Radical Analysis

The main radical is **工 (たくみ/こう)**, which represents a carpenter's square or a tool. In the kanji 左, the top component (the "撇" or "slash" and "horizontal") is said to represent a **hand (ナ)**. Historically, the kanji depicts a hand working with a tool, which evolved to specifically mean "left." 

*Note: Compare this to 右 (right), where the top component is 口 (mouth), representing a hand bringing food to the mouth.*

## Mnemonic

**The Left-Handed Carpenter:** 
Imagine a carpenter (**工**) using his left hand to hold a tool. The top two strokes look like a hand reaching out to the left. If you are "left-handed," you use your **左** (ひだり) hand to hold the **工** (tool).

## Common Confusion Pairs

| Kanji | Reading | Meaning | How to distinguish |
|-------|---------|---------|-------------------|
| **左** | ひだり | left | The top stroke is a slash (撇). |
| **右** | みぎ | right | The top stroke is a mouth (口). |
| **在** | ざい | exist/at | Contains the radical 才 (ability) inside. |

## Usage Frequency Note

**左** is a high-frequency kanji used daily in navigation, descriptions, and political discourse. It is essential for basic communication (giving directions) and is taught early in the Japanese education system (Grade 1). It is equally common in formal writing and casual speech.
