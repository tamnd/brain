## Stroke Order

The kanji **友** is written in 4 strokes following the standard top-to-bottom, left-to-right rule:

1. **Stroke 1:** The horizontal line at the top (left to right).
2. **Stroke 2:** The diagonal line starting from the center of the first stroke, sweeping down to the left.
3. **Stroke 3:** The horizontal stroke of the bottom radical (又).
4. **Stroke 4:** The diagonal stroke of the bottom radical, ending with a slight hook (harai).

## Radical Analysis

The kanji **友** consists of two parts:
*   **Top part:** A horizontal line and a diagonal stroke, representing two hands reaching out.
*   **Radical (又):** Meaning "again" or "also." 
*   **Combined Meaning:** The character depicts two right hands reaching out to grasp each other, symbolizing the bond of friendship—people who help each other "again and again."

## Mnemonic

Imagine two people reaching out their right hands to shake hands. The top part is the arms reaching out, and the bottom part (又) is the hands meeting. **Friends** are people who are always there to lend a hand!

## Common Confusion Pairs

| Kanji | Reading | Meaning | How to distinguish |
|-------|---------|---------|-------------------|
| **友** | とも | friend | Has the radical 又 (hand) at the bottom. |
| **反** | ハン | anti/oppose | The top part is different; it has a "cliff" radical (厂) and a different base. |
| **及** | キュウ | reach/extend | The right side is a different stroke shape (a hook). |

## Usage Frequency Note

**友** is a high-frequency kanji used daily in both speech and writing. While *tomodachi* (友達) is the most common way to say "friend" in casual conversation, the *on-reading* *yū* (ユウ) is very common in formal writing, compound nouns, and abstract concepts like *yūjō* (友情 - friendship). It is one of the first kanji learned by students in elementary school.
