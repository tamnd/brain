## Stroke Order

The kanji **好** follows standard Japanese calligraphy rules, moving from top-to-bottom and left-to-right.

1. **Stroke 1:** The top-left downward curve (part of the 女 radical).
2. **Stroke 2:** The diagonal stroke moving down-right.
3. **Stroke 3:** The horizontal bar connecting the two previous strokes.
4. **Stroke 4:** The top dot of the 子 component.
5. **Stroke 5:** The horizontal stroke of the 子 component.
6. **Stroke 6:** The final curved stroke of the 子 component.

## Radical Analysis

The kanji **好** is a phono-semantic compound.
*   **Radical:** **女** (Woman).
*   **Component:** **子** (Child).
*   **Meaning:** The character depicts a woman (女) holding a child (子). In ancient Chinese culture, a mother holding her child was considered the ultimate image of "goodness" and something to be "fond of" or "liked."

## Mnemonic

Imagine a **woman** (女) holding her **child** (子). Because she loves her child so much, she is **fond of** (好) everything about them. Whenever you see this kanji, think: "A mother's love for her child is the best thing to like."

## Common Confusion Pairs

| Kanji | Reading | Meaning | How to distinguish |
|-------|---------|---------|-------------------|
| **好** | す-く | Like | Contains 女 (woman) + 子 (child). |
| **始** | はじ-める | Begin | Contains 女 (woman) + 台 (platform). |

*   **Note:** While they share the 女 radical, **好** represents the emotional state of "liking," while **始** represents the start of an action.

## Usage Frequency Note

**好** is an extremely high-frequency kanji. It is one of the first characters taught to beginners because of the word *suki* (好き). It appears daily in casual conversation, news, and formal writing. It is essential for expressing personal preferences and describing positive trends or qualities.
