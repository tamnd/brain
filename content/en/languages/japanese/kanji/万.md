## 万

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Meaning | ten thousand |
| Strokes | 3 |
| JLPT Level | N5 |
| On-reading (音読み) | マン、バン |
| Kun-reading (訓読み) | よろず |
| Radical | 一 (ichi - one) [U+4E00] |

## Stroke Order

The kanji **万** is written in 3 strokes following the standard top-to-bottom, left-to-right rule:

1. **Stroke 1:** A horizontal line starting from the left, slightly curved upwards.
2. **Stroke 2:** A vertical line starting from the center of the first stroke, curving down and to the left.
3. **Stroke 3:** A small hook/dot starting from the end of the second stroke, sweeping upwards to the right.

## Radical Analysis

The radical for **万** is **一** (the "one" radical). Historically, this character is a simplified form of **萬** (a scorpion). The top part represented the head and pincers, while the bottom represented the body. Over time, it evolved into its current simple shape to represent the concept of "ten thousand" (a very large, uncountable number).

## Mnemonic

Imagine a **scorpion** (the original shape) that has grown so large it has **ten thousand** legs. You are looking at the scorpion from above: the top line is its back, the long stroke is its tail, and the little hook is its stinger.

## Common Confusion Pairs

| Kanji | Reading | Meaning | How to distinguish |
|-------|---------|---------|-------------------|
| **万** | マン | 10,000 | Has a hook at the end of the second stroke. |
| **方** | ホウ | Direction | Has a dot at the top; the second stroke is horizontal. |
| **力** | リョク | Power | The second stroke is a sharp, angular hook. |

## Usage Frequency Note

**万** is an extremely high-frequency kanji. Because the Japanese counting system groups numbers by units of 10,000 (unlike the English system which groups by thousands), **万** is used daily in all financial, mathematical, and general counting contexts. It is essential for basic literacy.
