N3 introduces 184 new kanji (651 cumulative). These kanji shift from the concrete vocabulary of N5/N4 to abstract, social, and intellectual concepts. Multiple readings become a bigger challenge — many N3 kanji have 3+ readings. Learning kanji through compound words (熟語, jukugo) rather than in isolation is the most effective approach at this level.

## How N3 Kanji Differ from N4

- **More abstract meanings**: 識 (knowledge/identify), 察 (perceive), 絡 (entangle/connect)
- **More multiple readings**: 下 (か/げ/した/しも/さ/お) appears constantly; N3 introduces similarly complex kanji
- **Kanji used in formal/written language**: Many N3 kanji appear mainly in newspapers, formal documents, signs
- **Compound-word density**: Most N3 vocabulary items are 2–4 kanji compounds; recognizing each component is essential

## Kanji Look-Alikes — N3 Troublemakers

These pairs are frequently confused in N3 reading questions:

| Kanji A | Kanji B | How to Tell Apart |
|---------|---------|-------------------|
| 感 (feel) | 惑 (confuse) | 感 has 心 directly; 惑 has 或 on top |
| 経 (pass/manage) | 軽 (light) | 経 = 糸 + 巠; 軽 = 車 + 巠 |
| 制 (control) | 製 (manufacture) | 製 has 衣 (clothing) at the bottom |
| 政 (politics) | 征 (conquer) | 政 = 正 + 攵; 征 = 彳 + 正 |
| 議 (discuss) | 義 (duty) | 議 has 言 (speech) on left |
| 証 (proof) | 症 (symptom) | 証 = 言 + 正; 症 = 疒 (illness radical) |
| 働 (work) | 動 (move) | 働 has 人 (person) on the left |
| 達 (reach) | 道 (road) | 達 = 幸 + 辶; 道 = 首 + 辶 |
| 係 (related to) | 系 (system/lineage) | 係 has 人 on left |
| 拡 (expand) | 広 (wide/spacious) | 拡 has 扌 (hand radical) |
