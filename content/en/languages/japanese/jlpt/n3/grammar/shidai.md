## Pattern: 〜次第

**Structure:**
- **Meaning 1:** Verb (masu-stem) + 次第 → "as soon as [action completes]"
- **Meaning 2:** Noun + 次第だ / Noun + 次第で(は) → "it depends on ~, it is up to ~"

**Formality:** Formal (Meaning 1 especially used in business)

**Meaning:** As soon as ~ / Depends entirely on ~

## Explanation

〜次第 has two distinct meanings at N3:

**Meaning 1 — "As soon as"** (verb masu-stem + 次第):  
A formal expression used frequently in business communication. It means "as soon as [the action in the first clause is completed], [the second action will follow]." The result clause must express a future event, not a past one. This is more formal than 〜たらすぐ.

**Meaning 2 — "Depends on"** (noun + 次第だ):  
Expresses that an outcome is entirely contingent on one decisive factor. 「結果は努力次第だ」— the result depends entirely on effort. Often followed by だ, で(は), or by: 「〜次第でどうにでもなる」(can go either way depending on ~).

## Common Mistakes

❌ 到着次第、昨日連絡した。(past tense result — impossible with 次第 Meaning 1)  
✓ 到着次第、連絡いたします。— Result must be future.

❌ 天気次第すれば行く。(wrong structure)  
✓ 天気次第で行くかどうか決める。— Meaning 2 uses 次第で or 次第だ.
