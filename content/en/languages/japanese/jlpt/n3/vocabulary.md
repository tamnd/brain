N3 vocabulary (~3,750 cumulative words, ~2,250 new beyond N4) marks the shift from basic daily vocabulary to abstract, professional, and academic language. Many N3 words are Chinese-origin compound nouns (漢語, kango) — learning to recognize kanji components becomes essential. This page organizes all N3-level vocabulary by semantic domain.

## How N3 Vocabulary Differs from N4

At N4, most vocabulary is concrete: food, directions, actions, simple adjectives. N3 introduces:
- **Abstract nouns**: 意識 (consciousness), 感覚 (sensation), 判断 (judgment)
- **Formal/written equivalents** of casual words: 利用する (riyou suru) instead of 使う (tsukau)
- **Compound verbs**: 取り上げる (toriageru, to take up/address), 乗り越える (norikoeru, to overcome)
- **Four-character compounds (四字熟語)**: Begin appearing in N3 reading passages

## 15. Frequently Confused N3 Words

These pairs appear on the exam and are commonly mixed up:

| Word A | Word B | Difference |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| 理由 (riyuu — reason/cause) | 原因 (genin — cause/factor) | 理由 = given reason; 原因 = underlying cause |
| 目的 (mokuteki — purpose) | 目標 (mokuhyou — goal/target) | 目的 = why you do something; 目標 = what you aim for |
| 機会 (kikai — opportunity) | 場合 (baai — case/situation) | 機会 = chance to do something; 場合 = when/if situation occurs |
| 変える (kaeru — to change sth) | 変わる (kawaru — to change intrans) | transitive vs. intransitive pair |
| 増やす (fuyasu — to increase sth) | 増える (fueru — to increase intrans) | transitive vs. intransitive pair |
| 続ける (tsuzukeru — to continue sth) | 続く (tsuzuku — to continue intrans) | transitive vs. intransitive pair |
| 利用する (riyou suru — to use/utilize) | 使う (tsukau — to use) | 利用 is more formal/functional |
| 実現する (jitsugen suru — to realize/achieve) | 達成する (tassei suru — to accomplish) | 実現 = becoming real; 達成 = completing a goal |
| 主張 (shuchou — assertion) | 意見 (iken — opinion) | 主張 is stronger, more forceful |
| 同僚 (douryou — colleague) | 仲間 (nakama — friend/companion) | 同僚 = professional context; 仲間 = informal group |
