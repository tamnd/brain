## Stroke Order Notes

When writing kanji, follow these general rules:
1. **Top to bottom** — strokes generally go from top to bottom
2. **Left to right** — horizontal strokes go left to right; when multiple vertical and horizontal strokes cross, horizontals generally come first
3. **Outside before inside** — for enclosed kanji (like 国), draw the frame first, then contents, then close the bottom
4. **Center before sides** — for vertically symmetrical kanji (like 小), draw the center stroke first

## Reading Patterns

### On-yomi (音読み) vs Kun-yomi (訓読み)
- **On-yomi** is used mainly when two or more kanji are combined: 学校 (gak**KOU**), 電車 (den**SHA**)
- **Kun-yomi** is used for single kanji with Japanese native reading: 学ぶ (mana**bu**), 車 (**kuruma**)
- Some kanji like 今日 (kyou), 今年 (kotoshi), 大人 (otona) have special readings (jukuji-kun) that do not follow standard patterns

Many N5 kanji share common radicals (building blocks) that hint at meaning:

| Radical | Meaning hint | Examples |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| 人 (イ) | person | 休 (rest = person leaning on tree) |
| 口 | mouth/opening | 話, 言, 語 |
| 日 | sun/day | 明, 時, 昨 |
| 木 | tree/wood | 休, 本, 校, 東 |
| 水 (氵) | water | 海, 泳, 飲 |
| 火 (灬) | fire | 燃, 照 |
| 女 | woman | 好, 姉, 始 |
| 山 | mountain | 島, 炭 |
| 土 | earth | 地, 場 |
| 金 (釒) | metal | 鉄, 銀, 銀行 |

### Mnemonic Examples
- **山** (mountain): three peaks of a mountain drawn simply
- **川** (river): three lines like flowing water
- **木** (tree): a trunk with branches above and roots below
- **日** (sun/day): a rectangle representing the sun with a centerline
- **月** (moon/month): a crescent moon shape
- **人** (person): a person walking with two legs
- **山** + **川** = landscape; nature vocabulary clusters around these radicals
- **休** (rest): a 人 (person) leaning against a 木 (tree) = resting
- **明** (bright): 日 (sun) + 月 (moon) = both luminous things = bright
- **林** (grove): two 木 (trees); **森** (forest): three trees (N5 level awareness)
