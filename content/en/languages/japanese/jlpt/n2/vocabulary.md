N2 introduces approximately **2,250 new vocabulary items** beyond N3 (~6,000 total cumulative). The defining characteristic of N2 vocabulary is its **formal, written register** — most new words come from newspapers, academic texts, formal correspondence, and broadcast speech. Casual conversational study alone will not cover this vocabulary; deliberate reading and SRS are essential.

---

## 12. Study Notes for N2 Vocabulary

### Priority tiers

**Tier 1 — Exam critical** (memorize first):
Abstract nouns in sections 1, 6, and 9 — these appear in every exam section. Formal conjunctions (section 9.1) are tested in grammar and enable reading comprehension.

**Tier 2 — High value** (second priority):
Political/legal (section 2), economic (section 3), formal verbs (section 10). These appear in reading passages and determine score on passage comprehension.

**Tier 3 — Domain-specific** (study by domain):
Medical (5), science (4), environment (7). Study the domain you encounter in reading passages; don't memorize all at once.

### Most commonly confused N2 word pairs

| Pair | Confusion point |
|------|----------------|
| 概念 vs. 概要 | gainen (concept/notion) vs. gaiyou (outline/overview) |
| 傾向 vs. 動向 | keikou (tendency/trend) vs. doukou (movement/trend) |
| 検討 vs. 検証 | kentou (consideration) vs. kenshou (verification) |
| 根拠 vs. 証拠 | konkyo (grounds/basis) vs. shouko (evidence/proof) |
| 規制 vs. 規則 | kisei (regulation/restriction) vs. kisoku (rules) |
| 格差 vs. 差別 | kakusa (disparity/gap) vs. sabetsu (discrimination) |
| 制度 vs. 体制 | seido (system/institution) vs. taisei (system/regime) |
| 立場 vs. 観点 | tachiba (standpoint/position) vs. kanten (viewpoint) |
