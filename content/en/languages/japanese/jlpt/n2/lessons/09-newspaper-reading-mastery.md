## Overview

Newspaper reading is a cornerstone of the JLPT N2 reading comprehension section. Unlike casual conversation, news articles and *shasetsu* (editorials) employ a highly structured, objective, and dense linguistic style. Mastering this genre requires an understanding of formal conjunctions, passive voice constructions used to maintain neutrality, and specific vocabulary related to politics and economics.

This lesson bridges the gap between intermediate conversational Japanese and professional-level literacy. By learning how to deconstruct complex sentences and identify the core argument in an editorial, you will gain the confidence to navigate Japanese media. This foundation is essential for your transition into N1-level materials and real-world professional communication in Japan.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson you can:
- Identify and interpret the structure of a Japanese *shasetsu* (editorial).
- Use formal modifiers like 〜に関する and 〜をめぐる to connect complex ideas.
- Recognize passive voice constructions used in news to report events objectively.
- Understand headline grammar, including the omission of particles and the use of noun-ending phrases.

## Grammar Points

### 〜に関する / 〜をめぐる (Regarding / Concerning)

**Structure:** [Noun] + に関する / [Noun] + をめぐる

These patterns are used to specify the topic of a discussion, report, or action. **〜に関する** (ni kansuru) is the standard formal way to say "regarding" or "related to." **〜をめぐる** (o meguru) is more specific to news; it implies a debate or conflict "surrounding" a particular issue (e.g., a controversy or a policy).

| Japanese | Reading | Romaji | English |
|----------|---------|--------|---------|
| 環境に関する調査 | かんきょうにかんするちょうさ | kankyou ni kansuru chousa | A survey regarding the environment |
| 予算をめぐる議論 | よさんをめぐるぎろん | yosan o meguru giron | The debate surrounding the budget |
| 計画に関する発表 | けいかくにかんするはっぴょう | keikaku ni kansuru happyou | An announcement regarding the plan |
| 土地をめぐる争い | とちをめぐるあらそい | tochi o meguru arasoi | The dispute surrounding the land |

**Common mistake:** ❌ ニュースについて記事 → ✓ ニュースに関する記事 — "について" is acceptable in speech, but "に関する" is the standard for written, formal news reports.

### Passive Voice for Objectivity (〜とされる / 〜が発表された)

**Structure:** [Noun] + とされる / [Noun] + が発表された

In news, the passive voice is used to remove the "actor" and focus on the event itself. **〜とされる** (to sareru) means "it is considered/said that..." and is used to report general consensus. **〜が発表された** (ga happyou sareta) is used to report official statements without needing to name the specific spokesperson.

| Japanese | Reading | Romaji | English |
|----------|---------|--------|---------|
| 深刻な問題とされる | しんこくなもんだいとされる | shinkoku na mondai to sareru | It is considered a serious problem |
| 新しい法案が発表された | あたらしいほうあんがはっぴょうされた | atarashii houan ga happyou sareta | A new bill was announced |
| 影響が出ると予想される | えいきょうがでるとよそうされる | eikyou ga deru to yosou sareru | It is expected that an effect will emerge |
| 調査結果が公表された | ちょうさけっかがこうひょうされた | chousakekka ga kouhyou sareta | The survey results were made public |

**Common mistake:** ❌ 政府が発表した → ✓ 政府によって発表された (or simply 発表された) — In formal writing, focus on the information, not the person doing the action.

## Dialogues

### Dialogue 1: Discussing an Editorial
*Two colleagues, Tanaka and Sato, discuss a morning newspaper editorial.*

Tanaka: 今日の社説、読みましたか？
Reading/Romaji: きょうのしゃせつ、よみましたか？ / Kyou no shasetsu, yomimashita ka?
English: Did you read today's editorial?

Sato: はい。経済の改善に関する内容でしたね。
Reading/Romaji: はい。けいざいのかいぜんにかんするないようでしたね。 / Hai. Keizai no kaizen ni kansuru naiyou deshita ne.
English: Yes. It was about the improvement of the economy.

Tanaka: 対策が不十分だという指摘が深刻でした。
Reading/Romaji: たいさくがふじゅうぶんだというしてきがしんこくでした。 / Taisaku ga fujuubun da to iu shiteki ga shinkoku deshita.
English: The point that the countermeasures are insufficient was grave.

Sato: 本当に。今後の影響が懸念されます。
Reading/Romaji: ほんとうに。こんごのえいきょうがけねんされます。 / Hontou ni. Kongo no eikyou ga kenen saremasu.
English: Truly. Future effects are a concern.

### Dialogue 2: Analyzing a Policy Debate
*A journalist interviewing a politician.*

Journalist: 予算をめぐる議論について伺います。
Reading/Romaji: よさんをめぐるぎろんについてうかがいます。 / Yosan o meguru giron ni tsuite ukagaimasu.
English: I would like to ask about the debate surrounding the budget.

Politician: はい。現在、新しい案が検討されています。
Reading/Romaji: はい。げんざい、あたらしいあんがけんとうされています。 / Hai. Genzai, atarashii an ga kentou sarete imasu.
English: Yes. Currently, a new plan is being considered.

Journalist: それはいつ発表されますか？
Reading/Romaji: それはいつはっぴょうされますか？ / Sore wa itsu happyou saremasu ka?
English: When will that be announced?

Politician: 来週、正式に公表される予定です。
Reading/Romaji: らいしゅう、せいしきにこうひょうされるよていです。 / Raishuu, seishiki ni kouhyou sareru yotei desu.
English: It is scheduled to be officially made public next week.

## Cultural Note

In Japan, *shasetsu* (editorials) are highly respected and often serve as a barometer for public opinion and intellectual discourse. Unlike Western op-eds, which may be highly personalized, Japanese editorials maintain a formal, collective voice, often using the passive or impersonal structures taught in this lesson to sound authoritative and objective.

Understanding these texts is not just about vocabulary; it is about recognizing the "logic of the argument." Japanese editorials typically follow a *ki-shou-ten-ketsu* (introduction, development, turn, conclusion) structure. Recognizing these markers helps you identify the writer's stance, even when it is expressed through subtle, formal language rather than direct opinion.
