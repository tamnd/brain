N2 introduces approximately **633 new kanji** beyond N3's 367, bringing the cumulative total to roughly **1,000 kanji** recognized at this level. The N2 kanji jump is the largest in the JLPT system, and these characters skew heavily toward formal, written-language usage. You will rarely encounter many of them in daily casual conversation — they appear primarily in news, academic texts, formal correspondence, and professional settings.

**Key N2 strategy**: At this level, kanji through vocabulary (not isolated character drilling) is the most efficient approach. Focus on compound words (熟語), since most N2 kanji appear almost exclusively in specific compound forms.

| 掌 / 堂 | ショウ / ドウ | てのひら | palm of hand | hall; temple | 掌 has 手 (hand) at bottom; 堂 has 土 |
| 裁 / 栽 | サイ / サイ | さば(く) / — | judge (裁判) | cultivate (栽培) | 裁 has 衣 (clothing) at base |
| 抽 / 袖 | チュウ / シュウ | — / そで | extract (抽出) | sleeve (袖口) | 抽 has 手 (扌) radical; 袖 has 衣 |
| 概 / 慨 | ガイ / ガイ | おおむ(ね) / — | general (概念) | lament (憤慨) | 概 has 木 (tree); 慨 has 心 (heart) |
| 奮 / 憤 | フン / フン | ふる(う) / — | stir up (奮発) | indignant (憤慨) | 奮 has 田+大 above 隹; 憤 has 心 |

## 12. Study Plan for N2 Kanji

### Recommended approach

1. **Do not study isolated kanji** at N2 level. Instead, learn through vocabulary (compound words).
2. **Frequency priority**: Focus on kanji that appear in the most N2 vocabulary items. The kanji in sections 1–7 above cover the highest-frequency N2 compounds.
3. **Use reading to reinforce**: For every kanji learned, encounter it in 5+ real sentences. NHK Web News is ideal.
4. **Confusion pair review**: Review the pairs in section 8 monthly. These are common exam traps.
5. **Radical grouping**: Use section 9 to batch-learn related kanji with similar forms.

### Monthly target (6-month plan from N3)

| Month | Focus area | Target new kanji |
