JLPT N2 is offered twice a year (July and December). The total exam time is **155 minutes**, making it significantly longer and more demanding than N3 (100 minutes). This page covers the exam format in detail, section-by-section strategies, 120 practice questions, and a complete 6-month study plan.

---

## 1. Exam Format and Structure

### Official Time Breakdown

| Section | Contents | Time |
|---------|----------|------|
| 言語知識（文字・語彙）| Kanji readings, vocabulary meaning, word formation, contextual words | 30 min |
| 言語知識（文法）・読解 | Grammar patterns, sentence ordering, text grammar, reading passages | 75 min total |
| 聴解 | Listening (various formats) | 50 min |
| **Total** | | **155 min** |

### Scoring

| Section | Max points | Minimum to pass |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| 言語知識（文字・語彙・文法） | 60 pts | 19 pts |
| 読解 | 60 pts | 19 pts |
| 聴解 | 60 pts | 19 pts |
| **Total** | **180 pts** | **90 pts** (+ section minimums) |

You must exceed **all three section minimums** AND reach 90 total. Failing one section means failing the entire exam, regardless of total score.

### Question Types by Section

**Section 1: Language Knowledge — Vocabulary (30 min)**

| Question type | Description | Questions |
|--------------|-------------|-----------|
| 漢字読み | Underlined kanji — choose correct reading | ~7-8 |
| 文脈規定 | Choose word that fits blank in context | ~6-7 |
| 言い換え類義 | Choose word/phrase closest in meaning | ~5-6 |
| 用法 | Choose sentence where underlined word is used correctly | ~5-6 |

**Section 2: Language Knowledge — Grammar + Reading (75 min)**

| Question type | Description | Questions |
|--------------|-------------|-----------|
| 文の文法1 | Choose grammar pattern for blank | ~12 |
| 文の文法2 | Sentence reordering (put 4 parts in order) | ~5-6 |
| 文章の文法 | Select best grammar for 5 blanks in a text | ~5 |
| 読解 — 内容理解(短文) | 2 short passages (~200-300 chars) | ~6 |
| 読解 — 内容理解(中文) | 2 medium passages (~500 chars) | ~9 |
| 読解 — 内容理解(長文) | 1 long passage (~900 chars) | ~4 |
| 読解 — 統合理解 | Compare 2 related texts | ~3 |
| 読解 — 情報検索 | Find info in a practical document (ad, notice) | ~2 |

**Section 3: Listening (50 min)**

| Question type | Description |
|--------------|-------------|
| 課題理解 | Task-based: what should someone do? |
| ポイント理解 | Point understanding: what is the key detail? |
| 概要理解 | Main point/theme: what is the main topic? |
| 発話表現 | Which response is appropriate (visual situation) |
| 即時応答 | Immediate response: which reply is correct |

## 2. Section-by-Section Strategies

### 2.1 Vocabulary Section Strategy

**Focus areas for N2 vocabulary** (ranked by exam frequency):

1. **Abstract formal nouns** — 概念, 前提, 根拠, 傾向, 観点, 格差, etc. These appear in 用法 and 文脈規定.
2. **Formal connective adverbs** — むしろ, かえって, あくまで, 概して, 徐々に — these appear in word substitution questions.
3. **Verb pairs** — 妨げる/阻む, 促す/勧める, 示す/表す — 言い換え questions.
4. **Word formation** — 複合語 (compound words) and 接辞 (prefixes/suffixes).

**For 言い換え类義 questions**:
- Train on word pair recognition: 維持する ≈ 保つ, 促す ≈ 勧める, 傾向 ≈ 動向
- Study the Shin Kanzen Master N2 Vocabulary book — it groups synonyms explicitly.

**For 用法 questions**:
- The WRONG choices often use a word grammatically correctly but semantically inappropriately.
- Example: 踏まえる is NOT synonymous with 踏む — it only means "to take into account."

### 2.2 Grammar Section Strategy

**文の文法1 (Grammar fill-in-the-blank)**: The single highest-value grammar question type.

Priority patterns for grammar section (most frequently tested):
1. Formal conjunctions (に従って, に基づいて, に沿って, をもとに)
2. Negation patterns (わけではない, とは限らない, わけにはいかない)
3. Obligation/compulsion (ざるを得ない, べきだ, ないわけにはいかない)
4. Possibility/potential (かねる, かねない, うる/える)
5. Limit/condition patterns (に限り, を問わず, にかかわらず)
6. Time patterns (にあたって, に際して, た上で, 次第)

**文の文法2 (Sentence reordering)**: 
- Four sentence fragments must be placed in correct order.
- Look for: the subject first, then verb complements, then main verb, then sentence-final elements.
- Grammar pattern markers (に、を、で、が) are clues to where fragments attach.

**文章の文法 (In-text grammar)**:
- Read the full passage FIRST for meaning, then choose grammar.
- The correct answer must be both grammatically valid AND coherent with the passage's logic.
- Wrong choices often work grammatically but contradict the passage's argument direction.

### 2.3 Reading Section Strategy

**Time allocation** (recommended within the 75-minute section):
- Grammar questions: 20-22 minutes
- Short passages: 10-12 minutes
- Medium passages: 18-20 minutes
- Long passage: 12-14 minutes
- Integrated/information: 8-10 minutes

**Long passage (900 characters) technique**:
1. Read the questions first (not answer choices).
2. Read paragraph 1 and paragraph 4/5 (first and last) for overall argument.
3. Read the passage at normal speed, marking relevant sections.
4. Answer questions using your marked sections.

**Integrated understanding question** (2 related texts):
- These ask you to compare opinions or synthesize information from two texts.
- Find: What do they agree on? Where do they differ? What is the main point of each?

**Information search question** (practical document):
- Looks like a class schedule, advertisement, or form.
- Questions ask for specific factual details — skim for the relevant column/row.
- Time: these should take only 3-4 minutes.

### 2.4 Listening Section Strategy

**Before the audio plays**:
- Read the question stem quickly.
- Identify: what type of answer is needed (action, reason, feeling, or factual detail)?
- Predict vocabulary domains likely to appear.

**During audio**:
- Focus on the QUESTION ANCHOR. For 課題理解, listen for what the task/action is.
- Note the final decision/conclusion — conversations often change direction.
- Don't fixate on words you miss — keep up with the flow.

**Common traps in N2 listening**:
- A character mentions something they WON'T do — listen for negatives.
- The first option discussed is often not the correct answer.
- Confirm the question type: 何をしますか (what will they do?) ≠ 何が問題ですか (what is the problem?).

## 3. 6-Month Study Plan from N3

This plan assumes you have passed N3 (or equivalent) and are starting N2 preparation. Total study hours: approximately 400–600 hours over 6 months.

### Month 1: Foundation Building

**Goal**: Activate N3 knowledge; begin N2 vocabulary and grammar exposure.

| Week | Activity | Daily time |
|------|----------|-----------|
| 1-2 | Review N3 grammar completely (Bunpro/Genki) | 45 min grammar |
| 1-4 | Start N2 Anki deck (formal vocabulary first) | 30 min flashcards |
| 1-4 | NHK Web Easy daily reading (transitioning from easy) | 20 min |
| 3-4 | Begin N2 grammar study (sections 1-3 in grammar.md) | 45 min grammar |

**Month 1 targets**:
- 300 N2 vocabulary items in Anki
- All N3 grammar patterns reviewed
- 20 N2 grammar patterns studied
- Reading: 15-20 NHK Web Easy articles completed

### Month 2: Grammar Deep Dive

**Goal**: Cover all core N2 grammar patterns (sections 1-7 in grammar.md).

| Week | Focus | Daily time |
|------|-------|-----------|
| 5-6 | Formal conjunctions (に基づいて, に従って, に伴い group) | 60 min grammar |
| 5-8 | Vocabulary: political, legal, economic categories | 30 min flashcards |
| 7-8 | Negation patterns (weeks dedicated to confusion pairs) | 60 min grammar |
| 5-8 | Reading: Switch from NHK Easy to standard NHK | 25 min |

**Month 2 targets**:
- 100 N2 grammar patterns studied
- 600 N2 vocabulary items in Anki
- 5 NHK standard articles read
- First practice test (diagnostic only)

### Month 3: Kanji and Vocabulary Acceleration

**Goal**: Systematic kanji study; vocabulary reach 1,500 items.

| Week | Focus | Daily time |
|------|-------|-----------|
| 9-10 | N2 kanji — abstract/intellectual + political groups | 30 min kanji |
| 9-12 | Vocabulary: science, medical, social categories | 30 min flashcards |
| 11-12 | N2 kanji — science, society, formal writing groups | 30 min kanji |
| 9-12 | Grammar: remaining patterns (sections 8-12) | 45 min grammar |
| 9-12 | Reading: Start reading newspaper editorials (社説) | 30 min |

**Month 3 targets**:
- 300 N2 kanji in active recognition
- 1,500 vocabulary items in Anki
- All 200+ grammar patterns surveyed
- 10 newspaper editorials read

### Month 4: Practice Tests and Gap Analysis

**Goal**: Take practice exams; identify weak areas; targeted remediation.

| Week | Focus | Daily time |
|------|-------|-----------|
| 13 | Full timed practice test (JLPT N2 practice book) | 2.5 hrs (test day) |
| 14 | Analyze test results; list wrong answers by category | 60 min analysis |
| 15-16 | Targeted remediation of lowest-scoring areas | 90 min targeted |
| 13-16 | Maintain Anki (aim for 90% retention) | 30 min |
| 13-16 | Listening: NHK news audio daily | 30 min |

**Month 4 targets**:
- Complete 2 full practice tests
- Vocabulary: 2,000 items in Anki
- Listening: 30 NHK audio clips processed
- Score pattern identified: know your weak sections

### Month 5: Immersion and Output

**Goal**: High-volume authentic input; reinforce through exposure, not drilling.

| Week | Focus | Daily time |
|------|-------|-----------|
| 17-20 | Reading: 1 full NHK article or short novel section daily | 40 min |
| 17-20 | Listening: Drama, podcast, news (mixed) | 45 min |
| 17-20 | Grammar: Review difficult patterns from practice tests | 30 min |
| 17-20 | Vocabulary: Reduce new cards; reinforce with reading | 20 min |

**Month 5 targets**:
- Read 25+ authentic texts
- Vocabulary: 2,100–2,200 items consolidated
- Complete 2 more practice tests
- Listening score on practice test: 40+/60

### Month 6: Final Sprint

**Goal**: Fine-tune exam strategy; solidify weak points; build confidence.

| Week | Focus | Daily time |
|------|-------|-----------|
| 21-22 | Final grammar review — confusion pairs ONLY | 45 min |
| 21-22 | Vocabulary: High-frequency N2 lists only | 20 min |
| 23 | Full timed practice test (most recent official one) | 2.5 hrs |
| 23-24 | Error review; light immersion; rest | 45 min |
| 24 | Exam simulation at actual time → light review | Easy review only |

**Final week** (exam week): No new material. Review mistake notebooks. Sleep well. Trust the preparation.

## 5. Grammar Drills — 60 Fill-in-the-Blank Questions

### Set 1: Formal Conjunctions (Q1-10)

1. 科学的な証拠（　）、この方針が決定された。

   **Answer**: b) に基づいて

   a) に従って b) に沿って c) に基づいて  
   **Answer**: b) に沿って

3. 領土問題（　）、二国の関係が緊張している。  
   a) をはじめ b) をめぐって c) に伴って  
   **Answer**: b) をめぐって

4. 高齢化（　）、社会保障費が増加している。  
   a) に伴い b) に従い c) に際して  
   **Answer**: a) に伴い

5. 法律（　）、処分が下された。  
   a) に沿った b) に基づく c) をもとの  
   **Answer**: b) に基づく

6. 師の指導（　）、研究を進めた。  
   a) のもとに b) をはじめ c) に先立って

## 6. Most Commonly Failed N2 Patterns

Based on exam analysis, these patterns have the highest error rates. Review them last — they are your final score boosters.

### Top 10 High-Error Patterns

| Rank | Pattern | Common mistake | Key to getting right |
|------|---------|----------------|----------------------|
| 1 | かねる vs. かねない | Confuse the two (opposite meanings!) | かねる = refusal; かねない = risk/might |
| 2 | に基づいて vs. に沿って | Use interchangeably | 基づいて = formal legal/data basis; 沿って = path-following |
| 3 | とは限らない vs. わけではない | Mix up scope | 限らない = "not always true"; わけではない = "not that specifically" |
| 4 | ざるを得ない vs. わけにはいかない | Confuse contexts | ざるを得ない = reluctant compulsion; わけにはいかない = social obligation |
| 5 | 〜次第 (two meanings) | Apply wrong meaning | V-masu + 次第 = as soon as; Noun + 次第で = depending on |
| 6 | にあたって vs. に先立って | Use interchangeably | にあたって = on the occasion of (includes prep); 先立って = strictly BEFORE |
| 7 | だけあって vs. だけに | Confuse evaluative direction | だけあって = positive match to reputation; だけに = causal emphasis |
| 8 | からといって vs. からには | Both use から | からといって = "just because (denial)"; からには = "now that (obligation)" |
| 9 | べく vs. ために | Both express purpose | べく = formal/literary; ために = neutral (both correct in most contexts) |
| 10 | にほかならない vs. というわけだ | Both express "is precisely" | にほかならない = emphatic identity; というわけだ = logical conclusion |

### Scoring Patterns for N2 Grammar Section

Exam statistics suggest these question distributions:

- **Easy (almost everyone gets)**: Basic pattern recognition (べきだ, ざるを得ない, からには)
- **Medium (distinguishes passers)**: Confusion pair discrimination (かねる/かねない, 基づいて/沿って)
- **Hard (distinguishes high scorers)**: Contextual nuance (次第 two meanings, だけあって/だけに in context)

To pass the grammar section, you need to reliably get the easy and medium questions. Target the hard questions for higher score optimization.
