These 10 lessons are designed for learners who have passed N3 (or equivalent) and are systematically building toward N2. Each lesson focuses on a practical competency area, combining explanation, targeted examples, and drills.

**Prerequisites**: JLPT N3 level grammar and vocabulary (~3,750 words), ability to read hiragana/katakana fluently, solid grasp of particles and basic verb forms.

## Lesson 1: Mastering Formal Written Japanese

### The Divide Between Written and Spoken Japanese

Japanese has one of the world's sharpest divides between its written (書き言葉, *kakikotoba*) and spoken (話し言葉, *hanashikotoba*) registers. At N3, you learned primarily conversational patterns. At N2, you must master formal written Japanese — the register used in newspapers, academic papers, business documents, and formal speeches.

### Key Structural Differences

| Feature | Spoken (informal) | Written (formal) |
|---------|------------------|-----------------|
| Sentence endings | 〜だ、〜よ、〜ね | 〜である、〜だ (no particles) |
| Negative | 〜ない | 〜ない (same, but 〜ぬ in literary) |
| Causatives | 〜てもらう、〜させる | 〜せしめる (classical, rare) |
| Conjunctions | だから、でも | したがって、しかしながら |
| Conditionals | 〜たら、〜と | 〜ならば、〜とすれば |
| Topic particle | は (frequent) | は (used strategically) |
| Copula | だ、です | である (neutral written) |

### The 〜である Construction

The most important written-Japanese marker at N2. 〜である replaces 〜だ in formal texts.

- 彼は専門家**である**。(written) = 彼は専門家**だ**。(spoken)  
- 問題が多いのは事実**である**。(written)

In attributive position, 〜である becomes 〜である + noun:
- 重要な問題**である**点（written）= 重要な問題**な**点（spoken）

### Formal Conjunctions to Master First

Memorize these pairs — spoken on left, written equivalent on right:

| Spoken | Written | Meaning |
|--------|---------|---------|
| だから | したがって・ゆえに | therefore |
| でも・けど | しかし・しかしながら | however |
| それに | さらに・加えて | furthermore |
| だって | なぜなら〜からである | because |
| もっとも | ただし | however; provided that |
| ところで | 一方 | meanwhile; on the other hand |

### Practice Exercise

**Convert from spoken to written register:**

1. あの人は学生だから、時間があるはずだ。  
   → あの人は学生**であるため**、時間がある**はずである**。

2. この問題は難しいけど、解決できると思う。  
   → この問題は難しいが、解決**し得る**と**考えられる**。

3. 調査したら、問題が見つかった。  
   → 調査した**ところ**、問題が発見された。

### Key Takeaway

At N2, you must be comfortable reading (not necessarily writing) texts that use 〜である, formal conjunctions, and passive/impersonal constructions. The N2 reading section contains extended texts written in this register.

## Lesson 2: Newspaper Japanese — Breaking Down 社説 and Headlines

### Why Newspapers Matter for N2

Japanese newspapers are the single richest source of N2-level vocabulary and reading patterns. The editorial section (社説, *shasetsu*) uses every grammar pattern you need for N2, plus the high-frequency formal vocabulary. Understanding how newspapers are structured dramatically speeds up reading.

### Headline Grammar

Japanese newspaper headlines follow special compressed grammar rules:

| Rule | Example | Expanded meaning |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| Omit particles | 政府・緊急会議開催 | 政府が緊急会議を開催 |
| Use に replace する | 経済成長に期待 | 経済成長が期待される |
| Active = passive ambiguous | 首相訪問 | 首相が訪問した OR 首相が訪問された |
| Noun strings | 少子化対策強化要請 | (government) requests strengthening measures for declining birthrate |
| 〜へ instead of 〜に | 日本へ | to Japan |

### Key Newspaper Vocabulary Patterns

**Opening phrases of editorials** (社説の書き出し):
- 〜が問われている (〜 is being questioned / at issue)
- 〜について考えてみたい (I would like to think about 〜)
- 〜の問題が浮上している (The issue of 〜 is coming to the surface)
- 〜が深刻化している (〜 is becoming more serious)

**Formal transitional phrases**:
- 一方で (On the other hand)
- これに対し (In response to this)
- そのためには (For that purpose)
- 問題は〜にある (The problem lies in 〜)

**Conclusion phrases**:
- 〜が求められる (〜 is required/demanded)
- 〜が必要不可欠だ (〜 is indispensable)
- 〜していくべきだろう (We should probably ~)
- 〜なければならない (Must ~)

### Parsing a Sample Editorial Paragraph

**Original** (adapted from a typical 社説):  
「少子化問題は深刻だ。出生率の低下に伴い、労働力不足が懸念されている。政府は対策を強化すべく、様々な施策を打ち出しているが、抜本的な解決には至っていない。」

**Breakdown**:
- 少子化問題は深刻だ — Topic sentence. 深刻 = serious/grave.
- 出生率の低下に伴い — Grammar: 〜に伴い (along with/as a result of). Formal conjunction.
- 労働力不足が懸念されている — Passive: labor shortage is being worried about. 懸念される = to be a concern.
- 政府は対策を強化すべく — Grammar: 〜べく (in order to). Formal purpose.
- 様々な施策を打ち出しているが — 施策 = measure/policy; 打ち出す = to launch/roll out.
- 抜本的な解決には至っていない — 抜本的 = fundamental/radical; 至る = to reach/arrive at.

**Translation**: "The declining birthrate issue is serious. Along with the drop in the birth rate, labor shortages are a growing concern. The government has rolled out various measures in order to strengthen its response, but a fundamental solution has not yet been reached."

### Reading Strategy for Newspaper Articles

1. **Read the headline first** — extract the core subject and action.
2. **Find the lead sentence** — it contains who/what/when/where.
3. **Track logical connectors** — したがって, 一方で, これにより signal reasoning.
4. **Identify conclusion keywords** — 求められる, 必要だ, 重要だ appear in thesis statements.
5. **Don't stop at unknown words** — context often reveals meaning; look up only after first read.

### Practice Reading Sources

- **NHK Web News** (not easy version): nhk.or.jp/news — shorter than print; ideal N2 difficulty.
- **朝日新聞 digital student edition**: Slightly adapted for clarity; free trial available.
- **毎日新聞 やさしい日本語**: Somewhat simplified but retains formal grammar.

## Lesson 3: Formal Conjunctions — Deep Dive Comparisons

### The Core Problem

N2 exam grammar questions frequently test whether you can choose between:
- 〜に基づいて vs. 〜に従って vs. 〜に沿って vs. 〜をもとに

These all mean something like "based on / following," but have distinct nuances.

### Full Comparison: "Following / Based On" Group

| Pattern | Core nuance | Best used with | Cannot use with |
|---------|------------|----------------|-----------------|
| 〜に従って | Following rules/authority; complying | 規則, 指示, 命令, 変化 | Creative inspiration |
| 〜に基づいて | Formal foundational basis (law, data, principles) | 法律, データ, 事実, 証拠 | Casual/everyday basis |
| 〜に沿って | Moving along a path or guideline (no authority) | 計画, 方針, 道, 川 | Rules you must obey |
| 〜をもとに | Source material as foundation (loose) | 資料, 話, 実話, 経験 | Official legal basis |
| 〜に基づく | Attributive form of に基づいて | before nouns directly | predicate position |

### Memory Anchors

- 〜に**従**って = "**follow** (obey)" — authority, rules, natural progression
- 〜に**基**づいて = "based at the **root**" (基 = foundation/base) — solid, formal foundation
- 〜に**沿**って = "running **along**side" (沿 = run along) — no authority; path-following
- 〜をもとに = "using as a **source**" (元 = origin/source) — looser, creative use

### Full Comparison: "Regardless Of" Group

| Pattern | Nuance | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| 〜にかかわらず | Any condition — regardless of how things stand | 結果にかかわらず |
| 〜を問わず | Without asking/checking a category | 性別を問わず |
| 〜いかんによらず | Regardless of the content/details (very formal) | 理由いかんによらず |
| 〜にかかわりなく | Regardless of concern/involvement | 自分の利害にかかわりなく |

### Full Comparison: "Since / Now That" Group

| Pattern | Nuance | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| 〜からには | Since X is given, you MUST do Y | 始めたからには最後までやる |
| 〜以上(は) | Since/now that X is decided (strong commitment) | 決めた以上は迷わない |
| 〜からといって | Just because X (doesn't necessarily mean Y) — DENIAL | 安いからといって悪いわけではない |

Note: からには and 以上は set up obligation. からといって DENIES the natural conclusion.

### Discrimination Drill

Choose the correct pattern:

1. 科学的証拠（　　）、この方針が決まった。  
   a) に基づいて b) に沿って c) に従って  
   **Answer**: a) に基づいて — scientific evidence = formal basis

2. 計画（　　）、作業を進めてください。  
   a) に基づいて b) に沿って c) に従って  
   **Answer**: b) に沿って — following a plan (path-following)

3. 上司の指示（　　）、報告書を書いた。  
   a) に基づいて b) に沿って c) に従って  
   **Answer**: c) に従って — following a superior's instructions (authority)

4. 実際の体験（　　）、この小説を書いた。  
   a) に基づいて b) をもとに c) に沿って  
   **Answer**: b) をもとに — creative source material

## Lesson 4: Academic Writing Patterns

### Core Patterns for Academic and Formal Argumentation

At N2, you must recognize patterns that express academic reasoning. These appear throughout the reading section.

### Pattern Group 1: Expressing what is thought/considered

| Pattern | Usage | Nuance |
|---------|-------|--------|
| 〜と考えられる | It is considered/thought that ~ | Widely accepted view |
| 〜と言える | It can be said that ~ | Supportable claim |
| 〜と考えられている | It is widely considered that ~ | Established view |
| 〜とされている | It is said/held that ~ (by others) | Established convention |
| 〜と言われている | It is said that ~ | Common belief |
| 〜と見られる | It is seen/observed as ~ | Observation-based |

**Usage in context**:  
「この傾向は、社会の変化を反映している**と考えられる**。」  
(This trend can be considered to reflect changes in society.)

「現在の政策には問題がある**と言えるだろう**。」  
(It can be said that there are problems with the current policy.)

### Pattern Group 2: Showing evidence / introducing data

| Pattern | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| 〜が示すように | As ~ shows/indicates |
| 〜によれば / 〜によると | According to ~ |
| 〜からわかるように | As can be seen from ~ |
| 〜を見ると | Looking at ~ |
| 〜に注目すると | When paying attention to ~ |
| 〜に関するデータによれば | According to data regarding ~ |

**Usage in context**:  
「統計**が示すように**、高齢化は急速に進んでいる。」  
(As the statistics show, aging is progressing rapidly.)

「政府の調査**によれば**、若者の就職率が改善している。」  
(According to the government survey, youth employment rates are improving.)

### Pattern Group 3: Introducing a counterargument then asserting

N2 academic texts frequently follow this structure:
1. Acknowledge an opposing view (〜という意見もある)
2. Concede (〜は事実だ / 〜は否定できない)
3. Assert your position (しかし / だが / それでも)

**Expressions for acknowledging other views**:
- 〜という意見もある / 〜という立場もある
- 確かに〜は事実だが (Certainly it's true that ~, but)
- 〜という点は理解できる (I understand the point that ~)
- 〜を否定するわけではないが (I'm not denying ~, but)

**Practice passage analysis**:

「確かに、経済成長は重要な課題である。しかしながら、成長のみを追求することは、環境問題を深刻化させかねない。持続可能な発展こそが、今求められているのではないだろうか。」

Structure:
- 確かに〜である。(Acknowledge) — economic growth is important.
- しかしながら〜かねない。(Concede with risk) — but pursuing growth alone might worsen the environment.
- 〜こそが〜ではないだろうか。(Assert) — isn't it precisely sustainable development that is needed now?

## Lesson 5: Nuanced Negation — The Most-Tested N2 Area

This lesson covers the most commonly confused negation patterns. These appear in grammar discrimination questions on almost every N2 exam.

### The Core Negation Patterns Compared

**Level 1 comparison: わけではない vs. とは限らない**

| わけではない | とは限らない |
|-------------|-------------|
| Denies a SPECIFIC conclusion that could be inferred from context | Denies a UNIVERSAL rule |
| "It's not that (specifically)" | "Not necessarily always true" |
| お金がないわけではない。(It's not that I have no money.) | お金があれば幸せとは限らない。(Having money doesn't always mean happiness.) |

**Level 2 comparison: ないわけではない vs. ないわけにはいかない**

| ないわけではない | ないわけにはいかない |
|----------------|---------------------|
| Double negative = partial admission: "It's not that I don't ~" = "I do, somewhat" | Strong obligation: "Cannot not do" = "Must do" |
| 興味がないわけではない。(It's not that I'm not interested.) = I'm somewhat interested. | 報告しないわけにはいかない。(I can't not report it.) = I must report it. |

**Level 3 comparison: ざるを得ない vs. ないわけにはいかない**

| ざるを得ない | ないわけにはいかない |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Reluctant compulsion (literary/formal) | Social obligation (natural speech) |
| 認めざるを得ない。(I have no choice but to admit.) | 謝らないわけにはいかない。(I can't not apologize.) |
| Focus: I'm forced to despite myself | Focus: Social/moral expectation requires it |

### Negation Pattern Drill

Choose the correct completion:

1. 彼女は優しくない（　　）が、少し厳しい。  
   a) わけではない b) わけにはいかない c) とは限らない  
   **Answer**: a) わけではない — "It's not that she's unkind, but she's a little strict."

2. 高いものが良いとは（　　）。  
   a) わけではない b) 限らない c) いえない  
   **Answer**: b) 限らない — "Expensive things are not necessarily good."

3. みんなが待っているので、帰る（　　）。  
   a) わけではない b) わけにはいかない c) はずがない  
   **Answer**: b) わけにはいかない — "I can't leave since everyone is waiting."

4. 状況を見れば、手伝わ（　　）。  
   a) ざるを得ない b) ないわけにはいかない c) either is correct  
   **Answer**: c) Either is correct — both express obligation; ざるを得ない is more formal.

5. 彼が来ない（　　）。彼は約束を大切にする人だ。  
   a) わけがない b) とは限らない c) わけではない  
   **Answer**: a) わけがない — "There's no way he won't come. He's someone who values promises."

## Lesson 6: Business and Professional Japanese

### The Three Registers of Professional Japanese

| Register | Name | Usage |
|----------|------|-------|
| Polite standard | 丁寧語 (teineigo) | 〜です/〜ます; universal polite |
| Respectful (elevating) | 尊敬語 (sonkeigo) | For superiors/clients |
| Humble (lowering) | 謙譲語 (kenjougo) | For yourself toward superiors |

N2 requires you to recognize all three. Active production of sonkeigo/kenjougo is primarily N1 territory.

### Business Email Structures

Japanese business emails follow a rigid structure:

**Opening**:
- お世話になっております。(Standard opening for existing contact)
- 突然のご連絡、失礼いたします。(For first contact)
- 〜の件でご連絡差し上げました。(Re: [matter], I am writing to you)

**Body patterns**:
- 〜についてご確認いただけますでしょうか。(Could you confirm 〜?)
- 〜をお願い申し上げます。(I humbly request 〜)
- ご不明な点がございましたら、お知らせください。(If there are any unclear points, please let me know)
- 何卒よろしくお願いいたします。(I sincerely ask for your kind consideration)

**Closing**:
- 以上、よろしくお願いいたします。(The above, thank you for your consideration)
- お返事お待ちしております。(I look forward to your reply)

### Key Business Vocabulary

| Japanese | Meaning | Context |
|----------|---------|---------|
| ご確認 | confirmation (honorific) | Asking superior to check something |
| ご連絡 | contact/communication (honorific) | Referring to communication |
| ご検討 | consideration (honorific) | Asking for consideration of a proposal |
| ご回答 | reply/answer (honorific) | Requesting a formal answer |
| お伝えする | to convey (humble) | Conveying a message on behalf of |
| 承知しました | understood; I will do so | Formal acceptance |
| かしこまりました | certainly; very well | Very formal acceptance |
| 恐れ入ります | I am sorry to trouble you | Polite apology/gratitude |
| 取り急ぎ | in haste; quickly (letter close) | End of quick message |
| 〜の件 | regarding ~ / the matter of ~ | Topic identifier |
| 拝受いたしました | I have received (very formal) | Acknowledging receipt |

### Report Writing Patterns

Business reports in Japanese use this structure:

1. **件名** (Subject): Short noun phrase
2. **要旨** (Summary): 1-2 sentences
3. **詳細** (Details): Body paragraphs using formal conjunctions
4. **課題・問題点** (Issues): Using 〜が問題だ、〜に懸念がある
5. **提案** (Proposals): Using 〜を提案する、〜が効果的と思われる
6. **結論** (Conclusion): Using 〜と考えられる、〜が重要だ

## Lesson 7: Complex Reading Strategies

### The N2 Reading Challenge

N2 reading passages are **800–1,000 characters** long — roughly 3–4 times the length of N3 passages. You have limited time (105 minutes total for the entire language knowledge + reading section). Speed and strategic reading are essential.

### Strategy 1: Question-First Reading

Before reading the passage, **read the questions** (but NOT the answer choices). This tells you what to look for:
- Questions about the author's opinion → look for 〜と思う, 〜べきだ, 〜ではないだろうか
- Questions about a specific detail → scan for the keyword from the question
- Questions about the overall point → focus on first and last paragraphs

### Strategy 2: Topic Sentence Extraction

Japanese formal texts follow Western-style paragraph logic:
- **First sentence** of paragraph = topic sentence (most of the time)
- **Last sentence** of paragraph = conclusion/transition

Read only the first and last sentences of each paragraph on your first pass. This gives you ~70% of the information in 30% of the time.

### Strategy 3: Tracking Logical Flow

N2 passages are dense with logical connectors. Map them:

| Connector found | What follows |
|----------------|-------------|
| しかし / ところが | Contrasting or opposing point |
| したがって / そのため | Conclusion or result |
| 一方で / 他方 | Alternative view or contrast |
| 確かに〜が | Acknowledgment + "but" turn follows |
| このように | Summary of previous discussion |
| 〜というわけだ | Conclusion derived from preceding |

### Strategy 4: Pronoun Tracking

N2 passages use demonstrative pronouns (この, その, そのような, こうした) to refer to previously mentioned concepts. Train yourself to track what these refer to — exam questions frequently ask "What does この問題 (this problem) refer to?"

### Practice Technique: Timed Summaries

1. Read a NHK article (standard, not easy).
2. Set a timer for 3 minutes.
3. After reading, write a 2-3 sentence summary in Japanese without looking at the text.
4. Check your summary against the article.

This trains both reading comprehension and the ability to extract main points under time pressure.

## Lesson 8: Listening to Broadcasts and Lectures

### N2 Listening Characteristics

N2 listening passages come from:
- News broadcasts (news reader pace, formal vocabulary)
- Announcements (train stations, department stores)
- Lectures and seminars (academic vocabulary, complex structure)
- Conversations between colleagues or in professional settings

### Key Listening Challenge: Fast Natural Speech

At N2, speech is at near-natural pace. Common phonetic phenomena:

| Phenomenon | Example | What you hear |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| Vowel devoicing | 学生 (gakusei) | ga**k**sei — short vowel disappears between unvoiced consonants |
| て-form linking | 食べている | 食べてる (tabeteru) |
| Contraction | というのは | ってのは |
| Particle drop | 私は行く | 私、行く |
| で ending sentences | そうなんです | そうなんで |

### Lecture Listening Structure

Academic-style N2 listening passages often follow:
1. **Introduction**: 今日は〜についてお話しします / 本日のテーマは〜
2. **First point**: まず、〜について / 第一に〜
3. **Transition**: 次に〜 / 続いて〜 / それでは〜
4. **Examples**: 例えば〜 / 具体的には〜
5. **Conclusion**: まとめると〜 / 以上のことから〜 / 結論として〜

Train yourself to take mental notes at each transition marker.

### N2 Listening Practice Sources

| Source | Level | Why it helps |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| NHK News (audio) | N2-N1 | Standard broadcast Japanese; formal vocabulary |
| Nihongo con Teppei | N3-N2 | Natural monologue; 7-min episodes; Tokyo dialect |
| ゆる言語学ラジオ | N2-N1 | Intellectual discussion; academic vocabulary |
| NHK World Radio Japan | N2-N1 | Slower than domestic NHK; formal content |
| Japanese drama (drama-style) | N3-N2 | Professional settings: 半沢直樹, リーガルハイ |

### Note-Taking Technique for N2 Listening

In the N2 listening section, you have time to read the question BEFORE the audio plays. Use this time to:
1. Identify the type of question (what/who/why/how the person feels)
2. Predict vocabulary that might appear
3. During listening, mark relevant information only
4. Answer based on what you marked, not what you remember holistically

## Lesson 9: Common N2 Trap Grammar

### Trap Type 1: Patterns that look identical but differ by verb form

**〜次第** — two completely different meanings!

| Structure | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| V-masu + 次第 | As soon as (complete, then do Y) | 準備でき次第、出発します。|
| Noun + 次第で | Depending on | 天気次第で変わります。|
| 〜次第だ (explanation) | It all depends on | 成功するかは努力次第だ。|

**〜ところ** — three meanings depending on tense!

| Structure | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| V-dictionary + ところ | About to do (not done yet) | 今から出かけるところです。|
| V-て(い)る + ところ | In the middle of doing | 今食べているところです。|
| V-た + ところ | Just finished / found that | 調べたところ、問題があった。|

### Trap Type 2: Patterns with similar meaning but different nuance

**〜ために vs. 〜ように**

| 〜ために | 〜ように |
|---------|---------|
| Purpose: volitional action | Purpose: reaching a state (often with potential/negative) |
| Subject of both clauses can be same or different | Subject of purpose clause is usually yourself changing |
| 合格するために勉強する。(Study in order to pass.) | 合格できるように勉強する。(Study so that I'll be able to pass.) |

**〜はずだ vs. 〜べきだ vs. 〜ことになっている**

| Pattern | Meaning | Nuance |
|---------|---------|--------|
| 〜はずだ | Should be (expectation/logic) | Based on reasoning, I expect |
| 〜べきだ | Should (moral/logical obligation) | One ought to |
| 〜ことになっている | It is scheduled/supposed to | External rule/schedule |

Example:
- 彼は来る**はずだ**。(He should be coming — I expect it.)
- 約束は守る**べきだ**。(Promises should be kept — moral.)
- 会議は3時から**ことになっている**。(The meeting is scheduled to start at 3.)

### Trap Type 3: Formal patterns with casual-looking forms

**〜ものだ (things about life)** vs. **〜ものだ (emotional reflection)**

| Usage | Example |
|-------|---------|
| General truth/custom | 人は誰でも間違えるものだ。(People naturally make mistakes.) |
| Emotional recollection | 子供のころは、よく遊んだものだ。(I used to play a lot as a child.) |
| Expressing strong desire | 一度は行ってみたいものだ。(I really want to go there once.) |

### Trap Type 4: かねる vs. かねない (opposite meanings!)

| Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| V-masu + **かねる** | Cannot; reluctant to (REFUSAL) | お答えしかねます。|
| V-masu + **かねない** | Might; risk of (CONCERN about bad outcome) | 問題を引き起こしかねない。|

**Memory trick**: かねる = "I can't do it." かねない = "It MIGHT [regrettably] happen."

### Practice: Identify the Trap

Which sentence is correct?

1a. 書類が届き次第、確認します。  
1b. 書類が届いたら次第、確認します。  
**Answer**: 1a — V-masu + 次第. 1b is grammatically wrong.

2a. 健康のために、タバコをやめた。  
2b. 健康になれるように、タバコをやめた。  
**Answer**: Both are acceptable, but 2a is more natural here. ために with a concrete action verb; ように with a potential or state change.

3a. 彼はもう帰ったはずだ。  
3b. 彼はもう帰ったことになっている。  
**Answer**: 3a = I expect he's gone home (my reasoning). 3b = It has been decided/is scheduled that he went home (external/formal).
