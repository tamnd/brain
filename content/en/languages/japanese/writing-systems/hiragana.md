## Dakuten Variants (20 characters — voiced)

| | a | i | u | e | o |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| g | が (ga) | ぎ (gi) | ぐ (gu) | げ (ge) | ご (go) |
| z | ざ (za) | じ (ji) | ず (zu) | ぜ (ze) | ぞ (zo) |
| d | だ (da) | ぢ (ji*) | づ (zu*) | で (de) | ど (do) |
| b | ば (ba) | び (bi) | ぶ (bu) | べ (be) | ぼ (bo) |

*ぢ and づ are rarely used; じ and ず are standard for "ji" and "zu" sounds.

## Special Characters

| Character | Name | Usage |
|-----------|------|-------|
| っ | small tsu | Geminate consonant: doubles next consonant. 切手 kitte (stamp) |
| ー | long vowel mark | Extends previous vowel (more common in katakana, also used in hiragana) |

## Special Reading Rules

| Character | Normal reading | Special reading | Context |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|---------|
| は | ha | wa | When used as topic particle |
| へ | he | e | When used as direction particle |
| を | wo | o | Object particle (always "o" in speech) |
| じ vs ぢ | ji | ji | ぢ used only in specific words (ちぢむ) |
| ず vs づ | zu | zu | づ used only in specific words (つづく) |
