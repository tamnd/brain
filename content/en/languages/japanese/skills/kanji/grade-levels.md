The Kyoiku kanji (教育漢字, literally "education kanji") are the 1,026 kanji mandated by Japan's Ministry of Education for Japanese elementary school students to learn by the end of 6th grade. They represent the core functional literacy foundation and cover most JLPT N5–N3 kanji.

## Overview

| Grade | Count | Cumulative | JLPT Overlap |
|-------|-------|-----------|--------------|
| Grade 1 | 80 | 80 | Mostly N5 |
| Grade 2 | 160 | 240 | N5–N4 |
| Grade 3 | 200 | 440 | N4–N3 |
| Grade 4 | 202 | 642 | N3 |
| Grade 5 | 193 | 835 | N3–N2 |
| Grade 6 | 191 | 1,026 | N2 |

## Using This Reference

For JLPT study, the most practical approach is:
1. **N5 target**: Master Grade 1 kanji (80) — they form the core of N5
2. **N4 target**: Master Grade 1–2 (240) + additional N4 kanji not in Kyoiku list
3. **N3 target**: Complete Grade 1–3 (440) provides a strong N3 foundation
4. **N2 target**: Grades 1–5 (835) covers ~80% of N2 kanji
5. **N1 target**: Complete all 1,026 Kyoiku kanji + remaining Joyo kanji

Note that not all JLPT kanji are in the Kyoiku list — some N2/N1 kanji are in the "general Joyo" category (grade 9–10 in the Joyo list), taught in high school or not explicitly taught at any grade level.
