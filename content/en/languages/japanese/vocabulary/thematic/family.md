Japanese family vocabulary has two sets of words: one for referring to your own family (plain/humble) and one for referring to someone else's family (honorific/polite). This distinction is important at N4+ level.

## Example Sentences

- 私の家族は5人です。Watashi no kazoku wa gonin desu. — My family has five members.
- 兄は医者です。Ani wa isha desu. — My older brother is a doctor.
- お母さんはどこにいますか？ Okaasan wa doko ni imasu ka? — Where is your mother?
- うちの子供は二人います。Uchi no kodomo wa futari imasu. — We have two children.
- 父と母は東京に住んでいます。Chichi to haha wa Tōkyō ni sunde imasu. — My father and mother live in Tokyo.
- 祖母は90歳です。Sobo wa kyuujussai desu. — My grandmother is 90 years old.
