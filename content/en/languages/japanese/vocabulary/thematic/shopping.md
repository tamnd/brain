## Shopping Vocabulary — JLPT N1

At the N1 level, shopping vocabulary transcends basic transactions. It encompasses the nuances of consumer behavior, high-end retail, economic terminology, and the sophisticated language used in professional customer service or formal negotiations. This list focuses on the vocabulary required to navigate complex retail environments, understand consumer trends, and engage in formal business-to-consumer interactions.

## Key Phrases

1. **お買い上げありがとうございます。** | O-kaiage arigatō gozaimasu. | Thank you for your purchase.
2. **在庫を確認してまいります。** | Zaiko o kakunin shite mairimasu. | I will go and check the stock.
3. **多少のお値引きは可能でしょうか。** | Tashō no o-nebiki wa kanō deshō ka. | Is a slight discount possible?
4. **予算内で収める必要があります。** | Yosan-nai ni osameru hitsuyō ga arimasu. | I need to keep it within budget.
5. **こちらの商品は現在、欠品中でございます。** | Kochira no shōhin wa genzai, keppin-chū de gozaimasu. | This item is currently out of stock.
6. **返品・交換は一週間以内にお願いします。** | Henpin/kōkan wa isshūkan inai ni onegai shimasu. | Please return or exchange within one week.
7. **領収書をいただけますか。** | Ryōshūsho o itadakemasu ka. | Could I have a receipt?
8. **品質を吟味した上で決めたいと思います。** | Hinshitsu o ginmi shita ue de kimetai to omoimasu. | I would like to decide after carefully examining the quality.
9. **分割払いは可能ですか。** | Bunkatsu-barai wa kanō desu ka. | Is installment payment possible?
10. **価格の駆け引きは苦手です。** | Kakaku no kakehiki wa nigate desu. | I am not good at price bargaining.

## Practice Dialogue

**店員:** いらっしゃいませ。何かお探しでしょうか。
(Ten'in: Irasshaimase. Nanika o-sagashi deshō ka.)
*Clerk: Welcome. Is there something you are looking for?*

**客:** はい、贈答用の品を探しているのですが、予算が限られていまして。
(Kyaku: Hai, zōtōyō no shina o sagashite iru no desu ga, yosan ga kagirarete imashite.)
*Customer: Yes, I'm looking for a gift, but my budget is limited.*

**店員:** かしこまりました。こちらの厳選されたセットはいかがでしょうか。
(Ten'in: Kashikomarimashita. Kochira no gensen sareta setto wa ikaga deshō ka.)
*Clerk: Understood. How about this carefully selected set?*

**客:** 素敵な趣向ですね。ただ、少し予算オーバーです。お値引きは可能ですか？
(Kyaku: Suteki na shukō desu ne. Tada, sukoshi yosan ōbā desu. O-nebiki wa kanō desu ka?)
*Customer: It's a lovely idea. However, it's a bit over budget. Is a discount possible?*

**店員:** 申し訳ございません。こちらの商品は人気のため、値引きはいたしかねます。
(Ten'in: Mōshiwake gozaimasen. Kochira no shōhin wa ninki no tame, nebiki wa itashikanemasu.)
*Clerk: I apologize. Because this item is popular, I cannot offer a discount.*

**客:** そうですか。では、在庫がある他のものを見せていただけますか。
(Kyaku: Sō desu ka. Dewa, zaiko ga aru hoka no mono o misete itadakemasu ka.)
*Customer: I see. Then, could you show me other items you have in stock?*

## Self-Study Tips

1. **Contextual Reading:** Read Japanese business news (e.g., Nikkei) to see how words like *高騰 (kōtō)* and *需要 (juyō)* are used in real economic contexts.
2. **Roleplay:** Practice the "Bargaining" phrases with a language partner. Focus on the polite, indirect way Japanese speakers negotiate prices (*"～は可能でしょうか"*).
3. **Flashcard Categorization:** Group these words into "Retailer" vs. "Consumer" categories to understand the perspective of the speaker.
4. **Real-world Application:** Next time you are in a store, mentally label the items using N1 vocabulary (e.g., *在庫 (zaiko)*, *包装 (hōsō)*, *欠品 (keppin)*).
