Food vocabulary is essential for daily life in Japan and appears frequently in N5–N4 JLPT material. Japanese food culture is rich and vocabulary-dense.

## Basic Food Vocabulary

| Japanese | Reading | Romaji | English | JLPT |
|----------|---------|--------|---------|------|
| 食べ物 | たべもの | tabemono | food | N5 |
| 飲み物 | のみもの | nomimono | drink; beverage | N5 |
| 料理 | りょうり | ryouri | cooking; cuisine; dish | N4 |
| 食事 | しょくじ | shokuji | meal | N4 |
| 朝ご飯 | あさごはん | asagohan | breakfast | N5 |
| 昼ご飯 | ひるごはん | hirugohan | lunch | N5 |
| 夕ご飯 | ゆうごはん | yuugohan | dinner | N5 |
| おやつ | おやつ | oyatsu | snack; between-meal treat | N4 |
| お腹が空く | おなかがすく | onaka ga suku | to be hungry | N5 |
| お腹いっぱい | おなかいっぱい | onaka ippai | full; stuffed | N5 |
| 美味しい | おいしい | oishii | delicious; tasty | N5 |
| まずい | まずい | mazui | bad-tasting; disgusting | N4 |
