Time vocabulary is fundamental to everyday Japanese and is heavily tested at N5–N4 level. Japanese has clear, systematic patterns for most time expressions.

## Days of the Week

| Japanese | Reading | Romaji | English | Character meaning |
|----------|---------|--------|---------|-------------------|
| 月曜日 | げつようび | Getsuyoubi | Monday | Moon day |
| 火曜日 | かようび | Kayoubi | Tuesday | Fire day |
| 水曜日 | すいようび | Suiyoubi | Wednesday | Water day |
| 木曜日 | もくようび | Mokuyoubi | Thursday | Wood day |
| 金曜日 | きんようび | Kin'youbi | Friday | Gold/Metal day |
| 土曜日 | どようび | Doyoubi | Saturday | Earth day |
| 日曜日 | にちようび | Nichiyoubi | Sunday | Sun day |
| 何曜日 | なんようび | nan'youbi | what day of the week? | |

The 7 days follow the 5 Chinese elements (wood, fire, earth, metal, water) plus sun and moon. The 曜日 (youbi) suffix means "day of the week."

## Example Sentences

- 今日は何曜日ですか？ Kyou wa nan'youbi desu ka? — What day of the week is today?
- 今、何時ですか？ Ima, nanji desu ka? — What time is it now?
- 駅まで十分かかります。Eki made juppun kakarimasu. — It takes ten minutes to the station.
- 三時ごろ来てください。Sanji goro kite kudasai. — Please come around 3 o'clock.
- 昨日から頭が痛いです。Kinou kara atama ga itai desu. — My head has been hurting since yesterday.
- 来週の金曜日に会いましょう。Raishuu no kin'youbi ni aimashou. — Let's meet next Friday.
