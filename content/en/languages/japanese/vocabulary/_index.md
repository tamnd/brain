## The Three Layers of Japanese Vocabulary

### Wago (和語) — Native Japanese
- Usually written in hiragana or with kun'yomi kanji
- Core everyday vocabulary: body, nature, emotions, basic actions
- Examples: 水 (みず, water), 山 (やま, mountain), 食べる (たべる, eat), うれしい (happy)

### Kango (漢語) — Sino-Japanese
- Written in kanji using on'yomi readings
- ~60% of dictionary vocabulary; academic, formal, technical
- Examples: 経済 (けいざい, economy), 科学 (かがく, science), 社会 (しゃかい, society)

### Gairaigo (外来語) — Foreign Loanwords
- Written in katakana; mostly from English
- Technology, food, pop culture, modern lifestyle
- Examples: テレビ (terebi, TV), パソコン (pasokon, PC), アニメ (anime)

## Vocabulary Learning Principles

1. **SRS is non-negotiable**: Anki with daily reviews is the most efficient retention method
2. **Frequency-first**: Core 2K → Core 6K → sentence mining gives the best coverage
3. **Context over isolation**: Example sentences create stronger memories than single-word cards
4. **Kanji + reading together**: Always learn the kanji form alongside the reading; don't rely on romaji
5. **Encounter multiple times**: New words need ~7–10 exposures in different contexts to fully internalize
