## 調べる — しらべる (shiraberu)

**Part of speech:** verb-ru
**JLPT level:** N4
**Reading:** しらべる (shiraberu)
**Meaning:** to investigate, look up, check, examine

## Word Breakdown

**調** (Kanji): 
*   **Meaning:** investigate, tune, tone, prepare
*   **On-yomi:** チョウ (chō)
*   **Kun-yomi:** しら(べる) (shiraberu), ととの(う) (totonou)

The kanji **調** combines the radical for "speech/words" (言) with the character for "surrounding/all around" (周). It carries the nuance of putting things in order or checking the details of something to ensure it is in harmony or correct.

## All Readings

*   **On-yomi (音読み):** チョウ (chō)
*   **Kun-yomi (訓読み):** しら(べる) (shiraberu)

*In this word, the kun-yomi reading is used.*

## Common Mistakes

*   ❌ **辞書を調べる** (Jisho o shiraberu) → ✓ **辞書で調べる** (Jisho de shiraberu) — When using a tool to perform an action, use the particle **で** (de), not **を** (o).
*   ❌ **調べるする** (Shiraberu suru) → ✓ **調べる** (Shiraberu) — *Shiraberu* is a standard verb-ru; it does not take *suru*.

## In Context — Mini Dialogue

**A:** この漢字、読み方がわからないな。
(Kono kanji, yomikata ga wakaranai na.)
*I don't know the reading for this kanji.*

**B:** スマホで調べてみたら？
(Sumaho de shirabete mitara?)
*Why don't you look it up on your smartphone?*

**A:** そうするよ。ありがとう。
(Sō suru yo. Arigatō.)
*I'll do that. Thanks.*

## Usage Notes

*Shiraberu* is a versatile verb used for both physical and intellectual "checking." It ranges from simple tasks like looking up a word in a dictionary or checking a train schedule, to more serious actions like police investigations or scientific research.

When you want to emphasize that you are looking into something *in advance* to be prepared, you can use the construction **調べておく** (shirabete oku). This is a very common and natural way to express that you have done your homework or preparation before an event or meeting.

While *shiraberu* is the most common term, in formal or academic contexts, you might see **調査する** (chōsa suru). Use *shiraberu* for daily life, and *chōsa suru* for formal reports, government inquiries, or professional research.
