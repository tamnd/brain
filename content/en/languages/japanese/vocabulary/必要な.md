## 必要な — ひつような (hitsuyou na)

**Part of speech:** adj-na (adjectival noun)
**JLPT level:** N4
**Reading:** ひつような (hitsuyou na)
**Meaning:** necessary, needed

## Word Breakdown

*   **必 (ひつ):** Certain, inevitable.
    *   On-yomi: ヒツ (hitsu)
    *   Kun-yomi: かなら(ず) (kanarazu)
*   **要 (よう):** Need, essence, pivot.
    *   On-yomi: ヨウ (you)
    *   Kun-yomi: い(る) (iru), かなめ (kaname)

Together, **必要 (hitsuyou)** literally means "the essence of what is certain/inevitable," which translates to "necessity."

## Common Mistakes

❌ **必要だもの** → ✓ **必要なもの**
*   **Explanation:** Since *hitsuyou* is an *adj-na*, you must use the particle **な** (na) when modifying a noun. *Hitsuyou na mono* (necessary thing).

❌ **私は水が必要します** → ✓ **私には水が必要です**
*   **Explanation:** *Hitsuyou* is not a verb. You cannot conjugate it like *suru*. Use the structure [Noun] + が + 必要です (is necessary).

## In Context — Mini Dialogue

**A:** 明日の会議の準備は終わった？
(Ashita no kaigi no junbi wa owatta?)
*Did you finish the preparation for tomorrow's meeting?*

**B:** まだです。資料を作るために時間が必要です。
(Mada desu. Shiryou o tsukuru tame ni jikan ga hitsuyou desu.)
*Not yet. I need time to make the materials.*

**A:** 必要なものがあれば言ってね。
(Hitsuyou na mono ga areba itte ne.)
*Let me know if you need anything.*

## Usage Notes

*   **Grammar:** *Hitsuyou* functions as an *adj-na* (e.g., *hitsuyou na shorui* - necessary documents) or as a noun (e.g., *hitsuyou ga aru* - there is a need). It is very common to see it used with the particle *ga* to indicate what is needed: *[X] ga hitsuyou desu* (I need X / X is necessary).
*   **Nuance:** While *hitsuyou* is a neutral, standard term for "necessary," it can sound slightly formal or objective. In casual conversation, people often use the verb *iru* (要る) instead (e.g., *Sore, iru?* - Do you need that?).
*   **Collocations:** You will frequently see it paired with *fukaketsu* (indispensable) in the set phrase *hitsuyou fukaketsu* (absolutely essential), which is common in business and formal writing.
