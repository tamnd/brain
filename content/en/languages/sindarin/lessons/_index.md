This course teaches Sindarin — the Grey-elven language of Middle-earth created by J.R.R. Tolkien — from first sounds to advanced composition. Tolkien was a professional philologist, and Sindarin is a fully consistent linguistic system with its own phonology, morphology, and grammar. It draws heavily on Welsh in its sound patterns and uses a system of initial consonant mutations that will feel familiar to Celtic language learners.

The 41 lessons are organised into three levels. **Beginner** lessons (1–10) cover pronunciation, core vocabulary, basic phrases attested in Tolkien's texts, and the fundamentals of plural formation. **Intermediate** lessons (11–31) work through the mutation system, verb conjugation across all tenses, prepositions, numbers, and specialised vocabulary domains. **Advanced** lessons (32–41) address the finer points of the suffix system, dialect differences between Gondorian and Doriathrin Sindarin, rare mutation types, elision, and the art of name-making. By the end you will be able to read Tolkien's Sindarin passages with confidence, construct grammatically correct original sentences, and compose Elvish names and poetry.

A note on sources: attested forms — words and phrases that appear in Tolkien's published writings — are marked **S.** (Sindarin) or **N.** (Noldorin, an earlier form that feeds directly into Sindarin). Scholarly reconstructions accepted by the Neo-Sindarin community are marked **ᴺS.**. Where multiple scholars disagree, the most conservative reconstruction is used and the uncertainty is noted.
