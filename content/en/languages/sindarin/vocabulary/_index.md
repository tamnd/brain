Sindarin vocabulary is different from any natural language vocabulary you've encountered. Instead of a fixed dictionary, there are overlapping layers of Tolkien's own revisions — each with a different period label — plus fan reconstructions. Understanding these layers is essential before memorizing any words.

## The Layer System

| Period | Label | Time | Usability |
|--------|-------|------|-----------|
| **Sindarin** | S. | 1951–1973 (LotR period) | Fully canonical; always prefer |
| **Noldorin** | N. | 1930–1950 | Phonologically compatible; usually usable |
| **Gnomish** | G. | 1910–1920 | Earliest; often incompatible; rarely used |
| **Neo-Sindarin** | ᴺS. | Post-1973 | Fan reconstruction; must be labeled |
| **ᴺS. [N.]** | — | — | Neo-Sindarin derived from Noldorin original |

Always label which period a word comes from in your notes. In compositions, indicate ᴺS. words clearly.

## Word Formation

Sindarin builds many words through **compounding** (combining two roots):

| Pattern | Example | Breakdown |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| Modifier + Head | *Minas Tirith* | *minas* (tower) + *tirith* (watching) |
| Head + Modifier | *Barad-dûr* | *barad* (tower) + *dûr* (dark) |
| Prefix + Root | *Edhelharn* | *edhel* (elf) + *harn* (stone) |

The second element of a compound often undergoes **lenition**:
- *Minas* + *tirith* → *Minas Tirith* (t unchanged — tirith starts with t which would → d under lenition, but compound names often resist)
- *Barad* + *dûr* → *Barad-dûr* (d unchanged in this case)

Lenition in compounds is inconsistent — follow attested examples rather than strict rules.

## Building Vocabulary: Recommended Approach

1. **Start with the 50 most common attested words** — these appear in Tolkien's texts
2. **Learn the major word-building elements** (prefixes, suffixes) — multiply your vocabulary
3. **Learn thematically** — nature words first (they appear in place names you already know)
4. **Use Parf Edhellen daily** — look up every word in Tolkien's Sindarin passages
5. **Build a personal Anki deck** — create flashcards from words you encounter

## Important Vocabulary Notes

- **Many words are known only from place names and character names** — infer meaning from context
- *Amon* (hill), *dûn* (west), *galadh* (tree) appear in dozens of names you already know
- Learning Sindarin vocabulary is inseparable from understanding Tolkien's place names and character names throughout LotR
