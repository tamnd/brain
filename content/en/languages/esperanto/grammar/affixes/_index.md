## Overview

Affixes are one of Esperanto's most powerful features. A single set of **9 prefixes** and **18+ suffixes** allows you to derive thousands of words from a smaller set of roots. Crucially, these affixes are themselves roots — they can stand alone as words when given an appropriate ending.

**Example of productivity:** From the single root *labor-* (work):
- labori (to work), laboro (work/n.), labora (working/adj.), labore (laboriously)
- laboristo (worker), laborejo (workplace), laboraĵo (a work/product)
- laborema (hardworking), laborado (sustained work), laborado (continuous working)
- ellabori (to work out/develop), prilabori (to work on/process)
- maltaŭge + labori combinations...

## Prefixes (9)

### bo- — in-law relationship

Forms words for in-law family relationships.

| Root | With bo- | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| patro | bopatro | father-in-law |
| patrino | bopatrino | mother-in-law |
| frato | bofrato | brother-in-law |
| fratino | bofratino | sister-in-law |
| filo | bofilo | son-in-law |
| filino | bofilino | daughter-in-law |
| edzo | boedzo | (step-husband / spouse's former husband — less common) |

**Usage note:** *bo-* attaches to any family relationship word to indicate the equivalent by marriage.

### dis- — dispersion, separation

Indicates that an action scatters, divides, or distributes outward.

| Root | With dis- | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| doni | disdoni | to distribute, hand out |
| iri | disiri | to disperse, go in different directions |
| ŝiri | disŝiri | to tear apart |
| rompi | disrompi | to shatter, break into pieces |

| flugi | disflugi | to fly apart, scatter (of birds) |
| meti | dismeti | to spread out, lay apart |

**Contrast:** *disdoni* (distribute to many) vs. *doni* (give to one).

| Root | With fi- | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| homo | fihomo | scoundrel, despicable person |
| aĵo | fiaĵo | vile thing, nastiness |

| Root | With ge- | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| patro | gepatroj | parents (father and mother) |
| frato | gefratoj | siblings (brothers and sisters) |
| edzo | geedzoj | married couple (husband and wife) |

| instruisto | geinstruistoj | teachers (mixed gender) |

**Usage note:** *ge-* is most natural in the plural. In the singular it's unusual unless both people are meant as a unit (*la gepatroj* = the parents).
