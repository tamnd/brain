## What You Can Do at C2

C2 represents complete mastery of Esperanto. At this level you can:
- Understand virtually everything heard or read with ease
- Summarize information from different spoken and written sources, reconstructing arguments and accounts in a coherent presentation
- Express yourself spontaneously, very fluently, and precisely — differentiating finer shades of meaning even in complex situations
- Write sophisticated literary and technical texts
- Interpret professionally between Esperanto and other languages
- Teach Esperanto at advanced levels with full command of grammar and pedagogy
- Contribute to the evolution of the language — neologisms, terminology work
- Function as an authority in Esperantological debate
- Produce original creative work (novels, poetry, drama, film scripts)

---

## Characteristics of a C2 Speaker

A C2 Esperanto speaker has internalized not just the grammar and vocabulary, but the **culture, aesthetics, and spirit** of the Esperanto community:

- **Denaskuloj** (native speakers) are by definition C2 — approximately 1,000–2,000 people worldwide were raised speaking Esperanto as a first language
- **Longstanding fluent speakers** who have been active in the community for 10+ years
- Scholars of **Esperantology** — the academic study of Esperanto and interlinguistics
- **Translators and interpreters** who work professionally in Esperanto
- Authors published in Esperanto literary journals

## The Fundamento de Esperanto

Any C2 speaker should have read and understood the **Fundamento de Esperanto** — Zamenhof's founding document from 1905, which consists of:
1. **Antaŭparolo** — Preface
2. **Gramatiko** — The 16 grammar rules
3. **Ekzercaro** — Exercise collection (graduated sentences)
4. **Universala Vortaro** — Universal vocabulary (the original root list)

The Fundamento is the untouchable core of the language — even the Akademio de Esperanto cannot change it. Understanding it gives insight into the original spirit and design of Esperanto.

## The Universala Kongreso

The **Universala Kongreso** (UK) is the annual world congress of Esperanto, held in a different country each year since 1905 (with interruptions during the World Wars). Attendance runs from 2,000–4,000 people; speakers come from 60–90 countries.

For a C2 speaker the UK is the ultimate environment: a full week of Esperanto 24/7, covering everything from academic symposia to amateur theater to cultural excursions. Major events include:
- **Ĝenerala Programo** — plenary sessions, culture, entertainment
- **Internacia Junulara Kongreso (IJK)** — youth parallel event
- **ILEI konferenco** — teachers' conference
- **TEJO seminaro** — youth organization sessions
- **Scholarly symposia** — linguistics, pedagogy, Esperantology

## Denaskuloj — Native Speakers

Approximately 1,000–2,000 people worldwide learned Esperanto as their first language (*denaskuloj* — from the root *naskiĝi*, to be born). These are typically children of Esperantist parents who chose to raise their children in Esperanto.

The most famous denaskulo family is the **Schwartz family**; many denaskuloj are active in the community today. Their speech is naturalistic and often deviates slightly from textbook grammar, providing a fascinating window into how Esperanto evolves as a living language.

## Continued Learning at C2

Even at C2, there is always more to explore:
- **Historical Esperanto** — reading texts from 1887–1910 shows the evolution of the language
- **Dialects and registers** — formal vs. colloquial, different national community styles
- **Rare roots** — the full *Plena Ilustrita Vortaro* contains thousands of roots you may not yet know
- **Comparative linguistics** — comparing Esperanto structures to natural languages enriches your understanding of both
- **Original creation** — writing original literature is a lifelong endeavor with no ceiling
